## silver
---
- **traditional:** 銀
- **simplified:** 银
- **pronunciation:** yín
- **definition:** silver
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 白银(白銀)
    - bái yín
    - silver
  - 银仔(銀仔)
    - yín zǎi
    - coins
  - 两蚊银(两蚊銀)
    - liǎng wén yín
    - two-dollar coin
  - 几千银(几千銀)
    - jǐ qiān yín
    - a few thousand bucks
- **etymology (銀):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phono-semantic: semantic: 金 (jīn, "metal") + phonetic: 艮 (gèn, "stopping")
  - **interpretation:** 金 (jīn, "metal") is the semantic determinative, marking the character as related to metal. 艮 (gèn, "stopping") provides the sound, approximating the historical reading that led to yín; the metal determinative fits the core meaning "silver" and its extension to money.
  - **component characters:**
    - 金
      - jīn
      - metal
    - 艮
      - gèn
      - stopping
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%8A%80
  - **simplification rule (银):** Simplified by replacing the left-side 金 with the simplified metal radical 钅 while retaining the phonetic 艮.
