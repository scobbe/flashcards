## metal
### sub-component
#### silver
---
- **traditional:** 金
- **simplified:** 金
- **pronunciation:** jīn
- **definition:** metal
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 合金 (hé jīn, "alloy")
  - 现(現)金 (xiàn jīn, "cash")
  - 资(資)金 (zī jīn, "funds")
  - 金玉良言 (jīn yù liáng yán, "precious advice")
- **etymology (金):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic: semantic: 士 (shì, "ritual axe") + semantic: 吕(呂) (lǚ, "two blocks of metal") + phonetic: 亼 (jí, "open mouth") → 金.
  - **interpretation:** 亼 (jí, "open mouth") serves as the phonetic, reflecting an OC reading close to jīn. 士 (shì, "ritual axe") and 吕(呂) (lǚ, "two blocks of metal") supply the semantic field of metal, matching the modern senses of metal and gold. Shuowen’s alternative view sees metal in earth, but early forms lack earth; the meaning developed from “copper” to “metals” and later “gold.”
  - **component characters:**
    - 士 (shì, "ritual axe")
    - 吕(呂) (lǚ, "two blocks of metal")
    - 呂 (lǚ, "two blocks of metal")
    - 亼 (jí, "open mouth")
