Translingual

[edit]

Glyph origin

[edit]

Variant of 兌.

Han character

[edit]

兑 (Kangxi radical 10, 儿+5, 7 strokes, cangjie input 金口竹山 (CRHU) or 難金口竹山 (XCRHU), four-corner 8021, composition ⿱丷兄 or ⿱𠮦儿)

Derived characters

[edit]
  • 説(说), 税, 悦, 脱, 鋭(锐), 閲(阅), 敚, 蜕

[edit]

Chinese

[edit]

Etymology 1

[edit]
For pronunciation and definitions of 兑 – see 兌 (“opening, aperture; one of the sense organs; etc.”).
(This character is the simplified and variant traditional form of 兌).
Notes:
  • Simplified Chinese is mainly used in Mainland China, Malaysia, and Singapore.
  • Traditional Chinese is mainly used in Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan.

Etymology 2

[edit]
For pronunciation and definitions of 兑 – see 免 (“to take off; to remove from the body; to avoid; to escape; to evade; etc.”).
(This character is a variant form of 免).

Japanese

[edit]

Kanji

[edit]
兑

(Hyōgai kanji)

  1. exchange
  2. convert
  3. dui (one of the trigrams of the I Ching: swamp, west)

Readings

[edit]
  • On (unclassified): だ (da)、たい (tai)、えい (ei)
  • Kun: よろこぶ (yorokobu)

Usage notes

[edit]

This character lacks JIS support and is not used on its own. 兌 (U+514C) is used instead. However, 兑 is used as the component form of several jōyō kanji such as 説 税 悦 脱 鋭.

In the oracle bone script, it is a ideogrammic compound (會意 / 会意): 八 (“speech; breath”) + 口 (“mouth”) + 人 (“person”) – speech; original character of 說 (OC *hljod, “to speak”). It is also a phono-semantic compound (形聲 / 形声, OC *l'oːds): phonetic 㕣 (OC *lon) + semantic 人 (“person”).

Alternatively, 八 may be interpreted as smile lines above the mouth (Gao Hongjin) or as meaning “to divide” (Lin Yiguang, i.e. the mouth is divided when smiling); in either case, the character would be the original character of 悅 (OC *lod, “pleased”).

Definitions

[edit]

兌

  1. (literary, obsolete, anatomy) opening, aperture; (especially) one of the sense organs (eyes, ears, nose, mouth)
  2. (literary, obsolete) (of a way or path) to clear; to make accessible
  3. ☱, the 2nd of the 8 trigrams
    1. the west
  4. 58th hexagram of the I Ching
  5. to exchange; to redeem
    1. (finance) to redeem; to withdraw; to exchange
    2. (xiangqi) to sacrifice one's piece to capture an opponent's piece; similar to an exchange sacrifice in chess
  6. (historical, trading) to weigh gold or silver as currency using a balance
  7. to mix with, blend with
    兌水／兑水  ―  duì shuǐ  ―  to dilute with water
    • 韋四太爺就叫把這罈酒拿出來，兌上十斤新酒。 [Written Vernacular Chinese, trad.]
      韦四太爷就叫把这坛酒拿出来，兑上十斤新酒。 [Written Vernacular Chinese, simp.]
      From: Wu Jingzi, The Scholars, 1750 CE
      Wéi sì tàiyé jiù jiào bǎ zhè tán jiǔ ná chūlái, duì shàng shí jīn xīn jiǔ. [Pinyin]
      (please add an English translation of this quotation)
  8. (historical) Duì (a skilled craftsperson who makes halberds)
  9. a surname

Noun

[edit]

兌(だ) • (da) 

  1. the second of the eight trigrams of the I Ching ☱
    Hypernym: 八卦
    Coordinate terms: 乾, 兌, 離, 震, 巽, 坎, 艮, 坤
  2. the fifty-eighth of the sixty-four hexagrams of the I Ching ䷹
    Hypernym: 六十四卦
