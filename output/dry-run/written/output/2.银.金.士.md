## ritual axe
### sub-component
#### metal
---
- **traditional:** 士
- **simplified:** 士
- **pronunciation:** shì
- **definition:** ritual axe
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 士兵 (shìbīng, "enlisted soldier")
  - 士气(氣) (shìqì, "troop morale")
  - 护(護)士 (hùshì, "hospital nurse")
  - 博士 (bóshì, "doctoral degree")
- **etymology (士):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: depiction of a ritual axe → semantic: "soldier" → semantic: "officer, intellectual".
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a ritual axe, a concrete emblem of authority and martial status. The meaning broadened to denote a class of trained men, hence "soldier", and later extended to rank and learning, yielding "officer" and by extension "intellectual" in civil contexts.

    - None, character is in atomic form
