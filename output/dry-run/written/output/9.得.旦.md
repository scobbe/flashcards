## dawn
### sub-component
#### (1) structural particle (verb complements); (2) to get, obtain; (3) must, have to
---
- **traditional:** 旦
- **simplified:** 旦
- **pronunciation:** dàn
- **definition:** dawn
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 元旦 (yuán dàn, "New Year's Day")
  - 一旦 (yí dàn, "once it happens")
  - 通宵达(達)旦 (tōng xiāo dá dàn, "all night till dawn")
  - 只争(爭)旦夕 (zhǐ zhēng dàn xī, "race against time")
- **etymology (旦):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: semantic: 日 (rì, "sun") + phonetic: 丁 (dīng, "fourth heavenly stem").
  - **interpretation:** Combines the semantic 日 (rì, "sun") with the phonetic 丁 (dīng, "fourth heavenly stem"), yielding the reading dàn and a sense connected with the sun and the start of the day. This underlies the modern meaning of early morning or dawn. An alternative Shuowen view analyzes it as the sun rising over a horizon 一, but that is a later folk etymology.
  - **component characters:**
    - 日
    - 丁
