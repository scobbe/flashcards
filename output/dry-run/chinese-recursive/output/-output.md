## 山
---
- **pinyin:** shān
- **definition:** mountain
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: stylized mountain with three peaks.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a mountain range, with a tall central peak flanked by two lower ones. Over time the drawing was simplified but still looks like three rising peaks, clearly suggesting rugged mountains.
- **examples:**
  - 那座山很高(那座山很高)。
    - Nà zuò shān hěn gāo.
    - That mountain is very tall.
  - 我们去爬山(我們去爬山)。
    - Wǒmen qù pá shān.
    - We are going to climb a mountain.

## 银(銀)
---
- **pinyin:** yín
- **definition:** silver
- **components:**
  - 金
    - jīn
    - metal
  - 艮
    - gèn
    - tough
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 金 (jīn, "metal") + phonetic: 艮 (gèn, "tough").
  - **interpretation:** Signals a type of metal via the metal component, while 艮 (gèn, "tough") supplies the sound (yín). The notion of firmness in 艮 also fits a hard, lustrous metal like silver.
- **examples:**
  - 这个戒指是纯银做的(這個戒指是純銀做的)。
    - Zhège jièzhǐ shì chún yín zuò de.
    - This ring is made of pure silver.
  - 我喜欢银色的手机(我喜歡銀色的手機)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān yínsè de shǒujī.
    - I like silver-colored phones.
### 金
- **breadcrumbs:** 银
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **definition:** metal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram of a metal nugget/ingot with glints indicating shine.
  - **interpretation:** Originally drew the image of pieces or an ingot of shiny metal, with small strokes suggesting sparkling. Over time it generalized to mean metal in general and specifically gold, and it serves as the metal-related radical in many characters.
### 艮
- **breadcrumbs:** 银
- **pinyin:** gèn
- **definition:** tough
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** ideogram: abstract sign for "stopping/firmness"; no phonetic.
  - **interpretation:** Conveys the idea of stopping or standing firm, which naturally extends to a sense of hardness, stubbornness, or toughness. By evoking immobility and resistance to change, it came to mean tough or unyielding in character.

## 人口
---
- **pinyin:** rénkǒu
- **definition:** population
- **characters:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person, human being; people; others, someone
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening, entrance; classifier for family members; measure word for mouthfuls, bites
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 人 (rén, "person") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth; classifier for people") = population
  - **interpretation:** Here 口 (kǒu, "mouth") functions as a counter for people (mouths to feed), so paired with 人 (rén, "person") it denotes the number of persons in a group or region—population.
- **examples:**
  - 由于出生率下降(由於出生率下降)，这个国家的人口开始减少(這個國家的人口開始減少)。
    - Yóuyú chūshēnglǜ xiàjiàng, zhège guójiā de rénkǒu kāishǐ jiǎnshǎo.
    - Due to the declining birth rate, the country's population has begun to decrease.
  - 中国人口很多(中國人口很多)。
    - Zhōngguó rénkǒu hěn duō.
    - China has a large population.
### 人
- **breadcrumbs:** 人口
- **pinyin:** rén
- **definition:** person, human being; people; others, someone
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: stylized figure of a standing person with two legs.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person standing in profile, with two legs extending from a torso. Over time it was simplified into two strokes but still clearly evokes a human figure.
### 口
- **breadcrumbs:** 人口
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **definition:** mouth; opening, entrance; classifier for family members; measure word for mouthfuls, bites
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictograph of a mouth/opening; no phonetic component.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawing of a human mouth, it came to mean mouth and, by extension, any opening or entrance. It is also used as a classifier for people in a household and as a measure word for mouthfuls or bites.

## 说话(說話)
---
- **pinyin:** shuōhuà
- **definition:** to speak
- **characters:**
  - 说(說)
    - shuō
    - say, speak, tell; talk about, discuss; criticize, scold; theory, doctrine
  - 话(話)
    - huà
    - words, speech; talk, conversation; language, dialect; remark, saying
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 说(說) (shuō, "say/speak") + 话(話) (huà, "words/speech") = to speak
  - **interpretation:** A verb–object construction where saying words denotes the act of producing speech, hence 'to speak' or 'to talk'.
- **examples:**
  - 他在会议上不敢说话(他在會議上不敢說話)。
    - Tā zài huìyì shàng bù gǎn shuōhuà.
    - He doesn't dare to speak at the meeting.
  - 请小声说话(請小聲說話)。
    - Qǐng xiǎo shēng shuōhuà.
    - Please speak softly.
### 说(說)
- **breadcrumbs:** 说话
- **pinyin:** shuō
- **definition:** say, speak, tell; talk about, discuss; criticize, scold; theory, doctrine
- **components:**
  - 言
    - yán
    - speech; words
  - 兑(兌)
    - duì
    - exchange; open; pleased
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 言 (yán, "speech") + phonetic: 兑(兌) (duì, "exchange; pleased").
  - **interpretation:** Combines the speech element with 兑(兌) as the sound, whose sense of “exchange/opening” aligns with exchanging words and persuasion. It came to cover general speaking (“to say/talk”), extended to persuading and even delight, with modern usage centered on saying and discussing.
### 言
- **breadcrumbs:** 说话 → 说
- **pinyin:** yán
- **definition:** speech; words
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + 舌 (shé, "tongue") depicting speech.
  - **interpretation:** Early forms depict a mouth with a tongue and lines indicating emitted sounds. It visually conveys the act of speaking, hence meanings like speech and words.
### 口
- **breadcrumbs:** 说话 → 说 → 言
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **definition:** mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictograph depicting a mouth/opening.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawing of an open mouth, simplified into a square outline. It later generalized to mean any opening or orifice, and is also used as a classifier for people in counting household members.
### 舌
- **breadcrumbs:** 说话 → 说 → 言
- **pinyin:** shé
- **definition:** tongue
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: tongue in a mouth; earliest forms show it within 口 (kǒu, "mouth").
  - **interpretation:** Draws the image of a tongue inside or sticking out of a mouth, so the meaning is straightforward. The form was later stylized but still points to the tongue and its roles in speaking and tasting.
### 兑(兌)
- **breadcrumbs:** 说话 → 说
- **pinyin:** duì
- **definition:** exchange; open; pleased
- **components:**
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight; split/open
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 儿(兒)
    - ér
    - child; legs
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 八 (bā, "split/open") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + 儿(兒) (ér, "person/legs").
  - **interpretation:** Shows a person with the mouth opened, suggesting opening and speaking, which associates with joy or pleasure. The sense of "opening up" extends metaphorically to making change or exchanging items in transactions.
### 八
- **breadcrumbs:** 说话 → 说 → 兑
- **pinyin:** bā
- **definition:** eight; split/open
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Indicative of two strokes parting; original meaning "to split", later borrowed for "eight."
  - **interpretation:** Two diverging strokes visually suggest something being split or opened. That simple image was later extended and borrowed to write the numeral eight, while the sense of separation survives in related usages and as a component in other characters.
### 儿(兒)
- **breadcrumbs:** 说话 → 说 → 兑
- **pinyin:** ér
- **definition:** child; legs
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a child with a large head and two splayed legs.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a sketch of a young child with an oversized head and small body, clearly showing the two legs. In modern use it means “child/son,” and as a component it often conveys the idea of “legs” or a person standing.
### 话(話)
- **breadcrumbs:** 说话
- **pinyin:** huà
- **definition:** words, speech; talk, conversation; language, dialect; remark, saying
- **components:**
  - 言
    - yán
    - speech; words
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 言 (yán, "speech") + phonetic: 舌 (shé, "tongue").
  - **interpretation:** Built with 言 (yán, "speech") to signal meaning related to speaking, and 舌 (shé, "tongue") providing a phonetic cue. Think of 'speech' plus 'tongue'—sounds and talking—coming together to denote words and conversation.

## 得
---
- **pinyin:** de/dé/děi
- **definition:** structural particle; to get, obtain; must, have to
- **components:**
  - 彳
    - chì
    - step; small steps
  - 㝵
    - dé
    - ancient phonetic graph
  - 旦
    - dàn
    - dawn; daybreak
  - 寸
    - cùn
    - inch; hand/measure
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 彳 (chì, "step") + phonetic: 㝵 (dé, "ancient phonetic graph").
  - **interpretation:** Left-side 彳 suggests movement or reaching a place, pointing to the idea of arriving at or attaining something. The right-side 㝵 provides the sound dé, with its lower 寸 hinting at a hand/action nuance, reinforcing the sense of acquisition. From the core idea of reaching/attaining comes both the verb "to get/obtain" and its grammatical use marking how well or to what extent an action is achieved.
- **examples:**
  - 他说得很快(他說得很快)。
    - Tā shuō de hěn kuài.
    - He speaks very fast.
  - 她昨天得了一个奖(她昨天得了一個獎)。
    - Tā zuótiān dé le yí gè jiǎng.
    - She got an award yesterday.
  - 你得早点回家(你得早點回家)。
    - Nǐ děi zǎo diǎn huí jiā.
    - You have to go home early.
### 彳
- **breadcrumbs:** 得
- **pinyin:** chì
- **definition:** step; small steps
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Simple indicative showing a small, left-foot step.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the motion of moving the left foot forward in short, deliberate steps, hence the sense of 'step; small steps.' Often contrasted with 亍 (chù, "right step"), the pair evoking a slow, alternating walk.
### 㝵
- **breadcrumbs:** 得
- **pinyin:** dé
- **definition:** ancient phonetic graph
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Indicative phonetic sign; no reliable internal decomposition attested.
  - **interpretation:** Used historically as a conventional mark to indicate pronunciation in early texts, not to depict an object or combine semantic and phonetic parts. Its shape functions as a fixed symbol for sound and does not transparently break down into meaningful components.
### 旦
- **breadcrumbs:** 得
- **pinyin:** dàn
- **definition:** dawn; daybreak
- **components:**
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun; day
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; a line (ground/horizon)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 日 (rì, "sun; day") + semantic: 一 (yī, "ground/horizon").
  - **interpretation:** Shows the sun sitting on the horizon, indicating the moment of daybreak. By combining the sun with a horizon line, it conveys dawn and the start of a new day.
### 日
- **breadcrumbs:** 得 → 旦
- **pinyin:** rì
- **definition:** sun; day
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictograph depicting the sun (originally a circle with a central dot).
  - **interpretation:** Draws the image of the sun’s bright disk, once shown as a circle with a dot or a horizontal stroke inside. By association it came to mean “day,” as days are counted by the sun’s daily rise and set.
### 一
- **breadcrumbs:** 得 → 旦
- **pinyin:** yī
- **definition:** one; a line (ground/horizon)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** indicative: a single horizontal stroke representing the quantity one and, by extension, a line/horizon.
  - **interpretation:** A single line visually equals the count of one, so the meaning is immediate and self-evident. Because it is literally a straight line, it naturally evokes the ground or horizon, yielding the sense of a line.
### 寸
- **breadcrumbs:** 得
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **definition:** inch; hand/measure
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - right hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** indicative: 又 (yòu, "right hand") + a small mark indicating the wrist/pulse measure.
  - **interpretation:** Shows a hand with a short stroke marking the point at the wrist where one measures, conveying a small unit based on the hand. It later generalized to the unit "inch" and often serves as a hand/measurement semantic in compounds.
### 又
- **breadcrumbs:** 得 → 寸
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **definition:** right hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictograph of a right hand; no phonetic component.
  - **interpretation:** Draws a stylized right hand with a bent wrist/thumb, representing the concept of the right hand. Over time it also developed extended uses (like "again"), but the original picture is of a hand.
