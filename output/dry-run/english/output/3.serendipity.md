## serendipity
---
- **definition:**
  - the chance finding of something good or useful when you weren’t looking for it
  - a knack for making happy discoveries by accident
- **origin:**
  - coined in 1754 by English writer Horace Walpole from Serendip, an old name for Sri Lanka
  - inspired by the Persian tale “The Three Princes of Serendip,” whose heroes made lucky discoveries through accidents and cleverness
  - formed with the suffix -ity (“state or quality”); the term later spread widely, especially in science and innovation
- **pronunciation:**
  - seh-ren-DIP-ih-tee
