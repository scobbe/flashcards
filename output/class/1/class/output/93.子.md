## child; noun suffix
### sub-word
#### house; building
---
- **traditional:** 子
- **simplified:** 子
- **pronunciation:** zǐ
- **definition:** child; noun suffix
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 子目录(錄) (zǐ mù lù, "subdirectory")
  - 女子 (nǚ zǐ, "woman")
  - 独(獨)生子 (dú shēng zǐ, "only son")
  - 爱(愛)子 (ài zǐ, "beloved son")
- **etymology (子):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: depiction of a baby with a large head and outstretched arms wrapped in a blanket; later elaborated with head-top 囟 (xìn, "fontanelle") and seated on a stool 几 (jī, "stool").
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a baby, originally referring to an infant. By extension it came to mean child/son and then developed respectful and suffixal uses for ‘person’ or a sub-/diminutive sense in compounds.
  - **component characters:**
    - 囟 (xìn, "fontanelle")
    - 几 (jī, "stool")
