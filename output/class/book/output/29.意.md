## meaning; intention
### sub-word
#### to pay attention
---
- **traditional:** 意
- **simplified:** 意
- **pronunciation:** yì
- **definition:** meaning; intention
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 好意 (hǎo yì, "good intention")
  - 随(隨)意 (suí yì, "as one pleases")
  - 意外 (yì wài, "unexpected event")
  - 词(詞)不达(達)意 (cí bù dá yì, "words fail to convey meaning")
- **etymology (意):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 言 (yán, "speech") + 中 (zhōng, "middle") → ideogrammic: 音 (yīn, "sound") + 心 (xīn, "heart").
  - **interpretation:** Early forms combined 言 and 中 to express the core of what is said, yielding the sense of meaning or intention. In the modern graph, 心 marks the semantic field of thought/feeling, while the upper part was later shaped like 音 though historically unrelated; together they suggest expression issuing from the heart, matching senses like intention and meaning.
  - **component characters:**
    - 言
    - 中
    - 音
    - 心
