Definitions

[edit]

奇

  1. strange; odd; weird; unusual
    奇怪  ―  qíguài  ―  strange
    奇特  ―  qítè  ―  peculiar
  2. wonderful; fantastic; remarkable
    奇跡／奇迹  ―  qíjì  ―  miracle
    奇妙  ―  qímiào  ―  fantastic

Definitions

[edit]

奇

  1. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) unsmooth; disadvantageous
  2. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) surplus
  3. (mathematics) odd
    奇數／奇数  ―  jīshù  ―  odd number
  4. (Eastern Min, Southern Min) Classifier for a single object of a pair or a large collection, also used for certain objects that do not necessarily form a pair or collection.
    Alternative forms: (Hokkien, Teochew) 腳 / 脚, (Hokkien) 跤, (Hokkien) 卡
    蜀奇箸 [Eastern Min]  ―  siŏh-kiă dê̤ṳ / [suoʔ⁵⁻⁵⁵ (kʰ-)ia⁵⁵ tøy²⁴²] [Bàng-uâ-cê / IPA]  ―  a single chopstick
    • 2000 October 23, 黃余秋雁, “大甲媽割香”, in 胡萬川, editor, 《桃園市閩南語故事（三）》, Taoyuan: 桃縣文化局, published 2007, →ISBN, page 86:
      有啦，人伊原仔有買一个紀念品送阮婆婆啦，一奇手指抑啥貨予伊紀念，講彼答謝伊安呢。 [Taiwanese Hokkien, trad.]

Adjective

[edit]

奇 (cả, kỳ)

  1. Nôm form of cả (“large; elegant”).
    字经史共都聀奇
    Dử kinh sử cụng đo chức cả
    (please add an English translation of this usage example)
  2. Nôm form of kỳ (“strange”).
    𱒰帝法奇兵变化
    Son đảy phép kỳ binh biến hoá
    Learned the magical power of summoning troops

Pronoun

[edit]

奇 (cá)

  1. Nôm form of cá (“you, someone presumably older than me”).
    把禿圤𦙟開許奇
    Pjá thúc khỏi te̱ khai hẩư cá
    If [paid] well, I will sell it to you
