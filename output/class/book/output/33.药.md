## medicine or drug; to medicate
### sub-word
#### medical expenses
---
- **traditional:** 藥
- **simplified:** 药
- **pronunciation:** yào
- **definition:** medicine or drug; to medicate
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 吃药(藥) (chī yào, "take medicine")
  - 药(藥)店 (yào diàn, "drug store")
  - 药(藥)品 (yào pǐn, "medicinal products")
  - 药(藥)物 (yào wù, "pharmaceutical substances")
- **etymology (藥):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 艹 (cǎo, "grass") + phonetic: 乐(樂) (lè, "music").
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 艹 (cǎo, "grass") as the semantic determinative with 乐(樂) (lè, "music") as the phonetic, indicating a plant-based substance and providing the yào sound. This fits the modern sense of medicines and drugs, many historically derived from herbs. Some scholars propose it is etymologically related to 疗(療) (liáo, "treat"), suggesting a broader word-family connection.
  - **component characters:**
    - 艹 (cǎo, "grass")
    - 乐(樂) (lè, "music")
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%97%A5
  - **simplification rule (药):** Simplified from 藥 by replacing the complex phonetic 樂 with its simplified form 乐 and reducing overall strokes, yielding 药.
