## arm, colloquial form
### sub-word
#### arm
---
- **traditional:** 胳
- **simplified:** 胳
- **pronunciation:** gē
- **definition:** arm, colloquial form
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 捋胳膊 (luō gē bo, "roll up sleeves")
  - 胳膊 (gē bo, "the arm")
  - 胳臂 (gē bei, "the arm")
  - 跨胳膊 (kuà gē bo, "link arms")
- **etymology (胳):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 肉 (ròu, "flesh") + phonetic: 各 (gè, "each") → 胳 (gē, "arm").
  - **interpretation:** 肉 serves as the semantic determinative indicating a body/flesh domain, while 各 provides the phonetic value leading to Middle Chinese kak and modern gē. The semantic component aligns with the modern meanings referring to parts of the body such as the arm and, historically, the armpit.
  - **component characters:**
    - 肉
    - 各
    - 胳
