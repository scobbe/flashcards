## dawn
### sub-component
#### to check; to search
---
- **traditional:** 旦
- **simplified:** 旦
- **pronunciation:** dàn
- **definition:** dawn
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 通宵达(達)旦 (tōngxiāo dá dàn, "all night until dawn")
  - 元旦 (yuándàn, "New Year's Day")
  - 一旦 (yídàn, "once; in case")
  - 旦夕 (dànxī, "at any moment")
- **etymology (旦):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 日 (rì, "sun") + phonetic: 丁 (dīng, "nail").
  - **interpretation:** Formed with 日 (rì, "sun") as the semantic component indicating daylight and time of day, and 丁 (dīng, "nail") as the phonetic approximating the historical reading that yields modern dàn. This explains the core senses related to dawn and day. An alternative Shuowen view that it shows the sun above the horizon is considered a later folk interpretation.
  - **component characters:**
    - 日 (rì, "sun")
    - 丁 (dīng, "nail")
