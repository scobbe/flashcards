## oatmeal porridge
### yànmàizhōu
---
- **definition:** oatmeal porridge
- **pinyin:** yànmàizhōu
- **components:**
  - 燕麦(燕麥)
    - yànmài
    - oats; oat; oatmeal (grain); Avena sativa
  - 粥
    - zhōu
    - porridge; congee; gruel; rice porridge
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 燕麦 + 粥 = oats + porridge → oatmeal porridge
  - **interpretation:** Combines the grain name with the generic term for porridge to denote porridge made from oats. Focuses on preparation (粥) rather than a specific recipe.
  - **simplification:** Only 麥 → 麦 was simplified, reducing strokes by compressing components; 燕 and 粥 remain the same.
- **examples:**
  - 我早餐常喝燕麦粥(我早餐常喝燕麥粥)。
    - Wǒ zǎocān cháng hē yànmàizhōu.
    - I often drink oatmeal porridge for breakfast.
  - 燕麦粥容易消化(燕麥粥容易消化)，适合老人和孩子(適合老人和孩子)。
    - Yànmàizhōu róngyì xiāohuà, shìhé lǎorén hé háizi.
    - Oatmeal porridge is easy to digest and is suitable for the elderly and children.
  - 有时我会在燕麦粥里加蜂蜜和蓝莓(有時我會在燕麥粥裡加蜂蜜和藍莓)。
    - Yǒushí wǒ huì zài yànmàizhōu lǐ jiā fēngmì hé lánméi.
    - Sometimes I add honey and blueberries to my oatmeal porridge.
### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 燕麦(燕麥)
- **definition:** oats; oat; oatmeal (grain); Avena sativa
- **pinyin:** yànmài
- **components:**
  - 燕
    - yàn
    - swallow (bird); Yan (ancient northern state); northern; used in toponyms
  - 麦(麥)
    - mài
    - wheat; cereal; grain; barley
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 燕(‘Yan’/northern) + 麦(‘grain’) = a northern grain → oats.
  - **interpretation:** A modifier–head compound where 麦 names the cereal class and 燕 likely points to the northern Yan region, marking it as a northern grain. Pattern parallels 大麦、荞麦, with 麦 as the head.
  - **simplification:** 麦 is the simplified form of 麥, reduced by removing inner strokes; adopted in 20th‑century script reform due to the high frequency of 麥-based graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 我早餐常喝燕麦粥(我早餐常喝燕麥粥)。
    - Wǒ zǎocān cháng hē yànmài zhōu.
    - I often drink oatmeal porridge for breakfast.
  - 燕麦含有丰富的膳食纤维(燕麥含有豐富的膳食纖維)。
    - Yànmài hányǒu fēngfù de shànshí xiānwéi.
    - Oats are rich in dietary fiber.
  - 北方地区广泛种植燕麦(北方地區廣泛種植燕麥)。
    - Běifāng dìqū guǎngfàn zhòngzhí yànmài.
    - Oats are widely cultivated in northern regions.

  - 他姓燕(他姓燕)。

### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 燕麦(燕麥) → 燕 → 廿
- **definition:** twenty
- **pinyin:** niàn
- **components:**
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Two 十 (“ten”) written side by side, with a horizontal stroke at the bottom that fuses them; see also 卅. In characters such as 漢 and 黄, it represents 口, while in 度 and 席 it represents a stylized 石.
  - **interpretation:** Two tens placed together communicate the value twenty, with the bottom stroke uniting them into a single form. As a component, the shape can be borrowed to stand for a mouth (口) or a rock (石) depending on historical usage in different characters.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是农历廿一日(今天是農曆廿一日)。
    - Jīntiān shì nónglì niàn yī rì.
    - Today is the twenty-first day of the lunar month.
  - 他已经在此居住廿年(他已經在此居住廿年)。
    - Tā yǐjīng zài cǐ jūzhù niàn nián.
    - He has lived here for twenty years.
  - 会议将在本月廿五日举行(會議將在本月廿五日舉行)。
    - Huìyì jiāng zài běn yuè niàn wǔ rì jǔxíng.
    - The meeting will be held on the twenty-fifth of this month.
### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 燕麦(燕麥) → 燕 → 廿 → 十
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phonetic loan (rebus)
  - **description:** Originally a vertical stroke later crossed by 一; possibly a pictogram of a needle, rebus-borrowed for the word ‘ten’.
  - **interpretation:** Early forms may have depicted a needle, and that form’s sound was repurposed to write the word for “ten.” The cross shape developed when a horizontal stroke was added to the original vertical line.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十个苹果(我有十個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí ge píngguǒ.
    - I have ten apples.
  - 现在是十点(現在是十點)。
    - Xiànzài shì shí diǎn.
    - It is ten o’clock now.
  - 我们十个人一起去(我們十個人一起去)。
    - Wǒmen shí ge rén yìqǐ qù.
    - Ten of us are going together.
### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 燕麦(燕麥) → 燕 → 廿 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**

    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.

    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.

### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 燕麦(燕麥) → 麦(麥)
- **definition:** wheat; cereal; grain; barley
- **pinyin:** mài
- **components:**
  - 來
    - lái
    - to come
  - 夊
    - suī
    - to walk slowly
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 夊 (‘slow step’) + phonetic: 來; 來 was originally used for ‘wheat’.
  - **interpretation:** Meaning is anchored by a ‘slow step/footprint’ semantic hint that evokes reaping or drooping stalks, while 來 supplies the sound. In early usage, 來 itself denoted ‘wheat’, which explains the tight semantic link that later specialized into 麥/麦.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 麥 by streamlining the phonetic 來 and altering its 八 component to a single horizontal stroke (per Wiktionary), reducing strokes and compressing the original phono-semantic layout into 麦.
- **examples:**
  - 我家种了很多小麦(我家種了很多小麥)。
    - Wǒ jiā zhòng le hěn duō xiǎomài.
    - My family planted a lot of wheat.
  - 啤酒的麦芽很香(啤酒的麥芽很香)。
    - Píjiǔ de màiyá hěn xiāng.
    - The malt in the beer smells fragrant.
  - 大麦和小麦都是重要的粮食作物(大麥和小麥都是重要的糧食作物)。
    - Dàmài hé xiǎomài dōu shì zhòngyào de liángshí zuòwù.
    - Barley and wheat are both important food crops.
### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 燕麦(燕麥) → 麦(麥) → 來
- **definition:** to come
- **pinyin:** lái
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram of wheat: middle line = ear, upward lines = leaves, downward lines = stem/roots; sometimes an extra top line. Borrowed early to mean “to come”; later additions like 止/辵 were not retained.
  - **interpretation:** Originally drew a stalk of wheat, with the central stroke as the ear and other strokes as leaves, stem, and roots. It was borrowed early to write “to come,” while 麥 later carried the grain meaning; their similar Old Chinese onsets likely facilitated the semantic shift.
  - **simplification:** Simplified to 来 by reducing and regularizing strokes, following common clerical/cursive forms to lower stroke count and ease writing.
- **examples:**
  - 请你早点来(請你早點來)。
    - Qǐng nǐ zǎodiǎn lái.
    - Please come earlier.
  - 他今天从上海来开会(他今天從上海來開會)。
    - Tā jīntiān cóng Shànghǎi lái kāihuì.
    - He came from Shanghai to attend a meeting.
  - 如果你有时间，明天再来我们家坐坐(如果你有時間，明天再來我們家坐坐)。
    - Rúguǒ nǐ yǒu shíjiān, míngtiān zài lái wǒmen jiā zuòzuo.
    - If you have time, come to our place again tomorrow for a visit.
### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 燕麦(燕麥) → 麦(麥) → 夊

### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 粥

### 燕麦粥(燕麥粥) → 粥 → 米
- **definition:** rice
- **pinyin:** mǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: dehusked rice kernels; horizontal stroke as a bamboo sieve; cf. 粟 (with husk); unrelated to 木/禾/来.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts dehusked rice grains being sifted on a bamboo sieve, contrasting with 粟 which shows husked kernels. Despite visual resemblance, its form is not derived from 木, 禾, or 来.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了两斤米(我買了兩斤米)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le liǎng jīn mǐ.
    - I bought two jin of rice.
  - 这家店的东北米很香(這家店的東北米很香)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn de Dōngběi mǐ hěn xiāng.
    - The Northeastern rice at this shop is very fragrant.
  - 请把米洗一洗(請把米洗一洗)，再煮饭(再煮飯)。
    - Qǐng bǎ mǐ xǐ yì xǐ, zài zhǔ fàn.
    - Please rinse the rice, then cook it.
