## 这是什么(這是什麼)
---
- **pinyin:** zhè shì shénme
- **definition:** what is this?
- **characters:**
  - 这(這)
    - zhè
    - this, this one; these
  - 是
    - shì
    - to be; yes; right, correct
  - 什
    - shén
    - what (in 什麼/什么)
  - 么(麼)
    - me
    - interrogative suffix, particle (in 什么/什麼, 怎么/怎麼); small, tiny (in 幺么/幺麽)
- **etymology:** An equational clause with an unknown predicate: 这(這) (zhè, "this") presents the pointed entity, 是 (shì, "to be") asserts identity, and 什 (shén, "what")—a vernacular loan of 甚 (shèn, "how; what")—plus 么(麼) (me, "interrogative suffix" from the diminutive 幺 (yāo, "tiny")) yields 什麼, the in‑situ 'what' filling the predicate slot, hence literally 'This is what?'. This vernacular pattern replaced Classical turns like 此 (cǐ, "this") 是 (shì, "to be") 何 (hé, "what") 物 (wù, "thing") by keeping the wh‑word in place and needing no extra question particle.
- **examples:**
  - 这是什么(這是什麼)？
    - zhè shì shénme
    - What is this?
