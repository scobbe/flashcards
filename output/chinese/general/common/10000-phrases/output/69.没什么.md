## 没什么(沒什麼)
---
- **pinyin:** méi shénme
- **definition:** it's nothing; never mind
- **characters:**
  - 没(沒)
    - méi
    - have not, there is not; did not
  - 什
    - shén
    - what
  - 么(麼)
    - me
    - interrogative particle, suffix
- **etymology:** Negative-existential 没(沒) (méi, "have not") placed before the interrogative-based indefinite from 什 (shén, "what") + 么(麼) (me, "interrogative suffix") triggers a negative-polarity reading of 什么 as “anything,” so the literal sense is “do not have anything,” i.e., nothing of consequence. In conversation an understood noun like 事 (shì, "matter") is ellipsed, making it a polite downplaying formula—“it’s nothing”/“never mind”—in line with Chinese norms of modestly deflecting thanks or apologies.
- **examples:**
  - 没什么(沒什麼)，我刚刚不小心滑了一下(我剛剛不小心滑了一下)。
    - méi shénme wǒ gānggāng bù xiǎoxīn huá le yí xià
    - It's nothing, I just slipped a bit.
  - 没什么(沒什麼)，别在意了(別在意了)，我们改天再试(我們改天再試)。
    - méi shénme bié zàiyì le wǒmen gǎitiān zài shì
    - Never mind, don't worry about it; we'll try another day.
