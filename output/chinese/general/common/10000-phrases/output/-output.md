## 是的
---
- **pinyin:** shì de
- **definition:** yes
- **characters:**
  - 是
    - shì
    - to be, is, are, am; yes, correct; indeed, truly
  - 的
    - de
    - possessive particle, of; attributive marker linking modifiers to nouns; nominalizer, turns verbs or clauses into nouns
- **etymology:** 是 (shì, "to be; correct") gives the core affirmation "it is so." Adding sentence-final 的 (de, "nominalizer") wraps that judgment into a noun-like "the fact is thus," softening it into a polite, completed confirmation—much like classical statements sealed with 也 (yě, "assertive final particle").
- **examples:**
  - 是的(是的)。
    - shì de
    - Yes.
  - 是的(是的)，我同意(我同意)。
    - shì de, wǒ tóngyì
    - Yes, I agree.

## 没有(沒有)
---
- **pinyin:** méiyǒu
- **definition:** not have; there is not
- **characters:**
  - 没(沒)
    - méi
    - not; have not; without; to sink, submerge
  - 有
    - yǒu
    - to have, possess, own; there is, there are
- **etymology:** 没(沒) (méi, "not; to sink, submerge") negates by evoking something swallowed by water; placed before 有 (yǒu, "to have; there is") it depicts 'having' as having sunk/vanished, yielding 'do not have/there isn't.' In Classical Chinese, existential negation was 無 (wú, "without; there is not"); in vernacular Mandarin this role shifted to 没(沒), and since 不 (bù, "not") does not pair with 有 (yǒu, "to have; there is"), the fixed collocation 没(沒)+有 became standard (often shortened to 没(沒)).
- **examples:**
  - 我没有钱(我沒有錢)。
    - wǒ méiyǒu qián
    - I don't have money.
  - 这儿没有人(這兒沒有人)。
    - zhèr méiyǒu rén
    - There is no one here.

## 那是什么(那是什麼)
---
- **pinyin:** nà shì shénme
- **definition:** what's that?
- **characters:**
  - 那
    - nà
    - that, those; then, in that case
  - 是
    - shì
    - to be; yes; correct, right
  - 什
    - shén
    - what (in 什麼/什么)
  - 么(麼)
    - me
    - suffix forming interrogatives or degree adverbs, as in 什麼/什么, 怎麼/怎么, 那麼/那么
- **etymology:** Distal pointer 那 (nà, "that") and equative 是 (shì, "to be") set up an identification clause, while the vernacular interrogative 什麼 formed by 什 (shén, "what; originally ‘assorted’ used phonetically here") + 么(麼) (me, "interrogative suffix") fills the predicate slot; in Chinese wh-in-situ grammar, you ask by leaving the unknown as 什麼, so it literally poses "That is what?". Historically 什麼 condenses earlier 甚 (shén, "what") + 麼 (me) in 甚麼, which explains the shén reading of 什 in this compound.
- **examples:**
  - 那是什么(那是什麼)？
    - nà shì shénme
    - What's that?
