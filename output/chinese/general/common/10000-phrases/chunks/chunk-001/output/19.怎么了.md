## what's up?; what's wrong?
### zěnme le
---
- **traditional:** 怎麼了
- **definition:** what's up?; what's wrong?
- **pinyin:** zěnme le
- **components:**
  - 怎
    - zěn
    - how; why
  - 么(麼)
    - me
    - suffix for forming questions or adverbs of manner
  - 了
    - le
    - particle indicating change, completion, or new situation
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 怎 (zěn, "how; why") + 麼 (me, "suffix for forming questions or adverbs of manner") + 了 (le, "particle indicating change, completion, or new situation")
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 怎( )麼(么)了( ) (zěnme le) combines 怎 (zěn, "how") with the interrogative suffix 麼 (me), and the particle 了 (le), which indicates a change or new situation. It is used to inquire about someone's condition or situation, like asking "what's up?" or "what's wrong?" in English. The use of 了 (le) suggests that the speaker is referring to a situation that has changed or developed.
- **examples:**
  - 你( )怎( )么(麼)了( )？

### 怎( )么(麼)了( ) → 了
- **definition:** particle indicating change, completion, or new situation
- **pinyin:** le

  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a baby without arms (compare 子 with arms) ->
    - entangled legs ->

'Finish' likely arose from a sound loan rather than semantic combination, as 'entangled' implies coming to an end or conclusion in a motion.
