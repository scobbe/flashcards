## Lingnan region
### lǐng nán
---
- **traditional:** 嶺南
- **definition:** Lingnan region
- **pinyin:** lǐng nán
- **components:**
  - 岭(嶺)
    - lǐng
    - mountain ridge; peak
  - 南
    - nán
    - south; southern part of country
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 岭(嶺) (lǐng, "mountain ridge") is used to describe a series of connected mountains forming a continuous elevated crest. 南 (nán, "south") indicates the direction towards the south. Together, these characters describe the geographic region south of the mountain range, particularly referring to the area south of the Nanling Mountains in southern China.
  - **interpretation:** 岭(嶺) (lǐng, "mountain ridge") visually depicts a path or crest between heights, signaling a droplet-like shape, which naturally signifies a high, narrow point. 南 (nán, "south") culturally aligns with directions, land divisions, and cardinality essential in Chinese climatology and geography, indicating the region beneath the mountain ridges. Hence, 岭(嶺)南 describes the geographical area lying south of these natural barriers, famously known as the region south of the Nanling mountain ranges, with historical implications of being a culturally distinctive and economically significant space within southern China.
- **examples:**
  - 岭(嶺)南地区(區)以其独(獨)特的文化而闻(聞)名。
    - Lǐngnán dìqū yǐ qí dútè de wénhuà ér wénmíng.
    - The Lingnan area is famous for its unique culture.
  - 许(許)多岭(嶺)南菜肴(餚)在全国(國)各地都很受欢(歡)迎。
    - Xǔduō lǐngnán càiyáo zài quánguó gèdì dōu hěn shòu huānyíng.
    - Many Lingnan dishes are very popular across the country.
  - 他打算去岭(嶺)南旅游(遊)。
    - Tā dǎsuàn qù lǐngnán lǚyóu.
    - He plans to travel to Lingnan.
### 岭(嶺)南 → 岭(嶺)
- **traditional:** 嶺
- **definition:** mountain ridge; peak
- **pinyin:** lǐng
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 領
    - lǐng
    - neck; collar
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 山 (shān, "mountain") + phonetic 領 (lǐng, "neck; collar") ->
    - ridge
  - **interpretation:** In the character 嶺 (lǐng, "ridge"), the semantic component 山 (shān, "mountain") suggests the geographical feature, while the phonetic component 領 (lǐng) provides the sound. The character evokes the image of a mountain's ridge or crest, akin to the line of a neck, which it resembles in shape, leading logically to the meaning "ridge" or "mountain range."
  - **simplification:** 嶺 was simplified to 岭 by replacing the semantic component 領 (lǐng, "neck; collar") with 领, retaining phonetic similarity while reducing complexity.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)早晨爬上了高高的山岭(嶺)。
    - Wǒmen zǎochén pá shàng le gāogāo de shānlǐng.
    - We climbed the high mountain ridge in the morning.
  - 这(這)条(條)山岭(嶺)横(橫)跨整个(個)地区(區)。
    - Zhè tiáo shānlǐng héngkuà zhěnggè dìqū.
    - This mountain range spans the entire region.
  - 云(雲)层(層)遮住了山岭(嶺)的顶(頂)峰。
    - Yúncéng zhē zhù le shānlǐng de dǐngfēng.
    - The cloud layer obscured the mountain ridge's peak.
### 岭(嶺)南 → 岭(嶺) → 山
- **definition:** mountain
- **pinyin:** shān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three mountain peaks
  - **interpretation:** The character 山(shān, "mountain") visually represents the concept of a mountain using three vertical lines that suggest peaks. This depiction is consistent with early forms found in Oracle Bone Script, emphasizing the notion of natural elevation associated with mountains.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)去爬山。
    - Wǒmen qù pá shān.
    - We go climb the mountain.
  - 那座大山让(讓)人惊(驚)叹(嘆)。

### 岭(嶺)南 → 岭(嶺) → 領 → 頁
- **definition:** head
- **pinyin:** yè
- **components:**
  - 首
    - shǒu
    - head; first
  - 卩
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 首 (head/face) + 卩 (kneeling person) ->
    - depicts a person's head and body
  - **interpretation:** Originally represented a head and its body in a kneeling posture, emphasizing “head”. The depiction was extended semantically to represent a 'page', as pages bear leading information like a head does for a body. The concept of 'head' is also reflected in its modern usage as a radical in characters related to parts of the head or face.
  - **simplification:** 页 is a cursive shorthand of the traditional 頁, simplifying the structure while maintaining the core shape of a head.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)打开(開)书(書)的第十页(頁)。
    - Qǐng dǎkāi shū de dì shí yè.
    - Please open to page ten of the book.
  - 这(這)本书(書)每一页(頁)都有插图(圖)。
    - Zhè běn shū měi yī yè dōu yǒu chātú.
    - Every page of this book has illustrations.
  - 我昨天晚上读(讀)了三十页(頁)。
    - Wǒ zuótiān wǎnshang dúle sānshí yè.
    - I read thirty pages last night.

### 岭(嶺)南 → 岭(嶺) → 領 → 頁 → 卩
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.
- **examples:**
  - 那位卩正虔诚(誠)祈祷(禱)。
    - Nà wèi jié zhèng qiánchéng qídǎo.
    - That kneeling person is praying devoutly.
  - 礼(禮)仪(儀)师(師)指出，卩代表谦(謙)卑与(與)敬意。
    - Lǐyí shī zhǐchū, jié dàibiǎo qiānbēi yǔ jìngyì.
    - The ritual master pointed out that the kneeling person represents humility and respect.
  - 在庄(莊)严(嚴)仪(儀)式中，卩象征(徵)着(著)内(內)心的虔敬。
    - Zài zhuāngyán yíshì zhōng, jié xiàngzhēngzhe nèixīn de qiánjìng.
    - In the solemn ceremony, the kneeling figure symbolizes inner devotion.

### 岭(嶺)南 → 南
- **definition:** south; southern part of country
- **pinyin:** nán
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a hanging percussion instrument (bell) ->
    - Phonetically borrowed for 'south'
  - **interpretation:** 南 originally depicted a bell, indicating hanging percussion instruments, and was later borrowed for its sound to mean 'south'. This borrowing is based on phonetic resemblance rather than a direct conceptual link with directionality.
- **examples:**
  - 南方的天气(氣)很温(溫)暖。
    - Nánfāng de tiānqì hěn wēnnuǎn.
    - The weather in the south is very warm.
  - 我们(們)计(計)划(劃)去南部旅行。
    - Wǒmen jìhuà qù nánbù lǚxíng.
    - We plan to travel to the south.
  - 他住在城市的南边(邊)。
    - Tā zhù zài chéngshì de nánbiān.
    - He lives on the south side of the city.
