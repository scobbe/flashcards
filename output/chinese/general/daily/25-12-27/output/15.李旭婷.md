## Chinese personal name
### Lǐ Xùtíng
---
- **definition:** Chinese personal name
- **pinyin:** Lǐ Xùtíng
- **components:**
  - 李
    - Lǐ
    - a common Chinese surname
  - 旭
    - xù
    - dawn; rising sun
  - 婷
    - tíng
    - graceful; elegant; attractive
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** A Chinese personal name, typically consisting of a family name followed by two given names.
  - **interpretation:** 李 (Lǐ, "a common Chinese surname") is one of the most common Chinese surnames, originating from the plum tree. 旭 (xù, "dawn; rising sun") represents the rising sun and often symbolizes new beginnings or hope. 婷 (tíng, "graceful; elegant; attractive") suggests grace and elegance. Together, the name 李旭婷 (Lǐ Xùtíng) combines the common surname 李 with a given name evoking the hopeful beauty of a new dawn and grace, typical for personal names that reflect positive attributes or aspirations for the individual.
- **examples:**
  - 李旭婷是我的好朋友。
    - Lǐ Xùtíng shì wǒ de hǎo péngyǒu.
    - Li Xuting is my good friend.
  - 我昨天见(見)到了李旭婷，她看起来(來)很开(開)心。
    - Wǒ zuótiān jiàn dào le Lǐ Xùtíng, tā kàn qǐlái hěn kāixīn.
    - I met Li Xuting yesterday, she looked very happy.
  - 李旭婷喜欢(歡)旅行，她去过(過)很多国(國)家。
    - Lǐ Xùtíng xǐhuān lǚxíng, tā qù guò hěn duō guójiā.
    - Li Xuting likes traveling, she has visited many countries.
### 李旭婷 → 李
- **definition:** a common Chinese surname
- **pinyin:** Lǐ
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 來 (lái, originally representing 'wheat') became corrupted to 木 (mù, 'tree') + semantic 子 (zǐ, 'child') ->
    - 李
  - **interpretation:** Originally a phono-semantic compound with 來 (lái, originally 'wheat') providing sound and 子 (zǐ, 'child') lending meaning. Over time, 來 was corrupted to 木 (mù, 'tree'), suggesting a 'plum tree', which is also associated with the surname Li, well-known in Chinese culture.
- **examples:**
  - 李老师(師)是我们(們)的数(數)学(學)老师(師)。
    - Lǐ lǎoshī shì wǒmen de shùxué lǎoshī.

    - Lǐ xiānshēng yāoqǐng wǒmen qù tā de cháguǎn.
    - Mr. Li invited us to his tea house.
  - 我常常和李小姐去公园(園)散步。
    - Wǒ chángcháng hé Lǐ xiǎojiě qù gōngyuán sànbù.

### 李旭婷 → 旭 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
- **examples:**

  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。

### 李旭婷 → 旭 → 九
- **definition:** nine
- **pinyin:** jiǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized hand with bent wrist ->
    - original 'elbow' 肘 (zhǒu) meaning ->
    - associated with nine as metaphorical bumping up against ten
  - **interpretation:** 九 originally depicted the elbow, symbolizing movement akin to pulling back in momentum, which culturally reflects nearing a complete cycle at ten. Later, 九 came to symbolize the number nine, a fist bumping up against ten, the last count on one's fingers.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是九号(號)。
    - Jīntiān shì jiǔ hào.
    - Today is the ninth.

    - Wǒmen jiǔ diǎn zhōng chūfā, qù tànwàng péngyǒu.
