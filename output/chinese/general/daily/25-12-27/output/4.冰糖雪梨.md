## rock sugar pear drink
### bīng táng xuě lí
---
- **definition:** rock sugar pear drink
- **pinyin:** bīng táng xuě lí
- **components:**

    - bīng

  - 雪

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 冰 (bīng, "ice") + 糖 (táng, "sugar") + 雪 (xuě, "snow") + 梨 (lí, "pear")
  - **interpretation:** 冰 (bīng, "ice") suggests coldness or something refreshing, setting the context for a chilled drink. 糖 (táng, "sugar") refers to sweetness, specifically the use of rock sugar which is less refined and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine for milder sweetness. 雪 (xuě, "snow") symbolizes purity and the idea of freshness, aligning with the cooling effect of the drink. 梨 (lí, "pear") provides the main flavor component, indicating that the drink is pear-based. Combining these elements, 冰糖雪梨 refers to a refreshing beverage made of pear, typically sweetened with rock sugar, served cold to enhance its cooling effect.
