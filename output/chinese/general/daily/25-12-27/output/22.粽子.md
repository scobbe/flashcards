## rice dumpling
### zòng zi
---
- **definition:** rice dumpling
- **pinyin:** zòng zi
- **components:**
  - 粽
    - zòng
    - sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves
  - 子
    - zi
    - child; small thing
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 粽 (zòng, "sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves"), 子 (zi, "child; small thing")
  - **interpretation:** 粽 (zòng, "sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves") refers directly to the traditional food item of sticky rice cooked in leaves. 子 (zi, "child; small thing") is often used in Chinese to denote a small or compact version of an object, essentially referring to a 'dumpling' or small parcel of food. Putting these together, 粽子 refers to the dish known as a rice dumpling, small packages of sticky rice encased in leaves, especially popular during the Dragon Boat Festival.
- **examples:**
  - 端午节(節)的时(時)候，我们(們)吃粽子。
    - Duānwǔ jié de shíhòu, wǒmen chī zòngzi.
    - During the Dragon Boat Festival, we eat rice dumplings.
  - 她亲(親)手包了很多粽子。
    - Tā qīnshǒu bāo le hěn duō zòngzi.
    - She made many rice dumplings by hand.
  - 粽子有甜的，也有咸(鹹)的。
    - Zòngzi yǒu tián de, yě yǒu xián de.
    - Rice dumplings can be sweet or savory.
### 粽子 → 粽(糉)
- **traditional:** 糉
- **definition:** sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves
- **pinyin:** zòng
- **components:**
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
  - 㚇
    - zōng
    - carefree, easygoing
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 米 (mǐ, "rice") + phonetic 㚇 (zōng) ->
    - sticky rice wrapped in leaves
  - **interpretation:** The character 糉 originated as a phono-semantic compound where 米 (mǐ, "rice") indicates the semantic field relevant to rice, and 㚇 (zōng) provides the sound. Together they denote sticky rice wrapped in leaves, commonly referred to as 粽子 (zòngzi) in modern usage.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)在端午节(節)吃粽(糉)子。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān zài duānwǔjié chī zòngzi.
    - I like to eat sticky rice wrapped in leaves during the Dragon Boat Festival.
  - 她在市场(場)上买(買)了很多粽(糉)子。
    - Tā zài shìchǎng shàng mǎile hěn duō zòngzi.
    - She bought a lot of sticky rice wrapped in leaves at the market.
  - 粽(糉)子的味道让(讓)我想起了童年。
    - Zòngzi de wèidào ràng wǒ xiǎngqǐle tóngnián.
    - The taste of sticky rice wrapped in leaves reminds me of my childhood.
### 粽子 → 粽(糉) → 米
- **definition:** rice
- **pinyin:** mǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts rice kernels ->
    - 米(mǐ, 'rice')
  - **interpretation:** The character 米(mǐ, 'rice') visually represents dehusked rice kernels to differentiate it from 粟. The horizontal stroke may symbolize a bamboo sieve, emphasizing the process of separating grains from their husks.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)吃米饭(飯)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī mǐfàn.
    - I like to eat rice.
  - 她从(從)农(農)村带(帶)回一袋米，用来(來)煮饭(飯)。
    - Tā cóng nóngcūn dài huí yī dài mǐ, yòng lái zhǔ fàn.
    - She brought a bag of rice from the countryside to cook.
  - 市场(場)上有新米出售。
    - Shìchǎng shàng yǒu xīn mǐ chūshòu.
    - New rice is available in the market.

  - **description:**
    - 子 (zǐ, "child") + 乑 (yín, "together") =

### 粽子 → 粽(糉) → 㚇 → 子
- **definition:** child
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a baby with a large head and spread arms, with legs wrapped in a blanket
  - **interpretation:** The character 子(zǐ, "child") is a pictorial representation of a baby, emphasizing characteristics like a large head and outstretched arms typical of infants, which conveys the essence of a child. This visual element helps infer its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)小子很调(調)皮。
    - Zhè xiǎo zǐ hěn tiáopí.
    - This kid is very naughty.
  - 他的小子爱(愛)打篮(籃)球，每天都锻(鍛)炼(煉)身体(體)。
    - Tā de xiǎo zǐ ài dǎ lánqiú, měitiān dōu duànliàn shēntǐ.
    - His child loves playing basketball, and practices every day.
  - 孤独(獨)的子需要关(關)爱(愛)。
    - Gūdú de zǐ xūyào guān'ài.
    - A lonely child needs care.

### 粽子 → 粽(糉) → 㚇 → 乑
- **definition:** together
- **pinyin:** yín
- **components:**
  - 眾
    - zhòng
    - multitude
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a sun (日) above three humans (㐺) ->
    - sun modified to resemble a horizontal eye (early form of 目) ->
    - a crowd under someone’s gaze ->
    - final form resembles 罒.
  - **interpretation:** The character 乑 is derived from 眾(众) (zhòng, "multitude"), symbolizing a crowd gathered under the gaze of the sun, reinterpreted as the eye of authority overseeing a collective group, representing the concept of 'together' or 'gathered'.
### 粽子 → 粽(糉) → 㚇 → 乑 → 眾
- **definition:** multitude
- **pinyin:** zhòng
- **components:**
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 日(日, "sun") above three 人(人, "person") ->
    - sun over a crowd ->
    - modified to 罒(resembles "eye") + three 人(人, "person") ->
    - crowd under gaze
  - **interpretation:** The character originally depicted the sun shining over three figures, indicating people gathered or working outdoors. This was modified over time with the top element stylized to resemble an eye watching over a crowd, symbolizing a multitude under observation.
- **examples:**
  - 人群聚集在广(廣)场(場)上，引起了围(圍)观(觀)者的注意。
    - Rénqún jùjí zài guǎngchǎng shàng, yǐnqǐle wéiguān zhě de zhùyì.
    - The crowd gathered in the square, attracting the attention of onlookers.
  - 市长(長)在大会(會)上向群众(眾)发(發)表了讲(講)话(話)。
    - Shìzhǎng zài dàhuì shàng xiàng qúnzhòng fābiǎo le jiǎnghuà.
    - The mayor delivered a speech to the multitude at the conference.
  - 为(為)了满(滿)足大众(眾)的需求，公司推出了新产(產)品。
    - Wèile mǎnzú dàzhòng de xūqiú, gōngsī tuīchū le xīn chǎnpǐn.
    - To meet the needs of the multitude, the company launched a new product.

### 粽子 → 粽(糉) → 㚇 → 乑 → 眾 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
- **examples:**
  - 晨曦来(來)临(臨)时(時)，日出东(東)方。
    - Chénxī láilín shí, rìchū dōngfāng.
    - At the break of dawn, the sun rises in the east.
  - 温(溫)暖的日光洒(灑)满(滿)大地。
    - Wēnnuǎn de rìguāng sǎ mǎn dàdì.
    - The warm sunlight spreads over the earth.
  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。
    - Zhōngwǔ shífēn, rì gāo guà tiān.
    - At noon, the sun hangs high in the sky.

### 粽子 → 粽(糉) → 㚇 → 乑 → 眾 → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**

  - **description:**
    - Depicts side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg ->

  - **interpretation:** The character 人 (rén, 'person') effectively uses simple lines to depict the essential form of a human, making it immediately recognizable and intuitive as meaning 'person.'

  - 人在旅途中，心灵(靈)获(獲)得了成长(長)。
