## rice dumpling
### zòng zi
---
- **definition:** rice dumpling
- **pinyin:** zòng zi
- **components:**
  - 粽
    - zòng
    - sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves
  - 子
    - zi
    - child; small thing
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 粽 (zòng, "sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves"), 子 (zi, "child; small thing")
  - **interpretation:** 粽 (zòng, "sticky rice wrapped in bamboo leaves") refers directly to the traditional food item of sticky rice cooked in leaves. 子 (zi, "child; small thing") is often used in Chinese to denote a small or compact version of an object, essentially referring to a 'dumpling' or small parcel of food. Putting these together, 粽子 refers to the dish known as a rice dumpling, small packages of sticky rice encased in leaves, especially popular during the Dragon Boat Festival.
- **examples:**
  - 端( )午( )节(節)的( )时(時)候( )，我( )们(們)吃( )粽( )子( )。
    - Duānwǔ jié de shíhòu, wǒmen chī zòngzi.
    - During the Dragon Boat Festival, we eat rice dumplings.
  - 她( )亲(親)手( )包( )了( )很( )多( )粽( )子( )。
    - Tā qīnshǒu bāo le hěn duō zòngzi.
    - She made many rice dumplings by hand.
  - 粽子有甜的，也( )有( )咸(鹹)的( )。
    - Zòngzi yǒu tián de, yě yǒu xián de.
    - Rice dumplings can be sweet or savory.

### 粽子 → 粽(糉) → 㚇 → 乑
- **definition:** together
- **pinyin:** yín
- **components:**
  - 眾
    - zhòng
    - multitude
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a sun (日) above three humans (㐺) ->
    - sun modified to resemble a horizontal eye (early form of 目) ->
    - a crowd under someone’s gaze ->
    - final form resembles 罒.
  - **interpretation:** The character 乑 is derived from 眾(众) (zhòng, "multitude"), symbolizing a crowd gathered under the gaze of the sun, reinterpreted as the eye of authority overseeing a collective group, representing the concept of 'together' or 'gathered'.

### 粽子 → 粽(糉) → 㚇 → 乑 → 眾 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
