## Moutai liquor
### máo tái
---
- **traditional:** 茅臺
- **definition:** Moutai liquor
- **pinyin:** máo tái
- **components:**
  - 茅
    - máo
    - reeds; grass
  - 台(臺)
    - tái
    - platform; stage
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 茅 (máo, "reeds") refers to a type of grass, which might have been abundant or distinctive in the geographical area. 臺(台) (tái, "platform") can refer to a flat area or a place of significance. In this compound, it forms the name of the place 茅台 (Máotáitáij, "Moutai"), which is famous for its liquor.
  - **interpretation:** 茅 (máo, "reeds") and 臺(台) (tái, "platform") together specify a particular geographical setting associated with the production of high-quality liquor. This compound strictly represents a place name, famously associated with 茅臺(台)酒 (Máotái jiǔ), a type of distilled Chinese liquor. The name suggests a unique locality or community known for its skilled liquor production, akin to terroir in winemaking.
- **examples:**
  - 茅台是一种(種)非常著名的中国(國)白酒。
    - Máotái shì yī zhǒng fēicháng zhùmíng de Zhōngguó báijiǔ.
    - Moutai is a very famous Chinese spirit.
  - 在婚宴上，他们(們)总(總)会(會)喝茅台。
    - Zài hūnyàn shàng, tāmen zǒng huì hē máotái.
    - At weddings, they always drink Moutai.
  - 他收藏了许(許)多不同年份的茅台。
    - Tā shōucángle xǔduō bùtóng niánfèn de máotái.
    - He has collected many different vintages of Moutai.
