## traditional tea whisking method
### diǎn chá
---
- **traditional:** 點茶
- **definition:** traditional tea whisking method
- **pinyin:** diǎn chá
- **components:**
  - 点(點)
    - diǎn
    - point; dot; order
  - 茶
    - chá
    - tea
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 點 (diǎn, "point; dot; order") + 茶 (chá, "tea")
  - **interpretation:** 點 (diǎn, "point; dot; order") originally refers to making a small mark or selecting. In the context of 點茶 (diǎn chá), it refers to the action of whisking or preparing tea, traditionally involving adding water drop by drop while whisking the tea into a frothy mixture. This process mirrors the careful, precise act of 点 (diǎn, "point; dot") where tea is methodically introduced and transformed. 茶 (chá, "tea") brings in the context of the beverage itself, emphasizing the ritualistic and artisanal process involved.
- **examples:**
