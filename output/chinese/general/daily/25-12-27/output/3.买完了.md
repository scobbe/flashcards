## finished buying
### mǎi wán le
---
- **traditional:** 買完了
- **definition:** finished buying
- **pinyin:** mǎi wán le
- **components:**
  - 买(買)
    - mǎi
    - buy
  - 完
    - wán
    - finish; complete
  - 了
    - le
    - completed action; particle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 買 (mǎi, "buy") + 完 (wán, "finish") + 了 (le, "completed action") combine to express the completion of a buying action.
  - **interpretation:** 買 (mǎi, "buy") indicates the action of purchasing goods, while 完 (wán, "finish") suggests that the action reaches its end. 了 (le, "completed action") is a particle that marks the completion, indicating finality.
- **examples:**

### 买(買)完( )了( ) → 了
- **definition:** completed action; particle
- **pinyin:** le
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a baby without arms (compare 子 with arms) ->
    - entangled legs ->
    - sound loan for 'finish'
  - **interpretation:** The original depiction is of a baby shape illustrating a state of vulnerability or incompleteness. Over time, the idea of 'entangled legs' metaphorically transitioned into 'something tied or completed.' 'Finish' likely arose from a sound loan rather than semantic combination, as 'entangled' implies coming to an end or conclusion in a motion.
