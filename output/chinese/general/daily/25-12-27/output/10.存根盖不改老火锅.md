- **traditional:** 存根蓋不改老火鍋
- **definition:** Cungen Gai Old Style Hot Pot
- **pinyin:** cún gēn gài · bù gǎi lǎo huǒ guō
- **components:**

  - 老火鍋

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word

  - **interpretation:** The phrase 存根蓋不改老火鍋 can be understood as a name indicating a hot pot house that prides itself on retaining traditional or original recipes. 存根(gēn) emphasizes maintaining foundational aspects of the food. 不改 (bù gǎi) suggests an unwavering adherence to tradition, while 老火鍋 (lǎo huǒ guō) specifies the type of cuisine, known for its warming, communal dining nature.

### 存( )根( )盖(蓋)不( )改( )老( )火( )锅(鍋) → 老火鍋 → 鍋 → 咼 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
