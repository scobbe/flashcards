---
- **traditional:** 存根蓋不改老火鍋
- **definition:** Cungen Gai Old Style Hot Pot
- **pinyin:** cún gēn gài · bù gǎi lǎo huǒ guō
- **components:**
  - 存根
    - cún gēn
    - store root; receipt stub
  - 蓋
    - gài
    - cover; lid
  - 不改
    - bù gǎi
    - not change
  - 老火鍋
    - lǎo huǒ guō
    - old-style hot pot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The phrase is a specific name and should be understood as such without a breakdown that provides further meaning. However, each part involves morphemes with distinct roles. 存 (cún, 'exist; store') suggests preservation, 根 (gēn, 'root; origin') hints at foundational aspects; together 存根 stores foundational essence. 蓋 (gài, 'cover; lid') literally refers to a cover. 不改 (bù gǎi, 'not change') implies consistency. 老 (lǎo, 'old; experienced') suggests traditional or authentic. 火鍋(锅) (huǒ guō, 'hot pot') indicates a type of meal involving cooking at the table.
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 存根蓋不改老火鍋 can be understood as a name indicating a hot pot house that prides itself on retaining traditional or original recipes. 存根(gēn) emphasizes maintaining foundational aspects of the food. 不改 (bù gǎi) suggests an unwavering adherence to tradition, while 老火鍋 (lǎo huǒ guō) specifies the type of cuisine, known for its warming, communal dining nature.
