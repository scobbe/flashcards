## fried dough stick
### yóu tiáo
---
- **traditional:** 油條
- **definition:** fried dough stick
- **pinyin:** yóu tiáo
- **components:**
  - 油
    - yóu
    - oil
  - 条(條)
    - tiáo
    - strip; streak; item
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 油 (yóu, "oil") + 条(條) (tiáo, "strip") =
    - 油条(條) (yóutiáo, "fried dough stick")
  - **interpretation:** The term refers to a strip of dough that is deep-fried in oil, common in Chinese cuisine as a breakfast item or snack. The name directly describes its preparation method and shape: frying (油) in oil and its oblong, strip-like form (条).
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 油條
- **examples:**
  - 早餐常常配有油条(條)和豆浆(漿)。
    - Zǎocān chángcháng pèiyǒu yóutiáo hé dòujiāng.
    - Breakfast is often served with fried dough sticks and soy milk.
  - 中式早餐里(裡)有油条(條)，口感松软(軟)细(細)腻(膩)。
    - Zhōngshì zǎocān lǐ yǒu yóutiáo, kǒugǎn sōngruǎn xìnì.
    - In Chinese-style breakfasts, fried dough sticks are present, and they have a soft and delicate texture.
  - 唐代的市集常见(見)油条(條)，深受市民喜爱(愛)。
    - Tángdài de shìjí chángjiàn yóutiáo, shēnshòu shìmín xǐ'ài.
    - In the Tang Dynasty markets, fried dough sticks were commonly seen and were much loved by citizens.

- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 氵

    - water
  - 由

- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

    - liquid that flows ->
    - oil, sauce
  - **interpretation:** The character 油(yóu) combines the water radical 氵 (shuǐ, 'water') with the phonetic 由 (yóu, 'reason'). This suggests something liquid, aligning with the meanings 'oil' and 'sauce', both of which flow like water.
- **examples:**

    - Jiān yú shí kěyǐ xiān zài yú shàng mǒ diǎn yóu, zhèyàng yú gèng huá nèn.
    - When pan-frying fish, you can first spread a little oil on the fish, so that the fish becomes more tender.

### 油条(條) → 条(條)
- **traditional:** 條
- **definition:** strip; streak; item
- **pinyin:** tiáo
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood
  - 攸
    - yōu
    - a place far away
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 木 (mù, "wood") + phonetic 攸 (yōu) ->
    - originally indicates a small branch or wooden stick
  - **interpretation:** The character 條 (tiáo, "strip; streak; item") combines the semantic 木 (mù, "wood") to suggest something made of wood, and the phonetic 攸 (yōu) to provide the pronunciation. Initially, it referred specifically to a small branch or stick made of wood, which logically leads to the modern extended meanings of a 'strip' or 'item' in more general contexts because of the similar shape and form.
  - **simplification:** 条(條) simplifies 條 by removing the 木 (mù, "wood") component and simplifying 攸 (yōu) to a simpler form, focusing on phonetics rather than semantic resemblance to wood.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)条(條)裙子很好看。
    - Zhè tiáo qúnzi hěn hǎokàn.
    - This dress looks very nice.
  - 河上架了一座长(長)长(長)的桥(橋)。
    - Hé shàng jiàle yī zuò cháng cháng de qiáo.
    - A long bridge was built over the river.
  - 他发(發)送了一条(條)短信，通知朋友聚会(會)时(時)间(間)。
    - Tā fāsòngle yī tiáo duǎnxìn, tōngzhī péngyǒu jùhuì shíjiān.
    - He sent a text message, informing friends of the gathering time.

- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén

  - 攴
    - pū

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 人 (rén, "person") + 攴 (pū, "strike" or "hit") ->
    - depiction may include drops of water ->
    - combines to suggest 'warning' or 'purification'
  - **interpretation:** The character 攸 is an ideogrammic compound with 人 (rén, "person") + 攴 (pū, "strike" or "hit"). Two interpretations exist: one posits it as a depiction of a person about to be disciplined, thus suggesting 'warning'; another suggests a ritual washing, connected to ceremonial purification, and thus involves water symbolism. The sense of 'distant' likely emerged as a metaphorical extension from the idea of warning or cleansing, which sets one apart or corrects a path.
