### Sūnzǐ Bīngfǎ
---
- **traditional:** 孫子兵法
- **definition:** The Art of War by Sun Tzu
- **pinyin:** Sūnzǐ Bīngfǎ
- **components:**
  - 孫子
    - Sūnzǐ
    - Master Sun
  - 兵法
    - bīngfǎ

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The term 孫子兵法 (Sūnzǐ Bīngfǎ) refers to 'The Art of War,' an ancient Chinese military treatise. It was traditionally attributed to the military strategist 孫 (Sūn, "surname Sun") 子 (zǐ, "master; son") whose name collectively forms 孫子 (Sūnzǐ, "Master Sun"). The surname 孫 (Sūn, "surname Sun") connects him to his lineage, while 子 (zǐ, "master") conveys reverence and mastery. 兵 (bīng, "soldier; military") emphasizes the military focus of his teachings. 法 (fǎ, "method; law") indicates a comprehensive systematic methodology, reflecting the structured and strategic nature of the text.
  - **interpretation:** The name 孫子 (Sūnzǐ, "Master Sun") acknowledges Sun Tzu's status as a venerated figure in military strategy. 兵法 (bīngfǎ, "military methods") signifies the strategic doctrines and tactics explained in the text, making it an authoritative guide on warfare. 'Master Sun's Military Methods' highlights the treatise's focus on strategic principles employed by military leaders.
