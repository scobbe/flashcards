## Shanghai style
### hǎi pài
---
- **definition:** Shanghai style
- **pinyin:** hǎi pài
- **components:**
  - 海
    - hǎi
    - sea; ocean; maritime; large
  - 派
    - pài
    - style; group; faction; school
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 海 (hǎi, "sea") refers to the coastal and maritime element of Shanghai, a city near the East China Sea, indicating the openness and cosmopolitan nature of the city due to its sea connections. 派 (pài, "style; school; faction") describes a distinct approach or characteristic appearance related to a group or region. Here, it indicates a distinctive style that is associated with Shanghai.
  - **interpretation:** 海 (hǎi, "sea") suggests the openness, trade, and external influences due to Shanghai's maritime location, reflecting a cosmopolitan lifestyle. 派 (pài, "style; school") implies a unique cultural or artistic expression, and in this context, refers to the innovative and avant-garde aspects of Shanghai's culture and lifestyle shaped by its international interactions.
- **examples:**
  - 他( )的( )穿( )着(著)非( )常( )海( )派( )。
    - Tā de chuānzhuó fēicháng hǎi pài.
    - His clothing style is very Shanghai style.
  - 这(這)种(種)建( )筑(築)体(體)现(現)了( )海( )派( )风(風)格( )。
    - Zhè zhǒng jiànzhù tǐxiànle hǎi pài fēnggé.
    - This type of architecture embodies the Shanghai style.
  - 我( )喜( )欢(歡)海( )派( )的( )艺(藝)术(術)作( )品( )。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hǎi pài de yìshù zuòpǐn.
    - I love Shanghai style art pieces.

### 海派 → 海 → 每 → 母
- **definition:** mother
- **pinyin:** mǔ
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - woman
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** differentiated form
  - **description:**
    - 女 (nǚ, "woman") + addition of two dots ->
    - distinguished as 母 (mǔ, "mother")
  - **interpretation:** The character 母 (mǔ, "mother") is a differentiated form of 女 (nǚ, "woman") with the addition of two dots to distinguish it. In early script forms, both 母 (mǔ, "mother") and 女 (nǚ, "woman") were represented by the same form as 女, necessitating the addition of dots to create a visual distinction. The character 毋, originally borrowed from 母, further specialized by changing the dots to a stroke to avoid confusion between the characters.

### 海派 → 派 → 𠂢
- **definition:** original form
- **pinyin:** mǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a long river with tributaries ->
    - original form
  - **interpretation:** The character 𠂢 is a pictographic representation of a long river with tributaries, symbolizing its original form as it visually attempts to capture the imagery of flowing water and branching paths. It is described as the horizontal flip of 永 (yǒng, meaning 'eternal'), though its depiction clearly relates more to an aquatic landscape than an everlasting concept.
