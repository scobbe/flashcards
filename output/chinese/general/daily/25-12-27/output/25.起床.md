## to get out of bed; to wake up
### qǐ chuáng
---
- **definition:** to get out of bed; to wake up
- **pinyin:** qǐ chuáng
- **components:**
  - 起
    - qǐ
    - to rise; to start; to get up from
  - 床
    - chuáng
    - bed
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 起 (qǐ, "to rise; to start; to get up from") combined with 床 (chuáng, "bed") refers to the action of getting up from a bed, embodying the process of transitioning from a lying position to standing.
  - **interpretation:** 起 (qǐ, "to rise") conveys the action of moving upward or beginning an action. In this context, it refers explicitly to starting the day by getting up. When paired with 床 (chuáng, "bed"), it specifies the exact place from which one rises, thus directly invoking the act of getting out of bed. The sense of 'to wake up' derives from this physical action as waking is the mental state accompanying rising.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天早上六点(點)起床。
    - Wǒ měitiān zǎoshang liù diǎn qǐ chuáng.
    - I wake up at six every morning.
  - 天气(氣)冷时(時)，我不想起床。
    - Tiānqì lěng shí, wǒ bù xiǎng qǐ chuáng.
    - When the weather is cold, I don't want to get out of bed.
  - 他迟(遲)到了因为(為)起床太晚。
    - Tā chídào le yīnwèi qǐ chuáng tài wǎn.
    - He was late because he got out of bed too late.

- **components:**
  - 走

    - walk
  - 巳
    - sì

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 走 (zǒu, "walk") + phonetic 巳 (sì) ->
    - rise; lift; begin; get up
  - **interpretation:** The semantic component 走 (zǒu, "walk") suggests movement or action, fitting with the meaning of rising or getting up. The phonetic component 巳 (sì) was chosen for its sound, contributing to the pronunciation without directly affecting the meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 太阳(陽)升起，天边(邊)染上橘色。
    - Tàiyáng shēng qǐ, tiānbiān rǎn shàng jú sè.

  - 我们(們)要起床了。
    - Wǒmen yào qǐchuáng le.
    - We need to get up.
  - 新的一天开(開)始了，大家都充满(滿)活力。
    - Xīn de yī tiān kāishǐ le, dàjiā dōu chōngmǎn huólì.

    - tǔ

    - yuè

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**

### 起床 → 起 → 走 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning

It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').

### 起床 → 床
- **definition:** bed
- **pinyin:** chuáng

  - 广(廣)
    - guǎng

  - 木

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 广(廣) (guǎng, "house") + 木 (mù, "tree/wood") =

  - **interpretation:** The character 床 (chuáng, "bed") signifies a piece of wooden furniture located within a house. ### 起床 → 床 → 广(廣) → 廣 → 黃
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally a pictogram depicting a disabled person or man with a ring of jade on the chest ->
    - phonetically borrowed for 'yellow' ->

  - **interpretation:** The character 黃 (huáng, 'yellow') originated from a pictogram representing either a disabled person or a man with a jade ring, showing a connection to cultural rituals or symbols of status. In modern Chinese, its primary use is to denote the color yellow, disconnecting entirely from its debated pictographic origins.
