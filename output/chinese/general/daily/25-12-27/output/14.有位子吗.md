## Is there a seat available?
### yǒu wèi zi ma
---
- **traditional:** 有位子嗎
- **definition:** Is there a seat available?
- **pinyin:** yǒu wèi zi ma
- **components:**
  - 有
    - yǒu
    - have; there is
  - 位子
    - wèi zi
    - seat
  - 嗎
    - ma
    - question particle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 有 (yǒu, "have; there is"), 位 (wèi, "position; seat"), 子 (zi, "noun suffix"), 嗎 (ma, "question particle")
  - **interpretation:** 有 (yǒu, "have; there is") suggests possession or existence, implying the presence of something. 位 (wèi, "position; seat") denotes a specific location or available spot, often related to seating or rank. 子 (zi, "noun suffix") creates a noun phrase, finalizing the concept of a 'seat.' 嗎 (ma, "question particle") turns the phrase into a question, asking whether such a seat exists.
- **examples:**
  - 餐厅(廳)里(裡)的人很多，你可以问一下有位子吗(嗎)？
    - Cāntīng lǐ de rén hěn duō, nǐ kěyǐ wèn yīxià yǒu wèi zi ma?
    - There are many people in the restaurant, can you ask if there is a seat available?
  - 请(請)问(問)这(這)里(裡)有位子吗(嗎)？
    - Qǐng wèn zhèlǐ yǒu wèi zi ma?
    - Excuse me, is there a seat available here?
  - 如果你去电影院，你应该先查有位子吗(嗎)？
    - Rúguǒ nǐ qù diànyǐngyuàn, nǐ yīnggāi xiān chá yǒu wèi zi ma?
    - If you go to the cinema, you should check first if there is a seat available.

### 有位子吗(嗎) → 位子
- **definition:** seat
- **pinyin:** wèi zi
- **components:**
  - 位
    - wèi
    - position; place; stand
  - 子
    - zi
    - child; seed; noun suffix
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 位 (wèi, "position") refers to a place or status, often used to specify a particular location in space. The concept of "position" naturally includes the idea of a seat, as it is a specific location meant for someone to occupy. 子 (zi, "child; noun suffix") originally means "child" but in this context, it serves as a noun suffix that doesn't alter the inherent meaning significantly but emphasizes the substantive aspect of the term.
  - **interpretation:** 位 (wèi, "position") provides the core sense of a specific location designated for an individual, which directly translates to the idea of a 'seat' as a particular space reserved for sitting. 子 (zi, "child; noun suffix") acts as a functional suffix that classifies 位 (wèi, "position") clearly as a noun, reinforcing the substantive meaning without altering the fundamental concept of a specified place for someone. The result is a natural intuition of a defined spot meant for occupation by a person, thus, a 'seat'.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)电(電)影的位子很好。
    - Zhè ge diànyǐng de wèi zi hěn hǎo.
    - This seat at the movie is very good.
  - 请(請)问(問)这(這)里(裡)有空位子吗(嗎)？
    - Qǐng wèn zhèlǐ yǒu kōng wèi zi ma?
    - Excuse me, is there an available seat here?
  - 我们(們)需要预(預)订(訂)位子，因为(為)餐厅(廳)很忙。
    - Wǒmen xūyào yùdìng wèi zi, yīnwèi cāntīng hěn máng.
    - We need to book a seat because the restaurant is busy.
