## pink rose
### fěn hóng méi guī
---
- **traditional:** 粉紅玫瑰
- **definition:** pink rose
- **pinyin:** fěn hóng méi guī
- **components:**
  - 粉紅
    - fěnhóng
    - pink
  - 玫瑰
    - méiguī
    - rose
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 粉 (fěn, "powder; pink") and 紅 (hóng, "red") together form 粉紅 (fěnhóng, "pink"), indicating the light shade of red. 玫瑰 (méiguī, "rose") refers to the flower.
  - **interpretation:** The term 粉紅 (fěnhóng, "pink") combines 粉 (fěn, "powder") to imply a softer, lighter version of 紅 (hóng, "red"), creating the pastel color pink. 玫瑰 (méiguī, "rose") refers to the flower known for its association with romance and beauty. Together, 粉紅玫瑰 (fěnhóng méiguī, "pink rose") evokes the image of a rose colored in a gentle shade of pink.
- **examples:**
  - 她收到了很多粉红(紅)玫瑰。
    - Tā shōu dào le hěn duō fěn hóng méi guī.
    - She received many pink roses.
  - 粉红(紅)玫瑰的香味令人陶醉。
    - Fěn hóng méi guī de xiāngwèi lìng rén táozuì.
    - The fragrance of pink roses is intoxicating.
  - 在花园(園)里(裡)，粉红(紅)玫瑰盛开(開)的时(時)候，令人感到无(無)比幸福。
    - Zài huāyuán lǐ, fěn hóng méi guī shèngkāi de shíhòu, lìng rén gǎndào wúbǐ xìngfú.
    - In the garden, when the pink roses are in full bloom, it brings incomparable happiness.

### 粉红(紅)玫瑰 → 玫瑰
- **definition:** rose
- **pinyin:** méiguī
- **components:**
  - 玫
    - méi
    - rose gemstone; beautiful small stone
  - 瑰
    - guī
    - rare; precious; exceptional
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 玫 (méi, "rose gemstone; beautiful small stone") has a historical association with something small, precious, and beautiful, similar to a gemstone. 瑰 (guī, "rare; precious; exceptional") emphasizes uniqueness and value, often referring to something remarkable or extraordinary. Together, 玫瑰 (méiguī, "rose") evokes the image of a precious and exceptionally beautiful flower.
  - **interpretation:** 玫 (méi, "rose gemstone; beautiful small stone") focuses on beauty and preciousness, suggesting something delicate and charming like a gemstone. 瑰 (guī, "rare; precious; exceptional") highlights the unique and extraordinary qualities of the rose, marking it as a rare flower of great beauty. The combination implies an exquisite, highly valued flower—like a 'gem among flowers', hence the concept of a rose.
- **examples:**
  - 玫瑰花开(開)得很美。
    - Méi guī huā kāi de hěn měi.
    - The rose blooms beautifully.
  - 她收到了一束红(紅)玫瑰。
    - Tā shōu dào le yī shù hóng méi guī.
    - She received a bouquet of red roses.
  - 玫瑰象征(徵)爱(愛)情。
    - Méi guī xiàng zhēng ài qíng.
    - Roses symbolize love.

### 粉红(紅)玫瑰 → 玫瑰 → 玫 → 𤣩
- **definition:** a type of jade
- **pinyin:** (jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cong (ritual jade totem)
  - **interpretation:** The character 𤣩 is a variant form of 玉 (yù, "jade"), which originally depicted a cong, a type of ritual jade totem. Over time, a dot was added to distinguish it from 王 (wáng, "king") due to the visual similarity between the two characters. Jade played a significant role in art and rituals, which is why its pictogram was distinctly designed. The character represents the material jade and its cultural significance as a precious and spiritual object.
