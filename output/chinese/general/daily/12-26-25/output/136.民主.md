## democracy
### mínzhǔ
---
- **definition:** democracy
- **pinyin:** mínzhǔ
- **components:**
  - 民
    - mín
    - people; citizens
  - 主
    - zhǔ
    - master; lord; main
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 民(民) (mín, "people") + 主(主) (zhǔ, "master") =
    - people as masters ->
    - democracy
  - **interpretation:** The term 民主 describes a system of government where the people are the masters or have the ultimate authority, highlighting the idea of rule by the people.
- **examples:**
  - 公民参与政治推动民主(公民參與政治推動民主)。
    - Gōngmín cānyù zhèngzhì tuīdòng mínzhǔ.
    - Citizens participate in politics to promote democracy.
  - 自由与法治是民主的基石(自由與法治是民主的基石)。
    - Zìyóu yǔ fǎzhì shì mínzhǔ de jīshí.
    - Freedom and the rule of law are the cornerstones of democracy.
### 民主(民主) → 民(民)
- **definition:** people; citizens
- **pinyin:** mín
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted an eye pierced by a dagger (claimed) ->
    - later borrowed for 民 (mín, 'the people')
  - **interpretation:** The character 民(mín) primarily serves as a loan character, having lost its original pictorial depiction possibly related to an eye and a dagger. Its current meaning as 'the people' is a result of its phonetic borrowing and widespread use.
- **examples:**
  - 民众拥有参与决策的权利(民眾擁有參與決策的權利)。
    - Mín zhòng yǒngyǒu cānyù juécè de quánlì.
    - The people have the right to participate in decision-making.
  - 国家依靠民意趋向稳定(國家依靠民意趨向穩定)，推动社会进步(推動社會進步)。
    - Guójiā yīkào mínyì qūxiàng wěndìng, tuīdòng shèhuì jìnbù.
    - The country relies on the people's opinions for stability, promoting social progress.
  - 选民积极行使权力(選民積極行使權力)，维护国家安全(維護國家安全)。
    - Xuǎn mín jījí xíngshǐ quánlì, wéihù guójiā ānquán.
    - Voters actively exercise their rights, safeguarding national security.
### 民主(民主) → 主(主)
- **definition:** master; lord; main
- **pinyin:** zhǔ
- **components:**
  - 丶
    - diǎn
    - dot
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 丶(𠄠) (diǎn, "dot") + phonetic: 王(王) (wáng, "king") =
    - element indicating the primary or leading role ->
    - master or lord
  - **interpretation:** The character 主(主) (zhǔ, "master") combines the semantic component 丶(𠄠) (diǎn, "dot"), which can represent a central point or focus, with the phonetic component 王(王) (wáng, "king"), which suggests authority. Together, they convey the idea of a central authority or main figure, consistent with meanings like "master" or "lord."
- **examples:**
  - 他是家里的主人(他是家裡的主人)。
    - Tā shì jiālǐ de zhǔrén.
    - He is the master of the house.
  - 会议上，她主导流程(會議上，她主導流程)，确保讨论有效(確保討論有效)。
    - Huìyì shàng, tā zhǔdǎo liúchéng, quèbǎo tǎolùn yǒuxiào.
    - At the meeting, she led the process and ensured the discussion was effective.
  - 公司把主要责任交给了他(公司把主要責任交給了他)，希望他带领团队(希望他帶領團隊)。
    - Gōngsī bǎ zhǔyào zérèn jiāogěi le tā, xīwàng tā dàilǐng tuánduì.
    - The company entrusted him with the main responsibility, hoping he would lead the team.
### 民主(民主) → 主(主) → 丶(丶)
- **definition:** dot
- **pinyin:** diǎn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a small dot or a small fire on a torch; used in myriad forms representing various small or singular elements
  - **interpretation:** The character 丶(diǎn, "dot") is a pictogram representing the concept of a small point or dot. Over time, it has been used to symbolize a wide array of small elements or features within different characters.
- **examples:**
  - 书法中每一笔都充满韵律(書法中每一筆都充滿韻律)，包括那细小的丶(包括那細小的丶)。
    - Shūfǎ zhōng měi yī bǐ dōu chōngmǎn yùnlǜ, bāokuò nà xìxiǎo de diǎn.
    - Every stroke in calligraphy is full of rhythm, including that tiny dot.
  - 毛笔字中(毛筆字中)，一抹丶显得极为生动(一抹丶顯得極為生動)。
    - Máobǐ zì zhōng, yī mǒu diǎn xiǎnde jíwéi shēngdòng.
    - In calligraphy, a single dot appears extremely vivid.
### 民主(民主) → 主(主) → 王(王)
- **definition:** king
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a ritual axe symbolizing royal authority ->
    - ceremonial tool near the throne ->
    - final form for king
  - **interpretation:** The character 王(wáng, "king") visually represents a ritual axe, a powerful symbol of authority and royal rituals in ancient China. This connection to ceremonial objects underscores the king's role in maintaining order.
- **examples:**
  - 王是中国的常见姓氏(王是中國的常見姓氏)。
    - Wáng shì Zhōngguó de chángjiàn xìngshì.
    - Wang is a common surname in China.
  - 古时的王统治天下(古時的王統治天下)，人民敬仰他的威严(人民敬仰他的威嚴)。
    - Gǔ shí de wáng tǒngzhì tiānxià, rénmín jìngyǎng tā de wēiyán.
    - In ancient times, kings ruled the world, and the people admired their authority.
