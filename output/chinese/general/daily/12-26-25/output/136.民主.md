## democracy
### mínzhǔ
---
- **definition:** democracy
- **pinyin:** mínzhǔ
- **components:**
  - 民
    - mín
    - people; populace; civilian; folk
  - 主
    - zhǔ
    - master; owner; lord; primary
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 民(people) + 主(master; primary) = people as masters (democracy).
  - **interpretation:** Emphasizes political authority vested in the people; a calque for 'rule by the people'. Contrasts with 君主 ('monarch'), shifting mastery from ruler to the populace.
- **examples:**
  - 我们珍惜民主和自由(我們珍惜民主和自由)。
    - Wǒmen zhēnxī mínzhǔ hé zìyóu.
    - We cherish democracy and freedom.
  - 这个国家通过选举实现民主(這個國家通過選舉實現民主)。
    - Zhège guójiā tōngguò xuǎnjǔ shíxiàn mínzhǔ.
    - This country achieves democracy through elections.
  - 民主制度需要公民参与与监督(民主制度需要公民參與與監督)。
    - Mínzhǔ zhìdù xūyào gōngmín cānyù yǔ jiāndū.
    - Democratic institutions require citizen participation and oversight.
### 民主 → 民
- **definition:** people; populace; civilian; folk
- **pinyin:** mín
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram; later jiajie (phonetic loan) for “the people”.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a pictorial graph whose precise image is uncertain, it was later borrowed (jiajie) to write the word ‘people’. Claims that it depicts an eye pierced by a tool are likely folk etymology and are not supported by phonetic evidence linking it to 盲.
- **examples:**
  - 中国人民团结一致(中國人民團結一致)。
    - Zhōngguó rénmín tuánjié yīzhì.
    - The Chinese people are united.
  - 政府应倾听民意(政府應傾聽民意)。
    - Zhèngfǔ yīng qīngtīng mínyì.
    - The government should listen to public opinion.
  - 战乱时期，许多平民流离失所(戰亂時期，許多平民流離失所)。
    - Zhànluàn shíqī, xǔduō píngmín liúlí shīsuǒ.
    - During wartime, many civilians are displaced.
### 民主 → 主
- **definition:** master; owner; lord; primary
- **pinyin:** zhǔ
- **components:**
  - 丶
    - zhǔ
    - dot
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形). Three distinct forms can be observed in ancient scripts, all of which have merged in modern script; unrelated to the right component of 往 (< 𢓸 ).
  - **interpretation:** Originally drawn forms varied but later standardized into the present graph, while the sense ‘master/primary’ became fixed. Do not confuse it with the right-side element of 往, which is etymologically unrelated.
- **examples:**
  - 他是这家店的主人(他是這家店的主人)。
    - Tā shì zhè jiā diàn de zhǔrén.
    - He is the owner of this shop.
  - 这个计划的主要目标很明确(這個計劃的主要目標很明確)。
    - Zhège jìhuà de zhǔyào mùbiāo hěn míngquè.
    - The primary goal of this plan is very clear.
  - 他向主祈祷(他向主祈禱)。
    - Tā xiàng zhǔ qídǎo.
    - He prays to the Lord.
### 民主 → 主 → 丶
- **definition:** dot
- **pinyin:** zhǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram: a point/dot; also depicted a small fire on a torch when used as the original form of 主.
  - **interpretation:** At its core, it is the simple indicative mark of a dot, corresponding to the brush stroke 点. In early graphs it could also picture a small flame perched on a torch, a role that fed into the ancient form of 主.
- **examples:**
  - 汉字里有一笔叫“点”(漢字裡有一筆叫「點」)，写作“丶”(寫作「丶」)。
    - Hànzì lǐ yǒu yī bǐ jiào “diǎn”, xiězuò “zhǔ”.
    - In Chinese characters, there is a stroke called “dot,” written as “丶.”
  - 写汉字时(寫漢字時)，这一笔“丶”要从上往下点(這一筆「丶」要從上往下點)。
    - Xiě Hànzì shí, zhè yī bǐ “zhǔ” yào cóng shàng wǎng xià diǎn.
    - When writing Chinese characters, the stroke “丶” should be made from top to bottom.
  - 雨字上面的两点是两个“丶”(雨字上面的兩點是兩個「丶」)。
    - Yǔ zì shàngmiàn de liǎng diǎn shì liǎng gè “zhǔ”.
    - The two dots on the character 雨 are two “丶.”
### 民主 → 主 → 王
- **definition:** king
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram of a ritual (jade) axe kept near the throne for ceremonies; symbol of royal power.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a ceremonial axe used in royal rites, a concrete emblem of sovereign authority. A later folk reading treats the three horizontals as Heaven–Man–Earth joined by the vertical, and it is unrelated to 玉 or 主.
- **examples:**
  - 他姓王(他姓王)。
    - Tā xìng Wáng.
    - His surname is Wang.
  - 国王召见大臣(國王召見大臣)。
    - Guówáng zhàojiàn dàchén.
    - The king summoned the ministers.
  - 在古代，王举行祭祀以祈福(在古代，王舉行祭祀以祈福)。
    - Zài gǔdài, wáng jǔxíng jìsì yǐ qífú.
    - In ancient times, the king performed rituals to pray for blessings.
