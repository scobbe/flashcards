## 巴金(巴金)
---
## 
### Ba Jin
---
- **pinyin:** Ba Jin
- **components:**
  - 巴
    - bā
    - to cling; to hold on; to hope for; to wait for
  - 金
    - jīn
    - gold; metal; money; wealth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 巴(巴) (bā, 'to cling; to hold on') + 金(金) (jīn, 'gold; metal') — cling to gold
  - **interpretation:** The name 巴金 suggests a desire to 'cling to gold' or wealth. This might reflect both aspiration and value, aligning with the significance of gold in culture.
- **examples:**
  - 巴金是中国著名的作家。(巴金是中國著名的作家。)
    - Bā jīn shì Zhōngguó zhùmíng de zuòjiā.
    - Ba Jin is a famous writer from China.
  - 我喜欢读巴金的作品。(我喜歡讀巴金的作品。)
    - Wǒ xǐhuān dú Bā jīn de zuòpǐn.
    - I enjoy reading works by Ba Jin.
### 巴金(巴金) → 巴(巴)
- **definition:** to cling; to hold on; to hope for; to wait for
- **pinyin:** bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a person or animal holding on to something tightly.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the sense of holding on or clinging to something, which is central to its meanings of waiting and hoping.
- **examples:**
  - 我想要巴着你(我想要巴著你)。
    - wǒ xiǎng yào bā zhe nǐ
    - I want to cling to you.
  - 他在巴望着好消息(他在巴望著好消息)。
    - tā zài bā wàng zhe hǎo xiāo xī
    - He is hoping for good news.
### 巴金(巴金) → 金(金)
- **definition:** gold; metal; money; wealth
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **components:**
  - 士
    - shì
    - upside-down ritual axe
  - 呂
    - lǚ
    - two blocks of metal
  - 亼
    - jīn
    - phonetic component
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Semantic 士 (shì, 'upside-down ritual axe') + semantic 呂 (lǚ, 'two blocks of metal') + phonetic 亼 (indicates pronunciation).
  - **interpretation:** The components collectively suggest an object associated with precious materials and metal, emphasizing its material significance.
- **examples:**
  - 金属的性质(金屬的性質)
    - jīnshǔ de xìngzhì
    - The properties of metal.
  - 他喜欢金(金)
    - tā xǐhuān jīn
    - He likes gold.
### 巴金(巴金) → 金(金) → 士(士)
- **definition:** upside-down ritual axe
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** A war axe, which later evolved to represent 'soldier' and 'officer, intellectual'.
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts an upside-down ritual axe, reflecting the association with military and governance roles historically.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一位士(士)，很有学问。
    - Tā shì yī wèi shì, hěn yǒu xuéwèn.
    - He is a scholar, very knowledgeable.
  - 士(士)兵正在执行任务。
    - Shìbīng zhèng zài zhíxíng rènwu.
    - The soldier is carrying out a mission.
  - 古代士(士)人受人尊敬。
    - Gǔdài shìrén shòu rén zūnjìng.
    - In ancient times, scholars were respected.
### 巴金(巴金) → 金(金) → 呂(呂)
- **definition:** two blocks of metal
- **pinyin:** lǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts two blocks of metal. Originally disconnected, the connecting line was added in later scripts.
  - **interpretation:** The two blocks evoke the image of separate pieces of metal, which can signify both physical objects and the idea of connection when linked.
- **examples:**
  - 他给了我一个呂(他給了我一個呂)，我很高兴(我很高興)。
    - tā gěi le wǒ yī gè lǚ, wǒ hěn gāoxìng.
    - He gave me a lǚ, and I was very happy.
  - 在音乐中，呂(在音樂中，呂)的音符很美(的音符很美)。
    - zài yīnyuè zhōng, lǚ de yīnfú hěn měi.
    - In music, the notes of lǚ are beautiful.
