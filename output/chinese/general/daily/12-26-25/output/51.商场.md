## shopping mall
### shāngchǎng
---
- **traditional:** 商場
- **definition:** shopping mall
- **pinyin:** shāngchǎng
- **components:**
  - 商
    - shāng
    - commerce; business
  - 场(場)
    - chǎng
    - open space; place; field; stage
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 商 (shāng, "commerce") + 场(場) (chǎng, "open space" or "place") =
    - a place for commerce
  - **interpretation:** A 商场(場) (shāngchǎng, "shopping mall") is a large open space dedicated to commerce where various shops and businesses are situated. The combination of 商 (shāng, "commerce") with 场(場) (chǎng, "open space" or "place") conveys the idea of a designated area for trade and services, aligning with modern commercial practices.
  - **simplification:** Customary simplification by converting 場 to 场
- **examples:**
  - 她喜欢(歡)在商场(場)，在那里(裏)逛街。
    - Tā xǐhuān zài shāngchǎng, zài nàlǐ guàngjiē.
    - She likes shopping at the mall, strolling around there.
  - 我打算去商场(場)，看看新款服装(裝)。
    - Wǒ dǎsuàn qù shāngchǎng, kànkan xīnkuǎn fúzhuāng.
    - I plan to go to the mall to check out the new fashion clothes.
  - 周末时(時)，我们(們)在商场(場)放松(鬆)休闲(閒)。
    - Zhōumò shí, wǒmen zài shāngchǎng fàngsōng xiūxián.
    - On weekends, we relax at the shopping mall.
### 商( )场(場) → 商
- **definition:** commerce; business
- **pinyin:** shāng
- **components:**
  - 㕯
    - yīn
    - to speak slowly
  - 章
    - zhāng
    - chapter
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 㕯 ("to speak slowly") + phonetic 章 ("zhāng") ->
    - originally meant estimating from the outside ->
    - developed to mean trading/commerce
  - **interpretation:** The character 商(shāng) originally described the concept of estimating, which is linked to trading where one evaluates items before purchasing or exchanging them. Over time, the meaning shifted to specifically denote commerce or trade. The semantic component 㕯 relates to the idea of communication or deliberation, which is fundamental in trading contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 我对(對)商机(機)充满兴(滿興)趣。
    - Wǒ duì shāngjī chōngmǎn xìngqù.
    - I am very interested in business opportunities.
  - 他进(進)入商界谋(謀)求发(發)展。
    - Tā jìnrù shāngjiè móuqiú fāzhǎn.
    - He entered the business world to seek development.
  - 商人讲(講)求信誉(譽)，消费(費)者追求质(質)量。
    - Shāngrén jiǎngqiú xìn yù, xiāofèizhě zhuīqiú zhìliàng.
    - Businesspeople value reputation, and consumers pursue quality.

    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

- **examples:**

    - Zhège zì de yīn shǐ wǒmen liánxiǎng dào yuánshǐ fàyīn.
    - The phonetic component of this character reminds us of its original pronunciation.

### 商( )场(場) → 商 → 章
- **definition:** chapter
- **pinyin:** zhāng
- **components:**
  - 辛
    - xīn
    - chisel
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 辛 (xīn, "chisel") + 日 (rì, "sun") =
    - chisel piercing a piece of jade ->
    - originally meant "to treat jade" ->
    - later evolved to "chapter"
  - **interpretation:** The character 章 initially depicted a chisel (辛) piercing a piece of jade, represented by the 日 symbol. This depiction symbolized the process of treating jade. Over time, it likely evolved to be used for the concept of "chapter," as jade carvings and inscriptions often represented chapters or sections in a work.
- **examples:**
  - 第一章让(讓)人印象深刻。
    - Dì yī zhāng ràng rén yìnxiàng shēnkè.
    - The first chapter left a deep impression.
  - 他们讨论(們討論)了第二章的内(內)容。
    - Tāmen tǎolùn le dì èr zhāng de nèiróng.
    - They discussed the content of the second chapter.
  - 第一章启发(啟發)了读(讀)者，第二章展开(開)了故事。
    - Dì yī zhāng qǐfā le dúzhě, dì èr zhāng zhǎnkāi le gùshì.
    - The first chapter inspired the readers, and the second chapter unfolded the story.

### 商( )场(場) → 商 → 章 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
- **examples:**
  - 晨曦来临时(來臨時)，日出东(東)方。
    - Chénxī láilín shí, rìchū dōngfāng.
    - At the break of dawn, the sun rises in the east.
  - 温(溫)暖的日光洒满(灑滿)大地。
    - Wēnnuǎn de rìguāng sǎ mǎn dàdì.
    - The warm sunlight spreads over the earth.
  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。
    - Zhōngwǔ shífēn, rì gāo guà tiān.
    - At noon, the sun hangs high in the sky.

  - 演唱会场(會場)内，歌迷热(熱)情洋溢。

### 商( )场(場) → 场(場) → 昜 → 彡
