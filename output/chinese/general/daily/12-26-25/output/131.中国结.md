## Chinese knot
### Zhōngguó jié
---
- **definition:** Chinese knot
- **pinyin:** Zhōngguó jié
- **components:**
  - 中国(中國)
    - Zhōngguó
    - China; Chinese (cultural/national); Middle Kingdom
  - 结(結)
    - jié
    - knot; to tie; knotwork; bond
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 中国 + 结 = Chinese-style decorative knot.
  - **interpretation:** Names the traditional decorative knots associated with Chinese culture, combining the country/culture term with “knot” to signal origin and style.
  - **simplification:** 國→国 reduces the interior components; 結→结 uses the simplified silk radical 纟 to lower stroke count, improving writing efficiency.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢在门口挂中国结(我喜歡在門口掛中國結)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan zài ménkǒu guà Zhōngguó jié.
    - I like to hang a Chinese knot at the doorway.
  - 她送我一个红色的中国结(她送我一個紅色的中國結)，祝我好运(祝我好運)。
    - Tā sòng wǒ yí gè hóngsè de Zhōngguó jié, zhù wǒ hǎo yùn.
    - She gave me a red Chinese knot and wished me good luck.
  - 春节时(春節時)，家家户户都用中国结装饰房子(家家戶戶都用中國結裝飾房子)。
    - Chūnjié shí, jiājiā hùhù dōu yòng Zhōngguó jié zhuāngshì fángzi.
    - During Spring Festival, every household uses Chinese knots to decorate the house.
### 中国结(中國結) → 中国(中國)
- **definition:** China; Chinese (cultural/national); Middle Kingdom
- **pinyin:** Zhōngguó
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 中 (middle; central) + 国/國 (state; nation) = the central state (‘Middle Kingdom’).
  - **interpretation:** Names the central states in the classical Sino-centric worldview; in modern usage, the country of China or the Chinese nation.
  - **simplification:** 國 was simplified to 国 by replacing the complex interior 或 with the simpler 玉/王 component to reduce strokes; 中 is unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我在中国工作(我在中國工作)。
    - Wǒ zài Zhōngguó gōngzuò.
    - I work in China.
  - 中国历史很长(中國歷史很長)，文化多样(文化多樣)。
    - Zhōngguó lìshǐ hěn cháng, wénhuà duōyàng.
    - China’s history is long, and its culture is diverse.
  - 他们热爱中国(他們熱愛中國)。
    - Tāmen rè’ài Zhōngguó.
    - They love China.
### 中国结(中國結) → 结(結)
- **definition:** knot; to tie; knotwork; bond
- **pinyin:** jié
- **components:**
  - 吉
    - jí
    - auspicious; lucky
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 糸 + phonetic: 吉
  - **interpretation:** 糸 evokes threads and tying, pointing to meanings like knotting and binding. 吉 contributes the sound (OC 吉 *klid ~ 結 *kiːd) and by association can suggest an auspicious tying together or conclusion.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by replacing the left-side 糸 with 纟 under the standard silk-radical simplification; the phonetic 吉 was retained unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我把绳子打结(我把繩子打結)。
    - Wǒ bǎ shéngzi dǎ jié.
    - I tie a knot in the rope.
  - 我们今天结婚(我們今天結婚)。
    - Wǒmen jīntiān jiéhūn.
    - We are getting married today.
  - 会议圆满结束(會議圓滿結束)。
    - Huìyì yuánmǎn jiéshù.
    - The meeting concluded successfully.
### 中国结(中國結) → 结(結) → 吉
- **definition:** auspicious; lucky
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 士
    - shì
    - scholar; gentleman; soldier
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram (指事)
  - **description:** Derived from 士 ‘ritual axe’ + 口 ‘open mouth’, referring to rituals seeking the favor of ancestors and deities.
  - **interpretation:** Combining a ritual implement with an open mouth evokes solemn prayers or proclamations made during sacrificial rites. Through the idea that proper ritual brings divine approval, the sense extends to auspiciousness and good fortune.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是个吉日(今天是個吉日)。
    - Jīntiān shì gè jí rì.
    - Today is an auspicious day.
  - 祝你吉祥安康(祝你吉祥安康)。
    - Zhù nǐ jíxiáng ānkāng.
    - Wishing you good fortune and health.
  - 吉先生到了(吉先生到了)。
    - Jí Xiānsheng dàole.
    - Mr. Ji has arrived.
### 中国结(中國結) → 结(結) → 吉 → 士
- **definition:** scholar; gentleman; soldier
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: war/ritual axe; later ‘soldier’ → ‘officer/intellectual’; related to 王, 圭, 金.
  - **interpretation:** Earliest forms depicted an axe, a symbol of martial power and authority, which naturally extended to mean a soldier and then an officer or learned gentleman. Connections to 王, 圭, and 金 reflect the ritual or jade/metal axe lineage in related graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一名士兵(他是一名士兵)。
    - Tā shì yì míng shìbīng.
    - He is a soldier.
  - 他是个绅士(他是個紳士)。
    - Tā shì ge shēnshì.
    - He is a gentleman.
  - 古代的士人重视礼仪(古代的士人重視禮儀)。
    - Gǔdài de shìrén zhòngshì lǐyí.
    - In ancient times, scholar-gentry valued ritual propriety.
### 中国结(中國結) → 结(結) → 吉 → 口
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
