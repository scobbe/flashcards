## electric charge
### diànhè
---
- **definition:** electric charge
- **pinyin:** diànhè
- **components:**
  - 电(電)
    - diàn
    - electric; electricity
  - 荷
    - hè
    - load; burden
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 电(電) (diàn, "electric; electricity") + 荷(荷) (hè, "load; burden")
  - **interpretation:** In 电荷(電荷), 电(電) (diàn, "electric; electricity") refers to energy or electric phenomenon, while 荷(荷) (hè, "load; burden") speaks to something being carried or transferred. Together, they describe the concept of electric charge, which carries electrical energy.
- **examples:**
  - 电荷在电场中运动(電荷在電場中運動)。
    - Diànhè zài diànchǎng zhōng yùndòng.
    - Electric charge moves in an electric field.
  - 带电物体释放电荷(帶電物體釋放電荷)，形成雷电(形成雷電)。
    - Dài diàn wùtǐ shìfàng diànhè, xíngchéng léidiàn.
    - A charged object releases electric charge, forming lightning.
### 电荷(電荷) → 电(電)
- **definition:** electric; electricity
- **pinyin:** diàn
- **components:**
  - 雨
    - yǔ
    - rain
  - 申
    - shēn
    - extend
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic 雨(雨) (yǔ, "rain") + Phonetic 申(申) (shēn, "extend") =
    - rain + lightning =
    - electricity ->
    - 电(電) (diàn, "electricity")
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 雨(雨) (yǔ, "rain") to imply natural phenomena and 申(申) (shēn, "extend") as a phonetic component suggesting lightning or electrical energy. This illustrates the concept of electricity as a harnessing of natural, rain-related energy like lightning.
  - **simplification:** 电(電) was simplified by retaining only the key phonetic component 申(申) (shēn, "extend") while omitting additional strokes from the lightning element, simplifying the visual representation.
- **examples:**
  - 电使我们的生活更加便利(電使我們的生活更加便利)。
    - Diàn shǐ wǒmen de shēnghuó gèngjiā biànlì.

    - Gōngchǎng yòng diàn, bǎozhèng shēngchǎn xiàolǜ.
