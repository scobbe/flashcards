## the Bund
### wàitān
---
- **traditional:** 外灘
- **definition:** the Bund
- **pinyin:** wàitān
- **components:**
  - 外
    - wài
    - outside; external
  - 滩(灘)
    - tān
    - beach; shore
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 外 (wài, "outside") refers to something external or foreign, while 滩(灘) (tān, "beach; shore") denotes a flat area of sand or small stones near the water's edge. Therefore, 外( )滩(灘) (wàitān, "the Bund") describes an external or outside beach shore, most famously referring to the waterfront area in central Shanghai along the Huangpu River.
  - **interpretation:** The term 外( )滩(灘) (wàitān, "the Bund") describes a waterfront area, highlighting its feature as the external shore in the bustling city of Shanghai, offering historical and cultural significance tied to its colonial past.
  - **simplification:** 外滩 is the simplified form; the traditional form is 外灘.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )昨( )天( )去( )了( )外( )滩(灘)。
