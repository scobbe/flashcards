## pet
### chǒngwù
---
- **definition:** pet
- **pinyin:** chǒngwù
- **components:**
  - 宠(寵)
    - chǒng
    - to pamper; to favor
  - 物
    - wù
    - thing; object; matter
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 宠(寵) (chǒng, "to pamper") + 物(物) (wù, "thing; object") refers to animals kept at home that are often pampered or taken care of, therefore called "pets."
  - **interpretation:** The term 宠物(寵物) (chǒngwù, "pet") makes sense because pets, as companion animals, are often regarded with affection and care, as suggested by the characters 'to pamper' and 'thing.'
  - **simplification:** The simplified form is 宠, where the traditional form is 寵. 宠 simplifies the component structure of 寵.
- **examples:**
  - 我养了一只宠物(我養了一隻寵物)。
    - Wǒ yǎng le yī zhī chǒngwù.
    - I have a pet.
  - 宠物不仅仅是朋友(寵物不僅僅是朋友)，而且也是家庭的一员(而且也是家庭的一員)。
    - Chǒngwù bù jǐn jǐn shì péngyǒu, érqiě yě shì jiātíng de yī yuán.
    - A pet is not only a friend, but also a member of the family.
  - 带宠物散步(帶寵物散步)，能使心情愉快(能使心情愉快)。
    - Dài chǒngwù sànbù, néng shǐ xīnqíng yúkuài.
    - Walking with a pet can lift your mood.
### 宠物(寵物) → 宠(寵)
- **definition:** to pamper; to favor
- **pinyin:** chǒng
- **components:**
  - 宀
    - mián
    - roof
  - 龍
    - lóng
    - dragon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 宀(宀) (mián, "roof") + phonetic 龍(龍) (lóng, "dragon") =
    - sheltering dragon ->
    - pampered, favored
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 宀(宀) (mián, "roof") symbolizing a protective shelter, with 龍(龍) (lóng, "dragon"), which provides the pronunciation. The concept suggests something valuable enough to be protected, thus conveying the idea of being pampered or favored.
  - **simplification:** The simplified character 宠 replaces the phonetic component 龍(龍) (lóng, "dragon") with a simpler structure compatible with 宍(宍) (ròu, "flesh"), maintaining the semantic of comfort and protection under a roof.
- **examples:**
  - 妈妈总是宠你(媽媽總是寵你)。
    - Mā mā zǒng shì chǒng nǐ.
    - Mom always pampers you.
  - 他宠儿子(他寵兒子)，常常给他买玩具(常常給他買玩具)。
    - Tā chǒng ér zi, cháng cháng gěi tā mǎi wán jù.
    - He spoils his son by often buying him toys.
  - 爷爷宠孙子(爺爺寵孫子)，讲述过去的故事(講述過去的故事)。
    - Yé yé chǒng sūn zi, jiǎng shù guò qù de gù shì.
    - Grandpa pampers his grandson while recounting stories from the past.
### 宠物(寵物) → 宠(寵) → 宀(宀)
- **definition:** roof
- **pinyin:** mián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a roof
  - **interpretation:** 宀(宀) (mián, 'roof') is a pictogram representing the shape of a roof, illustrating the concept of shelter or covering typically associated with roofs.
- **examples:**

    - This Chinese character contains the radical mián, indicating the structure of a house.
  - 汉字中有宀部(漢字中有宀部)，代表与家有关的概念(代表與家有關的概念)。
    - Hànzì zhōng yǒu mián bù, dàibiǎo yǔ jiā yǒuguān de gàiniàn.
    - Chinese characters with the mián radical represent concepts associated with home.

### 宠物(寵物) → 物(物)
- **definition:** thing; object; matter
- **pinyin:** wù
- **components:**
  - 牛
    - niú
    - cow
  - 勿
    - wù
    - do not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 牛(牛) (niú, "cow") + 勿(勿) (wù, "do not") =
    - cow + phonetic 勿 ->
    - object, thing
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 牛(牛) (niú, "cow"), symbolizing an object of value or importance, with 勿(勿) (wù, "do not"), which provides the phonetic component, to denote a general sense of an object or thing.
- **examples:**

    - Zhè ge wù lìng rén jīng yà.
    - This object is surprising.
  - 这个物很古老(這個物很古老)，充满传说(充滿傳說)。
    - Zhè ge wù hěn gǔ lǎo, chōng mǎn chuán shuō.
    - This object is ancient and full of legends.
### 宠物(寵物) → 物(物) → 牛(牛)
- **definition:** cow
- **pinyin:** niú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bull's head with two horns and two ears
  - **interpretation:** The character 牛(牛) (niú, "cow") visually represents the distinct features of a bull, with the two upward curving horns and the two ears forming its shape. This direct pictographic representation makes it easily recognizable and intuitive as a depiction of a cow.
- **examples:**
  - 农夫养牛(農夫養牛)。
    - Nóngfū yǎng niú.
    - The farmer raises cows.
  - 田野开阔(田野開闊)，牛自由漫步(牛自由漫步)。
    - Tiányě kāikuò, niú zìyóu mànbù.
    - In the vast fields, the cows stroll freely.
### 宠物(寵物) → 物(物) → 勿(勿)
- **definition:** do not
- **pinyin:** wù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an ancient depiction of fingers pinching a cloth indicating 'not to handle'
  - **interpretation:** 勿(勿) (wù, "do not") originates as a pictogram symbolizing a taboo against handling. The character resembles fingers pinching cloth, metaphorically representing prohibition.
- **examples:**
  - 考试前勿紧张(考試前勿緊張)。
    - kǎoshì qián wù jǐnzhāng
    - Do not be nervous before the exam.
  - 勿忘初衷(勿忘初衷)，保持清醒(保持清醒)。
    - wù wàng chūzhōng, bǎochí qīngxǐng
    - Do not forget your original intention, remain clear-headed.
  - 勿触碰禁区(勿觸碰禁區)。
    - wù chùpèng jìnqū
    - Do not touch the forbidden area.
