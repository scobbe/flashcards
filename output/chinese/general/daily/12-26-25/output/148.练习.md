## 练习(練習)
---
## 
### practice
---
- **pinyin:** practice
- **components:**
  - 练(練)
    - liàn
    - practice; to train; to refine; to forge
  - 习(習)
    - xí
    - to study; to cultivate; to learn; custom
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 练(練) (liàn, "practice") + 习(習) (xí, "to study; to cultivate") — practice study
  - **interpretation:** The combination makes sense as 练(練) (liàn, "practice") refers to the act of training or honing a skill, while 习(習) (xí, "to study; to cultivate") emphasizes the learning aspect involved in that practice.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天练习(我每天練習)汉字。
    - Wǒ měitiān liànxí hànzì.
    - I practice Chinese characters every day.
  - 为了考试，我需要更多的练习(更多的練習)。
    - Wèile kǎoshì, wǒ xūyào gèng duō de liànxí.
    - I need more practice for the exam.
### 练习(練習) → 练(練)
- **definition:** practice; to train; to refine; to forge
- **pinyin:** liàn
- **components:**
  - 糸(絲)
    - sī
    - silk
  - 聯
    - lián
    - to join
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Composed of the semantic component 糸 (mì, 'silk') indicating something related to textiles or threads and the phonetic component 聯 (lián, 'to join'), suggesting the act of refining or training something intricately.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of silk and joining implies a meticulous process of refining skills or materials through training. This reflects the careful attention needed in both practice and weaving.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天都练习钢琴(我每天都練習鋼琴)。
    - Wǒ měi tiān dōu liànxí gāngqín.
    - I practice piano every day.
  - 她在练武术(她在練武術)。
    - Tā zài liàn wǔshù.
    - She is training in martial arts.
  - 我们需要练习口语(我們需要練習口語)。
    - Wǒmen xūyào liànxí kǒuyǔ.
    - We need to practice spoken language.
### 练习(練習) → 练(練) → 糸(糸)
- **definition:** silk
- **pinyin:** sī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a thread, originally used to represent silk.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents the delicate threads of silk, emphasizing its significance in textiles.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān sīchóu.
    - I like silk.
  - 他买了一条丝带(他買了一條絲帶)。
    - Tā mǎile yītiáo sīdài.
    - He bought a silk ribbon.
### 练习(練習) → 练(練) → 聯(聯)
- **definition:** to join
- **pinyin:** lián
- **components:**
  - 耳
    - ěr
    - ear
  - 絲
    - sī
    - silk
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Combines 耳 (耳) (ěr, 'ear') and 絲 (丝) (sī, 'silk')
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts the connection between hearing (耳) and the fine, delicate texture of silk (絲), suggesting a joining or linking of elements.
  - **simplification:** 聯(联) simplified to 联 by reducing the complexity of the character; it substituted the traditional component with a simpler form.
- **examples:**
  - 我們需要聯繫(我们需要联系)。
    - wǒmen xūyào liánxì.
    - We need to make contact.
  - 這些作品聯合展出(这些作品联合展出)。
    - zhèxiē zuòpǐn liánhé zhǎnchū.
    - These works are exhibited together.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Combines 糸 (mì, 'silk') + 糸 (mì, 'silk') — two representations of silk to signify that it is made of multiple strands.

### 练习(練習) → 习(習)
- **definition:** to study; to cultivate; to learn; custom
- **pinyin:** xí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Derived from the depiction of a bird resting on a branch, symbolizing practice and learning through repetition.
  - **interpretation:** The image of a bird settling suggests the idea of returning and practicing skills, akin to how one learns through repetition. The connection is intuitive, as repetition often leads to mastery, much like a bird returning to its perch.
- **examples:**
  - 我正在学习中文(我正在學習中文)。
    - Wǒ zhèngzài xuéxí zhōngwén.
    - I am studying Chinese.
  - 习惯了(習慣了)。
    - Xíguànle.
    - I have gotten used to it.
