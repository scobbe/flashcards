## practice
### liànxí
---
- **definition:** practice
- **pinyin:** liànxí
- **components:**
  - 练(練)
    - liàn
    - train; practice
  - 习(習)
    - xí
    - study; learn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 练(練) (liàn, "train; practice") + 习(習) (xí, "study; learn") =
    - engaging in exercises to reinforce learning
  - **interpretation:** 练习(練習) (liànxí, "practice") combines the idea of training and studying to emphasize actively engaging in exercises to improve skills or knowledge.
  - **simplification:** 练(練) and 习(習) both simplified individually.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天练习书法(他每天練習書法)。
    - Tā měitiān liànxí shūfǎ.
    - He practices calligraphy every day.
  - 我们一起练习中文(我們一起練習中文)，提高发音(提高發音)。
    - Wǒmen yīqǐ liànxí zhōngwén, tígāo fāyīn.
    - We practice Chinese together to improve our pronunciation.
### 练习(練習) → 练(練)
- **definition:** train; practice
- **pinyin:** liàn
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - sī
    - silk
  - 柬
    - jiǎn
    - select
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 糸(糸) (sī, "silk") + 柬(柬) (jiǎn, "select") =
    - processing silk ->
    - to refine ->
    - 练(練) (liàn, "train")
  - **interpretation:** The character originally referred to the refinement of silk, which metaphorically extends to the idea of refining skills or training. 糸(糸) (sī, "silk") indicates the semantic field of working with silk, while 柬(柬) (jiǎn, "select") provides the phonetic element.
  - **simplification:** 练(練): 柬(柬) was simplified to 𫠣 (an abbreviated form) for visual simplicity while retaining the phonetic element.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天练字(他每天練字)。
    - Tā měitiān liàn zì.
    - He practices writing every day.
  - 我们练习太极(我們練習太極)，以求内心平静(以求內心平靜)。
    - Wǒmen liànxí tàijí, yǐ qiú nèixīn píngjìng.
    - We practice Tai Chi to seek inner peace.
  - 他在操场上练跑步(他在操場上練跑步)，为比赛做准备(爲比賽做準備)。
    - Tā zài cāochǎng shàng liàn pǎobù, wèi bǐsài zuò zhǔnbèi.
    - He trains by running on the playground to prepare for the competition.
### 练习(練習) → 练(練) → 糸(糸)
- **definition:** silk
- **pinyin:** sī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a skein of silk
  - **interpretation:** The character 糸(糸) (sī, "silk") visually represents twisted threads of silk, capturing the appearance and texture of a silk skein. This depiction makes intuitive sense for representing the concept of silk due to the familiar form of such threads in historical textile production.
- **examples:**
  - 工匠用糸织布(工匠用糸織布)，制作精美服饰(製作精美服飾)。
    - Gōngjiàng yòng sī zhī bù, zhìzuò jīngměi fúshì.
    - The artisan weaves cloth with silk and creates exquisite garments.
  - 博物馆展出古代糸织品(博物館展出古代糸織品)。
    - Bówùguǎn zhǎnchū gǔdài sī zhī pǐn.
    - The museum exhibits ancient silk-woven artifacts.
  - 在传统纺织技艺中(在傳統紡織技藝中)，糸扮演了重要的角色(糸扮演了重要的角色)。
    - Zài chuántǒng fǎngzhī jìyì zhōng, sī bànyǎn le zhòngyào de juésè.
    - In traditional textile craftsmanship, silk plays an important role.
### 练习(練習) → 练(練) → 柬(柬)
- **definition:** select
- **pinyin:** jiǎn
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** 木(木) (mù, 'wood') + phonetic: 柬(柬) (jiǎn, 'bag stuffed with herbs')
  - **interpretation:** The character 柬(柬) combines 木(木) (mù, 'wood') as the semantic component, indicating something made from or related to wood, with another 柬(柬) (jiǎn, 'bag stuffed with herbs') acting as the phonetic component. This suggests a phonetic borrowing where the original meaning was likely related to something wooden, reinforced by the sound similarity to a bag or form.
- **examples:**
  - 他急忙从箱子里取出柬(他急忙從箱子裡取出柬)，里面装有救命的草药(裡面裝有救命的草藥)。
    - Tā jímáng cóng xiāngzi lǐ qǔchū jiǎn, lǐmiàn zhuāng yǒu jiùmìng de cǎoyào.
    - He hurriedly took out the herb bag from the box, which was filled with life-saving herbs.
  - 老中医用精制草药装满柬(老中醫用精製草藥裝滿柬)，去山中采药以制良方(去山中採藥以制良方)。
    - Lǎo zhōngyī yòng jīngzhì cǎoyào zhuāng mǎn jiǎn, qù shān zhōng cǎi yào yǐ zhì liángfāng.
    - The seasoned doctor filled the herb bag with refined herbs, then went to the mountains to collect herbs for an effective remedy.

### 练习(練習) → 习(習)
- **definition:** study; learn
- **pinyin:** xí
- **components:**
  - 習
    - xí
    - study
  - 白
    - bái
    - white
  - 羽
    - yǔ
    - feather
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Simplified character
  - **description:**
    - 習 (xí, "study") ->
    - elimination of 白(白) (bái, "white") and 羽(羽) (yǔ, "feather") ->
    - 习 (xí, "study")
  - **interpretation:** The character 習 (xí, "study") depicts repeated flapping like bird wings. The simplification process removed components 白(白) (bái, "white") and 羽(羽) (yǔ, "feather") to form 习, retaining the core meaning of studying or practicing.
  - **simplification:** The 1956 simplification by the People's Republic of China removed the components 白(白) (bái, "white") and 羽(羽) (yǔ, "feather"), creating a streamlined character 习 that maintained the pronunciation and meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我习英文(我習英文)。
    - Wǒ xí Yīngwén.
    - I study English.
  - 她每天习书法(她每天習書法)，朋友总夸她勤奋(朋友總誇她勤奮)。
    - Tā měitiān xí shūfǎ, péngyǒu zǒng kuā tā qínfèn.
    - She practices calligraphy every day, and her friends always praise her diligence.
### 练习(練習) → 习(習) → 習(習)
- **definition:** study
- **pinyin:** xí
- **components:**
  - 羽
    - yǔ
    - wings
  - 白
    - bái
    - white
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 羽(羽) (yǔ, "wings") + 日(日) (rì, "sun") ->
    - practice flying ->
    - 日 changed to 白(白) (bái, "white") ->
    - final form 習
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts the idea of learning or practicing through the imagery of wings and the sun, symbolizing the concept of practice making one perfect, akin to learning to fly.
  - **simplification:** 習(習) (xí, "study") -> elimination of 白(白) (bái, "white") and 羽(羽) (yǔ, "wings") -> simplified to 习(習) (xí, "study")
- **examples:**
  - 我習書法(我習書法)。
    - Wǒ xí shūfǎ.
    - I study calligraphy.
  - 小明每晚習古詩(小明每晚習古詩)，陶冶情操(陶冶情操)。
    - Xiǎomíng měi wǎn xí gǔshī, táoyě qíngcāo.
    - Xiaoming studies ancient poems every night, cultivating his character.
