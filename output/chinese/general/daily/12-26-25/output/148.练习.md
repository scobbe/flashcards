## practice
### liànxí
---
- **traditional:** 練習
- **definition:** practice
- **pinyin:** liànxí
- **components:**
  - 练(練)
    - liàn
    - practice; train; exercise
  - 习(習)
    - xí
    - study; practice; learn; become familiar with
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 练(練) (liàn, "to practice") refers to the act of training or honing a skill through repetition. 习(習) (xí, "to study; practice") denotes learning or developing proficiency in an activity. Together, 练(練)习(習) (liànxí) means "practice" in the sense of engaging in activities to improve skill through repetition and learning.
  - **interpretation:** 练(練)习(習) (liànxí, "practice") emphasizes the combination of active training and study to achieve mastery or proficiency in various skills. The individual elements highlight both the repetitive act and the learning process critical to practice.
  - **simplification:** 练(練): 纟 (silk radical) + 柬 (cards); simplification involves removing strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )每( )天( )练(練)习(習)英( )语(語)。
    - Wǒ měitiān liànxí yīngyǔ.
    - I practice English every day.
  - 老( )师(師)建( )议(議)我( )们(們)先( )温(溫)习(習)课(課)文( )，再( )练(練)习(習)发(發)音( )。
    - Lǎoshī jiànyì wǒmen xiān wēnxí kèwén, zài liànxí fāyīn.
    - The teacher suggested that we first review the text, then practice our pronunciation.
  - 练(練)习(習)使( )人( )进(進)步( )。
    - Liànxí shǐ rén jìnbù.
    - Practice makes perfect.

- **definition:** morning
- **pinyin:** zǎo
- **components:**
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun
  - 甲
    - jiǎ
    - first
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - Early pictogram depicts an acorn ->
    - borrowed phonetically for 'morning' ->
    - Late bronze inscriptions: semantic 日 (rì, 'sun') + phonetic 棗(枣) (zǎo) ->
    - small seal script: 日(日, 'sun') + 甲(甲, 'first') =
    - when the sun first rises ->
    - 'early'
  - **interpretation:** The character evolved from depicting an acorn to incorporating the image of the sun and the concept of 'first,' symbolizing the sun's rise to indicate 'early.' It's a combination of both semantic elements and phonetic borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天起床很早。
    - Wǒ jīntiān qǐchuáng hěn zǎo.
    - I woke up very early today.
  - 他( )早( )到( )办(辦)公( )室( )，开(開)始( )了( )紧(緊)张(張)的( )会(會)议(議)。
    - Tā zǎo dào bàngōngshì, kāishǐ le jǐnzhāng de huìyì.
    - He arrived at the office early and started a busy meeting.
  - 他( )喜( )欢(歡)早( )睡( )，早( )起( )的( )习(習)惯(慣)让(讓)他( )精( )力( )充( )沛( )。
    - Tā xǐhuān zǎo shuì, zǎo qǐ de xíguàn ràng tā jīnglì chōngpèi.
    - He likes going to bed early, and his habit of getting up early makes him energetic.
