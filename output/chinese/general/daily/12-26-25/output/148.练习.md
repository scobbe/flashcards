## practice
### liànxí
---
- **traditional:** 練習
- **definition:** practice
- **pinyin:** liànxí
- **components:**
  - 练(練)
    - liàn
    - practice; train; exercise
  - 习(習)
    - xí
    - study; practice; learn; become familiar with
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 练(練) (liàn, "to practice") refers to the act of training or honing a skill through repetition. 习(習) (xí, "to study; practice") denotes learning or developing proficiency in an activity. Together, 练习(練習) (liànxí) means "practice" in the sense of engaging in activities to improve skill through repetition and learning.
  - **interpretation:** 练习(練習) (liànxí, "practice") emphasizes the combination of active training and study to achieve mastery or proficiency in various skills. The individual elements highlight both the repetitive act and the learning process critical to practice.
  - **simplification:** 练(練): 纟 (silk radical) + 柬 (cards); simplification involves removing strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天练习(練習)英语(語)。
    - Wǒ měitiān liànxí yīngyǔ.
    - I practice English every day.
  - 老师(師)建议(議)我们(們)先温习课(溫習課)文，再练习发(練習發)音。
    - Lǎoshī jiànyì wǒmen xiān wēnxí kèwén, zài liànxí fāyīn.
    - The teacher suggested that we first review the text, then practice our pronunciation.
  - 练习(練習)使人进(進)步。
    - Liànxí shǐ rén jìnbù.
    - Practice makes perfect.
### 练(練)习(習) → 练(練)
- **traditional:** 練
- **definition:** practice; train; exercise
- **pinyin:** liàn
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk
  - 柬
    - jiǎn
    - card
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 糸 (mì, "silk") + 柬 (jiǎn, "card") =
    - silk related to sorting or selecting ->
    - practice
  - **interpretation:** 練 originally referred to refining or processing silk, implying the act of making material smooth and usable. Over time, this idea of refinement was metaphorically extended to training and practice, where skills are refined and polished.
  - **simplification:** 練 → 练(練): 糸 (mì, "silk") simplified to 纟 for ease of writing.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天练(練)书(書)法。
    - Wǒ měitiān liàn shūfǎ.
    - I practice calligraphy every day.
  - 他在武馆练(練)武术(術)，并(並)指导(導)年轻(輕)人。
    - Tā zài wǔguǎn liàn wǔshù, bìng zhǐdǎo niánqīng rén.
    - He trains in martial arts at the gym, and he guides young people.
