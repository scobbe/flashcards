## 
### attempt; try; intend
---
- **pinyin:** attempt; try; intend
- **components:**
  - 企
    - qǐ
    - to plan; to intend; to stand on tiptoe; to expect
  - 图(圖)
    - tú
    - to draw; to scheme; map; plan
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 企(企) (qǐ, "to plan") + 图(圖) (tú, "to draw or scheme") — planning scheme
  - **interpretation:** The combination emphasizes the act of planning or scheming towards an intention or goal. A 企图(企圖) (qǐtú) reflects a deliberate effort to achieve something.
- **examples:**
  - 他有一个不好的企图(他有一個不好的企圖)。
    - Tā yǒu yīgè bù hǎo de qǐtú。
    - He has a bad intention.
  - 他们的企图很明显(他們的企圖很明顯)。
    - Tāmen de qǐtú hěn míngxiǎn。
    - Their intention is very obvious.
### 企图(企圖) → 企(企)
- **definition:** to plan; to intend; to stand on tiptoe; to expect
- **pinyin:** qǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Originally depicts a person standing upright and anticipating something, reflecting the idea of planning or intending.
  - **interpretation:** The character shows a person poised in a standing position, suggesting readiness or expectation, aligning well with the meanings of intending or planning. This visual representation intuitively connects the act of standing tiptoe with the anticipation of something forthcoming.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一些计划(我有一些計劃)，想和你讨论(想和你討論)。
    - Wǒ yǒu yīxiē jìhuà, xiǎng hé nǐ tǎolùn.
    - I have some plans and would like to discuss them with you.
  - 他在想未来的事情(他在想未來的事情)，总是期待着好的结果(總是期待著好的結果)。
    - Tā zài xiǎng wèilái de shìqíng, zǒng shì qídàizhe hǎo de jiéguǒ.
    - He thinks about future matters, always expecting good results.

- **definition:** to draw; to scheme; map; plan
- **pinyin:** tú
- **components:**
  - 囗
    - wéi
    - walled city
  - 啚
    - dà
    - remote areas
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Depicts a walled city (囗) combined with an early form of remote areas (啚), representing planning or mapping out territory.
  - **interpretation:** This character intuitively combines the idea of a walled city, symbolizing boundaries, with the notion of distant regions. Together, they evoke the concept of drawing or scheming regarding territories.
