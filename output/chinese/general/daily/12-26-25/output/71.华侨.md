## overseas Chinese
### huáqiáo
---
- **traditional:** 華僑
- **definition:** overseas Chinese
- **pinyin:** huáqiáo
- **components:**
  - 华(華)
    - huá
    - China; magnificent
  - 侨(僑)
    - qiáo
    - to live abroad
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 华(華) (huá, "China; magnificent") + 侨(僑) (qiáo, "to live abroad")
  - **interpretation:** 华侨(華僑) (huáqiáo) means 'overseas Chinese.' The word combines 华(華) (huá), representing 'China' and symbolizing magnificence, and 侨(僑) (qiáo), which means 'to live abroad.' This combination reflects Chinese people who establish themselves outside of China while maintaining cultural ties to their homeland.
- **examples:**
  - 许(許)多华侨(華僑)在国(國)外投资(資)。
    - xǔduō huáqiáo zài guówài tóuzī.
    - Many overseas Chinese invest abroad.
  - 他出生于(於)中国(國)，后来(後來)成为(為)成功的华侨(華僑)商人。
    - tā chūshēng yú zhōngguó, hòulái chéngwéi chénggōng de huáqiáo shāngrén.
    - He was born in China and later became a successful overseas Chinese businessman.
  - 不少华侨热(華僑熱)衷于(於)保护(護)中国(國)文化。
    - bùshǎo huáqiáo rèzhōng yú bǎohù zhōngguó wénhuà.
    - Many overseas Chinese are keen on protecting Chinese culture.
### 华(華)侨(僑) → 华(華)
- **traditional:** 華
- **definition:** China; magnificent
- **pinyin:** huá
- **components:**
  - 化
    - huà
    - transform
  - 十
    - shí
    - stalk and leaves
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 化 (huà, "transform") + Semantic: 十 ("stalk and leaves") ->
    - 華 ("magnificent") ->
    - Simplified to 华 with 化
  - **interpretation:** The traditional character 華 uses 十 to evoke a stalk with leaves, representing growth and magnificence. In the simplified form 华, 化 helps retain the original sound while removing visual complexity.
  - **simplification:** The character was simplified by using 化 to streamline its phonetic component and reduce stroke count, while 十 was omitted.
- **examples:**
  - 中国(國)古代文明辉(輝)煌，成就了华(華)夏的美名。
    - Zhōngguó gǔdài wénmíng huīhuáng, chéngjiùle huáxià de měimíng.
    - The brilliance of ancient Chinese civilization has earned China a magnificent reputation.
  - 他穿着(著)一件华(華)服，显(顯)得格外自信。
    - Tā chuānzhuó yī jiàn huáfú, xiǎnde géwài zìxìn.
    - He wears a magnificent garment, looking exceptionally confident.
### 华(華)侨(僑) → 华(華) → 化
- **definition:** transform
- **pinyin:** huà
- **components:**
  - 亻
    - rén
    - person
  - 𠤎
    - rén
    - person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 亻 (rén, "person") + 𠤎 (rén, "person") upside down =
    - two people, one upright, one reversed ->
    - transformation or change
  - **interpretation:** The character 化 (huà, "transform") visually represents change or transformation, signified by two 人 (rén, "person") characters in opposite orientations, suggesting reversal or alteration.
- **examples:**
  - 科学进(學進)步，推动(動)社会(會)化转(轉)型。
    - Kēxué jìnbù, tuīdòng shèhuì huà zhuǎnxíng.
    - Scientific progress transforms social structures.
  - 时间(時間)能让伤(讓傷)痛化作记忆(記憶)。
    - Shíjiān néng ràng shāngtòng huà zuò jìyì.
    - Time transforms pain into memories.
  - 困境中他不断(斷)努力，生命终将(終將)化为辉(為輝)煌。
    - Kùnjìng zhōng tā bùduàn nǔlì, shēngmìng zhōng jiāng huà wéi huīhuáng.
    - Amid difficulties he perseveres, and his life will eventually transform into brilliance.
### 华(華)侨(僑) → 华(華) → 化 → 亻
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 亻 (rén, "person") is a stylization of 人 (rén, "person") ->
    - simplified for use as a component in other characters
  - **interpretation:** 亻 (rén, "person") is used as a component form of 人 (rén, "person") in compound characters, representing the human or person-related aspect.
- **examples:**
  - 亻是构(構)成许(許)多汉(漢)字的关键(關鍵)部首。
    - Rén shì gòuchéng xǔduō hànzì de guānjiàn bùshǒu.
    - The radical 亻 is a key component in many Chinese characters.
  - 学习汉(學習漢)字时(時)，了解亻的意义(義)很重要。
    - Xuéxí hànzì shí, liǎojiě rén de yìyì hěn zhòngyào.
    - When learning Chinese characters, understanding the meaning of 亻 is important.
  - 古人造字时(時)常取亻以表达(達)人物情感，因此亻贯(貫)穿于(於)文明史中。
    - Gǔrén zào zì shí cháng qǔ rén yǐ biǎodá rénwù qínggǎn, yīncǐ rén guànchuān yú wénmíng shǐ zhōng.
    - Ancient people often used the radical 亻 to express human emotions in character formation, and thus 亻 runs through the history of civilization.

### 华(華)侨(僑) → 华(華) → 十
- **definition:** stalk and leaves
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园里(園裡)有十棵树(樹)，景色十分优(優)美。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.
### 华(華)侨(僑) → 侨(僑)
- **traditional:** 僑
- **definition:** to live abroad
- **pinyin:** qiáo
- **components:**
  - 亻
    - rén
    - person
  - 喬(乔)
    - qiáo
    - tall, high
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 亻 (rén, "person") + 喬(乔) (qiáo, "tall") =
    - person living abroad, tall metaphorically representing distance ->
    - to live abroad
  - **interpretation:** The character 侨(僑) (qiáo) combines 亻 (rén, "person") indicating that it relates to people, and 喬(乔) (qiáo, "tall") as the phonetic component, suggesting the idea of living afar or abroad. The character evokes the image of a person living at a distance.
  - **simplification:** The simplification involves replacing the phonetic component 喬(乔) (qiáo, "tall") with its simplified form 乔 (qiáo), simplifying the character structure while retaining its phonetic and semantic essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他选择侨(選擇僑)居欧(歐)美。
    - Tā xuǎnzé qiáo jū Ōuměi.
    - He chose to live abroad in Europe and America.
  - 许(許)多华侨(華僑)在海外创业(創業)，为(為)家乡带来(鄉帶來)投资(資)。
    - Xǔduō Huáqiáo zài hǎiwài chuàngyè, wèi jiāxiāng dài lái tóuzī.
    - Many overseas Chinese start businesses abroad, bringing investments to their hometown.
  - 他的家族曾侨(僑)居东(東)南亚(亞)。
    - Tā de jiāzú céng qiáo jū Dōngnán Yà.
    - His family once lived abroad in Southeast Asia.

### 华(華)侨(僑) → 侨(僑) → 喬
- **definition:** tall, high
- **pinyin:** qiáo
- **components:**
  - 高
    - gāo
    - high
  - 夭
    - yāo
    - to bend
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:** Originally 高 (gāo, "high") → Differentiated with various components like 屮, 止, 力, 九, or 夭 → Finalized with 夭 for the form of 喬
  - **interpretation:** The character 喬 (qiáo, "tall, high") differentiates from 高 (gāo, "high") by using additional components like 夭 to indicate a similar meaning. Despite variant interpretations, the use of 夭 stabilizes the character's form, primarily reflecting the idea of height or elevation.
- **examples:**
  - 这座大楼很喬(乔)。
    - Zhè zuò dàlóu hěn qiáo.
    - This building is tall.
  - 那棵树(樹)看起来(來)特别喬(別乔)，映衬着蓝(襯著藍)天。
    - Nà kē shù kàn qǐlái tèbié qiáo, yìngchèn zhe lántiān.
    - That tree looks especially tall, complementing the blue sky.
### 华(華)侨(僑) → 侨(僑) → 喬 → 高
- **definition:** high
- **pinyin:** gāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tall building
  - **interpretation:** The character 高(gāo, "high; tall") visually represents a tall structure or building, symbolizing height or elevation. Unlike 京(jīng, "capital"), it is straightforwardly a representation of height.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)座山高。
    - Zhè zuò shān gāo.
    - This mountain is high.
  - 那棵树长(樹長)得高，比其他树(樹)高很多。
    - Nà kē shù zhǎng de gāo, bǐ qítā shù gāo hěn duō.
    - That tree grows tall, much taller than the other trees.
  - 飞机(飛機)在高空飞(飛)行。
    - Fēijī zài gāokōng fēixíng.
    - The airplane flies in high altitude.
### 华(華)侨(僑) → 侨(僑) → 喬 → 夭
- **definition:** to bend
- **pinyin:** yāo
- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 夭(yāo) ("young") visually represents someone leaning forward in a motion that suggests energy and vitality associated with youthfulness. This imagery aligns with the concept of being lively or young.
- **examples:**
  - 他还夭，意气风发(氣風發)。
    - Tā hái yāo, yìqì fēngfā。
    - He is still young, full of youthful vigor.
  - 小女孩还夭，充满无(滿無)限活力。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái hái yāo, chōngmǎn wúxiàn huólì。
