## overseas Chinese
### huáqiáo
---
- **definition:** overseas Chinese
- **pinyin:** huáqiáo
- **components:**
  - 华(華)
    - huá
    - China; magnificent
  - 侨(僑)
    - qiáo
    - to live abroad
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 华(華) (huá, "China; magnificent") + 侨(僑) (qiáo, "to live abroad")
  - **interpretation:** 华侨(華僑) (huáqiáo) means 'overseas Chinese.' The word combines 华(華) (huá), representing 'China' and symbolizing magnificence, and 侨(僑) (qiáo), which means 'to live abroad.' This combination reflects Chinese people who establish themselves outside of China while maintaining cultural ties to their homeland.
- **examples:**
  - 许多华侨在国外投资(許多華僑在國外投資)。
    - xǔduō huáqiáo zài guówài tóuzī.
    - Many overseas Chinese invest abroad.
  - 他出生于中国(他出生於中國)，后来成为成功的华侨商人(後來成為成功的華僑商人)。
    - tā chūshēng yú zhōngguó, hòulái chéngwéi chénggōng de huáqiáo shāngrén.
    - He was born in China and later became a successful overseas Chinese businessman.
  - 不少华侨热衷于保护中国文化(不少華僑熱衷於保護中國文化)。
    - bùshǎo huáqiáo rèzhōng yú bǎohù zhōngguó wénhuà.
    - Many overseas Chinese are keen on protecting Chinese culture.
### 华侨(華僑) → 华(華)
- **definition:** China; magnificent
- **pinyin:** huá
- **components:**
  - 化
    - huà
    - transform
  - 十
    - shí
    - stalk and leaves
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 化(化) (huà, "transform") + Semantic: 十(十) ("stalk and leaves") ->
    - 華(華) ("magnificent") ->
    - Simplified to 华 with 化(化)
  - **interpretation:** The traditional character 華(華) uses 十(十) to evoke a stalk with leaves, representing growth and magnificence. In the simplified form 华, 化(化) helps retain the original sound while removing visual complexity.
  - **simplification:** The character was simplified by using 化(化) to streamline its phonetic component and reduce stroke count, while 十(十) was omitted.
- **examples:**
  - 中国古代文明辉煌(中國古代文明輝煌)，成就了华夏的美名(成就了華夏的美名)。
    - Zhōngguó gǔdài wénmíng huīhuáng, chéngjiùle huáxià de měimíng.
    - The brilliance of ancient Chinese civilization has earned China a magnificent reputation.
  - 他穿着一件华服(他穿著一件華服)，显得格外自信(顯得格外自信)。
    - Tā chuānzhuó yī jiàn huáfú, xiǎnde géwài zìxìn.
    - He wears a magnificent garment, looking exceptionally confident.

### 华侨(華僑) → 华(華) → 十(十)
- **definition:** stalk and leaves
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(我有十本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园里有十棵树(花園裡有十棵樹)，景色十分优美(景色十分優美)。

    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(十年磨一劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.
### 华侨(華僑) → 侨(僑)

- **pinyin:** qiáo
- **components:**
  - 亻
    - rén
    - person
  - 喬(乔)
    - qiáo
    - tall, high
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 亻(亻) (rén, "person") + 喬(乔) (qiáo, "tall") =
    - person living abroad, tall metaphorically representing distance ->
    - to live abroad
  - **interpretation:** The character 侨(僑) (qiáo) combines 亻(亻) (rén, "person") indicating that it relates to people, and 喬(乔) (qiáo, "tall") as the phonetic component, suggesting the idea of living afar or abroad. The character evokes the image of a person living at a distance.
  - **simplification:** The simplification involves replacing the phonetic component 喬(乔) (qiáo, "tall") with its simplified form 乔(乔) (qiáo), simplifying the character structure while retaining its phonetic and semantic essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他选择侨居欧美(他選擇僑居歐美)。
    - Tā xuǎnzé qiáo jū Ōuměi.
    - He chose to live abroad in Europe and America.
  - 许多华侨在海外创业(許多華僑在海外創業)，为家乡带来投资(為家鄉帶來投資)。
    - Xǔduō Huáqiáo zài hǎiwài chuàngyè, wèi jiāxiāng dài lái tóuzī.
    - Many overseas Chinese start businesses abroad, bringing investments to their hometown.
  - 他的家族曾侨居东南亚(他的家族曾僑居東南亞)。
    - Tā de jiāzú céng qiáo jū Dōngnán Yà.
    - His family once lived abroad in Southeast Asia.

- **etymology:**

  - **description:** Originally 高(高) (gāo, "high") → Differentiated with various components like 屮(屮), 止(止), 力(力), 九(九), or 夭(夭) → Finalized with 夭(夭) for the form of 喬(喬)

### 华侨(華僑) → 侨(僑) → 喬(喬) → 夭(夭)
- **definition:** to bend
- **pinyin:** yāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - A figure leaning forward (presumably running) ->
    - energetic, young ->
    - later associated with dying young
  - **interpretation:** The character 夭(yāo, "to die young") originally depicted youthful energy and vitality but over time became associated with the concept of dying young, reflecting the frailty of youthful life. It emphasizes impermanence, contrasting with the vigor of motion that the leaning figure initially suggested.
- **examples:**
