## clumsy; awkward
### bènshǒu bènjiǎo
---
- **definition:** clumsy; awkward
- **pinyin:** bènshǒu bènjiǎo
- **components:**
  - 笨手
    - bènshǒu
    - clumsy-handed; awkward with hands; ham-fisted; butterfingered
  - 笨脚(笨腳)
    - bènjiǎo
    - clumsy-footed; awkward on one's feet; ungainly; uncoordinated
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨手 + 笨脚 = clumsy in actions and movement.
  - **interpretation:** Highlights awkwardness with both hands and feet; reduplication intensifies the sense of clumsiness and adds a colloquial tone.
  - **simplification:** Only 腳 was simplified to 脚 by adopting the simplified phonetic 却 (from 卻); 笨 and 手 remain unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 他做家务时笨手笨脚(他做家務時笨手笨腳)。
    - Tā zuò jiāwù shí bènshǒu bènjiǎo.
    - He’s clumsy when doing housework.
  - 别着急(別著急)，越紧张越会笨手笨脚(越緊張越會笨手笨腳)。
    - Bié zháojí, yuè jǐnzhāng yuè huì bènshǒu bènjiǎo.
    - Don’t panic; the more nervous you get, the clumsier you become.
  - 第一次上台(第一次上臺)，他走路和拿话筒都显得笨手笨脚(他走路和拿話筒都顯得笨手笨腳)。
    - Dì yī cì shàngtái, tā zǒulù hé ná huàtǒng dōu xiǎnde bènshǒu bènjiǎo.
    - On his first time on stage, he walked and held the mic awkwardly.
### 笨手笨脚(笨手笨腳) → 笨手
- **definition:** clumsy-handed; awkward with hands; ham-fisted; butterfingered
- **pinyin:** bènshǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨 (clumsy; dull) + 手 (hand) = clumsy-handed; awkward with hands.
  - **interpretation:** Labels someone as lacking manual dexterity, especially in tasks like crafts, repairs, or cooking. Often contrasts with 巧手/灵巧 when talking about handiwork.
- **examples:**
  - 我做手工很笨手(我做手工很笨手)。
    - Wǒ zuò shǒugōng hěn bènshǒu.
    - I'm clumsy at handicrafts.
  - 他修水龙头很笨手(他修水龍頭很笨手)。
    - Tā xiū shuǐlóngtóu hěn bènshǒu.
    - He's ham-fisted at fixing faucets.
  - 别笑我(別笑我)，我对电器维修很笨手(我對電器維修很笨手)。
    - Bié xiào wǒ, wǒ duì diànqì wéixiū hěn bènshǒu.
    - Don't laugh at me; I'm awkward with appliance repairs.
### 笨手笨脚(笨手笨腳) → 笨脚(笨腳)
- **definition:** clumsy-footed; awkward on one's feet; ungainly; uncoordinated
- **pinyin:** bènjiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨 (clumsy) + 脚/腳 (foot) = clumsy-footed; awkward in movement
  - **interpretation:** Combines an adjective of awkwardness with 'foot' to label someone unsteady or poorly coordinated on their feet. Often used for walking, running, or dancing that looks ungainly.
  - **simplification:** 腳 → 脚 via character simplification: the phonetic element 卻 was simplified to 却; 笨 unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 他走路有点笨脚(他走路有點笨腳)。
    - Tā zǒulù yǒudiǎn bènjiǎo.
    - He’s a bit clumsy on his feet when he walks.
  - 她跳舞时显得笨脚(她跳舞時顯得笨腳)。
    - Tā tiàowǔ shí xiǎnde bènjiǎo.
    - She looks awkward when dancing.
  - 下雨天路滑(下雨天路滑)，他更显得笨脚(他更顯得笨腳)。
    - Xiàyǔtiān lù huá, tā gèng xiǎnde bènjiǎo.
    - On rainy days the road is slippery, and he seems even more unsteady on his feet.
