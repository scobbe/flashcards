## clumsy; awkward
### bènshǒu bènjiǎo
---
- **definition:** clumsy; awkward
- **pinyin:** bènshǒu bènjiǎo
- **components:**
  - 笨手
    - bènshǒu
    - clumsy hands
  - 笨脚(笨腳)
    - bènjiǎo
    - clumsy feet
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨手笨脚(笨手笨腳) (bènshǒu bènjiǎo, "clumsy; awkward") combines two sets of characters: 笨(bèn, "stupid; clumsy") and 手(shǒu, "hand") with 脚(腳) (jiǎo, "foot") to describe someone whose hands and feet are both clumsy and awkward.
  - **interpretation:** The combination emphasizes clumsiness involving both hands and feet, suggesting an overall physical awkwardness.
- **examples:**
  - 他做事情总是笨手笨脚(他做事情總是笨手笨腳)。
    - Tā zuò shìqíng zǒng shì bènshǒu bènjiǎo.
    - He is always clumsy when doing things.
  - 在体育课上(在體育課上)，他因笨手笨脚错过了接球(他因笨手笨腳錯過了接球)。
    - Zài tǐyùkè shàng, tā yīn bènshǒu bènjiǎo cuòguò le jiē qiú.
    - In PE class, he missed catching the ball because of his clumsiness.
