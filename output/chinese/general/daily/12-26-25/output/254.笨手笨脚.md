## clumsy; awkward
### bènshǒu bènjiǎo
---
- **definition:** clumsy; awkward
- **pinyin:** bènshǒu bènjiǎo
- **components:**
  - 笨
    - bèn
    - stupid; clumsy
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
  - 笨
    - bèn
    - stupid; clumsy
  - 脚(腳)
    - jiǎo
    - foot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨(笨) (bèn, "stupid; clumsy") refers to a lack of dexterity or intelligence. 手(手) (shǒu, "hand") and 腳(脚) (jiǎo, "foot") represent the physical extremities that are associated with actions and movements. The repetition of 笨(笨) (bèn, "stupid; clumsy") with 手(手) (shǒu, "hand") and 腳(脚) (jiǎo, "foot") emphasizes an overall clumsiness or awkwardness in physical movements.
  - **interpretation:** By stating that both the hands and feet are clumsy, 笨手笨腳(笨手笨腳) (bènshǒu bènjiǎo) conveys a strong sense of overall awkwardness in physical coordination.
- **examples:**
  - 他做事总是笨手笨脚(他做事總是笨手笨腳)。
    - Tā zuòshì zǒng shì bènshǒu bènjiǎo.
    - He is always clumsy in his work.
  - 因为他笨手笨脚(因為他笨手笨腳)，常常打翻杯子(常常打翻杯子)。
    - Yīnwèi tā bènshǒu bènjiǎo, chángcháng dǎ fān bēizi.
    - Because he is clumsy, he often spills his cup.
