### clumsy; awkward
---
- **pinyin:** clumsy; awkward
- **components:**
  - 笨手
    - bèn shǒu
    - clumsy hands
  - 笨脚(笨腳)
    - bèn jiǎo
    - clumsy feet
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨(笨) (bèn, "clumsy") + 手(手) (shǒu, "hand") + 笨(笨) (bèn, "clumsy") + 脚(腳) (jiǎo, "foot") — clumsy hands, clumsy feet
  - **interpretation:** The expression describes a person who is clumsy in both their hands and feet. This dual emphasis on both body parts highlights their overall awkwardness.
- **examples:**
  - 他真是笨手笨脚(他真是笨手笨腳)，做什么事情都不灵活(做什麼事情都不靈活)。
    - tā zhēn shì bèn shǒu bèn jiǎo, zuò shénme shìqíng dōu bù línghuó.
    - He is truly clumsy; he is uncoordinated in everything he does.
  - 我总是笨手笨脚(我總是笨手笨腳)，难以保持平衡(難以保持平衡)。
    - wǒ zǒng shì bèn shǒu bèn jiǎo, nán yǐ bǎochí pínghéng.
    - I am always clumsy; I have difficulty keeping my balance.
### 笨手笨脚(笨手笨腳) → 笨手(笨手)
- **definition:** clumsy hands
- **pinyin:** bèn shǒu
- **components:**
  - 笨
    - bèn
    - clumsy; stupid; foolish; awkward
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand; worker; creator; skill
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨(惡) (bèn, "clumsy; stupid") + 手(手) (shǒu, "hand") — clumsy hand
  - **interpretation:** This term describes a lack of skill in using one's hands, typically associated with clumsiness. The combination makes sense as it directly relates to the physical awkwardness about dexterity.
- **examples:**
  - 他真是个笨手，常常打翻东西。
    - tā zhēn shì gè bèn shǒu, chángcháng dǎfān dōngxi.
    - He really is a clumsy person, often knocking things over.
  - 虽然她有笨手，但她的心意很好。
    - suīrán tā yǒu bèn shǒu, dàn tā de xīnyì hěn hǎo.
    - Though she has clumsy hands, her intentions are very good.
