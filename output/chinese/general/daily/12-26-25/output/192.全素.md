## vegan
### quánsù
---
- **definition:** vegan
- **pinyin:** quánsù
- **components:**
  - 全
    - quán
    - whole; complete; entire; all
  - 素
    - sù
    - plain; vegetarian; simple; element
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 全 (whole/entire) + 素 (vegetarian/plain) = completely vegetarian (vegan).
  - **interpretation:** Emphasizes a diet that is entirely plant-based, excluding meat, eggs, dairy, and other animal-derived products. Often used on menus and labels to mark vegan options.
- **examples:**
  - 我吃全素(我吃全素)，不吃任何动物性产品(不吃任何動物性產品)。
    - Wǒ chī quánsù, bù chī rènhé dòngwùxìng chǎnpǐn.
    - I am vegan and do not eat any animal products.
  - 这家餐厅提供全素菜单(這家餐廳提供全素菜單)，也有无麸质选项(也有無麩質選項)。
    - Zhè jiā cāntīng tígōng quánsù càidān, yě yǒu wú fūzhì xuǎnxiàng.
    - This restaurant offers a vegan menu and also has gluten-free options.
  - 他改吃全素是为了环保和动物福利(他改吃全素是為了環保和動物福利)。
    - Tā gǎi chī quánsù shì wèile huánbǎo hé dòngwù fúlì.
    - He switched to a vegan diet for environmental reasons and animal welfare.
### 全素(全素) → 全(全)
- **definition:** whole; complete; entire; all
- **pinyin:** quán
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound (會意 / 会意)
  - **description:** 入 + 玉 (“jade”) — a whole piece of jade; pure jade.
  - **interpretation:** Combining 入 over 玉 evokes an intact, unbroken piece of jade, emphasizing purity and wholeness. By extension, it came to express the ideas of completeness and entirety in general usage.
- **examples:**
  - 我们需要全力以赴(我們需要全力以赴)。
    - Wǒmen xūyào quánlì yǐfù.
    - We need to go all out.
  - 全部门票已经售完(全部門票已經售完)。
    - Quánbù ménpiào yǐjīng shòuwán.
    - All tickets have already been sold out.
  - 我们要全面了解情况(我們要全面瞭解情況)。
    - Wǒmen yào quánmiàn liǎojiě qíngkuàng.
    - We need to fully understand the situation.
### 全素(全素) → 全(全) → 入(入)
- **definition:** enter
- **pinyin:** rù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram (指事) — an arrowhead indicating “to enter”; compare 人.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicts a pointed wedge or arrowhead moving inward, conveying the action of going in. Its form resembles 人 but with a tighter, inward-tapering shape that emphasizes entry rather than a person.
- **examples:**
  - 禁止入内(禁止入內)。
    - jìnzhǐ rùnèi.
    - No entry.
  - 他考入北京大学(他考入北京大學)。
    - tā kǎorù běijīng dàxué.
    - He was admitted to Peking University.
  - 请入座(請入座)。
    - qǐng rùzuò.
    - Please be seated.
### 全素(全素) → 全(全) → 玉(玉)
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: image of a cong (琮), a ritual jade totem; also interpreted as three pieces of jade strung on a cord. Historically similar to 王; a dot was added to distinguish them.
  - **interpretation:** Evokes pieces of jade threaded on a cord or a tall ritual cong, conveying the idea of precious jade. The added dot differentiates it from 王 while suggesting a distinct bead-like element.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱玉(我愛玉)。
    - Wǒ ài yù.
    - I love jade.
  - 这块玉很温润(這塊玉很溫潤)。
    - Zhè kuài yù hěn wēnrùn.
    - This piece of jade is very smooth and lustrous.
  - 他送我一个玉镯(他送我一個玉鐲)。
    - Tā sòng wǒ yí gè yù zhuó.
    - He gave me a jade bracelet.
### 全素(全素) → 素(素)
- **definition:** plain; vegetarian; simple; element
- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands; to hold up
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Hands 廾 braiding thread 糸; top resembles 生 without the diagonal stroke (cf. 責, 青).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two hands working with threads, evoking the idea of woven or plain silk. Over time it extended from unadorned silk to senses like plain, simple style and vegetarian, and appears in compounds like 元素 for scientific ‘element’.
- **examples:**
  - 我吃素(我吃素)。
    - Wǒ chī sù.
    - I eat vegetarian / I’m vegetarian.
  - 她喜欢素色的衣服(她喜歡素色的衣服)。
    - Tā xǐhuan sùsè de yīfu.
    - She likes plain-colored clothes.
  - 化学里(化學裡)，氢是最轻的元素(氫是最輕的元素)。
    - Huàxué lǐ, qīng shì zuì qīng de yuánsù.
    - In chemistry, hydrogen is the lightest element.
### 全素(全素) → 素(素) → 廾(廾)
- **definition:** two hands; to hold up
- **pinyin:** gǒng
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - right hand; again
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 𠂇 (“left hand”) + 又 (“right hand”) — both hands; also written 𠬞.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the left and right hands to depict a pair of hands acting together, hence the sense of lifting or offering with both hands. Its form later served as a common component in characters related to holding or presenting.
- **examples:**
  - 在古文字中(在古文字中)，廾表示两只手一起举起东西(廾表示兩隻手一起舉起東西)。
    - Zài gǔ wénzì zhōng, gǒng biǎoshì liǎng zhī shǒu yīqǐ jǔqǐ dōngxī.
    - In ancient script, 廾 signifies two hands lifting something together.
  - 作为部件(作為部件)，它常见于表示双手奉献或合举的字(它常見於表示雙手奉獻或閤舉的字)，如奉、奏(如奉、奏)。
    - Zuòwéi bùjiàn, tā chángjiàn yú biǎoshì shuāngshǒu fèngxiàn huò héjǔ de zì, rú Fèng, Zòu.
    - As a component, it appears in characters that depict offering or jointly lifting with two hands,such as 奉 and 奏.
### 全素(全素) → 素(素) → 廾(廾) → 又(又)
- **definition:** right hand; again
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 全素(全素) → 素(素) → 糸(糸)
- **definition:** silk; thread
- **pinyin:** mì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** a skein of silk.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bundle of silk threads tied in the middle, with strands hanging above and below. The stylized sketch highlights twisted filaments, which later served as the standard radical for thread- and textile-related characters.
  - **simplification:** In Simplified Chinese the silk radical was streamlined to 纟 to reduce strokes and speed writing; 糸 is rarely used independently, but 纟 replaces it as a left-side component.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan sīchóu.
    - I like silk fabrics.
  - 这件衣服是用蚕丝做的(這件衣服是用蠶絲做的)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfu shì yòng cánsī zuò de.
    - This piece of clothing is made from silkworm silk.
  - 丝线很细(絲線很細)。
    - Sīxiàn hěn xì.
    - Silk thread is very thin.
