## vegan
### quánsù
---
- **definition:** vegan
- **pinyin:** quánsù
- **components:**
  - 全
    - quán
    - whole; entire
  - 素
    - sù
    - plain; vegetarian; simple
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 全 (quán, "whole") refers to something complete or entire, and 素 (sù, "plain; vegetarian") originally means simple, plain, or referring to vegetarian food. In this context, 全素 describes a diet that is entirely vegetarian, better known as vegan.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 全 (quán, "whole") and 素 (sù, "plain; vegetarian") indicates a diet that is entirely plant-based, without any animal products. This reflects the concept of veganism, emphasizing a complete adherence to vegetarian principles.
- **examples:**
  - 我选择(選擇)全素。
    - Wǒ xuǎnzé quánsù.
    - I choose a vegan lifestyle.
  - 他坚(堅)持全素，注重环(環)保。
    - Tā jiānchí quánsù, zhùzhòng huánbǎo.
    - He adheres to a vegan lifestyle, emphasizing environmental protection.
  - 全素食物营养丰(營養豐)富。
    - Quánsù shíwù yíngyǎng fēngfù.
    - Vegan food is nutritionally rich.
### 全素 → 全
- **definition:** whole; entire
- **pinyin:** quán
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 入 (rù, "enter") + 玉 (yù, "jade") =
    - a whole piece of jade; pure jade ->
    - complete
  - **interpretation:** 全 (quán, "complete") combines 入 (rù, "enter") and 玉 (yù, "jade") to symbolize a complete or unblemished piece of jade, reflecting purity and wholeness.
- **examples:**
  - 他的家全都整洁(潔)有序。
    - Tā de jiā quán dōu zhěngjié yǒuxù.
    - His whole house is neat and orderly.
  - 他全班都参(參)加了比赛(賽)。
    - Tā quán bān dōu cānjiāle bǐsài.
    - His entire class participated in the contest.
### 全素 → 全 → 入
- **definition:** enter
- **pinyin:** rù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** An arrowhead indicating 'to enter'
  - **interpretation:** The character 入(rù, 'enter') visually represents the concept of entering, with the form resembling an arrowhead suggesting movement through an opening.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)入座。
    - Qǐng rùzuò.
    - Please take a seat.
  - 学(學)生走入校园(園)，开(開)始新学(學)期。
    - Xuéshēng zǒu rù xiàoyuán, kāishǐ xīn xuéqī.
    - The students enter the campus and start a new semester.
  - 游(遊)客走入古城，探索历(歷)史。
    - Yóukè zǒu rù gǔchéng, tànsuǒ lìshǐ.
    - The tourists enter the ancient city and explore its history.
### 全素 → 全 → 玉
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cong (琮, a ritual jade totem)
  - **interpretation:** 玉 (yù, "jade") visually represents the concept of jade, either as a cong or three pieces of jade strung together. It is distinct from 王 (wáng, "king") thanks to the added dot, ensuring clarity in their usage and meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他收藏了一块(塊)古玉。
    - Tā shōucáng le yī kuài gǔ yù.
    - He collected a piece of antique jade.
  - 晴空万(萬)里时(時)，玉石在阳(陽)光下闪烁(閃爍)。
    - Qíngkōng wànlǐ shí, yù shí zài yángguāng xià shǎnshuò.
    - Under a clear sky, the jade sparkled under the sunlight.
  - 在宫(宮)廷中，玉制(製)的饰(飾)物象征着(徵著)高贵(貴)。
    - Zài gōngtíng zhōng, yù zhì de shìwù xiàngzhēng zhe gāoguì.
    - In the palace, jade ornaments symbolize nobility.
### 全素 → 素
- **definition:** plain; vegetarian; simple
- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 糸(絲)
    - sī
    - silk
  - 粟
    - sù
    - millet
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 糸(絲) (sī, "silk") + phonetic: 粟 (sù, "millet") =
    - combining silk and millet ->
    - suggests plainness or simplicity
  - **interpretation:** The character 素 originally depicted plain, undyed silk, suggesting simplicity or purity. The phonetic element 粟 (sù, "millet") supports the sound and also relates to a basic, unadorned life.
- **examples:**
  - 这条围(這條圍)巾设计(設計)得很素。
    - Zhè tiáo wéijīn shèjì de hěn sù.
    - This scarf is designed very simply.
  - 他坚(堅)持吃素，拒绝(絕)肉食。
    - Tā jiānchí chī sù, jùjué ròushí.
    - He insists on a vegetarian diet and rejects meat.
  - 她向(嚮)往一种(種)素雅的风(風)格。
    - Tā xiàngwǎng yì zhǒng sùyǎ de fēnggé.
    - She aspires to a plain and elegant style.
### 全素 → 素 → 糸
- **definition:** silk
- **pinyin:** sī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a skein of silk
  - **interpretation:** The character 糸(mì, "silk; thread") represents a pictorial representation of a skein of silk, visually capturing the concept of thread or fiber.
- **examples:**
  - 她手工制(製)作了一条(條)漂亮的丝(絲)巾。
    - Tā shǒugōng zhìzuòle yī tiáo piàoliang de sī jīn.
    - She handcrafted a beautiful silk scarf.
  - 这个设计图(這個設計圖)必须(須)用细(細)糸画(畫)出来(來)。
    - Zhège shèjìtú bìxū yòng xì mì huà chūlái.
    - This design drawing must be sketched with fine thread.
  - 我们参观(們參觀)了古代的织(織)布技术(術)，对丝绸制(對絲綢製)作产(產)生了兴(興)趣。
    - Wǒmen cānguānle gǔdài de zhībù jìshù, duì sīchóu zhìzuò chǎnshēngle xìngqù.
    - We visited ancient weaving techniques and developed an interest in silk production.
### 全素 → 素 → 粟
- **definition:** millet
- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 角
    - jiǎo
    - horn
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
  - 西
    - xī
    - west
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Oracle bone script shows seeds → 角 (jiǎo, "horn") for phonetic + 米 (mǐ, "rice") for semantic → 角 evolved to 𠧪 → further changed to 西 (xī, "west")
  - **interpretation:** Initially depicting seeds, 粟(sù, "millet") combined phonetic and semantic elements. 米 (mǐ, "rice") indicates the grain aspect, while 角 (jiǎo) initially added sound. Over time, the original depiction evolved through several script forms to match pronunciation changes.
- **examples:**
  - 秋天丰(豐)收时(時)，田野中的粟闪着(閃著)金光。
    - Qiūtiān fēngshōu shí, tiányě zhōng de sù shǎnzhe jīnguāng.
    - During the autumn harvest, the millet in the fields glimmers with golden light.
  - 农(農)夫把粟从(從)田里(裡)收集，准备储(準備儲)存过(過)冬。
    - Nóngfū bǎ sù cóng tián lǐ shōují, zhǔnbèi chúcún guòdōng.
    - The farmer collects the millet from the fields, preparing to store it for the winter.
  - 古代诗(詩)人常以粟象征丰(徵豐)收，歌颂农(頌農)事的喜悦(悅)。
    - Gǔdài shīrén cháng yǐ sù xiàngzhēng fēngshōu, gēsòng nóngshì de xǐyuè.
    - Ancient poets often used millet to symbolize a bountiful harvest, praising the joy of farming.
### 全素 → 素 → 粟 → 角
- **definition:** horn
- **pinyin:** jiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an animal horn
  - **interpretation:** The character 角 (jiǎo, "horn") visually represents the distinctive shape of an animal's horn.
- **examples:**
  - 牛角是一种坚(種堅)固的天然武器。
    - niú jiǎo shì yī zhǒng jiān gù de tiān rán wǔ qì。
    - Cow horns are sturdy natural weapons.
  - 鹿角丰(豐)富了森林的传说(傳說)，象征着(徵著)自由与(與)力量。
    - lù jiǎo fēng fù le sēn lín de chuán shuō, xiàng zhēng zhe zì yóu yǔ lì liàng。
    - Deer antlers enrich the legends of the forest and symbolize freedom and strength.
  - 犀牛角在古代被视(視)作珍贵药(貴藥)材。
    - xī niú jiǎo zài gǔ dài bèi shì zuò zhēn guì yào cái。
    - Rhinoceros horns were regarded as precious medicinal materials in ancient times.
### 全素 → 素 → 粟 → 米
- **definition:** rice
- **pinyin:** mǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts rice kernels ->
    - 米(mǐ, 'rice')
  - **interpretation:** The character 米(mǐ, 'rice') visually represents dehusked rice kernels to differentiate it from 粟. The horizontal stroke may symbolize a bamboo sieve, emphasizing the process of separating grains from their husks.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)吃米饭(飯)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī mǐfàn.
    - I like to eat rice.
  - 她从农(從農)村带(帶)回一袋米，用来(來)煮饭(飯)。
    - Tā cóng nóngcūn dài huí yī dài mǐ, yòng lái zhǔ fàn.
    - She brought a bag of rice from the countryside to cook.
  - 市场(場)上有新米出售。
    - Shìchǎng shàng yǒu xīn mǐ chūshòu.
    - New rice is available in the market.
### 全素 → 素 → 粟 → 西
- **definition:** west
- **pinyin:** xī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a bag or basket ->
    - borrowed for phonetic value to mean 'west'
  - **interpretation:** The character 西(xī, 'west') originally depicted a bag or basket and was phonetically borrowed to represent 'west'. Although traditionally linked to the image of a bird in a nest symbolizing the setting sun, this interpretation is disputed. Instead, it reflects a simple phonetic borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他家住在西边(邊)，附近有一座大山。
    - Tā jiā zhù zài xībiān, fùjìn yǒu yī zuò dà shān.
    - His home is on the west side, and there is a big mountain nearby.
  - 风筝(風箏)向西飞(飛)去。
    - Fēngzhēng xiàng xī fēi qù.
    - The kite flies toward the west.
