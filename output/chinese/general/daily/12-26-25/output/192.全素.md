## vegan
### quánsù
---
- **definition:** vegan
- **pinyin:** quánsù
- **components:**
  - 全
    - quán
    - whole; complete; entirely; all
  - 素
    - sù
    - vegetarian; plain; element; essence
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 全(全) (quán, "whole; complete") + 素(素) (sù, "vegetarian") =
    - completely vegetarian ->
    - vegan
  - **interpretation:** The term combines 全(全) (quán, "whole; complete") with 素(素) (sù, "vegetarian") to mean someone who is entirely committed to a vegetarian lifestyle, extending the concept to mean 'vegan', which excludes all animal products.
- **examples:**
  - 他选择全素(他選擇全素)，过着健康生活(過著健康生活)。
    - Tā xuǎnzé quánsù, guòzhe jiànkāng shēnghuó.
    - He chooses vegan and lives a healthy life.
  - 这家餐厅只供应全素(這家餐廳只供應全素)，吸引了众多顾客(吸引了眾多顧客)。
    - Zhè jiā cāntīng zhǐ gōngyìng quánsù, xīyǐnle zhòngduō gùkè.
    - This restaurant only serves vegan dishes and attracts many customers.

- **pinyin:** quán
- **components:**
  - 入

    - enter
  - 玉

    - jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 入(入) (rù, "enter") + 玉(玉) (yù, "jade") =
    - a whole piece of jade ->
    - complete
  - **interpretation:** The character 全(全) (quán, "complete") combines 入(入) (rù, "enter") with 玉(玉) (yù, "jade") to signify a complete or pure piece of jade, alluding to the idea of wholeness or integrity.
- **examples:**
  - 他的计划全实行了(他的計劃全實行了)。
    - Tā de jìhuà quán shíxíng le.

    - Wǒmen quánjiā jīnwǎn jùcān, xiǎngshòu wēnxīn shíguāng.
    - Our whole family is having dinner together tonight, enjoying a warm time.
  - 这件事全都是误会(這件事全都是誤會)。
    - Zhè jiàn shì quán dōu shì wùhuì.

### 全素(全素) → 素(素)
- **definition:** vegetarian; plain; element; essence
- **pinyin:** sù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 廾(廾) (gǒng, "two hands") + 糸(糸) (mì, "silk thread") =
    - hands braiding thread ->
    - plain/simplistic ->
    - modern form resembles 生 without diagonal stroke
  - **interpretation:** The character 素(sù, "plain") represents the action of hands braiding a silk thread, signifying simplicity and purity. It visually encapsulates the idea of creating something basic yet essential by hand.
- **examples:**
  - 他喜欢穿素色衣服(他喜歡穿素色衣服)。
    - Tā xǐhuān chuān sù sè yīfú.
    - He likes wearing plain-colored clothes.
  - 他不吃荤菜，只吃素食(他不吃葷菜，只吃素食)。
    - Tā bù chī hūncài, zhǐ chī sùshí.
    - He doesn't eat meat; he only eats vegetarian food.
  - 她坚持素食生活(她堅持素食生活)。
    - Tā jiānchí sùshí shēnghuó.
    - She adheres to a vegetarian lifestyle.
