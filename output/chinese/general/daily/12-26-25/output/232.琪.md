## fine jade; surname Qi
### Qí
---
- **definition:** fine jade; surname Qi
- **pinyin:** Qí
- **components:**
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
  - 其
    - qí
    - its; that
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 玉 (“jade”) + phonetic 其 (OC *kɯ, *ɡɯ)
  - **interpretation:** Jade (玉) supplies the meaning domain of jade or precious stones, while 其 contributes the pronunciation close to qí. The structure points to a jade-related word whose sound follows that of 其.
- **examples:**
  - 我认识一位叫王琪的老师(我認識一位叫王琪的老師)。
    - Wǒ rènshi yí wèi jiào Wáng Qí de lǎoshī.
    - I know a teacher named Wang Qi.
  - 庭院里有琪花瑶草(庭院裡有琪花瑤草)。
    - Tíngyuàn lǐ yǒu qíhuā yáocǎo.
    - There are exquisite and rare plants in the courtyard.
  - 她在玉器店挑选了一枚带“琪”字的吊坠(她在玉器店挑選了一枚帶「琪」字的吊墜)。
    - Tā zài yùqìdiàn tiāoxuǎn le yì méi dài “Qí” zì de diàozhuì.
    - She picked out a pendant with the character “Qi” at the jade shop.
### 琪(琪) → 玉(玉)
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: image of a cong (琮), a ritual jade totem; also interpreted as three pieces of jade strung on a cord. Historically similar to 王; a dot was added to distinguish them.
  - **interpretation:** Evokes pieces of jade threaded on a cord or a tall ritual cong, conveying the idea of precious jade. The added dot differentiates it from 王 while suggesting a distinct bead-like element.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱玉(我愛玉)。
    - Wǒ ài yù.
    - I love jade.
  - 这块玉很温润(這塊玉很溫潤)。
    - Zhè kuài yù hěn wēnrùn.
    - This piece of jade is very smooth and lustrous.
  - 他送我一个玉镯(他送我一個玉鐲)。
    - Tā sòng wǒ yí gè yù zhuó.
    - He gave me a jade bracelet.
### 琪(琪) → 其(其)
- **definition:** its; that
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 丌
    - jī
    - stand; table
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Originally a pictogram of a basket; later had 丌 ‘stand’ added (possibly as a phonetic), and was then borrowed to write a pronoun; the original sense survives in 箕.
  - **interpretation:** First depicted a basket, later drawn as a basket on a stand, with 丌 possibly contributing sound. Its common pronoun meaning comes from loan usage, while words like 箕 preserve the original ‘basket’ sense.
- **examples:**
  - 其结果令人意外(其結果令人意外)。
    - Qí jiéguǒ lìng rén yìwài.
    - Its result was unexpected.
  - 我们尊重其选择(我們尊重其選擇)。
    - Wǒmen zūnzhòng qí xuǎnzé.
    - We respect his/her/its choice.
  - 研究指出(研究指出)，其影响深远(其影響深遠)。
    - Yánjiū zhǐchū, qí yǐngxiǎng shēnyuǎn.
    - Research indicates that its impact is far-reaching.
### 琪(琪) → 其(其) → 丌(丌)
- **definition:** stand; table
- **pinyin:** jī
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 丨
    - gǔn
    - vertical line
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: depicts a stand/table with a horizontal board over a vertical support.
  - **interpretation:** A stylized drawing of a tabletop with a central support conveys the idea of a small stand or altar. Early forms show a horizontal board above a vertical post, aligning with the meanings 'stand; table'.
- **examples:**
  - 他把器皿放在丌上(他把器皿放在丌上)。
    - Tā bǎ qìmǐn fàng zài jī shàng.
    - He put the utensils on the stand.
  - 祭司立于丌旁(祭司立於丌旁)。
    - Jìsī lì yú jī páng.
    - The priest stood beside the altar table.
  - 博物馆展出了一张古丌(博物館展出了一張古丌)。
    - Bówùguǎn zhǎnchūle yì zhāng gǔ jī.
    - The museum displayed an ancient stand.
### 琪(琪) → 其(其) → 丌(丌) → 一(一)
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 琪(琪) → 其(其) → 丌(丌) → 丨(丨)
- **definition:** vertical line
- **pinyin:** gǔn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a vertical wooden stick; appears in early 朕 (originally “to mend a boat”) and in 尹, where the stick symbolizes authority.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an upright staff, emphasizing straightness and verticality. Its use inside graphs like 朕 and 尹 shows it originally pictured a tool or staff and later generalized to the basic vertical stroke.
- **examples:**
  - 汉字的第二号部首是丨(漢字的第二號部首是丨)。
    - Hànzì de dì-èr hào bùshǒu shì gǔn.
    - The second Chinese radical is 丨.
  - 写字时(寫字時)，要把这一笔丨写直(要把這一筆丨寫直)。
    - Xiězì shí, yào bǎ zhè yì bǐ gǔn xiě zhí.
    - When writing,this stroke 丨 should be written straight.
  - 他写了一笔丨(他寫了一筆丨)。
    - Tā xiě le yì bǐ gǔn.
    - He wrote a single vertical stroke.
