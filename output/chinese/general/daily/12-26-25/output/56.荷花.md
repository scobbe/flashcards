## lotus flower
### héhuā
---
- **definition:** lotus flower
- **pinyin:** héhuā
- **components:**
  - 荷
    - hé
    - lotus; burden
  - 花
    - huā
    - flower
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 荷 (hé, "lotus") + 花 (huā, "flower") creates the word 荷花 (héhuā, "lotus flower"). 荷 (hé, "lotus") refers to the lotus plant, a significant symbol in various Asian cultures, often representing purity and spiritual awakening, while 花 (huā, "flower") denotes the flowering part of the plant. Together, they specify the blooming part of the lotus plant.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 荷 (hé, "lotus") and 花 (huā, "flower") specifies the flower part of the lotus plant, highlighting its importance in many Eastern cultural, religious, and philosophical contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 夏日降临时(臨時)，荷花绽(綻)放光彩。
    - xiàrì jiànglín shí, héhuā zhànfàng guāngcǎi.
    - When summer arrives, the lotus flower blooms brilliantly.
  - 池边(邊)有荷花，迎风摇(風搖)曳动(動)人。
    - chí biān yǒu héhuā, yíng fēng yáoyè dòngrén.
    - By the pond, there are lotus flowers, swaying gracefully in the breeze.
### 荷花 → 荷
- **definition:** lotus; burden
- **pinyin:** hé
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass; plant
  - 何
    - hé
    - phonetic component
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 艸 (cǎo, "grass; plant") + 何 (hé) =
    - plant-related meaning with 何 as phonetic ->
    - meaning of lotus or burden
  - **interpretation:** The character 荷 combines the radical 艸 (cǎo, "grass; plant") to indicate its association with plants, specifically the lotus, while 何 (hé) provides the phonetic clue. The lotus is considered both a plant and symbolically associated with roles and burdens.
- **examples:**
  - 夏日的池塘边(邊)，荷花优(優)雅盛开(開)。
    - Xiàrì de chítáng biān, héhuā yōuyǎ shèngkāi。
    - By the pond on a summer day, lotus flowers bloom gracefully.
  - 他肩负(負)重担(擔)，难(難)以释怀(釋懷)那沉沉的荷。
    - Tā jiānfù zhòngdān, nán yǐ shìhuái nà chénchén de hé。
    - He bears a heavy load, unable to let go of that persistent burden.
### 荷花 → 荷 → 艸
- **definition:** grass; plant
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè
    - grass
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts grass blades ->
    - duplication of 屮 (chè, 'grass') ->
    - grass imagery
  - **interpretation:** The character 艸(cǎo, 'grass') is a pictorial representation of grass blades. It is essentially a duplication of the character 屮 (chè, 'grass'), visually suggesting the appearance of grass tufts through repetition, emphasizing the concept of grass or vegetation.
- **examples:**
  - 公园里绿(園裡綠)艸茵茵。
    - Gōngyuán lǐ lǜ cǎo yīn yīn.
    - The park is filled with lush grass.
  - 晨露滋润(潤)艸丛(叢)，微风(風)使它们(們)起舞。
    - Chénlù zīrùn cǎo cóng, wēifēng shǐ tāmen qǐwǔ.
    - The morning dew nourishes the cluster of grass, and the breeze makes them dance.
  - 秋天时(時)艸变黄(變黃)，冬天它又枯萎。
    - Qiūtiān shí cǎo biàn huáng, dōngtiān tā yòu kūwěi.
    - In autumn, the grass turns yellow, and in winter, it withers.
### 荷花 → 荷 → 艸 → 屮
- **definition:** grass
- **pinyin:** chè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a sprout emerging from the ground
  - **interpretation:** 屮 (chè, 'sprout') is a pictogram that directly represents a young plant or sprout emerging from the ground. Its visual form captures the essence of new growth, and it historically served as an early representation of plant life or new vegetation.
- **examples:**
  - 春雨滋润后(潤後)，小屮冲(沖)破泥土而出。
    - chūn yǔ zī rùn hòu, xiǎo chè chōng pò ní tǔ ér chū.
    - After the spring rain nurtured the earth, the little sprout broke through the soil.
  - 草屮初生露尖端。
    - cǎo chè chū shēng lù jiān duān.
    - The grass sprout's tip has just emerged.
### 荷花 → 荷 → 何
- **definition:** phonetic component
- **pinyin:** hé
- **components:**
  - 亻
    - rén
    - person
  - 可
    - kě
    - can, may
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 亻 (rén, "person") + phonetic: 可 (kě, "can, may") =
    - person's ability to do something ->
    - interrogative 'what, why'
  - **interpretation:** The character 何 (hé, "what") combines the person radical 亻 (rén, "person") with 可 (kě, "can, may") as the phonetic component, indicating actions or attributes related to people, hence its use in asking questions starting with 'what' or 'why'.
- **examples:**
  - 何是汉(漢)字中常见(見)的声(聲)旁。
    - Hé shì hànzì zhōng chángjiàn de shēngpáng.
    - ‘Hé’ is a common phonetic component in Chinese characters.
  - 研究者探讨(討)了何在不同字中的作用，并强调(並強調)它的重要性。
    - Yánjiū zhě tàntǎo le hé zài bùtóng zì zhōng de zuòyòng, bìng qiángdiào tā de zhòngyào xìng.
    - Researchers discussed the role of 'hé' in various characters and emphasized its importance.
  - 学(學)者研究何的音韵(韻)功能。
    - Xuézhě yánjiū hé de yīnyùn gōngnéng.
    - Scholars study the phonological function of 'hé'.

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** 亻 (rén, "person") is used as a component form of 人 (rén, "person") in compound characters, representing the human or person-related aspect.
- **examples:**

    - Rén shì gòuchéng xǔduō hànzì de guānjiàn bùshǒu.

  - 学习汉(學習漢)字时(時)，了解亻的意义(義)很重要。
    - Xuéxí hànzì shí, liǎojiě rén de yìyì hěn zhòngyào.
    - When learning Chinese characters, understanding the meaning of 亻 is important.
  - 古人造字时(時)常取亻以表达(達)人物情感，因此亻贯(貫)穿于(於)文明史中。
    - Gǔrén zào zì shí cháng qǔ rén yǐ biǎodá rénwù qínggǎn, yīncǐ rén guànchuān yú wénmíng shǐ zhōng.
    - Ancient people often used the radical 亻 to express human emotions in character formation, and thus 亻 runs through the history of civilization.

### 荷花 → 荷 → 何 → 可 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。

  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
