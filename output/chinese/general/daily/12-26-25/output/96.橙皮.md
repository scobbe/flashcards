## orange peel
### chéngpí
---
- **definition:** orange peel
- **pinyin:** chéngpí
- **components:**
  - 橙
    - chéng
    - orange
  - 皮
    - pí
    - skin; peel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 橙 (chéng, "orange") + 皮 (pí, "skin; peel") combines to refer directly to the literal outer layer or "peel" of the "orange" fruit. This combination is straightforward, with each character providing a specific meaning that, when combined, directly describes the nature of the object.
  - **interpretation:** The term 橙皮 (chéngpí, "orange peel") is a straightforward description of the outer layer (皮) of an orange (橙), which is commonly peeled off in order to eat the fruit.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)橙皮。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chéngpí.
    - I like orange peel.
  - 做蛋糕时(時)，他加入了橙皮。
    - Zuò dàngāo shí, tā jiārùle chéngpí.
    - When making cake, he added orange peel.
  - 市场(場)上，橙皮卖(賣)得不错(錯)。
    - Shìchǎng shàng, chéngpí mài de bùcuò.
    - In the market, orange peel sells well.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 木 (mù, "tree; wood") + phonetic 登 (dēng, "ascend") =
    - a fruit from a tree ->
    - orange
  - **interpretation:** The character 橙(chéng) combines 木(mù, 'tree') to signify the material nature, with 登(dēng) as the phonetic element. This illustrates that oranges come from trees.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)吃橙。

    - Chéngzi fùhán wéishēngsù C, duì jiànkāng yǒuyì.
    - Oranges are rich in vitamin C, which is beneficial for health.

### 橙皮 → 橙 → 登 → 癶

- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

    - diverging direction
  - **interpretation:** The character 癶(bá) represents two feet moving in opposite directions, symbolizing divergence and movement. It contrasts with 舛 (chuǎn, "contradict"), which depicts convergent feet, highlighting different types of movement orientations.

    - In ancient scripts, bá appears, symbolizing two outstretched feet.
  - 汉(漢)字构(構)造中含有癶，展现(現)出足部迅猛伸展的形态(態)。
    - Hànzì gòuzào zhōng hányǒu bá, zhǎnxiàn chū zúbù xùnměng shēnzhǎn de xíngtài.
    - Within the structure of Chinese characters, bá is incorporated, revealing the form of swiftly outstretched feet.
  - 癶象征着两只(徵著兩隻)外伸的脚(腳)。
    - Bá xiàngzhēngzhe liǎng zhǐ wài shēn de jiǎo.
    - Bá symbolizes two outstretched feet.

### 橙皮 → 橙 → 登 → 癶 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a footprint with toes and sole ->
    - stylized as feet in characters
  - **interpretation:** The character 龰(yù, "stylized feet") represents a footprint with an upward orientation, which is indicative of a foot. This depiction is related to characters like 趾 (zhǐ, "toe"), which retain the meaning of a foot, and contrasts with other similarly shaped characters pointing in different directions, like 夊 and 夂.

### 橙皮 → 橙 → 登 → 豆

  - **description:**

### 橙皮 → 皮
- **definition:** skin; peel
- **pinyin:** pí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a surface or the skin of an animal
  - **interpretation:** The character 皮 (pí, 'surface') visually represents an animal hide or skin stretched out, emphasizing the idea of an outer covering or surface. It captures the concept of a boundary between the interior and exterior, as seen in animal skins used as material.
- **examples:**
  - 这张(這張)桌子的皮很光滑。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi de pí hěn guānghuá.
    - The surface of this table is very smooth.
  - 新鞋的皮非常柔软(軟)，适(適)合长时间(長時間)行走。
    - Xīn xié de pí fēicháng róuruǎn, shìhé cháng shíjiān xíngzǒu.
    - The surface of the new shoes is very soft and suitable for long walks.
