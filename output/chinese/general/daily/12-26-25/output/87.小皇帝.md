## spoiled child
### xiǎohuángdì
---
- **definition:** spoiled child
- **pinyin:** xiǎohuángdì
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little
  - 皇帝
    - huángdì
    - emperor
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 小 (xiǎo, "small; little") + 皇帝 (huángdì, "emperor") =
    - a metaphor for a child treated like an emperor ->
    - spoiled child
  - **interpretation:** The term 小皇帝 (xiǎohuángdì, "spoiled child") describes a child who is overly pampered and indulged by their parents, as an emperor would be by his court. This metaphor reflects the increasing trend of indulged children in modern society.
- **examples:**
  - 妈妈总会满(媽媽總會滿)足他的所有要求，因为(為)他就像个(個)小皇帝。
    - Māmā zǒng huì mǎnzú tā de suǒyǒu yāoqiú, yīnwèi tā jiù xiàng ge xiǎohuángdì.
    - Mom always meets all his demands because he acts like a spoiled child.
  - 他的行为总让(為總讓)人哭笑不得，仿佛小皇帝在发号(發號)施令。
    - Tā de xíngwéi zǒng ràng rén kū xiào bù dé, fǎngfú xiǎohuángdì zài fāhào shīlìng.
    - His behavior always confounds people, as if a spoiled child were giving orders.

### 小皇帝 → 皇帝
- **definition:** emperor
- **pinyin:** huángdì
- **components:**
  - 皇
    - huáng
    - imperial; royal
  - 帝
    - dì
    - emperor; sovereign; god
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 皇 (huáng, "imperial; royal") + 帝 (dì, "emperor; sovereign") =
    - emperor, the supreme ruler
  - **interpretation:** 皇帝 (huángdì, "emperor") combines the ideas of being imperial or royal and the concept of a supreme sovereign. In ancient China, the title of emperor was considered the highest authority, both politically and spiritually, as emperors were often seen as the 'Son of Heaven' and the intermediary between heaven and earth.
- **examples:**
  - 他继(繼)承帝位后称(後稱)皇帝。
    - Tā jìchéng dìwèi hòu chēng huángdì.
    - He ascended to the throne and became the emperor.
  - 唐朝皇帝勤政爱(愛)民，后(後)世传颂(傳頌)佳话(話)。
    - Tángcháo huángdì qínzhèng ài mín, hòushì chuánsòng jiāhuà.
    - The Tang dynasty emperor governed diligently and loved his people, and later generations celebrated this fine reputation.
  - 他梦(夢)想成为(為)皇帝，统领万(統領萬)民。
    - Tā mèngxiǎng chéngwéi huángdì, tǒnglǐng wànmín.
    - He dreams of becoming the emperor and leading the people.
