## spoiled child
### xiǎohuángdì
---
- **definition:** spoiled child
- **pinyin:** xiǎohuángdì
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little; young
  - 皇帝
    - huángdì
    - emperor
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 小(小) (xiǎo, "small") + 皇帝(皇帝) (huángdì, "emperor") =
    - "small emperor" ->
    - spoiled child
  - **interpretation:** The term 小皇帝 (xiǎohuángdì, "little emperor") draws a parallel between a spoiled child and an emperor who is used to being indulged. The implication is that the child, like an emperor, receives excessive attention and leniency from those around them.
- **examples:**
  - 他是家里的小皇帝(他是家裡的小皇帝)。
    - Tā shì jiā lǐ de xiǎohuángdì.
    - He is the spoiled child in the family.
  - 每次买玩具时(每次買玩具時)，他都会吵闹(他都會吵鬧)。
    - Měi cì mǎi wánjù shí, tā dōu huì chǎonào.
    - Every time he buys toys, he always throws a tantrum.
  - 在学校里(在學校裡)，他就像个小皇帝(他就像個小皇帝)。
    - Zài xuéxiào lǐ, tā jiù xiàng gè xiǎohuángdì.
    - At school, he behaves like a little emperor.

### 小皇帝(小皇帝) → 皇帝(皇帝) → 皇(皇)
- **definition:** emperor; majestic
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **components:**
  - 白
    - bái
    - flame
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Pictogram initially depicting a lit oil lamp ->
    - semantic 白(白) (bái, "flame") + phonetic 王(王) (wáng, "king") =
    - bright and majestic ->
    - denotes 'emperor'
  - **interpretation:** The character 皇 originally symbolized a lit oil lamp, conveying brightness and majesty. Over time, 白(白) (bái, "flame") was added to maintain the luminous aspect, while 王(王) (wáng, "king") served as a phonetic and semantic anchor to represent royalty and grandeur.
- **examples:**
  - 皇上登基时(皇上登基時)，全国上下皆感恩戴德(全國上下皆感恩戴德)。

    - When the emperor ascended the throne, the entire nation expressed heartfelt gratitude.
  - 皇恩浩荡(皇恩浩蕩)。
    - huáng ēn hào dàng
    - The emperor's grace is immense.
  - 他目睹了皇权的威严(他目睹了皇權的威嚴)，民众无不倾心效忠(民眾無不傾心效忠)。
    - tā mù dǔ le huáng quán de wēi yán, mín zhòng wú bù qīng xīn xiào zhōng

### 小皇帝(小皇帝) → 皇帝(皇帝) → 帝(帝)
- **definition:** emperor; sovereign
- **pinyin:** dì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts an ancient symbol related to divinity or celestial phenomena ->
    - associated with heavenly power ->
    - used to symbolize rulers or emperors
  - **interpretation:** The character 帝(dì, "emperor") originally depicted a heavenly or celestial concept, which naturally extended to signify divine or sovereign authority. Over time, it became associated with the notion of supreme rulers as 'emperors.'
- **examples:**
  - 汉帝勤政爱民(漢帝勤政愛民)。
    - Hàn dì qínzhèng ài mín.
    - The Han emperor governed diligently and cared for his people.
  - 古代帝制崩溃后(古代帝制崩潰後)，民心开始涣散(民心開始渙散)。
    - Gǔdài dì zhì bēngkuì hòu, míngxīn kāishǐ huànsàn.
    - After the collapse of the ancient imperial system, the people's loyalty began to dissipate.
  - 新帝登基之日(新帝登基之日)，万民共庆太平(萬民共慶太平)。
    - Xīn dì dēngjī zhī rì, wàn mín gòng qìng tàipíng.
    - On the day the new emperor ascended the throne, all the people celebrated peace together.
