### spoiled child
---
- **pinyin:** spoiled child
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little; young; minor
  - 皇帝
    - huángdì
    - emperor; sovereign; monarch; ruler
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小(小) (xiǎo, "small") + 皇帝(皇帝) (huángdì, "emperor") — small emperor
  - **interpretation:** The term describes a child who is pampered and treated as if they were an emperor. This combination aptly encapsulates a young child given excessive attention and privileges.
- **examples:**
  - 他是个小皇帝，什么都由父母决定。(他是個小皇帝，什麼都由父母決定。)
    - Tā shì gè xiǎo huángdì, shénme dōu yóu fùmǔ juédìng.
    - He is a spoiled child, with everything decided by his parents.
  - 这些小皇帝总是想要更多的玩具。(這些小皇帝總是想要更多的玩具。)
    - Zhèxiē xiǎo huángdì zǒng shì xiǎng yào gèng duō de wánjù.
    - These spoiled children always want more toys.

- **etymology:**

- **examples:**

### 小皇帝(小皇帝) → 皇帝(皇帝)
- **definition:** emperor; sovereign; monarch; ruler
- **pinyin:** huángdì
- **components:**
  - 皇
    - huáng
    - imperial; sovereign; majestic; supreme
  - 帝
    - dì
    - emperor; deity; ruler; supreme leader
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 皇(皇) (huáng, 'imperial') + 帝(帝) (dì, 'emperor') — imperial emperor
  - **interpretation:** The term 皇帝 (huángdì) refers to a sovereign with imperial authority. The combination emphasizes supreme rule and majesty, indicating a central figure of governance and power.
- **examples:**
  - 中国历史上有许多伟大的皇帝。(中國歷史上有許多偉大的皇帝。)
    - Zhōngguó lìshǐ shàng yǒu xǔduō wěidà de huángdì.
    - There were many great emperors in China's history.
  - 传说中的皇帝是中国古代的统治者。(傳說中的皇帝是中國古代的統治者。)
    - Chuánshuō zhōng de huángdì shì Zhōngguó gǔdài de tǒngzhìzhě.
    - The legendary emperor was an ancient ruler of China.
### 小皇帝(小皇帝) → 皇帝(皇帝) → 皇(皇)
- **definition:** imperial; sovereign; majestic; supreme
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character depicts a majestic figure, often associated with the emperor or sovereignty.
  - **interpretation:** The character represents authority and majesty, directly reflecting the concept of imperial power.
- **examples:**
  - 皇帝的威严(皇帝的威嚴)，令百姓们敬畏(令百姓們敬畏)。

  - 他的梦想是成为皇帝(他的夢想是成為皇帝)。
    - tā de mèngxiǎng shì chéngwéi huángdì.
  - 这是一个关于皇室的故事(這是一個關於皇室的故事)。
    - zhè shì yīgè guānyú huángshì de gùshì.
### 小皇帝(小皇帝) → 皇帝(皇帝) → 帝(帝)
- **definition:** emperor; deity; ruler; supreme leader
- **pinyin:** dì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character 帝 (dì) represents authority or supreme power, combined with elements indicating a ruler or divine figure. It is often associated with emperors and deities.
  - **interpretation:** This character evokes the idea of someone holding the highest authority, symbolizing both governance and divinity.
- **examples:**
  - 他是伟大的帝王(他是偉大的帝王)。
    - Tā shì wěidà de dìwáng.
    - He is a great emperor.
  - 在古代, 帝通常被视为神灵(在古代，帝通常被視為神靈)。
    - Zài gǔdài, dì tōngcháng bèi shì wéi shénlíng.
    - In ancient times, emperors were often regarded as deities.
