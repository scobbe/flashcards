## reserve; book
### yùdìng
---
- **definition:** reserve; book
- **pinyin:** yùdìng
- **components:**
  - 预(預)
    - yù
    - in advance
  - 订(訂)
    - dìng
    - order
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 预(預) (yù, "in advance") + 订(訂) (dìng, "order") =
    - to reserve or book something in advance
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 预(預) (yù, "in advance") and 订(訂) (dìng, "order") makes sense because it refers to the action of ordering or reserving something before it is needed, which is the essence of making a reservation or booking.
- **examples:**
  - 我预订了明天的晚餐(我預訂了明天的晚餐)。
    - Wǒ yùdìng le míngtiān de wǎncān.
    - I booked dinner for tomorrow.
  - 我预订了火车票(我預訂了火車票)，确保旅行无忧(確保旅行無憂)。
    - Wǒ yùdìng le huǒchēpiào, quèbǎo lǚxíng wúyōu.
    - I booked a train ticket to ensure a worry-free journey.
  - 为了保证位置(為了保證位置)，我预订了餐厅(我預訂了餐廳)，以免临近时客满(以免臨近時客滿)。
    - Wèile bǎozhèng wèizhì, wǒ yùdìng le cāntīng, yǐmiǎn línjìn shí kèmǎn.
    - In order to secure a spot, I booked a restaurant to prevent it from being fully booked as the time drew near.
### 预订(預訂) → 预(預)
- **definition:** in advance
- **pinyin:** yù
- **components:**
  - 予
    - yǔ
    - to give
  - 頁
    - yè
    - page
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic: 予(予) (yǔ, "to give") + semantic: 頁(頁) (yè, "page") ->
    - 預 ->
    - simplification 頁(頁) (yè, "page") to 页(页) (yè, "page") ->
    - 预
  - **interpretation:** The character 預 incorporates 頁(頁) (yè, "page") to convey the idea of preliminary thinking or planning, with 予(予) (yǔ, "to give") illustrating the sound. This combination suggests preparing or thinking ahead.
  - **simplification:** The component 頁(頁) (yè, "page") is simplified to 页(页) (yè, "page"), retaining phonetic and semantic aspects in a streamlined form.
- **examples:**
  - 我们预定了晚餐(我們預定了晚餐)。
    - Wǒmen yùdìngle wǎncān.
    - We reserved dinner in advance.
  - 他提前预习了课文(他提前預習了課文)。
    - Tā tíqián yùxíle kèwén.
    - He reviewed the lesson in advance.
  - 公司预安排了会议(公司預安排了會議)，员工们积极准备(員工們積極準備)。
    - Gōngsī yù ānpái le huìyì, yuángōngmen jījí zhǔnbèi.
    - The company scheduled the meeting in advance, and the employees prepared actively.
### 预订(預訂) → 预(預) → 予(予)
- **definition:** to give
- **pinyin:** yǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized hand holding a tool ->
    - by extension, giving
  - **interpretation:** The character 予(予) (yǔ, "to give") originally depicted a hand holding an object, symbolizing the action of giving. Over time, this simple pictogram was used to convey the concept of granting or bestowing.
- **examples:**
  - 老师予同学机会(老師予同學機會)。
    - Lǎoshī yǔ tóngxué jīhuì.
    - The teacher gives the students an opportunity.
  - 父亲总是予孩子温暖(父親總是予孩子溫暖)，激励他们勇敢前行(激勵他們勇敢前行)。
    - Fùqīn zǒngshì yǔ háizi wēnnuǎn, jīlì tāmen yǒnggǎn qiánxíng.
    - The father always gives his children warmth, inspiring them to move forward bravely.
  - 我予朋友诚挚祝福(我予朋友誠摯祝福)。
    - Wǒ yǔ péngyǒu chéngzhì zhùfú.
    - I give my friends sincere blessings.
### 预订(預訂) → 预(預) → 頁(頁)
- **definition:** page
- **pinyin:** yè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 首(首) (shǒu, "head") + 卩(卩) (jié, "kneeling person") =
    - head of kneeling person, emphasizing head
  - **interpretation:** The character 頁 originally depicted the head of a kneeling person, combining 首(首) (shǒu, "head") with the body addition of 卩(卩) (jié, "kneeling person"), emphasizing the concept of 'head'. It evolved to mean 'page' or 'leaf' as an alternative form of 葉 and retains its original meaning in the radical form.
  - **simplification:** 頁 was simplified to 页 by adopting a cursive script shorthand of the traditional form 頁.
- **examples:**
  - 书的最后一页(書的最後一頁)，写着作者的名字(寫著作者的名字)。
    - Shū de zuìhòu yī yè, xiězhe zuòzhě de míngzì.
    - The last page of the book bears the author's name.
  - 请翻到第十页(請翻到第十頁)，开始阅读(開始閱讀)。
    - Qǐng fān dào dì shí yè, kāishǐ yuèdú.
    - Please turn to the tenth page to start reading.
  - 这张杂志的封面在第三页(這張雜誌的封面在第三頁)，呈现了美丽的图案(呈現了美麗的圖案)。
    - Zhè zhāng zázhì de fēngmiàn zài dì sān yè, chéngxiàn le měilì de tú'àn.
    - The cover of this magazine on the third page displays a beautiful pattern.
### 预订(預訂) → 订(訂)
- **definition:** order
- **pinyin:** dìng
- **components:**
  - 言
    - yán
    - speech
  - 丁
    - dīng
    - fourth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 言(言) (yán, "speech") + 丁(丁) (dīng) =
    - phonetic 丁 reinforces sound, 言 represents meaning of words or speech ->
    - ordering with words
  - **interpretation:** 订(訂) (dìng, "order") combines 言(言) (yán, "speech") with the phonetic 丁(丁) (dīng). The semantic component 言 suggests it relates to speech or agreements, and 丁 adds the phonetic element.
  - **simplification:** 訂(訂) was simplified by removing the 言(言) (yán, "speech") component, leaving just 丁(丁) (dīng) for simplicity while retaining the original pronunciation element.
- **examples:**
  - 我订了明天的机票(我訂了明天的機票)。
    - Wǒ dìng le míngtiān de jīpiào.
    - I ordered tomorrow's plane ticket.
  - 老板要求我们提前订货(老闆要求我們提前訂貨)，以确保库存充足(以確保庫存充足)。
    - Lǎobǎn yāoqiú wǒmen tíqián dìng huò, yǐ quèbǎo kùcún chōngzú.
    - The boss required us to order the goods in advance to ensure sufficient inventory.
### 预订(預訂) → 订(訂) → 言(言)
- **definition:** speech
- **pinyin:** yán
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 舌(舌) (shé, "tongue") + horizontal stroke =
    - movement of tongue ->
    - speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 言(yán, "speech") originates from the depiction of a tongue 舌(舌) with an added stroke to represent its movement, signifying the action of speaking. This visual representation highlights the tongue's role in speech, which is central to communication.
- **examples:**
  - 言简意赅可以表达深刻思想(言簡意賅可以表達深刻思想)。
    - yán jiǎn yì gāi kěyǐ biǎo dá shēn kè sī xiǎng。
    - Concise speech can express profound thoughts.
  - 他的言论总能激发思考(他的言論總能激發思考)。
    - tā de yán lùn zǒng néng jī fā sī kǎo。
    - His speech always inspires reflection.
  - 老师常说(老師常說)，言出必行(言出必行)。
    - lǎo shī cháng shuō, yán chū bì xíng。
    - The teacher often says that one should act on one's words.
### 预订(預訂) → 订(訂) → 丁(丁)
- **definition:** fourth
- **pinyin:** dīng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a simple shape ->
    - interpreted as head of a nail, head, or city wall
  - **interpretation:** The character 丁 is a pictogram with multiple interpretations, such as the head of a nail or a city wall, reflecting its simple, straight-line shape. It is used to denote the number 'fourth' as part of the Chinese heavenly stems, though its original pictographic representation has various possible meanings.
- **examples:**
  - 排列顺序为甲、乙、丙、丁(排列順序為甲、乙、丙、丁)。
    - Páiliè shùnxù wéi jiǎ, yǐ, bǐng, dīng.
    - The order is arranged as Jia, Yi, Bing, and Ding (with Ding being the fourth).
  - 家中老大叫甲(家中老大叫甲)，老二叫乙(老二叫乙)，老三叫丙(老三叫丙)，老四称丁(老四稱丁)。
    - Jiāzhōng lǎodà jiào jiǎ, lǎoèr jiào yǐ, lǎosān jiào bǐng, lǎosì chēng dīng.
    - In the family, the eldest is called Jia, the second is Yi, the third is Bing, and the fourth is called Ding.
