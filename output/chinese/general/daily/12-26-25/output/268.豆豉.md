## fermented black beans
### dòuchǐ
---
- **definition:** fermented black beans
- **pinyin:** dòuchǐ
- **components:**
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean; pea
  - 豉
    - chǐ
    - fermented beans
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 豆 (dòu, "bean") refers to legumes, which are a common staple food in Chinese cuisine. 豉 (chǐ, "fermented beans") indicates the fermentation process. Together, 豆豉 (dòuchǐ, "fermented black beans") refers to a traditional seasoning made by fermenting soybeans with salt and spices, often used in Chinese cooking for its deep, umami flavor.
  - **interpretation:** 豆豉 (dòuchǐ) refers to fermented beans, specifically black beans, which are a culinary staple in Chinese culture. The fermentation process enriches the beans with a savory flavor, making it a popular ingredient in various dishes.
