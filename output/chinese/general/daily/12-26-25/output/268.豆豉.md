## fermented black beans
### dòuchǐ
---
- **definition:** fermented black beans
- **pinyin:** dòuchǐ
- **components:**
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean; pea
  - 豉
    - chǐ
    - fermented beans
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 豆(豆) (dòu, "bean") refers to legumes, which are a common staple food in Chinese cuisine. 豉(豉) (chǐ, "fermented beans") indicates the fermentation process. Together, 豆豉 (dòuchǐ, "fermented black beans") refers to a traditional seasoning made by fermenting soybeans with salt and spices, often used in Chinese cooking for its deep, umami flavor.
  - **interpretation:** 豆豉 (dòuchǐ) refers to fermented beans, specifically black beans, which are a culinary staple in Chinese culture. The fermentation process enriches the beans with a savory flavor, making it a popular ingredient in various dishes.
- **examples:**
  - 厨师用豆豉调味(廚師用豆豉調味)，烹饪出独特风味(烹飪出獨特風味)。
    - Chúshī yòng dòuchǐ tiáowèi, pēngrèn chū dútè fēngwèi.
    - The chef uses fermented black beans for seasoning, cooking up a unique flavor.
  - 他喜欢在炒菜时加入豆豉(他喜歡在炒菜時加入豆豉)，增添香气(增添香氣)。
    - Tā xǐhuān zài chǎocài shí jiārù dòuchǐ, zēngtiān xiāngqì.
    - He likes to add fermented black beans when stir-frying to enhance the aroma.
  - 豆豉是一种传统调味品(豆豉是一種傳統調味品)。
    - Dòuchǐ shì yī zhǒng chuántǒng tiáowèi pǐn.
    - Fermented black beans are a traditional seasoning.
### 豆豉(豆豉) → 豆(豆)
- **definition:** bean; pea
- **pinyin:** dòu
- **components:**
  - 菽
    - shū
    - pulse
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a container ->
    - borrowed phonetically for the plant name
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a container, 豆(豆) (dòu, 'drum') was phonetically borrowed to represent the plant name for beans, eventually displacing the term 菽(菽) (shū, 'pulse'). It is unrelated to the 豊(豊) (fēng, 'abundant') character.
- **examples:**
  - 祭司敲豆奏乐(祭司敲豆奏樂)，引来神灵祝福(引來神靈祝福)。
    - Jìsī qiāo dòu zòuyuè, yǐn lái shénlíng zhùfú.
    - The priest beats the drum, summoning divine blessings.
  - 古风庆典上敲响豆(古風慶典上敲響豆)，唤起怀古情怀(喚起懷古情懷)。
    - Gǔfēng qìngdiǎn shàng qiāoxiǎng dòu, huànqǐ huáigǔ qínghuái.
    - At the ancient-style celebration, the beating of the drum evokes nostalgic sentiments.
  - 豆声震天(豆聲震天)。
    - Dòu shēng zhèntiān.
    - The sound of the drum shakes the heavens.

- **definition:** pulse
- **pinyin:** shū
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 艹(艹) (cǎo, "grass") + 叔(叔) (shū, "uncle") =
    - plant-related term with phonetic value from 叔
  - **interpretation:** The character 菽(shū, "pulse") combines the semantic 艹(艹) (cǎo, "grass") to indicate a plant-related concept, with the phonetic element 叔(叔) (shū) to suggest its pronunciation. This reflects the agricultural context of 'pulse' as a type of leguminous plant.

    - He grinds pulses to make fragrant soy milk, enjoying a healthy life.
  - 春天里菽花绽放(春天裏菽花綻放)。
    - Chūntiān lǐ shū huā zhànfàng.

- **pinyin:** shū
- **components:**

    - wooden stake
  - 又

- **examples:**

    - I sought advice on classical literature from my uncle, and he seriously shared his experience.
