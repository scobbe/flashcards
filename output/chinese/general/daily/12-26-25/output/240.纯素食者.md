## vegan
### chúnsùshízhě
---
- **definition:** vegan
- **pinyin:** chúnsùshízhě
- **components:**
  - 纯素(純素)
    - chún sù
    - purely vegetarian; vegan; entirely plant-based; strict vegetarian
  - 食者
    - shí zhě
    - eater; one who eats; food consumer; person (suffix)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 纯素 + 食者 = person who eats an entirely plant-based diet (vegan).
  - **interpretation:** Emphasizes a strictly plant-based diet with no animal-derived products. 者 is a nominalizer meaning “person.”
  - **simplification:** 純 was simplified to 纯 by reducing the 糸 radical to 纟 and simplifying the rest; other characters are unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 很多纯素食者不吃任何动物产品(很多純素食者不吃任何動物產品)。
    - Hěn duō chún sù shí zhě bù chī rènhé dòngwù chǎnpǐn.
    - Many vegans don't eat any animal products.
  - 她是严格的纯素食者，只吃蔬菜、水果和谷物(她是嚴格的純素食者，只吃蔬菜、水果和穀物)。
    - Tā shì yángé de chún sù shí zhě, zhǐ chī shūcài, shuǐguǒ hé gǔwù.
    - She is a strict vegan and only eats vegetables, fruit, and grains.
  - 为了环境和健康，越来越多的人成为纯素食者(為了環境和健康，越來越多的人成為純素食者)。
    - Wèile huánjìng hé jiànkāng, yuèláiyuè duō de rén chéngwéi chún sù shí zhě.
    - For the environment and health, more and more people are becoming vegans.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素)
- **definition:** purely vegetarian; vegan; entirely plant-based; strict vegetarian
- **pinyin:** chún sù
- **components:**
  - 纯(純)
    - chún
    - pure; unmixed; genuine; simple
  - 素
    - sù
    - plain; vegetarian; element; unadorned
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 纯(純) ‘pure’ + 素 ‘plain; vegetarian’ = strictly plant-based (vegan).
  - **interpretation:** Emphasizes complete avoidance of animal products, stronger than 素/素食 which may include eggs or dairy. Common in compounds like 纯素饮食 and 纯素者; also corresponds to 全素 in Taiwan usage.
  - **simplification:** 纯 was simplified from 純 by replacing the silk radical 糸 with 纟 in the PRC script reform; phonetic 屯 retained. 素 is unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我吃纯素(我吃純素)。
    - Wǒ chī chúnsù.
    - I eat a vegan diet.
  - 他坚持纯素饮食(他堅持純素飲食)。
    - Tā jiānchí chúnsù yǐnshí.
    - He sticks to a vegan diet.
  - 这家餐厅提供纯素选项(這家餐廳提供純素選項)。
    - Zhè jiā cāntīng tígōng chúnsù xuǎnxiàng.
    - This restaurant offers vegan options.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素) → 纯(純)
- **definition:** pure; unmixed; genuine; simple
- **pinyin:** chún
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
  - 屯
    - tún
    - to station; camp
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 糸 ‘thread’ + phonetic: 屯.
  - **interpretation:** Silk/thread (糸) evokes fineness and lack of mixture, while 屯 supplies the sound. The image of even, single-strand thread extends to meanings like “pure, unmixed” and by extension “solely/exclusively.”
  - **simplification:** Semantic 糸 was simplified to 纟 as part of the general silk-radical reduction; the phonetic 屯 was kept unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 这水很纯(這水很純)。
    - Zhè shuǐ hěn chún.
    - This water is very pure.
  - 她有一颗纯真的心(她有一顆純真的心)。
    - Tā yǒu yì kē chúnzhēn de xīn.
    - She has a pure and innocent heart.
  - 纯棉衣服很舒服(純棉衣服很舒服)。
    - Chúnmián yīfú hěn shūfu.
    - Pure-cotton clothes are very comfortable.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素) → 纯(純) → 糸
- **definition:** silk; thread
- **pinyin:** mì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** a skein of silk.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bundle of silk threads tied in the middle, with strands hanging above and below. The stylized sketch highlights twisted filaments, which later served as the standard radical for thread- and textile-related characters.
  - **simplification:** In Simplified Chinese the silk radical was streamlined to 纟 to reduce strokes and speed writing; 糸 is rarely used independently, but 纟 replaces it as a left-side component.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan sīchóu.
    - I like silk fabrics.
  - 这件衣服是用蚕丝做的(這件衣服是用蠶絲做的)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfu shì yòng cánsī zuò de.
    - This piece of clothing is made from silkworm silk.
  - 丝线很细(絲線很細)。
    - Sīxiàn hěn xì.
    - Silk thread is very thin.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素) → 纯(純) → 屯
- **definition:** to station; camp
- **pinyin:** tún
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a plant sprout (屮) with a dot in the middle, later transformed into a horizontal line; compare the top component of 春 in early forms.

- **examples:**

    - Jūnduì zài biānjìng tún bīng.

    - Tā zài Sōnghuājiāng biān de yí gè xiǎo tún zhǎngdà.
    - He grew up in a small hamlet by the Songhua River.
  - 朝廷在北地实行屯田(朝廷在北地實行屯田)。
    - Cháotíng zài běidì shíxíng tún tián.
    - The court implemented garrison-farming in the north.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素) → 纯(純) → 屯 → 屮
- **definition:** sprout
- **pinyin:** chè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): a sprout emerging from the ground.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a shoot pushing up through the soil: the horizontal stroke suggests the ground, while the central vertical stroke and side strokes evoke the stem and small leaves. Early oracle and bronze forms closely resemble a budding plant.
- **examples:**
  - 在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽(在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén zhōng, chè biǎoshì zhíwù de nènyá.
    - In oracle script, 屮 represents a plant sprout.
  - 春天雨后，田里冒出许多嫩芽，像“屮”的形状(春天雨後，田裡冒出許多嫩芽，像「屮」的形狀)。
    - Chūntiān yǔ hòu, tián lǐ mào chū xǔduō nènyá, xiàng “chè” de xíngzhuàng.
    - After spring rains, many sprouts emerge in the fields, resembling the shape of “屮”.
  - 屮在古文字中象征嫩芽与新生(屮在古文字中象徵嫩芽與新生)。
    - Chè zài gǔ wénzì zhōng xiàngzhēng nènyá yǔ xīnshēng.
    - In ancient scripts, 屮 symbolizes sprouts and new life.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素) → 纯(純) → 屯 → 一
- **definition:** one; horizontal line
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素) → 素
- **definition:** plain; vegetarian; element; unadorned
- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands; to hold up
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Hands 廾 braiding thread 糸; top resembles 生 without the diagonal stroke (cf. 責, 青).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two hands working with threads, evoking the idea of woven or plain silk. Over time it extended from unadorned silk to senses like plain, simple style and vegetarian, and appears in compounds like 元素 for scientific ‘element’.
- **examples:**
  - 我吃素(我吃素)。
    - Wǒ chī sù.
    - I eat vegetarian / I’m vegetarian.
  - 她喜欢素色的衣服(她喜歡素色的衣服)。
    - Tā xǐhuan sùsè de yīfu.
    - She likes plain-colored clothes.
  - 化学里(化學裡)，氢是最轻的元素(氫是最輕的元素)。
    - Huàxué lǐ, qīng shì zuì qīng de yuánsù.
    - In chemistry, hydrogen is the lightest element.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素) → 素 → 廾
- **definition:** two hands; to hold up
- **pinyin:** gǒng
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - right hand; again
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 𠂇 (“left hand”) + 又 (“right hand”) — both hands; also written 𠬞.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the left and right hands to depict a pair of hands acting together, hence the sense of lifting or offering with both hands. Its form later served as a common component in characters related to holding or presenting.
- **examples:**
  - 在古文字中，廾表示两只手一起举起东西(在古文字中，廾表示兩隻手一起舉起東西)。
    - Zài gǔ wénzì zhōng, gǒng biǎoshì liǎng zhī shǒu yīqǐ jǔqǐ dōngxī.
    - In ancient script, 廾 signifies two hands lifting something together.
  - 作为部件，它常见于表示双手奉献或合举的字，如奉、奏(作為部件，它常見於表示雙手奉獻或合舉的字，如奉、奏)。
    - Zuòwéi bùjiàn, tā chángjiàn yú biǎoshì shuāngshǒu fèngxiàn huò héjǔ de zì, rú Fèng, Zòu.
    - As a component, it appears in characters that depict offering or jointly lifting with two hands, such as 奉 and 奏.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 纯素(純素) → 素 → 廾 → 又
- **definition:** right hand; again
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 纯素食者(純素食者) → 食者
- **definition:** eater; one who eats; food consumer; person (suffix)
- **pinyin:** shí zhě
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word (verb + nominalizer)
  - **description:** 食 (eat/food) + 者 (nominalizer 'one who') = eater; person who eats; consumer.
  - **interpretation:** Uses the classical X者 pattern that turns a verb into “one who does X,” so 食者 is literally “one who eats.” In modern contexts it can extend to consumers or beneficiaries in food-related discourse.
- **examples:**
  - 肉食者鄙(肉食者鄙)，未能远谋(未能遠謀)。
    - ròu shí zhě bǐ, wèi néng yuǎn móu.
    - Meat-eaters are petty and cannot plan far ahead.
  - 对食者而言(對食者而言)，成分表越清晰越好(成分表越清晰越好)。
    - duì shí zhě ér yán, chéngfèn biǎo yuè qīngxī yuè hǎo.
    - For eaters/consumers, the clearer the ingredient list, the better.
  - 这款产品的主要食者是儿童(這款產品的主要食者是兒童)。
    - zhè kuǎn chǎnpǐn de zhǔyào shí zhě shì értóng.
    - The primary eaters of this product are children.
