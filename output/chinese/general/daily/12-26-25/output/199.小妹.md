## little sister; young woman
### xiǎomèi
---
- **definition:** little sister; young woman
- **pinyin:** xiǎomèi
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little
  - 妹
    - mèi
    - younger sister
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小 (xiǎo, "small; little") combined with 妹 (mèi, "younger sister")
  - **interpretation:** 小妹 refers to a 'little sister' and is also commonly used to refer to a 'young woman.' This term highlights the familial and respectful way young women are sometimes addressed, often in informal or familial settings. In traditional Chinese culture, family relationships and their respective titles hold significant importance in demonstrating respect and hierarchy.
- **examples:**
  - 我的小妹很聪(聰)明。
    - Wǒ de xiǎomèi hěn cōngmíng.
    - My little sister is very smart.
  - 昨天我带(帶)小妹去公园(園)，她玩得很开(開)心。
    - Zuótiān wǒ dài xiǎomèi qù gōngyuán, tā wán dé hěn kāixīn.
    - Yesterday, I took my little sister to the park, and she had a lot of fun.
### 小妹 → 小
- **definition:** small; little
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three small dots arranged symmetrically to represent something small
  - **interpretation:** The character 小(xiǎo, 'small') uses three small strokes to symbolize size or quantity, visually capturing the essence of 'smallness'.
- **examples:**
  - 这个(這個)小猫(貓)很可爱(愛)。
    - Zhège xiǎo māo hěn kě'ài.
    - This small cat is very cute.
  - 这(這)座小桥横(橋橫)跨河流，连(連)接两(兩)岸。
    - Zhè zuò xiǎo qiáo héngkuà héliú, liánjiē liǎng àn.
    - This small bridge spans the river, connecting both banks.
  - 请给(請給)我一小杯水。
    - Qǐng gěi wǒ yī xiǎo bēi shuǐ.
    - Please give me a small cup of water.
### 小妹 → 妹
- **definition:** younger sister
- **pinyin:** mèi
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - female, woman
  - 未
    - mèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 女 (nǚ, "female, woman") + phonetic 未 (mèi) ->
    - female sibling ->
    - younger sister
  - **interpretation:** 妹 (mèi, "younger sister") combines 女 (nǚ, "female, woman") to indicate gender, with 未 (mèi) as a phonetic component to guide pronunciation. This reflects the traditional familial structure where siblings are often distinguished by relative age.
- **examples:**
  - 我有妹妹。
    - Wǒ yǒu mèimei.
    - I have a younger sister.
  - 昨天见(見)到妹妹，她笑得很开(開)心。
    - Zuótiān jiàndào mèimei, tā xiào dé hěn kāixīn.
    - I saw my younger sister yesterday, and she was smiling happily.
  - 我的妹妹喜欢画画(歡畫畫)，她梦(夢)想成为艺术(為藝術)家。
    - Wǒ de mèimei xǐhuān huàhuà, tā mèngxiǎng chéngwéi yìshùjiā.
    - My younger sister loves painting, and she dreams of becoming an artist.
### 小妹 → 妹 → 女
- **definition:** female, woman
- **pinyin:** nǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a woman kneeling or standing ->
    - enclosed area as remnant of right breast
  - **interpretation:** The character 女(nǚ, "woman") is a pictogram originally depicting a woman with highlighted bodily features, such as the breasts, symbolizing femininity. Its orientation changed from the Oracle Bone script to face right, simplifying over time while still retaining the essence of its original depiction.
- **examples:**
  - 那位才华横(華橫)溢的女，赢(贏)得了全场(場)注目。
    - Nà wèi cáihuá héngyì de nǚ, yíng dé le quánchǎng zhùmù.
    - That talented woman won everyone's attention.
  - 古时候‘女’常被用来象征温柔(古時候‘女’常被用來象徵溫柔)，而如今它代表独(獨)立自信。
    - Gǔshíhòu ‘nǚ’ cháng bèi yòng lái xiàngzhēng wēnróu, ér rújīn tā dàibiǎo dúlì zìxìn.
    - In ancient times, the character '女' was often used to symbolize gentleness, whereas today it represents independence and confidence.
### 小妹 → 妹 → 未
- **pinyin:** mèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tree with vertical growth not yet reaching full maturity.
  - **interpretation:** The character 未(mèi) represents a tree that has not yet reached its full height, symbolizing the concept of 'not yet' or 'not yet fulfilled'. This pictorial representation highlights the state of incompleteness or being unfinished.
- **examples:**
  - 他未到家。
    - Tā mèi dào jiā.
    - He has not arrived home.
  - 我未看见(見)他，也未听(聽)到消息。
    - Wǒ mèi kànjiàn tā, yě mèi tīngdào xiāoxī.
    - I did not see him, nor did I hear any news.
  - 他未明白问题(問題)。
    - Tā mèi míngbái wèntí.
    - He did not understand the problem.
