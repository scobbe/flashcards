### be in a daze
---
- **pinyin:** be in a daze
- **components:**
  - 发(發)
    - fā
    - to emit; to send out; to develop; to occur
  - 呆(喏)
    - dāi
    - dazed; foolish; to stay; to remain
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 发(發) (fā, 'to emit; to send out') + 呆(dāi, 'dazed; foolish') — to emit a dazed expression
  - **interpretation:** This combination describes a state where one emits a blank expression, often due to distraction or contemplation. It captures the essence of being mentally absent or lost in thought.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天在会上发呆(我今天在會上發呆)，什么都没有听到。
    - Wǒ jīntiān zài huì shàng fā dāi, shénme dōu méiyǒu tīng dào.
    - I was in a daze at the meeting today, not hearing anything.
  - 他常常发呆(他常常發呆)，总是走神。
    - Tā chángcháng fā dāi, zǒng shì zǒu shén.
    - He often zones out, always lost in thought.
