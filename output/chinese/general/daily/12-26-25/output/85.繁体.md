## traditional Chinese characters
### fántǐ
---
- **traditional:** 繁體
- **definition:** traditional Chinese characters
- **pinyin:** fántǐ
- **components:**
  - 繁
    - fán
    - complex; numerous
  - 体(體)
    - tǐ
    - body; style; form
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 繁 (fán, "complex or numerous") + 體 (tǐ, "body; style") =
    - style of complex characters, referring to traditional Chinese characters
  - **interpretation:** 繁體 (fántǐ, "traditional characters") refers to the traditional form of Chinese characters, which are often more complex in structure than simplified characters. These characters preserve more of the original strokes and forms that have been used historically across Chinese literature and documents.
  - **simplification:** 繁体→繁體
- **examples:**
  - 很多人认为(認為)繁体(體)更有韵(韻)味。
    - Hěn duō rén rènwéi fántǐ gèng yǒu yùnwèi.
    - Many people think that traditional Chinese characters have more charm.
  - 书(書)法作品常采(採)用繁体书写(體書寫)。
    - Shūfǎ zuòpǐn cháng cǎiyòng fántǐ shūxiě.
    - Calligraphy works are often written in traditional Chinese characters.
  - 老一辈(輩)喜爱(愛)繁体(體)字。
    - Lǎo yī bèi xǐ'ài fántǐ zì.
    - The older generation loves traditional Chinese characters.
