## 免费(免費)
---
## 
### free of charge
---
- **pinyin:** free of charge
- **components:**
  - 免
    - miǎn
    - exempt; avoid; lighten; free
  - 費
    - fèi
    - cost; fee; expenses; waste
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 免(免) (miǎn, "exempt") + 費(費) (fèi, "cost") — exempt from cost
  - **interpretation:** This term means that something is provided without any cost. It combines the idea of being exempt from payment with the concept of an expense, making it clear that no financial charge is involved.
- **examples:**
  - 这个活动是免费的(這個活動是免費的)。
    - Zhège huódòng shì miǎnfèi de.
    - This activity is free of charge.
  - 他们提供免费的咨询服务(他們提供免費的諮詢服務)。
    - Tāmen tígōng miǎnfèi de zīxún fúwù.
    - They offer free consultation services.
### 免费(免費) → 免(免)
- **definition:** exempt; avoid; lighten; free
- **pinyin:** miǎn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Originally depicted an animal with a large horn, suggesting a sense of freedom or exemption from obligation.
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a sense of being free or exempt, evoking the idea of avoiding burdens or responsibilities.
- **examples:**
  - 我想免受处罚(我想免受處罰)。
    - wǒ xiǎng miǎn shòu chǔfá.
    - I want to be exempt from punishment.
  - 你可以免去这个任务(你可以免去這個任務)。
    - nǐ kěyǐ miǎn qù zhège rènwu.
    - You can avoid this task.
### 免费(免費) → 費(費)
- **definition:** cost; fee; expenses; waste
- **pinyin:** fèi
- **components:**
  - 弗
    - fú
    - not
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Combination of the phonetic component 弗 (fú, 'not') and the semantic component 貝 (bèi, 'cowry'). Cowry shells were historically used as currency.
  - **interpretation:** This character intuitively connects to the concept of costs and expenses, as cowry shells represent money and trade. The phonetic element suggests a negation, which could link to the idea of loss or expenditure.
- **examples:**
  - 这件衣服的费用很高(這件衣服的費用很高)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfú de fèiyòng hěn gāo.
    - The cost of this clothing is very high.
  - 我们需要考虑这些费用(我們需要考慮這些費用)。
    - Wǒmen xūyào kǎolǜ zhèxiē fèiyòng.
    - We need to consider these expenses.
  - 他每个月都花很多钱(他每個月都花很多錢)。
    - Tā měi gè yuè dōu huā hěn duō qián.
    - He spends a lot of money every month.
### 免费(免費) → 費(費) → 弗(弗)
- **definition:** not
- **pinyin:** fú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character originally signifies the concept of negation or refusal, often used in the context of 'not' or 'to refuse'.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the idea of denying or not accepting something, connecting directly to its meaning of 'not' or 'to refuse'.
- **examples:**
  - 他不来(他不來)，我感到失望(我感到失望)。
    - tā bù lái, wǒ gǎndào shīwàng.
    - He is not coming, I feel disappointed.
  - 我拒绝(我拒絕)这个提议(這個提議)。
    - wǒ jùjué zhège tíyì.
    - I refuse this proposal.
### 免费(免費) → 費(費) → 貝(貝)
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cowrie shell which was used as currency in ancient China.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a cowrie shell, linking it directly to its historical use as money. This makes it intuitive as currency symbolizing wealth and trade.
- **examples:**
  - 我用貝(我用贝)买东西。
    - Wǒ yòng bèi mǎi dōngxi.
    - I use cowries to buy things.
  - 古代人用貝(古代人用贝)交易。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi jiāoyì.
    - Ancient people used cowries for trade.
