## free of charge
### miǎnfèi
---
- **definition:** free of charge
- **pinyin:** miǎnfèi
- **components:**
  - 免
    - miǎn
    - exempt; dismiss; avoid; release
  - 费(費)
    - fèi
    - fee; expense; cost; spend
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 免(免) (miǎn, "exempt") and 费(費) (fèi, "fee") combined mean 'exempt from fee', indicating something is free of charge.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 免(免) (miǎn, "exempt") and 费(費) (fèi, "fee") clearly indicates the concept of not having to pay a fee. In contexts such as promotions or services, 免费(免費) (miǎnfèi) effectively communicates that there is no cost involved.
- **examples:**
  - 这家店提供免费饮料(這家店提供免費飲料)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn tígōng miǎnfèi yǐnliào.
    - This store offers free drinks.
  - 网上购物时(網上購物時)，运费是免费的(運費是免費的)。
    - Wǎng shàng gòuwù shí, yùn fèi shì miǎnfèi de.
    - When shopping online, shipping is free of charge.
  - 参加讲座能享受免费咨询(參加講座能享受免費諮詢)。
    - Cānjiā jiǎngzuò néng xiǎngshòu miǎnfèi zīxún.
    - Attending the lecture allows you to enjoy free consultation.
### 免费(免費) → 免(免)
- **definition:** exempt; dismiss; avoid; release
- **pinyin:** miǎn
- **components:**
  - 冕
    - miǎn
    - crown
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a man wearing a ceremonial hat ->
    - borrowed for abstract meanings
  - **interpretation:** The character originally depicted a ceremonial figure, emphasizing authority. Over time, it was borrowed for abstract concepts like 'exempt' or 'dismiss.' The derivative 冕(冕) (miǎn, 'crown') retains the original meaning related to ceremonial apparel.
- **examples:**
  - 学生们免缴学费(學生們免繳學費)。
    - Xuéshēngmen miǎn jiǎo xuéfèi.
    - Students are exempt from paying tuition.
  - 因工作繁忙(因工作繁忙)，他被免去部分职责(他被免去部分職責)。
    - Yīn gōngzuò fánmáng, tā bèi miǎn qù bùfen zhízé.
    - Due to his busy work schedule, he was dismissed from some of his duties.
  - 为了节省开支(為了節省開支)，公司决定免发年终奖(公司決定免發年終獎)。
    - Wèile jiéshěng kāizhī, gōngsī juédìng miǎn fā niánzhōng jiǎng.
    - In order to save expenses, the company decided to avoid giving out the year-end bonus.
### 免费(免費) → 免(免) → 冕(冕)
- **definition:** crown
- **pinyin:** miǎn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a ceremonial hat with a flat top and broad brim ->
    - symbolic of authority and rank
  - **interpretation:** The character 冕(miǎn, 'ceremonial hat') illustrates the structure and appearance of a traditional ceremonial hat, which served as a symbol of authority and rank in ancient China.
- **examples:**
  - 皇帝头戴冕(皇帝頭戴冕)。
    - Huángdì tóu dài miǎn.
    - The emperor wears a ceremonial hat.
  - 古时宫廷礼仪森严(古時宮廷禮儀森嚴)，官员参加登基典礼时必须佩冕(官員參加登基典禮時必須佩冕)。
    - Gǔshí gōngtíng lǐyí sēn yán, guānyuán cānjiā dēngjī diǎnlǐ shí bìxū pèi miǎn.
    - In ancient times, when palace rituals were strict, officials had to wear the hat during enthronement ceremonies.
  - 学者研读史书时常提及帝王冕(學者研讀史書時常提及帝王冕)。
    - Xuézhě yándú shǐshū shícháng tíjí dìwáng miǎn.
    - Scholars often refer to the imperial hat when studying historical texts.
### 免费(免費) → 费(費)
- **definition:** fee; expense; cost; spend
- **pinyin:** fèi
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - shell/money
  - 弗
    - fú
    - not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 貝(貝) (bèi, 'shell/money') + 弗(弗) (fú, 'not') =
    - related to payments and expenses ->
    - fee
  - **interpretation:** 費(費) (fèi) represents matters related to expenses by combining 貝(貝) (bèi, 'shell/money'), which symbolizes wealth or money, with 弗(弗) (fú, 'not'), serving as the phonetic component. Historically, shells were used as currency, linking the character to financial transactions.
  - **simplification:** 費(費)→费(費): 貝(貝) (bèi, 'shell/money') was simplified by removing the inner strokes for a more streamlined form.
- **examples:**
  - 我花了很多钱(我花了很多錢)，支付学费(支付學費)。
    - Wǒ huā le hěnduō qián, zhīfù xuéfèi.
    - I spent a lot of money to pay tuition fees.
  - 公司为新项目支出了高额费用(公司為新項目支出了高額費用)。
    - Gōngsī wèi xīn xiàngmù zhīchū le gāo’é fèiyòng.
    - The company incurred high expenses for the new project.
### 免费(免費) → 费(費) → 貝(貝)
- **definition:** shell/money
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cowry shell, which was used as currency in ancient China.
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") visually represents a cowry shell, a valuable item used in trade and as currency in ancient Chinese societies.
  - **simplification:** 贝(貝) is the simplified form, derived from a cursive script version of 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry"), maintaining the visual representation while making the character faster to write.
- **examples:**
  - 古代人用貝(古代人用貝)作为货币(作為貨幣)，也用来装饰(也用來裝飾)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì, yě yòng lái zhuāngshì.
    - Ancient people used cowrie shells as currency and also for decoration.
  - 一些古文献记载了貝(一些古文獻記載了貝)的神秘价值(的神秘價值)，激发了人们对古代文化的兴趣(激發了人們對古代文化的興趣)。
    - Yīxiē gǔ wénxiàn jìzǎi le bèi de shénmì jiàzhí, jīfā le rénmen duì gǔdài wénhuà de xìngqù.
    - Some ancient texts recorded the mysterious value of cowrie shells, sparking people’s interest in ancient culture.
  - 海边曾有传说(海邊曾有傳說)，珊瑚和貝点缀着沙滩的美丽(珊瑚和貝點綴著沙灘的美麗)。
    - Hǎibiān céng yǒu chuánshuō, shānhú hé bèi diǎnzuì zhe shātān de měilì.
    - There was a legend by the seaside that corals and cowrie shells adorned the beauty of the beach.
### 免费(免費) → 费(費) → 弗(弗)
- **definition:** not
- **pinyin:** fú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a rope wrapped around two pieces of wood ->
    - later used phonetically for 'no'
  - **interpretation:** The original pictographic representation of tied wood reflects its ancient usage. Over time, the symbol was repurposed for its phonetic value to mean 'no'.
- **examples:**
  - 古代文献中常用‘弗’表示否定(古代文獻中常用‘弗’表示否定)。
    - Gǔdài wénxiàn zhōng cháng yòng 'Fú' biǎoshì fǒudìng.
    - In ancient texts, '弗' is commonly used to indicate negation.
  - 在《史记》中(在《史記》中)，司马迁巧妙运用‘弗’来突显人物性格(司馬遷巧妙運用‘弗’來突顯人物性格)。
    - Zài 'Shǐjì' zhōng, Sīmǎqiān qiǎomiào yùnyòng 'Fú' lái tūxiǎn rénwù xìnggé.
    - In 'Records of the Grand Historian', Sima Qian skillfully uses '弗' to highlight a character's personality.
