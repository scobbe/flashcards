---

### what
---
- **pinyin:** what
- **components:**
  - 什
    - shén
    - what; any; that
  - 么(麼)
    - me
    - suffix used in questions; how; that
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 什(什) (shén, 'what') + 么(麼) (me, 'suffix used in questions') — what
  - **interpretation:** The term is used for inquiring about something. Combining 什(什) (shén, 'what') with 么(麼) (me, 'suffix used in questions') transforms it into a question word.
- **examples:**
  - 你想吃什么(你想吃什麼)？
    - Nǐ xiǎng chī shénme？
    - What do you want to eat?
  - 他在问什么(他在問什麼)？
    - Tā zài wèn shénme？
    - What is he asking?
  - 你说的是什么(你說的是什麼)？
    - Nǐ shuō de shì shénme？
    - What are you talking about?

### 什么(什麼) → 么(麼)
- **definition:** suffix used in questions; how; that
- **pinyin:** me
- **components:**
  - 麻
    - má
    - hemp
  - 幺
    - yāo
    - small, tiny
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Phonetic 麻 (má, 'hemp') combined with semantic 幺 (yāo, 'small, tiny') gives a compound that conveys a sense of smallness in the context of questioning.
  - **interpretation:** The character suggests a diminutive or questioning tone, making it suitable for forming questions or expressing uncertainty.
  - **simplification:** 麼(麼) uses stroke reduction to create a more streamlined version of the traditional character.
- **examples:**
  - 你要什么？(你要什麼？)
    - Nǐ yào shénme？
    - What do you want?
  - 那是什么？(那是什麼？)
    - Nà shì shénme？
    - What is that?
  - 这个怎么做？(這個怎麼做？)
    - Zhège zěnme zuò？
    - How is this done?
