## virtuous; worthy
### xián
---
- **traditional:** 賢
- **definition:** virtuous; worthy
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 臤
    - xián
    - strong
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 貝 (bèi, "cowry") + phonetic: 臤 (xián, associated with "strong") =
    - wealth managed by a capable person ->
    - virtuous
  - **interpretation:** In ancient times, virtue or worthiness was often correlated with the ability to manage wealth or valuable resources effectively. The 貝 (bèi, "cowry") indicates value or resources, while 臤 (xián) provides the pronunciation and hints at strength or capability, reinforcing the idea of a worthy individual.
  - **simplification:** The simplified form 贤(賢) retains the same phonetic component 臤 and semantic component 貝, making it a direct simplification without altering the structural formula or meaning.

    - Zhǎngzhě tuīchóng xiánrén, wǎnbiè zūnjìng xiánzhě.

### 贤(賢) → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身( )符( )，放在口( )袋( )里(裡)。

### 贤(賢) → 臤 → 賢
- **definition:** virtuous
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 貝

- **etymology:**
