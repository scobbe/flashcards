## virtuous; worthy
### xián
---
- **traditional:** 賢
- **definition:** virtuous; worthy
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 臤
    - xián
    - strong
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 貝 (bèi, "cowry") + phonetic: 臤 (xián, associated with "strong") =
    - wealth managed by a capable person ->
    - virtuous
  - **interpretation:** In ancient times, virtue or worthiness was often correlated with the ability to manage wealth or valuable resources effectively. The 貝 (bèi, "cowry") indicates value or resources, while 臤 (xián) provides the pronunciation and hints at strength or capability, reinforcing the idea of a worthy individual.
  - **simplification:** The simplified form 贤(賢) retains the same phonetic component 臤 and semantic component 貝, making it a direct simplification without altering the structural formula or meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他为(為)人贤(賢)良。
    - Tā wéi rén xiánliáng.
    - He is virtuous.
  - 长(長)者推崇贤(賢)人，晚辈(輩)尊敬贤(賢)者。
    - Zhǎngzhě tuīchóng xiánrén, wǎnbiè zūnjìng xiánzhě.
    - Elders esteem the virtuous, and the younger generations respect the wise.
  - 君子常怀贤(懷賢)心。
    - Jūnzǐ cháng huái xiánxīn.
    - A gentleman always harbors a virtuous heart.
### 贤(賢) → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为货币(為貨幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
### 贤(賢) → 臤
- **definition:** strong
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 臣
    - chén
    - minister
  - 賢
    - xián
    - worthy
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 臣 (chén, "minister") + phonetic: 賢 (xián, "worthy") =
    - represents an ancient form with phonetic value from 賢 ->
    - strong
  - **interpretation:** The character 臤(xián, "strong") is an ancient form derived from 賢 (xián, "worthy"), with 臣 (chén, "minister") serving as the semantic component, indicating a quality associated with strength or virtue.
### 贤(賢) → 臤 → 臣
- **definition:** minister
- **pinyin:** chén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a vertical eye of a man looking downwards
  - **interpretation:** The character 臣(chén) originally depicted an eye oriented vertically, symbolizing a person looking downwards, conveying subordination or observance. Over time, it evolved to represent roles such as 'slave,' 'servant,' and 'minister,' emphasizing relationships defined by subservience.
- **examples:**
  - 臣愿为国(願為國)家尽(盡)忠。
    - Chén yuàn wèi guójiā jìnzhōng。
    - I, as a loyal subject, am willing to serve the nation faithfully.
  - 陛下召见(見)臣，臣必不辱使命。
    - Bìxià zhàojiàn chén, chén bì bù rǔ shǐmìng。
    - Your Majesty summons me, and I will not fail in my duty.
  - 昔日臣拜见(見)明君，如今仍怀(懷)赤诚(誠)之心。
    - Xīrì chén bàijiàn míngjūn, rújīn réng huái chìchéng zhī xīn。
    - In the past, I paid my respects to a wise ruler, and even now I remain sincerely devoted.
### 贤(賢) → 臤 → 賢
- **definition:** worthy
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - money
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 臤 + semantic 貝 ('money') ->
    - valuable or worthy like money
  - **interpretation:** The character 賢 (xián, 'worthy') combines 貝 (bèi, 'money') which signifies value, with 臤 as the phonetic component suggesting sound. This illustrates the idea of someone being 'worthy' or 'valuable' akin to 'money,' thus worthy of esteem or respect.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一位賢人。
    - Tā shì yī wèi xián rén.
    - He is a worthy man.
  - 他不仅聪(僅聰)明，而且賢。
    - Tā bùjǐn cōngmíng, érqiě xián.
    - He is not only intelligent, but also worthy.
  - 古时(時)人们(們)以忠诚(誠)兼賢，作为(為)立身之本。
    - Gǔshí rénmen yǐ zhōngchéng jiān xián, zuòwéi lìshēn zhī běn.
    - In ancient times, people regarded loyalty and worthiness as the foundation of one's existence.
