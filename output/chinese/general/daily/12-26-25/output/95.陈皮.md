## dried tangerine peel
### chénpí
---
- **definition:** dried tangerine peel
- **pinyin:** chénpí
- **components:**
  - 陈(陳)
    - chén
    - old; aged; display; state
  - 皮
    - pí
    - peel; skin; hide; fur
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 陳(陳) (chén, "old") refers to something aged or mature, hinting at the process of aging the tangerine peel. 皮(皮) (pí, "peel") means the outer layer of the fruit. Together, 陳皮(陳皮) (chénpí, "dried tangerine peel") denotes tangerine peel that has been aged and dried, used often in traditional Chinese medicine and cooking for its aromatic and medicinal qualities.
  - **interpretation:** 陳皮(陳皮) (chénpí) is significant in Chinese culture for its culinary and medicinal uses, offering aromatic flavor in cooking and believed to aid digestion.
- **examples:**
  - 中医常用陈皮(陳皮)。
    - Zhōngyī cháng yòng chénpí.
    - Traditional Chinese medicine often uses dried tangerine peel.
  - 厨房里陈皮(陳皮)熬成药汤(熬成藥湯)，滋味独特(滋味獨特)。
    - Chúfáng lǐ chénpí áo chéng yàotāng, zīwèi dútè.
    - In the kitchen, dried tangerine peel is simmered into a medicinal soup, offering a unique flavor.
  - 古代医书记载陈皮(陳皮)，功效神奇(功效神奇)。
    - Gǔdài yīshū jìzǎi chénpí, gōngxiào shénqí.
    - Ancient medical texts record dried tangerine peel, which has miraculous effects.
### 陈皮(陳皮) → 陈(陳)
- **definition:** old; aged; display; state
- **pinyin:** chén
- **components:**
  - 阝(阜)
    - fù
    - mound
  - 东(東)
    - dōng
    - east
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 阝(阜) (fù, "mound") + phonetic: 東(東) (dōng, "east") =
    - a place with mounds; display or state in an orderly manner
  - **interpretation:** The character 陈(陳) (chén, "old; aged; display; state") combines the semantic component 阝(阜) (fù, "mound"), suggesting a place or terrain, with the phonetic component 東(東) (dōng, "east"), which contributes to the pronunciation. This combination conveys ideas of arranging or displaying things in an orderly manner, evoking a sense of structure.
  - **simplification:** 陳(陳)→陈(陳): The simplified character retains the phonetic 東(東) (dōng, "east") while using a simplified form of 阝(阜) (fù, "mound"). This change likely reduces strokes while maintaining phonetic cues.
- **examples:**
  - 那块木板显得十分陈旧(那塊木板顯得十分陳舊)。
    - Nà kuài mùbǎn xiǎnde shífēn chénjiù.

- **examples:**

### 陈皮(陳皮) → 陈(陳) → 东(東)
- **definition:** east
- **pinyin:** dōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Originally a pictogram of a bundle; simplified to 东 (dōng, "east") in cursive, mirroring 車 (chē, "vehicle") to 车
  - **interpretation:** The character 東 originally depicted a bundle of objects tied together, visually symbolizing the concept of 'east' through its form. As writing evolved, the character was simplified to 东, following a similar reduction from 車 to 车. This simplification focused on retaining core visual elements while removing complexity.
  - **simplification:** The simplification from 東 to 东 involved a reduction to a cursive shorthand form, parallel to 車 becoming 车.
- **examples:**
  - 太阳从东边升起(太陽從東邊升起)。
    - Tàiyáng cóng dōngbiān shēngqǐ.
    - The sun rises from the east.
  - 风从东面吹来(風從東面吹來)。
    - Fēng cóng dōngmiàn chuī lái.
    - The wind blows from the east.
### 陈皮(陳皮) → 皮(皮)
- **definition:** peel; skin; hide; fur
- **pinyin:** pí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a surface or the skin of an animal
  - **interpretation:** The character 皮(皮) (pí, 'surface') visually represents an animal hide or skin stretched out, emphasizing the idea of an outer covering or surface. It captures the concept of a boundary between the interior and exterior, as seen in animal skins used as material.
- **examples:**
  - 这张桌子的皮很光滑(這張桌子的皮很光滑)。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi de pí hěn guānghuá.
    - The surface of this table is very smooth.
  - 新鞋的皮非常柔软(新鞋的皮非常柔軟)，适合长时间行走(適合長時間行走)。
    - Xīn xié de pí fēicháng róuruǎn, shìhé cháng shíjiān xíngzǒu.
    - The surface of the new shoes is very soft and suitable for long walks.
