### dried tangerine peel
---
- **pinyin:** dried tangerine peel
- **components:**
  - 陈(陳)
    - chén
    - to exhibit; to display; old; ancient; surname
  - 皮
    - pí
    - skin; peel; hide; surface
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 陈(陳) (chén, 'to exhibit; to display') + 皮(皮) (pí, 'skin; peel') — exhibited peel
  - **interpretation:** The combination makes sense as 陈(陳) (chén, 'to exhibit; to display') refers to the traditional usage and processing of the peel, while 皮(pí, 'skin; peel') refers to the outer layer of the tangerine. Thus, it signifies the displayed or processed outer peel of the tangerine in culinary use.
- **examples:**
  - 我常用陈皮(我常用陳皮)煮汤。
    - Wǒ cháng yòng chénpí zhǔ tāng.
    - I often use dried tangerine peel to make soup.
  - 陈皮(陳皮)有助于消化。
    - Chénpí yǒu zhùyú xiāohuà.
    - Dried tangerine peel helps with digestion.
### 陈皮(陳皮) → 陈(陳)
- **definition:** to exhibit; to display; old; ancient; surname
- **pinyin:** chén
- **components:**
  - 阝
    - fǔ
    - hill
  - 辰
    - chén
    - time
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Comprises the semantic element 阝 (a hill) and the phonetic element 辰 (chén, 'time').
  - **interpretation:** A hill represents a place or a way of showcasing something, and 'time' suggests it's a display of something from the past.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by replacing the component 陳 (chén) with 陈 (chén), which has fewer strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 他在展览会上展示了许多古董(他在展覽會上展示了許多古董)。
    - Tā zài zhǎnlǎn huì shàng zhǎnshìle xǔduō gǔdǒng。
    - He exhibited many antiques at the exhibition.
  - 这件作品表现出古代文化(這件作品表現出古代文化)。
    - Zhè jiàn zuòpǐn biǎoxiàn chū gǔdài wénhuà。
    - This piece of work displays ancient culture.
  - 他的姓是陈(他的姓是陳)。
    - Tā de xìng shì Chén。
    - His surname is Chen.

- **definition:** skin; peel; hide; surface
- **pinyin:** pí
- **etymology:**

  - **description:** Depicts a surface or hide, resembling a thin layer covering something.
  - **interpretation:** The character represents the idea of skin or hide, as the pictograph captures the notion of a surface layer that covers an object.
- **examples:**
