## dried tangerine peel
### chénpí
---
- **definition:** dried tangerine peel
- **pinyin:** chénpí
- **components:**
  - 陈(陳)
    - chén
    - aged; exhibit
  - 皮
    - pí
    - peel; skin
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 陳(陳) (chén, 'aged; exhibit') + 皮(皮) (pí, 'peel; skin') =
    - aged peel ->
    - dried tangerine peel
  - **interpretation:** 陳(陳) (chén, 'aged; exhibit') suggests the peel has been aged, which is typical in traditional Chinese medicine to enhance its properties, while 皮(皮) (pí, 'peel; skin') directly refers to the outer layer of the tangerine.
- **examples:**
  - 厨师加陈皮(廚師加陳皮)，以增加香味(以增加香味)。
    - Chúshī jiā chénpí, yǐ zēngjiā xiāngwèi.
    - The chef adds dried tangerine peel to enhance the aroma.
  - 中医常用陈皮(中醫常用陳皮)，以调理脾胃(以調理脾胃)。
    - Zhōngyī cháng yòng chénpí, yǐ tiáolǐ píwèi.
    - Traditional Chinese medicine often uses dried tangerine peel to regulate the spleen and stomach.
