## dried tangerine peel
### chénpí
---
- **traditional:** 陳皮
- **definition:** dried tangerine peel
- **pinyin:** chénpí
- **components:**
  - 陈(陳)
    - chén
    - old; aged; display; state
  - 皮
    - pí
    - peel; skin; hide; fur
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 陳 (chén, "old") refers to something aged or mature, hinting at the process of aging the tangerine peel. 皮 (pí, "peel") means the outer layer of the fruit. Together, 陳皮 (chénpí, "dried tangerine peel") denotes tangerine peel that has been aged and dried, used often in traditional Chinese medicine and cooking for its aromatic and medicinal qualities.
  - **interpretation:** 陳皮 (chénpí) is significant in Chinese culture for its culinary and medicinal uses, offering aromatic flavor in cooking and believed to aid digestion.
- **examples:**

### 陈(陳)皮( ) → 陈(陳) → 东(東)
- **traditional:** 東
- **definition:** east
- **pinyin:** dōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Originally a pictogram of a bundle; simplified to 东 (dōng, "east") in cursive, mirroring 車 (chē, "vehicle") to 车
  - **interpretation:** The character 東 originally depicted a bundle of objects tied together, visually symbolizing the concept of 'east' through its form. As writing evolved, the character was simplified to 东, following a similar reduction from 車 to 车. This simplification focused on retaining core visual elements while removing complexity.
  - **simplification:** The simplification from 東 to 东 involved a reduction to a cursive shorthand form, parallel to 車 becoming 车.
- **examples:**
