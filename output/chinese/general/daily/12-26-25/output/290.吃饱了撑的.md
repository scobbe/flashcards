### have nothing better to do (lit. stuffed from eating)
---
- **pinyin:** have nothing better to do (lit. stuffed from eating)
- **components:**
  - 吃
    - chī
    - eat; consume; dine; devour
  - 饱(飽)
    - bǎo
    - full; satiated; filled; satisfied
  - 了
    - le
    - completed action particle; softens tone; change of state
  - 撑(撐)
    - chēng
    - to swell; to be stuffed; to hold up; to support
  - 的
    - de
    - possessive particle; indicates modification
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 吃(吃) (chī, "eat") + 饱(飽) (bǎo, "full") + 了(了) (le, "completed action particle") + 撑(撐) (chēng, "to swell; to be stuffed") + 的(的) (de, "possessive particle") — having eaten to fullness and being stuffed
  - **interpretation:** This phrase humorously describes a state of being overly full from eating. It conveys the idea of having nothing better to do after indulging in food.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天吃饱了撑的，没事做。
    - Wǒ jīntiān chī bǎo le chēng de, méi shì zuò.
    - I have nothing better to do today after eating to fullness.
  - 吃饱了撑的，真是无聊。
    - Chī bǎo le chēng de, zhēn shì wúliáo.
    - Being stuffed from eating is really boring.
