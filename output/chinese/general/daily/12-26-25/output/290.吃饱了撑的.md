## 吃饱了撑的(吃飽了撐的)
---
## have nothing better to do (lit. stuffed from eating)
### chī bǎo le chēng de
---
- **definition:** have nothing better to do (lit. stuffed from eating)
- **pinyin:** chī bǎo le chēng de
- **components:**
  - 吃饱了(吃飽了)
    - chī bǎo le
    - ate to fullness; full; finished eating; satiated
  - 撑的(撐的)
    - chēng de
    - so stuffed; overfull; to the point of bursting; (colloq.) idle and looking for trouble
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** colloquial idiom
  - **description:** 吃饱了(ate one’s fill) + 撑的(so stuffed that ... ) = mocking someone as idle and doing pointless things.
  - **interpretation:** Used snidely to say someone is messing around because they’re too idle, as if overstuffed after a meal. Common in scolding like 你这是吃饱了撑的吧.
  - **simplification:** 飽→饱 compresses 食 to 饣 and keeps phonetic 包; 撐→撑 uses 扌 + 争 (simplified 爭), both reducing strokes for high-frequency colloquial forms.
- **examples:**
  - 你这是吃饱了撑的吧(你這是吃飽了撐的吧)。
    - Nǐ zhè shì chī bǎo le chēng de ba.
    - Are you just doing this because you have nothing better to do?
  - 他闲得没事(他閒得沒事)，真是吃饱了撑的(真是吃飽了撐的)。
    - Tā xián de méi shì, zhēn shì chī bǎo le chēng de.
    - He’s so idle—clearly has nothing better to do.
  - 别在这儿瞎折腾(別在這兒瞎折騰)，吃饱了撑的才这样(吃飽了撐的才這樣)。
    - Bié zài zhèr xiā zhēteng, chī bǎo le chēng de cái zhèyàng.
    - Don’t fool around here; only someone with nothing better to do acts like this.
### 吃饱了撑的(吃飽了撐的) → 吃饱了(吃飽了)
- **definition:** ate to fullness; full; finished eating; satiated
- **pinyin:** chī bǎo le
- **components:**
  - 吃饱(吃飽)
    - chī bǎo
    - eat to fullness; be full; have eaten enough
  - 了
    - le
    - completed action; change of state; perfective particle; modal particle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** verb–complement phrase + aspect particle
  - **description:** 吃饱 + 了 = completed state of being full after eating.
  - **interpretation:** States that one has eaten enough and is now full; 了 marks completion or a new resultant state. In context it can also mean simply "finished eating."
  - **simplification:** 飽 → 饱 by simplifying the food radical 飠 to 饣 to reduce strokes; 吃 and 了 are unchanged. This follows the regular radical simplification pattern in simplified Chinese.
- **examples:**
  - 我吃饱了(我吃飽了)，先休息一下(先休息一下)。
    - Wǒ chī bǎo le, xiān xiūxi yíxià.
    - I’m full; I’ll rest a bit first.
  - 孩子吃饱了(孩子吃飽了)，就去玩了(就去玩了)。
    - Háizi chī bǎo le, jiù qù wán le.
    - The child ate to fullness and then went to play.
  - 我们已经吃饱了(我們已經吃飽了)，不用再点菜了(不用再點菜了)。
    - Wǒmen yǐjīng chī bǎo le, bùyòng zài diǎn cài le.
    - We’ve already eaten our fill; no need to order more dishes.
### 吃饱了撑的(吃飽了撐的) → 吃饱了(吃飽了) → 吃饱(吃飽)
- **definition:** eat to fullness; be full; have eaten enough
- **pinyin:** chī bǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** resultative compound
  - **description:** 吃(eat) + 饱(full) = eat to fullness; be full.
  - **interpretation:** Indicates the action of eating has reached the result of being full, i.e., having eaten enough. Common in patterns like 吃饱了/没吃饱 to state completion or lack thereof.
  - **simplification:** 飽 → 饱 by simplifying the 飠/食 radical to 饣; 吃 is unchanged. Standard radical reduction in Simplified Chinese.
- **examples:**
  - 我已经吃饱了(我已經吃飽了)。
    - Wǒ yǐjīng chī bǎo le.
    - I’m already full.
  - 中午没吃饱(中午沒吃飽)，下午很快又饿了(下午很快又餓了)。
    - Zhōngwǔ méi chī bǎo, xiàwǔ hěn kuài yòu è le.
    - I didn’t eat enough at noon, and I got hungry again in the afternoon.
  - 他吃饱了就去散步(他吃飽了就去散步)。
    - Tā chī bǎo le jiù qù sànbù.
    - After he ate his fill, he went for a walk.
### 吃饱了撑的(吃飽了撐的) → 吃饱了(吃飽了) → 了
- **definition:** completed action; change of state; perfective particle; modal particle
- **pinyin:** le
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: baby without arms; Shuowen says “crossing of lower legs,” Shuowen Jiezi Zhu says entangled legs; “finish” likely a sound loan; unrelated to 亨.
  - **interpretation:** Earliest graphs depict an infant with crossed or tangled legs, a simple pictorial form later repurposed in use. Verbal senses like “to finish/understand/be able to” developed via sound loan, while the original image survives only in historical explanations.
- **examples:**
  - 我终于了结了这件事(我終於了結了這件事)。
    - Wǒ zhōngyú liǎojié le zhè jiàn shì.
    - I finally settled this matter.
  - 他对历史很了解(他對歷史很了解)。
    - Tā duì lìshǐ hěn liǎojiě.
    - He understands history well.
  - 我们解决不了这个问题(我們解決不了這個問題)。
    - Wǒmen jiějué bùliǎo zhège wèntí.
    - We can’t solve this problem.
### 吃饱了撑的(吃飽了撐的) → 撑的(撐的)
- **definition:** so stuffed; overfull; to the point of bursting; (colloq.) idle and looking for trouble
- **pinyin:** chēng de
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** verb + structural particle
  - **description:** 撑(撐, to be overfull; to prop up) + 的(structural particle) = state/adjectival phrase meaning 'so stuffed/overfull,' also implying 'idle and itching for trouble.'
  - **interpretation:** Colloquial state phrase where 的 turns 撑 into a descriptive condition; often written as 撑得 when marking degree/complement. Figuratively suggests being so full and idle that one looks for trouble.
  - **simplification:** 撐 → 撑 by simplifying component 爭 → 争 under the hand radical; 的 unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我午饭吃多了，肚子撑的难受(我午飯吃多了，肚子撐的難受)。
    - Wǒ wǔfàn chī duō le, dùzi chēng de nánshòu.
    - I ate too much at lunch; my stomach feels uncomfortably full.
  - 你这是撑的没事干，非要去惹事(你這是撐的沒事幹，非要去惹事)。
    - Nǐ zhè shì chēng de méi shì gàn, fēi yào qù rěshì.
    - You're so idle from being overfull that you’re looking for trouble.
  - 包子别再塞了，袋子都撑的鼓鼓的了(包子別再塞了，袋子都撐的鼓鼓的了)。
    - Bāozi bié zài sāi le, dàizi dōu chēng de gǔgǔ de le.
    - Stop stuffing buns in; the bag is already stretched full.
