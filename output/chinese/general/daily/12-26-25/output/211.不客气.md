## you’re welcome; don’t mention it
### bú kèqi
---
- **definition:** you’re welcome; don’t mention it
- **pinyin:** bú kèqi
- **components:**
  - 不
    - bù
    - not; no; un-; non-
  - 客气(客氣)
    - kèqi
    - polite; courteous; to stand on ceremony; formal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** set phrase
  - **description:** 不 + 客气 = not be (overly) polite/formal.
  - **interpretation:** Literally ‘don’t be polite,’ it downplays thanks as “you’re welcome.” In broader use it tells someone not to stand on ceremony and to speak or act freely.
  - **simplification:** 氣 → 气 under character simplification to reduce strokes; 客氣 → 客气, 不 unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 谢谢你(謝謝你)，不客气(不客氣)。
    - Xièxie nǐ, bú kèqi.
    - Thank you. You’re welcome.
  - 我们都是朋友(我們都是朋友)，有什么需要就说(有什麼需要就說)，不客气一点(不客氣一點)。
    - Wǒmen dōu shì péngyou, yǒu shénme xūyào jiù shuō, bú kèqi yìdiǎn.
    - We’re all friends—say what you need and don’t be too polite.
  - 他帮了我一个小忙(他幫了我一個小忙)，我道谢后他说不客气(我道謝後他說不客氣)。
    - Tā bāng le wǒ yí ge xiǎo máng, wǒ dàoxiè hòu tā shuō bú kèqi.
    - He did me a small favor; after I thanked him, he said “you’re welcome.”
### 不客气(不客氣) → 不
- **definition:** not; no; un-; non-
- **pinyin:** bù
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: calyx of a flower; negation sense back-formed from 否 (口 + 不); also interpreted as a bird flying toward the sky (一).
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawing of a flower’s calyx, the graph later acquired the meaning ‘not’ when it participated in the formation of 否 (“mouth” + 不) for negation, and that sense was transferred back to 不. The botanical meaning was reassigned to 柎, while some traditional scholars reimagined the form as a bird flying upward toward the sky line to evoke the idea of negation.
- **examples:**
  - 我不喜欢咖啡(我不喜歡咖啡)。
    - Wǒ bù xǐhuan kāfēi.
    - I don’t like coffee.
  - 今天不去学校(今天不去學校)。
    - Jīntiān bù qù xuéxiào.
    - I’m not going to school today.
  - 这不容易(這不容易)。
    - Zhè bù róngyì.
    - This is not easy.
### 不客气(不客氣) → 不 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 不客气(不客氣) → 客气(客氣)
- **definition:** polite; courteous; to stand on ceremony; formal
- **pinyin:** kèqi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 客 ‘guest’ + 气 ‘air; manner’ ⇒ ‘guest-like manner’ = polite, ceremonious; stand on ceremony.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the idea of a guest with one’s bearing to describe courteous, restrained behavior. Often appears in the set phrase 别客气 to tell someone not to be overly formal.
  - **simplification:** 氣 → 气: simplified by adopting an ancient/abbreviated form that evokes ‘air/breath’ and reduces strokes; 客 unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 别客气，请随便坐(別客氣，請隨便坐)。
    - Bié kèqi, qǐng suíbiàn zuò.
    - Don’t be formal—please make yourself at home.
  - 他对客人很客气，说话很有礼貌(他對客人很客氣，說話很有禮貌)。
    - Tā duì kèrén hěn kèqi, shuōhuà hěn yǒu lǐmào.
    - He is very polite to guests and speaks courteously.
  - 老板对新同事一点也不客气，直接指出问题(老闆對新同事一點也不客氣，直接指出問題)。
    - Lǎobǎn duì xīn tóngshì yìdiǎn yě bù kèqi, zhíjiē zhǐchū wèntí.
    - The boss wasn’t polite to the new colleague and pointed out problems directly.

### 不客气(不客氣) → 客气(客氣) → 气(氣)
- **definition:** 
- **pinyin:** 
- **components:**
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: clouds flowing through the sky; traditional 氣 is phono-semantic: semantic 米 (“rice”) + phonetic 气.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawing of vapor drifting across the sky, capturing the idea of unseen flow and movement. - **examples:**

- **definition:** rice
- **pinyin:** mǐ
- **etymology:**
