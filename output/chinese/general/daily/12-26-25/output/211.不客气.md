## you’re welcome; don’t mention it
### bú kèqi
---
- **traditional:** 不客氣
- **definition:** you’re welcome; don’t mention it
- **pinyin:** bú kèqi
- **components:**
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
  - 客
    - kè
    - guest
  - 气(氣)
    - qì
    - manner; air
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 不 (bù, "not") + 客 (kè, "guest") + 气(氣) (qì, "manner; air") =
    - "you're welcome" or "don't mention it"
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 不客气(氣) (bú kèqì, "you're welcome") is used as a polite response to 'thank you,' emphasizing that no formalities or special courtesy (客气) is necessary. Originally, the characters 客 (kè, "guest") and 气(氣) (qì, "manner") together suggest the idea of showing etiquette or hospitality as one would to a guest. By adding 不 (bù, "not"), it conveys the relaxed, friendly sense that there's no need for the person to feel like a guest engaging in formalities.
- **examples:**
  - 谢谢(謝謝)你的帮(幫)助，不客气(氣)。
    - Xièxiè nǐ de bāngzhù，bú kèqi。
    - Thank you for your help, you're welcome.
  - 有困难时(難時)，你可以说(說)不客气(氣)。
    - Yǒu kùnnán shí，nǐ kěyǐ shuō bú kèqi。
    - When in difficulty, you can say 'you're welcome'.

  - **interpretation:** The character 不(bù, "not") started as a pictographic representation of a calyx or incomplete form, symbolizing the idea of negation or absence.

    - Tiānqì bù hǎo, wǒmen bù chūmén.

### 不( )客( )气(氣) → 客
- **definition:** guest
- **pinyin:** kè
- **components:**
  - 宀
    - mián
    - roof
  - 各
    - gè
    - each
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 宀 (mián, "roof") + 各 (gè, "each") =
    - under each roof ->
    - guest
  - **interpretation:** 客 (kè, "guest") combines 宀 (mián, "roof") to indicate a building or home, and 各 (gè, "each") as the phonetic component. Together, they symbolize a person staying under a roof other than their own, thus representing a guest.
- **examples:**
  - 欢(歡)迎客人进来(進來)。
    - Huānyíng kèrén jìnlái.
    - Welcome, guest, to come in.
  - 他是国(國)外来(來)的客，留宿在旅馆(館)。
    - Tā shì guówài lái de kè, liú sù zài lǚguǎn.
    - He is a guest from overseas, staying overnight at the hotel.
  - 她是热(熱)情的客，为(為)我们带来(們帶來)快乐(樂)。
    - Tā shì rèqíng de kè, wèi wǒmen dài lái kuàilè.
    - She is a friendly guest, bringing joy to us.
### 不( )客( )气(氣) → 客 → 宀
- **definition:** roof
- **pinyin:** mián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a house with four walls ->
    - roof
  - **interpretation:** 宀(mián, "roof") is a pictogram representing a house with four walls. It emphasizes an enclosed space, distinguishing it from similar structures like 广(guǎng, "broad") which depicts a building with three walls. This makes 宀 a logical choice for representing a roof in various characters.
- **examples:**
  - 含有宀的汉(漢)字代表家的温(溫)暖。
    - Hán yǒu mián de hànzì dàibiǎo jiā de wēnnuǎn.
    - Chinese characters that include the radical '宀' represent the warmth of home.
  - 屋顶(頂)遮风挡(風擋)雨，家园(園)散发温(發溫)馨。
    - Wūdǐng zhē fēng dǎng yǔ, jiāyuán sànfā wēnxīn.
    - The roof shields from wind and rain, and the home radiates warmth.

    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.

### 不( )客( )气(氣) → 气(氣)
- **traditional:** 氣
- **definition:** manner; air
- **pinyin:** qì
- **components:**
  - 三
    - sān
    - three
  - 乞
    - qǐ
    - beg
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Clouds flowing through the sky ->
    - altered to avoid confusion with 三 (sān, "three") ->
    - 乞 (qǐ, "beg") as a variant for phonetic borrowing
  - **interpretation:** The character 气(qì, "air") started as a pictogram representing clouds in the sky, which evolved to differentiate from the character for "three." Over time, 乞 was used as a variant for phonetic reasons, specifically for the meaning "to beg." The meanings 'air, flow' were later borrowed from 气.
- **examples:**
  - 房间里(間裡)的气(氣)很清新。
    - Fángjiān lǐ de qì hěn qīngxīn。
    - The air in the room is very fresh.
  - 打开(開)窗户(戶)，让(讓)新鲜(鮮)的气进来(氣進來)。
    - Dǎkāi chuānghù, ràng xīnxiān de qì jìnlái。
    - Open the window to let fresh air in.
  - 他深吸一口气(氣)，感觉(覺)心情舒畅(暢)。
    - Tā shēn xī yīkǒu qì, gǎnjué xīnqíng shūchàng。
    - He took a deep breath and felt his mood lift.
### 不( )客( )气(氣) → 气(氣) → 三
- **definition:** three
- **pinyin:** sān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 一 (yī, "one") triplicated ->
    - 三 (sān, "three")
  - **interpretation:** The character 三 (sān, "three") is an ideogram depicting the concept of the number three through the use of three parallel horizontal strokes. This straightforward representation makes it consistent with related numeric characters like 一 (yī, "one") and 二 (èr, "two").
- **examples:**

  - 他有三支笔(筆)，我只想要其中一支。

    - He has three pens, but I only want one of them.
  - 他们(們)吃了三碗面(麵)，觉(覺)得非常满(滿)意。
    - Tāmen chī le sān wǎn miàn, juéde fēicháng mǎnyì.
    - They ate three bowls of noodles and felt very satisfied.

- **examples:**
