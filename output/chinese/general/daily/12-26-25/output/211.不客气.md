## you’re welcome; don’t mention it
### bú kèqi
---
- **definition:** you’re welcome; don’t mention it
- **pinyin:** bú kèqi
- **components:**
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
  - 客气(客氣)
    - kèqi
    - polite; courteous
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 不(不) (bù, "not") negates or denies something. 客气(客氣) (kèqi, "polite") refers to showing courtesy or politeness. Together, 不客气(不客氣) (bú kèqi) means "to not be polite" in the sense of not standing on formalities, hence "you’re welcome".
  - **interpretation:** Saying 不客气(不客氣) (bú kèqi) indicates that formal politeness is unnecessary, commonly used in response to thanks to convey that the favor was not a burden.
  - **simplification:** 氣 simplified to 气
- **examples:**
  - 谢谢你的帮助(謝謝你的幫助)，不客气(不客氣)。
    - Xièxiè nǐ de bāngzhù, bú kèqi.
    - Thank you for your help; you're welcome.
  - 听到夸奖时(聽到誇獎時)，他总是轻声说不客气(他總是輕聲說不客氣)。
    - Tīngdào kuājiǎng shí, tā zǒng shì qīngshēng shuō bú kèqi.
    - When hearing compliments, he always softly says 'you're welcome.'
  - 当别人说谢谢时(當別人說謝謝時)，你可以回复不客气(你可以回復不客氣)。
    - Dāng biérén shuō xièxiè shí, nǐ kěyǐ huífù bú kèqi.
    - When someone says thank you, you can reply 'you're welcome.'

### 不客气(不客氣) → 客气(客氣)

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The term combines 客(客) (kè, "guest"), representing the notion of treating others with respect and consideration as you would a guest, and 气(氣) (qì, "atmosphere"), implying an air or manner. Together, 客气(客氣) (kèqi) suggests maintaining a polite or courteous atmosphere.

### 不客气(不客氣) → 客气(客氣) → 客(客) → 各(各) → 夂(夂)
- **definition:** sole of foot
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Footprint pointing down
  - **interpretation:** The character 夂(夂) (zhǐ, 'sole of foot') is a visual representation of a downward-pointing footprint, indicating the sole of a foot. - **definition:** atmosphere; air; manner
- **pinyin:** qì
- **etymology:**
