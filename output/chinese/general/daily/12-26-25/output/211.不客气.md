## you’re welcome; don’t mention it
### bú kèqi
---
- **traditional:** 不客氣
- **definition:** you’re welcome; don’t mention it
- **pinyin:** bú kèqi
- **components:**
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
  - 客
    - kè
    - guest
  - 气(氣)
    - qì
    - manner; air
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 不 (bù, "not") + 客 (kè, "guest") + 气(氣) (qì, "manner; air") =
    - "you're welcome" or "don't mention it"
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 不( )客( )气(氣) (bú kèqì, "you're welcome") is used as a polite response to 'thank you,' emphasizing that no formalities or special courtesy (客气) is necessary. Originally, the characters 客 (kè, "guest") and 气(氣) (qì, "manner") together suggest the idea of showing etiquette or hospitality as one would to a guest. By adding 不 (bù, "not"), it conveys the relaxed, friendly sense that there's no need for the person to feel like a guest engaging in formalities.
- **examples:**
  - 谢(謝)谢(謝)你( )的( )帮(幫)助( )，不( )客( )气(氣)。
    - Xièxiè nǐ de bāngzhù，bú kèqi。

  - 有( )困( )难(難)时(時)，你( )可( )以( )说(說)不( )客( )气(氣)。
    - Yǒu kùnnán shí，nǐ kěyǐ shuō bú kèqi。
    - When in difficulty, you can say 'you're welcome'.
