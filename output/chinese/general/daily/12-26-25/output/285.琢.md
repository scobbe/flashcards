## carve; polish (jade)
### zhuó
---
- **definition:** carve; polish (jade)
- **pinyin:** zhuó
- **components:**
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
  - 豖
    - chù
    - castrated boar; castrated pig
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 𤣩 “jade” + phonetic: 豖; the phonetic depicts a castrated boar and may also suggest the idea of cutting.
  - **interpretation:** Jade marks the domain of meaning: working with jade. The phonetic 豖 gives the sound and, being associated with cutting (castration), reinforces the sense of carving/polishing.
- **examples:**
  - 工匠细心琢玉(工匠細心琢玉)。
    - Gōngjiàng xìxīn zhuó yù.
    - The craftsman carefully carves/polishes jade.
  - 雕琢玉器需要高超的技艺(雕琢玉器需要高超的技藝)。
    - Diāozhuó yùqì xūyào gāochāo de jìyì.
    - Carving jade artifacts requires superb craftsmanship.
  - 这段文字过于雕琢(這段文字過於雕琢)，显得不自然(顯得不自然)。
    - Zhè duàn wénzì guòyú diāozhuó, xiǎnde bù zìrán.
    - This passage is overly polished and feels unnatural.
### 琢(琢) → 玉(玉)
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: image of a cong (琮), a ritual jade totem; also interpreted as three pieces of jade strung on a cord. Historically similar to 王; a dot was added to distinguish them.
  - **interpretation:** Evokes pieces of jade threaded on a cord or a tall ritual cong, conveying the idea of precious jade. The added dot differentiates it from 王 while suggesting a distinct bead-like element.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱玉(我愛玉)。
    - Wǒ ài yù.
    - I love jade.
  - 这块玉很温润(這塊玉很溫潤)。
    - Zhè kuài yù hěn wēnrùn.
    - This piece of jade is very smooth and lustrous.
  - 他送我一个玉镯(他送我一個玉鐲)。
    - Tā sòng wǒ yí gè yù zhuó.
    - He gave me a jade bracelet.
### 琢(琢) → 豖(豖)
- **definition:** castrated boar; castrated pig
- **pinyin:** chù
- **components:**
  - 豕
    - shǐ
    - pig; swine
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a male pig with the penis detached, indicating a castrated boar; later ‘shackled pig’ and Shuowen foot readings are folk/secondary.
  - **interpretation:** Ancient scribes depicted a boar and marked the genitals as severed to convey the idea of castration directly. Later readings like a shackled pig or a pig tripping reflect stylized forms and do not match the oracle-bone evidence.
- **examples:**
  - 这只豖很温顺(這隻豖很溫順)。
    - Zhè zhī chù hěn wēnshùn.
    - This castrated boar is very docile.
  - 农夫把公猪阉后称作豖(農夫把公豬閹後稱作豖)。
    - Nóngfū bǎ gōngzhū yān hòu chēngzuò chù.
    - After castrating a male pig, farmers call it a castrated boar (chù).
  - 古书有时用豖指代阉猪(古書有時用豖指代閹豬)。
    - Gǔshū yǒushí yòng chù zhǐdài yānzhū.
    - Old texts sometimes use 豖 to refer to a castrated pig.
### 琢(琢) → 豖(豖) → 豕(豕)
- **definition:** pig; swine
- **pinyin:** shǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram (象形): a pig; compare 亥, 𢑓 ‘male pig’, and 豖 ‘castrated male pig’.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a pig in profile with head, body, and legs. Related forms differentiate types of pigs; in 㒸, the pig element serves as a phonetic.
- **examples:**
  - 古书中常以“豕”指代猪(古書中常以「豕」指代豬)。
    - Gǔshū zhōng cháng yǐ “shǐ” zhǐdài zhū.
    - In ancient texts, “豕” refers to a pig.
  - 在甲骨文里(在甲骨文裏)，豕的形状清晰可见(豕的形狀清晰可見)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén lǐ, shǐ de xíngzhuàng qīngxī kějiàn.
    - In oracle bone inscriptions,the form of 豕 is clearly visible.
  - 成语“豕突狼奔”中的“豕”意为猪(成語「豕突狼奔」中的「豕」意為豬)。
    - Chéngyǔ “shǐ tū láng bēn” zhōng de “shǐ” yìwéi zhū.
    - In the idiom "豕突狼奔",  “豕” means pig.
