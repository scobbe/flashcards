### ocean; seas
---
- **pinyin:** ocean; seas
- **components:**
  - 海
    - hǎi
    - sea; ocean; coast; maritime
  - 洋
    - yáng
    - ocean; boundless; foreign; vast
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 海(海) (hǎi, 'sea/ocean') + 洋(洋) (yáng, 'ocean/boundless') — sea ocean
  - **interpretation:** The term '海洋' directly evokes the concept of vast and expansive bodies of water, combining '海' which refers to 'sea' and '洋' which refers to a larger 'ocean'. This combination aptly describes the overall concept of oceans and seas.
- **examples:**
  - 海洋是地球上最大的生态系统。(海洋是地球上最大的生態系統。)
    - Hǎiyáng shì dìqiú shàng zuìdà de shēngtài xìtǒng.
    - The ocean is the largest ecosystem on Earth.
  - 我们去海洋馆看海洋生物吧。(我們去海洋館看海洋生物吧。)
    - Wǒmen qù hǎiyángguǎn kàn hǎiyáng shēngwù ba.
    - Let's visit the oceanarium to see ocean creatures.

- **pinyin:** hǎi

    - water
  - 每
    - měi
    - each; every
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Semantic component 氵 (water) + phonetic component 每 (pronounced 'měi').
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the idea of water (氵) associated with the phonetic element (每), indicating a connection to vast bodies of water.
- **examples:**
  - 海洋很广阔(海洋很廣闊)，我们可以去游泳(我們可以去游泳)。

### 海洋(海洋) → 海(海) → 每(每)
- **definition:** each; every
- **pinyin:** měi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character 每 represents the concept of 'every' or 'each', often depicted as a person (representing individual) and a line below indicating a repetitive action or inclusion.
  - **interpretation:** This makes intuitive sense as it conveys the idea of every individual represented by the person being included in a group.
- **examples:**

### 海洋(海洋) → 洋(洋)
- **definition:** ocean; boundless; foreign; vast
- **pinyin:** yáng
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 羊
    - yáng
    - sheep
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** The character 洋 consists of the water radical 水 (氵) indicating something related to water, and 羊 (yáng, 'sheep'), suggesting the vast and expansive nature of the ocean. This reflects the character's meanings related to the ocean and foreignness.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of water and sheep evokes the imagery of a vast ocean, suggesting boundlessness and foreignness, tying in with the meanings of the character.
- **examples:**
  - 洋流(洋流)，对气候有很大的影响(對氣候有很大的影響)。

  - 我喜欢洋葱(我喜歡洋蔥)。
    - wǒ xǐhuān yángcōng.
    - I like onions.
  - 这是一片洋面(這是一片洋面)。
    - zhè shì yī piàn yángmiàn.
