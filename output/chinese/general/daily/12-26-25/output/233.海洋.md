## ocean; seas
### hǎiyáng
---
- **definition:** ocean; seas
- **pinyin:** hǎiyáng
- **components:**
  - 海
    - hǎi
    - sea
  - 洋
    - yáng
    - ocean; vast
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 海 (hǎi, "sea") refers to a body of saline water, often part of the ocean. 洋 (yáng, "ocean; vast") signifies something expansive and broad, usually referencing the larger bodies of water that cover much of the Earth's surface. Together, 海洋 (hǎiyáng, "ocean; seas") denotes the vast, interconnected bodies of saltwater that cover the majority of the Earth's surface.
  - **interpretation:** 海 (hǎi, "sea") combined with 洋 (yáng, "ocean; vast") creates a term that emphasizes the vastness and interconnectivity of the seas, covering the Earth's surface.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)海洋。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hǎiyáng.
    - I like the ocean.
  - 科学(學)家研究海洋，探索未知。
    - Kēxuéjiā yánjiū hǎiyáng, tànsuǒ wèizhī.
    - Scientists study the ocean and explore the unknown.
  - 深邃的海洋孕育生命。
    - Shēnsuì de hǎiyáng yùnyù shēngmìng.
    - The deep ocean nurtures life.
### 海洋 → 海
- **definition:** sea
- **pinyin:** hǎi
- **components:**
  - 氵
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 每
    - měi
    - each
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 氵 (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic: 每 (měi) =
    - body of water suggested by pronunciation ->
    - sea or ocean
  - **interpretation:** 海 (hǎi, "sea; ocean") combines 氵 (shuǐ, "water") to suggest its relation to water bodies, with 每 (měi) providing the phonetic component to shape the pronunciation, effectively communicating the concept of a vast body of water.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)在海边(邊)散步。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān zài hǎibiān sànbù.
    - I enjoy taking walks by the sea.
  - 夕阳(陽)映照在海面上，波光闪烁(閃爍)。
    - Xīyáng yìngzhào zài hǎimiàn shàng, bōguāng shǎnsuò.
    - The setting sun reflects on the sea surface, and the waves sparkle.
  - 海风(風)送来凉(來涼)意，旅人心生惬(愜)意。
    - Hǎifēng sòng lái liángyì, lǚrén xīnshēng qièyì.
    - The sea breeze brings coolness, and the traveler feels content.
### 海洋 → 海 → 氵
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 氵is the radical form of 水 (shuǐ, "water") ->
    - depicts flowing water, compressed and stylized next to characters.
  - **interpretation:** The character 氵is a simplified variant used as a radical to represent water-related meanings in other characters. It retains the flowing water depiction of 水 (shuǐ, "water") but is stylized to fit alongside other components.
- **examples:**
  - 水是生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 汉(漢)字中偏旁氵，代表水。
    - Hànzì zhōng piān páng shuǐ, dàibiǎo shuǐ.
    - In Chinese characters, the radical '氵' signifies water.
  - 河水清澈透明，鱼儿欢(魚兒歡)快戏(戲)水。
    - Hé shuǐ qīngchè tòumíng, yú er huānkuaì xì shuǐ.
    - The river water is clear and transparent, and the fish happily play in it.
### 海洋 → 海 → 每
- **definition:** each
- **pinyin:** měi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts grain plant with two leaves ->
    - symbolizing multiplicity and repetition ->
    - each, every
  - **interpretation:** This character originally depicted a grain plant with two leaves emerging from the sides, symbolizing the idea of multiplicity and is used to mean 'each' or 'every' in contexts, suggesting repetition.
- **examples:**
  - 每个(個)人都有机会(機會)。
    - Měi gèrén dōu yǒu jīhuì.
    - Each person has a chance.
  - 我每天练习(練習)一小时(時)。
    - Wǒ měitiān liànxí yī xiǎoshí.
    - I practice for an hour each day.
  - 每朵花都有它的香气(氣)。
    - Měi duǒ huā dōu yǒu tā de xiāngqì.
    - Each flower has its own fragrance.
### 海洋 → 洋
- **definition:** ocean; vast
- **pinyin:** yáng
- **components:**
  - 氵
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 羊
    - yáng
    - sheep
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 氵 (shuǐ, "water") + 羊 (yáng, "sheep") =
    - vastness of water ->
    - ocean; by extension ->
    - foreign
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 氵 (shuǐ, "water") to denote a connection to water and 羊 (yáng, "sheep") for its phonetic value. The vastness associated with oceans was conceptually extended to anything foreign.
- **examples:**
  - 他喜欢(歡)洋服，认为(認為)它们时(們時)尚。
    - Tā xǐhuān yángfú, rènwéi tāmen shíshàng.
    - He likes foreign clothes and thinks they are trendy.
  - 遥远(遙遠)的洋隐(隱)藏着无数谜团(著無數謎團)。
    - Yáoyuǎn de yáng yǐncángzhe wúshù mítuán.
    - The distant ocean conceals countless mysteries.
  - 她听(聽)洋乐(樂)，感受异国风(異國風)情。
    - Tā tīng yángyuè, gǎnshòu yìguó fēngqíng.
    - She listens to foreign music and experiences exotic charm.

### 海洋 → 洋 → 羊
- **definition:** sheep
- **pinyin:** yáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a ram's head
  - **interpretation:** The character 羊(yáng, 'sheep') visually represents the head of a sheep or ram, capturing the animal's distinctive features. It serves as a straightforward pictogram, portraying one of the common animals in ancient agrarian societies.
- **examples:**
  - 农场里(農場裡)有羊。
    - Nóngchǎng lǐ yǒu yáng.
    - There are sheep on the farm.
  - 春天来(來)了，羊群在草地上吃草。
    - Chūntiān lái le, yángqún zài cǎodì shàng chī cǎo.
    - In spring, the flock of sheep grazes on the meadow.
  - 那只(隻)羊看起来(來)很温顺(溫順)。
    - Nà zhī yáng kàn qǐlái hěn wēnshùn.
    - That sheep looks very gentle.
