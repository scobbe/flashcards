## ease; leisure; escape
### yì
---
- **definition:** ease; leisure; escape
- **pinyin:** yì
- **components:**
  - 辵
    - chuò
    - walk
  - 兔
    - tù
    - rabbit
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 止(止) (zhǐ, "stop") + 㚔(㚔) (unknown) ->
    - original idea of stopping ->
    - evolved to 辵(辵) (chuò, "walk") + 兔(兔) (tù, "rabbit") ->
    - concept of a rabbit fleeing ->
    - ease/leisure/escape
  - **interpretation:** The character 逸(逸) (yì) originally combined symbols for 'stop' and an abstract element, suggesting an initial idea of stopping. Later, it merged 'walking' with 'a rabbit,' illustrating an image of a rabbit escaping swiftly, which ties to senses of both leisure and escape.
- **examples:**
  - 他过着逸趣生活(他過著逸趣生活)。
    - Tā guòzhe yìqù shēnghuó.
    - He lives a life of leisure.
  - 工作之余(工作之餘)，他逸出尘嚣(他逸出塵囂)。
    - Gōngzuò zhī yú, tā yì chū chénxiāo.
    - In his spare time from work, he escapes the hustle and bustle.
  - 他常在诗文中寻得逸致(他常在詩文中尋得逸致)。
    - Tā cháng zài shīwén zhōng xún dé yìzhì.
    - He often finds elegant leisure in poetry and prose.
### 逸(逸) → 辵(辵)
- **definition:** walk
- **pinyin:** chuò
- **components:**
  - 彳
    - chì
    - walk
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 彳(彳) (chì, "walk") + 止(止) (zhǐ, "foot" or "to stop") ->
    - walking and stopping ->
    - stylized as 龰 ->
    - final form
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 彳(彳) (chì, "walk") and 止(止) (zhǐ, "foot" or "to stop") to convey the concept of movement through intermittent stopping and going, reflecting its meaning related to walking.
- **examples:**
  - 我辵在街上漫步(我辵在街上漫步)。
    - Wǒ chuò zài jiē shàng mànbù.
    - I walk leisurely on the street.
  - 他辵进城(他辵進城)，探索新世界(探索新世界)。
    - Tā chuò jìn chéng, tànsuǒ xīn shìjiè.
    - He walks into the city and explores a new world.

### 逸(逸) → 辵(辵) → 止(止)
- **definition:** foot
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a footprint pointing up originally meaning 'foot'

- **examples:**
