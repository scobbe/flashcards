## appear; now
### xiàn
---
- **definition:** appear; now
- **pinyin:** xiàn
- **components:**
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king
  - 见(見)
    - jiàn
    - see
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 王(王) (wáng, "king") + phonetic: 見(見) (jiàn, "see") =
    - seeing reality, things as they are ->
    - current
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 王(王) (wáng, "king") as a semantic element suggesting value or importance, with 見(見) (jiàn, "see") as the phonetic element, indicating bringing forth or manifesting what is seen, thus referring to the concept of the present or current reality.
  - **simplification:** The traditional component 見(見) (jiàn, "see") is retained in a simplified form using 见(见) (jiàn, "see") to maintain phonetic properties while 王(王) (wáng, "king") remains unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 现在的技术十分先进(現在的技術十分先進)。
    - Xiànzài de jìshù shífēn xiānjìn.
    - The current technology is very advanced.
  - 社会现呈繁荣景象(社會現呈繁榮景象)，人们享受幸福生活(人們享受幸福生活)。
    - Shèhuì xiàn chéng fánróng jǐngxiàng, rénmen xiǎngshòu xìngfú shēnghuó.
    - Society is showing a flourishing scene, and people are enjoying a happy life.
### 现(現) → 王(王)
- **definition:** king
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a ritual axe symbolizing royal authority ->
    - ceremonial tool near the throne ->
    - final form for king
  - **interpretation:** The character 王(wáng, "king") visually represents a ritual axe, a powerful symbol of authority and royal rituals in ancient China. This connection to ceremonial objects underscores the king's role in maintaining order.
- **examples:**
  - 王是中国的常见姓氏(王是中國的常見姓氏)。
    - Wáng shì Zhōngguó de chángjiàn xìngshì.
    - Wang is a common surname in China.
  - 古时的王统治天下(古時的王統治天下)，人民敬仰他的威严(人民敬仰他的威嚴)。
    - Gǔ shí de wáng tǒngzhì tiānxià, rénmín jìngyǎng tā de wēiyán.
    - In ancient times, kings ruled the world, and the people admired their authority.
### 现(現) → 见(見)
- **definition:** see
- **pinyin:** jiàn
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 卩
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 目(目) (mù, "eye") + 卩(卩) (jié, "kneeling person") =
    - a kneeling human figure with a large eye for a head ->
    - "see"
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the eye 目(目) (mù, "eye") with a kneeling person 卩(卩) (jié, "kneeling person"), representing the act of perceiving or seeing, which aligns intuitively with the meaning "to see."
  - **simplification:** 见 is a simplified form derived from 見 by adopting a cursive script version, maintaining the essential elements in a more compact form.
- **examples:**
  - 我见到了新朋友(我見到了新朋友)。
    - Wǒ jiàn dào le xīn péngyǒu.
    - I met a new friend.
  - 他在路上见到她(他在路上見到她)，并且打了个招呼(並且打了個招呼)。
    - Tā zài lù shàng jiàn dào tā, bìngqiě dǎ le gè zhāohu.
    - He saw her on the road, and he greeted her warmly.
  - 你一见他(你一見他)，就露出笑容(就露出笑容)。
    - Nǐ yī jiàn tā, jiù lù chū xiàoróng.
    - The moment you see him, you break into a smile.
### 现(現) → 见(見) → 目(目)
- **definition:** eye
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an eye
  - **interpretation:** The character 目(目) (mù, "eye") represents a human eye but is now commonly depicted rotated by 90 degrees. It serves as the basis for several other characters related to vision or perception.
- **examples:**
  - 医生检查我的目(醫生檢查我的目)，确认没有异常(確認沒有異常)。
    - Yīshēng jiǎnchá wǒ de mù, quèrèn méiyǒu yìcháng.
    - The doctor examined my eye and confirmed there was nothing abnormal.
  - 他左目受伤(他左目受傷)，依旧炯炯有神(依舊炯炯有神)。
    - Tā zuǒ mù shòushāng, yījiù jiǒngjiǒng yǒu shén.
    - His left eye was injured, yet it still shone with a determined spark.
  - 那只猫的目似乎闪烁智慧(那隻貓的目似乎閃爍智慧)。
    - Nà zhī māo de mù sìhū shǎnshuò zhìhuì.
    - The cat's eye seemed to shimmer with wisdom.
### 现(現) → 见(見) → 卩(卩)
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from the side view
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩(卩) (jié, "kneel") visually represents a person in a kneeling position seen from the side, offering a straightforward depiction of the concept.
- **examples:**
  - 读《说文解字》时(讀《說文解字》時)，我们了解到卩是‘跪’的意思(我們了解到卩是‘跪’的意思)。
    - Dú 'Shuōwén Jiězì' shí, wǒmen liǎojiě dào jié shì 'guì' de yìsi.
    - When reading Shuowen Jiezi, we learned that 卩 means 'to kneel'.
  - 仪式开始时(儀式開始時)，他卩下以示虔诚(他卩下以示虔誠)。
    - Yíshì kāishǐ shí, tā jié xià yǐ shì qiánchéng.
    - When the ceremony began, he knelt to show his piety.
  - 幼时他常听长辈说(幼時他常聽長輩說)，卩是一种谦卑的行为(卩是一種謙卑的行為)。
    - Yòushí tā cháng tīng zhǎngbèi shuō, jié shì yī zhǒng qiānbēi de xíngwéi.
    - In his youth, he often heard elders say that 卩 is an act of humility.
