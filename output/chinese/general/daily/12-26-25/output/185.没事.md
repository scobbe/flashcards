## 没事(沒事)
---
## be free; no problem
### méishì
---
- **definition:** be free; no problem
- **pinyin:** méishì
- **components:**
  - 没(沒)
    - méi
    - no; not
  - 事
    - shì
    - matter; affair
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 没(沒) (méi, "no; not"), 事(事) (shì, "matter; affair") - literally "no matter"
  - **interpretation:** This combination makes sense as 没(沒) (méi, "no; not") combined with 事(事) (shì, "matter; affair") expresses the absence of an issue or problem. It is used to assure someone that everything is fine or to indicate that one is available or not busy.
- **examples:**
  - 你太客气了(你太客氣了)，真的没事(真的沒事)。
    - Nǐ tài kèqì le, zhēn de méishì.
    - You're too kind, it's really nothing.
  - 我今天没事(我今天沒事)，可以陪你散步(可以陪你散步)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān méishì, kěyǐ péi nǐ sànbù.
    - I'm free today, I can accompany you for a walk.
### 没事(沒事) → 没(沒)
- **definition:** no; not
- **pinyin:** méi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Variant of 沒
  - **description:** Variant form of 沒(沒) (méi, "sink")
  - **interpretation:** The character 没 is a simplified variant of 沒(沒). It retains the same elements that imply negation or absence from 沒(沒).
  - **simplification:** The simplification from 沒(沒) to 没 removes the 氵(氵) component, making the character more streamlined.
- **examples:**
  - 我没去过图书馆(我沒去過圖書館)。
    - Wǒ méi qùguò túshūguǎn.
    - I have never been to the library.
  - 小李没完成作业(小李沒完成作業)，老师没批评他(老師沒批評他)。
    - Xiǎo Lǐ méi wánchéng zuòyè, lǎoshī méi pīpíng tā.
    - Xiao Li did not finish his homework, and the teacher did not criticize him.
  - 他们没计划去旅行(他們沒計劃去旅行)。
    - Tāmen méi jìhuà qù lǚxíng.
    - They have no plans to travel.
### 没事(沒事) → 事(事)
- **definition:** matter; affair
- **pinyin:** shì
- **components:**
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
  - 亅
    - jué
    - hook
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 舌(舌) (shé, 'tongue') + phonetic: 亅(亅) ->
    - combining elements for 'matter; affair'
  - **interpretation:** The character 事(事) (shì, 'matter; affair') combines 舌(舌) (shé, 'tongue'), which might suggest speaking or communication, but primarily serves as the semantic component, with 亅(亅), contributing the phonetic sound. The combination suggests matters or affairs that are expressed or communicated, capturing the concept of events or things to be dealt with.
- **examples:**
  - 这件事很复杂(這件事很複雜)。
    - Zhè jiàn shì hěn fùzá.
    - This matter is complicated.
  - 他总是忙于处理各种事(他總是忙於處理各種事)，从不怠慢任何人(從不怠慢任何人)。
    - Tā zǒng shì máng yú chǔlǐ gè zhǒng shì, cóng bù dài màn rènhé rén.
    - He is always busy handling various affairs, never neglecting anyone.
### 没事(沒事) → 事(事) → 舌(舌)
- **definition:** tongue
- **pinyin:** shé
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tongue sticking out
  - **interpretation:** The character 舌 (shé, "tongue") visually represents the image of a tongue extending out of the mouth, highlighting its function in speech and taste.
- **examples:**
  - 他调皮地伸出舌(他調皮地伸出舌)，引人发笑(引人發笑)。
    - Tā tiáopí de shēnchū shé, yǐn rén fāxiào.
    - He playfully stuck out his tongue, making people laugh.
  - 寒冷令舌变得僵硬(寒冷令舌變得僵硬)。
    - Hánlěng lìng shé biàn dé jiāngyìng.
    - The cold makes the tongue stiff.
  - 病痛侵袭(病痛侵襲)，他的舌失去味觉(他的舌失去味覺)。
    - Bìngtòng qīn xí, tā de shé shīqù wèijué.
    - Ailments attack, and his tongue loses its sense of taste.
### 没事(沒事) → 事(事) → 亅(亅)
- **definition:** hook
- **pinyin:** jué
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a hook

- **examples:**

    - In ancient texts, 亅 is commonly used to denote a hook-shaped structure.
