### be free; no problem
---
- **pinyin:** be free; no problem
- **components:**
  - 没(沒)
    - méi
    - not; no; negative particle
  - 事
    - shì
    - matter; thing; affair
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 没(沒) (méi, "not; no") + 事(事) (shì, "matter; thing") — not matter
  - **interpretation:** This combination expresses the idea that there are no problems or matters to worry about. It conveys a sense of reassurance and calmness.
- **examples:**
  - 没事(沒事)，我很开心(我很開心)。
    - méi shì, wǒ hěn kāixīn.
    - No problem, I am very happy.
  - 你要问我吗？没事(沒事)！
    - nǐ yào wèn wǒ ma? méi shì!
    - Do you want to ask me? No problem!
  - 最近有点忙，但没事(沒事)。
    - zuìjìn yǒudiǎn máng, dàn méi shì.
    - I've been a bit busy lately, but it's no problem.

### 没事(沒事) → 事(事)
- **definition:** matter; thing; affair
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character depicts the concept of 'affair' or 'matter', often reflecting the complexity of various activities or situations humans engage in.
  - **interpretation:** The character intuitively evokes the notion of various matters or affairs as it encompasses different activities, prompting thoughts of duties or business.
- **examples:**
  - 这件事很重要(這件事很重要)。
    - Zhè jiàn shì hěn zhòngyào.
    - This matter is very important.
  - 我有很多事情要做(我有很多事情要做)。
    - Wǒ yǒu hěn duō shìqíng yào zuò.
    - I have many things to do.
  - 这只是个小事(這只是一個小事)。
    - Zhè zhǐ shì gè xiǎo shì.
    - This is just a small matter.
