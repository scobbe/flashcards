## monkey
### hóuzi
---
- **definition:** monkey
- **pinyin:** hóuzi
- **components:**
  - 猴
    - hóu
    - monkey
  - 子
    - zi
    - child; offspring
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 猴 (hóu, "monkey") refers to the animal itself, while 子 (zi, "child; offspring") is often used as a suffix in nouns to denote a smaller or diminutive form. Together, 猴子 (猴子) (hóuzi, "monkey") refers to the monkey both in the sense of the animal and as a common noun.
  - **interpretation:** The word 猴子 (猴子) (hóuzi, "monkey") combines the animal's name with a suffix to create the common and familiar term for a monkey in Chinese.

### 猴子 → 猴
- **definition:** monkey
- **pinyin:** hóu
- **components:**
  - 犭
    - quǎn
    - dog
  - 侯
    - hóu
    - marquis
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 犭 (quǎn, "dog") + phonetic 侯 (hóu, "marquis") =
    - animal phoneticized by 侯 ->
    - monkey
  - **interpretation:** 猴 (hóu, "monkey") combines 犭 (quǎn, "dog") as a radical indicating an animal, with 侯 (hóu, "marquis") as a phonetic element that provides the sound. This forms the character for monkey by linking the concept of an animal with the sound similar to '侯'.

### 猴子 → 猴 → 侯
- **definition:** marquis
- **pinyin:** hóu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts target for arrows ->
    - representation of marquis as distant goal
  - **interpretation:** Originally a pictogram showing a target for arrows, this character captures the idea of a marquis as a distant, lofty position to aim for. The visual metaphor aligns the role of a marquis, often considered a noble target or goal within a hierarchy, with the target imagery.
- **examples:**
