## monkey
### hóuzi
---
- **definition:** monkey
- **pinyin:** hóuzi
- **components:**
  - 猴
    - hóu
    - monkey
  - 子
    - zi
    - child
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 猴(猴) (hóu, "monkey") is a direct reference to the animal, and 子(子) (zi, "child") is often added to nouns to create diminutives or familiar forms. The combination 猴子(猴子) (hóuzi, "monkey"), therefore, refers to the monkey as a playful or familiar creature.
  - **interpretation:** Adding 子(子) (zi, "child") to 猴(猴) (hóu, "monkey") gives a sense of familiarity or endearment, much like calling a monkey a 'little monkey' in English.
- **examples:**
  - 猴子喜欢吃香蕉(猴子喜歡吃香蕉)。
    - Hóuzi xǐhuān chī xiāngjiāo.
    - The monkey likes to eat bananas.
  - 猴子在树上荡秋千(猴子在樹上盪秋千)，玩得非常开心(玩得非常開心)。
    - Hóuzi zài shù shàng dàng qiūqiān, wán de fēicháng kāixīn.
    - The monkey swings on the tree and plays happily.
  - 森林里有很多动物(森林裡有很多動物)，猴子总是在树间跳跃(猴子總是在樹間跳躍)。
    - Sēnlín lǐ yǒu hěn duō dòngwù, hóuzi zǒng shì zài shù jiān tiàoyuè.
    - There are many animals in the forest, and the monkey is always jumping among the trees.
### 猴子(猴子) → 猴(猴)
- **definition:** monkey
- **pinyin:** hóu
- **components:**
  - 犭
    - quǎn
    - dog
  - 侯
    - hóu
    - marquis
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 犭(犭) (quǎn, "dog") + phonetic 侯(侯) (hóu, "marquis") =
    - animal related to 侯 phonetic ->
    - monkey
  - **interpretation:** 猴(猴) (hóu, "monkey") combines the 犭(犭) (quǎn, "dog") radical, indicating an animal, with 侯(侯) (hóu, "marquis") for the phonetic component. It makes sense as monkeys are animals and the phonetic 侯 contributes to its pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 猴喜欢吃水果(猴喜歡吃水果)。
    - Hóu xǐhuān chī shuǐguǒ.
    - The monkey likes to eat fruits.
  - 丛林中猴跳上跳下(叢林中猴跳上跳下)，吸引了许多游客(吸引了許多遊客)。
    - Cónglín zhōng hóu tiào shàng tiào xià, xīyǐn le xǔduō yóukè.
    - In the jungle, the monkey jumps up and down, attracting many tourists.

### 猴子(猴子) → 子(子)
- **definition:** child
- **pinyin:** zi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts an infant swaddled in cloth ->
    - symbolizes child
  - **interpretation:** The character 子(子) (zǐ, "child") originally depicted an infant wrapped in a cloth, illustrating the concept of a young child. This symbolic representation has expanded over time to include meanings such as "seed" or "particle," aligning with the concept of beginnings.
- **examples:**
  - 他的小子成绩优异(他的小子成績優異)。
    - Tā xiǎo zǐ chéngjì yōuyì.
    - His child has excellent grades.
  - 这颗子将发芽生长(這顆子將發芽生長)。
    - Zhè kē zǐ jiāng fāyá shēngzhǎng.
    - This seed will sprout and grow.
  - 当太阳升起时(當太陽升起時)，小子在院子里欢笑(小子在院子裡歡笑)。
    - Dāng tàiyáng shēngqǐ shí, xiǎo zǐ zài yuànzi lǐ huānxiào.
    - When the sun rises, the child laughs in the courtyard.
