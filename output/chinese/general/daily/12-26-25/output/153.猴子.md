## monkey
### hóuzi
---
- **definition:** monkey
- **pinyin:** hóuzi
- **components:**
  - 猴
    - hóu
    - monkey; macaque; lively like a monkey; Monkey (zodiac sign)
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child; son; noun suffix; seed
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 猴 (monkey) + 子 (noun suffix) = monkey (animal).
  - **interpretation:** 猴 names the animal; 子 is a noun-forming suffix giving a casual or diminutive feel. Combined, it is the everyday term for a monkey.
- **examples:**
  - 这只猴子在树上跳来跳去(這隻猴子在樹上跳來跳去)。
    - Zhè zhī hóuzi zài shù shàng tiàolái-tiàoqù.
    - This monkey is jumping around in the tree.
  - 小孩子喜欢看猴子表演(小孩子喜歡看猴子表演)。
    - Xiǎo háizi xǐhuan kàn hóuzi biǎoyǎn.
    - Children like to watch monkey performances.
  - 动物园里有很多猴子(動物園裡有很多猴子)。
    - Dòngwùyuán lǐ yǒu hěn duō hóuzi.
    - There are many monkeys in the zoo.
### 猴子 → 猴
- **definition:** monkey; macaque; lively like a monkey; Monkey (zodiac sign)
- **pinyin:** hóu
- **components:**
  - 犬
    - quǎn
    - dog
  - 侯
    - hóu
    - marquis; lord
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 犭 + phonetic: 侯
  - **interpretation:** Built with the animal-signifying component 犭 to indicate an animal, while 侯 provides the sound (OC *ɡoː, modern hóu). The phonetic guides pronunciation, and the semantic element situates the meaning in the animal domain, later specified to monkeys.
- **examples:**
  - 动物园里有很多猴子(動物園裡有很多猴子)。
    - Dòngwùyuán lǐ yǒu hěn duō hóuzi.
    - There are many monkeys in the zoo.
  - 他属猴(他屬猴)，性格活泼(性格活潑)。
    - Tā shǔ hóu, xìnggé huópō.
    - He is a Monkey in the zodiac and has a lively personality.
  - 那只小猴跳来跳去(那隻小猴跳來跳去)，惹得大家发笑(惹得大家發笑)。
    - Nà zhī xiǎo hóu tiàoláitiàoqù, rě de dàjiā fāxiào.
    - The little monkey jumped around, making everyone laugh.
### 猴子 → 猴 → 犬
- **definition:** dog
- **pinyin:** quǎn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: side view of a dog.
  - **interpretation:** Shows a side profile of a dog, with the head, legs, and tail simplified into a few strokes. Early forms were more zoomorphic; the modern shape remains an abstract outline that still evokes a dog.
- **examples:**
  - 我的爱犬每天陪我散步(我的愛犬每天陪我散步)。
    - Wǒ de àiquǎn měitiān péi wǒ sànbù.
    - My beloved dog accompanies me on walks every day.
  - 警犬在机场嗅闻行李(警犬在機場嗅聞行李)。
    - Jǐngquǎn zài jīchǎng xiùwén xíngli.
    - Police dogs sniff the luggage at the airport.
  - 动物园里展出了多种犬类(動物園裡展出了多種犬類)。
    - Dòngwùyuán lǐ zhǎnchū le duō zhǒng quǎnlèi.
    - The zoo showcased many kinds of canines.
### 猴子 → 猴 → 侯
- **definition:** marquis; lord
- **pinyin:** hóu
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 矢
    - shǐ
    - arrow
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a target for arrows.
  - **interpretation:** Early forms depicted an archery target; the graph was later borrowed to write the feudal title ‘marquis/lord’. The modern shape resembles 人 plus 矢 and is often reinterpreted as a person and an arrow, but that is a later analysis rather than the original design.
- **examples:**
  - 战国时，许多诸侯争夺土地(戰國時，許多諸侯爭奪土地)。
    - Zhànguó shí, xǔduō zhūhóu zhēngduó tǔdì.
    - During the Warring States period, many feudal lords fought for land.
  - 国王封他为侯(國王封他為侯)。
    - Guówáng fēng tā wéi hóu.
    - The king enfeoffed him as a marquis.
  - 他家世代为侯爵，但他更关心百姓(他家世代為侯爵，但他更關心百姓)。
    - Tā jiā shìdài wéi hóujué, dàn tā gèng guānxīn bǎixìng.
    - His family held a marquisate for generations, but he cared more about the common people.
### 猴子 → 猴 → 侯 → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 猴子 → 猴 → 侯 → 矢
- **definition:** arrow
- **pinyin:** shǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: an arrow; more recognizable in older forms.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a straight shaft with a pointed head, representing an arrow in early scripts. Usage later extended to mean “to vow/pledge,” as in set phrases like 矢志, evoking firmness and unwavering intent.
- **examples:**
  - 他拉弓射矢(他拉弓射矢)。
    - tā lā gōng shè shǐ.
    - He draws the bow and shoots an arrow.
  - 我们矢志不渝(我們矢志不渝)。
    - wǒmen shǐzhì bù yú.
    - We are steadfast in our resolve.
  - 古人以矢为信(古人以矢為信)。
    - gǔrén yǐ shǐ wéi xìn.
    - In ancient times, people used an arrow as a token of trust.
### 猴子 → 子
- **definition:** child; son; noun suffix; seed
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: image of a baby with a large head and spread arms; legs wrapped in a blanket.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a swaddled baby with a prominent head and outstretched arms. Over time the figure was stylized into the modern form while still evoking an infant.
- **examples:**
  - 他是我唯一的儿子(他是我唯一的兒子)。
    - Tā shì wǒ wéiyī de érzi.
    - He is my only son.
  - 她抱起孩子(她抱起孩子)。
    - Tā bào qǐ háizi.
    - She picked up the child.
  - 这个孩子三岁了(這個孩子三歲了)。
    - Zhège háizi sān suì le.
    - This child is three years old.
