## recharge card
### chōngzhíkǎ
---
- **definition:** recharge card
- **pinyin:** chōngzhíkǎ
- **components:**
  - 充值
    - chōngzhí
    - recharge; top up; add value; credit (an account)
  - 卡
    - kǎ
    - card; bank/ID card; checkpoint; to get stuck
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 充值 + 卡 = card used to add value to an account.
  - **interpretation:** 充值 means adding funds or credit to an account, while 卡 refers to a physical or virtual card. Together it names a card or code used to top up balances like mobile phone credit or digital wallets.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了一张充值卡(我買了一張充值卡)，用来给手机话费充值(用來給手機話費充值)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le yì zhāng chōngzhíkǎ, yònglái gěi shǒujī huàfèi chōngzhí.
    - I bought a recharge card to top up my mobile phone balance.
  - 这张充值卡已经被使用过了(這張充值卡已經被使用過了)，你需要再买一张新的(你需要再買一張新的)。
    - Zhè zhāng chōngzhíkǎ yǐjīng bèi shǐyòng guò le, nǐ xūyào zài mǎi yì zhāng xīn de.
    - This recharge card has already been used; you need to buy a new one.
  - 你可以在便利店购买充值卡(你可以在便利店購買充值卡)，或者用APP直接充值(或者用APP直接充值)。
    - Nǐ kěyǐ zài biànlìdiàn gòumǎi chōngzhíkǎ, huòzhě yòng APP zhíjiē chōngzhí.
    - You can buy a recharge card at a convenience store, or recharge directly with an app.
### 充值卡 → 充值
- **definition:** recharge; top up; add value; credit (an account)
- **pinyin:** chōngzhí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 充 ‘fill/charge’ + 值 ‘value’ = add value/top up.
  - **interpretation:** Literally “fill value,” used for adding prepaid credit to phones, transport cards, games, or digital wallets. By extension, it can also refer to the top-up amount itself.
- **examples:**
  - 我需要给手机话费充值(我需要給手機話費充值)。
    - Wǒ xūyào gěi shǒujī huàfèi chōngzhí.
    - I need to top up my mobile phone credit.
  - 他在网上银行给账户充值，很方便(他在網上銀行給帳戶充值，很方便)。
    - Tā zài wǎngshàng yínháng gěi zhànghù chōngzhí, hěn fāngbiàn.
    - He recharges his account via online banking; it’s very convenient.
  - 这个游戏要求先充值才能解锁新关卡(這個遊戲要求先充值才能解鎖新關卡)。
    - Zhège yóuxì yāoqiú xiān chōngzhí cáinéng jiěsuǒ xīn guānkǎ.
    - This game requires a top-up before new levels can be unlocked.
### 充值卡 → 卡
- **definition:** card; bank/ID card; checkpoint; to get stuck
- **pinyin:** kǎ
- **components:**
  - 上
    - shàng
    - up
  - 下
    - xià
    - down
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 上 ‘up’ + 下 ‘down’; fused horizontal lines depict being squeezed between above and below.
  - **interpretation:** Combining 上 and 下 evokes opposite forces pressing together, with the merged line suggesting a tight fit or obstruction. This imagery aligns with senses like a slotted card or a controlled checkpoint, where passage or insertion is constrained.
- **examples:**
  - 我办了一张会员卡(我辦了一張會員卡)。
    - Wǒ bàn le yì zhāng huìyuán kǎ.
    - I applied for a membership card.
  - 警察在路口设卡检查(警察在路口設卡檢查)。
    - Jǐngchá zài lùkǒu shè kǎ jiǎnchá.
    - The police set up a checkpoint at the intersection to inspect vehicles.
  - 他每天八点打卡上班(他每天八點打卡上班)。
    - Tā měitiān bā diǎn dǎkǎ shàngbān.
    - He clocks in at work at eight every day.
### 充值卡 → 卡 → 上
- **definition:** up
- **pinyin:** shàng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram: a line above another—‘up’; originally 𠄞, later changed to avoid confusion with 二.
  - **interpretation:** A short stroke sits above a longer baseline, picturing something positioned over another. In contrast with 下, it indicates upward or superior position. The form was standardized to distinguish it clearly from 二.
- **examples:**
  - 书在桌子上(書在桌子上)。
    - Shū zài zhuōzi shàng.
    - The book is on the table.
  - 我们上楼去(我們上樓去)。
    - Wǒmen shàng lóu qù.
    - Let's go upstairs.
  - 上课时(上課時)，请把手机放在桌上(請把手機放在桌上)。
    - Shàng kè shí, qǐng bǎ shǒujī fàng zài zhuō shàng.
    - During class, please put your phone on the desk.
### 充值卡 → 卡 → 下
- **definition:** down
- **pinyin:** xià
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; a horizontal line
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram (指事): a line below another (contrast 上).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a mark placed beneath a baseline, expressing the idea of “down” or “below.” In contrast with 上, which marks above the line, the pair visually encodes relative position.
- **examples:**
  - 我下楼(我下樓)。
    - Wǒ xià lóu.
    - I go downstairs.
  - 把书放在桌子下(把書放在桌子下)。
    - Bǎ shū fàng zài zhuōzi xià.
    - Put the book under the table.
  - 我们下午三点见(我們下午三點見)。
    - Wǒmen xiàwǔ sān diǎn jiàn.
    - See you at three in the afternoon.
### 充值卡 → 卡 → 下 → 一
- **definition:** one; a horizontal line
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
