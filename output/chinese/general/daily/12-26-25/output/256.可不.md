## exactly; right
### kěbù
---
- **definition:** exactly; right
- **pinyin:** kěbù
- **components:**
  - 可
    - kě
    - can; may; able to
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 可(可) (kě, "can; may; able to") + 不(不) (bù, "not") =
    - The combination implies agreement or affirmation, capturing a sense of confirmation or correctness akin to saying "indeed" or "exactly" in English.
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 可不(kěbù) is used to affirm or agree with what someone else has said, similar to confirming with 'exactly' when one wants to show concurrence.
- **examples:**
  - 这家餐厅的菜真好吃(這家餐廳的菜真好吃)，可不(可不)？
    - Zhè jiā cāntīng de cài zhēn hǎochī, kěbù?
    - The food at this restaurant is really delicious; exactly, right?
  - 你说的话一点儿没夸张(你說的話一點兒沒誇張)，可不(可不)？
    - Nǐ shuō de huà yīdiǎnr méi kuāzhāng, kěbù?
    - What you said is not exaggerated at all; exactly, right?
### 可不(可不) → 可(可)
- **definition:** can; may; able to
- **pinyin:** kě
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 丂
    - kǎo
    - exhaled air
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") + 丂(丂) (kǎo, "exhaled air") =
    - sound from mouth ->
    - express agreement or permission ->
    - can
  - **interpretation:** The character 可(kě, "can") combines the semantic element 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") with a phonetic component 丂(丂) (kǎo, "exhaled air"), indicating an action performed with the mouth such as singing or exhaling. Over time, it has become associated with the sense of being allowed or able to do something, captured by the modern meaning 'can'.
- **examples:**
  - 只要你认真练习(只要你認真練習)，就可提高技能(就可提高技能)。
    - Zhǐyào nǐ rènzhēn liànxí, jiù kě tígāo jìnéng.

  - 你可相信自己的能力(你可相信自己的能力)。

### 可不(可不) → 不(不)
- **definition:** not
- **pinyin:** bù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the calyx of a flower
  - **interpretation:** The character 不 originally depicted a simple negation, with its shape resembling the calyx of a flower. Over time, it came to universally express negation, meaning 'not'.
- **examples:**
  - 我不喜欢喝茶(我不喜歡喝茶)。
    - Wǒ bù xǐhuān hē chá.
    - I do not like drinking tea.
  - 他不学习(他不學習)，也不锻炼(也不鍛煉)。
    - Tā bù xuéxí, yě bù duànliàn.
    - He does not study, nor does he exercise.
  - 这道菜不辣(這道菜不辣)。
    - Zhè dào cài bù là.
    - This dish is not spicy.
