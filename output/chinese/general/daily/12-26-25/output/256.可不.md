## exactly; right
### kěbù
---
- **definition:** exactly; right
- **pinyin:** kěbù
- **components:**
  - 可
    - kě
    - can; able to
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 可 (kě, "can; able to") and 不 (bù, "not") together form the phrase 可不 (kěbù, "exactly; right"). This expression is often used to express agreement or affirmation, similar to saying "that's right" or "exactly" in English. The combination uses 可 as an affirmative and keeps 不 in its negative form, but the expression as a whole conveys a positive agreement.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)菜( )好( )吃( )，可不？
    - Zhè cài hǎochī, kěbù?
    - This dish is delicious, right?
  - 今天工作很忙，可不！
    - Jīntiān gōngzuò hěn máng, kěbù!
    - Work was really busy today, exactly!
### 可不 → 可

- **pinyin:** kě
- **components:**
  - 口

  - 丂
    - kǎo

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic: 丂 (kǎo) =
    - reference to exhaled air "to sing" ->
    - capability to perform actions
  - **interpretation:** This character combines the semantic element 口 (kǒu, "mouth") with the phonetic 丂 (kǎo), referencing exhaling or vocal actions like singing, which suggests expressing capability or permission.

- **pinyin:** bù
