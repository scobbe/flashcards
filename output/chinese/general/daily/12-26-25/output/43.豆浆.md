### soy milk
---
- **pinyin:** soy milk
- **components:**
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean; legume; pea; pulse
  - 浆(漿)
    - jiāng
    - liquid; paste; pulp; emulsion
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 豆(豆) (dòu, "bean") + 漿(漿) (jiāng, "liquid") — bean liquid
  - **interpretation:** This term combines beans, the main ingredient, with liquid, indicating it is a drink made from beans. Soy milk is a popular beverage made by extracting the liquid from soybeans.
- **examples:**
  - 我想喝豆浆。
    - Wǒ xiǎng hē dòujiāng.
    - I want to drink soy milk.
  - 豆浆和油条是很好的早餐。
    - Dòujiāng hé yóutiáo shì hěn hǎo de zǎocān.
    - Soy milk and fried dough sticks make a great breakfast.
### 豆浆(豆漿) → 豆(豆)
- **definition:** bean; legume; pea; pulse
- **pinyin:** dòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a container, originally representing a type of seed.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a container that holds seeds, evoking the idea of beans that grow inside it.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃豆腐(我喜歡吃豆腐)，它很美味(它很好吃)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī dòufu, tā hěn měiwèi.
    - I like to eat tofu; it is very delicious.
  - 豆子(豆子)很营养(很營養)。
    - Dòuzi hěn yíngyǎng.
    - Beans are very nutritious.
### 豆浆(豆漿) → 浆(漿)
- **definition:** liquid; paste; pulp; emulsion
- **pinyin:** jiāng
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 薑
    - jiāng
    - ginger
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Consists of the semantic element 水 (water) and the phonetic element 薑 (ginger) which suggests a watery substance, hinting towards a liquid or paste.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of water and ginger evokes the idea of a liquid form, as ginger often appears in pastes or emulsions.
  - **simplification:** 漿 is simplified to 浆 by replacing the traditional form's component with a reduced character, maintaining the same meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我需要一些浆来做蛋糕(我需要一些漿來做蛋糕)。
    - Wǒ xūyào yīxiē jiāng lái zuò dànkāo.
    - I need some paste to make the cake.
  - 这浆的颜色很漂亮(這漿的顏色很漂亮)。
    - Zhè jiāng de yánsè hěn piàoliang.
    - The color of this paste is beautiful.
### 豆浆(豆漿) → 浆(漿) → 水(水)
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flowing water.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents the movement of water, evoking a sense of flow and fluidity. It is a direct representation of water as an element essential for life.
- **examples:**
  - 水是生命的来源(水是生命的來源)。
    - Shuǐ shì shēngmìng de láiyuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 我喜欢喝水(我喜歡喝水)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē shuǐ.
    - I like to drink water.
  - 海水很咸(海水很鹹)。
    - Hǎishuǐ hěn xián.
    - Sea water is very salty.
### 豆浆(豆漿) → 浆(漿) → 薑(薑)
- **definition:** ginger
- **pinyin:** jiāng
- **components:**
  - 姜
    - jiāng
    - ginger
  - 艹(艸)
    - cǎo
    - grass/plant
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** 薑 is composed of the phonetic component 姜 (jiāng, 'ginger') and the semantic component 艹, which relates to plants.
  - **interpretation:** The character emphasizes that ginger is a type of plant, combining the core meaning of 'ginger' with a representation of vegetation.
- **examples:**
  - 我们买了新鲜的姜(我們買了新鮮的薑)，准备做菜(準備做菜)。
    - wǒmen mǎile xīnxiān de jiāng, zhǔnbèi zuò cài.
    - We bought fresh ginger, preparing to cook.
  - 姜可以增加菜肴的风味(薑可以增加菜餚的風味)。
    - jiāng kěyǐ zēngjiā cài yáo de fēngwèi.
    - Ginger can enhance the flavor of dishes.

### 豆浆(豆漿) → 浆(漿) → 薑(薑) → 姜(姜) → 女(女)
- **definition:** female
- **pinyin:** nǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a woman with breasts kneeling or standing, facing the right side.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a woman, emphasizing the female form with the inclusion of breasts and body posture. This makes it intuitive for signifying femininity.
- **examples:**

  - 这个女孩非常善良。
    - zhège nǚhái fēicháng shànliáng。

### 豆浆(豆漿) → 浆(漿) → 薑(薑) → 艹(艸)
- **definition:** grass/plant
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts grass or plants, typically represented above ground.
  - **interpretation:** The character shows tufts of grass, symbolizing vegetation that grows close to the earth.
  - **simplification:** 艹 is the simplified form of 艸; the top radicals are reduced for ease of writing.
- **examples:**
  - 艹是草字头(艸是草字頭)。
    - cǎo shì cǎo zì tóu.
    - 艹 is the grass radical.
  - 许多汉字都有艹(許多漢字都有艸)。
    - xǔduō hànzì dōu yǒu cǎo.
    - Many Chinese characters have 艹.
