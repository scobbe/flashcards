## banyan tree
### róngshù
---
- **traditional:** 榕樹
- **definition:** banyan tree
- **pinyin:** róngshù
- **components:**
  - 榕
    - róng
    - banyan
  - 树(樹)
    - shù
    - tree
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 榕 (róng, "banyan"), 树(樹) (shù, "tree")
  - **interpretation:** 榕树(樹) (róngshù, "banyan tree") refers specifically to a type of tree known for its expansive roots and ability to grow in various shapes and forms. Banyan trees are significant in many cultures and landscapes, known for providing ample shade and often growing to impressive sizes.
- **examples:**
  - 村子里(裡)有一棵古老的榕树(樹)。
    - Cūnzi lǐ yǒu yī kē gǔlǎo de róngshù.
    - There is an ancient banyan tree in the village.
  - 公园里(園裡)有榕树(樹)，供人乘凉(涼)。
    - Gōngyuán lǐ yǒu róngshù, gōng rén chéngliáng.
    - There are banyan trees in the park, providing shade for people.
  - 大榕树(樹)下人们(們)聚集，谈(談)天说(說)地。
    - Dà róngshù xià rénmen jùjí, tán tiān shuō dì.
    - Under the large banyan tree, people gather and chat about everything.
### 榕( )树(樹) → 榕
- **definition:** banyan
- **pinyin:** róng
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 容
    - róng
    - contain
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 木 (mù, "tree") + phonetic 容 (róng, "contain") =
    - wood-related and pronounced as róng ->
    - banyan
  - **interpretation:** The character 榕(róng, "banyan") combines 木(mù, "tree") as the semantic component indicating relation to trees and nature with 容(róng, "contain") to suggest the sound, producing a character referring to a specific type of large, spreading tree known for its extensive branches.
- **examples:**
  - 榕树(樹)是热带(熱帶)雨林的重要组(組)成部分。
    - Róngshù shì rèdài yǔlín de zhòngyào zǔchéng bùfèn.
    - The banyan is an important part of the tropical rainforest.
  - 夏日里(裡)榕树(樹)下乘凉(涼)，学(學)童们(們)嬉戏(戲)玩耍。
    - Xiàrì lǐ róngshù xià chéngliáng, xuétóngmen xīxì wánshuǎ.
    - On a summer day, children cool off beneath the banyan while playing happily.
  - 古老的榕树(樹)庇护着(護著)小镇(鎮)。
    - Gǔlǎo de róngshù bìhù zhe xiǎozhèn.
    - The ancient banyan shelters the small town.

### 榕( )树(樹) → 榕 → 容 → 宀

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** 宀(mián, "roof") is a pictogram representing a house with four walls. It emphasizes an enclosed space, distinguishing it from similar structures like 广(guǎng, "broad") which depicts a building with three walls. This makes 宀 a logical choice for representing a roof in various characters.
- **examples:**

### 榕( )树(樹) → 榕 → 容 → 公 → 八

    - Wǒ zài bā diǎn, qǐchuáng kāishǐ yītiān de shēnghuó.
    - I wake up at eight o'clock to start my day.

### 榕( )树(樹) → 榕 → 容 → 公 → 厶
- **definition:** private
- **pinyin:** sī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Revolving around oneself ->
    - original character for 私 (sī, 'selfish; private')
  - **interpretation:** The character 厶(sī, 'private') visually represents the concept of 'revolving around oneself,' symbolizing the inward-focused nature of privacy and self-interest. It originally denoted the idea of 'private' by itself and is compared to 公(gōng, 'public'). It later became part of the character 私 (sī, 'selfish; private'), where 禾 (hé, 'grain') was added for the semantic aspect.
- **examples:**
  - 每个(個)人都有一片厶土供其独处(獨處)。
    - Měi gèrén dōu yǒu yī piàn sī tǔ gōng qí dúchǔ。
    - Everyone has a private patch of land for solitude.
  - 他将(將)所有厶事深藏心底。
    - Tā jiāng suǒyǒu sī shì shēncáng xīndǐ。
    - He hides all his private matters deep in his heart.

### 榕( )树(樹) → 树(樹) → 尌

  - 壴

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 木 (mù, "tree") + 寸 (cùn, "hand") =

The phonetic element 豆 was later added to address pronunciation, leading to components merging into 壴. However, today it is often mistaken as a combination of 壴 and 寸.
### 榕( )树(樹) → 树(樹) → 尌 → 寸
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Line on the hand (ideographic) emphasizing the elbow ->
    - represents measurement unit between wrist and specific forearm position ->
    - meaning 'inch'
  - **interpretation:** 寸 (cùn, "inch") originated as a depiction of a line on the hand, indicating the area near the elbow. This specific placement was considered significant for pulse palpation, originally suggesting a forearm position, which then came to denote the measurement 'inch'.
- **examples:**

### 榕( )树(樹) → 树(樹) → 尌 → 壴

- **pinyin:** zhù

  - **type:** pictogram
