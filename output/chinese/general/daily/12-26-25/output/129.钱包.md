## wallet
### qiánbāo
---
- **definition:** wallet
- **pinyin:** qiánbāo
- **components:**
  - 钱(錢)
    - qián
    - money
  - 包
    - bāo
    - bag
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The word 钱包(錢包) (qiánbāo) is a compound word which combines 钱(錢) (qián, "money") and 包(包) (bāo, "bag"). The combination literally means a 'money bag', which refers to a wallet.
  - **interpretation:** Combining 钱(錢) (qián, "money") and 包(包) (bāo, "bag"), 钱包(錢包) signifies a container or pouch for carrying money, fitting the modern concept of a wallet.
  - **simplification:** Traditional '錢' simplified to '钱'.
- **examples:**
  - 我丢了钱包(我丟了錢包)。
    - Wǒ diū le qiánbāo.
    - I lost my wallet.
  - 今天出门时(今天出門時)，我发现钱包不见了(我發現錢包不見了)。
    - Jīntiān chūmén shí, wǒ fāxiàn qiánbāo bùjiàn le.
    - Today when leaving home, I discovered that my wallet was missing.
### 钱包(錢包) → 钱(錢)
- **definition:** money
- **pinyin:** qián
- **components:**
  - 钅(釒)
    - jīn
    - metal
  - 戋(戔)
    - jiān
    - small
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 釒(釒) (jīn, "metal") + 戔(戔) (jiān, "small") ->
    - denotes coinage or currency made of metal
  - **interpretation:** The character 钱(錢) (qián, "money") combines 釒(釒) (jīn, "metal"), signifying currency or coins made from metal, and 戔(戔) (jiān, "small"), indicating small or fractional amounts. This suggests money as both metal-based and divisible.
  - **simplification:** The traditional component 釒(釒) (jīn, "metal") was simplified to 钅(钅) (jīn, "metal"). The component 戔(戔) (jiān, "small") was also simplified to 戋(戋) (jiān, "small").
- **examples:**
  - 我有钱(我有錢)。
    - Wǒ yǒu qián.
    - I have money.
  - 他花钱(他花錢)，买了新衣服(買了新衣服)。
    - Tā huā qián, mǎi le xīn yīfú.
    - He spent money and bought new clothes.
  - 没有钱(沒有錢)，我们不能旅行(我們不能旅行)。
    - Méiyǒu qián, wǒmen bùnéng lǚxíng.
    - Without money, we cannot travel.
