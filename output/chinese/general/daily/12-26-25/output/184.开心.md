### kāixīn
---
- **definition:** happy
- **pinyin:** kāixīn
- **components:**
  - 开(開)
    - kāi
    - open; turn on; begin; operate
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart; mind; center; intention
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 开(開) (kāi, "open") and 心(心) (xīn, "heart") together suggest the idea of one's heart being open, which metaphorically conveys a sense of joy or happiness.
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 开心(開心) conveys the image of an 'open heart,' symbolizing a state of happiness or contentment, as if one's heart is unrestrained and free.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天很开心(我今天很開心)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān hěn kāixīn.
    - I am very happy today.
  - 她和朋友一起出游(她和朋友一起出遊)，感到非常开心(感到非常開心)。
    - Tā hé péngyǒu yīqǐ chūyóu, gǎndào fēicháng kāixīn.
    - She went out with her friends and felt very happy.
### 开心(開心) → 开(開)
- **definition:** open; turn on; begin; operate
- **pinyin:** kāi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 門(門) (mén, "door") + 一(一) (yī, "latch") + 廾(廾) (gǒng, "hands") → pair of hands opening latched door → opening
  - **interpretation:** The traditional form 開(開) (kāi, "open") symbolizes opening a door with hands, where 門(門) (mén, "door") represents the door itself, while 一(一) (yī, "latch") and 廾(廾) (gǒng, "hands") act together to indicate removal of a barrier.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 開 by eliminating 門(門) (mén, "door"), retaining only the 幵(幵) (components resembling two 干) which focuses on the concept of trunk parts, visually simplifying it without changing the meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 请开门(請開門)。
    - Qǐng kāi mén.
    - Please open the door.
  - 他开了灯(他開了燈)。
    - Tā kāile dēng.
    - He turned on the light.
  - 天微亮时(天微亮時)，商店开门营业(商店開門營業)。
    - Tiān wēiliàng shí, shāngdiàn kāimén yíngyè.
    - At dawn, the store opened for business.
### 开心(開心) → 心(心)
- **definition:** heart; mind; center; intention
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the shape of a heart organ with three lobes
  - **interpretation:** The character 心(xīn, 'heart') visually resembles a heart organ with its three internal sections, emphasizing its role as a core or central part of the human body.
- **examples:**
  - 我的心充满了爱(我的心充滿了愛)。
    - Wǒ de xīn chōngmǎnle ài.
    - My heart is filled with love.
  - 他把心放在艺术上(他把心放在藝術上)，追求完美(追求完美)。
    - Tā bǎ xīn fàng zài yìshù shàng, zhuīqiú wánměi.
    - He devotes his heart to art and pursues perfection.
