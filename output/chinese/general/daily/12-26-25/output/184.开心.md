## happy
### kāixīn
---
- **definition:** happy
- **pinyin:** kāixīn
- **components:**
  - 开(開)
    - kāi
    - open; start
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart; mind
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 開心(开心) comprises 開(开) (kāi, "open") and 心(心) (xīn, "heart"). The combination suggests opening one's heart, allowing happiness and joy to enter.
  - **interpretation:** The term 開心(开心) evokes the image of opening one's heart to let in happiness and positive emotions. It is a vivid way of expressing the state of feeling happy or joyous.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天感觉很开心(我今天感覺很開心)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān gǎnjué hěn kāixīn.
    - I feel happy today.
  - 他听到好消息(他聽到好消息)，变得异常开心(變得異常開心)。
    - Tā tīngdào hǎo xiāoxī, biàn de yìcháng kāixīn.
    - He became extremely happy after hearing the good news.
  - 朋友聚会时(朋友聚會時)，大家都很开心(大家都很開心)。
    - Péngyǒu jùhuì shí, dàjiā dōu hěn kāixīn.
    - Everyone is happy during the friends' gathering.
### 开心(開心) → 开(開)
- **definition:** open; start
- **pinyin:** kāi
- **components:**
  - 門
    - mén
    - door
  - 一
    - yī
    - latch or pole
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - pair of hands
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 門(門) (mén, 'door') + 一(一) (yī, 'latch or wooden pole') + 廾(廾) (gǒng, 'pair of hands') =
    - hands opening a latched door ->
    - open; simplified to 开 by removing 門
  - **interpretation:** The traditional character 開 shows a pair of hands opening a latched door. In the simplified form 开, the representation of 門. (door) was removed to streamline writing while maintaining the essence of the action of 'opening'.
  - **simplification:** Simplified 开 from 開 by removing the 門 (mén, 'door') to keep the focus on the action with 干 (gān) representing the trunks or latches.
- **examples:**
  - 我开车去上班(我開車去上班)。
    - Wǒ kāichē qù shàngbān.
    - I drive to work.
  - 花儿开得很灿烂(花兒開得很燦爛)。
    - Huār kāi de hěn cànlàn.
    - The flowers are blooming brilliantly.
  - 他打开窗户(他打開窗戶)，迎进清新的空气(迎進清新的空氣)。
    - Tā dǎkāi chuānghù, yíng jìn qīngxīn de kōngqì.
    - He opens the window to let in the fresh air.
### 开心(開心) → 开(開) → 門(門)
- **definition:** door
- **pinyin:** mén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a double-door gate
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts a pair of doors, visually representing the concept of a door or gate, which is fundamental in indicating entrance and exit points in structures.
- **examples:**
  - 我走到门口(我走到門口)。
    - Wǒ zǒu dào ménkǒu.
    - I walk to the door.
  - 门轻轻打开(門輕輕打開)，房客进来了(房客進來了)。
    - Mén qīngqīng dǎkāi, fángkè jìn láile.
    - The door opened gently, and the tenant came in.
### 开心(開心) → 开(開) → 一(一)
- **definition:** latch or pole
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** A horizontal stroke representing the concept of 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** The character 一(yī, "one") is a simple horizontal line signifying the singular and unified concept of 'one'. It directly represents being singular or the number one.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一个苹果(我有一個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ yǒu yī gè píngguǒ.
    - I have one apple.
  - 他只看到一只鸟(他只看到一隻鳥)，飞进了花园(飛進了花園)。
    - Tā zhǐ kàn dào yī zhī niǎo, fēi jìn le huā yuán.
    - He only saw one bird, which flew into the garden.
  - 一条直路通向远方(一條直路通向遠方)。
    - Yī tiáo zhí lù tōng xiàng yuǎn fāng.
    - One straight road leads to the distance.
### 开心(開心) → 开(開) → 廾(廾)
- **definition:** pair of hands
- **pinyin:** gǒng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a pair of hands
  - **interpretation:** The character 廾(gǒng, "pair of hands") is a simple pictogram representing two hands facing each other, symbolizing the act of holding or carrying something.
- **examples:**
  - 字‘廾’表示一双手(字‘廾’表示一雙手)。
    - Zì 'gǒng' biǎoshì yī shuāng shǒu.
    - The character '廾' represents a pair of hands.
  - 古代学者观察到汉字中用廾构成手的形状(古代學者觀察到漢字中用廾構成手的形狀)，以表达劳动的意义(以表達勞動的意義)。
    - Gǔdài xuézhě guānchá dào hànzì zhōng yòng gǒng gòuchéng shǒu de xíngzhuàng, yǐ biǎodá láodòng de yìyì.
    - Ancient scholars observed that in Chinese characters, '廾' is used to form the shape of a hand in order to express the meaning of labor.
  - 艺术家以廾为灵感创作雕塑(藝術家以廾為靈感創作雕塑)，展示人与工艺的智慧(展示人與工藝的智慧)。
    - Yìshùjiā yǐ gǒng wéi línggǎn chuàngzuò diāosù, zhǎnshì rén yǔ gōngyì de zhìhuì.
    - The artist used '廾' as inspiration to create a sculpture, showcasing the wisdom of human craft.
### 开心(開心) → 心(心)
- **definition:** heart; mind
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stylized representation of a heart
  - **interpretation:** The character 心(xīn, 'heart') visually represents the shape of a heart, emphasizing its central and essential role in both a physical and metaphorical sense.
- **examples:**
  - 他的心温暖无比(他的心溫暖無比)。
    - Tā de xīn wēnnuǎn wúbǐ.
    - His heart is incredibly warm.
  - 她把心放在艺术上(她把心放在藝術上)，追求完美(追求完美)。
    - Tā bǎ xīn fàng zài yìshù shàng, zhuīqiú wánměi.
    - She dedicates her heart to art, pursuing perfection.
  - 音乐直击心灵(音樂直擊心靈)。
    - Yīnyuè zhí jī xīnlíng.
    - Music strikes straight at the heart (soul).
