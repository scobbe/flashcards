---
## 
### vegan
---
- **pinyin:** vegan
- **components:**
  - 纯(純)
    - chún
    - pure; genuine; unadulterated; clear
  - 素
    - sù
    - vegetarian; plain; simple; element
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 纯(純) (chún, 'pure') + 素 (sù, 'vegetarian; plain') — pure vegetarian
  - **interpretation:** This term refers to a diet that is entirely plant-based, with no animal products. The combination of 'pure' and 'vegetarian' emphasizes a strict adherence to not consuming any animal-derived ingredients.
- **examples:**
  - 我开始吃纯素食(我開始吃純素食)。
    - Wǒ kāishǐ chī chún sù shí.
    - I have started eating vegan food.
  - 这家餐厅提供纯素的菜单(這家餐廳提供純素的菜單)。
    - Zhè jiā cāntīng tígōng chún sù de càidān.
    - This restaurant offers a vegan menu.
### 纯素(純素) → 纯(純)
- **definition:** pure; genuine; unadulterated; clear
- **pinyin:** chún
- **components:**
  - 糸(絲)
    - sī
    - thread
  - 屯
    - tún
    - to reside
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Semantic 糸 (絲) (sī, "thread") + phonetic 屯 (屯) (tún, "to reside") — conveys the meaning of 'without any impurities' and 'exclusively'.
  - **interpretation:** The character intuitively suggests a pure state, like a single thread that is free from impurities and distinct from others, which emphasizes its clarity and genuineness.
- **examples:**
  - 这件衣服的质地非常纯(這件衣服的質地非常純)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfú de zhìdì fēicháng chún.
    - The fabric of this garment is very pure.
  - 他的人格非常纯(他的人格非常純)。
    - Tā de réngé fēicháng chún.
    - His character is very genuine.
  - 这种水是纯净的(這種水是純淨的)。
    - Zhè zhǒng shuǐ shì chúnjìng de.
    - This kind of water is pure.
### 纯素(純素) → 纯(純) → 糸(糸)
- **definition:** thread
- **pinyin:** sī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a thread, originally used to represent silk.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents the delicate threads of silk, emphasizing its significance in textiles.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān sīchóu.
    - I like silk.
  - 他买了一条丝带(他買了一條絲帶)。
    - Tā mǎile yītiáo sīdài.
    - He bought a silk ribbon.

### 纯素(純素) → 素(素)

- **pinyin:** sù

  - 须(須)
    - xū
    - sound, must
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk, thread
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** The character 素 is composed of the phonetic element '须' (xū), which suggests sound, and the semantic element '糸' (mì), which relates to threads or silk. This reflects the idea of plainness or simplicity in textiles and materials.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of a sound element and a visual element related to silk creates a sense of simplicity and purity, as silk is often associated with fine fabrics that can also have a plain or basic style.
- **examples:**

    - His sketch is beautiful.
  - 这件素衣很舒服(這件素衣很舒服)。
    - Zhè jiàn sù yī hěn shūfú.

### 纯素(純素) → 素(素) → 须(須) → 虯(虯) → 虫(蟲)
- **definition:** insect
- **pinyin:** huǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Represents the concept of insects through the repetition of 虫, which denotes 'snake; insect'.
  - **interpretation:** The repeated use of 虫 suggests a multitude of insects, evoking the idea of various creeping or crawling creatures.
  - **simplification:** 蟲 (cóng) was simplified to 虫 (huǐ) by removing the component '虫' (虫) while retaining its meaning related to insects.
- **examples:**
  - 我看到了虫子(我看到了蟲子)。
    - Wǒ kàn dào le huǐzi.
    - I saw the insect.
  - 这儿有很多虫子(這兒有很多蟲子)。
    - Zhè er yǒu hěn duō huǐzi.
    - There are many insects here.
