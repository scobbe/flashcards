## vegan
### chúnsù
---
- **traditional:** 純素
- **definition:** vegan
- **pinyin:** chúnsù
- **components:**
  - 纯(純)
    - chún
    - pure
  - 素
    - sù
    - simple; plain; vegetarian
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 纯(純) (chún, "pure") and 素 (sù, "simple; plain; vegetarian") come together to describe a diet or lifestyle free of animal products. The use of 素 (sù, "simple; plain; vegetarian") has historical roots as a term for vegetarianism, and 纯(純) (chún, "pure") emphasizes the complete exclusion of animal-derived ingredients.
  - **interpretation:** The term 纯(純)素 (chúnsù, "vegan") reflects an emphasis on a wholly plant-based lifestyle, avoiding any animal products. By combining the ideas of purity and simplicity or plainness, it effectively conveys a focus on ethical and dietary clarity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只吃纯(純)素食物。
    - Wǒ zhǐ chī chúnsù shíwù.
    - I only eat vegan food.
  - 她开(開)了一家纯(純)素餐厅(廳)，提供各种(種)美味菜肴(餚)。
    - Tā kāile yī jiā chúnsù cāntīng, tígōng gèzhǒng měiwèi càiyáo.
    - She opened a vegan restaurant, offering a variety of delicious dishes.
  - 选择纯(選擇純)素生活，关爱动(關愛動)物。
    - Xuǎnzé chúnsù shēnghuó, guān'ài dòngwù.
    - Choose a vegan lifestyle, care for animals.
### 纯(純)素( ) → 纯(純)
- **traditional:** 純
- **definition:** pure
- **pinyin:** chún
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk
  - 屯
    - tún
    - station
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 糸 (mì, "silk") + phonetic: 屯 (tún, "station") =
    - silk quality ->
    - pure
  - **interpretation:** 纯(純) (chún, "pure") uses silk, symbolizing fine quality, combined with 屯 (tún, "station") to express the idea of pure and unadulterated silk.
  - **simplification:** The character 纯(純) was simplified by removing the middle component from 屯 while retaining the recognizable phonetic and semantic elements.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)水真纯(純)。
    - Zhè shuǐ zhēn chún.
    - This water is very pure.
  - 她的心灵纯洁(靈純潔)，她的笑容纯(純)真。
    - Tā de xīnlíng chúnjié, tā de xiàoróng chúnzhēn.
    - Her heart is pure, and her smile is genuine.
  - 这(這)首音乐纯(樂純)粹动(動)人。
    - Zhè shǒu yīnyuè chúncuì dòngrén.
    - This piece of music is purely moving.

### 纯(純)素( ) → 纯(純) → 屯
- **definition:** station
- **pinyin:** tún
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè
    - sprout
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 屮 (chè, "sprout") + dot ->
    - line modified, emphasizing sprout ->
    - modern form 屯
  - **interpretation:** The character 屯 originally depicted a plant sprout, represented by 屮 (chè, "sprout") with a central dot that was later transformed into a horizontal line to emphasize the sprout. It symbolizes the idea of sprouting or beginning, which connects with its broader meaning of 'station,' a place where things start or are based.
- **examples:**
  - 军队(軍隊)在边(邊)境屯，准备随时(準備隨時)出击(擊)。
    - Jūnduì zài biānjìng tún, zhǔnbèi suíshí chūjī.
    - The army is stationed at the border, ready to launch an attack at any moment.
  - 新军(軍)屯选(選)址在交通枢纽(樞紐)，利于(於)快速集结(結)。
    - Xīn jūn tún xuǎnzhǐ zài jiāotōng shūniǔ, lì yú kuàisù jíjié.
    - The new military station is located at a transportation hub, which facilitates rapid assembly.
### 纯(純)素( ) → 纯(純) → 屯 → 屮
- **definition:** sprout
- **pinyin:** chè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a sprout emerging from the ground
  - **interpretation:** 屮 (chè, 'sprout') is a pictogram that directly represents a young plant or sprout emerging from the ground. Its visual form captures the essence of new growth, and it historically served as an early representation of plant life or new vegetation.
- **examples:**
  - 春雨滋润后(潤後)，小屮冲(沖)破泥土而出。

  - 草屮初生露尖端。
    - cǎo chè chū shēng lù jiān duān.
    - The grass sprout's tip has just emerged.

### 纯(純)素( ) → 素 → 粟
- **definition:** millet
- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 角
    - jiǎo
    - horn
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
  - 西
    - xī
    - west
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Oracle bone script shows seeds → 角 (jiǎo, "horn") for phonetic + 米 (mǐ, "rice") for semantic → 角 evolved to 𠧪 → further changed to 西 (xī, "west")
  - **interpretation:** Initially depicting seeds, 粟(sù, "millet") combined phonetic and semantic elements. 米 (mǐ, "rice") indicates the grain aspect, while 角 (jiǎo) initially added sound. Over time, the original depiction evolved through several script forms to match pronunciation changes.
- **examples:**
  - 秋天丰(豐)收时(時)，田野中的粟闪着(閃著)金光。
    - Qiūtiān fēngshōu shí, tiányě zhōng de sù shǎnzhe jīnguāng.
    - During the autumn harvest, the millet in the fields glimmers with golden light.
  - 农(農)夫把粟从(從)田里(裡)收集，准备储(準備儲)存过(過)冬。
    - Nóngfū bǎ sù cóng tián lǐ shōují, zhǔnbèi chúcún guòdōng.
    - The farmer collects the millet from the fields, preparing to store it for the winter.
  - 古代诗(詩)人常以粟象征丰(徵豐)收，歌颂农(頌農)事的喜悦(悅)。
    - Gǔdài shīrén cháng yǐ sù xiàngzhēng fēngshōu, gēsòng nóngshì de xǐyuè.
    - Ancient poets often used millet to symbolize a bountiful harvest, praising the joy of farming.

### 纯(純)素( ) → 素 → 粟 → 西
- **definition:** west
- **pinyin:** xī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a bag or basket ->
    - borrowed for phonetic value to mean 'west'
  - **interpretation:** The character 西(xī, 'west') originally depicted a bag or basket and was phonetically borrowed to represent 'west'. Although traditionally linked to the image of a bird in a nest symbolizing the setting sun, this interpretation is disputed. Instead, it reflects a simple phonetic borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他家住在西边(邊)，附近有一座大山。
    - Tā jiā zhù zài xībiān, fùjìn yǒu yī zuò dà shān.
    - His home is on the west side, and there is a big mountain nearby.
  - 风筝(風箏)向西飞(飛)去。
    - Fēngzhēng xiàng xī fēi qù.
    - The kite flies toward the west.
