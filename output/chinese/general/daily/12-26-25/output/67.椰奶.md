## coconut milk
### yēnǎi
---
- **definition:** coconut milk
- **pinyin:** yēnǎi
- **components:**
  - 椰
    - yē
    - coconut
  - 奶
    - nǎi
    - milk
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 椰 (yē, "coconut") + 奶 (nǎi, "milk") =
    - milk from coconut
  - **interpretation:** 椰奶 (yēnǎi, "coconut milk") combines 椰 (yē, "coconut") and 奶 (nǎi, "milk") to refer to milk that is derived from coconuts. This makes sense as it describes the liquid often used in cooking and beverages, providing a creamy, tropical flavor.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)椰奶。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān yēnǎi.
    - I like coconut milk.
  - 夏天来临时(來臨時)，椰奶搭配新鲜(鮮)水果。
    - Xiàtiān láilín shí, yēnǎi dāpèi xīnxiān shuǐguǒ.
    - When summer arrives, coconut milk pairs with fresh fruits.
  - 他做的咖喱需要椰奶，以增加浓(濃)郁风(風)味。
    - Tā zuò de gālí xūyào yēnǎi, yǐ zēngjiā nóngyù fēngwèi.
    - The curry he makes requires coconut milk to enhance its rich flavor.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 木 (mù, "tree") + phonetic: 耶 (yē, a phonetic component) =

  - 我买(買)了一颗(顆)椰子。

- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**

  - **description:** Depicts a tree with branches and roots
  - **interpretation:** The character 木(mù) depicts a tree, showing the trunk in the center and two slanting lines representing branches or roots. It directly signifies the concept of wood or a tree.
- **examples:**

### 椰奶 → 椰 → 耶
- **definition:** phonetic component
- **pinyin:** yē
- **components:**
  - 邪
    - yé
    - wrong
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** corruption
  - **description:**
    - Corruption of 邪 (yé, "wrong") ->
    - phonetic component
  - **interpretation:** The character 耶(yē) evolved as a corruption of 邪 (yé, "wrong") to serve as a phonetic component in Chinese characters. The original character 邪 combined 牙 (yá, "tooth") as the phonetic part and 邑 (yì, "town; city") as the semantic part, suggesting its use in place names like 琅邪(lángyé).
- **examples:**
  - 这个(這個)字的声(聲)旁是耶。
    - Zhège zì de shēngpáng shì yē.
    - The phonetic component of this character is yē.
  - 他研究汉(漢)字构(構)造，发现(發現)耶常作表音符号(號)。
    - Tā yánjiū hànzì gòuzào， fāxiàn yē cháng zuò biǎoyīn fúhào.
    - He studies the structure of Chinese characters, and finds that yē often serves as the phonetic element.
### 椰奶 → 椰 → 耶 → 邪
- **definition:** wrong
- **pinyin:** yé
- **components:**
  - 牙
    - yá
    - tooth
  - 邑
    - yì
    - town; city
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 牙 (yá, "tooth") + Semantic: 邑 (yì, "town; city") =
    - originally a place name 琅邪 ->
    - borrowed for 'wrong; evil'
  - **interpretation:** The character 邪(yé, 'wrong') originated from its use in place names, with the phonetic 牙(yá, 'tooth') suggesting sound and the semantic 邑(yì, 'town; city') indicating its geographical associations. Over time, this character was borrowed to represent the idea of wrongness or evil.
- **examples:**
  - 有人散布(播)邪说(說)。
    - Yǒurén sànbù yéshuō.
    - Some people spread wrong doctrines.

    - He believes in the truth and rejects wrong views.
  - 流言蜚语(語)常常掺杂(摻雜)邪成分。
    - Liúyán fēiyǔ chángcháng chānzá yé chéngfèn.
    - Rumors are often tainted with wrong elements.
### 椰奶 → 椰 → 耶 → 邪 → 牙
- **definition:** tooth
- **pinyin:** yá
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a pair of elephant tusks ->
    - used to indicate human molars
  - **interpretation:** The character 牙 (yá, 'tooth') originated as a pictogram representing elephant tusks, which resemble human molars in shape. This specific imagery was chosen to signify back teeth rather than incisors.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天刷牙。
    - Wǒ měitiān shuā yá.
    - I brush my teeth every day.
  - 医(醫)生检(檢)查牙齿(齒)，建议(議)定期看牙。
    - Yīshēng jiǎnchá yá chǐ, jiànyì dìngqī kàn yá.
    - The doctor examines the teeth and advises regular dental check-ups.

### 椰奶 → 奶
- **definition:** milk
- **pinyin:** nǎi
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - woman
  - 乃
    - nǎi
    - so
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 女 (nǚ, 'woman') + phonetic 乃 (nǎi, 'so') =
    - related to women, sound similar to 乃 ->
    - 'milk'
  - **interpretation:** 奶(nǎi) combines the semantic component 女 (nǚ, 'woman') to indicate a connection with women, specifically relating to breastfeeding, and 乃 (nǎi, 'so') as a phonetic component. This structure makes intuitive sense as it suggests a term linked to women, utilizing a sound similar to the word for nurturing.
- **examples:**
  - 妈妈给宝宝(媽媽給寶寶)喝奶。
    - Māmā gěi bǎobǎo hē nǎi.
    - Mom gives the baby milk.
  - 牛奶可以帮(幫)助人体(體)吸收营养(營養)，尤其是儿(兒)童。
    - Niúnǎi kěyǐ bāngzhù réntǐ xīshōu yíngyǎng, yóuqí shì értóng.
    - Milk can help the body absorb nutrients, especially for children.
  - 他在超市买(買)了一瓶奶，打算明天做甜点(點)。
    - Tā zài chāoshì mǎile yī píng nǎi, dǎsuàn míngtiān zuò tiándiǎn.
    - He bought a bottle of milk at the supermarket and plans to make dessert tomorrow.
### 椰奶 → 奶 → 女
- **definition:** woman
- **pinyin:** nǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a woman kneeling or standing ->
    - enclosed area as remnant of right breast
  - **interpretation:** The character 女(nǚ, "woman") is a pictogram originally depicting a woman with highlighted bodily features, such as the breasts, symbolizing femininity. Its orientation changed from the Oracle Bone script to face right, simplifying over time while still retaining the essence of its original depiction.
- **examples:**
  - 那位才华横(華橫)溢的女，赢(贏)得了全场(場)注目。
    - Nà wèi cáihuá héngyì de nǚ, yíng dé le quánchǎng zhùmù.
    - That talented woman won everyone's attention.
  - 古时候‘女’常被用来象征温柔(古時候‘女’常被用來象徵溫柔)，而如今它代表独(獨)立自信。
    - Gǔshíhòu ‘nǚ’ cháng bèi yòng lái xiàngzhēng wēnróu, ér rújīn tā dàibiǎo dúlì zìxìn.
    - In ancient times, the character '女' was often used to symbolize gentleness, whereas today it represents independence and confidence.
### 椰奶 → 奶 → 乃
- **definition:** so
- **pinyin:** nǎi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a simplified hand holding a thread
  - **interpretation:** 乃 (nǎi, 'so') originally depicted a hand holding an object, symbolizing a connection or linkage, which lends itself to meanings related to causation and result, like 'so' or 'then.'
- **examples:**
  - 他受尽(盡)磨难(難)，乃成大器。
    - Tā shòu jìn mó nàn, nǎi chéng dà qì.
    - He endured many hardships, so he became great.

    - Shān míng shuǐ xiù, nǎi lìng rén xīn kuàng shén yí.
    - The scenery is picturesque, so it refreshes one's mind.
