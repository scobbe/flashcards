## strange
### qíguài
---
- **definition:** strange
- **pinyin:** qíguài
- **components:**
  - 奇
    - qí
    - strange; odd; unique; rare
  - 怪
    - guài
    - weird; strange; blame; monster
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 奇 (qí, "strange; odd") and 怪 (guài, "weird; strange") combine to form 奇怪 (qíguài, "strange").
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 奇 (qí, "strange; odd") and 怪 (guài, "weird; strange") strengthens the idea of 'strange' by bringing together two characters that convey unusualness or abnormality.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)件事情真奇怪。
    - Zhè jiàn shìqíng zhēn qíguài.
    - This matter is really strange.
  - 他看起来(來)的表情很奇怪，让(讓)大家感到困惑。
    - Tā kàn qǐlái de biǎoqíng hěn qíguài, ràng dàjiā gǎndào kùnhuò.
    - His expression looked strange, making everyone feel puzzled.
### 奇怪 → 奇
- **definition:** strange; odd; unique; rare
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big
  - 可
    - kě
    - to allow
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 大 (dà, "big") + phonetic: 可 (kě, "to allow") =
    - something that can be unusually large or remarkable ->
    - strange or unique
  - **interpretation:** The character 奇 (qí, "strange") combines 大 (dà, "big") as its semantic component, suggesting size or prominence, with 可 (kě, "to allow") as a phonetic component, indicating sound and the possibility of something being unusual or distinctive.
- **examples:**
  - 他的想法真奇。
    - Tā de xiǎngfǎ zhēn qí.
    - His ideas are truly unique.
  - 他戴了一顶(頂)奇帽。
    - Tā dài le yī dǐng qí mào.
    - He wore an unusual hat.
  - 空中的云(雲)彩形状(狀)奇，令人联(聯)想到梦(夢)幻世界。
    - Kōng zhōng de yún cǎi xíng zhuàng qí, lìng rén liánxiǎng dào mènghuàn shì jiè.
    - The shapes of the clouds in the sky are peculiar, evoking a dreamlike world.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 大
- **definition:** big
- **pinyin:** dà
- **components:**
  - 夫
    - fū
    - man
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a person facing forward ->
    - Borrowed phonetically for 'big'; original form of 夫 (fū, 'man')
  - **interpretation:** The character 大(dà) depicts a person facing forward, representing 'big' by suggesting the idea of someone standing prominently. Although often thought of as a person with outstretched arms, early scripts do not support this interpretation. It was originally the form of 夫 (fū, 'man') and was later phonetically borrowed for the meaning 'big.'
- **examples:**
  - 这个(這個)房子很大。
    - Zhè ge fángzi hěn dà.
    - This house is very big.
  - 大海波涛汹涌(濤洶湧)，展现(現)出大自然的力量。
    - Dàhǎi bōtāo xiōngyǒng, zhǎnxiàn chū dàzìrán de lìliàng.
    - The vast ocean waves surge, showcasing the power of nature.
  - 大人物登场(場)，留下传(傳)奇。
    - Dà rénwù dēngchǎng, liúxià chuánqí.
    - Great figures make an appearance, leaving behind legends.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 大 → 夫
- **definition:** man
- **pinyin:** fū
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a variant form of 大 (dà, "big") representing an adult man with a hairpin ->
    - became associated with 夫 (fū, "man").
  - **interpretation:** 夫 (fū, "man") was visually similar to 大 (dà, "big") and was originally interchangeable. The addition of the horizontal stroke was interpreted as a hairpin, symbolizing adulthood for men.
- **examples:**
  - 此夫心怀(懷)大志。
    - Cǐ fū xīn huái dà zhì.
    - This man has lofty aspirations.
  - 他勤劳(勞)努力，堪称(稱)良夫。
    - Tā qín láo nǔ lì, kān chēng liáng fū.
    - He works diligently and is regarded as an exemplary man.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 可
- **definition:** to allow
- **pinyin:** kě
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 丂
    - kǎo
    - exhaled air
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic: 丂 (kǎo) =
    - reference to exhaled air "to sing" ->
    - capability to perform actions
  - **interpretation:** This character combines the semantic element 口 (kǒu, "mouth") with the phonetic 丂 (kǎo), referencing exhaling or vocal actions like singing, which suggests expressing capability or permission.
- **examples:**
  - 你可尝试(嘗試)一下新方法。
    - Nǐ kě chángshì yīxià xīn fāngfǎ.
    - You may try a new method.
  - 此事虽难(雖難)，你可坚(堅)持到底。
    - Cǐ shì suī nán, nǐ kě jiānchí dàodǐ.
    - Although this matter is difficult, you may persist to the end.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 可 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 可 → 丂
- **definition:** exhaled air
- **pinyin:** kǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts air arising or exhaled from the mouth
  - **interpretation:** The character 丂(kǎo, "exhaled air") visually represents the action of exhaling, capturing the concept through its shape resembling air moving upward.
### 奇怪 → 怪
- **definition:** weird; strange; blame; monster
- **pinyin:** guài
- **components:**
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart
  - 圣(聖)
    - shèng
    - sacred
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 心 (xīn, "heart") + phonetic: 圣(聖) (shèng, "sacred") =
    - heart associated with sacred or unusual ->
    - weird, strange
  - **interpretation:** The character 怪 (guài, "weird") combines 心 (xīn, "heart") to represent emotions or feelings and 圣(聖) (shèng, "sacred") to convey a sense of the extraordinary or unusual. Together, they emphasize a feeling connected with strange or unusual occurrences.
- **examples:**
  - 他的笑容看起来(來)很怪。
    - Tā de xiàoróng kàn qǐlái hěn guài.
    - His smile looks very strange.
  - 妈妈(媽媽)不怪我，因为(為)我已经尽(經盡)力了。
    - Māmā bù guài wǒ, yīnwèi wǒ yǐjīng jìnlì le.
    - Mom doesn't blame me because I have done my best.
  - 傍晚时(時)分，远处传来(遠處傳來)怪响(響)。
    - Bàngwǎn shífēn, yuǎnchù chuán lái guài xiǎng.
    - At dusk, strange sounds came from afar.
### 奇怪 → 怪 → 心
- **definition:** heart
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stylized representation of a heart
  - **interpretation:** The character 心(xīn, 'heart') visually represents the shape of a heart, emphasizing its central and essential role in both a physical and metaphorical sense.
- **examples:**
  - 他的心温(溫)暖无(無)比。
    - Tā de xīn wēnnuǎn wúbǐ.
    - His heart is incredibly warm.
  - 她把心放在艺术(藝術)上，追求完美。
    - Tā bǎ xīn fàng zài yìshù shàng, zhuīqiú wánměi.
    - She dedicates her heart to art, pursuing perfection.
  - 音乐(樂)直击(擊)心灵(靈)。
    - Yīnyuè zhí jī xīnlíng.
    - Music strikes straight at the heart (soul).
### 奇怪 → 怪 → 圣(聖)
- **traditional:** 聖
- **definition:** sacred
- **pinyin:** shèng
- **components:**
  - 耳
    - ěr
    - ear
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 耳 (ěr, "ear") + 王 (wáng, "king") =
    - ear of the king ->
    - sacred
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 耳 (ěr, "ear") and 王 (wáng, "king") in its traditional form, symbolizing the concept of sacredness or holiness by alluding to a ruler who listens attentively.
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 聖 was simplified to 圣 by removing the components 耳 (ěr, "ear") and modifying the remaining part to retain phonetic and semantic elements in a more concise form.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)座寺庙(廟)被视为圣(視為聖)地。
    - Zhè zuò sìmiào bèi shìwéi shèngdì.
    - This temple is regarded as a sacred place.
  - 她的微笑让(讓)人感受到一种圣洁(種聖潔)的温(溫)暖。
    - Tā de wéixiào ràng rén gǎnshòudào yī zhǒng shèngjié de wēnnuǎn.
    - Her smile evokes a sense of sacred warmth.
  - 他在祈祷时(禱時)虔诚(誠)地低语(語)，心中充满圣(滿聖)意。
    - Tā zài qídǎo shí qiánchéng de dīyǔ, xīnzhōng chōngmǎn shèngyì.
    - He whispered devoutly in prayer, his heart full of sacred intent.
### 奇怪 → 怪 → 圣(聖) → 耳
- **definition:** ear
- **pinyin:** ěr
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an ear
  - **interpretation:** The character 耳 (ěr, "ear") is a pictogram that visually represents an ear with its distinctive shape. This straightforward representation made it intuitive for ancient speakers to associate the character directly with the concept of an ear.
- **examples:**
  - 音乐(樂)直入耳。
    - Yīn yuè zhí rù ěr.
    - The music goes straight to the ear.
  - 他亲(親)耳听(聽)到谣(謠)言，心中充满(滿)疑虑(慮)。
    - Tā qīn ěr tīng dào yáo yán, xīn zhōng chōng mǎn yí lǜ.
    - He heard the rumor with his own ears, and his heart was filled with doubt.
### 奇怪 → 怪 → 圣(聖) → 王
- **definition:** king
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a ritual axe representing the king's authority
  - **interpretation:** This character represents the form of a ceremonial axe, which was the symbol of a king's power in ancient China. The structure emphasizes the sacred and authoritative role of the king, separate from the nuanced folk interpretations linking Heaven, Man, and Earth.
- **examples:**
  - 他被称为(稱為)王，领导(領導)人民。
    - Tā bèi chēng wéi wáng, lǐngdǎo rénmín.
    - He is called the king, leading his people.
  - 古代的君主常被尊称为(稱為)王。
    - Gǔdài de jūnzhǔ cháng bèi zūnchēng wéi wáng.
    - Ancient rulers were often respectfully called kings.
  - 在祭祀仪(儀)式上，人们(們)向王致敬。
    - Zài jìsì yíshì shàng, rénmen xiàng wáng zhìjìng.
    - During the ceremonial sacrifices, people salute the king.
