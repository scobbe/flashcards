## strange
### qíguài
---
- **definition:** strange
- **pinyin:** qíguài
- **components:**
  - 奇
    - qí
    - odd; strange
  - 怪
    - guài
    - weird; unusual
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 奇(奇) (qí, "odd; strange") + 怪(怪) (guài, "weird; unusual") =
    - strange or peculiar in nature.
  - **interpretation:** The word 奇怪 (qíguài, "strange") combines 奇(奇) (qí, "odd; strange") and 怪(怪) (guài, "weird; unusual") to convey a sense of something being out of the ordinary or peculiar.
- **examples:**
  - 这件衣服看起来很奇怪(這件衣服看起來很奇怪)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfú kàn qǐ lái hěn qíguài.
    - This piece of clothing looks very strange.
  - 他的笑容有点奇怪(他的笑容有點奇怪)，让人不安(讓人不安)。
    - Tā de xiàoróng yǒudiǎn qíguài, ràng rén bù'ān.
    - His smile is a bit strange, making people uneasy.
  - 那只猫的行为真奇怪(那隻貓的行為真奇怪)。
    - Nà zhī māo de xíngwéi zhēn qíguài.
    - That cat's behavior is really strange.

### 奇怪(奇怪) → 奇(奇) → 大(大)
- **definition:** big
- **pinyin:** dà
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts 人(人) (rén, "person") ->
    - borrowed for 大(大) (dà, "big")
  - **interpretation:** The character 大(大) (dà, "big") originally depicted a person standing, which later evolved to express the meaning 'big.' The resemblance to a person was used phonetically to convey largeness, not necessarily to portray outstretched arms as sometimes explained.
- **examples:**

    - Chéngbǎo dàmén chǎngkāi, yíngjiē yuǎnfāng de kèrén.

  - **type:** phono-semantic compound

- **pinyin:** guài

  - **description:** Depicts a hand wearing an archer's thumb ring

- **examples:**
