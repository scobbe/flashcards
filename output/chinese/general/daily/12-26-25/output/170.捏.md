### pinch
---
- **pinyin:** pinch
- **components:**
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** The character 捏 is composed of the hand radical 手 (shǒu,
  - **interpretation:** This character represents the action of pinching with the hand. The combination of the hand element and the phonetic part effectively conveys its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他用手捏了捏面团(他用手捏了捏麵團)。
    - tā yòng shǒu niēle niē miàntuán.
    - He pinched the dough with his hands.
  - 我轻轻地捏了一下(我輕輕地捏了一下)。
    - wǒ qīngqīng de niēle yīxià.
    - I gently pinched it.
  - 她捏着他的手(她捏著他的手)。
    - tā niēzhe tā de shǒu.
    - She was pinching his hand.
### 捏(捏) → 手(手)
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a human hand with fingers, where the top stroke represents the bent over middle finger and horizontal strokes represent two fingers each.
  - **interpretation:** This character intuitively represents a hand by depicting its five distinct fingers, distinguishing it from other characters that represent claws or simplified forms of hands.
- **examples:**
  - 我用手(我用手)写字。
    - Wǒ yòng shǒu xiě zì.
    - I write with my hand.
  - 请用手(請用手)打电话。
    - Qǐng yòng shǒu dǎ diànhuà.
    - Please use your hand to make a phone call.
