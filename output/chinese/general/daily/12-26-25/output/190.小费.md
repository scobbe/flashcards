## tip; gratuity
### xiǎofèi
---
- **definition:** tip; gratuity
- **pinyin:** xiǎofèi
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little
  - 费(費)
    - fèi
    - expenditure; fee; cost; spend
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 小(小) (xiǎo, "small") + 费(費) (fèi, "expenditure") =
    - a small expenditure ->
    - tip; gratuity
  - **interpretation:** The word 小费(小費) (xiǎofèi) can be understood as a 'small expenditure'. This makes sense as a tip or gratuity is typically a small monetary amount given as an expression of appreciation for service.
- **examples:**
  - 他每次用餐都会留小费(他每次用餐都會留小費)。
    - Tā měi cì yòng cān dōu huì liú xiǎofèi.
    - He always leaves a tip when dining.
  - 在餐厅(在餐廳)，服务员服务得很好(服務員服務得很好)，所以我给了小费(所以我給了小費)。
    - Zài cāntīng, fúwùyuán fúwù dé hěn hǎo, suǒyǐ wǒ gěi le xiǎofèi.
    - At the restaurant, the waiter provided excellent service, so I left a tip.
  - 顾客给小费(顧客給小費)，认为这是对服务的认可(認為這是對服務的認可)。
    - Gùkè gěi xiǎofèi, rènwéi zhè shì duì fúwù de rènkě.
    - The customer gives a tip, considering it as recognition of the service.
### 小费(小費) → 小(小)
- **definition:** small; little
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Three small dots ->
    - small
  - **interpretation:** The character 小(xiǎo, 'small') is an ideogram that originally used three small dots to visually represent the concept of smallness. Over time, these dots became elongated, maintaining the meaning of 'small.'
- **examples:**
  - 我有小猫(我有小貓)，它喜欢睡觉(它喜歡睡覺)。
    - Wǒ yǒu xiǎo māo, tā xǐhuān shuìjiào.
    - I have a small cat, it loves to sleep.
  - 这本书很小(這本書很小)。
    - Zhè běn shū hěn xiǎo.
    - This book is very small.
  - 小花在院子里玩耍(小花在院子裡玩耍)，笑声传遍四方(笑聲傳遍四方)。
    - Xiǎo Huā zài yuànzi lǐ wánshuǎ, xiàoshēng chuánbiàn sìfāng.
    - Little Hua plays in the yard, and her laughter spreads in all directions.
### 小费(小費) → 费(費)
- **definition:** expenditure; fee; cost; spend
- **pinyin:** fèi
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 弗
    - fú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") + phonetic 弗(弗) (fú) =
    - sound and idea of valuing, spending ->
    - expenditure
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry"), which often symbolizes money or valuables, with the phonetic 弗(弗) (fú), giving it the sound and further emphasizing the concept of spending or valuing. This reinforces the idea of an expense or cost.
  - **simplification:** 费(費): 贝(贝) (bèi, "cowry") retains the meaning and the sound is conveyed through simplified phonetic components.
- **examples:**
  - 他支付高额管理费(他支付高額管理費)，认为这是合理花费(認為這是合理花費)。
    - Tā zhīfù gāo'é guǎnlǐ fèi, rènwéi zhè shì hélǐ huāfèi。
    - He pays a high management fee and believes it is a reasonable expense.
  - 学校收取报名费(學校收取報名費)，用于购买设施(用於購買設施)。
    - Xuéxiào shōuqǔ bàomíng fèi, yòng yú gòumǎi shèshī。
    - The school charges an enrollment fee to purchase facilities.
### 小费(小費) → 费(費) → 貝(貝)
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China
  - **interpretation:** 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") depicts the distinctive shape of a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China due to its unique appearance and rarity.
- **examples:**
  - 他收藏一枚贝(他收藏一枚貝)，用作古代贸易的信物(用作古代貿易的信物)。
    - Tā shōucáng yī méi bèi, yòng zuò gǔdài màoyì de xìnwù.
    - He collects a cowry shell, using it as a token in ancient trade.
  - 清朝时贝(清朝時貝)，常被镶嵌于玉器(常被鑲嵌於玉器)。
    - Qīngcháo shí bèi, cháng bèi xiāngqiàn yú yùqì.
    - During the Qing dynasty, cowries were often inlaid in jade pieces.
