## 
### tip; gratuity
---
- **pinyin:** tip; gratuity
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little; young; minor
  - 费(費)
    - fèi
    - expense; cost; fee; waste
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小(小) (xiǎo, "small") + 费(費) (fèi, "expense") — small expense
  - **interpretation:** The term refers to a small sum of money given as a gesture of appreciation for service. This makes sense because a tip is typically a minor additional expense beyond the main payment for a service received.
- **examples:**
  - 我给了他小费(我給了他小費)。
    - Wǒ gěile tā xiǎofèi.
    - I gave him a tip.
  - 服务很好，所以我留下了小费(服務很好，所以我留下了小費)。
    - Fúwù hěn hǎo, suǒyǐ wǒ liú xiàle xiǎofèi.
    - The service was great, so I left a tip.
### 小费(小費) → 小(小)
- **definition:** small; little; young; minor
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a small or small-scale object.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents something that is small or compact in size, resembling a dot or a short stroke.
- **examples:**
  - 这个房间很小(這個房間很小)。
    - Zhège fángjiān hěn xiǎo。
    - This room is very small.
  - 我有一个小猫(我有一個小貓)。
    - Wǒ yǒu yīgè xiǎo māo。
    - I have a small cat.
### 小费(小費) → 费(費)
- **definition:** expense; cost; fee; waste
- **pinyin:** fèi
- **components:**
  - 石
    - shí
    - stone
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - shell
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** The character is composed of the semantic component '石' (rock, stone) and the phonetic component '貝' (shell, treasure), suggesting a connection between cost and valuable items.
  - **interpretation:** The connection between stones and shells implies that costs are associated with valuable materials used in transactions.
- **examples:**
  - 这个项目的费用太高了(這個項目的費用太高了)。
    - Zhège xiàngmù de fèiyòng tài gāole.
    - The cost of this project is too high.
  - 请问，服务费包括在内吗？(請問，服務費包括在內嗎？)
    - Qǐngwèn, fúwù fèi bāokuò zài nèi ma?
    - May I ask if the service fee is included?
### 小费(小費) → 费(費) → 石(石)
- **definition:** stone
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stone beneath a cliff, which resembles a cave set into the side of a cliff or mountain.
  - **interpretation:** This image intuitively represents a stone's natural habitat at the base of a cliff, emphasizing its solidity and connection to the earth.
- **examples:**
  - 我找到了石头。
    - Wǒ zhǎodàole shítou.
    - I found a stone.
  - 这座山上有很多石块。
    - Zhè zuò shān shàng yǒu hěnduō shíkuài.
    - There are many stones on this mountain.
### 小费(小費) → 费(費) → 貝(貝)
- **definition:** shell
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cowrie shell which was used as currency in ancient China.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a cowrie shell, linking it directly to its historical use as money. This makes it intuitive as currency symbolizing wealth and trade.
- **examples:**
  - 我用貝(我用贝)买东西。
    - Wǒ yòng bèi mǎi dōngxi.
    - I use cowries to buy things.
  - 古代人用貝(古代人用贝)交易。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi jiāoyì.
    - Ancient people used cowries for trade.
