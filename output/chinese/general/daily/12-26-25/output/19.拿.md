## take; hold
### ná
---
- **definition:** take; hold
- **pinyin:** ná
- **components:**
  - 合
    - hé
    - to combine; to close; together
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 合 + 手; later alternative glyph of 拏/挐 for ‘to take with hand’.
  - **interpretation:** Combines 合 ‘to close, to join’ placed over 手 ‘hand’, evoking a hand bringing something together or grasping it. The imagery points to taking or holding with the hand, and this form later displaced earlier variants 拏/挐 in common usage.
- **examples:**
  - 请把这个杯子拿好(請把這個杯子拿好)。
    - Qǐng bǎ zhège bēizi ná hǎo.
    - Please hold this cup steady.
  - 他拿起书就走了(他拿起書就走了)。
    - Tā ná qǐ shū jiù zǒu le.
    - He picked up the book and left.
  - 出门别忘了拿雨伞(出門別忘了拿雨傘)。
    - Chūmén bié wàng le ná yǔsǎn.
    - Don’t forget to take an umbrella when you go out.
### 拿 → 合
- **definition:** to combine; to close; together
- **pinyin:** hé
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (會意)
  - **description:** 亼 + 口 (“mouth”): Two mouths speaking together.
  - **interpretation:** Two mouths coming together evokes speaking in unison or agreement, which extends to the senses of joining and fitting together. From this image also comes the idea of closing, as parts meet to make a whole.
- **examples:**
  - 我们把两份文件合在一起(我們把兩份文件合在一起)。
    - Wǒmen bǎ liǎng fèn wénjiàn hé zài yīqǐ.
    - We combined the two documents.
  - 请把门合上(請把門合上)。
    - Qǐng bǎ mén hé shàng.
    - Please close the door.
  - 我们合作完成了项目(我們合作完成了項目)。
    - Wǒmen hézuò wánchéng le xiàngmù.
    - We worked together to finish the project.
### 拿 → 合 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 拿 → 手
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: hand and fingers; five digits, with top stroke as bent middle finger and horizontal strokes as fingers.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a human hand: the top stroke stands for a bent middle finger, and the horizontal strokes stand for the remaining fingers. In contrast to related claw forms that show three digits, it consistently presents a five‑finger human hand.
- **examples:**
  - 我手上有伤口(我手上有傷口)。
    - Wǒ shǒu shàng yǒu shāngkǒu.
    - I have a wound on my hand.
  - 请举起右手(請舉起右手)。
    - Qǐng jǔ qǐ yòu shǒu.
    - Please raise your right hand.
  - 他动手做饭(他動手做飯)。
    - Tā dòngshǒu zuò fàn.
    - He started cooking by hand.
