### take; hold
---
- **pinyin:** take; hold
- **components:**
  - 奴
    - nú
    - slave
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Composed of the phonetic component 奴 (nǔ, 'slave') and the semantic component 手 (shǒu, 'hand').
  - **interpretation:** This character intuitively represents the action of taking or holding something with the hand, linking both the sound and meaning through its components.
- **examples:**
  - 我可以拿这个吗？(我可以拿這個嗎？)
    - Wǒ kěyǐ ná zhège ma?
    - Can I take this?
  - 请把书拿给我。(請把書拿給我。)
    - Qǐng bǎ shū ná gěi wǒ.
    - Please hand the book to me.
### 拿(拿) → 奴(奴)
- **definition:** slave
- **pinyin:** nú
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - woman
  - 又
    - yòu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 女 (女) (nǚ, 'woman') + 又 (又) (yòu, 'hand') – a hand capturing and ordering a woman around.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the idea of a hand controlling or commanding a woman, suggesting servitude.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个奴隶(他是一個奴隸)。
    - tā shì yī gè núlì。
    - He is a slave.
  - 历史上奴隶制度非常残酷(歷史上奴隸制度非常殘酷)。
    - lìshǐ shàng núlì zhìdù fēicháng cánkù。
    - The system of slavery in history was very cruel.
### 拿(拿) → 奴(奴) → 女(女)
- **definition:** woman
- **pinyin:** nǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a woman with breasts kneeling or standing, facing the right side.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a woman, emphasizing the female form with the inclusion of breasts and body posture. This makes it intuitive for signifying femininity.
- **examples:**
  - 她是一位聪明的女学生。
    - tā shì yī wèi cōngmíng de nǚ xuéshēng。
    - She is a smart female student.
  - 这个女孩非常善良。
    - zhège nǚhái fēicháng shànliáng。
    - This girl is very kind.
### 拿(拿) → 奴(奴) → 又(又)
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a right hand.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a hand, suggesting the action or agency associated with it. This is intuitive as hands are often involved in actions and decisions.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)！
    - tā yòu lái le！
    - He has come again!
  - 我又忘记了(我又忘記了)。
    - wǒ yòu wàng jì le。
    - I have forgotten again.
  - 她又在笑(她又在笑)。
    - tā yòu zài xiào。
    - She is laughing again.
### 拿(拿) → 手(手)
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a human hand with five fingers, emphasizing the bent over middle finger and horizontal strokes representing two additional fingers.
  - **interpretation:** The design clearly illustrates a human hand, ensuring the representation of five fingers distinguishes it from claw characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我用手写字(我用手寫字)。
    - Wǒ yòng shǒu xiě zì.
    - I write with my hand.
  - 他的手很大(他的手很大)。
    - Tā de shǒu hěn dà.
    - His hand is very big.
