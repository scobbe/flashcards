## take; hold
### ná
---
- **definition:** take; hold
- **pinyin:** ná
- **components:**
  - 合
    - hé
    - combine
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 合(合) (hé, "combine") + 手(手) (shǒu, "hand") =
    - taking or holding by hand ->
    - later form of 拏(拏)
  - **interpretation:** The character 拿(ná,"take") combines 手(手)(shǒu,"hand") for meaning and a variation of 合(合)(hé,"combine") based on seeing its use as related to grasping or taking with the hand. 拏(拏)(ná,"take") was modified, simplifying the phonetic component.
  - **simplification:** The character 拿 is not simplification of a traditional one, but rather a later alternative to 拏 that became standardized.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿起书(他拿起書)，认真阅读(認真閱讀)。
    - Tā ná qǐ shū, rènzhēn yuèdú.
    - He picked up the book and read it carefully.
  - 请把东西拿(請把東西拿)，别放下(別放下)。
    - Qǐng bǎ dōngxī ná, bié fàngxià.
    - Please take the items and don't put them down.
  - 他拿着伞呢(他拿著傘呢)。
    - Tā názhe sǎn ne.
    - He is holding an umbrella.
### 拿(拿) → 合(合)
- **definition:** combine
- **pinyin:** hé
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 一(一) (yī, "one") + 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") =
    - lips sealing one mouth ->
    - unite
  - **interpretation:** The character 合(hé, "combine") visually represents the concept of unity or combination by depicting a mouth being sealed or unified by one stroke, symbolizing things coming together as one.
- **examples:**
  - 他们把知识合并应用(他們把知識合並應用)，创新出妙计(創新出妙計)。
    - Tāmen bǎ zhīshì hé bìng yìngyòng, chuàngxīn chū miàojì.
    - They combine knowledge and apply it, creating ingenious plans.
  - 古今智慧合而为一(古今智慧合而為一)。
    - Gǔjīn zhìhuì hé ér wéi yī.
    - The wisdom of the past and present merges into one.
  - 画家把颜色合成新色彩(畫家把顏色合成新色彩)，创作出绝美画卷(創作出絕美畫卷)。
    - Huàjiā bǎ yánsè héchéng xīn sècǎi, chuàngzuò chū jué měi huàjuàn.
    - The artist blends colors to create new hues, crafting an exquisite masterpiece.
### 拿(拿) → 合(合) → 一(一)
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Horizontal stroke ->
    - representing the number 'one'
  - **interpretation:** The character 一(yī, "one") is a simple horizontal line symbolizing singularity or unity, directly capturing the essence of oneness.
- **examples:**
  - 我只要一杯水(我只要一杯水)。
    - Wǒ zhǐyào yī bēi shuǐ.
    - I only need one glass of water.
  - 他一生追求自由(他一生追求自由)，一心为理想(一心為理想)。
    - Tā yīshēng zhuīqiú zìyóu, yīxīn wéi lǐxiǎng.
    - He dedicated his life to pursuing freedom, and wholeheartedly cherished his ideals.
  - 请给我一支笔(請給我一支筆)。
    - Qǐng gěi wǒ yī zhī bǐ.
    - Please give me one pen.
### 拿(拿) → 合(合) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") visually represents an open mouth, making it straightforward to connect to its meaning related to speaking and openings.
- **examples:**
  - 请张开你的口(請張開你的口)。
    - Qǐng zhāngkāi nǐ de kǒu.
    - Please open your mouth.
  - 他家有三口人(他家有三口人)，个个活泼可爱(個個活潑可愛)。
    - Tā jiā yǒu sān kǒu rén, gègè huópō kě'ài.
    - His family consists of three people, and each one is lively and adorable.
  - 医生检查了他的口(醫生檢查了他的口)，确认健康无虞(確認健康無虞)。
    - Yīshēng jiǎnchále tā de kǒu, quèrèn jiànkāng wú yú.
    - The doctor examined his mouth and confirmed there were no health concerns.
### 拿(拿) → 手(手)
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a hand
  - **interpretation:** 手(手) (shǒu, "hand") is a pictogram that visually represents a human hand, which aligns directly with its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他的手很灵巧(他的手很靈巧)。
    - tā de shǒu hěn língqiǎo。
    - His hand is very dexterous.
  - 他伸出手(他伸出手)，示意停下(示意停下)。
    - tā shēn chū shǒu, shì yì tíng xià。
    - He extended his hand, signaling to stop.
  - 小朋友握紧手(小朋友握緊手)，准备接住飞来的球(準備接住飛來的球)。
    - xiǎopéngyǒu wò jǐn shǒu, zhǔnbèi jiē zhù fēi lái de qiú。
    - The child clenched his hand, ready to catch the incoming ball.
