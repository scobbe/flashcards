## programmer (slang)
### mǎnóng
---
- **traditional:** 碼農
- **definition:** programmer (slang)
- **pinyin:** mǎnóng
- **components:**
  - 码(碼)
    - mǎ
    - code; number; yard
  - 农(農)
    - nóng
    - farmer; agriculture; to farm
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 码(碼) (mǎ, "code; number") + 农(農) (nóng, "farmer") =
    - a humorous slang term for a programmer, suggesting someone who works hard writing lines of code like a farmer tending a field.
  - **interpretation:** The term 码农(碼農) (mǎnóng, "programmer") humorously implies that programmers tirelessly produce lines of code as farmers cultivate crops. This reflects the repetitive and labor-intensive nature of programming work.
  - **simplification:** 码: 碼, 农: 農
- **examples:**
  - 他是码农(碼農)。
    - Tā shì mǎnóng.
    - He is a programmer (slang).
  - 码农(碼農)加班晚，生活也不轻松(輕鬆)。
    - Mǎnóng jiābān wǎn, shēnghuó yě bù qīngsōng.
    - The programmer works overtime until late, and life is not easy.
### 码(碼)农(農) → 码(碼)
- **traditional:** 碼
- **definition:** code; number; yard
- **pinyin:** mǎ
- **components:**
  - 石
    - shí
    - stone
  - 马(馬)
    - mǎ
    - horse
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 石 (shí, 'stone') + Phonetic: 馬 (mǎ, 'horse') =
    - used for counting stones ->
    - number, code
  - **interpretation:** The character 码(碼) combines 石 (shí, 'stone') symbolizing things that can be counted, with 馬 (mǎ, 'horse'), chosen as a phonetic match. Together, they formed a character initially used for counting objects like stones, which later evolved to mean 'number' or 'code'.
  - **simplification:** The simplified character uses the exact same components as the traditional one.
- **examples:**
  - 请输(請輸)入密码(碼)。
    - Qǐng shūrù mìmǎ.
    - Please enter the password.
  - 系统(統)自动(動)生成验证码(驗證碼)。
    - Xìtǒng zìdòng shēngchéng yànzhèngmǎ.
    - The system automatically generates a verification code.
  - 扫(掃)描二维码(維碼)，即可获(獲)取信息。
    - Sǎomiáo èrwéi mǎ, jí kě huòqǔ xìnxī.
    - Scan the QR code to get the information.
### 码(碼)农(農) → 码(碼) → 石
- **definition:** stone
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stone beneath a cliff, where the upper part originally looked like a cliff (厂) but was distorted into 丆 over time.
  - **interpretation:** The character 石(shí, 'stone') visually represents a stone in a natural setting, highlighting the element of a cliff or mountain. Its visual design aims to reflect the appearance of rocks lodged or embedded in a mountainous or cliff-like setting.
- **examples:**
  - 他在河边发现(邊發現)一块(塊)石。
    - Tā zài hé biān fāxiàn yī kuài shí.
    - He discovered a piece of stone by the riverside.
  - 孩子们(們)喜欢(歡)堆石，玩得开(開)心。
    - Háizimen xǐhuān duī shí, wán de kāixīn.
    - Children like to stack stones, playing happily.
### 码(碼)农(農) → 码(碼) → 马(馬)
- **traditional:** 馬
- **definition:** horse
- **pinyin:** mǎ
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 火
    - huǒ
    - fire
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a horse with head facing left and flowing mane ->
    - head often simplified into an eye 目 (mù, "eye") ->
    - legs evolved into four dots 灬 unrelated to 火 (huǒ, "fire")
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a horse with its mane flowing sideways, and the simplification over time captured the essence of its most visible features.
  - **simplification:** 馬 was simplified to 马(馬) by fusing the head and body, using the simplified line forms and dots to represent its features more compactly.
- **examples:**
  - 小马(馬)在草地上奔跑。
    - Xiǎo mǎ zài cǎodì shàng bēnpǎo.
    - The little horse runs on the grassland.
  - 我骑马(騎馬)看风(風)景，感觉(覺)非常自在。
    - Wǒ qí mǎ kàn fēngjǐng, gǎnjué fēicháng zìzài.
    - I ride a horse to enjoy the scenery, feeling very at ease.
  - 马(馬)是人类历(類歷)史上的好伙(夥)伴。
    - Mǎ shì rénlèi lìshǐ shàng de hǎo huǒbàn.
    - Horses are good companions in human history.

### 码(碼)农(農) → 农(農)
- **traditional:** 農
- **definition:** farmer; agriculture; to farm
- **pinyin:** nóng
- **components:**
  - 辰
    - chén
    - fifth earthly branch
  - 曲
    - qū
    - bent
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 辰 (chén, 'fifth earthly branch') + phonetic: 曲 (qū, 'bent') =
    - farm work at the time of 辰 ->
    - agriculture, farming
  - **interpretation:** The character 农(農) (nóng, 'farmer; agriculture') conveys the concept of farming activities aligned with the cycles of time, represented by 辰 (chén, 'fifth earthly branch') which signifies a specific time, and 曲 (qū, 'bent') indicating the physical nature of farm work.
  - **simplification:** 农(農) was simplified by removing the 田 (tián, 'field') radical and using a simplified form of the original components.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一位农(農)民。
    - Tā shì yī wèi nóngmín.
    - He is a farmer.
  - 他专(專)注于农业(於農業)研究，推广农(廣農)耕技术(術)。
    - Tā zhuānzhù yú nóngyè yánjiū, tuīguǎng nónggēng jìshù.
    - He focuses on agricultural research and promotes farming techniques.
  - 古时(時)候有农(農)夫耕作，辛勤劳(勞)作，换来丰(換來豐)收。
    - Gǔ shíhòu yǒu nóngfū gēngzuò, xīnqín láozuò, huàn lái fēngshōu.
    - In ancient times, farmers toiled in the fields, working hard and reaped a bountiful harvest.
### 码(碼)农(農) → 农(農) → 辰
- **definition:** fifth earthly branch
- **pinyin:** chén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a heavenly body or a celestial presence, often associated with dragons in ancient mythology.
  - **interpretation:** 辰 (chén, "early form of dragon") originally depicted a celestial body that was believed to influence time and was associated with dragons due to mythological links between dragons and celestial phenomena.
- **examples:**
  - 古代传说(傳說)中的辰是神秘而强(強)大的生物。
    - Gǔdài chuánshuō zhōng de chén shì shénmì ér qiángdà de shēngwù.
    - In ancient legends, the chén is a mysterious and powerful creature.
  - 东(東)方初升的朝阳(陽)映照着(著)辰的鳞(鱗)光，唤(喚)起了古老的神话(話)。
    - Dōngfāng chūshēng de cháoyáng yìngzhào zhe chén de línguāng, huànqǐ le gǔlǎo de shénhuà.
    - The rising morning sun in the east illuminates the scales of the chén, evoking ancient myths.
  - 传(傳)承了古老智慧的文献记载(獻記載)了辰的奇迹(蹟)，使后(後)人对(對)它充满(滿)敬畏。
    - Chuánchéng le gǔlǎo zhìhuì de wénxiàn jìzǎi le chén de qíjī, shǐ hòurén duì tā chōngmǎn jìngwèi.
    - Ancient texts carrying old wisdom recorded the miracles of the chén, filling later generations with awe.
### 码(碼)农(農) → 农(農) → 曲
- **definition:** bent
- **pinyin:** qū
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - C (outer curves) + 曲 (bent lines) =
    - object depicted with a curve ->
    - bent
  - **interpretation:** The character 曲 (qū, "bent") visually represents the concept of bending or curving with its structure. The two curved lines symbolize bending, illustrating the fundamental meaning of the character.
- **examples:**
  - 小河弯(彎)曲。
    - Xiǎo hé wān qū.
    - The small river is bent.
  - 山路曲折，尽显(盡顯)雄伟(偉)。
    - Shān lù qūzhé, jǐn xiǎn xióngwěi.
    - The mountain road is winding, fully showcasing its grandeur.
  - 他的胡须(鬍鬚)微微曲，仿佛(彷彿)在诉说(訴說)故事。
    - Tā de húxū wēi wēi qū, fǎngfú zài sùshuō gùshi.
    - His whiskers are slightly bent, as if telling a story.
