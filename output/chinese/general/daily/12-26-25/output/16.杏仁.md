## almond
### xìngrén
---
- **definition:** almond
- **pinyin:** xìngrén
- **components:**
  - 杏
    - xìng
    - apricot; apricot tree; apricot-colored; surname
  - 仁
    - rén
    - kernel (of nut/seed); benevolence; humane; kindness
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 杏 (apricot) + 仁 (kernel/seed) = apricot kernel → ‘almond’ in culinary usage.
  - **interpretation:** Literally “apricot kernel,” pointing to the edible seed inside an apricot pit; in common food contexts it often refers to almonds or almond-like kernels, though botanically almonds are 扁桃仁/巴旦木.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢喝杏仁露(我喜歡喝杏仁露)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan hē xìngrén lù.
    - I like drinking almond beverage.
  - 烘焙时常用杏仁粉(烘焙時常用杏仁粉)。
    - Hōngbèi shí cháng yòng xìngrén fěn.
    - Almond flour is often used in baking.
  - 这道甜品用的是南杏仁，不是苦杏仁(這道甜品用的是南杏仁，不是苦杏仁)。
    - Zhè dào tiánpǐn yòng de shì nán xìngrén, bù shì kǔ xìngrén.
    - This dessert uses sweet apricot kernels, not bitter ones.
### 杏仁 → 杏
- **definition:** apricot; apricot tree; apricot-colored; surname
- **pinyin:** xìng
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood; tree
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 木 ‘wood’ + 口 ‘mouth’—perhaps referring to the tastiness of its fruit; Shuowen: phono-semantic 木 (semantic) + 可 (phonetic), but considered unlikely.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the graph for a tree with a mouth to suggest something on a tree that is eaten, i.e., the apricot and its tree. An alternative Shuowen reading treats 可 as a phonetic, but modern scholarship generally rejects that analysis.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了两斤杏子(我買了兩斤杏子)。
    - Wǒ mǎile liǎng jīn xìngzi.
    - I bought two jin of apricots.
  - 院里有一棵老杏树(院裡有一棵老杏樹)。
    - Yuàn li yǒu yì kē lǎo xìngshù.
    - There is an old apricot tree in the yard.
  - 这条围巾是杏色的(這條圍巾是杏色的)。
    - Zhè tiáo wéijīn shì xìngsè de.
    - This scarf is apricot-colored.
### 杏仁 → 杏 → 木
- **definition:** wood; tree
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram (象形)
  - **description:** a tree: branches on top, roots on the bottom (more visible in earlier forms).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a tree with spreading branches above and roots below, as seen clearly in early scripts. The vertical stroke is the trunk, while the short horizontal strokes became stylized limbs and roots in the regular form.
- **examples:**
  - 这张桌子是木头做的(這張桌子是木頭做的)。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi shì mùtou zuò de.
    - This table is made of wood.
  - 山上长满了树木(山上長滿了樹木)。
    - Shān shang zhǎng mǎn le shùmù.
    - The mountainside is covered with trees.
  - 木材被运到工厂加工(木材被運到工廠加工)。
    - Mùcái bèi yùn dào gōngchǎng jiāgōng.
    - The timber was transported to the factory for processing.
### 杏仁 → 杏 → 口
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 杏仁 → 仁
- **definition:** kernel (of nut/seed); benevolence; humane; kindness
- **pinyin:** rén
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 二
    - èr
    - two
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic: 人 (OC *njin) + semantic: 二
  - **interpretation:** 人 supplies the sound rén, while 二 adds the idea of “two,” often read as the bond between people that grounds benevolence. By phonetic extension, the graph also came to write the homophonous sense “kernel.”
- **examples:**
  - 他待人仁慈(他待人仁慈)，乐于助人(樂於助人)。
    - Tā dàirén réncí, lèyú zhùrén.
    - He treats others with benevolence and is eager to help.
  - 儒家强调“仁义”(儒家強調「仁義」)，以爱人为本(以愛人為本)。
    - Rújiā qiángdiào “rényì”, yǐ àirén wéi běn.
    - Confucianism emphasizes “benevolence and righteousness,” taking love for others as foundational.
  - 核桃仁很香(核桃仁很香)，可以入菜(可以入菜)。
    - Hétáo rén hěn xiāng, kěyǐ rù cài.
    - Walnut kernels are very fragrant and can be used in cooking.
### 杏仁 → 仁 → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 杏仁 → 仁 → 二
- **definition:** two
- **pinyin:** èr
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** ideogram: two parallel strokes; duplication of 一.
  - **interpretation:** Two horizontal lines directly depict the quantity ‘two’ as simple tally marks. It parallels 一 and 三 as incremental strokes, with historical 亖 for four, emphasizing straightforward counting by repetition.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是二月二日(今天是二月二日)。
    - Jīntiān shì èr yuè èr rì.
    - Today is February 2nd.
  - 我住在二楼(我住在二樓)。
    - Wǒ zhù zài èr lóu.
    - I live on the second floor.
  - 二加二等于四(二加二等於四)。
    - Èr jiā èr děngyú sì.
    - Two plus two equals four.
### 杏仁 → 仁 → 二 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
