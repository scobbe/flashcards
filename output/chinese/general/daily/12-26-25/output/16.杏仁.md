## almond
### xìngrén
---
- **definition:** almond
- **pinyin:** xìngrén
- **components:**
  - 杏
    - xìng
    - apricot
  - 仁
    - rén
    - kernel; seed
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 杏 (xìng, "apricot") + 仁 (rén, "kernel; seed") =
    - kernel or seed of the apricot, by extension refers to almond
  - **interpretation:** The term 杏仁 (xìngrén) translates directly to 'apricot kernel', as almonds were historically associated with apricot kernels due to their similar appearance and use in Chinese cuisine and medicine. Over time, the term became synonymous with 'almond'. This usage likely reflects historical trade and agricultural practices in which true almonds became more familiar to Chinese culture.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)杏仁。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān xìngrén.
    - I like almonds.
  - 早餐时(時)，吃杏仁可以增加营养(營養)。
    - Zǎocān shí, chī xìngrén kěyǐ zēngjiā yíngyǎng.
    - Eating almonds at breakfast can boost nutrition.
  - 这种(這種)蛋糕，放入杏仁后(後)更香甜。
    - Zhè zhǒng dàngāo, fàng rù xìngrén hòu gèng xiāngtián.
    - This type of cake becomes sweeter after adding almonds.
### 杏仁 → 杏
- **definition:** apricot
- **pinyin:** xìng
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 木 (mù, "wood") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") =
    - possibly describes the tastiness of apricot fruit
  - **interpretation:** The character 杏 (xìng, "apricot") represents an apricot tree, with 木 (mù, "wood") standing for a tree or plant and 口 (kǒu, "mouth") possibly suggesting the enjoyment or edibility of the apricot's fruit. Though Shuowen proposed a phono-semantic origin with 可 (kě, "can"), the ideogram explanation focused on the fruit's appeal is more intuitive.
- **examples:**
  - 公园里(園裡)有杏。
    - Gōngyuán lǐ yǒu xìng.
    - There are apricots in the park.
  - 夏天来临时(來臨時)，杏果成熟饱满(飽滿)。
    - Xiàtiān láilín shí, xìng guǒ chéngshú bǎomǎn.
    - When summer arrives, the apricot fruits ripen fully.
  - 老农种(農種)杏树(樹)，收获满满(穫滿滿)喜悦(悅)溢于(於)言表。
    - Lǎo nóng zhòng xìngshù, shōuhuò mǎnmǎn xǐyuè yì yú yánbiǎo.
    - The old farmer plants apricot trees, and his harvest overflows with joy.
### 杏仁 → 杏 → 木
- **definition:** wood
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tree with branches and roots
  - **interpretation:** The character 木(mù) depicts a tree, showing the trunk in the center and two slanting lines representing branches or roots. It directly signifies the concept of wood or a tree.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)把椅子的主要材料是木。
    - Zhè bǎ yǐzi de zhǔyào cáiliào shì mù.
    - The main material of this chair is wood.
  - 工匠精心雕刻木，制成精美家具。
    - Gōngjiàng jīngxīn diāo kè mù, zhìchéng jīngměi jiājù.
    - The craftsman carefully carved the wood, crafting exquisite furniture.
### 杏仁 → 杏 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 杏仁 → 仁
- **definition:** kernel; seed
- **pinyin:** rén
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 二
    - èr
    - two
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic: 人 (rén, "person") + semantic: 二 (èr, "two") =
    - combining personal relationships ->
    - benevolence
  - **interpretation:** The character 仁(rén) uses 人 (rén, "person") to convey sound and 二 (èr, "two") to suggest interaction or connection between two people, leading to the idea of benevolence or humaneness in relationships.
- **examples:**
  - 农(農)民精选(選)玉米仁，用心制(製)作爆米花。
    - Nóngmín jīngxuǎn yùmǐ rén, yòngxīn zhìzuò bàomǐhuā.
    - The farmer carefully selected corn kernels and made delicious popcorn.
  - 这(這)粒仁体积虽(體積雖)小，蕴(蘊)含着(著)大自然的力量。
    - Zhè lì rén tǐjī suī xiǎo, yùnhán zhe dàzìrán de lìliàng.
    - Although the kernel is small in size, it contains the power of nature.
  - 厨师将(廚師將)花生仁，研磨成细腻(細膩)的酱(醬)料。
    - Chúshī jiāng huāshēng rén, yánmó chéng xì nì de jiàngliào.
    - The chef grinds peanut kernels into a smooth sauce.
### 杏仁 → 仁 → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg ->
    - visual representation of a person
  - **interpretation:** The character 人 (rén, 'person') effectively uses simple lines to depict the essential form of a human, making it immediately recognizable and intuitive as meaning 'person.'
- **examples:**
  - 他是个(個)好人。
    - Tā shì gè hǎo rén.
    - He is a good person.
  - 每个(個)人都有责(責)任，为(為)社会(會)作出贡献(貢獻)。
    - Měi gè rén dōu yǒu zérèn, wèi shèhuì zuòchū gòngxiàn.
    - Every person has a responsibility to contribute to society.
  - 人在旅途中，心灵获(靈獲)得了成长(長)。
    - Rén zài lǚtú zhōng, xīnlíng huòdé le chéngzhǎng.
    - People gain growth along their journey.
### 杏仁 → 仁 → 二
- **definition:** two
- **pinyin:** èr
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts two parallel strokes ->
    - represents the number two
  - **interpretation:** The character 二 (èr, "two") consists of two horizontal lines that visually represent the concept of 'two'. This method of using parallel strokes to denote numbers is a straightforward representation of quantity, similar to how 一 (yī, "one") represents 'one' and 三 (sān, "three") represents 'three'.
- **examples:**
  - 我有二支笔(筆)。
    - Wǒ yǒu èr zhī bǐ.
    - I have two pens.
  - 他买(買)了二个苹(個蘋)果，我们(們)一起分享。
    - Tā mǎi le èr gè píngguǒ, wǒmen yīqǐ fēnxiǎng.
    - He bought two apples, and we shared them together.
