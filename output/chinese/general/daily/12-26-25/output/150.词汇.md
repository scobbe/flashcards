## vocabulary
### cíhuì
---
- **definition:** vocabulary
- **pinyin:** cíhuì
- **components:**
  - 词(詞)
    - cí
    - word; term; expression; lyrics
  - 汇(彙)
    - huì
    - collection; to gather; category; compilation
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 词(word) + 汇(collection) = collection of words (vocabulary/lexicon).
  - **interpretation:** Means the stock or collection of words used in a language, field, or by a person. Common in linguistics, education, and lexicography.
  - **simplification:** 詞 → 词 simplifies the 言 radical to 讠 to reduce strokes; 彙 → 汇 merges with 匯 into a single simplified form, leveraging the idea of ‘gathering/converging’ and streamlining high‑frequency usage.
- **examples:**
  - 学习新词汇有助于提高阅读理解(學習新詞彙有助於提高閱讀理解)。
    - Xuéxí xīn cíhuì yǒu zhù yú tígāo yuèdú lǐjiě.
    - Learning new vocabulary helps improve reading comprehension.
  - 他的英语词汇量很大(他的英語詞彙量很大)。
    - Tā de Yīngyǔ cíhuìliàng hěn dà.
    - His English vocabulary size is large.
  - 这本词典按主题分类词汇(這本詞典按主題分類詞彙)。
    - Zhè běn cídiǎn àn zhǔtí fēnlèi cíhuì.
    - This dictionary organizes vocabulary by topic.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 词(詞)
- **definition:** word; term; expression; lyrics
- **pinyin:** cí
- **components:**
  - 言
    - yán
    - speech; to say
  - 司
    - sī
    - to manage; to administer
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 言 (“speech”) + phonetic 司; simplified from 詞 (言 → 讠).
  - **interpretation:** Combines the speech element 言/讠 with the phonetic 司, signaling a meaning related to spoken language while borrowing its sound from 司. The form points to utterances or expressions, aligning with senses like “word” and “lyrics.”
  - **simplification:** Component reduction in the PRC script reform: the semantic component 言 was contracted to 讠 on the left side; the phonetic 司 remained unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 这个词很难理解(這個詞很難理解)。
    - Zhège cí hěn nán lǐjiě.
    - This word is hard to understand.
  - 学习新词能丰富表达(學習新詞能豐富表達)。
    - Xuéxí xīn cí néng fēngfù biǎodá.
    - Learning new words can enrich expression.
  - 这首歌的歌词很感人(這首歌的歌詞很感人)。
    - Zhè shǒu gē de gēcí hěn gǎnrén.
    - The song’s lyrics are very moving.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 词(詞) → 言
- **definition:** speech; to say
- **pinyin:** yán
- **components:**
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram: formed by adding a horizontal stroke to ancient 舌 to show tongue movement (speech). Shuowen erroneously treats it as phono-semantic: phonetic 䇂 (qiān) + semantic 口 (“mouth”).
  - **interpretation:** Built on 舌 (tongue), an added stroke marks motion, abstracting the act of speaking into a simple sign for “speech/words.” Later standardization added a dot on top and simplified the tongue shape, moving from a pictorial tongue to a conventional symbol for utterance. The Shuowen’s phono-semantic reading is a historical misinterpretation rather than the true origin.
- **examples:**
  - 请注意你的言语(請注意你的言語)。
    - Qǐng zhùyì nǐ de yányǔ.
    - Please mind your language.
  - 他发言很简短(他發言很簡短)。
    - Tā fāyán hěn jiǎnduǎn.
    - His remarks were brief.
  - 言行一致(言行一致)，方能取信于人(方能取信於人)。
    - Yán xíng yízhì, fāng néng qǔxìn yú rén.
    - Only by matching words with deeds can one gain others’ trust.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 词(詞) → 言 → 舌
- **definition:** tongue
- **pinyin:** shé
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a forked tongue emerging upwards from a mouth (口)
  - **interpretation:** Graph depicts a tongue sticking up from an open mouth, with the fork hinting at the split tip. Later forms stylized the drawing, but the mouth element remains visible at the bottom; it is unrelated to 舍.
- **examples:**
  - 医生让他伸出舌头检查(醫生讓他伸出舌頭檢查)。
    - Yīshēng ràng tā shēnchū shétou jiǎnchá.
    - The doctor asked him to stick out his tongue for an exam.
  - 辣得我舌头发麻(辣得我舌頭發麻)。
    - Là de wǒ shétou fā má.
    - It’s so spicy that my tongue is numb.
  - 紧张得他舌头打结了(緊張得他舌頭打結了)。
    - Jǐnzhāng de tā shétou dǎjié le.
    - He was so nervous that his tongue got tied.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 词(詞) → 言 → 舌 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 词(詞) → 司
- **definition:** to manage; to administer
- **pinyin:** sī
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** inconclusive
  - **description:** Inconclusive.
  - **interpretation:** Many analyses treat it as an ideogrammic compound: 口 ‘mouth’ referring to orders or speech, with an upper component suggesting a person/hand overseeing, yielding the sense of taking charge. Others simply view it as depicting an official in charge; the lack of consensus leaves its precise formation uncertain.
- **examples:**
  - 他司此事(他司此事)。
    - Tā sī cǐ shì.
    - He is in charge of this matter.
  - 她司财务(她司財務)。
    - Tā sī cáiwù.
    - She manages the finances.
  - 他久司礼仪(他久司禮儀)。
    - Tā jiǔ sī lǐyí.
    - He has long administered the rites.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 汇(彙)
- **definition:** collection; to gather; category; compilation
- **pinyin:** huì
- **components:**
  - 㣇
    - huì
    - to gather; assemble
  - 胃
    - wèi
    - stomach (phonetic component here)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 㣇 + abbreviated phonetic 胃
  - **interpretation:** 㣇 conveys the idea of gathering/bringing things together, matching the meaning of ‘collection/category’. 胃 (wèi) supplies the sound value huì in Old Chinese, guiding the pronunciation. In simplified Chinese, this meaning is written with 汇, which was standardized by merging with the simplified form of 匯/滙.
  - **simplification:** Adopted the existing simplified form 汇 (from 匯/滙 by removing 隹), merging 彙 and 匯 into the single simplified graph 汇 for ease and uniformity.
- **examples:**
  - 这本词典按主题分类词汇，方便查找。(這本詞典按主題分類詞彙，方便查找。)
    - Zhè běn cídiǎn àn zhǔtí fēnlèi cíhuì, fāngbiàn cházhǎo.
    - This dictionary categorizes vocabulary by topic for easy lookup.
  - 编辑正在汇编资料，准备出版专题合集。(編輯正在彙編資料，準備出版專題合集。)
    - Biānjí zhèngzài huìbiān zīliào, zhǔnbèi chūbǎn zhuāntí héjí.
    - The editors are compiling materials to publish a themed collection.
  - 我们把各地的案例汇集起来，做成一份报告。(我們把各地的案例彙集起來，做成一份報告。)
    - Wǒmen bǎ gèdì de ànlì huìjí qǐlái, zuòchéng yí fèn bàogào.
    - We gathered cases from various regions and made them into a report.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 汇(彙) → 㣇
- **definition:** to gather; assemble
- **pinyin:** huì
- **components:**
  - 豕
    - shǐ
    - pig
  - 彑
    - jì
    - pig’s head; snout
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: pig-like creature (豕) with fur; seal script shows pig’s head (彑) on top with fur and hooves below
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bristly pig, with the snout/head rendered as 彑 above and the body and hooves suggested below. Its attested sense ‘to gather; assemble’ likely reflects later borrowing or semantic extension to the huì morpheme rather than the original zoomorphic picture.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 汇(彙) → 㣇 → 豕
- **definition:** pig
- **pinyin:** shǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram (象形): a pig; compare 亥, 𢑓 ‘male pig’, and 豖 ‘castrated male pig’.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a pig in profile with head, body, and legs. Related forms differentiate types of pigs; in 㒸, the pig element serves as a phonetic.
- **examples:**
  - 古书中常以“豕”指代猪(古書中常以「豕」指代豬)。
    - Gǔshū zhōng cháng yǐ “shǐ” zhǐdài zhū.
    - In ancient texts, “豕” refers to a pig.
  - 在甲骨文里，豕的形状清晰可见(在甲骨文裡，豕的形狀清晰可見)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén lǐ, shǐ de xíngzhuàng qīngxī kějiàn.
    - In oracle bone inscriptions, the form of 豕 is clearly visible.
  - 成语“豕突狼奔”中的“豕”意为猪(成語「豕突狼奔」中的「豕」意為豬)。
    - Chéngyǔ “shǐ tū láng bēn” zhōng de “shǐ” yìwéi zhū.
    - In the idiom “豕突狼奔,” “豕” means pig.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 汇(彙) → 㣇 → 彑
- **definition:** pig’s head; snout
- **pinyin:** jì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: pig’s head/snout.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the front of a pig’s head, with short strokes suggesting the snout and bristles. As a standalone word it names the object, and as a component it often contributes the semantic idea of pigs or snouts in compound characters.
- **examples:**
  - 古文中，彑指猪的头或猪鼻(古文中，彑指豬的頭或豬鼻)。
    - Gǔwén zhōng, jì zhǐ zhū de tóu huò zhū bí.
    - In classical texts, jì refers to a pig’s head or snout.
  - 祭祀时，人们常用彑作供品，寓意丰收(祭祀時，人們常用彑作供品，寓意豐收)。
    - Jìsì shí, rénmen cháng yòng jì zuò gòngpǐn, yùyì fēngshōu.
    - During rituals, people often use a pig’s head as an offering, symbolizing a good harvest.
  - 在字书里，彑也作部首，用来表示与猪或口鼻有关的意思(在字書裡，彑也作部首，用來表示與豬或口鼻有關的意思)。
    - Zài zìshū lǐ, jì yě zuò bùshǒu, yònglái biǎoshì yǔ zhū huò kǒubí yǒuguān de yìsi.
    - In dictionaries, 彑 also serves as a radical, indicating meanings related to pigs or snouts.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 汇(彙) → 胃
- **definition:** stomach (phonetic component here)
- **pinyin:** wèi
- **components:**
  - 肉
    - ròu
    - flesh; meat
  - 田
    - tián
    - field
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram (ideogrammic compound)
  - **description:** semantic: 肉 (“flesh”) + pictograph of stomach/food (later simplified to 田, referencing rice 米).
  - **interpretation:** Combines the body-part indicator 肉 with an early pictograph of a stomach filled with food. The later 田 shape preserves the idea of grains/food (rice) inside the organ, pointing to the stomach’s function.
- **examples:**
  - 我胃疼，需要去看医生(我胃疼，需要去看醫生)。
    - Wǒ wèi téng, xūyào qù kàn yīshēng.
    - My stomach hurts; I need to see a doctor.
  - 她的胃口很好，什么都能吃(她的胃口很好，什麼都能吃)。
    - Tā de wèikǒu hěn hǎo, shénme dōu néng chī.
    - She has a good appetite and can eat anything.
  - 饭后别立刻跑步，以免伤胃(飯後別立刻跑步，以免傷胃)。
    - Fàn hòu bié lìkè pǎobù, yǐmiǎn shāng wèi.
    - Don’t run immediately after meals to avoid hurting the stomach.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 汇(彙) → 胃 → 肉
- **definition:** flesh; meat
- **pinyin:** ròu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: ribs/section of meat; stylized as 月 when beside a phonetic, and as 𱼀 or 夕 inside some characters.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the cross-section of flesh with ribs or a slice of meat. In compounds it often appears as a component written like 月, or reduced to 𱼀/夕, which can be mistaken for the moon component but actually signals ‘meat/flesh.’
- **examples:**
  - 我不吃猪肉(我不吃豬肉)。
    - Wǒ bù chī zhūròu.
    - I don't eat pork.
  - 这道菜肉很多，很鲜嫩(這道菜肉很多，很鮮嫩)。
    - Zhè dào cài ròu hěn duō, hěn xiānnèn.
    - This dish has a lot of meat; it's very tender.
  - 受伤后，他手上的肉裂开了(受傷後，他手上的肉裂開了)。
    - Shòushāng hòu, tā shǒushang de ròu liè kāi le.
    - After being injured, the flesh on his hand split open.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 汇(彙) → 胃 → 田
- **definition:** field
- **pinyin:** tián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: pictographic representation of a field divided into four sections.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a top-down view of a plot divided by paths or furrows, which naturally came to denote farmland. The square-with-cross motif also lent itself in some ancient contexts to a mask-like image, influencing related graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 这片田很肥沃(這片田很肥沃)。
    - Zhè piàn tián hěn féiwò.
    - This field is very fertile.
  - 他们在田里劳动(他們在田裡勞動)。
    - Tāmen zài tián lǐ láodòng.
    - They are working in the fields.
  - 春天农民在田里插秧(春天農民在田裡插秧)。
    - Chūntiān nóngmín zài tián lǐ chāyāng.
    - In spring, farmers transplant rice seedlings in the fields.
### 词汇(詞彙) → 汇(彙) → 胃 → 米
- **definition:** rice
- **pinyin:** mǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: dehusked rice kernels; horizontal stroke as a bamboo sieve; cf. 粟 (with husk); unrelated to 木/禾/来.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts dehusked rice grains being sifted on a bamboo sieve, contrasting with 粟 which shows husked kernels. Despite visual resemblance, its form is not derived from 木, 禾, or 来.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了两斤米(我買了兩斤米)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le liǎng jīn mǐ.
    - I bought two jin of rice.
  - 这家店的东北米很香(這家店的東北米很香)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn de Dōngběi mǐ hěn xiāng.
    - The Northeastern rice at this shop is very fragrant.
  - 请把米洗一洗(請把米洗一洗)，再煮饭(再煮飯)。
    - Qǐng bǎ mǐ xǐ yì xǐ, zài zhǔ fàn.
    - Please rinse the rice, then cook it.
