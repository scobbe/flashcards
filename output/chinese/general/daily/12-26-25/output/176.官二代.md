## children of officials
### guān’èrdài
---
- **definition:** children of officials
- **pinyin:** guān’èrdài
- **components:**
  - 官
    - guān
    - official
  - 二
    - èr
    - two; second
  - 代
    - dài
    - generation
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 官(官) (guān, "official") + 二(二) (èr, "two; second") + 代(代) (dài, "generation")
  - **interpretation:** The term 官二代 (guān’èrdài) refers to the children of government officials. '二代' (èrdài, "second generation") is often used in Chinese to denote the children or descendants of a certain group, and 官(官) (guān, "official") specifies that these are the offspring of people in official positions. The phrase carries connotations of privilege and inherited status in contemporary China.
- **examples:**
  - 官二代在社会中备受关注(官二代在社會中備受關注)，他们享有许多优于普通人的机会(他們享有許多優於普通人的機會)。
    - Guān’èrdài zài shèhuì zhōng bèishòu guānzhù, tāmen xiǎngyǒu xǔduō yōuyú pǔtōng rén de jīhuì.
    - Children of officials receive considerable attention in society, and they enjoy many opportunities superior to those of ordinary people.
  - 有些官二代不愿背负家族的期望(有些官二代不願背負家族的期望)。
    - Yǒuxiē guān’èrdài bù yuàn bèifù jiāzú de qīwàng.
    - Some children of officials are reluctant to bear their family's expectations.

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 官(官) (guān, "official") originally depicted the concept of an official by representing a building associated with government officials. This pictogram conveyed the idea of someone holding an official position within such a building.

### 官二代(官二代) → 二(二)
- **definition:** two; second
- **pinyin:** èr
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts two parallel strokes ->
    - represents the number two
  - **interpretation:** The character 二(二) (èr, "two") consists of two horizontal lines that visually represent the concept of 'two'. This method of using parallel strokes to denote numbers is a straightforward representation of quantity, similar to how 一(一) (yī, "one") represents 'one' and 三(三) (sān, "three") represents 'three'.
- **examples:**
  - 我有二支笔(我有二支筆)。
    - Wǒ yǒu èr zhī bǐ.
    - I have two pens.
  - 他买了二个苹果(他買了二個蘋果)，我们一起分享(我們一起分享)。
    - Tā mǎi le èr gè píngguǒ, wǒmen yīqǐ fēnxiǎng.
    - He bought two apples, and we shared them together.
