## children of officials
### guān’èrdài
---
- **definition:** children of officials
- **pinyin:** guān’èrdài
- **components:**
  - 官
    - guān
    - official
  - 二
    - èr
    - two; second
  - 代
    - dài
    - generation
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 官 (guān, "official") + 二 (èr, "two; second") + 代 (dài, "generation")
  - **interpretation:** The term 官二代 (guān’èrdài) refers to the children of government officials. '二代' (èrdài, "second generation") is often used in Chinese to denote the children or descendants of a certain group, and 官 (guān, "official") specifies that these are the offspring of people in official positions. The phrase carries connotations of privilege and inherited status in contemporary China.
- **examples:**
  - 官二代在社会(會)中备(備)受关(關)注，他们(們)享有许(許)多优于(優於)普通人的机会(機會)。
    - Guān’èrdài zài shèhuì zhōng bèishòu guānzhù, tāmen xiǎngyǒu xǔduō yōuyú pǔtōng rén de jīhuì.
    - Children of officials receive considerable attention in society, and they enjoy many opportunities superior to those of ordinary people.
  - 有些官二代不愿(願)背负(負)家族的期望。
    - Yǒuxiē guān’èrdài bù yuàn bèifù jiāzú de qīwàng.
    - Some children of officials are reluctant to bear their family's expectations.
### 官二代 → 官
- **definition:** official
- **pinyin:** guān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a building used as an official's residence or office ->
    - official
  - **interpretation:** The character 官 (guān, "official") originally depicted the concept of an official by representing a building associated with government officials. This pictogram conveyed the idea of someone holding an official position within such a building.
- **examples:**
  - 他勤奋学习(奮學習)，成功走上官途。
    - Tā qínfèn xuéxí, chénggōng zǒu shàng guān tú.
    - He studied diligently and eventually embarked on an official career.
  - 朝廷任命新官，治理国(國)家。
    - Cháotíng rènmìng xīn guān, zhìlǐ guójiā.
    - The imperial court appointed a new official to govern the nation.
### 官二代 → 二
- **definition:** two; second
- **pinyin:** èr
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts two parallel strokes ->
    - represents the number two
  - **interpretation:** The character 二 (èr, "two") consists of two horizontal lines that visually represent the concept of 'two'. This method of using parallel strokes to denote numbers is a straightforward representation of quantity, similar to how 一 (yī, "one") represents 'one' and 三 (sān, "three") represents 'three'.
- **examples:**
  - 我有二支笔(筆)。
    - Wǒ yǒu èr zhī bǐ.
    - I have two pens.
  - 他买(買)了二个苹(個蘋)果，我们(們)一起分享。
    - Tā mǎi le èr gè píngguǒ, wǒmen yīqǐ fēnxiǎng.
    - He bought two apples, and we shared them together.
### 官二代 → 代
- **definition:** generation
- **pinyin:** dài
- **components:**
  - 亻
    - rén
    - person
  - 弋
    - yì
    - phonetic component
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic 亻 (rén, "person") + phonetic 弋 (yì) =
    - aids in pronunciation ->
    - concept of replacing or succeeding, leading to the meaning 'generation'
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the idea of a 'person' with a phonetic component to suggest a succession of people or generations. This association with both an individual and the continuity through different generations underpins its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我这(這)一代人肩负着责(負著責)任。
    - Wǒ zhè yī dài rén jiānfù zhe zérèn.
    - Our generation bears the responsibility.
  - 老一代传(傳)授智慧，年轻(輕)一代追求梦(夢)想。
    - Lǎo yī dài chuánshòu zhìhuì, niánqīng yī dài zhuīqiú mèngxiǎng.
    - The older generation imparts wisdom, while the younger generation pursues dreams.
  - 新技术(術)推动这(動這)代人创(創)新。
    - Xīn jìshù tuīdòng zhè dài rén chuàngxīn.
    - New technology drives innovation in this generation.
### 官二代 → 代 → 亻
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 亻 (rén, "person") is a stylization of 人 (rén, "person") ->
    - simplified for use as a component in other characters
  - **interpretation:** 亻 (rén, "person") is used as a component form of 人 (rén, "person") in compound characters, representing the human or person-related aspect.
- **examples:**
  - 亻是构(構)成许(許)多汉(漢)字的关键(關鍵)部首。
    - Rén shì gòuchéng xǔduō hànzì de guānjiàn bùshǒu.
    - The radical 亻 is a key component in many Chinese characters.
  - 学习汉(學習漢)字时(時)，了解亻的意义(義)很重要。
    - Xuéxí hànzì shí, liǎojiě rén de yìyì hěn zhòngyào.
    - When learning Chinese characters, understanding the meaning of 亻 is important.
  - 古人造字时(時)常取亻以表达(達)人物情感，因此亻贯(貫)穿于(於)文明史中。
    - Gǔrén zào zì shí cháng qǔ rén yǐ biǎodá rénwù qínggǎn, yīncǐ rén guànchuān yú wénmíng shǐ zhōng.
    - Ancient people often used the radical 亻 to express human emotions in character formation, and thus 亻 runs through the history of civilization.
### 官二代 → 代 → 弋
- **definition:** phonetic component
- **pinyin:** yì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bow with an arrow ready to be shot
  - **interpretation:** The character 弋(yì, "phonetic component") represents a bow with an arrow, an imagery that signifies shooting or catching. As such, it has been used as a phonetic component in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 这个(這個)字以弋作声(聲)旁。
    - Zhège zì yǐ yì zuò shēngpáng.
    - This character uses yì as its phonetic component.
  - 古代学(學)者研究弋的音韵(韻)，认为(認爲)其具有独(獨)特功能。
    - Gǔdài xuézhě yánjiū yì de yīnyùn, rènwéi qí jùyǒu dútè gōngnéng.
    - Ancient scholars studied the rhyme of yì, believing that it possessed a unique function.
  - 书(書)法艺术(藝術)中常见(見)弋的应(應)用。
    - Shūfǎ yìshù zhōng chángjiàn yì de yìngyòng.
    - The use of yì is common in calligraphic art.
