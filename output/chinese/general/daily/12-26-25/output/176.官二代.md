## children of officials
### guān’èrdài
---
- **definition:** children of officials
- **pinyin:** guān’èrdài
- **components:**
  - 官
    - guān
    - official; government; public office; authority
  - 二代
    - èrdài
    - second generation; 2nd-generation; offspring; descendants
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 官 (official) + 二代 (second generation) = children of officials.
  - **interpretation:** Colloquial and often pejorative label for offspring of government officials, implying inherited privilege or connections. Common in media and online discourse in Mainland China.
- **examples:**
  - 很多人以为他是官二代(很多人以為他是官二代)，其实他家很普通(其實他家很普通)。
    - Hěn duō rén yǐwéi tā shì guān’èrdài, qíshí tā jiā hěn pǔtōng.
    - Many people think he is a ‘guan er dai’, but his family is actually very ordinary.
  - 媒体关注官二代在就业中的资源与特权(媒體關注官二代在就業中的資源與特權)。
    - Méitǐ guānzhù guān’èrdài zài jiùyè zhōng de zīyuán yǔ tèquán.
    - The media pays attention to the resources and privileges of officials’ children in employment.
  - 并不是每个官二代都滥用权力(並不是每個官二代都濫用權力)，很多人努力靠实力发展(很多人努力靠實力發展)。
    - Bìng bù shì měi gè guān’èrdài dōu lànyòng quánlì, hěn duō rén nǔlì kào shílì fāzhǎn.
    - Not every official’s child abuses power; many work hard and rely on their own abilities.
### 官二代(官二代) → 官(官)
- **definition:** official; government; public office; authority
- **pinyin:** guān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (會意)
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 宀 + 𠂤 — multiple rooms under a roof, an official building.
  - **interpretation:** A roof placed over a cluster of rooms evokes a government office or administrative building. From that image, the word extended to mean officials, officialdom, and related institutional senses.
- **examples:**
  - 他当了官(他當了官)。
    - Tā dāng le guān.
    - He became an official.
  - 他不愿涉足官场(他不願涉足官場)。
    - Tā bùyuàn shèzú guānchǎng.
    - He is unwilling to step into officialdom.
  - 在中医里(在中醫裡)，肝被称为将军之官(肝被稱為將軍之官)。
    - Zài Zhōngyī lǐ, gān bèi chēngwéi jiāngjūn zhī guān.
    - In traditional Chinese medicine, the liver is called the organ of the general.
### 官二代(官二代) → 二代(二代)
- **definition:** second generation; 2nd-generation; offspring; descendants
- **pinyin:** èrdài
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 二(=第二) + 代(一代/世代) = second generation; successor generation.
  - **interpretation:** Refers to the second generation of a family, group, or product; often used as a suffix in compounds like 移民二代 or 富二代. Can broadly mean the offspring or successor cohort that follows the first generation.
- **examples:**
  - 他是艺术家的二代(他是藝術家的二代)，继承了家族的天赋(繼承了家族的天賦)。
    - Tā shì yìshùjiā de èrdài, jìchéngle jiāzú de tiānfù.
    - He is the second generation of artists and has inherited the family's talent.
  - 这家公司在二代接班后发展更快(這家公司在二代接班後發展更快)。
    - Zhè jiā gōngsī zài èrdài jiēbān hòu fāzhǎn gèng kuài.
    - After the second generation took over, the company grew faster.
  - 移民二代在两种文化中成长(移民二代在兩種文化中成長)。
    - Yímín èrdài zài liǎng zhǒng wénhuà zhōng chéngzhǎng.
    - Second-generation immigrants grow up between two cultures.

### 官二代(官二代) → 二代(二代) → 二(二) → 一(一)
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 官二代(官二代) → 二代(二代) → 代(代)
- **definition:** 
- **pinyin:**

    - rén

  - 弋

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 亻 ('person') + phonetic 弋 (OC *lɯɡ)

    - Jīntiān jiù yǐ chá dài jiǔ ba.

### 官二代(官二代) → 二代(二代) → 代(代) → 人(人)
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 官二代(官二代) → 二代(二代) → 代(代) → 弋(弋)
- **definition:** to shoot; to catch; dart
- **pinyin:** yì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a sharp peg — original form of 杙 (OC *lɯɡ, “peg”); same as 才 and 尗.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a pointed peg or dart, a simple tool meant to pierce or stick into something. From this image arose senses related to shooting or striking game, and it also served as the base form for characters like 杙.
- **examples:**
  - 古人常用弋射鸟兽(古人常用弋射鳥獸)。
    - Gǔrén cháng yòng yìshè niǎo shòu.
    - In ancient times people often used yi-shooting to take birds and beasts.
  - 猎人以弋猎为生(獵人以弋獵為生)。
    - Lièrén yǐ yìliè wéi shēng.
    - Hunters made a living by fowling or hunting with yi.
  - 这件青铜器上刻有弋的形象(這件青銅器上刻有弋的形象)。
    - Zhè jiàn qīngtóngqì shàng kè yǒu yì de xíngxiàng.
    - This bronze vessel is engraved with the image of a yi-peg/dart.
