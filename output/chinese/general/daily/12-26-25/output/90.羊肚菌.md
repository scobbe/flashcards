## morel mushroom
### yángdùjùn
---
- **definition:** morel mushroom
- **pinyin:** yángdùjùn
- **components:**
  - 羊肚
    - yángdǔ
    - sheep tripe; sheep stomach; sheep belly; sheep offal
  - 菌
    - jūn
    - fungus; mushroom; microbe; germ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 羊肚 (sheep tripe) + 菌 (fungus) = a fungus whose cap resembles sheep tripe (morel).
  - **interpretation:** Highlights the honeycomb, wrinkled cap reminiscent of a sheep’s stomach lining. Common name for edible morels in Chinese.
- **examples:**
  - 春天常见到羊肚菌在林地里生长(春天常見到羊肚菌在林地裡生長)。
    - Chūntiān cháng jiàn dào yángdǔjūn zài líndì lǐ shēngzhǎng.
    - In spring, morels are often seen growing in woodlands.
  - 它的菌盖像羊肚(它的菌蓋像羊肚)，因此得名(因此得名)。
    - Tā de jūngài xiàng yángdǔ, yīncǐ démíng.
    - Its cap looks like sheep tripe, hence the name.
  - 羊肚菌味道鲜美(羊肚菌味道鮮美)，常被用来煲汤或炒菜(常被用來煲湯或炒菜)。
    - Yángdǔjūn wèidào xiānměi, cháng bèi yònglái bāotāng huò chǎocài.
    - Morels taste delicious and are often used in soups or stir-fries.

### 羊肚菌(羊肚菌) → 菌(菌)
- **definition:** fungus; mushroom; microbe; germ
- **pinyin:** jūn
- **components:**
  - 囷
    - qūn
    - granary; grain stack
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 艹 + phonetic: 囷 (OC *kʰrun, *ɡrun).
  - **interpretation:** 艹 points to a plant/biological domain, fitting senses like fungus, mold, and microbes. 囷 supplies the sound value historically and, being associated with grain stores, loosely echoes environments where molds and fungi may grow.
- **examples:**
  - 医生说这种细菌对抗生素有抗性(醫生說這種細菌對抗生素有抗性)。

  - 真菌在森林的腐木上生长(真菌在森林的腐木上生長)。
