## spray
### pēn
---
- **traditional:** 噴
- **definition:** spray
- **pinyin:** pēn
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 賁
    - bēn
    - related to "decorate"
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic 賁 (bēn, related to "decorate") =
    - sound coming from the mouth ->
    - spray
  - **interpretation:** The character 噴 (pēn, "spray") combines the semantic element 口 (kǒu, "mouth") with the phonetic element 賁 (bēn), suggesting the action of emitting something like spray or mist from the mouth.
  - **simplification:** 噴 -> 喷(噴): 壴 (zhù, "drum") replaced by 巾 (jīn, "towel") for a simpler phonetic match.
- **examples:**
  - 他用喷壶(噴壺)，给(給)花浇(澆)水。
    - Tā yòng pēn hú, gěi huā jiāo shuǐ.
    - He uses a spray bottle to water the flowers.
  - 水龙头(龍頭)在下雨时(時)，不断喷(斷噴)出清水。
    - Shuǐ lóngtóu zài xià yǔ shí, bù duàn pēn chū qīng shuǐ.
    - The faucet continuously sprays clear water during the rain.
### 喷(噴) → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 喷(噴) → 賁
- **definition:** related to "decorate"
- **pinyin:** bēn
- **components:**
  - 卉
    - huì
    - plant
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 卉 (huì, 'plant') + Semantic: 貝 (bèi, 'cowry') ->
    - loaned for 奔 (bēn, 'rush') ->
    - used for 'decorate'
  - **interpretation:** Originally, 賁(bēn) used 貝 ('cowry') symbolizing valuable decoration and paired with phonetic 卉 ('plant'). It was borrowed early on to represent 奔 ('rush'), diversifying its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他賁飾了院子，使之焕(煥)然一新。
    - Tā bēn shì le yuànzi, shǐ zhī huànrán yī xīn.
    - He decorated the courtyard, making it look completely renewed.
  - 节(節)日来临时(來臨時)，市集被彩灯(燈)賁耀。
    - Jiérì láilín shí, shìjí bèi cǎidēng bēn yào.
    - When the festival arrived, the market was brilliantly decorated with colorful lights.
### 喷(噴) → 賁 → 卉
- **definition:** plant
- **pinyin:** huì
- **components:**
  - 丗
    - shì
    - generation
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 丗 (shì, "generation") + 十 (shí, "ten") =
    - plants growing in a field
  - **interpretation:** The character 卉(huì, "plant") is composed of 丗 (shì, "generation") and 十 (shí, "ten"), symbolizing a multitude of plants sprouting in a field, representing abundance and growth.
- **examples:**
  - 花卉争艳(爭艷)。
    - Huā huì zhēng yàn.
    - The plants compete in beauty.
  - 公园里种满(園裡種滿)各色卉，营(營)造了生机(機)勃勃的景象。
    - Gōngyuán lǐ zhòng mǎn gè sè huì, yíngzào le shēngjī bóbó de jǐngxiàng.
    - The park is filled with a variety of plants, creating a vibrant scene.
  - 古人赞(讚)美卉木，吟诗咏(詩詠)其秀丽(麗)。
    - Gǔrén zànměi huì mù, yín shī yǒng qí xiùlì.
    - Ancient scholars praised the flora and composed poems to admire its beauty.
### 喷(噴) → 賁 → 卉 → 丗
- **definition:** generation
- **pinyin:** shì
- **components:**
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 十 (shí, "ten") repeated three times =
    - thirty
  - **interpretation:** The character 丗 (shì) symbolizes the number thirty by visually representing the concept of 'ten' (十) repeated three times, indicating a generation as a span of thirty years.
### 喷(噴) → 賁 → 卉 → 丗 → 十
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园里(園裡)有十棵树(樹)，景色十分优(優)美。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.
### 喷(噴) → 賁 → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为货币(為貨幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
