## spray
### pēn
---
- **definition:** spray
- **pinyin:** pēn
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 賁
    - bēn
    - related to "decorate"
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic 賁(賁) (bēn, related to "decorate") =
    - sound coming from the mouth ->
    - spray
  - **interpretation:** The character 噴(噴) (pēn, "spray") combines the semantic element 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") with the phonetic element 賁(賁) (bēn), suggesting the action of emitting something like spray or mist from the mouth.
  - **simplification:** 噴(噴) -> 喷(噴): 壴(壴) (zhù, "drum") replaced by 巾(巾) (jīn, "towel") for a simpler phonetic match.
- **examples:**
  - 他用喷壶(他用噴壺)，给花浇水(給花澆水)。
    - Tā yòng pēn hú, gěi huā jiāo shuǐ.
    - He uses a spray bottle to water the flowers.
  - 水龙头在下雨时(水龍頭在下雨時)，不断喷出清水(不斷噴出清水)。
    - Shuǐ lóngtóu zài xià yǔ shí, bù duàn pēn chū qīng shuǐ.
    - The faucet continuously sprays clear water during the rain.
### 喷(噴) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，大声喊叫(大聲喊叫)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这口饭入口即化(這口飯入口即化)。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹果(小女孩咬了一口蘋果)，露出甜美的笑容(露出甜美的笑容)。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 喷(噴) → 賁(賁)
- **definition:** related to "decorate"
- **pinyin:** bēn
- **components:**
  - 卉
    - huì
    - plant
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 卉(卉) (huì, 'plant') + Semantic: 貝(貝) (bèi, 'cowry') ->
    - loaned for 奔(奔) (bēn, 'rush') ->
    - used for 'decorate'
  - **interpretation:** Originally, 賁(bēn) used 貝(貝) ('cowry') symbolizing valuable decoration and paired with phonetic 卉(卉) ('plant'). It was borrowed early on to represent 奔(奔) ('rush'), diversifying its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他賁飾了院子(他賁飾了院子)，使之焕然一新(使之煥然一新)。
    - Tā bēn shì le yuànzi, shǐ zhī huànrán yī xīn.
    - He decorated the courtyard, making it look completely renewed.
  - 节日来临时(節日來臨時)，市集被彩灯賁耀(市集被彩燈賁耀)。
    - Jiérì láilín shí, shìjí bèi cǎidēng bēn yào.
    - When the festival arrived, the market was brilliantly decorated with colorful lights.

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 卉(huì, "plant") is composed of 丗(丗) (shì, "generation") and 十(十) (shí, "ten"), symbolizing a multitude of plants sprouting in a field, representing abundance and growth.

### 喷(噴) → 賁(賁) → 卉(卉) → 丗(丗) → 卅(卅) → 十(十)
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(我有十本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园里有十棵树(花園裡有十棵樹)，景色十分优美(景色十分優美)。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(十年磨一劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.
### 喷(噴) → 賁(賁) → 貝(貝)
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护身符(護身符)，放在口袋里(口袋裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝作为货币(古代人用貝作為貨幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
