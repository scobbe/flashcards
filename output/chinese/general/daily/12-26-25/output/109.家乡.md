## hometown
### jiāxiāng
---
- **traditional:** 家鄉
- **definition:** hometown
- **pinyin:** jiāxiāng
- **components:**
  - 家
    - jiā
    - home; family; house
  - 乡(鄉)
    - xiāng
    - village; countryside; hometown
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 家 (jiā, "home") + 乡(鄉) (xiāng, "village") refers to one's place of origin or hometown. Together they form 家乡(鄉) (jiāxiāng, "hometown"), emphasizing the idea of a home village or the place where one feels a deep connection due to personal roots or heritage.
  - **interpretation:** 家乡(鄉) (jiāxiāng, "hometown") signifies the place that is culturally and sentimentally significant to an individual, often where they were born and raised.
  - **simplification:** 家乡 is the simplified form of 家鄉.
- **examples:**
  - 我热爱家乡(鄉)。
    - Wǒ rè'ài jiāxiāng.
    - I love my hometown.
  - 每当(當)春节(節)到来时(來時)，我都会想念家乡(鄉)。
    - Měi dāng chūnjié dào lái shí, wǒ dōu huì xiǎngniàn jiāxiāng.
    - Whenever the Spring Festival arrives, I always miss my hometown.
  - 经历长(經歷長)途跋涉后(後)，我终于回到了家乡(鄉)。
    - Jīnglì chángtú báshè hòu, wǒ zhōngyú huí dào le jiāxiāng.
    - After a long journey, I finally returned to my hometown.
### 家( )乡(鄉) → 家
- **definition:** home; family; house
- **pinyin:** jiā
- **components:**
  - 宀
    - mián
    - roof
  - 豕
    - shǐ
    - pig
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 宀 (mián, 'roof') + 豕 (shǐ, 'pig') =
    - pig under a roof ->
    - home, house
  - **interpretation:** The character 家 (jiā, 'home; family; house') uses the pictorial elements of a 'roof' and a 'pig' to evoke an ancient image of a house, where pigs were commonly kept under the same roof as a family, indicating the concept of a home.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱(愛)我的家。
    - Wǒ ài wǒ de jiā.
    - I love my home.
  - 回家路上遇见(見)老朋友，聊起往事。
    - Huí jiā lùshàng yùjiàn lǎo péngyǒu, liáo qǐ wǎngshì.
    - On the way home, I met an old friend, and we reminisced about the past.
### 家( )乡(鄉) → 家 → 宀
- **definition:** roof
- **pinyin:** mián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a house with four walls ->
    - roof
  - **interpretation:** 宀(mián, "roof") is a pictogram representing a house with four walls. It emphasizes an enclosed space, distinguishing it from similar structures like 广(guǎng, "broad") which depicts a building with three walls. This makes 宀 a logical choice for representing a roof in various characters.
- **examples:**
  - 含有宀的汉(漢)字代表家的温(溫)暖。
    - Hán yǒu mián de hànzì dàibiǎo jiā de wēnnuǎn.
    - Chinese characters that include the radical '宀' represent the warmth of home.
  - 屋顶(頂)遮风挡(風擋)雨，家园(園)散发温(發溫)馨。
    - Wūdǐng zhē fēng dǎng yǔ, jiāyuán sànfā wēnxīn.
    - The roof shields from wind and rain, and the home radiates warmth.
### 家( )乡(鄉) → 家 → 豕
- **definition:** pig
- **pinyin:** shǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a pig with distinct features like snout and legs.
  - **interpretation:** The character 豕 (shǐ, 'pig') is a pictogram that visually represents a pig's profile, emphasizing features such as its snout and legs, which help to convey the idea of a pig or boar.
- **examples:**
  - 这头(這頭)豕很肥。
    - Zhè tóu shǐ hěn féi.
    - This pig is very fat.
  - 农(農)民饲养(飼養)豕，供应(應)市场(場)。
    - Nóngmín sìyǎng shǐ, gōngyìng shìchǎng.
    - Farmers raise pigs to supply the market.
  - 豕在泥潭中嬉戏(戲)。
    - Shǐ zài ní tán zhōng xīxì.
    - The pig frolics in the mud.
### 家( )乡(鄉) → 乡(鄉)
- **traditional:** 鄉
- **definition:** village; countryside; hometown
- **pinyin:** xiāng
- **components:**
  - 𠨍
    - two people facing each other
    - two people facing each other
  - 皀
    - round-mouthed food vessel
    - round-mouthed food vessel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 𠨍 (𠨍) (two people facing each other) + 皀 (皀) (round-mouthed food vessel) ->
    - original meaning 'to feast' ->
    - phonetic borrowing or semantic shifting to mean 'village'
  - **interpretation:** The character initially represented two people facing a food vessel, symbolizing communal feasting, which was a central part of village life. Over time, it came to denote 'village' through borrowing or semantic shift, aligning with the social aspect it originally depicted.
  - **simplification:** 乡(鄉) simplifies by using a component resembling 良 (liáng, 'good'), unrelated to 郎 (láng, 'young man'), to streamline its form, based on 皀 during the clerical script development.
- **examples:**
  - 我来(來)自乡(鄉)。
    - Wǒ láizì xiāng.
    - I come from the countryside.
  - 我怀(懷)念乡(鄉)，那里(裡)有我的故事。
    - Wǒ huáiniàn xiāng, nàlǐ yǒu wǒ de gùshì.
    - I miss my hometown, where my stories lie.
  - 他常回乡(鄉)，寻(尋)找旧(舊)日情怀(懷)。
    - Tā cháng huí xiāng, xúnzhǎo jiù rì qínghuái.
    - He often returns to his hometown, seeking the old sentiments.
### 家( )乡(鄉) → 乡(鄉) → 𠨍
- **definition:** two people facing each other
- **pinyin:** two people facing each other
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** variant form
  - **description:** 𠨍 is a variant form of 卯 (mǎo, "4th earthly branch") depicting the fourth stage in the Chinese zodiac related to the rabbit hour, but specifically featuring two people facing each other.
  - **interpretation:** The character 𠨍 as a variant form reflects an alternative depiction of the concept associated with the 4th earthly branch in the Chinese zodiac, illustrating a social interaction through the imagery of two people facing each other.
