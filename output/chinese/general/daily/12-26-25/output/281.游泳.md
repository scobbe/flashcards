## swim; swimming
### yóuyǒng
---
- **definition:** swim; swimming
- **pinyin:** yóuyǒng
- **components:**
  - 游(遊)
    - yóu
    - to swim; to travel; to wander
  - 泳
    - yǒng
    - to swim
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 游(遊) (yóu, "to swim; to travel") + 泳 (yǒng, "to swim") =
    - swim; swimming
  - **interpretation:** The word 游泳 (yóuyǒng) combines 游(遊) (yóu, "to swim; to travel") and 泳 (yǒng, "to swim") to depict the action of swimming. It emphasizes the physical act as well as the potential enjoyment or leisure activity associated with it.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)游泳。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān yóuyǒng.
    - I like swimming.
  - 夏天来(來)了，我们(們)去游泳。
    - Xiàtiān láile, wǒmen qù yóuyǒng.
    - When summer comes, we go swimming.
  - 他每天练习(練習)游泳，以备参(備參)加比赛(賽)。
    - Tā měitiān liànxí yóuyǒng, yǐ bèi cānjiā bǐsài.
    - He practices swimming every day in preparation for competitions.
### 游泳 → 游(遊)
- **traditional:** 遊
- **definition:** to swim; to travel; to wander
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 斿
    - yóu
    - travel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 水 (shuǐ, "water") + Phonetic: 斿 (yóu, originally having meaning related to travel) =
    - movement through or on water ->
    - swim/travel/wander
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the water radical with a component originally meaning travel, highlighting both the aspect of swimming in water and wandering or traveling. This reflects the versatility in its meanings.
  - **simplification:** Traditional 遊 (yóu, "wander") uses 辵 (chuò, "walk") for traveling, simplified by replacing it with 水 (shuǐ, "water"), focusing on swimming, which in Chinese culture is closely related to wandering or traveling freely.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)游泳。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān yóuyǒng.
    - I like swimming.
  - 我们周(們週)末去郊外游(遊)，感受大自然。
    - Wǒmen zhōumò qù jiāowài yóu, gǎnshòu dàzìrán.
    - We will travel to the countryside on the weekend and enjoy nature.
  - 他梦(夢)想环游(環遊)世界。
    - Tā mèngxiǎng huányóu shìjiè.
    - He dreams of traveling the world.
### 游泳 → 游(遊) → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flowing water
  - **interpretation:** 水 (shuǐ, 'water') is a pictogram that directly represents the appearance of flowing water, making it intuitive and easy to recognize. Its simplicity and direct visual reference to its meaning have allowed it to remain unchanged over millennia.
- **examples:**
  - 水是一种(種)生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì yī zhǒng shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 夏天很热(熱)，喝水能解暑。
    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, hē shuǐ néng jiě shǔ.
    - It is very hot in summer, drinking water can relieve the heat.
  - 河水潺潺，沿岸绿树(綠樹)成荫(蔭)。
    - Hé shuǐ chánchán, yán'àn lǜshù chéng yīn.
    - The river flows babbling, and green trees along the bank provide shade.
### 游泳 → 游(遊) → 斿
- **definition:** travel
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 㫃
    - jī
    - flag
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 㫃 ("flag") + 子 ("child") =
    - a child holding a flag (to travel) ->
    - travel
  - **interpretation:** 斿(yóu, "travel") combines 㫃 ("flag") and 子 ("child") to depict a child holding a flag, symbolizing the act of traveling or moving from one place to another.
### 游泳 → 游(遊) → 斿 → 㫃
- **definition:** flag
- **pinyin:** jī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a flag on a pole with a decoration on top ->
    - stylized as 方
  - **interpretation:** The character 㫃 is a pictogram representing a flag on a pole, indicative of its original use to depict banners or flags. Over time, the flag and pole have been stylized, resulting in the modern depiction resembling 方 (fāng, 'square').
### 游泳 → 游(遊) → 斿 → 子
- **definition:** child
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a baby with a large head and spread arms, with legs wrapped in a blanket
  - **interpretation:** The character 子(zǐ, "child") is a pictorial representation of a baby, emphasizing characteristics like a large head and outstretched arms typical of infants, which conveys the essence of a child. This visual element helps infer its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)小子很调(調)皮。
    - Zhè xiǎo zǐ hěn tiáopí.
    - This kid is very naughty.
  - 他的小子爱(愛)打篮(籃)球，每天都锻炼(鍛煉)身体(體)。
    - Tā de xiǎo zǐ ài dǎ lánqiú, měitiān dōu duànliàn shēntǐ.
    - His child loves playing basketball, and practices every day.
  - 孤独(獨)的子需要关爱(關愛)。
    - Gūdú de zǐ xūyào guān'ài.
    - A lonely child needs care.
### 游泳 → 泳
- **definition:** to swim
- **pinyin:** yǒng
- **components:**
  - 氵
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 永
    - yǒng
    - "to swim"
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 氵 (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic: 永 (yǒng) =
    - swimming "water"
  - **interpretation:** The character 泳 (yǒng, "to swim") combines the water radical 氵 to emphasize its relation to water, with 永 (yǒng) providing the pronunciation. Swimming is inherently linked to the concept of water, making this combination intuitive.
- **examples:**
  - 小明喜欢(歡)游泳。
    - Xiǎomíng xǐhuān yóuyǒng.
    - Xiao Ming likes swimming.
  - 夏天来临时(來臨時)，大家纷纷(紛紛)下水游泳。
    - Xiàtiān láilín shí, dàjiā fēnfēn xiàshuǐ yóuyǒng.
    - When summer arrives, everyone jumps into the water to swim.
  - 教练(練)指导运动员(導運動員)游泳。
    - Jiàoliàn zhǐdǎo yùndòngyuán yóuyǒng.
    - The coach instructs the athletes in swimming.
### 游泳 → 泳 → 氵
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 氵is the radical form of 水 (shuǐ, "water") ->
    - depicts flowing water, compressed and stylized next to characters.
  - **interpretation:** The character 氵is a simplified variant used as a radical to represent water-related meanings in other characters. It retains the flowing water depiction of 水 (shuǐ, "water") but is stylized to fit alongside other components.
- **examples:**
  - 水是生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 汉(漢)字中偏旁氵，代表水。
    - Hànzì zhōng piān páng shuǐ, dàibiǎo shuǐ.
    - In Chinese characters, the radical '氵' signifies water.
  - 河水清澈透明，鱼儿欢(魚兒歡)快戏(戲)水。
    - Hé shuǐ qīngchè tòumíng, yú er huānkuaì xì shuǐ.
    - The river water is clear and transparent, and the fish happily play in it.
### 游泳 → 泳 → 永
- **definition:** "to swim"
- **pinyin:** yǒng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a long river with tributaries ->
    - meaning "long"
  - **interpretation:** The character 永(yǒng, "long") represents a continuously flowing river, symbolizing eternity or longevity through its perpetually moving water. This aligns with its use to denote duration.
- **examples:**
  - 他们的友谊会永长存(他們的友誼會永長存)。
    - Tāmen de yǒuyì huì yǒng yuǎn chángcún.
    - Their friendship will last forever.
  - 学(學)者探讨(討)古籍时(時)，发现‘永’的用法源远流长(發現‘永’的用法源遠流長)。
    - Xuézhě tàntǎo gǔjí shí, fāxiàn ‘yǒng’ de yòngfǎ yuányuǎn liúcháng.
    - When scholars study ancient texts, they discovered that the usage of '永' carries timeless wisdom.
  - 记(記)住他的承诺时(諾時)，我们应以永不放弃的精神面对挑战(我們應以永不放棄的精神面對挑戰)。
    - Jìzhù tā de chéngnuò shí, wǒmen yīng yǐ yǒng bù fàngqì de jīngshén miànduì tiǎozhàn.
    - Remembering his promise, we should face challenges with an unyielding spirit.
