## everything
### yīqiè
---
- **definition:** everything
- **pinyin:** yīqiè
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; single; alone; unity
  - 切
    - qiè
    - all; every; everything; to cut
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 一 (yī, "one; single") denotes unity or a singular entity, while 切 (qiè, "all; every") expands this to encompass all, forming 'everything'.
  - **interpretation:** 一切 (yīqiè, "everything") combines 一 (yī, "one") with 切 (qiè, "all") to express the totality of existence or all aspects of a situation or environment. The term is often used to denote comprehensive inclusion, reflecting the idea that all things are considered as one.
- **examples:**
  - 他( )放( )弃(棄)一( )切( )。
    - Tā fàngqì yīqiè.
    - He gave up everything.
  - 她努力工作，追( )求( )一( )切( )梦(夢)想( )。
    - Tā nǔlì gōngzuò, zhuīqiú yīqiè mèngxiǎng.
    - She works hard, pursuing all her dreams.
  - 一切皆有可能。
    - Yīqiè jiē yǒu kěnéng.
    - Everything is possible.
