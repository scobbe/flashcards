## everything
### yīqiè
---
- **definition:** everything
- **pinyin:** yīqiè
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; single; alone; unity
  - 切
    - qiè
    - all; every; everything; to cut
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 一 (yī, "one; single") denotes unity or a singular entity, while 切 (qiè, "all; every") expands this to encompass all, forming 'everything'.
  - **interpretation:** 一切 (yīqiè, "everything") combines 一 (yī, "one") with 切 (qiè, "all") to express the totality of existence or all aspects of a situation or environment. The term is often used to denote comprehensive inclusion, reflecting the idea that all things are considered as one.
- **examples:**
  - 他放弃(棄)一切。
    - Tā fàngqì yīqiè.
    - He gave up everything.
  - 她努力工作，追求一切梦(夢)想。
    - Tā nǔlì gōngzuò, zhuīqiú yīqiè mèngxiǎng.
    - She works hard, pursuing all her dreams.
  - 一切皆有可能。
    - Yīqiè jiē yǒu kěnéng.
    - Everything is possible.
### 一切 → 一
- **definition:** one; single; alone; unity
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** A horizontal stroke representing the concept of 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** The character 一(yī, "one") is a simple horizontal line signifying the singular and unified concept of 'one'. It directly represents being singular or the number one.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一个苹(個蘋)果。
    - Wǒ yǒu yī gè píngguǒ.
    - I have one apple.
  - 他只看到一只鸟(隻鳥)，飞进(飛進)了花园(園)。
    - Tā zhǐ kàn dào yī zhī niǎo, fēi jìn le huā yuán.
    - He only saw one bird, which flew into the garden.
  - 一条(條)直路通向远(遠)方。
    - Yī tiáo zhí lù tōng xiàng yuǎn fāng.
    - One straight road leads to the distance.
### 一切 → 切
- **definition:** all; every; everything; to cut
- **pinyin:** qiè
- **components:**
  - 七
    - qī
    - seven
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 七 (qī, 'seven') + Semantic: 刀 (dāo, 'knife') =
    - sharp object ->
    - to cut
  - **interpretation:** The character 切(qiè, 'to cut') combines the sound component 七(qī, 'seven') with the semantic component 刀(dāo, 'knife'). This implies the action of cutting with a sharp tool.
- **examples:**
  - 厨(廚)房的刀非常锋(鋒)利，切菜时请(時請)小心。
    - chúfáng de dāo fēicháng fēnglì, qiè cài shí qǐng xiǎoxīn.
    - The kitchen knife is very sharp, so be careful when cutting vegetables.
  - 一切问题(問題)都能解决(決)。
    - yīqiè wèntí dōu néng jiějué.
    - Every problem can be solved.
### 一切 → 切 → 七
- **definition:** seven
- **pinyin:** qī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a horizontal line (一) intersected by a short vertical line (|) ->
    - symbolic representation of the number seven
  - **interpretation:** The character 七(qī, "seven") is a pictogram that visually abstracts the number seven with a simple shape that suggests counting or enumeration.
- **examples:**
  - 我有七个苹(個蘋)果。
    - Wǒ yǒu qī gè píngguǒ.
    - I have seven apples.
  - 花园里(園裡)有七只(隻)蝴蝶，在阳(陽)光下翩翩起舞。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu qī zhī húdié, zài yángguāng xià piānpiān qǐwǔ.
    - There are seven butterflies in the garden, dancing gracefully under the sunlight.
  - 会议(會議)定在七点(點)，不容迟(遲)到。
    - Huìyì dìng zài qī diǎn, bù róng chídào.
    - The meeting is scheduled at seven o'clock, and lateness is not allowed.
### 一切 → 切 → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a knife
  - **interpretation:** 刀 (dāo, "knife") is a pictographic representation of a knife or blade, with a sharp edge and a handle, resembling the form of a basic cutting tool.
- **examples:**
  - 他用锋(鋒)利的刀，切开(開)蔬菜。
    - tā yòng fēnglì de dāo, qiē kāi shūcài.
    - He uses a sharp knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这(這)把刀非常锋(鋒)利。
    - zhè bǎ dāo fēicháng fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
  - 厨师(廚師)小心地持刀，为(為)客人切肉。
    - chúshī xiǎoxīn de chí dāo, wèi kèrén qiē ròu.
    - The chef holds the knife carefully and slices meat for the guest.
