## everything
### yīqiè
---
- **definition:** everything
- **pinyin:** yīqiè
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; single; entire; whole
  - 切
    - qiè
    - all (classical); appropriate/close; earnest/urgent; to cut
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 一 ‘one/entire’ + 切 ‘all; the whole’ = everything; all.
  - **interpretation:** Combines numeral 一 with classical 切 meaning “all/every” to denote the totality of things without exception. Common in both literary and modern usage for “everything; all.”
- **examples:**
  - 请把一切准备好(請把一切準備好)。
    - Qǐng bǎ yíqiè zhǔnbèi hǎo.
    - Please get everything ready.
  - 他愿意为你放弃一切(他願意為你放棄一切)。
    - Tā yuànyì wèi nǐ fàngqì yíqiè.
    - He is willing to give up everything for you.
  - 面对困难时(面對困難時)，我们要相信一切都会好起来(我們要相信一切都會好起來)。
    - Miànduì kùnnan shí, wǒmen yào xiāngxìn yíqiè dōu huì hǎo qǐlái.
    - When facing difficulties, we should believe that everything will get better.
### 一切(一切) → 一(一)
- **definition:** one; single; entire; whole
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 一切(一切) → 切(切)
- **definition:** all (classical); appropriate/close; earnest/urgent; to cut
- **pinyin:** qiè
- **components:**
  - 七
    - qī
    - seven
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic: 七 + semantic: 刀 “knife”
  - **interpretation:** Built with 刀 supplying the cutting/edge sense and 七 providing the approximate sound. Core meaning centers on cutting, while later extensions like “close/appropriate” and “urgent” likely arise from the notion of cutting close or pressing sharply on the point.
- **examples:**
  - 一切准备就绪(一切準備就緒)。
    - Yīqiè zhǔnbèi jiùxù.
    - Everything is ready.
  - 局势紧张(局勢緊張)，需求迫切(需求迫切)。
    - Júshì jǐnzhāng, xūqiú pòqiè.
    - The situation is tense, and the need is urgent.
  - 你的发言很切题(你的發言很切題)。
    - Nǐ de fāyán hěn qiètí.
    - Your remarks are very relevant to the topic.
### 一切(一切) → 切(切) → 七(七)
- **definition:** seven
- **pinyin:** qī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – a vertical stroke and a horizontal stroke bisecting each other; original character of 切 (“to cut”).
  - **interpretation:** A cross-like cut mark is depicted, suggesting an incision where one line cuts across another. This graph was later employed to write the numeral seven; the modern form preserves the crossing strokes that reflect the original cutting imagery.
- **examples:**
  - 我今年七岁(我今年七歲)。
    - Wǒ jīnnián qī suì.
    - I am seven years old this year.
  - 我们七个人一起去旅行(我們七個人一起去旅行)。
    - Wǒmen qī gè rén yìqǐ qù lǚxíng.
    - The seven of us are traveling together.
  - 比赛在七点开始(比賽在七點開始)。
    - Bǐsài zài qī diǎn kāishǐ.
    - The game starts at seven o’clock.
### 一切(一切) → 切(切) → 刀(刀)
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — a knife; original form 𠚣, seen in a variant of 利 (𥝢) and atop 黎.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a knife with a distinct blade and handle. The graph was streamlined into the current two-stroke form and also serves as a component in characters like 利 and 黎.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿着一把刀(他拿著一把刀)。
    - Tā názhe yì bǎ dāo.
    - He is holding a knife.
  - 厨师用刀切蔬菜(廚師用刀切蔬菜)。
    - Chúshī yòng dāo qiē shūcài.
    - The chef uses a knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这把刀很锋利(這把刀很鋒利)。
    - Zhè bǎ dāo hěn fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
