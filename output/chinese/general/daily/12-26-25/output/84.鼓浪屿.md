## Gulangyu Island
### Gǔlàngyǔ
---
- **definition:** Gulangyu Island
- **pinyin:** Gǔlàngyǔ
- **components:**
  - 鼓浪
    - gǔlàng
    - to beat/rouse waves; surging waves
  - 屿(嶼)
    - yǔ
    - islet; small island
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound toponym
  - **description:** 鼓浪 (beating/rousing waves) + 屿 (islet) = islet where waves drum.
  - **interpretation:** Named for the drum-like booming when waves strike shoreline rocks. Refers to the small island off Xiamen famed for its surf and scenery.
  - **simplification:** 嶼 was simplified to 屿 by dropping the mountain radical to reduce strokes; 鼓、浪 unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我们明天去鼓浪屿参观(我們明天去鼓浪嶼參觀)。
    - Wǒmen míngtiān qù Gǔlàngyǔ cānguān.
    - We’re going to visit Gulangyu tomorrow.
  - 鼓浪屿因钢琴文化和历史建筑闻名(鼓浪嶼因鋼琴文化和歷史建築聞名)。
    - Gǔlàngyǔ yīn gāngqín wénhuà hé lìshǐ jiànzhù wénmíng.
    - Gulangyu is famous for its piano culture and historic architecture.
  - 传说海浪拍打岩石会发出如鼓的声音，因此得名“鼓浪屿”(傳說海浪拍打岩石會發出如鼓的聲音，因此得名「鼓浪嶼」)。
    - Chuánshuō hǎilàng pāidǎ yánshí huì fāchū rú gǔ de shēngyīn, yīncǐ dé míng “Gǔlàngyǔ”.
    - Legend says waves hitting the rocks make drum-like sounds, hence the name “Gulangyu.”
### 鼓浪屿(鼓浪嶼) → 鼓浪
- **definition:** to beat/rouse waves; surging waves
- **pinyin:** gǔlàng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 鼓(beat; rouse) + 浪(waves) = waves being beaten up; surging surf.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the sense of drumming/rousing with waves to depict surf stirred into motion or pounding swells. Can be literal (billowing waves) or figurative (stirring up a commotion).
- **examples:**
  - 海风渐起(海風漸起)，海面鼓浪(海面鼓浪)。
    - Hǎifēng jiàn qǐ, hǎimiàn gǔlàng.
    - As the sea breeze picks up, the water surface heaves with surging waves.
  - 台风来临时(颱風來臨時)，巨石被鼓浪拍击(巨石被鼓浪拍擊)。
    - Táifēng láilín shí, jùshí bèi gǔlàng pāijī.
    - When the typhoon arrives, the boulders are pounded by the billowing surf.
  - 炮声隆隆(炮聲隆隆)，江水鼓浪奔流(江水鼓浪奔流)。
    - Pàoshēng lónglóng, jiāngshuǐ gǔlàng bēnliú.
    - With cannons rumbling, the river surges in wave after wave.

### 鼓浪屿(鼓浪嶼) → 鼓浪 → 鼓 → 支 → 十
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**

### 鼓浪屿(鼓浪嶼) → 屿(嶼)
- **definition:** islet; small island
- **pinyin:** yǔ
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 与(與)
    - yǔ

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 山 + phonetic: 與 (→ 与)

  - 屿上只有一座白色灯塔(嶼上只有一座白色燈塔)。

### 鼓浪屿(鼓浪嶼) → 屿(嶼) → 山
- **definition:** mountain
- **pinyin:** shān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) — three mountain peaks; oracle forms show triangles rather than vertical strokes. Unrelated to 出, the top of 岁/豐, 屮, and 巾.
  - **interpretation:** A visual sketch of a jagged ridge: one tall central peak flanked by two smaller ones. Earlier inscriptions rendered the peaks as simple triangles that later regularized into three vertical lines. Similar-looking forms like 出 or elements in 岁/豐 are lookalikes with no historical connection.
- **examples:**
  - 我们去爬山(我們去爬山)。
    - Wǒmen qù pá shān.
    - We’re going to hike/climb in the mountains.
  - 那座山很高(那座山很高)。
    - Nà zuò shān hěn gāo.
    - That mountain is very tall.
  - 山上有很多树(山上有很多樹)。
    - Shān shàng yǒu hěn duō shù.
    - There are many trees on the mountain.

### 鼓浪屿(鼓浪嶼) → 屿(嶼) → 与(與) → 牙
- **definition:** tooth; tusk
- **pinyin:** yá
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram of a pair of elephant tusks; per Xu Shen, used for human molars but not incisors (齒).
  - **interpretation:** Early forms depict two protruding tusks, a vivid image of hard, pointed teeth. Traditional scholarship narrowed its reference to back teeth, contrasting with 齒 for general teeth or incisors.
- **examples:**

  - 大象的象牙很珍贵(大象的象牙很珍貴)。
    - Dàxiàng de xiàngyá hěn zhēnguì.
    - Elephant ivory is very valuable.
  - 小孩开始换牙(小孩開始換牙)，长出新门牙了(長出新門牙了)。
    - Xiǎohái kāishǐ huàn yá, zhǎngchū xīn ményá le.
