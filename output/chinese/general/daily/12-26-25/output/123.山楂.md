## 
### hawthorn
---
- **pinyin:** hawthorn
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 楂
    - zhā
    - hawthorn tree
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 山(山) (shān, 'mountain') + 楂(楂) (zhā, 'hawthorn tree') — mountain hawthorn
  - **interpretation:** The term describes a tree that often grows in mountainous areas. The combination makes sense because hawthorn trees are frequently found in hilly or elevated regions.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃山楂果(我喜歡吃山楂果)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī shānzhā guǒ.
    - I like to eat hawthorn fruit.
  - 山楂树在果园里(山楂樹在果園裡)。
    - Shānzhā shù zài guǒyuán lǐ.
    - The hawthorn tree is in the orchard.
### 山楂(山楂) → 山(山)
- **definition:** mountain
- **pinyin:** shān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three mountain peaks.
  - **interpretation:** The visual representation of three peaks captures the essence of a mountain, symbolizing height and majesty.
- **examples:**
  - 我们去爬山。
    - Wǒmen qù pá shān.
    - We are going to climb the mountain.
  - 山上有很多树。
    - Shān shàng yǒu hěn duō shù.
    - There are many trees on the mountain.
### 山楂(山楂) → 楂(楂)
- **definition:** hawthorn tree
- **pinyin:** zhā
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 者
    - zhě
    - one who
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Combines the semantic element 木 (mù, 'tree') with the phonetic element 者 (zhě), which is used for its sound.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the idea of a specific type of tree and sounds similar to the word for hawthorn.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢楂树(我喜歡楂樹)，它的果实很好吃(它的果實很好吃)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān zhā shù, tā de guǒshí hěn hǎochī.
    - I like hawthorn trees, their fruits are very delicious.
  - 公园里有很多楂树(公園裡有很多楂樹)。
    - Gōngyuán lǐ yǒu hěn duō zhā shù.
    - There are many hawthorn trees in the park.
### 山楂(山楂) → 楂(楂) → 木(木)
- **definition:** tree
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tree: branches on top, roots on the bottom.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a tree with its distinguishing features, conveying the idea of wood directly through its shape.
- **examples:**
  - 这棵树是木的代表。(這棵樹是木的代表。)
    - Zhè kē shù shì mù de dàibiǎo.
    - This tree is a representation of wood.
  - 木材是建筑的重要材料。(木材是建築的重要材料。)
    - Mùcái shì jiànzhú de zhòngyào cáiliào.
    - Wood is an important material for construction.
### 山楂(山楂) → 楂(楂) → 者(者)
- **definition:** one who
- **pinyin:** zhě
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a sugarcane with full stems, with some points around representing either leaves or drops of sweet liquid, and an open and smiling mouth at the bottom.
  - **interpretation:** The image of the sugarcane conveys a sense of sweetness and abundance, suggesting a person who embodies these positive qualities.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个好者。
    - Tā shì yīgè hǎo zhě。
    - He is a good person.
  - 学习者应该多多实践。
    - Xuéxí zhě yīnggāi duōduō shíjiàn。
    - Learners should practice more.
