## scrambled eggs
### chǎodàn
---
- **definition:** scrambled eggs
- **pinyin:** chǎodàn
- **components:**
  - 炒
    - chǎo
    - stir-fry
  - 蛋
    - dàn
    - egg
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 炒 (chǎo, "stir-fry") and 蛋 (dàn, "egg") come together to describe a dish where eggs are stir-fried, resulting in scrambled eggs.
  - **interpretation:** 炒 (chǎo, "stir-fry") is a common cooking method in Chinese cuisine, which involves stirring and cooking ingredients in hot oil, while 蛋 (dàn, "egg") refers to the primary ingredient of this dish. The word 炒蛋 (chǎodàn, "scrambled eggs") makes perfect sense because it describes how the eggs are prepared using this technique.
- **examples:**
  - 我早餐喜欢(歡)吃炒蛋。
    - Wǒ zǎocān xǐhuān chī chǎodàn.
    - I like scrambled eggs for breakfast.
  - 妈妈(媽媽)炒蛋时(時)喜欢(歡)放点葱(點蔥)，增加风(風)味。
    - Māmā chǎodàn shí xǐhuān fàng diǎn cōng, zēngjiā fēngwèi.
    - When making scrambled eggs, mom likes to add some scallions to enhance the flavor.
  - 炒蛋配上吐司味道绝(絕)佳。
    - Chǎodàn pèi shàng tǔsī wèidào jué jiā.
    - Scrambled eggs go perfectly with toast.

  - **interpretation:** The character 炒(chǎo) combines 火 (huǒ, "fire") to indicate cooking with heat, specifically stir-frying, with 少 (shǎo, "little") as a phonetic component. This suggests cooking with a small amount of heat or quickly.

- **pinyin:** dàn

  - 延
    - yán

- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

This reflects the later coinage of the character, indicating it wasn't part of very early Chinese script.

- **components:**
  - 尢
    - wān
    - man with bent legs
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** variant form
  - **description:** 兀 is a variant form of 尢 (wān, "man with bent legs")
  - **interpretation:** 兀 originally represented a man with bent legs, indicative of jumping or collapsing. It connects to this meaning by representing something that stands out or is elevated, as in 'towering.' It reflects the semantic notion of standing out both physically and metaphorically.
- **examples:**
  - 那座山峰兀立，宛如巨人昂首。
    - Nà zuò shānfēng wùlì, wǎnrú jùrén ángshǒu.
    - That mountain peak towers, like a giant with its head held high.
  - 古树(樹)枝干(幹)兀然裸露，诉说着岁(訴說著歲)月沧(滄)桑。
    - Gǔ shù zhīgàn wùrán luǒlù, sùshuōzhe suìyuè cāngsāng.
    - The ancient tree's branches stand bald and exposed, telling tales of time’s vicissitudes.
### 炒蛋 → 蛋 → 虫(蟲) → 虺 → 兀 → 尢
- **definition:** man with bent legs
- **pinyin:** wān
- **components:**
  - 尤
    - yóu
    - especially
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** variant form of 尢
  - **description:**
    - Pictogram: Originally depicts a hand with a wart ->
    - Variant form of 尤: retaining connotations of deformity
  - **interpretation:** 尢 is identified as a variant of 尤 and represents the notion of a man with bent legs, closely associating with deformity or deviation in form. The character still conveys variations perceived as abnormalities, similar to the original meaning of a hand with a wart.
- **examples:**
  - 他行走时显(時顯)露出尢样(樣)步态(態)，引得众(眾)人侧(側)目。
    - tā xíngzǒu shí xiǎnlù chū wān yàng bùtài, yǐn dé zhòngrén cèmù.
    - When he walked, his bent-legged gait was evident, attracting sidelong glances from the crowd.
  - 古籍记载(記載)尢人的形态(態)。
    - gǔjí jìzǎi wān rén de xíngtài.
    - Ancient texts record the appearance of a man with bent legs.
