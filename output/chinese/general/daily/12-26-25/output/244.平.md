## surname Ping
### Píng
---
- **definition:** surname Ping
- **pinyin:** Píng
- **components:**
  - 釆
    - biàn
    - divide
  - 二
    - èr
    - two equal lines
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 釆(釆) (biàn, 'divide') + semantic 二(二) (èr, 'two equal lines') =
    - flat like equal lines ->
    - flat; level
  - **interpretation:** The character 平(píng, 'flat; level') combines the phonetic element 釆(釆) (biàn, 'divide') and the semantic element 二(二) (èr, 'two equal lines'), symbolizing the idea of balance or equality, akin to a flat, even surface.
- **examples:**
  - 这张桌子很平(這張桌子很平)。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi hěn píng.
    - This table is flat.
  - 河面平静(河面平靜)，能倒映蓝天(能倒映藍天)。
    - Hé miàn píngjìng, néng dǎoyìng lán tiān.
    - The surface of the river is smooth and reflects the blue sky.
### 平(平) → 釆(釆)
- **definition:** divide
- **pinyin:** biàn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts animal paw with spread toes ->
    - linked to 蹯(蹯) (fán, 'paw')
  - **interpretation:** The character 釆(釆) (biàn, 'divide') visually represents an animal paw with spread toes, suggesting the concept of division as it relates to the separation of toes or digging actions. It is linked visually to the character for 'paw'.
- **examples:**
  - 他釆合理办法(他釆合理辦法)，将问题釆为主次两部分(將問題釆爲主次兩部分)。
    - Tā biàn hélǐ bànfǎ, jiāng wèntí biàn wèi zhǔ cì liǎng bùfèn.
    - He divided the problem into primary and secondary parts using a rational method.
  - 农民釆种方式不同(農民釆種方式不同)，土地釆为灌溉区与休耕区(土地釆爲灌溉區與休耕區)。
    - Nóngmín biàn zhǒng fāngshì bùtóng, tǔdì biàn wèi guàngài qū yǔ xiūgēng qū.
    - Farmers, adopting different planting methods, divided the land into irrigation and fallow zones.
  - 学者釆理论阐释(學者釆理論闡釋)，将文化釆为现代与传统两部分(將文化釆爲現代與傳統兩部分)。
    - Xuézhě biàn lǐlùn chǎnshì, jiāng wénhuà biàn wèi xiàndài yǔ chuántǒng liǎng bùfèn.
    - The scholar, through his theoretical exposition, divided culture into modern and traditional parts.
### 平(平) → 二(二)
- **definition:** two equal lines
- **pinyin:** èr
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 一(一) (yī, "one") duplicated =
    - two parallel lines ->
    - 二(二) (èr, "two")
  - **interpretation:** The character 二(二) (èr, "two") uses two parallel strokes to clearly depict the concept of two, building on the single stroke of 一(一) (yī, "one"). This visual representation directly conveys the number two.
- **examples:**
  - 我有二只鸟(我有二隻鳥)。
    - Wǒ yǒu èr zhī niǎo.
    - I have two birds.
  - 今天是二月(今天是二月)，气候开始转暖(氣候開始轉暖)。
    - Jīntiān shì èr yuè, qìhòu kāishǐ zhuǎn nuǎn.
    - Today is February, and the weather is starting to warm up.
