### decluttering; minimalism
---
- **pinyin:** decluttering; minimalism
- **components:**
  - 断(斷)
    - duàn
    - to cut off; to break; to end; to stop
  - 舍(捨)
    - shě
    - to discard; to abandon; to give up; to let go
  - 离(離)
    - lí
    - to separate; to leave; to part from; distance
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 断(斷) (duàn, 'to cut off') + 捨(捨) (shě, 'to discard') + 离(離) (lí, 'to separate') — cut off, discard, separate
  - **interpretation:** This term refers to the philosophy or practice of letting go of unnecessary possessions. The combination of the three morphemes emphasizes the actions of cutting off, discarding, and separating oneself from material excess.
- **examples:**
  - 我在进行断舍离，整理我的房间。
    - Wǒ zài jìnxíng duànshělí, zhěnglǐ wǒ de fángjiān.
    - I am practicing decluttering, organizing my room.
  - 断舍离的理念帮助我减轻了生活负担。
    - Duànshělí de lǐniàn bāngzhù wǒ jiǎnqīngle shēnghuó fùdān.
    - The concept of decluttering has helped me lighten my life's burdens.

- **pinyin:** lí
- **components:**
  - 林
    - lín

  - 𠦒

    - net

  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Combines 林 (lín, 'forest') and 𠦒 (a character associated with 'net') to convey the idea of setting up a net in the forest for hunting.
  - **interpretation:** The imagery of a forest combined with a net suggests separation from the natural environment, as one would abduct game animals. This links to the idea of leaving or parting from something.
