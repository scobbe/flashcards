## decluttering; minimalism
### duànshělí
---
- **traditional:** 斷捨離
- **definition:** decluttering; minimalism
- **pinyin:** duànshělí
- **components:**
  - 断(斷)
    - duàn
    - break; sever; decide
  - 舍(捨)
    - shě
    - abandon; give up; renounce
  - 离(離)
    - lí
    - separate; leave; part from
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 斷 (duàn, "break"), 捨 (shě, "abandon"), 離 (lí, "separate") comes from 斷 which means to "break away," 捨 which involves "abandoning or letting go," and 離 which signifies "separation." Together, they form a concept of decluttering by breaking attachment, letting go, and separating oneself from unnecessary material possessions.
  - **interpretation:** 斷(断)捨(舍)離(离) (duànshělí) encourages living a simplified life by minimizing material possessions and eliminating unnecessary items, a philosophy aligning with the principles of minimalism. Originally rooted in the Japanese philosophy of 'Danshari,' it has been embraced more broadly in Chinese-speaking contexts.
  - **simplification:** 断舍离 to 斷捨離
- **examples:**
  - 我( )决(決)定( )开(開)始( )断(斷)舍(捨)离(離)。
    - Wǒ juédìng kāishǐ duànshělí.
    - I decide to start decluttering.
  - 家( )里(裏)太( )拥(擁)挤(擠)时(時)，断(斷)舍(捨)离(離)让(讓)生( )活( )更( )简(簡)约(約)。
    - Jiālǐ tài yōngjǐ shí, duànshělí ràng shēnghuó gèng jiǎnyuē.
    - When home is too crowded, minimalism makes life simpler.
