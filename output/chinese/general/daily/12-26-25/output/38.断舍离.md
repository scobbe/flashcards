## decluttering; minimalism
### duànshělí
---
- **traditional:** 斷捨離
- **definition:** decluttering; minimalism
- **pinyin:** duànshělí
- **components:**
  - 断(斷)
    - duàn
    - break; sever; decide
  - 舍(捨)
    - shě
    - abandon; give up; renounce
  - 离(離)
    - lí
    - separate; leave; part from
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 斷 (duàn, "break"), 捨 (shě, "abandon"), 離 (lí, "separate") comes from 斷 which means to "break away," 捨 which involves "abandoning or letting go," and 離 which signifies "separation." Together, they form a concept of decluttering by breaking attachment, letting go, and separating oneself from unnecessary material possessions.
  - **interpretation:** 斷捨離(断舍离) (duànshělí) encourages living a simplified life by minimizing material possessions and eliminating unnecessary items, a philosophy aligning with the principles of minimalism. Originally rooted in the Japanese philosophy of 'Danshari,' it has been embraced more broadly in Chinese-speaking contexts.
  - **simplification:** 断舍离 to 斷捨離
- **examples:**
  - 我决(決)定开(開)始断舍离(斷捨離)。
    - Wǒ juédìng kāishǐ duànshělí.
    - I decide to start decluttering.
  - 家里(裏)太拥挤时(擁擠時)，断舍离让(斷捨離讓)生活更简约(簡約)。
    - Jiālǐ tài yōngjǐ shí, duànshělí ràng shēnghuó gèng jiǎnyuē.
    - When home is too crowded, minimalism makes life simpler.

### 断(斷)舍(捨)离(離) → 断(斷) → 𢇍 → 絕

- **pinyin:** jué
- **components:**
  - 糹
    - sī
    - silk
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
  - 巴
    - bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Originally: 刀 (dāo, 'knife') + 幺 (yāo, 'silk') =
    - to cut silk threads ->
    - Now: semantic 糹 (sī, 'silk') + semantic 刀 (dāo, 'knife') + phonetic 巴 (bā) =

  - **simplification:** 糹 (sī, 'silk') was simplified as 纟(丝) in simplified characters, retaining its essence while reducing stroke count.
- **examples:**
  - 他决(決)然断绝(斷絕)了旧(舊)友的联系(聯繫)。
    - Tā juérán duànjué le jiùyǒu de liánxì.

    - She wants to abandon the past and sever entanglements of the mundane world.
  - 我必须断绝(須斷絕)所有不良影响(響)。

### 断(斷)舍(捨)离(離) → 舍
- **definition:** abandon; give up; renounce
- **pinyin:** shě
- **components:**
  - 余
    - yú
    - "I"
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - "mouth"
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** 余 (yú, "I") as phonetic + semantic 口 (kǒu, "mouth") added for distinction
  - **interpretation:** The character 舍 (shě, "abandon") combines the phonetic element 余 (yú, "I") with the semantic element 口 (kǒu, "mouth"). The original pictogram for 'abandon' was 余, and the addition of 口 distinguished it from other uses of 余. This construction suggests an action related to speaking or deciding, reflecting the act of giving up or renouncing something verbally or through action.
- **examples:**
  - 她舍弃(捨棄)了舒适(適)的生活。
    - Tā shě qì le shūshì de shēnghuó.
    - She gave up a comfortable life.
  - 他舍(捨)不得离开(離開)家乡(鄉)。
    - Tā shě bù dé líkāi jiāxiāng.
    - He is reluctant to leave his hometown.
  - 他舍(捨)去旧(舊)有的观(觀)念，迎接新的挑战(戰)。
    - Tā shě qù jiùyǒu de guānniàn, yíngjiē xīn de tiǎozhàn.
    - He renounces old beliefs and embraces new challenges.

### 断(斷)舍(捨)离(離) → 离(離)

- **examples:**
