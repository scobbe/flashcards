## chant; eulogize
### yǒng
---
- **definition:** chant; eulogize
- **pinyin:** yǒng
- **components:**
  - 言
    - yán
    - speech
  - 永
    - yǒng
    - eternal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 言(言) (yán, "speech") + 永(永) (yǒng, "eternal") =
    - speech that lasts ->
    - chant; eulogize
  - **interpretation:** The character 咏(詠) (yǒng, "chant; eulogize") combines 言(言) (yán, "speech"), indicating the act of speaking or singing, with 永(永) (yǒng, "eternal"), providing the sound element and implying that the speech or song has lasting significance.
- **examples:**
  - 他咏出壮丽的诗篇(他詠出壯麗的詩篇)。
    - Tā yǒng chū zhuànglì de shīpiān.
    - He chants a magnificent poem.
  - 诗人咏怀古意(詩人詠懷古意)，在月光下沉思(在月光下沉思)。
    - Shīrén yǒng huái gǔ yì, zài yuèguāng xià chén sī.
    - The poet chants ancient sentiments, pondering under the moonlight.
  - 老者咏经传之义(老者詠經傳之義)，颂扬智慧(頌揚智慧)。
    - Lǎo zhě yǒng jīng chuán zhī yì, sòng yáng zhì huì.
    - The elder chants the meaning of the classics and eulogizes wisdom.
### 咏(詠) → 言(言)
- **definition:** speech
- **pinyin:** yán
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 舌(舌) (shé, "tongue") + horizontal stroke =
    - movement of tongue ->
    - speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 言(yán, "speech") originates from the depiction of a tongue 舌(舌) with an added stroke to represent its movement, signifying the action of speaking. This visual representation highlights the tongue's role in speech, which is central to communication.
- **examples:**
  - 言简意赅可以表达深刻思想(言簡意賅可以表達深刻思想)。
    - yán jiǎn yì gāi kěyǐ biǎo dá shēn kè sī xiǎng。
    - Concise speech can express profound thoughts.
  - 他的言论总能激发思考(他的言論總能激發思考)。
    - tā de yán lùn zǒng néng jī fā sī kǎo。
    - His speech always inspires reflection.
  - 老师常说(老師常說)，言出必行(言出必行)。
    - lǎo shī cháng shuō, yán chū bì xíng。
    - The teacher often says that one should act on one's words.
### 咏(詠) → 永(永)
- **definition:** eternal
- **pinyin:** yǒng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted a long river with tributaries or a person swimming in the water ->
    - eternal
  - **interpretation:** The character 永(永) (yǒng, 'eternal') originally represented the flowing nature or continuous movement of a river, symbolizing the idea of eternity through the unending flow of water.
- **examples:**
  - 爱情永不凋零(愛情永不凋零)。
    - Àiqíng yǒng bù diāoliú.
    - Love never fades.
  - 梦想永远指引前行(夢想永遠指引前行)，希望照亮人生(希望照亮人生)。
    - Mèngxiǎng yǒngyuǎn zhǐyǐn qiánxíng, xīwàng zhào liàng rénshēng.
    - Dreams always guide our journey, and hope illuminates life.
