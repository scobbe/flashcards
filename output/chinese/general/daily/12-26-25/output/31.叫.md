### call; shout
---
- **pinyin:** call; shout
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 丩
    - jiū
    - phonetic component
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Semantic 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') + phonetic 丩 (jiū).
  - **interpretation:** The character visually combines the idea of a mouth with a sound component, intuitively representing the act of calling or shouting.
- **examples:**
  - 我叫你(我叫你)，快来！
    - Wǒ jiào nǐ, kuài lái!
    - I am calling you, come quickly!
  - 她在叫(她在叫)，不要打扰！
    - Tā zài jiào, bùyào dǎrǎo!
    - She is shouting, don't disturb her!
  - 他叫我(他叫我)去买东西。
    - Tā jiào wǒ qù mǎi dōngxī.
    - He called me to buy something.
### 叫(叫) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character is a pictograph depicting the shape of a mouth, which intuitively represents the concept of 'mouth' itself.
- **examples:**
  - 我吃东西(我吃東西)，需要用口(需要用口)。
    - Wǒ chī dōngxi, xūyào yòng kǒu.
    - I eat food, and I need to use my mouth.
  - 口腔(口腔)健康很重要(健康很重要)。
    - Kǒuqiāng jiànkāng hěn zhòngyào.
    - Oral health is very important.
### 叫(叫) → 丩(丩)
- **definition:** phonetic component
- **pinyin:** jiū
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts two entangled ropes, conveying the idea of intertwining or entangling.
  - **interpretation:** The image of intertwined ropes suggests concepts of connection and complexity, fitting well with its meanings.
- **examples:**
  - 旧字丩(舊字丩)，表示纠缠(糾纏)。
    - jiùzì jiū, biǎoshì jiūchán.
  - 在古文中常见丩(常見丩)，用来形容交织(交織)。
    - zài gǔwén zhōng chángjiàn jiū, yòng lái xíngróng jiāozhī.
