## takeout
### wàimài
---
- **definition:** takeout
- **pinyin:** wàimài
- **components:**
  - 外
    - wài
    - outside; exterior; foreign
  - 卖(賣)
    - mài
    - sell; betray; show off
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 外(外) (wài, "outside") + 卖(賣) (mài, "sell")
  - **interpretation:** The term 外卖(外賣) (wàimài) represents the concept of selling food that is meant to be eaten outside the premises of a restaurant or shop. It captures the modern convenience of ordering meals to be enjoyed elsewhere, a practice that has grown increasingly common in urban settings.
  - **simplification:** 卖 simplified to 卖
- **examples:**
  - 我今天订了外卖(我今天訂了外賣)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān dìng le wàimài.
    - I ordered takeout today.
  - 工作忙碌时(工作忙碌時)，他喜欢点外卖(他喜歡點外賣)。
    - Gōngzuò mánglù shí, tā xǐhuān diǎn wàimài.
    - When work is busy, he likes to order takeout.

### 外卖(外賣) → 卖(賣)
- **definition:** sell; betray; show off
- **pinyin:** mài
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") + Phonetic: 卖(賣) (mài, "sell") =
    - selling valuables ->
    - sell
  - **interpretation:** 賣(賣) originally combines 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry"), a symbol of money and valuables in ancient China, with a phonetic component indicating the sound. Selling involved valuables, making the connection logical.
  - **simplification:** 賣(賣) → 卖(賣): The shell radical 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") was replaced by 单(單) (dān, "single") in a simplified phonetic and semantic reduction.
- **examples:**
  - 我去市场卖水果(我去市場賣水果)。
    - Wǒ qù shìchǎng mài shuǐguǒ。
    - I go to the market to sell fruit.
  - 他卖友求荣(他賣友求榮)。
    - Tā mài yǒu qiú róng。
    - He betrays his friends to seek glory.
  - 他总是喜欢卖弄(他總是喜歡賣弄)，自己的才华(自己的才華)。
    - Tā zǒngshì xǐhuān mài nòng, zìjǐ de cáihuá。
    - He always likes to show off his talent.
### 外卖(外賣) → 卖(賣) → 貝(貝)
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cowry shell, which was used as currency in ancient China.
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") visually represents a cowry shell, a valuable item used in trade and as currency in ancient Chinese societies.
  - **simplification:** 贝(貝) is the simplified form, derived from a cursive script version of 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry"), maintaining the visual representation while making the character faster to write.
- **examples:**
  - 古代人用貝(古代人用貝)作为货币(作為貨幣)，也用来装饰(也用來裝飾)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì, yě yòng lái zhuāngshì.
    - Ancient people used cowrie shells as currency and also for decoration.
  - 一些古文献记载了貝(一些古文獻記載了貝)的神秘价值(的神秘價值)，激发了人们对古代文化的兴趣(激發了人們對古代文化的興趣)。
    - Yīxiē gǔ wénxiàn jìzǎi le bèi de shénmì jiàzhí, jīfā le rénmen duì gǔdài wénhuà de xìngqù.
    - Some ancient texts recorded the mysterious value of cowrie shells, sparking people’s interest in ancient culture.
  - 海边曾有传说(海邊曾有傳說)，珊瑚和貝点缀着沙滩的美丽(珊瑚和貝點綴著沙灘的美麗)。
    - Hǎibiān céng yǒu chuánshuō, shānhú hé bèi diǎnzuì zhe shātān de měilì.
    - There was a legend by the seaside that corals and cowrie shells adorned the beauty of the beach.
