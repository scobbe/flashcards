### takeout
---
- **pinyin:** takeout
- **components:**
  - 外
    - wài
    - outside; foreign; external; external
  - 卖(賣)
    - mài
    - sell; trade; market; peddle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 外(外) (wài, 'outside') + 卖(賣) (mài, 'sell') — sell outside
  - **interpretation:** The term refers to food sold to be consumed outside of the restaurant or establishment. The combination indicates food that is prepared for sale to customers who take it away to eat elsewhere.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天点外卖(我今天點外賣)，因为没时间去餐厅(因為沒時間去餐廳)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān diǎn wàimài, yīnwèi méi shíjiān qù cāntīng.
    - I ordered takeout today because I don't have time to go to the restaurant.
  - 外卖越来越受欢迎(外賣越來越受歡迎)，许多人喜欢在家吃饭(許多人喜歡在家吃飯)。
    - Wàimài yuè lái yuè shòu huānyíng, xǔduō rén xǐhuān zài jiā chīfàn.
    - Takeout is becoming increasingly popular, as many people enjoy dining at home.

### 外卖(外賣) → 卖(賣)
- **definition:** sell; trade; market; peddle
- **pinyin:** mài
- **components:**
  - 货(貨)
    - huò
    - goods
  - 麦(麥)
    - mài
    - wheat
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Composed of the semantic element 货 (huò, 'goods') and the phonetic element 麦 (mài, 'wheat'), indicating the act of selling goods.
  - **interpretation:** The character suggests the selling of goods, as in trading wheat or other commodities. It intuitively connects the concept of selling with tangible items.
  - **simplification:** The character was simplified by replacing its traditional component 買 with the simpler form with fewer strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 我想卖书(我想賣書)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng mài shū.
    - I want to sell books.
  - 市场上有人卖衣服(市場上有人賣衣服)。
    - Shìchǎng shàng yǒu rén mài yīfú.
    - There are people selling clothes at the market.
