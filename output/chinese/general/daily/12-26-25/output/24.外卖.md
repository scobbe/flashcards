### wàimài
---
- **traditional:** 外賣
- **definition:** takeout
- **pinyin:** wàimài
- **components:**
  - 外
    - wài
    - outside; exterior; foreign
  - 卖(賣)
    - mài
    - sell; betray; show off
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 外 (wài, "outside") + 卖(賣) (mài, "sell")
  - **interpretation:** The term 外( )卖(賣) (wàimài) represents the concept of selling food that is meant to be eaten outside the premises of a restaurant or shop. It captures the modern convenience of ordering meals to be enjoyed elsewhere, a practice that has grown increasingly common in urban settings.
  - **simplification:** 卖 simplified to 卖
- **examples:**
  - 我( )今( )天( )订(訂)了( )外( )卖(賣)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān dìng le wàimài.
    - I ordered takeout today.
  - 工( )作( )忙( )碌( )时(時)，他( )喜( )欢(歡)点(點)外( )卖(賣)。
    - Gōngzuò mánglù shí, tā xǐhuān diǎn wàimài.
    - When work is busy, he likes to order takeout.

### 外( )卖(賣) → 卖(賣)
