## takeout
### wàimài
---
- **traditional:** 外賣
- **definition:** takeout
- **pinyin:** wàimài
- **components:**
  - 外
    - wài
    - outside; exterior; foreign
  - 卖(賣)
    - mài
    - sell; betray; show off
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 外 (wài, "outside") + 卖(賣) (mài, "sell")
  - **interpretation:** The term 外卖(賣) (wàimài) represents the concept of selling food that is meant to be eaten outside the premises of a restaurant or shop. It captures the modern convenience of ordering meals to be enjoyed elsewhere, a practice that has grown increasingly common in urban settings.
  - **simplification:** 卖 simplified to 卖
- **examples:**
  - 我今天订(訂)了外卖(賣)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān dìng le wàimài.
    - I ordered takeout today.
  - 工作忙碌时(時)，他喜欢点(歡點)外卖(賣)。
    - Gōngzuò mánglù shí, tā xǐhuān diǎn wàimài.
    - When work is busy, he likes to order takeout.
### 外( )卖(賣) → 外
- **definition:** outside; exterior; foreign
- **pinyin:** wài
- **components:**
  - 卜
    - bǔ
    - divination
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 卜 (bǔ, "divination") + Phonetic: 月 (yuè, "moon") ->
    - originally related to phases of moon for divination ->
    - exterior
  - **interpretation:** The character 外(wài, "outside") combines 卜 (bǔ, "divination") as a semantic element and 月 (yuè, "moon") as a phonetic element. This suggests divination practices related to moon phases, reflecting an understanding of measuring what is 'outside' or observable of the moon, thus extending to the concept of 'outside'.
- **examples:**
  - 孩子们(們)在外玩耍。
    - Háizimen zài wài wánshuǎ.
    - The children are playing outside.
  - 他离开(離開)喧嚣(囂)，向外探索。
    - Tā líkāi xuānxiāo, xiàng wài tànsuǒ.
    - He leaves the hustle and bustle to explore the outside.
  - 外國的文化充滿魅力。
    - Wàiguó de wénhuà chōngmǎn mèilì.
    - Foreign cultures are full of charm.
### 外( )卖(賣) → 外 → 卜
- **definition:** divination
- **pinyin:** bǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a divination crack in a tortoise shell or bone
  - **interpretation:** The character 卜(bǔ, "divination") represents the practice of divination used in ancient China, often involving the interpretation of cracks in tortoise shells or bones.
- **examples:**
  - 占卜是一种(種)神秘的艺术(藝術)。
    - Zhānbǔ shì yī zhǒng shénmì de yìshù.
    - Divination is a mysterious art.
  - 他通过(過)卜算未来(來)，探寻隐(尋隱)秘的命运(運)。
    - Tā tōngguò bǔsuàn wèilái, tànxún yǐnmì de mìngyùn.
    - He uses divination to predict the future, seeking out his hidden destiny.
  - 古代人常以卜问(問)吉凶，以求平安和顺(順)。
    - Gǔdài rén cháng yǐ bǔ wèn jíxiōng, yǐ qiú píng'ān hé shùn.
    - Ancient people often practiced divination to determine fortune, in order to pursue peace and order.
### 外( )卖(賣) → 外 → 月
- **definition:** moon
- **pinyin:** yuè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a crescent moon
  - **interpretation:** The character 月(yuè, "moon") is a pictographic representation of a crescent moon, straightforwardly capturing the curved shape often associated with the moon's appearance. Though interchangeable with 夕 (xī, "evening") in the past, it distinctly represents the moon in this usage.
- **examples:**
  - 夜空挂着(掛著)明月。
    - Yè kōng guà zhe míng yuè.
    - The bright moon hangs in the night sky.
  - 人们赏(們賞)月，寄托(託)相思。
    - Rén men shǎng yuè, jì tuō xiāng sī.
    - People enjoy the moon, expressing their longing.
### 外( )卖(賣) → 卖(賣)
- **traditional:** 賣
- **definition:** sell; betray; show off
- **pinyin:** mài
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 貝 (bèi, "cowry") + Phonetic: 卖(賣) (mài, "sell") =
    - selling valuables ->
    - sell
  - **interpretation:** 賣 originally combines 貝 (bèi, "cowry"), a symbol of money and valuables in ancient China, with a phonetic component indicating the sound. Selling involved valuables, making the connection logical.
  - **simplification:** 賣 → 卖(賣): The shell radical 貝 (bèi, "cowry") was replaced by 单(單) (dān, "single") in a simplified phonetic and semantic reduction.
- **examples:**
  - 我去市场卖(場賣)水果。
    - Wǒ qù shìchǎng mài shuǐguǒ。
    - I go to the market to sell fruit.
  - 他卖(賣)友求荣(榮)。
    - Tā mài yǒu qiú róng。
    - He betrays his friends to seek glory.
  - 他总(總)是喜欢卖(歡賣)弄，自己的才华(華)。
    - Tā zǒngshì xǐhuān mài nòng, zìjǐ de cáihuá。
    - He always likes to show off his talent.
### 外( )卖(賣) → 卖(賣) → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为货币(為貨幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
