## Taiwanese beverage chain Xing Fu Tang
### Xìngfú Táng
---
- **definition:** Taiwanese beverage chain Xing Fu Tang
- **pinyin:** Xìngfú Táng
- **components:**
  - 幸福
    - xìngfú
    - happiness; blessedness; well-being
  - 堂
    - táng
    - hall; main room; shop (in store names); establishment
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 幸福 + 堂 = “hall/shop of happiness” → auspicious-sounding shop/brand name.
  - **interpretation:** Evokes good fortune and well-being while using 堂, a traditional, respectable suffix for shops, to suggest heritage and quality. Used as a brand name for a Taiwanese beverage chain.
- **examples:**
  - 我们去幸福堂买一杯黑糖珍珠鲜奶(我們去幸福堂買一杯黑糖珍珠鮮奶)。
    - Wǒmen qù Xìngfú Táng mǎi yì bēi hēitáng zhēnzhū xiānnǎi.
    - Let's go to Xing Fu Tang to buy a cup of brown sugar boba fresh milk.
  - 幸福堂在很多城市都有分店(幸福堂在很多城市都有分店)。
    - Xìngfú Táng zài hěn duō chéngshì dōu yǒu fēndiàn.
    - Xing Fu Tang has branches in many cities.
  - 这个名字“幸福堂”听起来很吉利(這個名字「幸福堂」聽起來很吉利)。
    - Zhège míngzì “Xìngfú Táng” tīng qǐlái hěn jílì.
    - The name “Xing Fu Tang” sounds auspicious.
### 幸福堂 → 幸福
- **definition:** happiness; blessedness; well-being
- **pinyin:** xìngfú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 幸 (good fortune) + 福 (blessing) = happiness; well-being.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the idea of being fortunate with received blessings, conveying a state of lasting well-being and contentment.
- **examples:**
  - 她追求简单的生活(她追求簡單的生活)，就能感到幸福(就能感到幸福)。
    - Tā zhuīqiú jiǎndān de shēnghuó, jiù néng gǎndào xìngfú.
    - She pursues a simple life and can feel happiness.
  - 家人的支持(家人的支持)，让他觉得很幸福(讓他覺得很幸福)。
    - Jiārén de zhīchí, ràng tā juéde hěn xìngfú.
    - Support from family makes him feel very happy.
  - 祝你们永远幸福美满(祝你們永遠幸福美滿)。
    - Zhù nǐmen yǒngyuǎn xìngfú měimǎn.
    - Wishing you everlasting happiness and a perfect union.
### 幸福堂 → 堂
- **definition:** hall; main room; shop (in store names); establishment
- **pinyin:** táng
- **components:**
  - 尚
    - shàng
    - esteem; still; yet
  - 向
    - xiàng
    - toward; direction
  - 土
    - tǔ
    - earth; soil
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic 尚 + semantic 土; component 向 depicts a house
  - **interpretation:** Meaning is anchored by 土, suggesting earth, ground, and constructed space, while 尚 supplies the sound. The presence of 向 as a house-like element reinforces the idea of an interior building space used as a hall.
- **examples:**
  - 我们在礼堂集合(我們在禮堂集合)。
    - Wǒmen zài lǐtáng jíhé.
    - We assemble in the auditorium.
  - 中午去食堂吃饭(中午去食堂吃飯)，菜很实惠(菜很實惠)。
    - Zhōngwǔ qù shítáng chīfàn, cài hěn shíhuì.
    - At noon we go to the canteen to eat; the dishes are affordable.
  - 校长在大堂发表讲话(校長在大堂發表講話)。
    - Xiàozhǎng zài dàtáng fābiǎo jiǎnghuà.
    - The principal gave a speech in the main hall.
### 幸福堂 → 堂 → 尚
- **definition:** esteem; still; yet
- **pinyin:** shàng
- **components:**
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight
  - 向
    - xiàng
    - toward; direction
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 八 + phonetic: 向
  - **interpretation:** Built with 八 as the semantic element and 向 as the phonetic, aligning with their Old Chinese readings. 八 gives a general semantic tag while 向 contributes the shàng sound; the overall layout is similar to 冋.
- **examples:**
  - 事情尚未结束(事情尚未結束)。
    - Shìqíng shàng wèi jiéshù.
    - The matter is not over yet.
  - 他尚年轻(他尚年輕)，需要指导(需要指導)。
    - Tā shàng niánqīng, xūyào zhǐdǎo.
    - He is still young and needs guidance.
  - 他崇尚简约的生活方式(他崇尚簡約的生活方式)。
    - Tā chóngshàng jiǎnyuē de shēnghuó fāngshì.
    - He esteems a simple lifestyle.
### 幸福堂 → 堂 → 尚 → 八
- **definition:** eight
- **pinyin:** bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: two bent lines indicating “to divide”; later borrowed to mean “eight” via homophony (original sense now in 分, 別).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two strokes diverging to suggest splitting or separation. Later became a rebus for the number eight because it shared the same sound, while the original sense lived on in 分 and 別. Not the “two hands” component found in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我有八本书(我有八本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu bā běn shū.
    - I have eight books.
  - 她八岁了(她八歲了)。
    - Tā bā suì le.
    - She is eight years old.
  - 我买了八个苹果(我買了八個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le bā gè píngguǒ.
    - I bought eight apples.
### 幸福堂 → 堂 → 尚 → 向
- **definition:** toward; direction
- **pinyin:** xiàng
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 宀 ‘house’ + 口 ‘mouth’; echoes in a place. Original form of 響; later borrowed to write 𠨍 (嚮). Similar to 冋.
  - **interpretation:** A mouth sounding under a roof evokes reverberation indoors, matching the sense later written as 響 ‘echo, sound’. By rebus borrowing the graph came to write 嚮 ‘to face, toward; direction’, which is the sense carried by modern 向.
- **examples:**
  - 我们向前走吧(我們向前走吧)。
    - Wǒmen xiàng qián zǒu ba.
    - Let’s walk forward.
  - 她向着窗户微笑(她向著窗戶微笑)。
    - Tā xiàngzhe chuānghu wēixiào.
    - She smiles toward the window.
  - 他向医院的方向跑去(他向醫院的方向跑去)。
    - Tā xiàng yīyuàn de fāngxiàng pǎo qù.
    - He ran in the direction of the hospital.
### 幸福堂 → 堂 → 尚 → 向 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 幸福堂 → 堂 → 土
- **definition:** earth; soil
- **pinyin:** tǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a lump of clay on the ground or on a potter’s wheel; the central dot was later stylized into a short horizontal stroke.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a clod of earth resting on the ground, later streamlined into a vertical line with top and bottom bars. The upper bar developed from an earlier dot to distinguish it from similar graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 这里的土很干(這裡的土很乾)。
    - Zhèlǐ de tǔ hěn gān.
    - The soil here is very dry.
  - 他把一把土撒在花盆里(他把一把土撒在花盆裡)。
    - Tā bǎ yì bǎ tǔ sǎ zài huāpén lǐ.
    - He sprinkled a handful of soil into the flowerpot.
  - 建房前要先检测土质(建房前要先檢測土質)。
    - Jiàn fáng qián yào xiān jiǎncè tǔzhì.
    - Before building a house, the soil quality must be tested.
