### eat in
---
- **pinyin:** eat in
- **components:**
  - 堂
    - táng
    - hall; dining hall
  - 食
    - shí
    - eat; food
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 堂(堂) (táng, 'hall; dining hall') + 食(食) (shí, 'eat; food') — hall eat
  - **interpretation:** This term refers to eating in a dining hall or restaurant setting. The combination of a hall and food directly indicates the concept of dining on-site.
- **examples:**
  - 我们今天选择堂食(我們今天選擇堂食)。
    - Wǒmen jīntiān xuǎnzé tángshí.
    - We chose to eat in today.
  - 堂食比外卖更舒服(堂食比外賣更舒服)。
    - Tángshí bǐ wàimài gèng shūfú.
    - Eating in is more comfortable than takeout.

### 堂食(堂食) → 食(食)
- **definition:** eat; food
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a mouth (亼) over a bowl of rice on a stand.
  - **interpretation:** The imagery of a mouth above a bowl of rice represents the action of eating, directly linking the symbol to its meaning. This visual connection intuitively conveys the concept of food consumption.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢食物(我喜歡食物)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān shíwù.
    - I like food.
  - 她每天吃三餐(她每天吃三餐)。
    - Tā měitiān chī sān cān.
    - She eats three meals a day.
  - 我们去吃晚饭(我們去吃晚飯)。
    - Wǒmen qù chī wǎnfàn.
    - We are going to have dinner.
