## true; real
### zhēn
---
- **definition:** true; real
- **pinyin:** zhēn
- **components:**
  - 𠂈
    - (no pinyin)
    - knife
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 丁
    - dīng
    - nail
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
  - 匕
    - bǐ
    - spoon
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 丌
    - jī
    - table
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 𠂈 (a knife) + Semantic: 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") ->
    - denoting rarity/preciousness ->
    - 丁 (a nail) added as phonetic at bottom as black dot ->
    - corruption to 十, 匕, 目, and 丌 ->
    - final form 真
  - **interpretation:** The character 真(真) (zhēn, "true") emerged as a combination of 𠂈, a phonetic element related to knives, and semantic 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") to indicate value or rarity. The complex evolution involved the addition and transformation of 丁 and other elements, eventually settling in the form 真 commonly used today to mean "true" or "real."
- **examples:**
  - 这件衣服真漂亮(這件衣服真漂亮)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfú zhēn piàoliang.
    - This piece of clothing is really beautiful.
  - 他说的话真可信(他說的話真可信)，她的笑容真温暖(她的笑容真溫暖)。
    - Tā shuō de huà zhēn kěxìn, tā de xiàoróng zhēn wēnnuǎn.
    - What he said is truly credible, and her smile is really warm.

### 真(真) → 貝(貝)
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cowry shell, which was used as currency in ancient China.
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") visually represents a cowry shell, a valuable item used in trade and as currency in ancient Chinese societies.
  - **simplification:** 贝(貝) is the simplified form, derived from a cursive script version of 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry"), maintaining the visual representation while making the character faster to write.
- **examples:**
  - 古代人用貝(古代人用貝)作为货币(作為貨幣)，也用来装饰(也用來裝飾)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì, yě yòng lái zhuāngshì.
    - Ancient people used cowrie shells as currency and also for decoration.
  - 一些古文献记载了貝(一些古文獻記載了貝)的神秘价值(的神秘價值)，激发了人们对古代文化的兴趣(激發了人們對古代文化的興趣)。
    - Yīxiē gǔ wénxiàn jìzǎi le bèi de shénmì jiàzhí, jīfā le rénmen duì gǔdài wénhuà de xìngqù.
    - Some ancient texts recorded the mysterious value of cowrie shells, sparking people’s interest in ancient culture.
  - 海边曾有传说(海邊曾有傳說)，珊瑚和貝点缀着沙滩的美丽(珊瑚和貝點綴著沙灘的美麗)。
    - Hǎibiān céng yǒu chuánshuō, shānhú hé bèi diǎnzuì zhe shātān de měilì.
    - There was a legend by the seaside that corals and cowrie shells adorned the beauty of the beach.
### 真(真) → 丁(丁)

### 真(真) → 十(十)
- **definition:** ten

    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.

### 真(真) → 丌(丌) → 其(其)
- **definition:** his
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 箕
    - jī
    - basket
  - 竹
    - zhú
    - bamboo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 𠀠 (basket pictogram) ->
    - 丌 (stand) added under basket as phonetic and to enhance meaning ->
    - borrowed for pronoun 'his' ->
    - 箕(箕) (jī, 'basket') derived with additional 竹(竹) (zhú, 'bamboo')
  - **interpretation:** The character originally depicted a basket, symbolized by 𠀠, emphasizing its use. To reclaim the original meaning of a 'basket', 箕(箕) (jī, 'basket') was formed with an added bamboo radical.
- **examples:**
  - 将军因其英勇闻名(將軍因其英勇聞名)。
    - Jiāngjūn yīn qí yīngyǒng wénmíng。
    - The general is famous for his valor.

    - Tóngpáo zūn qí zhōngchéng, shìzú pèifú qí yīngmíng。
