## Spring Festival
### Chūn Jié
---
- **traditional:** 春節
- **definition:** Spring Festival
- **pinyin:** Chūn Jié
- **components:**
  - 春
    - chūn
    - spring
  - 节(節)
    - jié
    - festival; node; joint; segment
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 春 (chūn, "spring") and 節 (jié, "festival") combine directly to mean "Spring Festival." 春 (chūn, "spring") refers to the season marking new beginnings and renewal, and 節 (jié, "festival") signifies a celebratory time or occasion. The Spring Festival is a major holiday in Chinese culture, celebrated with various customs and traditions.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 春 (chūn, "spring") and 節 (jié, "festival") makes sense as the festival is celebrated at the beginning of the new lunar year during the spring season, marking renewal and new beginnings.
- **examples:**
  - 春节(節)是中国(國)最重要的节(節)日。
    - Chūn Jié shì Zhōngguó zuì zhòngyào de jiérì.
    - The Spring Festival is the most important festival in China.
  - 家人团(團)聚过(過)春节(節)，共享美食庆(慶)佳节(節)。
    - Jiārén tuánjù guò Chūn Jié, gòngxiǎng měishí qìng jiājié.
    - Family gathers during the Spring Festival and enjoys delicious food to celebrate the holiday.

- **components:**

    - cǎo
    - grass; plant
  - 早
    - zǎo
    - early
  - 皂
    - zào
    - acorn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, "grass; plant") + phonetic 早 (zǎo, "early") ->
    - originally refers to 皂 (zào, "acorn") ->
    - borrowed for "grass" sense, replacing 艸
  - **interpretation:** The character 草(cǎo, "grass") combines the semantic component 艹, representing "grass," with the phonetic component 早 which was originally used for "acorn." The phonetic was borrowed to represent the sound for the word "grass." This process shows how the character evolved from its original meaning to its current usage.
- **examples:**

  - 山坡上⾠丛(艸叢)生。
    - Shānpō shàng cǎo cóngshēng.
    - Grass grows densely on the hillside.

### 春( )节(節) → 春 → ⾠(草) → 早 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
- **examples:**
  - 晨曦来临时(來臨時)，日出东(東)方。
    - Chénxī láilín shí, rìchū dōngfāng.
    - At the break of dawn, the sun rises in the east.
  - 温(溫)暖的日光洒满(灑滿)大地。
    - Wēnnuǎn de rìguāng sǎ mǎn dàdì.
    - The warm sunlight spreads over the earth.
  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。
    - Zhōngwǔ shífēn, rì gāo guà tiān.
    - At noon, the sun hangs high in the sky.

### 春( )节(節) → 春 → 屯

  - 屮

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 屮 (chè, "sprout") + dot ->

    - festival segment relating to seasonal nodes
  - **interpretation:** 節 (jié) combines 艹 (cǎo, 'grass'), symbolizing plant growth and natural cycles, with 即 (jí, 'approach'), which may imply the approach of events or time segments, to represent festivals and segments of time. This reflects the seasonal and periodic nature of festivals.
  - **simplification:** 節 was simplified to 节(節) by reducing 艹 (cǎo, 'grass') to a simpler form and replacing 卽 with 卩(jié), representing the meaning-related concept of 'section'.

    - Every segment of the bamboo gives rise to a new leaf.
  - 手臂的关节(關節)，让(讓)人能够灵(夠靈)活挥动(揮動)。

### 春( )节(節) → 节(節) → 即 → 卩
