## telephone number
### diànhuà hàomǎ
---
- **definition:** telephone number
- **pinyin:** diànhuà hàomǎ
- **components:**
  - 电话(電話)
    - diànhuà
    - telephone; phone; phone call; to call
  - 号码(號碼)
    - hàomǎ
    - number; code; serial number; identifier
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 电话 (telephone) + 号码 (number) = telephone number
  - **interpretation:** Combines the communication medium with a numeric identifier to refer to the specific digits used to reach someone by phone. Emphasizes the identifying sequence rather than the device.
  - **simplification:** All four characters were simplified in the PRC to reduce strokes and standardize radicals: 電→电, 話→话 (speech radical 讠), 號→号, 碼→码 (马).
- **examples:**
  - 请把你的电话号码写在这里(請把你的電話號碼寫在這裡)。
    - Qǐng bǎ nǐ de diànhuà hàomǎ xiě zài zhèlǐ.
    - Please write your phone number here.
  - 他换了电话号码，所以我联系不上他(他換了電話號碼，所以我聯繫不上他)。
    - Tā huàn le diànhuà hàomǎ, suǒyǐ wǒ liánxì bù shàng tā.
    - He changed his phone number, so I can't reach him.
  - 这个公司有多个客服电话号码(這個公司有多個客服電話號碼)。
    - Zhège gōngsī yǒu duō gè kèfú diànhuà hàomǎ.
    - This company has multiple customer-service phone numbers.
### 电话号码(電話號碼) → 电话(電話)
- **definition:** telephone; phone; phone call; to call
- **pinyin:** diànhuà
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 电 ‘electricity’ + 话 ‘speech/words’ = electric speech → telephone/phone call.
  - **interpretation:** A modern Sino-Japanese coinage (電話, denwa) adopted into Chinese, literally ‘electric speech’. Highlights speech transmitted via electricity; in use it can mean both the device and a call.
  - **simplification:** Both characters were simplified in PRC reforms to reduce strokes and speed writing: 電 → 电 by compressing the lightning/rain elements; 話 → 话 by reducing 言 to 讠 and streamlining the right-side component.
- **examples:**
  - 他给我打电话(他給我打電話)。
    - Tā gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - He gave me a call.
  - 请接一下电话(請接一下電話)。
    - Qǐng jiē yíxià diànhuà.
    - Please pick up the phone.
  - 我忘了带手机，只能用办公室的电话(我忘了帶手機，只能用辦公室的電話)。
    - Wǒ wàng le dài shǒujī, zhǐnéng yòng bàngōngshì de diànhuà.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a horse with head facing left and flowing mane; in bronze, the head simplified to 目; legs evolved into four dots 灬 (unrelated to 火). Simplified 马 from 馬 via cursive script.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a sketch of a horse that gradually stylized its head and legs into simple strokes. Modern simplification to 马 follows a cursive handwriting form, compressing the body and abstracting the legs into a single sweeping shape.
  - **simplification:** Reduced from 馬 based on cursive (草书) form to speed writing, cutting strokes from about 10 to 3 and standardized in the PRC.
- **examples:**
  - 那匹马跑得很快(那匹馬跑得很快)。
    - Nà pǐ mǎ pǎo de hěn kuài.
    - That horse runs very fast.
  - 他骑马去学校(他騎馬去學校)。
    - Tā qí mǎ qù xuéxiào.
    - He rides a horse to school.
  - 马上开始比赛(馬上開始比賽)。
    - Mǎshàng kāishǐ bǐsài.
    - The competition will start right away.
