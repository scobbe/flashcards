### master; skilled worker
---
- **pinyin:** master; skilled worker
- **components:**
  - 师(師)
    - shī
    - master; teacher; expert; skilled worker
  - 傅
    - fù
    - to teach; to instruct; tutor; a respectful title
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 师(師) (shī, "master") + 傅(傅) (fù, "to teach, to instruct") — master instructor
  - **interpretation:** The word refers to someone who is a master or expert in a particular field, indicating both skill and a mentoring role. This combination makes sense as a 师(師) (shī, "master") provides guidance and instruction in the trade or craft.
- **examples:**
  - 这位师傅的技术真好(這位師傅的技術真好)。
    - Zhè wèi shīfù de j技術 zhēn hǎo.
    - This master’s skills are really great.
  - 我想向这位师傅学习(我想向這位師傅學習)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng xiàng zhè wèi shīfù xuéxí.
    - I want to learn from this master.
### 师傅(師傅) → 师(師)
- **definition:** master; teacher; expert; skilled worker
- **pinyin:** shī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Simplified from 師 (𠂤 → 刂), indicating a connection to the word's meaning of expertise, where the components suggest a skilled worker or master.
  - **interpretation:** The character implies a person who possesses expertise or skill, represented by the combination of components that suggest authority and knowledge.
  - **simplification:** The character was simplified by removing one stroke from the original form, making it visually simpler while retaining the core meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我想请老师(我想請老師)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng qǐng lǎoshī.
    - I want to ask the teacher.
  - 他是个优秀的教师(他是一個優秀的教師)。
    - Tā shì gè yōuxiù de jiàoshī.
    - He is an excellent teacher.
### 师傅(師傅) → 傅(傅)
- **definition:** to teach; to instruct; tutor; a respectful title
- **pinyin:** fù
- **components:**
  - 亻
    - rén
    - person
  - 尃
    - fǎ
    - phonetic component
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** This character combines the semantic component 亻 (rén, 'person') and the phonetic component 尃 (fǎ, indicating pronunciation).
  - **interpretation:** The character suggests a person (亻) who instructs or teaches, resonating with the meaning of a tutor or instructor.
- **examples:**
  - 他是我的老师(他是我的老師)，非常有名(非常有名)。
    - tā shì wǒ de lǎoshī, fēicháng yǒumíng.
    - He is my teacher, very famous.
  - 我需要一个辅导员(我需要一個輔導員)，来帮助我学习(來幫助我學習)。
    - wǒ xūyào yīgè fǔdǎoyuán, lái bāngzhù wǒ xuéxí.
    - I need a tutor to help me study.
### 师傅(師傅) → 傅(傅) → 亻(亻)
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** A stylization of 人, which represents a person or human figure.
  - **interpretation:** This character represents a simplified form of a person, emphasizing humanity and individual identity.
- **examples:**
  - 我是一个人(我是一个人)。
    - Wǒ shì yī gè rén.
    - I am a person.
  - 人的权利很重要(人的權利很重要)。
    - Rén de quánlì hěn zhòngyào.
    - The rights of a person are very important.
### 师傅(師傅) → 傅(傅) → 尃(扭)

- **pinyin:** fǎ
- **components:**

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** A phono-semantic compound formed from the phonetic component 尃 and the semantic component 手 (shǒu, 'hand').
  - **simplification:** 扭 is a simplified form of the traditional character 扭, which reflects stroke reduction.
- **examples:**

### 师傅(師傅) → 傅(傅) → 尃(扭) → 手(手)
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a human hand with fingers, where the top stroke represents the bent over middle finger and horizontal strokes represent two fingers each.
  - **interpretation:** This character intuitively represents a hand by depicting its five distinct fingers, distinguishing it from other characters that represent claws or simplified forms of hands.
- **examples:**
  - 我用手(我用手)写字。
    - Wǒ yòng shǒu xiě zì.
    - I write with my hand.
  - 请用手(請用手)打电话。
    - Qǐng yòng shǒu dǎ diànhuà.
    - Please use your hand to make a phone call.
