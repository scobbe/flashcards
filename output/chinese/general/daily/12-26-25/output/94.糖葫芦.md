### candied hawthorn
---
- **pinyin:** candied hawthorn
- **components:**
  - 糖
    - táng
    - sugar; sweets; candy; syrup
  - 葫芦(葫蘆)
    - húlu
    - gourd; bottle gourd; calabash; any gourd-like shape
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 糖(糖) (táng, "sugar") + 葫芦(葫蘆) (húlu, "gourd") — sugar gourd
  - **interpretation:** 糖葫芦 is a popular snack made by coating hawthorn berries with sugar syrup, often served on a stick. The term literally translates to 'sugar gourd', emphasizing the candy's sweet and gourd-like appearance.
- **examples:**
  - 我想吃糖葫芦。(我想吃糖葫蘆。)
    - Wǒ xiǎng chī tánghúlú.
    - I want to eat candied hawthorn.
  - 市场上有很多糖葫芦。(市場上有很多糖葫蘆。)
    - Shìchǎng shàng yǒu hěn duō tánghúlú.
    - There are many candied hawthorns in the market.
