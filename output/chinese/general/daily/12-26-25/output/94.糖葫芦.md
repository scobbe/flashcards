## candied hawthorn
### tánghúlu
---
- **definition:** candied hawthorn
- **pinyin:** tánghúlu
- **components:**
  - 糖
    - táng
    - sugar; candy; sweets; sweetened
  - 葫芦(葫蘆)
    - húlu
    - gourd; bottle gourd; calabash; gourd-shaped
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 糖 (sugar) + 葫芦 (gourd) = ‘sugar(-coated) gourd’ → a sugar-coated skewer resembling a gourd.
  - **interpretation:** Literally ‘sugar gourd’, naming a skewer of fruit—traditionally hawthorn—coated in hard sugar syrup so it looks gourd-like. Widely known as a classic northern Chinese street snack.
  - **simplification:** 葫蘆 → 葫芦 by simplifying 蘆 to 芦 in the grass-radical series to reduce strokes; 糖 unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我最爱冬天买糖葫芦(我最愛冬天買糖葫蘆)。
    - Wǒ zuì ài dōngtiān mǎi táng húlu.
    - I love buying candied hawthorns in winter.
  - 这家店的冰糖葫芦很脆(這家店的冰糖葫蘆很脆)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn de bīngtáng húlu hěn cuì.
    - The bingtanghulu here is very crunchy.
  - 他把山楂串好再裹上糖浆做成糖葫芦(他把山楂串好再裹上糖漿做成糖葫蘆)。
    - Tā bǎ shānzhā chuàn hǎo zài guǒ shàng tángjiāng zuò chéng táng húlu.
    - He skewers the hawthorns, coats them with syrup, and makes tanghulu.
