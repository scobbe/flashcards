## candied hawthorn
### tánghúlu
---
- **traditional:** 糖葫蘆
- **definition:** candied hawthorn
- **pinyin:** tánghúlu
- **components:**
  - 糖
    - táng
    - sugar
  - 葫
    - hú
    - bottle gourd
  - 芦(蘆)
    - lu
    - reed; rush; bottle gourd
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 糖 (táng, "sugar"), 葫 (hú, "bottle gourd"), 芦(蘆) (lu, "reed")
  - **interpretation:** 糖葫芦(蘆) (tánghúlu, "candied hawthorn") combines 糖 (táng, "sugar"), reflecting the sweetness and sugared coating, with 葫芦(蘆) (húlu, "bottle gourd"), referring to its shape. Originally, the skewered hawthorns might have resembled small gourds. It is a traditional snack in China with a distinctive sweet and sour taste, popular during winter.
  - **simplification:** 葫蘆 -> 葫芦
- **examples:**
  - 我今天买(買)了糖葫芦(蘆)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān mǎile tánghúlu.
    - I bought candied hawthorn today.
  - 糖葫芦(蘆)很甜，吸引了很多孩子。
    - Tánghúlu hěn tián, xīyǐnle hěn duō háizi.
    - Candied hawthorn is very sweet, attracting many children.
  - 在庙会(廟會)上买(買)糖葫芦(蘆)，令我心情愉快。
    - Zài miàohuì shàng mǎi tánghúlu, lìng wǒ xīnqíng yúkuài.
    - Buying candied hawthorn at the fair makes me feel happy.
