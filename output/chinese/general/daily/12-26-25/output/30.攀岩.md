- **definition:** rock climbing
- **pinyin:** pānyán
- **components:**
  - 攀
    - pān
    - climb
  - 岩
    - yán
    - rock; cliff
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 攀 (pān, "climb"), 岩 (yán, "rock; cliff")
  - **interpretation:** The word 攀岩 (pānyán) translates to "rock climbing," referring specifically to the activity of climbing on natural rock formations or artificial rock walls. 攀 (pān, "climb") represents the act of ascending, while 岩 (yán, "rock; cliff") specifies the natural surface being climbed. This combination effectively conveys the idea of physically climbing rock surfaces, either as a sport or recreational activity.

### 攀岩 → 岩(巖)
- **traditional:** 巖
- **definition:** rock; cliff
- **pinyin:** yán
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - hill
  - 石
    - shí
    - rock
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 山 (shān, "hill") + 石 (shí, "rock") =
    - combined elements representing a rocky hill or cliff ->
    - 岩(巖) (yán, "rock; cliff")
  - **interpretation:** The character 岩(巖) (yán, "rock; cliff") combines 山 (shān, "hill") and 石 (shí, "rock") to convey the image of a rocky hill or a cliff. The original form 巖 emphasized the phonetic 嚴 (yán), but later 岩 became the more common form.
  - **simplification:** The character 岩(巖) was originally 巖 in traditional script, simplified by dropping the 嚴 component and directly combining 山 and 石 to retain both the sound and meaning efficiently.
