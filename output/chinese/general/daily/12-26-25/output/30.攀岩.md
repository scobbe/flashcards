## rock climbing
### pānyán
---
- **definition:** rock climbing
- **pinyin:** pānyán
- **components:**
  - 攀
    - pān
    - climb
  - 岩
    - yán
    - rock; cliff
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 攀 (pān, "climb"), 岩 (yán, "rock; cliff")
  - **interpretation:** The word 攀岩 (pānyán) translates to "rock climbing," referring specifically to the activity of climbing on natural rock formations or artificial rock walls. 攀 (pān, "climb") represents the act of ascending, while 岩 (yán, "rock; cliff") specifies the natural surface being climbed. This combination effectively conveys the idea of physically climbing rock surfaces, either as a sport or recreational activity.
- **examples:**
  - 他喜欢(歡)攀岩。
    - Tā xǐhuān pānyán.
    - He likes rock climbing.
  - 周(週)末他去海边(邊)攀岩，体验(體驗)自然乐(樂)趣。
    - Zhōumò tā qù hǎibiān pānyán, tǐyàn zìrán lèqù.
    - On the weekend, he goes rock climbing by the seaside, experiencing the joy of nature.
  - 小明通过(過)攀岩，不断(斷)挑战(戰)自己。
    - Xiǎomíng tōngguò pānyán, bùduàn tiǎozhàn zìjǐ.
    - Xiaoming continuously challenges himself through rock climbing.

### 攀岩 → 攀 → 手
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a hand with fingers; top stroke is a bent over middle finger, horizontal strokes represent fingers
  - **interpretation:** The character 手 (shǒu, "hand") is a direct pictorial representation of a human hand with all five fingers, which distinguishes it from similar characters representing claws or simplified fingers. This visually conveys its meaning as a hand clearly.
- **examples:**
  - 他用手写(寫)字。
    - Tā yòng shǒu xiě zì.
    - He writes with his hand.
  - 妈妈牵着(媽媽牽著)我的手走路。
    - Māmā qiān zhe wǒ de shǒu zǒu lù.
    - Mom holds my hand while walking.
  - 他的手很温(溫)暖，带给(帶給)我力量。
    - Tā de shǒu hěn wēn nuǎn, dài gěi wǒ lìliàng.
    - His hand is very warm, giving me strength.
### 攀岩 → 岩(巖)
- **traditional:** 巖
- **definition:** rock; cliff
- **pinyin:** yán
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - hill
  - 石
    - shí
    - rock
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 山 (shān, "hill") + 石 (shí, "rock") =
    - combined elements representing a rocky hill or cliff ->
    - 岩(巖) (yán, "rock; cliff")
  - **interpretation:** The character 岩(巖) (yán, "rock; cliff") combines 山 (shān, "hill") and 石 (shí, "rock") to convey the image of a rocky hill or a cliff. The original form 巖 emphasized the phonetic 嚴 (yán), but later 岩 became the more common form.
  - **simplification:** The character 岩(巖) was originally 巖 in traditional script, simplified by dropping the 嚴 component and directly combining 山 and 石 to retain both the sound and meaning efficiently.
- **examples:**
  - 那块(塊)岩静静(靜靜)矗立在河边(邊)。
    - Nà kuài yán jìng jìng chùlì zài hé biān.
    - That rock stands quietly by the river bank.
  - 登上岩顶(頂)，看尽(盡)山川风(風)光。
    - Dēng shàng yán dǐng, kàn jìn shān chuān fēng guāng.
    - Climb to the top of the rock and behold the scenic mountains and rivers.
  - 海浪拍打着悬(著懸)岩，发(發)出低沉的轰鸣(轟鳴)。
    - Hǎi làng pāidǎ zhe xuán yán, fāchū dīchén de hōngmíng.
    - The waves crash against the cliff, producing a deep, rumbling sound.
### 攀岩 → 岩(巖) → 山
- **definition:** hill
- **pinyin:** shān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three mountain peaks
  - **interpretation:** The character 山(shān, "mountain") visually represents the concept of a mountain using three vertical lines that suggest peaks. This depiction is consistent with early forms found in Oracle Bone Script, emphasizing the notion of natural elevation associated with mountains.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)去爬山。
    - Wǒmen qù pá shān.
    - We go climb the mountain.
  - 那座大山让(讓)人惊叹(驚嘆)。
    - Nà zuò dà shān ràng rén jīngtàn.
    - That mountain astonishes people.
  - 远处(遠處)的山隐约(隱約)在云(雲)中，令人心旷(曠)神怡。
    - Yuǎnchù de shān yǐnyuē zài yún zhōng, lìng rén xīnkuàng shényí.
    - The distant mountain fades into the clouds, leaving one in a state of peace.
### 攀岩 → 岩(巖) → 石
- **definition:** rock
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stone beneath a cliff, where the upper part originally looked like a cliff (厂) but was distorted into 丆 over time.
  - **interpretation:** The character 石(shí, 'stone') visually represents a stone in a natural setting, highlighting the element of a cliff or mountain. Its visual design aims to reflect the appearance of rocks lodged or embedded in a mountainous or cliff-like setting.
- **examples:**
  - 他在河边发现(邊發現)一块(塊)石。
    - Tā zài hé biān fāxiàn yī kuài shí.
    - He discovered a piece of stone by the riverside.
  - 孩子们(們)喜欢(歡)堆石，玩得开(開)心。
    - Háizimen xǐhuān duī shí, wán de kāixīn.
    - Children like to stack stones, playing happily.
