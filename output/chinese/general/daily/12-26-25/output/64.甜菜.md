### tiáncài
---
- **definition:** beet
- **pinyin:** tiáncài
- **components:**
  - 甜
    - tián
    - sweet
  - 菜
    - cài
    - vegetable; dish; cuisine; greens
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 甜 (tián, "sweet") + 菜 (cài, "vegetable")
  - **interpretation:** 甜菜 (tiáncài, "beet") is named for its sweet flavor, which is notable in beets. The use of 甜 (tián, "sweet") reflects the characteristic taste, while 菜 (cài, "vegetable") indicates it is a plant used in food.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )买(買)了( )一( )些( )甜( )菜( )。
    - Wǒ mǎile yīxiē tiáncài.
    - I bought some beets.
  - 甜( )菜( )富( )含( )维(維)生( )素( )，颜(顏)色( )鲜(鮮)艳(豔)。
    - Tiáncài fùhán wéishēngsù, yánsè xiānyàn.
    - Beets are rich in vitamins, and their color is vibrant.

  - **interpretation:** 甜 (tián, "sweet") combines 舌 (shé, "tongue") and 甘 (gān, "sweet") to represent the concept of tasting sweetness, which conveys the idea of something sweet to the taste.

### 甜菜 → 菜 → 采(採)
- **traditional:** 採
- **definition:** pluck
- **pinyin:** cǎi
- **components:**
  - 扌(手)
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 扌(hand) + 采(to pick) =
    - action of picking with hand ->
    - pluck
  - **interpretation:** The character 採 represents the action of picking or plucking using one's hand. The semantic component 扌(shǒu, "hand") indicates the action being performed, while 采(cǎi, "to pick") serves the phonetic role. Over time, 采 was retained in simplified usage, whereas 採 is used traditionally.
  - **simplification:** 采 is a simplification of 採 by using the original form that omits the 手 hand radical for brevity while retaining the phonetic element.
- **examples:**

### 甜菜 → 菜 → 采(採) → 扌
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stylized hand. The top stroke is the bent middle finger, and the horizontal strokes each represent two fingers.

  - **simplification:** 扌 is derived as a cursive or shorthand form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), making it easier to incorporate into complex characters as a radical or component.
