### tiáncài
---
- **definition:** beet
- **pinyin:** tiáncài
- **components:**
  - 甜
    - tián
    - sweet
  - 菜
    - cài
    - vegetable; dish; cuisine
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 甜(甜) (tián, "sweet") + 菜(菜) (cài, "vegetable") =
    - sweet vegetable ->
    - beet
  - **interpretation:** The word 甜菜 describes a beet, highlighting its natural sweetness with 甜(甜) (tián, "sweet") and categorizing it as a type of vegetable with 菜(菜) (cài, "vegetable").
- **examples:**
  - 农民种植甜菜(農民種植甜菜)。
    - Nóngmín zhòngzhí tiáncài.
    - The farmers grow beets.
  - 我喜欢甜菜(我喜歡甜菜)，它很甜(它很甜)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān tiáncài, tā hěn tián.
    - I like beets, they are very sweet.
  - 甜菜不仅营养丰富(甜菜不僅營養豐富)，而且颜色鲜艳(而且顏色鮮豔)。
    - Tiáncài bùjǐn yíngyǎng fēngfù, érqiě yánsè xiānyàn.
    - Beets are not only nutritious, but also brightly colored.
### 甜菜(甜菜) → 甜(甜)
- **definition:** sweet
- **pinyin:** tián
- **components:**
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
  - 甘
    - gān

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 舌(舌) (shé, "tongue") + 甘(甘) (gān, "sweet") =
    - perception of sweetness on the tongue ->
    - sweet
  - **interpretation:** The character 甜(甜) (tián, "sweet") combines 舌(舌) (shé, "tongue") representing the organ that perceives taste, with 甘(甘) (gān, "sweet"), conveying the sensation of sweetness experienced by the tongue. This demonstrates a direct relationship between taste and sensation.
- **examples:**

    - Tā pào de chá, jì xiāng yòu tián.

  - 他喝牛奶(他喝牛奶)，觉得有点甜(覺得有點甜)。
    - Tā hē niúnǎi, juéde yǒudiǎn tián.
