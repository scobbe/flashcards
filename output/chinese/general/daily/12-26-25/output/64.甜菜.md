### tiáncài

- **definition:** beet
- **pinyin:** tiáncài
- **components:**
  - 甜
    - tián
    - sweet
  - 菜
    - cài
    - vegetable; dish; cuisine; greens
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 甜(甜) (tián, "sweet") + 菜(菜) (cài, "vegetable")
  - **interpretation:** 甜菜(甜菜) (tiáncài, "beet") is named for its sweet flavor, which is notable in beets. The use of 甜(甜) (tián, "sweet") reflects the characteristic taste, while 菜(菜) (cài, "vegetable") indicates it is a plant used in food.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了一些甜菜(我買了一些甜菜)。
    - Wǒ mǎile yīxiē tiáncài.
    - I bought some beets.
  - 甜菜富含维生素(甜菜富含維生素)，颜色鲜艳(顏色鮮豔)。
    - Tiáncài fùhán wéishēngsù, yánsè xiānyàn.
    - Beets are rich in vitamins, and their color is vibrant.

### 甜菜(甜菜) → 菜(菜) → 采(採)

- **pinyin:** cǎi
- **components:**

  - 木

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 爪(爪) (zhǎo, 'claw') + 木(木) (mù, 'tree') =
    - hand grasping tree branch ->
    - pluck
  - **interpretation:** The character 采(cǎi, 'pluck') visually represents a claw or hand reaching out to a tree, symbolizing the action of plucking or gathering. This illustrates the intuitive meaning through its pictorial components.
  - **simplification:** In contemporary Chinese, 采(cǎi) serves both phonetic and semantic functions. The simplified form replaces the traditional form 採(cǎi), which is composed of 扌(shǒu, 'hand') + 采(cǎi, 'pluck'), illustrating the action of plucking with a hand.
- **examples:**
