- **pinyin:** beet
- **components:**
  - 甜
    - tián
    - sweet; pleasant; to cheer; delicious
  - 菜
    - cài
    - vegetable; dish; cuisine; food
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 甜(甜) (tián, 'sweet') + 菜(菜) (cài, 'vegetable') — sweet vegetable
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 甜(甜) (tián, 'sweet') and 菜(菜) (cài, 'vegetable') aptly describes beets, known for their sweet flavor and classification as a vegetable. This reflects the essence of the food's characteristics.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃甜菜(我喜歡吃甜菜)。
    - wǒ xǐhuān chī tiáncài。
    - I like to eat beets.
  - 甜菜非常健康(甜菜非常健康)。
    - tiáncài fēicháng jiànkāng。
    - Beets are very healthy.
### 甜菜(甜菜) → 甜(甜)
- **definition:** sweet; pleasant; to cheer; delicious
- **pinyin:** tián
- **components:**
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
  - 甘
    - gān
    - sweet
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 舌 (舌) (shé, 'tongue') + 甘 (甘) (gān, 'sweet') — tongue tasting sweetness
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the concept of the tongue with sweetness, reflecting the sensory experience of tasting something sweet.
- **examples:**
  - 这个蛋糕真甜(這個蛋糕真甜)，我想再吃一块(我想再吃一塊)。
    - Zhège dàngāo zhēn tián, wǒ xiǎng zài chī yī kuài.
    - This cake is really sweet, I want to have another piece.
  - 糖果甜(糖果甜)，小孩喜欢吃(小孩喜歡吃)。
    - Tángguǒ tián, xiǎohái xǐhuān chī.
    - Candy is sweet, children like to eat it.
### 甜菜(甜菜) → 甜(甜) → 舌(舌)
- **definition:** tongue
- **pinyin:** shé
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a shape resembling a tongue, emphasizing its function and form.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a tongue with a wavy line that suggests its flexibility and shape, effectively associating it with its meaning.
- **examples:**

### 甜菜(甜菜) → 甜(甜) → 甘(甘)
- **definition:** sweet
- **pinyin:** gān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Represents something sweet in an open mouth; originally depicted as 𠁿.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the idea of sweetness being tasted, highlighting the connection between flavor and expression.
- **examples:**

### 甜菜(甜菜) → 菜(菜)
- **definition:** vegetable; dish; cuisine; food
- **pinyin:** cài
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 才
    - cái
    - ability
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** This character is a combination of the semantic component of '野' (yě, 'wild') and the phonetic component '才' (cái, 'ability').
  - **interpretation:** The character suggests the idea of 'ability' related to 'grass' or 'plants,' which aligns intuitively with the meaning of vegetables and dishes derived from them.
- **examples:**
  - 这些菜很好吃(這些菜很好吃)，我每天都吃(我每天都吃)。
  - 你喜欢吃什么菜(你喜歡吃什麼菜)？
  - 我们去餐厅点菜(我們去餐廳點菜)！
### 甜菜(甜菜) → 菜(菜) → 艹(艸)
- **definition:** grass
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts grass or plants, typically represented above ground.
  - **interpretation:** The character shows tufts of grass, symbolizing vegetation that grows close to the earth.
  - **simplification:** 艹 is the simplified form of 艸; the top radicals are reduced for ease of writing.
- **examples:**
  - 艹是草字头(艸是草字頭)。
    - cǎo shì cǎo zì tóu.
    - 艹 is the grass radical.
  - 许多汉字都有艹(許多漢字都有艸)。
    - xǔduō hànzì dōu yǒu cǎo.
    - Many Chinese characters have 艹.
### 甜菜(甜菜) → 菜(菜) → 才(才)
- **definition:** ability
- **pinyin:** cái
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Originally depicted as a person with arms raised, symbolizing an individual with the ability to produce or create.
  - **interpretation:** The character conveys the sense of ability or talent that comes from a person who can produce something remarkable.
- **examples:**
  - 他有很多才华(他有很多才華)。
    - Tā yǒu hěn duō cáihuá.
    - He has a lot of talents.
  - 这是一种很特别的才能(這是一種很特別的才能)。
    - Zhè shì yī zhǒng hěn tèbié de cáinéng.
