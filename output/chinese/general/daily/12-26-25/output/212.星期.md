## week; weekday
### xīngqī
---
- **definition:** week; weekday
- **pinyin:** xīngqī
- **components:**
  - 星
    - xīng
    - star
  - 期
    - qī
    - period; phase
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 星(星) (xīng, "star") + 期(期) (qī, "period; phase") =
    - counting of weeks originally based on the cycles or phases of stars
  - **interpretation:** The term 星期 (xīngqī) refers to a 'week' or 'weekday'. It originally related to the cycles or phases of stars (星(星)) over a certain period (期(期)). Time periods on Earth were often associated with celestial movements, such as stars or planets.
- **examples:**
  - 我每个星期读三本书(我每個星期讀三本書)。
    - Wǒ měi gè xīngqī dú sān běn shū。
    - I read three books each week.
  - 这个星期很忙(這個星期很忙)，我没时间休息(我沒時間休息)。
    - Zhège xīngqī hěn máng, wǒ méi shíjiān xiūxí。
    - This week is very busy, and I have no time to rest.
### 星期(星期) → 星(星)
- **definition:** star
- **pinyin:** xīng
- **components:**
  - 晶
    - jīng
    - twinkling stars
  - 生
    - shēng
    - None
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic 晶(晶) (jīng, 'twinkling stars') + phonetic 生(生) (shēng) ->
    - 晶(晶) (jīng, 'twinkling stars') simplified to 日(日) (rì, 'sun') ->
    - 星(星) (xīng, 'star')
  - **interpretation:** The character 星(星) (xīng, 'star') combines two phonetic elements, 晶(晶) (jīng, 'twinkling stars') and 生(生) (shēng), to emphasize the star's radiant and life-like qualities. The original element 晶(晶) (jīng) denoted twinkling stars and was visually simplified to 日(日) (rì, 'sun').
  - **simplification:** None, as both traditional and simplified forms use the same components in modern contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 夜空中有很多星(夜空中有很多星)。
    - Yè kōng zhōng yǒu hěn duō xīng.
    - There are many stars in the night sky.
  - 她爱摘下夜空中的一颗星(她愛摘下夜空中的一顆星)，许下美丽愿望(許下美麗願望)。
    - Tā ài zhāi xià yè kōng zhōng de yī kē xīng, xǔ xià měi lì yuàn wàng.
    - She loves to pluck a star from the night sky and make a beautiful wish.
  - 古人曾仰望满天星(古人曾仰望滿天星)，激发无数梦想(激發無數夢想)。
    - Gǔ rén céng yǎng wàng mǎn tiān xīng, jī fā wú shù mèng xiǎng.
    - Ancient people gazed at the starry sky, inspiring countless dreams.
### 星期(星期) → 星(星) → 晶(晶)
- **definition:** twinkling stars
- **pinyin:** jīng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 日(日) (rì, 'sun') triplicated =
    - multiple suns ->
    - brightness ->
    - crystal
  - **interpretation:** The character 晶(jīng) represents the intense brightness associated with having three suns, metaphorically capturing the lustrous and clear qualities of a crystal.
- **examples:**
  - 这颗晶闪烁着迷人的光辉(這顆晶閃爍著迷人的光輝)。
    - Zhè kē jīng shǎnshuò zhe mírén de guānghuī.
    - This crystal shines with enchanting radiance.
  - 这枚戒指中镶嵌着一颗晶(這枚戒指中鑲嵌著一顆晶)，显得格外璀璨(顯得格外璀璨)。
    - Zhè méi jièzhǐ zhōng xiāngqiàn zhe yī kē jīng, xiǎnde géwài cuǐcàn.
    - A crystal is inlaid in this ring, making it exceptionally brilliant.

### 星期(星期) → 期(期)
- **definition:** period; phase
- **pinyin:** qī
- **components:**
  - 其
    - qí
    - his
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 其(其) (qí, "his") + 月(月) (yuè, "moon") =
    - time/moment associated with the moon ->
    - period; phase
  - **interpretation:** 其(其) (qí, "his") serves as the phonetic component, while 月(月) (yuè, "moon") represents the passage of time, together conveying the meaning of a time period or phase.
- **examples:**
  - 春天的花期最美(春天的花期最美)。
    - Chūntiān de huāqī zuì měi.
    - The blooming period of spring is the most beautiful.
  - 他正处在事业的黄金期(他正處在事業的黃金期)，不断挑战自我(不斷挑戰自我)。
    - Tā zhèng chǔ zài shìyè de huángjīn qī, búduàn tiǎozhàn zìwǒ.
    - He is in the golden period of his career, constantly challenging himself.
  - 新的学期开始了(新的學期開始了)。
    - Xīn de xuéqī kāishǐ le.
    - The new semester has begun.
### 星期(星期) → 期(期) → 其(其)
- **definition:** his
- **pinyin:** qí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally 𠀠 as a basket ->
    - 丌 added as a stand/phono-semantic component ->
    - 𠔝 ->
    - borrowed as pronoun ->
    - 其
  - **interpretation:** Initially depicting a basket, the character 其 evolved by adding 丌, which might have functioned as a phonetic cue in ancient Chinese, resulting in a form that eventually became a pronoun. To reclaim the original meaning of 'basket', 箕 was created with the 竹 radical.
- **examples:**
  - 这字读作其(這字讀作其)。
    - Zhè zì dú zuò qí.
    - This character is pronounced as qí.
  - 作者选择字其(作者選擇字其)用于诗句末尾(用於詩句末尾)，显示其风韵(顯示其風韻)。
    - Zuòzhě xuǎnzé zì qí yòng yú shījù mòwěi, xiǎnshì qí fēngyùn.
    - The author chooses the character '其' for the end of verses, demonstrating its phonetic charm.
  - 这字既简单又具有独特的音韵(這字既簡單又具有獨特的音韻)，其发音清脆悦耳(其發音清脆悅耳)。
    - Zhè zì jì jiǎndān yòu jùyǒu dútè de yīnyùn, qí fāyīn qīngcuì yuè'ěr.
    - This character is not only simple but also has a unique musicality, and its pronunciation is crisp and pleasant.
