## auspicious; jade token; surname Rui
### ruì
---
- **definition:** auspicious; jade token; surname Rui
- **pinyin:** ruì
- **components:**
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
  - 睿
    - ruì
    - bright
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 玉 (yù, "jade") + phonetic: 瑞(睿) (ruì, "bright") =
    - jade as a symbol of brightness and good fortune ->
    - auspicious
  - **interpretation:** The character 瑞(rui) combines 玉 (yù, "jade"), symbolizing value and purity, with the original sense of brightness or clarity from the phonetic component. This association captures the idea of jade as a token of good fortune.
- **examples:**
  - 瑞雪兆丰(豐)年。
    - Ruì xuě zhào fēng nián.
    - Auspicious snowfall signifies a bountiful year.
  - 李瑞出席晚宴。
    - Lǐ ruì chūxí wǎnyàn.
    - Mr. Li Rui attended the dinner party.
  - 玉器中的瑞玉十分珍贵(貴)。
    - Yùqì zhōng de ruì yù shífēn zhēnguì.
    - The jade token among the jade artifacts is extremely precious.
### 瑞 → 玉
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cong (琮, a ritual jade totem)
  - **interpretation:** 玉 (yù, "jade") visually represents the concept of jade, either as a cong or three pieces of jade strung together. It is distinct from 王 (wáng, "king") thanks to the added dot, ensuring clarity in their usage and meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他收藏了一块(塊)古玉。
    - Tā shōucáng le yī kuài gǔ yù.
    - He collected a piece of antique jade.
  - 晴空万(萬)里时(時)，玉石在阳(陽)光下闪烁(閃爍)。
    - Qíngkōng wànlǐ shí, yù shí zài yángguāng xià shǎnshuò.
    - Under a clear sky, the jade sparkled under the sunlight.
  - 在宫(宮)廷中，玉制(製)的饰(飾)物象征着(徵著)高贵(貴)。
    - Zài gōngtíng zhōng, yù zhì de shìwù xiàngzhēng zhe gāoguì.
    - In the palace, jade ornaments symbolize nobility.
### 瑞 → 睿(叡)
- **traditional:** 叡
- **definition:** bright
- **pinyin:** ruì
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 爰
    - yuán
    - because
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 目 (mù, 'eye') + 爰 (yuán, 'therefore; because') =
    - sharp and discerning eyes ->
    - bright
  - **interpretation:** The character 睿(叡) (ruì, 'bright') combines 目 (mù, 'eye') to represent vision, with 爰 (yuán, 'therefore') serving as a phonetic indicator. This implies heightened perception or insight, thus denoting 'bright' in terms of intelligence or wisdom.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by removing 爰 (yuán) and leaving a simplified form with just 目 (mù) + 一 (yī, 'one') as a representation of clarity or singular focus.
- **examples:**
  - 他有着(著)睿的智慧。
    - Tā yǒuzhe ruì de zhìhuì.
    - He possesses bright wisdom.
  - 面对复杂问题(對複雜問題)，他总(總)能做出睿断(斷)的选择(選擇)。
    - Miànduì fùzá wèntí, tā zǒng néng zuòchū ruì duàn de xuǎnzé.
    - In the face of complex issues, he always makes wise decisions.
### 瑞 → 睿(叡) → 目
- **definition:** eye
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a human eye, now represented and rotated 90 degrees ->
    - eye
  - **interpretation:** The character 目(mù, 'eye') is a pictogram representing a human eye. Its original orientation has changed over time, resembling an eye seen from a sideways view.
- **examples:**
  - 他的目光炯炯有神。
    - Tā de mùguāng jiǒngjiǒng yǒu shén.
    - His gaze is bright and full of spirit.
  - 她目不转(轉)睛，注视(視)前方。
    - Tā mù bù zhuǎn jīng, zhùshì qiánfāng.
    - She stared intently, gazing ahead.
  - 雄鹰(鷹)展翅，双(雙)目炯炯看天际(際)。
    - Xióng yīng zhǎn chì, shuāng mù jiǒngjiǒng kàn tiānjì.
    - The eagle spreads its wings, its eyes piercingly gaze at the horizon.
### 瑞 → 睿(叡) → 爰
- **definition:** because
- **pinyin:** yuán
- **components:**
  - 爫
    - zhǎo
    - hand; claw
  - 又
    - yòu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 爫("hand, claw") + 又("hand") =
    - action performed by hands ->
    - indicates direction or cause ->
    - final form
  - **interpretation:** The character 爰 (yuán, "because") combines two hand-related components, 爫("hand, claw") and 又("hand"), to depict an action or effect initiated or influenced by one’s hands, suggesting causation or relation. The middle component's transformation over time underscores the character's development to its current meaning related to causation or reasoning.
### 瑞 → 睿(叡) → 爰 → 爫
- **definition:** hand; claw
- **pinyin:** zhǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a hand grabbing from above or the side in earlier forms ->
    - Represents a claw or hand
  - **interpretation:** The character 爫(爪) (zhuǎ, 'hand, claw') historically represented a human hand depicted from a side view, grabbing something, although it can extend its meaning to indicate a claw. This visual depiction closely associates with functions performed by a hand or a claw, making the graphical representation intuitive as both human and animal hands or claws engage in similar grasping actions.
- **examples:**
  - 野猫(貓)舒展爫，捕捉小动(動)物。
    - Yě māo shūzhǎn zhuǎ, bǔzhuō xiǎo dòngwù.
    - The wild cat extends its claws and catches small animals.
  - 雕弯(鵰彎)曲爫，紧(緊)握猎(獵)物。
    - Diāo wānqū zhuǎ, jǐn wò lièwù.
    - The eagle curves its claws and tightly grips its prey.
### 瑞 → 睿(叡) → 爰 → 又
- **definition:** hand

  - **description:** Depicts a right hand

- **examples:**

    - The character '又' originally comes from the hand.
  - 很多汉(漢)字含有又部首，表示与(與)手有关(關)的动(動)作。
    - Hěn duō hànzì hányǒu Yòu bùshǒu, biǎoshì yǔ shǒu yǒuguān de dòngzuò。

  - 古人用又描绘(繪)手的形态(態)，体现(體現)了古汉(漢)字的独(獨)特魅力。
    - Gǔrén yòng Yòu miáohuì shǒu de xíngtài, tǐxiàn le gǔ hànzì de dútè mèilì。
    - Ancient people used '又' to depict the shape of the hand, showcasing the unique charm of ancient Chinese characters.
