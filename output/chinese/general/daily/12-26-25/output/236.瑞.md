## auspicious; jade token; surname Rui
### ruì
---
- **definition:** auspicious; jade token; surname Rui
- **pinyin:** ruì
- **components:**
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
  - 睿
    - ruì
    - bright
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 玉 (yù, "jade") + phonetic: 瑞(睿) (ruì, "bright") =
    - jade as a symbol of brightness and good fortune ->
    - auspicious
  - **interpretation:** The character 瑞(rui) combines 玉 (yù, "jade"), symbolizing value and purity, with the original sense of brightness or clarity from the phonetic component. This association captures the idea of jade as a token of good fortune.

- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**

  - **interpretation:** 玉 (yù, "jade") visually represents the concept of jade, either as a cong or three pieces of jade strung together. It is distinct from 王 (wáng, "king") thanks to the added dot, ensuring clarity in their usage and meaning.

### 瑞 → 睿(叡)
- **traditional:** 叡
- **definition:** bright
- **pinyin:** ruì
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 爰
    - yuán
    - because
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 目 (mù, 'eye') + 爰 (yuán, 'therefore; because') =
    - sharp and discerning eyes ->
    - bright
  - **interpretation:** The character 睿(叡) (ruì, 'bright') combines 目 (mù, 'eye') to represent vision, with 爰 (yuán, 'therefore') serving as a phonetic indicator. This implies heightened perception or insight, thus denoting 'bright' in terms of intelligence or wisdom.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by removing 爰 (yuán) and leaving a simplified form with just 目 (mù) + 一 (yī, 'one') as a representation of clarity or singular focus.
- **examples:**
  - 他( )有( )着(著)睿( )的( )智( )慧( )。

- **definition:** because

- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 爰(yuán, 'phonetic') depicts two hands represented by 爫('hand, claw') and 又('hand') working together, symbolizing the concept of grasping or handling something. This visual representation informs its use in contexts related to action or handling.
