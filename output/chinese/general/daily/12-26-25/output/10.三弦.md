## three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
### sānxián
---
- **definition:** three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
- **pinyin:** sānxián
- **components:**
  - 三
    - sān
    - three
  - 弦
    - xián
    - string
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 三(三) (sān, "three") + 弦(弦) (xián, "string")
  - **interpretation:** The word 三弦(三弦) (sānxián, "three-stringed lute") refers to a traditional Chinese musical instrument with three strings. It is often used in the storytelling art form known as Pingtan, popular in the Jiangsu and Zhejiang provinces.
- **examples:**
  - 他听三弦的旋律陶醉(他聽三弦的旋律陶醉)。
    - Tā tīng sānxián de xuànlǜ táozuì.
    - He becomes enchanted by the melody of the three-stringed lute.
  - 在评弹表演中(在評彈表演中)，艺人弹奏三弦伴奏(藝人彈奏三弦伴奏)。
    - Zài píngtán biǎoyǎn zhōng, yìrén dànzòu sānxián bànzòu.
    - During the Pingtan performance, the artist plays the three-stringed lute to accompany the show.
  - 古时的文人常以三弦为乐器(古時的文人常以三弦為樂器)。
    - Gǔshí de wénrén cháng yǐ sānxián wéi yuèqì.
    - In ancient times, scholars often used the three-stringed lute as a musical instrument.
