### three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
---
- **pinyin:** three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
- **components:**
  - 三
    - sān
    - three; 3; several
  - 弦
    - xián
    - string; cord; wire
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 三(三) (sān, "three") + 弦(弦) (xián, "string") — three string
  - **interpretation:** This term describes a lute with three strings, which is essential for its identity as a musical instrument. The combination explicitly conveys the number of strings involved, indicating its specific type.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢弹三弦(我喜歡彈三弦)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān tán sānxián.
    - I enjoy playing the three-stringed lute.
  - 三弦在传统音乐中很重要(三弦在傳統音樂中很重要)。
    - Sānxián zài chuántǒng yīnyuè zhōng hěn zhòngyào.
    - The three-stringed lute is very important in traditional music.
### 三弦(三弦) → 三(三)
- **definition:** three; 3; several
- **pinyin:** sān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Represents three parallel strokes, signifying the concept of three. The character's construction follows a pattern similar to 一 (one) and 二 (two).
  - **interpretation:** The character uses three horizontal strokes to visually represent the number three, making it intuitive and clear.
- **examples:**
  - 我有三本书(我有三本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu sān běn shū.
    - I have three books.
  - 他买了三件衣服(他買了三件衣服)。
    - Tā mǎile sān jiàn yīfú.
    - He bought three pieces of clothing.
### 三弦(三弦) → 弦(弦)
- **definition:** string; cord; wire
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 弓
    - gōng
    - bow
  - 玄
    - xuán
    - mysterious
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** A combination of 弓 (gōng, 'bow') which indicates sound and 玄 (xuán, 'mysterious') which contributes to the phonetic aspect.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the idea of a bow string, with the bow representing tension and the string embodying the means to produce sound.
- **examples:**
  - 这根弦很紧(這根弦很緊)，声音很好听(聲音很好聽)！
    - Zhè gēn xián hěn jǐn, shēngyīn hěn hǎotīng！
    - This string is very tight, and the sound is beautiful!
### 三弦(三弦) → 弦(弦) → 弓(弓)
- **definition:** bow
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a simplified representation of a bow, which is bent and used for shooting arrows.
  - **interpretation:** The shape of the character visually resembles the curved form of a bow, emphasizing its function as a weapon.
- **examples:**
  - 他会拉弓(他會拉弓)。
    - Tā huì lā gōng.
    - He can pull the bow.
  - 弓箭是古代的武器(弓箭是古代的武器)。
    - Gōngjiàn shì gǔdài de wǔqì.
    - Bows and arrows were ancient weapons.
### 三弦(三弦) → 弦(弦) → 玄(玄)
- **definition:** mysterious
- **pinyin:** xuán
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character conveys concepts of mystery and depth. It may derive from ancient usages related to dark colors, which often symbolize the unknown.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes a sense of depth and enigma, as darkness can hide knowledge and secrets.
- **examples:**
  - 这个问题很玄(這個問題很玄)，我需要更多的信息(我需要更多的信息)。
  - 他的想法很玄(他的想法很玄)，我理解起来有点困难(我理解起來有點困難)。
  - 书中的故事充满了玄妙(書中的故事充滿了玄妙)。
