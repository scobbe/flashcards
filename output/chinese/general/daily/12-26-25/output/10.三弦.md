## three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
### sānxián
---
- **definition:** three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
- **pinyin:** sānxián
- **components:**
  - 三
    - sān
    - three; third; triple; trio
  - 弦
    - xián
    - string (instrument); bowstring; chord; arc
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 三 (three) + 弦 (string) = three-stringed lute.
  - **interpretation:** Names a lute with three strings, notably used to accompany Suzhou Pingtan storytelling. Emphasizes the defining feature—the number of strings.
- **examples:**
  - 评弹演员常用三弦伴奏(評彈演員常用三弦伴奏)。
    - Píngtán yǎnyuán chángyòng sānxián bànzòu.
    - Pingtan performers often use the sanxian for accompaniment.
  - 他拉起三弦(他拉起三弦)，曲调婉转(麯調婉轉)。
    - Tā lā qǐ sānxián, qǔdiào wǎnzhuǎn.
    - He began to play the sanxian, and the melody was graceful.
  - 这把三弦出自苏州的老作坊(這把三弦出自蘇州的老作坊)。
    - Zhè bǎ sānxián chūzì Sūzhōu de lǎo zuòfang.
    - This sanxian comes from an old workshop in Suzhou.
### 三弦(三弦) → 三(三)
- **definition:** three; third; triple; trio
- **pinyin:** sān
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Three parallel strokes; triplication of 一 (“one”). Compare 一, 二; unrelated to 丰.
  - **interpretation:** Three horizontal lines directly depict the quantity three, following the simple additive pattern of one and two. It is purely semantic with no phonetic component, visually stacking 'one' three times.
- **examples:**
  - 我有三个问题(我有三個問題)。
    - Wǒ yǒu sān gè wèntí.
    - I have three questions.
  - 我们三点见(我們三點見)。
    - Wǒmen sān diǎn jiàn.
    - Let’s meet at three o’clock.
  - 他住在三楼(他住在三樓)。
    - Tā zhù zài sān lóu.
    - He lives on the third floor.
### 三弦(三弦) → 三(三) → 一(一)
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 三弦(三弦) → 弦(弦)
- **definition:** string (instrument); bowstring; chord; arc
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 弓
    - gōng
    - bow
  - 玄
    - xuán
    - mysterious; profound
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 弓 “bow” + phonetic: 玄 (OC *ɡʷeːn).
  - **interpretation:** Built from 弓 for meaning and 玄 for sound, pointing first to the string drawn on a bow and then extended to instrument strings and the geometric chord. The phonetic 玄 guides the reading while the semantic 弓 anchors the sense in bow/string imagery.
- **examples:**
  - 他拉紧弓弦(他拉緊弓弦)。
    - Tā lā jǐn gōng xián.
    - He pulls the bowstring tight.
  - 音乐家更换琴弦(音樂家更換琴弦)。
    - Yīnyuèjiā gēnghuàn qínxián.
    - The musician replaces the instrument strings.
  - 这条弦连接圆上的两点(這條弦連接圓上的兩點)。
    - Zhè tiáo xián liánjiē yuán shàng de liǎng diǎn.
    - This chord connects two points on the circle.
### 三弦(三弦) → 弦(弦) → 弓(弓)
- **definition:** bow
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a bow.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the curved bow and its string in a simple outline. The shape evokes the bend and draw of archery and later serves as a component in characters related to pulling or shooting.
- **examples:**
  - 他拉开弓(他拉開弓)。
    - Tā lā kāi gōng.
    - He draws the bow.
  - 弓箭手瞄准目标(弓箭手瞄準目標)。
    - Gōngjiànshǒu miáozhǔn mùbiāo.
    - The archer aims at the target.
  - 这把弓很结实(這把弓很結實)。
    - Zhè bǎ gōng hěn jiēshi.
    - This bow is very sturdy.
### 三弦(三弦) → 弦(弦) → 玄(玄)
- **definition:** mysterious; profound
- **pinyin:** xuán
- **components:**
  - 幺
    - yāo
    - tiny; youngest
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram (指事)
  - **description:** Two interwoven threads 幺 with a distinguishing mark 亠 on top; depicts a rope, cf. 率 and 弦; distinct from 己 and 乃.
  - **interpretation:** Interwoven strands suggest a cord, with the top mark signaling something set apart or hidden. From the idea of a special braided thread came meanings of subtle depth and mystery.
- **examples:**
  - 这道理很玄妙(這道理很玄妙)。
    - Zhè dàolǐ hěn xuánmiào.
    - This principle is quite subtle.
  - 他的理论过于玄奥(他的理論過於玄奧)。
    - Tā de lǐlùn guòyú xuán’ào.
    - His theory is overly abstruse.
  - 别把科学说成玄学(別把科學說成玄學)。
    - Bié bǎ kēxué shuō chéng xuánxué.
    - Don't call science mysticism.
### 三弦(三弦) → 弦(弦) → 玄(玄) → 幺(幺)
- **definition:** tiny; youngest
- **pinyin:** yāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depiction of a silk thread; compare 糸.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a sketch of a thin silk strand, later stylized into the two hooked strokes seen today. By association with something slender and slight, it extended to meanings like tiny and, in family ranking, the youngest.
- **examples:**
  - 报数时(報數時)，用“幺”代替“一”(用“幺”代替“一”)，以免混淆(以免混淆)。
    - Bàoshù shí, yòng “yāo” dàitì “yī”, yǐmiǎn hùnxiáo.
    - When reporting numbers, use “yāo” instead of “yī” to avoid confusion.
  - 他是家里的老幺(他是傢裏的老幺)。
    - Tā shì jiālǐ de lǎoyāo.
    - He is the youngest in the family.
  - 在麻将里(在麻將裏)，幺九指一和九(幺九指一和九)。
    - Zài májiàng lǐ, yāojiǔ zhǐ yī hé jiǔ.
    - In mahjong, yaojiu refers to the tiles one and nine.
