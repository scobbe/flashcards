## three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
### sānxián
---
- **definition:** three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
- **pinyin:** sānxián
- **components:**
  - 三
    - sān
    - three
  - 弦
    - xián
    - string
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 三 (sān, "three") + 弦 (xián, "string")
  - **interpretation:** The word 三弦 (sānxián, "three-stringed lute") refers to a traditional Chinese musical instrument with three strings. It is often used in the storytelling art form known as Pingtan, popular in the Jiangsu and Zhejiang provinces.
- **examples:**
  - 他听(聽)三弦的旋律陶醉。
    - Tā tīng sānxián de xuànlǜ táozuì.
    - He becomes enchanted by the melody of the three-stringed lute.
  - 在评弹(評彈)表演中，艺(藝)人弹(彈)奏三弦伴奏。
    - Zài píngtán biǎoyǎn zhōng, yìrén dànzòu sānxián bànzòu.
    - During the Pingtan performance, the artist plays the three-stringed lute to accompany the show.
  - 古时(時)的文人常以三弦为乐(為樂)器。
    - Gǔshí de wénrén cháng yǐ sānxián wéi yuèqì.
    - In ancient times, scholars often used the three-stringed lute as a musical instrument.
### 三弦 → 三
- **definition:** three
- **pinyin:** sān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 一 (yī, "one") triplicated ->
    - 三 (sān, "three")
  - **interpretation:** The character 三 (sān, "three") is an ideogram depicting the concept of the number three through the use of three parallel horizontal strokes. This straightforward representation makes it consistent with related numeric characters like 一 (yī, "one") and 二 (èr, "two").
- **examples:**
  - 今天我买(買)了三斤苹(蘋)果。
    - Jīntiān wǒ mǎi le sān jīn píngguǒ.
    - Today I bought three jin of apples.
  - 他有三支笔(筆)，我只想要其中一支。
    - Tā yǒu sān zhī bǐ, wǒ zhǐ xiǎng yào qízhōng yī zhī.
    - He has three pens, but I only want one of them.
  - 他们(們)吃了三碗面(麵)，觉(覺)得非常满(滿)意。
    - Tāmen chī le sān wǎn miàn, juéde fēicháng mǎnyì.
    - They ate three bowls of noodles and felt very satisfied.
### 三弦 → 弦
- **definition:** string
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk thread
  - 玄
    - xuán
    - dark/mysterious
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 糸 (mì, "silk thread") + 玄 (xuán) =
    - thread or string-themed meaning ->
    - string of a bow or musical instrument
  - **interpretation:** The character 弦(xián, "string") combines 糸 (mì, "silk thread") as the semantic part to indicate its association with threads or strings, and 玄 (xuán) provides the phonetic element. Together, it conveys the idea of threads used as strings in bows or musical instruments.
- **examples:**
  - 吉他有六根弦。
    - Jítā yǒu liù gēn xián.
    - The guitar has six strings.
  - 我拨动(撥動)古琴的弦，奏出优(優)美的旋律。
    - Wǒ bōdòng gǔqín de xián, zòuchū yōuměi de xuànlǜ.
    - I pluck the strings of the guqin and play a beautiful melody.
### 三弦 → 弦 → 糸
- **definition:** silk thread
- **pinyin:** mì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a skein of silk
  - **interpretation:** The character 糸(mì, "silk; thread") represents a pictorial representation of a skein of silk, visually capturing the concept of thread or fiber.
- **examples:**
  - 她手工制(製)作了一条(條)漂亮的丝(絲)巾。
    - Tā shǒugōng zhìzuòle yī tiáo piàoliang de sī jīn.
    - She handcrafted a beautiful silk scarf.
  - 这个设计图(這個設計圖)必须(須)用细(細)糸画(畫)出来(來)。
    - Zhège shèjìtú bìxū yòng xì mì huà chūlái.
    - This design drawing must be sketched with fine thread.
  - 我们参观(們參觀)了古代的织(織)布技术(術)，对丝绸制(對絲綢製)作产(產)生了兴(興)趣。
    - Wǒmen cānguānle gǔdài de zhībù jìshù, duì sīchóu zhìzuò chǎnshēngle xìngqù.
    - We visited ancient weaving techniques and developed an interest in silk production.
### 三弦 → 弦 → 玄
- **definition:** dark/mysterious
- **pinyin:** xuán
- **components:**
  - 幺
    - yāo
    - tiny threads
  - 亠
    - tóu
    - top mark
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 幺 (yāo, "tiny threads") + 亠 (tóu, "top mark") =
    - two interwoven threads with a top mark representing a rope ->
    - dark/mysterious
  - **interpretation:** The character combines tiny threads 幺 with a distinguishing top mark 亠, symbolizing complexity or intricacy akin to a rope. This imagery connects to its meaning of something dark or mysterious, possibly alluding to complexity not easily understood.
- **examples:**
  - 夜幕降临时(臨時)，大地笼(籠)罩着(著)玄秘的色彩。
    - Yèmù jiànglín shí, dàdì lǒngzhào zhe xuánmì de sècǎi.
    - When night falls, the earth is enveloped by mysterious, dark hues.
  - 这(這)幅山水画(畫)充满(滿)玄妙意境。
    - Zhè fú shānshuǐhuà chōngmǎn xuánmiào yìjìng.
    - This landscape painting is full of mystical allure.
  - 他的故事令人感到玄虚(虛)莫测(測)。
    - Tā de gùshì lìngrén gǎndào xuánxū mòcè.
    - His story gives an impression of enigmatic inscrutability.
### 三弦 → 弦 → 玄 → 幺
- **definition:** tiny threads
- **pinyin:** yāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a thin silk thread
  - **interpretation:** The character 幺 (yāo, "tiny threads") illustrates a thin silk thread, reflecting its meaning directly through its shape. It resembles a representation of something small and delicate, like the strands of silk used in weaving.
- **examples:**
  - 这个(這個)微小的幺在织(織)物中闪(閃)耀。
    - Zhège wéixiǎo de yāo zài zhīwù zhōng shǎnyào.
    - The tiny thread sparkles within the fabric.
  - 织(織)布工巧妙地利用幺，将(將)其编织进(編織進)精美的锦缎(錦緞)中。
    - Zhībùgōng qiǎomiào de lìyòng yāo, jiāng qí biānzhī jìn jīngměi de jǐnduàn zhōng.
    - The weaver cleverly employs the tiny thread, integrating it into exquisite brocade.
### 三弦 → 弦 → 玄 → 亠
- **definition:** top mark
- **pinyin:** tóu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Couvercle with a handle ->
    - false friend: roof of a tower in 高 (gāo, "high") ->
    - head and open arms in 立 (lì, "stand")/交 (jiāo, "exchange") ->
    - roof of a hut in 六 (liù, "six") ->
    - top of traditional cloth in 衣 (yī, "clothing") ->
    - mark over silk threads in 玄 (xuán, "mysterious") ->
    - top of knife/chisel to mark prisoners in 辛 (xīn, "bitter") ->
    - grass in 离 (lí, "leave")
  - **interpretation:** 亠 is primarily a pictogram that initially represented a cover with a handle. However, its usage varies in different characters, symbolizing roofs, heads, tops of clothing, and marks, showing its flexibility and evolution in form and meaning across contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 在汉(漢)字结构(結構)中，亠作为顶标发挥(為頂標發揮)作用。
    - Zài hànzì jiégòu zhōng, tóu zuòwéi dǐngbiāo fāhuī zuòyòng.
    - In the structure of Chinese characters, 亠 acts as a top mark to exert its function.
  - 古代书(書)法作品中，亠显(顯)示出独(獨)特的审(審)美价(價)值。
    - Gǔdài shūfǎ zuòpǐn zhōng, tóu xiǎnshì chū dútè de shěnměi jiàzhí.
    - In ancient calligraphy, 亠 demonstrates a unique aesthetic value.
