## beetroot
### hóngcàitóu
---
- **definition:** beetroot
- **pinyin:** hóngcàitóu
- **components:**
  - 红(紅)
    - hóng
    - red; popular; bonus; revolutionary
  - 菜头(菜頭)
    - càitóu
    - radish; turnip; vegetable root; daikon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 红 + 菜头 = red ‘radish/turnip’ → beetroot.
  - **interpretation:** Literally “red radish/turnip,” naming the beet by its color and root form. Common in Cantonese-influenced regions (e.g., Hong Kong); Mainland Mandarin more often says 甜菜根.
  - **simplification:** Standard character simplification in the PRC: 紅→红 and 頭→头; meaning unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 这家店卖新鲜的红菜头(這家店賣新鮮的紅菜頭)。

  - 我买了两个红菜头(我買了兩個紅菜頭)，准备做沙拉(準備做沙拉)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le liǎng gè hóngcàitóu, zhǔnbèi zuò shālā.
    - I bought two beetroots to make a salad.
  - 在香港的超市(在香港的超市)，常能看到标着红菜头的蔬菜(常能看到標著紅菜頭的蔬菜)。
    - Zài Xiānggǎng de chāoshì, cháng néng kàn dào biāo zhe hóngcàitóu de shūcài.
    - In Hong Kong supermarkets, you often see vegetables labeled “beetroot.”

### 红菜头(紅菜頭) → 菜头(菜頭)
- **definition:** radish; turnip; vegetable root; daikon
- **pinyin:** càitóu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 菜 ‘vegetable’ + 头 ‘head/root end’ = the “head” of a vegetable → radish/daikon.
  - **interpretation:** Literally points to the swollen root or “head” of certain vegetables, hence radish/daikon. Common in Southern Chinese varieties; contrasts with standard Mandarin 萝卜 for radish.
  - **simplification:** 頭 → 头 by standard character simplification, reducing the complex 頁 component and aligning with the productive suffix -头.
- **examples:**
  - 这家店的腌菜头很好吃(這家店的醃菜頭很好吃)。

    - Luóbo gāo zài Táiwān yě jiào càitóu guǒ.
