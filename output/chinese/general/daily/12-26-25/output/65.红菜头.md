## beetroot
### hóngcàitóu
---
- **definition:** beetroot
- **pinyin:** hóngcàitóu
- **components:**
  - 红(紅)
    - hóng
    - red; popular; bonus; revolutionary
  - 菜头(菜頭)
    - càitóu
    - radish; turnip; vegetable root; daikon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 红 + 菜头 = red ‘radish/turnip’ → beetroot.
  - **interpretation:** Literally “red radish/turnip,” naming the beet by its color and root form. Common in Cantonese-influenced regions (e.g., Hong Kong); Mainland Mandarin more often says 甜菜根.
  - **simplification:** Standard character simplification in the PRC: 紅→红 and 頭→头; meaning unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 这家店卖新鲜的红菜头(這家店賣新鮮的紅菜頭)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn mài xīnxiān de hóngcàitóu.
    - This shop sells fresh beetroots.
  - 我买了两个红菜头(我買了兩個紅菜頭)，准备做沙拉(準備做沙拉)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le liǎng gè hóngcàitóu, zhǔnbèi zuò shālā.
    - I bought two beetroots to make a salad.
  - 在香港的超市(在香港的超市)，常能看到标着红菜头的蔬菜(常能看到標著紅菜頭的蔬菜)。
    - Zài Xiānggǎng de chāoshì, cháng néng kàn dào biāo zhe hóngcàitóu de shūcài.
    - In Hong Kong supermarkets, you often see vegetables labeled “beetroot.”
### 红菜头(紅菜頭) → 红(紅)
- **definition:** red; popular; bonus; revolutionary
- **pinyin:** hóng
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 糸 (silk) + phonetic: 工
  - **interpretation:** Combines the silk component 糸 with 工 as the phonetic, pointing to a reading similar to gōng in Old Chinese and associating the meaning with textiles. The color sense likely came from the hue of dyed silk, later extending to figurative uses like popularity and revolutionary connotations.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 紅 by reducing 糹 to 纟 to lower stroke count and standardize the silk radical in simplified characters.
- **examples:**
  - 她脸红了(她臉紅了)。
    - Tā liǎn hóng le.
    - Her face turned red.
  - 这部电影很红(這部電影很紅)。
    - Zhè bù diànyǐng hěn hóng.
    - This movie is very popular.
  - 他在网上走红(他在網上走紅)。
    - Tā zài wǎngshàng zǒu hóng.
    - He went viral online.
### 红菜头(紅菜頭) → 红(紅) → 糸
- **definition:** silk
- **pinyin:** mì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** a skein of silk.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bundle of silk threads tied in the middle, with strands hanging above and below. The stylized sketch highlights twisted filaments, which later served as the standard radical for thread- and textile-related characters.
  - **simplification:** In Simplified Chinese the silk radical was streamlined to 纟 to reduce strokes and speed writing; 糸 is rarely used independently, but 纟 replaces it as a left-side component.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan sīchóu.
    - I like silk fabrics.
  - 这件衣服是用蚕丝做的(這件衣服是用蠶絲做的)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfu shì yòng cánsī zuò de.
    - This piece of clothing is made from silkworm silk.
  - 丝线很细(絲線很細)。
    - Sīxiàn hěn xì.
    - Silk thread is very thin.
### 红菜头(紅菜頭) → 红(紅) → 工
- **definition:** work
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a bladed tool to plow and mark a border; alternatively a carpenter’s square (cf. original meaning of 巨).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a tool used to prepare the ground and set boundaries, naturally extending to the idea of labor and work. Some traditions instead see a carpenter’s square, connecting it with making and constructing, and with early senses linked to 巨.
- **examples:**
  - 我在工厂工作(我在工廠工作)。
    - Wǒ zài gōngchǎng gōngzuò.
    - I work in a factory.
  - 这些工人正在劳动(這些工人正在勞動)。
    - Zhèxiē gōngrén zhèngzài láodòng.
    - These workers are laboring now.
  - 他的工钱很高(他的工錢很高)。
    - Tā de gōngqián hěn gāo.
    - His wages are high.
### 红菜头(紅菜頭) → 菜头(菜頭)
- **definition:** radish; turnip; vegetable root; daikon
- **pinyin:** càitóu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 菜 ‘vegetable’ + 头 ‘head/root end’ = the “head” of a vegetable → radish/daikon.
  - **interpretation:** Literally points to the swollen root or “head” of certain vegetables, hence radish/daikon. Common in Southern Chinese varieties; contrasts with standard Mandarin 萝卜 for radish.
  - **simplification:** 頭 → 头 by standard character simplification, reducing the complex 頁 component and aligning with the productive suffix -头.
- **examples:**
  - 这家店的腌菜头很好吃(這家店的醃菜頭很好吃)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn de yān càitóu hěn hǎochī.
    - The pickled radish at this shop tastes great.
  - 冬天常吃白色的菜头炖排骨(冬天常吃白色的菜頭燉排骨)。
    - Dōngtiān cháng chī báisè de càitóu dùn páigǔ.
    - In winter people often eat white radish stewed with pork ribs.
  - 萝卜糕在台湾也叫菜头粿(蘿蔔糕在臺灣也叫菜頭粿)。
    - Luóbo gāo zài Táiwān yě jiào càitóu guǒ.
    - Turnip cake is also called 'caitou kueh' in Taiwan.
