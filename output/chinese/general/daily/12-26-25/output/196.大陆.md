## mainland
### dàlù
---
- **definition:** mainland
- **pinyin:** dàlù
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big; large
  - 陆(陸)
    - lù
    - land; continent
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 大(大) (dà, "big; large") and 陆(陸) (lù, "land; continent") combine to form 大陆(大陸) (dàlù), meaning "mainland"
A big landmass, as opposed to islands or smaller regions, is the mainland.
  - **interpretation:** A large landmass is distinguished as the main or principal land, thus referred to as the mainland.
- **examples:**
  - 我从大陆回来(我從大陸回來)。
    - Wǒ cóng dàlù huílái.
    - I came back from the mainland.
  - 大陆经济迅速发展(大陸經濟迅速發展)，吸引了众多投资者(吸引了眾多投資者)。
    - Dàlù jīngjì xùnsù fāzhǎn, xīyǐnle zhòngduō tóuzīzhě.
    - The mainland's economy is rapidly developing, attracting many investors.
### 大陆(大陸) → 大(大)
- **definition:** big; large
- **pinyin:** dà
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts 人(人) (rén, "person") ->
    - borrowed for 大(大) (dà, "big")
  - **interpretation:** The character 大(大) (dà, "big") originally depicted a person standing, which later evolved to express the meaning 'big.' The resemblance to a person was used phonetically to convey largeness, not necessarily to portray outstretched arms as sometimes explained.
