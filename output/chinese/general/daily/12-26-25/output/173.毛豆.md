## edamame
### máodòu
---
- **definition:** edamame
- **pinyin:** máodòu
- **components:**
  - 毛
    - máo
    - hairy; fur; feather
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean; pea; pea-like plant
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 毛(毛) (máo, "hairy") refers to the small hairs or fuzz found on the pods, and 豆(豆) (dòu, "bean") refers to the bean itself, which is inside the pod.
  - **interpretation:** The term 毛豆(毛豆) (máodòu) literally means "hairy bean," which describes the edamame, a young soy bean typically still in the pod. The pods are covered in fine hairs, which is why the descriptor 毛(毛) (máo, "hairy") is used. Edamame are popular as a snack or in dishes in East Asian cuisine.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃毛豆(我喜歡吃毛豆)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī máodòu.
    - I like eating edamame.
  - 天热时(天熱時)，一盘新鲜毛豆成为美味佳肴(一盤新鮮毛豆成為美味佳餚)。
    - Tiān rè shí, yī pán xīnxiān máodòu chéngwéi měiwèi jiāyáo.
    - On hot days, a plate of fresh edamame becomes a delicious treat.
  - 毛豆不仅美味(毛豆不僅美味)，而且富有营养(而且富有營養)。
    - Máodòu bù jǐn měiwèi, érqiě fùyǒu yíngyǎng.
    - Edamame is not only delicious, but also nutritious.
