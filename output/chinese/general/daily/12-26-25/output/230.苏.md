## surname Su; also shorthand for Jiangsu
### Sū
---
- **definition:** surname Su; also shorthand for Jiangsu
- **pinyin:** Sū
- **components:**
  - 办(辦)
    - bàn
    - to handle; to do; manage
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 艸 ‘grass; plant’ + phonetic: 穌.
  - **interpretation:** The grass radical indicates a connection to plants or herbs, while 穌 provides the sound and historically conveys senses like “revive.” In the simplified form, the phonetic component was reshaped to 办 based on a cursive form of 穌, preserving pronunciation while reducing strokes.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 蘇 by replacing the phonetic 穌 with 办 via a cursive-derived abbreviation, retaining 艹 and reducing stroke count.
- **examples:**
  - 他姓苏(他姓蘇)。
    - Tā xìng Sū.
    - His surname is Su.
  - 江苏简称“苏”(江蘇簡稱「蘇」)。
    - Jiāngsū jiǎnchēng “Sū”.
    - Jiangsu’s abbreviation is “Su”.
  - 我们去苏州旅行(我們去蘇州旅行)。
    - Wǒmen qù Sūzhōu lǚxíng.
    - We are going to travel to Suzhou.
### 苏(蘇) → 办(辦)
- **definition:** to handle; to do; manage
- **pinyin:** bàn
- **components:**
  - 力
    - lì
    - strength; power
  - 辛
    - xīn
    - bitter; the eighth Heavenly Stem
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 力 (“strength”) + phonetic: 辡.
  - **interpretation:** Meaning comes from 力, suggesting exerting strength to handle or manage affairs. Pronunciation is signaled by 辡 (the doubled 辛 phonetic), which points to the bàn reading; in the simplified form, those two 辛 were reduced to brief strokes above 力, preserving the idea while streamlining the shape.
  - **simplification:** Adopted in 1956 from a cursive form of 辦, reducing the two 辛 to a left-falling stroke 丿 and a dot 丶 above 力.
- **examples:**
  - 这件事交给我来办(這件事交給我來辦)。
    - Zhè jiàn shì jiāo gěi wǒ lái bàn.
    - Leave this matter to me to handle.
  - 明天去办签证(明天去辦簽證)，记得带照片(記得帶照片)。
    - Míngtiān qù bàn qiānzhèng, jìde dài zhàopiàn.
    - Go apply for the visa tomorrow; remember to bring photos.
  - 出了问题(出了問題)，我们该怎么办(我們該怎麼辦)？
    - Chūle wèntí, wǒmen gāi zěnme bàn?
    - If something goes wrong, what should we do?
### 苏(蘇) → 办(辦) → 力
- **definition:** strength; power
- **pinyin:** lì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): interpreted as a bent arm extending from the body, or as a wooden plough; original form 𠠲.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an arm applying force, a concrete image that came to represent strength and effort. Some traditions also saw it as a plough, linking power with labor in the fields.
- **examples:**
  - 他力气很大(他力氣很大)。
    - Tā lìqi hěn dà.
    - He is very strong.
  - 我们要尽力完成任务(我們要盡力完成任務)。
    - Wǒmen yào jìn lì wánchéng rènwù.
    - We must do our best to finish the task.
  - 科技给人类带来巨大力量(科技給人類帶來巨大力量)。
    - Kējì gěi rénlèi dàilái jùdà lìliàng.
    - Technology brings great power to humanity.
### 苏(蘇) → 办(辦) → 辛
- **definition:** bitter; the eighth Heavenly Stem
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a tool (chisel/awl/spike) used to mark slaves and criminals; variants include 䇂 and 亲.
  - **interpretation:** An image of a sharp branding or engraving tool suggests cutting and marking, which later extended semantically to harsh, pungent qualities and to associated derivatives. It also serves as the name of the eighth Heavenly Stem in the traditional calendrical system.
- **examples:**
  - 这味药有点辛(這味藥有點辛)，不太适合孩子(不太適合孩子)。
    - Zhè wèi yào yǒudiǎn xīn, bú tài shìhé háizi.
    - This medicine tastes rather pungent/bitter and isn’t very suitable for children.
  - 你这几天工作很辛苦(你這幾天工作很辛苦)。
    - Nǐ zhè jǐ tiān gōngzuò hěn xīnkǔ.
    - You’ve been working very hard these last few days.
  - 今年的天干是辛(今年的天干是辛)，因此纪年写作辛丑(因此紀年寫作辛丑)。
    - Jīnnián de tiāngān shì xīn, yīncǐ jìnián xiězuò Xīnchǒu.
    - This year’s Heavenly Stem is Xin, so the year is written as Xinchou.
