## 扫码(掃碼)
---
## scan a code
### sǎomǎ
---
- **definition:** scan a code
- **pinyin:** sǎomǎ
- **components:**
  - 扫(掃)
    - sǎo
    - sweep; sweep away; scan; clean up
  - 码(碼)
    - mǎ
    - code; number; yard (unit); size
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 扫(掃) + 码(碼) = sweep/scan + code → scan a code.
  - **interpretation:** Refers to using a device (often a smartphone) to scan a QR code or barcode to get information or complete an action like payment. Common in digital check-ins, payments, and app logins.
  - **simplification:** Both characters are simplified: 掃 → 扫 (reduces the complex 帚 component) and 碼 → 码 (馬 → 马 under the 石 radical), lowering stroke count for faster writing.
- **examples:**
  - 请在门口扫码登记(請在門口掃碼登記)。
    - Qǐng zài ménkǒu sǎomǎ dēngjì.
    - Please scan the code at the entrance to register.
  - 我用手机扫码付款(我用手機掃碼付款)。
    - Wǒ yòng shǒujī sǎomǎ fùkuǎn.
    - I use my phone to scan the code to pay.
  - 扫码即可领取优惠券(掃碼即可領取優惠券)。
    - Sǎomǎ jí kě lǐngqǔ yōuhuìquàn.
    - Scan the code to receive a coupon.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 扫(掃)
- **definition:** sweep; sweep away; scan; clean up
- **pinyin:** sǎo
- **components:**
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
  - 帚
    - zhǒu
    - broom
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (会意)
  - **description:** semantic: 手 ‘hand’ + 帚 ‘broom’.
  - **interpretation:** A hand holding a broom conveys the action of sweeping. The meaning derives purely from combining two meaningful parts, which later extended to senses like scanning or brushing over.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 掃 by removing the 冖 and 巾 elements within 帚 to reduce strokes, paralleling patterns like 妇 from 婦.
- **examples:**
  - 我在客厅扫地(我在客廳掃地)。
    - Wǒ zài kètīng sǎo dì.
    - I sweep the floor in the living room.
  - 清洁工每天打扫办公室(清潔工每天打掃辦公室)。
    - Qīngjié gōng měitiān dǎsǎo bàngōngshì.
    - The cleaner tidies up the office every day.
  - 用手机扫描二维码(用手機掃描二維碼)。
    - Yòng shǒujī sǎomiáo èrwéimǎ.
    - Use a phone to scan the QR code.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 扫(掃) → 手
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: hand and fingers; five digits, with top stroke as bent middle finger and horizontal strokes as fingers.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a human hand: the top stroke stands for a bent middle finger, and the horizontal strokes stand for the remaining fingers. In contrast to related claw forms that show three digits, it consistently presents a five‑finger human hand.
- **examples:**
  - 我手上有伤口(我手上有傷口)。
    - Wǒ shǒu shàng yǒu shāngkǒu.
    - I have a wound on my hand.
  - 请举起右手(請舉起右手)。
    - Qǐng jǔ qǐ yòu shǒu.
    - Please raise your right hand.
  - 他动手做饭(他動手做飯)。
    - Tā dòngshǒu zuò fàn.
    - He started cooking by hand.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 扫(掃) → 帚
- **definition:** broom
- **pinyin:** zhǒu
- **components:**
  - 聿
    - yù
    - brush; to write
  - 巾
    - jīn
    - cloth; towel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a broom with bristles on top and a tied handle below; modern form resembles roughly 肀 + 冖 + 巾 (top like 聿/肀/彐).
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawn image of a broom—bristles above and a bound handle below. Later forms made the top look like components such as 聿/肀/彐 over a cloth-like 巾, but these are visual resemblances rather than functional phonetic or semantic parts.
- **examples:**
  - 她拿起扫帚(她拿起掃帚)，开始打扫院子(開始打掃院子)。
    - Tā ná qǐ sào zhǒu, kāi shǐ dǎ sǎo yuàn zi.
    - She picked up the broom and began to clean the yard.
  - 他用扫帚把地上的落叶扫成一堆(他用掃帚把地上的落葉掃成一堆)。
    - Tā yòng sào zhǒu bǎ dì shàng de luò yè sǎo chéng yī duī.
    - He used a broom to sweep the fallen leaves on the ground into a pile.
  - 请把扫帚放回墙角(請把掃帚放回牆角)。
    - Qǐng bǎ sào zhǒu fàng huí qiáng jiǎo.
    - Please put the broom back in the corner.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 扫(掃) → 帚 → 聿
- **definition:** brush; to write
- **pinyin:** yù
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - again; right hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram or ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** hand (又) holding a brush (𰀁); originally referred to 筆 ‘writing brush’; later jiajie (rebus) for 聿 ‘thereupon’.
  - **interpretation:** A right hand gripping a writing brush vividly depicts the act of writing. Its graph first named the brush itself and was later borrowed to represent the adverbial sense “thereupon/then” in classical usage.
- **examples:**
  - 古人以聿书竹帛(古人以聿書竹帛)。
    - Gǔrén yǐ yù shū zhúbó.
    - The ancients used a brush to write on bamboo and silk.
  - 他执聿临池练字(他執聿臨池練字)。
    - Tā zhí yù línchí liànzì.
    - He holds the brush by the water to practice calligraphy.
  - 史官持聿记事(史官持聿記事)。
    - Shǐguān chí yù jìshì.
    - The scribe holds a brush to record events.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 扫(掃) → 帚 → 聿 → 又
- **definition:** again; right hand
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 扫(掃) → 帚 → 巾
- **definition:** cloth; towel
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – hanging piece of cloth.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a cloth draped and hanging, with the long stroke as the edge and the shorter strokes suggesting the folds. The image conveys a towel or piece of fabric suspended from a line or rod.
- **examples:**
  - 我用湿毛巾擦脸(我用濕毛巾擦臉)。
    - Wǒ yòng shī máojīn cā liǎn.
    - I use a wet towel to wipe my face.
  - 她戴着红色的头巾(她戴著紅色的頭巾)。
    - Tā dài zhe hóngsè de tóujīn.
    - She is wearing a red headscarf.
  - 冬天出门要围上围巾(冬天出門要圍上圍巾)。
    - Dōngtiān chūmén yào wéi shàng wéijīn.
    - In winter, put on a scarf before going out.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 码(碼)
- **definition:** code; number; yard (unit); size
- **pinyin:** mǎ
- **components:**
  - 石
    - shí
    - stone
  - 马(馬)
    - mǎ
    - horse
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 石 (stone; rock) + phonetic: 馬 (OC *mraːʔ).
  - **interpretation:** Combines the stone semantic element with the phonetic 馬 to convey the reading mǎ. The stone component suggests concreteness or stacking (as in piling stones), while later senses like “number/code” and the unit “yard” developed through phonetic extension.
  - **simplification:** Replaced the phonetic component 馬 with its simplified form 马 while retaining 石; a right-side phonetic simplification.
- **examples:**
  - 请把代码发给我(請把代碼發給我)。
    - Qǐng bǎ dàimǎ fā gěi wǒ.
    - Please send me the code.
  - 一码等于三英尺(一碼等於三英尺)。
    - Yī mǎ děngyú sān yīngchǐ.
    - One yard equals three feet.
  - 把箱子码整齐(把箱子碼整齊)。
    - Bǎ xiāngzi mǎ zhěngqí.
    - Stack the boxes neatly.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 码(碼) → 石
- **definition:** stone
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a stone beneath a cliff (厂); the cliff later became 丆. Sometimes interpreted as a cave set into a cliff.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a rock under an overhanging cliff, directly conveying the idea of stone. Over time the cliff component 厂 was stylized into 丆; some traditions read the scene as a cave in a cliff, still pointing to rock and earth.
- **examples:**
  - 他捡起一块石头(他撿起一塊石頭)。
    - Tā jiǎnqǐ yí kuài shítou.
    - He picked up a stone.
  - 这些矿石含有丰富的铁(這些礦石含有豐富的鐵)。
    - Zhèxiē kuàngshí hányǒu fēngfù de tiě.
    - These ores contain abundant iron.
  - 他姓石(他姓石)。
    - Tā xìng Shí.
    - His surname is Shi.
### 扫码(掃碼) → 码(碼) → 马(馬)
- **definition:** horse
- **pinyin:** mǎ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a horse with head facing left and flowing mane; in bronze, the head simplified to 目; legs evolved into four dots 灬 (unrelated to 火). Simplified 马 from 馬 via cursive script.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a sketch of a horse that gradually stylized its head and legs into simple strokes. Modern simplification to 马 follows a cursive handwriting form, compressing the body and abstracting the legs into a single sweeping shape.
  - **simplification:** Reduced from 馬 based on cursive (草书) form to speed writing, cutting strokes from about 10 to 3 and standardized in the PRC.
- **examples:**
  - 那匹马跑得很快(那匹馬跑得很快)。
    - Nà pǐ mǎ pǎo de hěn kuài.
    - That horse runs very fast.
  - 他骑马去学校(他騎馬去學校)。
    - Tā qí mǎ qù xuéxiào.
    - He rides a horse to school.
  - 马上开始比赛(馬上開始比賽)。
    - Mǎshàng kāishǐ bǐsài.
    - The competition will start right away.
