## Chinese broccoli
### gàilán
---
- **traditional:** 芥藍
- **definition:** Chinese broccoli
- **pinyin:** gàilán
- **components:**
  - 芥
    - gài
    - mustard; herb
  - 蓝(藍)
    - lán
    - blue; indigo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 芥 (gài, "mustard; herb") + 蓝(藍) (lán, "blue; indigo") =
    - a type of vegetable resembling mustard, often with associated blue-green leaves, thus known as Chinese broccoli.
  - **interpretation:** 芥 (gài, "mustard; herb") and 蓝(藍) (lán, "blue; indigo") combine to describe a leafy green vegetable, commonly known as Chinese broccoli. The term likely refers to the vegetable's mustard-like qualities and its typical blue-green leaf color.
  - **simplification:** 芥 was simplified to 芥 and 藍 was simplified to 蓝.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃芥蓝(藍)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī gàilán.
    - I like eating Chinese broccoli.
  - 这道菜里有芥蓝(藍)，味道非常鲜(鮮)美。
    - Zhè dào cài lǐ yǒu gàilán, wèidào fēicháng xiānměi.
    - This dish contains Chinese broccoli, and its flavor is very fresh and delicious.
### 芥( )蓝(藍) → 芥
- **definition:** mustard; herb
- **pinyin:** gài
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass, plant
  - 介
    - jiè
    - to derive an idea from
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艸 (cǎo, 'grass, plant') + phonetic 介 (jiè, 'to derive an idea from') =
    - plant with specific qualities ->
    - mustard; herb
  - **interpretation:** The character 芥(gài) is composed of 艸, signifying its association with plants or herbs, combined with 介, which gives the sound and contributes to the idea of it being a specific kind of plant, namely mustard.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)道菜用了芥。
    - Zhè dào cài yòng le gài.
    - This dish used mustard.
  - 当(當)芥遇上蜂蜜时(時)，风(風)味变(變)得独(獨)特。
    - Dāng gài yù shàng fēngmì shí, fēngwèi biàn de dú tè.
    - When mustard meets honey, the flavor becomes unique.
  - 菜市场里卖(場裡賣)芥，新鲜(鮮)又香醇。
    - Cài shì chǎng lǐ mài gài, xīnxiān yòu xiāngchún.
    - At the market, they sell mustard that is fresh and aromatic.
### 芥( )蓝(藍) → 芥 → 艸
- **definition:** grass, plant
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè
    - grass
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts grass blades ->
    - duplication of 屮 (chè, 'grass') ->
    - grass imagery
  - **interpretation:** The character 艸(cǎo, 'grass') is a pictorial representation of grass blades. It is essentially a duplication of the character 屮 (chè, 'grass'), visually suggesting the appearance of grass tufts through repetition, emphasizing the concept of grass or vegetation.
- **examples:**

  - 秋天时(時)艸变黄(變黃)，冬天它又枯萎。
    - Qiūtiān shí cǎo biàn huáng, dōngtiān tā yòu kūwěi.
    - In autumn, the grass turns yellow, and in winter, it withers.

### 芥( )蓝(藍) → 蓝(藍)
- **traditional:** 藍
- **definition:** blue; indigo
- **pinyin:** lán
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 监(監)
    - jiān
    - supervise
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 艹 (cǎo, "grass") + 監(监) (jiān, "supervise") =
    - phonetic 监 with 草 radical for plants ->
    - indigo plant, blue color
  - **interpretation:** The character 蓝(藍) (lán, "blue") uses 艹 (cǎo, "grass") to signify its connection to plants, combined with 監(监) (jiān, "supervise") for phonetic purposes. It historically referred to the indigo dye derived from plants, which corresponds to its usage in denoting the color blue.
  - **simplification:** 蓝(藍) was simplified by altering the phonetic component 監(监) with its simpler variant 监, keeping the meaning intact.
- **examples:**
  - 天空是蓝(藍)的。
    - Tiānkōng shì lán de.
    - The sky is blue.
  - 海洋很蓝(藍)，让(讓)人心旷(曠)神怡。
    - Hǎiyáng hěn lán, ràng rén xīnkuàng shényí.
    - The ocean is blue, making people feel relaxed.
  - 她穿着蓝(著藍)裙子，看起来(來)十分迷人。
    - Tā chuānzhe lán qúnzi, kàn qǐlái shífēn mírén.
    - She wears a blue dress, looking very charming.

### 芥( )蓝(藍) → 蓝(藍) → 监(監) → 㕣 → 八

  - **description:**

    - Wǒ zài bā diǎn, qǐchuáng kāishǐ yītiān de shēnghuó.
