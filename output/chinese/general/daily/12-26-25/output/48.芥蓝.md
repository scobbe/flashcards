### Chinese broccoli
---
- **pinyin:** Chinese broccoli
- **components:**
  - 芥
    - jiè
    - mustard; mustard plant; to urge
  - 蓝(藍)
    - lán
    - blue; indigo; azure
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 芥(芥) (jiè, "mustard") + 蓝(藍) (lán, "blue; indigo") — mustard blue
  - **interpretation:** The term combines the idea of the mustard plant with the color blue, possibly referencing the specific variety of broccoli. This connection describes the vegetable known for its unique taste and appearance, distinguishing it from common broccoli.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃芥蓝(我喜歡吃芥藍)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī jièlán.
    - I like to eat Chinese broccoli.
  - 很多人认为芥蓝(很多人認為芥藍)比普通西兰花(比普通西蘭花)更美味。
    - Hěn duō rén rènwéi jièlán bǐ pǔtōng xīlánhuā gèng měiwèi.
    - Many people think Chinese broccoli is tastier than regular broccoli.
### 芥蓝(芥藍) → 芥(芥)
- **definition:** mustard; mustard plant; to urge
- **pinyin:** jiè
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 介
    - jiè
    - to introduce; between
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Consists of the semantic component 艸 (grass, plant) and the phonetic component 介, indicating a type of plant.
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a plant (grass) and is phonetically linked to another component, indicating its use in naming a type of plant. This connection makes sense as mustard is a type of plant categorized under grass.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃芥末(我喜歡吃芥末)。
    - wǒ xǐhuān chī jièmò.
    - I like to eat mustard.
  - 他在菜里放了芥菜(他在菜裡放了芥菜)。
    - tā zài cài lǐ fàng le jiècài.
    - He added mustard greens to the dish.
  - 芥是用来调味的(芥是用來調味的)。
    - jiè shì yòng lái tiáowèi de.
    - Mustard is used for seasoning.

- **definition:** grass
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts grass with leaves and stems.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents clusters of grass, emphasizing its natural, organic form in a simplistic design.
- **examples:**

    - Xiān huā hé cǎo yī qǐ shēng zhǎng.

    - Xiǎo yáng zài cǎo dì shàng chī cǎo.
    - The lamb eats grass in the meadow.
### 芥蓝(芥藍) → 芥(芥) → 介(介)
- **definition:** to introduce; between
- **pinyin:** jiè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Originally depicted a person standing between two objects, symbolizing the concept of mediation or introduction.
  - **interpretation:** The character intuitively suggests a position of someone facilitating a connection or introduction between two parties.
- **examples:**
  - 我想介入这个话题(我想介入這個話題)。
    - wǒ xiǎng jièrù zhège huàtí.
    - I want to introduce myself on this topic.
  - 他介于两者之间(他介於兩者之間)。
    - tā jièyú liǎng zhě zhījiān.
    - He is between the two.
### 芥蓝(芥藍) → 蓝(藍)
- **definition:** blue; indigo; azure
- **pinyin:** lán
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 監
    - jiān
    - to supervise
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Combines the semantic component 艸 (grass) to suggest a connection to plants or natural colors with the phonetic component 監 (to denote pronunciation).
  - **interpretation:** This character evokes images of blue-green grass, linking the color to natural elements. Its formulation captures both the essence of natural colors associated with plant life and the sound of the word.
  - **simplification:** 藍(蓝) follows stroke reduction rules, simplifying the艸 component to its more abstract form, while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 天空是蓝的(天空是藍的)。
    - Tiānkōng shì lán de.
    - The sky is blue.
  - 我喜欢蓝色(我喜歡藍色)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān lán sè.
    - I like the color blue.
