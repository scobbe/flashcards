## engineer
### gōngchéngshī
---
- **definition:** engineer
- **pinyin:** gōngchéngshī
- **components:**
  - 工程
    - gōngchéng
    - engineering; project; construction; undertaking
  - 师(師)
    - shī
    - master; specialist; teacher; professional
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 工程 (engineering; project) + 师 (master; specialist) = engineer.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the field ‘engineering/project’ with the professional suffix 师 to indicate a trained specialist in engineering. Follows the productive pattern of profession nouns like 医师 and 会计师.
  - **simplification:** Only 師 → 师 was simplified in the PRC to reduce strokes and regularize the component shape; 工程 is unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 她是土木工程师(她是土木工程師)，负责设计桥梁(負責設計橋梁)。
    - Tā shì tǔmù gōngchéngshī, fùzé shèjì qiáoliáng.
    - She is a civil engineer and is responsible for designing bridges.
  - 他是软件工程师(他是軟件工程師)，编写高性能程序(編寫高性能程序)。
    - Tā shì ruǎnjiàn gōngchéngshī, biānxiě gāo xìngnéng chéngxù.
    - He is a software engineer who writes high‑performance programs.
  - 在公司里(在公司裡)，工程师们与团队合作(工程師們與團隊合作)，解决复杂的技术问题(解決複雜的技術問題)。
    - Zài gōngsī lǐ, gōngchéngshīmen yǔ tuánduì hézuò, jiějué fùzá de jìshù wèntí.
    - At the company, engineers work with the team to solve complex technical problems.
### 工程师(工程師) → 工程
- **definition:** engineering; project; construction; undertaking
- **pinyin:** gōngchéng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 工(work/craft) + 程(course/procedure) = engineering; project/construction
  - **interpretation:** Combines the notion of work/craft with a course or procedure, emphasizing organized, methodical work. Extended to mean the discipline of engineering as well as concrete projects and construction works.
- **examples:**
  - 他学工程(他學工程)。
    - Tā xué gōngchéng.
    - He studies engineering.
  - 这个工程已经开工(這個工程已經開工)。
    - Zhège gōngchéng yǐjīng kāigōng.
    - This project has already begun construction.
  - 工程进展顺利(工程進展順利)。
    - Gōngchéng jìnzhǎn shùnlì.
    - The construction is progressing smoothly.
### 工程师(工程師) → 师(師)
- **definition:** master; specialist; teacher; professional
- **pinyin:** shī
- **components:**
  - 帀
    - zā
    - to go around; to surround
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (会意)
  - **description:** 𠂤 (“mound, hill”) + 帀 (“to surround”); simplified by replacing 𠂤 with 刂.
  - **interpretation:** A hill combined with the idea of encircling evokes troops camped around a rise, yielding the sense of an army and, by extension, a master/teacher who leads or trains. The simplified form keeps the encampment idea while swapping the hill for the knife component, leaving the core meaning intact.
  - **simplification:** 𠂤 was replaced with 刂 to reduce strokes, paralleling the simplification 帅 ← 帥; a graphic streamlining without phonetic or semantic reanalysis.
- **examples:**
  - 她是我的中文老师(她是我的中文老師)。
    - Tā shì wǒ de Zhōngwén lǎoshī.
    - She is my Chinese teacher.
  - 我向师傅请教(我向師傅請教)。
    - Wǒ xiàng shīfu qǐngjiào.
    - I seek advice from the master.
  - 第三师明天换防(第三師明天換防)。
    - Dì-sān shī míngtiān huànfáng.
    - The 3rd division will rotate duty tomorrow.
### 工程师(工程師) → 师(師) → 帀
- **definition:** to go around; to surround
- **pinyin:** zā
- **components:**
  - 巾
    - jīn
    - towel; cloth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** uncertain
  - **description:** Uncertain; Shuowen: inverted 之 (originally 㞢); Zhang Shichao: “roots” 柢; oracle bones: 之 represents a footprint.
  - **interpretation:** Scholars propose several origins: an inversion of 之, an image of spreading roots, or a footprint mark. Each points to motion or extension around something, which aligns with the sense of circling or surrounding.
- **examples:**
  - 敌军帀城而围之(敵軍帀城而圍之)。
    - Díjūn zā chéng ér wéi zhī.
    - The enemy army went around the city and laid siege to it.
  - 商人帀市叫卖(商人帀市叫賣)。
    - Shāngrén zā shì jiàomài.
    - Merchants went around the market hawking their goods.
  - 我们沿湖帀行一圈(我們沿湖帀行一圈)。
    - Wǒmen yán hú zā xíng yī quān.
    - We went around the lake for one lap.
### 工程师(工程師) → 师(師) → 帀 → 巾
- **definition:** towel; cloth
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – hanging piece of cloth.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a cloth draped and hanging, with the long stroke as the edge and the shorter strokes suggesting the folds. The image conveys a towel or piece of fabric suspended from a line or rod.
- **examples:**
  - 我用湿毛巾擦脸(我用濕毛巾擦臉)。
    - Wǒ yòng shī máojīn cā liǎn.
    - I use a wet towel to wipe my face.
  - 她戴着红色的头巾(她戴著紅色的頭巾)。
    - Tā dài zhe hóngsè de tóujīn.
    - She is wearing a red headscarf.
  - 冬天出门要围上围巾(冬天出門要圍上圍巾)。
    - Dōngtiān chūmén yào wéi shàng wéijīn.
    - In winter, put on a scarf before going out.
### 工程师(工程師) → 师(師) → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — a knife; original form 𠚣, seen in a variant of 利 (𥝢) and atop 黎.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a knife with a distinct blade and handle. The graph was streamlined into the current two-stroke form and also serves as a component in characters like 利 and 黎.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿着一把刀(他拿著一把刀)。
    - Tā názhe yì bǎ dāo.
    - He is holding a knife.
  - 厨师用刀切蔬菜(廚師用刀切蔬菜)。
    - Chúshī yòng dāo qiē shūcài.
    - The chef uses a knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这把刀很锋利(這把刀很鋒利)。
    - Zhè bǎ dāo hěn fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
