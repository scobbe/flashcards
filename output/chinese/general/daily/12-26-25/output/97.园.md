## garden
### yuán
---
- **definition:** garden
- **pinyin:** yuán
- **components:**
  - 囗
    - wéi
    - enclosure
  - 袁
    - yuán
    - phonetic
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 囗(囗) (wéi, "enclosure") + 袁(袁) (yuán, phonetic) =
    - enclosed space ->
    - garden
  - **interpretation:** The character 园(園) (yuán, "garden") combines 囗(囗) (wéi, "enclosure"), indicating a surrounded area, with the phonetic element 袁(袁) (yuán), contributing to the pronunciation. This effectively conveys the idea of a garden being an enclosed space.
  - **simplification:** 園(園) simplified to 园 by omitting the top part of 袁(袁).
- **examples:**
  - 我常去园(我常去園)，寻找灵感(尋找靈感)。
    - Wǒ cháng qù yuán, xúnzhǎo línggǎn.
    - I often visit the garden to seek inspiration.
  - 春雨过后(春雨過後)，园中梅花绽放(園中梅花綻放)。
    - Chūn yǔ guòhòu, yuán zhōng méihuā zhànfàng.
    - After the spring rains, the plum blossoms bloom in the garden.
  - 宁静的园(寧靜的園)，承载着岁月(承載著歲月)。
    - Níngjìng de yuán, chéngzài zhe suìyuè.
    - The serene garden carries the passage of time.
### 园(園) → 囗(囗)
- **definition:** enclosure
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an enclosure or boundary
  - **interpretation:** 囗(囗) (wéi, "enclosure") is a pictogram representing a square or rectangular boundary, signifying the concept of enclosing.
- **examples:**
  - 动物园内的狮舍设有宽敞的囗(動物園內的獅舍設有寬敞的囗)。
    - Dòngwùyuán nèi de shīshè shè yǒu kuānchǎng de wéi。
    - The zoo's lion enclosure is spacious.
  - 工程师设计了新型的囗(工程師設計了新型的囗)，保护了实验设备(保護了實驗設備)。
    - Gōngchéngshī shèjìle xīnxíng de wéi, bǎohùle shíyàn shèbèi。
    - The engineer designed a new type of enclosure, which protected the experimental equipment.
  - 运动场周围设置了牢固的囗(運動場周圍設定了牢固的囗)。
    - Yùndòngchǎng zhōuwéi shèzhìle láogù de wéi。
    - A sturdy enclosure has been installed around the sports field.
### 园(園) → 袁(袁)
- **definition:** phonetic
- **pinyin:** yuán
- **components:**
  - 衣
    - yī
    - clothes
  - 又
    - yòu
    - hand
  - 〇
    - early form of 圓 (circle), now 口
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 衣(衣) (yī, "clothes") + 又(又) (yòu, "hand") ->
    - "to put on clothes" ->
    - early 圓 phonetic 〇 added ->
    - 㞢 in seal script ->
    - 土 in clerical script ->
    - final form 袁
  - **interpretation:** 袁(yuán) originates from the idea of "putting on clothes" with a hand, reflecting its initial meaning related to wearing. Over time, numerous changes in form and the addition of phonetically similar elements occurred before stabilizing as 袁.
- **examples:**
  - 袁大叔喜欢钓鱼(袁大叔喜歡釣魚)。
    - Yuán dàshū xǐhuān diàoyú.
    - Uncle Yuan likes fishing.
  - 同学们称他为袁老师(同學們稱他為袁老師)，因为他有幽默感(因為他有幽默感)。
    - Tóngxuémen chēng tā wéi Yuán lǎoshī, yīnwèi tā yǒu yōumògǎn.
    - The classmates call him Teacher Yuan because he has a sense of humor.
### 园(園) → 袁(袁) → 衣(衣)
- **definition:** clothes
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts the outline of the chest and upper clothing ->
    - clothes
  - **interpretation:** The character 衣(yī, 'clothes') visually represents the shape of upper body garments by outlining the chest area. Despite its simplicity, this form effectively conveys the concept of clothing.
- **examples:**
  - 天冷时(天冷時)，你应该多穿衣服(你應該多穿衣服)。
    - Tiān lěng shí, nǐ yīnggāi duō chuān yīfú.
    - When it's cold, you should wear more clothes.
  - 旧衣服需要修补(舊衣服需要修補)。
    - Jiù yīfú xūyào xiūbǔ.
    - Old clothes need mending.
### 园(園) → 袁(袁) → 又(又)
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a right hand
  - **interpretation:** The character 又(又) (yòu, "hand") is a pictorial representation of a right hand, capturing its outline and structure. It was originally used to denote 'right' and emphasizes the focus on manual dexterity or action typically associated with the dominant hand.
- **examples:**
  - 他伸出一只又细又灵活的手(他伸出一隻又細又靈活的手)。
    - Tā shēnchū yī zhī yòu xì yòu línghuó de shǒu.
    - He stretched out a hand that was both slender and agile.
  - 他的手又锋利又温柔(他的手又鋒利又溫柔)，在阳光下闪闪发光(在陽光下閃閃發光)。
    - Tā de shǒu yòu fēnglì yòu wēnróu, zài yángguāng xià shǎnshǎn fāguāng.
    - His hand, both sharp and gentle, shone brilliantly in the sunlight.
  - 她借着那只又巧又迅速的手(她借著那隻又巧又迅速的手)，轻松拿起了书本(輕鬆拿起了書本)。
    - Tā jièzhe nà zhī yòu qiǎo yòu xùnsù de shǒu, qīngsōng ná qǐle shūběn.
    - She easily picked up the book with that dexterous and quick hand.
### 园(園) → 袁(袁) → 〇(〇)
- **definition:** early form of 圓 (circle), now 口
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** placeholder for a missing Chinese character
  - **description:**
    - Blank or empty character ->
    - placeholder or digit ->
    - used for 'zero'
  - **interpretation:** 〇 was historically used as a placeholder or blank character, evolving to represent the 'zero' digit in numbers. Its usage as a zero digit became integral as the concept of zero gained prominence in Chinese numeration.
  - **simplification:** None, as 〇 is already simplified and serves a unique purpose in representing zero.
- **examples:**
  - 在古代文献中(在古代文獻中)，〇被用作表示圆形的符号(〇被用作表示圓形的符號)。
    - Zài gǔdài wénxiàn zhōng, yuán bèi yòngzuò biǎoshì yuánxíng de fúhào.
    - In ancient texts, 〇 was used as a symbol to represent a circle.
  - 古人的刻印上常见〇的形状(古人的刻印上常見〇的形狀)，象征完整与和谐(象徵完整與和諧)。
    - Gǔrén de kèyìn shàng chángjiàn yuán de xíngzhuàng, xiàngzhēng wánzhěng yǔ héxié.
    - On ancient seals, the form of 〇 was commonly seen, symbolizing wholeness and harmony.
