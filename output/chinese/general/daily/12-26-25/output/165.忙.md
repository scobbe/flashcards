## 
### busy
---
- **pinyin:** busy
- **components:**
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart
  - 亡
    - wáng
    - to lose
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** The character is composed of 心 (心) representing 'heart' and 亡 (亡) meaning 'to lose' or 'to perish'. Together, they convey the idea that being busy causes a person to be emotionally overwhelmed or to have little time for their heart.
  - **interpretation:** This makes intuitive sense because feeling busy often leaves one feeling emotionally distressed or neglected.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天很忙(我今天很忙)。
    - Wǒ jīntiān hěn máng.
    - I am very busy today.
  - 你为什么不来？我在忙工作(你為什麼不來？我在忙工作)。
    - Nǐ wèishéme bù lái? Wǒ zài máng gōngzuò.
    - Why didn’t you come? I was busy with work.
### 忙(忙) → 心(心)
- **definition:** heart
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a highly stylized heart.
  - **interpretation:** The shape of the character evokes the physical form of a heart, symbolizing emotions and feelings.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱我的心。(我愛我的心。)
    - Wǒ ài wǒ de xīn.
    - I love my heart.
  - 他的心情很好。(他的心情很好。)
    - Tā de xīnqíng hěn hǎo.
    - His mood is very good.
### 忙(忙) → 亡(亡)
- **definition:** to lose
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** A line on 刀 (dāo, 'knife') representing the cutting edge, which is related to the concept of loss or being lost.
  - **interpretation:** This character intuitively connects the idea of loss with the sharpness of a cutting edge, suggesting that something is severed or no longer intact.
- **examples:**
  - 我觉得我快亡国了(我覺得我快亡國了)。
    - Wǒ juédé wǒ kuài wángguóle.
    - I feel that my country is about to be lost.
  - 他在战斗中亡了(他在戰鬥中亡了)。
    - Tā zài zhàndòu zhōng wángle.
    - He was lost in battle.
