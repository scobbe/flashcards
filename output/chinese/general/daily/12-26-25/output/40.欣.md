## happy; joyful
### xīn
---
- **definition:** happy; joyful
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **components:**
  - 斤
    - jīn
    - axe; catty
  - 欠
    - qiàn
    - to owe; yawn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic: 斤 + semantic: 欠
  - **interpretation:** Combines 欠, associated with breath, emotion, or lack, to suggest an emotional state, with 斤 providing the sound (later yielding xīn in Mandarin). The sense developed toward positive feeling, giving meanings like joyful, delighted, or gladly.
- **examples:**
  - 我们欣喜地迎接好消息(我們欣喜地迎接好消息)。
    - Wǒmen xǐnxǐ de yíngjiē hǎo xiāoxi.
    - We joyfully welcomed the good news.
  - 他欣然同意邀请(他欣然同意邀請)。
    - Tā xīnrán tóngyì yāoqǐng.
    - He gladly accepted the invitation.
  - 她的画让人欣赏(她的畫讓人欣賞)。
    - Tā de huà ràng rén xīnshǎng.
    - Her painting invites admiration.
### 欣(欣) → 斤(斤)
- **definition:** axe; catty
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – an axe.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawing of a hand axe: the long vertical suggests the handle and the hooked stroke the blade. Over time the form was regularized, and the word also came to denote the traditional weight unit ‘catty’.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了两斤苹果(我買了兩斤蘋果)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le liǎng jīn píngguǒ.
    - I bought two jin of apples.
  - 这块肉一斤多一点(這塊肉一斤多一點)。
    - Zhè kuài ròu yì jīn duō yìdiǎn.
    - This piece of meat is a bit over one jin.
  - 这两支队伍水平半斤八两(這兩支隊伍水平半斤八兩)。
    - Zhè liǎng zhī duìwǔ shuǐpíng bàn jīn bā liǎng.
    - These two teams are about the same level (six of one, half a dozen of the other).
### 欣(欣) → 欠(欠)
- **definition:** to owe; yawn
- **pinyin:** qiàn
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a kneeling person with mouth open for various actions (e.g., begging, speaking, yawning); reversed as 㒫 > 旡 (cf. 既), not to be confused with 无.
  - **interpretation:** A kneeling figure with an open mouth suggests exhaling or pleading, which extends to senses like owing or lacking and the physical act of yawning. The mirrored form later standardized as 旡 in compounds such as 既, distinct from 无 despite superficial similarity.
- **examples:**
  - 他欠我五块钱(他欠我五塊錢)。
    - Tā qiàn wǒ wǔ kuài qián.
    - He owes me five yuan.
  - 我一直打哈欠(我一直打哈欠)。
    - Wǒ yīzhí dǎ hāqian.
    - I keep yawning.
  - 我们欠经验(我們欠經驗)。
    - Wǒmen qiàn jīngyàn.
    - We lack experience.
### 欣(欣) → 欠(欠) → 人(人)
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 欣(欣) → 欠(欠) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
