### happy; joyful
---
- **pinyin:** happy; joyful
- **components:**
  - 斤
    - jīn
    - to cut
  - 欠
    - qiàn
    - to lack
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Combines the phonetic component 斤 (jīn, 'to cut') with the semantic component 欠 (qiàn, 'to lack'), suggesting a joyful state resulting from having something that was previously lacking.
  - **interpretation:** This character evokes a sense of joy that comes from fulfilling a need or desire, indicated by the combination of cutting away lack with joy.
- **examples:**
  - 我很欣慰(我很欣慰)。
    - wǒ hěn xīn wèi.
    - I feel very happy.
  - 她欣喜若狂(她欣喜若狂)。
    - tā xīn xǐ ruò kuáng.
    - She is overwhelmed with joy.
  - 欣逢佳节(欣逢佳節)。
    - xīn féng jiā jié.
    - Joyfully greeting a festive season.
### 欣(欣) → 斤(斤)
- **definition:** to cut
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an axe used for cutting.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents an axe, which is a tool explicitly used for cutting, aligning closely with its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 用斤切菜(用斤切菜)。
    - Yòng jīn qiē cài.
    - Use an axe to cut vegetables.
  - 斤是一种重单位(斤是一種重單位)。
    - Jīn shì yī zhǒng zhòng dānwèi.
    - Jin is a unit of weight.
### 欣(欣) → 欠(欠)
- **definition:** to lack
- **pinyin:** qiàn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person with their mouth open, which signifies a state of lacking or desiring something.
  - **interpretation:** The image of a kneeling person with an open mouth suggests a request or desire for something that is lacking. This visual evokes the idea of wanting or needing, aligning well with the character's meaning of 'to lack.'
- **examples:**
  - 我欠钱(我欠錢)，需要借一些。
    - Wǒ qiàn qián, xūyào jiè yīxiē.
    - I lack money and need to borrow some.
  - 他在欠缺方面努力(他在欠缺方面努力)。
    - Tā zài qiànquē fāngmiàn nǔlì.
    - He is working hard in areas where he lacks.
