## martial world
### jiānghú
---
- **definition:** martial world
- **pinyin:** jiānghú
- **components:**
  - 江
    - jiāng
    - river
  - 湖
    - hú
    - lake
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 江 (jiāng, "river") originally referred to the Yangtze River, and 湖 (hú, "lake") represents a body of water. Together, 江湖 (jiānghú) forms a concept that refers to an elusive space beyond the structured society where martial artists, wanderers, and hermits dwell. In classical Chinese literature, it evokes a sense of the wandering, adventurous, and sometimes dangerous life outside societal norms.
  - **interpretation:** 江湖 (jiānghú) symbolizes a life free from official duties, inhabited by martial arts practitioners and those seeking adventure and freedom from societal constraints.
- **examples:**
  - 江湖险恶(險惡)。
    - Jiānghú xiǎn'è.
    - The martial world is treacherous.
  - 他闯荡(闖蕩)江湖，寻(尋)找自由。
    - Tā chuǎngdàng jiānghú, xúnzhǎo zìyóu.
    - He ventures into the martial world, seeking freedom.
  - 在江湖之中，英雄辈(輩)出。
    - Zài jiānghú zhī zhōng, yīngxióng bèichū.
    - In the martial world, heroes emerge one after another.
### 江湖 → 江
- **definition:** river
- **pinyin:** jiāng
- **components:**
  - 氵
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 氵 (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic: 工 (gōng, from its Old Chinese pronunciation) =
    - water-related meaning reinforced by phonetic component ->
    - river
  - **interpretation:** The character 江 (jiāng, "river") combines the water radical 氵 (shuǐ, "water") with the phonetic component 工 (gōng). This combination signifies a large body of water, fitting the meaning "river."
- **examples:**
  - 这条(這條)江水流湍急。
    - Zhè tiáo jiāng shuǐ liú tuān jí.
    - This river flows rapidly.
  - 江面上漂(飄)浮着渔(著漁)船。
    - Jiāng miàn shàng piāo fú zhe yú chuán.
    - Fishing boats float on the river surface.
  - 夜晚月光映在江上。
    - Yè wǎn yuè guāng yìng zài jiāng shàng.
    - At night, the moonlight reflects on the river.
### 江湖 → 江 → 氵
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 氵is the radical form of 水 (shuǐ, "water") ->
    - depicts flowing water, compressed and stylized next to characters.
  - **interpretation:** The character 氵is a simplified variant used as a radical to represent water-related meanings in other characters. It retains the flowing water depiction of 水 (shuǐ, "water") but is stylized to fit alongside other components.
- **examples:**
  - 水是生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 汉(漢)字中偏旁氵，代表水。
    - Hànzì zhōng piān páng shuǐ, dàibiǎo shuǐ.
    - In Chinese characters, the radical '氵' signifies water.
  - 河水清澈透明，鱼儿欢(魚兒歡)快戏(戲)水。
    - Hé shuǐ qīngchè tòumíng, yú er huānkuaì xì shuǐ.
    - The river water is clear and transparent, and the fish happily play in it.
### 江湖 → 江 → 工
- **definition:** work
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bladed tool to plow the ground and to mark a border, or a carpenter's square
  - **interpretation:** 工 (gōng, "work") represents either a tool used in agriculture for marking or a carpenter's square, reflecting its connection to traditional manual labor and craftsmanship.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天下午去做工。
    - Tā měitiān xiàwǔ qù zuò gōng.
    - He goes to work every afternoon.
  - 工厂里(廠裡)的机(機)器不停地运转(運轉)。
    - Gōng chǎng lǐ de jīqì bù tíng de yùnzhuǎn.
    - The machines in the factory run nonstop.
  - 这(這)位工人技艺(藝)精湛。
    - Zhè wèi gōngrén jìyì jīngzhàn.
    - This worker is highly skilled.
### 江湖 → 湖
- **definition:** lake
- **pinyin:** hú
- **components:**
  - 氵
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 胡
    - hú
    - beard
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 氵 (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic 胡 (胡) (hú, "beard") =
    - water area shaped by land ->
    - lake
  - **interpretation:** The character 湖(hú, "lake") combines the water radical 氵 (shuǐ, "water") with the phonetic 胡 (胡) (hú, "beard") to denote a body of water. The use of 胡 suggests a broad, open water surface, similar to how a beard spreads across the face.
- **examples:**
  - 我在湖边(邊)散步，感觉(覺)心情放松(鬆)。
    - Wǒ zài hú biān sànbù, gǎnjué xīnqíng fàngsōng.
    - I take a walk by the lake, feeling relaxed.
  - 宁静(寧靜)的湖水映着蓝(著藍)天。
    - Níngjìng de húshuǐ yìngzhe lántiān.
    - The quiet lake reflects the blue sky.
  - 小船在湖上荡(蕩)漾，映出一轮(輪)明月。
    - Xiǎochuán zài hú shàng dàngyàng, yìng chū yī lún míngyuè.
    - A small boat drifts on the lake, reflecting a bright full moon.

### 江湖 → 湖 → 胡
- **definition:** beard
- **pinyin:** hú
- **components:**
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon/month
  - 古
    - gǔ
    - ancient
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 月 (yuè, 'moon/month') + phonetic: 古 (gǔ) =
    - sound element ->
    - associated with foreigners to the north of China
  - **interpretation:** The character 胡 originally denoted people from beyond the northern borders of ancient China, often referring to non-Chinese tribes. The phonetic 古 (gǔ) provides the sound while 月 (yuè, 'moon/month') may relate to nighttime activities or timing, aspects associated with distant lands and peoples.
- **examples:**
  - 他穿着(著)胡服，看起来(來)充满异(滿異)域风(風)情。
    - tā chuānzhe hú fú, kàn qǐlái chōngmǎn yìyù fēngqíng.
    - He is wearing foreign-style clothes, and he appears full of exotic charm.
  - 古代诗(詩)人喜欢(歡)描写(寫)胡地，以表示对异国(對異國)情调(調)的向(嚮)往。
    - gǔdài shīrén xǐhuān miáoxiě hú dì, yǐ biǎoshì duì yìguó qíngdiào de xiàngwǎng.
    - Ancient poets liked to depict the lands of foreigners, expressing their longing for exotic allure.
  - 他研究胡文化。
    - tā yánjiū hú wénhuà.
    - He studies foreign culture.
### 江湖 → 湖 → 胡 → 月
- **definition:** moon/month
- **pinyin:** yuè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a crescent moon
  - **interpretation:** The character 月(yuè, "moon") is a pictographic representation of a crescent moon, straightforwardly capturing the curved shape often associated with the moon's appearance. Though interchangeable with 夕 (xī, "evening") in the past, it distinctly represents the moon in this usage.
- **examples:**
  - 夜空挂着(掛著)明月。
    - Yè kōng guà zhe míng yuè.
    - The bright moon hangs in the night sky.
  - 人们赏(們賞)月，寄托(託)相思。
    - Rén men shǎng yuè, jì tuō xiāng sī.
    - People enjoy the moon, expressing their longing.
### 江湖 → 湖 → 胡 → 古
- **definition:** ancient
- **pinyin:** gǔ
- **components:**
  - 十
    - shí
    - shield
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 十 (shí, "shield") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") =
    - concept of "strong, firm" like a shield ->
    - original meaning of 固
  - **interpretation:** 古(gǔ, "ancient") combines a shield and a mouth, symbolizing the strength and firmness of traditions carried through speech. This original strong or firm concept eventually extended to refer to something ancient, as traditions tend to become firmly established over time.
- **examples:**
  - 古书记录着(書記錄著)智慧。
    - Gǔ shū jìlù zhe zhìhuì.
    - Ancient books record wisdom.
  - 我们参观(們參觀)了一个(個)古镇(鎮)，体验历(體驗歷)史韵(韻)味。
    - Wǒmen cānguān le yīgè gǔ zhèn, tǐyàn lìshǐ yùnwèi.
    - We visited an ancient town and experienced its historical charm.
  - 古建筑诉说着过(築訴說著過)去。
    - Gǔ jiànzhú sùshuō zhe guòqù.
    - Ancient architecture tells the stories of the past.
### 江湖 → 湖 → 胡 → 古 → 十
- **definition:** shield
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园里(園裡)有十棵树(樹)，景色十分优(優)美。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.
### 江湖 → 湖 → 胡 → 古 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
