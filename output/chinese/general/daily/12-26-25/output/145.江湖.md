## martial world
### jiānghú
---
- **definition:** martial world
- **pinyin:** jiānghú
- **components:**
  - 江
    - jiāng
    - river
  - 湖
    - hú
    - lake
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 江(江) (jiāng, "river") originally referred to the Yangtze River, and 湖(湖) (hú, "lake") represents a body of water. Together, 江湖(江湖) (jiānghú) forms a concept that refers to an elusive space beyond the structured society where martial artists, wanderers, and hermits dwell. In classical Chinese literature, it evokes a sense of the wandering, adventurous, and sometimes dangerous life outside societal norms.
  - **interpretation:** 江湖(江湖) (jiānghú) symbolizes a life free from official duties, inhabited by martial arts practitioners and those seeking adventure and freedom from societal constraints.
- **examples:**
  - 江湖险恶(江湖險惡)。
    - Jiānghú xiǎn'è.
    - The martial world is treacherous.
  - 他闯荡江湖(他闖蕩江湖)，寻找自由(尋找自由)。
    - Tā chuǎngdàng jiānghú, xúnzhǎo zìyóu.
    - He ventures into the martial world, seeking freedom.
  - 在江湖之中(在江湖之中)，英雄辈出(英雄輩出)。
    - Zài jiānghú zhī zhōng, yīngxióng bèichū.
    - In the martial world, heroes emerge one after another.
