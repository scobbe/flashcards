## Yaoxian (place name)
### yǎoxiàn
---
- **definition:** Yaoxian (place name)
- **pinyin:** yǎoxiàn
- **components:**
  - 崾
    - yǎo
    - high and steep
  - 岘
    - xiàn
    - ridge; peak
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 崾岘 (yǎoxiàn, "Yaoxian") is a place name that consists of 崾 (yǎo, "high and steep") and 岘 (xiàn, "ridge; peak"). This combination likely refers to a location characterized by high and steep terrain, typical of many place names that describe geographical features in Chinese.
  - **interpretation:** 崾 (yǎo, "high and steep") and 岘 (xiàn, "ridge; peak") combine to describe a location with prominent and steep geographical features, suggesting a rugged landscape.
- **examples:**
  - 我计划明年去崾岘(峴)旅行。
    - Wǒ jìhuà míngnián qù yǎoxiàn lǚxíng.
    - I plan to travel to Yaoxian next year.
  - 崾岘(峴)被誉为(譽為)美丽(麗)的古镇(鎮)，吸引了许(許)多游(遊)客。
    - Yǎoxiàn bèi yù wéi měilì de gǔzhèn, xīyǐnle xǔduō yóukè.
    - Yaoxian is acclaimed as a beautiful ancient town, attracting many tourists.
  - 传说(傳說)中，崾岘(峴)藏有仙泉。
    - Chuánshuō zhōng, yǎoxiàn cáng yǒu xiānquán.
    - According to legend, Yaoxian hides a fairy spring.
### 崾岘 → 崾
- **definition:** high and steep
- **pinyin:** yǎo
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 幺(么)
    - yāo
    - tiny
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 山 (shān, "mountain") + 幺(么) (yāo, "tiny") =
    - phonetically resembles 幺 (yāo) and semantically relates to mountain ->
    - high and steep
  - **interpretation:** The character 崾 combines the semantic element 山 (shān, "mountain") representing height with the phonetic element 幺(么) (yāo, "tiny"), indicating its sound. This results in the meaning connected to mountainous heights, describing something as high and steep.
### 崾岘 → 崾 → 山
- **definition:** mountain
- **pinyin:** shān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three mountain peaks
  - **interpretation:** The character 山(shān, "mountain") visually represents the concept of a mountain using three vertical lines that suggest peaks. This depiction is consistent with early forms found in Oracle Bone Script, emphasizing the notion of natural elevation associated with mountains.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)去爬山。
    - Wǒmen qù pá shān.
    - We go climb the mountain.
  - 那座大山让(讓)人惊叹(驚嘆)。
    - Nà zuò dà shān ràng rén jīngtàn.
    - That mountain astonishes people.
  - 远处(遠處)的山隐约(隱約)在云(雲)中，令人心旷(曠)神怡。
    - Yuǎnchù de shān yǐnyuē zài yún zhōng, lìng rén xīnkuàng shényí.
    - The distant mountain fades into the clouds, leaving one in a state of peace.
### 崾岘 → 崾 → 幺
- **definition:** tiny
- **pinyin:** yāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a thin silk thread
  - **interpretation:** The character 幺 (yāo, "tiny threads") illustrates a thin silk thread, reflecting its meaning directly through its shape. It resembles a representation of something small and delicate, like the strands of silk used in weaving.
- **examples:**
  - 这个(這個)微小的幺在织(織)物中闪(閃)耀。
    - Zhège wéixiǎo de yāo zài zhīwù zhōng shǎnyào.
    - The tiny thread sparkles within the fabric.
  - 织(織)布工巧妙地利用幺，将(將)其编织进(編織進)精美的锦缎(錦緞)中。
    - Zhībùgōng qiǎomiào de lìyòng yāo, jiāng qí biānzhī jìn jīngměi de jǐnduàn zhōng.
    - The weaver cleverly employs the tiny thread, integrating it into exquisite brocade.
### 崾岘 → 岘(峴)
- **traditional:** 峴
- **definition:** ridge; peak
- **pinyin:** xiàn
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 见(見)
    - xiàn
    - see
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 山 (shān, "mountain") + phonetic: 見(见) (xiàn, "see") =
    - mountain shape resembling a pass ->
    - mountain pass
  - **interpretation:** The character 岘(峴) (xiàn, "mountain pass") combines 山 (shān, "mountain") indicating the geographical nature of the character, with 見(见) (xiàn, "see") as a phonetic component. This combination conveys the idea of a mountain shape suggesting a pass.
  - **simplification:** Simplified to 岘(峴) by removing the character elements in 見(见) (xiàn, "see") while 山 (shān, "mountain") remains unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 古人踏云(雲)行路，经(經)由岘。
    - Gǔ rén tà yún xíng lù, jīng yóu xiàn.
    - Ancient people traveled among the clouds, passing by the mountain pass.
  - 陡峭的山路直通岘。
    - Dǒu qiào de shān lù zhí tōng xiàn.
    - The steep mountain road leads directly to the mountain pass.

### 崾岘 → 岘(峴) → 见(見)
- **traditional:** 見
- **definition:** see
- **pinyin:** xiàn
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Depicts an eye 目 (mù, "eye") over a person 人 (rén, "person") ->
    - representation of seeing or perception
  - **interpretation:** The character originally depicted an eye positioned over a person, symbolizing the act of seeing or encountering someone. This combination effectively conveys the idea of sight or meeting, aligning with its core meanings.
  - **simplification:** 見 (jiàn, "see") was simplified by reducing the number of strokes, using a simplified structure to retain both meaning and phonetic aspects effectively.
- **examples:**
  - 我见(見)到老朋友时(時)，心情十分愉快。
    - Wǒ jiàn dào lǎo péngyǒu shí, xīnqíng shífēn yúkuài.
    - I felt very happy when I met my old friend.
  - 我们见(們見)面了。
    - Wǒmen jiàn miàn le.
    - We met.
  - 在公园里见(園裡見)到一只(隻)小猫(貓)，让(讓)我十分高兴(興)。
    - Zài gōngyuán lǐ jiàn dào yī zhī xiǎo māo, ràng wǒ shífēn gāoxìng.
    - Seeing a little cat in the park made me very happy.
### 崾岘 → 岘(峴) → 见(見) → 目
- **definition:** eye
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a human eye, now represented and rotated 90 degrees ->
    - eye
  - **interpretation:** The character 目(mù, 'eye') is a pictogram representing a human eye. Its original orientation has changed over time, resembling an eye seen from a sideways view.
- **examples:**
  - 他的目光炯炯有神。
    - Tā de mùguāng jiǒngjiǒng yǒu shén.
    - His gaze is bright and full of spirit.
  - 她目不转(轉)睛，注视(視)前方。
    - Tā mù bù zhuǎn jīng, zhùshì qiánfāng.
    - She stared intently, gazing ahead.
  - 雄鹰(鷹)展翅，双(雙)目炯炯看天际(際)。
    - Xióng yīng zhǎn chì, shuāng mù jiǒngjiǒng kàn tiānjì.
    - The eagle spreads its wings, its eyes piercingly gaze at the horizon.
### 崾岘 → 岘(峴) → 见(見) → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg ->
    - visual representation of a person
  - **interpretation:** The character 人 (rén, 'person') effectively uses simple lines to depict the essential form of a human, making it immediately recognizable and intuitive as meaning 'person.'
- **examples:**
  - 他是个(個)好人。
    - Tā shì gè hǎo rén.
    - He is a good person.
  - 每个(個)人都有责(責)任，为(為)社会(會)作出贡献(貢獻)。
    - Měi gè rén dōu yǒu zérèn, wèi shèhuì zuòchū gòngxiàn.
    - Every person has a responsibility to contribute to society.
  - 人在旅途中，心灵获(靈獲)得了成长(長)。
    - Rén zài lǚtú zhōng, xīnlíng huòdé le chéngzhǎng.
    - People gain growth along their journey.
