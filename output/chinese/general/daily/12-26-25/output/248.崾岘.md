## Yaoxian (place name)
### yǎoxiàn

- **definition:** Yaoxian (place name)
- **pinyin:** yǎoxiàn
- **components:**

  - **interpretation:** 崾 (yǎo, "high and steep") and 岘 (xiàn, "ridge; peak") combine to describe a location with prominent and steep geographical features, suggesting a rugged landscape.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 山 (shān, "mountain") + 幺 (yāo, "tiny; short") =
    - mountain with a narrow, lofty height ->

This combination reflects the concept of a high and steep mountain.

### 崾岘 → 岘(峴)
- **traditional:** 峴
- **definition:** ridge; peak
- **pinyin:** xiàn
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 见(見)
    - xiàn
    - see
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 山 (shān, "mountain") + phonetic: 見(见) (xiàn, "see") =
    - mountain shape resembling a pass ->
    - mountain pass
  - **interpretation:** The character 岘(峴) (xiàn, "mountain pass") combines 山 (shān, "mountain") indicating the geographical nature of the character, with 見(见) (xiàn, "see") as a phonetic component. This combination conveys the idea of a mountain shape suggesting a pass.
  - **simplification:** Simplified to 岘(峴) by removing the character elements in 見(见) (xiàn, "see") while 山 (shān, "mountain") remains unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 古( )人( )踏( )云(雲)行( )路( )，经(經)由( )岘( )。

  - 陡峭的山路直通岘。
    - Dǒu qiào de shān lù zhí tōng xiàn.
    - The steep mountain road leads directly to the mountain pass.
