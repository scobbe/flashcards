## Starbucks
### Xīngbākè
---
- **definition:** Starbucks
- **pinyin:** Xīngbākè
- **components:**
  - 星
    - xīng
    - star
  - 巴
    - bā
    - area; wish
  - 克
    - kè
    - to overcome; gram
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 星 (xīng, "star") + 巴 (bā, "area; wish") + 克 (kè, "to overcome; gram")
  - **interpretation:** 星巴克 (Xīngbākè) is a phonetic transcription of the name Starbucks, the international coffee company. The components 星 (xīng, "star"), 巴 (bā, "area; wish"), and 克 (kè, "to overcome; gram") are selected to approximate the sound of 'Starbucks' rather than to convey a specific meaningful translation.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天去星巴克，尝试(嘗試)新品。
    - Wǒ jīntiān qù Xīngbākè, chángshì xīn pǐn.
    - Today I went to Starbucks and tried new products.
  - 星巴克是我最喜欢(歡)的聚会场(會場)所。
    - Xīngbākè shì wǒ zuì xǐhuān de jùhuì chǎngsuǒ.
    - Starbucks is my favorite venue for gatherings.
  - 晚上我在星巴克，坐下来写(來寫)作业(業)。
    - Wǎnshàng wǒ zài Xīngbākè, zuò xiàlái xiě zuòyè.
    - At night, I sat down at Starbucks to do my homework.
### 星巴克 → 星
- **definition:** star
- **pinyin:** xīng
- **components:**
  - 晶
    - jīng
    - twinkling stars
  - 生
    - shēng
    - grow
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 晶 (jīng, "twinkling stars") + Phonetic: 生 (shēng, "grow") ->
    - 晶 later simplified to 日 (rì, "sun") ->
    - 星(星, "star")
  - **interpretation:** The character 星 (xīng, "star") combines the idea of twinkling from 晶 (jīng, "twinkling stars") with the concept of growth or life from 生 (shēng, "grow"). This combination effectively captures the shimmer and presence of stars in the night sky, aided by the later simplification of 晶 into the 日 (rì, "sun") component, maintaining a celestial association.
- **examples:**
  - 那颗(顆)星在黑夜中熠熠生辉(輝)。
    - Nà kē xīng zài hēiyè zhōng yìyì shēnghuī.
    - That star shines brilliantly in the dark night.
  - 天空布满(佈滿)星光，夜色显(顯)得格外神秘。
    - Tiānkōng bù mǎn xīnguāng, yèsè xiǎnde géwài shénmì.
    - The sky is filled with starlight, and the night appears especially mysterious.
### 星巴克 → 星 → 晶
- **definition:** twinkling stars
- **pinyin:** jīng
- **components:**
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 日 (rì, "sun") tripled to depict multiple suns or sparkles ->
    - brightness or sparkle
  - **interpretation:** The character 晶(jīng) represents brightness by repeating the character 日 (rì, "sun") three times, symbolizing multiple sources of light or sparkles. This construction effectively conveys the sense of brilliance or clarity associated with crystals and sparkling objects.
- **examples:**
  - 透明的水晶，闪(閃)耀着(著)迷人的光芒。
    - Tòumíng de shuǐjīng, shǎnyào zhe mírén de guāngmáng.
    - The transparent crystal sparkles with enchanting light.
  - 湖面上映出倒影，散发着(發著)晶的光彩。
    - Húmiàn shàng yìngchū dàoyǐng, sànfā zhe jīng de guāngcǎi.
    - Reflections appear on the lake surface, emitting a sparkling brilliance.
  - 洁(潔)白的玉石中隐(隱)含着(著)晶的韵(韻)味。
    - Jiébái de yùshí zhōng yǐnhán zhe jīng de yùnwèi.
    - The pure jade subtly reveals a sparkling charm.
### 星巴克 → 星 → 晶 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
- **examples:**
  - 晨曦来临时(來臨時)，日出东(東)方。
    - Chénxī láilín shí, rìchū dōngfāng.
    - At the break of dawn, the sun rises in the east.
  - 温(溫)暖的日光洒满(灑滿)大地。
    - Wēnnuǎn de rìguāng sǎ mǎn dàdì.
    - The warm sunlight spreads over the earth.
  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。
    - Zhōngwǔ shífēn, rì gāo guà tiān.
    - At noon, the sun hangs high in the sky.

### 星巴克 → 巴
- **definition:** area; wish
- **pinyin:** bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a snake coiled around a branch
  - **interpretation:** The character 巴(bā, "to cling") originally depicted a snake wrapped around a branch or object, symbolizing the idea of clinging or sticking to something.
- **examples:**

    - Tā zǒngshì bā zhe māmā.

  - 巴先生今天出席会议(會議)，分享成功经验(經驗)。
    - Bā xiānshēng jīntiān chūxí huìyì, fēnxiǎng chénggōng jīngyàn.

### 星巴克 → 克
- **definition:** to overcome; gram
- **pinyin:** kè
- **components:**
  - 由
    - yóu
    - battle helmet
  - 皮
    - pí
    - pelt
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 由 (yóu, "battle helmet") + 皮 (pí, "pelt") =
    - to defeat or vanquish ->
    - 克
  - **interpretation:** The character 克 (kè) illustrates the concept of overcoming an opponent in battle, symbolized by the combination of a helmet and a pelt. Alternatively, it can depict a person carrying a load, signifying the ability to bear or undertake tasks competently.
- **examples:**
  - 这瓶药含50克(這瓶藥含50克)。
    - Zhè píng yào hán 50 kè.
    - This bottle of medicine contains 50 grams.
  - 他克服困难(難)，迈(邁)向成功。
    - Tā kèfú kùnnán, mài xiàng chénggōng.
    - He overcame difficulties and stepped towards success.
  - 她决(決)心克敌(敵)制胜(勝)，面对(對)挑战(戰)。
    - Tā juéxīn kè dí zhì shèng, miànduì tiǎozhàn.
    - She is determined to overcome her enemies and triumph when facing challenges.
### 星巴克 → 克 → 由
- **definition:** battle helmet
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 十 (shí, 'ten') + Semantic: 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') ->
    - container with a needle; oracle bones reflect original version of 怞 (yóu, 'cause') ->
    - final form 由
  - **interpretation:** The character 由 (yóu, 'cause') was originally a phono-semantic compound, combining 十 (shí, 'ten') as the phonetic component with 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') as the semantic component. This represents a container, indicating an ancient pronunciation. The character also has historical associations with oracle bone script and was an original form of 怞 (yóu, 'cause'), indicating the character's evolution from a depiction of an item to its modern meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)次事故由人为(為)失误(誤)造成。
    - Zhè cì shìgù yóu rénwéi shīwù zàochéng.
    - This accident was caused by human error.
  - 他的成功由坚(堅)持与(與)努力铸(鑄)就。
    - Tā de chénggōng yóu jiānchí yǔ nǔlì zhùjiù.
    - His success was built on perseverance and hard work.
  - 问题(問題)往往由细节(細節)引起。
    - Wèntí wǎngwǎng yóu xìjié yǐnqǐ.
    - Problems are often caused by details.
### 星巴克 → 克 → 由 → 十
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园里(園裡)有十棵树(樹)，景色十分优(優)美。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.
### 星巴克 → 克 → 由 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 星巴克 → 克 → 皮
- **definition:** pelt
- **pinyin:** pí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a surface or the skin of an animal
  - **interpretation:** The character 皮 (pí, 'surface') visually represents an animal hide or skin stretched out, emphasizing the idea of an outer covering or surface. It captures the concept of a boundary between the interior and exterior, as seen in animal skins used as material.
- **examples:**
  - 这张(這張)桌子的皮很光滑。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi de pí hěn guānghuá.
    - The surface of this table is very smooth.
  - 新鞋的皮非常柔软(軟)，适(適)合长时间(長時間)行走。
    - Xīn xié de pí fēicháng róuruǎn, shìhé cháng shíjiān xíngzǒu.
    - The surface of the new shoes is very soft and suitable for long walks.
