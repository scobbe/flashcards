## Wong Lo Kat (beverage brand)
### Wánglǎojí
---
- **definition:** Wong Lo Kat (beverage brand)
- **pinyin:** Wánglǎojí
- **components:**
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king; surname
  - 老
    - lǎo
    - old; venerable; always
  - 吉
    - jí
    - auspicious; lucky; good
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 王(王) (wáng, "king") + 老(老) (lǎo, "old; venerable") + 吉(吉) (jí, "auspicious") refers to a famous Chinese herbal tea brand known for its cooling properties.
  - **interpretation:** The name 王老吉(Wánglǎojí) gives a sense of tradition and good fortune. 'Wong' implies prestige or prominence ('king'), 'Lo' suggests venerability or seniority ('old'), and 'Kat' connotes positivity or success ('auspicious'). Together, they evoke a trusted and esteemed lineage, fitting for a brand.
- **examples:**
  - 夏天炎热(夏天炎熱)，一瓶王老吉解渴(一瓶王老吉解渴)。
    - Xiàtiān yánrè, yī píng Wánglǎojí jiěkě.
    - In the hot summer, a bottle of Wong Lo Kat quenches thirst.
  - 我爱喝王老吉(我愛喝王老吉)。
    - Wǒ ài hē Wánglǎojí.
    - I love drinking Wong Lo Kat.
  - 饭后饮一杯王老吉(飯後飲一杯王老吉)，让心情舒畅(讓心情舒暢)。
    - Fànhòu yǐn yī bēi Wánglǎojí, ràng xīnqíng shūchàng.
    - Having a cup of Wong Lo Kat after a meal makes the mood feel refreshing.

- **definition:** king; surname
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:**

    - ceremonial tool near the throne ->

  - **interpretation:** The character 王(wáng, "king") visually represents a ritual axe, a powerful symbol of authority and royal rituals in ancient China. This connection to ceremonial objects underscores the king's role in maintaining order.

- **pinyin:** jí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 士(士) (shì, 'scholar/ritual axe') + 口(口) (kǒu, 'mouth') ->

    - auspicious
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 士(士) (shì, 'ritual axe') and 口(口) (kǒu, 'mouth'), suggesting a ceremonial context where rituals are meant to please deities or ancestors, thus associating the character with favorable and auspicious outcomes.
