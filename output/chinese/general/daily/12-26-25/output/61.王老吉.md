## Wong Lo Kat (beverage brand)
### Wánglǎojí
---
- **definition:** Wong Lo Kat (beverage brand)
- **pinyin:** Wánglǎojí
- **components:**
  - 王
    - Wáng
    - Wang (surname); king
  - 老吉
    - lǎojí
    - Old Ji (nickname); venerable + auspicious; Lo Kat (Cantonese)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** eponymic compound
  - **description:** 王 (surname Wang) + 老吉 (Old Ji; honorific + given name/nickname) = brand name of a herbal tea.
  - **interpretation:** Formed from the founder’s surname Wang and his nickname “Old Ji” (Cantonese: Lo Kat), conveying respect and auspiciousness. Widely recognized as a Guangdong herbal tea brand.
- **examples:**
  - 我想喝一罐王老吉(我想喝一罐王老吉)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng hē yī guàn Wánglǎojí.
    - I want to drink a can of Wong Lo Kat.
  - 这款饮料在广东很受欢迎(這款飲料在廣東很受歡迎)。
    - Zhè kuǎn yǐnliào zài Guǎngdōng hěn shòu huānyíng.
    - This beverage is very popular in Guangdong.
  - 由于商标纠纷，内地产品改名为加多宝(由於商標糾紛，內地產品改名為加多寶)。
    - Yóuyú shāngbiāo jiūfēn, nèidì chǎnpǐn gǎimíng wéi Jiāduōbǎo.
    - Due to a trademark dispute, the mainland product was renamed JDB.
### 王老吉 → 王
- **definition:** Wang (surname); king
- **pinyin:** Wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram of a ritual (jade) axe kept near the throne for ceremonies; symbol of royal power.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a ceremonial axe used in royal rites, a concrete emblem of sovereign authority. A later folk reading treats the three horizontals as Heaven–Man–Earth joined by the vertical, and it is unrelated to 玉 or 主.
- **examples:**
  - 他姓王(他姓王)。
    - Tā xìng Wáng.
    - His surname is Wang.
  - 国王召见大臣(國王召見大臣)。
    - Guówáng zhàojiàn dàchén.
    - The king summoned the ministers.
  - 在古代，王举行祭祀以祈福(在古代，王舉行祭祀以祈福)。
    - Zài gǔdài, wáng jǔxíng jìsì yǐ qífú.
    - In ancient times, the king performed rituals to pray for blessings.
### 王老吉 → 老吉
- **definition:** Old Ji (nickname); venerable + auspicious; Lo Kat (Cantonese)
- **pinyin:** lǎojí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 老 + 吉 = respectful “old” + “auspicious” → “Old Lucky”; venerable, luck-invoking epithet/brand element.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the respectful prefix 老 with the auspicious morpheme 吉 to create a warm, luck‑invoking epithet or brand name. Common in nicknames or product names to signal venerability and good fortune.
- **examples:**
  - 大家都叫他老吉(大家都叫他老吉)，觉得这个外号很吉利(覺得這個外號很吉利)。
    - Dàjiā dōu jiào tā Lǎo Jí, juéde zhège wàihào hěn jílì.
    - Everyone calls him “Old Lucky,” finding the nickname auspicious.
  - 这款饮料叫“老吉”(這款飲料叫「老吉」)，名字听起来亲切又讨喜(名字聽起來親切又討喜)。
    - Zhè kuǎn yǐnliào jiào “Lǎo Jí”, míngzi tīng qǐlái qīnqiè yòu tǎoxǐ.
    - This beverage is called “Laoji,” a name that sounds friendly and likable.
  - 在北方方言里，“老”常用作尊称(在北方法言裡，「老」常用作尊稱)，所以“老吉”带有敬意和祝福(所以「老吉」帶有敬意和祝福)。
    - Zài Běifāng fāngyán lǐ, “lǎo” cháng yòngzuò zūnchēng, suǒyǐ “Lǎo Jí” dàiyǒu jìngyì hé zhùfú.
    - In northern dialects, “lao” is often a respectful prefix, so “Laoji” carries respect and blessings.
