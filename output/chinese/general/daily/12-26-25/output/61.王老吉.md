## Wong Lo Kat (beverage brand)
### Wánglǎojí
---
- **definition:** Wong Lo Kat (beverage brand)
- **pinyin:** Wánglǎojí
- **components:**
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king
  - 老
    - lǎo
    - old
  - 吉
    - jí
    - auspicious; lucky; propitious; good fortune
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 王 (wáng, "king"), 老 (lǎo, "old"), 吉 (jí, "auspicious")
  - **interpretation:** The name 王老吉 (王老吉) (Wánglǎojí) carries connotations of respect and good fortune. 王 (wáng, "king") denotes authority and prestige, 老 (lǎo, "old") conveys respect for age and tradition, and 吉 (jí, "auspicious") suggests good luck. Together, this name may reflect the brand's aspiration for esteemed status and prosperity.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱(愛)喝王老吉。
    - Wǒ ài hē Wánglǎojí.
    - I love drinking Wong Lo Kat.
  - 夏天来(來)了，王老吉让(讓)人感到清凉(涼)。
    - Xiàtiān láile, Wánglǎojí ràng rén gǎndào qīngliáng.
    - When summer comes, Wong Lo Kat makes people feel refreshed.
  - 在热(熱)天里(裡)，王老吉为(為)我解渴。
    - Zài rè tiān lǐ, Wánglǎojí wèi wǒ jiěkě.
    - On hot days, Wong Lo Kat quenches my thirst.
### 王老吉 → 王
- **definition:** king
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a ritual axe representing the king's authority
  - **interpretation:** This character represents the form of a ceremonial axe, which was the symbol of a king's power in ancient China. The structure emphasizes the sacred and authoritative role of the king, separate from the nuanced folk interpretations linking Heaven, Man, and Earth.
- **examples:**
  - 他被称为(稱為)王，领导(領導)人民。
    - Tā bèi chēng wéi wáng, lǐngdǎo rénmín.
    - He is called the king, leading his people.
  - 古代的君主常被尊称为(稱為)王。
    - Gǔdài de jūnzhǔ cháng bèi zūnchēng wéi wáng.
    - Ancient rulers were often respectfully called kings.
  - 在祭祀仪(儀)式上，人们(們)向王致敬。
    - Zài jìsì yíshì shàng, rénmen xiàng wáng zhìjìng.
    - During the ceremonial sacrifices, people salute the king.

### 王老吉 → 吉
- **definition:** auspicious; lucky; propitious; good fortune
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 士
    - shì
    - scholar
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 士 (shì, "scholar") (ritual axe) + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") ->
    - representation of rituals to acquire favor ->
    - auspiciousness
  - **interpretation:** The character 吉(jí) combines 士 (shì, "scholar") symbolizing a ritual axe and 口 (kǒu, "mouth"), suggesting ancient rituals for gaining ancestral or divine blessings. This association leads to the meaning of 'auspicious' or 'lucky'.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是吉日，适(適)合举办(舉辦)婚礼(禮)。
    - Jīntiān shì jírì, shìhé jǔbàn hūnlǐ.
    - Today is an auspicious day, suitable for holding a wedding.
  - 他抽到吉牌。
    - Tā chōu dào jí pái.
    - He drew a lucky card.
  - 这(這)次考试结(試結)果吉利，大家都感到欣慰。
    - Zhè cì kǎoshì jiéguǒ jílì, dàjiā dōu gǎndào xīnwèi.
    - The results of this exam were auspicious, and everyone felt relieved.

### 王老吉 → 吉 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**

- **examples:**

  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
