## try; attempt
### chángshì
---
- **definition:** try; attempt
- **pinyin:** chángshì
- **components:**
  - 尝(嘗)
    - cháng
    - taste; try; experience; sample
  - 试(試)
    - shì
    - test; try; exam; experiment
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 嘗/尝 (taste; sample) + 試/试 (test; examine) = try; attempt
  - **interpretation:** Combines the idea of tasting with testing, emphasizing an initial or exploratory effort to see if something works. Common for trying methods, tasks, or approaches before committing.
  - **simplification:** Both characters were simplified to reduce stroke count and speed writing: 嘗→尝 by compressing interior components; 試→试 by changing 言 to 讠 and simplifying the right-side phonetic.
- **examples:**
  - 我想尝试这个方法(我想嘗試這個方法)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng chángshì zhège fāngfǎ.
    - I want to try this method.
  - 我们先尝试小规模测试(我們先嘗試小規模測試)，再决定下一步(再決定下一步)。
    - Wǒmen xiān chángshì xiǎo guīmó cèshì, zài juédìng xià yī bù.
    - We will first try a small-scale test, then decide the next step.
  - 面对新的挑战(面對新的挑戰)，他愿意尝试不同的方法(他願意嘗試不同的方法)。
    - Miànduì xīn de tiǎozhàn, tā yuànyì chángshì bùtóng de fāngfǎ.
    - Facing new challenges, he is willing to try different approaches.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 尝(嘗)
- **definition:** taste; try; experience; sample
- **pinyin:** cháng
- **components:**
  - 尚
    - shàng
    - still; yet; to esteem
  - 旨
    - zhǐ
    - purpose; decree; delicious
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 旨 ("tasty") + phonetic: 尚 (OC *djaŋ, *djaŋs)
  - **interpretation:** Meaning is guided by 旨, which signals deliciousness and the idea of tasting or trying flavors. 尚 contributes the pronunciation (cháng) and places it within the 尚 phonetic family.
  - **simplification:** Mainland simplification reduces the complex 嘗 to 尝 by compressing internal strokes and omitting extra elements, while preserving the 尚-like phonetic framework to keep the sound.
- **examples:**
  - 我想尝一尝这道菜(我想嘗一嘗這道菜)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng cháng yī cháng zhè dào cài.
    - I want to have a taste of this dish.
  - 他从未尝过失败(他從未嘗過失敗)。
    - Tā cóngwèi cháng guò shībài.
    - He has never experienced failure.
  - 请先尝一点(請先嘗一點)，再决定买不买(再決定買不買)。
    - Qǐng xiān cháng yīdiǎn, zài juédìng mǎi bù mǎi.
    - Please sample a little first, then decide whether to buy.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 尝(嘗) → 尚(尚)
- **definition:** still; yet; to esteem
- **pinyin:** shàng
- **components:**
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight
  - 向
    - xiàng
    - toward; direction
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 八 + phonetic: 向
  - **interpretation:** Built with 八 as the semantic element and 向 as the phonetic, aligning with their Old Chinese readings. 八 gives a general semantic tag while 向 contributes the shàng sound; the overall layout is similar to 冋.
- **examples:**
  - 事情尚未结束(事情尚未結束)。
    - Shìqíng shàng wèi jiéshù.
    - The matter is not over yet.
  - 他尚年轻(他尚年輕)，需要指导(需要指導)。
    - Tā shàng niánqīng, xūyào zhǐdǎo.
    - He is still young and needs guidance.
  - 他崇尚简约的生活方式(他崇尚簡約的生活方式)。
    - Tā chóngshàng jiǎnyuē de shēnghuó fāngshì.
    - He esteems a simple lifestyle.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 尝(嘗) → 尚(尚) → 八(八)
- **definition:** eight
- **pinyin:** bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: two bent lines indicating “to divide”; later borrowed to mean “eight” via homophony (original sense now in 分, 別).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two strokes diverging to suggest splitting or separation. Later became a rebus for the number eight because it shared the same sound, while the original sense lived on in 分 and 別. Not the “two hands” component found in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我有八本书(我有八本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu bā běn shū.
    - I have eight books.
  - 她八岁了(她八歲了)。
    - Tā bā suì le.
    - She is eight years old.
  - 我买了八个苹果(我買了八個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le bā gè píngguǒ.
    - I bought eight apples.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 尝(嘗) → 尚(尚) → 向(嚮)
- **definition:** toward; direction
- **pinyin:** xiàng
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 宀 ‘house’ + 口 ‘mouth’; echoes in a place. Original form of 響; later borrowed to write 𠨍 (嚮). Similar to 冋.
  - **interpretation:** A mouth sounding under a roof evokes reverberation indoors, matching the sense later written as 響 ‘echo, sound’. By rebus borrowing the graph came to write 嚮 ‘to face, toward; direction’, which is the sense carried by modern 向.
- **examples:**
  - 我们向前走吧(我們向前走吧)。
    - Wǒmen xiàng qián zǒu ba.
    - Let’s walk forward.
  - 她向着窗户微笑(她向著窗戶微笑)。
    - Tā xiàngzhe chuānghu wēixiào.
    - She smiles toward the window.
  - 他向医院的方向跑去(他向醫院的方向跑去)。
    - Tā xiàng yīyuàn de fāngxiàng pǎo qù.
    - He ran in the direction of the hospital.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 尝(嘗) → 尚(尚) → 向(嚮) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 尝(嘗) → 旨(旨)
- **definition:** purpose; decree; delicious
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **components:**
  - 匕
    - bǐ
    - spoon
  - 甘
    - gān
    - sweet; sweetness
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound (會意 / 会意)
  - **description:** 會意: 匕 ‘spoon’ used to eat/drink something delicious (orig. 𠤔; cf. 嘗; unrelated to 皀). Shuowen: 形聲—phonetic 匕 + semantic 甘 (‘delicious’).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a spoon associated with tasting something flavorful, which extends to senses like “choice, fine” and later to “purport/aim” in texts. Traditional scholars also analyzed it as a phono-semantic spelling with 匕 supplying the sound and 甘 contributing the taste-related meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我们此行旨在考察市场(我們此行旨在考察市場)。
    - Wǒmen cǐ xíng zhǐ zài kǎochá shìchǎng.
    - Our trip aims to survey the market.
  - 皇帝下达圣旨(皇帝下達聖旨)，命他回京(命他迴京)。
    - Huángdì xiàdá shèngzhǐ, mìng tā huí jīng.
    - The emperor issued an imperial decree, ordering him to return to the capital.
  - 报告要突出宗旨和要点(報告要突出宗旨和要點)。
    - Bàogào yào tūchū zōngzhǐ hé yàodiǎn.
    - The report should highlight the purpose and key points.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 尝(嘗) → 旨(旨) → 匕(匕)
- **definition:** spoon
- **pinyin:** bǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – a spoon.
  - **interpretation:** A stylized drawing of a spoon with handle and bowl, later simplified into two strokes. Despite visual similarity to parts seen in 老, 眞, and 比, it developed independently and refers specifically to the utensil.
- **examples:**
  - 古人用匕进食(古人用匕進食)。
    - Gǔrén yòng bǐ jìnshí.
    - People in ancient times ate with spoons.
  - 他说“勺子”的古称是“匕”(他說「勺子」的古稱是「匕」)。
    - Tā shuō “sháozi” de gǔchēng shì “bǐ”.
    - He says the archaic term for a spoon is “匕”.
  - 出土的竹简上写有“匕”与“箸”(出土的竹簡上寫有「匕」與「箸」)。
    - Chūtǔ de zhújiǎn shàng xiě yǒu “bǐ” yǔ “zhù”.
    - The excavated bamboo slips bear the characters “匕” and “箸”.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 尝(嘗) → 旨(旨) → 甘(甘)
- **definition:** sweet; sweetness
- **pinyin:** gān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram (指事)
  - **description:** Something sweet in an open mouth; original drawing 𠁿; original form of 含.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an open mouth tasting sweetness, which directly yields the sense ‘sweet’. The early form 𠁿 later developed into 含 ‘to contain/hold in the mouth’, reflecting the same mouth-centered idea.
- **examples:**
  - 这水很甘甜(這水很甘甜)。
    - Zhè shuǐ hěn gāntián.
    - This water is very sweet.
  - 我们在田里种了甘蔗(我們在田裡種了甘蔗)。
    - Wǒmen zài tián lǐ zhòng le gānzhè.
    - We planted sugarcane in the field.
  - 他甘心为家人付出(他甘心為家人付出)。
    - Tā gānxīn wèi jiārén fùchū.
    - He is willing to make sacrifices for his family.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 试(試)
- **definition:** test; try; exam; experiment
- **pinyin:** shì
- **components:**
  - 言
    - yán
    - speech; words
  - 式
    - shì
    - pattern; style; formula
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 言 + phonetic: 式
  - **interpretation:** Combines the speech/words element 言 with the phonetic 式, indicating actions related to speaking or formal methods like trying or testing, while borrowing the shì sound from 式. The meaning aligns with evaluating or attempting through stated rules or procedures.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by replacing 言 with its simplified form 讠 while retaining the phonetic 式, reducing strokes but preserving sound and core meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我想试一下新方法(我想試一下新方法)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng shì yíxià xīn fāngfǎ.
    - I want to try a new method.
  - 我们下周有一次数学考试(我們下週有一次數學考試)。
    - Wǒmen xià zhōu yǒu yí cì shùxué kǎoshì.
    - We have a math exam next week.
  - 科学家在实验中不断试错(科學家在實驗中不斷試錯)。
    - Kēxuéjiā zài shíyàn zhōng búduàn shìcuò.
    - Scientists keep testing by trial and error in experiments.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 试(試) → 言(言)
- **definition:** speech; words
- **pinyin:** yán
- **components:**
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram: formed by adding a horizontal stroke to ancient 舌 to show tongue movement (speech). Shuowen erroneously treats it as phono-semantic: phonetic 䇂 (qiān) + semantic 口 (“mouth”).
  - **interpretation:** Built on 舌 (tongue), an added stroke marks motion, abstracting the act of speaking into a simple sign for “speech/words.” Later standardization added a dot on top and simplified the tongue shape, moving from a pictorial tongue to a conventional symbol for utterance. The Shuowen’s phono-semantic reading is a historical misinterpretation rather than the true origin.
- **examples:**
  - 请注意你的言语(請注意你的言語)。
    - Qǐng zhùyì nǐ de yányǔ.
    - Please mind your language.
  - 他发言很简短(他發言很簡短)。
    - Tā fāyán hěn jiǎnduǎn.
    - His remarks were brief.
  - 言行一致(言行一致)，方能取信于人(方能取信於人)。
    - Yán xíng yízhì, fāng néng qǔxìn yú rén.
    - Only by matching words with deeds can one gain others’ trust.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 试(試) → 言(言) → 舌(舌)
- **definition:** tongue
- **pinyin:** shé
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a forked tongue emerging upwards from a mouth (口)
  - **interpretation:** Graph depicts a tongue sticking up from an open mouth, with the fork hinting at the split tip. Later forms stylized the drawing, but the mouth element remains visible at the bottom; it is unrelated to 舍.
- **examples:**
  - 医生让他伸出舌头检查(醫生讓他伸出舌頭檢查)。
    - Yīshēng ràng tā shēnchū shétou jiǎnchá.
    - The doctor asked him to stick out his tongue for an exam.
  - 辣得我舌头发麻(辣得我舌頭發麻)。
    - Là de wǒ shétou fā má.
    - It’s so spicy that my tongue is numb.
  - 紧张得他舌头打结了(緊張得他舌頭打結了)。
    - Jǐnzhāng de tā shétou dǎjié le.
    - He was so nervous that his tongue got tied.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 试(試) → 式(式)
- **definition:** pattern; style; formula
- **pinyin:** shì
- **components:**
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work; labor; craft
  - 弋
    - yì
    - to shoot; capture
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 工 (work) + phonetic: 弋
  - **interpretation:** 工 suggests ideas of work, construction, and method, pointing toward senses like pattern, standard, or procedure. 弋 supplied the sound, whose ancient pronunciation approximated that of 式; the graph later generalized to mean a fixed method, style, or formula.
- **examples:**
  - 这种教学方式很有效(這種教學方式很有效)。
    - Zhè zhǒng jiàoxué fāngshì hěn yǒuxiào.
    - This teaching method is very effective.
  - 我们用了新的公式来计算结果(我們用了新的公式來計算結果)。
    - Wǒmen yòngle xīn de gōngshì lái jìsuàn jiéguǒ.
    - We used a new formula to calculate the result.
  - 设计要保持简洁的样式(設計要保持簡潔的樣式)。
    - Shèjì yào bǎochí jiǎnjié de yàngshì.
    - The design should maintain a simple style.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 试(試) → 式(式) → 工(工)
- **definition:** work; labor; craft
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a bladed tool to plow and mark a border; alternatively a carpenter’s square (cf. original meaning of 巨).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a tool used to prepare the ground and set boundaries, naturally extending to the idea of labor and work. Some traditions instead see a carpenter’s square, connecting it with making and constructing, and with early senses linked to 巨.
- **examples:**
  - 我在工厂工作(我在工廠工作)。
    - Wǒ zài gōngchǎng gōngzuò.
    - I work in a factory.
  - 这些工人正在劳动(這些工人正在勞動)。
    - Zhèxiē gōngrén zhèngzài láodòng.
    - These workers are laboring now.
  - 他的工钱很高(他的工錢很高)。
    - Tā de gōngqián hěn gāo.
    - His wages are high.
### 尝试(嘗試) → 试(試) → 式(式) → 弋(弋)
- **definition:** to shoot; capture
- **pinyin:** yì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a sharp peg — original form of 杙 (OC *lɯɡ, “peg”); same as 才 and 尗.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a pointed peg or dart, a simple tool meant to pierce or stick into something. From this image arose senses related to shooting or striking game, and it also served as the base form for characters like 杙.
- **examples:**
  - 古人常用弋射鸟兽(古人常用弋射鳥獸)。
    - Gǔrén cháng yòng yìshè niǎo shòu.
    - In ancient times people often used yi-shooting to take birds and beasts.
  - 猎人以弋猎为生(獵人以弋獵為生)。
    - Lièrén yǐ yìliè wéi shēng.
    - Hunters made a living by fowling or hunting with yi.
  - 这件青铜器上刻有弋的形象(這件青銅器上刻有弋的形象)。
    - Zhè jiàn qīngtóngqì shàng kè yǒu yì de xíngxiàng.
    - This bronze vessel is engraved with the image of a yi-peg/dart.
