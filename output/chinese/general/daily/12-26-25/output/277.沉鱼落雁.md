## beauty that sinks fish and makes birds fall
### chén yú luò yàn
---
- **traditional:** 沉魚落雁
- **definition:** beauty that sinks fish and makes birds fall
- **pinyin:** chén yú luò yàn
- **components:**
  - 沉(沈)
    - chén

  - 鱼(魚)
    - yú
    - fish
  - 落
    - luò
    - fall
  - 雁

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 沉(沈) (chén, "sink") is paired with 鱼(魚) (yú, "fish") and 落 (luò, "fall") is paired with 雁 (yàn, "wild goose"). The phrase 沉(沈)鱼(魚)落( )雁( ) (chén yú luò yàn) describes a beauty so profound that fish forget to swim and sink, while geese are so entranced they forget to fly and fall.
  - **interpretation:** In classical Chinese literature, 沉(沈)鱼(魚)落( )雁( ) (chén yú luò yàn) is a description of overwhelming beauty that captivates the natural world; it's often used to praise exceptional feminine beauty.

- **examples:**

    - Nà wèi jiā rén zǒu jìn yànhuì shàng, lìng zhòngrén jīngtàn chén yú luò yàn.
    - That beauty walked into the banquet, leaving everyone in awe with her sink-fish and falling-bird charm.
