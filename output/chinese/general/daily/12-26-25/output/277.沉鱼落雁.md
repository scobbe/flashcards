## beauty that sinks fish and makes birds fall
### chén yú luò yàn
---
- **definition:** beauty that sinks fish and makes birds fall
- **pinyin:** chén yú luò yàn
- **components:**
  - 沉(沈)
    - chén
    - sink
  - 鱼(魚)
    - yú
    - fish
  - 落
    - luò
    - fall
  - 雁
    - yàn
    - wild goose
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 沉(沈) (chén, "sink") is paired with 鱼(魚) (yú, "fish") and 落(落) (luò, "fall") is paired with 雁(雁) (yàn, "wild goose"). The phrase 沉鱼落雁(沈魚落雁) (chén yú luò yàn) describes a beauty so profound that fish forget to swim and sink, while geese are so entranced they forget to fly and fall.
  - **interpretation:** In classical Chinese literature, 沉鱼落雁(沈魚落雁) (chén yú luò yàn) is a description of overwhelming beauty that captivates the natural world; it's often used to praise exceptional feminine beauty.
  - **simplification:** traditional simplified to 沉鱼落雁
- **examples:**
  - 她的容貌沉鱼落雁(她的容貌沉魚落雁)。
    - Tā de róngmào chén yú luò yàn.
    - Her beauty is so extraordinary that it could sink fish and make birds fall.
  - 那位佳人走进宴会上(那位佳人走進宴會上)，令众人惊叹沉鱼落雁(令眾人驚嘆沉魚落雁)。
    - Nà wèi jiā rén zǒu jìn yànhuì shàng, lìng zhòngrén jīngtàn chén yú luò yàn.
    - That beauty walked into the banquet, leaving everyone in awe with her sink-fish and falling-bird charm.
