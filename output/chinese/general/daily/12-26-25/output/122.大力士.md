## strongman
### dàlìshì
---
- **definition:** strongman
- **pinyin:** dàlìshì
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big; great
  - 力
    - lì
    - strength
  - 士
    - shì
    - scholar; warrior
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 大(大) (dà, "big; great") + 力(力) (lì, "strength") + 士(士) (shì, "scholar; warrior") =
    - "strongman"
  - **interpretation:** The word 大力士 refers to someone who possesses great strength. 大(大) suggests magnitude, 力(力) means physical strength, and 士(士) refers to a person with a specialized skill or attribute, historically warriors or scholars. Thus, a 大力士 is a person known for their exceptional physical strength.
- **examples:**
  - 他是大力士(他是大力士)。
    - Tā shì dàlìshì.
    - He is a strongman.
  - 那位大力士(那位大力士)，在赛事上获胜(在賽事上獲勝)。
    - Nà wèi dàlìshì, zài sàishì shàng huòshèng.
    - That strongman won the competition.
### 大力士(大力士) → 大(大)
- **definition:** big; great
- **pinyin:** dà
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts 人(人) (rén, "person") ->
    - borrowed for 大(大) (dà, "big")
  - **interpretation:** The character 大(大) (dà, "big") originally depicted a person standing, which later evolved to express the meaning 'big.' The resemblance to a person was used phonetically to convey largeness, not necessarily to portray outstretched arms as sometimes explained.
- **examples:**
  - 这只狗很大(這隻狗很大)。
    - Zhè zhī gǒu hěn dà.
    - This dog is big.
  - 我们的新家很大(我們的新家很大)，设备也很齐全(設備也很齊全)。
    - Wǒmen de xīn jiā hěn dà, shèbèi yě hěn qí quán.
    - Our new home is big, and the equipment is also complete.
  - 城堡大门敞开(城堡大門敞開)，迎接远方的客人(迎接遠方的客人)。
    - Chéngbǎo dàmén chǎngkāi, yíngjiē yuǎnfāng de kèrén.
    - The castle's big gate is wide open, welcoming guests from afar.
### 大力士(大力士) → 力(力)
- **definition:** strength
- **pinyin:** lì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a muscular arm gripping something
  - **interpretation:** 力(力) (lì, 'strength') is a pictogram representing a powerful arm, conveying the concept of physical strength and force.
- **examples:**
  - 他全力以赴(他全力以赴)，实现梦想(實現夢想)。
    - Tā quánlì yǐfù, shíxiàn mèngxiǎng.
    - He goes all out to achieve his dreams.
  - 他用力气推动门(他用力氣推動門)。
    - Tā yòng lìqì tuīdòng mén.
    - He pushes the door with force.
  - 锻炼增强体力(鍛鍊增強體力)，工作也要靠实力(工作也要靠實力)。
    - Duànliàn zēngqiáng tǐlì, gōngzuò yě yào kào shílì.
    - Exercise strengthens physical power, and work also relies on strength.
### 大力士(大力士) → 士(士)
- **definition:** scholar; warrior
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a ritual axe ->
    - symbolizes soldier ->
    - officer, intellectual
  - **interpretation:** The character 士(士) (shì, "upside-down ritual axe") originated as a pictorial representation of a war axe. Over time, its meaning evolved from 'soldier' to 'officer' and 'intellectual', reflecting a broadened scope from military to scholarly domains.
- **examples:**
  - 考古学家在遗址中发现了士(考古學家在遺址中發現了士)，这种倒置的祭祀斧头曾见于史书(這種倒置的祭祀斧頭曾見於史書)。
    - Kǎogǔ xuéjiā zài yízhǐ zhōng fāxiàn le shì, zhèzhǒng dàozhì de jìsì fǔtóu céng jiànyú shǐshū.
    - Archaeologists discovered the shì at an excavation site; this upside‐down ritual axe once appeared in historical records.
  - 在古代礼仪中(在古代禮儀中)，士被尊为神圣工具(士被尊為神聖工具)。
    - Zài gǔdài lǐyí zhōng, shì bèi zūn wéi shénshèng gōngjù.
    - In ancient ceremonies, the shì was revered as a sacred tool.
