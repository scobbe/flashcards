## Zhongshan District, Taipei
### Zhōngshān Qū
---
- **traditional:** 中山區
- **definition:** Zhongshan District, Taipei
- **pinyin:** Zhōngshān Qū
- **components:**
  - 中山
    - zhōngshān
    - Zhongshan; Sun Yat-sen
  - 区(區)
    - qū
    - district; area; region; ward
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 中 (zhōng, "middle") + 山 (shān, "mountain") form 中山 (Zhōngshān, "Zhongshan") referring to Sun Yat-sen, a key figure in modern Chinese history. 區 (qū, "district") indicates an administrative division.
  - **interpretation:** 中山 (Zhōngshān, "Zhongshan") commemorates Sun Yat-sen, known as the 'Father of the Nation' in Republic of China (Taiwan). 中山区(區) (Zhōngshān Qū, "Zhongshan District") in Taipei is named in his honor.
- **examples:**
  - 我住在中山区(區)。
    - Wǒ zhù zài Zhōngshān Qū.
    - I live in Zhongshan District.
  - 中山区(區)是台(臺)北市的热门(熱門)商业区(業區)，生活节(節)奏很快。
    - Zhōngshān Qū shì Táiběi shì de rè mén shāng yè qū，shēng huó jié zòu hěn kuài.
    - Zhongshan District is a popular commercial area in Taipei, where life moves at a fast pace.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word

  - **interpretation:** In addition to referring to a central mountain, Zhongshan is widely recognized as the alter name of Sun Yat-sen, emphasizing his central role in Chinese history as a leader. The usage of 'mountain' in names can convey importance and stability.

    - Zhōngguó xǔduō dìfāng yǐ Zhōngshān mìngmíng, yǐ jìniàn guófù Sūn Zhōngshān.

### 中( )山( )区(區) → 区(區)
- **traditional:** 區
- **definition:** district; area; region; ward
- **pinyin:** qū
- **components:**
  - 匸
    - xì
    - enclosure
  - 品
    - pǐn
    - goods/people
  - 乂
    - yì
    - to manage
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 匸 (enclosed space) + 品 (many people) =
    - many people within an enclosure ->
    - area, district
  - **interpretation:** The character 區 (qū, 'district') visually represents an area where many people reside, symbolized by 品 (pǐn, 'goods/people') enclosed within 匸 (xì, 'enclosure'). This layout effectively conveys the idea of a defined space containing a community or district.
  - **simplification:** 區 was simplified to 区(區) by reducing 品 to 乂 (yì, 'to manage'), keeping the enclosure meaning while reducing strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 我住在繁华(華)的区(區)。
    - Wǒ zhù zài fán huá de qū.
    - I live in a bustling district.
  - 古代文人常在这区(這區)，寻觅灵(尋覓靈)感。
    - Gǔ dài wén rén cháng zài zhè qū, xún mì líng gǎn.
    - Ancient literati often sought inspiration in this region.
  - 他参观(參觀)了多个区(個區)，体验(體驗)了不同的文化。
    - Tā cān guān le duō gè qū, tǐ yàn le bù tóng de wén huà.
    - He visited several districts and experienced diverse cultures.
### 中( )山( )区(區) → 区(區) → 匸
- **definition:** enclosure
- **pinyin:** xì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 𠃊 (symbolic graphic) ->
    - depicts an enclosure hiding something or someone ->
    - evolved to represent a concealed space or item
  - **interpretation:** The character 匸(xì, 'box') is a pictogram representing a concealed or enclosed space. Its graphical form suggests something contained or hidden, which aligns with its meaning as a 'box' or an enclosure. Unlike other related pictograms that depict physical containers, 匸 emphasizes the aspect of concealment or secrecy.
- **examples:**
  - 他把匸搬到了房间(間)角落。
    - Tā bǎ xì bān dào le fángjiān jiǎoluò.
    - He moved the box to the corner of the room.
  - 我们(們)在仓库(倉庫)找到了匸，里(裡)面装(裝)有秘密文件。
    - Wǒmen zài cāngkù zhǎodào le xì, lǐmiàn zhuāng yǒu mìmì wénjiàn.
    - We found the box in the warehouse, and it contained secret documents.
  - 工厂(廠)工人把旧(舊)匸清理了，然后(後)再利用。
    - Gōngchǎng gōngrén bǎ jiù xì qīnglǐ le, ránhòu zài lìyòng.
    - Factory workers cleaned the old box and then reused it.
### 中( )山( )区(區) → 区(區) → 品

### 中( )山( )区(區) → 区(區) → 品 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 中( )山( )区(區) → 区(區) → 乂
- **definition:** to manage
- **pinyin:** yì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a tool (similar to a sickle or a pair of scissors) for mowing or weeding ->
    - used for governing ->
    - final form 乂
  - **interpretation:** The character 乂 originally depicted a tool used for cutting, and by extension, it was associated with governing due to the notion of cutting or managing weeds as a metaphor for ruling effectively. This sense of order is crucial in governance.
- **examples:**
  - 这些年，他致力于地方的治理(這些年，他致力於地方的治理)。
    - Zhèxiē nián, tā zhìlì yú dìfāng de zhìlǐ.
    - Over the years, he has dedicated himself to local governance.
  - 为民者，先自齐以治家(為民者，先自齊以治家)。
    - Wèi mín zhě, xiān zì qí yǐ zhì jiā.
    - Those who govern for the people, first manage their own households.
  - 长(長)者都希望后(後)代能好好治理天下。
    - Zhǎng zhě dōu xīwàng hòudài néng hǎohǎo zhìlǐ tiānxià.
    - The elders hope that future generations can govern the world well.
