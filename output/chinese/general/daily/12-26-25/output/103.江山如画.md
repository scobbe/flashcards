## picturesque scenery
### jiāngshān rúhuà
---
- **definition:** picturesque scenery
- **pinyin:** jiāngshān rúhuà
- **components:**
  - 江
    - jiāng
    - river
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 如
    - rú
    - like; as if
  - 画(畫)
    - huà
    - painting; drawing
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 江(江) (jiāng, "river") and 山(山) (shān, "mountain") refer to natural features, while 如(如) (rú, "like; as if") and 画(畫) (huà, "painting") compare them to a painting. Together, they create the phrase 江山如畫(江山如画) (jiāngshān rúhuà, "picturesque scenery"), evoking an image of nature so beautifully composed it resembles a work of art.
  - **interpretation:** This expression describes landscapes or scenery that are so magnificent and visually stunning that they seem like a painting. It invokes an appreciation for nature's beauty.
- **examples:**
  - 中国江山如画(中國江山如畫)，游客流连忘返(遊客流連忘返)。
    - Zhōngguó jiāngshān rúhuà, yóukè liúlián wàngfǎn.
    - China's picturesque scenery captivates tourists.
  - 祖国江山如画(祖國江山如畫)，诗人笔下常吟咏(詩人筆下常吟詠)。
    - Zǔguó jiāngshān rúhuà, shīrén bǐxià cháng yínyǒng.
    - The motherland's picturesque scenery is often celebrated by poets.
