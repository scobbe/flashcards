## herbal tea
### liángchá
---
- **traditional:** 涼茶
- **definition:** herbal tea
- **pinyin:** liángchá
- **components:**
  - 凉(涼)
    - liáng
    - cool
  - 茶
    - chá
    - tea
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 涼(凉) (liáng, "cool") + 茶 (chá, "tea") =
    - herbal tea
  - **interpretation:** 涼茶 (liángchá, "herbal tea") refers to a type of tea believed to have cooling properties, which helps reduce internal heat according to traditional Chinese medicinal beliefs. The combination makes sense as 涼(凉) (liáng, "cool") implies the cooling effect the tea is supposed to provide.
- **examples:**
  - 夏天很热(熱)，喝一杯凉(涼)茶。
    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, hē yī bēi liángchá.
    - It's very hot in summer, so drink a cup of herbal tea.
  - 凉(涼)茶解渴。
    - Liángchá jiě kě.
    - Herbal tea quenches thirst.
  - 忙碌一天后(後)，喝点凉(點涼)茶。
    - Mánglù yī tiān hòu, hē diǎn liángchá.
    - After a busy day, have some herbal tea.
### 凉(涼)茶( ) → 凉(涼)
- **traditional:** 涼
- **definition:** cool
- **pinyin:** liáng
- **components:**
  - 氵(水)
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 京
    - jīng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic 京 (jīng) =
    - coolness related to water ->
    - 涼
  - **interpretation:** The character 涼 (liáng, "cool") combines the water radical 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water") with the phonetic 京 (jīng) to convey the idea of coolness or freshness typically associated with water.
  - **simplification:** 涼 was simplified to 凉(涼) by removing the water radical 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water") and retaining the phonetic 京, thus reducing strokes while maintaining phonetic sound.
- **examples:**
  - 夏日午后(後)很凉(涼)。
    - Xià rì wǔhòu hěn liáng.
    - The summer afternoon is cool.
  - 微风(風)吹过(過)，带来丝丝凉(帶來絲絲涼)意。
    - Wēifēng chuīguò, dài lái sī sī liáng yì.
    - The breeze blows by, bringing a hint of coolness.
  - 夏夜乘凉(涼)，享受微风(風)。
    - Xià yè chéng liáng, xiǎngshòu wēifēng.
    - Taking in the coolness of a summer night while enjoying the gentle breeze.

### 凉(涼)茶( ) → 茶
- **definition:** tea
- **pinyin:** chá
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 余
    - yú
    - phonetic component
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 艹 (cǎo, "grass") + 余 (yú) =
    - plant component and phonetic ->
    - tea
  - **interpretation:** The character 茶(chá) combines 艹 (cǎo, "grass") implying a plant, with 余 (yú) for its phonetic aspect. Originally derived from a modification of 荼, which meant "bitter plant" in classical texts, 茶(chá) specifically came to signify "tea." This combines the concept of a plant with a sound similar to the word for tea.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)喝茶。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē chá.
    - I like to drink tea.
  - 早上喝茶，心情变(變)好。
    - Zǎoshang hē chá, xīnqíng biàn hǎo.
    - Drinking tea in the morning improves the mood.

### 凉(涼)茶( ) → 茶 → 余(餘)
- **traditional:** 餘
- **definition:** phonetic component
- **pinyin:** yú
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - "eat"
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic: 余 (yú) + semantic: 食 (shí, "eat") =
    - eating that is remaining ->
    - surplus, remainder
  - **interpretation:** The character 餘 (yú) combines 余 (yú), a phonetic element meaning "I," with 食 (shí, "eat") to convey the concept of a surplus or remainder after eating, which fittingly extends to the idea of something left over.
  - **simplification:** 余 retains the phonetic component without the semantic 食, focusing on simplicity by removing the eating element while keeping the core pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 余喜欢读书(歡讀書)。
    - Yú xǐhuān dúshū.
    - I enjoy reading.
  - 余游历(遊歷)名山，探索古今。
    - Yú yóulì míngshān, tànsuǒ gǔjīn.
    - I travel through famous mountains and explore the ancient and the modern.
  - 余静(靜)坐思过(過)。
    - Yú jìngzuò sīguò.
    - I sit quietly and reflect on my mistakes.
