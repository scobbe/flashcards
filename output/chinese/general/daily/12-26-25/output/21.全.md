### complete; whole
---
- **pinyin:** complete; whole
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 入(入) (rù, 'enter') + 玉(玉) (yù, 'jade') — signifies a whole piece of jade, representing completeness.
  - **interpretation:** This makes intuitive sense as a whole piece of jade is seen as undivided and perfect, conveying the meaning of completeness or wholeness.
- **examples:**
  - 这个盒子是全新的(這個盒子是全新的)。
    - zhège hézi shì quánxīn de.
    - This box is brand new.
  - 我们要保证信息的全貌(我們要保證信息的全貌)。
    - wǒmen yào bǎozhèng xìnxī de quánmào.
    - We need to ensure a complete picture of the information.
### 全(全) → 入(入)
- **definition:** enter
- **pinyin:** rù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** An arrowhead indicating 'to enter'.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents the action of entering through the pointed shape, evoking a sense of movement toward a specific point.
- **examples:**
  - 请进(請進)，我们开始吧(我們開始吧)！
    - qǐng jìn, wǒmen kāishǐ ba!
    - Please come in, let's begin!
  - 他已经入了学(他已經入了學)。
    - tā yǐjīng rùle xué.
    - He has already entered the school.
  - 门口有一个人(門口有一個人)，正要入内(正要入內)。
    - ménkǒu yǒu yīgè rén, zhèng yào rù nèi.
    - There is a person at the gate, about to enter.
### 全(全) → 玉(玉)
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a picture of a cong (琮), a sort of ritual jade totem.
  - **interpretation:** The character symbolizes jade, reflecting its importance and usage in ritualistic and decorative contexts, much like the cong itself.
- **examples:**
  - 这件玉器很美丽(這件玉器很美麗)。
    - Zhè jiàn yùqì hěn měilì.
    - This piece of jade is beautiful.
  - 我想买玉(我想買玉)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng mǎi yù.
    - I want to buy jade.
