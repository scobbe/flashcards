## cucumber
### huánggua

- **traditional:** 黃瓜
- **definition:** cucumber
- **pinyin:** huánggua
- **components:**
  - 黄(黃)
    - huáng
    - yellow
  - 瓜
    - guā
    - melon; squash; gourd
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 黄(黃) (huáng, "yellow") + 瓜 (guā, "melon") =
    - cucumber
  - **interpretation:** The term 黄(黃)瓜( ) (huángguā) translates literally to 'yellow melon.' It refers to the cucumber, which is botanically a type of gourd and can have a yellowish tinge when ripe. By naming the cucumber this way, the focus is on its classification among the gourd family rather than its usual green color.

### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 黄(黃) → 璜 → 𤣩
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cong (ritual jade totem)
  - **interpretation:** The character 𤣩 is a variant form of 玉 (yù, "jade"), which originally depicted a cong, a type of ritual jade totem. Over time, a dot was added to distinguish it from 王 (wáng, "king") due to the visual similarity between the two characters. Jade played a significant role in art and rituals, which is why its pictogram was distinctly designed. The character represents the material jade and its cultural significance as a precious and spiritual object.
