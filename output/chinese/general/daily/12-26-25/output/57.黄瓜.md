## cucumber
### huánggua
---
- **traditional:** 黃瓜
- **definition:** cucumber
- **pinyin:** huánggua
- **components:**
  - 黄(黃)
    - huáng
    - yellow
  - 瓜
    - guā
    - melon; squash; gourd
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 黄(黃) (huáng, "yellow") + 瓜 (guā, "melon") =
    - cucumber
  - **interpretation:** The term 黄(黃)瓜 (huángguā) translates literally to 'yellow melon.' It refers to the cucumber, which is botanically a type of gourd and can have a yellowish tinge when ripe. By naming the cucumber this way, the focus is on its classification among the gourd family rather than its usual green color.
- **examples:**
  - 我买(買)了一根黄(黃)瓜。
    - Wǒ mǎi le yī gēn huángguā.
    - I bought a cucumber.
  - 我洗了黄(黃)瓜，切成了薄片。
    - Wǒ xǐ le huángguā, qiē chéng le bó piàn.
    - I washed the cucumber and sliced it into thin pieces.
  - 夏天吃黄(黃)瓜，很解暑。
    - Xiàtiān chī huángguā, hěn jiěshǔ.
    - Eating cucumber in summer is very refreshing.
### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 黄(黃)
- **traditional:** 黃
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **components:**
  - 璜
    - huáng
    - semicircular jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Originally depicted a disabled person with a protruding chest or abdomen; phonetically borrowed for 'yellow' in oracle bone script.
  - **interpretation:** The character started as a pictogram of a protruding chest or a ring of jade, illustrating historical or ritual significance. Over time, it was phonetically adopted to mean 'yellow'.
  - **simplification:** 黄 is a direct simplification of 黃, removing the top glyph 廿 (niàn, 'twenty') as a result of character corruption, simplifying the form.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)件衣服是黄(黃)色的。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfú shì huángsè de.
    - This piece of clothing is yellow.
  - 金色阳(陽)光下，稻田变(變)得金黄(黃)。
    - Jīnsè yángguāng xià, dàotián biàn de jīn huáng.
    - Under the golden sunlight, the rice fields become bright yellow.
  - 秋天里，银(銀)杏叶(葉)呈黄(黃)色。
    - Qiūtiān lǐ, yínxìng yè chéng huángsè.
    - In autumn, the ginkgo leaves appear yellow.
### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 黄(黃) → 璜

- **pinyin:** huáng
- **components:**
  - 𤣩
    - yù
    - jade
  - 黃
    - huáng
    - yellow
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 𤣩 ("jade") + 黃 ("yellow," phonetic) =
    - jade ornament ->

  - **interpretation:** The character 璜 combines the semantic element of 𤣩("jade"), indicating its connection to jade, with the phonetic element 黃(huáng, "yellow") to convey the pronunciation. This combination refers to a specific type of jade with a semicircular shape.
### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 黄(黃) → 璜 → 𤣩
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **components:**
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** variant form
  - **description:** 𤣩 is a variant form of 玉 (yù, 'jade')
  - **interpretation:** 𤣩 represents a lesser-used variant of 玉 which historically depicted either a ritual jade totem or jade pieces strung together. Its use is rare and is primarily seen as an alternative to the more common 玉 form.
### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 黄(黃) → 璜 → 𤣩 → 玉
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a cong (琮, a ritual jade totem)
  - **interpretation:** 玉 (yù, "jade") visually represents the concept of jade, either as a cong or three pieces of jade strung together. It is distinct from 王 (wáng, "king") thanks to the added dot, ensuring clarity in their usage and meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他收藏了一块(塊)古玉。
    - Tā shōucáng le yī kuài gǔ yù.
    - He collected a piece of antique jade.
  - 晴空万(萬)里时(時)，玉石在阳(陽)光下闪烁(閃爍)。
    - Qíngkōng wànlǐ shí, yù shí zài yángguāng xià shǎnshuò.
    - Under a clear sky, the jade sparkled under the sunlight.
  - 在宫(宮)廷中，玉制(製)的饰(飾)物象征着(徵著)高贵(貴)。
    - Zài gōngtíng zhōng, yù zhì de shìwù xiàngzhēng zhe gāoguì.
    - In the palace, jade ornaments symbolize nobility.
### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 黄(黃) → 璜 → 黃
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 田
    - tián
    - field
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted 口 (kǒu, 'mouth/face') as a stylized depiction of a person or jade ring ->
    - borrowed for 'yellow' in oracle bone script
  - **interpretation:** Originally a pictogram depicting something related to either a person or jade, 黃 (huáng, 'yellow') was later phonetically borrowed for the meaning 'yellow'. The character's association with jade might also symbolize value and elegance, helping to explain its transition to the color 'yellow', often symbolic of wealth and opulence in Chinese culture.
  - **simplification:** 黄 replaces 黃 by dropping the radical 田 (tián, 'field') entirely, keeping only 廿 (nián, 'twenty'), a simplified pattern for phonetic or visual resemblance.
- **examples:**
  - 那件衬(襯)衫是黃的。
    - Nà jiàn chènshān shì huáng de.
    - That shirt is yellow.
  - 太阳(陽)升起时(時)，大地披上了黃的外衣。
    - Tàiyáng shēngqǐ shí, dàdì pī shàng le huáng de wàiyī.
    - When the sun rises, the earth is draped in yellow.
### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 黄(黃) → 璜 → 黃 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 黄(黃) → 璜 → 黃 → 田
- **definition:** field
- **pinyin:** tián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a field divided into four sections
  - **interpretation:** The character 田(tián, 'field') visually represents a field with sections, indicating its agricultural use and importance in ancient times.
- **examples:**
  - 农(農)民在田里(裡)耕作。
    - Nóngmín zài tián lǐ gēngzuò.
    - The farmer works in the field.
  - 那边(邊)的田长满(長滿)了庄(莊)稼，农(農)民收获(穫)忙碌。
    - Nàbiān de tián cháng mǎn le zhuāngjià, nóngmín shōuhuò mánglù.
    - The field over there is full of crops, and the farmer is busy harvesting.
  - 秋收时节(時節)，人们(們)忙于(於)收割田里(裡)的稻谷。
    - Qiūshōu shíjié, rénmen máng yú shōugē tián lǐ de dàogǔ.
    - During the harvest season, people are busy reaping the rice in the fields.
### 黄(黃)瓜( ) → 瓜
- **definition:** melon; squash; gourd
- **pinyin:** guā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a melon suspended by its vines
  - **interpretation:** The character 瓜 (guā, "melon") visually represents the shape and outline of a melon with its vines, emphasizing its organic form and growth pattern.
- **examples:**
  - 他喜爱(愛)吃瓜。
    - Tā xǐ'ài chī guā.
    - He loves eating melons.
  - 夏日炎炎时(時)，瓜农开(農開)始采摘瓜。
    - Xiàrì yányán shí, guānóng kāishǐ cǎizhāi guā.
    - On scorching summer days, melon farmers start harvesting melons.
