## cucumber
### huánggua
---
- **definition:** cucumber
- **pinyin:** huánggua
- **components:**
  - 黄(黃)
    - huáng
    - yellow
  - 瓜
    - guā
    - melon; squash; gourd
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 黄(黃) (huáng, "yellow") + 瓜(瓜) (guā, "melon") =
    - cucumber
  - **interpretation:** The term 黄瓜(黃瓜) (huángguā) translates literally to 'yellow melon.' It refers to the cucumber, which is botanically a type of gourd and can have a yellowish tinge when ripe. By naming the cucumber this way, the focus is on its classification among the gourd family rather than its usual green color.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了一根黄瓜(我買了一根黃瓜)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le yī gēn huángguā.
    - I bought a cucumber.
  - 我洗了黄瓜(我洗了黃瓜)，切成了薄片(切成了薄片)。
    - Wǒ xǐ le huángguā, qiē chéng le bó piàn.
    - I washed the cucumber and sliced it into thin pieces.
  - 夏天吃黄瓜(夏天吃黃瓜)，很解暑(很解暑)。
    - Xiàtiān chī huángguā, hěn jiěshǔ.
    - Eating cucumber in summer is very refreshing.
### 黄瓜(黃瓜) → 黄(黃)
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **components:**
  - 璜
    - huáng
    - semicircular jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Originally depicted a disabled person with a protruding chest or abdomen; phonetically borrowed for 'yellow' in oracle bone script.
  - **interpretation:** The character started as a pictogram of a protruding chest or a ring of jade, illustrating historical or ritual significance. Over time, it was phonetically adopted to mean 'yellow'.
  - **simplification:** 黄 is a direct simplification of 黃, removing the top glyph 廿(廿) (niàn, 'twenty') as a result of character corruption, simplifying the form.
- **examples:**

    - Jīnsè yángguāng xià, dàotián biàn de jīn huáng.
    - Under the golden sunlight, the rice fields become bright yellow.
  - 秋天里(秋天里)，银杏叶呈黄色(銀杏葉呈黃色)。

### 黄瓜(黃瓜) → 黄(黃) → 璜(璜) → 𤣩(𤣩)
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **components:**
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cong (琮, 'ritual jade totem') ->
    - interpreted as three pieces of jade strung on a string ->
    - simplified to 玉 as a distinct shape from 王 by adding a dot
  - **interpretation:** The character 𤣩(yù, 'jade') is a variant of 玉, which originally depicted a jade object used in rituals. The distinction with 王(wáng, 'king') was made through a simple dot to specify its unique meaning related to jade.
- **examples:**

    - Tā zǐxì dǎmó, lìng zhè kuài yù gèng xiǎn wēnrùn.
    - He polished it carefully, making the jade appear even more mellow.
  - 透明的𤣩显露出细腻的纹理(透明的𤣩顯露出細膩的紋理)。
    - Tòumíng de yù xiǎnlù chū xìnì de wénlǐ.
    - The translucent jade reveals delicate patterns.

### 黄瓜(黃瓜) → 黄(黃) → 璜(璜) → 黃(黃)
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 田
    - tián
    - field
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted 口 (kǒu, 'mouth/face') as a stylized depiction of a person or jade ring ->
    - borrowed for 'yellow' in oracle bone script
  - **interpretation:** Originally a pictogram depicting something related to either a person or jade, 黃(黃) (huáng, 'yellow') was later phonetically borrowed for the meaning 'yellow'. The character's association with jade might also symbolize value and elegance, helping to explain its transition to the color 'yellow', often symbolic of wealth and opulence in Chinese culture.
  - **simplification:** 黄 replaces 黃 by dropping the radical 田 (tián, 'field') entirely, keeping only 廿 (nián, 'twenty'), a simplified pattern for phonetic or visual resemblance.
- **examples:**
  - 那件衬衫是黃的(那件襯衫是黃的)。
    - Nà jiàn chènshān shì huáng de.
    - That shirt is yellow.
  - 太阳升起时(太陽升起時)，大地披上了黃的外衣(大地披上了黃的外衣)。
    - Tàiyáng shēngqǐ shí, dàdì pī shàng le huáng de wàiyī.
    - When the sun rises, the earth is draped in yellow.
