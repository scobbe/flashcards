## carry on the back
### fù
---
- **definition:** carry on the back
- **pinyin:** fù
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** simplified form of 負
  - **description:**
    - 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") + 人(人) (rén, "person") =
    - person with a burden ->
    - carrying valuables ->
    - 負(負)
  - **interpretation:** The traditional character 負(負) (fù, "carry on the back") combines 貝(貝) (bèi, "cowry") representing value or burden, and 人(人) (rén, "person") indicating a person bearing this burden. This conveys the action of carrying something valuable, often on the back.
  - **simplification:** Initially, 负(負) shared the same form since they are the same word, so this character doesn't have a simplified form.
- **examples:**
  - 他负书包(他負書包)，走在去学校的路上(走在去學校的路上)。
    - Tā fù shūbāo, zǒu zài qù xuéxiào de lùshàng.
    - He carries a schoolbag on his back as he walks on the way to school.
  - 士兵负盔甲(士兵負盔甲)，驰援前线(馳援前線)。
    - Shìbīng fù kuījiǎ, chíyuán qiánxiàn.
    - The soldier carries his armor on his back as he rushes to the frontline.
  - 登山者负登山包(登山者負登山包)，攀登险峰(攀登險峰)。
    - Dēngshānzhě fù dēngshānbāo, pāndēng xiǎnfēng.
    - The climber carries his backpack on his back as he ascends treacherous peaks.
### 负(負) → 貝(貝)
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护身符(護身符)，放在口袋里(口袋裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝作为货币(古代人用貝作為貨幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
### 负(負) → 人(人)
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg ->
    - visual representation of a person
  - **interpretation:** The character 人(人) (rén, 'person') effectively uses simple lines to depict the essential form of a human, making it immediately recognizable and intuitive as meaning 'person.'
- **examples:**
  - 他是个好人(他是個好人)。
    - Tā shì gè hǎo rén.
    - He is a good person.
  - 每个人都有责任(每個人都有責任)，为社会作出贡献(為社會作出貢獻)。
    - Měi gè rén dōu yǒu zérèn, wèi shèhuì zuòchū gòngxiàn.
    - Every person has a responsibility to contribute to society.
  - 人在旅途中(人在旅途中)，心灵获得了成长(心靈獲得了成長)。
    - Rén zài lǚtú zhōng, xīnlíng huòdé le chéngzhǎng.
    - People gain growth along their journey.
