## 小妹妹(小妹妹)
---
## little sister; young girl
### xiǎomèimei
---
- **definition:** little sister; young girl
- **pinyin:** xiǎomèimei
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small
  - 妹妹
    - mèimei
    - younger sister
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小妹妹 (xiǎomèimei, "little sister; young girl") combines 小(小) (xiǎo, "small") and 妹妹(妹妹) (mèimei, "younger sister"). Together, it refers to a younger sibling who is female, or more generally, a young girl.
  - **interpretation:** By combining 小(小) (xiǎo, "small") with 妹妹(妹妹) (mèimei, "younger sister"), the phrase typically describes either a younger female sibling or, in a broader sense, any young girl.
- **examples:**
  - 小妹妹在花园里玩耍(小妹妹在花園裡玩耍)。
    - xiǎomèimei zài huāyuán lǐ wánshuǎ.
    - The little girl is playing in the garden.
  - 小妹妹很聪明(小妹妹很聰明)，总能找到好玩的游戏(總能找到好玩的遊戲)。
    - xiǎomèimei hěn cōngmíng, zǒng néng zhǎodào hǎowán de yóuxì.
    - The little girl is very smart, and she always finds fun games.
  - 小妹妹喜欢唱歌(小妹妹喜歡唱歌)，她的声音很清脆(她的聲音很清脆)。
    - xiǎomèimei xǐhuān chànggē, tā de shēngyīn hěn qīngcuì.
    - The little girl likes singing, and her voice is very clear.
### 小妹妹(小妹妹) → 小(小)
- **definition:** small
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Three small dots ->
    - small
  - **interpretation:** The character 小(xiǎo, 'small') is an ideogram that originally used three small dots to visually represent the concept of smallness. Over time, these dots became elongated, maintaining the meaning of 'small.'
- **examples:**
  - 我有小猫(我有小貓)，它喜欢睡觉(它喜歡睡覺)。
    - Wǒ yǒu xiǎo māo, tā xǐhuān shuìjiào.
    - I have a small cat, it loves to sleep.
  - 这本书很小(這本書很小)。
    - Zhè běn shū hěn xiǎo.
    - This book is very small.
  - 小花在院子里玩耍(小花在院子裡玩耍)，笑声传遍四方(笑聲傳遍四方)。
    - Xiǎo Huā zài yuànzi lǐ wánshuǎ, xiàoshēng chuánbiàn sìfāng.
    - Little Hua plays in the yard, and her laughter spreads in all directions.
### 小妹妹(小妹妹) → 妹妹(妹妹)
- **definition:** younger sister
- **pinyin:** mèimei
- **components:**
  - 妹
    - mèi
    - younger sister
  - 妹
    - mèi
    - younger sister
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 妹(妹) (mèi, "younger sister") repeated emphasizes the familial role of a younger female sibling.
  - **interpretation:** The duplication of 妹(妹) (mèi, "younger sister") strengthens its meaning, clearly indicating the familial relationship of a younger female sibling in a family.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一个妹妹(我有一個妹妹)。
    - wǒ yǒu yī gè mèimei.
    - I have a younger sister.
  - 妹妹很可爱(妹妹很可愛)，她总是带来笑声(她總是帶來笑聲)。
    - mèimei hěn kě'ài, tā zǒng shì dài lái xiào shēng.
    - My younger sister is very cute, and she always brings laughter.
  - 夜深人静时(夜深人靜時)，妹妹在窗前轻轻弹琴(妹妹在窗前輕輕彈琴)。
    - yè shēn rén jìng shí, mèimei zài chuāng qián qīng qīng tán qín.
    - In the quiet of the deep night, my younger sister gently plays the zither by the window.
### 小妹妹(小妹妹) → 妹妹(妹妹) → 妹(妹)
- **definition:** younger sister
- **pinyin:** mèi
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - female, woman
  - 未
    - mèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 女(女) (nǚ, "female, woman") + phonetic: 未(未) (mèi) =
    - female with 未 sound ->
    - younger sister
  - **interpretation:** 妹(妹) (mèi, "younger sister") combines the semantic element 女(女) (nǚ, "female, woman") with the phonetic 未(未) (mèi). This suggests a female role specifically tied to being a younger sibling, with 未 lending its pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 我有个妹妹(我有個妹妹)，她很可爱(她很可愛)。
    - Wǒ yǒu gè mèimei, tā hěn kě'ài.
    - I have a younger sister, she is very cute.
  - 放学后(放學後)，妹妹一起跑去公园(妹妹一起跑去公園)。
    - Fàngxué hòu, mèimei yīqǐ pǎo qù gōngyuán.
    - After school, my younger sister runs to the park together.
### 小妹妹(小妹妹) → 妹妹(妹妹) → 妹(妹) → 女(女)
- **definition:** female, woman
- **pinyin:** nǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a woman with breasts kneeling or standing ->
    - modern form reversed facing right
  - **interpretation:** The character 女(nǚ, "woman") originally depicted a woman with pronounced breasts, illustrating the concept of 'woman.' Over time, the pictogram evolved to face right in its modern form, losing details like the left breast to simplification.
- **examples:**
  - 她是一位有魅力的女(她是一位有魅力的女)。
    - Tā shì yī wèi yǒu mèi lì de nǚ.
    - She is an attractive woman.
  - 那位女战士英勇无畏(那位女戰士英勇無畏)，带领大家前行(帶領大家前行)。
    - Nà wèi nǚ zhàn shì yīng yǒng wú wèi, dài lǐng dà jiā qián xíng.
    - That female warrior is brave and fearless, leading everyone forward.
### 小妹妹(小妹妹) → 妹妹(妹妹) → 妹(妹) → 未(未)
- **pinyin:** mèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts branches of tree before growth
  - **interpretation:** The character 未(未) (mèi, 'not yet') originally depicted a tree with incomplete branches, symbolizing something unfinished or not yet in existence. This visual metaphor accurately conveys the concept of 'not yet.'
- **examples:**

    - Tā mèi chūmén.
    - He has not left.
  - 消息未传来(消息未傳來)，大家很担心(大家很擔心)。
    - Xiāoxī mèi chuán lái, dàjiā hěn dānxīn.
    - The news hasn't arrived, and everyone is worried.
  - 会议未开始(會議未開始)，请稍等(請稍等)。
    - Huìyì mèi kāishǐ, qǐng shāo děng.
    - The meeting hasn't started, please wait a moment.
