## little sister; young girl
### xiǎomèimei
---
- **definition:** little sister; young girl
- **pinyin:** xiǎomèimei
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little; young; junior
  - 妹妹
    - mèimei
    - younger sister; little sister; young girl (colloquial); girl (informal address)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小 (small; young) + 妹妹 (younger sister) = little sister; young girl.
  - **interpretation:** Literally “small younger-sister,” used for one’s younger sister or as a friendly way to refer to a young girl. In address, it can sound affectionate or casual depending on context.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一个小妹妹(我有一個小妹妹)。
    - Wǒ yǒu yí gè xiǎo mèimei.
    - I have a little sister.
  - 她还是小妹妹，所以不能独自回家(她還是小妹妹，所以不能獨自回家)。
    - Tā hái shì xiǎo mèimei, suǒyǐ bùnéng dúzì huíjiā.
    - She’s still a young girl, so she can’t go home alone.
  - 阿姨对那位小妹妹很亲切(阿姨對那位小妹妹很親切)。
    - Āyí duì nà wèi xiǎo mèimei hěn qīnqiè.
    - The lady is very kind to that little girl.
### 小妹妹 → 小
- **definition:** small; little; young; junior
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: three small marks/dots representing smallness; later the dots elongated; Shuowen misread it as 会意: 八 + 丨.
  - **interpretation:** Three short strokes portray a tiny object or the idea of smallness. As the script evolved, the dots were drawn as short lines, which led to the later but incorrect analysis of 八 plus 丨.
- **examples:**
  - 这个苹果很小(這個蘋果很小)。
    - Zhège píngguǒ hěn xiǎo.
    - This apple is very small.
  - 他把字写得太小(他把字寫得太小)。
    - Tā bǎ zì xiě de tài xiǎo.
    - He wrote the characters too small.
  - 小问题也要重视(小問題也要重視)。
    - Xiǎo wèntí yě yào zhòngshì.
    - Small problems should also be taken seriously.
### 小妹妹 → 妹妹
- **definition:** younger sister; little sister; young girl (colloquial); girl (informal address)
- **pinyin:** mèimei
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** reduplicated kinship term
  - **description:** 妹 ×2 = younger sister; reduplication adds an affectionate/familiar tone.
  - **interpretation:** Reduplicating kinship terms makes them colloquial and warm (cf. 妈妈, 爸爸), so 妹妹 feels affectionate. It can refer to one’s younger sister or be used as a polite address to a young girl.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一个妹妹(我有一個妹妹)。
    - Wǒ yǒu yí gè mèimei.
    - I have a younger sister.
  - 我和妹妹去看电影(我和妹妹去看電影)。
    - Wǒ hé mèimei qù kàn diànyǐng.
    - My younger sister and I are going to watch a movie.
  - 那位妹妹，请这边走(那位妹妹，請這邊走)。
    - Nà wèi mèimei, qǐng zhè biān zǒu.
    - Young lady, please come this way.
