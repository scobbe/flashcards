## martial arts circles
### wǔlín
---
- **definition:** martial arts circles
- **pinyin:** wǔlín
- **components:**
  - 武
    - wǔ
    - martial; military
  - 林
    - lín
    - forest; circles
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 武 (wǔ, "martial; military") refers to martial arts or military matters, and 林 (lín, "forest; circles") metaphorically represents a circle or domain, often used to describe a community or field of interest.
  - **interpretation:** 武林 (wǔlín, "martial arts circles") refers to the community or society associated with martial arts practitioners and activities. The term evokes the idea of a wide network or 'forest' of martial arts practitioners and culture.
- **examples:**
  - 他热爱(熱愛)武林。
    - Tā rè'ài wǔlín.
    - He loves the martial arts circles.
  - 年轻(輕)人拜师(師)武林，渴望学习绝(學習絕)世武功。
    - Niánqīngrén bàishī wǔlín, kěwàng xuéxí juéshì wǔgōng.
    - Young people seek apprenticeship in the martial arts circles, eager to learn extraordinary martial skills.
  - 古代英雄驰骋(馳騁)武林，留下传(傳)奇故事。
    - Gǔdài yīngxióng chíchěng wǔlín, liú xià chuánqí gùshì.
    - Ancient heroes roamed the martial arts circles, leaving behind legendary tales.
### 武林 → 武
- **definition:** martial; military
- **pinyin:** wǔ
- **components:**
  - 戈
    - gē
    - blade
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot; to walk
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 戈 (gē, "blade") + 止 (zhǐ, "foot; to walk") =
    - army going on an expedition ->
    - displays strength
  - **interpretation:** The character 武(wǔ) combines 戈 (gē, "blade") representing weaponry and 止 (zhǐ, "foot; to walk") indicating movement or marching, to symbolize the movement of an armed force or military expedition, hence conveying the notion of martial strength.
- **examples:**
  - 战士依靠武(𠬜)，筑(築)起长(長)城防线(線)。
    - Zhànshì yīkào wǔ(𠬜), zhùqǐ chángchéng fángxiàn.
    - The soldier relies on martial strength to build a protective wall.
  - 古时将领以武(𠬜)守国(古時將領以武(𠬜)守國)，将敌(將敵)人拒于门(於門)外。
    - Gǔshí jiānglǐng yǐ wǔ(𠬜) shǒuguó, jiāng dírén jù yú ménwài.
    - In ancient times, generals defended the country with martial might, keeping enemies at bay.
  - 边塞隐约传来武(𠬜)之气势(邊塞隱約傳來武(𠬜)之氣勢)。
    - Biānsài yǐnyuē chuán lái wǔ(𠬜) zhī qìshì.
    - At the frontier, the aura of martial strength can be faintly felt.
### 武林 → 武 → 戈
- **definition:** blade
- **pinyin:** gē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a spear or halberd, a type of weapon
  - **interpretation:** The character 戈(gē, 'spear') visually represents a spear or halberd, highlighting its function and importance as a weapon in ancient Chinese warfare.
- **examples:**
  - 古代战(戰)士持戈，勇闯敌营(闖敵營)。
    - Gǔdài zhànshì chí gē, yǒng chuǎng dí yíng.
    - Ancient warriors wielded the spear, bravely attacking the enemy camp.
  - 博物馆(館)珍藏古兵器，展厅陈(廳陳)列戈。
    - Bówùguǎn zhēncáng gǔ bīngqì, zhǎntīng chénliè gē.
    - The museum preserves ancient weapons, and the exhibition hall displays a spear.
### 武林 → 武 → 止
- **definition:** foot; to walk
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **components:**
  - 趾
    - zhǐ
    - foot
  - 龰
    - yù
    - stylized feet
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original: Foot pointing up with 3 toes and a sole ->
    - Stylized as 龰 ->
    - Derivative 趾 refers to original 'foot' meaning
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicting a footprint with toes and a sole, 止 (zhǐ, "foot") represents the foot. The character's form resembles a foot with three toes, analogous to how the hand is represented with fingers in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 他受伤(傷)的止需要静养(靜養)。
    - Tā shòu shāng de zhǐ xū yào jìng yǎng.
    - His injured foot needs adequate rest.
  - 他走路时(時)小心谨(謹)慎，因为(爲)止若受伤(傷)便难(難)前行。
    - Tā zǒu lù shí xiǎo xīn jǐn shèn, yīn wèi zhǐ ruò shòu shāng biàn nán qián xíng.
    - He walks carefully because if his foot gets injured, it becomes difficult to move forward.
### 武林 → 武 → 止 → 趾
- **definition:** foot
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **components:**
  - 足
    - zú
    - foot
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - stop
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 足 (zú, "foot") + 止 (zhǐ, "stop") =
    - semantic: foot + phonetic: stop ->
    - toe
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 足 (zú, "foot") as the semantic indicator and 止 (zhǐ, "stop") as the phonetic component to represent the idea of a 'toe,' which is a part of the foot.
- **examples:**
  - 他的趾因寒冷而僵硬了。
    - tā de zhǐ yīn hánlěng ér jiāngyìng le.
    - His toe became stiff due to the cold.
  - 我走路时(時)，不小心扭到了趾。
    - wǒ zǒulù shí, bù xiǎoxīn niǔ dào le zhǐ.
    - While I was walking, I accidentally twisted my toe.
  - 古代剑(劍)客战斗时(戰鬥時)，常因疏忽伤(傷)及趾。
    - gǔdài jiànkè zhàndòu shí, cháng yīn shūhū shāng jí zhǐ.
    - Ancient swordsmen often injured their toes due to carelessness during battles.
### 武林 → 武 → 止 → 趾 → 足
- **definition:** foot
- **pinyin:** zú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a leg ->
    - originally depicted as ⽦ ->
    - represents 'foot'
  - **interpretation:** The character 足(zú, 'foot') is a pictographic representation of a human leg, which directly portrays the concept of a 'foot'. The pictographic origin emphasizes its fundamental meaning in the language.
- **examples:**
  - 他受伤后(傷後)，他的足不能动(動)。
    - tā shòushāng hòu, tā de zú bù néng dòng。
    - After he was injured, his foot couldn't move.
  - 小明打球时(時)，不小心扭伤(傷)了足。
    - xiǎo míng dǎ qiú shí, bù xiǎoxīn niǔ shāng le zú。
    - When Xiaoming was playing ball, he accidentally twisted his foot.
  - 我每天锻炼(鍛鍊)足部。
    - wǒ měitiān duànliàn zúbù。
    - I exercise my foot muscles every day.
### 武林 → 武 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a footprint pointing up, originally meaning 'foot'; stylized as 龰
  - **interpretation:** 龰 is a stylized representation of a footprint with three toes and a sole, indicating its original association with the meaning 'foot'. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this depiction to represent a more specific aspect of a foot. It is distinct from characters like 夊 (suī, 'walk slowly') and 夂 (zhǐ, 'go'), which feature a different orientation of the toes.
### 武林 → 武 → 止 → 手
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a hand with fingers; top stroke is a bent over middle finger, horizontal strokes represent fingers
  - **interpretation:** The character 手 (shǒu, "hand") is a direct pictorial representation of a human hand with all five fingers, which distinguishes it from similar characters representing claws or simplified fingers. This visually conveys its meaning as a hand clearly.
- **examples:**
  - 他用手写(寫)字。
    - Tā yòng shǒu xiě zì.
    - He writes with his hand.
  - 妈妈牵着(媽媽牽著)我的手走路。
    - Māmā qiān zhe wǒ de shǒu zǒu lù.
    - Mom holds my hand while walking.
  - 他的手很温(溫)暖，带给(帶給)我力量。
    - Tā de shǒu hěn wēn nuǎn, dài gěi wǒ lìliàng.
    - His hand is very warm, giving me strength.
### 武林 → 林
- **definition:** forest; circles
- **pinyin:** lín
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 木 (mù, "tree") + 木 (mù, "tree") =
    - multiple trees ->
    - forest
  - **interpretation:** 林 (lín, "forest") uses the repetition of 木 (mù, "tree") to visually and conceptually represent a forest, which consists of many trees grouped together. This ideogram effectively captures the collective meaning through duplication.
- **examples:**
  - 我走进(進)幽静(靜)的林。
    - Wǒ zǒu jìn yōujìng de lín.
    - I walked into the quiet forest.
  - 孩子们(們)在林中围(圍)成一圈，嬉戏声(戲聲)此起彼伏。
    - Háizimen zài lín zhōng wéi chéng yī quān, xīxì shēng cǐ qǐ bǐ fú.
    - The children formed a circle in the forest, and their laughter echoed continuously.
### 武林 → 林 → 木
- **definition:** tree
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tree with branches and roots
  - **interpretation:** The character 木(mù) depicts a tree, showing the trunk in the center and two slanting lines representing branches or roots. It directly signifies the concept of wood or a tree.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)把椅子的主要材料是木。
    - Zhè bǎ yǐzi de zhǔyào cáiliào shì mù.
    - The main material of this chair is wood.
  - 工匠精心雕刻木，制成精美家具。
    - Gōngjiàng jīngxīn diāo kè mù, zhìchéng jīngměi jiājù.
    - The craftsman carefully carved the wood, crafting exquisite furniture.
