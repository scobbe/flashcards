## martial arts circles
### wǔlín
---
- **definition:** martial arts circles
- **pinyin:** wǔlín
- **components:**
  - 武
    - wǔ
    - martial; military
  - 林
    - lín
    - forest
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 武(武) (wǔ, "martial; military") and 林(林) (lín, "forest") combine to form the word 武林(武林) (wǔlín, "martial arts circles"), which metaphorically refers to the 'world' or 'community' of martial artists.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 武(武) (wǔ, "martial") and 林(林) (lín, "forest") evokes the imagery of a 'forest' of martial artists, symbolizing a densely populated world filled with martial arts practitioners, traditions, and stories.
- **examples:**
  - 他在武林中习武(他在武林中習武)。
    - Tā zài wǔlín zhōng xí wǔ.
    - He practices martial arts in the martial arts circles.
  - 武林门派众多(武林門派眾多)，武术流派各异(武術流派各異)。
    - Wǔlín ménpài zhòngduō, wǔshù liúpài gè yì.
    - There are many schools in the martial arts world, and the martial arts styles vary.
  - 他踏入武林寻求挑战(他踏入武林尋求挑戰)。
    - Tā tàrù wǔlín xúnqiú tiǎozhàn.
    - He steps into the martial arts realm in search of challenges.
### 武林(武林) → 武(武)
- **definition:** martial; military
- **pinyin:** wǔ
- **components:**
  - 戈
    - gē
    - blade
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot; to walk; to stop
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 戈(戈) (gē, "blade") + 止(止) (zhǐ, "foot; to walk; to stop") =
    - army going on an expedition ->
    - martial
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 戈(戈) (gē, "blade") and 止(止) (zhǐ, "foot; to walk; to stop") suggests mobilizing with weapons, reflecting the concept of martial activities. Historically, the interpretation that it means 'to stop violence' was a likely misinterpretation of its components.
  - **simplification:** None, as 武 is used both in simplified and traditional script without modification.
- **examples:**
  - 他学习传统武术(他學習傳統武術)。
    - Tā xuéxí chuántǒng wǔshù.
    - He studies traditional martial arts.
  - 他追求文武双全(他追求文武雙全)，以实现理想(以實現理想)。
    - Tā zhuīqiú wénwǔ shuāngquán, yǐ shíxiàn lǐxiǎng.
    - He strives for excellence in both literary and martial arts to achieve his ideals.
### 武林(武林) → 武(武) → 戈(戈)
- **definition:** blade
- **pinyin:** gē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a dagger-axe weapon
  - **interpretation:** The character 戈(gē, "dagger-axe") visually resembles a simple depiction of an ancient weapon used in Chinese warfare. It illustrates the basic structure of a pole with a blade attached, which was typical for the weapon.
- **examples:**
  - 戈是一种古老的兵器(戈是一種古老的兵器)。
    - gē shì yī zhǒng gǔlǎo de bīngqì.
    - The dagger-axe is an ancient weapon.
  - 古人使用戈战斗(古人使用戈戰鬥)，赢得了许多胜利(贏得了許多勝利)。
    - gǔrén shǐyòng gē zhàndòu, yíngdé le xǔduō shènglì.
    - The ancients used dagger-axes to fight, winning many victories.
### 武林(武林) → 武(武) → 止(止)
- **definition:** foot; to walk; to stop
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a footprint pointing up originally meaning 'foot'
  - **interpretation:** 止(止) (zhǐ, 'foot') is a pictogram depicting a footprint pointing upwards, emphasizing its connection to the idea of 'foot'. Its visual form with three toes aligns with similar characters representing human appendages.
- **examples:**
  - 他走路时不慎扭伤了止(他走路時不慎扭傷了止)。
    - Tā zǒulù shí bù shèn niǔshāng le zhǐ.
    - He accidentally sprained his foot while walking.
  - 冬日寒风刺骨(冬日寒風刺骨)，使他的止感到冰冷(使他的止感到冰冷)。
    - Dōngrì hánfēng cìgǔ, shǐ tā de zhǐ gǎndào bīnglěng.
    - The piercing winter wind made his foot feel icy.
  - 长途跋涉后(長途跋涉後)，他的止疲惫不堪(他的止疲憊不堪)。
    - Chángtú báshè hòu, tā de zhǐ píbèi bùkān.
    - After the long journey, his foot was utterly exhausted.
### 武林(武林) → 林(林)

- **pinyin:** lín
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 木
    - mù

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 木(木) (mù, "tree") + 木(木) (mù, "tree") =
    - multiple trees ->
    - forest
  - **interpretation:** The character 林(lín, "forest") intuitively makes sense as it combines two 木(木, "tree") characters to represent the idea of multiple trees grouped together, signifying a forest.

    - Birdsong and the fragrance of flowers fill the forest.

    - Bàofēng yǔ guòhòu, lín chóngxīn sànfā chū shēngjī.

### 武林(武林) → 林(林) → 木(木)
- **definition:** tree
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tree: branches on top, roots on the bottom
  - **interpretation:** 木(木) (mù, "tree") visually represents a tree with branches at the top and roots below. This pictorial depiction is straightforward, resembling the structure of a tree and its natural growth.
- **examples:**

    - There is a towering ancient tree in the courtyard.
  - 他走到森林深处(他走到森林深處)，看见一棵孤零零的木矗立在路旁(看見一棵孤零零的木矗立在路旁)。
    - Tā zǒu dào sēnlín shēnchù, kànjiàn yī kē gū línglíng de mù chùlì zài lùpáng.
    - He walked into the deep forest and saw a solitary tree standing by the roadside.
