## martial arts circles
### wǔlín
---
- **definition:** martial arts circles
- **pinyin:** wǔlín
- **components:**
  - 武
    - wǔ
    - martial; military
  - 林
    - lín
    - forest; circles
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 武(武) (wǔ, "martial; military") refers to martial arts or military matters, and 林(林) (lín, "forest; circles") metaphorically represents a circle or domain, often used to describe a community or field of interest.
  - **interpretation:** 武林(武林) (wǔlín, "martial arts circles") refers to the community or society associated with martial arts practitioners and activities. The term evokes the idea of a wide network or 'forest' of martial arts practitioners and culture.
- **examples:**
  - 他热爱武林(他熱愛武林)。
    - Tā rè'ài wǔlín.
    - He loves the martial arts circles.
  - 年轻人拜师武林(年輕人拜師武林)，渴望学习绝世武功(渴望學習絕世武功)。
    - Niánqīngrén bàishī wǔlín, kěwàng xuéxí juéshì wǔgōng.
    - Young people seek apprenticeship in the martial arts circles, eager to learn extraordinary martial skills.
  - 古代英雄驰骋武林(古代英雄馳騁武林)，留下传奇故事(留下傳奇故事)。
    - Gǔdài yīngxióng chíchěng wǔlín, liú xià chuánqí gùshì.
    - Ancient heroes roamed the martial arts circles, leaving behind legendary tales.
