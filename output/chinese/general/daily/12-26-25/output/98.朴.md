## plain; simple
### pǔ
---
- **definition:** plain; simple
- **pinyin:** pǔ
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood; tree
  - 卜
    - bǔ
    - to divine; divination
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 木 + phonetic: 卜
  - **interpretation:** 木 conveys the idea of wood, evoking natural, unadorned qualities. 卜 supplies the sound (approximating OC *poːɡ), guiding the reading pǔ and aligning with the sense of being plain or simple, like unworked wood.
  - **simplification:** Adopted in 1956 as the official simplified form of 樸 by selecting the preexisting 朴; this effectively keeps the semantic 木 while reducing the complex phonetic 菐 to the simpler 卜.
- **examples:**
  - 她的穿着很朴素(她的穿著很樸素)。
    - Tā de chuānzhuó hěn pǔsù.
    - Her attire is very simple.
  - 我喜欢他朴实的性格(我喜歡他樸實的性格)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan tā pǔshí de xìnggé.
    - I like his plain, down-to-earth personality.
  - 村里保留着朴拙的风貌(村裡保留著樸拙的風貌)。
    - Cūn lǐ bǎoliúzhe pǔzhuō de fēngmào.
    - The village retains a rustic, unadorned look.
### 朴(樸) → 木(木)
- **definition:** wood; tree
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram (象形)
  - **description:** a tree: branches on top, roots on the bottom (more visible in earlier forms).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a tree with spreading branches above and roots below, as seen clearly in early scripts. The vertical stroke is the trunk, while the short horizontal strokes became stylized limbs and roots in the regular form.
- **examples:**
  - 这张桌子是木头做的(這張桌子是木頭做的)。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi shì mùtou zuò de.
    - This table is made of wood.
  - 山上长满了树木(山上長滿了樹木)。
    - Shān shang zhǎng mǎn le shùmù.
    - The mountainside is covered with trees.
  - 木材被运到工厂加工(木材被運到工廠加工)。
    - Mùcái bèi yùn dào gōngchǎng jiāgōng.
    - The timber was transported to the factory for processing.
### 朴(樸) → 卜(蔔)
- **definition:** to divine; divination
- **pinyin:** bǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram (象形): crack in an oracle bone used for divination.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the branching crack made on turtle shell or bone during pyromancy, whose patterns were read as omens. The simple vertical line with a side notch mirrors the appearance of such fissures.
- **examples:**
  - 我们请他卜一卦(我們請他卜一卦)。
    - Wǒmen qǐng tā bǔ yī guà.
    - We asked him to divine a hexagram.
  - 他会卜吉凶(他會卜吉凶)。
    - Tā huì bǔ jíxiōng.
    - He can divine whether things will be auspicious or inauspicious.
  - 用龟甲卜问前途(用龜甲卜問前途)。
    - Yòng guījiǎ bǔwèn qiántú.
    - They use turtle shells to divine the future.
