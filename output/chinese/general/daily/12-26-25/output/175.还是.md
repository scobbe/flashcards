### háishi
---
- **traditional:** 還是
- **definition:** still; yet
- **pinyin:** háishi
- **components:**
  - 还(還)
    - hái

  - 是

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 还(還) (hái, "still; yet") is a word indicating continuation or persistence in a state or action. 是 (shì, "be") is a copula, used to link the subject and predicate. The combination 还(還)是( ) means "still" or "yet," suggesting the persistence of a state or situation despite expectations to the contrary.
  - **interpretation:** The word 还(還)是( ) (háishi, "still; yet") conveys the idea of something remaining unchanged or continuing in spite of circumstances that might suggest otherwise. It merges the concept of continuity from 还(還) (hái, "still; yet") with the confirming nature of 是 (shì, "be").
  - **simplification:** simplified later from 還是
