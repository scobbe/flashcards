## bottle opener
### kāipíngqì
---
- **definition:** bottle opener
- **pinyin:** kāipíngqì
- **components:**
  - 开(開)
    - kāi
    - open
  - 瓶
    - píng
    - bottle
  - 器
    - qì
    - tool; device
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 开(開) (kāi, "open") refers to the action of opening something, 瓶(瓶) (píng, "bottle") indicates the object being opened, and 器(器) (qì, "tool; device") refers to a tool for performing a task, in this case, opening bottles.
  - **interpretation:** This word describes a tool specifically designed to open bottles, combining the action of opening and the object, a bottle, with a tool.
  - **simplification:** 开(開) is the simplified form of the traditional character 開.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了一个新开瓶器(我買了一個新開瓶器)。
    - Wǒ mǎile yīgè xīn kāipíngqì.
    - I bought a new bottle opener.
  - 他找不到开瓶器(他找不到開瓶器)，只好让朋友帮忙(只好讓朋友幫忙)。
    - Tā zhǎo bù dào kāipíngqì, zhǐhǎo ràng péngyǒu bāngmáng.
    - He couldn’t find the bottle opener, so he had to ask a friend for help.
  - 开瓶器是打开啤酒瓶的好工具(開瓶器是打開啤酒瓶的好工具)。
    - Kāipíngqì shì dǎkāi píjiǔpíng de hǎo gōngjù.
    - The bottle opener is a good tool for opening beer bottles.
### 开瓶器(開瓶器) → 开(開)
- **definition:** open
- **pinyin:** kāi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 門(門) (mén, "door") + 一(一) (yī, "latch") + 廾(廾) (gǒng, "hands") → pair of hands opening latched door → opening
  - **interpretation:** The traditional form 開(開) (kāi, "open") symbolizes opening a door with hands, where 門(門) (mén, "door") represents the door itself, while 一(一) (yī, "latch") and 廾(廾) (gǒng, "hands") act together to indicate removal of a barrier.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 開 by eliminating 門(門) (mén, "door"), retaining only the 幵(幵) (components resembling two 干) which focuses on the concept of trunk parts, visually simplifying it without changing the meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 请开门(請開門)。
    - Qǐng kāi mén.
    - Please open the door.
  - 他开了灯(他開了燈)。
    - Tā kāile dēng.
    - He turned on the light.
  - 天微亮时(天微亮時)，商店开门营业(商店開門營業)。
    - Tiān wēiliàng shí, shāngdiàn kāimén yíngyè.
    - At dawn, the store opened for business.
### 开瓶器(開瓶器) → 瓶(瓶)
- **definition:** bottle
- **pinyin:** píng
- **components:**
  - 并(併)
    - bìng
    - combine
  - 瓦
    - wǎ
    - earthenware
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 并(併) (bìng, "combine") + Semantic: 瓦(瓦) (wǎ, "earthenware") =
    - earthenware vessel ->
    - indicates bottles originally made of pottery
  - **interpretation:** The use of 并(併) (bìng) provides the sound element, while 瓦(瓦) (wǎ) emphasizes the material typical of early bottles. While originally referring to pottery or bronze vessels, 瓶(píng) is now generalized to mean bottles of any material.
- **examples:**
  - 这瓶是玻璃做的(這瓶是玻璃做的)。
    - Zhè píng shì bōlí zuò de.
    - This bottle is made of glass.
  - 他小心地握住瓶(他小心地握住瓶)，准备倒出水来(準備倒出水來)。
    - Tā xiǎoxīn de wò zhù píng, zhǔnbèi dào chū shuǐ lái.
    - He carefully held the bottle, ready to pour out the water.
  - 旧瓶可用来装花(舊瓶可用來裝花)，美化家园(美化家園)。
    - Jiù píng kě yòng lái zhuāng huā, měihuà jiāyuán.
    - Old bottles can be used to hold flowers, beautifying the home.
