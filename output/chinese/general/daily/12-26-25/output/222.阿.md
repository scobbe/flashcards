### prefix/particle used in names or address
---
- **pinyin:** prefix/particle used in names or address
- **components:**
  - 阜
    - fù
    - mound
  - 可
    - kě
    - can
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Semantic 阜 (fù, 'mound') + phonetic 可 (kě, 'can') — originally meaning a big mound.
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the concept of a mound, which suggests a physical presence, with a phonetic element that contributes to its pronunciation. This gives the character a grounded and approachable feel.
- **examples:**
  - 阿姨，您好吗？
    - Āyí, nín hǎo ma？
    - Auntie, how are you?
  - 我叫阿明。
    - Wǒ jiào Āmíng。
    - My name is Amin.

- **examples:**
