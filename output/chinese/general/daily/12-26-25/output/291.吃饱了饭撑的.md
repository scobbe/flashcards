## have nothing better to do
### chī bǎo le fàn chēng de
---
- **traditional:** 吃飽了飯撐的
- **definition:** have nothing better to do
- **pinyin:** chī bǎo le fàn chēng de
- **components:**
  - 吃
    - chī
    - eat
  - 饱(飽)
    - bǎo
    - full
  - 了
    - le
    - [modal particle indicating completed action]
  - 饭(飯)
    - fàn
    - meal; rice
  - 撑(撐)
    - chēng
    - support; prop up
  - 的
    - de
    - [possessive particle whose function is to express possession, modification, or description]
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 吃 (chī, "eat"), 饱(飽) (bǎo, "full"), 了 (le, "[modal particle indicating completed action]"), 饭(飯) (fàn, "meal; rice"), 撑(撐) (chēng, "support; prop up"), 的 (de, "[possessive particle whose function is to express possession, modification, or description]")
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 吃饱了饭撑的 (chī bǎo le fàn chēng de) is a colloquial expression implying that someone, having eaten a fulfilling meal, engages in unnecessary or frivolous activities for lack of anything better to do. It humorously suggests that one's over-satisfaction has led them to overextend into doing trivial or senseless things.
  - **simplification:** The simplified characters are 吃饱了饭撑的.
- **examples:**
  - 他吃饱(飽)了饭撑(飯撐)的，整天无(無)所事事。
    - Tā chī bǎo le fàn chēng de, zhěng tiān wú suǒ shì shì.
    - He has nothing better to do and spends his day idly.
  - 朋友嘲笑他吃饱(飽)了饭撑(飯撐)的，因为(為)他没(沒)什么(麼)目标(標)。
    - Péngyǒu cháoxiào tā chī bǎo le fàn chēng de, yīnwèi tā méi shénme mùbiāo.
    - His friends mock him for having nothing better to do because he lacks ambition.
