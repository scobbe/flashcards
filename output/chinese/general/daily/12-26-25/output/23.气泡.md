## air bubble
### qìpào
---
- **definition:** air bubble
- **pinyin:** qìpào
- **components:**
  - 气(氣)
    - qì
    - air; gas; spirit
  - 泡
    - pào
    - bubble; foam; blister
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 气(氣) (qì, "air; gas") refers to air or gas, often considered the vital force in traditional Chinese thought. 泡(泡) (pào, "bubble") signifies a bubble. Combined, 气泡(氣泡) (qìpào) refers to an "air bubble" as it is a bubble filled with air.
  - **interpretation:** The word 气泡(氣泡) (qìpào) directly combines the concepts of air and a bubble, clearly describing a bubble filled with air.
- **examples:**
  - 气泡在水中闪烁(氣泡在水中閃爍)。
    - qìpào zài shuǐ zhōng shǎnshuò.
    - Air bubbles shimmer in the water.
  - 他喝汽水时(他喝汽水時)，欣赏气泡上升(欣賞氣泡上升)。
    - tā hē qìshuǐ shí, xīnshǎng qìpào shàngshēng.
    - He admires the rising air bubbles while drinking soda.
### 气泡(氣泡) → 气(氣)
- **definition:** air; gas; spirit
- **pinyin:** qì
- **components:**
  - 三
    - sān
    - three
  - 乞
    - qǐ
    - beg
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Clouds flowing through the sky ->
    - altered to avoid confusion with 三(三) (sān, "three") ->
    - 乞(乞) (qǐ, "beg") as a variant for phonetic borrowing
  - **interpretation:** The character 气(qì, "air") started as a pictogram representing clouds in the sky, which evolved to differentiate from the character for "three." Over time, 乞 was used as a variant for phonetic reasons, specifically for the meaning "to beg." The meanings 'air, flow' were later borrowed from 气.
- **examples:**
  - 房间里的气很清新(房間裡的氣很清新)。
    - Fángjiān lǐ de qì hěn qīngxīn。

  - 打开窗户(打開窗戶)，让新鲜的气进来(讓新鮮的氣進來)。
    - Dǎkāi chuānghù, ràng xīnxiān de qì jìnlái。
    - Open the window to let fresh air in.
  - 他深吸一口气(他深吸一口氣)，感觉心情舒畅(感覺心情舒暢)。
    - Tā shēn xī yīkǒu qì, gǎnjué xīnqíng shūchàng。
    - He took a deep breath and felt his mood lift.
