## travel around
### zhōuyóu
---
- **traditional:** 周遊
- **definition:** travel around
- **pinyin:** zhōuyóu
- **components:**
  - 周
    - zhōu
    - circle; complete; cycle
  - 游(遊)
    - yóu
    - travel; wander; roam
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 周 (zhōu, "circle; complete") + 游(遊) (yóu, "travel; wander") =
    - to travel around; to roam
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 周 (zhōu, "circle; complete") and 游(遊) (yóu, "travel; wander") conveys the idea of traveling in a circular or comprehensive manner, suggesting extensive travel or wandering across different places.
- **examples:**
  - 他周游(遊)世界。
    - Tā zhōuyóu shìjiè.
    - He traveled around the world.
  - 艺术(藝術)家周游(遊)名城，寻(尋)找灵(靈)感源泉。
    - Yìshùjiā zhōuyóu míngchéng, xúnzhǎo línggǎn yuánquán.
    - The artist traveled around famous cities, seeking sources of inspiration.
  - 学(學)者周游(遊)古国(國)，采撷(採擷)典故。
    - Xuézhě zhōuyóu gǔguó, cǎixié diǎngù.
    - The scholar traveled around ancient kingdoms, collecting classic allusions.
### 周( )游(遊) → 周
- **definition:** circle; complete; cycle
- **pinyin:** zhōu
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 用
    - yòng
    - to use
  - 業
    - yè
    - to carve
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Pictogram: a jade artifact with carved patterns ->
    - Character 業 (yè, "to carve") evolved ->
    - borrowed as a country name ->
    - 口 (kǒu, "mouth") added for distinction ->
    - similar top to 用 (yòng, "to use") in later form
  - **interpretation:** 周 (zhōu, "circle") originally depicted a carved jade artifact, indicating completeness or thoroughness. Over time, it was borrowed for the country name and modified to distinguish it with an added component 口 (kǒu, "mouth"), resembling 用 (yòng, "to use") in later forms.
- **examples:**
  - 钟摆转(鐘擺轉)了一周。
    - Zhōngbǎi zhuǎn le yī zhōu.
    - The pendulum completed one full cycle.
  - 他沿公园(園)小径(徑)走了一周，满怀(滿懷)期待回到起点(點)。
    - Tā yán gōngyuán xiǎojìng zǒu le yī zhōu, mǎn huái qídài huí dào qǐdiǎn.
    - He walked a complete circle along the park path, returning to the starting point with anticipation.
  - 风车转动着(風車轉動著)一周。
    - Fēng chē zhuǎndòng zhe yī zhōu.
    - The windmill rotates in a full circle.
### 周( )游(遊) → 周 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 周( )游(遊) → 周 → 用
- **definition:** to use
- **pinyin:** yòng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a vessel with contents being poured out
  - **interpretation:** 用 (yòng, 'use') visually represents the action of utilizing or employing, as depicted by a container pouring out its contents. This imagery effectively conveys the core idea of using resources or applying effort in a task.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)用心体会这(體會這)首诗(詩)。
    - qǐng yòng xīn tǐhuì zhè shǒu shī。
    - Please use your heart to appreciate this poem.
  - 他用笔写(筆寫)字，也用心记录(記錄)生活。
    - tā yòng bǐ xiě zì，yě yòng xīn jìlù shēnghuó。
    - He writes with a pen, and also uses his heart to record life.
  - 老板(闆)用新策略吸引顾(顧)客。
    - lǎobǎn yòng xīn cèlüè xīyǐn gùkè。
    - The boss uses new strategies to attract customers.
### 周( )游(遊) → 周 → 業
- **definition:** to carve
- **pinyin:** yè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted a person lifting a beam from which bells and chimes hang ->
    - representation of a tool or implement ->
    - evolved to include chisel and large person for launching a great attack ->
    - current form
  - **interpretation:** The character originates as a pictogram of a person lifting a beam, symbolizing a tool used in work, reflecting the idea of effort or task associated with various types of work.
- **examples:**
  - 工匠專心業在石頭上，創造獨特紋理。
    - Gōngjiàng zhuānxīn yè zài shítou shàng, chuàngzào dútè wénlǐ.
    - The craftsman carved unique textures on the stone with full concentration.
  - 他用刻刀業出精美圖案。
    - Tā yòng kèdāo yè chū jīngměi tú'àn.
    - He carved exquisite patterns using a carving knife.
### 周( )游(遊) → 游(遊)
- **traditional:** 遊
- **definition:** travel; wander; roam
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 斿
    - yóu
    - travel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 水 (shuǐ, "water") + Phonetic: 斿 (yóu, originally having meaning related to travel) =
    - movement through or on water ->
    - swim/travel/wander
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the water radical with a component originally meaning travel, highlighting both the aspect of swimming in water and wandering or traveling. This reflects the versatility in its meanings.
  - **simplification:** Traditional 遊 (yóu, "wander") uses 辵 (chuò, "walk") for traveling, simplified by replacing it with 水 (shuǐ, "water"), focusing on swimming, which in Chinese culture is closely related to wandering or traveling freely.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)游泳。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān yóuyǒng.
    - I like swimming.
  - 我们周(們週)末去郊外游(遊)，感受大自然。
    - Wǒmen zhōumò qù jiāowài yóu, gǎnshòu dàzìrán.
    - We will travel to the countryside on the weekend and enjoy nature.
  - 他梦(夢)想环游(環遊)世界。
    - Tā mèngxiǎng huányóu shìjiè.
    - He dreams of traveling the world.
### 周( )游(遊) → 游(遊) → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flowing water
  - **interpretation:** 水 (shuǐ, 'water') is a pictogram that directly represents the appearance of flowing water, making it intuitive and easy to recognize. Its simplicity and direct visual reference to its meaning have allowed it to remain unchanged over millennia.
- **examples:**
  - 水是一种(種)生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì yī zhǒng shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 夏天很热(熱)，喝水能解暑。
    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, hē shuǐ néng jiě shǔ.
    - It is very hot in summer, drinking water can relieve the heat.
  - 河水潺潺，沿岸绿树(綠樹)成荫(蔭)。
    - Hé shuǐ chánchán, yán'àn lǜshù chéng yīn.
    - The river flows babbling, and green trees along the bank provide shade.

### 周( )游(遊) → 游(遊) → 斿 → 㫃 → 方
- **definition:** square
- **pinyin:** fāng
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī

  - 刀
    - dāo

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 一 (yī, "one") + 刀 (dāo, "sword") =
    - original depiction of blade's tip ->
    - borrowed for "side" ->
    - now "square"
  - **interpretation:** Initially depicting the blade of a sword, 方 (fāng, "square") was later borrowed for its current meaning due to its association with edges and boundaries, metaphorically expanded to define sides and squares.

    - Wǒ mǎi de huāpíng shì fāngxíng de, shèjì jiǎnjié yōuměi.
    - The vase I bought is square, with a simple and elegant design.
