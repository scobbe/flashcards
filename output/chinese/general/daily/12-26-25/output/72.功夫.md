## kung fu
### gōngfu
---
- **definition:** kung fu
- **pinyin:** gōngfu
- **components:**
  - 功
    - gōng
    - achievement; skill
  - 夫
    - fū
    - man; master
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 功 (gōng, "achievement; skill") + 夫 (fū, "man; master") =
    - skill or art of a master, referring to martial arts or any skill acquired with effort and patience.
  - **interpretation:** The term 功夫 (gōngfu) initially referred to any skilled practice requiring time and dedication. Today, it is broadly associated with Chinese martial arts, highlighting the mastery and effort involved.
- **examples:**
  - 他( )练(練)功( )夫( )。
    - Tā liàn gōngfu.
    - He practices kung fu.
  - 他( )练(練)习(習)功( )夫( )，追( )求( )内(內)心( )平( )静(靜)。
    - Tā liànxí gōngfu, zhuīqiú nèixīn píngjìng.
    - He practices kung fu, pursuing inner peace.
  - 学(學)好( )功( )夫( )，可以提高自信。
    - Xué hǎo gōngfu, kěyǐ tígāo zìxìn.
    - Mastering kung fu can boost self-confidence.
### 功夫 → 功
- **definition:** achievement; skill
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **components:**
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work
  - 力
    - lì
    - strength
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound and ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic: 工 (gōng, "work") + semantic: 力 (lì, "strength") =
    - work combined with strength ->
    - achievement or skill
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the idea of physical or mental work 工 (gōng, "work") with 力 (lì, "strength") to convey the concept of successfully applying effort towards an accomplishment.
- **examples:**

- **definition:** work
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**

  - **interpretation:** 工 (gōng, "work") represents either a tool used in agriculture for marking or a carpenter's square, reflecting its connection to traditional manual labor and craftsmanship.
- **examples:**

### 功夫 → 夫
- **definition:** man; master
- **pinyin:** fū
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a variant form of 大 (dà, "big") representing an adult man with a hairpin ->
    - became associated with 夫 (fū, "man").
  - **interpretation:** 夫 (fū, "man") was visually similar to 大 (dà, "big") and was originally interchangeable. The addition of the horizontal stroke was interpreted as a hairpin, symbolizing adulthood for men.
- **examples:**
  - 此( )夫( )心( )怀(懷)大( )志( )。
    - Cǐ fū xīn huái dà zhì.
    - This man has lofty aspirations.
  - 他( )勤( )劳(勞)努( )力( )，堪( )称(稱)良( )夫( )。
    - Tā qín láo nǔ lì, kān chēng liáng fū.
    - He works diligently and is regarded as an exemplary man.
### 功夫 → 夫 → 大
- **definition:** big
- **pinyin:** dà
- **components:**
  - 夫
    - fū
    - man
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a person facing forward ->
    - Borrowed phonetically for 'big'; original form of 夫 (fū, 'man')
  - **interpretation:** The character 大(dà) depicts a person facing forward, representing 'big' by suggesting the idea of someone standing prominently. Although often thought of as a person with outstretched arms, early scripts do not support this interpretation. It was originally the form of 夫 (fū, 'man') and was later phonetically borrowed for the meaning 'big.'

  - 大( )人( )物( )登( )场(場)，留( )下( )传(傳)奇( )。
