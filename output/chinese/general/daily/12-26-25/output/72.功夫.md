## 功夫(功夫)
---
## 
### kung fu
---
- **pinyin:** kung fu
- **components:**
  - 功
    - gōng
    - achievement; merit; result; work
  - 夫
    - fū
    - man; husband; person; gentleman
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 功(功) (gōng, 'achievement') + 夫(夫) (fū, 'man') — achievement man
  - **interpretation:** 功夫 combines the idea of achievement with that of a person being skilled or refined. This makes sense as it represents a person's skill level achieved through hard work and practice.
- **examples:**
  - 我在练习功夫(我在練習功夫)。
    - Wǒ zài liànxí gōngfu.
    - I am practicing kung fu.
  - 他是一个功夫大师(他是一個功夫大師)。
    - Tā shì yīgè gōngfu dàshī.
    - He is a kung fu master.
  - 功夫需要时间来磨练(功夫需要時間來磨練)。
    - Gōngfu xūyào shíjiān lái móliàn.
    - Kung fu requires time to hone.
### 功夫(功夫) → 功(功)
- **definition:** achievement; merit; result; work
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **components:**
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work
  - 力
    - lì
    - strength
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Phonetic 工 (gōng, 'work') + Semantic 力 (lì, 'strength') — work results from strength.
  - **interpretation:** The combination emphasizes that work powered by strength leads to achievements.
- **examples:**
  - 他的努力取得了巨大的功(功)。
    - tā de nǔlì qǔdéle jùdà de gōng(功)。
    - His efforts achieved great merit.
  - 成功需要付出很多功(功)。
    - chénggōng xūyào fùchū hěnduō gōng(功)。
    - Success requires a lot of work.
### 功夫(功夫) → 功(功) → 工(工)
- **definition:** work
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tool or instrument used for work, symbolizing craftsmanship.
  - **interpretation:** The character represents the concept of work or skill, depicted through the image of a tool, which is essential for craftsmanship.
- **examples:**
  - 我在工厂工作(我在工廠工作)。
    - Wǒ zài gōngchǎng gōngzuò.
    - I work in a factory.
  - 他是个很有工艺的人(他是個很有工藝的人)。
    - Tā shì gè hěn yǒu gōngyì de rén.
    - He is a very skilled craftsman.
### 功夫(功夫) → 功(功) → 力(力)
- **definition:** strength
- **pinyin:** lì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an arm bending out from the side of the body or a wooden plough.
  - **interpretation:** The representation of an arm suggests physical strength and effort, aligning with the character's meaning of strength.
- **examples:**
  - 我有力量(我有力量)。
    - wǒ yǒu lìliàng.
    - I have strength.
  - 通过努力(通過努力)，我变得更强壮(變得更強壯)。
    - tōngguò nǔlì, wǒ biàn dé gèng qiángzhuàng.
    - Through effort, I have become stronger.
### 功夫(功夫) → 夫(夫)
- **definition:** man; husband; person; gentleman
- **pinyin:** fū
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Originally depicts a man or husband, representing the concept of 'male' or 'husband'.
  - **interpretation:** The character shows a figure of a man, embodying the idea of a husband or male figure.
- **examples:**
  - 他是个好夫(他是個好夫)，总是照顾家庭(總是照顧家庭)。
    - Tā shì gè hǎo fū, zǒng shì zhàogù jiātíng.
    - He is a good husband, always taking care of the family.
  - 这个夫(這個夫)是我的朋友(是我的朋友)。
    - Zhège fū shì wǒ de péngyǒu.
    - This man is my friend.
