## sparkling water
### qìshuǐ
---
- **definition:** sparkling water
- **pinyin:** qìshuǐ
- **components:**
  - 汽
    - qì
    - steam; vapor
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 汽 (qì, "steam; vapor") + 水 (shuǐ, "water") =
    - water with vapor ->
    - sparkling water
  - **interpretation:** The word 汽水 (qìshuǐ) combines 汽 (qì, "steam; vapor") and 水 (shuǐ, "water"), suggesting water that contains bubbles of gas or vapor, typical of sparkling water. This aptly describes fizzy water beverages.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )喜( )欢(歡)喝( )汽( )水( )。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē qìshuǐ.
    - I like to drink sparkling water.
  - 夏( )天( )很( )热(熱)，一瓶汽水可以消暑。
    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, yī píng qìshuǐ kěyǐ xiāoshǔ.
    - It is very hot in summer, and a bottle of sparkling water can cool you down.

- **traditional:** 氣
- **definition:** air
- **pinyin:** qì
- **components:**
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 氣 (phono-semantic: 气 for sound + 米 for meaning) ->
    - originally 'steam from cooked rice' ->
    - later 'air, flow'
  - **interpretation:** The character 氣 combines 气, representing the sound, with 米 (mǐ, 'rice'), suggesting the original meaning of steam emanating from cooked rice. Over time, the meaning shifted to encompass 'air' or 'flow', capturing the essence of movement or circulation.
