## sparkling water
### qìshuǐ
---
- **definition:** sparkling water
- **pinyin:** qìshuǐ
- **components:**
  - 汽
    - qì
    - steam; vapor
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 汽 (qì, "steam; vapor") + 水 (shuǐ, "water") =
    - water with vapor ->
    - sparkling water
  - **interpretation:** The word 汽水 (qìshuǐ) combines 汽 (qì, "steam; vapor") and 水 (shuǐ, "water"), suggesting water that contains bubbles of gas or vapor, typical of sparkling water. This aptly describes fizzy water beverages.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )喜( )欢(歡)喝( )汽( )水( )。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē qìshuǐ.
    - I like to drink sparkling water.

    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, yī píng qìshuǐ kěyǐ xiāoshǔ.
    - It is very hot in summer, and a bottle of sparkling water can cool you down.

### 汽水 → 汽 → 气(氣)
- **traditional:** 氣
- **definition:** air
- **pinyin:** qì
- **components:**
  - 三
    - sān
    - three
  - 乞
    - qǐ
    - beg
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Clouds flowing through the sky ->
    - altered to avoid confusion with 三 (sān, "three") ->
    - 乞 (qǐ, "beg") as a variant for phonetic borrowing
  - **interpretation:** The character 气(qì, "air") started as a pictogram representing clouds in the sky, which evolved to differentiate from the character for "three." Over time, 乞 was used as a variant for phonetic reasons, specifically for the meaning "to beg." The meanings 'air, flow' were later borrowed from 气.
