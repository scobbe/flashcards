### drink
---
- **pinyin:** drink
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 何
    - hé
    - what
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Consists of the semantic component 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') and the phonetic component 何 (hé), suggesting the act of drinking with the mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the idea of the mouth with the sound of the phonetic component, relating directly to the action of drinking.
- **examples:**
  - 我渴了(我渴了)，想喝水(想喝水)。
    - Wǒ kě le, xiǎng hē shuǐ.
    - I am thirsty and want to drink water.
  - 喝茶(喝茶)是我的爱好(是我的愛好)。
    - Hē chá shì wǒ de àihào.
    - Drinking tea is my hobby.
### 喝(喝) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character is a pictograph depicting the shape of a mouth, which intuitively represents the concept of 'mouth' itself.
- **examples:**
  - 我吃东西(我吃東西)，需要用口(需要用口)。
    - Wǒ chī dōngxi, xūyào yòng kǒu.
    - I eat food, and I need to use my mouth.
  - 口腔(口腔)健康很重要(健康很重要)。
    - Kǒuqiāng jiànkāng hěn zhòngyào.
    - Oral health is very important.
### 喝(喝) → 何(何)
- **definition:** what
- **pinyin:** hé
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character 何 originally depicted a man with a pouch, reflecting the meaning of questioning or inquiry.
  - **interpretation:** The image of a man with a pouch symbolizes the act of searching for answers or 'what' one possesses.
- **examples:**
  - 这是什么？
    - Zhè shì shénme？
    - What is this?
  - 你要什么？
    - Nǐ yào shénme？
    - What do you want?
