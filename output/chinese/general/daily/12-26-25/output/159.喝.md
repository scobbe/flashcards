- **definition:** drink
- **pinyin:** hē
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 曷
    - hē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") + Phonetic: 曷(曷) (hē) =
    - incorporates mouth element for drinking + phonetic cue ->
    - drink
  - **interpretation:** The character 喝(hē, "drink") uses 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") to signify the act of drinking, logically involving the mouth. The phonetic component 曷(曷) (hē) suggests the pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢喝茶(我喜歡喝茶)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē chá.
    - I like drinking tea.
  - 他喝了一杯果汁(他喝了一杯果汁)，享受下午时光(享受下午時光)。
    - Tā hēle yī bēi guǒzhī, xiǎngshòu xiàwǔ shíguāng.
    - He drank a glass of juice and enjoyed the afternoon.
### 喝(喝) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu) represents a pictorial depiction of an open mouth, indicating its meaning as 'mouth.' Its pictographic origin makes it intuitively connected to the concept it represents—related to speaking or openings. This character is also a common radical, forming part of many complex characters with meanings related to mouths or openings.
- **examples:**
  - 我家有三口人(我家有三口人)。
    - Wǒ jiā yǒu sān kǒu rén.
    - My family has three members.
  - 地处河口的平台很美丽(地處河口的平台很美麗)。
    - Dì chǔ hé kǒu de píngtái hěn měilì.
    - The platform located at the river's mouth is beautiful.
