## drink
### hē
---
- **definition:** drink
- **pinyin:** hē
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 曷
    - hē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + Phonetic: 曷 (hē) =
    - incorporates mouth element for drinking + phonetic cue ->
    - drink
  - **interpretation:** The character 喝(hē, "drink") uses 口 (kǒu, "mouth") to signify the act of drinking, logically involving the mouth. The phonetic component 曷 (hē) suggests the pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)喝茶。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē chá.
    - I like drinking tea.
  - 他喝了一杯果汁，享受下午时(時)光。
    - Tā hēle yī bēi guǒzhī, xiǎngshòu xiàwǔ shíguāng.
    - He drank a glass of juice and enjoyed the afternoon.
### 喝 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开(張開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 喝 → 曷
- **pinyin:** hē
- **components:**
  - 匃
    - gài
    - empty
  - 曰
    - yuē
    - say
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 匃 (gài, "empty") + semantic 曰 (yuē, "say") =
    - articulated question ->
    - question word
  - **interpretation:** 曷(hē) combines 匃(gài) as the phonetic component, which means "empty," and 曰(yuē) "say," suggesting an enunciation or expression involving questioning. This structure is used to form a question word.
### 喝 → 曷 → 匃
- **definition:** empty
- **pinyin:** gài
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** variant form
  - **description:** Variant of 丐 (gài, "to beg")
  - **interpretation:** The character 匃(gài) is a variant form of 丐 (gài, "to beg") and likely shares its etymological roots, relating to the concept of emptiness or deprivation, which aligns with the meaning of "empty".
### 喝 → 曷 → 曰
- **definition:** say
- **pinyin:** yuē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth, signifying speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 曰 (yuē, "say") visually resembles a mouth or an opening, symbolizing the action of speaking or saying something.
- **examples:**
  - 老师(師)曰：勤奋(奮)是成功之母。
    - Lǎoshī yuē：qínfèn shì chénggōng zhī mǔ。
    - The teacher said, 'Diligence is the mother of success.'
  - 孔子曰，学(學)而不思则(則)罔。
    - Kǒngzǐ yuē, xué ér bù sī zé wǎng。
    - Confucius said, 'Learning without thought leads to confusion.'
  - 老子曰，无为(無為)而治。
    - Lǎozǐ yuē, wúwéi ér zhì。
    - Laozi said, 'Governance should be achieved through non-action.'
