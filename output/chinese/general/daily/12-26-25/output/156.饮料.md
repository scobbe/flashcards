### beverage
---
- **pinyin:** beverage
- **components:**
  - 饮(飲)
    - yǐn
    - drink; to consume liquid; beverage
  - 料
    - liào
    - material; stuff; substance
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 饮(飲) (yǐn,
  - **interpretation:** This combination makes sense because 饮(飲) (yǐn, "drink") refers to the action of consuming liquids, while 料 (liào, "material") refers to the substance of the beverages themselves.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢喝饮料(我喜歡喝飲料)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē yǐnliào.

  - 超市里有很多饮料(超市裡有很多飲料)。
    - Chāoshì lǐ yǒu hěn duō yǐnliào.
    - There are many beverages in the supermarket.
### 饮料(飲料) → 饮(飲)
- **definition:** drink; to consume liquid; beverage
- **pinyin:** yǐn
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - to eat
  - 欠
    - qiàn
    - yawn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Combines 食 (shí, 'to eat') as a phonetic element and 欠 (qiàn, 'yawn') as a semantic component, depicting the action of drinking.
  - **interpretation:** The character illustrates a person consuming drink from a bottle, evoking the bodily act of bringing the mouth to the bottle to drink.
  - **simplification:** 飲 was simplified to 食, which means 'to eat', likely for ease of writing and recognition.
- **examples:**
  - 我渴了(我渴了)，想喝水(想喝水)。
    - Wǒ kěle, xiǎng hē shuǐ.
    - I am thirsty, I want to drink water.
  - 他在饮酒(他在飲酒)，人们都在欢笑(人們都在歡笑)！
    - Tā zài yǐn jiǔ, rénmen dōu zài huān xiào!
    - He is drinking alcohol, everyone is laughing!
### 饮料(飲料) → 饮(飲) → 食(食)
- **definition:** to eat
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a mouth (亼) over a bowl of rice on a stand.
  - **interpretation:** The imagery of a mouth above a bowl of rice represents the action of eating, directly linking the symbol to its meaning. This visual connection intuitively conveys the concept of food consumption.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢食物(我喜歡食物)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān shíwù.
    - I like food.
  - 她每天吃三餐(她每天吃三餐)。
    - Tā měitiān chī sān cān.
    - She eats three meals a day.
  - 我们去吃晚饭(我們去吃晚飯)。
    - Wǒmen qù chī wǎnfàn.
    - We are going to have dinner.
### 饮料(飲料) → 饮(飲) → 欠(欠)
- **definition:** yawn
- **pinyin:** qiàn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person with their mouth open, which signifies a state of lacking or desiring something.
  - **interpretation:** The image of a kneeling person with an open mouth suggests a request or desire for something that is lacking. This visual evokes the idea of wanting or needing, aligning well with the character's meaning of 'to lack.'
- **examples:**
  - 我欠钱(我欠錢)，需要借一些。
    - Wǒ qiàn qián, xūyào jiè yīxiē.
    - I lack money and need to borrow some.
  - 他在欠缺方面努力(他在欠缺方面努力)。
    - Tā zài qiànquē fāngmiàn nǔlì.
    - He is working hard in areas where he lacks.
### 饮料(飲料) → 料(料)
- **definition:** material; stuff; substance
- **pinyin:** liào
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Composed of the phonetic component 料(料) (liào, 'to consider') and the semantic component 刂 (knife), which suggests something processed or prepared.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the idea of material being processed or shaped, much like how a knife is used in preparing substances.
- **examples:**
  - 这些材料(這些材料)都很好用。
    - Zhèxiē cáiliào dōu hěn hǎo yòng.
    - These materials are all very useful.
  - 我需要更多的原料(我需要更多的原料)。
    - Wǒ xūyào gèng duō de yuánliào.
    - I need more raw materials.
