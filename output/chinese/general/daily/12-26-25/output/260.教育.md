## education
### jiàoyù
---
- **definition:** education
- **pinyin:** jiàoyù
- **components:**
  - 教
    - jiào
    - teach; instruct; educate; religion
  - 育
    - yù
    - nurture; raise; educate; breed
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 教(教) (jiào, "teach") refers to the act of teaching or instructing. 育(育) (yù, "nurture") means to nurture or cultivate. Combined, 教育(教育) (jiàoyù, "education") signifies the process of teaching and nurturing individuals for personal and intellectual development.
- **examples:**
  - 教育是改变人生的重要途径(教育是改變人生的重要途徑)。
    - Jiàoyù shì gǎibiàn rénshēng de zhòngyào tújìng.
    - Education is an important way to change one's life.
  - 家庭重视教育(家庭重視教育)，社会支持学校建设(社會支持學校建設)。
    - Jiātíng zhòngshì jiàoyù, shèhuì zhīchí xuéxiào jiànshè.
    - The family values education, and society supports school construction.
  - 古人认为教育培养人的品德(古人認為教育培養人的品德)。
    - Gǔrén rènwéi jiàoyù péiyǎng rén de pǐndé.
    - Ancient people believed that education cultivates people's character.
### 教育(教育) → 教(教)
- **definition:** teach; instruct; educate; religion
- **pinyin:** jiào
- **components:**
  - 爻
    - yáo
    - false, change
  - 孝
    - xiào
    - filial piety
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 爻(爻) (yáo, 'false, change') + phonetic: 孝(孝) (xiào, 'filial piety') =
    - combining change with filial acts ->
    - instruct, teach
  - **interpretation:** 教(教) (jiào) conveys the idea of modifying behavior or passing down values, as represented by a mix of change/transformation from 爻(爻) (yáo) and the idea of governance or discipline from 孝(孝) (xiào). This reflects both teaching and the moral aspects associated with religious instruction.
- **examples:**
  - 老师在教室认真教学生(老師在教室認真教學生)。
    - Lǎoshī zài jiàoshì rènzhēn jiào xuéshēng.
    - The teacher diligently teaches the students in the classroom.
  - 他对佛教文化充满热情(他對佛教文化充滿熱情)。
    - Tā duì Fójiào wénhuà chōngmǎn rèqíng.
    - He is passionate about Buddhist culture.
### 教育(教育) → 教(教) → 爻(爻)
- **definition:** false, change
- **pinyin:** yáo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts two intersecting lines, often used to represent change or transformation.
  - **interpretation:** The character 爻(爻) (yáo, "false, change") depicts intersecting lines, symbolizing the concept of crossing and transformation as seen in the Yijing or Book of Changes.
- **examples:**
  - 在卜筮中(在卜筮中)，爻代表着变化的可能性(爻代表著變化的可能性)。
    - Zài bǔ shì zhōng, yáo dài biǎo zhe biàn huà de kě néng xìng.
    - In divination, the yao represents the potential for change.
  - 他研究易经爻辞(他研究易經爻辭)，探讨虚实界限(探討虛實界限)。
    - Tā yán jiū Yì jīng yáo cí, tàn tǎo xū shí jiè xiàn.
    - He studies the yao texts of the I Ching and explores the boundary between reality and illusion.
  - 爻的变化反映天命(爻的變化反映天命)。
    - Yáo de biàn huà fǎn yìng tiān mìng.
    - The change of the yao reflects destiny.
### 教育(教育) → 教(教) → 孝(孝)
- **definition:** filial piety
- **pinyin:** xiào
- **components:**
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child
  - 老
    - lǎo
    - old
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 子(子) (zǐ, "child") below 老(老) (lǎo, "old") =
    - child carrying the elder ->
    - filial piety
  - **interpretation:** 孝 represents the concept of filial piety by depicting a child 子(子) supporting an elder 老(老), symbolizing the responsibility and respect a child has towards their parents and elders.
- **examples:**
  - 他对父母非常孝(他對父母非常孝)。
    - Tā duì fùmǔ fēicháng xiào.
    - He is very filial to his parents.
  - 我们学习孝顺的传统(我們學習孝順的傳統)，以报答父母的恩情(以報答父母的恩情)。
    - Wǒmen xuéxí xiàoshùn de chuántǒng, yǐ bàodá fùmǔ de ēnqíng.
    - We study the tradition of filial piety to repay our parents' kindness.
### 教育(教育) → 教(教) → 孝(孝) → 子(子)
- **definition:** child
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a baby with a large head and spread arms, with legs wrapped in a blanket
  - **interpretation:** The character 子(zǐ, "child") is a pictorial representation of a baby, emphasizing characteristics like a large head and outstretched arms typical of infants, which conveys the essence of a child. This visual element helps infer its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 这小子很调皮(這小子很調皮)。
    - Zhè xiǎo zǐ hěn tiáopí.
    - This kid is very naughty.
  - 他的小子爱打篮球(他的小子愛打籃球)，每天都锻炼身体(每天都鍛煉身體)。
    - Tā de xiǎo zǐ ài dǎ lánqiú, měitiān dōu duànliàn shēntǐ.
    - His child loves playing basketball, and practices every day.
  - 孤独的子需要关爱(孤獨的子需要關愛)。
    - Gūdú de zǐ xūyào guān'ài.
    - A lonely child needs care.

### 教育(教育) → 育(育)

- **pinyin:** yù
- **components:**
  - ⺹
    - lǎo

  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - ⺹(⺹) (lǎo, "old") + 子(子) (zǐ, "child") =
    - associated with growing or raising ->
    - 育(育)
  - **interpretation:** The character 育(育) (yù, "nurture") combines ⺹(⺹) (lǎo, "old") to suggest wisdom or age, with 子(子) (zǐ, "child") to represent the young. This represents the concept of nurturing or educating younger generations which is an integral part of raising or breeding.
- **examples:**
  - 他用爱心育儿(他用愛心育兒)。
    - Tā yòng àixīn yù ér.

  - 学校严谨治学(學校嚴謹治學)，精心育才(精心育才)。
    - Xuéxiào yánjǐn zhìxué, jīngxīn yùcái.
    - The school pursues rigorous scholarship and carefully nurtures talented students.
  - 科学家致力于动物育种(科學家致力於動物育種)。
    - Kēxuéjiā zhìlì yú dòngwù yùzhǒng.
    - Scientists are committed to animal breeding.
