## food
### shíwù
---
- **definition:** food
- **pinyin:** shíwù
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - eat; meal
  - 物
    - wù
    - thing; object
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 食(食) (shí, "eat"; "meal") + 物(物) (wù, "thing") together form the compound "food."
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 食(食) (shí, "eat") and 物(物) (wù, "thing") refers to anything that is eaten, thus aptly describing "food."
- **examples:**
  - 那家餐厅的食物很美味(那家餐廳的食物很美味)。
    - Nà jiā cāntīng de shíwù hěn měiwèi.
    - The food at that restaurant is delicious.
  - 冰箱里有很多食物(冰箱裏有很多食物)。
    - Bīngxiāng lǐ yǒu hěn duō shíwù.
    - There is a lot of food in the refrigerator.
  - 他们整理了食物(他們整理了食物)，以备明天的聚会(以備明天的聚會)。
    - Tāmen zhěnglǐ le shíwù, yǐ bèi míngtiān de jùhuì.
    - They organized the food to prepare for tomorrow's gathering.
### 食物(食物) → 食(食)
- **definition:** eat; meal
- **pinyin:** shí
- **components:**
  - 亼
    - jí
    - assemble
  - 𠊊
    - jí
    - bowl with stand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:** 亼(亼) (jí, "assemble") over a pictographic bowl of rice on a stand, symbolized by 𠊊(𠊊) (jí, "bowl with stand")
  - **interpretation:** Initially, the character 食(食) (shí, "eat") visually represented a mouth over a bowl of rice, conveying the act of eating or a meal intuitively through this imagery.
- **examples:**
  - 他食得很快(他食得很快)。
    - Tā shí dé hěn kuài.
    - He eats very quickly.
  - 我准备午食(我準備午食)，与朋友共享佳肴(與朋友共享佳餚)。
    - Wǒ zhǔnbèi wǔ shí, yǔ péngyǒu gòngxiǎng jiāyáo.
    - I am preparing lunch to share a delicious meal with my friends.
  - 民以食为天(民以食為天)。
    - Mín yǐ shí wéi tiān.
    - Food is regarded as the most important element for the people.
### 食物(食物) → 食(食) → 亼(亼)
- **definition:** assemble
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 入(入) (rù, "enter") + 一(一) (yī, "one") =
    - gather from three sides ->
    - represents 集(集) (jí, "assemble") or an upside-down 口(口) (kǒu, "mouth") ->
    - original form of 今(今) (jīn, "today")
  - **interpretation:** 亼(亼) (jī, "open mouth flipped upside down") combines 入(入) (rù, "enter") and 一(一) (yī, "one") to convey the action of gathering. This form relates to 集(集) (jí, "assemble") and to being the early form of 今(今) (jīn, "today"), seen in 合(合), 今(今), 會(會), 令(令), 侖(侖), 食(食), 僉(僉), and 龠(龠).
- **examples:**
  - 学者研究文字时发现亼(學者研究文字時發現亼)，形似倒置的张口(形似倒置的張口)。
    - Xuézhě yánjiū wénzì shí fāxiàn jī, xíngsì dàozhì de zhāngkǒu.
    - The scholar discovered jī while studying characters, its form resembling an upside-down open mouth.
  - 篆刻匠人利用亼表现创作意趣(篆刻匠人利用亼表現創作意趣)，象征开口倒置的智慧(象徵開口倒置的智慧)。
    - Zhuànkè jiàngrén lìyòng jī biǎoxiàn chuàngzuò yìqù, xiàngzhēng kāikǒu dàozhì de zhìhuì.
    - The seal carver used jī to express creative interest, symbolizing the wisdom inherent in an inverted open mouth.

### 食物(食物) → 食(食) → 亼(亼) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，大声喊叫(大聲喊叫)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这口饭入口即化(這口飯入口即化)。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹果(小女孩咬了一口蘋果)，露出甜美的笑容(露出甜美的笑容)。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

### 食物(食物) → 物(物)
- **definition:** thing; object
- **pinyin:** wù
- **components:**
  - 牛
    - niú
    - ox
  - 勿
    - wù
    - do not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 牛(牛) (niú, 'ox') + phonetic: 勿(勿) (wù, 'do not') =
    - items related to oxen ->
    - generic 'thing' or 'object'
  - **interpretation:** The character 物 evolved from the combination of an ox, a familiar and essential domestic animal, and 勿(勿) (wù), which serves as the phonetic component. This suggests a broad category of things concerning daily life and utility.
- **examples:**
  - 这个物引起了人们的注意(這個物引起了人們的注意)。
    - Zhège wù yǐnqǐ le rénmen de zhùyì.
    - This object attracted people's attention.
  - 古代的学者重视物理现象(古代的學者重視物理現象)，探讨物质的本原(探討物質的本原)。
    - Gǔdài de xuézhě zhòngshì wùlǐ xiànxiàng, tàntǎo wùzhì de běnyuán.
    - Ancient scholars valued physical phenomena, exploring the essence of matter.
  - 生活中的每种物都承载着记忆(生活中的每種物都承載著記憶)。
    - Shēnghuó zhōng de měi zhǒng wù dōu chéngzài zhe jìyì.
    - Every object in life carries memories.

### 食物(食物) → 物(物) → 勿(勿)
- **definition:** do not
- **pinyin:** wù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts blood on a knife ->
    - original character of 刎 (wěn, "to cut") ->
    - jiajie for 勿 (wù, "do not")
  - **interpretation:** The character 勿(wù), originally illustrated blood on a knife, visually represented the action of cutting, connecting to its initial meaning. Over time, it was borrowed to express prohibition or negation, showing the evolution beyond its pictorial roots.
- **examples:**
  - 进入图书馆时(進入圖書館時)，请勿大声喧哗(請勿大聲喧嘩)。
    - Jìnrù túshūguǎn shí, qǐng wù dàshēng xuānhuá.
    - When entering the library, please do not speak loudly.
  - 办公区内勿吸烟(辦公區內勿吸煙)。
    - Bàngōng qū nèi wù xīyān.
    - Do not smoke in the office area.
  - 行路时(行路時)，勿追逐打闹(勿追逐打鬧)。
    - Xínglù shí, wù zhuīzhú dǎnào.
    - When walking, do not engage in chasing and rough play.
