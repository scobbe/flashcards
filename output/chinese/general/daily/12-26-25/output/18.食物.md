## food
### shíwù
---
- **definition:** food
- **pinyin:** shíwù
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - eat; meal
  - 物
    - wù
    - thing; object
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 食(食) (shí, "eat"; "meal") + 物(物) (wù, "thing") together form the compound "food."
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 食(食) (shí, "eat") and 物(物) (wù, "thing") refers to anything that is eaten, thus aptly describing "food."
- **examples:**
  - 那家餐厅的食物很美味(那家餐廳的食物很美味)。
    - Nà jiā cāntīng de shíwù hěn měiwèi.
    - The food at that restaurant is delicious.
  - 冰箱里有很多食物(冰箱裏有很多食物)。
    - Bīngxiāng lǐ yǒu hěn duō shíwù.
    - There is a lot of food in the refrigerator.
  - 他们整理了食物(他們整理了食物)，以备明天的聚会(以備明天的聚會)。
    - Tāmen zhěnglǐ le shíwù, yǐ bèi míngtiān de jùhuì.
    - They organized the food to prepare for tomorrow's gathering.
### 食物(食物) → 食(食)

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:** 亼(亼) (jí, "assemble") over a pictographic bowl of rice on a stand, symbolized by 𠊊(𠊊) (jí, "bowl with stand")
  - **interpretation:** Initially, the character 食(食) (shí, "eat") visually represented a mouth over a bowl of rice, conveying the act of eating or a meal intuitively through this imagery.
- **examples:**
  - 他食得很快(他食得很快)。

  - 民以食为天(民以食為天)。
    - Mín yǐ shí wéi tiān.

### 食物(食物) → 物(物)
- **definition:** thing; object
- **pinyin:** wù
- **components:**
  - 牛
    - niú
    - ox
  - 勿
    - wù
    - do not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 牛(牛) (niú, 'ox') + phonetic: 勿(勿) (wù, 'do not') =
    - items related to oxen ->
    - generic 'thing' or 'object'
  - **interpretation:** The character 物 evolved from the combination of an ox, a familiar and essential domestic animal, and 勿(勿) (wù), which serves as the phonetic component. This suggests a broad category of things concerning daily life and utility.
- **examples:**
  - 这个物引起了人们的注意(這個物引起了人們的注意)。
    - Zhège wù yǐnqǐ le rénmen de zhùyì.
    - This object attracted people's attention.
  - 古代的学者重视物理现象(古代的學者重視物理現象)，探讨物质的本原(探討物質的本原)。
    - Gǔdài de xuézhě zhòngshì wùlǐ xiànxiàng, tàntǎo wùzhì de běnyuán.
    - Ancient scholars valued physical phenomena, exploring the essence of matter.
  - 生活中的每种物都承载着记忆(生活中的每種物都承載著記憶)。
    - Shēnghuó zhōng de měi zhǒng wù dōu chéngzài zhe jìyì.
    - Every object in life carries memories.

### 食物(食物) → 物(物) → 勿(勿)
- **definition:** do not
- **pinyin:** wù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts blood on a knife ->
    - original character of 刎 (wěn, "to cut") ->
    - jiajie for 勿 (wù, "do not")
  - **interpretation:** The character 勿(wù), originally illustrated blood on a knife, visually represented the action of cutting, connecting to its initial meaning. Over time, it was borrowed to express prohibition or negation, showing the evolution beyond its pictorial roots.
- **examples:**
  - 进入图书馆时(進入圖書館時)，请勿大声喧哗(請勿大聲喧嘩)。
    - Jìnrù túshūguǎn shí, qǐng wù dàshēng xuānhuá.
    - When entering the library, please do not speak loudly.
  - 办公区内勿吸烟(辦公區內勿吸煙)。
    - Bàngōng qū nèi wù xīyān.
    - Do not smoke in the office area.
  - 行路时(行路時)，勿追逐打闹(勿追逐打鬧)。
    - Xínglù shí, wù zhuīzhú dǎnào.
    - When walking, do not engage in chasing and rough play.
