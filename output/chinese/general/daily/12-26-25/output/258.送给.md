## give as a present; send to
### sònggěi
---
- **definition:** give as a present; send to
- **pinyin:** sònggěi
- **components:**
  - 送
    - sòng
    - to deliver; to send; to present (as a gift); to see off
  - 给(給)
    - gěi
    - to give; to; for; to supply
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 送 + 给 = send/deliver + give/to → give as a present; send to (someone).
  - **interpretation:** Combines the act of sending with giving, highlighting transfer to a specific recipient. Commonly places the item before 送给 and the recipient after, or splits as 送…给….
  - **simplification:** 給 → 给: the silk radical 糹 was simplified to 纟 and strokes were reduced; 送 is unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我想把这本书送给你(我想把這本書送給你)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng bǎ zhè běn shū sòng gěi nǐ.
    - I want to give this book to you.
  - 生日时(生日時)，她送给老师一束花(她送給老師一束花)。
    - Shēngrì shí, tā sòng gěi lǎoshī yī shù huā.
    - On her birthday, she gave the teacher a bouquet of flowers.
  - 快递员已把包裹送给收件人了(快遞員已把包裹送給收件人了)。
    - Kuàidìyuán yǐ bǎ bāoguǒ sòng gěi shōujiànrén le.
    - The courier has already delivered the package to the recipient.
### 送给(送給) → 送(送)
- **definition:** to deliver; to send; to present (as a gift); to see off
- **pinyin:** sòng
- **components:**
  - 灷
    - zhuàn
    - to burn; blaze
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 辶 (“to walk”) + phonetic: 灷 (zhuàn)
  - **interpretation:** Movement indicated by 辶 suggests going, sending, or accompanying, aligning with meanings like to deliver or see off. 灷 supplies the sound value, approximating the historical pronunciation (OC *sloːŋs) that developed into modern sòng.
- **examples:**
  - 我送你回家(我送你回家)。
    - Wǒ sòng nǐ huí jiā.
    - I'll see you home.
  - 快递员把包裹送到公司(快遞員把包裹送到公司)。
    - Kuàidìyuán bǎ bāoguǒ sòng dào gōngsī.
    - The courier delivered the package to the company.
  - 生日时(生日時)，她送了我一本书(她送了我一本書)。
    - Shēngrì shí, tā sòng le wǒ yì běn shū.
    - She gave me a book for my birthday.
### 送给(送給) → 送(送) → 灷(灷)
- **definition:** to burn; blaze
- **pinyin:** zhuàn
- **components:**
  - 火
    - huǒ
    - fire
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands; to hold up
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound (會意/会意)
  - **description:** 火 ‘fire’ + 廾 ‘hands’ – fire that can be handled; embers, hot coals.
  - **interpretation:** Combines fire with two hands, evoking embers or coals hot enough to glow yet manageable by hand tools. Extends to the sense of blazing or burning heat.
- **examples:**
  - 炉中灷着一团火(爐中灷著一團火)。
    - Lú zhōng zhuàn zhe yì tuán huǒ.
    - A fire is blazing in the stove.
  - 风一吹(風一吹)，篝火灷得更旺(篝火灷得更旺)。
    - Fēng yī chuī, gōuhuǒ zhuàn de gèng wàng.
    - When the wind blows, the bonfire blazes even stronger.
  - 他不小心把手指灷伤(他不小心把手指灷傷)。
    - Tā bù xiǎoxīn bǎ shǒuzhǐ zhuàn shāng.
    - He accidentally burned his finger.
### 送给(送給) → 送(送) → 灷(灷) → 火(火)
- **definition:** fire
- **pinyin:** huǒ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram (象形)
  - **description:** Pictographic representation of a fire; written as 灬 at the bottom of characters; original form retained in 灸; related shapes in 叟 (top, original) and 赤 (bottom).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a flame with tongues of fire rising upward, which is why the shape is simple and angular. When used as a component at the bottom of other characters, it appears as the variant 灬 to fit the overall structure.
- **examples:**
  - 火很旺(火很旺)，别靠太近(別靠太近)。
    - Huǒ hěn wàng, bié kào tài jìn.
    - The fire is blazing; don't get too close.
  - 厨房着火了(廚房著火了)，马上打电话报警(馬上打電話報警)。
    - Chúfáng zháo huǒ le, mǎshàng dǎ diànhuà bàojǐng.
    - The kitchen is on fire; call the police immediately.
  - 请把火关小一点(請把火關小一點)，汤要慢慢煮(湯要慢慢煮)。
    - Qǐng bǎ huǒ guān xiǎo yīdiǎn, tāng yào mànmàn zhǔ.
    - Please turn the heat down a bit; the soup should simmer slowly.
### 送给(送給) → 送(送) → 灷(灷) → 廾(廾)
- **definition:** two hands; to hold up
- **pinyin:** gǒng
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - right hand; again
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 𠂇 (“left hand”) + 又 (“right hand”) — both hands; also written 𠬞.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the left and right hands to depict a pair of hands acting together, hence the sense of lifting or offering with both hands. Its form later served as a common component in characters related to holding or presenting.
- **examples:**
  - 在古文字中(在古文字中)，廾表示两只手一起举起东西(廾表示兩隻手一起舉起東西)。
    - Zài gǔ wénzì zhōng, gǒng biǎoshì liǎng zhī shǒu yīqǐ jǔqǐ dōngxī.
    - In ancient script, 廾 signifies two hands lifting something together.
  - 作为部件(作為部件)，它常见于表示双手奉献或合举的字(它常見於表示雙手奉獻或閤舉的字)，如奉、奏(如奉、奏)。
    - Zuòwéi bùjiàn, tā chángjiàn yú biǎoshì shuāngshǒu fèngxiàn huò héjǔ de zì, rú Fèng, Zòu.
    - As a component, it appears in characters that depict offering or jointly lifting with two hands,such as 奉 and 奏.
### 送给(送給) → 送(送) → 灷(灷) → 廾(廾) → 又(又)
- **definition:** right hand; again
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 送给(送給) → 给(給)
- **definition:** to give; to; for; to supply
- **pinyin:** gěi
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
  - 合
    - hé
    - to join; to combine
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 糸 + phonetic: 合; simplified: 糸 → 纟
  - **interpretation:** The silk component points to textiles, valuables, or the notion of provisioning, while 合 provides the sound and a sense of joining or bringing together. The original sense related to abundance or supply naturally extended to giving or providing something to someone.
  - **simplification:** The left-side silk component 糸 was simplified to 纟, part of a systematic reduction of the silk radical series in Simplified Chinese; the phonetic 合 was retained.
- **examples:**
  - 请给我一杯水(請給我一杯水)。
    - Qǐng gěi wǒ yì bēi shuǐ.
    - Please give me a glass of water.
  - 这封信是给老师的(這封信是給老師的)。
    - Zhè fēng xìn shì gěi lǎoshī de.
    - This letter is for the teacher.
  - 我给你发了一封邮件(我給你發了一封郵件)。
    - Wǒ gěi nǐ fā le yì fēng yóujiàn.
    - I sent you an email.
### 送给(送給) → 给(給) → 糸(糸)
- **definition:** silk; thread
- **pinyin:** mì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** a skein of silk.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bundle of silk threads tied in the middle, with strands hanging above and below. The stylized sketch highlights twisted filaments, which later served as the standard radical for thread- and textile-related characters.
  - **simplification:** In Simplified Chinese the silk radical was streamlined to 纟 to reduce strokes and speed writing; 糸 is rarely used independently, but 纟 replaces it as a left-side component.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan sīchóu.
    - I like silk fabrics.
  - 这件衣服是用蚕丝做的(這件衣服是用蠶絲做的)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfu shì yòng cánsī zuò de.
    - This piece of clothing is made from silkworm silk.
  - 丝线很细(絲線很細)。
    - Sīxiàn hěn xì.
    - Silk thread is very thin.
### 送给(送給) → 给(給) → 合(閤)
- **definition:** to join; to combine
- **pinyin:** hé
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (會意)
  - **description:** 亼 + 口 (“mouth”): Two mouths speaking together.
  - **interpretation:** Two mouths coming together evokes speaking in unison or agreement, which extends to the senses of joining and fitting together. From this image also comes the idea of closing, as parts meet to make a whole.
- **examples:**
  - 我们把两份文件合在一起(我們把兩份文件合在一起)。
    - Wǒmen bǎ liǎng fèn wénjiàn hé zài yīqǐ.
    - We combined the two documents.
  - 请把门合上(請把門合上)。
    - Qǐng bǎ mén hé shàng.
    - Please close the door.
  - 我们合作完成了项目(我們合作完成了項目)。
    - Wǒmen hézuò wánchéng le xiàngmù.
    - We worked together to finish the project.
### 送给(送給) → 给(給) → 合(閤) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
