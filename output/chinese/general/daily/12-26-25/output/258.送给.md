## give as a present; send to
### sònggěi
---
- **traditional:** 送給
- **definition:** give as a present; send to
- **pinyin:** sònggěi
- **components:**
  - 送
    - sòng
    - deliver; send; carry out; see off
  - 给(給)
    - gěi
    - to give; for; provide; allow
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 送 (sòng, "deliver; send") and 给(給) (gěi, "to give") combine to describe the action of giving or sending something to someone, often as a present or gift.
  - **interpretation:** 送给(給) (sònggěi) makes sense as it combines the concepts of sending or delivering (送) with the notion of giving (给), highlighting the act of gifting or presenting something to someone.
- **examples:**
  - 我写(寫)了一张贺(張賀)卡，送给远(給遠)方的朋友。
    - Wǒ xiěle yī zhāng hèkǎ, sònggěi yuǎnfāng de péngyǒu.
    - I wrote a greeting card and sent it to a friend far away.
  - 他把鲜(鮮)花送给(給)母亲(親)。
    - Tā bǎ xiānhuā sònggěi mǔqīn.
    - He sent flowers to his mother.
### 送( )给(給) → 送
- **definition:** deliver; send; carry out; see off
- **pinyin:** sòng
- **components:**
  - 辶
    - chuò
    - walk
  - 关(關)
    - guān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 辶 (chuò, "walk") + phonetic: 关(關) (guān) =
    - movement associated with giving or sending ->
    - send, deliver
  - **interpretation:** 送 (sòng, "send") combines the idea of movement or traveling, shown by 辶 (chuò, "walk"), with the phonetic 关(關) (guān) to indicate the action of sending off or delivering something. It emphasizes movement associated with transferring possession or accompanying someone on their way.
- **examples:**
  - 爸爸送子女上学(學)。
    - Bàba sòng zǐnǚ shàngxué.
    - Dad sees his children off to school.
  - 我送他到火车(車)站，陪他候车(車)。
    - Wǒ sòng tā dào huǒchēzhàn, péi tā hòuchē.
    - I take him to the train station and accompany him while waiting.
