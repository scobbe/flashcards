## morning glory
### qiānniúhuā
---
- **definition:** morning glory
- **pinyin:** qiānniúhuā
- **components:**
  - 牵(牽)
    - qiān
    - to lead
  - 牛
    - niú
    - ox
  - 花
    - huā
    - flower
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 牵(牽) (qiān, "to lead") + 牛(牛) (niú, "ox") + 花(花) (huā, "flower") =
    - morning glory, a flower that twines around like it is leading an ox
  - **interpretation:** The name 牵牛花(牽牛花) (qiānniúhuā, "morning glory") evokes the image of the flower's tendrils twining around supports in a manner reminiscent of how a person might lead an ox by a rope. The plant's climbing habit is likened to the motion of guiding an ox.
- **examples:**
  - 清晨时分(清晨時分)，我在花园里发现了美丽的牵牛花(我在花園裡發現了美麗的牽牛花)。
    - Qīngchén shífèn, wǒ zài huāyuán lǐ fāxiànle měilì de qiānniúhuā.
    - Early in the morning, I discovered beautiful morning glories in the garden.
  - 花架上爬满了牵牛花(花架上爬滿了牽牛花)。
    - Huājià shàng pá mǎnle qiānniúhuā.
    - The trellis is covered with morning glories.
  - 黄昏时刻(黃昏時刻)，夕阳映照的牵牛花(夕陽映照的牽牛花)，显得格外动人(顯得格外動人)。
    - Huánghūn shíkè, xīyáng yìngzhào de qiānniúhuā, xiǎnde géwài dòngrén.
    - At dusk, the morning glories illuminated by the setting sun looked exceptionally enchanting.
