## Qin Shi Huang (first emperor of Qin)
### Qín Shǐhuáng
---
- **definition:** Qin Shi Huang (first emperor of Qin)
- **pinyin:** Qín Shǐhuáng
- **components:**
  - 秦
    - qín
    - Qin state; Qin dynasty; surname Qin; the Qin polity
  - 始皇
    - shǐhuáng
    - first emperor; founding emperor; primal sovereign
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 秦 (Qin) + 始皇 (first emperor) = First Emperor of Qin.
  - **interpretation:** Refers to Yíng Zhèng, who unified the Warring States in 221 BCE and adopted the new imperial title 皇帝, hence styled as 秦始皇(帝). Often understood as the abbreviation of 秦始皇帝, meaning “the first emperor of the Qin.”
- **examples:**
  - 秦始皇统一六国，建立了秦朝(秦始皇統一六國，建立了秦朝)。
    - Qín Shǐhuáng tǒngyī liù guó, jiànlì le Qíncháo.
    - Qin Shi Huang unified the Six States and founded the Qin dynasty.
  - 许多兵马俑是为秦始皇的陵墓而制(許多兵馬俑是為秦始皇的陵墓而製)。
    - Xǔduō bīngmǎyǒng shì wèi Qín Shǐhuáng de língmù ér zhì.
    - Many terracotta warriors were made for Qin Shi Huang’s mausoleum.
  - 历史学家讨论秦始皇的功过(歷史學家討論秦始皇的功過)。
    - Lìshǐxuéjiā tǎolùn Qín Shǐhuáng de gōngguò.
    - Historians debate Qin Shi Huang’s merits and faults.
### 秦始皇 → 秦
- **definition:** Qin state; Qin dynasty; surname Qin; the Qin polity
- **pinyin:** qín
- **components:**
  - 午
    - wǔ
    - noon; (originally) pestle
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands; to hold up
  - 禾
    - hé
    - grain; cereal crop
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (会意)
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 午 (pestle) + 廾 (two hands) + 禾禾 (grain); later 午+廾 fused as 𡗗.
  - **interpretation:** Shows two hands using a pestle to pound grain, a visual of processing/husking grain. The graph later became the name of the Qin polity and dynasty; in modern form the hand-and-pestle elements have merged into 𡗗 above 禾.
- **examples:**
  - 秦朝统一六国(秦朝統一六國)。
    - Qíncháo tǒngyī liù guó.
    - The Qin dynasty unified the six states.
  - 他姓秦(他姓秦)。
    - Tā xìng Qín.
    - His surname is Qin.
  - 我很喜欢秦腔(我很喜歡秦腔)。
    - Wǒ hěn xǐhuan Qínqiāng.
    - I really like Qin opera (from Shaanxi).
### 秦始皇 → 秦 → 午
- **definition:** noon; (originally) pestle
- **pinyin:** wǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a pestle (now written 杵).
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a vertical pestle used for pounding grain, which later became stylized into the present form. Its common meaning of “noon” arose via its role as one of the Twelve Earthly Branches, rather than from the original object depicted.
- **examples:**
  - 我中午休息(我中午休息)。
    - Wǒ zhōngwǔ xiūxi.
    - I rest at noon.
  - 我们十二点吃午饭(我們十二點吃午飯)。
    - Wǒmen shí'èr diǎn chī wǔfàn.
    - We eat lunch at twelve o'clock.
  - 古文字中，午表示杵(古文字中，午表示杵)。
    - Gǔ wénzì zhōng, wǔ biǎoshì chǔ.
    - In ancient scripts, wǔ meant a pestle.
### 秦始皇 → 秦 → 廾
- **definition:** two hands; to hold up
- **pinyin:** gǒng
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - right hand; again
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 𠂇 (“left hand”) + 又 (“right hand”) — both hands; also written 𠬞.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the left and right hands to depict a pair of hands acting together, hence the sense of lifting or offering with both hands. Its form later served as a common component in characters related to holding or presenting.
- **examples:**
  - 在古文字中，廾表示两只手一起举起东西(在古文字中，廾表示兩隻手一起舉起東西)。
    - Zài gǔ wénzì zhōng, gǒng biǎoshì liǎng zhī shǒu yīqǐ jǔqǐ dōngxī.
    - In ancient script, 廾 signifies two hands lifting something together.
  - 作为部件，它常见于表示双手奉献或合举的字，如奉、奏(作為部件，它常見於表示雙手奉獻或合舉的字，如奉、奏)。
    - Zuòwéi bùjiàn, tā chángjiàn yú biǎoshì shuāngshǒu fèngxiàn huò héjǔ de zì, rú Fèng, Zòu.
    - As a component, it appears in characters that depict offering or jointly lifting with two hands, such as 奉 and 奏.
### 秦始皇 → 秦 → 廾 → 又
- **definition:** right hand; again
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 秦始皇 → 秦 → 禾
- **definition:** grain; cereal crop
- **pinyin:** hé
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — picture of a plant stalk; clearer in older forms; similar to 來 but unrelated to 釆; later gave 株 and 硃.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a stalk of grain with a head and leaves, representing cereal crops in general. Early forms make the plant shape more obvious; despite resembling 來, it has a separate origin and later served as a basis for forms in characters like 株 and 硃.
- **examples:**
  - 田里长满了禾苗(田裡長滿了禾苗)。
    - Tián lǐ zhǎng mǎn le hé miáo.
    - The field is full of grain seedlings.
  - 秋天田野里的禾熟了(秋天田野裡的禾熟了)。
    - Qiūtiān tiányě lǐ de hé shú le.
    - In autumn, the grain crops in the fields have ripened.
  - 禾本科作物需要充足的阳光(禾本科作物需要充足的陽光)。
    - Hé běnkē zuòwù xūyào chōngzú de yángguāng.
    - Crops of the grass family need plenty of sunlight.
### 秦始皇 → 始皇
- **definition:** first emperor; founding emperor; primal sovereign
- **pinyin:** shǐhuáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 始 (begin/first) + 皇(帝 ‘emperor’) = first/inaugural emperor.
  - **interpretation:** Refers to the inaugural emperor, most prominently Qin Shihuang; often appears as 秦始皇 or 始皇帝. Can also be used figuratively for a founder-like sovereign.
- **examples:**
  - 秦始皇统一六国(秦始皇統一六國)，自称始皇(自稱始皇)。
    - Qín Shǐhuáng tǒngyī liù guó, zìchēng Shǐhuáng.
    - Qin Shihuang unified the six states and called himself the First Emperor.
  - 在史书中(在史書中)，“始皇”多指秦始皇(「始皇」多指秦始皇)。
    - Zài shǐshū zhōng, “Shǐhuáng” duō zhǐ Qín Shǐhuáng.
    - In historical texts, “Shihuang” usually refers to Qin Shihuang.
  - 在现代语境中(在現代語境中)，称某位开创性的领导者为“始皇”(稱某位開創性的領導者為「始皇」)是比喻用法(是比喻用法)。
    - Zài xiàndài yǔjìng zhōng, chēng mǒu wèi kāichuàngxìng de lǐngdǎozhě wéi “Shǐhuáng” shì bǐyù yòngfǎ.
    - In modern contexts, calling a pioneering leader a “Shihuang” is figurative usage.
