## Qin Shi Huang (first emperor of Qin)
### Qín Shǐhuáng
---
- **definition:** Qin Shi Huang (first emperor of Qin)
- **pinyin:** Qín Shǐhuáng
- **components:**
  - 秦
    - Qín
    - Qin
  - 始
    - shǐ
    - beginning; first
  - 皇
    - huáng
    - emperor; sovereign
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 秦 (Qín, "Qin") refers to the Qin state, which was one of the warring states in ancient China. 始 (shǐ, "beginning") indicates the notion of beginning or being the first. 皇 (huáng, "emperor") signifies an emperor or sovereign. Together, 秦始皇(Qín Shǐhuáng) refers to the first emperor of the unified China under the Qin dynasty.
  - **interpretation:** 秦始皇(Qín Shǐhuáng), or Emperor Qin Shi Huang, is renowned for being the first emperor to unify China in 221 BCE. His rule marked the foundation of imperial China, and he is famed for monumental projects like the initial construction of the Great Wall.

    - Qin Shi Huang built the Great Wall, forging an everlasting legend.
