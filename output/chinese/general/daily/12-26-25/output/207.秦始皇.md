---
- **definition:** Qin Shi Huang (first emperor of Qin)
- **pinyin:** Qín Shǐhuáng
- **components:**
  - 秦
    - Qín
    - Qin
  - 始
    - shǐ
    - begin; start
  - 皇
    - huáng
    - emperor
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 秦(秦) (Qín, "Qin") + 始(始) (shǐ, "begin; start") + 皇(皇) (huáng, "emperor") refers to Qin Shi Huang, the first emperor of a unified China, signifying the beginnings of a new era under his rule.

- **examples:**
  - 秦始皇统一六国(秦始皇統一六國)。

    - Qín Shǐhuáng bānbùle fénshū kēngrú lìng, duì wénhuà chǎnshēngle shēnyuǎn yǐngxiǎng.
    - Qin Shi Huang issued the order to burn books and bury scholars, which had a profound impact on culture.
