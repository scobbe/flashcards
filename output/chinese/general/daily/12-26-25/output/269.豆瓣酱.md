## doubanjiang; chili bean paste
### dòubànjiàng
---
- **traditional:** 豆瓣醬
- **definition:** doubanjiang; chili bean paste
- **pinyin:** dòubànjiàng
- **components:**
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean
  - 瓣
    - bàn
    - petal; segment
  - 酱(醬)
    - jiàng
    - paste
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 豆 (dòu, "bean"), 瓣 (bàn, "petal"; "segment"), 酱(醬) (jiàng, "paste")
  - **interpretation:** 豆瓣醬 (dòubànjiàng, "doubanjiang") is a type of paste made from fermented broad beans, soybeans, and usually chili. The term 豆 (dòu, "bean") indicates the primary ingredient, while 瓣 (bàn, "petal"; "segment") suggests the segmented appearance of the ingredients, and 酱(醬) (jiàng, "paste") denotes its form as a thick sauce. It is a staple in Sichuan cuisine, used for its rich umami flavor and spiciness.
- **examples:**
  - 我用豆瓣酱(醬)，做鱼(魚)香茄子。
    - Wǒ yòng dòubànjiàng, zuò yúxiāng qiézi.
    - I use doubanjiang to cook fish-flavored eggplant.
  - 豆瓣酱(醬)提升菜肴，带来独(獨)特味道。
    - Dòubànjiàng tíshēng càiyáo, dàilái dútè wèidào.
    - Doubanjiang enhances dishes and brings a unique flavor.
  - 她学(學)做菜时(時)，总用豆瓣酱(醬)调(調)味。
    - Tā xué zuòcài shí, zǒng yòng dòubànjiàng tiáowèi.
    - When she learns to cook, she always uses doubanjiang for seasoning.
