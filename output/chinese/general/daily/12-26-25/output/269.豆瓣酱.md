## doubanjiang; chili bean paste
### dòubànjiàng
---
- **definition:** doubanjiang; chili bean paste
- **pinyin:** dòubànjiàng
- **components:**
  - 豆瓣
    - dòubàn
    - bean halves; split broad beans; broad-bean pieces; fermented broad-bean chunks
  - 酱(醬)
    - jiàng
    - sauce; paste; thick condiment; bean-based sauce
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 豆瓣 ‘split/broad-bean pieces’ + 酱 ‘sauce/paste’ = sauce made from fermented bean pieces, often with chili.
  - **interpretation:** Names a thick fermented condiment based on split broad beans, commonly blended with chilies, salt, and wheat flour. Highlights the visible bean fragments (瓣) that give texture and depth of flavor.
  - **simplification:** 酱 was simplified from 醬 by streamlining the 酉 radical and using 将 as the phonetic, reducing strokes while preserving sound and meaning; 豆瓣 is unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 这道回锅肉离不开郫县豆瓣酱(這道回鍋肉離不開郫縣豆瓣醬)。
    - Zhè dào huíguōròu lí bù kāi Píxiàn dòubànjiàng.
    - Twice-cooked pork depends on Pixian doubanjiang.
  - 他用豆瓣酱炒辣椒(他用豆瓣醬炒辣椒)，香味很浓(香味很濃)。
    - Tā yòng dòubànjiàng chǎo làjiāo, xiāngwèi hěn nóng.
    - He stir-fries chilies with doubanjiang, and the aroma is intense.
  - 超市里有不同牌子的豆瓣酱(超市裡有不同牌子的豆瓣醬)。
    - Chāoshì lǐ yǒu bùtóng páizi de dòubànjiàng.
    - The supermarket carries different brands of doubanjiang.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 豆瓣
- **definition:** bean halves; split broad beans; broad-bean pieces; fermented broad-bean chunks
- **pinyin:** dòubàn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 豆 (bean) + 瓣 (segment/petal) = split bean pieces.
  - **interpretation:** Refers to halves or segments of beans, especially broad beans; by extension, the split fermented bits used in seasonings like 豆瓣酱.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了一些干的豆瓣，用来煮汤(我買了一些乾的豆瓣，用來煮湯)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le yīxiē gān de dòubàn, yòng lái zhǔ tāng.
    - I bought some dried bean halves to make soup.
  - 四川菜常用发酵的豆瓣来做调味，比如豆瓣酱(四川菜常用發酵的豆瓣來做調味，比如豆瓣醬)。
    - Sìchuān cài cháng yòng fājiào de dòubàn lái zuò tiáowèi, bǐrú dòubànjiàng.
    - Sichuan cuisine often uses fermented bean bits for seasoning, such as in doubanjiang.
  - 这些豆瓣太碎了，挑一些完整的(這些豆瓣太碎了，挑一些完整的)。
    - Zhèxiē dòubàn tài suì le, tiāo yīxiē wánzhěng de.
    - These bean halves are too broken; pick out some intact ones.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬)
- **definition:** sauce; paste; thick condiment; bean-based sauce
- **pinyin:** jiàng
- **components:**
  - 酉
    - yǒu
    - wine; alcohol; the 10th Earthly Branch
  - 将(將)
    - jiāng
    - to lead; will; about to; take (coverb)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 酉 + phonetic: 将(將)
  - **interpretation:** 酉 signals fermentation, alcoholic liquids, and by extension condiments, pointing to the meaning of a fermented sauce. 将/將 contributes the sound jiang and helps form the word family, while the overall structure keeps the flavor-related sense.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by replacing the phonetic 將 with its simplified form 将 (⿰丬夕), reducing strokes while preserving pronunciation and the original phono-semantic structure.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃辣酱(我喜歡吃辣醬)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan chī làjiàng.
    - I like to eat spicy sauce.
  - 她把面条拌上豆瓣酱(她把麵條拌上豆瓣醬)。
    - Tā bǎ miàntiáo bàn shàng dòubànjiàng.
    - She tossed the noodles with doubanjiang.
  - 这道菜需要加一点酱油(這道菜需要加一點醬油)。
    - Zhè dào cài xūyào jiā yīdiǎn jiàngyóu.
    - This dish needs a little soy sauce.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬) → 酉
- **definition:** wine; alcohol; the 10th Earthly Branch
- **pinyin:** yǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram (象形)
  - **description:** A wine vessel with a pointed bottom; original character for 酒.
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a pointed-bottom wine vessel used in antiquity, which had to be set in a stand or into the ground. As the original graph for 酒 ‘alcohol’, it became associated with fermented beverages and later designated the tenth Earthly Branch.
- **examples:**
  - 现在是酉时(現在是酉時)。
    - Xiànzài shì yǒu shí.
    - It is the You hour (around 5–7 p.m.).
  - 地支的第十位是酉(地支的第十位是酉)。
    - Dìzhī de dì shí wèi shì yǒu.
    - The tenth Earthly Branch is You.
  - 卯酉之间代表东西方向(卯酉之間代表東西方向)。
    - Mǎo-yǒu zhījiān dàibiǎo dōngxī fāngxiàng.
    - The span from Mao to You represents the east–west direction.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬) → 将(將)
- **definition:** to lead; will; about to; take (coverb)
- **pinyin:** jiāng
- **components:**
  - 爿
    - qiáng
    - bed; small table
  - 肉
    - ròu
    - meat
  - 寸
    - cùn
    - inch; hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic 爿 + semantic 肉 (‘meat’) + semantic 寸 (‘hand’) — offering meat as tribute; ⺼ is on its side (resembles 夕), and 爿 may also suggest a cutting board/altar.
  - **interpretation:** A hand presenting meat suggests offering or bringing something forward, which underlies senses like ‘to lead/bring’ and later ‘will’. 爿 supplies the sound and can also evoke the board or altar on which the meat is set.
  - **simplification:** 将 streamlines 將 by keeping the phonetic 爿 as its left-side form 丬, reducing ⺼ to 夕, and dropping 寸, preserving pronunciation cues while cutting strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 我将去北京(我將去北京)。
    - Wǒ jiāng qù Běijīng.
    - I will go to Beijing.
  - 他将书放在桌上(他將書放在桌上)。
    - Tā jiāng shū fàng zài zhuō shàng.
    - He put the book on the table.
  - 他是将军(他是將軍)。
    - Tā shì jiāngjūn.
    - He is a general.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬) → 将(將) → 爿
- **definition:** bed; small table
- **pinyin:** qiáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a bed; original form of 牀; contracted in 臧.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a side view of a bed or wooden frame. Later served as the left-hand element in words for bed (牀/床) and survives in reduced form within 臧.
- **examples:**
  - 床字左边是爿(床字左邊是爿)。
    - Chuáng zì zuǒbiān shì qiáng.
    - On the left side of the character 床 is 爿.
  - 古文字中，爿象床的形状(古文字中，爿象床的形狀)。
    - Gǔ wénzì zhōng, qiáng xiàng chuáng de xíngzhuàng.
    - In ancient scripts, 爿 resembles the shape of a bed.
  - 臧字里有个简化的爿(臧字裡有個簡化的爿)。
    - Zāng zì lǐ yǒu gè jiǎnhuà de qiáng.
    - Inside the character 臧 there is a contracted form of 爿.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬) → 将(將) → 肉
- **definition:** meat
- **pinyin:** ròu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: ribs/section of meat; stylized as 月 when beside a phonetic, and as 𱼀 or 夕 inside some characters.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the cross-section of flesh with ribs or a slice of meat. In compounds it often appears as a component written like 月, or reduced to 𱼀/夕, which can be mistaken for the moon component but actually signals ‘meat/flesh.’
- **examples:**
  - 我不吃猪肉(我不吃豬肉)。
    - Wǒ bù chī zhūròu.
    - I don't eat pork.
  - 这道菜肉很多，很鲜嫩(這道菜肉很多，很鮮嫩)。
    - Zhè dào cài ròu hěn duō, hěn xiānnèn.
    - This dish has a lot of meat; it's very tender.
  - 受伤后，他手上的肉裂开了(受傷後，他手上的肉裂開了)。
    - Shòushāng hòu, tā shǒushang de ròu liè kāi le.
    - After being injured, the flesh on his hand split open.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬) → 将(將) → 寸
- **definition:** inch; hand
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - again; (archaic) right hand
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram (指事)
  - **description:** Indicative of the position on the forearm where the pulse is palpated (寸口); the unit sense derives from the wrist-to-point distance. Compare 又, 手, 九, 爪.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a wrist/hand with a small mark showing the pulse point (寸口). From the short span used to locate that point, it generalized to the unit meaning “inch,” and as a component it often relates to hand/action or small measures.
- **examples:**
  - 这块木板只有三寸厚(這塊木板只有三寸厚)。
    - Zhè kuài mùbǎn zhǐyǒu sān cùn hòu.
    - This plank is only three inches thick.
  - 医生在我的寸口上按脉(醫生在我的寸口上按脈)。
    - Yīshēng zài wǒ de cùnkǒu shàng àn mài.
    - The doctor presses my pulse at the cun point.
  - 城市里寸土寸金(城市裡寸土寸金)。
    - Chéngshì lǐ cùn tǔ cùn jīn.
    - In the city, every inch of land is as precious as gold.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬) → 将(將) → 寸 → 又
- **definition:** again; (archaic) right hand
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬) → 将(將) → 寸 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
