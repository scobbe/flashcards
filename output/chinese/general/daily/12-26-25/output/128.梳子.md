### comb
---
- **pinyin:** comb
- **components:**
  - 梳
    - shū
    - comb; combing; to comb
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child; small thing; son; seed
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 梳(梳) (shū, "comb") + 子(子) (zǐ, "small thing") — small thing for combing
- **examples:**
  - 你需要一把梳子(你需要一把梳子)。
    - Nǐ xūyào yī bǎ shūzi.
    - You need a comb.
  - 我在用梳子(我在用梳子)来整理头发。
    - Wǒ zài yòng shūzi lái zhěnglǐ tóufǎ.
    - I am using a comb to tidy my hair.
  - 她的梳子(她的梳子)很好看！
    - Tā de shūzi hěn hǎokàn!
    - Her comb is very beautiful!
### 梳子(梳子) → 梳(梳)
- **definition:** comb; combing; to comb
- **pinyin:** shū
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood
  - 余
    - yú
    - surplus; remainder
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** The character combines the semantic component 木 (wood) which relates to the material of the comb, and the phonetic component 余 (yú), suggesting the pronunciation.
  - **interpretation:** A comb is typically made from wood, and the character reflects this through the inclusion of the wood component. The phonetic component aids in conveying the pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 我需要一把梳(我需要一把梳)。
    - Wǒ xūyào yī bǎ shū。
    - I need a comb.
  - 她每天都梳头发(她每天都梳頭髮)。
    - Tā měitiān dōu shū tóufǎ。
    - She combs her hair every day.
### 梳子(梳子) → 梳(梳) → 木(木)
- **definition:** wood
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tree: branches on top, roots on the bottom.
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a tree with its distinguishing features, conveying the idea of wood directly through its shape.
- **examples:**
  - 这棵树是木的代表。(這棵樹是木的代表。)
    - Zhè kē shù shì mù de dàibiǎo.
    - This tree is a representation of wood.
  - 木材是建筑的重要材料。(木材是建築的重要材料。)
    - Mùcái shì jiànzhú de zhòngyào cáiliào.
    - Wood is an important material for construction.
### 梳子(梳子) → 梳(梳) → 余(余)
- **definition:** surplus; remainder
- **pinyin:** yú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character 余 represents the notion of surplus or excess, possibly picturing the idea of having more than what is needed.
  - **interpretation:** The character intuitively conveys the idea of having something left over or in excess, reflecting the abundance or surplus concept.
- **examples:**
  - 我的余钱不多(我的餘錢不多)，但足够用(但足夠用)。
  - 我们还有余粮(我們還有餘糧)，可以再待几天(可以再待幾天)。
  - 这是他的余生(這是他的餘生)，希望能过得快乐(希望能過得快樂)。
### 梳子(梳子) → 子(子)
- **definition:** child; small thing; son; seed
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a child or infant.
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a small person or child, intuitively symbolizing youth and innocence.
- **examples:**
  - 这是我的孩子(這是我的孩子)。
    - Zhè shì wǒ de háizi.
    - This is my child.
  - 子女(子女)都很聪明(都很聰明)。
    - Zǐnǚ dōu hěn cōngmíng.
    - The children are all very smart.
