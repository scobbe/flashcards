### Renminbi (RMB)
---
- **pinyin:** Renminbi (RMB)
- **components:**
  - 人民
    - rénmín
    - people; populace
  - 币(幣)
    - bì
    - currency; money
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 人(人民) (rén, "people") + 民(人民) (mín, "people") + 币(幣) (bì, "currency") — people's currency
  - **interpretation:** This term describes the official currency used by the people of China. It emphasizes that this is the currency for the general populace.
- **examples:**
  - 我用人民币支付。(我用人民幣支付。)
    - Wǒ yòng rénmínbi zhīfù.
    - I pay with Renminbi.
  - 人民币是中国的官方货币。(人民幣是中國的官方貨幣。)
    - Rénmínbì shì Zhōngguó de guānfāng huòbì.
    - Renminbi is the official currency of China.

### 人民币(人民幣) → 币(幣)
- **definition:** currency; money
- **pinyin:** bì
- **components:**
  - 敝
    - bì
    - to wear out
  - 巾
    - jīn
    - towel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Phonetic 敝 (bì, 'to wear out') + semantic 巾 (jīn, 'towel') — originally indicating cloth used for gifts or sacrifices.
  - **interpretation:** This character illustrates the use of cloth, particularly in the context of gifting or value, connecting the idea of silk or cloth to currency.
  - **simplification:** The simplified form replaces the complex component 干 with 币 (bì) in a phonetic role, simplifying the character while maintaining its primary phonetic and semantic function.
- **examples:**
  - 我需要一些币(我需要一些幣)，用于交易(用於交易)。
    - Wǒ xūyào yīxiē bì, yòng yú jiāoyì.
    - I need some currency for transactions.
  - 这张币(這張幣)是新的(是新的)。
    - Zhè zhāng bì shì xīn de.
    - This currency note is new.
  - 他用币(他用幣)买了很多东西(買了很多東西)。
    - Tā yòng bì mǎile hěnduō dōngxi.
    - He bought many things with currency.
### 人民币(人民幣) → 币(幣) → 敝(敝)

- **pinyin:** bì
- **components:**
  - 巾

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Phonetic 巾 (jīn, 'cloth') + semantic 攴 (pū, 'to tap/strike') — the evolution of 巾 created a character representing dirty or worn cloth.
  - **interpretation:** This makes intuitive sense as it visually connects the idea of cloth becoming worn and dirty, which aligns with the meaning of 'to wear out.'
- **examples:**

    - Tā de xiézi zǎo yǐ bì, kàn shàngqù hěn bù zhěngjié.
