## Renminbi (RMB)
### rénmínbì
---
- **definition:** Renminbi (RMB)
- **pinyin:** rénmínbì
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person; people
  - 民
    - mín
    - people; public
  - 币(幣)
    - bì
    - currency; money
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 人(人) (rén, "person") + 民(民) (mín, "people") + 币(幣) (bì, "currency") =
    - currency of the people ->
    - Renminbi, the official currency of China
  - **interpretation:** The name 人民币(人民幣) (rénmínbì) signifies that the currency belongs to and represents the people of the nation, emphasizing its national significance.
- **examples:**
  - 我用人民币买了一杯咖啡(我用人民幣買了一杯咖啡)。
    - wǒ yòng rénmínbì mǎile yī bēi kāfēi.
    - I used Renminbi to buy a cup of coffee.
  - 商店接受人民币(商店接受人民幣)，优惠幅度很大(優惠幅度很大)。
    - shāngdiàn jiēshòu rénmínbì, yōuhuì fúdù hěn dà.
    - The shop accepts Renminbi, and the discounts are substantial.
  - 人民银行发行人民币(人民銀行發行人民幣)，支持经济发展(支持經濟發展)。
    - rénmínyínháng fāxíng rénmínbì, zhīchí jīngjì fāzhǎn.
    - The People's Bank issues Renminbi to support economic development.

### 人民币(人民幣) → 民(民)
- **definition:** people; public
- **pinyin:** mín
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted an eye pierced by a dagger (claimed) ->
    - later borrowed for 民 (mín, 'the people')
  - **interpretation:** The character 民(mín) primarily serves as a loan character, having lost its original pictorial depiction possibly related to an eye and a dagger. Its current meaning as 'the people' is a result of its phonetic borrowing and widespread use.
- **examples:**
  - 民众拥有参与决策的权利(民眾擁有參與決策的權利)。
    - Mín zhòng yǒngyǒu cānyù juécè de quánlì.
    - The people have the right to participate in decision-making.
  - 国家依靠民意趋向稳定(國家依靠民意趨向穩定)，推动社会进步(推動社會進步)。
    - Guójiā yīkào mínyì qūxiàng wěndìng, tuīdòng shèhuì jìnbù.
    - The country relies on the people's opinions for stability, promoting social progress.
  - 选民积极行使权力(選民積極行使權力)，维护国家安全(維護國家安全)。
    - Xuǎn mín jījí xíngshǐ quánlì, wéihù guójiā ānquán.
    - Voters actively exercise their rights, safeguarding national security.
### 人民币(人民幣) → 币(幣)
- **definition:** currency; money
- **pinyin:** bì
- **components:**
  - 敝
    - bì
    - broken
  - 巾
    - jīn
    - towel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 敝(敝) (bì) + Semantic: 巾(巾) (jīn, 'towel') =
    - silk as gift or sacrifice ->
    - currency
  - **interpretation:** The character 幣(幣) (bì) originally depicts a gift of cloth, with 敝(敝) (bì) contributing the sound and 巾(巾) (jīn, 'towel') suggesting the material. This association with valuable cloth evolved into the concept of currency.
  - **simplification:** 幣(幣) was simplified to 币(幣) by reducing 敝(敝) (bì) to the stroke 丿, maintaining the overall shape while creating a more concise form.
- **examples:**
  - 他刚换了一些币(他剛換了一些幣)，准备出国旅行(準備出國旅行)。
    - Tā gāng huàn le yīxiē bì, zhǔnbèi chūguó lǚxíng.
    - He just exchanged some currency, preparing to travel abroad.
  - 这种币非常流通(這種幣非常流通)。
    - Zhè zhǒng bì fēicháng liútōng.
    - This type of currency is widely circulated.
### 人民币(人民幣) → 币(幣) → 敝(敝)
- **definition:** broken
- **pinyin:** bì
- **components:**
  - 巾
    - jīn
    - cloth
  - 攴
    - pū
    - tap
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 巾(巾) (jīn, 'cloth') + Semantic: 攴(攴) (pū, 'tap') ->
    - Evolved left component into 㡀, representing dust/dirt on cloth ->
    - Final form 敝 (bì, 'broken')
  - **interpretation:** The character 敝 (bì, 'broken') combines the phonetic element 巾(巾) (jīn, 'cloth') and the semantic element 攴(攴) (pū, 'tap'). The overall imagery evokes the idea of a worn or dirty cloth, supporting the meaning 'broken' or 'worn out.'
- **examples:**
  - 敝车年久失修(敝車年久失修)。
    - Bì chē nián jiǔ shī xiū.
    - The broken car has long been in disrepair.
  - 他的敝鞋破旧不堪(他的敝鞋破舊不堪)。
    - Tā de bì xié pò jiù bù kān.
    - His broken shoes are in a sorry state.
### 人民币(人民幣) → 币(幣) → 敝(敝) → 巾(巾)
- **definition:** cloth
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a hanging piece of cloth
  - **interpretation:** The character 巾(jīn) is a straightforward pictogram, visually representing a piece of cloth. Its simple design captures the essence of a cloth item hanging down, making the character intuitive to understand.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿出一块巾(他拿出一塊巾)，擦拭脸上的汗水(擦拭臉上的汗水)。
    - tā ná chū yī kuài jīn, cā shì liǎn shàng de hàn shuǐ.
    - He took out a cloth and wiped the sweat off his face.
  - 小店推荐一种新型的绣花巾(小店推薦一種新型的繡花巾)，既美观又实用(既美觀又實用)。
    - xiǎo diàn tuī jiàn yī zhǒng xīn xíng de xiù huā jīn, jì měi guān yòu shí yòng.
    - The small shop recommends a new embroidered cloth, which is both beautiful and practical.
### 人民币(人民幣) → 币(幣) → 敝(敝) → 攴(攴)
- **definition:** tap
- **pinyin:** pū
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts hand (又) holding a tool ->
    - stylized as 卜
  - **interpretation:** The character started as a pictogram of a hand holding a tool, signifying an action to hit. Over time, the tool element became stylized into the component 卜, which is now recognized as the phonetic part.
- **examples:**
  - 他的攴很灵巧(他的攴很靈巧)。
    - Tā de pū hěn língqiǎo.
    - His hand is very dexterous.
  - 受伤后(受傷後)，他的攴失去了灵活性(他的攴失去了靈活性)，医生建议他康复(醫生建議他康復)。
    - Shòushāng hòu, tā de pū shīqùle línghuóxìng, yīshēng jiànyì tā kāngfù.
    - After the injury, his hand lost its agility, and the doctor advised him to recover.
