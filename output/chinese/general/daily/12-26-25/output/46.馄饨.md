## wonton
### húntun
---
- **traditional:** 餛飩
- **definition:** wonton
- **pinyin:** húntun
- **components:**
  - 馄(餛)
    - hún
    - mix; confused
  - 饨(飩)
    - tún
    - swallow; round food
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 餛(馄) (hún, "mix; confused") and 飩(饨) (tún, "swallow; round food") together refer to a type of dumpling with mixed ingredients inside, typically boiled and served in soup.
  - **interpretation:** The word 馄饨(餛飩) (húntun, "wonton") describes a dumpling with various ingredients inside, represented by the notion of mixing in 餛(馄) (hún, "mix; confused"). The 饨(飩) (tún, "swallow; round food") highlights its round shape and that it is meant to be eaten whole, often served in soup.
  - **simplification:** Simplification: 餛→馄, 飩→饨
- **examples:**
  - 我点了馄饨(餛飩)。
    - Wǒ diǎnle hún tún.
    - I ordered wonton.
  - 早餐吃馄饨(餛飩)，营养丰(營養豐)富。
    - Zǎocān chī hún tún, yíngyǎng fēngfù.
    - Having wonton for breakfast makes it nutritious.
  - 他在街角的摊位买馄饨(餛飩)，味道极(極)佳。
    - Tā zài jiējiǎo de tānwèi mǎi hún tún, wèidào jí jiā.
    - He buys wonton at the street corner stall; the taste is excellent.

### 馄(餛)饨(飩) → 馄(餛) → 昆

- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 昆 (kūn, 'descendants') uses the sun 日 (rì, 'sun') to symbolize extension or brightness, while 比 (bǐ, 'compare'), as a phonetic component, suggests reproduction or a continuation of a lineage. The combined imagery of two lights metaphorically communicates to the continuation of the family line through descendants.
