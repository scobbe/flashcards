- **traditional:** 餛飩
- **definition:** wonton
- **pinyin:** húntun
- **components:**
  - 馄(餛)
    - hún
    - mix; confused
  - 饨(飩)
    - tún
    - swallow; round food
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 餛(馄) (hún, "mix; confused") and 飩(饨) (tún, "swallow; round food") together refer to a type of dumpling with mixed ingredients inside, typically boiled and served in soup.
  - **interpretation:** The word 馄(餛)饨(飩) (húntun, "wonton") describes a dumpling with various ingredients inside, represented by the notion of mixing in 餛(馄) (hún, "mix; confused"). The 饨(飩) (tún, "swallow; round food") highlights its round shape and that it is meant to be eaten whole, often served in soup.
  - **simplification:** Simplification: 餛→馄, 飩→饨
- **examples:**
