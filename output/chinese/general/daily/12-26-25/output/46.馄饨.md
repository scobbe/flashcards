## wonton
### húntun
---
- **definition:** wonton
- **pinyin:** húntun
- **components:**
  - 馄(餛)
    - hún
    - mix
  - 饨(飩)
    - tún
    - swallow
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 餛(馄) (hún, "mix") + 飩(饨) (tún, "swallow") =
    - mixed dough that is swallowed easily ->
    - wonton
  - **interpretation:** The word 餛飩(馄饨) describes a type of dumpling that is easy to swallow, emphasizing its smooth and delicate texture. This highlights its culinary characteristic as a food that is both mixed and refined for consumption.
- **examples:**
  - 她每天都在街角的小店里吃馄饨(她每天都在街角的小店裡吃餛飩)。
    - Tā měitiān dōu zài jiējiǎo de xiǎodiàn lǐ chī húntun.
    - She eats wontons at the small shop on the street corner every day.
  - 天很冷(天很冷)，馄饨汤温暖你的心(餛飩湯溫暖你的心)。
    - Tiān hěn lěng, húntun tāng wēnnuǎn nǐ de xīn.
    - It is very cold, but wonton soup warms your heart.
  - 老爷爷坐在门前(老爺爺坐在門前)，吃着热气腾腾的馄饨(吃著熱氣騰騰的餛飩)。
    - Lǎoyéyé zuò zài mén qián, chīzhe rèqì téngténg de húntun.
    - The old man sits by the door and eats steaming hot wontons.
### 馄饨(餛飩) → 馄(餛)
- **definition:** mix
- **pinyin:** hún
- **components:**
  - 饣(食)
    - shí
    - food
  - 昆
    - kūn
    - 昆
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 饣(食) (shí, "food") + phonetic: 昆(昆) (kūn) =
    - food mixture ->
    - mix
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 饣(食) (shí, "food") indicating relation to food, with 昆(昆) (kūn) suggesting the sound, to convey the idea of mixing food ingredients, fitting the culinary context.
  - **simplification:** 饣(食) (shí, "food") replaces 餠 component for a simplified semantic representation.
- **examples:**
  - 他把红色和蓝色馄(餛)在一起，制成了美丽的紫色(製成了美麗的紫色)。
    - Tā bǎ hóngsè hé lánsè hún zài yìqǐ, zhìchéngle měilì de zǐsè.
    - He mixed red and blue together to make a beautiful purple.
  - 厨师将多种香料馄融合后(廚師將多種香料餛融合後)，做出了一道美味佳肴(做出了一道美味佳餚)。
    - Chúshī jiāng duōzhǒng xiāngliào hún rónghé hòu, zuò chūle yīdào měiwèi jiāyáo.
    - The chef mixed various spices and then created a delicious dish.
### 馄饨(餛飩) → 馄(餛) → 饣(食)
- **definition:** food
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram ( 象形 )
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a mouth 亼 (jié, 'assemble') over a bowl of rice on a stand, represented by 皀 (bó, 'pictogram'), resembling but unrelated to 良 (liáng, 'good') or 艮 (gěn, 'stopping') ->
    - food
  - **interpretation:** The character 食(食) (shí, 'food') visually represents a scene of eating, with a mouth positioned above a bowl or stand of food, encapsulating the concept of nourishment.
  - **simplification:** 饣 is a simplified radical form representing 食(食). It retains the core idea by using a cursive form that resembles the original components, primarily focusing on the 'food' aspect.
- **examples:**
  - 许多汉字中含有饣部(許多漢字中含有食部)，它们与食物有密切关联(它們與食物有密切關聯)。
    - Xǔduō hànzì zhōng hányǒu shí bù, tāmen yǔ shíwù yǒu mìqiè guānlián.
    - Many Chinese characters contain the '饣' radical, which is closely related to food.
  - 博物馆展览介绍了饣的演变(博物館展覽介紹了食的演變)，反映出人们对食的热爱(反映出人們對食的熱愛)。
    - Bówùguǎn zhǎnlǎn jièshào le shí de yǎnbiàn, fǎnyìng chū rénmen duì shí de rè'ài.
    - The museum exhibition introduced the evolution of the '饣' radical, reflecting people's love for food.

    - shí
    - food
  - 屯

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 饣(飠) (shí, "food") + phonetic: 屯(屯) (tún, "assembled") =
    - eating an assembled, wrapped food item ->
    - swallow dumpling
  - **interpretation:** The character 饨(飩) (tún, "swallow") combines 饣(飠) (shí, "food") indicating a food item, with 屯(屯) (tún, "assembled") as the phonetic component, hinting at its pronunciation. This is associated with a 'swallow' or dumpling, a type of food.

- **examples:**

    - Tā cháng le yī kǒu tāng, lìkè tún le xiàqù.
