## 先辈(先輩)
---
## forebears
### xiānbèi
---
- **definition:** forebears
- **pinyin:** xiānbèi
- **components:**
  - 先
    - xiān
    - first; earlier; ahead; before
  - 辈(輩)
    - bèi
    - generation; cohort; peers; seniority
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 先 (earlier; first) + 辈/輩 (generation; cohort) = earlier generation → forebears.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the idea of being earlier with the notion of a generation to denote people from earlier generations. Often conveys respect for predecessors in a family or field.
  - **simplification:** 輩 was simplified to 辈 by reducing the 車 component and streamlining the structure around 非 to cut strokes; 先 remained unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我们缅怀先辈(我們緬懷先輩)，学习他们的精神(學習他們的精神)。
    - Wǒmen miǎnhuái xiānbèi, xuéxí tāmen de jīngshén.
    - We commemorate our forebears and learn from their spirit.
  - 她向科学界的先辈致敬(她向科學界的先輩致敬)。
    - Tā xiàng kēxuéjiè de xiānbèi zhìjìng.
    - She pays tribute to the forebears of the scientific community.
  - 作为后生(作為後生)，我们要尊敬先辈(我們要尊敬先輩)。
    - Zuòwéi hòushēng, wǒmen yào zūnjìng xiānbèi.
    - As the younger generation, we should respect our predecessors.
### 先辈(先輩) → 先(先)
- **definition:** first; earlier; ahead; before
- **pinyin:** xiān
- **components:**
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - stop; to stop
  - 儿(兒)
    - ér
    - son; child
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 止 ("foot") + 儿 ("person") — to go forward.
  - **interpretation:** Combines a foot and a person to depict someone stepping ahead, giving senses like 'before' and 'first.' Originally conveyed advancing; later generalized to precedence in time or position.
- **examples:**
  - 我们先走吧(我們先走吧)。
    - Wǒmen xiān zǒu ba.
    - Let’s go first.
  - 你先吃(你先吃)，我一会儿到(我一會兒到)。
    - Nǐ xiān chī, wǒ yíhuìr dào.
    - You eat first; I’ll arrive in a moment.
  - 先到先得(先到先得)。
    - Xiān dào xiān dé.
    - First come, first served.
### 先辈(先輩) → 先(先) → 止(止)
- **definition:** stop; to stop
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a footprint pointing up (originally “foot”), later stylized as 龰; oracle forms show three toes and a sole.
  - **interpretation:** A footprint with toes pointing upward represents the foot. The sense broadened from the physical foot to the act of halting one’s step, hence “to stop”; the specific ‘toe’ meaning was preserved in 趾.
- **examples:**
  - 请立即停止前进(請立即停止前進)。
    - Qǐng lìjí tíngzhǐ qiánjìn.
    - Please stop advancing immediately.
  - 此处止步(此處止步)，非请勿入(非請勿入)。
    - Cǐ chù zhǐbù, fēi qǐng wù rù.
    - Stop here; do not enter without invitation.
  - 药到病除(藥到病除)，疼痛已止(疼痛已止)。
    - Yào dào bìng chú, téngtòng yǐ zhǐ.
    - With the right medicine, the illness is cured and the pain has stopped.
### 先辈(先輩) → 先(先) → 儿(兒)
- **definition:** son; child
- **pinyin:** ér
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 臼
    - jiù
    - mortar
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a standing person; radical form of 人; also simplified from 兒 by eliminating 臼
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person standing on two legs, which is why it doubles as the person radical form. In traditional 兒, an added 臼 element evoked a baby’s soft skull, but simplification removed that part, leaving only the person/legs shape.
  - **simplification:** Stroke reduction by dropping 臼 from 兒 and reusing the existing person-radical form 儿.
- **examples:**
  - 我儿子在学校(我兒子在學校)。
    - Wǒ érzi zài xuéxiào.
    - My son is at school.
  - 她是我的女儿(她是我的女兒)。
    - Tā shì wǒ de nǚ'ér.
    - She is my daughter.
  - 这些电影很适合儿童观看(這些電影很適合兒童觀看)。
    - Zhèxiē diànyǐng hěn shìhé értóng guānkàn.
    - These movies are very suitable for children to watch.
### 先辈(先輩) → 先(先) → 儿(兒) → 人(人)
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 先辈(先輩) → 先(先) → 儿(兒) → 臼(臼)
- **definition:** mortar
- **pinyin:** jiù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a mortar. See the original version of 舂, in which a pestle is visible on top.
  - **interpretation:** A direct drawing of a mortar, showing the bowl used for pounding. Connection to 舂 (to pound) is historical, where a pestle appears above. Similar shapes seen in 學、兒、䳔、鼠、叟 are coincidental and unrelated.
- **examples:**
  - 他用杵在臼里舂米(他用杵在臼裡舂米)。
    - Tā yòng chǔ zài jiù lǐ chōng mǐ.
    - He uses a pestle to pound rice in the mortar.
  - 这个石臼很重(這個石臼很重)。
    - Zhège shíjiù hěn zhòng.
    - This stone mortar is very heavy.
  - 实验室里常用研钵和研杵(實驗室裡常用研鉢和研杵)。
    - Shíyànshì lǐ cháng yòng yánbō hé yánchǔ.
    - In laboratories, a mortar and pestle are commonly used.
### 先辈(先輩) → 辈(輩)
- **definition:** generation; cohort; peers; seniority
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **components:**
  - 车(車)
    - chē
    - vehicle; cart
  - 非
    - fēi
    - not; wrong
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 車 + phonetic 非; simplified 車 → 车.
  - **interpretation:** Combines 車 as the semantic element, hinting at grouping or arrangement, with 非 contributing the sound. The sense developed to mean a class or generation of people, i.e., those grouped together by age or rank.
  - **simplification:** Simplified form of 輩 by component simplification 車 → 车, following the general simplification of the 車 radical/component.
- **examples:**
  - 作为晚辈(作為晚輩)，我们应该尊敬长辈(我們應該尊敬長輩)。
    - Zuòwéi wǎnbèi, wǒmen yīnggāi zūnjìng zhǎngbèi.
    - As juniors, we should respect our elders.
  - 他们是同辈(他們是同輩)，所以说话比较随便(所以說話比較隨便)。
    - Tāmen shì tóngbèi, suǒyǐ shuōhuà bǐjiào suíbiàn.
    - They are peers, so they speak more casually.
  - 在这个家族(在這個傢族)，辈分很严格(輩分很嚴格)。
    - Zài zhège jiāzú, bèifèn hěn yángé.
    - In this family, seniority is strictly observed.
### 先辈(先輩) → 辈(輩) → 车(車)
- **definition:** vehicle; cart
- **pinyin:** chē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — originally a carriage seen from above; 车 is from the cursive script of 車.
  - **interpretation:** A top-down depiction of a cart: two wheels and a central frame, later regularized into vertical strokes. The simplified 车 follows a cursive shorthand of 車, keeping the overall outline while dropping interior detail.
  - **simplification:** Derived from the cursive form of 車 and adopted as a simplified shape to reduce strokes and ease writing.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了一辆新车(我買了一輛新車)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le yī liàng xīn chē.
    - I bought a new car.
  - 这条路上车很多(這條路上車很多)。
    - Zhè tiáo lù shàng chē hěn duō.
    - There are many vehicles on this road.
  - 火车八点到站(火車八點到站)。
    - Huǒchē bā diǎn dào zhàn.
    - The train arrives at eight.
### 先辈(先輩) → 辈(輩) → 非(非)
- **definition:** not; wrong
- **pinyin:** fēi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Two wings facing opposite directions; originally ‘wings’, later ‘leave; disobey’, then used as a phonetic loan for ‘not’.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a pair of wings set in opposition. The sense shifted from a concrete image to the ideas of divergence or disobedience, and was later borrowed to express negation “not.” Often confused in shape with 韭, but unrelated.
- **examples:**
  - 这并非你的错(這並非你的錯)。
    - Zhè bìng fēi nǐ de cuò.
    - This is not your fault.
  - 他的做法非法(他的做法非法)。
    - Tā de zuòfǎ fēifǎ.
    - His approach is illegal.
  - 不要把是非颠倒(不要把是非顛倒)。
    - Búyào bǎ shìfēi diāndǎo.
    - Don’t turn right and wrong upside down.
