## long time no see
### hǎojiǔ bújiàn
---
- **traditional:** 好久不見
- **definition:** long time no see
- **pinyin:** hǎojiǔ bújiàn
- **components:**
  - 好
    - hǎo
    - very; good
  - 久
    - jiǔ
    - long time
  - 不
    - bù
    - not; no
  - 见(見)

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 好 (hǎo, "very") + 久 (jiǔ, "long time") + 不 (bù, "not") + 见(見) (jiàn, "see"). This phrase is used when meeting someone after not having seen them for a while.
  - **interpretation:** 好( )久( )不( )见(見) (hǎojiǔ bújiàn, "long time no see") is a greeting used when you meet someone again after a long period has passed. It conveys the idea of being pleased to see each other after a long absence, reflecting friendliness and warmth.
