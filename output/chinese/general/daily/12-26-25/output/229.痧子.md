## measles
### shāzi
---
- **definition:** measles
- **pinyin:** shāzi
- **components:**
  - 痧
    - shā
    - rash; measles
  - 子
    - zi
    - child; small thing
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 痧(痧) (shā, "rash") + 子(子) (zi, "child; small thing"), referring to measles
  - **interpretation:** The word 痧子 (shāzi) refers to measles by combining 痧(痧) (shā, "rash"), which represents the characteristic rash associated with the disease, and 子(子) (zi, "child; small thing"), indicating something small or relating to children, as measles commonly affects young children.
- **examples:**
  - 他得了痧子(他得了痧子)。
    - Tā déle shāzi.
    - He contracted measles.
  - 当孩子暴露在阳光下时(當孩子暴露在陽光下時)，痧子容易传播(痧子容易傳播)。
    - Dāng háizi bàolù zài yángguāng xià shí, shāzi róngyì chuánbò.
    - When children are exposed to sunlight, measles spreads easily.
  - 医生建议及时治疗痧子(醫生建議及時治療痧子)。
    - Yīshēng jiànyì jíshí zhìliáo shāzi.
    - The doctor advised prompt treatment for measles.
### 痧子(痧子) → 痧(痧)
- **definition:** rash; measles
- **pinyin:** shā
- **components:**
  - 疒
    - nè
    - illness; disease
  - 沙
    - shā
    - sand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 疒(疒) (nè, "illness; disease") + phonetic: 沙(沙) (shā, "sand") =
    - conveys the idea of a sand-like rash ->
    - rash; measles
  - **interpretation:** The character 痧(shā, "rash; measles") combines the semantic component 疒(疒) (nè, "illness; disease") with the phonetic 沙(沙) (shā, "sand"). This pairing suggests an illness characterized by rash-like symptoms, akin to the texture of sand.
- **examples:**
  - 他皮肤上出现痧(他皮膚上出現痧)。
    - Tā pífū shàng chūxiàn shā.
    - A rash appeared on his skin.
  - 高温天气时(高溫天氣時)，部分人会得痧(部分人會得痧)。
    - Gāowēn tiānqì shí, bùfèn rén huì dé shā.
    - In hot weather, some people may develop a rash.

### 痧子(痧子) → 痧(痧) → 沙(沙) → 少(少)

- **pinyin:** shǎo
- **components:**

    - shā
    - sand
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts four dots as grains of sand to indicate "a few, a little"; the form is also phonetically borrowed from 小(xiǎo, "small")
  - **interpretation:** The character 少(shǎo, "few") uses dots to suggest the idea of smallness or scarcity, similar to grains of sand. It is visually and phonetically associated with 小(xiǎo, "small").
- **examples:**

    - He has little time, which he uses to rest.
  - 这部电影票房少得可怜(這部電影票房少得可憐)。
    - Zhè bù diànyǐng piàofáng shǎo de kělián.
    - The movie's box office was pitifully low.
