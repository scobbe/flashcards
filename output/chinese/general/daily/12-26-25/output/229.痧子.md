## measles
### shāzi
---
- **definition:** measles
- **pinyin:** shāzi
- **components:**
  - 痧
    - shā
    - rash; heat-rash; summer-heat illness; cholera (TCM)
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child; son; seed; noun suffix
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 痧 (rash) + 子 (noun suffix) = rash illness; measles.
  - **interpretation:** Colloquial/regional name for a rash illness, often equivalent to measles (麻疹). Emphasizes the rash aspect signaled by 痧.
- **examples:**
  - 孩子出痧子了(孩子出痧子了)，别着凉(別著涼)。
    - Háizi chū shāzi le, bié zháo liáng.
    - The child has come out with measles; don’t let them catch cold.
  - 医生说他得了痧子(醫生說他得了痧子)，需要休息(需要休息)。
    - Yīshēng shuō tā dé le shāzi, xūyào xiūxi.
    - The doctor said he has measles and needs rest.
  - 出痧子时要注意隔离(出痧子時要注意隔離)，防止传染(防止傳染)。
    - Chū shāzi shí yào zhùyì gélí, fángzhǐ chuánrǎn.
    - When measles breaks out, isolation is important to prevent transmission.
### 痧子 → 痧
- **definition:** rash; heat-rash; summer-heat illness; cholera (TCM)
- **pinyin:** shā
- **components:**
  - 沙
    - shā
    - sand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 疒 + phonetic: 沙
  - **interpretation:** Combines the illness radical 疒 indicating disease with 沙 as the sound component shā. This structure signals a sickness-related term, later applied to heat rash/summer-heat illness and cholera in traditional medical usage.
- **examples:**
  - 他得了暑痧(他得了暑痧)，需要中医调理(需要中醫調理)。
    - Tā déle shǔshā, xūyào zhōngyī tiáolǐ.
    - He has summer-heat illness and needs traditional Chinese medical treatment.
  - 在旧籍中(在舊籍中)，霍乱被称为痧症(霍亂被稱為痧症)。
    - Zài jiùjí zhōng, huòluàn bèi chēngwéi shāzhèng.
    - In old texts, cholera was called the shā-illness.
  - 他胸闷腹泻(他胸悶腹瀉)，被诊断为暑痧(被診斷為暑痧)。
    - Tā xiōngmèn fùxiè, bèi zhěnduàn wéi shǔshā.
    - He had chest oppression and diarrhea and was diagnosed with summer-heat illness.
### 痧子 → 痧 → 沙
- **definition:** sand
- **pinyin:** shā
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 少
    - shǎo
    - few; little
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 水 (“water”) + 少 (“to lack; to be short of”).
  - **interpretation:** Combines “water” with “few/less” to suggest a place where water is scant, such as shallow edges or dried areas where grit remains. By extension it came to denote the fine granular material known as sand.
- **examples:**
  - 沙漠很广阔(沙漠很廣闊)。
    - Shāmò hěn guǎngkuò.
    - The desert is vast.
  - 海风把沙吹得到处都是(海風把沙吹得到處都是)。
    - Hǎifēng bǎ shā chuī de dàochù dōu shì.
    - The sea breeze blew sand everywhere.
  - 小孩在沙滩上堆城堡(小孩在沙灘上堆城堡)。
    - Xiǎohái zài shātān shàng duī chéngbǎo.
    - Children build sandcastles on the beach.
### 痧子 → 痧 → 沙 → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram of water flowing; sometimes written as the variant 氺; next to a character it is compressed and stylized into 氵 (unrelated to 冫).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts flowing water with a main stream and side droplets. In compound characters it condenses to the left-side form 氵 to signal a water-related meaning, distinct from 冫 which indicates ice or cold.
- **examples:**
  - 我想喝一杯水(我想喝一杯水)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng hē yì bēi shuǐ.
    - I want to drink a glass of water.
  - 城市的自来水很干净(城市的自來水很乾淨)。
    - Chéngshì de zìláishuǐ hěn gānjìng.
    - The tap water in the city is very clean.
  - 旱季来了(旱季來了)，我们要节约用水(我們要節約用水)。
    - Hànjì lái le, wǒmen yào jiéyuē yòngshuǐ.
    - The dry season has arrived; we should conserve water.
### 痧子 → 痧 → 沙 → 少
- **definition:** few; little
- **pinyin:** shǎo
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: four dots (like grains of sand) indicating “a few, a little”; phonetically borrowed from 小 (“small,” depicted by three dots). Unrelated to 步.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a small cluster of dots, evoking a tiny quantity such as grains of sand, hence the sense “few; little.” Its form and sound link to 小, which also shows small marks and means “small,” but it is not connected to 步/歲 despite visual resemblance.
- **examples:**
  - 我们的时间很少(我們的時間很少)。
    - Wǒmen de shíjiān hěn shǎo.
    - We have very little time.
  - 今天来的人很少(今天來的人很少)。
    - Jīntiān lái de rén hěn shǎo.
    - Few people came today.
  - 他话不多(他話不多)，笑得也少(笑得也少)。
    - Tā huà bù duō, xiào de yě shǎo.
    - He doesn't talk much, and he also smiles little.
### 痧子 → 痧 → 沙 → 少 → 小
- **definition:** small; little
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: three small marks/dots representing smallness; later the dots elongated; Shuowen misread it as 会意: 八 + 丨.
  - **interpretation:** Three short strokes portray a tiny object or the idea of smallness. As the script evolved, the dots were drawn as short lines, which led to the later but incorrect analysis of 八 plus 丨.
- **examples:**
  - 这个苹果很小(這個蘋果很小)。
    - Zhège píngguǒ hěn xiǎo.
    - This apple is very small.
  - 他把字写得太小(他把字寫得太小)。
    - Tā bǎ zì xiě de tài xiǎo.
    - He wrote the characters too small.
  - 小问题也要重视(小問題也要重視)。
    - Xiǎo wèntí yě yào zhòngshì.
    - Small problems should also be taken seriously.
### 痧子 → 子
- **definition:** child; son; seed; noun suffix
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: image of a baby with a large head and spread arms; legs wrapped in a blanket.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a swaddled baby with a prominent head and outstretched arms. Over time the figure was stylized into the modern form while still evoking an infant.
- **examples:**
  - 他是我唯一的儿子(他是我唯一的兒子)。
    - Tā shì wǒ wéiyī de érzi.
    - He is my only son.
  - 她抱起孩子(她抱起孩子)。
    - Tā bào qǐ háizi.
    - She picked up the child.
  - 这个孩子三岁了(這個孩子三歲了)。
    - Zhège háizi sān suì le.
    - This child is three years old.
