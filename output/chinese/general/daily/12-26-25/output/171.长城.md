## Great Wall
### Chángchéng
---
- **definition:** Great Wall
- **pinyin:** Chángchéng
- **components:**
  - 长(長)
    - cháng
    - long
  - 城
    - chéng
    - wall; city
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 长(長) (cháng, "long") and 城(城) (chéng, "wall; city") refer to the Great Wall of China, a long defensive wall.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 长(長) (cháng, "long") and 城(城) (chéng, "wall") aptly describes the Great Wall as an extensive and massive structure built for protection.
- **examples:**
  - 长城壮观(長城壯觀)。
    - Chángchéng zhuàngguān.
    - The Great Wall is magnificent.
  - 你参观了长城(你參觀了長城)，感受到了中华文明(感受到了中華文明)。
    - Nǐ cānguānle Chángchéng, gǎnshòu dàole Zhōnghuá wénmíng.
    - You visited the Great Wall and experienced Chinese civilization.
  - 夜晚灯光映衬下的长城(夜晚燈光映襯下的長城)，显得更加神秘(顯得更加神秘)。
    - Yèwǎn dēngguāng yìngchèn xià de Chángchéng, xiǎnde gèngjiā shénmì.
    - Under the night lights, the Great Wall appears even more mysterious.
### 长城(長城) → 长(長)
- **definition:** long
- **pinyin:** cháng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a person with long hair and distinctive features
  - **interpretation:** The character 長(長) (cháng, 'long') visually represents its meaning through the depiction of a figure with long hair, highlighting the concept of 'long' or 'length'.
  - **simplification:** 長(長) was simplified to 长(長) by reducing the complexity of the strokes in the hair portion of the character.
- **examples:**
  - 这条路很长(這條路很長)。
    - Zhè tiáo lù hěn cháng.
    - This road is long.
  - 树枝伸得很长(樹枝伸得很長)。
    - Shùzhī shēn de hěn cháng.
    - The branches extend long.
  - 河流缓缓流淌(河流緩緩流淌)，思绪绵长(思緒綿長)。
    - Héliú huǎn huǎn liútǎng, sīxù mián cháng.
    - The river flows slowly, and thoughts are long-lasting.
### 长城(長城) → 城(城)
- **definition:** wall; city
- **pinyin:** chéng
- **components:**
  - ⺍
    - tu
    - earth
  - 成
    - chéng
    - complete
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 土(土) (tǔ, "earth") + phonetic 成(成) (chéng, "complete") =
    - earth embankment ->
    - city walls
  - **interpretation:** The character 城 combines 土(土) (tǔ, "earth"), indicating a structure built from earth, with 成(成) (chéng, "complete"), suggesting completeness or the demarcation of an area, to signify city walls made from earth.
- **examples:**
  - 古城映衬了夕阳(古城映襯了夕陽)。
    - Gǔchéng yìngchèn le xīyáng.
    - The ancient city reflects the setting sun.
  - 我迷恋这座城(我迷戀這座城)，它充满历史韵味(它充滿歷史韻味)。
    - Wǒ míliàn zhè zuò chéng, tā chōngmǎn lìshǐ yùnwèi.
    - I am fascinated by this city, as it is full of historical charm.
  - 城墙承载着岁月变迁(城牆承載著歲月變遷)，守护着居民的梦想(守護著居民的夢想)。
    - Chéngqiáng chéngzài zhe suìyuè biànqiān, shǒuhù zhe jūmín de mèngxiǎng.
    - The city walls bear witness to the passage of time, protecting the dreams of the residents.

### 长城(長城) → 城(城) → 成(成) → 丁(丁)
- **definition:** nail
- **pinyin:** dīng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a simple shape ->
    - interpreted as head of a nail, head, or city wall
  - **interpretation:** The character 丁 is a pictogram with multiple interpretations, such as the head of a nail or a city wall, reflecting its simple, straight-line shape. It is used to denote the number 'fourth' as part of the Chinese heavenly stems, though its original pictographic representation has various possible meanings.
- **examples:**
  - 排列顺序为甲、乙、丙、丁(排列順序為甲、乙、丙、丁)。

    - Jiāzhōng lǎodà jiào jiǎ, lǎoèr jiào yǐ, lǎosān jiào bǐng, lǎosì chēng dīng.
    - In the family, the eldest is called Jia, the second is Yi, the third is Bing, and the fourth is called Ding.
