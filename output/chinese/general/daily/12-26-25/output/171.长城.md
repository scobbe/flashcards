## Great Wall
### Chángchéng
---
- **definition:** Great Wall
- **pinyin:** Chángchéng
- **components:**
  - 长(長)
    - cháng
    - long
  - 城
    - chéng
    - wall; city wall
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 长(長) (cháng, "long") and 城(城) (chéng, "wall; city wall") combine to refer to the Great Wall of China.
  - **interpretation:** 长城(長城) (Chángchéng, "Great Wall") describes the long fortifications built to protect the Chinese states against invasions and raids. The word literally means 'long wall,' highlighting its extensive structure.
- **examples:**
  - 长城坚不可摧(長城堅不可摧)。
    - Chángchéng jiān bù kě cuī.
    - The Great Wall is indestructible.
  - 游客登上长城(遊客登上長城)，感受历史沧桑(感受歷史滄桑)。
    - Yóukè dēng shàng Chángchéng, gǎn shòu lìshǐ cāngsāng.
    - Tourists climb the Great Wall and experience the vicissitudes of history.
  - 长城雄伟壮丽(長城雄偉壯麗)。
    - Chángchéng xióngwěi zhuànglì.
    - The Great Wall is majestic and magnificent.

  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 彡(彡) (shān, "pattern") + Semantic: 镸(镸) (cháng, "long") =
    - emphasizes length and the phonetic sound cháng ->

  - **interpretation:** The character combines the phonetic component 彡(彡) (shān, "pattern") with the semantic component 镸(镸) (cháng, "long") to denote the concept of length, emphasizing the characteristic of being long.

### 长城(長城) → 长(長) → 彡(彡)
- **definition:** pattern
- **pinyin:** shān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts three strands of hair ->
    - used to indicate three light rays in certain contexts
  - **interpretation:** 彡(shān, "rays") originally depicted strands of hair but also came to represent light rays, reflecting a change in symbolic meaning over time.
