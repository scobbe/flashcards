## pine tree
### sōngshù
---
- **traditional:** 松樹
- **definition:** pine tree
- **pinyin:** sōngshù
- **components:**
  - 松
    - sōng
    - pine
  - 树(樹)
    - shù
    - tree
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 松 (sōng, "pine") + 树(樹) (shù, "tree")
  - **interpretation:** The word 松树(樹) (sōngshù, "pine tree") combines 松 (sōng, "pine"), which refers to the type of tree known for its evergreen needles and cones, and 树(樹) (shù, "tree"), indicating a plant with a woody stem. Together, the term directly means 'pine tree,' highlighting the species of tree being described.
  - **simplification:** 松树 is the simplified form of 松樹.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)是一棵高大的松树(樹)。
    - Zhè shì yī kē gāodà de sōngshù.
    - This is a tall pine tree.
  - 秋风扫过(風掃過)山坡，带来(帶來)松树(樹)的清香。
    - Qiūfēng sǎoguò shānpō, dàilái sōngshù de qīngxiāng.
    - The autumn wind sweeps over the hill, carrying the fresh scent of pine trees.
### 松( )树(樹) → 松(鬆)
- **traditional:** 鬆
- **definition:** pine
- **pinyin:** sōng
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood
  - 公
    - gōng
    - public
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 木 (mù, "wood") + phonetic: 公 (gōng, "public") =
    - type of soft wood ->
    - pine
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 木 (mù, "wood") indicating its relation to trees, with 公 (gōng, "public") providing the phonetic component, representing a type of tree known for its tall and strong, yet flexible structure.
  - **simplification:** 松(鬆) (sōng, "pine") simplifies 鬆 by removing the radical 鬆, which indicated relaxation or loosening, retaining only the essential 木 (mù, "wood") and 公 (gōng, "public") for phonetic reading.
- **examples:**
  - 那棵松树(樹)依然挺立。
    - Nà kē sōng shù yīrán tǐnglì.
    - That pine still stands tall.
  - 公园(園)里种满(種滿)了松树(樹)，空气(氣)也格外清新。
    - Gōngyuán lǐ zhǒng mǎn le sōng shù, kōngqì yě géwài qīngxīn.
    - The park is filled with pine trees, and the air is exceptionally fresh.
