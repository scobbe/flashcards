## Bin (ancient place name)
### Bīn
---
- **definition:** Bin (ancient place name)
- **pinyin:** Bīn
- **components:**
  - 分
    - fēn
    - divide
  - 邑
    - yì
    - city
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 分(分) (fēn, "divide") + semantic 邑(邑) (yì, "city") =
    - sound based on 分(分) + meaning of city ->
    - ancient place name
  - **interpretation:** The character 邠(bīn, "Bin") combines the phonetic element 分(fēn, "divide") to suggest its sound and the semantic element 邑(yì, "city") to indicate it's a place name, reflecting its use as the name of an ancient city.
- **examples:**
  - 学者研究邠(學者研究邠)，探寻古地风貌(探尋古地風貌)。
    - Xuézhě yánjiū Bīn, tànxún gǔ dì fēngmào.
    - Scholars study Bin, exploring the ancient landscape.
  - 考古学家在邠(考古學家在邠)发现了珍贵遗迹(發現了珍貴遺蹟)。
    - Kǎogǔ xuéjiā zài Bīn fāxiàn le zhēnguì yíjì.
    - Archaeologists discovered precious relics in Bin.

    - to separate with a knife ->
    - divide
  - **interpretation:** The character 分(fēn, "divide") vividly depicts the action of using a knife to split or separate something into parts. 八(八) (bā, "to separate") emphasizes the notion of division, while 刀(刀) (dāo, "knife") signifies the tool necessary for such an action, illustrating the concept of dividing clearly and physically.
- **examples:**

### 邠(邠) → 分(分) → 八(八)
- **definition:** to separate
- **pinyin:** bā

  - 分

    - divide
  - 別

- **etymology:**

  - **description:**
