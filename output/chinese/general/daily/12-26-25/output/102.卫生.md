## hygiene
### wèishēng
---
- **definition:** hygiene
- **pinyin:** wèishēng
- **components:**
  - 卫(衛)
    - wèi
    - guard; defend; protect; satellite (in compounds)
  - 生
    - shēng
    - life; to be born; grow; raw
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 卫(guard/protect) + 生(life/health) = safeguarding life/health (hygiene)
  - **interpretation:** Literally ‘guard life,’ it denotes practices and conditions that protect health. In modern usage it spans personal cleanliness and public sanitation.
  - **simplification:** 衛 was simplified to 卫 by adopting an ancient variant and reducing strokes for ease of writing; 生 is unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 请注意卫生(請注意衛生)，勤洗手(勤洗手)。
    - Qǐng zhùyì wèishēng, qín xǐshǒu.
    - Please pay attention to hygiene and wash your hands frequently.
  - 公共场所要保持卫生(公共場所要保持衛生)。
    - Gōnggòng chǎngsuǒ yào bǎochí wèishēng.
    - Public places should maintain hygiene.
  - 学校定期进行卫生检查(學校定期進行衛生檢查)。
    - Xuéxiào dìngqī jìnxíng wèishēng jiǎnchá.
    - The school conducts regular hygiene inspections.
### 卫生(衛生) → 卫(衛)
- **definition:** guard; defend; protect; satellite (in compounds)
- **pinyin:** wèi
- **components:**
  - 行
    - xíng
    - go; walk; row; line
  - 韦(韋)
    - wéi
    - tanned leather; surname
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 行 + phonetic 韋.
  - **interpretation:** Meaning draws on 行 ‘to go, patrol’, suggesting movement associated with guarding, while 韋 provides the sound wèi and hints at encircling or binding. Together they convey the idea of protecting by patrolling or enclosing a space.
  - **simplification:** PRC created a new simple graph in 1956 to replace 衛, likely influenced by katakana ヱ and unorthodox variants like 𠀄 or by extracting the top of 韋, primarily to reduce stroke count.
- **examples:**
  - 我们要保卫家园(我們要保衛家園)。
    - Wǒmen yào bǎowèi jiāyuán.
    - We must defend our homeland.
  - 这颗卫星进入了轨道(這顆衛星進入了軌道)。
    - Zhè kē wèixīng jìnrùle guǐdào.
    - This satellite entered orbit.
  - 注意卫生(注意衛生)，保持环境清洁(保持環境清潔)。
    - Zhùyì wèishēng, bǎochí huánjìng qīngjié.
    - Pay attention to hygiene and keep the environment clean.

### 卫生(衛生) → 卫(衛) → 行(行) → 亍(亍)
- **definition:** small step; to step
- **pinyin:** chù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** simplified
  - **description:** Simplified from 行 – the right side of the character.
  - **interpretation:** Essentially the right-hand component of 行 abstracted as an independent graph. Since 行 relates to walking, the extracted half came to convey the idea of a small step or the act of stepping.

    - Zài bēiwén lǐ chángjiàn “chù” biǎoshì xiǎo bù.
    - In inscriptions, “亍” is often seen to denote a small step.
### 卫生(衛生) → 卫(衛) → 韦(韋)
- **definition:** tanned leather; surname
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **components:**
  - 舛
    - chuǎn
    - contrary; reversed steps; to be at variance
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (會意)
  - **description:** semantic: 舛 + 囗 — footsteps around an enclosure; later phonetic loan for “tanned leather”.
  - **interpretation:** Combines reversed footsteps with an enclosure to depict moving around or surrounding a boundary, a sense linked to 圍 ‘to surround’. The graph was later borrowed for the homophonous word wéi meaning “tanned leather,” so the leather sense is a phonetic loan unrelated to the original picture.
  - **simplification:** PRC simplification reduced 韋 to 韦 by compressing the enclosure-and-footstep strokes into a single streamlined form, enabling it to serve as a common simplified component.
- **examples:**
  - 韦先生今天来了(韋先生今天來了)。
    - Wéi xiānsheng jīntiān lái le.
    - Mr. Wei came today.
  - 古人用韦编竹简(古人用韋編竹簡)。
    - Gǔrén yòng wéi biān zhújiǎn.
    - In ancient times, people used leather thongs to bind bamboo slips.
  - 这件衣服是用韦制成的(這件衣服是用韋製成的)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfu shì yòng wéi zhìchéng de.
    - This garment is made of tanned leather.
### 卫生(衛生) → 卫(衛) → 韦(韋) → 舛(舛)
- **definition:** contrary; reversed steps; to be at variance
- **pinyin:** chuǎn
- **components:**
  - 夊
    - suī
    - go slowly; walk
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 夊 + 𡕒 — two feet facing in the same direction.
  - **interpretation:** Two footprint components point the same way but sit out of alignment, evoking mismatch, disorder, and being at odds. This visual misalignment underlies senses like “contrary” and “at variance,” with the left element having evolved in shape more than the right in later scripts.
- **examples:**

    - Due to erroneous data, the report needs to be rewritten.
  - 两人的意见颇多舛异(兩人的意見頗多舛異)。
    - Liǎng rén de yìjiàn pō duō chuǎnyì.
