### hygiene
---
- **pinyin:** hygiene
- **components:**
  - 卫(衛)
    - wèi
    - guard; defend; protect
  - 生
    - shēng
    - life; living; birth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 卫(衛) (wèi, 'guard; defend') + 生(生) (shēng, 'life; living') — guarding life
  - **interpretation:** The term combines the notion of 'guarding' with 'life', emphasizing the role of cleanliness and sanitation in protecting health and well-being.
- **examples:**
  - 保持卫生(保持衛生)，对健康非常重要。
    - bǎochí wèishēng, duì jiànkāng fēicháng zhòngyào.
    - Maintaining hygiene is very important for health.
  - 我们应该重视卫生(我們應該重視衛生)。
    - wǒmen yīnggāi zhòngshì wèishēng.
    - We should pay attention to hygiene.
### 卫生(衛生) → 卫(衛)
- **definition:** guard; defend; protect
- **pinyin:** wèi
- **components:**
  - 行
    - xíng
    - to walk
  - 韋
    - wéi
    - soft leather
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Combines the semantic component 行 (xíng, 'to walk') with the phonetic component 韋 (wéi, 'soft leather') to convey a sense of guarding or defending.
  - **interpretation:** The imagery of movement combined with the idea of a protective material like leather suggests the action of guarding or defending something.
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 衛 was simplified to 卫 primarily through stroke reduction, making it visually simpler and easier to write.
- **examples:**
  - 我在这里卫护你。(我在這裡衛護你。)
    - Wǒ zài zhèlǐ wèi hù nǐ.
    - I am here to guard you.
  - 军队需要卫兵。(軍隊需要衛兵。)
    - Jūnduì xūyào wèi bīng.
    - The army needs guards.
### 卫生(衛生) → 卫(衛) → 行(行)
- **definition:** to walk
- **pinyin:** xíng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a person walking.
  - **interpretation:** The character shows a figure in motion, emphasizing the action of walking or traveling.
- **examples:**
  - 我行走在公园里(我行走在公園裡)。
    - wǒ xíngzǒu zài gōngyuán lǐ.
    - I walk in the park.
  - 她行得很快(她行得很快)。
    - tā xíng dé hěn kuài.
    - She walks very fast.
  - 他每天都要行走(他每天都要行走)。
    - tā měitiān dōu yào xíngzǒu.
    - He has to walk every day.
### 卫生(衛生) → 卫(衛) → 韋(韋)
- **definition:** soft leather
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** The character is historically associated with leather or hide, indicating a form of protection, which connects to the idea of surrounding or enclosing.
  - **interpretation:** The protective quality of leather evokes the notion of surrounding or enclosing, similar to how an animal hide might envelop and protect what is inside.
- **examples:**
  - 韋編三絕(韋編三絕)，書法藝術無人能敵(書法藝術無人能敵)。
    - wéi biān sān jué, shūfǎ yìshù wú rén néng dí.
    - The leather binding is exceptional, and no one can rival his calligraphy.
  - 韋昭(韋昭)是三國時期的名將(三國時期的名將)。
    - wéi zhāo shì sān guó shíqī de míngjiàng.
    - Wei Zhao was a famous general of the Three Kingdoms period.
### 卫生(衛生) → 生(生)
- **definition:** life; living; birth
- **pinyin:** shēng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Represents the concept of life or birth, depicting a sprout emerging from the ground.
  - **interpretation:** The character intuitively symbolizes the emergence of new life, as it represents a plant coming to life from the earth.
- **examples:**
  - 生命(生命)，是宝贵的。
    - shēngmìng, shì bǎoguì de.
    - Life is precious.
  - 他的生日(他的生日)是四月一日。
    - tā de shēngrì shì sì yuè yī rì.
    - His birthday is April 1st.
