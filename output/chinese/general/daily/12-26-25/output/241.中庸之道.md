## Doctrine of the Mean; middle way
### zhōngyōng zhī dào
---
- **definition:** Doctrine of the Mean; middle way
- **pinyin:** zhōngyōng zhī dào
- **components:**
  - 中
    - zhōng
    - middle; center; in
  - 庸
    - yōng
    - ordinary; mediocre; common
  - 之
    - zhī
    - of; him; it; to go
  - 道
    - dào
    - way; path; road; doctrine
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 中(中) (zhōng, "middle") + 庸(庸) (yōng, "ordinary") + 之(之) (zhī, "of") + 道(道) (dào, "way") =
    - middle path or doctrine
  - **interpretation:** The phrase refers to a philosophical approach that emphasizes moderation, balance, and harmony. 中(中) (zhōng, "middle") signifies balance, while 庸(庸) (yōng, "ordinary") suggests avoiding extremes. Together with 之(之) (zhī, "of") and 道(道) (dào, "way"), this forms the concept of adhering to the 'middle way' in thought and actions.
- **examples:**
  - 遵循中庸之道(遵循中庸之道)，有助于创造和谐环境(有助於創造和諧環境)。
    - Zūnxún zhōngyōng zhī dào，yǒuzhù yú chuàngzào héxié huánjìng。
    - Following the Doctrine of the Mean can help create a harmonious environment.
  - 他坚守中庸之道(他堅守中庸之道)，在生活中追求平衡(在生活中追求平衡)。
    - Tā jiānshǒu zhōngyōng zhī dào，zài shēnghuó zhōng zhuīqiú pínghéng。
    - He adheres to the Doctrine of the Mean, always seeking balance in life.
