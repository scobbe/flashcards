## snake (zodiac)
### xiǎolóng
---
- **definition:** snake (zodiac)
- **pinyin:** xiǎolóng
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small
  - 龙(龍)
    - lóng
    - dragon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小(小) (xiǎo, "small") + 龙(龍) (lóng, "dragon")
  - **interpretation:** In the context of the Chinese zodiac, 小龙(小龍) (xiǎolóng, "small dragon") refers to the "snake," symbolically representing a smaller version of a dragon. In Chinese culture, the dragon is a mythical creature symbolizing power and strength, and the snake is often seen as its earthly counterpart. By referring to the snake as a small dragon, it acknowledges the snake's link to the similarly agile and revered dragon.
  - **simplification:** 小龙
- **examples:**
  - 在十二生肖中(在十二生肖中)，有时人们称蛇为小龙(有時人們稱蛇為小龍)。
    - Zài shí'èr shēngxiào zhōng, yǒu shí rénmen chēng shé wéi xiǎolóng.
    - Among the twelve zodiac animals, sometimes people refer to the snake as Little Dragon.
  - 小龙(小龍)在民间传说中代表智慧(在民間傳說中代表智慧)。
    - Xiǎolóng zài mínjiān chuánshuō zhōng dàibiǎo zhìhuì.
    - Little Dragon represents wisdom in folklore.
  - 许多人在新年时(許多人在新年時)，会挂起小龙形状的装饰(會掛起小龍形狀的裝飾)。
    - Xǔduō rén zài xīnnián shí, huì guà qǐ xiǎolóng xíngzhuàng de zhuāngshì.
    - During the New Year, many people hang decorations shaped like Little Dragon.
### 小龙(小龍) → 小(小)
- **definition:** small
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three small dots arranged symmetrically to represent something small
  - **interpretation:** The character 小(xiǎo, 'small') uses three small strokes to symbolize size or quantity, visually capturing the essence of 'smallness'.
- **examples:**
  - 这个小猫很可爱(這個小貓很可愛)。
    - Zhège xiǎo māo hěn kě'ài.
    - This small cat is very cute.
  - 这座小桥横跨河流(這座小橋橫跨河流)，连接两岸(連接兩岸)。
    - Zhè zuò xiǎo qiáo héngkuà héliú, liánjiē liǎng àn.
    - This small bridge spans the river, connecting both banks.
  - 请给我一小杯水(請給我一小杯水)。
    - Qǐng gěi wǒ yī xiǎo bēi shuǐ.
    - Please give me a small cup of water.
### 小龙(小龍) → 龙(龍)
- **definition:** dragon
- **pinyin:** lóng
- **components:**
  - 辰
    - chén
    - early form of dragon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 辰(辰) (chén, "early form of dragon") as phonetic + stylized depiction of a dragon body =
    - 龙 ->
    - dragon
  - **interpretation:** 龙(龍) (lóng, "dragon") combines a phonetic element from 辰(辰) (chén, "early form of dragon") with a stylized representation of a dragon's body, capturing both sound and essence to represent the mythical creature.
  - **simplification:** 龙(龍) simplified by combining phonetic 辰(辰) with a less detailed body form to represent the dragon.
- **examples:**
  - 传说中的龙能呼风唤雨(傳說中的龍能呼風喚雨)。
    - Chuánshuō zhōng de lóng néng hū fēng huàn yǔ.
    - The legendary dragon can summon wind and rain.
  - 皇宫中悬挂着龙袍(皇宮中懸掛著龍袍)，象征着至高无上的权力(象徵著至高無上的權力)。
    - Huánggōng zhōng xuánguà zhe lóng páo, xiàngzhēng zhe zhìgāo wúshàng de quánlì.
    - In the imperial palace, the dragon robe hangs, symbolizing supreme power.
### 小龙(小龍) → 龙(龍) → 辰(辰)
- **definition:** early form of dragon
- **pinyin:** chén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a heavenly body or a celestial presence, often associated with dragons in ancient mythology.
  - **interpretation:** 辰(辰) (chén, "early form of dragon") originally depicted a celestial body that was believed to influence time and was associated with dragons due to mythological links between dragons and celestial phenomena.
- **examples:**
  - 古代传说中的辰是神秘而强大的生物(古代傳說中的辰是神秘而強大的生物)。
    - Gǔdài chuánshuō zhōng de chén shì shénmì ér qiángdà de shēngwù.
    - In ancient legends, the chén is a mysterious and powerful creature.
  - 东方初升的朝阳映照着辰的鳞光(東方初升的朝陽映照著辰的鱗光)，唤起了古老的神话(喚起了古老的神話)。
    - Dōngfāng chūshēng de cháoyáng yìngzhào zhe chén de línguāng, huànqǐ le gǔlǎo de shénhuà.
    - The rising morning sun in the east illuminates the scales of the chén, evoking ancient myths.
  - 传承了古老智慧的文献记载了辰的奇迹(傳承了古老智慧的文獻記載了辰的奇蹟)，使后人对它充满敬畏(使後人對它充滿敬畏)。
    - Chuánchéng le gǔlǎo zhìhuì de wénxiàn jìzǎi le chén de qíjī, shǐ hòurén duì tā chōngmǎn jìngwèi.
    - Ancient texts carrying old wisdom recorded the miracles of the chén, filling later generations with awe.
