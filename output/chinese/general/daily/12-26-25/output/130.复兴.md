## revive; rejuvenate
### fùxīng
---
- **definition:** revive; rejuvenate
- **pinyin:** fùxīng
- **components:**
  - 复(復)
    - fù
    - return; again; restore; repeat
  - 兴(興)
    - xīng
    - rise; flourish; promote; start
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 复(again; return) + 兴(rise; flourish) = revive; rejuvenate.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the sense of doing something again with rising/flourishing to express bringing something back to vitality or prosperity. Common in phrases like 经济复兴、文化复兴、民族复兴.
  - **simplification:** Traditional 復興 was simplified to 复兴 by adopting the standard simplified forms: 復 → 复 (stroke reduction and dropping components) and 興 → 兴 (condensing the multi-part structure). The change streamlines handwriting while preserving the original semantics of ‘again’ + ‘rise’.
- **examples:**
  - 国家推动经济复兴(國家推動經濟復興)。
    - Guójiā tuīdòng jīngjì fùxīng.
    - The country is promoting economic revival.
  - 战后城市逐步复兴(戰後城市逐步復興)。
    - Zhànhòu chéngshì zhúbù fùxīng.
    - After the war, the cities gradually rejuvenated.
  - 我们致力于民族复兴(我們致力於民族復興)。
    - Wǒmen zhìlì yú mínzú fùxīng.
    - We are committed to national rejuvenation.
### 复兴(復興) → 复(復)
- **definition:** return; again; restore; repeat
- **pinyin:** fù
- **components:**
  - 夊
    - suī
    - to go slowly; footprint
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram (复); phono-semantic compound (traditional 復)
  - **description:** ideogram: top depicts a room in a corridor + bottom 夊 “foot/step” (‘go back’); traditional 復 is semantic 彳 ‘walk’ + phonetic 复.
  - **interpretation:** A footprint returning toward a room conveys the idea of going back, hence ‘return/again’. Later, the walker radical 彳 was added to form 復, clarifying the motion sense while keeping 复 as the sound and core idea.
  - **simplification:** PRC simplification adopted the older phonetic form 复 by dropping the semantic 彳 from 復, effectively reverting to the original graphic that already expressed ‘return’.
- **examples:**
  - 战后经济逐步复苏(戰後經濟逐步復甦)。
    - Zhànhòu jīngjì zhúbù fùsū.
    - The postwar economy gradually recovered.
  - 公司下月复工(公司下月復工)。
    - Gōngsī xiàyuè fùgōng.
    - The company will resume work next month.
  - 她已经回复了你的邮件(她已經回覆了你的郵件)。
    - Tā yǐjīng huífù le nǐ de yóujiàn.
    - She has already replied to your email.
### 复兴(復興) → 复(復) → 夊(夊)
- **definition:** to go slowly; footprint
- **pinyin:** suī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram—footprint (pointing down); variant of 夂. Compare 止 (leg up) and 𡕒 (toe left).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a downward-pointing footprint, evoking a trailing, unhurried step. Its orientation sets it apart from related foot forms, aligning with the sense of slow, following movement.
- **examples:**
  - 他慢慢走(他慢慢走)。
    - Tā mànman zǒu.
    - He walks slowly.
  - 她步履缓慢(她步履緩慢)。
    - Tā bùlǚ huǎnmàn.
    - Her steps are slow.
  - 孩子跟在后面(孩子跟在後面)，徐徐而行(徐徐而行)。
    - Háizi gēn zài hòumiàn, xúxú ér xíng.
    - The child follows behind, moving slowly.
### 复兴(復興) → 兴(興)
- **definition:** rise; flourish; promote; start
- **pinyin:** xīng
- **components:**
  - 舁
    - yú
    - to lift; to carry (on the shoulders)
  - 同
    - tóng
    - same; together
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Ideogrammic compound: 舁 (“both hands of two people”) + 同 (“a generic object”) — two lift something up together; Shuowen takes 同 as “together”.
  - **interpretation:** Two people jointly lifting an object conveys the act of raising or bringing something up, which extends to starting or setting things in motion. By metaphor, it came to express flourishing and promotion as activities that are lifted up together.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 興 by dropping the bottom 同 and condensing 舁 into a streamlined form 兴, reflecting long-used handwritten/clerical variants and reducing stroke count while keeping the ‘lifting/raising’ idea.
- **examples:**
  - 新公司兴办了一个社区图书馆(新公司興辦了一個社區圖書館)。
    - Xīn gōngsī xīngbàn le yī gè shèqū túshūguǎn.
    - The new company started a community library.
  - 政府计划振兴经济(政府計畫振興經濟)。
    - Zhèngfǔ jìhuà zhènxīng jīngjì.
    - The government plans to revitalize the economy.
  - 这个小镇近年很兴旺(這個小鎮近年很興旺)。
    - Zhège xiǎo zhèn jìnnián hěn xīngwàng.
    - This small town has been very prosperous in recent years.
### 复兴(復興) → 兴(興) → 舁(舁)
- **definition:** to lift; to carry (on the shoulders)
- **pinyin:** yú
- **components:**
  - 臼
    - jiù
    - mortar
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands; to hold up
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (会意)
  - **description:** semantic: 𦥑/臼 ‘pair of hands’ + semantic: 廾 ‘pair of hands’; upper was originally 𦥑, so 臼 as ‘mortar’ is a false friend.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two sets of hands working together to raise a load, conveying the act of lifting or carrying on the shoulders. The top was originally the hand component 𦥑, later misread as the mortar 臼.
- **examples:**
  - 两人共舁重鼎(兩人共舁重鼎)。
    - Liǎng rén gòng yú zhòng dǐng.
    - Two people together hoisted a heavy ding.
  - 士卒舁伤者而行(士卒舁傷者而行)。
    - Shìzú yú shāngzhě ér xíng.
    - The soldiers carried the wounded and moved on.
  - 民夫舁柴入城(民夫舁柴入城)。
    - Mínfū yú chái rù chéng.
    - Porters carried firewood into the city.
### 复兴(復興) → 兴(興) → 舁(舁) → 臼(臼)
- **definition:** mortar
- **pinyin:** jiù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a mortar. See the original version of 舂, in which a pestle is visible on top.
  - **interpretation:** A direct drawing of a mortar, showing the bowl used for pounding. Connection to 舂 (to pound) is historical, where a pestle appears above. Similar shapes seen in 學、兒、䳔、鼠、叟 are coincidental and unrelated.
- **examples:**
  - 他用杵在臼里舂米(他用杵在臼裡舂米)。
    - Tā yòng chǔ zài jiù lǐ chōng mǐ.
    - He uses a pestle to pound rice in the mortar.
  - 这个石臼很重(這個石臼很重)。
    - Zhège shíjiù hěn zhòng.
    - This stone mortar is very heavy.
  - 实验室里常用研钵和研杵(實驗室裡常用研鉢和研杵)。
    - Shíyànshì lǐ cháng yòng yánbō hé yánchǔ.
    - In laboratories, a mortar and pestle are commonly used.
### 复兴(復興) → 兴(興) → 舁(舁) → 廾(廾)
- **definition:** two hands; to hold up
- **pinyin:** gǒng
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - right hand; again
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 𠂇 (“left hand”) + 又 (“right hand”) — both hands; also written 𠬞.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the left and right hands to depict a pair of hands acting together, hence the sense of lifting or offering with both hands. Its form later served as a common component in characters related to holding or presenting.
- **examples:**
  - 在古文字中(在古文字中)，廾表示两只手一起举起东西(廾表示兩隻手一起舉起東西)。
    - Zài gǔ wénzì zhōng, gǒng biǎoshì liǎng zhī shǒu yīqǐ jǔqǐ dōngxī.
    - In ancient script, 廾 signifies two hands lifting something together.
  - 作为部件(作為部件)，它常见于表示双手奉献或合举的字(它常見於表示雙手奉獻或閤舉的字)，如奉、奏(如奉、奏)。
    - Zuòwéi bùjiàn, tā chángjiàn yú biǎoshì shuāngshǒu fèngxiàn huò héjǔ de zì, rú Fèng, Zòu.
    - As a component, it appears in characters that depict offering or jointly lifting with two hands,such as 奉 and 奏.
### 复兴(復興) → 兴(興) → 舁(舁) → 廾(廾) → 又(又)
- **definition:** right hand; again
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 复兴(復興) → 兴(興) → 同(同)
- **definition:** same; together
- **pinyin:** tóng
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a pipe (original form of 筒); a decorative 口 was added later. Alternative interpretation: 凡 (heavy dish/bucket/pole/stretcher) + 口 (“mouth/order”) → cooperation; togetherness.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicts a tube; the added 口 is decorative and not a functional component, with meaning later extending to ‘together/same’. Folk analyses parse it as a shared load (凡) coordinated by commands (口), reinforcing the idea of collective action and uniformity.
- **examples:**
  - 我们同意这个计划(我們同意這個計劃)。
    - Wǒmen tóngyì zhège jìhuà.
    - We agree on this plan.
  - 他们住在同一个城市(他們住在同一個城市)。
    - Tāmen zhù zài tóng yí gè chéngshì.
    - They live in the same city.
  - 请和我一起同去(請和我一起同去)。
    - Qǐng hé wǒ yìqǐ tóng qù.
    - Please go together with me.
### 复兴(復興) → 兴(興) → 同(同) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
