### say goodbye; farewell
---
- **pinyin:** say goodbye; farewell
- **components:**
  - 告
    - gào
    - say; tell; inform; announce
  - 别(別)
    - bié
    - part; leave; farewell; distinguish
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 告(告) (gào, "say; tell") + 别(別) (bié, "part; leave") — say leave
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 告(告) (gào, "say; tell") and 别(別) (bié, "part; leave") signifies the act of saying farewell. It conveys the intention of informing someone that a parting is taking place.
- **examples:**
  - 我将要告别(我將要告別)这段旅程。
    - Wǒ jiāng yào gàobié zhè duàn lǚchéng.
    - I am about to say goodbye to this journey.
  - 在最后的时刻，大家都流下了告别的泪水(在最後的時刻，大家都流下了告別的淚水)。
    - Zài zuìhòu de shíkè, dàsī dōu liú xiàle gàobié de lèishuǐ.
    - At the last moment, everyone shed tears of farewell.
### 告别(告別) → 告(告)
- **definition:** say; tell; inform; announce
- **pinyin:** gào
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram/ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Can be interpreted as a pictogram depicting a tongue sticking out from the mouth or as an ideogrammic compound combining a decorated drum and an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The imagery of a tongue, or a mouth communicating, suggests the act of speaking or informing, while the combination of a drum and mouth reinforces the idea of announcing or making an announcement.
- **examples:**
  - 我告诉你。(我告訴你。)
    - Wǒ gàosù nǐ.
    - I tell you.
  - 请告诉他们。(請告訴他們。)
    - Qǐng gàosù tāmen.
    - Please inform them.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別)
- **definition:** part; leave; farewell; distinguish
- **pinyin:** bié
- **components:**
  - 冎
    - jué
    - bone
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Combines 冎 (bone) and 刂 (knife) suggesting cutting or distinguishing between parts.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of a bone and a knife intuitively suggests the act of cutting away or separating, which aligns with the meanings of parting and distinguishing.
- **examples:**
  - 我们分别了(我們分別了)，真让人伤心(真讓人傷心)！
    - Wǒmen fēnbié le, zhēn ràng rén shāngxīn!
    - We parted, it really made people sad!
  - 请不要别无选择(請不要別無選擇)。
    - Qǐng bùyào biéwú xuǎnzé.
    - Please do not have no choice.
  - 你能区别(你能區別)好坏吗(好壞嗎)？
    - Nǐ néng qūbié hǎo huài ma?
    - Can you distinguish between good and bad?
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 冎(冎)
- **definition:** bone
- **pinyin:** jué
- **components:**
  - 冊
    - cè
    - book
  - 卻
    - què
    - to retreat
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** This character combines the semantic element 冊 (cè, 'book') with the phonetic element 卻 (què, 'to retreat')
  - **interpretation:** The combination suggests a connection to books and the idea of retreating to knowledge. This makes intuitive sense as reading can provide an escape from reality.
- **examples:**
  - 书店有很多杂志(書店有很多雜誌)。
    - Shūdiàn yǒu hěn duō zázhì.
    - The bookstore has many magazines.
  - 他喜欢读书(他喜歡讀書)。
    - Tā xǐhuān dúshū.
    - He enjoys reading.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 冎(冎) → 冊(冊)
- **definition:** book
- **pinyin:** cè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts bamboo slips strung together.
  - **interpretation:** This imagery evokes the idea of a collection of writings or documents, as bamboo slips were an early form of book.
  - **simplification:** The simplification replaces the bamboo depiction with a simpler structure for ease of writing.
- **examples:**
  - 我正在读一本书(我正在讀一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhèngzài dú yī běn shū.
    - I am reading a book.
  - 这本册子非常有趣(這本冊子非常有趣)。
    - Zhè běn cèzi fēicháng yǒuqù.
    - This booklet is very interesting.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 冎(冎) → 卻(卻)
- **definition:** to retreat
- **pinyin:** què
- **components:**
  - 却(卻)
    - què
    - retreat
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Combines the phonetic 吗(嗎) (ma, 'interrogative particle') with the semantic component 匕 (bǐ, 'knife'), suggesting action or movement.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the idea of retreat or pulling back, as if one is withdrawing from a dangerous situation signified by the knife.
- **examples:**
  - 他却选择了离开(他卻選擇了離開)。
    - Tā què xuǎnzé le líkāi.
    - He chose to leave instead.
  - 她说却快点(她說卻快點)。
    - Tā shuō què kuài diǎn.
    - She said to hurry up.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 冎(冎) → 卻(卻) → 却(卻)
- **definition:** retreat
- **pinyin:** què
- **components:**
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - stop
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Part of the character is 止 (zhǐ, 'stop') which suggests going back or retreating, combined with 却 (què, 'however') indicates the action of returning or retreating.
  - **interpretation:** The character evokes the concept of stopping and moving backward, suggesting a retreating action. This aligns with the meaning of 'retreat.'
- **examples:**
  - 他决定却步(他決定卻步)，以避免危险(以避免危險)。
    - tā juédìng quèbù, yǐ bìmiǎn wēixiǎn.
    - He decided to retreat to avoid danger.
  - 虽然计划很完善(雖然計劃很完善)，但我们还是选择却走(但我們還是選擇卻走)。
    - suīrán jìhuà hěn wánshàn, dàn wǒmen háishì xuǎnzé què zǒu.
    - Although the plan is perfect, we still choose to retreat.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 冎(冎) → 卻(卻) → 却(卻) → 止(止)

### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 刀(刀)

- **etymology:**

- **examples:**
