## say goodbye; farewell
### gàobié
---
- **definition:** say goodbye; farewell
- **pinyin:** gàobié
- **components:**
  - 告
    - gào
    - inform; announce; tell
  - 别(別)
    - bié
    - leave; part; farewell; separate
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 告(告) (gào, "inform") + 别(別) (bié, "leave; part") =
    - say goodbye; farewell
  - **interpretation:** The word 告别(告別) (gàobié, "say goodbye; farewell") consists of 告(告) (gào, "inform") and 别(別) (bié, "leave; part"). When you 'inform' someone about a 'parting,' you are essentially saying goodbye. This concept is relevant in both personal departures and formal farewells.
- **examples:**
  - 我们告别(我們告別)。
    - Wǒmen gàobié.
    - We say farewell.
  - 他在码头告别(他在碼頭告別)，依依不舍地离去(依依不舍地離去)。
    - Tā zài mǎtóu gàobié, yīyī bùshě dì líqù.
    - He said farewell at the dock and left reluctantly.
  - 朋友聚会后告别(朋友聚會後告别)，彼此期待重逢(彼此期待重逢)。
    - Péngyǒu jùhuì hòu gàobié, bǐcǐ qídài chóngféng.
    - After the gathering, friends said farewell, each looking forward to reuniting.
### 告别(告別) → 告(告)
- **definition:** inform; announce; tell
- **pinyin:** gào
- **components:**
  - 壴
    - zhù
    - drum
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 初(初) (chū, 'initial') depiction unclear ->
    - later interpretations consider abbreviated 壴(壴) (zhù, 'drum') + 口(口) (kǒu, 'mouth') =
    - drum with open mouth ->
    - 'announce, tell'
  - **interpretation:** The character 告(告) (gào, 'inform; announce; tell') likely evolved from a depiction related to announcing or informing others using a combination of sound (drum) and speech (mouth). The interpretation of 告(告) as a drum and mouth aligns with its meaning of announcing or speaking out loud.
- **examples:**
  - 老师告学生(老師告學生)，考试规则已更新(考試規則已更新)。
    - Lǎoshī gào xuéshēng, kǎoshì guīzé yǐ gēngxīn.
    - The teacher informed the students that the exam rules have been updated.
  - 父亲告我(父親告我)，晚上早點回家(晚上早點回家)。
    - Fùqīn gào wǒ, wǎnshàng zǎodiǎn huí jiā.
    - My father informed me to come home early tonight.
### 告别(告別) → 告(告) → 壴(壴)
- **definition:** drum
- **pinyin:** zhù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a drum with its stand and sticks arranged on top, representing a musical instrument.
  - **interpretation:** 壴(壴) (zhù, "drum") visually depicts a traditional Chinese drum, with its structure and accessories, making the character intuitively understandable as representing a drum.
- **examples:**
  - 古人用壴打节奏(古人用壴打節奏)。
    - Gǔrén yòng zhù dǎ jiézòu.
    - Ancient people used the drum to beat the rhythm.
  - 夜晚沙场上(夜晚沙場上)，战鼓和壴同时响起(戰鼓和壴同時響起)。
    - Yèwǎn shāchǎng shàng, zhàngǔ hé zhù tóngshí xiǎngqǐ.
    - On the battlefield at night, both the war drum and the drum sounded simultaneously.
### 告别(告別) → 告(告) → 口(口)
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，大声喊叫(大聲喊叫)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这口饭入口即化(這口飯入口即化)。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹果(小女孩咬了一口蘋果)，露出甜美的笑容(露出甜美的笑容)。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別)
- **definition:** leave; part; farewell; separate
- **pinyin:** bié
- **components:**
  - 刂(刀)
    - dāo
    - knife
  - 彐
    - jì
    - snout
  - 卩
    - jié
    - seal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 刂 (dāo, "knife") + phonetic: another part representing 刀 (dāo, "knife") ->
    - initial separation meaning; changed to add 彐 (jì, "snout") later ->
    - become separate ->
    - final form to express concepts related to separation or differentiation
  - **interpretation:** The character 别(別) (bié, "separate") incorporates 刂(dāo, "knife"), symbolizing cutting or dividing, with a phonetic component that originally represented another knife, indicating parting or separating. The addition of 彐(jì, "snout") might have been a later addition to clarify pronunciation or sand for the semantic distinction in usage.
  - **simplification:** 别(別): 刂(卩) dropped top to combine two components into a simpler character.
- **examples:**
  - 别走(別走)，我们以后再见(我們以後再見)。
    - Bié zǒu, wǒmen yǐhòu zài jiàn.
    - Don't leave, we'll meet again.
  - 在分别时(在別離時)，保持冷静(保持冷靜)。
    - Zài fēnbié shí, bǎochí lěngjìng.
    - At the time of parting, keep calm.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 刂(刂)
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Variant of 刀 ( dāo ).
  - **description:**
    - 刁(刁) (diāo, "clever") is a variant of 刀(刀) (dāo, "knife") ->
    - simplification for clarity
  - **interpretation:** The character 刂(dāo) is a simplified variant of 刀(dāo), which is a pictogram depicting a knife. The two-stroke 刂(dāo) is often used as a radical in other characters related to cutting or knives. It maintains the core meaning and functionality of 刀(dāo) in a more streamlined form.
  - **simplification:** 刂(dāo) is a cursive shorthand of 刀(dāo), used as a radical for clarity and conciseness in compound characters.
- **examples:**
  - 他用刂(刀)，迅速劈开木头(迅速劈開木頭)。
    - Tā yòng dāo, xùnsù pī kāi mùtou.
    - He uses a knife to quickly chop wood.
  - 厨房里挂着一把闪亮的刂(刀)，让人看了食欲大增(讓人看了食慾大增)。
    - Chúfáng lǐ guà zhe yī bǎ shǎnliàng de dāo, ràng rén kàn le shíyù dà zēng.
    - A shining knife hangs in the kitchen, arousing one's appetite.
  - 刂(刀)是厨房中不可缺少的工具(廚房中不可缺少的工具)。
    - Dāo shì chúfáng zhōng bùkě quēshǎo de gōngjù.
    - The knife is an indispensable tool in the kitchen.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 彐(彐)
- **definition:** snout
- **pinyin:** jì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicts a stylized hand, resembling a snout or three-fingered grip ->
    - used in various characters representing a hand or something held, not literally a snout.
  - **interpretation:** 彐(彐) (jì, "snout") may resemble a pig's snout but frequently serves as a stylized hand with three fingers in characters like 事(事) (shì, "affair") and 爭(爭) (zhēng, "struggle"), diverging from its literal meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 那只猪的彐短而有力(那隻豬的彐短而有力)。
    - Nà zhī zhū de jì duǎn ér yǒulì.
    - That pig's snout is short yet powerful.
  - 农家养的猪常常被人称赞(農家養的豬常常被人稱讚)，因为它们的彐显得非常灵敏(因為它們的彐顯得非常靈敏)。
    - Nóngjiā yǎng de zhū chángcháng bèi rén chēngzàn, yīnwèi tāmen de jì xiǎndé fēicháng língmǐn.
    - Pigs raised on the farm are often praised because their snouts appear exceptionally sensitive.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 卩(卩)
- **definition:** seal
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: represents a kneeling person from a side view
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩(卩) (jié, "kneeling person") visually depicts someone kneeling, highlighting the physical posture. It is distinct and unrelated to other characters like 欠(欠) (qiàn, "owe") and 女(女) (nǚ, "woman") despite similar appearances.
- **examples:**
  - 在祭祀仪式中(在祭祀儀式中)，卩虔诚跪拜(卩虔誠跪拜)。

    - Ancient paintings often depict the posture of a kneeling person.
