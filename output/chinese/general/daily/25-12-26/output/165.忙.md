## busy
### máng
---
- **definition:** busy
- **pinyin:** máng
- **components:**
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart
  - 亡
    - wáng
    - perish
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 心 (xīn, "heart") + phonetic: 亡 (wáng, "perish") =
    - heart in turmoil ->
    - busy
  - **interpretation:** 忙 (máng, "busy") combines 心 (xīn, "heart") for the emotional aspect with 亡 (wáng, "perish") which lends its sound, capturing the sense of a heart overwhelmed with activities.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天很忙。
    - Wǒ jīntiān hěn máng.
    - I am very busy today.
  - 他虽(雖)然很忙，仍然抽时(時)间(間)学(學)习(習)。
    - Tā suīrán hěn máng, réngrán chōu shíjiān xuéxí.
    - Although he is busy, he still finds time to study.
  - 忙于(於)工作的人，总(總)是忘记(記)休息。
    - Máng yú gōngzuò de rén, zǒng shì wàngjì xiūxí.
    - People who are busy at work always forget to rest.
### 忙 → 心
- **definition:** heart
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a heart
  - **interpretation:** The character 心(xīn, "heart") originally depicted the shape of an actual heart, representing thoughts, emotions, and the core of a person. Over time, it has become highly stylized, but it continues to convey the notion of the heart as the center of emotions and consciousness.
- **examples:**
  - 她的心一直都在帮(幫)助别(別)人。
    - Tā de xīn yīzhí dōu zài bāngzhù biérén.
    - Her heart is always in helping others.
  - 在城市的中心，有一个(個)美丽(麗)的公园(園)。
    - Zài chéngshì de zhōngxīn, yǒu yí gè měilì de gōngyuán.
    - At the center of the city, there is a beautiful park.
  - 对(對)于(於)许(許)多人来(來)说(說)，家庭是生活的核心。
    - Duìyú xǔduō rén lái shuō, jiātíng shì shēnghuó de héxīn.
    - For many people, family is the core of life.

### 忙 → 亡
- **definition:** perish
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **components:**
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
  - 芒
    - máng
    - edge of a knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - A line on 刀 (dāo, 'knife') ->
    - cutting edge of a knife ->
    - original form of 芒 (máng, 'edge of a knife')
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts a line on a knife, representing the sharp edge. This connection to sharpness and cutting leads to the concept of 'perish,' as an edge can cut or end something, symbolically indicating destruction or the end of life.
- **examples:**
  - 战(戰)士们(們)在战(戰)场(場)上英勇战(戰)斗(鬥)，许(許)多人不幸亡。
    - Zhànshìmen zài zhànchǎng shàng yīngyǒng zhàndòu, xǔduō rén bùxìng wáng.
    - The soldiers fought bravely on the battlefield, and many unfortunately perished.
  - 如果不努力经(經)营(營)，这(這)家公司可能会(會)亡。
    - Rúguǒ bù nǔlì jīngyíng, zhè jiā gōngsī kěnéng huì wáng.
    - If the company does not operate diligently, it may perish.
  - 古代帝国(國)由于(於)内(內)外问(問)题(題)而一朝亡。
    - Gǔdài dìguó yóuyú nèiwài wèntí ér yī zhāo wáng.
    - The ancient empire perished overnight due to internal and external issues.

### 忙 → 亡 → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a knife
  - **interpretation:** 刀 (dāo, "knife") is a pictographic representation of a knife or blade, with a sharp edge and a handle, resembling the form of a basic cutting tool.
- **examples:**
  - 他用锋(鋒)利的刀，切开(開)蔬菜。
    - tā yòng fēnglì de dāo, qiē kāi shūcài.
    - He uses a sharp knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这(這)把刀非常锋(鋒)利。
    - zhè bǎ dāo fēicháng fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
  - 厨(廚)师(師)小心地持刀，为(為)客人切肉。
    - chúshī xiǎoxīn de chí dāo, wèi kèrén qiē ròu.
    - The chef holds the knife carefully and slices meat for the guest.

### 忙 → 亡 → 芒
- **definition:** edge of a knife
- **pinyin:** máng
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 亡
    - wáng
    - to flee
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, "grass") + phonetic 亡 (wáng) ->
    - edge of a knife
  - **interpretation:** The character 芒(máng, "edge of a knife") uses the 艹 (cǎo, "grass") component to highlight its relation to plant life or natural elements, while 亡 (wáng) provides the phonetic indication. Originally it may have depicted something like 方 but evolved to illustrate the thinness or sharpness of grass edges, metaphorically linking to the sharp edge of a knife.
- **examples:**
  - 刀的芒在阳(陽)光下闪(閃)闪(閃)发(發)光。
    - Dāo de máng zài yángguāng xià shǎnshǎn fāguāng.
    - The edge of the knife glistens in the sunlight.
  - 我们(們)要小心刀的芒，因为(為)它非常锋(鋒)利。
    - Wǒmen yào xiǎoxīn dāo de máng, yīnwèi tā fēicháng fēnglì.
    - We must be careful of the knife's edge because it is very sharp.
  - 他用手指轻(輕)轻(輕)触(觸)摸刀的芒，感受到它的锋(鋒)利。
    - Tā yòng shǒuzhǐ qīngqīng chùmō dāo de máng, gǎnshòudào tā de fēnglì.
    - He gently touched the blade's edge with his finger and felt its sharpness.

### 忙 → 亡 → 芒 → 艹
- **definition:** grass
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts blades of grass
  - **interpretation:** 艹 is a pictogram representing grass or plants, resembling blades of grass. It is commonly used as a radical in other characters related to grass or vegetation.
- **examples:**
  - 他写(寫)字时(時)用艹作草字的偏旁。
    - Tā xiězì shí yòng cǎo zuò cǎozì de piānpáng.
    - He uses '艹' as the radical for grass in his characters.
  - 春天到来(來)时(時)，艹生长(長)得很快。
    - Chūntiān dàolái shí, cǎo shēngzhǎng de hěn kuài.
    - When spring arrives, grass grows rapidly.
