## morning glory
### qiānniúhuā
---
- **traditional:** 牽牛花
- **definition:** morning glory
- **pinyin:** qiānniúhuā
- **components:**
  - 牵(牽)
    - qiān
    - to lead
  - 牛
    - niú
    - ox
  - 花
    - huā
    - flower
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 牵(牽) (qiān, "to lead") + 牛 (niú, "ox") + 花 (huā, "flower") =
    - morning glory, a flower that twines around like it is leading an ox
  - **interpretation:** The name 牵(牽)牛花 (qiānniúhuā, "morning glory") evokes the image of the flower's tendrils twining around supports in a manner reminiscent of how a person might lead an ox by a rope. The plant's climbing habit is likened to the motion of guiding an ox.
- **examples:**
  - 清晨时(時)分，我在花园(園)里(裡)发(發)现(現)了美丽(麗)的牵(牽)牛花。
    - Qīngchén shífèn, wǒ zài huāyuán lǐ fāxiànle měilì de qiānniúhuā.
    - Early in the morning, I discovered beautiful morning glories in the garden.
  - 花架上爬满(滿)了牵(牽)牛花。
    - Huājià shàng pá mǎnle qiānniúhuā.
    - The trellis is covered with morning glories.
  - 黄(黃)昏时(時)刻，夕阳(陽)映照的牵(牽)牛花，显(顯)得格外动(動)人。
    - Huánghūn shíkè, xīyáng yìngzhào de qiānniúhuā, xiǎnde géwài dòngrén.
    - At dusk, the morning glories illuminated by the setting sun looked exceptionally enchanting.
### 牵(牽)牛花 → 牵(牽)
- **traditional:** 牽
- **definition:** to lead
- **pinyin:** qiān
- **components:**
  - 牛
    - niú
    - cow
  - 玄
    - xuán
    - deep; mysterious
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 牛 (niú, "cow") + phonetic 玄 (xuán) ->
    - leading an ox by hand
  - **interpretation:** In the character 牽 (qiān, "to lead"), 牛 (niú, "cow") represents the act of leading or guiding a cow, which was a common agricultural practice. The phonetic 玄 (xuán) provides the pronunciation. Together, they form the concept of leading an animal, specifically an ox.
  - **simplification:** 牽 (qiān) was simplified by removing 冖 (mì, "cover; crown") to create 牵 (qiān).
- **examples:**
  - 小女孩牵(牽)着(著)狗在公园(園)里(裡)散步。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái qiān zhe gǒu zài gōngyuán lǐ sànbù.
    - The little girl is leading the dog for a walk in the park.
  - 老人牵(牽)着(著)孩子的手，慢慢走过(過)马(馬)路。
    - Lǎorén qiān zhe háizi de shǒu, mànmàn zǒu guò mǎlù.
    - The old man is leading the child by the hand, slowly crossing the street.
  - 她牵(牽)着(著)马(馬)走向草地。
    - Tā qiān zhe mǎ zǒu xiàng cǎodì.
    - She is leading the horse towards the meadow.
### 牵(牽)牛花 → 牵(牽) → 牛
- **definition:** cow
- **pinyin:** niú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the head and horns of an ox
  - **interpretation:** The character 牛 (niú, "ox") is a pictogram representing the head and horns of an ox, reflecting its importance in agriculture and daily life in ancient China.
- **examples:**
  - 农(農)民牵(牽)着(著)牛，去市场(場)买(買)东(東)西。
    - Nóngmín qiānzhe niú，qù shìchǎng mǎi dōngxī。
    - The farmer leads the ox and goes to the market to buy things.
  - 农(農)家养(養)牛，生活更加富足。
    - Nóngjiā yǎng niú，shēnghuó gèngjiā fùzú。
    - Rural families raise oxen, making their lives more prosperous.
  - 这(這)个(個)牛非常听(聽)话(話)。
    - Zhège niú fēicháng tīnghuà。
    - This ox is very obedient.

### 牵(牽)牛花 → 牵(牽) → 玄 → 亠
- **definition:** top mark
- **pinyin:** tóu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Couvercle with a handle ->
    - false friend: roof of a tower in 高 (gāo, "high") ->
    - head and open arms in 立 (lì, "stand")/交 (jiāo, "exchange") ->
    - roof of a hut in 六 (liù, "six") ->
    - top of traditional cloth in 衣 (yī, "clothing") ->
    - mark over silk threads in 玄 (xuán, "mysterious") ->
    - top of knife/chisel to mark prisoners in 辛 (xīn, "bitter") ->
    - grass in 离 (lí, "leave")
  - **interpretation:** 亠 is primarily a pictogram that initially represented a cover with a handle. However, its usage varies in different characters, symbolizing roofs, heads, tops of clothing, and marks, showing its flexibility and evolution in form and meaning across contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 在汉(漢)字结(結)构(構)中，亠作为(為)顶(頂)标(標)发(發)挥(揮)作用。
    - Zài hànzì jiégòu zhōng, tóu zuòwéi dǐngbiāo fāhuī zuòyòng.
    - In the structure of Chinese characters, 亠 acts as a top mark to exert its function.
  - 古代书(書)法作品中，亠显(顯)示出独(獨)特的审(審)美价(價)值。
    - Gǔdài shūfǎ zuòpǐn zhōng, tóu xiǎnshì chū dútè de shěnměi jiàzhí.
    - In ancient calligraphy, 亠 demonstrates a unique aesthetic value.

### 牵(牽)牛花 → 花
- **definition:** flower
- **pinyin:** huā
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass; plant
  - 化
    - huà
    - transform
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, "grass; plant") + phonetic 化 (huà, "transform") =
    - plant + phonetic indicator ->
    - flower
  - **interpretation:** 花 (huā, "flower") combines 艹 (cǎo, "grass; plant") indicating its association with vegetation, and 化 (huà, "transform") for its phonetic value. This illustrates both the natural aspect and the phonetic pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 花香四溢。
    - huā xiāng sì yì。
    - The fragrance of flowers fills the air.
  - 阳(陽)光明媚，花儿(兒)争(爭)艳(艷)。
    - yáng guāng míng mèi，huā ér zhēng yàn。
    - The bright sunshine shines as the flowers compete in beauty.
  - 她用花装(裝)点(點)房间(間)。
    - tā yòng huā zhuāng diǎn fáng jiān。
    - She decorates the room with flowers.
