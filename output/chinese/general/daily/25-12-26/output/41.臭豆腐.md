## stinky tofu
### chòudòufu
---
- **definition:** stinky tofu
- **pinyin:** chòudòufu
- **components:**
  - 臭
    - chòu
    - stinky
  - 豆腐
    - dòufu
    - tofu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 臭 (chòu, "stinky") + 豆腐 (dòufu, "tofu") combine to describe a type of tofu known for its strong odor.
  - **interpretation:** 臭 (chòu, "stinky") refers to a strong or unpleasant smell. 豆腐 (dòufu, "tofu") is a versatile food product made from soybeans. Together, they describe a fermented tofu dish that is characterized by its pungent smell. This strong odor is the key feature that distinguishes 臭豆腐 (chòudòufu, "stinky tofu") from regular tofu, which is generally bland in smell and taste. The term emphasizes the unique aspect of this delicacy, where the odor signifies the fermentation process it undergoes, giving it a distinct flavor celebrated in many regions of China.
- **examples:**
  - 臭豆腐是闻(聞)起来(來)很臭，但吃起来(來)很香的小吃。
    - Chòudòufu shì wén qǐlái hěn chòu，dàn chī qǐlái hěn xiāng de xiǎochī.
    - Stinky tofu is a snack that smells bad but tastes delicious.
  - 每次去夜市，我都一定会(會)买(買)臭豆腐。
    - Měicì qù yèshì，wǒ dōu yídìng huì mǎi chòudòufu.
    - Every time I go to the night market, I always buy stinky tofu.
  - 他不喜欢(歡)臭豆腐的味道，但非常喜欢(歡)它的口感。
    - Tā bù xǐhuān chòudòufu de wèidào，dàn fēicháng xǐhuān tā de kǒugǎn.
    - He doesn't like the smell of stinky tofu, but he loves its texture.
