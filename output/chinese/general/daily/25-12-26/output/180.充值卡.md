- **pinyin:** chōngzhíkǎ
- **components:**

    - chōngzhí

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 充值 (chōngzhí, "to recharge") + 卡 (kǎ, "card") forms the word 充值卡, which refers to a card used for adding credit or value, typically to a prepaid account.
  - **interpretation:** 充值 (chōngzhí, "to recharge") combines 充 (chōng, "fill") with 值 (zhí, "value") to describe the action of adding value or balance to an account. 卡 (kǎ, "card") refers to a physical card, often used for storing or facilitating the transfer of this balance. Thus, a 充值卡 is a card used specifically for recharging or topping up the balance of an account, such as a prepaid phone plan.

### 充值卡 → 充值
- **definition:** to recharge; to top up
- **pinyin:** chōngzhí
- **components:**
  - 充
    - chōng
    - fill; full; satisfy
  - 值
    - zhí
    - value; worth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 充 (chōng, "fill; full; satisfy") originally suggests filling a space, like pouring liquid into a container until it is full. It extends metaphorically to the action of adding value to a balance, completing or satisfying a requirement. 值 (zhí, "value; worth") refers to the concept of worth or numerical value, especially in terms of money or morals. The combination of these characters forms the action of replenishing or increasing value, particularly in credit or prepaid accounts.
  - **interpretation:** 充值 portrays the act of filling (充) a monetary balance or value (值). 充 implies actively making something complete or reaching its full potential, while 值 denotes the aspect of monetary worth or available credit. When combined, it applies primarily to the function of adding credit to accounts such as phone balances or transportation cards, mirroring the act of filling them with usable value.
