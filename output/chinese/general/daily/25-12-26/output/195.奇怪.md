### qíguài
---
- **definition:** strange
- **pinyin:** qíguài
- **components:**
  - 奇
    - qí
    - strange; odd
  - 怪
    - guài
    - odd; strange; to blame
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 奇 (qí, "strange; odd") denotes something unusual or out of the ordinary. 怪 (guài, "odd; strange; to blame") further emphasizes strangeness or peculiarity, and can also be associated with moral judgment or assigning blame.
  - **interpretation:** The word 奇怪 (qíguài) combines two characters that both relate to concepts of irregularity and difference from the norm. 奇 (qí, "strange; odd") refers to something that stands out due to its rarity or surprising nature. 怪 (guài, "odd; strange; to blame") adds an additional layer, not only marking something as strange but also featuring a sense of moral judgment or emotional reaction, like something being so unusual it may invoke curiosity or suspicion. Together, they form a phrase that strongly conveys a sense of strangeness or peculiarity.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)地方有很多奇怪的声(聲)音。
    - Zhège dìfāng yǒu hěn duō qíguài de shēngyīn.
    - There are many strange sounds in this place.
  - 他今天的行为(為)很奇怪。
    - Tā jīntiān de xíngwéi hěn qíguài.
    - His behavior today is very strange.
  - 我觉(覺)得这(這)件事情有点(點)奇怪。
    - Wǒ juéde zhè jiàn shìqíng yǒu diǎn qíguài.
    - I think this matter is a bit strange.
### 奇怪 → 奇
- **definition:** strange; odd
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big; great
  - 可
    - kě
    - can; may
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 大 (dà, "big; great") + phonetic 可 (kě) ->
    - strange; odd; unique; rare
  - **interpretation:** The character 奇(qí) combines 大 (dà, "big; great") as the semantic component indicating something substantial or significant, with 可 (kě) providing the phonetic element. Together, this combination suggests something that stands out as significant due to its unusual or unique nature, leading to meanings such as 'strange', 'odd', 'unique', and 'rare'.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)是一场(場)奇怪的梦(夢)。
    - Zhè shì yī chǎng qíguài de mèng.
    - This was a strange dream.
  - 他有一种(種)奇异(異)的天赋(賦)。
    - Tā yǒu yī zhǒng qíyì de tiānfù.
    - He has a unique talent.
  - 这(這)本书(書)中包含许(許)多奇闻(聞)异(異)事。
    - Zhè běn shū zhōng bāohán xǔduō qíwén yìshì.
    - This book contains many strange tales.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 大
- **definition:** big; great
- **pinyin:** dà
- **components:**
  - 夫
    - fū
    - man
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a person facing forward ->
    - Borrowed phonetically for 'big'; original form of 夫 (fū, 'man')
  - **interpretation:** The character 大(dà) depicts a person facing forward, representing 'big' by suggesting the idea of someone standing prominently. Although often thought of as a person with outstretched arms, early scripts do not support this interpretation. It was originally the form of 夫 (fū, 'man') and was later phonetically borrowed for the meaning 'big.'
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)房子很大。
    - Zhè ge fángzi hěn dà.
    - This house is very big.
  - 大海波涛(濤)汹(洶)涌(湧)，展现(現)出大自然的力量。
    - Dàhǎi bōtāo xiōngyǒng, zhǎnxiàn chū dàzìrán de lìliàng.
    - The vast ocean waves surge, showcasing the power of nature.
  - 大人物登场(場)，留下传(傳)奇。
    - Dà rénwù dēngchǎng, liúxià chuánqí.
    - Great figures make an appearance, leaving behind legends.

### 奇怪 → 奇 → 大 → 夫
- **definition:** man
- **pinyin:** fū
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a variant form of 大 (dà, "big") representing an adult man with a hairpin ->
    - became associated with 夫 (fū, "man").
  - **interpretation:** 夫 (fū, "man") was visually similar to 大 (dà, "big") and was originally interchangeable. The addition of the horizontal stroke was interpreted as a hairpin, symbolizing adulthood for men.
- **examples:**

  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.

    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.

### 奇怪 → 怪
- **definition:** odd; strange; to blame
- **pinyin:** guài
- **components:**
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart
  - 聖
    - shèng
    - saint
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 忄(心) (xīn, "heart") + phonetic 圣(聖) (shèng, "saint") ->
    - weird; strange
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 忄(心) (xīn, "heart"), suggesting a feeling or emotion, with 圣(聖) (shèng, "saint"), which phonetically suggests the sound of the word. The combination implies a sense of an emotional response to something unusual or unexpected, leading to meanings like "weird" or "strange." The extension to "blame" and "monster" comes from attributing strangeness or malice when things are off or different.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)故事真是太怪了！
    - Zhège gùshi zhēnshi tài guài le!
    - This story is really too weird!
  - 他总(總)是怪天气(氣)不好。
    - Tā zǒng shì guài tiānqì bù hǎo.
    - He always blames the weather for being bad.
  - 在森林里遇到怪兽(獸)，真是令人毛骨悚然。
    - Zài sēnlín lǐ yùdào guàishòu, zhēnshi lìngrén máogǔsǒngrán.
    - Encountering a monster in the forest is truly spine-chilling.

- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**

  - **description:** Depicts a heart

Over time, it has become highly stylized, but it continues to convey the notion of the heart as the center of emotions and consciousness.
- **examples:**

    - Duìyú xǔduō rén lái shuō, jiātíng shì shēnghuó de héxīn.
