## education
### jiàoyù
---
- **definition:** education
- **pinyin:** jiàoyù
- **components:**
  - 教
    - jiào
    - teach
  - 育
    - yù
    - raise; nurture; education; development
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 教 (jiào, "teach") + 育 (yù, "raise; nurture; education; development")
  - **interpretation:** 教 (jiào, "teach") suggests imparting knowledge or skill, while 育 (yù, "raise; nurture; education; development") implies fostering growth or development. Together, they form 教育 (jiàoyù), which means "education", capturing both aspects of instruction and development, highlighting the dual focus of providing knowledge and nurturing personal growth and character.
- **examples:**
  - 教育是社会(會)发(發)展的基础(礎)。
    - Jiàoyù shì shèhuì fāzhǎn de jīchǔ.
    - Education is the foundation of social development.
  - 他在大学(學)的教育经(經)历(歷)丰(豐)富。
    - Tā zài dàxué de jiàoyù jīnglì fēngfù.
    - His educational experience in university is extensive.
  - 政府正在努力改善农(農)村地区(區)的教育条(條)件。
    - Zhèngfǔ zhèngzài nǔlì gǎishàn nóngcūn dìqū de jiàoyù tiáojiàn.
    - The government is working hard to improve the educational conditions in rural areas.
### 教育 → 教
- **definition:** teach
- **pinyin:** jiào
- **components:**
  - 爻
    - yáo
    - diagram
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child
  - 攵
    - pū
    - teaching cane
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 爻 + semantic 子 (child) + semantic 攵 (teaching cane) ->
    - teaching a child
  - **interpretation:** The character 教 (jiào, "teach") combines the phonetic element 爻 with semantic elements 子 (zǐ, "child") and 攵 (pū, "teaching cane"). The 子(子, "child") represents the learners or the ones being taught. The 攵(攵, "teaching cane") symbolizes authority and the traditional method of instruction, emphasizing discipline in teaching. Together, these elements depict the act of instructing or guiding a child, which leads to the meanings of 'teach' and by extension, 'religion' as a system of teaching beliefs.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一名经(經)验(驗)丰(豐)富的教师(師)。
    - Tā shì yī míng jīngyàn fēngfù de jiàoshī.
    - He is an experienced teacher.
  - 佛教是世界上最古老的宗教之一。
    - Fójiào shì shìjiè shàng zuì gǔlǎo de zōngjiào zhī yī.
    - Buddhism is one of the oldest religions in the world.
  - 我的母亲(親)教我游泳。
    - Wǒ de mǔqīn jiào wǒ yóuyǒng.
    - My mother taught me how to swim.

### 教育 → 教 → 爻
- **definition:** diagram
- **pinyin:** yáo
- **components:**
  - 乂
    - yì
    - to govern
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 乂 (yì, "to govern") + 乂 =
    - two stacked 'X' marks representing divination lines
  - **interpretation:** The character 爻(yáo, "divination lines") depicts two crossing lines, resembling 'X' marks, symbolizing the divination lines used in traditional methods. These marks can be interpreted as representing either pairs of yarrow stalks used for divination or bamboo slips used for calculations. Though it might visually resemble 交 (jiāo, "cross") or 駁 (bó, "contradict"), its primary function is depicting the meaningful crossing patterns in divination.
- **examples:**
  - 易经(經)卜卦时(時)，观(觀)察爻的变(變)化。
    - Yì jīng bǔ guà shí, guānchá yáo de biànhuà.
    - When consulting the I Ching, one observes the changes of the divination lines.
  - 卦中的每一个(個)爻都蕴(蘊)含宇宙奥(奧)秘。
    - Guà zhōng de měi yīgè yáo dōu yùnhán yǔzhòu àomì.
    - Every divination line in the hexagram contains the mysteries of the universe.
  - 大师(師)解读(讀)时(時)，详(詳)细(細)说(說)明爻的象征(徵)意义(義)。
    - Dàshī jiědú shí, xiángxì shuōmíng yáo de xiàngzhēng yìyì.
    - When the master interprets, he elaborates on the symbolic meaning of the divination lines.

### 教育 → 教 → 爻 → 乂
- **definition:** to govern
- **pinyin:** yì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a tool (similar to a sickle or a pair of scissors) for mowing or weeding ->
    - used for governing ->
    - final form 乂
  - **interpretation:** The character 乂 originally depicted a tool used for cutting, and by extension, it was associated with governing due to the notion of cutting or managing weeds as a metaphor for ruling effectively. This sense of order is crucial in governance.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)些年，他致力于(於)地方的治理。
    - Zhèxiē nián, tā zhìlì yú dìfāng de zhìlǐ.
    - Over the years, he has dedicated himself to local governance.
  - 为(為)民者，先自齐(齊)以治家。
    - Wèi mín zhě, xiān zì qí yǐ zhì jiā.
    - Those who govern for the people, first manage their own households.
  - 长(長)者都希望后(後)代能好好治理天下。
    - Zhǎng zhě dōu xīwàng hòudài néng hǎohǎo zhìlǐ tiānxià.
    - The elders hope that future generations can govern the world well.

### 教育 → 教 → 攵

- **pinyin:** pū
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - hand
  - 卜
    - bǔ
    - divination
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a hand 又 (yòu, "hand") holding a tool, stylized as 卜 (bǔ, "divination") ->
    - meaning "to hit"
  - **interpretation:** 攵 is a stylized pictogram originally depicting a hand holding a tool or weapon, representing action or hitting. The hand component evolved into a more uniform stylistic form using 卜 while preserving the core meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他埋怨我的方式总(總)是冲(衝)动(動)而不经(經)过(過)思考。

    - Xūyào de bù shì kōngtán, ér shì qiēshí de xíngdòng.
    - What is needed is not empty talk, but concrete action.

### 教育 → 教 → 攵 → 卜
- **definition:** divination
- **pinyin:** bǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a crack in an oracle bone
  - **interpretation:** The character 卜 (bǔ, 'divination') directly depicts a crack being used in oracle bones to divine future events. This literal representation naturally extends to the concept of divination.
- **examples:**
  - 在古代，人们(們)常用龟(龜)甲进(進)行卜卦。

    - Tā duì wèilái chōngmǎn yílǜ, suǒyǐ juédìng zhǎo rén bǔguà.
    - He was full of doubts about the future, so he decided to find someone for divination.
  - 在占星术(術)中，卜法常被用来(來)预(預)测(測)未来(來)。
    - Zài zhānxīngshù zhōng, bǔfǎ cháng bèi yòng lái yùcè wèilái.

### 教育 → 育
- **definition:** raise; nurture; education; development
- **pinyin:** yù
- **components:**
  - 肉
    - ròu
    - flesh
  - 朴
    - pǔ
    - simple
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 育 phono-semantic compound: semantic 肉 (ròu, "flesh") + phonetic 朴 (pǔ) ->
    - nurture and raise through nurturing the body
  - **interpretation:** The character 育(yù, 'nurture; raise; educate; breed') combines the semantic 肉 (ròu, 'flesh') indicating something related to the physical body with the phonetic 朴 (pǔ), to convey the idea of nourishing and raising through physical and educational means. The sense of 'education' arises from the fundamental role of raising and caring for children in shaping their intellectual and moral capabilities.
- **examples:**
  - 教育孩子是父母的重要责(責)任。

### 教育 → 育 → 朴(樸)
- **traditional:** 樸
- **definition:** simple
- **pinyin:** pǔ
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 卜
    - bǔ
    - divine
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 木 (mù, 'tree') + phonetic 卜 (bǔ) ->
    - representing simplicity and plainness associated with untreated wood
  - **interpretation:** The semantic component 木 (mù, 'tree') signifies untreated or natural wood, conveying the notion of something simple and unembellished. The phonetic component 卜 (bǔ) lends its sound to form the character. Together, these elements suggest the quality of something that remains plain or unworked, akin to raw wood.
  - **simplification:** As part of the Chinese Character Simplification Scheme, the character 樸 was simplified to 朴 by removing the additional components and retaining the simpler structure.
- **examples:**
  - 她的穿着(著)风(風)格非常朴(樸)素。
    - Tā de chuānzhuó fēnggé fēicháng pǔsù.
    - Her style of dressing is very plain.
  - 这(這)间(間)房子的设(設)计(計)很朴(樸)实(實)。
    - Zhè jiān fángzi de shèjì hěn pǔshí.
    - The design of this house is very simple.
  - 他过(過)着(著)朴(樸)素的生活，不喜欢(歡)奢华(華)。
    - Tā guòzhe pǔsù de shēnghuó, bù xǐhuān shēhuá.
    - He leads a plain life and does not like luxury.
