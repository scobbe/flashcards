## education
### jiàoyù
---
- **definition:** education
- **pinyin:** jiàoyù
- **components:**
  - 教
    - jiào
    - teach
  - 育
    - yù
    - raise; nurture; education; development
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 教 (jiào, "teach") + 育 (yù, "raise; nurture; education; development")
  - **interpretation:** 教 (jiào, "teach") suggests imparting knowledge or skill, while 育 (yù, "raise; nurture; education; development") implies fostering growth or development. Together, they form 教育 (jiàoyù), which means "education", capturing both aspects of instruction and development, highlighting the dual focus of providing knowledge and nurturing personal growth and character.

- **definition:** teach

- **components:**
  - 爻
    - yáo
    - diagram
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child
  - 攵
    - pū
    - teaching cane
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 爻 + semantic 子 (child) + semantic 攵 (teaching cane) ->
    - teaching a child
  - **interpretation:** The character 教 (jiào, "teach") combines the phonetic element 爻 with semantic elements 子 (zǐ, "child") and 攵 (pū, "teaching cane"). The 子(子, "child") represents the learners or the ones being taught. The 攵(攵, "teaching cane") symbolizes authority and the traditional method of instruction, emphasizing discipline in teaching. Together, these elements depict the act of instructing or guiding a child, which leads to the meanings of 'teach' and by extension, 'religion' as a system of teaching beliefs.

### 教育 → 教 → 爻
- **definition:** diagram
- **pinyin:** yáo
- **components:**
  - 乂
    - yì
    - to govern
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 乂 (yì, "to govern") + 乂 =
    - two stacked 'X' marks representing divination lines
  - **interpretation:** The character 爻(yáo, "divination lines") depicts two crossing lines, resembling 'X' marks, symbolizing the divination lines used in traditional methods. These marks can be interpreted as representing either pairs of yarrow stalks used for divination or bamboo slips used for calculations. Though it might visually resemble 交 (jiāo, "cross") or 駁 (bó, "contradict"), its primary function is depicting the meaningful crossing patterns in divination.
