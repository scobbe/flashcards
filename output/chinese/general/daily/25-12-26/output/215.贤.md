## virtuous; worthy
### xián
---
- **traditional:** 賢
- **definition:** virtuous; worthy
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - money
  - 臤
    - qiān
    - harmonious
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 貝 (bèi, "money") + phonetic 臤 (qiān) ->
    - associated with value and worthiness ->
    - virtuous, worthy
  - **interpretation:** The character 賢 (xián, "virtuous; worthy") uses 貝 (bèi, "money") to convey the idea of value and worth, while 臤 functions purely as a sound element. This aligns the concept of worthiness with the tangible value associated with money, thus establishing a link between being virtuous and being of great worth.
  - **simplification:** The traditional form 賢 was simplified to 贤 by removing the 貝 (bèi, "money") component, retaining the primary visual form derived from the phonetic 臤, simplifying the overall stroke count.

### 贤(賢) → 貝
- **definition:** money
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身( )符( )，放在口( )袋( )里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.

  - 古( )代( )人( )用( )贝(貝)作( )为(為)货(貨)币(幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.

### 贤(賢) → 臤
- **definition:** harmonious
- **pinyin:** qiān
- **components:**
  - 艮
    - gèn
    - stopping; posture
  - 干
    - qián
    - to dry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Phono-semantic compound: semantic 艮(身) (gèn, "stopping; posture") + phonetic 干(乾) (qián)
  - **interpretation:** The character 臤 initially combined 艮 which conveys a sense of firmness or stability, with 干 for phonetic purposes, suggesting strength. The concept of being 'strong' arises from the stability and firmness denoted by the stopping posture, which reflects strength in physical or moral standing.

### 贤(賢) → 臤 → 艮

  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 艮(gěn) depicts a person turning around to look back, symbolizing hesitation or obstruction. The association with stopping or limiting arises from the act of looking back as a check on forward movement.
