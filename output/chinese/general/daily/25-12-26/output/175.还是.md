## still; yet
### háishi
---
- **traditional:** 還是
- **definition:** still; yet
- **pinyin:** háishi
- **components:**
  - 还(還)
    - hái
    - still; yet; return
  - 是
    - shì
    - to be; yes; this; correct
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 還 (hái, "still; yet") indicates continuity or persistence; 是 (shì, "to be; yes") marks certainty or affirmation about a state or situation. Together, 還是 (háishi, "still; yet") denotes a sense of continuity or persistence in time or conditions.
  - **interpretation:** 還 (hái, "still; yet") suggests continuation or persistence over time, an ongoing state. 是 (shì, "to be; yes") implies certainty or affirmation. Together in 還是 (háishi), this continuation and certainty combine to describe something that persists, enduring in its state, resulting in meanings like 'still' or 'yet'.
- **examples:**
  - 他还(還)是不来(來)。
    - Tā háishi bù lái.
    - He still isn't coming.
  - 你喜欢(歡)茶，还(還)是咖啡？
    - Nǐ xǐhuān chá, háishi kāfēi?
    - Do you like tea or coffee?
  - 我们(們)还(還)是早点(點)走吧。
    - Wǒmen háishi zǎodiǎn zǒu ba.
    - We should still leave early.
