## practice
### liànxí
---
- **traditional:** 練習
- **definition:** practice
- **pinyin:** liànxí
- **components:**
  - 练(練)
    - liàn
    - practice; refine
  - 习(習)
    - xí
    - practice; habit
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 練 (liàn, "practice; refine") means to practice or train, often implying the refining or improving of skills. 習 (xí, "practice; habit") involves repetition or habit, often employed in the context of learning something through regular repetition.
  - **interpretation:** 練 (liàn, "practice; refine") conveys the idea of honing skills through disciplined training, likened to refining raw materials into something more valuable. 習 (xí, "practice; habit") emphasizes the repetitive aspect, akin to mastering a skill through regular use which becomes habitual. Thus, 練習 (liànxí, "practice") combines the ideas of active training and repetitive learning, resulting in the improvement and internalization of skills.
- **examples:**
  - 每天练(練)习(習)是提高技能的关(關)键(鍵)。
    - Měitiān liànxí shì tígāo jìnéng de guānjiàn.
    - Practicing every day is key to improving skills.
  - 她在为(為)比赛(賽)认(認)真练(練)习(習)。
    - Tā zài wèi bǐsài rènzhēn liànxí.
    - She is practicing diligently for the competition.
  - 孩子们(們)喜欢(歡)在放学(學)后(後)练(練)习(習)钢(鋼)琴。
    - Háizimen xǐhuān zài fàngxué hòu liànxí gāngqín.
    - The children like to practice piano after school.
### 练(練)习(習) → 练(練)
- **traditional:** 練
- **definition:** practice; refine
- **pinyin:** liàn
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk
  - 柬
    - jiǎn
    - card
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 糸 (mì, "silk") + 柬 (jiǎn, "card") =
    - silk related to sorting or selecting ->
    - practice
  - **interpretation:** 練 originally referred to refining or processing silk, implying the act of making material smooth and usable. Over time, this idea of refinement was metaphorically extended to training and practice, where skills are refined and polished.
  - **simplification:** 練 → 练(練): 糸 (mì, "silk") simplified to 纟 for ease of writing.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天练(練)书(書)法。
    - Wǒ měitiān liàn shūfǎ.
    - I practice calligraphy every day.
  - 他在武馆练(練)武术(術)，并(並)指导(導)年轻(輕)人。
    - Tā zài wǔguǎn liàn wǔshù, bìng zhǐdǎo niánqīng rén.
    - He trains in martial arts at the gym, and he guides young people.

### 练(練)习(習) → 习(習)
- **traditional:** 習
- **definition:** practice; habit
- **pinyin:** xí
- **components:**
  - 羽
    - yǔ
    - feather
  - 白
    - bái
    - white
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 羽 (yǔ, "feather") + 白 (bái, "white") =
    - imitation of the repeated action of birds flapping their wings ->
    - to practice, study
  - **interpretation:** The character 習 (xí) draws on the image of birds practicing flight through repetitive flapping of wings, which parallels the concept of learning through practice and repetition in humans.
  - **simplification:** 習→习(習): simplified by removing 羽 (yǔ, "feather") details; represented by shorthand.
- **examples:**
  - 我习(習)音乐(樂)。
    - wǒ xí yīnyuè
    - I study music.
  - 他习(習)历(歷)史，习(習)诗(詩)词(詞)。
    - tā xí lìshǐ, xí shīcí
    - He studies history and practices poetry.
  - 师(師)傅让(讓)徒弟习(習)武，日渐(漸)精进(進)。
    - shīfù ràng túdì xí wǔ, rìjiàn jīngjìn
    - The master has his apprentice practice martial arts, gradually improving.
