## wonton
### húntun
---
- **traditional:** 餛飩
- **definition:** wonton
- **pinyin:** húntun
- **components:**
  - 馄(餛)
    - hún
    - mix; confused
  - 饨(飩)
    - tún
    - swallow; round food
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 餛(馄) (hún, "mix; confused") and 飩(饨) (tún, "swallow; round food") together refer to a type of dumpling with mixed ingredients inside, typically boiled and served in soup.
  - **interpretation:** The word 馄(餛)饨(飩) (húntun, "wonton") describes a dumpling with various ingredients inside, represented by the notion of mixing in 餛(馄) (hún, "mix; confused"). The 饨(飩) (tún, "swallow; round food") highlights its round shape and that it is meant to be eaten whole, often served in soup.
  - **simplification:** Simplification: 餛→馄, 飩→饨
- **examples:**
  - 我点了馄(餛)饨(飩)。
    - Wǒ diǎnle hún tún.
    - I ordered wonton.
  - 早餐吃馄(餛)饨(飩)，营(營)养(養)丰(豐)富。
    - Zǎocān chī hún tún, yíngyǎng fēngfù.
    - Having wonton for breakfast makes it nutritious.
  - 他在街角的摊位买馄(餛)饨(飩)，味道极(極)佳。
    - Tā zài jiējiǎo de tānwèi mǎi hún tún, wèidào jí jiā.
    - He buys wonton at the street corner stall; the taste is excellent.
### 馄(餛)饨(飩) → 馄(餛)
- **traditional:** 餛
- **definition:** mix; confused
- **pinyin:** hún
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - food
  - 圂
    - hùn
    - confused
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 食 (shí, "food") + phonetic 圂 (hùn, "confused") ->
    - mix; confused
  - **interpretation:** 餛 combines the semantic element 食 (shí, "food") to indicate something related to food, with 圂 (hùn, "confused") providing the sound. The combination suggests the concept of mixed or confused, typically in the context of food preparation.
  - **simplification:** 食 (shí, "food") was simplified to 饣(食) and phonetic 圂 (hùn, "confused") remains as the phonetic component.
- **examples:**
  - 他把所有材料都混(餛)了在一起。
    - Tā bǎ suǒyǒu cáiliào dōu húnle zài yīqǐ.
    - He mixed all the ingredients together.
  - 我觉(覺)得他的想法有点(點)混(餛)乱(亂)。
    - Wǒ juéde tā de xiǎngfǎ yǒudiǎn húnluàn.
    - I think his ideas are a bit confused.
  - 事情变(變)得非常混(餛)杂(雜)。
    - Shìqíng biàndé fēicháng húnzá.
    - Things became very mixed up.
### 馄(餛)饨(飩) → 馄(餛) → 食
- **definition:** food
- **pinyin:** shí
- **components:**
  - 亼
    - jí
    - assemble
  - 𠊊
    - jí
    - bowl with stand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:** 亼 (jí, "assemble") over a pictographic bowl of rice on a stand, symbolized by 𠊊 (jí, "bowl with stand")
  - **interpretation:** Initially, the character 食 (shí, "eat") visually represented a mouth over a bowl of rice, conveying the act of eating or a meal intuitively through this imagery.
- **examples:**

### 馄(餛)饨(飩) → 馄(餛) → 食 → 亼
- **definition:** assemble
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 今
    - jīn
    - now
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 入 (rù, "enter") + 一 (yī, "one") =
    - gathering from three sides ->
    - original form of 今 (jīn, "now")

The visual comparison to an open mouth reversed is a less common interpretation, relating to a shape rather than function.

### 馄(餛)饨(飩) → 饨(飩)
- **traditional:** 飩
- **definition:** swallow; round food
- **pinyin:** tún
- **components:**
  - 飠
    - shí
    - eat
  - 豚
    - tún
    - piglet
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 飠 (shí, "eat") + phonetic: 豚 (tún, "piglet") =
    - round food ->
    - swallow
  - **interpretation:** The character 饨(飩) (tún, "swallow; round food") combines the semantic component 飠 (shí, "eat") with the phonetic component 豚 (tún, "piglet"), suggesting the concept of a round edible item, analogous to how piglets round out when well-fed.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 飩 by omitting the top component of the radical on the right side, maintaining the sound and meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱(愛)吃饨(飩)。
    - Wǒ ài chī tún.
    - I love eating wontons.
  - 他用汤(湯)做饨(飩)，味道很好。
    - Tā yòng tāng zuò tún, wèidào hěn hǎo.
    - He makes wontons with soup, and they taste very good.
