## takeout
### wàimài
---
- **traditional:** 外賣
- **definition:** takeout
- **pinyin:** wàimài
- **components:**
  - 外
    - wài
    - outside; foreign
  - 卖(賣)
    - mài
    - sell
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 外 (wài, "outside; foreign") + 賣(卖) (mài, "sell") =
    - food sold to eat outside (the vendor's premises) ->
    - takeout
  - **interpretation:** 外 (wài, "outside") suggests something external or beyond the usual boundary, while 賣(卖) (mài, "sell") indicates the commercial activity of selling a product. The combination signifies selling food that is intended to be consumed outside the vendor's establishment, which naturally aligns with the concept of takeout food.
- **examples:**
  - 我点(點)了外卖(賣)。
    - Wǒ diǎnle wàimài.
    - I ordered takeout.
  - 今晚我想吃外卖(賣)，因为(為)太累了。
    - Jīn wǎn wǒ xiǎng chī wàimài, yīnwèi tài lèi le.
    - I want to eat takeout tonight because I'm too tired.
  - 她常常在周末订(訂)外卖(賣)。
    - Tā chángcháng zài zhōumò dìng wàimài.
    - She often orders takeout on weekends.
### 外卖(賣) → 外
- **definition:** outside; foreign
- **pinyin:** wài
- **components:**
  - 卜
    - bǔ
    - divination
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 卜 (bǔ, "divination") + phonetic 月 (yuè, "moon") ->
    - represents divination based on the moon's phase ->
    - outside
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the idea of divination 卜 (bǔ, "divination") with the crescent moon 月 (yuè, "moon") as a phonetic element. The visual of a crescent moon indicates a part that is 'missing' or external, hence implying 'outside' or 'foreign'. This suggests an observation from 'outside' the entirety of the moon, leading to the sense of being external or foreign.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)在外面等你。
    - Wǒmen zài wàimiàn děng nǐ.
    - We will wait for you outside.
  - 这(這)个(個)包来(來)自国(國)外。
    - Zhège bāo láizì guówài.
    - This bag is from abroad.
  - 雨下得很大，我们(們)在屋外等雨停。
    - Yǔ xià de hěn dà，wǒmen zài wū wài děng yǔ tíng.
    - It was raining heavily, and we waited for the rain to stop outside the house.

### 外卖(賣) → 外 → 卜
- **definition:** divination
- **pinyin:** bǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a crack in an oracle bone
  - **interpretation:** The character 卜 (bǔ, 'divination') directly depicts a crack being used in oracle bones to divine future events. This literal representation naturally extends to the concept of divination.
- **examples:**
  - 在古代，人们(們)常用龟(龜)甲进(進)行卜卦。
    - Zài gǔdài, rénmen cháng yòng guījiǎ jìnxíng bǔguà.
    - In ancient times, people often used turtle shells for divination.
  - 他对(對)未来(來)充满(滿)疑虑(慮)，所以决(決)定找人卜卦。
    - Tā duì wèilái chōngmǎn yílǜ, suǒyǐ juédìng zhǎo rén bǔguà.
    - He was full of doubts about the future, so he decided to find someone for divination.
  - 在占星术(術)中，卜法常被用来(來)预(預)测(測)未来(來)。
    - Zài zhānxīngshù zhōng, bǔfǎ cháng bèi yòng lái yùcè wèilái.
    - In astrology, divination methods are often used to predict the future.

### 外卖(賣) → 外 → 月
- **definition:** moon
- **pinyin:** yuè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a crescent moon
  - **interpretation:** The character 月(yuè, "moon") is a pictographic representation of a crescent moon, straightforwardly capturing the curved shape often associated with the moon's appearance. Though interchangeable with 夕 (xī, "evening") in the past, it distinctly represents the moon in this usage.
- **examples:**
  - 夜空挂(掛)着(著)明月。
    - Yè kōng guà zhe míng yuè.
    - The bright moon hangs in the night sky.
  - 人们(們)赏(賞)月，寄托(託)相思。
    - Rén men shǎng yuè, jì tuō xiāng sī.

### 外卖(賣) → 卖(賣)
- **traditional:** 賣
- **definition:** sell
- **pinyin:** mài
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 貝 (bèi, "cowry") + Phonetic: 卖(賣) (mài, "sell") =
    - selling valuables ->
    - sell
  - **interpretation:** 賣 originally combines 貝 (bèi, "cowry"), a symbol of money and valuables in ancient China, with a phonetic component indicating the sound. Selling involved valuables, making the connection logical.
  - **simplification:** 賣 → 卖(賣): The shell radical 貝 (bèi, "cowry") was replaced by 单(單) (dān, "single") in a simplified phonetic and semantic reduction.
- **examples:**
  - 我去市场(場)卖(賣)水果。
    - Wǒ qù shìchǎng mài shuǐguǒ。
    - I go to the market to sell fruit.
  - 他卖(賣)友求荣(榮)。
    - Tā mài yǒu qiú róng。
    - He betrays his friends to seek glory.
  - 他总(總)是喜欢(歡)卖(賣)弄，自己的才华(華)。
    - Tā zǒngshì xǐhuān mài nòng, zìjǐ de cáihuá。
    - He always likes to show off his talent.
### 外卖(賣) → 卖(賣) → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为(為)货(貨)币(幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
