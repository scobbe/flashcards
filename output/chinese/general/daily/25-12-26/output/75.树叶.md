### shùyè
---
- **traditional:** 樹葉
- **definition:** leaves
- **pinyin:** shùyè
- **components:**
  - 树(樹)
    - shù
    - tree
  - 叶(葉)

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 树(樹) (shù, "tree") + 叶(葉) (yè, "leaf")
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 树(樹) (shù, "tree") and 叶(葉) (yè, "leaf") to form 树(樹)叶(葉) (shùyè, "leaves") makes sense as leaves are directly associated with trees. Trees produce leaves, so this word describes the plant part that grows on branches.
  - **simplification:** 树(樹) is the simplified form of 樹, and 叶(葉) is the simplified form of 葉.

### 树(樹)叶(葉) → 叶(葉) → 枼
- **definition:** leaf
- **pinyin:** yè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts leaves growing from a tree (original form of 葉 (yè, "leaf"))
  - **interpretation:** Depicts leaves on a branch, representing both the specific physical forms of 'leaf' as well as the larger conceptual category of things that are flat like leaves. This intuitive extension made it a fitting choice to also represent the concept of 'flat' due to the physical characteristic of flatness associated with leaves.
