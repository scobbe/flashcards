### Huáihǎi zhànyì
---
- **traditional:** 淮海戰役
- **definition:** Huaihai Campaign
- **pinyin:** Huáihǎi zhànyì
- **components:**
  - 淮
    - huái

  - 海
    - hǎi

  - 战(戰)
    - zhàn
    - war; battle
  - 役
    - yì
    - campaign; service
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 淮 (huái, "Huai River"), 海 (hǎi, "sea; large body of water"), 战(戰) (zhàn, "war; battle"), 役 (yì, "campaign; service")
  - **interpretation:** 淮 (huái, "Huai River") specifies the geographical region associated with the campaign, which took place near the Huai River. 海 (hǎi, "sea") here does not imply an actual sea but rather emphasizes vastness and strategic importance, reflecting the major scale of the campaign area. 战(戰) (zhàn, "war; battle") denotes the military engagement aspect, indicating an organized combat situation. 役 (yì, "campaign; service") further specifies this as a large-scale planned military operation. Together, 淮海戰役 (Huáihǎi zhànyì) refers to the significant battle campaign fought in the region near the Huai River, known historically as the Huaihai Campaign.
