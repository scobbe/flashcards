## thermo-sensitive
### rèmǐn
---
- **traditional:** 熱敏
- **definition:** thermo-sensitive
- **pinyin:** rèmǐn
- **components:**
  - 热(熱)
    - rè
    - heat; hot
  - 敏
    - mǐn
    - sensitive; quick
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 熱 (rè, "heat; hot") refers to high temperature or warmth. 敏 (mǐn, "sensitive; quick") denotes being perceptive or responsive, especially to external stimuli. Together, these characters form a term for something that is sensitive or responsive to heat, specifically 'thermo-sensitive' in English.
  - **interpretation:** 熱 (rè, "heat; hot") implies the application of temperature or warmth. 敏 (mǐn, "sensitive; quick") suggests an ability or readiness to respond. Combined, 熱敏 (rèmǐn, "thermo-sensitive") describes a condition or material that reacts or changes in response to heat, capturing the idea of sensitivity through the presence of warmth.
- **examples:**
