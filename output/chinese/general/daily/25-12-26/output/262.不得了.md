## terrible; extremely; amazing
### bùdéliǎo
---
- **definition:** terrible; extremely; amazing
- **pinyin:** bùdéliǎo
- **components:**
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
  - 得
    - dé
    - obtain; will; can; must
  - 了
    - liǎo
    - end; finish; able to; understand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 不(bù) (bù, "not") + 得 (dé, "obtain") + 了 (liǎo, "end") together convey a sense of reaching an extreme or unbearable point, hence the meanings "terrible," "extremely," or "amazing."
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 不得了 (bùdéliǎo) expresses a strong degree of emphasis, where 不(bù) (bù, "not") combined with 得 (dé, "obtain") and 了 (liǎo, "end") conveys something that cannot be achieved or ended, highlighting extremity or intensity.
- **examples:**
  - 他的笑容真是不得了。
    - Tā de xiàoróng zhēn shì bùdéliǎo.
    - His smile is truly amazing.
  - 暑假到了，学(學)生( )们(們)的( )热(熱)情( )不( )得( )了( )。
    - Shǔjià dào le, xuéshēngmen de rèqíng bùdéliǎo.
    - When summer vacation begins, the students' enthusiasm is incredible.

### 不得了 → 得
- **definition:** obtain; will; can; must
- **pinyin:** dé
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 又
    - yòu
    - hand
  - 彳
    - chì
    - step
  - 寸
    - cùn
    - inch
  - 旦
    - dàn
    - dawn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 貝 (bèi, 'cowry') + 又 (yòu, 'hand') ->
    - obtaining valuables ->
    - 彳 (chì, 'step') added, 又 changed to 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), 貝 corrupted to 旦 (dàn, 'dawn') ->
    - final form
  - **interpretation:** The character originated as a depiction of a hand picking up a cowry, symbolizing 'obtaining' valuables. The 彳 (chì, 'step') was added to suggest movement on a road, enhancing the idea of acquisition. Over time, script evolutions saw substitute elements: the component 又 (yòu, 'hand') changed to 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), reflecting the Qin script's tendency, and 貝 (bèi, 'cowry') was simplified to 旦 (dàn, 'dawn'). These changes led to the character's modern form. This transition mirrors the evolved meanings in usage: 'to get', from obtaining something physically; 'must', representing the necessity of movement or action; and its role as a structural particle in grammar for completion or necessity in expressions.
  - **simplification:** In simplified form, 得 remains unchanged from its traditional representation.
- **examples:**
  - 他跑得很快。
    - Tā pǎo de hěn kuài.
    - He runs very fast.
  - 我得去工作。
    - Wǒ děi qù gōngzuò.
    - I have to go to work.
  - 她( )昨( )天( )赢(贏)得( )了( )比( )赛(賽)。
    - Tā zuótiān yíngdé le bǐsài.
