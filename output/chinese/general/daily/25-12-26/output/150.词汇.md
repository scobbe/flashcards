## vocabulary
### cíhuì
---
- **traditional:** 詞彙
- **definition:** vocabulary
- **pinyin:** cíhuì
- **components:**
  - 词(詞)
    - cí
    - word; term; expression
  - 汇(彙)
    - huì
    - to collect; collection
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 詞 (cí, "word; term; expression") and 彙(匯) (huì, "to collect; collection").
  - **interpretation:** 詞 (cí, "word; term; expression") represents the basic linguistic unit, a word or a term. 彙(匯) (huì, "to collect; collection") denotes the gathering or grouping of items. Together, 詞彙 (cíhuì, "vocabulary") describes a collection or compilation of words and expressions, much like a word list or glossary that groups individual words into a comprehensive set.
  - **simplification:** 词汇
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)学(學)生的词(詞)汇(彙)量很大。
    - Zhège xuéshēng de cíhuì liàng hěn dà.
    - This student has a large vocabulary.
  - 老师(師)要求我们(們)每天学(學)习(習)新词(詞)汇(彙)。
    - Lǎoshī yāoqiú wǒmen měitiān xuéxí xīn cíhuì.
    - The teacher requires us to learn new vocabulary every day.
  - 提高词(詞)汇(彙)量可以帮(幫)助你更好地表达(達)自己。
    - Tígāo cíhuì liàng kěyǐ bāngzhù nǐ gèng hǎo de biǎodá zìjǐ.
    - Improving your vocabulary can help you express yourself better.
### 词(詞)汇(彙) → 词(詞)
- **traditional:** 詞
- **definition:** word; term; expression
- **pinyin:** cí
- **components:**
  - 讠(言)
    - yán
    - speech
  - 司
    - sī
    - manage
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 言 (yán, "speech") + phonetic: 司 (sī, "manage") =
    - word or term relating to speech ->
    - simplified by replacing 言 (yán, "speech") with 讠 (yán, "speech") ->
    - final form 词(詞)
  - **interpretation:** The character 词(詞) (cí, "word; term") combines the speech radical 言 (yán, "speech") with 司 (sī, "manage") to indicate a managed or structured form of speech, i.e., a word or term. The simplified form retains this concept, making writing faster with 讠 (yán, "speech") being a cursive form of the speech radical.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by changing the full radical 言 (yán, "speech") to its cursive form 讠 (yán, "speech"), facilitating quicker writing while retaining the original meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)词(詞)意义(義)丰(豐)富。
    - Zhè ge cí yìyì fēngfù.
    - This word has a rich meaning.
  - 诗(詩)歌中的每个(個)词(詞)都富有韵(韻)律，展现(現)了作者的才华(華)。
    - Shīgē zhōng de měi ge cí dōu fùyǒu yùnlǜ, zhǎnxiànle zuòzhě de cáihuá.
    - Every word in the poem is full of rhythm, revealing the author's talent.
### 词(詞)汇(彙) → 词(詞) → 讠(言)
- **traditional:** 言
- **definition:** speech
- **pinyin:** yán
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 辛
    - xīn
    - bitter
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + 辛 (xīn) combination ->
    - phonetic development possibly mixed with 舌 (shé, "tongue") ->
    - reference to speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 言 (yán, "speech") originates from depicting a mouth 口 (kǒu, "mouth") in combination with another form, possibly 永 or 辛, symbolizing the action or place of speaking. Over time, it may have involved 舌 (shé, "tongue") to reinforce its association with speech. This combination intuitively leads to the concept of 'speech' as it directly relates to vocal articulation and communication.
  - **simplification:** 讠(言): Simplified by adopting a single-stroke variant from cursive script of 訁, reducing strokes dramatically to represent speech.
- **examples:**
  - 在讨(討)论(論)会(會)上，他的发(發)言很有深度。
    - Zài tǎolùn huì shàng, tā de fāyán hěn yǒu shēndù.
    - In the discussion meeting, his speech was very profound.
  - 他总(總)是用简(簡)洁(潔)的言词(詞)表达(達)想法。
    - Tā zǒngshì yòng jiǎn jié de yán cí biǎodá xiǎngfǎ.
    - He always uses concise speech to express his ideas.
  - 他们(們)的辩(辯)论(論)十分激烈，双(雙)方的言辞(辭)都很犀利。
    - Tāmen de biànlùn shífēn jīliè, shuāngfāng de yáncí dōu hěn xīlì.
    - Their debate was very intense, and the remarks from both sides were sharp.
### 词(詞)汇(彙) → 词(詞) → 讠(言) → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

### 词(詞)汇(彙) → 词(詞) → 讠(言) → 辛
- **definition:** bitter
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a tool—chisel or awl—used to mark slaves and criminals ->
    - 辛
  - **interpretation:** The character 辛 (xīn) originally depicted a tool such as a chisel, used for marking or branding. The association with a sharp tool helps explain its extended meaning of 'spice,' as spices can have a pungent, sharp quality reminiscent of a tool's point or effect.
- **examples:**

    - He likes to add spices to the food.
  - 这(這)种(種)辛口的调(調)料非常受欢(歡)迎。
    - Zhè zhǒng xīnkǒu de tiáoliào fēicháng shòu huānyíng.

### 词(詞)汇(彙) → 汇(彙)
- **traditional:** 彙
- **definition:** to collect; collection
- **pinyin:** huì
- **components:**
  - 㣇
    - n/a
    - silk threads
  - 胃
    - wèi
    - stomach
  - 氵
    - shuǐ
    - water
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Semantic: 㣇 (silk threads); Phonetic: 胃 (wèi, "stomach")
  - **interpretation:** The character 彙 combines the semantic component 㣇, which indicates a collection or grouping, with the phonetic element 胃 (wèi, "stomach"), suggesting a relation to accumulation or gathering. This illustrates the idea of assembling or collecting items, akin to gathering threads.
  - **simplification:** 汇(彙): The simplified form replaces 㣇 with 氵(shuǐ, "water"), retaining phonetic similarity while reducing strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)计(計)划(劃)阶(階)段需要大量的数(數)据(據)汇(彙)总(總)。

  - 他们(們)在那里(裡)进(進)行汇(彙)报(報)。
    - Tāmen zài nàlǐ jìnxíng huìbào.
    - They are conducting a briefing there.
  - 这(這)些文件是公司重要的信息汇(彙)编(編)。
    - Zhèxiē wénjiàn shì gōngsī zhòngyào de xìnxī huìbiān.

### 词(詞)汇(彙) → 汇(彙) → 胃
- **definition:** stomach
- **pinyin:** wèi
- **components:**
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
  - 田
    - tián
    - field
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stomach with food inside ->
    - two concentric circles representing stomach ->
    - 米(mǐ, "rice") added for grains ->
    - small seal script form 𦞅 ->
    - clerical script simplified to 田 for top component
  - **interpretation:** The character 胃(wèi, "stomach") originally depicted a stomach containing food. Over time, a visual component representing grains (米(mǐ, "rice")) was added to emphasize content in the stomach. The evolution of character forms led to the simplification of this depiction into the current form, which includes 田(tián, "field") as the top component due to practical changes in writing styles.
- **examples:**
  - 我的胃很痛。

  - 吃得太快会(會)伤(傷)害胃。

    - Māma wèi wǒ zhǔle wèi tiáo hǎo shòu de zhōu.
