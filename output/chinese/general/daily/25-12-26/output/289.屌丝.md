## loser (internet slang)
### diǎosī
---
- **traditional:** 屌絲
- **definition:** loser (internet slang)
- **pinyin:** diǎosī
- **components:**
  - 屌
    - diǎo
    - male genitalia; slang for cocky
  - 丝(絲)
    - sī
    - silk; thread
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 屌 (diǎo, "male genitalia/slang for cocky/), 丝(絲) (sī, "silk/thread")
  - **interpretation:** 屌丝(絲) (diǎosī) is a slang term originating from Chinese internet culture, humorously referring to someone seen as a 'loser' or lacking in success by the traditional standards of wealth, prestige, or relationships. It juxtaposes the raw and blunt connotation of 屌 (diǎo, "male genitalia/slang for cocky") with the soft, delicate imagery of 丝(絲) (sī, "silk/thread"), creating an ironic label for individuals who might not meet conventional success criteria but embrace their circumstances with humor and defiance.
  - **simplification:** 丝→絲
- **examples:**
  - 他觉(覺)得自己是个(個)屌丝(絲)。
    - Tā juéde zìjǐ shìgè diǎosī.
    - He thinks he is a loser.
  - 他的朋友总(總)嘲笑他，认(認)为(為)他是个(個)屌丝(絲)。
    - Tā de péngyǒu zǒng cháoxiào tā, rènwéi tā shìgè diǎosī.
    - His friends always laugh at him, thinking he is a loser.
### 屌丝(絲) → 屌
- **definition:** male genitalia; slang for cocky
- **pinyin:** diǎo
- **components:**
  - 尸
    - shī
    - body
  - 吊
    - diào
    - hanging
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 尸 (shī, "body") + phonetic 吊 (diào, "hanging") ->
    - something that hangs from the body
  - **interpretation:** The character 屌(dǐao, "male genitalia") combines the semantic component 尸 (shī, "body") with the phonetic component 吊 (diào, "hanging") to convey the idea of something hanging from the body, referring to male genitalia. The slang sense of 'cocky' links to boldness and assertiveness, metaphorically extending from the physical feature.
- **examples:**
  - 他总(總)是表现(現)得很屌。
    - Tā zǒng shì biǎoxiàn de hěn diǎo.
    - He always acts very cocky.
  - 那件事情让(讓)他觉(覺)得自己很屌。
    - Nà jiàn shìqíng ràng tā juéde zìjǐ hěn diǎo.
    - That incident made him feel very cocky.
  - 他以为(為)自己很屌，其实(實)并(並)没(沒)有。
    - Tā yǐwéi zìjǐ hěn diǎo, qíshí bìng méiyǒu.
    - He thinks he is very cocky, but he isn't.

  - **interpretation:** 尸 (shī, "corpse") is a pictogram depicting a person in a position indicating death, mirroring a sitting or lying posture. This form captures the essence of a non-living body.
- **examples:**

### 屌丝(絲) → 屌 → 吊
- **definition:** hanging
- **pinyin:** diào
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 人 (rén, "person") over an arrow tied with 糸 (mì, "silk thread") ->
    - a person hanging a thread
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicting a man above an arrow tied to a silk thread, 吊 captures the image of a person utilizing a thread for attachment or suspension. The semantic component of a thread conveys the action, while the depiction of a man emphasizes direct involvement, leading to the sense of 'hanging'.
- **examples:**
  - 他把画(畫)吊在墙(牆)上。
    - Tā bǎ huà diào zài qiáng shàng.
    - He hung the painting on the wall.
  - 风(風)筝(箏)在空中自由地吊着(著)。
    - Fēngzhēng zài kōngzhōng zìyóu de diào zhe.
    - The kite is freely hanging in the air.
  - 他用吊车(車)吊起重物。
    - Tā yòng diàochē diào qǐ zhòngwù.
    - He used the crane to lift the heavy object.

- **examples:**

### 屌丝(絲) → 丝(絲)
- **traditional:** 絲
- **definition:** silk; thread
- **pinyin:** sī
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 糸 (mì, "silk; thread") + 糸 (mì, "silk; thread") =
    - intertwined threads ->
    - silk; thread
  - **interpretation:** In 丝(絲) (sī, "silk; thread"), the repetition of 糸 (mì, "silk; thread") signifies multiple threads intertwined, emphasizing the nature of silk or thread as consisting of numerous thin threads combined together.
  - **simplification:** 絲 was simplified to 丝(絲) by streamlining the duplication of 糸 to a simpler form.
- **examples:**
  - 她穿了一条(條)丝(絲)裙。
    - Tā chuānle yì tiáo sī qún.
    - She wore a silk dress.
  - 这(這)条(條)丝(絲)巾是从(從)意大利买(買)的。
    - Zhè tiáo sī jīn shì cóng Yìdàlì mǎi de.
    - This silk scarf was bought in Italy.
  - 舟子的汗水如丝(絲)般滑落。
    - Zhōuzǐ de hànshuǐ rú sī bān huáluò.
    - The boatman's sweat slid down like silk.
