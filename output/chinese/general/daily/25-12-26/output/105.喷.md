## spray
### pēn
---
- **traditional:** 噴
- **definition:** spray
- **pinyin:** pēn
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 賁
    - bēn
    - related to "decorate"
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic 賁
  - **interpretation:** The character 噴(pēn, "spray") combines the semantic component 口 (kǒu, "mouth") indicating an action involving the mouth, with the phonetic component 賁 (bēn), suggesting the pronunciation. The image evoked is that of something being forcefully expelled from the mouth, extending to the idea of 'spraying.'
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 噴 was simplified to 喷 by replacing 賁 with the simpler phonetic 夯, reducing stroke count while maintaining the phonetic sound.
- **examples:**
  - 花园(園)里(裡)的喷(噴)水器每天都会(會)喷(噴)水。
    - Huāyuán lǐ de pēnshuǐqì měitiān dūhuì pēnshuǐ.
    - The sprinkler in the garden sprays water every day.
  - 消防员(員)用水枪(槍)喷(噴)灭(滅)了大火。
    - Xiāofángyuán yòng shuǐqiāng pēn miè le dàhuǒ.
    - The firefighter extinguished the fire using a water hose.
  - 我喜欢(歡)那些会(會)喷(噴)香味的空气(氣)清新剂(劑)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān nàxiē huì pēn xiāngwèi de kōngqì qīngxīn jì.
    - I like those air fresheners that spray fragrance.
### 喷(噴) → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

### 喷(噴) → 賁
- **definition:** related to "decorate"
- **pinyin:** bēn
- **components:**
  - 卉
    - huì
    - plant
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 卉 (huì, 'plant') + Semantic: 貝 (bèi, 'cowry') ->
    - loaned for 奔 (bēn, 'rush') ->
    - used for 'decorate'
  - **interpretation:** Originally, 賁(bēn) used 貝 ('cowry') symbolizing valuable decoration and paired with phonetic 卉 ('plant'). It was borrowed early on to represent 奔 ('rush'), diversifying its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他賁飾了院子，使之焕(煥)然一新。
    - Tā bēn shì le yuànzi, shǐ zhī huànrán yī xīn.
    - He decorated the courtyard, making it look completely renewed.
  - 节(節)日来(來)临(臨)时(時)，市集被彩灯(燈)賁耀。
    - Jiérì láilín shí, shìjí bèi cǎidēng bēn yào.
    - When the festival arrived, the market was brilliantly decorated with colorful lights.

### 喷(噴) → 賁 → 卉
- **definition:** plant
- **pinyin:** huì
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè
    - sprout
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 屮 (chè, "sprout") + 八 (bā, "eight") =
    - various plants emerging
  - **interpretation:** The character 卉 (huì, "plant") combines 屮 (chè, "sprout"), depicting vegetation, with 八 (bā, "eight"), implying multiplicity or variety. Together, they convey the idea of numerous plants or a diversity of vegetation growing forth, reflecting the character's use to represent 'plant.'
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)花园(園)里(裏)有各种(種)各样(樣)的卉。
    - Zhège huāyuán lǐ yǒu gèzhǒng gè yàng de huì.

- **definition:** sprout
- **pinyin:** chè

  - **interpretation:** 屮 (chè, 'sprout') is a pictogram that directly represents a young plant or sprout emerging from the ground. Its visual form captures the essence of new growth, and it historically served as an early representation of plant life or new vegetation.

### 喷(噴) → 賁 → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为(為)货(貨)币(幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
