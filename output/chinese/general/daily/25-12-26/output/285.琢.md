## carve; polish (jade)
### zhuó
---
- **definition:** carve; polish (jade)
- **pinyin:** zhuó
- **components:**
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
  - 石
    - shí
    - stone
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 玉 (yù, "jade") + 石 (shí, "stone") =
    - related to working with jade ->
    - phonetic: 豆 (dòu) suggests pronunciation
  - **interpretation:** The character 琢(zhuó, "carve; polish") suggests the process of shaping and refining jade, a task that involves skillful use of stone tools. The presence of 玉 (yù, "jade") highlights the context of working with valuable stones, while 豆 (dòu) provides a phonetic clue to the character's pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 工匠琢玉雕龙(龍)。
    - Gōngjiàng zhuó yù diāo lóng.
    - The craftsman carves jade into a dragon.
  - 他琢磨古玉，寻(尋)找其中的美。
    - Tā zhuó mó gǔ yù, xúnzhǎo qízhōng de měi.
    - He polishes ancient jade, seeking the beauty within.
  - 他不断(斷)琢玉，雕琢出美丽(麗)纹(紋)路。
    - Tā bùduàn zhuó yù, diāozhuó chū měilì wénlù.
    - He constantly polishes jade, carving out beautiful patterns.
### 琢 → 玉
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a picture of a cong (琮), a sort of ritual jade totem ->
    - dot added to differentiate from 王 (wáng, "king") ->
    - final form 玉 (yù, "jade")
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a picture of a jade cong, emphasizing its role in rituals and its cultural significance. The image eventually included a distinguishing dot to ensure clarity from similar characters like 王, underscoring the value jade held as a material in ancient contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 她收到了一个漂亮的玉手镯(鐲)。
    - Tā shōu dào le yī gè piàoliang de yù shǒuzhuó.
    - She received a beautiful jade bracelet.
  - 皇帝的王冠上镶(鑲)嵌着(著)闪(閃)耀的玉石。
    - Huángdì de wángguān shàng xiāngqiànzhe shǎnyào de yùshí.
    - The emperor's crown was inlaid with shining jade stones.
  - 古代的玉器常常被视(視)为(為)珍贵(貴)的艺(藝)术(術)品。
    - Gǔdài de yùqì chángcháng bèi shìwéi zhēnguì de yìshùpǐn.
    - Ancient jade artifacts are often regarded as precious works of art.

### 琢 → 石
- **definition:** stone
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stone beneath a cliff (厂) ->
    - distorted to 丆 ->
    - stone
  - **interpretation:** The character 石(shí, 'stone') originally depicted a stone under a cliff, representing the idea of a solid, natural object found at the base of rocky formations.
- **examples:**
  - 那个(個)花园(園)里(裡)有一块(塊)很大的石头(頭)。
    - Nàge huāyuán lǐ yǒu yīkuài hěn dà de shítou.
    - There is a big stone in the garden.
  - 石头(頭)变(變)得很滑，因为(為)下雨了。
    - Shítou biàn de hěn huá, yīnwèi xià yǔ le.
    - The stone became very slippery because it rained.
  - 他收集了很多不同种(種)类(類)的石头(頭)。
    - Tā shōujíle hěnduō bùtóng zhǒnglèi de shítou.

### 琢 → 豆
- **definition:** bean
- **pinyin:** dòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a container ->
    - borrowed phonetically for the plant name
  - **interpretation:** The character 豆(dòu, 'drum') originally depicted a type of container. It was later borrowed for its phonetic value to represent the plant name 'bean', distinguishing it from a container. This transition indicates how the physical shape of the container, resembling a drum or storage pot, facilitated a phonetic borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)在课(課)堂上学(學)习(習)如何制(製)作豆。
    - Wǒmen zài kètáng shàng xuéxí rúhé zhìzuò dòu.
    - We are learning how to make drums in class.
  - 村庄(莊)里(裡)有一个(個)古老的豆，它的声(聲)音很有魅力。
    - Cūnzhuāng lǐ yǒu yí gè gǔlǎo de dòu, tā de shēngyīn hěn yǒu mèilì.
    - There is an ancient drum in the village, and its sound is captivating.
  - 音乐(樂)会(會)以豆声(聲)开(開)场(場)，让(讓)观(觀)众(眾)立刻沉(沈)浸在气(氣)氛中。
    - Yīnyuèhuì yǐ dòu shēng kāichǎng, ràng guānzhòng lìkè chénjìn zài qìfēn zhōng.
    - The concert opened with the sound of drums, immersing the audience in the atmosphere immediately.
