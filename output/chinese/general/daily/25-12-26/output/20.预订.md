## reserve; book
### yùdìng
---
- **traditional:** 預訂
- **definition:** reserve; book
- **pinyin:** yùdìng
- **components:**
  - 预(預)
    - yù
    - beforehand
  - 订(訂)
    - dìng
    - fix; set
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 預 (yù, "beforehand") indicating anticipation or planning in advance, and 訂 (dìng, "fix; set") meaning to establish or confirm. This combination suggests the action of confirming something in advance.
  - **interpretation:** The sense of 'reserve' or 'book' in 預訂 derives from the concept of making arrangements (訂) beforehand (預). The act of setting or fixing something (such as a schedule, seat, or service) ahead of time leads to the concept of reserving or booking.
- **examples:**
  - 我想预(預)订(訂)一张(張)餐桌。
    - Wǒ xiǎng yùdìng yī zhāng cānzhuō.
    - I would like to reserve a table.
  - 请(請)提前预(預)订(訂)机(機)票。
    - Qǐng tíqián yùdìng jīpiào.
    - Please book the plane tickets in advance.
  - 她已经(經)预(預)订(訂)了这(這)家酒店，我们(們)可以直接入住。
    - Tā yǐjīng yùdìng le zhè jiā jiǔdiàn, wǒmen kěyǐ zhíjiē rùzhù.
    - She has already booked this hotel, so we can check in directly.
### 预(預)订(訂) → 预(預)
- **traditional:** 預
- **definition:** beforehand
- **pinyin:** yù
- **components:**
  - 頁
    - yè
    - page
  - 予
    - yǔ
    - to give
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 頁 (yè, "page") + phonetic 予 (yǔ) ->
    - anticipated response or preparation
  - **interpretation:** 頁 (yè, "page") suggests information or a plan, while 予 (yǔ) provides the sound, leading to the idea of preparation or arranging in advance. The concept of preparing or planning naturally precedes an event, reflecting the meaning 'in advance' or 'prepare'.
  - **simplification:** Simplified to 预 by reducing components 頁 to 页, retaining phonetic 予.
- **examples:**
  - 他提前通知了我们(們)，让(讓)我们(們)做好准(準)备(備)。
    - Tā tíqián tōngzhī le wǒmen, ràng wǒmen zuò hǎo zhǔnbèi.
    - He informed us in advance, letting us prepare well.
  - 他们(們)正在预(預)热(熱)机(機)器。
    - Tāmen zhèngzài yùrè jīqì.
    - They are preheating the machine.
  - 会(會)议(議)已经(經)预(預)定了时(時)间(間)和地点(點)。
    - Huìyì yǐjīng yùdìng le shíjiān hé dìdiǎn.
    - The meeting has already set the time and location beforehand.
### 预(預)订(訂) → 预(預) → 頁
- **definition:** page
- **pinyin:** yè
- **components:**
  - 首
    - shǒu
    - head; first
  - 卩
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 首 (head/face) + 卩 (kneeling person) ->
    - depicts a person's head and body
  - **interpretation:** Originally represented a head and its body in a kneeling posture, emphasizing “head”. The depiction was extended semantically to represent a 'page', as pages bear leading information like a head does for a body. The concept of 'head' is also reflected in its modern usage as a radical in characters related to parts of the head or face.
  - **simplification:** 页 is a cursive shorthand of the traditional 頁, simplifying the structure while maintaining the core shape of a head.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)打开(開)书(書)的第十页(頁)。
    - Qǐng dǎkāi shū de dì shí yè.
    - Please open to page ten of the book.
  - 这(這)本书(書)每一页(頁)都有插图(圖)。
    - Zhè běn shū měi yī yè dōu yǒu chātú.
    - Every page of this book has illustrations.
  - 我昨天晚上读(讀)了三十页(頁)。
    - Wǒ zuótiān wǎnshang dúle sānshí yè.
    - I read thirty pages last night.

### 预(預)订(訂) → 预(預) → 頁 → 卩

- **pinyin:** jié

  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling.     - Zài zhuāngyán yíshì zhōng, jié xiàngzhēngzhe nèixīn de qiánjìng.

### 预(預)订(訂) → 预(預) → 予
- **definition:** to give
- **pinyin:** yǔ
- **components:**
  - 呂
    - lǚ
    - spine
  - 亅
    - jué
    - hook
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** jiajie
  - **description:**
    - originally written as 呂 (lǚ, 'spine') phonetically borrowed ->
    - 亅 added for distinction and stylized ->
    - final form 予 (yǔ, 'give')
  - **interpretation:** The character 予(yǔ) ('give') is a borrowing for the meanings 'I; give', initially represented by 呂(lǚ) (‘spine’) due to phonetic similarity. A vertical stroke 亅 was later added at the bottom to differentiate it visually, resulting in a distinct character for the meaning 'to give'.
- **examples:**

    - Wǒ yuànyì yǔ nǐ bāngzhù.
    - I am willing to give you help.

    - Zhè xiàng róngyù bèi yǔ le tā.
    - This honor was given to him.
  - 她将(將)一份礼(禮)物予了我。
    - Tā jiāng yī fèn lǐwù yǔ le wǒ.
    - She gave a gift to me.

### 预(預)订(訂) → 预(預) → 予 → 呂
- **definition:** spine
- **pinyin:** lǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts two disconnected blocks of metal, later connected in seal script ->
    - Original form of 鋁 (lǚ, 'ingot') ->
    - Borrowed phonetically for 'spine' and 'music notes'
  - **interpretation:** The character 呂(lǚ) ('two blocks of metal') visually represents two separate blocks aligned vertically. In its earliest forms, these blocks were disconnected, symbolizing distinct metal segments, which aligns with its early use for 'ingot'. Over time, as seen in seal script, a connecting line was added to form the modern appearance. This visual interpretation supports its original meaning related to metal. It was later phonetically borrowed to convey concepts like 'spine' and 'music notes', where no direct semantic link exists with metal, but the sound similarity allowed its reuse in these contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 他用两(兩)个(個)大的金属(屬)块(塊)铸(鑄)造了一个(個)雕像。
    - Tā yòng liǎng gè dà de jīnshǔ kuài zhùzào le yī gè diāoxiàng.
    - He cast a statue using two large blocks of metal.
  - 现(現)代工业(業)中，金属(屬)块(塊)常用于(於)制(製)造重型设(設)备(備)。
    - Xiàndài gōngyè zhōng, jīnshǔ kuài cháng yòng yú zhìzào zhòngxíng shèbèi.
    - In modern industry, metal blocks are often used for manufacturing heavy equipment.
  - 这(這)个(個)设(設)计(計)师(師)用金属(屬)块(塊)建造了一个(個)现(現)代化建筑(築)。

### 预(預)订(訂) → 订(訂)

- **definition:** fix; set
- **pinyin:** dìng
- **components:**
  - 讠(言)
    - yán
    - speech
  - 丁
    - dīng

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 讠(言) (yán, "speech") + 丁 (dīng, "nail") =
    - to fix with words ->
    - order; agree; conclude
  - **interpretation:** The character 订(訂) (dìng, "order; agree; conclude") combines 讠(言) (yán, "speech"), indicating verbal communication, with 丁 (dīng, "nail"), giving the sense of securing or fixing an agreement with words.

- **examples:**
  - 我在餐厅(廳)订(訂)了一份面(麵)条(條)。

    - Wǒmen dìng hǎo jīpiào, zhǔnbèi míngtiān chūfā.
    - We have booked our tickets and are ready to depart tomorrow.
  - 经(經)过(過)讨(討)论(論)，双(雙)方订(訂)立协(協)议(議)。
    - Jīngguò tǎolùn, shuāngfāng dìnglì xiéyì.

### 预(預)订(訂) → 订(訂) → 讠(言)
- **traditional:** 言
- **definition:** speech
- **pinyin:** yán
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 辛
    - xīn
    - bitter
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + 辛 (xīn) combination ->
    - phonetic development possibly mixed with 舌 (shé, "tongue") ->
    - reference to speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 言 (yán, "speech") originates from depicting a mouth 口 (kǒu, "mouth") in combination with another form, possibly 永 or 辛, symbolizing the action or place of speaking. Over time, it may have involved 舌 (shé, "tongue") to reinforce its association with speech. This combination intuitively leads to the concept of 'speech' as it directly relates to vocal articulation and communication.
  - **simplification:** 讠(言): Simplified by adopting a single-stroke variant from cursive script of 訁, reducing strokes dramatically to represent speech.
- **examples:**
  - 在讨(討)论(論)会(會)上，他的发(發)言很有深度。

    - Tāmen de biànlùn shífēn jīliè, shuāngfāng de yáncí dōu hěn xīlì.
