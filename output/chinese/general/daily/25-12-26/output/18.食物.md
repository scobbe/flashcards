## food
### shíwù
---
- **definition:** food
- **pinyin:** shíwù
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - eat; meal
  - 物
    - wù
    - thing; object
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 食 (shí, "eat"; "meal") + 物 (wù, "thing") together form the compound "food."
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 食 (shí, "eat") and 物 (wù, "thing") refers to anything that is eaten, thus aptly describing "food."
- **examples:**
  - 那家餐厅(廳)的食物很美味。
    - Nà jiā cāntīng de shíwù hěn měiwèi.
    - The food at that restaurant is delicious.
  - 冰箱里(裏)有很多食物。
    - Bīngxiāng lǐ yǒu hěn duō shíwù.
    - There is a lot of food in the refrigerator.
  - 他们(們)整理了食物，以备(備)明天的聚会(會)。
    - Tāmen zhěnglǐ le shíwù, yǐ bèi míngtiān de jùhuì.
    - They organized the food to prepare for tomorrow's gathering.
### 食物 → 食
- **definition:** eat; meal
- **pinyin:** shí
- **components:**
  - 亼
    - jí
    - assemble
  - 𠊊
    - jí
    - bowl with stand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:** 亼 (jí, "assemble") over a pictographic bowl of rice on a stand, symbolized by 𠊊 (jí, "bowl with stand")
  - **interpretation:** Initially, the character 食 (shí, "eat") visually represented a mouth over a bowl of rice, conveying the act of eating or a meal intuitively through this imagery.
- **examples:**
  - 他食得很快。
    - Tā shí dé hěn kuài.
    - He eats very quickly.
  - 我准(準)备(備)午食，与(與)朋友共享佳肴(餚)。
    - Wǒ zhǔnbèi wǔ shí, yǔ péngyǒu gòngxiǎng jiāyáo.
    - I am preparing lunch to share a delicious meal with my friends.
  - 民以食为(為)天。
    - Mín yǐ shí wéi tiān.
    - Food is regarded as the most important element for the people.
### 食物 → 食 → 亼
- **definition:** assemble
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 今
    - jīn
    - now
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 入 (rù, "enter") + 一 (yī, "one") =
    - gathering from three sides ->
    - original form of 今 (jīn, "now")
  - **interpretation:** 亼 (jī, "open mouth flipped upside down") originally represented the action of gathering, with 入 (rù, "enter") suggesting bringing things together and 一 (yī, "one") implying unification into a single entity. This concept aligns traditionally with 集 (jí, "gather"), where the sense of gathering from three sides connects to assembling or coming together. The visual comparison to an open mouth reversed is a less common interpretation, relating to a shape rather than function.

### 食物 → 食 → 亼 → 入
- **definition:** enter
- **pinyin:** rù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** An arrowhead indicating 'to enter'
  - **interpretation:** The character 入(rù, 'enter') visually represents the concept of entering, with the form resembling an arrowhead suggesting movement through an opening.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)入座。
    - Qǐng rùzuò.
    - Please take a seat.
  - 学(學)生走入校园(園)，开(開)始新学(學)期。
    - Xuéshēng zǒu rù xiàoyuán, kāishǐ xīn xuéqī.
    - The students enter the campus and start a new semester.
  - 游(遊)客走入古城，探索历(歷)史。
    - Yóukè zǒu rù gǔchéng, tànsuǒ lìshǐ.
    - The tourists enter the ancient city and explore its history.

### 食物 → 食 → 亼 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** A horizontal stroke representing the concept of 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** The character 一(yī, "one") is a simple horizontal line signifying the singular and unified concept of 'one'. It directly represents being singular or the number one.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一个(個)苹(蘋)果。
    - Wǒ yǒu yī gè píngguǒ.
    - I have one apple.
  - 他只看到一只(隻)鸟(鳥)，飞(飛)进(進)了花园(園)。
    - Tā zhǐ kàn dào yī zhī niǎo, fēi jìn le huā yuán.
    - He only saw one bird, which flew into the garden.
  - 一条(條)直路通向远(遠)方。
    - Yī tiáo zhí lù tōng xiàng yuǎn fāng.
    - One straight road leads to the distance.

### 食物 → 食 → 亼 → 今
- **definition:** now
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **components:**
  - 曰
    - yuē
    - to speak
  - 亼
    - jí
    - collect; assemble
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 曰 (yuē, "to speak") inverted with 亼-like structure + horizontal stroke ->
    - original form implying 'not to speak' or 'keep in mouth'
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts an inverted version of 曰 (yuē, "to speak"), with a structure resembling 亼 and a horizontal stroke, representing something placed in the mouth, leading to the sense of 'keeping in the mouth' or 'not speaking'. This image of being contained or holding leads to 'now', as in holding or having the present moment.
- **examples:**
  - 今天的天气(氣)不错(錯)。
    - Jīntiān de tiānqì bùcuò.
    - Today's weather is nice.
  - 现(現)在是吃晚饭(飯)的时(時)候。
    - Xiànzài shì chī wǎnfàn de shíhòu.
    - Now is the time to have dinner.
  - 她今天特别(別)忙，没(沒)时(時)间(間)见(見)面。
    - Tā jīntiān tèbié máng, méi shíjiān jiànmiàn.
    - She is especially busy today and doesn't have time to meet.
### 食物 → 食 → 亼 → 今 → 曰
- **definition:** to speak
- **pinyin:** yuē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth, signifying speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 曰 (yuē, "say") visually resembles a mouth or an opening, symbolizing the action of speaking or saying something.
- **examples:**
  - 老师(師)曰：勤奋(奮)是成功之母。
    - Lǎoshī yuē：qínfèn shì chénggōng zhī mǔ。
    - The teacher said, 'Diligence is the mother of success.'
  - 孔子曰，学(學)而不思则(則)罔。
    - Kǒngzǐ yuē, xué ér bù sī zé wǎng。
    - Confucius said, 'Learning without thought leads to confusion.'
  - 老子曰，无(無)为(為)而治。
    - Lǎozǐ yuē, wúwéi ér zhì。
    - Laozi said, 'Governance should be achieved through non-action.'

  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**

The component 皀 specifically conveys the idea of a container or bowl elevated, which situates its primary meaning in the context of serving or holding food.

### 食物 → 物
- **definition:** thing; object
- **pinyin:** wù
- **components:**
  - 牛
    - niú
    - cow
  - 勿
    - wù
    - do not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 牛 (niú, "cow") + phonetic 勿 (wù) ->
    - thing; object
  - **interpretation:** The character 物 (wù, "thing; object") combines 牛 (niú, "cow") as the semantic component, signifying tangible entities or physical objects, with 勿 (wù) as the phonetic component to provide the sound. The usage of 牛, representing cattle, suggests that the meanings originally included tangible, concrete items before broadening to general objects.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)物品很贵(貴)重。
    - Zhège wùpǐn hěn guìzhòng.
    - This object is very valuable.
  - 我在寻(尋)找一个(個)特别(別)的东(東)西。
    - Wǒ zài xúnzhǎo yī gè tèbié de dōngxi.
    - I am looking for a special thing.
  - 每样(樣)物品都有其用途。
    - Měi yàng wùpǐn dōu yǒu qí yòngtú.
    - Every object has its use.

### 食物 → 物 → 牛
- **definition:** cow
- **pinyin:** niú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the head and horns of an ox
  - **interpretation:** The character 牛 (niú, "ox") is a pictogram representing the head and horns of an ox, reflecting its importance in agriculture and daily life in ancient China.
- **examples:**
  - 农(農)民牵(牽)着(著)牛，去市场(場)买(買)东(東)西。
    - Nóngmín qiānzhe niú，qù shìchǎng mǎi dōngxī。
    - The farmer leads the ox and goes to the market to buy things.
  - 农(農)家养(養)牛，生活更加富足。
    - Nóngjiā yǎng niú，shēnghuó gèngjiā fùzú。
    - Rural families raise oxen, making their lives more prosperous.
  - 这(這)个(個)牛非常听(聽)话(話)。
    - Zhège niú fēicháng tīnghuà。
    - This ox is very obedient.

### 食物 → 物 → 勿
- **definition:** do not
- **pinyin:** wù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts blood on a knife ->
    - original character of 刎 (wěn, "to cut") ->
    - jiajie for 勿 (wù, "do not")
  - **interpretation:** The character 勿(wù), originally illustrated blood on a knife, visually represented the action of cutting, connecting to its initial meaning. Over time, it was borrowed to express prohibition or negation, showing the evolution beyond its pictorial roots.
- **examples:**
  - 进(進)入图(圖)书(書)馆(館)时(時)，请(請)勿大声(聲)喧哗(嘩)。
    - Jìnrù túshūguǎn shí, qǐng wù dàshēng xuānhuá.
    - When entering the library, please do not speak loudly.
  - 办(辦)公区(區)内(內)勿吸烟(煙)。
    - Bàngōng qū nèi wù xīyān.
    - Do not smoke in the office area.
  - 行路时(時)，勿追逐打闹(鬧)。
    - Xínglù shí, wù zhuīzhú dǎnào.
    - When walking, do not engage in chasing and rough play.
