## celebrate
### qìng
---
- **traditional:** 慶
- **definition:** celebrate
- **pinyin:** qìng
- **components:**
  - 廌
    - zhì
    - antelope
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 廌 (antelope) + 心 (heart) =
    - auspiciousness ->
    - celebration
  - **interpretation:** The character 慶 (qìng, 'celebrate') originally combines 廌 (zhì, 'antelope') and 心 (xīn, 'heart'). The antelope, due to its beauty, symbolizes something worth celebrating or a blessing when obtained, while the heart represents emotions like goodness. This combination conveys the idea of celebration through the joy and positivity associated with possessing something precious.
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 慶 was simplified to 庆 by eliminating the bottom right component and replacing it with 大 (dà, 'big').

### 庆(慶) → 廌
- **definition:** antelope
- **pinyin:** zhì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a deer-like mythical beast
  - **interpretation:** The character 廌 (zhì, "antelope") depicts a mythical creature resembling a deer, which symbolizes this ancient representation of antelope.
### 庆(慶) → 心
- **definition:** heart
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a heart
  - **interpretation:** The character 心(xīn, "heart") originally depicted the shape of an actual heart, representing thoughts, emotions, and the core of a person. Over time, it has become highly stylized, but it continues to convey the notion of the heart as the center of emotions and consciousness.
- **examples:**
