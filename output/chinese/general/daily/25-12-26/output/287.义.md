## surname Yi; righteousness
### Yì
---
- **traditional:** 義
- **definition:** surname Yi; righteousness
- **pinyin:** Yì
- **components:**
  - 我
    - wǒ
    - I
  - 羊
    - yáng
    - good; beautiful; auspicious
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 我 (wǒ, "I") + semantic 羊 (yáng, "good; beautiful; auspicious") ->
    - righteousness
  - **interpretation:** The character 義 combines the phonetic component 我 (wǒ, "I") with the semantic component 羊 (yáng, "good; beautiful; auspicious"). The 羊 component signifies qualities like goodness and beauty, indicating righteousness as something inherently valuable or noble. The pronunciation element 我 contributes the sound without semantic value in this context.
  - **simplification:** 义 is a simplified form of 義, where 羊 (yáng, "good") is removed, and 我 (wǒ, "I") is simplified to form a new character emphasizing ease of writing while retaining the general phonetic guide.

### 义(義) → 羊
- **definition:** good; beautiful; auspicious
- **pinyin:** yáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a ram's head
  - **interpretation:** The character 羊(yáng, 'sheep') visually represents the head of a sheep or ram, capturing the animal's distinctive features. It serves as a straightforward pictogram, portraying one of the common animals in ancient agrarian societies.
