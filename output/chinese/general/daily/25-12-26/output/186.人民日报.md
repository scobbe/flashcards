## People’s Daily
### Rénmín Rìbào
---
- **traditional:** 人民日報
- **definition:** People’s Daily
- **pinyin:** Rénmín Rìbào
- **components:**
  - 人民
    - rénmín
    - people; citizens
  - 日( )报(報)
    - rìbào

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 人民 (rénmín, "people"; "citizens") + 日報 (rìbào, "daily newspaper") =
    - People’s Daily, a newspaper meant for the general public
  - **interpretation:** 人民 (rénmín, "people"; "citizens") combines 人 (rén, "person") to emphasize individual members of society and 民 (mín, "people") referring to the masses, creating the collective idea of a populace or nation. 日報 (rìbào, "daily newspaper") combines 日 (rì, "day") indicating a daily occurrence and 報 (bào, "report"; "newspaper") to signify a daily publication of news and reports.

### 人( )民( )日( )报(報) → 日( )报(報) → 报(報)
- **traditional:** 報
- **definition:** report; newspaper
- **pinyin:** bào
- **components:**
  - 㚔
    - none
    - handcuffs
  - 𠬝
    - none
    - to subdue
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 㚔 (handcuffs) + 𠬝 (to subdue) =
    - to convict; to punish; to declare guilty
  - **interpretation:** The character 報 (bào, 'report; newspaper; recompense; respond') combines the imagery of handcuffs, symbolizing restraint, and an action of subduing, suggesting punishment or declaration of a verdict. This combination reflects the idea of delivering judgment or punishment, which extends metaphorically to mean 'reporting' or 'giving an account' of something. From delivering justice comes the idea of reciprocating actions, hence the sense of 'recompense' or 'respond'.
  - **simplification:** 报 is simplified from 報 by removing the 'handcuff' component 㚔, retaining the core representing the idea of reporting and responding without the punitive nuance.
