- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 龍 (lóng, "dragon") and 華 (huá, "splendor; China") suggest a name evoking grandeur and cultural richness.
  - **interpretation:** 龍 (lóng, "dragon") is often associated with power, authority, and good fortune, while 華 (huá, "splendor; China") conveys beauty and cultural brilliance. Together, 龍華 (Lónghuá) evokes an image of a culturally rich and prosperous place, which is fitting for areas named 龙(龍)华(華) in China, such as districts known for their historical or cultural significance.

- **examples:**
  - 龙(龍)华(華)闪(閃)耀着(著)古典魅力。

    - In Longhua, history and modern life intertwine.
### 龙(龍)华(華) → 龙(龍)
- **traditional:** 龍
- **definition:** dragon; imperial
- **pinyin:** lóng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted a serpent with a prominent whiskered mouth and eyes ->
    - large seal script developed into a dragon's body ->
    - simplified abstraction influenced by 立 (lì, "stand") and ⺼ (ròu, "flesh")
  - **interpretation:** The traditional character 龍(lóng, "dragon") began as a pictogram illustrating a serpent's key features, particularly its whiskered mouth and eyes, fundamental to the cultural depiction of dragons as mythical creatures with such traits. Despite changes and simplifications over time, this form has retained its original essence within the body and structural elements adapted over centuries, evidencing a complex fusion of visual components and cultural evolution.
  - **simplification:** The character 龙 is a simplified version of the traditional 龍, where the intricate elements were streamlined by removing sections like 帝 and simplifying 彡, reducing the complexity for easier writing.
- **examples:**
  - 龙(龍)是中国(國)文化的重要象征(徵)。
    - Lóng shì Zhōngguó wénhuà de zhòngyào xiàngzhēng.
    - The dragon is an important symbol in Chinese culture.
  - 舞龙(龍)是春节(節)的传(傳)统(統)活动(動)。
    - Wǔlóng shì Chūnjié de chuántǒng huódòng.
    - Dragon dance is a traditional activity during the Spring Festival.
  - 皇帝被称(稱)为(為)真龙(龍)天子。
    - Huángdì bèi chēng wéi zhēnlóng tiānzǐ.
    - The emperor was called the true dragon son of heaven.
### 龙(龍)华(華) → 华(華)
- **traditional:** 華
- **definition:** splendor; China
- **pinyin:** huá
- **components:**
  - 化
    - huà
    - to transform
  - 十
    - shí
    - stalk and leaves
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 化 (huà, "to transform") + semantic 十 (shí, "stalk and leaves") ->
    - 華 (huá, "magnificent")
  - **interpretation:** 華 (huá, "magnificent") combines 化 (huà, "to transform") providing the pronunciation, with 十 (shí, "stalk and leaves") symbolizing growth and abundance, resulting in the sense of magnificence and richness. This imagery naturally bridges to the idea of 'magnificent', depicting flourishing plants as a symbol of splendor, which extends to refer to China as a culturally rich and illustrious place.
  - **simplification:** 华(華): The top component of 華 was simplified by using 化 (huà, "to transform") in the simplified version 华, retaining phonetic resemblance while removing the detailed component 十.
- **examples:**
  - 中国(國)是一个(個)充满(滿)魅力的国(國)家。
    - Zhōngguó shì yīge chōngmǎn mèilì de guójiā.
    - China is a country full of charm.
  - 他的家在华(華)北平原。
    - Tā de jiā zài Huáběi píngyuán.
    - His home is on the North China Plain.
  - 这(這)座建筑(築)非常华(華)丽(麗)。
    - Zhè zuò jiànzhù fēicháng huálì.
    - This building is very magnificent.

### 龙(龍)华(華) → 华(華) → 十
- **definition:** stalk and leaves
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园(園)里(裡)有十棵树(樹)，景色十分优(優)美。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.
