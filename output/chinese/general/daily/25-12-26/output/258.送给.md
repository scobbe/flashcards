## give as a present; send to
### sònggěi
---
- **traditional:** 送給
- **definition:** give as a present; send to
- **pinyin:** sònggěi
- **components:**
  - 送
    - sòng
    - send; deliver; see off
  - 给(給)
    - gěi
    - give; for; to
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 送 (sòng, "send; deliver; see off") refers to the act of transferring or conveying something to another person. 給 (gěi, "give; for; to") involves bestowing or providing something to someone else.
  - **interpretation:** 送 (sòng, "send; deliver; see off") emphasizes the action of conveying or dispatching an item or person to a particular destination or recipient, which aligns with giving something. 給 (gěi, "give; for; to") adds the sense of transferring possession or making a gift to another person. Together, these components naturally combine to convey the idea of sending or giving something as a present.
- **examples:**
  - 他送给(給)我一本书(書)。
    - Tā sònggěi wǒ yī běn shū.
    - He gave me a book as a present.
  - 我想送给(給)她一朵玫瑰花。
    - Wǒ xiǎng sònggěi tā yī duǒ méiguī huā.
    - I want to give her a rose.
  - 过(過)生日时(時)，她送给(給)了我一个(個)惊(驚)喜。
    - Guò shēngrì shí, tā sònggěi le wǒ yī gè jīngxǐ.
    - On my birthday, she gave me a surprise.
