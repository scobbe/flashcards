## scrambled eggs
### chǎodàn
---
- **definition:** scrambled eggs
- **pinyin:** chǎodàn
- **components:**
  - 炒
    - chǎo
    - stir-fry; sauté
  - 蛋
    - dàn
    - egg; ovarium
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 炒 (chǎo, "stir-fry") refers to a method of cooking that involves frying food quickly in hot oil while stirring. 蛋 (dàn, "egg") is the common word for eggs. Together, 炒蛋 indicates a dish made by stir-frying eggs, resulting in scrambled eggs.
  - **interpretation:** 炒 (chǎo, "stir-fry") involves quickly cooking food in hot oil with continuous stirring, which changes the texture of the egg proteins and creates a fluffy consistency. 蛋 (dàn, "egg") is the base ingredient. The combination evokes the process of agitating eggs over high heat to produce the dish known as scrambled eggs.
- **examples:**
  - 早餐我常常吃炒蛋。
    - Zǎocān wǒ chángcháng chī chǎodàn.
    - I often eat scrambled eggs for breakfast.
  - 炒蛋是快手的美味早餐。
    - Chǎodàn shì kuàishǒu de měiwèi zǎocān.
    - Scrambled eggs are a quick and delicious breakfast.
  - 为(為)了( )增( )加( )味( )道( )，她( )在( )炒( )蛋( )里(裡)加( )了( )香( )葱(蔥)。
    - Wèile zēngjiā wèidào, tā zài chǎodàn lǐ jiāle xiāngcōng.
    - To enhance the flavor, she added green onions to the scrambled eggs.

  - **description:**

This reflects the later coinage of the character, indicating it wasn't part of very early Chinese script.

### 炒蛋 → 蛋 → 延 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').
