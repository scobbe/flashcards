## bottle opener
### kāipíngqì
---
- **traditional:** 開瓶器
- **definition:** bottle opener
- **pinyin:** kāipíngqì
- **components:**
  - 开(開)
    - kāi
    - open; start
  - 瓶
    - píng
    - bottle
  - 器
    - qì
    - tool; device; instrument
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 開 (kāi, "open") refers to the act of opening something, which is a core function of the tool in question. 瓶 (píng, "bottle") is the object specifically being opened. 器 (qì, "tool; device") signifies an instrument or implement used to carry out a task. Together, these characters convey the meaning of a device specifically designed for opening bottles.
  - **interpretation:** 開 (kāi, "open") describes the function of the tool, which involves removing a cap or seal. 瓶 (píng, "bottle") specifies the item to be opened, focusing the application of the tool. 器 (qì, "tool; device") denotes that it is a device crafted for such a purpose. Together, they imply a functional device for the specific action of opening bottles.
- **examples:**
  - 我需要一个(個)开(開)瓶器。
    - Wǒ xūyào yī gè kāipíngqì.
    - I need a bottle opener.
  - 厨(廚)房抽屉(屜)里(裡)有开(開)瓶器。
    - Chúfáng chōutì lǐ yǒu kāipíngqì.
    - There is a bottle opener in the kitchen drawer.
  - 她用开(開)瓶器打开(開)了饮(飲)料。
    - Tā yòng kāipíngqì dǎkāi le yǐnliào.
    - She opened the drink with a bottle opener.
### 开(開)瓶器 → 开(開)
- **traditional:** 開
- **definition:** open; start
- **pinyin:** kāi
- **components:**
  - 門
    - mén
    - door
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; latch
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 門 (mén, "door") + 一 (latch/pole) + 廾 (gǒng, "two hands") ->
    - hands opening a latched door ->
    - 開
  - **interpretation:** The character 開 (kāi, "open") visually represents the action of opening a door with hands, symbolized by combining the door 門 with the latch 一 and two hands 廾. This conveys the core idea of opening something secured by a latch.
  - **simplification:** 开 is a visual and structural shorthand of 開, created by merging 一 and 廾 into a simplified form, while retaining the reference to opening a door.
- **examples:**
  - 老师(師)在上课(課)时(時)总(總)是很开(開)心。
    - Lǎoshī zài shàngkè shí zǒng shì hěn kāixīn.
    - The teacher is always happy during class.
  - 我们(們)要早上开(開)会(會)，讨(討)论(論)计(計)划(劃)。
    - Wǒmen yào zǎoshang kāihuì, tǎolùn jìhuà.
    - We need to have a meeting in the morning to discuss the plan.
  - 请(請)把门(門)打开(開)，让(讓)我进(進)去。
    - Qǐng bǎ mén dǎkāi, ràng wǒ jìnqù.
    - Please open the door and let me in.

### 开(開)瓶器 → 瓶
- **definition:** bottle
- **pinyin:** píng
- **components:**
  - 并(並)
    - bìng
    - combine
  - 瓦
    - wǎ
    - earthenware
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 并(並) (bìng, "combine") + Semantic: 瓦 (wǎ, "earthenware") =
    - initially an earthenware vessel ->
    - now any kind of bottle
  - **interpretation:** The character 瓶(píng) represents a vessel made of clay due to the 瓦(wǎ, "earthenware") component, while 并(並) (bìng, "combine") likely influenced the pronunciation. Initially referring to clay vessels, it now broadly indicates bottles in modern usage.
- **examples:**
  - 我买(買)了一瓶水。
    - Wǒ mǎile yī píng shuǐ.
    - I bought a bottle of water.
  - 厨(廚)房里(裏)摆(擺)着(著)一瓶橄榄(欖)油，餐桌上也放着(著)一瓶苹(蘋)果醋。
    - Chúfáng lǐ bǎizhe yī píng gǎnlǎn yóu, cānzhuō shàng yě fàngzhe yī píng píngguǒ cù.
    - There is a bottle of olive oil in the kitchen, and there is also a bottle of apple cider on the dining table.
### 开(開)瓶器 → 瓶 → 并(並)
- **traditional:** 並
- **definition:** combine
- **pinyin:** bìng
- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

  - **simplification:** The character 并 simplifies 並 by reducing the repetition of strokes representing two people to a single form, conveying the same meaning through a straightforward design.
- **examples:**
