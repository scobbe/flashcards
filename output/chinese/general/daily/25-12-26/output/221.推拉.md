## push and pull
### tuīlā
---
- **definition:** push and pull
- **pinyin:** tuīlā
- **components:**
  - 推
    - tuī
    - push; shove
  - 拉
    - lā
    - pull; drag; draw
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 推 (tuī, "push") and 拉 (lā, "pull") are combined directly to express the reciprocal actions of physically exerting force on an object away from and towards a person or center, respectively.
  - **interpretation:** 推 (tuī, "push") involves exerting force to move something away, while 拉 (lā, "pull") involves drawing something closer by force. In combination, 推拉 describes the dual physical actions or motions often encountered in daily activities, such as opening doors or operating machinery that requires both pushing and pulling actions.
- **examples:**
  - 推( )拉( )门(門)很( )方( )便( )。
    - Tuīlā mén hěn fāngbiàn.
    - Sliding doors are very convenient.

    - Qǐng wù yòng lì tuīlā zhè shàn mén.

  - 这(這)个(個)游(遊)戏(戲)需( )要( )团(團)队(隊)的( )推( )拉( )合( )作( )。
