## ocean; foreign
### yáng
---
- **definition:** ocean; foreign
- **pinyin:** yáng
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 羊
    - yáng
    - sheep
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic 羊 (yáng) ->
    - meaning
  - **interpretation:** The character 洋(yáng, "ocean; foreign") combines 氵(shuǐ, "water") as the semantic component, indicating a connection to water or large bodies of water such as the ocean. The phonetic component 羊(yáng) lends its sound to the character. The expansive and vast nature of the ocean extends to the concept of 'foreign', mirroring the idea of distant or overseas places.

### 洋 → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flowing water
  - **interpretation:** 水 (shuǐ, 'water') is a pictogram that directly represents the appearance of flowing water, making it intuitive and easy to recognize. Its simplicity and direct visual reference to its meaning have allowed it to remain unchanged over millennia.

### 洋 → 羊
- **definition:** sheep
- **pinyin:** yáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a ram's head
  - **interpretation:** The character 羊(yáng, 'sheep') visually represents the head of a sheep or ram, capturing the animal's distinctive features. It serves as a straightforward pictogram, portraying one of the common animals in ancient agrarian societies.
