### 懿 → 心
- **definition:** heart
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a heart
  - **interpretation:** The character 心(xīn, "heart") originally depicted the shape of an actual heart, representing thoughts, emotions, and the core of a person. Over time, it has become highly stylized, but it continues to convey the notion of the heart as the center of emotions and consciousness.
- **examples:**
  - 她的心一直都在帮(幫)助别(別)人。
    - Tā de xīn yīzhí dōu zài bāngzhù biérén.
    - Her heart is always in helping others.
  - 在城市的中心，有一个(個)美丽(麗)的公园(園)。
    - Zài chéngshì de zhōngxīn, yǒu yí gè měilì de gōngyuán.
    - At the center of the city, there is a beautiful park.
  - 对(對)于(於)许(許)多人来(來)说(說)，家庭是生活的核心。
    - Duìyú xǔduō rén lái shuō, jiātíng shì shēnghuó de héxīn.
    - For many people, family is the core of life.

### 懿 → 壹
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **components:**
  - 壺
    - hú
    - jar
  - 吉
    - jí
    - lucky; auspicious
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 壺 (hú, "jar") + phonetic 吉 (jí, "lucky; auspicious") ->
    - a complete or whole item ->
    - reinforced meaning of "one"
  - **interpretation:** 壹 (yī, "one") uses 壺 (hú, "jar") as the semantic component suggesting entirety or completeness, while 吉 (jí, "lucky; auspicious") provides the phonetic element. This character emphasizes a complete or singular unit, unlike 一 (yī, "one"). 壹 often appears in formal contexts to prevent alterations, such as in checks and legal documents.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天只喝壹杯茶。
    - Tā měitiān zhǐ hē yī bēi chá.
    - He only drinks one cup of tea every day.
  - 他用壹只(隻)手就能提起那包东(東)西。
    - Tā yòng yī zhī shǒu jiù néng tí qǐ nà bāo dōngxī.
    - He can lift that bag with one hand.
  - 壹个(個)人守着(著)壹座空房子，感觉(覺)十分孤独(獨)。
    - Yī gè rén shǒuzhe yī zuò kōng fángzi, gǎnjué shífēn gūdú.
    - Guarding an empty house alone feels very lonely.

### 懿 → 壹 → 壺 → 壴
- **definition:** drum
- **pinyin:** zhù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a drum 壴, with a base and feathers 土 on top ->
    - stylized in the Kangxi radical 鼓
  - **interpretation:** The character 壴 (zhù, "drum") visually represents a drum with two ends, with elements contributing to its structure, such as a base and feathers. It was later incorporated into characters like 鼓 (gǔ, "drum") by adding elements that illustrate additional actions like hands holding a drumstick.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)面壴声(聲)震天动(動)地。
    - Zhè miàn zhù shēng zhèn tiān dòng dì.
    - The sound of this drum shakes the heavens and the earth.
  - 战(戰)士敲响(響)壴，示警敌(敵)人。
    - Zhànshì qiāo xiǎng zhù, shì jǐng dírén.
    - The soldier beats the drum to warn the enemy.
  - 庄(莊)严(嚴)仪(儀)式上，祭司击(擊)打壴，宣告传(傳)统(統)。
    - Zhuāngyán yíshì shàng, jìsī jī dǎ zhù, xuānguò chuántǒng.

### 懿 → 壹 → 壺 → 冏
- **definition:** an ancient phonetic component
- **pinyin:** jiǒng
- **components:**
  - 穴
    - xuè
    - cave
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Hut (stylized as 穴) + mouth 口 =
    - window opening
  - **interpretation:** The character 冏 (jiǒng) originally depicted a structure resembling a hut with an opening. This design, where the hut is stylized as 穴 (xuè, "cave") and the central 口 (kǒu, "mouth") suggests an opening, led to the concept of a window or ventilation. Historically, its usage broadened to serve as a phonetic component in other characters.
### 懿 → 壹 → 壺 → 冏 → 穴
- **definition:** cave

- **examples:**
  - 山里(裡)隐(隱)匿着(著)一个(個)深深的穴。

    - The explorer discovered a quiet cave beneath the cliff, and it is said that ancient spirits dwell inside.
  - 河岸边(邊)的岩石上有一个(個)小穴。

### 懿 → 壹 → 壺 → 冏 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

### 懿 → 壹 → 吉
- **definition:** lucky; auspicious
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 士
    - shì
    - scholar
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 士 (shì, "scholar") (ritual axe) + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") ->
    - representation of rituals to acquire favor ->
    - auspiciousness
  - **interpretation:** The character 吉(jí) combines 士 (shì, "scholar") symbolizing a ritual axe and 口 (kǒu, "mouth"), suggesting ancient rituals for gaining ancestral or divine blessings. This association leads to the meaning of 'auspicious' or 'lucky'.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是吉日，适(適)合举(舉)办(辦)婚礼(禮)。
    - Jīntiān shì jírì, shìhé jǔbàn hūnlǐ.
    - Today is an auspicious day, suitable for holding a wedding.
  - 他抽到吉牌。
    - Tā chōu dào jí pái.
    - He drew a lucky card.
  - 这(這)次考试(試)结(結)果吉利，大家都感到欣慰。
    - Zhè cì kǎoshì jiéguǒ jílì, dàjiā dōu gǎndào xīnwèi.
    - The results of this exam were auspicious, and everyone felt relieved.

### 懿 → 壹 → 吉 → 士
- **definition:** scholar
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a ritual axe, possibly made of jade ->
    - symbolized soldier ->
    - intellectual or officer over time
  - **interpretation:** The character 士(shì, 'scholar') originally depicted a ritual axe used in ceremonies, implying a person of a certain rank or status who could wield such symbols. This connection to ceremonial authority evolved to mean 'soldier' and later 'intellectual' or 'officer', roles associated with leadership and knowledge in societal hierarchies.
- **examples:**
  - 历(歷)史上，士被视(視)为(為)一种(種)权(權)力的象征(徵)。
    - Lìshǐ shàng, shì bèi shì wéi yī zhǒng quánlì de xiàngzhēng.
    - Historically, the 'shi' was seen as a symbol of power.
  - 在古代，只有贵(貴)族才拥(擁)有士。
    - Zài gǔdài, zhǐyǒu guìzú cái yǒngyǒu shì.
    - In ancient times, only nobles possessed the 'shi'.
  - 士的制(製)作材料可能是珍贵(貴)的金属(屬)。
    - Shì de zhìzuò cáiliào kěnéng shì zhēnguì de jīnshǔ.
    - The materials used to make the 'shi' might have been precious metals.
