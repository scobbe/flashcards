## call; shout
### jiào
---
- **definition:** call; shout
- **pinyin:** jiào
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 丩
    - jiū
    - hook
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic 丩 ->
    - shout
  - **interpretation:** The mouth 口 (kǒu, "mouth") indicates an action related to speaking or making noise, while the phonetic 丩 suggests the sound of shouting or calling. Together, they convey the concept of calling out or shouting.
- **examples:**
  - 他叫了一声(聲)，但没(沒)有人回答。
    - Tā jiào le yī shēng, dàn méiyǒu rén huídá.
    - He shouted, but no one answered.
  - 我叫他来(來)帮(幫)忙，他马(馬)上就答应(應)了。
    - Wǒ jiào tā lái bāngmáng, tā mǎshàng jiù dāyìng le.
    - I called him to help, and he agreed immediately.
  - 医(醫)生叫我每天多喝水。
    - Yīshēng jiào wǒ měitiān duō hē shuǐ.
    - The doctor told me to drink more water every day.

### 叫 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

### 叫 → 丩
- **definition:** hook
- **pinyin:** jiū
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts two entangled ropes ->
    - to entangle; to intertwine
  - **interpretation:** The character 丩 (jiū, "twist") depicts two ropes twisted together, symbolizing the concept of entangling or intertwining.
