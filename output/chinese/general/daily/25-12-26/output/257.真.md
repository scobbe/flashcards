## true; real
### zhēn
---
- **definition:** true; real
- **pinyin:** zhēn
- **components:**
  - 𠂈
    - lǘ
    - knife; dagger
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 丁
    - dīng
    - nail
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 𠂈 (knife/dagger, phonetic) + 貝 (cowry, semantic) ->
    - phonetic 丁 (nail) added ->
    - phonetic 𠂈 corrupted to 十 ->
    - final form
  - **interpretation:** The character 真 (zhēn, "true; real") originally involved 𠂈 as both phonetic and visual representation of a knife or dagger, with 貝 (bèi, "cowry") providing semantic context, presumably indicating value or authenticity. Over time, 丁 (dīng, "nail") was added as a secondary phonetic element, reflecting the joining or fixing aspect of 'true'. As forms evolved into script styles, 𠂈's shape was corrupted into 十, simplifying the visual complexity, while retaining the meaning of 'true' and 'real'.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)是真正的友情。
    - Zhè shì zhēnzhèng de yǒuqíng.
    - This is true friendship.
  - 他说(說)的话(話)句句都是真的。
    - Tā shuō de huà jùjù dōu shì zhēn de.
    - Everything he said is true.
  - 世界上的每一个(個)事物都是真实(實)的。
    - Shìjiè shàng de měi yī gè shìwù dōu shì zhēnshí de.
    - Everything in the world is real.

### 真 → 𠂈
- **definition:** knife; dagger
- **pinyin:** lǘ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** variant form
  - **description:** Variant form of 𠄍 (knife)
  - **interpretation:** This character is a variant form that closely resembles 𠄍, which was used as a pictogram for a traditional knife or cutting tool.
### 真 → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为(為)货(貨)币(幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
### 真 → 丁
- **definition:** nail
- **pinyin:** dīng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts the head of a nail ->
    - 丁
  - **interpretation:** The character 丁 originally depicted the head of a nail, emphasizing simplicity and directness. While it has developed other interpretations over time, such as a head or city wall, these relate to similar shapes and functions. Today, 釘 (dīng, 'nail') reflects this meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 工人熟练(練)地把丁敲进(進)了墙(牆)壁。
    - Gōngrén shúliàn de bǎ dīng qiāng jìnle qiángbì.
    - The worker skillfully hammered the nail into the wall.
  - 他精心挑选(選)了铁(鐵)质(質)丁，确(確)保家(傢)具(俱)稳(穩)固。
    - Tā jīngxīn tiāoxuǎnle tiě zhì dīng, quèbǎo jiājù wěngù.
    - He carefully selected the iron nail to ensure the furniture was stable.
  - 请(請)把散落的丁收好。
    - Qǐng bǎ sànluò de dīng shōuhǎo.
    - Please gather the scattered nails.
