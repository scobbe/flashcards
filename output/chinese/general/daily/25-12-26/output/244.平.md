## surname Ping
### Píng
---
- **definition:** surname Ping
- **pinyin:** Píng
- **components:**
  - 釆
    - biàn
    - distinguish
  - 二
    - èr
    - two
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 釆 (biàn) + semantic 二 (èr, "two equal lines") ->
    - flatness, equality
  - **interpretation:** The composition combines 釆 (biàn) serving as a phonetic component, with 二 (èr, "two equal lines") providing the idea of equality or flatness. Together, they represent the concept of something being flat or level, as with a balanced surface or peaceful state where everything is even and steady.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)桌面是平的。
    - Zhège zhuōmiàn shì píng de.
    - This tabletop is flat.
  - 我们(們)的关(關)系(係)一直很平和。
    - Wǒmen de guānxì yīzhí hěn pínghé.
    - Our relationship has always been peaceful.
  - 每天晚上我都享受平静(靜)的时(時)光。
    - Měitiān wǎnshàng wǒ dū xiǎngshòu píngjìng de shíguāng.
    - Every evening I enjoy peaceful times.

### 平 → 二
- **definition:** two
- **pinyin:** èr
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts two parallel strokes ->
    - represents the number two
  - **interpretation:** The character 二 (èr, "two") consists of two horizontal lines that visually represent the concept of 'two'. This method of using parallel strokes to denote numbers is a straightforward representation of quantity, similar to how 一 (yī, "one") represents 'one' and 三 (sān, "three") represents 'three'.
- **examples:**
  - 我有二支笔(筆)。
    - Wǒ yǒu èr zhī bǐ.
    - I have two pens.
  - 他买(買)了二个(個)苹(蘋)果，我们(們)一起分享。
    - Tā mǎi le èr gè píngguǒ, wǒmen yīqǐ fēnxiǎng.
    - He bought two apples, and we shared them together.
