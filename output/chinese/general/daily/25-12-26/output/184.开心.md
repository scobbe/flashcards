## happy
### kāixīn
---
- **traditional:** 開心
- **definition:** happy
- **pinyin:** kāixīn
- **components:**
  - 开(開)
    - kāi
    - open; start
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart; mind
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 開(开)心 comprises 開(开) (kāi, "open") and 心 (xīn, "heart"). The combination suggests opening one's heart, allowing happiness and joy to enter.
  - **interpretation:** The term 開(开)心 evokes the image of opening one's heart to let in happiness and positive emotions. It is a vivid way of expressing the state of feeling happy or joyous.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天感觉(覺)很开(開)心。
    - Wǒ jīntiān gǎnjué hěn kāixīn.
    - I feel happy today.
  - 他听(聽)到好消息，变(變)得异(異)常开(開)心。
    - Tā tīngdào hǎo xiāoxī, biàn de yìcháng kāixīn.
    - He became extremely happy after hearing the good news.
  - 朋友聚会(會)时(時)，大家都很开(開)心。
    - Péngyǒu jùhuì shí, dàjiā dōu hěn kāixīn.
    - Everyone is happy during the friends' gathering.
### 开(開)心 → 开(開)
- **traditional:** 開
- **definition:** open; start
- **pinyin:** kāi
- **components:**
  - 門
    - mén
    - door
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; latch
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 門 (mén, "door") + 一 (latch/pole) + 廾 (gǒng, "two hands") ->
    - hands opening a latched door ->
    - 開
  - **interpretation:** The character 開 (kāi, "open") visually represents the action of opening a door with hands, symbolized by combining the door 門 with the latch 一 and two hands 廾. This conveys the core idea of opening something secured by a latch.
  - **simplification:** 开 is a visual and structural shorthand of 開, created by merging 一 and 廾 into a simplified form, while retaining the reference to opening a door.
- **examples:**
  - 老师(師)在上课(課)时(時)总(總)是很开(開)心。
    - Lǎoshī zài shàngkè shí zǒng shì hěn kāixīn.
    - The teacher is always happy during class.
  - 我们(們)要早上开(開)会(會)，讨(討)论(論)计(計)划(劃)。
    - Wǒmen yào zǎoshang kāihuì, tǎolùn jìhuà.
    - We need to have a meeting in the morning to discuss the plan.
  - 请(請)把门(門)打开(開)，让(讓)我进(進)去。
    - Qǐng bǎ mén dǎkāi, ràng wǒ jìnqù.
    - Please open the door and let me in.
### 开(開)心 → 开(開) → 門
- **definition:** door
- **pinyin:** mén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a double-door gate
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts a pair of doors, visually representing the concept of a door or gate, which is fundamental in indicating entrance and exit points in structures.
- **examples:**
  - 我走到门(門)口。
    - Wǒ zǒu dào ménkǒu.
    - I walk to the door.
  - 门(門)轻(輕)轻(輕)打开(開)，房客进(進)来(來)了。
    - Mén qīngqīng dǎkāi, fángkè jìn láile.
    - The door opened gently, and the tenant came in.

### 开(開)心 → 开(開) → 一

- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**

  - **description:** A horizontal stroke representing the concept of 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** The character 一(yī, "one") is a simple horizontal line signifying the singular and unified concept of 'one'. It directly represents being singular or the number one.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一个(個)苹(蘋)果。
    - Wǒ yǒu yī gè píngguǒ.
    - I have one apple.

  - 一条(條)直路通向远(遠)方。
    - Yī tiáo zhí lù tōng xiàng yuǎn fāng.

### 开(開)心 → 心
- **definition:** heart; mind
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a heart
  - **interpretation:** The character 心(xīn, "heart") originally depicted the shape of an actual heart, representing thoughts, emotions, and the core of a person. Over time, it has become highly stylized, but it continues to convey the notion of the heart as the center of emotions and consciousness.
- **examples:**
  - 她的心一直都在帮(幫)助别(別)人。
    - Tā de xīn yīzhí dōu zài bāngzhù biérén.
    - Her heart is always in helping others.
  - 在城市的中心，有一个(個)美丽(麗)的公园(園)。
    - Zài chéngshì de zhōngxīn, yǒu yí gè měilì de gōngyuán.
    - At the center of the city, there is a beautiful park.
  - 对(對)于(於)许(許)多人来(來)说(說)，家庭是生活的核心。
    - Duìyú xǔduō rén lái shuō, jiātíng shì shēnghuó de héxīn.
    - For many people, family is the core of life.
