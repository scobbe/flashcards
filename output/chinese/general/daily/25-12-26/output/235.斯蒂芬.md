---
- **definition:** Stephen
- **pinyin:** Sīdìfēn
- **components:**
  - 斯
    - sī
    - this
  - 蒂
    - dì
    - stem
  - 芬
    - fēn
    - perfume; fragrance
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 斯 (sī, "this") is often used as a phonetic representation in transliterated names. 蒂 (dì, "stem") and 芬 (fēn, "perfume; fragrance") are also used for phonetic matching in transliterations, rather than their literal meanings.
  - **interpretation:** 斯蒂芬 is a transliteration of the Western name 'Stephen.' The choice of characters is based on their phonetic similarity to the original name, rather than their individual meanings.

### 斯蒂芬 → 斯 → 其

  - 丌

  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a knife
  - **interpretation:** 刀 (dāo, "knife") is a pictographic representation of a knife or blade, with a sharp edge and a handle, resembling the form of a basic cutting tool.
- **examples:**
  - 他用锋(鋒)利的刀，切开(開)蔬菜。
    - tā yòng fēnglì de dāo, qiē kāi shūcài.
    - He uses a sharp knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这(這)把刀非常锋(鋒)利。
    - zhè bǎ dāo fēicháng fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
  - 厨(廚)师(師)小心地持刀，为(為)客人切肉。
    - chúshī xiǎoxīn de chí dāo, wèi kèrén qiē ròu.
    - The chef holds the knife carefully and slices meat for the guest.
