## Kaifeng
### Kāifēng
---
- **traditional:** 開封
- **definition:** Kaifeng
- **pinyin:** Kāifēng
- **components:**
  - 开(開)
    - kāi
    - open; begin; establish; operate
  - 封
    - fēng
    - seal; envelope; confer; bestow
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 開 (kāi, "open") + 封 (fēng, "seal; envelope") =
    - a place name; historically, the city was originally 'opened' or established by breaking a seal or barrier.
  - **interpretation:** 開 (kāi, "open") suggests the initiation or establishment of something new, like breaking open a seal. 封 (fēng, "seal; envelope") typically means to close or seal something, but in combination with 開 (kāi, "open"), it suggests the act of breaking through or opening a seal. Historically, this refers to the establishment of the city, metaphorically as opening the sealed land for settlement.
- **examples:**
  - 开(開)封是一个(個)历(歷)史悠久的城市。
    - Kāifēng shì yī gè lìshǐ yōujiǔ de chéngshì.
    - Kaifeng is a city with a long history.
  - 我计(計)划(劃)去开(開)封旅行。
    - Wǒ jìhuà qù Kāifēng lǚxíng.
    - I plan to travel to Kaifeng.
  - 在开(開)封，你可以看到许(許)多古老的建筑(築)。
    - Zài Kāifēng, nǐ kěyǐ kàn dào xǔduō gǔlǎo de jiànzhú.
    - In Kaifeng, you can see many ancient buildings.
### 开(開)封 → 开(開)
- **traditional:** 開
- **definition:** open; begin; establish; operate
- **pinyin:** kāi
- **components:**
  - 門
    - mén
    - door
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; latch
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 門 (mén, "door") + 一 (latch/pole) + 廾 (gǒng, "two hands") ->
    - hands opening a latched door ->
    - 開
  - **interpretation:** The character 開 (kāi, "open") visually represents the action of opening a door with hands, symbolized by combining the door 門 with the latch 一 and two hands 廾. This conveys the core idea of opening something secured by a latch.
  - **simplification:** 开 is a visual and structural shorthand of 開, created by merging 一 and 廾 into a simplified form, while retaining the reference to opening a door.
- **examples:**
  - 老师(師)在上课(課)时(時)总(總)是很开(開)心。
    - Lǎoshī zài shàngkè shí zǒng shì hěn kāixīn.
    - The teacher is always happy during class.
  - 我们(們)要早上开(開)会(會)，讨(討)论(論)计(計)划(劃)。
    - Wǒmen yào zǎoshang kāihuì, tǎolùn jìhuà.
    - We need to have a meeting in the morning to discuss the plan.
  - 请(請)把门(門)打开(開)，让(讓)我进(進)去。
    - Qǐng bǎ mén dǎkāi, ràng wǒ jìnqù.
    - Please open the door and let me in.

### 开(開)封 → 封
- **definition:** seal; envelope; confer; bestow
- **pinyin:** fēng
- **components:**
  - 土
    - tǔ
    - dirt
  - 豐
    - fēng
    - seedling
  - 寸
    - cùn
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 土 (tǔ, "dirt") + phonetic 丰(豐) (fēng, "seedling") + semantic 寸 (cùn, "hand") ->
    - marking boundaries with seedlings ->
    - seal; close
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 丰(豐) (fēng, "seedling") as a phonetic element suggesting the sound, and 土 (tǔ, "dirt") and 寸 (cùn, "hand") as semantic elements. The combination symbolizes marking a boundary by planting seedlings in the ground, which is a way of sealing or enclosing an area. This concept naturally extends to sealing or closing something.
- **examples:**
  - 他亲(親)手封好信封。
    - Tā qīnshǒu fēng hǎo xìnfēng.
    - He personally sealed the envelope.
  - 我们(們)需要把窗户(戶)封住，以防寒风(風)进(進)入。
    - Wǒmen xūyào bǎ chuānghù fēng zhù, yǐ fáng hánfēng jìnrù.
    - We need to seal the windows to prevent the cold wind from entering.
  - 信件已经(經)被谨(謹)慎地密封。
    - Xìnjiàn yǐjīng bèi jǐnshèn de mìfēng.
    - The letter has been carefully sealed.

### 开(開)封 → 封 → 豐 → 豆
- **definition:** pot
- **pinyin:** dòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a container ->
    - borrowed phonetically for the plant name
  - **interpretation:** The character 豆(dòu, 'drum') originally depicted a type of container. ### 开(開)封 → 封 → 寸
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Line on the hand (ideographic) emphasizing the elbow ->
    - represents measurement unit between wrist and specific forearm position ->
    - meaning 'inch'
  - **interpretation:** 寸 (cùn, "inch") originated as a depiction of a line on the hand, indicating the area near the elbow. This specific placement was considered significant for pulse palpation, originally suggesting a forearm position, which then came to denote the measurement 'inch'.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)块(塊)布每一寸都是精心制(製)作。
    - Zhè kuài bù měi yī cùn dōu shì jīngxīn zhìzuò.

    - Qǐng yòng juǎnchǐ liàng yī cùn, měi yī cùn shùjù dōu zhǔnquè.
