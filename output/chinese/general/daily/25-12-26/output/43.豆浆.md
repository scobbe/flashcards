## soy milk
### dòujiāng
---
- **traditional:** 豆漿
- **definition:** soy milk
- **pinyin:** dòujiāng
- **components:**
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean
  - 浆(漿)
    - jiāng
    - thick liquid; starch; broth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 豆 (dòu, "bean") + 浆(漿) (jiāng, "thick liquid") =
    - 豆浆(漿) (dòujiāng, "soy milk") refers to the thick liquid made from soybeans.
  - **interpretation:** This combination makes sense as 豆 (dòu, "bean") indicates the main ingredient, soybeans, and 浆(漿) (jiāng, "thick liquid") describes its liquid form. Together, they precisely describe soy milk, a popular beverage made by soaking and grinding soybeans.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天早晨都喝豆浆(漿)。
    - Wǒ měitiān zǎochén dōu hē dòujiāng.
    - I drink soy milk every morning.
  - 早餐配豆浆(漿)，让(讓)身体(體)充满(滿)活力。
    - Zǎocān pèi dòujiāng, ràng shēntǐ chōngmǎn huólì.
    - Having soy milk with breakfast energizes the body.
  - 豆浆(漿)营(營)养(養)丰(豐)富。
    - Dòujiāng yíngyǎng fēngfù.
    - Soy milk is rich in nutrients.
### 豆浆(漿) → 豆
- **definition:** bean
- **pinyin:** dòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a container ->
    - borrowed phonetically for the plant name
  - **interpretation:** The character 豆(dòu, 'drum') originally depicted a type of container. It was later borrowed for its phonetic value to represent the plant name 'bean', distinguishing it from a container. This transition indicates how the physical shape of the container, resembling a drum or storage pot, facilitated a phonetic borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)在课(課)堂上学(學)习(習)如何制(製)作豆。
    - Wǒmen zài kètáng shàng xuéxí rúhé zhìzuò dòu.
    - We are learning how to make drums in class.
  - 村庄(莊)里(裡)有一个(個)古老的豆，它的声(聲)音很有魅力。
    - Cūnzhuāng lǐ yǒu yí gè gǔlǎo de dòu, tā de shēngyīn hěn yǒu mèilì.
    - There is an ancient drum in the village, and its sound is captivating.
  - 音乐(樂)会(會)以豆声(聲)开(開)场(場)，让(讓)观(觀)众(眾)立刻沉(沈)浸在气(氣)氛中。
    - Yīnyuèhuì yǐ dòu shēng kāichǎng, ràng guānzhòng lìkè chénjìn zài qìfēn zhōng.
    - The concert opened with the sound of drums, immersing the audience in the atmosphere immediately.

### 豆浆(漿) → 浆(漿)
- **traditional:** 漿
- **definition:** thick liquid; starch; broth
- **pinyin:** jiāng
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 將
    - jiāng
    - take; to lead
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 水 (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic 將 (jiāng) ->
    - thick liquid such as starch, broth
  - **interpretation:** In 漿, the semantic component 水 (shuǐ, "water") indicates a liquid, while the phonetic component 將 (jiāng) suggests the pronunciation. This combination implies a thick or substantial liquid, leading to meanings like starch or broth, where a liquid base is thickened with other substances.
  - **simplification:** The traditional form 漿 was simplified by reducing the component 水 to the simpler form 氵.
- **examples:**
  - 早上我喝了一杯豆浆(漿)。
    - Zǎoshang wǒ hē le yī bēi dòujiāng.
    - In the morning, I drank a cup of soybean milk.
  - 粘稠的米浆(漿)非常适(適)合这(這)个(個)菜。
    - Zhānchóu de mǐjiāng fēicháng shìhé zhège cài.
    - The thick rice paste is perfect for this dish.
  - 她喜欢(歡)用玉米浆(漿)做汤(湯)。
    - Tā xǐhuān yòng yùmǐjiāng zuò tāng.
    - She likes to use corn broth to make soup.
### 豆浆(漿) → 浆(漿) → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flowing water
  - **interpretation:** 水 (shuǐ, 'water') is a pictogram that directly represents the appearance of flowing water, making it intuitive and easy to recognize. Its simplicity and direct visual reference to its meaning have allowed it to remain unchanged over millennia.
- **examples:**

### 豆浆(漿) → 浆(漿) → 將
- **definition:** take; to lead
- **pinyin:** jiāng
- **components:**
  - 爿
    - pán
    - bed; small table
  - ⺼
    - ròu
    - meat
  - 寸
    - cùn
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 爿 + semantic ⺼ + semantic 寸 ->
    - offering meat as tribute
  - **interpretation:** The character 將 (jiāng, "lead") combines the phonetic component 爿 ("bed; small table") with semantic elements ⺼ ("meat") and 寸 ("hand"). This configuration suggests the concept of offering meat on a table, with a hand indicating the action of presenting or leading in a ritual context. Over time, this notion of directing or leading became associated with the act of guiding or commanding.
  - **simplification:** 将(將): The simplified form removes the components ⺼ ("meat") and 寸 ("hand"), merging into a single simplified character while maintaining the phonetic similarity through the shape resemblance.
- **examples:**
  - 他将(將)成为(為)一个(個)伟(偉)大的领(領)袖。
    - Tā jiāng chéngwéi yīgè wěidà de lǐngxiù.
    - He will become a great leader.
  - 这(這)次胜(勝)利将(將)再次激励(勵)我们(們)。
    - Zhè cì shènglì jiāng zàicì jīlì wǒmen.
    - This victory will inspire us again.
  - 我们(們)将(將)来(來)可能会(會)遇到挑战(戰)。
    - Wǒmen jiānglái kěnéng huì yùdào tiǎozhàn.
    - We may encounter challenges in the future.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a bed ->
    - original form of 牀 (chuáng, "bed") ->
    - contracted form in 臧 (zāng, "possess")
  - **interpretation:** The character 爿 (pán, "bed") visually represents a bed, emphasizing its usage in denoting places for resting or sleeping. It served as the original form for the character 牀 (chuáng, "bed"), reinforcing its meaning. Additionally, in the character 臧 (zāng, "possess"), it appears as a contracted form, reflecting its historical usage in composite forms.

### 豆浆(漿) → 浆(漿) → 將 → 寸
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Line on the hand (ideographic) emphasizing the elbow ->
    - represents measurement unit between wrist and specific forearm position ->
    - meaning 'inch'
  - **interpretation:** 寸 (cùn, "inch") originated as a depiction of a line on the hand, indicating the area near the elbow. This specific placement was considered significant for pulse palpation, originally suggesting a forearm position, which then came to denote the measurement 'inch'.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)块(塊)布每一寸都是精心制(製)作。
    - Zhè kuài bù měi yī cùn dōu shì jīngxīn zhìzuò.
    - Every inch of this cloth is crafted with care.
  - 请(請)用卷尺量一寸，每一寸数(數)据(據)都准(準)确(確)。
    - Qǐng yòng juǎnchǐ liàng yī cùn, měi yī cùn shùjù dōu zhǔnquè.
    - Please use a tape measure to measure an inch, every inch's data is accurate.
