## the Bund
### wàitān
---
- **traditional:** 外灘
- **definition:** the Bund
- **pinyin:** wàitān
- **components:**
  - 外
    - wài
    - outside
  - 滩(灘)
    - tān
    - beach; sandbank; shoal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 外 (wài, "outside") + 滩(灘) (tān, "beach") =
    - an area outside the regular land, associated with a waterfront ->
    - the Bund (a famous waterfront area in Shanghai).
  - **interpretation:** 外 (wài, "outside") indicates something beyond the usual boundary, in this case beyond the city's typical inland areas. 滩(灘) (tān, "beach") refers to the land near water, often sandy and flat. Together, 外( )滩(灘) evokes the 'outside beach' or waterfront areas developed for commerce and trade, leading to its historical significance and current identity as a prominent urban district along the Huangpu River in Shanghai.
