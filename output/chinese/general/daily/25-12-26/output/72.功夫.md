## kung fu
### gōngfu
---
- **definition:** kung fu
- **pinyin:** gōngfu
- **components:**
  - 功
    - gōng
    - achievement; work
  - 夫
    - fū
    - man; husband
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 功 (gōng, "achievement; work") + 夫 (fū, "man; husband") =
    - skill developed through work ->
    - martial arts
  - **interpretation:** 功 (gōng, "achievement") refers to the effort and work one puts into mastering a particular skill. 夫 (fū, "man") can imply the idea of a person or practitioner. "功夫" (gōngfu) therefore signifies the result of hard work and persistent practice leading to skill and expertise. While often associated with martial arts, these components initially suggested any skill or merit achieved through time and effort. Over time, it became most closely associated with Chinese martial arts, hence 'kung fu'.
- **examples:**
  - 学(學)习(習)功夫需要很多时(時)间(間)。
    - Xuéxí gōngfu xūyào hěn duō shíjiān.
    - Learning kung fu requires a lot of time.
  - 他的功夫很厉(厲)害。
    - Tā de gōngfu hěn lìhai.
    - His kung fu is very impressive.
  - 我们(們)可以在公园(園)里(裡)练(練)功夫。
    - Wǒmen kěyǐ zài gōngyuán lǐ liàn gōngfu.
    - We can practice kung fu in the park.
### 功夫 → 功
- **definition:** achievement; work
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **components:**
  - 力
    - lì
    - strength
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 力 (strength) + phonetic 工 (work) ->
    - work plus strength ->
    - achieving results
  - **interpretation:** The character 功 (gōng, "achievement; skill") combines 力 (lì, "strength") as the semantic component, indicating the effort or energy expended, with 工 (gōng, "work") as the phonetic, also emphasizing the idea of labor. The combination implies utilizing strength in work to achieve success or skill, highlighting the relationship between effort and achievement.
- **examples:**
  - 他花了很多时(時)间(間)在功课(課)上。
    - Tā huāle hěnduō shíjiān zài gōngkè shàng.
    - He spent a lot of time on his homework.
  - 努力工作会(會)带(帶)来(來)成功。
    - Nǔlì gōngzuò huì dàilái chénggōng.
    - Hard work will bring success.
  - 这(這)个(個)计(計)划(劃)终(終)于(於)取得了预(預)期的成功。
    - Zhège jìhuà zhōngyú qǔdéle yùqī de chénggōng.
    - The plan finally achieved the expected success.

### 功夫 → 功 → 力
- **definition:** strength
- **pinyin:** lì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts either an arm bending out from the side of the body, or a wooden plough
  - **interpretation:** The character 力(lì, 'strength') visually represents physical power through the image of an arm or tool, emphasizing the fundamental concept of muscular force.
- **examples:**
  - 他用尽(盡)全力，赢(贏)得比赛(賽)。
    - Tā yòng jìn quánlì, yíng dé bǐsài.
    - He used all his strength to win the competition.
  - 他把力气(氣)用在学(學)习(習)上。
    - Tā bǎ lìqì yòng zài xuéxí shàng.
    - He applies his strength to his studies.
  - 面对(對)困难(難)，他毫不退缩(縮)，全力以赴。
    - Miànduì kùnnán, tā háo bù tuìsuō, quánlì yǐ fù.
    - Facing difficulties, he neither retreats nor gives up, but goes all out.

### 功夫 → 功 → 工
- **definition:** work
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bladed tool to plow the ground and to mark a border, or a carpenter's square
  - **interpretation:** 工 (gōng, "work") represents either a tool used in agriculture for marking or a carpenter's square, reflecting its connection to traditional manual labor and craftsmanship.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天下午去做工。
    - Tā měitiān xiàwǔ qù zuò gōng.
    - He goes to work every afternoon.
  - 工厂(廠)里(裡)的机(機)器不停地运(運)转(轉)。
    - Gōng chǎng lǐ de jīqì bù tíng de yùnzhuǎn.
    - The machines in the factory run nonstop.
  - 这(這)位工人技艺(藝)精湛。
    - Zhè wèi gōngrén jìyì jīngzhàn.
    - This worker is highly skilled.

### 功夫 → 夫
- **definition:** man; husband
- **pinyin:** fū
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a variant form of 大 (dà, "big") representing an adult man with a hairpin ->
    - became associated with 夫 (fū, "man").
  - **interpretation:** 夫 (fū, "man") was visually similar to 大 (dà, "big") and was originally interchangeable. The addition of the horizontal stroke was interpreted as a hairpin, symbolizing adulthood for men.
- **examples:**
  - 此夫心怀(懷)大志。
    - Cǐ fū xīn huái dà zhì.
    - This man has lofty aspirations.
  - 他勤劳(勞)努力，堪称(稱)良夫。
    - Tā qín láo nǔ lì, kān chēng liáng fū.
    - He works diligently and is regarded as an exemplary man.

### 功夫 → 夫 → 大
- **definition:** big
- **pinyin:** dà
- **components:**
  - 夫
    - fū
    - man
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a person facing forward ->
    - Borrowed phonetically for 'big'; original form of 夫 (fū, 'man')
  - **interpretation:** The character 大(dà) depicts a person facing forward, representing 'big' by suggesting the idea of someone standing prominently. Although often thought of as a person with outstretched arms, early scripts do not support this interpretation. It was originally the form of 夫 (fū, 'man') and was later phonetically borrowed for the meaning 'big.'
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)房子很大。
    - Zhè ge fángzi hěn dà.
    - This house is very big.
  - 大海波涛(濤)汹(洶)涌(湧)，展现(現)出大自然的力量。
    - Dàhǎi bōtāo xiōngyǒng, zhǎnxiàn chū dàzìrán de lìliàng.
    - The vast ocean waves surge, showcasing the power of nature.
  - 大人物登场(場)，留下传(傳)奇。
    - Dà rénwù dēngchǎng, liúxià chuánqí.
    - Great figures make an appearance, leaving behind legends.
