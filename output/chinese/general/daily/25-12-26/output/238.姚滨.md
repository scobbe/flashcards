## Yao Bin (name)
### Yáo Bīn
---
- **traditional:** 姚濱
- **definition:** Yao Bin (name)
- **pinyin:** Yáo Bīn
- **components:**
  - 姚
    - yáo
    - a surname; handsome
  - 濱
    - bīn
    - shore; edge
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 姚 (yáo, "a surname; handsome") + 滨(濱) (bīn, "shore; edge") =
    - Yao Bin is a Chinese given name.
  - **interpretation:** 姚 (yáo, "a surname; handsome") is a common Chinese surname, and one who is handsome is often associated with notable or distinguished features. 滨(濱) (bīn, "shore; edge") refers to the boundary or periphery, suggesting a meeting place, or can metaphorically imply being distinguished by remaining at the forefront or edge of one's environment.
### 姚滨(濱) → 姚
- **definition:** a surname; handsome
- **pinyin:** yáo
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - woman
  - 兆
    - zhào
    - omen; sign
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 女 (nǚ, "woman") + phonetic 兆 (zhào) combined to indicate an elegant woman or handsome person
  - **interpretation:** The character 姚(yáo, "handsome; elegant") combines the semantic 女 (nǚ, "woman") indicating gender or grace with the phonetic 兆 (zhào), which does not directly inform the meaning but provides the pronunciation. The resulting sense of "handsome" or "elegant" suggests qualities of beauty or stylishness traditionally associated with women or persons of elegance.
- **examples:**
  - 他的举(舉)止很姚。
    - Tā de jǔzhǐ hěn yáo.
    - His demeanor is very elegant.
  - 姚姓在中国(國)很常见(見)。
    - Yáo xìng zài Zhōngguó hěn chángjiàn.
    - The surname Yao is very common in China.
  - 他是一位姚士，总(總)是吸引很多目光。
    - Tā shì yī wèi yáoshì, zǒng shì xīyǐn hěn duō mùguāng.
    - He is a handsome gentleman who always attracts a lot of attention.

### 姚滨(濱) → 姚 → 女

- **pinyin:** nǚ
- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

    - enclosed area as remnant of right breast
  - **interpretation:** The character 女(nǚ, "woman") is a pictogram originally depicting a woman with highlighted bodily features, such as the breasts, symbolizing femininity. ### 姚滨(濱) → 姚 → 兆
- **definition:** omen; sign
- **pinyin:** zhào
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a person escaping from a flood ->
    - original form of 逃 (táo, "escape") ->
    - later phonetically borrowed for "omen"
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a person fleeing from a flood, symbolizing escape. This imagery was later phonetically borrowed to represent the concept of an 'omen' due to the character's pronunciation resembling words for 'omen' in early Chinese.
- **examples:**
  - 乌(烏)云(雲)密布是暴风(風)雨的兆。
    - Wūyún mìbù shì bàofēngyǔ de zhào.

    - Tā mèngjiàn yī tiáo shé, zhè kěnéng shì yīgè bù hǎo de zhào.
    - He dreamed of a snake, which could be a bad omen.
  - 星星突然闪(閃)烁(爍)，被古人视(視)为(為)兆。
    - Xīngxīng tūrán shǎnshuò, bèi gǔrén shì wéi zhào.

### 姚滨(濱) → 濱
- **definition:** shore; edge
- **pinyin:** bīn
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 賓
    - bīn
    - guest
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 水 (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic 賓 (bīn) ->
    - shore; edge
  - **interpretation:** The semantic component 水 (shuǐ, "water") indicates a connection to water, suggesting proximity to bodies of water such as rivers or seas. The phonetic component 賓 (bīn), which shares a similar pronunciation, provides the sound. Together, they create a character for 'shore' or 'edge', places typically bordering water.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)在河滨(濱)散步。
    - Wǒmen zài hébīn sànbù.
    - We stroll by the riverside.
  - 湖滨(濱)长(長)满(滿)了绿(綠)草。
    - Húbīn zhǎng mǎn le lǜcǎo.
    - The lakeshore is covered with green grass.
  - 城市滨(濱)海区(區)发(發)展迅速。
    - Chéngshì bīnhǎi qū fāzhǎn xùnsù.
    - The city's coastal area is developing rapidly.

### 姚滨(濱) → 濱 → 賓

- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 宀
    - mián
    - roof
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - valuables
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 人 (rén, "person") in 宀 (mián, "roof") =
    - guest in a house + 貝 (bèi, "valuables") added later for gift ->
    - 賓 (bīn, "guest")
  - **interpretation:** The combination of a person under a roof signifies a guest, while the addition of valuables implies the offering of gifts to a host, encapsulating the full meaning of a welcomed guest.
  - **simplification:** 賓 -> 宀 (mián, "roof") + 兵 (bīng, "soldier") as a phonetic replacement given its simplicity and similar pronunciation, officially adopted in 1956.
- **examples:**
  - 宴会(會)开(開)始前，主人已经(經)迎接宾(賓)客。
    - Yànhuì kāishǐ qián, zhǔrén yǐjīng yíngjiē bīn kè.

### 姚滨(濱) → 濱 → 賓 → 宀
- **definition:** roof
- **pinyin:** mián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a house with four walls ->
    - roof
  - **interpretation:** 宀(mián, "roof") is a pictogram representing a house with four walls. It emphasizes an enclosed space, distinguishing it from similar structures like 广(guǎng, "broad") which depicts a building with three walls. This makes 宀 a logical choice for representing a roof in various characters.
- **examples:**
  - 含有宀的汉(漢)字代表家的温(溫)暖。
    - Hán yǒu mián de hànzì dàibiǎo jiā de wēnnuǎn.
    - Chinese characters that include the radical '宀' represent the warmth of home.
  - 屋顶(頂)遮风(風)挡(擋)雨，家园(園)散发(發)温(溫)馨。
    - Wūdǐng zhē fēng dǎng yǔ, jiāyuán sànfā wēnxīn.

### 姚滨(濱) → 濱 → 賓 → 貝
- **definition:** valuables
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为(為)货(貨)币(幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
