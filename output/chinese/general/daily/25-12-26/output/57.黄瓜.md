## cucumber
### huánggua
---
- **traditional:** 黃瓜
- **definition:** cucumber
- **pinyin:** huánggua
- **components:**
  - 黄(黃)
    - huáng
    - yellow
  - 瓜
    - guā
    - melon; squash; gourd
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 黄(黃) (huáng, "yellow") + 瓜 (guā, "melon") =
    - cucumber
  - **interpretation:** The term 黄(黃)瓜 (huángguā) translates literally to 'yellow melon.' It refers to the cucumber, which is botanically a type of gourd and can have a yellowish tinge when ripe. By naming the cucumber this way, the focus is on its classification among the gourd family rather than its usual green color.
- **examples:**
  - 我买(買)了一根黄(黃)瓜。
    - Wǒ mǎi le yī gēn huángguā.
    - I bought a cucumber.
  - 我洗了黄(黃)瓜，切成了薄片。
    - Wǒ xǐ le huángguā, qiē chéng le bó piàn.
    - I washed the cucumber and sliced it into thin pieces.
  - 夏天吃黄(黃)瓜，很解暑。
    - Xiàtiān chī huángguā, hěn jiěshǔ.
    - Eating cucumber in summer is very refreshing.
### 黄(黃)瓜 → 黄(黃)
- **traditional:** 黃
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted a person with a pronounced chest or wearing a jade ring ->
    - borrowed for yellow.
  - **interpretation:** The character 黃 was initially a pictogram linked to either the depiction of a disabled person with a protruding chest or a person wearing a semi-circular jade. It was phonetically borrowed for the meaning 'yellow', possibly due to associations with earthly tones or rituals involving jade, which may have shared similar phonetics or context. The narrative around disabilities and significant physical traits might also symbolize complex cultural rituals or confusions in historical documentation.
  - **simplification:** The simplified 黄 presumably retains the overall structure but loses some complexity of components like 田 (field).
- **examples:**
  - 太阳(陽)升起时(時)，天空变(變)成了一种(種)淡淡的黄(黃)色。
    - Tàiyáng shēng qǐ shí, tiānkōng biàn chéng le yī zhǒng dàndàn de huángsè.
    - When the sun rises, the sky turns into a faint yellow color.
  - 她喜欢(歡)穿黄(黃)色的衣服。
    - Tā xǐhuān chuān huángsè de yīfú.
    - She likes to wear yellow clothes.
  - 秋天时(時)，树(樹)叶(葉)变(變)黄(黃)了。
    - Qiūtiān shí, shùyè biàn huángle.
    - In autumn, the leaves turn yellow.

### 黄(黃)瓜 → 瓜
- **definition:** melon; squash; gourd
- **pinyin:** guā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a melon suspended by its vines
  - **interpretation:** The character 瓜 (guā, "melon") visually represents the shape and outline of a melon with its vines, emphasizing its organic form and growth pattern.
- **examples:**
  - 他喜爱(愛)吃瓜。
    - Tā xǐ'ài chī guā.
    - He loves eating melons.
  - 夏日炎炎时(時)，瓜农(農)开(開)始采摘瓜。
    - Xiàrì yányán shí, guānóng kāishǐ cǎizhāi guā.
    - On scorching summer days, melon farmers start harvesting melons.
