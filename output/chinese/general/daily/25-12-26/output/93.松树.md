## pine tree
### sōngshù
---
- **traditional:** 松樹
- **definition:** pine tree
- **pinyin:** sōngshù
- **components:**
  - 松
    - sōng
    - pine
  - 树(樹)
    - shù
    - tree
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 松 (sōng, "pine") + 樹 (shù, "tree") =
    - pine tree.
  - **interpretation:** 松 (sōng, "pine") refers to the type of tree known for its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing qualities. 樹 (shù, "tree") represents any type of woody perennial plant. Together, they specify the particular species of tree, 'pine tree', by naming both its general category and its distinctive type.
- **examples:**
  - 山( )上( )的( )松( )树(樹)十分高大。

### 松( )树(樹) → 松(鬆)
- **traditional:** 鬆
- **definition:** pine
- **pinyin:** sōng
- **components:**
  - 髟
    - biāo
    - hair
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 髟 ("hair") + phonetic 松 (sōng) ->
    - meaning
  - **interpretation:** 鬆 (sōng, "loose") combines 髟 (biāo, "hair") as the semantic element expressing the idea of looseness or something flowing freely, similar to hair, with 松 (sōng, "pine") as the phonetic element. This links to the concept of being loose or relaxed, as hair can freely flow and pine branches are not tightly bound.
  - **simplification:** The simplified form 松(鬆) (sōng, "pine") retains 毛 (máo, "fur") as a reduced element replacing 髟 (biāo, "hair") and removing excess components for simplicity.

### 松( )树(樹) → 树(樹)

- **definition:** tree
- **pinyin:** shù
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 尌
    - shù
    - to plant a tree
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Originally 尌 (phonetic, 'to plant a tree') + 木 (mù, 'tree') added for clarity ->
    - tree
  - **interpretation:** 樹 combines the semantic 木 (mù, 'tree') with the phonetic 尌 to convey the meaning of 'tree'. The phonetic element 尌 originally depicted the act of planting a tree, and 木 emphasizes the botanical aspect.

### 松( )树(樹) → 树(樹) → 尌 → 寸
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Line on the hand (ideographic) emphasizing the elbow ->
    - represents measurement unit between wrist and specific forearm position ->
    - meaning 'inch'
  - **interpretation:** 寸 (cùn, "inch") originated as a depiction of a line on the hand, indicating the area near the elbow. This specific placement was considered significant for pulse palpation, originally suggesting a forearm position, which then came to denote the measurement 'inch'.
