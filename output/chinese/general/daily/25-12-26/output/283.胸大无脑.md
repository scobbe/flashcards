## bimbo (lit. big chest no brains)
### xiōng dà wú nǎo
---
- **traditional:** 胸大無腦
- **definition:** bimbo (lit. big chest no brains)
- **pinyin:** xiōng dà wú nǎo
- **components:**
  - 胸
    - xiōng
    - chest
  - 大
    - dà
    - big; large; great; major
  - 无(無)
    - wú
    - no; not; without
  - 脑(腦)
    - nǎo
    - brain; mind; head
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 胸 (xiōng, "chest"), 大 (dà, "big"), 无(無) (wú, "no"), 脑(腦) (nǎo, "brain")
  - **interpretation:** This expression combines 胸 (xiōng, "chest") and 大 (dà, "big") to refer to a prominent physical feature, with 无(無) (wú, "no") and 脑(腦) (nǎo, "brain") implying a lack of intelligence. It is often used disparagingly to describe someone perceived as physically attractive but lacking in intelligence.
- **examples:**
  - 她常常被人称(稱)为(為)胸大无(無)脑(腦)。
    - Tā chángcháng bèi rén chēngwéi xiōng dà wú nǎo.
    - She is often labeled a bimbo.
  - 有的明星虽(雖)然外表光鲜(鮮)，但其实(實)她们(們)不过(過)是胸大无(無)脑(腦)。
    - Yǒu de míngxīng suīrán wàibiǎo guāngxían, dàn qíshí tāmen bùguò shì xiōng dà wú nǎo.
    - Some celebrities may appear glamorous on the outside, but in reality, they are just bimbos.
