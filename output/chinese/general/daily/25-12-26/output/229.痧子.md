## measles
### shāzi
---
- **definition:** measles
- **pinyin:** shāzi
- **components:**
  - 痧
    - shā
    - rash; measles
  - 子
    - zi
    - child; small thing
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 痧 (shā, "rash") + 子 (zi, "child; small thing"), referring to measles
  - **interpretation:** The word 痧子 (shāzi) refers to measles by combining 痧 (shā, "rash"), which represents the characteristic rash associated with the disease, and 子 (zi, "child; small thing"), indicating something small or relating to children, as measles commonly affects young children.
- **examples:**
  - 他得了痧子。
    - Tā déle shāzi.
    - He contracted measles.
  - 当(當)孩子暴露在阳(陽)光下时(時)，痧子容易传(傳)播。
    - Dāng háizi bàolù zài yángguāng xià shí, shāzi róngyì chuánbò.
    - When children are exposed to sunlight, measles spreads easily.
  - 医(醫)生建议(議)及时(時)治疗(療)痧子。
    - Yīshēng jiànyì jíshí zhìliáo shāzi.
    - The doctor advised prompt treatment for measles.
### 痧子 → 痧
- **definition:** rash; measles
- **pinyin:** shā
- **components:**
  - 疒
    - nè
    - illness; disease
  - 沙
    - shā
    - sand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 疒 (nè, "illness; disease") + phonetic: 沙 (shā, "sand") =
    - conveys the idea of a sand-like rash ->
    - rash; measles
  - **interpretation:** The character 痧(shā, "rash; measles") combines the semantic component 疒 (nè, "illness; disease") with the phonetic 沙 (shā, "sand"). This pairing suggests an illness characterized by rash-like symptoms, akin to the texture of sand.
- **examples:**
  - 他皮肤(膚)上出现(現)痧。
    - Tā pífū shàng chūxiàn shā.
    - A rash appeared on his skin.
  - 高温(溫)天气(氣)时(時)，部分人会(會)得痧。
    - Gāowēn tiānqì shí, bùfèn rén huì dé shā.
    - In hot weather, some people may develop a rash.

- **definition:** illness; disease
- **pinyin:** nè

  - **interpretation:** The character vividly portrays the image of a person in a reclining or bedridden state, directly symbolizing the concept of illness or disease due to the association of lying in bed with being unwell.

### 痧子 → 痧 → 沙
- **definition:** sand
- **pinyin:** shā
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 少
    - shǎo
    - to lack; to be short of
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 水 (shuǐ, "water") + 少 (shǎo, "to lack; to be short of") =
    - water with few particles ->
    - sand
  - **interpretation:** The character 沙(shā, "sand") represents the presence of sparse particles (sand) in water, suggesting how sand is often found in conjunction with water, like along riverbanks.
- **examples:**
  - 海滩(灘)上的沙很细(細)。
    - Hǎi tān shàng de shā hěn xì.
    - The sand on the beach is very fine.
  - 孩子们(們)带(帶)着(著)桶和铲(鏟)子，在沙滩(灘)上建城堡。
    - Háizimen dài zhe tǒng hé chǎnzi, zài shā tān shàng jiàn chéngbǎo.
    - The children carry buckets and shovels, building castles on the beach.
  - 风(風)吹起沙尘(塵)，让(讓)人有点(點)不适(適)。
    - Fēng chuī qǐ shāchén, ràng rén yǒudiǎn bùshì.
    - The wind kicks up sand dust, making people feel a bit uncomfortable.

### 痧子 → 痧 → 沙 → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flowing water
  - **interpretation:** 水 (shuǐ, 'water') is a pictogram that directly represents the appearance of flowing water, making it intuitive and easy to recognize. Its simplicity and direct visual reference to its meaning have allowed it to remain unchanged over millennia.
- **examples:**
  - 水是一种(種)生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì yī zhǒng shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.

    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, hē shuǐ néng jiě shǔ.
    - It is very hot in summer, drinking water can relieve the heat.
  - 河水潺潺，沿岸绿(綠)树(樹)成荫(蔭)。
    - Hé shuǐ chánchán, yán'àn lǜshù chéng yīn.
    - The river flows babbling, and green trees along the bank provide shade.

### 痧子 → 痧 → 沙 → 少
- **definition:** to lack; to be short of
- **pinyin:** shǎo
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts four dots, perhaps three grains of sand ->
    - indicates "a few, a little" ->
    - phonetically borrowed from 小 (xiǎo, "small")
  - **interpretation:** The character 少 (shǎo, "few") originally depicted four dots, possibly representing grains of sand, conveying the impression of a small quantity. It was phonetically borrowed from 小 (xiǎo, "small"), which uses three dots to signify smallness, reinforcing the sense of 'few' through the idea of minimal amounts.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)本书(書)有很少的字。
    - Zhè běn shū yǒu hěn shǎo de zì.
    - This book has very few words.
  - 来(來)参(參)加演出的人很少。
    - Lái cānjiā yǎnchū de rén hěn shǎo.
    - Few people came to attend the performance.
  - 这(這)个(個)季节(節)雨水很少。
    - Zhège jìjié yǔshuǐ hěn shǎo.
    - There is very little rain this season.

### 痧子 → 子
- **definition:** child; small thing
- **pinyin:** zi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a baby with a large head and spread arms, with legs wrapped in a blanket
  - **interpretation:** The character 子(zǐ, "child") is a pictorial representation of a baby, emphasizing characteristics like a large head and outstretched arms typical of infants, which conveys the essence of a child. This visual element helps infer its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)小子很调(調)皮。
    - Zhè xiǎo zǐ hěn tiáopí.
    - This kid is very naughty.
  - 他的小子爱(愛)打篮(籃)球，每天都锻(鍛)炼(煉)身体(體)。
    - Tā de xiǎo zǐ ài dǎ lánqiú, měitiān dōu duànliàn shēntǐ.
    - His child loves playing basketball, and practices every day.
  - 孤独(獨)的子需要关(關)爱(愛)。
    - Gūdú de zǐ xūyào guān'ài.
    - A lonely child needs care.
