## garden
### yuán
---
- **traditional:** 園
- **definition:** garden
- **pinyin:** yuán
- **components:**
  - 囗
    - wéi
    - fence
  - 袁
    - yuán
    - a surname
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 囗 (wéi, "fence") + phonetic 袁 (yuán) ->
    - garden enclosed by a fence
  - **interpretation:** The character 囗 (wéi, "fence") suggests an enclosed area, a common feature of gardens. 袁 (yuán) provides the sound. Together, they form the concept of a garden, which is typically a fenced or confined area designated for cultivation or enjoyment.
  - **simplification:** 园(園): Simplified by substituting 袁 (yuán) with the more straightforward character 元 (yuán, "origin"), reducing stroke complexity while keeping the phonetic element intact.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)花园(園)里(裡)有很多种(種)花。
    - Zhège huāyuán lǐ yǒu hěn duō zhǒng huā.
    - There are many kinds of flowers in this garden.
  - 她喜欢(歡)在园(園)子里(裡)种(種)花。
    - Tā xǐhuān zài yuánzi lǐ zhòng huā.
    - She likes to plant flowers in the garden.
  - 我们(們)周末常去那座公园(園)。
    - Wǒmen zhōumò cháng qù nà zuò gōngyuán.
    - We often go to that park on weekends.
### 园(園) → 囗
- **definition:** fence
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an enclosure
  - **interpretation:** The character 囗 (wéi, 'enclosure') is a pictogram representing a square or rectangular enclosure, emblematic of walls or boundaries. It visually reflects a basic enclosed area delineated by straight lines.
- **examples:**
  - 博物馆(館)外有坚(堅)固的囗，保护(護)着(著)历(歷)史文物。
    - Bówùguǎn wài yǒu jiāngù de wéi, bǎohùzhe lìshǐ wénwù.
    - Outside the museum, a sturdy enclosure protects historical artifacts.
  - 古城外围(圍)的囗，确(確)保了居民的安全。
    - Gǔchéng wàiwéi de wéi, quèbǎole jūmín de ānquán.
    - The ancient city's enclosure ensures the residents' safety.

### 园(園) → 袁
- **definition:** a surname
- **pinyin:** yuán
- **components:**
  - 衣
    - yī
    - clothes
  - 又
    - yòu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 衣("clothes") + 又("hand") =
    - to put on clothes
  - **interpretation:** The character 袁 originally depicted the action of putting on or wearing clothes, with 衣 (yī, "clothes") representing garments and 又 (yòu, "hand") indicating the action of using hands to dress. The hand component, which later evolved into other forms, indicates the interaction with clothes, hence forming the meaning of wearing. Over time, this character came to be associated with a long garment and used as a surname.
- **examples:**
  - 袁先生是一个(個)伟(偉)大的作家。
    - Yuán xiānshēng shì yī gè wěidà de zuòjiā.
    - Mr. Yuan is a great writer.
  - 他们(們)都称(稱)呼他为(為)袁博士。
    - Tāmen dōu chēnghū tā wéi Yuán bóshì.
    - They all call him Dr. Yuan.
  - 袁家大院有一个(個)美丽(麗)的花园(園)。
    - Yuán jiā dàyuàn yǒu yī gè měilì de huāyuán.
    - The Yuan family's large courtyard has a beautiful garden.

### 园(園) → 袁 → 衣
- **definition:** clothes
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a basic outline of a garment with sleeves hanging down.
  - **interpretation:** The character 衣 (yī, 'clothes') clearly resembles a simple garment, with parts representing sleeves and the body of clothing. This visual representation aligns with its meaning of 'clothes' or 'clothing.'
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)件衣很特别(別)。
    - Zhè jiàn yī hěn tè bié.
    - This piece of clothing is very special.
  - 冬天来(來)了，人们(們)换(換)上了暖和的衣。
    - Dōngtiān lái le, rénmen huàn shàng le nuǎn huó de yī.

### 园(園) → 袁 → 又
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a right hand
  - **interpretation:** The character 又 (yòu, "hand") is a pictogram representing a right hand, showing the fingers and palm, which highlights its original meaning related to manual actions.
- **examples:**
  - 「又」这(這)个(個)字最早来(來)源于(於)手。
    - 「Yòu」 zhè gè zì zuì zǎo láiyuán yú shǒu。
    - The character '又' originally comes from the hand.
  - 很多汉(漢)字含有又部首，表示与(與)手有关(關)的动(動)作。
    - Hěn duō hànzì hányǒu Yòu bùshǒu, biǎoshì yǔ shǒu yǒuguān de dòngzuò。
    - Many Chinese characters include the '又' radical, indicating actions related to the hand.
  - 古人用又描绘(繪)手的形态(態)，体(體)现(現)了古汉(漢)字的独(獨)特魅力。
    - Gǔrén yòng Yòu miáohuì shǒu de xíngtài, tǐxiàn le gǔ hànzì de dútè mèilì。
    - Ancient people used '又' to depict the shape of the hand, showcasing the unique charm of ancient Chinese characters.
