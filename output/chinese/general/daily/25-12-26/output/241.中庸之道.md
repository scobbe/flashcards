## Doctrine of the Mean; middle way
### zhōngyōng zhī dào
---
- **definition:** Doctrine of the Mean; middle way
- **pinyin:** zhōngyōng zhī dào
- **components:**
  - 中
    - zhōng
    - middle; center; mean
  - 庸
    - yōng
    - mediocre; usual; ordinary
  - 之
    - zhī
    - of; possessive particle
  - 道
    - dào
    - way; path; doctrine
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 中 (zhōng, "middle"), 庸 (yōng, "mediocre"), 之 (zhī, "of"), 道 (dào, "way")
  - **interpretation:** 中庸之道 (zhōngyōng zhī dào, "Doctrine of the Mean") comes from Confucian philosophy, specifically the Book of Doctrine of the Mean (中庸), one of the Four Books in Confucianism. It emphasizes moderation and balance as a path to harmony and virtue. By combining 中 (zhōng, "middle") and 庸 (yōng, "mediocre"), it suggests avoiding extremes to achieve a balanced 'way' (道, dào).
- **examples:**
  - 儒( )家( )思( )想( )重( )视(視)中( )庸( )之( )道( )，认(認)为(為)调(調)和( )各( )方( )最( )为(為)重( )要( )。
    - Rújiā sīxiǎng zhòngshì zhōngyōng zhī dào, rènwéi tiáohé gèfāng zuìwéi zhòngyào.
    - Confucian thought emphasizes the doctrine of the mean, believing that balancing various interests is paramount.
  - 追( )求( )内(內)心( )和( )谐(諧)是( )中( )庸( )之( )道( )的( )体(體)现(現)。
    - Zhuīqiú nèixīn héxié shì zhōngyōng zhī dào de tǐxiàn.
