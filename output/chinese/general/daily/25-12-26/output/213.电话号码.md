## telephone number
### diànhuà hàomǎ
---
- **traditional:** 電話號碼
- **definition:** telephone number
- **pinyin:** diànhuà hàomǎ
- **components:**
  - 電
    - diàn
    - electric
  - 話
    - huà
    - speech
  - 號
    - hào
    - number
  - 碼
    - mǎ
    - code
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 電 (diàn, "electric") + 話 (huà, "speech") form 'telephone'; 號 (hào, "number") + 碼 (mǎ, "code") form 'number'. A 電話 (diànhuà, "telephone") lets speech travel via electricity. A 號碼 (hàomǎ, "number") is given to connect calls.
  - **interpretation:** 電 (diàn, "electric") + 話 (huà, "speech"): talking through electricity makes long-distance communication possible, thus 'telephone'. 號 (hào, "number") + 碼 (mǎ, "code"): a precise digit sequence or 'code' is necessary for dialing, hence 'number' in phone contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 你能给(給)我你的电(電)话(話)号(號)码(碼)吗(嗎)？
    - Nǐ néng gěi wǒ nǐ de diànhuà hàomǎ ma?
    - Can you give me your telephone number?
  - 我错(錯)记(記)了他的电(電)话(話)号(號)码(碼)。
    - Wǒ cuòjì le tā de diànhuà hàomǎ.
    - I misremembered his telephone number.
  - 我们(們)联(聯)系(繫)的方式是通过(過)电(電)子邮(郵)件和电(電)话(話)号(號)码(碼)。
    - Wǒmen liánxì de fāngshì shì tōngguò diànzǐ yóujiàn hé diànhuà hàomǎ.
    - Our ways of communication are through email and telephone number.
### 电(電)话(話)号(號)码(碼) → 電
- **definition:** electric
- **pinyin:** diàn
- **components:**
  - 雨
    - yǔ
    - rain
  - 申
    - shēn
    - extend
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 雨 (yǔ, "rain") + phonetic 申 (shēn, "extend") ->
    - lightning as rain's powerful emission ->
    - representative of electricity
  - **interpretation:** 電 (diàn) combines 雨 (yǔ, "rain") as a semantic component indicating the natural association with weather, specifically storms, and 申 (shēn, "extend") as a phonetic component that gives sound. The powerful visual and auditory impact of lightning during storms led to its conceptual link to the invisible force of electricity.
  - **simplification:** 电(電) simplifies by eliminating 雨 (yǔ, "rain") and maintaining 申 (shēn, "extend") as a sole component with a reduced stroke count.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)台风(風)扇需要电(電)才能运(運)作。
    - Zhè tái fēngshàn xūyào diàn cái néng yùnzuò.
    - This fan needs electricity to operate.
  - 我们(們)准(準)备(備)了一根备(備)用电(電)线(線)。
    - Wǒmen zhǔnbèi le yī gēn bèiyòng diànxiàn.
    - We prepared a spare electrical wire.
  - 停电(電)时(時)我们(們)用蜡(蠟)烛(燭)。
    - Tíngdiàn shí wǒmen yòng làzhú.
    - We use candles during a power outage.

### 电(電)话(話)号(號)码(碼) → 號
- **definition:** number
- **pinyin:** hào
- **components:**
  - 号(號)
    - hào
    - to wail
  - 虎
    - hǔ
    - tiger
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 言
    - yán
    - speech
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - original ideogram: tiger with exhaled air ->
    - tiger's roar (號)口 (kǒu, "mouth") and 言 (yán, "speech") added to emphasize calling ->
    - final form: phonetic 号 (hào, "to wail") + semantic 虎 (hǔ, "tiger")
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a tiger exhaling air, signifying its roar. To emphasize the concept of making sounds, later forms incorporated 口 (kǒu, "mouth") indicating vocal expressions. In the current form, the character combines phonetic 号 (hào, "to wail") with the semantic element 虎 (hǔ, "tiger"), implying the sound made by a tiger. Over time, the meaning was extended to include 'number', possibly due to its phonetic similarity.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是十月十号(號)。
    - Jīntiān shì shí yuè shí hào.
    - Today is October 10th.
  - 我的电(電)话(話)号(號)码(碼)是三八九号(號)。
    - Wǒ de diànhuà hàomǎ shì sān bā jiǔ hào.
    - My phone number is 389.
  - 请(請)到三号(號)台办(辦)手续(續)。
    - Qǐng dào sān hào tái bàn shǒuxù.
    - Please go to counter number three to handle the procedure.
