## pet
### chǒngwù
---
- **traditional:** 寵物
- **definition:** pet
- **pinyin:** chǒngwù
- **components:**
  - 宠(寵)
    - chǒng
    - to pamper
  - 物
    - wù
    - thing
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 寵 (chǒng, "to pamper") + 物 (wù, "thing") =
    - an object of pampering ->
    - pet
  - **interpretation:** 寵 (chǒng, "to pamper") suggests an action of showing affection, treating someone or something with great care. 物 (wù, "thing") refers to an object or being. Together, 寵物 creates the idea of an entity that receives affection and pampering, logically leading to the sense of a "pet," which is often cared for and loved as part of the family.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)有一只(隻)可爱(愛)的宠(寵)物狗。
    - Wǒmen yǒu yī zhī kě'ài de chǒngwù gǒu.
    - We have a cute pet dog.
  - 小猫(貓)是最受欢(歡)迎的宠(寵)物之一。
    - Xiǎo māo shì zuì shòu huānyíng de chǒngwù zhī yī.
    - Kittens are one of the most popular pets.
  - 带(帶)宠(寵)物去看兽(獸)医(醫)是很重要的。
    - Dài chǒngwù qù kàn shòuyī shì hěn zhòngyào de.
    - It's important to take pets to the veterinarian.
### 宠(寵)物 → 宠(寵)
- **traditional:** 寵
- **definition:** to pamper
- **pinyin:** chǒng
- **components:**
  - 宀
    - mián
    - roof
  - 龍
    - lóng
    - dragon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 宀 (mián, "roof") + phonetic 龍 (lóng, "dragon") ->
    - favor
  - **interpretation:** The character 寵 (chǒng) combines the semantic component 宀 (mián, "roof"), suggesting the home or a nurturing space, with the phonetic component 龍 (lóng, "dragon"), which contributes the pronunciation. The combination suggests the idea of conferring favor or special care, akin to how one might cherish something precious within one's own home.
  - **simplification:** 寵 (chǒng, "favor") was simplified by replacing the complex 龍 (lóng, "dragon") with the simpler component 宀 and removing multiple strokes, creating 宠(寵).
- **examples:**
  - 他很宠(寵)他的狗。
    - Tā hěn chǒng tā de gǒu.
    - He pampers his dog a lot.
  - 这(這)个(個)孩子被父母宠(寵)坏(壞)了。
    - Zhège háizi bèi fùmǔ chǒng huài le.
    - This child has been spoiled by the parents.
  - 她一直是个(個)备(備)受宠(寵)爱(愛)的公主。
    - Tā yīzhí shì gè bèi shòu chǒng'ài de gōngzhǔ.
    - She has always been a much-pampered princess.
### 宠(寵)物 → 宠(寵) → 宀
- **definition:** roof
- **pinyin:** mián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a house with four walls ->
    - roof
  - **interpretation:** 宀(mián, "roof") is a pictogram representing a house with four walls. It emphasizes an enclosed space, distinguishing it from similar structures like 广(guǎng, "broad") which depicts a building with three walls. This makes 宀 a logical choice for representing a roof in various characters.
- **examples:**
  - 含有宀的汉(漢)字代表家的温(溫)暖。
    - Hán yǒu mián de hànzì dàibiǎo jiā de wēnnuǎn.
    - Chinese characters that include the radical '宀' represent the warmth of home.
  - 屋顶(頂)遮风(風)挡(擋)雨，家园(園)散发(發)温(溫)馨。
    - Wūdǐng zhē fēng dǎng yǔ, jiāyuán sànfā wēnxīn.
    - The roof shields from wind and rain, and the home radiates warmth.

### 宠(寵)物 → 宠(寵) → 龍
- **definition:** dragon
- **pinyin:** lóng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a sacred composite creature with features of snake, crocodile, fish, deer, etc. ->
    - Large-mouthed beast head, S-shaped body, possibly scale-like patterns ->
    - Head ornament elements resemble antlers or clan emblems ->
    - Evolved into separate head (left) and body (right) parts in writing
  - **interpretation:** The character 龍 (lóng, 'dragon') is a pictogram representing a mythical creature combining attributes like scales, a beast head, and an elongated body. Its depiction included antler-like ornaments or clan symbols, suggesting its status as a sacred animal in ancient China. The evolution of the character involved separating the head and body elements, reflecting practical adaptations in script forms over time.
- **examples:**
  - 在中国(國)文化中，龙(龍)象征(徵)着(著)权(權)力和幸运(運)。
    - Zài Zhōngguó wénhuà zhōng, lóng xiàngzhēng zhe quánlì hé xìngyùn.
    - In Chinese culture, the dragon symbolizes power and luck.
  - 龙(龍)年被认(認)为(為)是特别(別)吉祥的一年。
    - Lóngnián bèi rènwéi shì tèbié jíxiáng de yī nián.
    - The Year of the Dragon is considered especially auspicious.
  - 传(傳)说(說)中的龙(龍)能够(夠)呼风(風)唤(喚)雨。
    - Chuánshuō zhōng de lóng nénggòu hū fēng huàn yǔ.
    - Legendary dragons have the ability to call forth wind and rain.

### 宠(寵)物 → 物
- **definition:** thing
- **pinyin:** wù
- **components:**
  - 牛
    - niú
    - cow
  - 勿
    - wù
    - do not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 牛 (niú, "cow") + phonetic 勿 (wù) ->
    - thing; object
  - **interpretation:** The character 物 (wù, "thing; object") combines 牛 (niú, "cow") as the semantic component, signifying tangible entities or physical objects, with 勿 (wù) as the phonetic component to provide the sound. The usage of 牛, representing cattle, suggests that the meanings originally included tangible, concrete items before broadening to general objects.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)物品很贵(貴)重。
    - Zhège wùpǐn hěn guìzhòng.
    - This object is very valuable.
  - 我在寻(尋)找一个(個)特别(別)的东(東)西。
    - Wǒ zài xúnzhǎo yī gè tèbié de dōngxi.
    - I am looking for a special thing.
  - 每样(樣)物品都有其用途。
    - Měi yàng wùpǐn dōu yǒu qí yòngtú.
    - Every object has its use.

- **definition:** cow
- **pinyin:** niú
- **etymology:**

  - **description:** Depicts the head and horns of an ox

### 宠(寵)物 → 物 → 勿
- **definition:** do not
- **pinyin:** wù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts blood on a knife ->
    - original character of 刎 (wěn, "to cut") ->
    - jiajie for 勿 (wù, "do not")
  - **interpretation:** The character 勿(wù), originally illustrated blood on a knife, visually represented the action of cutting, connecting to its initial meaning. Over time, it was borrowed to express prohibition or negation, showing the evolution beyond its pictorial roots.
- **examples:**
  - 进(進)入图(圖)书(書)馆(館)时(時)，请(請)勿大声(聲)喧哗(嘩)。
    - Jìnrù túshūguǎn shí, qǐng wù dàshēng xuānhuá.
    - When entering the library, please do not speak loudly.
  - 办(辦)公区(區)内(內)勿吸烟(煙)。
    - Bàngōng qū nèi wù xīyān.
    - Do not smoke in the office area.
  - 行路时(時)，勿追逐打闹(鬧)。
    - Xínglù shí, wù zhuīzhú dǎnào.
    - When walking, do not engage in chasing and rough play.
