### huáqiáo
---
- **traditional:** 華僑
- **definition:** overseas Chinese
- **pinyin:** huáqiáo
- **components:**
  - 华(華)
    - huá
    - China; magnificent
  - 侨(僑)
    - qiáo

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 华(華) (huá, "China; magnificent") + 侨(僑) (qiáo, "to live abroad")
  - **interpretation:** 华(華)侨(僑) (huáqiáo) means 'overseas Chinese.' The word combines 华(華) (huá), representing 'China' and symbolizing magnificence, and 侨(僑) (qiáo), which means 'to live abroad.' This combination reflects Chinese people who establish themselves outside of China while maintaining cultural ties to their homeland.

### 华(華)侨(僑) → 华(華)
- **traditional:** 華
- **definition:** China; magnificent
- **pinyin:** huá
- **components:**
  - 化
    - huà
    - to transform
  - 十
    - shí
    - stalk and leaves
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 化 (huà, "to transform") + semantic 十 (shí, "stalk and leaves") ->
    - 華 (huá, "magnificent")
  - **interpretation:** 華 (huá, "magnificent") combines 化 (huà, "to transform") providing the pronunciation, with 十 (shí, "stalk and leaves") symbolizing growth and abundance, resulting in the sense of magnificence and richness. This imagery naturally bridges to the idea of 'magnificent', depicting flourishing plants as a symbol of splendor, which extends to refer to China as a culturally rich and illustrious place.
  - **simplification:** 华(華): The top component of 華 was simplified by using 化 (huà, "to transform") in the simplified version 华, retaining phonetic resemblance while removing the detailed component 十.
- **examples:**
  - 中( )国(國)是( )一( )个(個)充( )满(滿)魅( )力( )的( )国(國)家( )。
    - Zhōngguó shì yīge chōngmǎn mèilì de guójiā.
    - China is a country full of charm.
  - 他( )的( )家( )在( )华(華)北( )平( )原( )。
    - Tā de jiā zài Huáběi píngyuán.
    - His home is on the North China Plain.
  - 这(這)座( )建( )筑(築)非( )常( )华(華)丽(麗)。
    - Zhè zuò jiànzhù fēicháng huálì.

- **pinyin:** qiáo

    - qiáo

  - **interpretation:** The character 僑 (qiáo), meaning 'to live abroad', combines the semantic element 亻 (rén, 'person') with the phonetic 喬 (qiáo, 'tall'). This suggests the notion of a 'person' living 'high or distant', metaphorically extending to residing in a different land, or 'abroad'.
