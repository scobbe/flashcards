## Qin Shi Huang (first emperor of Qin)
### Qín Shǐhuáng
---
- **definition:** Qin Shi Huang (first emperor of Qin)
- **pinyin:** Qín Shǐhuáng
- **components:**
  - 秦
    - qín
    - Qin dynasty
  - 始
    - shǐ
    - beginning; to start
  - 皇
    - huáng
    - emperor; royal; majestic
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 秦 (qín, "Qin dynasty") + 始 (shǐ, "beginning") + 皇 (huáng, "emperor")
  - **interpretation:** 秦 (qín, "Qin dynasty") refers to the state and dynasty that ultimately unified China. 始 (shǐ, "beginning") marks the commencement of the imperial rule under the unification. 皇 (huáng, "emperor") indicates the supreme ruler. Together, the title 秦始皇 (Qín Shǐhuáng) identifies the first emperor of the Qin dynasty, who began the imperial era by centralizing power.
- **examples:**
  - 秦始皇创(創)造了中国(國)的第一个(個)中央集权(權)国(國)家。
    - Qín Shǐhuáng chuàngzàole Zhōngguó de dì yī gè zhōngyāng jíquán guójiā.
    - Qin Shi Huang created the first centralized government in China.
  - 秦始皇下令修建万(萬)里长(長)城。
    - Qín Shǐhuáng xiàlìng xiūjiàn Wànlǐ Chángchéng.
    - Qin Shi Huang ordered the construction of the Great Wall.
  - 很多历(歷)史故事都围(圍)绕(繞)秦始皇展开(開)。
    - Hěn duō lìshǐ gùshì dōu wéirào Qín Shǐhuáng zhǎnkāi.
    - Many historical stories revolve around Qin Shi Huang.
