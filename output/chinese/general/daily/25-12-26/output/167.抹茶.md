## matcha
### mǒchá
---
- **definition:** matcha
- **pinyin:** mǒchá
- **components:**
  - 抹
    - mǒ
    - apply; rub; grind
  - 茶
    - chá
    - tea
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 抹 (mǒ, "apply; rub; grind") 茶 (chá, "tea")
  - **interpretation:** 抹茶 (mǒchá, "matcha") refers to a type of finely ground powdered green tea. The term combines 抹 (mǒ, "apply; rub; grind"), highlighting the process of grinding the tea leaves, with 茶 (chá, "tea"), indicating the primary ingredient. This describes the unique method of preparing matcha, distinct from other forms of tea, which involves grinding tea leaves into a powder.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)喝抹茶。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē mǒchá.
    - I like drinking matcha.
  - 她用抹茶做蛋糕，味道非常独(獨)特。
    - Tā yòng mǒchá zuò dàngāo, wèidào fēicháng dútè.
    - She makes cakes with matcha, and the taste is very unique.
  - 清晨时(時)我品尝(嚐)抹茶，感受晨光美好。
    - Qīngchén shí wǒ pǐncháng mǒchá, gǎnshòu chénguāng měihǎo.
    - In the early morning, I taste matcha and feel the beauty of the morning light.
### 抹茶 → 抹
- **definition:** apply; rub; grind
- **pinyin:** mǒ
- **components:**
  - 扌(手)

  - 末

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 扌(手) (shǒu, "hand") + 末 (mò, "end") =

  - **interpretation:** The character 抹(mǒ) combines 扌(手) (shǒu, "hand") indicating an action performed by the hand, and 末 (mò, "end"), which contributes to the phonetic element. Together, they suggest the action of applying or spreading something with the hand.
- **examples:**

### 抹茶 → 抹 → 扌
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stylized hand. The top stroke is the bent middle finger, and the horizontal strokes each represent two fingers.
  - **interpretation:** 扌 is a simplified form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), retaining the key elements that visually represent a human hand. It is commonly used as a component in other characters related to hand actions.
  - **simplification:** 扌 is derived as a cursive or shorthand form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), making it easier to incorporate into complex characters as a radical or component.
- **examples:**
  - 他伸出扌(手)接住了飞(飛)来(來)的球。
    - Tā shēnchū shǒu jiēzhù le fēilái de qiú.
    - He stretched out his hand to catch the flying ball.
  - 她用扌(手)制(製)作美丽(麗)的陶艺(藝)，展现(現)她的匠心。
    - Tā yòng shǒu zhìzuò měilì de táoyì, zhǎnxiàn tā de jiàngxīn.
    - She used her hand to create beautiful pottery, showcasing her craftsmanship.
  - 艺(藝)术(術)家细(細)心雕刻扌(手)的每一细(細)节(節)。
    - Yìshùjiā xìxīn diāokè shǒu de měiyī xìjié.
    - The artist meticulously carved every detail of the hand.

### 抹茶 → 茶
- **definition:** tea
- **pinyin:** chá
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 余
    - yú
    - I; surplus
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, "grass") + phonetic 余 (yú) ->
    - modified from archaic 荼 (bitter plant) for tea
  - **interpretation:** The character 茶 (chá, "tea") combines 艹 (cǎo, "grass") as the semantic element, indicating a plant-related concept, with 余 (yú) serving as the phonetic component. Originally, 茶 was a modification of the archaic character 荼 which referred to a 'bitter plant.' This association with a bitter plant reflects the historical use of tea leaves, which have a bitter taste, thereby making 茶 appropriate for representing 'tea.'
- **examples:**
  - 奶奶每天早上喝茶。
    - Nǎinai měitiān zǎoshang hē chá.
    - Grandma drinks tea every morning.
  - 这(這)家茶馆(館)的绿(綠)茶味道很好。
    - Zhè jiā cháguǎn de lǜchá wèidào hěn hǎo.
    - The green tea at this teahouse tastes very good.
  - 你想喝红(紅)茶还(還)是绿(綠)茶？
    - Nǐ xiǎng hē hóngchá háishì lǜchá?
    - Do you want to drink black tea or green tea?

### 抹茶 → 茶 → 余(餘)
- **traditional:** 餘
- **definition:** I; surplus
- **pinyin:** yú
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - food
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 食 (shí, 'food') + phonetic 余(餘) (yú, 'I') ->
    - remains of food ->
    - 'excess, surplus'
  - **interpretation:** The character 餘 evolved to represent the concept of 'excess' or 'surplus' through a phono-semantic compound. It combines the semantic component 食 (shí, 'food'), representing food or consumption, with the phonetic component 余(餘) (yú, 'I'), to indicate leftovers or remains of food. This directly suggests an aspect of having more than what is needed, thus an 'excess' or 'surplus'.
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 餘 was simplified to 余 by removing the semantic component 食 (shí, 'food'), leaving only the phonetic component.
- **examples:**
  - 余(餘)愿(願)以诗(詩)书(書)为(為)乐(樂)。
    - Yú yuàn yǐ shīshū wéi lè.
    - I wish to take pleasure in poetry and books.
  - 余(餘)在此地，心安吾归(歸)。
    - Yú zài cǐdì, xīn ān wú guī.
    - I am here, at peace and at home.
  - 余(餘)莫负(負)此生。
    - Yú mò fù cǐ shēng.
    - I should not waste this life.
