- **definition:** matcha
- **pinyin:** mǒchá
- **components:**
  - 抹
    - mǒ
    - apply; rub; grind
  - 茶
    - chá
    - tea
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 抹 (mǒ, "apply; rub; grind") 茶 (chá, "tea")
  - **interpretation:** 抹茶 (mǒchá, "matcha") refers to a type of finely ground powdered green tea. The term combines 抹 (mǒ, "apply; rub; grind"), highlighting the process of grinding the tea leaves, with 茶 (chá, "tea"), indicating the primary ingredient. This describes the unique method of preparing matcha, distinct from other forms of tea, which involves grinding tea leaves into a powder.

### 抹茶 → 茶
- **definition:** tea
- **pinyin:** chá
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 余
    - yú
    - I; surplus
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, "grass") + phonetic 余 (yú) ->
    - modified from archaic 荼 (bitter plant) for tea
  - **interpretation:** The character 茶 (chá, "tea") combines 艹 (cǎo, "grass") as the semantic element, indicating a plant-related concept, with 余 (yú) serving as the phonetic component. Originally, 茶 was a modification of the archaic character 荼 which referred to a 'bitter plant.' This association with a bitter plant reflects the historical use of tea leaves, which have a bitter taste, thereby making 茶 appropriate for representing 'tea.'
- **examples:**

- **definition:** I; surplus
- **pinyin:** yú
- **components:**

  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 食 (shí, 'food') + phonetic 余(餘) (yú, 'I') ->
    - remains of food ->
    - 'excess, surplus'
  - **interpretation:** The character 餘 evolved to represent the concept of 'excess' or 'surplus' through a phono-semantic compound. It combines the semantic component 食 (shí, 'food'), representing food or consumption, with the phonetic component 余(餘) (yú, 'I'), to indicate leftovers or remains of food. This directly suggests an aspect of having more than what is needed, thus an 'excess' or 'surplus'.
