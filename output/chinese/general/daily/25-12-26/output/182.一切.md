## everything
### yīqiè
---
- **definition:** everything
- **pinyin:** yīqiè
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; single; entire
  - 切
    - qiè
    - all; each; every; to cut
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The word 一切 (yīqiè, "everything") is composed of two characters: 一 (yī, "one") and 切 (qiè, "all; each").
  - **interpretation:** The character 一 (yī, "one") signifies singularity and entirety, suggesting totality when combined with 切 (qiè, "all; each"), which extends the concept to mean 'collectively' or 'every single one.' Together, 一切 (yīqiè) embodies the idea of 'everything' by emphasizing the inclusion of all possible parts or elements as a single unified whole.
- **examples:**
  - 一切都会(會)好起来(來)。
    - Yīqiè dōu huì hǎo qǐlái.
    - Everything will get better.
  - 我们(們)需要为(為)一切做好准(準)备(備)。
    - Wǒmen xūyào wèi yīqiè zuò hǎo zhǔnbèi.
    - We need to prepare for everything.
  - 他失去了对(對)一切的信心。
    - Tā shīqùle duì yīqiè de xìnxīn.
    - He lost confidence in everything.
### 一切 → 一
- **definition:** one; single; entire
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** A horizontal stroke representing the concept of 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** The character 一(yī, "one") is a simple horizontal line signifying the singular and unified concept of 'one'. It directly represents being singular or the number one.
- **examples:**
  - 我有一个(個)苹(蘋)果。
    - Wǒ yǒu yī gè píngguǒ.
    - I have one apple.
  - 他只看到一只(隻)鸟(鳥)，飞(飛)进(進)了花园(園)。
    - Tā zhǐ kàn dào yī zhī niǎo, fēi jìn le huā yuán.
    - He only saw one bird, which flew into the garden.
  - 一条(條)直路通向远(遠)方。
    - Yī tiáo zhí lù tōng xiàng yuǎn fāng.
    - One straight road leads to the distance.

### 一切 → 切
- **definition:** all; each; every; to cut
- **pinyin:** qiè
- **components:**
  - 七
    - qī
    - seven
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 七 (qī, 'seven') + Semantic: 刀 (dāo, 'knife') =
    - sharp object ->
    - to cut
  - **interpretation:** The character 切(qiè, 'to cut') combines the sound component 七(qī, 'seven') with the semantic component 刀(dāo, 'knife'). This implies the action of cutting with a sharp tool.
- **examples:**
  - 厨(廚)房的刀非常锋(鋒)利，切菜时(時)请(請)小心。
    - chúfáng de dāo fēicháng fēnglì, qiè cài shí qǐng xiǎoxīn.
    - The kitchen knife is very sharp, so be careful when cutting vegetables.
  - 一切问(問)题(題)都能解决(決)。
    - yīqiè wèntí dōu néng jiějué.
    - Every problem can be solved.

### 一切 → 切 → 七
- **definition:** seven
- **pinyin:** qī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a vertical stroke intersecting a horizontal stroke
  - **interpretation:** The character 七(qī, 'seven') is a pictogram that visually represents a structure formed by intersecting strokes. Its form does not directly convey numerical value but was historically used because of its visual structure, which resembles cuts or divisions.
- **examples:**
  - 他有七个(個)苹(蘋)果。
    - Tā yǒu qī gè píngguǒ.
    - He has seven apples.
  - 今天是七月七日。
    - Jīntiān shì qī yuè qī rì.
    - Today is July 7th.
  - 我们(們)七点(點)钟(鐘)见(見)面。
    - Wǒmen qī diǎn zhōng jiànmiàn.
    - We'll meet at seven o'clock.

### 一切 → 切 → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a knife
  - **interpretation:** 刀 (dāo, "knife") is a pictographic representation of a knife or blade, with a sharp edge and a handle, resembling the form of a basic cutting tool.
- **examples:**
  - 他用锋(鋒)利的刀，切开(開)蔬菜。
    - tā yòng fēnglì de dāo, qiē kāi shūcài.
    - He uses a sharp knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这(這)把刀非常锋(鋒)利。
    - zhè bǎ dāo fēicháng fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
  - 厨(廚)师(師)小心地持刀，为(為)客人切肉。
    - chúshī xiǎoxīn de chí dāo, wèi kèrén qiē ròu.
    - The chef holds the knife carefully and slices meat for the guest.
