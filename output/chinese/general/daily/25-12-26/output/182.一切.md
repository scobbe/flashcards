## everything
### yīqiè
---
- **definition:** everything
- **pinyin:** yīqiè
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; single; entire
  - 切
    - qiè
    - all; each; every; to cut
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The word 一切 (yīqiè, "everything") is composed of two characters: 一 (yī, "one") and 切 (qiè, "all; each").
  - **interpretation:** The character 一 (yī, "one") signifies singularity and entirety, suggesting totality when combined with 切 (qiè, "all; each"), which extends the concept to mean 'collectively' or 'every single one.' Together, 一切 (yīqiè) embodies the idea of 'everything' by emphasizing the inclusion of all possible parts or elements as a single unified whole.
- **examples:**
  - 一( )切( )都( )会(會)好( )起( )来(來)。
    - Yīqiè dōu huì hǎo qǐlái.
    - Everything will get better.
  - 我( )们(們)需( )要( )为(為)一( )切( )做( )好( )准(準)备(備)。
    - Wǒmen xūyào wèi yīqiè zuò hǎo zhǔnbèi.
    - We need to prepare for everything.
  - 他( )失( )去( )了( )对(對)一( )切( )的( )信( )心( )。
    - Tā shīqùle duì yīqiè de xìnxīn.
    - He lost confidence in everything.
