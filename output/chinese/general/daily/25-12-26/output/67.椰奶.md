## coconut milk
### yēnǎi
---
- **definition:** coconut milk
- **pinyin:** yēnǎi
- **components:**
  - 椰
    - yē
    - coconut
  - 奶
    - nǎi
    - milk
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 椰 (yē, "coconut") refers to the coconut palm and its fruit, which provides the core ingredient in this word. 奶 (nǎi, "milk") describes the liquid aspect, which is derived from the coconut and resembles milk in both texture and use.
  - **interpretation:** 椰 (yē, "coconut") refers to the coconut fruit, which is rich in a creamy liquid used similarly to milk. 奶 (nǎi, "milk") indicates the white, milk-like liquid extracted from the coconut, used in cooking and beverages for its similar properties to animal milk.
- **examples:**
  - 椰奶是我最喜欢(歡)的饮(飲)品之一。
    - Yēnǎi shì wǒ zuì xǐhuān de yǐnpǐn zhī yī.
    - Coconut milk is one of my favorite drinks.
  - 他在咖啡里(裡)加了一点(點)椰奶，味道非常独(獨)特。
    - Tā zài kāfēi lǐ jiā le yīdiǎn yēnǎi, wèidào fēicháng dútè.
    - He added a bit of coconut milk to the coffee, and the taste was very unique.
  - 这(這)个(個)菜需要椰奶，请(請)帮(幫)我买(買)一些。
    - Zhège cài xūyào yēnǎi, qǐng bāng wǒ mǎi yīxiē.
    - This dish needs coconut milk, please help me buy some.
### 椰奶 → 椰
- **definition:** coconut
- **pinyin:** yē
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 耶
    - yē
    - question particle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 木 (mù, "tree") + phonetic 耶 (yē) ->
    - coconut
  - **interpretation:** The semantic component 木 (mù, "tree") indicates that 椰(yē, "coconut") is related to trees, specifically the coconut tree, while the phonetic component 耶 (yē) suggests the pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 椰子的汁很甜美。

    - The juice of the coconut is very sweet.
  - 他们(們)在沙滩(灘)上喝椰子水。
    - Tāmen zài shātān shàng hē yēzi shuǐ.
    - They are drinking coconut water on the beach.
  - 我喜欢(歡)把椰子和水果一起搅(攪)拌。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān bǎ yēzi hé shuǐguǒ yīqǐ jiǎobàn.
    - I like to mix coconut with fruits.

### 椰奶 → 椰 → 耶
- **definition:** question particle
- **pinyin:** yē
- **components:**
  - 邪
    - xié
    - evil
  - 牙
    - yá
    - tooth
  - 邑
    - yì
    - town; city
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** Corruption of 邪 (牙 + 邑)
  - **interpretation:** The character 耶 is a corruption of 邪, which originally combined 牙(yá, "tooth") as phonetic and 邑(yì, "town; city") as semantic component, suggesting a place name. The character was later borrowed for various meanings, including its use as a question particle in contemporary usage.
- **examples:**
  - 你今天不参(參)加活动(動)耶？
    - Nǐ jīntiān bù cānjiā huódòng yē？
    - You're not attending the event today, huh?
  - 这(這)件事你不知道耶！
    - Zhè jiàn shì nǐ bù zhīdào yē！
    - You didn't know about this, huh!
  - 他昨天没(沒)有来(來)上班耶？
    - Tā zuótiān méiyǒu lái shàngbān yē？
    - He didn't come to work yesterday, huh?
### 椰奶 → 椰 → 耶 → 邪
- **definition:** evil
- **pinyin:** xié
- **components:**
  - 牙
    - yá
    - tooth
  - 邑
    - yì
    - town; city
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 牙 (yá, "tooth") + semantic 邑 (yì, "town; city") ->
    - originally a place name ->
    - borrowed for "wrong; evil"
  - **interpretation:** The character originally referred to a place name, as indicated by the semantic component 邑 (yì, "town; city"), suggesting a geographic context. The phonetic component 牙 (yá, "tooth") contributes the sound. Over time, it was phonically repurposed for the sense "wrong; evil," which was initially represented by the different character 衺.
- **examples:**
  - 他的动(動)机(機)看起来(來)不太邪。
    - Tā de dòngjī kàn qǐlái bù tài yé.
    - His motives do not seem very wrong.
  - 如果做坏(壞)事是邪的，那么(麼)做好事就是对(對)的。
    - Rúguǒ zuò huàishì shì yé de, nàme zuò hǎoshì jiù shì duì de.
    - If doing bad things is wrong, then doing good things is right.

### 椰奶 → 椰 → 耶 → 邪 → 牙
- **definition:** tooth
- **pinyin:** yá
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a pair of elephant tusks ->
    - used to indicate human molars
  - **interpretation:** The character 牙 (yá, 'tooth') originated as a pictogram representing elephant tusks, which resemble human molars in shape. This specific imagery was chosen to signify back teeth rather than incisors.

### 椰奶 → 奶
- **definition:** milk
- **pinyin:** nǎi
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - woman
  - 乃
    - nǎi
    - thus; so
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 女 (nǚ, "woman") + phonetic 乃 (nǎi) ->
    - milk
  - **interpretation:** 奶(nǎi, "milk") combines 女 (nǚ, "woman"), linking to maternal qualities or nurturing, with the phonetic 乃 (nǎi), providing the sound. This reflects the role of women in breastfeeding or producing milk.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)喝牛奶。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan hē niúnǎi.
    - I like to drink milk.
  - 婴(嬰)儿(兒)需要母奶。
    - Yīng'ér xūyào mǔnǎi.
    - The baby needs mother's milk.
  - 奶茶是我最喜欢(歡)的饮(飲)料。
    - Nǎichá shì wǒ zuì xǐhuān de yǐnliào.
    - Milk tea is my favorite drink.
### 椰奶 → 奶 → 女
- **definition:** woman
- **pinyin:** nǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a woman kneeling or standing ->
    - enclosed area as remnant of right breast
  - **interpretation:** The character 女(nǚ, "woman") is a pictogram originally depicting a woman with highlighted bodily features, such as the breasts, symbolizing femininity. Its orientation changed from the Oracle Bone script to face right, simplifying over time while still retaining the essence of its original depiction.
- **examples:**
  - 那位才华(華)横(橫)溢的女，赢(贏)得了全场(場)注目。
    - Nà wèi cáihuá héngyì de nǚ, yíng dé le quánchǎng zhùmù.
    - That talented woman won everyone's attention.
  - 古时(時)候‘女’常被用来(來)象征(徵)温(溫)柔，而如今它代表独(獨)立自信。
    - Gǔshíhòu ‘nǚ’ cháng bèi yòng lái xiàngzhēng wēnróu, ér rújīn tā dàibiǎo dúlì zìxìn.
    - In ancient times, the character '女' was often used to symbolize gentleness, whereas today it represents independence and confidence.

- **definition:** thus; so
- **pinyin:** nǎi
- **etymology:**

  - **description:** Depicts a simplified hand holding a thread
  - **interpretation:** 乃 (nǎi, 'so') originally depicted a hand holding an object, symbolizing a connection or linkage, which lends itself to meanings related to causation and result, like 'so' or 'then.'
- **examples:**
