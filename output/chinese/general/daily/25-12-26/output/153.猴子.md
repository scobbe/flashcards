## monkey
### hóuzi
---
- **definition:** monkey
- **pinyin:** hóuzi
- **components:**
  - 猴
    - hóu
    - monkey
  - 子
    - zi
    - child; small thing
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 猴 (hóu, "monkey") + 子 (zi, "child; small thing") forms "monkey"; here 子 (zi, "child") functions as a suffix indicating a small or familiar object/creature.
  - **interpretation:** 猴 (hóu, "monkey") represents the main concept of the animal. The addition of 子 (zi, "child") acts as a diminutive or familiarizing suffix, commonly used in Chinese to indicate a small or endearing form of the noun it follows. This combination suggests a small or familiar version of the animal, akin to how "monkey" is understood in English. The use of 子 reflects a linguistic pattern common in Chinese to create affectionate or casual noun forms.
- **examples:**
  - 猴子在树(樹)上跳来(來)跳去。
    - Hóuzi zài shù shàng tiào lái tiào qù.
    - The monkey is jumping around in the tree.
  - 我们(們)在动(動)物园(園)看到了一只(隻)猴子。
    - Wǒmen zài dòngwùyuán kàn dàole yī zhī hóuzi.
    - We saw a monkey at the zoo.
  - 猴子的动(動)作非常敏捷。
    - Hóuzi de dòngzuò fēicháng mǐnjié.
    - The monkey's movements are very agile.
### 猴子 → 猴
- **definition:** monkey
- **pinyin:** hóu
- **components:**
  - 犬
    - quǎn
    - dog
  - 侯
    - hóu
    - marquis; nobleman
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 犭(犬) (quǎn, "dog") + phonetic 侯 (hóu) ->
    - monkey
  - **interpretation:** The character 猴(hóu, "monkey") combines 犭(犬) (quǎn, "dog"), which represents animals, with 侯 (hóu), serving as the phonetic component to suggest the sound. The combination naturally denotes an animal resembling a monkey.
  - **simplification:** 猴 → 猴: remains unchanged in simplification.
- **examples:**
  - 猴子喜欢(歡)吃香蕉。
    - Hóuzi xǐhuān chī xiāngjiāo.
    - Monkeys like to eat bananas.
  - 山上的猴子很顽(頑)皮。
    - Shān shàng de hóuzi hěn wánpí.
    - The monkeys on the mountain are very mischievous.
  - 他像个(個)猴子一样(樣)灵(靈)活。
    - Tā xiàng gè hóuzi yīyàng línghuó.
    - He is as agile as a monkey.

### 猴子 → 猴 → 犬
- **definition:** dog
- **pinyin:** quǎn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the side view of a dog
  - **interpretation:** The character 犬 (quǎn, 'dog') visually resembles a dog viewed from the side, with features that suggest the head and body of the animal. This pictorial representation allows for an intuitive understanding of its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他的犬非常温(溫)顺(順)。
    - Tā de quǎn fēicháng wēnshùn。
    - His dog is very gentle.
  - 我家的犬喜欢(歡)跑步，也爱(愛)安静(靜)。
    - Wǒ jiā de quǎn xǐhuān pǎobù, yě ài ānjìng。
    - The dog at my home likes to run and also loves tranquility.

### 猴子 → 猴 → 侯
- **definition:** marquis; nobleman
- **pinyin:** hóu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a target for arrows
  - **interpretation:** The character 侯(hóu, "marquis") originally depicted a target used in archery. This visual connection stems from the idea that a marquis, as a noble rank, could be symbolically associated with such a prestigious or targeted position in society.
- **examples:**
  - 古代的皇帝会(會)封爵位给(給)侯。
    - Gǔdài de huángdì huì fēng juéwèi gěi hóu.
    - In ancient times, emperors would grant titles to marquises.
  - 这(這)个(個)地区(區)曾经(經)由一位侯统(統)治。
    - Zhège dìqū céngjīng yóu yī wèi hóu tǒngzhì.
    - This region was once ruled by a marquis.
  - 侯爵拥(擁)有广(廣)阔(闊)的领(領)地，他们(們)的权(權)力不容小觑(覷)。
    - Hóujué yǒngyǒu guǎngkuò de lǐngdì, tāmen de quánlì bùróng xiǎoqù.
    - Marquises had vast territories, and their power was not to be underestimated.

### 猴子 → 子
- **definition:** child; small thing
- **pinyin:** zi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a baby with a large head and spread arms, with legs wrapped in a blanket
  - **interpretation:** The character 子(zǐ, "child") is a pictorial representation of a baby, emphasizing characteristics like a large head and outstretched arms typical of infants, which conveys the essence of a child. This visual element helps infer its meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)小子很调(調)皮。
    - Zhè xiǎo zǐ hěn tiáopí.
    - This kid is very naughty.
  - 他的小子爱(愛)打篮(籃)球，每天都锻(鍛)炼(煉)身体(體)。
    - Tā de xiǎo zǐ ài dǎ lánqiú, měitiān dōu duànliàn shēntǐ.
    - His child loves playing basketball, and practices every day.
  - 孤独(獨)的子需要关(關)爱(愛)。
    - Gūdú de zǐ xūyào guān'ài.
    - A lonely child needs care.
