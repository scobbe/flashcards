## Zhongshan District, Taipei
### Zhōngshān Qū
---
- **traditional:** 中山區
- **definition:** Zhongshan District, Taipei
- **pinyin:** Zhōngshān Qū
- **components:**
  - 中
    - zhōng
    - middle; center; within
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain; hill
  - 區
    - qū
    - district; area; region
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 中 (zhōng, "middle") 山 (shān, "mountain") 區 (qū, "district")
  - **interpretation:** 中 (zhōng, "middle") suggests centrality or being at the center, often used in names to signify the importance or central position of a place. 山 (shān, "mountain") refers to a mountain or a hilly area, commonly appearing in geographical names to indicate natural topography or to honor Sun Yat-sen (孫中山), a revered figure in Chinese history known as the "Father of the Nation." Therefore, 中山 (Zhōngshān, "central mountain") often signifies a place named in honor of Sun Yat-sen. Finally, 區 (qū, "district") denotes an administrative division or area. In the context of 中山區 (Zhōngshān Qū), it indicates a district named Zhongshan.
- **examples:**
  - 中山区(區)是台北的重要商业(業)区(區)。
    - Zhōngshān Qū shì Táiběi de zhòngyào shāngyè qū.
    - Zhongshan District is an important commercial area in Taipei.
  - 许(許)多游(遊)客喜欢(歡)在中山区(區)购(購)物。
    - Xǔduō yóukè xǐhuān zài Zhōngshān Qū gòuwù.
    - Many tourists enjoy shopping in Zhongshan District.
  - 中山区(區)有许(許)多咖啡馆(館)，晚上特别(別)热(熱)闹(鬧)。
    - Zhōngshān Qū yǒu xǔduō kāfēiguǎn, wǎnshàng tèbié rènào.
    - Zhongshan District has many cafes, and it is especially lively at night.

### 中山区(區) → 山
- **definition:** mountain; hill
- **pinyin:** shān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three mountain peaks
  - **interpretation:** The character 山(shān, "mountain") visually represents the concept of a mountain using three vertical lines that suggest peaks. This depiction is consistent with early forms found in Oracle Bone Script, emphasizing the notion of natural elevation associated with mountains.
- **examples:**

    - Yuǎnchù de shān yǐnyuē zài yún zhōng, lìng rén xīnkuàng shényí.

### 中山区(區) → 區
- **definition:** district; area; region
- **pinyin:** qū
- **components:**
  - 匸
    - xì
    - concealed
  - 品
    - pǐn
    - goods
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 匸 (xì, "concealed") + 品 (pǐn, "goods") =
    - many people or goods concealed in a space ->
    - area
  - **interpretation:** The character 區 (qū, "area") illustrates the concept of an enclosed space with multiple items or individuals represented by 品 (pǐn, "goods") within 匸 (xì, "concealed"). This evokes the sense of a defined area or district where such items are contained.
  - **simplification:** The traditional component 品 (pǐn, "goods") was replaced by 乂 (yì, "to govern") in the simplified form, 区 (区), keeping the enclosure concept.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)一区(區)的房价(價)很高。
    - Zhè yī qū de fángjià hěn gāo.
    - The house prices in this area are very high.
  - 他们(們)住在城市的南区(區)。
    - Tāmen zhù zài chéngshì de nán qū.
    - They live in the southern district of the city.
  - 这(這)个(個)区(區)以其丰(豐)富的文化遗(遺)产(產)而闻(聞)名。
    - Zhège qū yǐ qí fēngfù de wénhuà yíchǎn ér wénmíng.
    - This area is famous for its rich cultural heritage.

### 中山区(區) → 區 → 匸
- **definition:** concealed
- **pinyin:** xì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 𠃊 (symbolic graphic) ->
    - depicts an enclosure hiding something or someone ->
    - evolved to represent a concealed space or item
  - **interpretation:** The character 匸(xì, 'box') is a pictogram representing a concealed or enclosed space. Its graphical form suggests something contained or hidden, which aligns with its meaning as a 'box' or an enclosure. Unlike other related pictograms that depict physical containers, 匸 emphasizes the aspect of concealment or secrecy.
- **examples:**
  - 他把匸搬到了房间(間)角落。

  - 工厂(廠)工人把旧(舊)匸清理了，然后(後)再利用。
    - Gōngchǎng gōngrén bǎ jiù xì qīnglǐ le, ránhòu zài lìyòng.

### 中山区(區) → 區 → 品
- **definition:** goods
- **pinyin:** pǐn
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - Three 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') arranged together =
    - multiple mouths speaking or consuming ->
    - representing many or various items
  - **interpretation:** The use of three mouths suggests plurality or variety. It's similar in structure to 晶 (jīng, 'sparkle'), which uses three 日 (rì, 'sun') to suggest brightness or vibrancy with multiple light sources.
- **examples:**

### 中山区(區) → 區 → 品 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。

    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
