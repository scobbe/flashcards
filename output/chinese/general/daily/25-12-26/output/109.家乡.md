## hometown
### jiāxiāng
---
- **traditional:** 家鄉
- **definition:** hometown
- **pinyin:** jiāxiāng
- **components:**
  - 家
    - jiā
    - home; family
  - 乡(鄉)
    - xiāng
    - rural; hometown
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 家 (jiā, "home; family") refers to one's house or the family residing there, capturing the sense of a personal base; 鄉 (xiāng, "rural; hometown") denotes a village or rural area where one may have origins or familial connections.
  - **interpretation:** 家 (jiā, "home; family") conveys the idea of an individual's central living space or familial nucleus. 鄉 (xiāng, "hometown") implies one's place of origin, associated with rural roots or local communities. Thus, 家鄉 (jiāxiāng, "hometown") brings together the concept of a family base and local origins, capturing the emotional and historical ties to one's birthplace or formative environment.
- **examples:**
  - 我的家乡(鄉)在一个(個)美丽(麗)的山谷。
    - Wǒ de jiāxiāng zài yīgè měilì de shāngǔ.
    - My hometown is in a beautiful valley.
  - 每年春节(節)我都会(會)回家乡(鄉)。
    - Měinián chūnjié wǒ dūhuì huí jiāxiāng.
    - Every Spring Festival, I return to my hometown.
  - 他离(離)开(開)家乡(鄉)去大城市工作。
    - Tā líkāi jiāxiāng qù dà chéngshì gōngzuò.
    - He left his hometown to work in the big city.
### 家乡(鄉) → 家
- **definition:** home; family
- **pinyin:** jiā
- **components:**
  - 宀
    - mián
    - roof
  - 豕
    - shǐ
    - pig
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 宀 (mián, 'roof') + 豕 (shǐ, 'pig') =
    - pig under a roof ->
    - home, house
  - **interpretation:** The character 家 (jiā, 'home; family; house') uses the pictorial elements of a 'roof' and a 'pig' to evoke an ancient image of a house, where pigs were commonly kept under the same roof as a family, indicating the concept of a home.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱(愛)我的家。
    - Wǒ ài wǒ de jiā.
    - I love my home.
  - 回家路上遇见(見)老朋友，聊起往事。
    - Huí jiā lùshàng yùjiàn lǎo péngyǒu, liáo qǐ wǎngshì.
    - On the way home, I met an old friend, and we reminisced about the past.

### 家乡(鄉) → 家 → 宀
- **definition:** roof
- **pinyin:** mián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a house with four walls ->
    - roof
  - **interpretation:** 宀(mián, "roof") is a pictogram representing a house with four walls. It emphasizes an enclosed space, distinguishing it from similar structures like 广(guǎng, "broad") which depicts a building with three walls. This makes 宀 a logical choice for representing a roof in various characters.
- **examples:**
  - 含有宀的汉(漢)字代表家的温(溫)暖。
    - Hán yǒu mián de hànzì dàibiǎo jiā de wēnnuǎn.
    - Chinese characters that include the radical '宀' represent the warmth of home.
  - 屋顶(頂)遮风(風)挡(擋)雨，家园(園)散发(發)温(溫)馨。
    - Wūdǐng zhē fēng dǎng yǔ, jiāyuán sànfā wēnxīn.
    - The roof shields from wind and rain, and the home radiates warmth.

### 家乡(鄉) → 家 → 豕
- **definition:** pig
- **pinyin:** shǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a pig
  - **interpretation:** The character 豕(shǐ, "pig") directly depicts the side profile of a pig, with elements suggesting the animal's physical characteristics. This form visually represents the animal itself, making it easy to associate the character with the meaning of 'pig' or 'boar'.
- **examples:**
  - 古人用豕作为(為)祭品。
    - Gǔrén yòng shǐ zuòwéi jìpǐn.
    - The ancients used pigs as offerings.
  - 在山中，我们(們)发(發)现(現)了豕的踪(蹤)迹(跡)。
    - Zài shānzhōng, wǒmen fāxiànle shǐ de zōngjì.
    - In the mountains, we found traces of boars.
  - 他们(們)家养(養)了一些豕，用于(於)农(農)耕。
    - Tāmen jiā yǎngle yīxiē shǐ, yòng yú nónggēng.
    - Their family raised some pigs for farming.

### 家乡(鄉) → 乡(鄉)
- **traditional:** 鄉
- **definition:** rural; hometown
- **pinyin:** xiāng
- **components:**
  - 𠨍
    - two people facing each other
    - two people facing each other
  - 皀
    - round-mouthed food vessel
    - round-mouthed food vessel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 𠨍 (𠨍) (two people facing each other) + 皀 (皀) (round-mouthed food vessel) ->
    - original meaning 'to feast' ->
    - phonetic borrowing or semantic shifting to mean 'village'
  - **interpretation:** The character initially represented two people facing a food vessel, symbolizing communal feasting, which was a central part of village life. Over time, it came to denote 'village' through borrowing or semantic shift, aligning with the social aspect it originally depicted.
  - **simplification:** 乡(鄉) simplifies by using a component resembling 良 (liáng, 'good'), unrelated to 郎 (láng, 'young man'), to streamline its form, based on 皀 during the clerical script development.
- **examples:**
  - 我来(來)自乡(鄉)。
    - Wǒ láizì xiāng.
    - I come from the countryside.
  - 我怀(懷)念乡(鄉)，那里(裡)有我的故事。
    - Wǒ huáiniàn xiāng, nàlǐ yǒu wǒ de gùshì.
    - I miss my hometown, where my stories lie.
  - 他常回乡(鄉)，寻(尋)找旧(舊)日情怀(懷)。
    - Tā cháng huí xiāng, xúnzhǎo jiù rì qínghuái.
    - He often returns to his hometown, seeking the old sentiments.

### 家乡(鄉) → 乡(鄉) → 𠨍 → 卯
- **definition:** fourth earthly branch
- **pinyin:** mǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a pair of knives used for butchering ->
    - associated with sacrifice ->
    - lost original violent meaning after cultural shifts
  - **interpretation:** Initially depicted the physical act of using knives for ritual sacrifices, a practice associated with the Shang dynasty. As cultural practices changed and this ritual fell out of favor, the original sense of the character became obscure. The folk etymology has misinterpreted it as a reversed open door or woodworking mortise, but these interpretations are not based on historical evidence.
- **examples:**
  - 在地支系统(統)中，卯是十二地支之一。
    - Zài dì zhī xìtǒng zhōng, mǎo shì shí'èr dì zhī zhī yī.
    - In the Earthly Branches system, 'mǎo' is one of the twelve Earthly Branches.
  - 兔年对(對)应(應)的地支是卯。
    - Tù nián duìyìng de dì zhī shì mǎo.
    - The Earthly Branch corresponding to the Year of the Rabbit is 'mǎo'.
  - 很多汉(漢)字都有卯这(這)个(個)声(聲)旁。
    - Hěn duō hànzì dōu yǒu mǎo zhège shēngpáng.
    - Many Chinese characters have 'mǎo' as a phonetic component.
