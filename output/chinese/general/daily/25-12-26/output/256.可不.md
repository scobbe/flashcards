### kěbù
---
- **definition:** exactly; right
- **pinyin:** kěbù
- **components:**
  - 可
    - kě
    - possible; can
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 可 (kě, "possible; can") + 不 (bù, "not")
  - **interpretation:** 可 (kě, "possible;7 can") suggests ability or possibility, while 不 (bù, "not") negates a statement. Together, they form an expression used for strong affirmation or agreement; it's like saying 'how could it not be!', conveying 'exactly!' or 'right!'. It creates an emphatic acceptance or agreement with what was stated.
