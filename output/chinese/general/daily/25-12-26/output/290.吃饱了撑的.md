## have nothing better to do (lit. stuffed from eating)
### chī bǎo le chēng de
---
- **traditional:** 吃飽了撐的
- **definition:** have nothing better to do (lit. stuffed from eating)
- **pinyin:** chī bǎo le chēng de
- **components:**
  - 吃
    - chī
    - eat
  - 饱(飽)
    - bǎo
    - full; satisfied
  - 了
    - le
    - particle indicating completion
  - 撑(撐)
    - chēng
    - stuffed; support
  - 的
    - de
    - particle for emphasis
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 吃 (chī, "eat") + 饱(飽) (bǎo, "full") + 了 (le, a particle indicating completion) + 撑(撐) (chēng, "stuffed") + 的 (de, a particle used for emphasis)
  - **interpretation:** This expression humorously suggests being so overly full from eating, one has nothing better to do than find trivial activities. It plays on the idea that after being 拷(bao) (饱,"full") and 撑(chēng,"stuffed"), one idles and finds unnecessary tasks due to excessive satiety.
- **examples:**
  - 他吃饱(飽)了撑(撐)的，在楼(樓)下闲(閒)逛。
    - Tā chī bǎo le chēng de, zài lóu xià xián guàng.
    - He has nothing better to do but wander around downstairs.
  - 真是吃饱(飽)了撑(撐)的！
    - Zhēn shì chī bǎo le chēng de!
    - Really, you've got nothing better to do!

- **pinyin:** chī

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic 契 (qì)
  - **interpretation:** The character 喫 (chī, "to eat") is composed of the semantic component 口 (kǒu, "mouth"), indicating the action is related to the mouth, and the phonetic component 契 (qì), suggesting the sound. The choice of 契 as a phonetic element was based on its phonological match rather than semantic relevance, guiding the pronunciation in the context of using the mouth—fundamentally linked to acts like eating.
  - **simplification:** The character 喫 was simplified to 吃 by replacing 契 (qì) with 乞 (qǐ), retaining the mouth radical to preserve the core semantic connection related to eating.
