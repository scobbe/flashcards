## surname Su; also shorthand for Jiangsu
### Sū
---
- **traditional:** 蘇
- **definition:** surname Su; also shorthand for Jiangsu
- **pinyin:** Sū
- **components:**
  - 艹(艸)
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 穌
    - sū
    - revive
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 艹(艸) (cǎo, "grass") + 穌 (sū, "revive") =
    - grass reviving ->
    - used for the surname Su
  - **interpretation:** The character 苏(蘇) (sū) combines the semantic 艹(艸) (cǎo, "grass") with the phonetic 穌 (sū, "revive"). The association of grass with revival is intuitive, as grass regenerates and grows back after unfavorable conditions. This makes it a fitting character for representing regeneration or a return to life, and by extension, the surname Su.
  - **simplification:** 艹(艸) (cǎo, "grass") retained to suggest plant-like revival, following simplification patterns common in Chinese script reform, while keeping the overall meaning recognizable.

### 苏(蘇) → 穌
- **definition:** revive
- **pinyin:** sū
- **components:**
  - 魚
    - yú
    - fish
  - 禾
    - hé
    - grain; cereal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 禾 (hé, "grain; cereal") + phonetic 魚 (yú, "fish") ->
    - resuscitate
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 魚 (yú, "fish") as a phonetic component, providing the sound, with 禾 (hé, "grain; cereal") acting as the semantic component. The association with grain suggests nourishment or sustenance, leading to the sense of bringing something back to life or resuscitating it.
  - **simplification:** Simplified form 稣 replaces the phonetic 魚 (yú, "fish") with the simpler component 苏 (sū, "revive") directly retaining the sound while keeping the semantic element intact.
