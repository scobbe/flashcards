## free of charge
### miǎnfèi
---
- **traditional:** 免費
- **definition:** free of charge
- **pinyin:** miǎnfèi
- **components:**
  - 免
    - miǎn
    - to exempt; to spare; to excuse; to dismiss
  - 费(費)
    - fèi
    - cost; fee; expenditure; expense
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 免 (miǎn, "to exempt") + 費 (fèi, "cost") =
    - to exempt from cost ->
    - free of charge
  - **interpretation:** 免 (miǎn, "to exempt") involves the idea of releasing or sparing someone from an obligation, leading to the concept of exemption. 費 (fèi, "cost") refers to a required expenditure or amount needing to be paid. The notion of sparing someone from having to pay the cost directly connects to the idea of being 'free of charge'. This makes use of the concept of alleviating a financial burden altogether.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)家博物馆(館)每周(週)日免费(費)开(開)放。
    - Zhè jiā bówùguǎn měi zhōurì miǎnfèi kāifàng.
    - This museum is free of charge on Sundays.
  - 您可以免费(費)领(領)取一份礼(禮)物。
    - Nín kěyǐ miǎnfèi lǐngqǔ yī fèn lǐwù.
    - You can receive a gift for free.
  - 他们(們)提供免费(費)的咖啡和茶。
    - Tāmen tígōng miǎnfèi de kāfēi hé chá.
    - They offer free coffee and tea.
### 免费(費) → 免
- **definition:** to exempt; to spare; to excuse; to dismiss
- **pinyin:** miǎn
- **components:**
  - 冕
    - miǎn
    - crown
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a man wearing a ceremonial hat ->
    - borrowed for sound and abstract meanings (e.g., 'exempt', 'dismiss', 'avoid') ->
    - 冕 (miǎn, 'crown') confirms original ceremonial context

This ceremonial context still lingers in the derived character 冕 (miǎn, 'crown'), reinforcing the visual origin.
- **examples:**

    - Wèi bìmiǎn wùjiě, wǒmen yào zǐxì gōutōng.

    - Tā bèi miǎnchúle zérèn.

### 免费(費) → 免 → 冕
- **definition:** crown
- **pinyin:** miǎn
- **components:**
  - 冃
    - mào
    - crown
  - 頁(页)
    - yè
    - head
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 冃 (mào, "crown") + 頁(页) (yè, "head") =
    - crown on a head ->
    - crown
  - **interpretation:** The character 冕(miǎn, "crown") combines 冃 (mào, "crown"), depicting the shape of a crown, with 頁(页) (yè, "head"), representing the positioning on a head. This composition effectively illustrates the concept of a crown being worn on one's head.
- **examples:**
  - 国(國)王戴冕，威仪(儀)四射。
    - Guówáng dài miǎn, wēiyí sìshè.
    - The king wears a crown, exuding majestic authority.
  - 古代帝王的冕象征(徵)着(著)无(無)上的荣(榮)耀。
    - Gǔdài dìwáng de miǎn xiàngzhēngzhe wúshàng de róngyào.
    - The crown of ancient emperors symbolizes supreme glory.
### 免费(費) → 免 → 冕 → 冃
- **definition:** crown
- **pinyin:** mào
- **components:**
  - 帽
    - mào
    - hat
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a hat ->
    - original form of 帽
  - **interpretation:** The character 冃 (mào, "crown") is a pictogram depicting a hat, which serves as the original form for 帽 (mào, "hat") where 冃 appears as the component ⺜. The simplicity of its design reflects its function, representing a crown or cap. Over time, as the meaning became more specific, 帽 incorporated additional components like 巾 (jīn, "cloth") to emphasize its connection to headwear.

### 免费(費) → 免 → 冕 → 頁

- **etymology:**

### 免费(費) → 费(費)
- **traditional:** 費
- **definition:** cost; fee; expenditure; expense
- **pinyin:** fèi
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 弗
    - fú
    - not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 貝 (bèi, "cowry") + phonetic 弗 (fú) ->
    - fee; expense; cost
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 貝 (bèi, "cowry"), which is associated with money and value, symbolizing financial transactions, with 弗 (fú) as the phonetic component providing the sound. The association with cowries, historically used as currency, reinforces the notion of 'fee' or 'expense'.
  - **simplification:** 費 was simplified to 费. 貝 (bèi, "cowry") was simplified to 贝(貝) to reduce the character's stroke count.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)项(項)目的费(費)用很高。
    - Zhège xiàngmù de fèiyòng hěn gāo.
    - The expenses for this project are very high.
  - 他每个(個)月的电(電)费(費)都超过(過)了预(預)算。
    - Tā měi gè yuè de diànfèi dōu chāoguòle yùsuàn.
    - His monthly electricity bill always exceeds the budget.
  - 学(學)费(費)是家庭的一大负担(是一個家庭的一大負擔)。
    - Xuéfèi shì yíge jiātíng de yí dà fùdān.
    - Tuition fees are a major burden for a family.
### 免费(費) → 费(費) → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.

  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为(為)货(貨)币(幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
