## electric charge
### diànhè
---
- **traditional:** 電荷
- **definition:** electric charge
- **pinyin:** diànhè
- **components:**
  - 电(電)
    - diàn
    - electric; electricity
  - 荷
    - hè
    - load; burden
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 电(電) (diàn, "electric; electricity") + 荷 (hè, "load; burden") =
    - electric charge
  - **interpretation:** The term 电(電)荷 (diànhè, "electric charge") combines 电(電) (diàn, "electric") and 荷 (hè, "load") to describe the concept of an electric charge as the 'load' or 'burden' carried by particles such as electrons and ions. This concept is foundational in physics and relates to the way electricity is transmitted and stored.
- **examples:**
  - 科学(學)家测(測)量电(電)荷。
    - Kēxuéjiā cèliàng diànhè.
    - Scientists measure electric charge.
  - 电(電)子带(帶)负(負)电(電)荷，质(質)子带(帶)正电(電)荷。
    - Diànzǐ dài fù diànhè, zhìzǐ dài zhèng diànhè.
    - Electrons carry negative electric charge, while protons carry positive electric charge.
  - 根据(據)库(庫)仑(侖)定律，电(電)荷之间(間)的吸引力与(與)距离(離)成反比。
    - Gēnjù Kùlún dìnglǜ, diànhè zhījiān de xīyǐnlì yǔ jùlí chéng fǎnbǐ.
    - According to Coulomb's law, the attractive force between electric charges is inversely proportional to the distance.
### 电(電)荷 → 电(電)
- **traditional:** 電
- **definition:** electric; electricity
- **pinyin:** diàn
- **components:**
  - 雨
    - yǔ
    - rain
  - 申
    - shēn
    - lightning
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 雨 ('rain') + 申 ('lightning') =
    - depiction of a thunderstorm ->
    - electric
  - **interpretation:** The character 電 combines 雨 (yǔ, 'rain') as the semantic element suggesting a natural event involving water, with 申 (shēn, 'lightning') indicating the associated sound and visual phenomenon of a thunderstorm, representing electric energy.
  - **simplification:** 电(電): The rain radical 雨 (yǔ, 'rain') was simplified to a single horizontal line and reduced with fewer strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 商店已经(經)没(沒)有电(電)。

  - 这(這)些电(電)器都是最新型号(號)。

### 电(電)荷 → 荷
- **definition:** load; burden
- **pinyin:** hè
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass; plant
  - 何
    - hé
    - what; why
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艸 (cǎo, "grass; plant") + phonetic 何 (hé) ->
    - lotus; burden
  - **interpretation:** The component 艸 (cǎo, "grass; plant") indicates the character's association with plants, while 何 (hé) provides the sound. This combination reflects the character's primary sense of 'lotus,' a type of water plant. The secondary sense 'burden' comes from the idea of carrying or holding something, which is orthographically unrelated to its parts and is borrowed for its phonetic value rather than derived directly from the component meanings.
- **examples:**

    - Héhuā zài chítáng lǐ kāifàng.
    - The lotus flowers are blooming in the pond.

    - Tā bèifùzhe chénzhòng de zérèn.
    - He bears a heavy burden of responsibility.
  - 莲(蓮)叶(葉)何田田，鱼(魚)戏(戲)莲(蓮)叶(葉)间(間)。
    - Lián yè hé tián tián, yú xì lián yè jiān.

### 电(電)荷 → 荷 → 何
- **definition:** what; why
- **pinyin:** hé
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 戈
    - gē
    - dagger-axe
  - 可
    - kě
    - can
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Originally a pictogram of a man carrying something on their shoulder ->
    - some variants as phono-semantic compound with semantic 人 (rén, "person") + phonetic 戈 (gē, "dagger-axe") or 可 (kě, "can") ->
    - 現 modern form derives from 人 + phonetic 可
  - **interpretation:** 何 depicts a person, 人 (rén, "person"), potentially carrying a 戈 (gē, "dagger-axe") to convey the act of carrying. The phonetic aspect with 戈 indicates the sound, while 可 (kě, "can") in later forms reinforces the phonetic component. The idea of 'what' or 'which' extends from the nature of indicating or querying involvement or carrying of objects, as a question of identity or role.
- **examples:**
  - 何时(時)出发(發)？
    - Hé shí chūfā?
