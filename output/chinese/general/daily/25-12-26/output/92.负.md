## carry on the back
### fù
---
- **traditional:** 負
- **definition:** carry on the back
- **pinyin:** fù
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 否
    - fǒu
    - no; not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 貝 (bèi, "cowry") as 'value' + phonetic 否 (fǒu) ->
    - bear burdens ->
    - bear; suffer
  - **interpretation:** In 負 (fù, "bear; suffer"), the 貝 (bèi, "cowry") represents carrying value or burdens, while 否 (fǒu) supplies the sound. The idea of carrying a burden connects to 'bear' and the related weight of responsibility links to 'suffer'.
  - **simplification:** The traditional 負 was simplified by removing the 貝 (bèi, "cowry") radical, retaining only the main strokes to reduce its complexity, reflecting 否 (fǒu).
- **examples:**
  - 他( )负(負)担(擔)了( )家( )庭( )的( )所( )有( )开(開)支( )。

### 负(負) → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**

### 负(負) → 否 → 不 → 柎 → 付 → 寸
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram

  - **interpretation:** 寸 (cùn, "inch") originated as a depiction of a line on the hand, indicating the area near the elbow. This specific placement was considered significant for pulse palpation, originally suggesting a forearm position, which then came to denote the measurement 'inch'.
