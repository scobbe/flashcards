## martial arts circles
### wǔlín
---
- **definition:** martial arts circles
- **pinyin:** wǔlín
- **components:**
  - 武
    - wǔ
    - martial; military
  - 林
    - lín
    - forest; gathering; subset when referring to 'circle of people'
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 武 (wǔ, "martial") denotes martial arts or military affairs. 林 (lín, "forest") figuratively represents a vast gathering or community. Together, 武林 (wǔlín) conveys the notion of a community or world centered around martial arts.
  - **interpretation:** 武 (wǔ, "martial") provides the context—martial arts. 林 (lín, "forest") suggests a vast, dense collection. The 'forest' metaphorically alludes to a flourishing community, much as trees form a part of a dense forest. Therefore, 武林 (wǔlín) depicts the world or society where those skilled in martial arts gather, akin to a cultural community or network involving martial arts practitioners and enthusiasts.
- **examples:**
  - 在武林中，许(許)多高手都被尊敬。
    - Zài wǔlín zhōng, xǔduō gāoshǒu dōu bèi zūnjìng.
    - In the martial arts circles, many masters are respected.
  - 他梦(夢)想成为(為)武林中的一员(員)。
    - Tā mèngxiǎng chéngwéi wǔlín zhōng de yīyuán.
    - He dreams of becoming a member of the martial arts circles.
  - 江湖险(險)恶(惡)，武林高手随(隨)时(時)要小心。
    - Jiānghú xiǎn'è, wǔlín gāoshǒu suíshí yào xiǎoxīn.
    - The martial arts world is dangerous, and martial arts masters must always be cautious.
### 武林 → 武
- **definition:** martial; military
- **pinyin:** wǔ
- **components:**
  - 戈
    - gē
    - blade
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot; to walk; to stop
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 戈 (gē, "blade") + 止 (zhǐ, "foot; to walk; to stop") =
    - army going on an expedition ->
    - later associated with just warfare
  - **interpretation:** The character 武 originally combines the idea of a blade (戈) with movement or marching (止), representing an army on the move. While traditionally reinterpreted as 'to stop violence', linguistically it aligns better with the idea of martial activity or skill. The connection to just warfare is a later reinterpretation.
- **examples:**
  - 他喜歡看武俠小說。
    - Tā xǐhuān kàn wǔxiá xiǎoshuō.
    - He likes to read martial arts novels.
  - 我們去看了一場武術表演。
    - Wǒmen qù kànle yī chǎng wǔshù biǎoyǎn.
    - We went to watch a martial arts performance.
  - 他正在學習武功招式。
    - Tā zhèngzài xuéxí wǔgōng zhāoshì.
    - He is learning martial arts moves.

  - **description:**

  - **description:**

### 武林 → 武 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').
