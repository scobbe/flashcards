## popular; prevalent
### liúxíng
---
- **definition:** popular; prevalent
- **pinyin:** liúxíng
- **components:**
  - 流
    - liú
    - flow; stream
  - 行
    - xíng
    - move; walk; conduct
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 流 (liú, "flow") and 行 (xíng, "move; walk") together imply something that flows or moves through a society, much like water or people moving across a landscape, hence becoming widespread or popular.
  - **interpretation:** In the combination 流行, 流 (liú, "flow") suggests movement or spreading, while 行 (xíng, "move; walk") refers to being in motion or action. Together, they describe trends or ideas that spread and become prevalent, much like how water flows through a river or people traverse pathways.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)首歌曲非常流行。
    - Zhè shǒu gēqǔ fēicháng liúxíng.
    - This song is extremely popular.
  - 他的服装(裝)设(設)计(計)流行，引领(領)时(時)尚。
    - Tā de fúzhuāng shèjì liúxíng, yǐnlǐng shíshàng.
    - His fashion designs are popular, setting trends.
### 流行 → 流
- **definition:** flow; stream
- **pinyin:** liú
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 㐬
    - lì
    - no definition
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 水 (shuǐ, 'water') + phonetic 㐬 ->
    - flow of water
  - **interpretation:** The character 流 (liú, 'flow; stream') combines the semantic element 水 (shuǐ, 'water'), indicating its association with liquid, with the phonetic element 㐬, which provides the pronunciation. This combination naturally conveys the concept of flowing water, leading to its meanings of 'flow' and 'stream'.
- **examples:**
  - 溪水缓(緩)缓(緩)地流向大海。
    - Xīshuǐ huǎnhuǎn de liú xiàng dàhǎi.
    - The stream gently flows toward the sea.
  - 时(時)间(間)像流水一样(樣)匆匆而过(過)。
    - Shíjiān xiàng liúshuǐ yīyàng cōngcōng ér guò.
    - Time rushes by like flowing water.
  - 他的眼泪(淚)不停地流下来(來)。
    - Tā de yǎnlèi bù tíng de liú xiàlái.
    - His tears keep flowing down.
### 流行 → 流 → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flowing water
  - **interpretation:** 水 (shuǐ, 'water') is a pictogram that directly represents the appearance of flowing water, making it intuitive and easy to recognize. Its simplicity and direct visual reference to its meaning have allowed it to remain unchanged over millennia.
- **examples:**
  - 水是一种(種)生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì yī zhǒng shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 夏天很热(熱)，喝水能解暑。
    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, hē shuǐ néng jiě shǔ.
    - It is very hot in summer, drinking water can relieve the heat.
  - 河水潺潺，沿岸绿(綠)树(樹)成荫(蔭)。
    - Hé shuǐ chánchán, yán'àn lǜshù chéng yīn.
    - The river flows babbling, and green trees along the bank provide shade.

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 㐬 is a pictogram representing a newborn baby, depicted in an upside-down position with the upper portion as an inverted 子 (zǐ, "child"), and the lower lines symbolizing amniotic fluid or hair. This imagery captures the typical birth position and context.

### 流行 → 流 → 㐬 → 子
- **definition:** child
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a baby with a large head and spread arms, with legs wrapped in a blanket
  - **interpretation:** The character 子(zǐ, "child") is a pictorial representation of a baby, emphasizing characteristics like a large head and outstretched arms typical of infants, which conveys the essence of a child. This visual element helps infer its meaning.
- **examples:**

### 流行 → 行
- **definition:** move; walk; conduct
- **pinyin:** xíng
- **components:**
  - 彳
    - chì
    - step
  - 亍
    - chù
    - step
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally a symmetric depiction of a street intersection; simplified asymmetrically ->
    - 彳 (chì, "step") + 亍 (chù, "step")
  - **interpretation:** The character 行(xíng) visually represented a street intersection, with two symmetrical parts symbolizing the act of going or walking along paths. Despite its current breakdown into separate elements, it was originally a unified pictogram rather than a compound.
- **examples:**
  - 我行往学(學)校。
    - Wǒ xíng wǎng xuéxiào.
    - I go to school.
  - 他行于(於)青山之间(間)，徒步旅行至古城。
    - Tā xíng yú qīngshān zhī jiān, túbù lǚxíng zhì gǔchéng.
    - He walks among the green mountains and hikes to the ancient city.
  - 他们(們)行在林间(間)小路上。
    - Tāmen xíng zài línjiān xiǎolù shàng.
    - They walk on the forest trail.

### 流行 → 行 → 彳
- **definition:** step
- **pinyin:** chì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a foot or person walking
  - **interpretation:** 彳(chì, 'walking') visually represents steps or a person moving, suggesting the idea of walking or movement. Despite a simplified history from characters like 行(xíng, 'walk'), its pictographic heritage directly connects with the notion of stepping or proceeding.
- **examples:**
  - 他喜欢(歡)在河边(邊)慢慢地走。
    - Tā xǐhuān zài hé biān mànmàn de zǒu.
    - He likes to walk slowly by the river.
  - 我们(們)每天退出喧嚣(囂)，享受安静(靜)的散步。
    - Wǒmen měitiān tuìchū xuānxiāo, xiǎngshòu ānjìng de sànbù.
    - We withdraw from the hustle and bustle every day to enjoy a quiet walk.
  - 他常常独(獨)自彳亍于(於)公园(園)小径(徑)。
    - Tā chángcháng dúzì chìchù yú gōngyuán xiǎojìng.
    - He often walks alone on the park trails.
