## Wuchang Uprising
### Wǔchāng Qǐyì
---
- **traditional:** 武昌起義
- **definition:** Wuchang Uprising
- **pinyin:** Wǔchāng Qǐyì
- **components:**
  - 武
    - wǔ
    - martial; military
  - 昌
    - chāng
    - prosperous
  - 起
    - qǐ
    - rise; start
  - 義
    - yì
    - righteous; cause
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 武 (wǔ, "martial; military") + 昌 (chāng, "prosperous") + 起 (qǐ, "rise; start") + 義 (yì, "righteous; cause")
  - **interpretation:** 武 (wǔ, "martial; military") indicates the involvement of military action. 昌 (chāng, "prosperous") can hint at a flourishing or significant place, specifically referring to Wuchang, a key city. 起 (qǐ, "rise; start") signifies the action of beginning or rising. 義 (yì, "righteous; cause") conveys the idea of a just cause or movement, emphasizing the moral or political justification behind the uprising. Combined, this refers to the historical revolt known as the Wuchang Uprising, which was a pivotal event leading to the Xinhai Revolution and the fall of the Qing Dynasty.

- **pinyin:** wǔ
- **components:**
  - 戈
    - gē
    - blade

    - zhǐ

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 戈 (gē, "blade") + 止 (zhǐ, "foot; to walk; to stop") =
