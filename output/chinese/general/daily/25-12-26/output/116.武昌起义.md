## 武昌起义(義)
---
## Wuchang Uprising
### Wǔchāng Qǐyì
---
- **traditional:** 武昌起義
- **definition:** Wuchang Uprising
- **pinyin:** Wǔchāng Qǐyì
- **components:**
  - 武
    - wǔ
    - martial; military
  - 昌
    - chāng
    - prosperous
  - 起
    - qǐ
    - rise; start
  - 義
    - yì
    - righteous; cause
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 武 (wǔ, "martial; military") + 昌 (chāng, "prosperous") + 起 (qǐ, "rise; start") + 義 (yì, "righteous; cause")
  - **interpretation:** 武 (wǔ, "martial; military") indicates the involvement of military action. 昌 (chāng, "prosperous") can hint at a flourishing or significant place, specifically referring to Wuchang, a key city. 起 (qǐ, "rise; start") signifies the action of beginning or rising. 義 (yì, "righteous; cause") conveys the idea of a just cause or movement, emphasizing the moral or political justification behind the uprising. Combined, this refers to the historical revolt known as the Wuchang Uprising, which was a pivotal event leading to the Xinhai Revolution and the fall of the Qing Dynasty.
### 武昌起义(義) → 武
- **definition:** martial; military
- **pinyin:** wǔ
- **components:**
  - 戈
    - gē
    - blade
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot; to walk; to stop
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 戈 (gē, "blade") + 止 (zhǐ, "foot; to walk; to stop") =
    - army going on an expedition ->
    - later associated with just warfare
  - **interpretation:** The character 武 originally combines the idea of a blade (戈) with movement or marching (止), representing an army on the move. While traditionally reinterpreted as 'to stop violence', linguistically it aligns better with the idea of martial activity or skill. The connection to just warfare is a later reinterpretation.
- **examples:**
  - 他喜歡看武俠小說。
    - Tā xǐhuān kàn wǔxiá xiǎoshuō.
    - He likes to read martial arts novels.
  - 我們去看了一場武術表演。
    - Wǒmen qù kànle yī chǎng wǔshù biǎoyǎn.
    - We went to watch a martial arts performance.
  - 他正在學習武功招式。
    - Tā zhèngzài xuéxí wǔgōng zhāoshì.
    - He is learning martial arts moves.

### 武昌起义(義) → 武 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').

### 武昌起义(義) → 昌 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.

    - Wēnnuǎn de rìguāng sǎ mǎn dàdì.
    - The warm sunlight spreads over the earth.
  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。
    - Zhōngwǔ shífēn, rì gāo guà tiān.
    - At noon, the sun hangs high in the sky.

    - Yīshēng fāxiàn sì yìcháng, tíxǐng jiāzhǎng zǎoqī zhùyì.
