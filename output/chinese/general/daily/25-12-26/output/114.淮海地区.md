## Huaihai region
### Huáihǎi dìqū
---
- **traditional:** 淮海地區
- **definition:** Huaihai region
- **pinyin:** Huáihǎi dìqū
- **components:**
  - 淮
    - huái
    - Huai River
  - 海
    - hǎi
    - sea
  - 地區
    - dìqū
    - region
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** Huái(淮) (huái, "Huai River") refers to the Huai River, an important river in China that serves as a geographical marker. Hǎi(海) (hǎi, "sea") refers to the sea, but in this context, it is part of a proper name rather than a reference to the ocean. Together, Huáihǎi(淮海) refers to a specific area that historically takes its name from the two elements, focusing on regions influenced by both the Huai River and the eastern seaboard. Dì(地) (dì, "earth; land") refers to land or ground, while qū(區) (qū, "region") denotes a specific district or area. Combining dì(地) and qū(區) yields the meaning "region".
  - **interpretation:** Huái refers to the Huai River, a distinct geographical feature, marking the northern part of the region. Hǎi, meaning "sea", traditionally indicated influences from the eastern seaboard or the connectivity to broader regions. The Huaihai combination denotes the historical and geographical significance of this area, associated with both river and coastal influences. Dìqū refers to the concept of a region or area, marking the specific territorial focus.
