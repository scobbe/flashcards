### Yán
---
- **traditional:** 顏
- **definition:** surname Yan
- **pinyin:** Yán
- **components:**
  - 頁
    - yè
    - head
  - 彥
    - yàn
    - elegant; virtuous
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 頁 (yè, "head") + phonetic 彥 (yàn) ->
    - face/head
  - **interpretation:** 顏 (yán, "face; facial appearance") combines the semantic component 頁 (yè, "head") indicating head or face, with the phonetic component 彥 (yàn), which contributes its sound. This composition naturally leads to the meaning related to facial features or appearance, as the head is associated with one's face.
  - **simplification:** Simplified 顏 to 颜(顏) by simplifying 彥 to 彦 and 頁 to 页, reducing stroke count while maintaining the original components' roles.
- **examples:**
  - 颜(顏)家是当(當)地的名门(門)望族。
    - Yán jiā shì dāngdì de míngmén wàngzú.
    - The Yan family is a prominent family in the area.
  - 小颜(顏)在学(學)校里(裡)非常受欢(歡)迎。
    - Xiǎo Yán zài xuéxiào lǐ fēicháng shòu huānyíng.
    - Little Yan is very popular at school.
  - 颜(顏)先生是这(這)篇文章的作者。
    - Yán xiānshēng shì zhè piān wénzhāng de zuòzhě.
    - Mr. Yan is the author of this article.
### 颜(顏) → 頁
- **definition:** head
- **pinyin:** yè
- **components:**
  - 首
    - shǒu
    - head; first
  - 卩
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 首 (head/face) + 卩 (kneeling person) ->
    - depicts a person's head and body
  - **interpretation:** Originally represented a head and its body in a kneeling posture, emphasizing “head”. The depiction was extended semantically to represent a 'page', as pages bear leading information like a head does for a body. The concept of 'head' is also reflected in its modern usage as a radical in characters related to parts of the head or face.
  - **simplification:** 页 is a cursive shorthand of the traditional 頁, simplifying the structure while maintaining the core shape of a head.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)打开(開)书(書)的第十页(頁)。
    - Qǐng dǎkāi shū de dì shí yè.
    - Please open to page ten of the book.
  - 这(這)本书(書)每一页(頁)都有插图(圖)。
    - Zhè běn shū měi yī yè dōu yǒu chātú.
    - Every page of this book has illustrations.
  - 我昨天晚上读(讀)了三十页(頁)。
    - Wǒ zuótiān wǎnshang dúle sānshí yè.
    - I read thirty pages last night.

### 颜(顏) → 頁 → 卩
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.
- **examples:**
  - 那位卩正虔诚(誠)祈祷(禱)。
    - Nà wèi jié zhèng qiánchéng qídǎo.
    - That kneeling person is praying devoutly.
  - 礼(禮)仪(儀)师(師)指出，卩代表谦(謙)卑与(與)敬意。
    - Lǐyí shī zhǐchū, jié dàibiǎo qiānbēi yǔ jìngyì.
    - The ritual master pointed out that the kneeling person represents humility and respect.
  - 在庄(莊)严(嚴)仪(儀)式中，卩象征(徵)着(著)内(內)心的虔敬。
    - Zài zhuāngyán yíshì zhōng, jié xiàngzhēngzhe nèixīn de qiánjìng.
    - In the solemn ceremony, the kneeling figure symbolizes inner devotion.

### 颜(顏) → 彥 → 厂(廠) → 广(廣) → 廣 → 黃
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally a pictogram depicting a disabled person or man with a ring of jade on the chest ->
    - phonetically borrowed for 'yellow' ->
    - 黃
  - **interpretation:** The character 黃 (huáng, 'yellow') originated from a pictogram representing either a disabled person or a man with a jade ring, showing a connection to cultural rituals or symbols of status. It was later phonetically borrowed to represent the color 'yellow' without semantic connection to its original depiction. In modern Chinese, its primary use is to denote the color yellow, disconnecting entirely from its debated pictographic origins.
