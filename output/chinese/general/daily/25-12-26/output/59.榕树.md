## banyan tree
### róngshù
---
- **traditional:** 榕樹
- **definition:** banyan tree
- **pinyin:** róngshù
- **components:**
  - 榕
    - róng
    - banyan
  - 树(樹)
    - shù
    - tree
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 榕 (róng, "banyan") refers specifically to the banyan tree, known for its sprawling branches and large canopy. 樹 (shù, "tree") is a general term for tree, representing the plant form.
  - **interpretation:** 榕 (róng, "banyan") directly indicates the banyan tree, a species known for its unique root system and broad coverage. 樹 (shù, "tree") places it in the broader category of trees as a natural organism; together, they clearly specify the type of tree being referred to as a banyan.
- **examples:**
  - 榕树(樹)长寿而强壮。
    - Róngshù chángshòu ér qiángzhuàng.
    - The banyan tree is long-lived and strong.
  - 在老家，村口有一棵榕树(樹)。
    - Zài lǎojiā, cūnkǒu yǒu yī kē róngshù.
    - In my hometown, there is a banyan tree at the entrance of the village.
### 榕树(樹) → 榕
- **definition:** banyan
- **pinyin:** róng
- **components:**
  - 木

  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 木 (mù, "wood") + phonetic 容 (róng, "contain") ->
    - banyan
  - **interpretation:** The character 榕 (róng, "banyan") combines the semantic 木 (mù, "wood") to indicate a connection with trees, with the phonetic 容 (róng, "contain") to convey the sound. This reflects its meaning related to a type of tree, specifically the banyan.
- **examples:**
  - 榕树(樹)是我家附近的标(標)志(誌)性植物。

    - Zài gōngyuán lǐ, wǒ cháng zài róngshù xià yuèdú.
    - In the park, I often read under the banyan tree.

### 榕树(樹) → 树(樹)
- **traditional:** 樹
- **definition:** tree
- **pinyin:** shù
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 尌
    - shù
    - to plant a tree
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Originally 尌 (phonetic, 'to plant a tree') + 木 (mù, 'tree') added for clarity ->
    - tree
  - **interpretation:** 樹 combines the semantic 木 (mù, 'tree') with the phonetic 尌 to convey the meaning of 'tree'. The phonetic element 尌 originally depicted the act of planting a tree, and 木 emphasizes the botanical aspect.
  - **simplification:** 壴 (壴, 'drum or musical instrument') was replaced by 又 (又, 'again') to simplify stroke count, losing the original phonetic connection in favor of visual simplicity.
- **examples:**
  - 公园(園)里(裡)的树(樹)很高大。
    - Gōngyuán lǐ de shù hěn gāodà.
    - The trees in the park are very tall.
  - 秋天时(時)树(樹)叶(葉)变(變)黄(黃)。
    - Qiūtiān shí shùyè biàn huáng.
    - In autumn, the tree leaves turn yellow.
  - 那棵老树(樹)见(見)证(證)了许(許)多历(歷)史。
    - Nà kē lǎo shù jiànzhèngle xǔduō lìshǐ.
    - That old tree has witnessed a lot of history.

### 榕树(樹) → 树(樹) → 尌 → 寸
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Line on the hand (ideographic) emphasizing the elbow ->
    - represents measurement unit between wrist and specific forearm position ->
    - meaning 'inch'
  - **interpretation:** 寸 (cùn, "inch") originated as a depiction of a line on the hand, indicating the area near the elbow. This specific placement was considered significant for pulse palpation, originally suggesting a forearm position, which then came to denote the measurement 'inch'.

### 榕树(樹) → 树(樹) → 尌 → 豆
- **definition:** drum
- **pinyin:** dòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a container ->
    - borrowed phonetically for the plant name
  - **interpretation:** The character 豆(dòu, 'drum') originally depicted a type of container. It was later borrowed for its phonetic value to represent the plant name 'bean', distinguishing it from a container. This transition indicates how the physical shape of the container, resembling a drum or storage pot, facilitated a phonetic borrowing.
- **examples:**

    - Cūnzhuāng lǐ yǒu yí gè gǔlǎo de dòu, tā de shēngyīn hěn yǒu mèilì.
    - There is an ancient drum in the village, and its sound is captivating.
