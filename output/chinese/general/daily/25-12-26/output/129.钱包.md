## wallet
### qiánbāo
---
- **traditional:** 錢包
- **definition:** wallet
- **pinyin:** qiánbāo
- **components:**
  - 钱(錢)
    - qián
    - money
  - 包
    - bāo
    - bag
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The word 钱(錢)包 (qiánbāo) is a compound word which combines 钱(錢) (qián, "money") and 包 (bāo, "bag"). The combination literally means a 'money bag', which refers to a wallet.
  - **interpretation:** Combining 钱(錢) (qián, "money") and 包 (bāo, "bag"), 钱(錢)包 signifies a container or pouch for carrying money, fitting the modern concept of a wallet.
  - **simplification:** Traditional '錢' simplified to '钱'.
- **examples:**
  - 我丢(丟)了钱(錢)包。
    - Wǒ diū le qiánbāo.
    - I lost my wallet.
  - 今天出门(門)时(時)，我发(發)现(現)钱(錢)包不见(見)了。
    - Jīntiān chūmén shí, wǒ fāxiàn qiánbāo bùjiàn le.
    - Today when leaving home, I discovered that my wallet was missing.

  - **interpretation:** The character 钱(錢) (qián, "money") combines 釒 (jīn, "metal"), signifying currency or coins made from metal, and 戔 (jiān, "small"), indicating small or fractional amounts. This suggests money as both metal-based and divisible.
