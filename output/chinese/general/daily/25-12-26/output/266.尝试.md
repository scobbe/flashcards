## try; attempt
### chángshì
---
- **traditional:** 嘗試
- **definition:** try; attempt
- **pinyin:** chángshì
- **components:**
  - 尝(嘗)
    - cháng
    - taste; try
  - 试(試)
    - shì
    - test; experiment
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 尝(嘗) (cháng, "taste; try") and 试(試) (shì, "test; experiment") together form the concept of making an attempt or trial.
  - **interpretation:** The word 尝(嘗)试(試) (chángshì, "try; attempt") combines the idea of 'trying' something akin to 'tasting' it, with the action of a 'test' or 'experiment', signifying an effort to do something in order to see the result.
  - **simplification:** 尝试 is the simplified form, 嘗試 is the traditional form.
- **examples:**
  - 他尝(嘗)试(試)新方法，解决(決)难(難)题(題)。
    - tā chángshì xīn fāngfǎ, jiějué nán tí.
    - He tries a new method, solving a difficult problem.
  - 她尝(嘗)试(試)解决(決)问(問)题(題)，然后(後)成功了。
    - tā chángshì jiějué wèntí, ránhòu chénggōng le.
    - She tries to solve the problem, and then she succeeds.
### 尝(嘗)试(試) → 尝(嘗)
- **traditional:** 嘗
- **definition:** taste; try
- **pinyin:** cháng
- **components:**
  - 尚
    - shàng
    - still; yet
  - 旨
    - zhǐ
    - tasty
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 旨 (zhǐ, "tasty") + phonetic 尚 (shàng) ->
    - taste
  - **interpretation:** The character 嘗 represents the act of tasting, with 尚 providing the sound and 旨 contributing the meaning of 'tasty'. The semantic component 旨 evokes the idea of a delicious flavor, leading naturally to the concept of tasting or trying food.
  - **simplification:** 尝(嘗) reduced the character by removing the semantic element 旨, leaving only the phonetic 尚.
- **examples:**
  - 我想尝(嘗)一下这(這)道菜。
    - Wǒ xiǎng cháng yīxià zhè dào cài.
    - I want to taste this dish.
  - 她总(總)是尝(嘗)试(試)新事物。
    - Tā zǒng shì chángshì xīn shìwù.
    - She always tries new things.
  - 我们(們)在集市上尝(嘗)了很多美食。
    - Wǒmen zài jíshì shàng cháng le hěn duō měishí.

### 尝(嘗)试(試) → 试(試) → 式 → 弋 → 杙 → 二
- **definition:** two
- **pinyin:** èr
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts two parallel strokes ->
    - represents the number two
  - **interpretation:** The character 二 (èr, "two") consists of two horizontal lines that visually represent the concept of 'two'. This method of using parallel strokes to denote numbers is a straightforward representation of quantity, similar to how 一 (yī, "one") represents 'one' and 三 (sān, "three") represents 'three'.
- **examples:**
  - 我有二支笔(筆)。
    - Wǒ yǒu èr zhī bǐ.
    - I have two pens.
  - 他买(買)了二个(個)苹(蘋)果，我们(們)一起分享。
    - Tā mǎi le èr gè píngguǒ, wǒmen yīqǐ fēnxiǎng.
    - He bought two apples, and we shared them together.
