## three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
### sānxián
---
- **definition:** three-stringed Chinese lute used in Pingtan
- **pinyin:** sānxián
- **components:**
  - 三
    - sān
    - three
  - 弦
    - xián
    - string; chord
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 三 (sān, "three"), 弦 (xián, "string")
  - **interpretation:** The word 三弦 (sānxián) literally describes a musical instrument with 'three strings'. The numeral 三 (sān, "three") specifies the number of strings on the instrument, while 弦 (xián, "string; chord") denotes the part of the lute that produces sound. This particular lute, used in traditional Chinese music like Pingtan, emphasizes the design feature of having exactly three strings, distinguishing it from other string instruments.

### 三弦 → 弦(絃)
- **traditional:** 絃
- **definition:** string; chord
- **pinyin:** xián
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - sī
    - silk; thread
  - 玄
    - xuán
    - dark; profound
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 糸 (sī, "silk; thread") + phonetic 玄 (xuán) ->
    - string
  - **interpretation:** The character 絃 (xián, "string") combines the semantic component 糸 (sī, "silk; thread"), which indicates its association with threads or strings, with the phonetic component 玄 (xuán) to provide the sound. The use of 'silk' (糸) reflects the traditional material for making strings in musical instruments, directly linking to its meaning 'string'.
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 絃 was simplified to 弦 by replacing 糸 (sī, "silk; thread") with 弓 (gōng, "bow"). This changes the initial component to one less related to the material but still retains the phonetic 玄 (xuán).
- **examples:**

    - Tā de jítā shàng quēle yī gēn xián.
