## clumsy; awkward
### bènshǒu bènjiǎo
---
- **traditional:** 笨手笨腳
- **definition:** clumsy; awkward
- **pinyin:** bènshǒu bènjiǎo
- **components:**
  - 笨
    - bèn
    - stupid; clumsy
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
  - 腳
    - jiǎo
    - foot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨 (bèn, "stupid; clumsy") refers to lack of intelligence or dexterity. 手 (shǒu, "hand") and 腳 (jiǎo, "foot") refer to the physical limbs for manipulation and movement. The combination of 笨 (bèn, "clumsy") with 手 (shǒu, "hand") and 腳 (jiǎo, "foot") conveys unskillfulness and lack of coordination in using one's hands and feet, leading to the overall meaning of being "clumsy" or "awkward."
  - **interpretation:** 笨 (bèn, "clumsy") in conjunction with 手 (shǒu, "hand") directly paints the picture of someone who is awkward in handling or manipulating objects. Similarly, 笨 (bèn, "clumsy") with 腳 (jiǎo, "foot") creates the image of a person who is unsteady on their feet. Collectively, these depict someone whose awkwardness extends from precise manipulation to general movement, effectively embodying the essence of clumsiness or awkwardness.
- **examples:**
  - 他总(總)是笨手笨脚(腳)地打破东(東)西。
    - Tā zǒng shì bènshǒu bènjiǎo de dǎpò dōngxī.
    - He always clumsily breaks things.
  - 她笨手笨脚(腳)，但非常可爱(愛)。
    - Tā bènshǒu bènjiǎo, dàn fēicháng kě'ài.
    - She is clumsy but very adorable.
  - 小明在体(體)育课(課)上显(顯)得笨手笨脚(腳)。
    - Xiǎomíng zài tǐyù kè shàng xiǎndé bènshǒu bènjiǎo.
    - Xiaoming appears clumsy in gym class.
### 笨手笨脚(腳) → 笨
- **definition:** stupid; clumsy
- **pinyin:** bèn
- **components:**
  - 竹
    - zhú
    - bamboo
  - 本
    - běn
    - root
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 竹 (zhú, "bamboo") + phonetic 本 (běn, "root") ->
    - clumsy
  - **interpretation:** The character 笨(bèn, "stupid; clumsy") consists of the semantic component 竹 (zhú, "bamboo") merged with the phonetic 本 (běn, "root"). The semantic component 竹 implies materials or objects, which together with the sound and metaphorical sense of 本, associates with something that is slow or unwieldy like a piece of bamboo, hence 'clumsy' or 'stupid'.
- **examples:**
  - 他做事很笨。
    - Tā zuòshì hěn bèn.
    - He is very clumsy at doing things.
  - 她总(總)是说(說)一些笨话(話)。
    - Tā zǒng shì shuō yīxiē bèn huà.
    - She always says foolish things.
  - 为(為)什么(麼)要做这(這)么(麼)笨的决(決)定？
    - Wèishéme yào zuò zhème bèn de juédìng?
    - Why make such a foolish decision?

- **components:**
  - 木

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 木 (mù, "tree") with horizontal stroke at root ->

    - "root"
  - **interpretation:** The character 本(běn, "root") is formed by taking 木 (mù, "tree") and highlighting its base with an extra horizontal stroke, symbolizing the root. This visualization directly leads to the meaning 'root,' as the base of a tree is its foundation and source of growth.

### 笨手笨脚(腳) → 腳
- **definition:** foot
- **pinyin:** jiǎo
- **components:**
  - ⺼(肉)
    - ròu
    - meat; body
  - 卻
    - què
    - to retreat
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic ⺼ (meat, body) + phonetic 卻 (sound) ->
    - part of the body, the foot or leg
  - **interpretation:** The character 腳 (jiǎo, "foot") combines the semantic component ⺼(肉) (ròu, "meat, body") which indicates a part of the body, with the phonetic component 卻 (què) to suggest the sound. This blend points to a specific body part, here denoting the foot or leg.
- **examples:**
  - 我的腳很痛。
    - Wǒ de jiǎo hěn tòng.
    - My foot hurts.
  - 他的腳比我的大。
    - Tā de jiǎo bǐ wǒ de dà.
    - His foot is bigger than mine.
  - 她踢足球的时(時)候受伤(傷)了她的腳。
    - Tā tī zúqiú de shíhòu shòushāngle tā de jiǎo.
    - She injured her foot while playing soccer.

### 笨手笨脚(腳) → 腳 → 卻
- **definition:** to retreat
- **pinyin:** què
- **components:**
  - 𧮫
    - jué
    - type of snake
  - 卩
    - jié
    - seal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 𧮫 (jué) + semantic 卩 (seal) ->
    - que sound with idea of sealing, blocking ->
    - however
  - **interpretation:** The character 卻 (què, 'however') combines the phonetic element 𧮫 (jué) to indicate the pronunciation and the semantic element 卩 (seal) which traditionally conveys the idea of sealing or blockading. This conveys the sense of blocking or negating expectations, resulting in the meaning 'however'.
  - **simplification:** 却 is a simplified variant of 卻, retaining the phonetic and semantic elements while reducing stroke complexity.
- **examples:**
  - 他努力工作，却(卻)未能成功。
    - Tā nǔlì gōngzuò, què wèinéng chénggōng.
    - He worked hard, however, he couldn't succeed.
  - 我很累，却(卻)无(無)法入睡。
    - Wǒ hěn lèi, què wúfǎ rùshuì.
    - I am very tired, however, I can't fall asleep.
  - 她答应(應)了，却(卻)没(沒)有出现(現)。
    - Tā dāyìng le, què méiyǒu chūxiàn.
    - She agreed, however, she didn't show up.

### 笨手笨脚(腳) → 腳 → 卻 → 卩
- **definition:** seal
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.
- **examples:**
  - 那位卩正虔诚(誠)祈祷(禱)。
    - Nà wèi jié zhèng qiánchéng qídǎo.
    - That kneeling person is praying devoutly.
  - 礼(禮)仪(儀)师(師)指出，卩代表谦(謙)卑与(與)敬意。
    - Lǐyí shī zhǐchū, jié dàibiǎo qiānbēi yǔ jìngyì.
    - The ritual master pointed out that the kneeling person represents humility and respect.
  - 在庄(莊)严(嚴)仪(儀)式中，卩象征(徵)着(著)内(內)心的虔敬。
    - Zài zhuāngyán yíshì zhōng, jié xiàngzhēngzhe nèixīn de qiánjìng.
    - In the solemn ceremony, the kneeling figure symbolizes inner devotion.
