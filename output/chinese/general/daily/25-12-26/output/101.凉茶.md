## herbal tea
### liángchá
---
- **traditional:** 涼茶
- **definition:** herbal tea
- **pinyin:** liángchá
- **components:**
  - 凉(涼)
    - liáng
    - cool
  - 茶
    - chá
    - tea
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 涼(凉) (liáng, "cool") + 茶 (chá, "tea") =
    - herbal tea
  - **interpretation:** 涼茶 (liángchá, "herbal tea") refers to a type of tea believed to have cooling properties, which helps reduce internal heat according to traditional Chinese medicinal beliefs. The combination makes sense as 涼(凉) (liáng, "cool") implies the cooling effect the tea is supposed to provide.
- **examples:**
  - 夏天很热(熱)，喝一杯凉(涼)茶。
    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, hē yī bēi liángchá.
    - It's very hot in summer, so drink a cup of herbal tea.
  - 凉(涼)茶解渴。
    - Liángchá jiě kě.
    - Herbal tea quenches thirst.
  - 忙碌一天后(後)，喝点(點)凉(涼)茶。
    - Mánglù yī tiān hòu, hē diǎn liángchá.
    - After a busy day, have some herbal tea.
### 凉(涼)茶 → 凉(涼)
- **traditional:** 涼
- **definition:** cool
- **pinyin:** liáng
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 京
    - jīng
    - capital (city); used phonetically
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic 京 (jīng) ->
    - cool temperature
  - **interpretation:** The character 涼 (liáng, "cool") combines the semantic component 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water"), representing the cooling effect of water, with the phonetic component 京 (jīng) for its sound. This combination intuitively suggests a sense of coolness, as water often has a cooling effect, while 京 provides the sound for the word.
  - **simplification:** 凉 is a simplified form where the radical 氵was replaced with 冫(bīng, "ice") to keep the reference to coldness and further reduce stroke count.
- **examples:**
  - 风(風)吹来(來)的时(時)候很凉(涼)。
    - Fēng chuī lái de shíhòu hěn liáng.
    - It's cool when the wind blows.
  - 凉(涼)爽的夜晚让(讓)人感到舒适(適)。
    - Liángshuǎng de yèwǎn ràng rén gǎndào shūshì.
    - A cool night feels comfortable.
  - 这(這)杯茶已经(經)凉(涼)了。
    - Zhè bēi chá yǐjīng liáng le.
    - This cup of tea has already cooled down.

### 凉(涼)茶 → 茶
- **definition:** tea
- **pinyin:** chá
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 余
    - yú
    - I; surplus
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, "grass") + phonetic 余 (yú) ->
    - modified from archaic 荼 (bitter plant) for tea
  - **interpretation:** The character 茶 (chá, "tea") combines 艹 (cǎo, "grass") as the semantic element, indicating a plant-related concept, with 余 (yú) serving as the phonetic component. Originally, 茶 was a modification of the archaic character 荼 which referred to a 'bitter plant.' This association with a bitter plant reflects the historical use of tea leaves, which have a bitter taste, thereby making 茶 appropriate for representing 'tea.'
- **examples:**
  - 奶奶每天早上喝茶。
    - Nǎinai měitiān zǎoshang hē chá.
    - Grandma drinks tea every morning.
  - 这(這)家茶馆(館)的绿(綠)茶味道很好。
    - Zhè jiā cháguǎn de lǜchá wèidào hěn hǎo.
    - The green tea at this teahouse tastes very good.
  - 你想喝红(紅)茶还(還)是绿(綠)茶？
    - Nǐ xiǎng hē hóngchá háishì lǜchá?
    - Do you want to drink black tea or green tea?

### 凉(涼)茶 → 茶 → 余(餘)
- **traditional:** 餘
- **definition:** I; surplus
- **pinyin:** yú
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - food
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 食 (shí, 'food') + phonetic 余(餘) (yú, 'I') ->
    - remains of food ->
    - 'excess, surplus'
  - **interpretation:** The character 餘 evolved to represent the concept of 'excess' or 'surplus' through a phono-semantic compound. It combines the semantic component 食 (shí, 'food'), representing food or consumption, with the phonetic component 余(餘) (yú, 'I'), to indicate leftovers or remains of food. This directly suggests an aspect of having more than what is needed, thus an 'excess' or 'surplus'.
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 餘 was simplified to 余 by removing the semantic component 食 (shí, 'food'), leaving only the phonetic component.
- **examples:**
  - 余(餘)愿(願)以诗(詩)书(書)为(為)乐(樂)。
    - Yú yuàn yǐ shīshū wéi lè.
    - I wish to take pleasure in poetry and books.
  - 余(餘)在此地，心安吾归(歸)。
    - Yú zài cǐdì, xīn ān wú guī.
    - I am here, at peace and at home.
  - 余(餘)莫负(負)此生。
    - Yú mò fù cǐ shēng.
    - I should not waste this life.

### 凉(涼)茶 → 茶 → 余(餘) → 食
- **definition:** food
- **pinyin:** shí
- **components:**
  - 亼
    - jí
    - assemble
  - 𠊊
    - jí
    - bowl with stand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:** 亼 (jí, "assemble") over a pictographic bowl of rice on a stand, symbolized by 𠊊 (jí, "bowl with stand")
  - **interpretation:** Initially, the character 食 (shí, "eat") visually represented a mouth over a bowl of rice, conveying the act of eating or a meal intuitively through this imagery.
- **examples:**

    - I am preparing lunch to share a delicious meal with my friends.

- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 今
    - jīn
    - now
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 入 (rù, "enter") + 一 (yī, "one") =
    - gathering from three sides ->
    - original form of 今 (jīn, "now")
  - **interpretation:** 亼 (jī, "open mouth flipped upside down") originally represented the action of gathering, with 入 (rù, "enter") suggesting bringing things together and 一 (yī, "one") implying unification into a single entity. This concept aligns traditionally with 集 (jí, "gather"), where the sense of gathering from three sides connects to assembling or coming together. The visual comparison to an open mouth reversed is a less common interpretation, relating to a shape rather than function.
