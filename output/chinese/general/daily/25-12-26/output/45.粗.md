## coarse; rough
### cū
---
- **definition:** coarse; rough
- **pinyin:** cū
- **components:**
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
  - 且
    - qiě
    - furthermore
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 米 (mǐ, "rice") + phonetic 且 (qiě) ->
    - coarse; rough
  - **interpretation:** The character 粗(cū, "coarse; rough") combines semantic 米 (mǐ, "rice"), suggesting something granular or coarse, with phonetic 且 (qiě), which supplies the sound. This links to the notion of coarse grains or texture, which is the primary meaning of the character.
- **examples:**

### 粗 → 米

- **etymology:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 米(mǐ, 'rice') visually represents dehusked rice kernels to differentiate it from 粟. The horizontal stroke may symbolize a bamboo sieve, emphasizing the process of separating grains from their husks.

### 粗 → 且
- **definition:** furthermore
- **pinyin:** qiě
- **components:**
  - 祖
    - zǔ
    - ancestor
  - 俎
    - zǔ
    - sacrificial vessel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stone altar or a sacrificial vessel from above ->
    - original form of 俎 (zǔ, "sacrificial vessel") ->
    - borrowed for ancestor rites in 祖 (zǔ, "ancestor")
  - **interpretation:** The character 且(qiě) originally depicted a stone altar used in sacrificial rites or a vessel for holding meat, reflecting its association with mealtime offerings or ancestor worship in characters like 祖(zǔ, "ancestor").

### 粗 → 且 → 祖

  - 示
