## decluttering; minimalism
### duànshělí
---
- **traditional:** 斷捨離
- **definition:** decluttering; minimalism
- **pinyin:** duànshělí
- **components:**
  - 断(斷)
    - duàn
    - cut off; sever; break; absolutely
  - 舍(捨)
    - shě
    - discard; abandon; give up
  - 离(離)
    - lí
    - separate; leave; apart from; different
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 斷 (duàn, "cut off") + 捨 (shě, "discard") + 離 (lí, "separate").
  - **interpretation:** 斷 (duàn, "cut off") involves decisively breaking away from attachments, typically physical or emotional. 捨 (shě, "discard") suggests letting go of unnecessary items or burdens, emphasizing the action of removal. 離 (lí, "separate") implies creating distance from the clutter or allure of material possessions. Together, 斷捨離 (duànshělí) embodies the philosophy of reducing excess and embracing minimalism by separating oneself from the physical and mental clutter, promoting a lifestyle of simplicity and focus.

  - 我( )决(決)定( )开(開)始( )断(斷)舍(捨)离(離)，因( )为(為)家( )里(裡)东(東)西( )太( )多( )了( )。
