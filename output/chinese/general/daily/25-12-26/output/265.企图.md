## attempt; try; intend
### qǐtú
---
- **traditional:** 企圖
- **definition:** attempt; try; intend
- **pinyin:** qǐtú
- **components:**
  - 企
    - qǐ
    - to plan; to stand on tiptoe
  - 图(圖)
    - tú
    - map; plan; diagram; to intend
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 企 (qǐ, "to plan") + 图(圖) (tú, "map; plan; diagram") =
    - the act of planning or plotting something.
  - **interpretation:** 企 (qǐ, "to plan") involves a deliberate positioning, standing on tiptoe emblematizes reaching for something, signifying preparation or intent. 图(圖) (tú, "map; plan; diagram") traditionally refers to a drawn plan or project. Together, the characters form the concept of formulating a strategy or making an "attempt" or "intention" to achieve something. The sense of 'intention' derives from the idea of establishing a visual or mental outline of one's goals before acting upon them.
- **examples:**
  - 他企图(圖)逃跑。
    - Tā qǐtú táopǎo.
    - He attempted to escape.
  - 他们(們)的企图(圖)被发(發)现(現)了。
    - Tāmen de qǐtú bèi fāxiàn le.
    - Their attempt was discovered.
  - 即使失败(敗)了，他仍然不放弃(棄)他的企图(圖)。
    - Jíshǐ shībàile, tā réngrán bù fàngqì tā de qǐtú.
    - Even if he failed, he still didn't give up his attempt.

### 企图(圖) → 企 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').

### 企图(圖) → 图(圖)
- **traditional:** 圖
- **definition:** map; plan; diagram; to intend
- **pinyin:** tú
- **components:**
  - 囧
    - jiǒng
    - window
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic: 囧 (jiǒng, "window") + semantic: 口 (kǒu, "mouth") =
    - a picture drawn within a boundary ->
    - picture/map
  - **interpretation:** The character 图 (tú) combines 囧 (jiǒng, "window") as the phonetic element, suggesting a rectangular shape like a window or a frame, with 口 (kǒu, "mouth") as a semantic component, indicating an enclosed area, apt for depicting enclosed spaces like a map or diagram.
  - **simplification:** 圖 → 图: Simplified by removing the inner complexity of 囧 and representing the frame with a simpler structure, retaining the outer 口 for consistency.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)幅图(圖)展示了城市历(歷)史。
    - Zhè fú tú zhǎnshì le chéngshì lìshǐ.
    - This picture displays the city's history.
  - 我根据(據)图(圖)来(來)规(規)划(劃)路线(線)，并(並)成功到达(達)目的地。
    - Wǒ gēnjù tú lái guīhuà lùxiàn, bìng chénggōng dàodá mùdìdì.
    - I used the map to plan the route and successfully reached my destination.
  - 他绘(繪)制(製)了一张(張)图(圖)，记(記)录(錄)了人生旅程。
    - Tā huìzhì le yī zhāng tú, jìlù le rénshēng lǚchéng.
    - He drew a picture to document his life journey.
