## democracy
### mínzhǔ
---
- **definition:** democracy
- **pinyin:** mínzhǔ
- **components:**
  - 民
    - mín
    - people; citizen
  - 主
    - zhǔ
    - master; lord; main
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 民 (mín, "people; citizen") is derived from an eye pierced by a needle, representing a person who is subjugated yet sees. 主 (zhǔ, "master; lord") combines a dot representing a lamp above a curling roof or hand, evoking the image of something held or placed centrally, indicating control or a primary position. Together, the focus shifts to the people being the central authority, thus forming the idea of 'democracy'.
  - **interpretation:** 民主 (mínzhǔ, "democracy") indicates a system where the people (民 (mín, "people; citizen")) are the central or ruling authority (主 (zhǔ, "master; lord")). The basic semantic bridge is that the citizens, traditionally viewed as the subjects, are instead taking the primary or leading role in governance and decision-making. This becomes 'democracy', where governance is by the people and for the people.
- **examples:**
  - 民主制度让(讓)人民有更多的发(發)言权(權)。
    - Mínzhǔ zhìdù ràng rénmín yǒu gèng duō de fāyán quán.
    - The democratic system gives people more say.
  - 我们(們)需要保护(護)我们(們)的民主。
    - Wǒmen xūyào bǎohù wǒmen de mínzhǔ.
    - We need to protect our democracy.
  - 在民主国(國)家，选(選)举(舉)是非常重要的。
    - Zài mínzhǔ guójiā, xuǎnjǔ shì fēicháng zhòngyào de.
    - In a democratic country, elections are very important.
### 民主 → 民
- **definition:** people; citizen
- **pinyin:** mín
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts an eye stabbed by a needle, symbolizing oppression of the people ->
    - people
  - **interpretation:** The character 民 (mín, 'people') originally depicted an eye being pierced by a tool, a metaphor for the suffering and experiences of the common people. This imagery evolved to represent the broader concept of the populace.
- **examples:**
  - 勤劳(勞)的民，构(構)成国(國)家的支柱。
    - Qínláo de mín, gòuchéng guójiā de zhīzhù.
    - Hardworking people form the backbone of the nation.
  - 民是国(國)家不可忽视(視)的力量。
    - Mín shì guójiā bùkě hūshì de lìliàng.
    - The people are an indispensable force in the country.
  - 民聚沙成塔。
    - Mín jù shā chéng tǎ.
    - People can achieve great feats through unity.

### 民主 → 主
- **definition:** master; lord; main
- **pinyin:** zhǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a torch with a flame on top, symbolizing guidance or leadership
  - **interpretation:** The character 主 (zhǔ, "master") represents a torch with a flame, symbolizing a guiding or leading figure, akin to a master or lord providing direction.
- **examples:**
  - 他是我们(們)家的主。
    - Tā shì wǒmen jiā de zhǔ.
    - He is the master of our family.
  - 僧侣(侶)虔诚(誠)祷(禱)告主。
    - Sēnglǚ qiánchéng dǎogào zhǔ.
    - The monks devoutly prayed to the Lord.
  - 臣子忠心辅(輔)佐主。
    - Chénzi zhōngxīn fǔzuǒ zhǔ.
    - The ministers loyally assist their master.
