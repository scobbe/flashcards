## democracy
### mínzhǔ
---
- **definition:** democracy
- **pinyin:** mínzhǔ
- **components:**
  - 民
    - mín
    - people; citizen
  - 主
    - zhǔ
    - master; lord; main
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 民 (mín, "people; citizen") is derived from an eye pierced by a needle, representing a person who is subjugated yet sees. 主 (zhǔ, "master; lord") combines a dot representing a lamp above a curling roof or hand, evoking the image of something held or placed centrally, indicating control or a primary position. Together, the focus shifts to the people being the central authority, thus forming the idea of 'democracy'.
  - **interpretation:** 民主 (mínzhǔ, "democracy") indicates a system where the people (民 (mín, "people; citizen")) are the central or ruling authority (主 (zhǔ, "master; lord")). The basic semantic bridge is that the citizens, traditionally viewed as the subjects, are instead taking the primary or leading role in governance and decision-making. This becomes 'democracy', where governance is by the people and for the people.
