## run; jog
### pǎobù
---
- **definition:** run; jog
- **pinyin:** pǎobù
- **components:**
  - 跑
    - pǎo
    - run
  - 步
    - bù
    - step; pace; walk
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 跑 (pǎo, "run") indicates the action of running. 步 (bù, "step") refers to steps or walking, highlighting the movement aspect.
  - **interpretation:** 跑 (pǎo, "run") depicts swift movement, so it naturally implies rapid foot motion. 步 (bù, "step") focuses on the basic unit of motion, a single step. Combining these, 跑步 (pǎobù) emphasizes the repeated, rapid steps characteristic of running or jogging.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天早上跑步。
    - Wǒ měitiān zǎoshang pǎobù.
    - I run every morning.
  - 跑步可以增强(強)心肺功能。
    - Pǎobù kěyǐ zēngqiáng xīnfèi gōngnéng.
    - Running can enhance cardiovascular function.
  - 在公园(園)里跑步，感觉(覺)非常舒适(適)。
    - Zài gōngyuán lǐ pǎobù, gǎnjué fēicháng shūshì.
    - Running in the park feels very comfortable.
### 跑步 → 跑
- **definition:** run
- **pinyin:** pǎo
- **components:**
  - 足
    - zú
    - foot
  - 包
    - bāo
    - wrap
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 足 (zú, "foot") + phonetic: 包 (bāo, "wrap") =
    - movement involving feet ->
    - run
  - **interpretation:** 跑 (pǎo, "run") combines the semantic element 足 (zú, "foot"), indicating movement, with the phonetic component 包 (bāo, "wrap"), which suggests pronunciation. This pairing highlights the action of using one's feet to move rapidly.
- **examples:**
  - 他跑得很快，想赢(贏)得比赛(賽)。
    - Tā pǎo dé hěn kuài, xiǎng yíngdé bǐsài.
    - He runs very fast, hoping to win the race.
  - 孩子们(們)在操场(場)上跑。
    - Háizimen zài cāochǎng shàng pǎo.
    - The children are running on the playground.
  - 危险(險)来(來)临(臨)时(時)，他迅速跑向出口。
    - Wēixiǎn láilín shí, tā xùnsù pǎo xiàng chūkǒu.
    - When danger approaches, he quickly runs toward the exit.

### 跑步 → 步
- **definition:** step; pace; walk
- **pinyin:** bù
- **components:**
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 止 (zhǐ, "foot") facing forwards + 止 (zhǐ, "foot") reversed =
    - one foot in front of the other ->
    - step
  - **interpretation:** The character 步 (bù, "step") visually represents two feet positioned for walking, symbolizing the act of taking a step. This ideogrammatic compound effectively conveys movement through the arrangement of its components.
- **examples:**
  - 他迈(邁)出坚(堅)实(實)的一步。
    - Tā mài chū jiānshí de yī bù.
    - He took a sturdy step.
  - 他缓(緩)缓(緩)走来(來)，每一步都充满(滿)期待。
    - Tā huǎn huǎn zǒu lái, měi yī bù dōu chōngmǎn qídài.
    - He came slowly, each step filled with anticipation.
  - 他走出一步一个(個)脚(腳)印。
    - Tā zǒu chū yī bù yī gè jiǎoyìn.

- **pinyin:** zhǐ

    - yù
    - stylized feet
  - 手
    - shǒu

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original: Foot pointing up with 3 toes and a sole ->
    - Stylized as 龰 ->

### 跑步 → 步 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').
