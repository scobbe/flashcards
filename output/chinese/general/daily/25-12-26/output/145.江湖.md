## martial world
### jiānghú
---
- **definition:** martial world
- **pinyin:** jiānghú
- **components:**
  - 江
    - jiāng
    - river
  - 湖
    - hú
    - lake
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 江 (jiāng, "river") + 湖 (hú, "lake") together traditionally refer to the geographical spaces that were outside of official jurisdiction, often metaphorically associated with a space of freedom or wilderness.
  - **interpretation:** 江 (jiāng, "river") and 湖 (hú, "lake") collectively refer to bodies of water. Historically, these represented the vast natural landscapes beyond the grasp of tight societal structures, symbolizing autonomy and rebellion. In ancient Chinese literature and culture, 江湖 (jiānghú) became symbolic of a realm outside conventional society where martial artists, outlaws, and wanderers operate. This metaphor extends to the idea of the 'martial world', where individuals lead lives governed by personal honor codes, distinct from mundane official life.
- **examples:**
