## dried tangerine peel
### chénpí
---
- **traditional:** 陳皮
- **definition:** dried tangerine peel
- **pinyin:** chénpí
- **components:**
  - 陈(陳)
    - chén
    - display; aged; stale
  - 皮
    - pí
    - skin; peel; husk
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 陳 (chén, "display; aged") refers to being laid out or aged. 皮 (pí, "skin; peel") refers to the outer layer or skin of fruits. Together, 陳皮 (chénpí, "dried tangerine peel") implies the concept of the aged or dried skin of tangerines.
  - **interpretation:** 陳 (chén, "display; aged") suggests something that has been laid out, often indicating the passage of time and thereby aging, which aligns with the drying process. 皮 (pí, "skin; peel") literally refers to the peel of a fruit. The combination denotes the peel of tangerines that has been allowed to dry out with the passage of time, a common practice in Chinese culinary and medicinal traditions.
- **examples:**
  - 陈(陳)皮( )是传统中药的一种。
    - Chénpí shì chuántǒng zhōngyào de yī zhǒng.
    - Dried tangerine peel is a type of traditional Chinese medicine.
  - 我( )们(們)常( )用( )陈(陳)皮( )来煮汤。
    - Wǒmen chángyòng chénpí lái zhǔ tāng.
    - We often use dried tangerine peel to make soup.
  - 在冬天，陈(陳)皮( )茶( )能( )驱(驅)寒( )。
    - Zài dōngtiān, chénpí chá néng qū hán.

### 陈(陳)皮( ) → 陈(陳)
- **traditional:** 陳
- **definition:** display; aged; stale
- **pinyin:** chén
- **components:**
  - 阜
    - fù
    - mound
  - 東
    - dōng
    - east
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 阜 (fù, "mound") + phonetic 東 (dōng) ->
    - to display; to arrange
  - **interpretation:** The character 陳 (chén, "old; aged; display; state") combines 阜 (fù, "mound") as the semantic component, indicating the idea of laying out or displaying, and 東 (dōng) as the phonetic component, providing the sound. This union suggests the sense of arranging or setting in order, which extends to the meaning of displaying or stating. The notion of 'old' or 'aged' comes from the idea of things laid out or arranged for a long time.
  - **simplification:** The character 陳 was simplified to 陈 by replacing 東 with 东, thereby reducing the number of strokes.
- **examples:**

### 陈(陳)皮( ) → 陈(陳) → 東
- **definition:** east
- **pinyin:** dōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bundle or bag tied at both ends
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicting a bundle or bag tied at both ends, it suggests convergence or collection, visually resembling a tied shape. This pictorial representation is historically connected to the direction 'east', as a bag captures and holds items, analogous to the rising sun being 'held' in the east. Despite alternative interpretations involving the sun and tree, the pictogram of a bundle remains the most direct visual link.

### 陈(陳)皮( ) → 皮
- **definition:** skin; peel; husk
- **pinyin:** pí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a surface or the skin of an animal
  - **interpretation:** The character 皮 (pí, 'surface') visually represents an animal hide or skin stretched out, emphasizing the idea of an outer covering or surface. It captures the concept of a boundary between the interior and exterior, as seen in animal skins used as material.
