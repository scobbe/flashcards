## drink
### hē
---
- **definition:** drink
- **pinyin:** hē
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 曷
    - hé
    - question; why
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic 曷 ->
    - expression involving the mouth ->
    - to drink
  - **interpretation:** The semantic element 口 (kǒu, "mouth") indicates an action related to the mouth, such as drinking, while the phonetic component 曷 provides the sound. This combination naturally leads to the meaning of 'drink', as drinking involves using the mouth.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天早上都喝一杯咖啡。
    - Wǒ měitiān zǎoshang dōu hē yī bēi kāfēi.
    - I drink a cup of coffee every morning.
  - 她喜欢(歡)在夏天喝冰茶。
    - Tā xǐhuān zài xiàtiān hē bīngchá.
    - She likes to drink iced tea in the summer.
  - 小明喝完了整瓶水。
    - Xiǎomíng hē wán le zhěng píng shuǐ.
    - Xiaoming drank the entire bottle of water.

### 喝 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

### 喝 → 曷
- **definition:** question; why
- **pinyin:** hé
- **components:**
  - 曰
    - yuē
    - say
  - 匃
    - gài
    - empty vessel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 曰 (yuē, 'say') + phonetic 匃 (gài) ->
    - meaning
  - **interpretation:** The character 曷(hē, 'why, what') combines the semantic element 曰(yuē, 'say') with the phonetic element 匃(gài). The semantic part suggests an interrogative aspect, as if questioning speech or something said. Thus, it forms the basis for meanings like 'why' and 'what' that indicate inquiries or questioning.

### 喝 → 曷 → 曰
- **definition:** say
- **pinyin:** yuē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth, signifying speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 曰 (yuē, "say") visually resembles a mouth or an opening, symbolizing the action of speaking or saying something.
- **examples:**
  - 老师(師)曰：勤奋(奮)是成功之母。
    - Lǎoshī yuē：qínfèn shì chénggōng zhī mǔ。
    - The teacher said, 'Diligence is the mother of success.'
  - 孔子曰，学(學)而不思则(則)罔。
    - Kǒngzǐ yuē, xué ér bù sī zé wǎng。
    - Confucius said, 'Learning without thought leads to confusion.'
  - 老子曰，无(無)为(為)而治。
    - Lǎozǐ yuē, wúwéi ér zhì。
    - Laozi said, 'Governance should be achieved through non-action.'
