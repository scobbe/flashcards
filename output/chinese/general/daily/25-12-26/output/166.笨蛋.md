## idiot
### bèndàn
---
- **definition:** idiot
- **pinyin:** bèndàn
- **components:**
  - 笨
    - bèn
    - stupid; clumsy; foolish; slow-witted
  - 蛋
    - dàn
    - egg; oval; testicle; coward
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 笨 (bèn, "stupid") and 蛋 (dàn, "egg") combine to form 笨蛋 (bèndàn, "idiot").
  - **interpretation:** The word 笨蛋 (bèndàn) uses the metaphor of an egg to describe someone considered stupid or foolish. In some contexts, an 'egg' is used humorously or derogatorily to imply emptiness or lack of intelligence, similar to calling someone an 'airhead' in English.
- **examples:**
  - 你( )这(這)个(個)笨( )蛋( )！

  - 他( )经(經)常( )搞( )错(錯)方( )向( )，因( )为(為)他( )是( )个(個)笨( )蛋( )。

### 笨蛋 → 蛋 → 延 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').
