## etymology
### cíyuán
---
- **traditional:** 詞源
- **definition:** etymology
- **pinyin:** cíyuán
- **components:**
  - 词(詞)
    - cí
    - word; term; expression
  - 源
    - yuán
    - source; origin; source of a river
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 詞 (cí, 'word; term; expression') refers to a word or composition within a language. 源 (yuán, 'source; origin; source of a river') denotes the beginning or origin of something, similar to the source of a river.
  - **interpretation:** 詞 (cí, 'word') signifies language units or linguistic expressions. 源 (yuán, 'origin') is about the place something starts or arises from, like a river's source. Therefore, 詞源 (cíyuán, 'etymology') indicates the origin or development history of words, tracing how language elements originate, evolve, and form over time, much like tracing the source of a river to understand its journey.
- **examples:**
  - 词(詞)源学(學)研究词(詞)语(語)的历(歷)史和演变(變)。
    - Cíyuán xué yánjiū cíyǔ de lìshǐ hé yǎnbiàn.
    - Etymology studies the history and development of words.
  - 我对(對)词(詞)源很感兴(興)趣，所以喜欢(歡)阅(閱)读(讀)关(關)于(於)语(語)文学(學)的书(書)籍。
    - Wǒ duì cíyuán hěn gǎn xìngqù, suǒyǐ xǐhuān yuèdú guānyú yǔwénxué de shūjí.
    - I'm very interested in etymology, so I like reading books about philology.
  - 词(詞)源不仅(僅)揭示词(詞)语(語)的起源，还(還)帮(幫)助我们(們)理解文化。
    - Cíyuán bùjǐn jiēshì cíyǔ de qǐyuán, hái bāngzhù wǒmen lǐjiě wénhuà.
    - Etymology not only reveals the origins of words, but also helps us understand culture.
### 词(詞)源 → 词(詞)
- **traditional:** 詞
- **definition:** word; term; expression
- **pinyin:** cí
- **components:**
  - 讠(言)
    - yán
    - speech
  - 司
    - sī
    - manage
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 言 (yán, "speech") + phonetic: 司 (sī, "manage") =
    - word or term relating to speech ->
    - simplified by replacing 言 (yán, "speech") with 讠 (yán, "speech") ->
    - final form 词(詞)
  - **interpretation:** The character 词(詞) (cí, "word; term") combines the speech radical 言 (yán, "speech") with 司 (sī, "manage") to indicate a managed or structured form of speech, i.e., a word or term. The simplified form retains this concept, making writing faster with 讠 (yán, "speech") being a cursive form of the speech radical.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by changing the full radical 言 (yán, "speech") to its cursive form 讠 (yán, "speech"), facilitating quicker writing while retaining the original meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)词(詞)意义(義)丰(豐)富。
    - Zhè ge cí yìyì fēngfù.
    - This word has a rich meaning.
  - 诗(詩)歌中的每个(個)词(詞)都富有韵(韻)律，展现(現)了作者的才华(華)。
    - Shīgē zhōng de měi ge cí dōu fùyǒu yùnlǜ, zhǎnxiànle zuòzhě de cáihuá.
    - Every word in the poem is full of rhythm, revealing the author's talent.

    - xīn
    - bitter
  - 舌
    - shé

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + 辛 (xīn) combination ->

    - reference to speech

Over time, it may have involved 舌 (shé, "tongue") to reinforce its association with speech. This combination intuitively leads to the concept of 'speech' as it directly relates to vocal articulation and communication.
  - **simplification:** 讠(言): Simplified by adopting a single-stroke variant from cursive script of 訁, reducing strokes dramatically to represent speech.
- **examples:**
