## little sister; young girl
### xiǎomèimei
---
- **definition:** little sister; young girl
- **pinyin:** xiǎomèimei
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little
  - 妹妹
    - mèimei
    - younger sister
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小 (xiǎo, "small; little") indicates size or age, commonly used to denote something smaller or younger. 妹妹 (mèimei, "younger sister") specifically means a female sibling of a younger age. When combined, 小 (xiǎo, "small; little") modifies 妹妹 (mèimei, "younger sister") to enhance the notion of youth or smallness. Together, 小妹妹 (xiǎomèimei) conveys the concept of a 'little sister' or 'young girl', emphasizing both diminutiveness and endearment commonly seen in referring to younger female individuals.
  - **interpretation:** For 'little sister', 小 (xiǎo, "small; little") paired with 妹妹 (mèimei, "younger sister") underlines both youth and affection, as the prefix is often used fondly to highlight a familial bond. For 'young girl', the use of 小 (xiǎo, "small; little") signifies general youthfulness, while 妹妹 (mèimei, "younger sister") provides a familiar and tender context, often applying to any young female child in informal settings.
- **examples:**
  - 小妹妹喜欢看卡通片。
    - Xiǎomèimei xǐhuān kàn kǎtōngpiàn.
    - The little sister likes to watch cartoons.
