## Zhongzheng District, Taipei
### Zhōngzhèng Qū
---
- **traditional:** 中正區
- **definition:** Zhongzheng District, Taipei
- **pinyin:** Zhōngzhèng Qū
- **components:**
  - 中
    - zhōng
    - middle; central
  - 正
    - zhèng
    - correct; standard
  - 区(區)
    - qū
    - district
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 中 (zhōng, "middle; central") is used to indicate 'central' or 'middle', while 正 (zhèng, "correct; standard") invokes the ideas of righteousness and correctness as per traditional Chinese values found in Confucianism. 區 (qū, "district") refers to a division of a city or area. The term 中正區 refers to a district named after Chiang Kai-shek, who is often referred to by his formal name, 中正 (Zhōngzhèng). This highlights the combination of central/conventional value with leadership and governance found in the district's administrative importance.
  - **interpretation:** 中 (zhōng, "middle; central") represents centrality, reflecting the focus or core area, ideal as a part of a prominent leader's name or a key geographical region. 正 (zhèng, "correct; standard") involves a sense of moral righteousness, reflecting an ideal vision of propriety or standard, suited to name a district for a leader considered a righteous or standard figure. Together, 中正 denotes a central focus on proper governance, appropriate for a district named in honor of a significant national leader. 區 (qū, "district") points logically to an urban or administrative area.
- **examples:**
