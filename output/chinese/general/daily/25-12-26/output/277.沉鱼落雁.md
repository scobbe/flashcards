---
## beauty that sinks fish and makes birds fall
### chén yú luò yàn
---
- **traditional:** 沉魚落雁
- **definition:** beauty that sinks fish and makes birds fall
- **pinyin:** chén yú luò yàn
- **components:**
  - 沉
    - chén
    - sink; submerge
  - 魚
    - yú
    - fish
  - 落
    - luò
    - fall; drop
  - 雁
    - yàn
    - wild goose
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 沉 (chén, "sink") + 魚 (yú, "fish") + 落 (luò, "fall") + 雁 (yàn, "wild goose") form an idiomatic expression describing a level of beauty so profound that fish in water would dive down and wild geese in flight would descend, in awe of such beauty.
  - **interpretation:** This idiom describes a beauty that is so enchanting that it surpasses the natural elements, capable of captivating both aquatic and avian creatures. The idea is that a woman's beauty is so stunning that fish, feeling unworthy, sink to the depths of the water, while geese mistake it for nightfall and descend from the sky. This colorful imagery conveys the idea of an otherworldly grace and allure. The expression originates from legendary stories of beautiful women in Chinese history whose beauty was spoken of in such grandiose metaphors.
- **examples:**
  - 她的美貌让(讓)人想到古代绝(絕)世美女的沉鱼(魚)落雁。
    - Tā de měimào ràng rén xiǎngdào gǔdài juéshì měinǚ de chényúluòyàn.
    - Her beauty reminds people of the legendary beauties of ancient times.
  - 每次看到她，我都会(會)感叹(嘆)沉鱼(魚)落雁的真实(實)存在。
    - Měi cì kàn dào tā, wǒ dūhuì gǎntàn chényúluòyàn de zhēnshí cúnzài.
    - Every time I see her, I marvel at the real existence of 'beauty that sinks fish and makes birds fall'.
  - 她的姿态(態)如沉鱼(魚)落雁般令人惊(驚)叹(嘆)。
    - Tā de zītài rú chényúluòyàn bān lìng rén jīngtàn.
    - Her demeanor is as stunning as the legendary beauty that sinks fish and makes birds fall.
