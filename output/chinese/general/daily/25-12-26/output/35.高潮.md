## high tide; climax
### gāocháo
---
- **definition:** high tide; climax
- **pinyin:** gāocháo
- **components:**
  - 高
    - gāo
    - high; tall; above; elevated
  - 潮
    - cháo
    - tide; wave; current; moist
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 高 (gāo, "high") + 潮 (cháo, "tide") =
    - high tide or climax
  - **interpretation:** The word combines the idea of a tide reaching its highest point with the metaphorical use of reaching a peak experience, such as a climax in a narrative or emotional build-up. This dual meaning reflects the physical phenomenon of a tide and the figurative peak in various situations.

### 高潮 → 潮 → 朝
- **definition:** morning; dynasty
- **pinyin:** cháo
- **components:**
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 𠦝 (early form of 朝) + 月 (yuè, "moon") =
    - semantic 𠦝 (morning) + phonetic 月 ->
    - morning
  - **interpretation:** 朝 (zhāo, "morning") visually represents the transition from night to day, capturing the concept of the morning when both the sun and the fading moon can be seen. Originally, it was an ideogrammic compound with components such as sun and moon to depict morning. In later forms, the character evolved into a phono-semantic compound with 𠦝 as the semantic component referring to dawn, and 月 月(yuè, "moon") contributing phonetic value, reflecting the combination of nighttime and daytime elements in the morning twilight.

- **definition:** moon
