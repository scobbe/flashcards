## exceed; surpass
### chāo
---
- **definition:** exceed; surpass
- **pinyin:** chāo
- **components:**
  - 走
    - zǒu
    - run
  - 召
    - zhào
    - summon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 走 (zǒu, "run") + phonetic: 召 (zhào, "summon") =
    - running to summon or surpass ->
    - exceed
  - **interpretation:** The character 超 (chāo) combines the concept of running 走 (zǒu, "run") with a sound from 召 (zhào, "summon"), implying going beyond or surpassing norms or limits, just like running might surpass a walking pace.
- **examples:**
  - 他的成绩(績)超出班级(級)平均水平。
    - tā de chéngjì chāochū bānjí píngjūn shuǐpíng
    - His performance exceeds the class average.
  - 他在比赛(賽)中超越对(對)手，赢(贏)得冠军(軍)。
    - tā zài bǐsài zhōng chāoyuè duìshǒu, yíngdé guànjūn
    - He surpassed his opponents in the competition and won the championship.
  - 他的热(熱)情超乎想象，鼓舞了全体(體)队(隊)员(員)。
    - tā de rèqíng chāohū xiǎngxiàng, gǔwǔ le quántǐ duìyuán
    - His enthusiasm exceeded all expectations and inspired the whole team.
### 超 → 走
- **definition:** run
- **pinyin:** zǒu
- **components:**
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot
  - 夭
    - yāo
    - young
  - 土
    - tǔ
    - earth
  - 龰
    - yuè
    - walking
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Running man (original depiction) + 止 (zhǐ, "foot") added later ->
    - top 夭 (yāo, "young") simplified to 土 (tǔ, "earth"), bottom stylized as 龰 ->
    - modern 走
  - **interpretation:** The character originally depicted a man running, with an added foot to emphasize movement. Over time, elements like the top 夭 were simplified to 土, and the bottom was stylized to 龰, although they are unrelated to the original meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 看到前方火光时(時)，他急忙走向安全地带(帶)。
    - Kàndào qiánfāng huǒguāng shí, tā jímáng zǒu xiàng ānquán dìdài.
    - When he saw the firelight ahead, he hurriedly ran toward safety.
  - 比赛(賽)开(開)始后(後)，选(選)手们(們)奋(奮)力走向终(終)点(點)。
    - Bǐsài kāishǐ hòu, xuǎnshǒu men fènlì zǒu xiàng zhōngdiǎn.
    - After the race began, the contestants ran towards the finish line.

### 超 → 走 → 止
- **definition:** foot
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **components:**
  - 趾
    - zhǐ
    - foot
  - 龰
    - yù
    - stylized feet
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original: Foot pointing up with 3 toes and a sole ->
    - Stylized as 龰 ->
    - Derivative 趾 refers to original 'foot' meaning
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicting a footprint with toes and a sole, 止 (zhǐ, "foot") represents the foot. The character's form resembles a foot with three toes, analogous to how the hand is represented with fingers in other characters.
- **examples:**

    - Tā zǒu lù shí xiǎo xīn jǐn shèn, yīn wèi zhǐ ruò shòu shāng biàn nán qián xíng.

### 超 → 走 → 止 → 趾
- **definition:** foot
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **components:**
  - 足
    - zú
    - foot
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - halt
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 足 (zú, "foot") + phonetic 止 (zhǐ) ->
    - toe
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 足 (zú, "foot"), indicating the general area involved, with 止 (zhǐ) providing the sound and potentially suggesting the idea of a part of the foot that 'halts' or 'stops' progression, which naturally leads to the notion of a toe.
- **examples:**
  - 他的脚(腳)趾受伤(傷)了。
    - Tā de jiǎozhǐ shòushāng le.
    - His toe is injured.
  - 她用脚(腳)趾轻(輕)轻(輕)地踢了一下球。
    - Tā yòng jiǎozhǐ qīngqīng de tī le yīxià qiú.
    - She gently kicked the ball with her toe.
  - 在沙滩(灘)上散步时(時)，我感受到沙子在我的脚(腳)趾间(間)流动(動)。
    - Zài shātān shàng sànbù shí, wǒ gǎnshòu dào shāzi zài wǒ de jiǎozhǐ jiān liúdòng.
    - While walking on the beach, I felt the sand flowing between my toes.

### 超 → 走 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').

- **etymology:**

- **examples:**

### 超 → 召
- **definition:** summon
- **pinyin:** zhào
- **components:**
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - original hands over a wine vessel ->
    - phonetic 刀 (dāo) + semantic 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') ->
    - summon
  - **interpretation:** Originally, 召 depicted hands over a wine vessel, linked to the practice of serving wine to guests. The character evolved by adding a phonetic component 刀 (dāo) and a semantic component 口 (kǒu, 'mouth'). The mouth element implies calling out, and the knife contributes the zhào sound. The meaning extended to 'summoning' due to both calling out and the attentiveness required in serving. The concept of welcoming and calling guests informs its modern sense of summoning or calling.
- **examples:**
  - 国(國)王召集大臣们(們)商讨(討)对(對)策。
    - Guówáng zhàojí dàchénmen shāngtǎo duìcè.
    - The king summoned the ministers to discuss strategies.
  - 他被召回故乡(鄉)接受新的任务(務)。
    - Tā bèi zhào huí gùxiāng jiēshòu xīn de rènwù.
    - He was called back to his hometown to accept a new assignment.
  - 战(戰)争(爭)爆发(發)后(後)，军(軍)队(隊)迅速召集士兵。
    - Zhànzhēng bàofā hòu, jūnduì xùnsù zhàojí shìbīng.
    - After the outbreak of war, the army quickly summoned soldiers.

### 超 → 召 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
