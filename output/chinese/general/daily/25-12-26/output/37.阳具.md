## male genitals
### yángjù
---
- **traditional:** 陽具
- **definition:** male genitals
- **pinyin:** yángjù
- **components:**
  - 阳(陽)
    - yáng
    - male; positive
  - 具
    - jù
    - tool; implement
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 阳(陽) (yáng, "male; positive") + 具 (jù, "tool; implement") =
    - implements of the male, referring to male genitals
  - **interpretation:** The term 阳(陽)具 (yángjù, "male genitals") combines 阳(陽) (yáng, "male; positive"), which is associated with masculinity and the yang principle in Chinese philosophy, with 具 (jù, "tool; implement") to denote what can be seen as the tools or implements of the male form.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)只(隻)动(動)物的阳(陽)具异(異)常明显(顯)。
    - Zhè zhī dòngwù de yángjù yìcháng míngxiǎn.
    - The animal's male genitals are unusually conspicuous.
  - 古希腊(臘)艺(藝)术(術)常描绘(繪)阳(陽)具。
    - Gǔ Xīlà yìshù cháng miáohuì yángjù.
    - Ancient Greek art often depicted male genitals.
  - 在生物学(學)中，阳(陽)具是一个(個)重要的研究对(對)象。
    - Zài shēngwùxué zhōng, yángjù shì yīgè zhòngyào de yánjiū duìxiàng.
    - In biology, male genitals are an important subject of research.
### 阳(陽)具 → 阳(陽)
- **traditional:** 陽
- **definition:** male; positive
- **pinyin:** yáng
- **components:**
  - 昜
    - yáng
    - sunshine
  - 阜
    - fù
    - hill
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 昜 (yáng, "sunshine") + Semantic: 阜 (fù, "hill") ->
    - Brightness or influence from a hill exposed to the sun, extended to mean "male" or "positive."
  - **interpretation:** Originally, 昜 (yáng, "sunshine") depicted sunlight. The addition of 阜 (fù, "hill") emphasizes exposure to sunlight on a hill, leading to the association with brightness or positivity.
  - **simplification:** 阳(陽) simplifies the traditional 陽 by replacing 昜 with 日 (rì, "sun"), reflecting the focus on sunlight in the character's meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他是家中的阳(陽)。
    - Tā shì jiāzhōng de yáng.
    - He is the male figure in the family.
  - 在这(這)场(場)运(運)动(動)会(會)上，他的阳(陽)性能量感染了队(隊)友。
    - Zài zhè chǎng yùndònghuì shàng, tā de yángnéng liàng gǎnrǎn le duìyǒu.
    - At this sports meet, his positive energy inspired his teammates.

### 阳(陽)具 → 阳(陽) → 昜 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
- **examples:**
  - 晨曦来(來)临(臨)时(時)，日出东(東)方。
    - Chénxī láilín shí, rìchū dōngfāng.
    - At the break of dawn, the sun rises in the east.

    - Wēnnuǎn de rìguāng sǎ mǎn dàdì.
    - The warm sunlight spreads over the earth.
  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。
    - Zhōngwǔ shífēn, rì gāo guà tiān.
    - At noon, the sun hangs high in the sky.

### 阳(陽)具 → 阳(陽) → 昜 → 彡

- **pinyin:** xiǎn
- **etymology:**

- **etymology:**

  - **description:**
    - Depicts a three-legged bronze vessel ->
    - later used for divination
  - **interpretation:** The character 鼎(dǐng, 'tripod') is a pictogram that depicts a tripod vessel with three legs, symbolizing its use in cooking and ritual sacrifices. Over time, it was also borrowed phonetically to represent the concept of divination.
