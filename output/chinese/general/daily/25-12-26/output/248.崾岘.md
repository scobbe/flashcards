## Yaoxian (place name)
### yǎoxiàn
---
- **definition:** Yaoxian (place name)
- **pinyin:** yǎoxiàn
- **components:**
  - 崾
    - yǎo
    - high and steep
  - 岘
    - xiàn
    - ridge; peak
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 崾岘 (yǎoxiàn, "Yaoxian") is a place name that consists of 崾 (yǎo, "high and steep") and 岘 (xiàn, "ridge; peak"). This combination likely refers to a location characterized by high and steep terrain, typical of many place names that describe geographical features in Chinese.
  - **interpretation:** 崾 (yǎo, "high and steep") and 岘 (xiàn, "ridge; peak") combine to describe a location with prominent and steep geographical features, suggesting a rugged landscape.
- **examples:**
  - 我计划明年去崾岘(峴)旅行。
    - Wǒ jìhuà míngnián qù yǎoxiàn lǚxíng.
    - I plan to travel to Yaoxian next year.
  - 崾岘(峴)被誉(譽)为(為)美丽(麗)的古镇(鎮)，吸引了许(許)多游(遊)客。
    - Yǎoxiàn bèi yù wéi měilì de gǔzhèn, xīyǐnle xǔduō yóukè.
    - Yaoxian is acclaimed as a beautiful ancient town, attracting many tourists.
  - 传(傳)说(說)中，崾岘(峴)藏有仙泉。
    - Chuánshuō zhōng, yǎoxiàn cáng yǒu xiānquán.
    - According to legend, Yaoxian hides a fairy spring.
### 崾岘 → 崾
- **definition:** high and steep
- **pinyin:** yǎo
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 幺
    - yāo
    - tiny; short
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 山 (shān, "mountain") + 幺 (yāo, "tiny; short") =
    - mountain with a narrow, lofty height ->
    - high and steep
  - **interpretation:** 崾 (yǎo, "high and steep") combines 山 (shān, "mountain") as the semantic element, representing the landscape, and 幺 (yāo, "tiny; short") for its phonetic contribution, suggesting the narrowness or steepness of the terrain. This combination reflects the concept of a high and steep mountain.
### 崾岘 → 崾 → 山
- **definition:** mountain
- **pinyin:** shān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three mountain peaks
  - **interpretation:** The character 山(shān, "mountain") visually represents the concept of a mountain using three vertical lines that suggest peaks. This depiction is consistent with early forms found in Oracle Bone Script, emphasizing the notion of natural elevation associated with mountains.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)去爬山。
    - Wǒmen qù pá shān.
    - We go climb the mountain.
  - 那座大山让(讓)人惊(驚)叹(嘆)。

    - That mountain astonishes people.
  - 远(遠)处(處)的山隐(隱)约(約)在云(雲)中，令人心旷(曠)神怡。
    - Yuǎnchù de shān yǐnyuē zài yún zhōng, lìng rén xīnkuàng shényí.

### 崾岘 → 岘(峴)
- **traditional:** 峴
- **definition:** ridge; peak
- **pinyin:** xiàn
- **components:**
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
  - 見
    - jiàn
    - see
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 山 (mountain) + phonetic 見 (jiàn) ->
    - mountain pass
  - **interpretation:** The character 峴 captures the concept of a mountain pass by combining 山(shān, 'mountain') for its semantic indication of a mountainous region and 見 (jiàn) for its phonetic contribution. The phonetic element suggests the pronunciation, while the semantic component ties it to the landscape feature of a pass through mountains.
  - **simplification:** 岘 is the simplified form of 峴, with the simplification reducing components to maintain phonetic hints in a more visually streamlined form.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)经(經)过(過)岘(峴)山。
    - Wǒmen jīngguò xiàn shān.
    - We passed through Xiàn Mountain.
  - 岘(峴)口的风(風)景非常壮(壯)观(觀)。
    - Xiàn kǒu de fēngjǐng fēicháng zhuàngguān.
    - The scenery of the Xiàn Mountain pass is very spectacular.
  - 秋天时(時)，岘(峴)山会(會)被黄(黃)色的叶(葉)子覆盖(蓋)。
    - Qiūtiān shí, xiànshān huì bèi huángsè de yèzi fùgài.
    - In autumn, Xiàn Mountain is covered with yellow leaves.

### 崾岘 → 岘(峴) → 見
- **definition:** see
- **pinyin:** jiàn
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 卩
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 目 (mù, "eye") + 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") =
    - a kneeling figure with large eye ->
    - see
  - **interpretation:** The combination of a human figure and an eye visually represents the act of seeing. The eye symbolizes vision, while the person emphasizes the human context of this action, making the meaning intuitive.
  - **simplification:** 见(見) is derived from using a cursive variant of the character in place of the traditional form.
- **examples:**
  - 我昨天见(見)到她。
    - Wǒ zuótiān jiàn dào tā.
    - I saw her yesterday.
  - 太阳(陽)升起时(時)，我看见(見)了大海。
    - Tàiyáng shēng qǐ shí, wǒ kàn jiàn le dàhǎi.
    - When the sun rose, I saw the sea.
  - 在公园(園)里(裡)，我见(見)到了老朋友。
    - Zài gōngyuán lǐ, wǒ jiàn dào le lǎo péngyǒu.

### 崾岘 → 岘(峴) → 見 → 卩
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.
- **examples:**
  - 那位卩正虔诚(誠)祈祷(禱)。
    - Nà wèi jié zhèng qiánchéng qídǎo.
    - That kneeling person is praying devoutly.
  - 礼(禮)仪(儀)师(師)指出，卩代表谦(謙)卑与(與)敬意。
    - Lǐyí shī zhǐchū, jié dàibiǎo qiānbēi yǔ jìngyì.
    - The ritual master pointed out that the kneeling person represents humility and respect.
  - 在庄(莊)严(嚴)仪(儀)式中，卩象征(徵)着(著)内(內)心的虔敬。
    - Zài zhuāngyán yíshì zhōng, jié xiàngzhēngzhe nèixīn de qiánjìng.
    - In the solemn ceremony, the kneeling figure symbolizes inner devotion.
