## strawberry
### cǎoméi
---
- **definition:** strawberry
- **pinyin:** cǎoméi
- **components:**
  - 草
    - cǎo
    - grass; herbaceous plant; rough; draft
  - 莓
    - méi
    - berry; small fruit; bramble
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 草 (cǎo, "grass") refers to small or low plants, often used generically for herbaceous plants. 莓 (méi, "berry") is a term for small, soft fruits.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 草 (cǎo, "grass") and 莓 (méi, "berry") gives the impression of a berry-like fruit growing close to the ground, akin to grass and other low vegetation. This reflects the actual growth pattern of strawberries, which are ground-hugging plants. The name evokes the image of a berry that grows among grass or in fields like herbaceous plants.
- **examples:**
  - 草莓很甜。
    - Cǎoméi hěn tián.
    - Strawberries are very sweet.
  - 她喜欢(歡)吃草莓。
    - Tā xǐhuān chī cǎoméi.
    - She likes to eat strawberries.
  - 这(這)果盘(盤)里(裡)有草莓，香蕉和葡萄。
    - Zhè guǒpán lǐ yǒu cǎoméi, xiāngjiāo hé pútáo.
    - This fruit platter has strawberries, bananas, and grapes.
### 草莓 → 草
- **definition:** grass; herbaceous plant; rough; draft
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass; plant
  - 早
    - zǎo
    - early
  - 皂
    - zào
    - black; soap
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 ("grass; plant") + phonetic 早 ->
    - originally referred to 皂 ->
    - borrowed for "grass"
  - **interpretation:** The character 草 (cǎo, "grass; herb") combines the semantic 艹 ("grass; plant"), which visually represents vegetation, with the phonetic 早 (zǎo). Although originally linked to the meaning of 皂 (zào) as "acorn," it was later borrowed to denote "grass" due to the semantic component's direct depiction of plant life.
- **examples:**
  - 草地上有很多花。
    - Cǎodì shàng yǒu hěn duō huā.
    - There are many flowers on the grass.
  - 他正在割草。
    - Tā zhèngzài gē cǎo.
    - He is mowing the grass.
  - 春天时(時)，草变(變)得非常绿(綠)。
    - Chūntiān shí, cǎo biàn dé fēicháng lǜ.
    - In spring, the grass becomes very green.

### 草莓 → 莓 → 每 → 母
- **definition:** mother
- **pinyin:** mǔ
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - woman
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** differentiated form
  - **description:**
    - 女 (nǚ, "woman") + addition of two dots ->
    - distinguished as 母 (mǔ, "mother")
  - **interpretation:** The character 母 (mǔ, "mother") is a differentiated form of 女 (nǚ, "woman") with the addition of two dots to distinguish it. In early script forms, both 母 (mǔ, "mother") and 女 (nǚ, "woman") were represented by the same form as 女, necessitating the addition of dots to create a visual distinction. The character 毋, originally borrowed from 母, further specialized by changing the dots to a stroke to avoid confusion between the characters.
- **examples:**
