## master; skilled worker
### shīfu
---
- **traditional:** 師傅
- **definition:** master; skilled worker
- **pinyin:** shīfu
- **components:**
  - 师(師)
    - shī
    - master; teacher; army commander
  - 傅
    - fù
    - tutor; assist; attach
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 師 (shī, "master; teacher") originally meant "army commander", evoking an image of a leading figure with authority and wisdom. 傅 (fù, "tutor; assist") shows an idea of guidance and support, often in the role of a knowledgeable mentor.
  - **interpretation:** 師 (shī, "master; teacher") suggests authority, excellent skills, and leadership, connecting to the role of experts or skilled individuals as masters of their craft. 傅 (fù, "tutor; assist") conveys the idea of teaching and providing support, implying mastery through tutelage or craftsmanship. Together, 师(師)傅( ) (shīfu) describes someone both highly skilled and capable of instructing others, whether in education or technical expertise, thus capturing the dual aspects of mastery and mentorship.

    - Line on the hand (ideographic) emphasizing the elbow ->
    - represents measurement unit between wrist and specific forearm position ->
    - meaning 'inch'
  - **interpretation:** 寸 (cùn, "inch") originated as a depiction of a line on the hand, indicating the area near the elbow. This specific placement was considered significant for pulse palpation, originally suggesting a forearm position, which then came to denote the measurement 'inch'.
