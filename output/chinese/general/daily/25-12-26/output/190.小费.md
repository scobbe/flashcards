## tip; gratuity
### xiǎofèi
---
- **traditional:** 小費
- **definition:** tip; gratuity
- **pinyin:** xiǎofèi
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little
  - 费(費)
    - fèi
    - expense; fee; cost
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小 (xiǎo, "small") + 費 (fèi, "expense"): Together, 小費 (小费) (xiǎofèi) refers to a "small expense," aligning with the concept of a tip or gratuity, which is a minor cost offered for service
  - **interpretation:** In the context of service, a tip is viewed as a minor monetary gesture; thus, the character 小 (xiǎo, "small") represents the minor nature of the expense, while 費 (fèi, "expense") designates it as an additional monetary consideration beyond the primary cost. This minor financial gesture is given voluntarily to acknowledge service.
- **examples:**
  - 在餐厅(廳)吃饭(飯)后(後)，我给(給)服务(務)员(員)小费(費)。
    - Zài cāntīng chīfàn hòu, wǒ gěi fúwùyuán xiǎofèi.
    - After dining at the restaurant, I gave the waiter a tip.
  - 服务(務)很周到，所以我决(決)定给(給)更多小费(費)。
    - Fúwù hěn zhōudào, suǒyǐ wǒ juédìng gěi gèng duō xiǎofèi.
    - The service was very attentive, so I decided to give a bigger tip.
  - 在美国(國)，给(給)餐馆(館)服务(務)员(員)小费(費)是很常见(見)的。
    - Zài Měiguó, gěi cānguǎn fúwùyuán xiǎofèi shì hěn chángjiàn de.
    - In the United States, it's very common to give restaurant waitstaff a tip.
### 小费(費) → 小
- **definition:** small; little
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three small dots arranged symmetrically to represent something small
  - **interpretation:** The character 小(xiǎo, 'small') uses three small strokes to symbolize size or quantity, visually capturing the essence of 'smallness'.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)小猫(貓)很可爱(愛)。
    - Zhège xiǎo māo hěn kě'ài.
    - This small cat is very cute.
  - 这(這)座小桥(橋)横(橫)跨河流，连(連)接两(兩)岸。
    - Zhè zuò xiǎo qiáo héngkuà héliú, liánjiē liǎng àn.
    - This small bridge spans the river, connecting both banks.
  - 请(請)给(給)我一小杯水。
    - Qǐng gěi wǒ yī xiǎo bēi shuǐ.
    - Please give me a small cup of water.

### 小费(費) → 费(費)
- **traditional:** 費
- **definition:** expense; fee; cost
- **pinyin:** fèi
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 弗
    - fú
    - not
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 貝 (bèi, "cowry") + phonetic 弗 (fú) ->
    - fee; expense; cost
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 貝 (bèi, "cowry"), which is associated with money and value, symbolizing financial transactions, with 弗 (fú) as the phonetic component providing the sound. The association with cowries, historically used as currency, reinforces the notion of 'fee' or 'expense'.
  - **simplification:** 費 was simplified to 费. 貝 (bèi, "cowry") was simplified to 贝(貝) to reduce the character's stroke count.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)项(項)目的费(費)用很高。
    - Zhège xiàngmù de fèiyòng hěn gāo.
    - The expenses for this project are very high.
  - 他每个(個)月的电(電)费(費)都超过(過)了预(預)算。
    - Tā měi gè yuè de diànfèi dōu chāoguòle yùsuàn.
    - His monthly electricity bill always exceeds the budget.
  - 学(學)费(費)是家庭的一大负担(是一個家庭的一大負擔)。
    - Xuéfèi shì yíge jiātíng de yí dà fùdān.
    - Tuition fees are a major burden for a family.
### 小费(費) → 费(費) → 貝
- **definition:** cowry
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为(為)货(貨)币(幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
### 小费(費) → 费(費) → 弗
- **definition:** not
- **pinyin:** fú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts rope wrapped around two pieces of wood to keep them straight ->
    - later borrowed phonetically to mean 'no'
  - **interpretation:** The character 弗 originally depicted a rope wrapped around two sticks, an image that suggests containment or prevention. This visual was phonetically borrowed to express 'no', leveraging the form for its sound rather than its visual logic.
- **examples:**
  - 他对(對)此毫无(無)兴(興)趣。
    - Tā duì cǐ háo wú xìngqù.
    - He has no interest in this.
  - 事情并(並)非如我所愿(願)。
    - Shìqíng bìng fēi rú wǒ suǒ yuàn.
    - Things are not as I wished.
  - 这(這)并(並)不简(簡)单(單)。
    - Zhè bìng bù jiǎndān.
    - This is not simple.
