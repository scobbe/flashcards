## you’re welcome; don’t mention it
### bú kèqi
---
- **traditional:** 不客氣
- **definition:** you’re welcome; don’t mention it
- **pinyin:** bú kèqi
- **components:**
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
  - 客
    - kè
    - guest; visitor; stranger; traveler
  - 氣
    - qì
    - air; manner; atmosphere; energy
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 不 (bù, "not") negates something, implying its absence or lack. 客 (kè, "guest") refers to someone being treated with courtesy or politeness, stemming from the idea of hospitality. 气(氣) (qì, "air; manner") can mean someone's demeanor or the energy/atmosphere they project.
  - **interpretation:** The phrase 不客氣 (bú kèqi) literally means 'not acting as a guest'. In the context of responding to thanks, it signals that the formality of hospitality is not imposed, encouraging informality and indicating that no special thanks are necessary. Therefore, it translates to 'you’re welcome' as it deflects the need for gratitude, expressing the ease or willingness of doing something without expecting a return.
- **examples:**
  - 谢(謝)谢(謝)你的帮(幫)助，不客气(氣)。
    - Xièxiè nǐ de bāngzhù, bú kèqi.
    - Thank you for your help, you're welcome.
  - 你对(對)我这(這)么(麼)好，我送你一朵玫瑰，不客气(氣)。
    - Nǐ duì wǒ zhème hǎo, wǒ sòng nǐ yī duǒ méiguī, bú kèqi.
    - You've been so kind to me, I give you a rose, you're welcome.
  - 如果你有问(問)题(題)，随(隨)时(時)问(問)我，不客气(氣)。
    - Rúguǒ nǐ yǒu wèntí, suíshí wèn wǒ, bú kèqi.
    - If you have questions, feel free to ask me, you're welcome.
