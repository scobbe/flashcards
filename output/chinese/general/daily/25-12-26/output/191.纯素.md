## vegan
### chúnsù
---
- **traditional:** 純素
- **definition:** vegan
- **pinyin:** chúnsù
- **components:**
  - 纯(純)
    - chún
    - pure; simple
  - 素
    - sù
    - element; plainly; vegetable
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The term 純素 (chúnsù, "vegan") is a modern compound word representing a lifestyle choice centered around avoiding the consumption of animal products. The word combines the concept of purity or unmixed nature with basic simplicity.
  - **interpretation:** 純 (chún, "pure; simple") suggests an absence of impurities or a basic, unadulterated state. In the context of 純素 (chúnsù, "vegan"), it implies a diet that avoids complex mixtures, focusing on fundamental, non-animal ingredients. 素 (sù, "element; plainly; vegetable") historically refers to simplicity, often associated with vegetarianism or a plain, essential nature. Here, it embodies the principle of consuming only plant-based foods. The combination of these terms inherently suggests a plant-based diet characterized by simplicity and purity, devoid of animal products.
- **examples:**
  - 她只吃纯(純)素的食物。
    - Tā zhǐ chī chúnsù de shíwù.
    - She only eats vegan food.
  - 纯(純)素饮(飲)食对(對)健康有益。
    - Chúnsù yǐnshí duì jiànkāng yǒuyì.
    - A vegan diet is beneficial for health.
  - 许(許)多餐厅(廳)提供纯(純)素选(選)项(項)。
    - Xǔduō cāntīng tígōng chúnsù xuǎnxiàng.
    - Many restaurants offer vegan options.
### 纯(純)素 → 纯(純)
- **traditional:** 純
- **definition:** pure; simple
- **pinyin:** chún
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
  - 屯
    - tún
    - barracks; station troops
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 糸 (mì, "silk; thread") + phonetic 屯 (tún)
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the semantic element of 糸 (mì, "silk; thread"), representing something fine and uniform like a singular, untainted thread, with the phonetic 屯 (tún) to provide the pronunciation. Together, these components convey the sense of something being 'pure' or 'without impurities,' emphasizing the idea of uniformity and exclusivity in purity.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by reducing 糸 (mì, "silk; thread") to 纟, which is a simpler form used in several characters to maintain the semantic implication of 'silk; thread.'
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)水看起来(來)很纯(純)。
    - Zhège shuǐ kàn qǐlái hěn chún.
    - This water looks very pure.
  - 她有一颗(顆)纯(純)真的心。
    - Tā yǒu yī kē chúnzhēn de xīn.
    - She has a pure heart.
  - 为(為)了保持纯(純)净(淨)，不能往茶里(裡)加糖。
    - Wèile bǎochí chúnjìng，bùnéng wǎng chá lǐ jiā táng.
    - To keep it pure, you shouldn't add sugar to the tea.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**

    - emphasizes sprout
  - **interpretation:** The character 屯(tún, 'station') originally depicts a plant sprout with an added line to emphasize its growth. This imagery reflects the concept of development or establishment, leading to the sense of 'station' as a place where something is established or set up.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)附近有一个(個)储(儲)备(備)粮(糧)的屯。

### 纯(純)素 → 素

- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 糸(絲)
    - sī
    - silk
  - 粟
    - sù
    - millet
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 糸(絲) (sī, "silk") + phonetic: 粟 (sù, "millet") =
    - combining silk and millet ->
    - suggests plainness or simplicity
  - **interpretation:** The character 素 originally depicted plain, undyed silk, suggesting simplicity or purity. The phonetic element 粟 (sù, "millet") supports the sound and also relates to a basic, unadorned life.
- **examples:**

### 纯(純)素 → 素 → 粟
- **definition:** millet
- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
  - 角
    - jiǎo
    - horn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 米 (mǐ, "rice") as semantic + 角 as phonetic ->
    - intermediate form 𠧪 ->
    - further corrupted to 西
  - **interpretation:** The character underwent several stages, initially depicting a pictogram for grain or millet with 米 (mǐ, "rice") showing that it refers to grain. The phonetic 角 contributed to the sound, evolving through 𠧪 before finally becoming 西, which does not directly connect but forms the current appearance.
- **examples:**
  - 粟是古老的谷(穀)物之一。
    - Sù shì gǔlǎo de gǔwù zhī yī.
    - Millet is one of the ancient grains.
  - 在干(乾)旱的地区(區)，粟可以生长(長)得很好。
    - Zài gānhàn de dìqū, sù kěyǐ shēngzhǎng de hěn hǎo.
    - In arid regions, millet can grow very well.
  - 古代中国(國)人常用粟制(製)作为(為)主食。
    - Gǔdài Zhōngguó rén chángyòng sù zhìzuò wéi zhǔshí.
    - Ancient Chinese people often used millet as a staple food.

- **pinyin:** mǐ

  - **description:**
