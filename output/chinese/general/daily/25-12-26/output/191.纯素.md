### chúnsù
---
- **traditional:** 純素
- **definition:** vegan
- **pinyin:** chúnsù
- **components:**
  - 纯(純)
    - chún
    - pure; simple
  - 素
    - sù
    - element; plainly; vegetable
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The term 純素 (chúnsù, "vegan") is a modern compound word representing a lifestyle choice centered around avoiding the consumption of animal products. The word combines the concept of purity or unmixed nature with basic simplicity.
  - **interpretation:** 純 (chún, "pure; simple") suggests an absence of impurities or a basic, unadulterated state. In the context of 純素 (chúnsù, "vegan"), it implies a diet that avoids complex mixtures, focusing on fundamental, non-animal ingredients. 素 (sù, "element; plainly; vegetable") historically refers to simplicity, often associated with vegetarianism or a plain, essential nature. Here, it embodies the principle of consuming only plant-based foods. The combination of these terms inherently suggests a plant-based diet characterized by simplicity and purity, devoid of animal products.

### 纯(純)素( ) → 纯(純)
- **traditional:** 純

- **pinyin:** chún
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì

    - tún
    - barracks; station troops
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 糸 (mì, "silk; thread") + phonetic 屯 (tún)
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the semantic element of 糸 (mì, "silk; thread"), representing something fine and uniform like a singular, untainted thread, with the phonetic 屯 (tún) to provide the pronunciation. Together, these components convey the sense of something being 'pure' or 'without impurities,' emphasizing the idea of uniformity and exclusivity in purity.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by reducing 糸 (mì, "silk; thread") to 纟, which is a simpler form used in several characters to maintain the semantic implication of 'silk; thread.'
