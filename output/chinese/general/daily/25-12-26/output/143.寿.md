## longevity
### shòu
---
- **traditional:** 壽
- **definition:** longevity
- **pinyin:** shòu
- **components:**
  - 士
    - shì
    - scholar
  - 寸
    - cùn
    - inch
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 士 (shì, "scholar") + phonetic 寸 (cùn) ->
    - longevity
  - **interpretation:** 壽 (shòu, "longevity") consists of 士 (shì, "scholar"), representing an esteemed, noble quality, combined with the phonetic 寸 (cùn), indicating the measurement of life. Together they convey the notion of a long, honored life.
  - **simplification:** Simplified 寿 from traditional 壽 involves the omission of 示 (shì, "sign") and retaining the common phonetic component, 寸 (cùn), focusing on the pronunciation without the semantic complexity.
- **examples:**
  - 爷(爺)爷(爺)的寿(壽)命很长(長)，大家都希望他健康长(長)寿(壽)。
    - Yéye de shòumìng hěn cháng, dàjiā dōu xīwàng tā jiànkāng chángshòu.
    - Grandpa's life is very long; everyone hopes he stays healthy and has a long life.
  - 在传(傳)统(統)文化中，龟(龜)象征(徵)着(著)长(長)寿(壽)。
    - Zài chuántǒng wénhuà zhōng, guī xiàngzhēngzhe chángshòu.
    - In traditional culture, the turtle symbolizes longevity.
  - 他们(們)为(為)奶奶的八十大寿(壽)举行(舉)了(行)一(了)个(一)盛(個)大(盛)的(大)庆(的)祝(慶)。(祝)。
    - Tāmen wèi nǎinai de bāshí dàshòu jǔxíngle yīgè shèngdà de qìngzhù.
    - They held a grand celebration for grandma's 80th birthday.
### 寿(壽) → 士
- **definition:** scholar
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a ritual axe, possibly made of jade ->
    - symbolized soldier ->
    - intellectual or officer over time
  - **interpretation:** The character 士(shì, 'scholar') originally depicted a ritual axe used in ceremonies, implying a person of a certain rank or status who could wield such symbols. This connection to ceremonial authority evolved to mean 'soldier' and later 'intellectual' or 'officer', roles associated with leadership and knowledge in societal hierarchies.
- **examples:**
  - 历(歷)史上，士被视(視)为(為)一种(種)权(權)力的象征(徵)。
    - Lìshǐ shàng, shì bèi shì wéi yī zhǒng quánlì de xiàngzhēng.
    - Historically, the 'shi' was seen as a symbol of power.
  - 在古代，只有贵(貴)族才拥(擁)有士。
    - Zài gǔdài, zhǐyǒu guìzú cái yǒngyǒu shì.
    - In ancient times, only nobles possessed the 'shi'.
  - 士的制(製)作材料可能是珍贵(貴)的金属(屬)。
    - Shì de zhìzuò cáiliào kěnéng shì zhēnguì de jīnshǔ.
    - The materials used to make the 'shi' might have been precious metals.

### 寿(壽) → 寸
- **definition:** inch
- **pinyin:** cùn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Line on the hand (ideographic) emphasizing the elbow ->
    - represents measurement unit between wrist and specific forearm position ->
    - meaning 'inch'
  - **interpretation:** 寸 (cùn, "inch") originated as a depiction of a line on the hand, indicating the area near the elbow. This specific placement was considered significant for pulse palpation, originally suggesting a forearm position, which then came to denote the measurement 'inch'.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)块(塊)布每一寸都是精心制(製)作。
    - Zhè kuài bù měi yī cùn dōu shì jīngxīn zhìzuò.
    - Every inch of this cloth is crafted with care.
  - 请(請)用卷尺量一寸，每一寸数(數)据(據)都准(準)确(確)。
    - Qǐng yòng juǎnchǐ liàng yī cùn, měi yī cùn shùjù dōu zhǔnquè.
    - Please use a tape measure to measure an inch, every inch's data is accurate.
