## happy; joyful
### xīn
---
- **definition:** happy; joyful
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **components:**
  - 斤
    - jīn
    - axe
  - 欠
    - qiàn
    - yawn; lack
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 斤 (jīn, "axe") + semantic 欠 (qiàn, "yawn; lack") ->
    - happiness
  - **interpretation:** The character 欣 (xīn, "happy; joyful") combines 斤 (jīn, "axe") as the phonetic component providing the sound, with 欠 (qiàn, "yawn; lack") as the semantic component. The image of an open mouth 欠 combined with the sound from 斤 evokes the act of expressing joy or delight, as laughter often involves an open mouth. Therefore, the character represents a state of happiness or joy.
- **examples:**
  - 我欣然同意。
    - Wǒ xīnrán tóngyì.
    - I happily agreed.

- **pinyin:** qiàn

  - **interpretation:** The character 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") visually represents a person with their mouth open, capturing the physical gesture associated with yawning. It symbolizes a relaxed or tired state.
