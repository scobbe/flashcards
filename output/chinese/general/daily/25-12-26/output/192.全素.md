## vegan
### quánsù
---
- **definition:** vegan
- **pinyin:** quánsù
- **components:**
  - 全
    - quán
    - whole; complete; total
  - 素
    - sù
    - vegetarian; plain; simple
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 全 (quán, "whole; complete; total") + 素 (sù, "vegetarian; plain; simple") refers to a dietary lifestyle that is fully vegetarian without animal products.
  - **interpretation:** 全 (quán, "whole; complete; total") indicates entirety or completeness. 素 (sù, "vegetarian; plain; simple") refers to a diet or lifestyle that is free from meat, typically incorporating no animal products. Together, 全素 (quánsù) refers to a diet that is entirely vegetarian, emphasizing the absence of any animal-derived ingredients, hence 'vegan'.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天决(決)定吃全素。
    - Wǒ jīntiān juédìng chī quánsù.
    - I've decided to eat vegan today.
  - 全素饮(飲)食有益于(於)健康。
    - Quánsù yǐnshí yǒuyì yú jiànkāng.
    - A vegan diet is beneficial for health.
  - 在派对(對)上，提供了各种(種)全素食品。
    - Zài pàiduì shàng, tígōngle gèzhǒng quánsù shípǐn.
    - Various vegan foods were provided at the party.

- **pinyin:** quán
- **components:**

### 全素 → 全 → 玉
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a picture of a cong (琮), a sort of ritual jade totem ->
    - dot added to differentiate from 王 (wáng, "king") ->
    - final form 玉 (yù, "jade")
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a picture of a jade cong, emphasizing its role in rituals and its cultural significance. The image eventually included a distinguishing dot to ensure clarity from similar characters like 王, underscoring the value jade held as a material in ancient contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 她收到了一个漂亮的玉手镯(鐲)。
    - Tā shōu dào le yī gè piàoliang de yù shǒuzhuó.
    - She received a beautiful jade bracelet.
  - 皇帝的王冠上镶(鑲)嵌着(著)闪(閃)耀的玉石。
    - Huángdì de wángguān shàng xiāngqiànzhe shǎnyào de yùshí.
    - The emperor's crown was inlaid with shining jade stones.
  - 古代的玉器常常被视(視)为(為)珍贵(貴)的艺(藝)术(術)品。
    - Gǔdài de yùqì chángcháng bèi shìwéi zhēnguì de yìshùpǐn.
    - Ancient jade artifacts are often regarded as precious works of art.

### 全素 → 素
- **definition:** vegetarian; plain; simple
- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 糸(絲)
    - sī
    - silk
  - 粟
    - sù
    - millet
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 糸(絲) (sī, "silk") + phonetic: 粟 (sù, "millet") =
    - combining silk and millet ->
    - suggests plainness or simplicity
  - **interpretation:** The character 素 originally depicted plain, undyed silk, suggesting simplicity or purity. The phonetic element 粟 (sù, "millet") supports the sound and also relates to a basic, unadorned life.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)条(條)围(圍)巾设(設)计(計)得很素。
    - Zhè tiáo wéijīn shèjì de hěn sù.
    - This scarf is designed very simply.
  - 他坚(堅)持吃素，拒绝(絕)肉食。
    - Tā jiānchí chī sù, jùjué ròushí.
    - He insists on a vegetarian diet and rejects meat.
  - 她向(嚮)往一种(種)素雅的风(風)格。
    - Tā xiàngwǎng yì zhǒng sùyǎ de fēnggé.

### 全素 → 素 → 粟
- **definition:** millet
- **pinyin:** sù
- **components:**
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
  - 角
    - jiǎo
    - horn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 米 (mǐ, "rice") as semantic + 角 as phonetic ->
    - intermediate form 𠧪 ->
    - further corrupted to 西
  - **interpretation:** The character underwent several stages, initially depicting a pictogram for grain or millet with 米 (mǐ, "rice") showing that it refers to grain. The phonetic 角 contributed to the sound, evolving through 𠧪 before finally becoming 西, which does not directly connect but forms the current appearance.
- **examples:**
  - 粟是古老的谷(穀)物之一。
    - Sù shì gǔlǎo de gǔwù zhī yī.
    - Millet is one of the ancient grains.
  - 在干(乾)旱的地区(區)，粟可以生长(長)得很好。
    - Zài gānhàn de dìqū, sù kěyǐ shēngzhǎng de hěn hǎo.
    - In arid regions, millet can grow very well.
  - 古代中国(國)人常用粟制(製)作为(為)主食。
    - Gǔdài Zhōngguó rén chángyòng sù zhìzuò wéi zhǔshí.
    - Ancient Chinese people often used millet as a staple food.
