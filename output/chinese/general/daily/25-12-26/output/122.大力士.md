- **etymology:**

### 大力士 → 大
- **definition:** big
- **pinyin:** dà
- **components:**
  - 夫

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a person facing forward ->
    - Borrowed phonetically for 'big'; original form of 夫 (fū, 'man')

### 大力士 → 大 → 夫
- **definition:** man
- **pinyin:** fū
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a variant form of 大 (dà, "big") representing an adult man with a hairpin ->
    - became associated with 夫 (fū, "man").
  - **interpretation:** 夫 (fū, "man") was visually similar to 大 (dà, "big") and was originally interchangeable. The addition of the horizontal stroke was interpreted as a hairpin, symbolizing adulthood for men.

### 大力士 → 士
- **definition:** scholar; man
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a ritual axe, possibly made of jade ->
    - symbolized soldier ->
    - intellectual or officer over time
  - **interpretation:** The character 士(shì, 'scholar') originally depicted a ritual axe used in ceremonies, implying a person of a certain rank or status who could wield such symbols. This connection to ceremonial authority evolved to mean 'soldier' and later 'intellectual' or 'officer', roles associated with leadership and knowledge in societal hierarchies.
