## programmer (slang)
### mǎnóng
---
- **traditional:** 碼農
- **definition:** programmer (slang)
- **pinyin:** mǎnóng
- **components:**
  - 码(碼)
    - mǎ
    - code; number; yard
  - 农(農)
    - nóng
    - farmer; agriculture; to farm
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 码(碼) (mǎ, "code; number") + 农(農) (nóng, "farmer") =
    - a humorous slang term for a programmer, suggesting someone who works hard writing lines of code like a farmer tending a field.
  - **interpretation:** The term 码(碼)农(農) (mǎnóng, "programmer") humorously implies that programmers tirelessly produce lines of code as farmers cultivate crops. This reflects the repetitive and labor-intensive nature of programming work.
  - **simplification:** 码: 碼, 农: 農
- **examples:**
  - 他( )是( )码(碼)农(農)。
    - Tā shì mǎnóng.
    - He is a programmer (slang).
  - 码(碼)农(農)加( )班( )晚( )，生( )活( )也( )不( )轻(輕)松(鬆)。
    - Mǎnóng jiābān wǎn, shēnghuó yě bù qīngsōng.
    - The programmer works overtime until late, and life is not easy.
