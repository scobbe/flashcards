## long time no see
### hǎojiǔ bújiàn
---
- **traditional:** 好久不見
- **definition:** long time no see
- **pinyin:** hǎojiǔ bújiàn
- **components:**
  - 好
    - hǎo
    - good
  - 久
    - jiǔ
    - long time
  - 不
    - bù
    - not
  - 见(見)
    - jiàn
    - see
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 好 (hǎo, "good") + 久 (jiǔ, "long time") + 不 (bù, "not") + 见(見) (jiàn, "see") combine to create the phrase meaning "long time no see."
  - **interpretation:** The phrase literally translates to 'good long time not see.' 好 (hǎo, "good") here serves as an intensifier, emphasizing 久 (jiǔ, "long time"). Together, they set up the context for time passing. Combining with 不 (bù, "not") + 见(見) (jiàn, "see"), the phrase directly conveys the idea of not seeing someone for a significant duration, epitomizing a common greeting after an extended separation.
- **examples:**
  - 好久不见(見)，你最近怎么(麼)样(樣)？
    - Hǎojiǔ bújiàn, nǐ zuìjìn zěnmeyàng?
    - Long time no see, how have you been recently?
  - 我们(們)在街上偶遇了，真是好久不见(見)！
    - Wǒmen zài jiē shàng ǒuyù le, zhēnshì hǎojiǔ bújiàn!
    - We bumped into each other on the street, it's really been a long time!
  - 见(見)到你真的很高兴(興)，好久不见(見)！
    - Jiàndào nǐ zhēnde hěn gāoxìng, hǎojiǔ bújiàn!
    - I'm really happy to see you, long time no see!
