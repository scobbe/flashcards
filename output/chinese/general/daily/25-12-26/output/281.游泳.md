## swim; swimming
### yóuyǒng
---
- **definition:** swim; swimming
- **pinyin:** yóuyǒng
- **components:**
  - 游(遊)
    - yóu
    - swim; travel; roam; wander
  - 泳
    - yǒng
    - swim
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 游(遊) (yóu, "to swim; to travel") + 泳 (yǒng, "to swim") =
    - a specific activity of swimming
  - **interpretation:** 游(遊) (yóu, "to swim; to travel") initially involves moving lightly and freely, which suits the motion in water, making 'to swim' one of its direct senses. 泳 (yǒng, "to swim") specifically refers to swimming as an activity, focusing on the controlled movement in water. The combination highlights both the aspect of travel through water and the specialized activity of swimming, forming a precise term for moving through water by swimming.
- **examples:**
  - 夏天时(時)我喜欢(歡)去海边(邊)游泳。
    - Xiàtiān shí wǒ xǐhuān qù hǎibiān yóuyǒng.
    - In the summer, I like to go swimming at the beach.
  - 他们(們)每天早上在游泳池游泳。
    - Tāmen měitiān zǎoshang zài yóuyǒngchí yóuyǒng.
    - They swim every morning in the swimming pool.
  - 游泳是一项(項)很好的锻(鍛)炼(鍊)方式。
    - Yóuyǒng shì yī xiàng hěnhǎo de duànliàn fāngshì.
    - Swimming is a great form of exercise.
### 游泳 → 游(遊)
- **traditional:** 遊
- **definition:** swim; travel; roam; wander
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 斿
    - yóu
    - travel
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 水 (shuǐ, "water") + Phonetic: 斿 (yóu, originally having meaning related to travel) =
    - movement through or on water ->
    - swim/travel/wander
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the water radical with a component originally meaning travel, highlighting both the aspect of swimming in water and wandering or traveling. This reflects the versatility in its meanings.
  - **simplification:** Traditional 遊 (yóu, "wander") uses 辵 (chuò, "walk") for traveling, simplified by replacing it with 水 (shuǐ, "water"), focusing on swimming, which in Chinese culture is closely related to wandering or traveling freely.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)游泳。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān yóuyǒng.
    - I like swimming.
  - 我们(們)周(週)末去郊外游(遊)，感受大自然。
    - Wǒmen zhōumò qù jiāowài yóu, gǎnshòu dàzìrán.
    - We will travel to the countryside on the weekend and enjoy nature.
  - 他梦(夢)想环(環)游(遊)世界。
    - Tā mèngxiǎng huányóu shìjiè.
    - He dreams of traveling the world.

### 游泳 → 游(遊) → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flowing water
  - **interpretation:** 水 (shuǐ, 'water') is a pictogram that directly represents the appearance of flowing water, making it intuitive and easy to recognize. Its simplicity and direct visual reference to its meaning have allowed it to remain unchanged over millennia.
- **examples:**
  - 水是一种(種)生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì yī zhǒng shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 夏天很热(熱)，喝水能解暑。
    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, hē shuǐ néng jiě shǔ.
    - It is very hot in summer, drinking water can relieve the heat.
  - 河水潺潺，沿岸绿(綠)树(樹)成荫(蔭)。
    - Hé shuǐ chánchán, yán'àn lǜshù chéng yīn.
    - The river flows babbling, and green trees along the bank provide shade.

  - **description:**

### 游泳 → 游(遊) → 斿 → 㫃
- **definition:** flag
- **pinyin:** yǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a flag on a pole with a decoration on top ->
    - stylized as 方(㫃)
  - **interpretation:** The character 㫃 originally depicted a flag on a pole, complete with a decorative element on top. Over time, it was stylized into its current form with the flag and pole disconnected and the image abstracted as 方(㫃). Although it shares visual similarities with characters like 朝 (cháo, "morning"), it is unrelated to them in meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 撤下军(軍)㫃。
    - Chèxià jūn bì.
    - Take down the military flag.
  - 他站在城楼(樓)上挥(揮)动(動)㫃。
    - Tā zhàn zài chénglóu shàng huīdòng bì.
    - He waved the flag on the city gate tower.
  - 大风(風)把祭典上的㫃吹得猎(獵)猎(獵)作响(響)。
    - Dàfēng bǎ jìdiǎn shàng de bì chuī dé liè liè zuò xiǎng.
    - The strong wind made the flag at the ceremony flutter noisily.

### 游泳 → 泳
- **definition:** swim
- **pinyin:** yǒng
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 永
    - yǒng
    - forever; eternal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic 永 (yǒng) ->
    - to swim
  - **interpretation:** The character 泳(yǒng) combines the semantic component 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water") to denote the concept of water, with the phonetic component 永 (yǒng) to indicate the sound. Swimming is intrinsically associated with water, so the semantic aspect appropriately reflects the character's meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天早上在游泳池里(裡)游泳。
    - Tā měitiān zǎoshang zài yóuyǒngchí lǐ yóuyǒng.
    - He swims in the swimming pool every morning.
  - 夏天到了，我们(們)经(經)常去海边(邊)游泳。
    - Xiàtiān dào le, wǒmen jīngcháng qù hǎibiān yóuyǒng.
    - Summer has arrived, and we often go to the beach to swim.
  - 学(學)会(會)游泳是很重要的技能。
    - Xuéhuì yóuyǒng shì hěn zhòngyào de jìnéng.
    - Learning to swim is a very important skill.

### 游泳 → 泳 → 永
- **definition:** forever; eternal
- **pinyin:** yǒng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a long river with tributaries ->
    - original form of 泳 (yǒng, "swim")
  - **interpretation:** The character 永 depicts a long river, which visually represents its tributaries joining together. This imagery of an expansive and enduring water body conveys the sense of 'long-lasting' or 'perpetual' by suggesting continuity and flow. Additionally, the original association with water makes 永 also relate to 泳 (yǒng, "swim"), highlighting its aquatic connections.
- **examples:**
  - 友情是永恒(恆)的。
    - Yǒuqíng shì yǒnghéng de.
    - Friendship is eternal.
  - 她对(對)音乐(樂)的热(熱)爱(愛)永不会(會)改变(變)。
    - Tā duì yīnyuè de rè'ài yǒng bù huì gǎibiàn.
    - Her love for music will never change.
  - 他们(們)的故事将(將)在历(歷)史中永存。
    - Tāmen de gùshì jiāng zài lìshǐ zhōng yǒngcún.
    - Their story will endure in history.
