## ocean; seas
### hǎiyáng
---
- **definition:** ocean; seas
- **pinyin:** hǎiyáng
- **components:**
  - 海
    - hǎi
    - sea
  - 洋
    - yáng
    - ocean; vast
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 海 (hǎi, "sea") refers to a body of saline water, often part of the ocean. 洋 (yáng, "ocean; vast") signifies something expansive and broad, usually referencing the larger bodies of water that cover much of the Earth's surface. Together, 海洋 (hǎiyáng, "ocean; seas") denotes the vast, interconnected bodies of saltwater that cover the majority of the Earth's surface.
  - **interpretation:** 海 (hǎi, "sea") combined with 洋 (yáng, "ocean; vast") creates a term that emphasizes the vastness and interconnectivity of the seas, covering the Earth's surface.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )喜( )欢(歡)海( )洋( )。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hǎiyáng.
    - I like the ocean.
  - 科( )学(學)家( )研( )究( )海( )洋( )，探索未知。
    - Kēxuéjiā yánjiū hǎiyáng, tànsuǒ wèizhī.
    - Scientists study the ocean and explore the unknown.
  - 深邃的海洋孕育生命。
    - Shēnsuì de hǎiyáng yùnyù shēngmìng.
    - The deep ocean nurtures life.

### 海洋 → 海 → 每 → 母
- **definition:** mother
- **pinyin:** mǔ
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - woman
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** differentiated form
  - **description:**
    - 女 (nǚ, "woman") + addition of two dots ->
    - distinguished as 母 (mǔ, "mother")
  - **interpretation:** The character 母 (mǔ, "mother") is a differentiated form of 女 (nǚ, "woman") with the addition of two dots to distinguish it. In early script forms, both 母 (mǔ, "mother") and 女 (nǚ, "woman") were represented by the same form as 女, necessitating the addition of dots to create a visual distinction. The character 毋, originally borrowed from 母, further specialized by changing the dots to a stroke to avoid confusion between the characters.
