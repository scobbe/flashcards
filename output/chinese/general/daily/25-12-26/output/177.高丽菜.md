## cabbage
### gāolícài
---
- **traditional:** 高麗菜
- **definition:** cabbage
- **pinyin:** gāolícài
- **components:**
  - 高麗
    - gāolí
    - Korea; Goryeo dynasty
  - 菜
    - cài
    - vegetable; dish
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 高 (gāo, "high"), 麗 (lì, "beautiful"), 菜 (cài, "vegetable")
  - **interpretation:** 高麗 (gāolí, "Korea") refers historically to the Goryeo dynasty in Korea, known for its cultural influence in ancient times. 菜 (cài, "vegetable") indicates this is a type of vegetable. Thus, 高丽(麗)菜 (gāolícài) means 'Korean vegetable,' which is how cabbage is referred to in some regions, especially Taiwan.
- **examples:**
  - 我很喜欢(歡)吃高丽(麗)菜。
    - Wǒ hěn xǐhuān chī gāolícài.
    - I really like eating cabbage.
  - 今天市场(場)上有很多新鲜(鮮)的高丽(麗)菜，我们(們)打算买(買)一些回家做菜。
    - Jīntiān shìchǎng shàng yǒu hěn duō xīnxiān de gāolícài, wǒmen dǎsuàn mǎi yīxiē huíjiā zuò cài.
    - There are many fresh cabbages at the market today, and we plan to buy some to cook at home.
  - 妈(媽)妈(媽)常常用高丽(麗)菜，做一锅(鍋)清汤(湯)。
    - Māmā chángcháng yòng gāolícài, zuò yī guō qīngtāng.
    - Mom often uses cabbage to make a pot of clear soup.
### 高丽(麗)菜 → 高麗
- **definition:** Korea; Goryeo dynasty
- **pinyin:** gāolí
- **components:**
  - 高
    - gāo
    - high; tall
  - 麗
    - lí
    - beautiful; magnificent
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 高 (gāo, "high") + 麗 (lí, "beautiful")
  - **interpretation:** 高麗 (gāolí) refers to the Goryeo dynasty, a historical Korean kingdom lasting from 918 to 1392. The name is derived from 고구려(Goguryeo), one of the Three Kingdoms of Korea, and was adopted as a mark of pride and distinctiveness. 高 (gāo, "high") and 麗 (lí, "beautiful") highlight the elevated and beautiful nature attributed to the kingdom.
- **examples:**
  - 高麗拥(擁)有丰(豐)富的文化遗(遺)产(產)。
    - Gāolí yǒngyǒu fēngfù de wénhuà yíchǎn.
    - Goryeo had a rich cultural heritage.
  - 学(學)者研究高麗历(歷)史，揭示其独(獨)特魅力。
    - Xuézhě yánjiū Gāolí lìshǐ, jiēshì qí dútè mèilì.
    - Scholars study Goryeo history, revealing its unique charm.
  - 高麗时(時)期曾是文化交流的中心。
    - Gāolí shíqī céng shì wénhuà jiāoliú de zhōngxīn.
    - During the Goryeo period, it was a center of cultural exchange.
### 高丽(麗)菜 → 高麗 → 高
- **definition:** high; tall
- **pinyin:** gāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tall building
  - **interpretation:** The character 高(gāo, "high; tall") visually represents a tall structure or building, symbolizing height or elevation. Unlike 京(jīng, "capital"), it is straightforwardly a representation of height.
- **examples:**

    - That tree grows tall, much taller than the other trees.
  - 飞(飛)机(機)在高空飞(飛)行。
    - Fēijī zài gāokōng fēixíng.
    - The airplane flies in high altitude.

### 高丽(麗)菜 → 菜
- **definition:** vegetable; dish
- **pinyin:** cài
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass; plant
  - 采
    - cǎi
    - pluck
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (meaning 'grass; plant') + phonetic 采 (sound) ->
    - meaning
  - **interpretation:** The character 菜 (cài, 'vegetable; dish; cuisine; greens') combines the semantic 艹, representing plant life or vegetation, with 采 (cǎi), which provides the phonetic element. The semantic 艹 indicates that the meanings are related to plants or vegetation, while the phonetic 采 contributes to the character's pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 每道菜都有独(獨)特的风(風)味。
    - Měi dào cài dōu yǒu dútè de fēngwèi.
    - Every dish has a unique flavor.
  - 我喜欢(歡)吃绿(綠)色的蔬菜，因为(為)它们(們)对(對)健康有益。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī lǜsè de shūcài, yīnwèi tāmen duì jiànkāng yǒuyì.
    - I like eating green vegetables because they are good for health.
  - 中餐有很多不同的菜色，从(從)辣到甜都有。
    - Zhōngcān yǒu hěn duō bùtóng de càisè, cóng là dào tián dōu yǒu.

### 高丽(麗)菜 → 菜 → 采(採)
- **traditional:** 採
- **definition:** pluck
- **pinyin:** cǎi
- **components:**
  - 扌(手)
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 扌(hand) + 采(to pick) =
    - action of picking with hand ->
    - pluck
  - **interpretation:** The character 採 represents the action of picking or plucking using one's hand. The semantic component 扌(shǒu, "hand") indicates the action being performed, while 采(cǎi, "to pick") serves the phonetic role. Over time, 采 was retained in simplified usage, whereas 採 is used traditionally.
  - **simplification:** 采 is a simplification of 採 by using the original form that omits the 手 hand radical for brevity while retaining the phonetic element.
- **examples:**
  - 她喜欢(歡)在花园(園)里(裡)采(採)花。
    - Tā xǐhuān zài huāyuán lǐ cǎi huā.
    - She likes to pluck flowers in the garden.

    - Wǒmen qù shān shàng cǎi cǎoyào.
    - We go to the mountains to pluck herbs.

### 高丽(麗)菜 → 菜 → 采(採) → 扌
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stylized hand. The top stroke is the bent middle finger, and the horizontal strokes each represent two fingers.
  - **interpretation:** 扌 is a simplified form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), retaining the key elements that visually represent a human hand. It is commonly used as a component in other characters related to hand actions.
  - **simplification:** 扌 is derived as a cursive or shorthand form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), making it easier to incorporate into complex characters as a radical or component.
- **examples:**
  - 他伸出扌(手)接住了飞(飛)来(來)的球。
    - Tā shēnchū shǒu jiēzhù le fēilái de qiú.
    - He stretched out his hand to catch the flying ball.
  - 她用扌(手)制(製)作美丽(麗)的陶艺(藝)，展现(現)她的匠心。
    - Tā yòng shǒu zhìzuò měilì de táoyì, zhǎnxiàn tā de jiàngxīn.
    - She used her hand to create beautiful pottery, showcasing her craftsmanship.
  - 艺(藝)术(術)家细(細)心雕刻扌(手)的每一细(細)节(節)。
    - Yìshùjiā xìxīn diāokè shǒu de měiyī xìjié.
    - The artist meticulously carved every detail of the hand.
