### diànyǐng míngxīng
---
- **traditional:** 電影明星
- **definition:** movie star
- **pinyin:** diànyǐng míngxīng
- **components:**
  - 电(電)影( )
    - diànyǐng
    - film; movie
  - 明星
    - míngxīng
    - star; celebrity
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 電 (diàn, "electric") + 影 (yǐng, "shadow") forms 電影 (diànyǐng, "film; movie") as cinematic projections are essentially 'electric shadows'. 明 (míng, "bright; clear") + 星 (xīng, "star") form 明星 (míngxīng, "star; celebrity") indicating a 'bright star', symbolizing fame.
  - **interpretation:** 電影 (diànyǐng, "film; movie") suggests a movie as a 'play of shadows' that is powered by electricity, combining visual and technological elements for a film. 明星 (míngxīng, "star; celebrity") draws on the image of a 'bright star' in the sky, a guiding point—which in the celebrity context refers to someone prominent and widely admired. Together, 電影明星 (diànyǐng míngxīng, "movie star") combines these senses to refer to a highly recognizable and admired figure within the cinematic world.
