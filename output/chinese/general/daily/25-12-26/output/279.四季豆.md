## green bean; string bean
### sìjìdòu
---
- **definition:** green bean; string bean
- **pinyin:** sìjìdòu
- **components:**
  - 四
    - sì
    - four
  - 季
    - jì
    - season
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 四 (sì, "four"), 季 (jì, "season"), and 豆 (dòu, "bean"). The term literally refers to the 'bean of four seasons', implying a vegetable that is available throughout the year.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 四 (sì, "four") and 季 (jì, "season") highlights the bean's ability to be cultivated or consumed in all four seasons, pointing to its perennial availability and cultivation. 豆 (dòu, "bean") specifies the type of produce, highlighting its identity as a bean, thus referring specifically to 'green bean' or 'string bean'. This implies a versatility and reliability in the bean's availability in various seasons.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)吃四季豆。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan chī sìjìdòu.
    - I like to eat green beans.
  - 她在市场(場)上买(買)了很多新鲜(鮮)的四季豆。
    - Tā zài shìchǎng shàng mǎile hěn duō xīnxiān de sìjìdòu.
    - She bought a lot of fresh green beans at the market.
  - 四季豆可以用来(來)做沙拉，也可以用来(來)炒菜。
    - Sìjìdòu kěyǐ yòng lái zuò shālā, yě kěyǐ yòng lái chǎocài.
    - Green beans can be used to make salads or for stir-fry.
### 四季豆 → 四
- **definition:** four
- **pinyin:** sì
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 八
    - bā
    - divide
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') + 八 (bā, 'divide') ->
    - dispersal of breath ->
    - borrowed for 'four'
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted by 亖, representing four lines, the character later evolved to include 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') enclosing 八 (bā, 'divide') to symbolize a dispersal of breath. This representation was adapted over time for the numerical value 'four', borrowing phonetically from the original pronunciation linked to breath dispersion. The character was standardized during the Qin dynasty to its current form to avoid confusion with 'two' and 'three' in vertical script.
- **examples:**
  - 我有四本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu sì běn shū.
    - I have four books.
  - 四个(個)人在公园(園)里(裡)散步。
    - Sì gè rén zài gōngyuán lǐ sànbù.
    - Four people are walking in the park.
  - 比赛(賽)将(將)在四点(點)开(開)始。
    - Bǐsài jiāng zài sì diǎn kāishǐ.
    - The match will start at four o'clock.

### 四季豆 → 四 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.

    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.

### 四季豆 → 季
- **definition:** season
- **pinyin:** jì
- **components:**
  - 禾
    - hé
    - grain
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 禾 (hé, "grain") + 子 (zǐ, "child") =
    - grain producing offspring ->
    - season
  - **interpretation:** The character 季 represents the growth cycle of crops yielding new life, symbolized by grain (禾) producing offspring (子), thereby conveying the idea of a 'season' as a time for specific agricultural activities.

### 四季豆 → 豆
- **definition:** bean
- **pinyin:** dòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a container ->
    - borrowed phonetically for the plant name
  - **interpretation:** The character 豆(dòu, 'drum') originally depicted a type of container. It was later borrowed for its phonetic value to represent the plant name 'bean', distinguishing it from a container. This transition indicates how the physical shape of the container, resembling a drum or storage pot, facilitated a phonetic borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)在课(課)堂上学(學)习(習)如何制(製)作豆。
    - Wǒmen zài kètáng shàng xuéxí rúhé zhìzuò dòu.
    - We are learning how to make drums in class.
  - 村庄(莊)里(裡)有一个(個)古老的豆，它的声(聲)音很有魅力。
    - Cūnzhuāng lǐ yǒu yí gè gǔlǎo de dòu, tā de shēngyīn hěn yǒu mèilì.
    - There is an ancient drum in the village, and its sound is captivating.
  - 音乐(樂)会(會)以豆声(聲)开(開)场(場)，让(讓)观(觀)众(眾)立刻沉(沈)浸在气(氣)氛中。
