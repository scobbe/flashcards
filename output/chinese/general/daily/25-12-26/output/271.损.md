## decrease; damage
### sǔn
---
- **traditional:** 損
- **definition:** decrease; damage
- **pinyin:** sǔn
- **components:**
  - 扌
    - shǒu
    - hand
  - 員
    - yuán
    - a member; employee
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 扌(扌, "hand") + phonetic 員(員, "a member; employee") ->
    - damage or decrease related to actions involving hands
  - **interpretation:** The character 損 (sǔn, "decrease; damage") combines 扌(扌, "hand"), representing action or manipulation, with 員(員, "a member; employee") to suggest the act of diminishing or causing harm, typically through manual actions or handling.
  - **simplification:** Simplified from 損 by replacing 員 (yuán, "a member") with its simplified form 员(員).
- **examples:**
  - 天气(氣)寒冷会(會)损(損)坏(壞)老人的健康。
    - Tiānqì hánlěng huì sǔnhuài lǎorén de jiànkāng.
    - Cold weather can damage the health of the elderly.
  - 过(過)度的开(開)发(發)会(會)严(嚴)重损(損)害自然环(環)境。
    - Guòdù de kāifā huì yánzhòng sǔnhài zìrán huánjìng.
    - Excessive development can seriously damage the natural environment.
  - 长(長)时(時)间(間)使用电(電)子设(設)备(備)可能会(會)损(損)伤(傷)视(視)力。
    - Cháng shíjiān shǐyòng diànzǐ shèbèi kěnéng huì sǔnshāng shìlì.
    - Prolonged use of electronic devices may damage vision.
### 损(損) → 扌
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stylized hand. The top stroke is the bent middle finger, and the horizontal strokes each represent two fingers.
  - **interpretation:** 扌 is a simplified form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), retaining the key elements that visually represent a human hand. It is commonly used as a component in other characters related to hand actions.
  - **simplification:** 扌 is derived as a cursive or shorthand form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), making it easier to incorporate into complex characters as a radical or component.
- **examples:**
  - 他伸出扌(手)接住了飞(飛)来(來)的球。
    - Tā shēnchū shǒu jiēzhù le fēilái de qiú.
    - He stretched out his hand to catch the flying ball.
  - 她用扌(手)制(製)作美丽(麗)的陶艺(藝)，展现(現)她的匠心。
    - Tā yòng shǒu zhìzuò měilì de táoyì, zhǎnxiàn tā de jiàngxīn.
    - She used her hand to create beautiful pottery, showcasing her craftsmanship.
  - 艺(藝)术(術)家细(細)心雕刻扌(手)的每一细(細)节(節)。
    - Yìshùjiā xìxīn diāokè shǒu de měiyī xìjié.
    - The artist meticulously carved every detail of the hand.
### 损(損) → 員
- **definition:** a member; employee
- **pinyin:** yuán
- **components:**
  - 鼎
    - dǐng
    - ding
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 〇 (round) indicating the top edge of a 鼎 (dǐng, "ding") ->
    - round
  - **interpretation:** The character originally depicted the top view of a 鼎 (dǐng, "ding"), which is a round ancient cooking vessel, capturing its round shape to convey the concept of 'member' or 'person', as in a complete or rounded group.
  - **simplification:** To simplify 員, 貝 (bèi, "cowry") was reduced to 贝(貝), maintaining the concept while reducing stroke count.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一名优(優)秀的员(員)工。
    - Tā shì yī míng yōuxiù de yuángōng.
    - He is an outstanding employee.
  - 我们(們)团(團)队(隊)需要更多成员(員)。
    - Wǒmen tuánduì xūyào gèng duō chéngyuán.
    - Our team needs more members.
  - 退休后(後)，他不再是公司的职(職)员(員)。
    - Tuìxiū hòu, tā bùzài shì gōngsī de zhíyuán.
    - After retiring, he is no longer an employee of the company.

### 损(損) → 員 → 鼎
- **definition:** ding
- **pinyin:** dǐng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a three-legged bronze vessel ->
    - later used for divination
  - **interpretation:** The character 鼎(dǐng, 'tripod') is a pictogram that depicts a tripod vessel with three legs, symbolizing its use in cooking and ritual sacrifices. Over time, it was also borrowed phonetically to represent the concept of divination.
- **examples:**
  - 商朝的青铜(銅)鼎象征(徵)着(著)权(權)力。
    - Shāng cháo de qīngtóng dǐng xiàngzhēng zhe quánlì.
    - The bronze tripod from the Shang Dynasty symbolizes power.
  - 在古代，鼎是用来(來)烹饪(飪)食物的器具。
    - Zài gǔdài, dǐng shì yòng lái pēngrèn shíwù de qìjù.
    - In ancient times, the tripod was a utensil used for cooking food.
  - 这(這)个(個)博物馆(館)里(裏)展示了一尊精美的铜(銅)鼎。
    - Zhège bówùguǎn lǐ zhǎnshì le yī zūn jīngměi de tóngdǐng.
    - This museum exhibits an exquisite bronze tripod.
