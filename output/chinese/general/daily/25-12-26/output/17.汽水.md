## sparkling water
### qìshuǐ
---
- **definition:** sparkling water
- **pinyin:** qìshuǐ
- **components:**
  - 汽
    - qì
    - steam; vapor
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 汽 (qì, "steam; vapor") + 水 (shuǐ, "water") =
    - water with vapor ->
    - sparkling water
  - **interpretation:** The word 汽水 (qìshuǐ) combines 汽 (qì, "steam; vapor") and 水 (shuǐ, "water"), suggesting water that contains bubbles of gas or vapor, typical of sparkling water. This aptly describes fizzy water beverages.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )喜( )欢(歡)喝( )汽( )水( )。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān hē qìshuǐ.
    - I like to drink sparkling water.
  - 夏( )天( )很( )热(熱)，一瓶汽水可以消暑。
    - Xiàtiān hěn rè, yī píng qìshuǐ kěyǐ xiāoshǔ.
    - It is very hot in summer, and a bottle of sparkling water can cool you down.

### 汽水 → 汽 → 氣
- **definition:** air
- **pinyin:** qì
- **components:**
  - 气
    - qì
    - vapor
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 气 (originally a pictogram for 'vapor') + semantic 米 ('rice') ->
    - giving rice as a gift
  - **interpretation:** Initially, 氣(qì, 'air') depicted giving rice as a gift, incorporating the concept of something precious or sustaining. Over time, the character borrowed meanings associated with 气(qì, 'vapor'), taking on senses related to 'air', 'atmosphere', and 'energy'. The connection lies in the intangible, pervasive qualities common to both meanings.
  - **simplification:** 气 (气) (qì, 'air') was simplified by retaining the upper portion, which depicts vapor or steam, dropping the 米 (米) (mǐ, 'rice') component.
