## lotus root starch
### ǒufěn
---
- **definition:** lotus root starch
- **pinyin:** ǒufěn
- **components:**
  - 藕
    - ǒu
    - lotus root; lotus rhizome; lotus tuber; edible rhizome
  - 粉
    - fěn
    - powder; flour; starch; cosmetic powder
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 藕 (lotus root) + 粉 (powder) = lotus root starch/powder.
  - **interpretation:** Endocentric noun: 粉 marks the processed form of 藕. Refers to starch or powder extracted from lotus root, commonly used to make beverages, desserts, or as a thickener.
- **examples:**
  - 她用藕粉冲成一碗糊(她用藕粉沖成一碗糊)。
    - Tā yòng ǒufěn chōng chéng yì wǎn hú.
    - She brews lotus root starch into a bowl of porridge.
  - 夏天喝冰藕粉很解暑(夏天喝冰藕粉很解暑)。
    - Xiàtiān hē bīng ǒufěn hěn jiěshǔ.
    - Drinking chilled lotus root starch in summer is very refreshing.
  - 这家店卖的藕粉很细，很容易冲开(這家店賣的藕粉很細，很容易沖開)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn mài de ǒufěn hěn xì, hěn róngyì chōng kāi.
    - The lotus root starch sold at this shop is very fine and dissolves easily.
### 藕粉 → 藕
- **definition:** lotus root; lotus rhizome; lotus tuber; edible rhizome
- **pinyin:** ǒu
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass; herbs; plants
  - 耦
    - ǒu
    - pair; couple; yoked; to plow in pairs
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 艸 (“grass”) + phonetic 耦 (OC *ŋoːʔ)
  - **interpretation:** 艸 flags a plant-related meaning, fitting an edible plant rhizome. 耦 supplies the ǒu sound (sharing OC *ŋoːʔ), and its sense of coupling loosely echoes the knotted, segment-like joints of lotus rhizomes.
- **examples:**
  - 今天的凉拌藕片很爽脆(今天的涼拌藕片很爽脆)。
    - Jīntiān de liángbàn ǒupiàn hěn shuǎngcuì.
    - Today's chilled lotus-root slices are very crisp.
  - 我妈妈用藕炖排骨(我媽媽用藕燉排骨)。
    - Wǒ māma yòng ǒu dùn páigǔ.
    - My mom stews pork ribs with lotus root.
  - 市场上有新鲜的莲藕(市場上有新鮮的蓮藕)。
    - Shìchǎng shàng yǒu xīnxiān de lián'ǒu.
    - There is fresh lotus root at the market.
### 藕粉 → 藕 → 艸
- **definition:** grass; herbs; plants
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè
    - sprout; shoot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: grass blades; duplication of 屮 (“grass”).
  - **interpretation:** Two sprouting shoots are drawn to depict a clump of grass. The doubled sprout emphasizes vegetation and later standardized as the grass radical used atop many plant-related characters.
  - **simplification:** As a component it was simplified to 艹 to reduce strokes; the independent word 草 remained unchanged, and 艸 is rarely used as a standalone character in modern writing.
- **examples:**
  - 地上长满了草(地上長滿了草)。
    - Dìshang zhǎng mǎn le cǎo.
    - The ground is covered with grass.
  - 小羊在草地里吃草(小羊在草地裡吃草)。
    - Xiǎo yáng zài cǎodì lǐ chī cǎo.
    - The lambs eat grass on the meadow.
  - 古书中常用艸字表示草(古書中常用艸字表示草)。
    - Gǔshū zhōng cháng yòng cǎo zì biǎoshì cǎo.
    - In old books, the character 艸 is used to denote “grass.”
### 藕粉 → 藕 → 艸 → 屮
- **definition:** sprout; shoot
- **pinyin:** chè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): a sprout emerging from the ground.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a shoot pushing up through the soil: the horizontal stroke suggests the ground, while the central vertical stroke and side strokes evoke the stem and small leaves. Early oracle and bronze forms closely resemble a budding plant.
- **examples:**
  - 在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽(在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén zhōng, chè biǎoshì zhíwù de nènyá.
    - In oracle script, 屮 represents a plant sprout.
  - 春天雨后，田里冒出许多嫩芽，像“屮”的形状(春天雨後，田裡冒出許多嫩芽，像「屮」的形狀)。
    - Chūntiān yǔ hòu, tián lǐ mào chū xǔduō nènyá, xiàng “chè” de xíngzhuàng.
    - After spring rains, many sprouts emerge in the fields, resembling the shape of “屮”.
  - 屮在古文字中象征嫩芽与新生(屮在古文字中象徵嫩芽與新生)。
    - Chè zài gǔ wénzì zhōng xiàngzhēng nènyá yǔ xīnshēng.
    - In ancient scripts, 屮 symbolizes sprouts and new life.
### 藕粉 → 藕 → 耦
- **definition:** pair; couple; yoked; to plow in pairs
- **pinyin:** ǒu
- **components:**
  - 耒
    - lěi
    - tilling tool; plow
  - 禺
    - yú
    - region; corner
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 耒 (“tilling tool”) + phonetic: 禺 (OC *ŋo, *ŋos), paired working in the fields.
  - **interpretation:** 耒 signals an agricultural context, while 禺 supplies the sound that later developed to ǒu. Together they convey the idea of yoked or paired work in the fields, extending to senses like coupling in general.
- **examples:**
  - 科学家研究粒子之间的耦合(科學家研究粒子之間的耦合)。
    - Kēxuéjiā yánjiū lìzǐ zhījiān de ǒuhé.
    - Scientists study the coupling between particles.
  - 春耕时，农夫用两头牛耦耕田地(春耕時，農夫用兩頭牛耦耕田地)。
    - Chūngēng shí, nóngfū yòng liǎng tóu niú ǒugēng tiándì.
    - During spring plowing, the farmer uses two oxen yoked together to plow the fields.
  - 这些元件耦合得很紧密(這些元件耦合得很緊密)。
    - Zhèxiē yuánjiàn ǒuhé de hěn jǐnmì.
    - These components are tightly coupled.
### 藕粉 → 藕 → 耦 → 耒
- **definition:** tilling tool; plow
- **pinyin:** lěi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a wooden bifurcated plow/harrow with three cross lines (彡, 丯) marking furrows, later simplified to two.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a forked wooden tilling implement seen from above, with short strokes indicating the furrows it cuts. Over time scribes reduced the number of furrow marks, but the form still clearly evokes an agricultural tool, sometimes glossed as a harrow.
- **examples:**
  - 他手持耒在田间翻土(他手持耒在田間翻土)。
    - Tā shǒu chí lěi zài tiánjiān fān tǔ.
    - He held a plow to turn the soil in the fields.
  - 古人以耒耜耕田(古人以耒耜耕田)。
    - Gǔrén yǐ lěisì gēng tián.
    - The ancients tilled fields with a plow and hoe.
  - 文物展出了青铜耒(文物展出了青銅耒)。
    - Wénwù zhǎnchū le qīngtóng lěi.
    - The exhibit displayed a bronze plow.
### 藕粉 → 藕 → 耦 → 禺
- **definition:** region; corner
- **pinyin:** yú
- **components:**
  - 甶
    - fú
    - head of a ghost
  - 禸
    - róu
    - to trample; footprint
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** unclear; ideogrammic compound (Shuowen) or pictogram (Zhang et al.)
  - **description:** Shuowen: semantic 甶 (“head of a ghost”) + 禸 (“to trample”) → a kind of monkey; later view: differentiated from 禹, pictogram of an insect with a decorative mark (cf. 萬).
  - **interpretation:** Early analyses differ: one reading treats it as a compound of ‘ghost-head’ and ‘trampling’, evoking an animal like a monkey. Another line sees a pictograph of a marked creature, with bronze forms resembling masks or the shape of 禹. Its later senses ‘region; corner’ likely arose via semantic shift or phonetic borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 我们去了番禺区参观博物馆(我們去了番禺區參觀博物館)。
    - Wǒmen qùle Fānyú Qū cānguān bówùguǎn.
    - We went to Panyu District to visit the museum.
  - 文献里“禺”通“隅”，指角落或边隅(文獻裡「禺」通「隅」，指角落或邊隅)。
    - Wénxiàn lǐ “yú” tōng “yú”, zhǐ jiǎoluò huò biānyú.
    - In texts, ‘禺’ is interchangeable with ‘隅’, meaning a corner or edge.
  - 传说中的海神禺强掌管海域与风波(傳說中的海神禺強掌管海域與風波)。
    - Chuánshuō zhōng de Hǎishén Yúqiáng zhǎngguǎn hǎiyù yǔ fēngbō.
    - In legend, the sea god Yuqiang presides over the seas and their waves.
### 藕粉 → 藕 → 耦 → 禺 → 甶
- **definition:** head of a ghost
- **pinyin:** fú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: face/head of a ghost/spirit/demon; possibly a mask (per Shuowen).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a stylized spirit’s head, matching early graphic traditions where facial features were simplified into a compact emblem. Some scholars see it as a ritual mask, which would explain the squared, mask-like outline in archaic forms.
- **examples:**
  - 古文字中的“甶”表示鬼的头部(古文字中的「甶」表示鬼的頭部)。
    - Gǔ wénzì zhōng de “fú” biǎoshì guǐ de tóubù.
    - In ancient writing, 甶 denotes the head of a ghost.
  - 据《说文》，甶为象形字，也可能代表面具(據《說文》，甶為象形字，也可能代表面具)。
    - Jù “Shuōwén”, fú wéi xiàngxíngzì, yě kěnéng dàibiǎo miànjù.
    - According to Shuowen, 甶 is a pictograph and may also represent a mask.
  - 在现代汉语中，甶很少单独使用，多见于古文字研究(在現代漢語中，甶很少單獨使用，多見於古文字研究)。
    - Zài xiàndài Hànyǔ zhōng, fú hěn shǎo dāndú shǐyòng, duō jiàn yú gǔ wénzì yánjiū.
    - In modern Chinese, 甶 is rarely used alone and is mostly seen in the study of ancient scripts.
### 藕粉 → 藕 → 耦 → 禺 → 禸
- **definition:** to trample; footprint
- **pinyin:** róu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: animal paw print.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the impression of an animal’s paw—pad and toes—hence the senses “to trample” and “footprint.” As a component it conveys the idea of animal tracks or trampling, despite superficial resemblance to parts of other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 雪地上留有兽禸(雪地上留有獸禸)。
    - Xuědì shàng liú yǒu shòu róu.
    - Animal footprints remain on the snow.
  - 猎人循禸而行(獵人循禸而行)。
    - Lièrén xún róu ér xíng.
    - The hunter follows the footprints.
  - 切莫禸幼苗(切莫禸幼苗)。
    - Qiè mò róu yòumiáo.
    - Do not trample the seedlings.
### 藕粉 → 粉
- **definition:** powder; flour; starch; cosmetic powder
- **pinyin:** fěn
- **components:**
  - 米
    - mǐ
    - rice
  - 分
    - fēn
    - divide; part; minute
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 米 ‘rice’ + phonetic: 分; ‘face powder made of rice’ (OC *pɯnʔ).
  - **interpretation:** 米 signals a substance derived from rice, while 分 supplies the sound fěn and evokes the idea of dividing into fine particles. The original sense points to rice-based face powder, which broadened to general powders, flours, and starches.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了一袋面粉(我買了一袋麵粉)。
    - Wǒ mǎile yī dài miànfěn.
    - I bought a bag of flour.
  - 她擦了点儿粉，让脸色更好(她擦了點兒粉，讓臉色更好)。
    - Tā cāle diǎnr fěn, ràng liǎnsè gèng hǎo.
    - She applied some powder to improve her complexion.
  - 老师在黑板上用粉笔写字(老師在黑板上用粉筆寫字)。
    - Lǎoshī zài hēibǎn shàng yòng fěnbǐ xiězì.
    - The teacher writes on the blackboard with chalk.
### 藕粉 → 粉 → 米
- **definition:** rice
- **pinyin:** mǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: dehusked rice kernels; horizontal stroke as a bamboo sieve; cf. 粟 (with husk); unrelated to 木/禾/来.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts dehusked rice grains being sifted on a bamboo sieve, contrasting with 粟 which shows husked kernels. Despite visual resemblance, its form is not derived from 木, 禾, or 来.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了两斤米(我買了兩斤米)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le liǎng jīn mǐ.
    - I bought two jin of rice.
  - 这家店的东北米很香(這家店的東北米很香)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn de Dōngběi mǐ hěn xiāng.
    - The Northeastern rice at this shop is very fragrant.
  - 请把米洗一洗(請把米洗一洗)，再煮饭(再煮飯)。
    - Qǐng bǎ mǐ xǐ yì xǐ, zài zhǔ fàn.
    - Please rinse the rice, then cook it.
### 藕粉 → 粉 → 分
- **definition:** divide; part; minute
- **pinyin:** fēn
- **components:**
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (会意)
  - **description:** semantic: 八 ‘to separate’ + semantic: 刀 ‘knife’ — separate with a knife
  - **interpretation:** Combines 八, depicting separation or splitting, with 刀, a knife, to express cutting something apart. This core idea underlies senses like dividing, apportioning, and by extension small fractional units such as minutes and cents.
- **examples:**
  - 我们把蛋糕分开了(我們把蛋糕分開了)。
    - Wǒmen bǎ dàngāo fēnkāi le.
    - We divided the cake.
  - 现在是三点十分(現在是三點十分)。
    - Xiànzài shì sān diǎn shí fēn.
    - It is 3:10.
  - 他只给了我一分钱(他只給了我一分錢)。
    - Tā zhǐ gěi le wǒ yì fēnqián.
    - He only gave me one cent.
### 藕粉 → 粉 → 分 → 八
- **definition:** eight
- **pinyin:** bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: two bent lines indicating “to divide”; later borrowed to mean “eight” via homophony (original sense now in 分, 別).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two strokes diverging to suggest splitting or separation. Later became a rebus for the number eight because it shared the same sound, while the original sense lived on in 分 and 別. Not the “two hands” component found in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我有八本书(我有八本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu bā běn shū.
    - I have eight books.
  - 她八岁了(她八歲了)。
    - Tā bā suì le.
    - She is eight years old.
  - 我买了八个苹果(我買了八個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le bā gè píngguǒ.
    - I bought eight apples.
### 藕粉 → 粉 → 分 → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — a knife; original form 𠚣, seen in a variant of 利 (𥝢) and atop 黎.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a knife with a distinct blade and handle. The graph was streamlined into the current two-stroke form and also serves as a component in characters like 利 and 黎.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿着一把刀(他拿著一把刀)。
    - Tā názhe yì bǎ dāo.
    - He is holding a knife.
  - 厨师用刀切蔬菜(廚師用刀切蔬菜)。
    - Chúshī yòng dāo qiē shūcài.
    - The chef uses a knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这把刀很锋利(這把刀很鋒利)。
    - Zhè bǎ dāo hěn fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
