## 牵牛花(牽牛花)
---
- **pinyin:** qiānniúhuā
- **definition:** morning glory
- **characters:**
  - 牵(牽)
    - qiān
    - to pull, draw; to lead along; to involve, implicate, affect; to drag, tow
  - 牛
    - niú
    - cow, ox, cattle; bull; awesome, badass; stubborn, headstrong
  - 花
    - huā
    - flower, blossom; to spend, expend; gaudy, showy, flowery; mottled, speckled
- **etymology:** 牵(牽) (qiān, "to pull/lead") with 牛 (niú, "ox") evokes the vine’s twining that seems to tug like leading an ox, and 花 (huā, "flower") marks it as a flower—hence a twining, "ox-leading" flower (morning glory).
- **examples:**
  - 篱笆上爬满了牵牛花(籬笆上爬滿了牽牛花)，一大早就开得缤纷(一大早就開得繽紛)。
    - Líbā shàng pá mǎn le qiānniúhuā, yí dàzǎo jiù kāi de bīnfēn.
    - Morning glories climbed all over the fence, bursting into color at daybreak.
