## spray
### pēn
---
- **definition:** spray
- **pinyin:** pēn
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 贲(賁)
    - bēn/bì
    - rush; ornate
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 口 (mouth) + phonetic 賁
  - **interpretation:** Mouth signals an action of expelling from the mouth or an orifice. 賁 provides the sound, aligning with pēn and guiding the sense of spouting or spraying outward.
  - **simplification:** 賁 was simplified to 贲, reducing strokes while preserving the 口 + phonetic structure and pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 他往花园里喷水(他往花園裡噴水)。
    - Tā wǎng huāyuán lǐ pēn shuǐ.
    - He sprays water into the garden.
  - 我买了一个喷雾器(我買了一個噴霧器)。
    - Wǒ mǎile yí gè pēnwùqì.
    - I bought a sprayer.
  - 火山突然喷发(火山突然噴發)。
    - Huǒshān tūrán pēnfā.
    - The volcano suddenly spewed.
### 喷(噴) → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 喷(噴) → 贲(賁)
- **definition:** rush; ornate
- **pinyin:** bēn/bì
- **components:**
  - 卉
    - huì
    - plants; grasses
  - 贝(貝)
    - bèi
    - cowry; money
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 貝 “cowry” + phonetic: 卉 for the reading bì; later borrowed to write 奔, giving the reading bēn.
  - **interpretation:** For the bì reading, 貝 contributes the semantic field of valuables/ornament while 卉 provides the sound. In antiquity the graph was also used to write the verb 奔 “to rush,” which yielded the bēn reading; today the ‘rush’ sense is mainly written 奔, while 贲/賁 survives in specialized and classical contexts.
  - **simplification:** 貝 was simplified to 贝 as part of the regular script reforms; the phonetic 卉 remained unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 他姓贲(他姓賁)。
    - Tā xìng Bēn.
    - His surname is Ben (Bēn).
  - 医生检查了他的贲门(醫生檢查了他的賁門)。
    - Yīshēng jiǎnchále tā de bìmén.
    - The doctor examined his cardia.
  - 我在读《易经》的贲卦(我在讀《易經》的賁卦)。
    - Wǒ zài dú Yìjīng de bì guà.
    - I’m reading the Bi hexagram in the Book of Changes.
### 喷(噴) → 贲(賁) → 卉
- **definition:** plants; grasses
- **pinyin:** huì
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè
    - sprout; shoot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogram: three 屮 ‘sprout’, indicating grasses/plants.
  - **interpretation:** Three young shoots combined evokes a profusion of grasses and herbs. Repetition emphasizes plurality, so the graph came to denote plants in general.
- **examples:**
  - 春天里(春天裡)，百卉齐放(百卉齊放)。
    - Chūntiān lǐ, bǎi huì qí fàng.
    - In spring, hundreds of flowers and plants bloom.
  - 这家花卉市场很大(這家花卉市場很大)。
    - Zhè jiā huāhuì shìchǎng hěn dà.
    - This flower-and-plant market is very large.
  - 他研究草卉与树木(他研究草卉與樹木)。
    - Tā yánjiū cǎohuì yǔ shùmù.
    - He studies herbs and trees.
### 喷(噴) → 贲(賁) → 卉 → 屮
- **definition:** sprout; shoot
- **pinyin:** chè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): a sprout emerging from the ground.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a shoot pushing up through the soil: the horizontal stroke suggests the ground, while the central vertical stroke and side strokes evoke the stem and small leaves. Early oracle and bronze forms closely resemble a budding plant.
- **examples:**
  - 在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽(在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén zhōng, chè biǎoshì zhíwù de nènyá.
    - In oracle script, 屮 represents a plant sprout.
  - 春天雨后，田里冒出许多嫩芽，像“屮”的形状(春天雨後，田裡冒出許多嫩芽，像「屮」的形狀)。
    - Chūntiān yǔ hòu, tián lǐ mào chū xǔduō nènyá, xiàng “chè” de xíngzhuàng.
    - After spring rains, many sprouts emerge in the fields, resembling the shape of “屮”.
  - 屮在古文字中象征嫩芽与新生(屮在古文字中象徵嫩芽與新生)。
    - Chè zài gǔ wénzì zhōng xiàngzhēng nènyá yǔ xīnshēng.
    - In ancient scripts, 屮 symbolizes sprouts and new life.
### 喷(噴) → 贲(賁) → 贝(貝)
- **definition:** cowry; money
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – cowrie used as currency in ancient China; simplified form of 貝 in cursive script.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a cowry shell, an object that functioned as early money, which motivates the senses “cowry; money.” In the simplified set, its strokes were streamlined by adopting a cursive-style form of 貝.
  - **simplification:** Adopted the cursive-script variant of 貝, reducing strokes and complexity.
- **examples:**
  - 古代用贝(貝)当作货币(貨幣)。
    - Gǔdài yòng bèi dāngzuò huòbì.
    - In ancient times, cowries were used as currency.
  - 海滩(海灘)上有很多贝(貝)。
    - Hǎitān shang yǒu hěn duō bèi.
    - There are many shells on the beach.
  - 这串项链(這串項鍊)是用贝(貝)做的。
    - Zhè chuàn xiàngliàn shì yòng bèi zuò de.
    - This necklace is made of shells.
