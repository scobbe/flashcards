## sex fiend; pervert
### sèmó
---
- **definition:** sex fiend; pervert
- **pinyin:** sèmó
- **components:**
  - 色
    - sè
    - color; sexual desire; sensuality; facial expression
  - 魔
    - mó
    - demon; evil spirit; fiend; magic
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 色(sexual desire) + 魔(demon/fiend) = sex fiend; pervert
  - **interpretation:** Derogatory term for a lecherous person obsessed with sex; tone ranges from joking to strongly condemnatory depending on context.
- **examples:**
  - 他老盯着女生看，简直是个色魔(他老盯著女生看，簡直是個色魔)。
    - Tā lǎo dīngzhe nǚshēng kàn, jiǎnzhí shì gè sèmó.
    - He keeps staring at girls—he’s basically a pervert.
  - 大家要小心那个色魔，尽量别单独和他见面(大家要小心那個色魔，儘量別單獨和他見面)。
    - Dàjiā yào xiǎoxīn nàge sèmó, jǐnliàng bié dāndú hé tā jiànmiàn.
    - Be careful of that pervert; try not to meet him alone.
  - 媒体曝光后，这名色魔被警方逮捕(媒體曝光後，這名色魔被警方逮捕)。
    - Méitǐ bāoguāng hòu, zhè míng sèmó bèi jǐngfāng dàibǔ.
    - After the media exposed him, this pervert was arrested by the police.
### 色魔 → 色
- **definition:** color; sexual desire; sensuality; facial expression
- **pinyin:** sè
- **components:**
  - 爪
    - zhǎo
    - claw; talon
  - 人
    - rén
    - person; human
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound (會意 / 会意)
  - **description:** 會意: 爪 “claw/hand” + 卩 “kneeling person” → complexion; Shuowen: 人 “person” + 卩 “etiquette/kneeling person” → complexion.
  - **interpretation:** A hand over a kneeling figure evokes someone’s outward look or bearing, hence ‘complexion’ and ‘appearance.’ From appearance it broadened to ‘color,’ and its tie to human looks fostered senses about facial expression and sexual allure.
- **examples:**
  - 这幅画的颜色很鲜艳(這幅畫的顏色很鮮豔)。
    - Zhè fú huà de yánsè hěn xiānyàn.
    - The colors of this painting are very vivid.
  - 他今天脸色不好，可能生病了(他今天臉色不好，可能生病了)。
    - Tā jīntiān liǎnsè bù hǎo, kěnéng shēngbìng le.
    - His complexion looks poor today; he may be sick.
  - 不要沉迷色情内容(不要沉迷色情內容)。
    - Bùyào chénmí sèqíng nèiróng.
    - Don't get addicted to pornographic content.
### 色魔 → 色 → 爪
- **definition:** claw; talon
- **pinyin:** zhǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a hand grabbing from above (earlier forms from the side); compare 手, 寸, 九.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an overhead view of fingers curved downward in a grasping motion, originally representing a human hand. The sense broadened to include animal claws, which is why it now names claws/talons and functions as the ‘claw’ radical in many compounds.
- **examples:**
  - 猫的爪子很锋利(貓的爪子很鋒利)。
    - Māo de zhuǎzi hěn fēnglì.
    - A cat’s claws are very sharp.
  - 我买了一包鸡爪当小吃(我買了一包雞爪當小吃)。
    - Wǒ mǎile yī bāo jīzhuǎ dāng xiǎochī.
    - I bought a bag of chicken feet as a snack.
  - 地上有一串小狗的爪印(地上有一串小狗的爪印)。
    - Dì shàng yǒu yī chuàn xiǎogǒu de zhuǎyìn.
    - There is a trail of puppy paw prints on the ground.
### 色魔 → 色 → 人
- **definition:** person; human
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 色魔 → 魔
- **definition:** demon; evil spirit; fiend; magic
- **pinyin:** mó
- **components:**
  - 麻
    - má
    - hemp; numb
  - 鬼
    - guǐ
    - ghost; demon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic 麻 (OC *mraːl) + semantic 鬼 (“demon”).
  - **interpretation:** Combines 麻 as a sound cue with 鬼 signaling a spirit or demon, giving a pronunciation close to mó and a supernatural meaning. 麻 supplies the phonetic profile from Old Chinese, while 鬼 anchors the sense of an otherworldly being.
- **examples:**
  - 他像被恶魔附身了一样(他像被惡魔附身了一樣)。
    - Tā xiàng bèi èmó fùshēn le yíyàng.
    - He seems as if he’s been possessed by a demon.
  - 这部电影讲述人与魔的斗争(這部電影講述人與魔的鬥爭)。
    - Zhè bù diànyǐng jiǎngshù rén yǔ mó de dòuzhēng.
    - This film tells the story of the struggle between humans and demons.
  - 他沉迷于网络游戏，仿佛中了魔(他沉迷於網絡遊戲，彷彿中了魔)。
    - Tā chénmí yú wǎngluò yóuxì, fǎngfú zhòng le mó.
    - He is addicted to online games, as if under a spell.
