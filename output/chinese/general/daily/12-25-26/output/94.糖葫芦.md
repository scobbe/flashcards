## candied hawthorn
### tánghúlu
---
- **definition:** candied hawthorn
- **pinyin:** tánghúlu
- **components:**
  - 糖
    - táng
    - sugar; candy; sweets; to sweeten
  - 葫
    - hú
    - gourd; calabash; cucurbit; used in plant names
  - 芦(蘆)
    - lú
    - reed; bulrush; (in 葫芦) gourd; reed plant
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 糖(sugar) + 葫芦(gourd) = “sugary gourd,” a sugar-coated fruit skewer resembling a small gourd.
  - **interpretation:** Highlights the sugar coating and the gourd-like look of fruits strung on a stick; in practice it refers to hawthorn berries coated in hard syrup.
  - **simplification:** 蘆 → 芦 by adopting the simplified grass radical 艹 and simplifying the phonetic 盧 to 卢; other characters unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 冬天街头常能看到糖葫芦(冬天街頭常能看到糖葫蘆)。
    - Dōngtiān jiētóu cháng néng kàndào tánghúlu.
    - In winter you can often see candied hawthorn on the streets.
  - 红艳的糖衣包着山楂，酸甜可口(紅豔的糖衣包著山楂，酸甜可口)。
    - Hóngyàn de tángyī bāo zhe shānzhā, suāntián kěkǒu.
    - A bright red sugar shell coats the hawthorn, tasting sweet and tart.
  - 小贩把糖葫芦串在竹签上(小販把糖葫蘆串在竹籤上)。
    - Xiǎofàn bǎ tánghúlu chuàn zài zhúqiān shàng.
    - Vendors thread the candied hawthorn onto bamboo skewers.
