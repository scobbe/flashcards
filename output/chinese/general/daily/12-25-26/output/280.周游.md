## travel around
### zhōuyóu
---
- **definition:** travel around
- **pinyin:** zhōuyóu
- **components:**
  - 周
    - zhōu
    - circumference; week; cycle; all around
  - 游(遊)
    - yóu
    - to travel; to roam; to wander; to swim
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 周(around; all-around) + 游(travel; roam) = travel around.
  - **interpretation:** Conveys moving through many places or all around a region. Often used with scope words like 世界 or 全国 to indicate breadth.
  - **simplification:** 遊 was simplified to 游 by adopting the already common character 游, reducing strokes and merging the senses of roaming/traveling with the 'to swim/play' graph.
- **examples:**
  - 他计划周游世界(他計劃周遊世界)。
    - Tā jìhuà zhōu yóu shìjiè.
    - He plans to travel around the world.
  - 她喜欢独自周游各地(她喜歡獨自周遊各地)。
    - Tā xǐhuān dúzì zhōu yóu gèdì.
    - She likes to travel around different places alone.
  - 他们周游全国，体验不同的风俗(他們周遊全國，體驗不同的風俗)。
    - Tāmen zhōu yóu quán guó, tǐyàn bùtóng de fēngsú.
    - They travel around the country and experience different customs.
### 周游(周遊) → 周
- **definition:** circumference; week; cycle; all around
- **pinyin:** zhōu
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 用
    - yòng
    - to use; use
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a jade artifact with carved patterns; later borrowed as a country name and 口 was added as a distinguishing mark; top later resembled 用.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an ornamented jade piece, suggesting patterned, complete surfaces. When repurposed for the Zhou state/name, scribes appended 口 as a marker; later evolution made the top resemble 用, which encouraged the folk parsing as 口 + 用.
- **examples:**
  - 我们下周见(我們下週見)。
    - Wǒmen xià zhōu jiàn.
    - See you next week.
  - 圆的周长很大(圓的周長很大)。
    - Yuán de zhōucháng hěn dà.
    - The circle’s circumference is large.
  - 他环顾四周(他環顧四周)。
    - Tā huán gù sìzhōu.
    - He looked around in all directions.
### 周游(周遊) → 周 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 周游(周遊) → 周 → 用
- **definition:** to use; use
- **pinyin:** yòng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** a water bucket; compare 桶; similar but unrelated to 甬, 角; unrelated to the bottom of 備 and to 甫
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a bucket, likely viewed from above with a handle, conveying a tool or container. Its similarity to forms like 甬 or 角 is coincidental and not etymologically connected; over time the sense broadened to the general idea of employing or using something.
- **examples:**
  - 我用手机上网(我用手機上網)。
    - Wǒ yòng shǒujī shàngwǎng.
    - I use my phone to go online.
  - 请用筷子吃饭(請用筷子吃飯)。
    - Qǐng yòng kuàizi chīfàn.
    - Please use chopsticks to eat.
  - 这把钥匙不好用(這把鑰匙不好用)。
    - Zhè bǎ yàoshi bù hǎo yòng.
    - This key doesn’t work well.
### 周游(周遊) → 游(遊)
- **definition:** to travel; to roam; to wander; to swim
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 斿
    - yóu
    - streamer; pennant
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** 游: semantic 水 “water” + phonetic 斿; 遊: semantic 辵 “walk” + phonetic 斿.
  - **interpretation:** Traditional highlights overland movement with 辵, while simplified uses the water radical to evoke motion in water. The phonetic 斿 supplies the yóu sound and also evokes a fluttering streamer, reinforcing the idea of roaming or moving about.
  - **simplification:** Replaced 辵 with the water radical 氵 to reduce strokes and emphasize the ‘swim’ sense, while retaining the phonetic 斿; a semantic-substitution simplification.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢到处游玩(我喜歡到處遊玩)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan dàochù yóuwán.
    - I like to wander around.
  - 他在河里游得很快(他在河裡游得很快)。
    - Tā zài hé lǐ yóu de hěn kuài.
    - He swims quickly in the river.
  - 周末我们去海边游泳(週末我們去海邊游泳)。
    - Zhōumò wǒmen qù hǎibiān yóuyǒng.
    - On the weekend we go to the seaside to swim.
### 周游(周遊) → 游(遊) → 斿
- **definition:** streamer; pennant
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 㫃
    - qí
    - flag; banner
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child; son
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 㫃 ‘flag’ + 子 ‘child’ — a child holding a flag (to travel).
  - **interpretation:** Combines the flag element with a child to depict someone carrying a banner while moving, evoking travel or a leading standard. The sense narrows to the streamer or pennant itself, the part that flutters in motion.
- **examples:**
  - 旗上的斿随风飘扬(旗上的斿隨風飄揚)。
    - Qí shàng de yóu suí fēng piāoyáng.
    - The pennant on the flag flutters in the wind.
  - 仪仗队举着红色斿，引领队伍前行(儀仗隊舉著紅色斿，引領隊伍前行)。
    - Yízhàngduì jǔzhe hóngsè yóu, yǐnlǐng duìwǔ qiánxíng.
    - The honor guard held a red pennant, leading the procession forward.
### 周游(周遊) → 游(遊) → 斿 → 㫃
- **definition:** flag; banner
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 方
    - fāng
    - square; direction
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a flag on a pole with a decoration on top; in modern form the flag is detached and stylized as 方; related to 倝, unrelated to 朝; appears as 𭤨 as a side component, whose top is unrelated to 伤, 乞, 每.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicts a banner mounted on a pole with a finial, hence the meaning ‘flag’. In regular script the banner shape was abstracted to the square-like 方 element and, when used as a side component, takes the form 𭤨 that may resemble other parts but is etymologically distinct.
- **examples:**
  - 军营门口插着一面㫃(軍營門口插著一面㫃)。
    - Jūnyíng ménkǒu chāzhe yī miàn qí.
    - A flag was raised at the entrance of the barracks.
  - 风起时，㫃在高杆上猎猎作响(風起時，㫃在高桿上獵獵作響)。
    - Fēng qǐ shí, qí zài gāo gān shàng lièliè zuòxiǎng.
    - When the wind rose, the flag fluttered loudly on the tall pole.
  - 将军挥㫃下令进军(將軍揮㫃下令進軍)。
    - Jiāngjūn huī qí xiàlìng jìnjūn.
    - The general waved the flag to order the advance.
### 周游(周遊) → 游(遊) → 斿 → 㫃 → 方
- **definition:** square; direction
- **pinyin:** fāng
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife; sword
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 一 + 刀 — blade; tip of a blade; original form of 芒; later borrowed to mean “side”.
  - **interpretation:** Combines a horizontal line with a knife to picture the edge or tip of a blade, explaining the early sense “blade tip/awn.” That graph was later borrowed to write “side,” which broadened into modern senses like “square” and “direction.” It is unrelated to the “flag” graph 㫃.
- **examples:**
  - 我們繼續朝東南方向前進(我們繼續朝東南方向前進)。
    - Wǒmen jìxù cháo dōngnán fāngxiàng qiánjìn.
    - We continue to advance toward the southeast direction.
  - 這個地方很安靜(這個地方很安靜)。
    - Zhège dìfāng hěn ānjìng.
    - This place is very quiet.
  - 桌上有一個正方形的盒子(桌上有一個正方形的盒子)。
    - Zhuō shàng yǒu yí gè zhèngfāngxíng de hézi.
    - There is a square-shaped box on the table.
### 周游(周遊) → 游(遊) → 斿 → 㫃 → 方 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 周游(周遊) → 游(遊) → 斿 → 㫃 → 方 → 刀
- **definition:** knife; sword
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — a knife; original form 𠚣, seen in a variant of 利 (𥝢) and atop 黎.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a knife with a distinct blade and handle. The graph was streamlined into the current two-stroke form and also serves as a component in characters like 利 and 黎.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿着一把刀(他拿著一把刀)。
    - Tā názhe yì bǎ dāo.
    - He is holding a knife.
  - 厨师用刀切蔬菜(廚師用刀切蔬菜)。
    - Chúshī yòng dāo qiē shūcài.
    - The chef uses a knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这把刀很锋利(這把刀很鋒利)。
    - Zhè bǎ dāo hěn fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
### 周游(周遊) → 游(遊) → 斿 → 子
- **definition:** child; son
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: image of a baby with a large head and spread arms; legs wrapped in a blanket.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a swaddled baby with a prominent head and outstretched arms. Over time the figure was stylized into the modern form while still evoking an infant.
- **examples:**
  - 他是我唯一的儿子(他是我唯一的兒子)。
    - Tā shì wǒ wéiyī de érzi.
    - He is my only son.
  - 她抱起孩子(她抱起孩子)。
    - Tā bào qǐ háizi.
    - She picked up the child.
  - 这个孩子三岁了(這個孩子三歲了)。
    - Zhège háizi sān suì le.
    - This child is three years old.
