## 王老吉
---
- **pinyin:** Wánglǎojí
- **definition:** Wong Lo Kat (beverage brand)
- **characters:**
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king, monarch; Wang (surname)
  - 老
    - lǎo
    - old, aged; experienced, veteran; prefix before surnames to show familiarity; always, constantly (colloquial)
  - 吉
    - jí
    - auspicious, lucky; Ji (surname)
- **etymology:** A name-style compound: 王 (wáng, "Wang") + 老 (lǎo, "elder; familiar prefix") gives "Old Mr. Wang," and 吉 (jí, "auspicious") functions as the given name Ji while also evoking good fortune—matching the Cantonese reading "Wong Lo Kat."
- **examples:**
  - 天气很热(天氣很熱)，我买了一罐王老吉(我買了一罐王老吉)。
    - Tiānqì hěn rè, wǒ mǎile yī guàn Wánglǎojí.
    - It’s really hot, so I bought a can of Wong Lo Kat.
