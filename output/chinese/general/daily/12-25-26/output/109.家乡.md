## 家乡(家鄉)
---
## hometown
### jiāxiāng
---
- **definition:** hometown
- **pinyin:** jiāxiāng
- **components:**
  - 家
    - jiā
    - home; family; household; expert
  - 乡(鄉)
    - xiāng
    - countryside; native place; township; rustic
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 家(home; family) + 乡(native place; countryside) = hometown
  - **interpretation:** Combines the idea of one’s home/family with one’s native place to refer to where a person comes from. Conveys emotional attachment to the place of origin.
  - **simplification:** 乡 is the simplified form of 鄉, adopted to reduce stroke count and standardize the frequent 乡 component for easier, faster writing.
- **examples:**
  - 我很想念我的家乡(我很想念我的家鄉)。
    - Wǒ hěn xiǎngniàn wǒ de jiāxiāng.
    - I really miss my hometown.
  - 他大学毕业后回到家乡(他大學畢業後回到家鄉)，在那儿工作(在那兒工作)。
    - Tā dàxué bìyè hòu huídào jiāxiāng, zài nàr gōngzuò.
    - After graduating from college, he returned to his hometown and worked there.
  - 家乡的味道让人难忘(家鄉的味道讓人難忘)。
    - Jiāxiāng de wèidào ràng rén nánwàng.
    - The flavors of home are unforgettable.
### 家乡(家鄉) → 家
- **definition:** home; family; household; expert
- **pinyin:** jiā
- **components:**
  - 𢑓
    - xiá
    - male pig
  - 豕
    - shǐ
    - pig
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 宀 ‘roof’ + phonetic: 𢑓 (xiá, ‘male pig’); later misread/replaced by 豕.
  - **interpretation:** Meaning centers on a dwelling, while the embedded 𢑓 provided the sound in Old Chinese rather than literal meaning. Later graphic change to 豕 led to the folk idea of “pig under a roof,” but the original phonetic was a different pig-related graph.
- **examples:**
  - 我回家吃饭(我回家吃飯)。
    - Wǒ huí jiā chī fàn.
    - I go home to eat.
  - 这个家很温暖(這個家很溫暖)。
    - Zhège jiā hěn wēnnuǎn.
    - This home is very warm.
  - 他是著名的画家(他是著名的畫家)。
    - Tā shì zhùmíng de huàjiā.
    - He is a famous painter.
### 家乡(家鄉) → 家 → 𢑓
- **definition:** male pig
- **pinyin:** xiá
- **components:**
  - 彑
    - jì
    - pig’s head; snout
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram (象形)
  - **description:** Pictogram: a pig with penis, meaning “male pig”.
  - **interpretation:** Graphically shows a boar with an explicitly drawn phallus to mark maleness. Some traditional analyses misread the lower element as a foot; by contrast, 豖 depicts a castrated pig with the penis detached.
- **examples:**
  - 这头𢑓在圈里叫得很大声(這頭𢑓在圈裡叫得很大聲)。
    - Zhè tóu xiá zài juàn lǐ jiào de hěn dàshēng.
    - This boar is grunting loudly in the pen.
  - 农民把𢑓和母猪分开养(農民把𢑓和母豬分開養)。
    - Nóngmín bǎ xiá hé mǔzhū fēnkāi yǎng.
    - The farmer raises boars separately from sows.
  - 古书中用𢑓指未阉割的公猪(古書中用𢑓指未閹割的公豬)。
    - Gǔshū zhōng yòng xiá zhǐ wèi yān gē de gōngzhū.
    - In classical texts, 𢑓 refers to an uncastrated boar.
### 家乡(家鄉) → 家 → 𢑓 → 彑
- **definition:** pig’s head; snout
- **pinyin:** jì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: pig’s head/snout.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the front of a pig’s head, with short strokes suggesting the snout and bristles. As a standalone word it names the object, and as a component it often contributes the semantic idea of pigs or snouts in compound characters.
- **examples:**
  - 古文中，彑指猪的头或猪鼻(古文中，彑指豬的頭或豬鼻)。
    - Gǔwén zhōng, jì zhǐ zhū de tóu huò zhū bí.
    - In classical texts, jì refers to a pig’s head or snout.
  - 祭祀时，人们常用彑作供品，寓意丰收(祭祀時，人們常用彑作供品，寓意豐收)。
    - Jìsì shí, rénmen cháng yòng jì zuò gòngpǐn, yùyì fēngshōu.
    - During rituals, people often use a pig’s head as an offering, symbolizing a good harvest.
  - 在字书里，彑也作部首，用来表示与猪或口鼻有关的意思(在字書裡，彑也作部首，用來表示與豬或口鼻有關的意思)。
    - Zài zìshū lǐ, jì yě zuò bùshǒu, yònglái biǎoshì yǔ zhū huò kǒubí yǒuguān de yìsi.
    - In dictionaries, 彑 also serves as a radical, indicating meanings related to pigs or snouts.
### 家乡(家鄉) → 家 → 豕
- **definition:** pig
- **pinyin:** shǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram (象形): a pig; compare 亥, 𢑓 ‘male pig’, and 豖 ‘castrated male pig’.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a pig in profile with head, body, and legs. Related forms differentiate types of pigs; in 㒸, the pig element serves as a phonetic.
- **examples:**
  - 古书中常以“豕”指代猪(古書中常以「豕」指代豬)。
    - Gǔshū zhōng cháng yǐ “shǐ” zhǐdài zhū.
    - In ancient texts, “豕” refers to a pig.
  - 在甲骨文里，豕的形状清晰可见(在甲骨文裡，豕的形狀清晰可見)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén lǐ, shǐ de xíngzhuàng qīngxī kějiàn.
    - In oracle bone inscriptions, the form of 豕 is clearly visible.
  - 成语“豕突狼奔”中的“豕”意为猪(成語「豕突狼奔」中的「豕」意為豬)。
    - Chéngyǔ “shǐ tū láng bēn” zhōng de “shǐ” yìwéi zhū.
    - In the idiom “豕突狼奔,” “豕” means pig.
### 家乡(家鄉) → 乡(鄉)
- **definition:** countryside; native place; township; rustic
- **pinyin:** xiāng
- **components:**
  - 良
    - liáng
    - good; virtuous
  - 皀
    - jí
    - round-mouthed food vessel; fragrant
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 𠨍 (“two people facing each other”) + 皀 (“round-mouthed food vessel”) → 饗 ‘to feast’; ‘village’ sense via phonetic borrowing/semantic shift.
  - **interpretation:** Originally evoked two people sharing a vessel in a ritual feast, a scene central to communal life. Through semantic shift it came to mean a community or village; the simplified 乡 preserves only a streamlined outline of the older graph.
  - **simplification:** Graphic abbreviation of 鄉 by removing the right-hand 良-like component (historically from 皀) and compressing strokes for economy.
- **examples:**
  - 我们回到家乡(我們回到家鄉)。
    - Wǒmen huídào jiāxiāng.
    - We returned to our hometown.
  - 这个乡镇很安静(這個鄉鎮很安靜)。
    - Zhège xiāngzhèn hěn ānjìng.
    - This township is very quiet.
  - 他身上还有些乡气(他身上還有些鄉氣)。
    - Tā shēnshang hái yǒu xiē xiāngqì.
    - He still has a bit of rustic air.
### 家乡(家鄉) → 乡(鄉) → 良
- **definition:** good; virtuous
- **pinyin:** liáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Possibly depicts corridors and a room, in which case it would be the original form of 廊. See also the top component of 复. Unrelated to 食, 艮 and 即.
  - **interpretation:** Likely sketched a hallway leading into a room, an architectural layout that later became distinguished as 廊 ‘corridor.’ Over time the graph was borrowed to write liáng ‘good,’ while the corridor sense shifted to 廊; its upper element also appears in 复.
- **examples:**
  - 这家店的服务很优良(這家店的服務很優良)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn de fúwù hěn yōuliáng.
    - The service at this shop is excellent.
  - 我们取得了良好成绩(我們取得了良好成績)。
    - Wǒmen qǔdéle liánghǎo chéngjì.
    - We achieved good results.
  - 在关键时刻(在關鍵時刻)，她做出了良好的决定(她做出了良好的決定)。
    - Zài guānjiàn shíkè, tā zuòchūle liánghǎo de juédìng.
    - At a critical moment, she made a good decision.
### 家乡(家鄉) → 乡(鄉) → 皀
- **definition:** round-mouthed food vessel; fragrant
- **pinyin:** jí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a bowl of rice on a stand; part of the radical 食; unrelated to 良 and 旨.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a rice bowl set on a stand, an early food-related image that later associated with smell and fragrance in usage. Also serves historically as a component within the food radical 食, without any etymological link to 良 or 旨 despite visual resemblance.
- **examples:**
  - 此酒甚皀，香气四溢(此酒甚皀，香氣四溢)。
    - Cǐ jiǔ shèn jí, xiāngqì sìyì.
    - This wine is very fragrant, and its aroma spreads everywhere.
  - 花开时院中皆皀(花開時院中皆皀)。
    - Huā kāi shí yuàn zhōng jiē jí.
    - When the flowers bloom, the courtyard is filled with fragrance.
  - 皀字本为象形，像托座上的饭碗(皀字本為象形，像托座上的飯碗)。
    - Jí zì běn wéi xiàngxíng, xiàng tuōzuò shàng de fànwǎn.
    - The graph was originally pictographic, resembling a rice bowl on a stand.
