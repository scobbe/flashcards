## 武昌起义(武昌起義)
---
- **pinyin:** Wǔchāng Qǐyì
- **definition:** Wuchang Uprising
- **characters:**
  - 武
    - wǔ
    - martial
  - 昌
    - chāng
    - prosperous
  - 起
    - qǐ
    - rise
  - 义(義)
    - yì
    - justice
- **etymology:** 武 (wǔ, "martial") + 昌 (chāng, "prosperous") is an auspicious toponym from Eastern Wu meaning “military flourishing,” aptly naming the garrison center where the 1911 soldiers rose. 起 (qǐ, "rise") + 义(義) (yì, "justice") is the classical formula for a morally sanctioned revolt, so the phrase asserts a righteous rising at Wuchang rather than a mere mutiny.
- **examples:**
  - 武昌起义标志着辛亥革命的开始(武昌起義標誌著辛亥革命的開始)。
    - wǔchāng qǐyì biāozhìzhe xīnhài gémìng de kāishǐ
    - The Wuchang Uprising marked the beginning of the Xinhai Revolution.
