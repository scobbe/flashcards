## children of officials
### guān’èrdài
---
- **definition:** children of officials
- **pinyin:** guān’èrdài
- **components:**
  - 官
    - guān
    - government official; public office; officialdom; organ (TCM)
  - 二
    - èr
    - two; second; foolish (slang); double
  - 代
    - dài
    - generation; era; to substitute; to replace
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 官 (official) + 二 (second) + 代 (generation) = second generation of officials (children of officials).
  - **interpretation:** Refers to the offspring of government officials, highlighting lineage as the source of status. In public discourse it can imply access to connections, privilege, or influence.
- **examples:**
  - 许多人用官二代指官员的子女(許多人用官二代指官員的子女)。
    - Xǔduō rén yòng guān’èrdài zhǐ guānyuán de zǐnǚ.
    - Many people use guan’erdai to refer to the children of officials.
  - 有人认为官二代容易获得资源和机会(有人認為官二代容易獲得資源和機會)，也有人强调不能以偏概全(也有人強調不能以偏概全)。
    - Yǒurén rènwéi guān’èrdài róngyì huòdé zīyuán hé jīhuì, yě yǒurén qiángdiào bùnéng yǐpiān gàiquán.
    - Some think guan’erdai easily obtain resources and opportunities, while others stress you shouldn’t generalize.
  - 他不是官二代，却靠自身努力考入名校(他不是官二代，卻靠自身努力考入名校)。
    - Tā bù shì guān’èrdài, què kào zìshēn nǔlì kǎorù míngxiào.
    - He isn’t a guan’erdai, yet he got into a top school through his own effort.
### 官二代 → 官
- **definition:** government official; public office; officialdom; organ (TCM)
- **pinyin:** guān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (會意)
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 宀 + 𠂤 — multiple rooms under a roof, an official building.
  - **interpretation:** A roof placed over a cluster of rooms evokes a government office or administrative building. From that image, the word extended to mean officials, officialdom, and related institutional senses.
- **examples:**
  - 他当了官(他當了官)。
    - Tā dāng le guān.
    - He became an official.
  - 他不愿涉足官场(他不願涉足官場)。
    - Tā bùyuàn shèzú guānchǎng.
    - He is unwilling to step into officialdom.
  - 在中医里(在中醫裡)，肝被称为将军之官(肝被稱為將軍之官)。
    - Zài Zhōngyī lǐ, gān bèi chēngwéi jiāngjūn zhī guān.
    - In traditional Chinese medicine, the liver is called the organ of the general.
### 官二代 → 二
- **definition:** two; second; foolish (slang); double
- **pinyin:** èr
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** ideogram: two parallel strokes; duplication of 一.
  - **interpretation:** Two horizontal lines directly depict the quantity ‘two’ as simple tally marks. It parallels 一 and 三 as incremental strokes, with historical 亖 for four, emphasizing straightforward counting by repetition.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是二月二日(今天是二月二日)。
    - Jīntiān shì èr yuè èr rì.
    - Today is February 2nd.
  - 我住在二楼(我住在二樓)。
    - Wǒ zhù zài èr lóu.
    - I live on the second floor.
  - 二加二等于四(二加二等於四)。
    - Èr jiā èr děngyú sì.
    - Two plus two equals four.
### 官二代 → 二 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 官二代 → 代
- **definition:** generation; era; to substitute; to replace
- **pinyin:** dài
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 弋
    - yì
    - to shoot; to catch; dart
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 亻 ('person') + phonetic 弋 (OC *lɯɡ)
  - **interpretation:** Combines the person radical with 弋, where 弋 supplies the sound. The human-related semantic component suits meanings like acting on behalf of someone, which later extended to ideas of succession and generations.
- **examples:**
  - 他代我去开会(他代我去開會)。
    - Tā dài wǒ qù kāihuì.
    - He went to the meeting in my place.
  - 我们是同一代的人(我們是同一代的人)。
    - Wǒmen shì tóng yī dài de rén.
    - We are people of the same generation.
  - 今天就以茶代酒吧(今天就以茶代酒吧)。
    - Jīntiān jiù yǐ chá dài jiǔ ba.
    - Let’s use tea as a substitute for wine today.
### 官二代 → 代 → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 官二代 → 代 → 弋
- **definition:** to shoot; to catch; dart
- **pinyin:** yì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a sharp peg — original form of 杙 (OC *lɯɡ, “peg”); same as 才 and 尗.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a pointed peg or dart, a simple tool meant to pierce or stick into something. From this image arose senses related to shooting or striking game, and it also served as the base form for characters like 杙.
- **examples:**
  - 古人常用弋射鸟兽(古人常用弋射鳥獸)。
    - Gǔrén cháng yòng yìshè niǎo shòu.
    - In ancient times people often used yi-shooting to take birds and beasts.
  - 猎人以弋猎为生(獵人以弋獵為生)。
    - Lièrén yǐ yìliè wéi shēng.
    - Hunters made a living by fowling or hunting with yi.
  - 这件青铜器上刻有弋的形象(這件青銅器上刻有弋的形象)。
    - Zhè jiàn qīngtóngqì shàng kè yǒu yì de xíngxiàng.
    - This bronze vessel is engraved with the image of a yi-peg/dart.
