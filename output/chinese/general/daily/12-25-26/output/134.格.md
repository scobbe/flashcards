## 格
---
- **pinyin:** gé
- **definition:** grammatical unit
- **etymology:** Phono-semantic compound: left 木 (mù, "wood") supplies the semantic field of wooden objects/striking, while right 各 (gè, "each") provides the sound. Early senses were "to strike; meet; fit," which broadened to "pattern/standard; lattice," giving the modern linguistic use for a grammatical category or case.
- **examples:**
  - 德语有四个格(德語有四個格)：主格、宾格、与格和属格(主格、賓格、與格和屬格)。
    - Déyǔ yǒu sì gè gé: zhǔgé, bīngé, yǔgé hé shǔgé.
    - German has four cases: nominative, accusative, dative, and genitive.
  - 这个代词在句子里要用主格(這個代詞在句子裡要用主格)，不是宾格(不是賓格)。
    - Zhège dàicí zài jùzi lǐ yào yòng zhǔgé, bú shì bīngé.
    - This pronoun should be in the nominative case in the sentence, not the accusative.
