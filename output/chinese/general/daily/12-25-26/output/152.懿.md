## virtuous
### yì
---
- **definition:** virtuous
- **pinyin:** yì
- **components:**
  - 壹
    - yī
    - one (financial form)
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart; mind
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 心 + phonetic: 壹
  - **interpretation:** Combines the semantic element 心 (heart) with the phonetic 壹 to point to qualities rooted in the heart. Over time it came to denote lofty, admirable virtue and fine moral character.
- **examples:**
  - 皇后以懿德著称(皇后以懿德著稱)。
    - Huánghòu yǐ yìdé zhùchēng.
    - The empress was renowned for her virtuous conduct.
  - 他以懿行感化众人(他以懿行感化眾人)。
    - Tā yǐ yìxíng gǎnhuà zhòngrén.
    - He moved others through his exemplary virtue.
  - 先祖遗留懿名，后人当自勉(先祖遺留懿名，後人當自勉)。
    - Xiānzǔ yíliú yìmíng, hòurén dāng zìmiǎn.
    - The ancestors left a fine reputation; descendants should encourage themselves.
### 懿 → 壹
- **definition:** one (financial form)
- **pinyin:** yī
- **components:**
  - 壶(壺)
    - hú
    - pot; jar
  - 吉
    - jí
    - auspicious; lucky
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 壺 + phonetic 吉 (Shuowen).
  - **interpretation:** Combines 壺 ‘pot/jar’ as a semantic indicator with 吉 supplying the sound; the Old Chinese readings align phonetically. Over time it was repurposed as the fraud-resistant financial form of ‘one,’ while retaining the older phono-semantic structure historically.
- **examples:**
  - 请用大写金额填写壹仟贰佰叁拾元整(請用大寫金額填寫壹仟貳佰參拾元整)。
    - Qǐng yòng dàxiě jīn'é tiánxiě yī qiān èr bǎi sān shí yuán zhěng.
    - Please fill in the amount in uppercase as one thousand two hundred thirty yuan only.
  - 支票上误写为一，应改为壹以防涂改(支票上誤寫為一，應改為壹以防塗改)。
    - Zhīpiào shàng wù xiě wéi yī, yīng gǎi wéi yī yǐ fáng túgǎi.
    - The check mistakenly uses '一' and should be changed to '壹' to prevent alteration.
  - 此发票金额壹万元(此發票金額壹萬元)。
    - Cǐ fāpiào jīn'é yī wàn yuán.
    - The amount on this invoice is ten thousand yuan (in uppercase form).
### 懿 → 壹 → 壶(壺)
- **definition:** pot; jar
- **pinyin:** hú
- **components:**
  - 士
    - shì
    - scholar; gentleman; soldier
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic compound: lid 冖 + mouth 口 + stand 士 (vessel on a stand).
  - **interpretation:** Builds up the image of a lidded container: the cover and mouth of the pot are shown, resting on a base. The combined elements point to the function and shape of a pot or jar rather than supplying a phonetic cue.
  - **simplification:** The traditional 壺 was streamlined to 壶 by pruning interior strokes and compressing the vessel depiction into a simpler form while keeping the overall silhouette, reducing stroke count for easier writing.
- **examples:**
  - 我把茶倒进壶(我把茶倒進壺)。
    - Wǒ bǎ chá dào jìn hú.
    - I pour the tea into the pot.
  - 这个壶很古老(這個壺很古老)。
    - Zhège hú hěn gǔlǎo.
    - This pot is very old.
  - 他买了一个紫砂壶(他買了一個紫砂壺)。
    - Tā mǎi le yí gè zǐshā hú.
    - He bought a Yixing clay teapot.
### 懿 → 壹 → 壶(壺) → 士
- **definition:** scholar; gentleman; soldier
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: war/ritual axe; later ‘soldier’ → ‘officer/intellectual’; related to 王, 圭, 金.
  - **interpretation:** Earliest forms depicted an axe, a symbol of martial power and authority, which naturally extended to mean a soldier and then an officer or learned gentleman. Connections to 王, 圭, and 金 reflect the ritual or jade/metal axe lineage in related graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一名士兵(他是一名士兵)。
    - Tā shì yì míng shìbīng.
    - He is a soldier.
  - 他是个绅士(他是個紳士)。
    - Tā shì ge shēnshì.
    - He is a gentleman.
  - 古代的士人重视礼仪(古代的士人重視禮儀)。
    - Gǔdài de shìrén zhòngshì lǐyí.
    - In ancient times, scholar-gentry valued ritual propriety.
### 懿 → 壹 → 壶(壺) → 口
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 懿 → 壹 → 吉
- **definition:** auspicious; lucky
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 士
    - shì
    - scholar; gentleman; soldier
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram (指事)
  - **description:** Derived from 士 ‘ritual axe’ + 口 ‘open mouth’, referring to rituals seeking the favor of ancestors and deities.
  - **interpretation:** Combining a ritual implement with an open mouth evokes solemn prayers or proclamations made during sacrificial rites. Through the idea that proper ritual brings divine approval, the sense extends to auspiciousness and good fortune.
- **examples:**
  - 今天是个吉日(今天是個吉日)。
    - Jīntiān shì gè jí rì.
    - Today is an auspicious day.
  - 祝你吉祥安康(祝你吉祥安康)。
    - Zhù nǐ jíxiáng ānkāng.
    - Wishing you good fortune and health.
  - 吉先生到了(吉先生到了)。
    - Jí Xiānsheng dàole.
    - Mr. Ji has arrived.
### 懿 → 心
- **definition:** heart; mind
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – a heart, now highly stylized.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawing of the anatomical heart; its dots and curves abstracted into a compact, balanced form over time. Related side-forms like 忄 in compounds cue meanings tied to feelings, thought, or the mind.
- **examples:**
  - 我很关心你(我很關心你)。
    - Wǒ hěn guānxīn nǐ.
    - I care about you a lot.
  - 他一时分心，犯了错误(他一時分心，犯了錯誤)。
    - Tā yīshí fēnxīn, fàn le cuòwù.
    - He got distracted for a moment and made a mistake.
  - 放下心里的担忧，安心休息吧(放下心裡的擔憂，安心休息吧)。
    - Fàngxià xīnlǐ de dānyōu, ānxīn xiūxi ba.
    - Let go of the worries in your heart and rest.
