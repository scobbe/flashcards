## 凉茶(涼茶)
---
- **pinyin:** liángchá
- **definition:** herbal tea
- **characters:**
  - 凉(涼)
    - liáng
    - cool
  - 茶
    - chá
    - tea
- **etymology:** 凉(涼) (liáng, "cool") signals the TCM "cooling" nature (涼性) of herbs used to purge internal heat (上火), not the drink’s temperature, while 茶 (chá, "tea") names the brewed infusion method rather than Camellia sinensis specifically. Together they denote a cooling‑natured herbal decoction—rooted in the Lingnan/Cantonese practice of boiling medicinal plants to counter the region’s hot, humid climate.
- **examples:**
  - 我喉咙有点痛(我喉嚨有點痛)，奶奶让我喝一碗凉茶(奶奶讓我喝一碗涼茶)。
    - wǒ hóulóng yǒudiǎn tòng, nǎinai ràng wǒ hē yì wǎn liángchá
    - My throat hurts a little, and Grandma told me to drink a bowl of herbal tea.
