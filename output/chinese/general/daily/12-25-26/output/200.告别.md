## 告别(告別)
---
## say goodbye; farewell
### gàobié
---
- **definition:** say goodbye; farewell
- **pinyin:** gàobié
- **components:**
  - 告
    - gào
    - to tell; to inform; to announce; to sue/accuse
  - 别(別)
    - bié
    - to part/separate; to distinguish; other; don't (imperative)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 告(announce) + 别(separate/part) = announce separation → farewell.
  - **interpretation:** Announces a parting, often with a formal or emotional tone. Common in both everyday goodbyes and ceremonial farewells.
  - **simplification:** 別 → 别 adopts an older shorthand/variant that merges/simplifies the 刂 component, reducing strokes for easier writing; 告 unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我们在车站挥手告别(我們在車站揮手告別)。
    - Wǒmen zài chēzhàn huīshǒu gàobié.
    - We waved goodbye at the station.
  - 临行前，他向老师郑重告别(臨行前，他向老師鄭重告別)。
    - Línxíng qián, tā xiàng lǎoshī zhèngzhòng gàobié.
    - Before departing, he formally bid farewell to his teacher.
  - 这次告别不是终点，而是新的开始(這次告別不是終點，而是新的開始)。
    - Zhè cì gàobié bú shì zhōngdiǎn, ér shì xīn de kāishǐ.
    - This farewell is not the end, but a new beginning.
### 告别(告別) → 告
- **definition:** to tell; to inform; to announce; to sue/accuse
- **pinyin:** gào
- **components:**
  - 牛
    - niú
    - cow; ox
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** unclear
  - **description:** Unclear; Shuowen: 牛 + 口; Yu Xingwu: top component unrelated to 牛 per oracle bone and bronze inscriptions.
  - **interpretation:** Analyses differ: the traditional view reads it as cow plus mouth, but early forms suggest the upper element is a distinct graph not meaning ‘cow’. The 口 component plausibly marks speech or proclamation, which fits senses like informing, announcing, or accusing.
- **examples:**
  - 请及时告知我结果(請及時告知我結果)。
    - Qǐng jíshí gàozhī wǒ jiéguǒ.
    - Please inform me of the result promptly.
  - 他向法院起诉，告那家公司诈骗(他向法院起訴，告那家公司詐騙)。
    - Tā xiàng fǎyuàn qǐsù, gào nà jiā gōngsī zhàpiàn.
    - He filed a lawsuit, accusing that company of fraud.
  - 临别时，他轻声告诫我要小心(臨別時，他輕聲告誡我要小心)。
    - Línbié shí, tā qīngshēng gàojiè wǒ yào xiǎoxīn.
    - At parting, he softly warned me to be careful.
### 告别(告別) → 告 → 牛
- **definition:** cow; ox
- **pinyin:** niú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: bull’s head with two horns and two ears; perhaps unrelated to 告; unrelated to 先.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bull’s head with prominent horns and ears, a direct visual rendering of the animal. Stylization over time simplified the lines, but the bovine shape remains evident.
- **examples:**
  - 这头牛很壮(這頭牛很壯)。
    - Zhè tóu niú hěn zhuàng.
    - This ox is strong.
  - 牛奶很新鲜(牛奶很新鮮)。
    - Niúnǎi hěn xīnxiān.
    - The milk is very fresh.
  - 牧民在草原上赶牛(牧民在草原上趕牛)。
    - Mùmín zài cǎoyuán shàng gǎn niú.
    - The herders drive cattle on the grassland.
### 告别(告別) → 告 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別)
- **definition:** to part/separate; to distinguish; other; don't (imperative)
- **pinyin:** bié
- **components:**
  - 另
    - lìng
    - other; separate; besides
  - 冎
    - guǎ
    - bare bones; skeleton
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (會意)
  - **description:** 會意: 冎 “bone” + 刂 “knife”, hence 𠛰 ‘to cut up bone and flesh’; later stylized as 別. 别 is a variant where 𠮠 is replaced with visually similar 另; officially adopted in 1956 as the simplified form of 彆.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the ideas of bone and knife to depict cutting apart, which extends to meanings like separating and distinguishing. Over time the form was streamlined, and modern 别 uses 另 on the left for ease and resemblance to the older shape.
  - **simplification:** The left component 𠮠 was replaced by 另 to reduce strokes and improve legibility; PRC reform also merged 彆/別 to the single simplified form 别 (1956).
- **examples:**
  - 别说话(別說話)。
    - Bié shuōhuà.
    - Don't speak.
  - 我们走别的路吧(我們走別的路吧)。
    - Wǒmen zǒu bié de lù ba.
    - Let's take a different road.
  - 天色已晚(天色已晚)，我们在车站分别吧(我們在車站分別吧)。
    - Tiānsè yǐ wǎn, wǒmen zài chēzhàn fēnbié ba.
    - It's getting late; let's part at the station.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 另
- **definition:** other; separate; besides
- **pinyin:** lìng
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
  - 力
    - lì
    - strength; power
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 口 ‘mouth’ + phonetic: 力 (lì); also noted in 玉篇校釋 as a simplified form of 別/别.
  - **interpretation:** Mouth suggests a semantic field related to speech or counted units, while 力 contributes the lì ~ lìng pronunciation. Historical sources also link it graphically to 別 ‘to separate,’ aligning with meanings like ‘other’ and ‘separate.’
- **examples:**
  - 我们需要另一个方案(我們需要另一個方案)。
    - Wǒmen xūyào lìng yí gè fāng'àn.
    - We need another plan.
  - 费用另算，邮资另付(費用另算，郵資另付)。
    - Fèiyòng lìng suàn, yóuzī lìng fù.
    - Fees are calculated separately; postage is paid separately.
  - 会议时间待定，另行通知(會議時間待定，另行通知)。
    - Huìyì shíjiān dàidìng, lìng xíng tōngzhī.
    - The meeting time is to be determined and will be announced separately.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 另 → 力
- **definition:** strength; power
- **pinyin:** lì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): interpreted as a bent arm extending from the body, or as a wooden plough; original form 𠠲.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an arm applying force, a concrete image that came to represent strength and effort. Some traditions also saw it as a plough, linking power with labor in the fields.
- **examples:**
  - 他力气很大(他力氣很大)。
    - Tā lìqi hěn dà.
    - He is very strong.
  - 我们要尽力完成任务(我們要盡力完成任務)。
    - Wǒmen yào jìn lì wánchéng rènwù.
    - We must do our best to finish the task.
  - 科技给人类带来巨大力量(科技給人類帶來巨大力量)。
    - Kējì gěi rénlèi dàilái jùdà lìliàng.
    - Technology brings great power to humanity.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 冎
- **definition:** bare bones; skeleton
- **pinyin:** guǎ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: bones; original form of 骨.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts exposed bones/jaw, reflecting a skeletal shape. Served historically as the graph for “bone” and survives as a top component in characters like 骨 and 咼 (the latter distinguishing itself by adding 口), with related forms seen in 呙 and the variant 另.
- **examples:**
  - 考古学家在遗址里发现了几片冎(考古學家在遺址裡發現了幾片冎)。
    - Kǎogǔxuéjiā zài yízhǐ lǐ fāxiànle jǐ piàn guǎ.
    - Archaeologists found several jawbone fragments at the site.
  - 河岸上出现了冎(河岸上出現了冎)。
    - Hé'àn shàng chūxiànle guǎ.
    - Bare bones appeared on the riverbank.
  - 画中可见野兽的冎(畫中可見野獸的冎)。
    - Huà zhōng kějiàn yěshòu de guǎ.
    - The painting shows a beast’s jawbone.
### 告别(告別) → 别(別) → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — a knife; original form 𠚣, seen in a variant of 利 (𥝢) and atop 黎.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a knife with a distinct blade and handle. The graph was streamlined into the current two-stroke form and also serves as a component in characters like 利 and 黎.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿着一把刀(他拿著一把刀)。
    - Tā názhe yì bǎ dāo.
    - He is holding a knife.
  - 厨师用刀切蔬菜(廚師用刀切蔬菜)。
    - Chúshī yòng dāo qiē shūcài.
    - The chef uses a knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这把刀很锋利(這把刀很鋒利)。
    - Zhè bǎ dāo hěn fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
