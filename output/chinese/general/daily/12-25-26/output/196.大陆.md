## 大陆(大陸)
---
## mainland
### dàlù
---
- **definition:** mainland
- **pinyin:** dàlù
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big; great; major; very
  - 陆(陸)
    - lù
    - land; continent; dry land; to land
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 大 ‘big; great’ + 陆 ‘land; continent’ = ‘mainland (great landmass)’
  - **interpretation:** Literally “great land,” denoting the large continuous landmass in contrast with seas and islands. In modern contexts it often abbreviates 中国大陆, i.e., Mainland China.
  - **simplification:** 陸 was simplified to 陆 by reducing the right component’s strokes (from 坴 to a 击-like form) while keeping the阝 radical, following stroke-reduction patterns for 阜-radical characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我们从台湾飞往大陆(我們從臺灣飛往大陸)。
    - Wǒmen cóng Táiwān fēi wǎng Dàlù.
    - We are flying from Taiwan to the mainland.
  - 他在大陆工作多年(他在大陸工作多年)。
    - Tā zài Dàlù gōngzuò duō nián.
    - He has worked on the mainland for many years.
  - 中国大陆与香港之间往来频繁(中國大陸與香港之間往來頻繁)。
    - Zhōngguó Dàlù yǔ Xiānggǎng zhījiān wǎnglái pínfán.
    - There is frequent travel between Mainland China and Hong Kong.
### 大陆(大陸) → 大
- **definition:** big; great; major; very
- **pinyin:** dà
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a person facing forward; original form of 夫, later phonetically borrowed for “big”.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicts a human figure viewed from the front, not necessarily with exaggerated outstretched arms. The graph was later borrowed to write the word pronounced dà ‘big’, while 夫 specialized for ‘man’. The modern shape still evokes a torso with two legs and an arm stroke.
- **examples:**
  - 这个房间很大(這個房間很大)。
    - Zhège fángjiān hěn dà.
    - This room is very big.
  - 他是我们家最大的孩子(他是我們家最大的孩子)。
    - Tā shì wǒmen jiā zuì dà de háizi.
    - He is the eldest child in our family.
  - 大学开学了(大學開學了)。
    - Dàxué kāixué le.
    - The university term has begun.
### 大陆(大陸) → 陆(陸)
- **definition:** land; continent; dry land; to land
- **pinyin:** lù
