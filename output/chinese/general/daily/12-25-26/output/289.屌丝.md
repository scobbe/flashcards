## loser (internet slang)
### diǎosī
---
- **definition:** loser (internet slang)
- **pinyin:** diǎosī
- **components:**
  - 屌
    - diǎo
    - penis; dick (vulgar); awesome/cool (slang); badass (slang)
  - 丝(絲)
    - sī
    - silk; thread; filament; fan (abbrev in 粉丝)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 屌(“dick”; vulgar; also ‘edgy/cool’ in slang) + 丝(“silk/thread”; internet-slang suffix from 粉丝 ‘fan’) = self-deprecating ‘loser/ordinary guy’.
  - **interpretation:** Self-mocking internet slang for ordinary or underprivileged young men, often contrasted with 高富帅. Tone ranges from humorous self-deprecation to insult depending on context.
  - **simplification:** 絲 was simplified to 丝 by streamlining the silk radical and reducing strokes; widespread online use favored the simplified form for faster typing.
- **examples:**
  - 他自嘲自己是个屌丝(他自嘲自己是個屌絲)。
    - Tā zìcháo zìjǐ shì gè diǎosī.
    - He jokingly calls himself a loser.
  - 很多屌丝努力学习(很多屌絲努力學習)，希望改变命运(希望改變命運)。
    - Hěn duō diǎosī nǔlì xuéxí, xīwàng gǎibiàn mìngyùn.
    - Many diaosi study hard, hoping to change their fate.
  - 有人把屌丝当作自我调侃(有人把屌絲當作自我調侃)，也有人觉得这是贬义词(也有人覺得這是貶義詞)。
    - Yǒurén bǎ diǎosī dāngzuò zìwǒ tiáokǎn, yě yǒu rén juéde zhè shì biǎnyìcí.
    - Some people use diaosi as self-mockery, while others see it as a derogatory term.
### 屌丝(屌絲) → 屌
- **definition:** penis; dick (vulgar); awesome/cool (slang); badass (slang)
- **pinyin:** diǎo
- **components:**
  - 尸(屍)
    - shī
    - corpse; body
  - 吊
    - diào
    - hang; suspend
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound; ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic 尸 'body' + phonetic 吊 'hanging': 'something that hangs from the body'.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the meaning element for 'body' with the phonetic for 'to hang', evoking an object that hangs from the body. The phonetic 吊 contributes the sound diǎo while 尸 cues the anatomical sense and underlies later vulgar and slang uses.
- **examples:**
  - 这款新手机很屌(這款新手機很屌)。
    - Zhè kuǎn xīn shǒujī hěn diǎo.
    - This new phone is awesome.
  - 他骂人时会说“屌”这样的粗话(他罵人時會說「屌」這樣的粗話)。
    - Tā màrén shí huì shuō 'diǎo' zhèyàng de cūhuà.
    - He uses crude words like 'diao' when cursing.
  - 他说自己技术很屌(他說自己技術很屌)，帮团队解决了难题(幫團隊解決了難題)。
    - Tā shuō zìjǐ jìshù hěn diǎo, bāng tuánduì jiějué le nántí.
    - He said his skills are badass and helped the team solve a tough problem.
### 屌丝(屌絲) → 屌 → 尸(屍)
- **definition:** corpse; body
- **pinyin:** shī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a human figure with bent legs, side facing front; ancient form 𡰣, interpreted as sitting or lying. (Trad. 屍 is phono-semantic: phonetic 尸 + semantic 死.)
  - **interpretation:** Evokes a human body in profile with bent legs, suggesting someone seated or lying down, which aligns with the sense of a corpse or body. In the traditional form 屍, 死 reinforces the meaning ‘death’ while 尸 supplies the sound and body-related imagery.
  - **simplification:** 屍 → 尸 by dropping the semantic 死 and keeping the base 尸, reducing strokes and aligning with the long-used radical form; meaning remained clear from context.
- **examples:**
  - 警方在河边发现了一具尸体(警方在河邊發現了一具屍體)。
    - Jǐngfāng zài hébiān fāxiàn le yī jù shītǐ.
    - The police found a corpse by the river.
  - 法医正在进行尸检(法醫正在進行屍檢)。
    - Fǎyī zhèngzài jìnxíng shījiǎn.
    - The coroner is conducting an autopsy.
  - 孩子们在电影里害怕僵尸(孩子們在電影裡害怕殭屍)。
    - Háizimen zài diànyǐng lǐ hàipà jiāngshī.
    - The children were afraid of zombies in the movie.

### 屌丝(屌絲) → 丝(絲)
- **definition:** silk; thread; filament; fan (abbrev in 粉丝)
- **pinyin:** sī
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** simplified from 絲
  - **description:** Simplified from 絲 via variant 𢇁; traditional 絲 is ideogrammic compound (會意): 糸 + 糸.
  - **interpretation:** Traditional 絲 combines two ‘silk/thread’ elements (糸 + 糸) to depict many fine strands, conveying the idea of silk or thread. The simplified form 丝 streamlines this by adopting the cursive/variant shape 𢇁, compressing the two threads into a single, more abstract looped form while preserving the original meaning.
  - **simplification:** Adopted a long-used handwritten/variant form (𢇁) that merges the double 糸 into one compact shape, greatly reducing strokes and improving writing speed for a very common word.
- **examples:**
  - 这条丝带很亮(這條絲帶很亮)。
    - Zhè tiáo sīdài hěn liàng.
    - This silk ribbon is very bright.
  - 他用丝线缝衣服(他用絲線縫衣服)。
    - Tā yòng sīxiàn féng yīfu.
    - He sews clothes with silk thread.
  - 丝绸之路连接东西方(絲綢之路連接東西方)。
    - Sīchóu zhī Lù liánjiē dōng xīfāng.
    - The Silk Road connects East and West.
### 屌丝(屌絲) → 丝(絲) → 糸
- **definition:** silk; thread
- **pinyin:** mì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** a skein of silk.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bundle of silk threads tied in the middle, with strands hanging above and below. The stylized sketch highlights twisted filaments, which later served as the standard radical for thread- and textile-related characters.
  - **simplification:** In Simplified Chinese the silk radical was streamlined to 纟 to reduce strokes and speed writing; 糸 is rarely used independently, but 纟 replaces it as a left-side component.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan sīchóu.
    - I like silk fabrics.
  - 这件衣服是用蚕丝做的(這件衣服是用蠶絲做的)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfu shì yòng cánsī zuò de.
    - This piece of clothing is made from silkworm silk.
  - 丝线很细(絲線很細)。
    - Sīxiàn hěn xì.
    - Silk thread is very thin.
