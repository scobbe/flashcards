## strange
### qíguài
---
- **definition:** strange
- **pinyin:** qíguài
- **components:**
  - 奇
    - qí
    - strange; unusual; wonderful; rare
  - 怪
    - guài
    - strange; odd; to blame; rather
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 奇 + 怪 = strange/odd.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the sense of unusual (奇) with odd or suspicious (怪) to describe something out of the ordinary or hard to explain. Can also mean to find something odd in certain structures.
- **examples:**
  - 这件事很奇怪(這件事很奇怪)。
    - Zhè jiàn shì hěn qíguài.
    - This matter is very strange.
  - 我觉得他今天的行为很奇怪(我覺得他今天的行為很奇怪)。
    - Wǒ juéde tā jīntiān de xíngwéi hěn qíguài.
    - I think his behavior today is very strange.
  - 奇怪的是(奇怪的是)，他居然没有来(他居然沒有來)。
    - Qíguài de shì, tā jūrán méiyǒu lái.
    - What’s strange is that he didn’t show up.
### 奇怪 → 奇
- **definition:** strange; unusual; wonderful; rare
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big; great
  - 可
    - kě
    - can; may; approve; suitable
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 大 ('big; great') + phonetic: 可 (OC *kʰaːlʔ)
  - **interpretation:** 大 contributes the idea of greatness or standing out, while 可 provides the sound value. Combined, it evokes something notable or out of the ordinary, which develops senses like 'strange', 'unusual', and 'wonderful'.
- **examples:**
  - 这个想法很奇怪(這個想法很奇怪)。
    - Zhège xiǎngfǎ hěn qíguài.
    - This idea is very strange.
  - 她弹得真奇妙(她彈得真奇妙)。
    - Tā tán de zhēn qímiào.
    - She plays really wonderfully.
  - 这种鸟在这里很稀奇(這種鳥在這裡很稀奇)。
    - Zhè zhǒng niǎo zài zhèlǐ hěn xīqí.
    - This kind of bird is rare here.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 大
- **definition:** big; great
- **pinyin:** dà
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a person facing forward; original form of 夫, later phonetically borrowed for “big”.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicts a human figure viewed from the front, not necessarily with exaggerated outstretched arms. The graph was later borrowed to write the word pronounced dà ‘big’, while 夫 specialized for ‘man’. The modern shape still evokes a torso with two legs and an arm stroke.
- **examples:**
  - 这个房间很大(這個房間很大)。
    - Zhège fángjiān hěn dà.
    - This room is very big.
  - 他是我们家最大的孩子(他是我們家最大的孩子)。
    - Tā shì wǒmen jiā zuì dà de háizi.
    - He is the eldest child in our family.
  - 大学开学了(大學開學了)。
    - Dàxué kāixué le.
    - The university term has begun.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 可
- **definition:** can; may; approve; suitable
- **pinyin:** kě
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
  - 丂
    - kǎo
    - exhale; breath
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 口 + phonetic 丂 (OC *kʰluːʔ), with a reference to exhaled air; possibly originally “to sing”; cf. 訶 and 哥 (可 written twice).
  - **interpretation:** Mouth signals the domain of speech or breath, while 丂 supplies the sound and hints at exhalation. The sense likely began with vocalization and later extended to notions of permission or possibility, with related forms preserving the vocal or doubled structure.
- **examples:**
  - 我们可在这里休息(我們可在這裡休息)。
    - Wǒmen kě zài zhèlǐ xiūxi.
    - We may rest here.
  - 这个方案还可接受(這個方案還可接受)。
    - Zhège fāng'àn hái kě jiēshòu.
    - This plan is still acceptable.
  - 你这建议可行(你這建議可行)。
    - Nǐ zhè jiànyì kěxíng.
    - Your suggestion is feasible.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 可 → 口
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 可 → 丂
- **definition:** exhale; breath
- **pinyin:** kǎo
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** conflation
  - **description:** Conflation of several characters.
  - **interpretation:** Multiple early graphs with related shapes or meanings were merged into this single form. Because it is a conflation, it does not decompose cleanly into separate semantic and phonetic elements; its attested sense centers on exhalation and breath.
- **examples:**
  - 古人用丂表示呼气(古人用丂表示呼氣)。
    - Gǔrén yòng kǎo biǎoshì hūqì.
    - In antiquity, 丂 was used to denote exhalation.
  - 见字丂，可解作“气息”(見字丂，可解作「氣息」)。
    - Jiàn zì kǎo, kě jiě zuò “qìxī”.
    - When you see the graph 丂, it can be interpreted as “breath.”
  - 他爬山时丂短，得先休息(他爬山時丂短，得先休息)。
    - Tā páshān shí kǎo duǎn, dé xiān xiūxi.
    - He became short of breath while climbing and had to rest first.
### 奇怪 → 奇 → 可 → 丂 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 奇怪 → 怪
- **definition:** strange; odd; to blame; rather
- **pinyin:** guài
- **components:**
  - 心
    - xīn
    - heart; mind
  - 圣(聖)
    - shèng
    - sage; holy; sacred
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 忄 + phonetic: 圣
  - **interpretation:** Built with the 'heart' component to signal emotion or mental states, and the phonetic 圣 to suggest pronunciation. The blend conveys evaluations of things as unusual or odd and extends to the sense of assigning blame.
- **examples:**
  - 这件事很怪(這件事很怪)。
    - Zhè jiàn shì hěn guài.
    - This matter is very strange.
  - 别怪我来晚了(別怪我來晚了)。
    - Bié guài wǒ lái wǎn le.
    - Don't blame me for being late.
  - 今天天气怪冷的(今天天氣怪冷的)。
    - Jīntiān tiānqì guài lěng de.
    - It's rather cold today.
### 奇怪 → 怪 → 心
- **definition:** heart; mind
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – a heart, now highly stylized.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawing of the anatomical heart; its dots and curves abstracted into a compact, balanced form over time. Related side-forms like 忄 in compounds cue meanings tied to feelings, thought, or the mind.
- **examples:**
  - 我很关心你(我很關心你)。
    - Wǒ hěn guānxīn nǐ.
    - I care about you a lot.
  - 他一时分心，犯了错误(他一時分心，犯了錯誤)。
    - Tā yīshí fēnxīn, fàn le cuòwù.
    - He got distracted for a moment and made a mistake.
  - 放下心里的担忧，安心休息吧(放下心裡的擔憂，安心休息吧)。
    - Fàngxià xīnlǐ de dānyōu, ānxīn xiūxi ba.
    - Let go of the worries in your heart and rest.
### 奇怪 → 怪 → 圣(聖)
- **definition:** sage; holy; sacred
- **pinyin:** shèng
- **components:**
  - 耳
    - ěr
    - ear
  - 呈
    - chéng
    - to present; appear
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 耳 + phonetic: 呈
  - **interpretation:** 耳 supplies the ‘ear’ meaning, evoking keen listening as a mark of sagacity, while 呈 provides the sound. The simplified form 圣 is a later graphic borrowing and no longer preserves this semantic–phonetic structure.
  - **simplification:** Replaced the complex 聖 with the graphically simpler 圣, a formerly unorthodox variant that originally depicted a hand over earth meaning “to dig,” sacrificing the original ear+phonetic makeup for stroke reduction.
- **examples:**
  - 圣人受人尊敬(聖人受人尊敬)。
    - Shèngrén shòu rén zūnjìng.
    - Sages are respected.
  - 这座山被当地人视为圣地(這座山被當地人視為聖地)。
    - Zhè zuò shān bèi dāngdì rén shìwéi shèngdì.
    - This mountain is regarded as a sacred place.
  - 圣诞节快到了(聖誕節快到了)。
    - Shèngdànjié kuài dàole.
    - Christmas is coming soon.
### 奇怪 → 怪 → 圣(聖) → 耳
- **definition:** ear
- **pinyin:** ěr
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: an ear
  - **interpretation:** Sketches the side view of a human ear: the vertical stroke outlines the rim while shorter strokes suggest inner folds. Early forms were more curved and naturalistic; later writing regularized the lines but the image remains recognizable.
- **examples:**
  - 他耳朵疼(他耳朵疼)。
    - Tā ěrduo téng.
    - His ear hurts.
  - 戴上耳机(戴上耳機)，听音乐(聽音樂)。
    - Dài shàng ěrjī, tīng yīnyuè.
    - Put on headphones and listen to music.
  - 医生检查他的左耳(醫生檢查他的左耳)。
    - Yīshēng jiǎnchá tā de zuǒ ěr.
    - The doctor examined his left ear.
### 奇怪 → 怪 → 圣(聖) → 呈
- **definition:** to present; appear
- **pinyin:** chéng
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 口 + phonetic 𡈼 (not 壬 or 王)
  - **interpretation:** Mouth 口 provides the semantic field of speaking, presenting, or submitting, while 𡈼 supplies the sound value leading to chéng. The lower element is a distinct phonetic, not 壬 or 王, emphasizing its role in pronunciation rather than meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 请把报告呈交给主任(請把報告呈交給主任)。
    - Qǐng bǎ bàogào chéngjiāo gěi zhǔrèn.
    - Please present the report to the director.
  - 文件已呈报上级审批(文件已呈報上級審批)。
    - Wénjiàn yǐ chéngbào shàngjí shěnpī.
    - The document has been submitted for higher-level approval.
  - 天空呈现出淡粉色(天空呈現出淡粉色)。
    - Tiānkōng chéngxiàn chū dàn fěnsè.
    - The sky appears a light pink.
