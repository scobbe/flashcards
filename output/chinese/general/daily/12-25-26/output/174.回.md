## return; turn back
### huí
---
- **definition:** return; turn back
- **pinyin:** huí
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** rotation (originally a spiral)
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a spiral indicating a turning or circling motion; later regularized into a square enclosing another square, evoking going back within a boundary. This visual naturally extends to senses like returning, turning back, or circling to a previous point.
- **examples:**
  - 我马上回家(我馬上回家)。
    - Wǒ mǎshàng huí jiā.
    - I’m going home right away.
  - 他转了一圈又回到起点(他轉了一圈又回到起點)。
    - Tā zhuànle yì quān yòu huídào qǐdiǎn.
    - He made a turn and returned to the starting point.
  - 请把书还回去(請把書還回去)。
    - Qǐng bǎ shū huán huíqù.
    - Please return the book.
### 回 → 口
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
