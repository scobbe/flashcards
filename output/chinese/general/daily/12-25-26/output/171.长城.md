## Great Wall
### Chángchéng
---
- **definition:** Great Wall
- **pinyin:** Chángchéng
- **components:**
  - 长(長)
    - cháng
    - long; length; lasting; for a long time
  - 城
    - chéng
    - city; city wall; town; fortification
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 长(long) + 城(city wall) = long wall (Great Wall).
  - **interpretation:** Literally “long wall,” a proper noun for the Great Wall of China; the modifier 长 emphasizes the wall’s great length.
  - **simplification:** The character 長 was simplified to 长 in PRC reforms to reduce strokes, influenced by cursive forms; 城 remained unchanged. The compound itself was not otherwise altered.
- **examples:**
  - 我们明天去参观长城(我們明天去參觀長城)。
    - Wǒmen míngtiān qù cānguān Chángchéng.
    - We are going to visit the Great Wall tomorrow.
  - 长城蜿蜒穿过群山(長城蜿蜒穿過群山)。
    - Chángchéng wānyán chuānguò qúnshān.
    - The Great Wall winds through the mountains.
  - 长城是中国古代的防御工程(長城是中國古代的防禦工程)。
    - Chángchéng shì Zhōngguó gǔdài de fángyù gōngchéng.
    - The Great Wall is an ancient Chinese defensive project.
### 长城(長城) → 长(長)
- **definition:** long; length; lasting; for a long time
- **pinyin:** cháng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: an old man with long hair; simplified from 長 (ancient variant 镸).
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted an elder with long hair, linking physical length to the idea of being long or lasting. The streamlined simplified form 长 preserves the notion of extension in space or time.
  - **simplification:** Stroke reduction of 長 to 长, removing the hair-like details and standardizing the form for ease and speed of writing.
- **examples:**
  - 这条河很长(這條河很長)。
    - Zhè tiáo hé hěn cháng.
    - This river is very long.
  - 我们等了很长时间(我們等了很長時間)。
    - Wǒmen děng le hěn cháng shíjiān.
    - We waited for a long time.
  - 长期计划需要耐心(長期計劃需要耐心)。
    - Chángqī jìhuà xūyào nàixīn.
    - Long-term plans require patience.
### 长城(長城) → 城
- **definition:** city; city wall; town; fortification
- **pinyin:** chéng
- **components:**
  - 土
    - tǔ
    - earth; soil
  - 成
    - chéng
    - to become; accomplish
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 土 (“soil”) + phonetic 成 (OC *djeŋ); city walls were made of stamped earth.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the meaning of soil with the sound of 成 to depict an earthen-walled settlement. Evokes early cities fortified with packed earth, while 成 contributes the pronunciation chéng.
- **examples:**
  - 这座城很古老(這座城很古老)。
    - zhè zuò chéng hěn gǔlǎo.
    - This city is very old.
  - 城墙仍保存完好(城牆仍保存完好)。
    - chéngqiáng réng bǎocún wánhǎo.
    - The city wall is still well preserved.
  - 他们在城外修筑防御工事(他們在城外修築防禦工事)。
    - tāmen zài chéngwài xiūzhù fángyù gōngshì.
    - They are building fortifications outside the city.
### 长城(長城) → 城 → 土
- **definition:** earth; soil
- **pinyin:** tǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a lump of clay on the ground or on a potter’s wheel; the central dot was later stylized into a short horizontal stroke.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a clod of earth resting on the ground, later streamlined into a vertical line with top and bottom bars. The upper bar developed from an earlier dot to distinguish it from similar graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 这里的土很干(這裡的土很乾)。
    - Zhèlǐ de tǔ hěn gān.
    - The soil here is very dry.
  - 他把一把土撒在花盆里(他把一把土撒在花盆裡)。
    - Tā bǎ yì bǎ tǔ sǎ zài huāpén lǐ.
    - He sprinkled a handful of soil into the flowerpot.
  - 建房前要先检测土质(建房前要先檢測土質)。
    - Jiàn fáng qián yào xiān jiǎncè tǔzhì.
    - Before building a house, the soil quality must be tested.
### 长城(長城) → 城 → 成
- **definition:** to become; accomplish
- **pinyin:** chéng
- **components:**
  - 戊
    - wù
    - 5th heavenly stem; weapon/halberd
  - 丁
    - dīng
    - 4th heavenly stem; adult male; nail
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 戊 + phonetic: 丁 (OC *djeŋ; 丁 OC *rteːŋ, *teːŋ). Not 囗/口; original ‘city walls’ sense preserved in 城.
  - **interpretation:** 戊, depicting a weapon, contributes the protective/fortified semantic idea, while 丁 supplies the sound that evolved toward chéng. The original sense related to city walls later broadened to meanings like accomplish and become, with 城 retaining the older ‘walls/city’ meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 她努力多年，终于成了医生(她努力多年，終於成了醫生)。
    - Tā nǔlì duō nián, zhōngyú chéng le yīshēng.
    - After years of hard work, she finally became a doctor.
  - 这件事算是大功告成(這件事算是大功告成)。
    - Zhè jiàn shì suàn shì dàgōng gàochéng.
    - This matter can be considered a great success.
  - 他的想法成了现实(他的想法成了現實)。
    - Tā de xiǎngfǎ chéng le xiànshí.
    - His idea became reality.
### 长城(長城) → 城 → 成 → 戊
- **definition:** 5th heavenly stem; weapon/halberd
- **pinyin:** wù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a halberd
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a stylized polearm with a blade and shaft. Early forms resemble an actual weapon, which was later regularized into the compact modern strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 公历2018年是戊戌年(公曆2018年是戊戌年)。
    - Gōnglì èr líng yī bā nián shì Wùxū nián.
    - The year 2018 in the Gregorian calendar was the Wuxu year.
  - “戊”是天干的第五位(「戊」是天干的第五位)。
    - “Wù” shì tiāngān de dì wǔ wèi.
    - “Wù” is the fifth of the Heavenly Stems.
  - 古文中“戊”也象形为戈矛之兵器(古文中「戊」也象形為戈矛之兵器)。
    - Gǔwén zhōng “Wù” yě xiàngxíng wéi gēmáo zhī bīngqì.
    - In classical texts, “wù” also pictographically represents a halberd-like weapon.
### 长城(長城) → 城 → 成 → 丁
- **definition:** 4th heavenly stem; adult male; nail
- **pinyin:** dīng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形); different theories exist; see also 旦
  - **interpretation:** Often explained as the silhouette of a nail or spike, or a simple vertical post with a cap-like head. Its extremely simple form was later borrowed to label the fourth Heavenly Stem, and by administrative extension it came to denote an adult male for census/tax purposes.
- **examples:**
  - 甲乙丙丁是天干的前四位(甲乙丙丁是天干的前四位)。
    - Jiǎ Yǐ Bǐng Dīng shì tiāngān de qián sì wèi.
    - Jiǎ, Yǐ, Bǐng, Dīng are the first four of the Heavenly Stems.
  - 他出生在丁亥年(他出生在丁亥年)。
    - Tā chūshēng zài Dīnghài nián.
    - He was born in the year of Dīnghài.
  - 本县有三千丁(本縣有三千丁)。
    - Běn xiàn yǒu sān qiān dīng.
    - This county has three thousand adult males.
