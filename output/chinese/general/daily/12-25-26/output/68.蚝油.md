## oyster sauce
### háoyóu
---
- **definition:** oyster sauce
- **pinyin:** háoyóu
- **components:**
  - 蚝(蠔)
    - háo
    - oyster; oyster meat; edible bivalve; dried oyster
  - 油
    - yóu
    - oil; grease; petroleum; sauce
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 蚝(蠔) ‘oyster’ + 油 ‘oil/sauce’ = oyster sauce.
  - **interpretation:** Names a condiment made from oysters and seasonings, widely used to enhance umami in stir-fries and marinades. Often refers specifically to the bottled sauce in Chinese cooking.
  - **simplification:** 蠔 was simplified to 蚝 by replacing the phonetic component 豪 with the simpler 号 under the 虫 radical, reducing strokes; 油 remained unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢用蚝油(我喜歡用蠔油)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān yòng háoyóu.
    - I like to use oyster sauce.
  - 这道菜加一点蚝油更鲜(這道菜加一點蠔油更鮮)。
    - Zhè dào cài jiā yìdiǎn háoyóu gèng xiān.
    - This dish is tastier with a bit of oyster sauce.
  - 用蚝油炒青菜最方便(用蠔油炒青菜最方便)。
    - Yòng háoyóu chǎo qīngcài zuì fāngbiàn.
    - Using oyster sauce to stir-fry greens is very convenient.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔)
- **definition:** oyster; oyster meat; edible bivalve; dried oyster
- **pinyin:** háo
- **components:**
  - 虫(蟲)
    - chóng
    - insect; bug; worm
  - 豪
    - háo
    - grand; extravagant; heroic
  - 号(號)
    - hào
    - number; mark; to call
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 虫 + phonetic 豪 (OC *ɡaːw).
  - **interpretation:** 虫 marks it as a creature in the invertebrate realm, which in Chinese script extends to shellfish and similar animals. 豪 provides the sound háo; the simplified form uses 号 as a streamlined phonetic while preserving a similar pronunciation.
  - **simplification:** Right-side phonetic simplified from 豪/號 to 号 to reduce strokes while keeping the háo sound; this aligns with earlier variant usage (e.g., 𧐢) and 20th‑century standardization.
- **examples:**
  - 我爱吃生蚝(我愛吃生蠔)。
    - Wǒ ài chī shēngháo.
    - I love eating raw oysters.
  - 炒菜放点蚝油更鲜(炒菜放點蠔油更鮮)。
    - Chǎocài fàng diǎn háoyóu gèng xiān.
    - Adding a bit of oyster sauce makes stir-fries tastier.
  - 过年会买些蚝干煲汤(過年會買些蠔乾煲湯)。
    - Guònián huì mǎi xiē háogān bāo tāng.
    - We buy some dried oysters to make soup for the New Year.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔) → 虫(蟲)
- **definition:** insect; bug; worm
- **pinyin:** chóng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a snake; later borrowed for 蟲/虫 (worms, insects). Traditional 蟲: ideogrammic compound, triplication of 虫.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicting a snake, the graph was later repurposed to write ‘insect/bug/worm’, while the traditional form 蟲 triples 虫 to suggest many small crawlies. The original snake sense was reassigned to 虺.
  - **simplification:** Borrowing and stroke reduction: simplified Chinese replaced 蟲 with the older, simpler 虫 to reduce strokes and standardize usage; the snake meaning moved to 虺.
- **examples:**
  - 我怕虫子(我怕蟲子)。
    - Wǒ pà chóngzi.
    - I’m afraid of bugs.
  - 这只虫在叶子上爬(這隻蟲在葉子上爬)。
    - Zhè zhī chóng zài yèzi shàng pá.
    - This bug is crawling on a leaf.
  - 夏天蚊虫很多(夏天蚊蟲很多)。
    - Xiàtiān wénchóng hěn duō.
    - There are many mosquitoes and insects in summer.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔) → 豪

- **pinyin:** háo

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound

  - **interpretation:** High 高 supplies the sound háo, while 豕 ‘boar’ contributes a semantic cue linked to boar bristles and vigor. Over time the sense broadened from associations of strength and prominence to ideas of grandeur, extravagance, and heroic spirit.
- **examples:**

  - 他们出手大方(他們出手大方)，十分豪迈(十分豪邁)。
    - Tāmen chūshǒu dàfāng, shífēn háomài.
    - They are generous and very bold-spirited.

### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔) → 豪 → 高 → 口

- **pinyin:** kǒu

- **examples:**

    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔) → 豪 → 豕
- **definition:** pig; boar
- **pinyin:** shǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram (象形): a pig; compare 亥, 𢑓 ‘male pig’, and 豖 ‘castrated male pig’.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a pig in profile with head, body, and legs. Related forms differentiate types of pigs; in 㒸, the pig element serves as a phonetic.
- **examples:**
  - 古书中常以“豕”指代猪(古書中常以「豕」指代豬)。
    - Gǔshū zhōng cháng yǐ “shǐ” zhǐdài zhū.
    - In ancient texts, “豕” refers to a pig.
  - 在甲骨文里，豕的形状清晰可见(在甲骨文裡，豕的形狀清晰可見)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén lǐ, shǐ de xíngzhuàng qīngxī kějiàn.
    - In oracle bone inscriptions, the form of 豕 is clearly visible.
  - 成语“豕突狼奔”中的“豕”意为猪(成語「豕突狼奔」中的「豕」意為豬)。
    - Chéngyǔ “shǐ tū láng bēn” zhōng de “shǐ” yìwéi zhū.
    - In the idiom “豕突狼奔,” “豕” means pig.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔) → 号(號)
- **definition:** number; mark; to call
- **pinyin:** hào
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 丂
    - kǎo
    - exhaled air; a cry
  - 虎
    - hǔ
    - tiger
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound (号); phono-semantic compound (號)
  - **description:** 号: 口 “mouth” + 丂 “exhaled air; a tiger’s cry”. 號: semantic 虎 “tiger” + phonetic 号 (OC *ɦlaːws).
  - **interpretation:** Mouth plus a breath/cry mark evokes someone shouting, yielding senses like to cry out or call. The traditional form adds “tiger” as the semantic element for roaring while using 号 as the sound component, which later extended to meanings such as mark and number.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by adopting the phonetic component 号 as the full form and dropping the semantic 虎, reducing strokes while preserving pronunciation and core ‘call/cry’ sense.
- **examples:**
  - 我的房间号是305(我的房間號是305)。
    - Wǒ de fángjiān hào shì sān líng wǔ.
    - My room number is 305.
  - 门上有个红色的记号(門上有個紅色的記號)。
    - Mén shàng yǒu gè hóngsè de jìhào.
    - There is a red mark on the door.
  - 领队发出集合的号召(領隊發出集合的號召)。
    - Lǐngduì fāchū jíhé de hàozhào.
    - The team leader issued a call to assemble.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔) → 号(號) → 丂
- **definition:** exhaled air; a cry
- **pinyin:** kǎo
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; horizontal stroke
  - 丨
    - gǔn
    - vertical line; stroke
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** conflation
  - **description:** Conflation of several characters.
  - **interpretation:** Graphic elements from different early graphs were merged into a single form, so it does not fit neatly into the traditional liùshū categories. Meanings like “exhaled air” or “a cry” likely arose from overlapping semantic associations among the conflated sources.
- **examples:**
  - 他口中吐出一缕丂(他口中吐出一縷丂)。
    - Tā kǒu zhōng tǔchū yī lǚ kǎo.
    - He exhaled a wisp of breath.
  - 孩子受惊，忽发出一声丂(孩子受驚，忽發出一聲丂)。
    - Háizi shòu jīng, hū fāchū yī shēng kǎo.
    - Startled, the child suddenly let out a cry.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔) → 号(號) → 丂 → 一
- **definition:** one; horizontal stroke
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 蚝(蠔) → 号(號) → 丂 → 丨
- **definition:** vertical line; stroke
- **pinyin:** gǔn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a vertical wooden stick; appears in early 朕 (originally “to mend a boat”) and in 尹, where the stick symbolizes authority.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an upright staff, emphasizing straightness and verticality. Its use inside graphs like 朕 and 尹 shows it originally pictured a tool or staff and later generalized to the basic vertical stroke.
- **examples:**
  - 汉字的第二号部首是丨(漢字的第二號部首是丨)。
    - Hànzì de dì-èr hào bùshǒu shì gǔn.
    - The second Chinese radical is 丨.
  - 写字时，要把这一笔丨写直(寫字時，要把這一筆丨寫直)。
    - Xiězì shí, yào bǎ zhè yì bǐ gǔn xiě zhí.
    - When writing, this stroke 丨 should be written straight.
  - 他写了一笔丨(他寫了一筆丨)。
    - Tā xiě le yì bǐ gǔn.
    - He wrote a single vertical stroke.

### 蚝油(蠔油) → 油
- **definition:** oil; grease; petroleum; sauce
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 由
    - yóu
    - from; due to; reason; through
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 氵 (“water”) + phonetic: 由
  - **interpretation:** Combines the water radical to signal a liquid with 由 to suggest the sound yóu. The sense narrows to oily liquids and sauces, fitting the water-semantic category while borrowing pronunciation from 由.
- **examples:**
  - 这道菜油太多(這道菜油太多)。
    - Zhè dào cài yóu tài duō.
    - This dish is too greasy.
  - 我在加油站加油(我在加油站加油)。
    - Wǒ zài jiāyóuzhàn jiā yóu.
    - I refuel at the gas station.
  - 全球石油价格上涨(全球石油價格上漲)。
    - Quánqiú shíyóu jiàgé shàngzhǎng.
    - Global oil prices are rising.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 油 → 由
- **definition:** from; due to; reason; through
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic 十 + semantic 口 ("mouth"); a container with a needle on top to indicate the sound; compare the bottom of the original 缶. Alt. view: a helmet (less likely).
  - **interpretation:** Graph suggests an opening or container (口) with a needle-like element on top, where 十 provides the sound and 口 contributes the idea of an opening or vessel. The notion of an opening or passage aligns with uses meaning “from/by way of,” while the phonetic element guided its reading.
- **examples:**
  - 由这条路走得更快(由這條路走得更快)。
    - Yóu zhè tiáo lù zǒu de gèng kuài.

  - 事故是由刹车失灵引起的(事故是由剎車失靈引起的)。
    - Shìgù shì yóu shāchē shīlíng yǐnqǐ de.
    - The accident was caused by brake failure.
  - 这项决定由他来做(這項決定由他來做)。
    - Zhè xiàng juédìng yóu tā lái zuò.
    - This decision is to be made by him.
### 蚝油(蠔油) → 油 → 由 → 十
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phonetic loan (rebus)
  - **description:** Originally a vertical stroke later crossed by 一; possibly a pictogram of a needle, rebus-borrowed for the word ‘ten’.
  - **interpretation:** Early forms may have depicted a needle, and that form’s sound was repurposed to write the word for “ten.” The cross shape developed when a horizontal stroke was added to the original vertical line.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十个苹果(我有十個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí ge píngguǒ.
    - I have ten apples.
  - 现在是十点(現在是十點)。
    - Xiànzài shì shí diǎn.
    - It is ten o’clock now.
  - 我们十个人一起去(我們十個人一起去)。
    - Wǒmen shí ge rén yìqǐ qù.
    - Ten of us are going together.
