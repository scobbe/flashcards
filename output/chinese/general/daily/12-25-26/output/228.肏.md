### cào
---
- **definition:** fuck (vulgar)
- **pinyin:** cào
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 肉
    - ròu
    - flesh
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 會意: 入 “enter” + 肉 “flesh”.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the ideas of entering and flesh to depict sexual penetration, yielding the vulgar verbal meaning “to fuck.” This is a purely semantic (idea-combining) graph rather than a sound-based formation.
- **examples:**
  - 别在公共场合说“肏”这个粗话(別在公共場合說「肏」這個粗話)。
    - Bié zài gōnggòng chǎnghé shuō “cào” zhège cūhuà.
    - Don’t say the vulgar word “cào” in public.
  - 他生气时会骂“肏”，但同事觉得很冒犯(他生氣時會罵「肏」，但同事覺得很冒犯)。
    - Tā shēngqì shí huì mà “cào”, dàn tóngshì juéde hěn màofàn.
    - When he gets angry he curses “cào,” and his coworkers find it offensive.
  - 作为感叹词，“肏”常用来表达愤怒或震惊(作為感嘆詞，「肏」常用來表達憤怒或震驚)。
    - Zuòwéi gǎntàncí, “cào” cháng yònglái biǎodá fènnù huò zhènjīng.
    - As an interjection, “cào” is often used to express anger or shock.
### 肏 → 入
- **definition:** enter
- **pinyin:** rù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram (指事) — an arrowhead indicating “to enter”; compare 人.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicts a pointed wedge or arrowhead moving inward, conveying the action of going in. Its form resembles 人 but with a tighter, inward-tapering shape that emphasizes entry rather than a person.
- **examples:**
  - 禁止入内(禁止入內)。
    - jìnzhǐ rùnèi.
    - No entry.
  - 他考入北京大学(他考入北京大學)。
    - tā kǎorù běijīng dàxué.
    - He was admitted to Peking University.
  - 请入座(請入座)。
    - qǐng rùzuò.
    - Please be seated.
### 肏 → 肉
- **definition:** flesh
- **pinyin:** ròu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: ribs/section of meat; stylized as 月 when beside a phonetic, and as 𱼀 or 夕 inside some characters.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the cross-section of flesh with ribs or a slice of meat. In compounds it often appears as a component written like 月, or reduced to 𱼀/夕, which can be mistaken for the moon component but actually signals ‘meat/flesh.’
- **examples:**
  - 我不吃猪肉(我不吃豬肉)。
    - Wǒ bù chī zhūròu.
    - I don't eat pork.
  - 这道菜肉很多，很鲜嫩(這道菜肉很多，很鮮嫩)。
    - Zhè dào cài ròu hěn duō, hěn xiānnèn.
    - This dish has a lot of meat; it's very tender.
  - 受伤后，他手上的肉裂开了(受傷後，他手上的肉裂開了)。
    - Shòushāng hòu, tā shǒushang de ròu liè kāi le.
    - After being injured, the flesh on his hand split open.
