## spoiled child
### xiǎohuángdì
---
- **definition:** spoiled child
- **pinyin:** xiǎohuángdì
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small; little; young; minor
  - 皇
    - huáng
    - imperial; royal; sovereign; august
  - 帝
    - dì
    - emperor; sovereign; deity; ruler
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小 (little) + 皇帝 (emperor) = indulged child treated like royalty.
  - **interpretation:** Metaphor likening an overindulged child—often an only child—to a “little emperor,” especially noted in one‑child‑policy-era families. Conveys pampering, entitlement, and domineering behavior at home.
- **examples:**
  - 很多家庭把独生子女当成小皇帝(很多家庭把獨生子女當成小皇帝)。
    - Hěn duō jiātíng bǎ dúshēng zǐnǚ dāngchéng xiǎo huángdì.
    - Many families treat their only child as a little emperor.
  - 他被大人宠坏了(他被大人寵壞了)，行为像个小皇帝(行為像個小皇帝)。
    - Tā bèi dàrén chǒng huài le, xíngwéi xiàng gè xiǎo huángdì.
    - He’s been spoiled by adults and behaves like a little emperor.
  - 老师担心“家里的小皇帝”在学校难以适应(老師擔心「家裡的小皇帝」在學校難以適應)。
    - Lǎoshī dānxīn “jiālǐ de xiǎo huángdì” zài xuéxiào nányǐ shìyìng.
    - The teacher worries that the ‘little emperor’ at home will struggle to adapt at school.
### 小皇帝 → 小
- **definition:** small; little; young; minor
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: three small marks/dots representing smallness; later the dots elongated; Shuowen misread it as 会意: 八 + 丨.
  - **interpretation:** Three short strokes portray a tiny object or the idea of smallness. As the script evolved, the dots were drawn as short lines, which led to the later but incorrect analysis of 八 plus 丨.
- **examples:**
  - 这个苹果很小(這個蘋果很小)。
    - Zhège píngguǒ hěn xiǎo.
    - This apple is very small.
  - 他把字写得太小(他把字寫得太小)。
    - Tā bǎ zì xiě de tài xiǎo.
    - He wrote the characters too small.
  - 小问题也要重视(小問題也要重視)。
    - Xiǎo wèntí yě yào zhòngshì.
    - Small problems should also be taken seriously.
