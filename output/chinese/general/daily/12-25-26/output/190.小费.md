## 小费(小費)
---
## tip; gratuity
### xiǎofèi
---
- **definition:** tip; gratuity
- **pinyin:** xiǎofèi
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small;little;minor;young
  - 费(費)
    - fèi
    - expense;fee;to spend;costly
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 小 (small) + 费 (expense/fee) = a small fee given as a tip.
  - **interpretation:** Refers to a modest additional payment given to service staff as a gratuity. Emphasizes a small expense beyond the main bill.
  - **simplification:** Traditional 費 was simplified to 费 by replacing 貝 with 贝 and merging strokes around the phonetic 弗 to reduce complexity; 小 is unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 在餐厅用餐后，我们给服务员小费(在餐廳用餐後，我們給服務員小費)。
    - Zài cāntīng yòngcān hòu, wǒmen gěi fúwùyuán xiǎofèi.
    - After dining at the restaurant, we give the waiter a tip.
  - 在一些国家，给小费是习惯(在一些國家，給小費是習慣)。
    - Zài yīxiē guójiā, gěi xiǎofèi shì xíguàn.
    - In some countries, tipping is customary.
  - 这家餐厅自动收取服务费，不必另付小费(這家餐廳自動收取服務費，不必另付小費)。
    - Zhè jiā cāntīng zìdòng shōuqǔ fúwùfèi, búbì lìng fù xiǎofèi.
    - This restaurant automatically charges a service fee, so you don't need to pay an additional tip.
### 小费(小費) → 小
- **definition:** small;little;minor;young
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: three small marks/dots representing smallness; later the dots elongated; Shuowen misread it as 会意: 八 + 丨.
  - **interpretation:** Three short strokes portray a tiny object or the idea of smallness. As the script evolved, the dots were drawn as short lines, which led to the later but incorrect analysis of 八 plus 丨.
- **examples:**
  - 这个苹果很小(這個蘋果很小)。
    - Zhège píngguǒ hěn xiǎo.
    - This apple is very small.
  - 他把字写得太小(他把字寫得太小)。
    - Tā bǎ zì xiě de tài xiǎo.
    - He wrote the characters too small.
  - 小问题也要重视(小問題也要重視)。
    - Xiǎo wèntí yě yào zhòngshì.
    - Small problems should also be taken seriously.
### 小费(小費) → 费(費)
- **definition:** expense;fee;to spend;costly
- **pinyin:** fèi
- **components:**
  - 弗
    - fú
    - not
  - 贝(貝)
    - bèi
    - cowry; money
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 貝 (‘cowry; money’) + phonetic: 弗
  - **interpretation:** 貝 points to the semantic field of money and valuables, fitting meanings like fees and expenses. 弗 supplies the sound, and over time the graph came to cover spending and the idea of being costly.
  - **simplification:** Simplified to 费 by streamlining strokes, effectively retaining the phonetic 弗 and using the simplified 貝 component (贝) to reduce complexity.
- **examples:**
  - 这次报销的费用太高(這次報銷的費用太高)。
    - Zhè cì bàoxiāo de fèiyòng tài gāo.
    - The reimbursable expenses this time are too high.
  - 学校每学期都要交学费(學校每學期都要交學費)。
    - Xuéxiào měi xuéqī dōu yào jiāo xuéfèi.
    - The school requires tuition to be paid every semester.
  - 这台空调很费电，少开一会儿吧(這臺空調很費電，少開一會兒吧)。
    - Zhè tái kòngtiáo hěn fèidiàn, shǎo kāi yíhuìr ba.
    - This air conditioner uses a lot of electricity; let’s run it a bit less.
### 小费(小費) → 费(費) → 弗
- **definition:** not
- **pinyin:** fú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): rope wrapped around two pieces of wood to keep them straight; later borrowed phonetically for “no”. The derivative 拂 stands for the original word.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two sticks bound by a cord to keep them straight. Its common negative meaning arose from a phonetic loan, unrelated to the original image. The concrete sense later shifted to the character 拂.
- **examples:**
  - 他对命令弗从(他對命令弗從)。
    - tā duì mìnglìng fú cóng.
    - He did not comply with the order.
  - 吾弗知其故(吾弗知其故)。
    - wú fú zhī qí gù.
    - I do not know the reason.
  - 医者仁心(醫者仁心)，弗可欺(弗可欺)。
    - yī zhě rén xīn, fú kě qī.
    - Physicians are benevolent; they must not be deceived.
### 小费(小費) → 费(費) → 贝(貝)
- **definition:** cowry; money
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – cowrie used as currency in ancient China; simplified form of 貝 in cursive script.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a cowry shell, an object that functioned as early money, which motivates the senses “cowry; money.” In the simplified set, its strokes were streamlined by adopting a cursive-style form of 貝.
  - **simplification:** Adopted the cursive-script variant of 貝, reducing strokes and complexity.
- **examples:**
  - 古代用贝(貝)当作货币(貨幣)。
    - Gǔdài yòng bèi dāngzuò huòbì.
    - In ancient times, cowries were used as currency.
  - 海滩(海灘)上有很多贝(貝)。
    - Hǎitān shang yǒu hěn duō bèi.
    - There are many shells on the beach.
  - 这串项链(這串項鍊)是用贝(貝)做的。
    - Zhè chuàn xiàngliàn shì yòng bèi zuò de.
    - This necklace is made of shells.
