## takeout
### wàimài
---
- **definition:** takeout
- **pinyin:** wàimài
- **components:**
  - 外
    - wài
    - outside; external; foreign; other
  - 卖(賣)
    - mài
    - sell; betray; show off; peddle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 外(outside) + 卖(sell) = sell to the outside; takeout/delivery.
  - **interpretation:** Indicates goods—especially meals—sold for off‑premise consumption or delivery. In modern Mandarin, commonly refers to restaurant takeout and app-based delivery.
  - **simplification:** Only 賣→卖 changed: the 貝 component was dropped to reduce strokes, following the general simplification of 貝-series characters, so 外賣 → 外卖.
- **examples:**
  - 我们点了外卖(我們點了外賣)。
    - Wǒmen diǎnle wàimài.
    - We ordered takeout.
  - 这家店只做外卖(這家店只做外賣)，不提供堂食(不提供堂食)。
    - Zhè jiā diàn zhǐ zuò wàimài, bù tígōng tángshí.
    - This shop only does takeout and doesn’t offer dine-in.
  - 外卖小哥已经到楼下(外賣小哥已經到樓下)，请下去取餐(請下去取餐)。
    - Wàimài xiǎogē yǐjīng dào lóuxià, qǐng xiàqù qǔ cān.
    - The delivery rider has arrived downstairs; please go down to pick up the food.
### 外卖 → 外
- **definition:** outside; external; foreign; other
- **pinyin:** wài
- **components:**
  - 卜
    - bǔ
    - to divine; divination
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon; month
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 卜 + phonetic: 月 (OC *ŋod).
  - **interpretation:** 卜 contributes the semantic idea (originally associated with divination), while 月 provides the sound, aligning with the Old Chinese reconstruction. The semantic link to “outside” is not transparent in the phono-semantic analysis; some scholars propose an alternative ideogrammic reading involving the moon’s partial phases, but the mainstream view treats 月 here as phonetic.
- **examples:**
  - 我们对外贸易增长很快(我們對外貿易增長很快)。
    - Wǒmen duìwài màoyì zēngzhǎng hěn kuài.
    - Our foreign trade is growing very quickly.
  - 他住在城外的一个小村子(他住在城外的一個小村子)。
    - Tā zhù zài chéngwài de yí gè xiǎo cūnzi.
    - He lives in a small village outside the city.
  - 这家餐厅接待很多外国游客(這家餐廳接待很多外國遊客)。
    - Zhè jiā cāntīng jiēdài hěn duō wàiguó yóukè.
    - This restaurant serves many foreign tourists.
### 外卖 → 外 → 卜
- **definition:** to divine; divination
- **pinyin:** bǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram (象形): crack in an oracle bone used for divination.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the branching crack made on turtle shell or bone during pyromancy, whose patterns were read as omens. The simple vertical line with a side notch mirrors the appearance of such fissures.
- **examples:**
  - 我们请他卜一卦(我們請他卜一卦)。
    - Wǒmen qǐng tā bǔ yī guà.
    - We asked him to divine a hexagram.
  - 他会卜吉凶(他會卜吉凶)。
    - Tā huì bǔ jíxiōng.
    - He can divine whether things will be auspicious or inauspicious.
  - 用龟甲卜问前途(用龜甲卜問前途)。
    - Yòng guījiǎ bǔwèn qiántú.
    - They use turtle shells to divine the future.
### 外卖 → 外 → 月
- **definition:** moon; month
- **pinyin:** yuè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – pictographic representation of a crescent moon; related to 夕 (xī) and once interchangeable, but unrelated to 多.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a crescent moon, which naturally extended to mean a calendar month due to the lunar cycle. Historically confused with 夕 in some contexts, but their functions diverged, and the resemblance to elements in 多 is coincidental.
- **examples:**
  - 今晚的月亮很圆(今晚的月亮很圓)。
    - jīn wǎn de yuèliàng hěn yuán.
    - The moon is very round tonight.
  - 每个月我们开一次会(每個月我們開一次會)。
    - měi gè yuè wǒmen kāi yí cì huì.
    - We hold a meeting once every month.
  - 下个月我想去旅行(下個月我想去旅行)。
    - xià gè yuè wǒ xiǎng qù lǚxíng.
    - I want to travel next month.
### 外卖 → 卖(賣)
- **definition:** sell; betray; show off; peddle
- **pinyin:** mài
- **components:**
  - 士
    - shì
    - scholar; gentleman; soldier
  - 买(買)
    - mǎi
    - buy; purchase
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound; phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 出 (“go out”) + phonetic 買 (“buy”); later 出 reduced to 士; simplified from 賣 (買 → 买).
  - **interpretation:** Combines the 'go out' idea (出) with the sound from 'buy' (買), evoking goods taken out to be sold. Over time the top component was reduced to 士, and the simplified form replaces 買 with 买 while keeping the same pronunciation and sense.
  - **simplification:** Replaced 買 with its simplified form 买 to reduce strokes; the top component remained as 士. Overall simplified from 賣.
- **examples:**
  - 我在市场卖水果(我在市場賣水果)。
    - Wǒ zài shìchǎng mài shuǐguǒ.
    - I sell fruit at the market.
  - 他为了利益出卖朋友(他為了利益出賣朋友)。
    - Tā wèile lìyì chūmài péngyǒu.
    - He sold out his friend for profit.
  - 她喜欢卖弄学问(她喜歡賣弄學問)。
    - Tā xǐhuan màinong xuéwèn.
    - She likes to show off her learning.
### 外卖 → 卖(賣) → 士
- **definition:** scholar; gentleman; soldier
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: war/ritual axe; later ‘soldier’ → ‘officer/intellectual’; related to 王, 圭, 金.
  - **interpretation:** Earliest forms depicted an axe, a symbol of martial power and authority, which naturally extended to mean a soldier and then an officer or learned gentleman. Connections to 王, 圭, and 金 reflect the ritual or jade/metal axe lineage in related graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一名士兵(他是一名士兵)。
    - Tā shì yì míng shìbīng.
    - He is a soldier.
  - 他是个绅士(他是個紳士)。
    - Tā shì ge shēnshì.
    - He is a gentleman.
  - 古代的士人重视礼仪(古代的士人重視禮儀)。
    - Gǔdài de shìrén zhòngshì lǐyí.
    - In ancient times, scholar-gentry valued ritual propriety.
### 外卖 → 卖(賣) → 买(買)
- **definition:** buy; purchase
- **pinyin:** mǎi
- **components:**
  - 网(網)
    - wǎng
    - net
  - 贝(貝)
    - bèi
    - cowrie; money
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (會意 / 会意)
  - **description:** semantic: 网 ‘net’ + semantic: 貝 ‘money cowrie’. Original form: 𧵽.
  - **interpretation:** Combining a net with a cowrie shell evokes catching or obtaining valuables, a visual metaphor for acquiring goods. By depicting the tools and object of value together, the graph conveys the act of procurement that later specialized to ‘buy’.
  - **simplification:** Stroke reduction: the top ‘网’ component was dropped and the shell element was compressed into the 4-stroke form 买, following the general simplification trend of 貝-based graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 我想买这个(我想買這個)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng mǎi zhège.
    - I want to buy this.
  - 他们买了很多书(他們買了很多書)。
    - Tāmen mǎi le hěn duō shū.
    - They bought many books.
  - 我们去市场买菜吧(我們去市場買菜吧)。
    - Wǒmen qù shìchǎng mǎi cài ba.
    - Let’s go to the market to buy groceries.
### 外卖 → 卖(賣) → 买(買) → 网(網)
- **definition:** net
- **pinyin:** wǎng
- **components:**
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
  - 罔
    - wǎng
    - not; without; to deceive
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a net; top web stylized as 罒 (unrelated to 目). Trad: phono-semantic—semantic 糸 (“rope/silk”) + phonetic 罔.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a woven mesh, with the lattice of cords suggested by the frame-like 罒 at the top, which is not the eye component. The traditional form augments the meaning with the silk/thread element and borrows 罔 for sound.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by adopting an ancient pictographic form, dropping the 糸 semantic and the phonetic 罔 to reduce strokes while preserving the net imagery.
- **examples:**
  - 他把渔网撒向水面(他把漁網撒向水面)。
    - Tā bǎ yúwǎng sǎ xiàng shuǐmiàn.
    - He cast the fishing net onto the water’s surface.
  - 这张网很结实，可以捕捉很多鱼(這張網很結實，可以捕捉很多魚)。
    - Zhè zhāng wǎng hěn jiéshi, kěyǐ bǔzhuō hěn duō yú.
    - This net is sturdy and can catch many fish.
  - 晚上我上网查资料(晚上我上網查資料)。
    - Wǎnshang wǒ shàng wǎng chá zīliào.
    - At night I go online to look up information.
### 外卖 → 卖(賣) → 买(買) → 网(網) → 糸
- **definition:** silk; thread
- **pinyin:** mì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** a skein of silk.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bundle of silk threads tied in the middle, with strands hanging above and below. The stylized sketch highlights twisted filaments, which later served as the standard radical for thread- and textile-related characters.
  - **simplification:** In Simplified Chinese the silk radical was streamlined to 纟 to reduce strokes and speed writing; 糸 is rarely used independently, but 纟 replaces it as a left-side component.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan sīchóu.
    - I like silk fabrics.
  - 这件衣服是用蚕丝做的(這件衣服是用蠶絲做的)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfu shì yòng cánsī zuò de.
    - This piece of clothing is made from silkworm silk.
  - 丝线很细(絲線很細)。
    - Sīxiàn hěn xì.
    - Silk thread is very thin.
### 外卖 → 卖(賣) → 买(買) → 网(網) → 罔
- **definition:** not; without; to deceive
- **pinyin:** wǎng
- **components:**
  - 网(網)
    - wǎng
    - net; web
  - 亡
    - wáng
    - perish; lose
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 网 “net” + phonetic: 亡
  - **interpretation:** The net component points to the idea of covering or ensnaring, which aligns with meanings like obscuring or misleading. The phonetic 亡 contributes the sound (wǎng/wáng) and resonates with senses of loss or lack, extending to uses meaning “not; without” and “to deceive.”
- **examples:**
  - 他罔顾事实，只为自保(他罔顧事實，只為自保)。
    - Tā wǎnggù shìshí, zhǐ wèi zìbǎo.
    - He disregards the facts just to protect himself.
  - 他以假消息欺罔群众(他以假消息欺罔群眾)。
    - Tā yǐ jiǎ xiāoxi qīwǎng qúnzhòng.
    - He deceives the public with fake news.
  - 此事罔有根据(此事罔有根據)。
    - Cǐ shì wǎng yǒu gēnjù.
    - This matter has no basis.
### 外卖 → 卖(賣) → 买(買) → 网(網) → 罔 → 亡
- **definition:** perish; lose
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram (指事)
  - **description:** a line on 刀 – cutting edge of a knife; the original form of 芒 (OC *maːŋ, *maŋ, “edge of a knife”).
  - **interpretation:** Originally marked the cutting edge on a knife, functioning as a simple indicator rather than a depiction. Over time this indicator became a standalone graph and later contributed to 芒, while meanings like “perish; lose” arose through semantic extension/borrowing into broader usage.
- **examples:**
  - 战败后，这个王朝走向灭亡(戰敗後，這個王朝走向滅亡)。
    - Zhànbài hòu, zhège wángcháo zǒuxiàng mièwáng.
    - After the defeat, the dynasty moved toward ruin.
  - 他在乱世中四处逃亡(他在亂世中四處逃亡)。
    - Tā zài luànshì zhōng sìchù táowáng.
    - He fled everywhere during the chaotic times.
  - 亡者为大，家人谨守遗愿(亡者為大，家人謹守遺願)。
    - Wángzhě wéi dà, jiārén jǐnshǒu yíyuàn.
    - The deceased deserve the utmost respect, and the family carefully honors the last wishes.
### 外卖 → 卖(賣) → 买(買) → 贝(貝)
- **definition:** cowrie; money
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – cowrie used as currency in ancient China; simplified form of 貝 in cursive script.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a cowry shell, an object that functioned as early money, which motivates the senses “cowry; money.” In the simplified set, its strokes were streamlined by adopting a cursive-style form of 貝.
  - **simplification:** Adopted the cursive-script variant of 貝, reducing strokes and complexity.
- **examples:**
  - 古代用贝(貝)当作货币(貨幣)。
    - Gǔdài yòng bèi dāngzuò huòbì.
    - In ancient times, cowries were used as currency.
  - 海滩(海灘)上有很多贝(貝)。
    - Hǎitān shang yǒu hěn duō bèi.
    - There are many shells on the beach.
  - 这串项链(這串項鍊)是用贝(貝)做的。
    - Zhè chuàn xiàngliàn shì yòng bèi zuò de.
    - This necklace is made of shells.
