## breakup; change in relationship
### qíngbiàn
---
- **definition:** breakup; change in relationship
- **pinyin:** qíngbiàn
- **components:**
  - 情
    - qíng
    - feeling; emotion; affection; situation
  - 变(變)
    - biàn
    - change; transform; become; alter
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 情 (feelings; relationship) + 变 (change) = change in a relationship; breakup.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the domain of feelings/relationships (情) with change (变), indicating a shift—often negative—in a romantic bond. Commonly used as a euphemism for breakup, marital crisis, or infidelity.
  - **simplification:** 變 was simplified to 变 by adopting a long-used cursive/variant form to reduce strokes; 情 remained unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 他们的婚姻出现了情变(他們的婚姻出現了情變)。
    - Tāmen de hūnyīn chūxiàn le qíngbiàn.
    - Their marriage has undergone a relationship crisis.
  - 听说他经历了情变，最近心情很低落(聽說他經歷了情變，最近心情很低落)。
    - Tīngshuō tā jīnglì le qíngbiàn, zuìjìn xīnqíng hěn dīluò.
    - I heard he went through a breakup, and he’s been feeling very low lately.
  - 两人因小事发生情变(兩人因小事發生情變)。
    - Liǎng rén yīn xiǎoshì fāshēng qíngbiàn.
    - The two had a breakup over a small matter.
### 情变(情變) → 情
- **definition:** feeling; emotion; affection; situation
- **pinyin:** qíng
### 情变(情變) → 变(變)
- **definition:** change; transform; become; alter
- **pinyin:** biàn
- **components:**
  - 亦
    - yì
    - also; too
  - 又
    - yòu
    - again; right hand
  - 攴
    - pū
    - tap; strike
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 攴 + phonetic: 䜌
  - **interpretation:** Combines the action-related element 攴, hinting at striking or causing an effect, with 䜌 to indicate pronunciation. The composition suggests initiating an action that leads something to become different.
  - **simplification:** Mainland simplification removed the right-side 攴 and compressed the complex phonetic 䜌, streamlining the form into 变 (top 亦 + bottom 又) to reduce strokes while keeping a distinctive outline.
- **examples:**
  - 我们需要改变计划(我們需要改變計劃)。
    - Wǒmen xūyào gǎibiàn jìhuà.
    - We need to change the plan.
  - 天气突然变冷了(天氣突然變冷了)。
    - Tiānqì tūrán biàn lěng le.
    - The weather suddenly turned cold.
  - 他几个月内变成了教练(他幾個月內變成了教練)。
    - Tā jǐ gè yuè nèi biànchéng le jiàoliàn.
    - He became a coach within a few months.
### 情变(情變) → 变(變) → 亦
- **definition:** also; too
- **pinyin:** yì
- **components:**
  - 大
    - dà
    - big
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Human figure (大) with emphasis marks at the armpits; originally 腋 ‘armpit’, later phonetic loan for 亦 ‘also’.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person with marks highlighting the armpits, explaining the original meaning ‘armpit’ (腋). Because it matched the sound, the same graph was borrowed to write the function word meaning ‘also’.
- **examples:**
  - 我亦喜欢古文(我亦喜歡古文)。
    - Wǒ yì xǐhuan gǔwén.
    - I also like classical Chinese.
  - 他亦参与讨论(他亦參與討論)。
    - Tā yì cānyù tǎolùn.
    - He also participates in the discussion.
  - 今日亦可出发(今日亦可出發)。
    - Jīnrì yì kě chūfā.
    - Departing today is also acceptable.
### 情变(情變) → 变(變) → 亦 → 大
- **definition:** big
- **pinyin:** dà
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a person facing forward; original form of 夫, later phonetically borrowed for “big”.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicts a human figure viewed from the front, not necessarily with exaggerated outstretched arms. The graph was later borrowed to write the word pronounced dà ‘big’, while 夫 specialized for ‘man’. The modern shape still evokes a torso with two legs and an arm stroke.
- **examples:**
  - 这个房间很大(這個房間很大)。
    - Zhège fángjiān hěn dà.
    - This room is very big.
  - 他是我们家最大的孩子(他是我們家最大的孩子)。
    - Tā shì wǒmen jiā zuì dà de háizi.
    - He is the eldest child in our family.
  - 大学开学了(大學開學了)。
    - Dàxué kāixué le.
    - The university term has begun.
### 情变(情變) → 变(變) → 又
- **definition:** again; right hand
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 情变(情變) → 变(變) → 攴
- **definition:** tap; strike
- **pinyin:** pū
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - again; right hand (as component)
  - 卜
    - bǔ
    - to divine
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Hand 又 holding a tool (later stylized to resemble phonetic 卜), depicting “to hit”.
  - **interpretation:** Shows a right hand grasping an implement used to strike, capturing the action of tapping or hitting. Later stylization made the implement look like 卜, a graphic accident rather than a link to divination or to characters that only look similar.
- **examples:**
  - 古籍中偶见攴(古籍中偶見攴)，表示用棍轻敲(表示用棍輕敲)。
    - Gǔjí zhōng ǒu jiàn pū, biǎoshì yòng gùn qīng qiāo.
    - In old texts, one occasionally sees 攴, meaning to tap lightly with a stick.
  - 这个偏旁叫攴字旁(這個偏旁叫攴字旁)，多与打、教等义有关(多與打、教等義有關)。
    - Zhège piānpáng jiào pū zìpáng, duō yǔ dǎ, jiào děng yì yǒuguān.
    - This side component is called the ‘攴’ radical and often relates to meanings like hitting or teaching.
### 情变(情變) → 变(變) → 攴 → 卜
- **definition:** to divine
- **pinyin:** bǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram (象形): crack in an oracle bone used for divination.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the branching crack made on turtle shell or bone during pyromancy, whose patterns were read as omens. The simple vertical line with a side notch mirrors the appearance of such fissures.
- **examples:**
  - 我们请他卜一卦(我們請他卜一卦)。
    - Wǒmen qǐng tā bǔ yī guà.
    - We asked him to divine a hexagram.
  - 他会卜吉凶(他會卜吉凶)。
    - Tā huì bǔ jíxiōng.
    - He can divine whether things will be auspicious or inauspicious.
  - 用龟甲卜问前途(用龜甲卜問前途)。
    - Yòng guījiǎ bǔwèn qiántú.
    - They use turtle shells to divine the future.
