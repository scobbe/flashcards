## overseas Chinese
### huáqiáo
---
- **definition:** overseas Chinese
- **pinyin:** huáqiáo
- **components:**
  - 华(華)
    - huá
    - Chinese; China; splendid; flowery
  - 侨(僑)
    - qiáo
    - sojourn; reside abroad; temporary; overseas (in compounds)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 华(華 ‘Chinese; splendid’) + 侨(僑 ‘sojourn; reside abroad’) = overseas Chinese.
  - **interpretation:** Refers to Chinese nationals or ethnic Chinese residing outside China, often with continuing ties to the homeland. In stricter usage, it denotes citizens living abroad, contrasted with 华人 for ethnic Chinese who have taken foreign nationality.
  - **simplification:** 華 → 华 by adopting an older variant/cursive form with fewer strokes; 僑 → 侨 by simplifying the phonetic 喬 to 乔 under the person radical 亻 to reduce strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 他说自己是华侨(他說自己是華僑)，在国外工作多年(在國外工作多年)。
    - Tā shuō zìjǐ shì huáqiáo, zài guówài gōngzuò duō nián.
    - He says he is an overseas Chinese and has worked abroad for many years.
  - 许多华侨在春节回乡探亲(許多華僑在春節回鄉探親)。
    - Xǔduō huáqiáo zài Chūnjié huíxiāng tànqīn.
    - Many overseas Chinese return home to visit relatives during the Spring Festival.
  - 政府设有华侨事务办公室(政府設有華僑事務辦公室)，服务海外同胞(服務海外同胞)。
    - Zhèngfǔ shè yǒu huáqiáo shìwù bàngōngshì, fúwù hǎiwài tóngbāo.
    - The government has an Overseas Chinese affairs office to serve compatriots overseas.
### 华侨(華僑) → 华(華)
- **definition:** Chinese; China; splendid; flowery
- **pinyin:** huá
- **components:**
  - 化
    - huà
    - to transform; change
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten; cross
  - 于
    - yú
    - in; at; from
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic: 化 + semantic: 十 (“stalk and leaves”). Traditional 華 was originally a pictogram of a flowering plant, later also analyzed as phono-semantic with 于 (phonetic) + 艸 (semantic).
  - **interpretation:** In the simplified form, 化 supplies the sound huá, while 十 preserves a vestige of the plant imagery inherited from 華. The traditional form depicted a flower on a stalk and later gained a phonetic 于 with the plant marker 艸, linking the senses of ‘splendid/flowery’ and ‘Chinese.’
  - **simplification:** Top floral component of 華 was replaced by the phonetic 化 to reduce strokes and emphasize pronunciation; 华 was introduced in the 1956 PRC simplification and had no prior attested form.
- **examples:**
  - 我为中华文化自豪(我為中華文化自豪)。
    - Wǒ wèi Zhōnghuá wénhuà zìháo.
    - I am proud of Chinese culture.
  - 他写了一篇华丽的文章(他寫了一篇華麗的文章)。
    - Tā xiěle yì piān huálì de wénzhāng.
    - He wrote a splendid article.
  - 许多外企在华投资(許多外企在華投資)。
    - Xǔduō wàiqǐ zài Huá tóuzī.
    - Many foreign companies invest in China.
### 华侨(華僑) → 华(華) → 化
- **definition:** to transform; change
- **pinyin:** huà
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Ideogrammic: two 人—upright 亻 + upside-down 𠤎—indicating reversal/change; Shuowen: 𠤎 as phonetic.
  - **interpretation:** One person is upright while the other is inverted, visually expressing a flip or transformation in state. Some early scholars read 𠤎 as giving a hint to the sound, but the graphic idea centers on the notion of change.
- **examples:**
  - 水受热会汽化(水受熱會汽化)。
    - Shuǐ shòurè huì qìhuà.
    - Water vaporizes when heated.
  - 他下定决心要自我变化(他下定決心要自我變化)。
    - Tā xiàdìng juéxīn yào zìwǒ biànhuà.
    - He resolved to change himself.
  - 政策使农村快速现代化(政策使農村快速現代化)。
    - Zhèngcè shǐ nóngcūn kuàisù xiàndàihuà.
    - The policy is rapidly modernizing the countryside.
### 华侨(華僑) → 华(華) → 化 → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 华侨(華僑) → 华(華) → 十
- **definition:** ten; cross
- **pinyin:** shí
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phonetic loan (rebus); originally pictogram (uncertain)
  - **description:** Borrowed phonetically for “ten”; possibly a pictogram of a needle (later 針), with the form evolving from a vertical stroke plus added 一.
  - **interpretation:** Earliest forms were just a vertical line, to which a horizontal stroke was later added, creating the cross we recognize today. Many scholars think this cross once depicted a needle and was later repurposed as a phonetic loan for the word ‘ten’.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(我有十本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 现在是十点(現在是十點)。
    - Xiànzài shì shí diǎn.
    - It is ten o'clock.
  - 今天我十分高兴(今天我十分高興)。
    - Jīntiān wǒ shí fēn gāoxìng.
    - Today I’m very happy.
### 华侨(華僑) → 华(華) → 十 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.

### 华侨(華僑) → 侨(僑)
- **definition:** sojourn; reside abroad; temporary; overseas (in compounds)
- **pinyin:** qiáo
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 乔(喬)
    - qiáo
    - tall; lofty
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 亻 + phonetic: 喬 → 乔 (simplified).
  - **interpretation:** The person component 亻 signals a meaning related to people or human affairs. 喬/乔 supplies the pronunciation qiáo and, while mainly phonetic here, also evokes an elevated or shifted position, fitting senses like residing abroad or being away from one’s native place.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by replacing the phonetic 喬 with its simplified form 乔 to reduce strokes while preserving pronunciation and meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他是第三代华侨(他是第三代華僑)。
    - Tā shì dì-sān dài Huáqiáo.
    - He is a third-generation overseas Chinese.
  - 她的父母长期侨居海外(她的父母長期僑居海外)。
    - Tā de fùmǔ chángqī qiáojū hǎiwài.
    - Her parents have long resided overseas.
  - 政府为归国侨胞提供服务(政府為歸國僑胞提供服務)。
    - Zhèngfǔ wèi guīguó qiáobāo tígōng fúwù.
    - The government provides services for returned overseas compatriots.
### 华侨(華僑) → 侨(僑) → 乔(喬)
- **definition:** tall; lofty
- **pinyin:** qiáo
- **components:**
  - 高
    - gāo
    - high; tall
  - 夭
    - yāo
    - tender; young; to die young
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram (指事), differentiated form (分化字)
  - **description:** Differentiated form of 高 (“high, tall”), marked with an added element—variously a stroke, 屮, 止, 力, 九, or 夭; later Shuowen misread the 夭 form and treated it as phono-semantic.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a marked variant of 高 used to indicate the idea of height/loftiness while distinguishing it graphically with small markers. The 夭-shaped variant became standard in later scripts, which led some scholars to mistakenly analyze 夭 as a semantic component and reclassify it.
  - **simplification:** Simplified to 乔 by generalizing the later 夭-based outline and removing the complex 高 framework, reducing strokes and regularizing the shape in the PRC simplification scheme.
- **examples:**
  - 庭院里种着几株乔木(庭院裡種著幾株喬木)。
    - Tíngyuàn lǐ zhòng zhe jǐ zhū qiáomù.
    - Several tall trees are planted in the courtyard.
  - 他搬新家，亲友前来道贺，祝他乔迁之喜(他搬新家，親友前來道賀，祝他喬遷之喜)。
    - Tā bān xīnjiā, qīnyǒu qiánlái dàohè, zhù tā qiáoqiān zhī xǐ.
    - He moved to a new home, and friends and relatives came to congratulate him, wishing him joy on his relocation.
  - 此地乔木参天(此地喬木參天)。
    - Cǐ dì qiáomù cāntiān.
    - The tall trees here reach to the sky.
### 华侨(華僑) → 侨(僑) → 乔(喬) → 高
- **definition:** high; tall
- **pinyin:** gāo
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a tall building (compare 京).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a multi-storied structure with roof and interior floors, evoking vertical height. The concrete image extended to abstract senses like tall/high and by extension eminent or expensive.
- **examples:**
  - 这座塔很高(這座塔很高)。
    - Zhè zuò tǎ hěn gāo.
    - This tower is very tall.
  - 物价太高(物價太高)，很多人负担不起(很多人負擔不起)。
    - Wùjià tài gāo, hěn duō rén fùdān bù qǐ.
    - Prices are too high, and many people can’t afford them.
  - 他地位很高(他地位很高)，但为人谦虚(但為人謙虛)。
    - Tā dìwèi hěn gāo, dàn wéi rén qiānxū.
    - His status is high, but he is humble.
### 华侨(華僑) → 侨(僑) → 乔(喬) → 高 → 口
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 华侨(華僑) → 侨(僑) → 乔(喬) → 夭
- **definition:** tender; young; to die young
- **pinyin:** yāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram (指事): a figure leaning forward, presumably running, hence energetic and young; cf. the top of 走.
  - **interpretation:** A stylized person bent forward suggests motion and vigor, evoking the sense of youthful energy and tender growth. By extension, it also came to denote an early or premature end, as in dying young or something being cut short.
- **examples:**
  - 他年纪轻轻就夭折(他年紀輕輕就夭折)。
    - Tā niánjì qīngqīng jiù yāozhé.
    - He died young.
  - 计划因资金不足而夭折(計劃因資金不足而夭折)。
    - Jìhuà yīn zījīn bùzú ér yāozhé.
    - The plan was aborted due to insufficient funds.
  - 桃之夭夭(桃之夭夭)，灼灼其华(灼灼其華)。
    - Táo zhī yāoyāo, zhuózhuó qí huá.
    - The peach tree is luxuriant in blossom, its flowers blazing bright.
