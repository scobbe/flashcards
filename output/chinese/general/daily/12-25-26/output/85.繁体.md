## traditional Chinese characters
### fántǐ
---
- **definition:** traditional Chinese characters
- **pinyin:** fántǐ
- **components:**
  - 繁
    - fán
    - numerous; many; complex; luxuriant
  - 体(體)
    - tǐ
    - body; form; style; system
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word (clipped from 繁体字)
  - **description:** 繁 (numerous/complex) + 体 (form/style) = “complex form” → traditional script.
  - **interpretation:** Literally “complex form,” a shorthand for 繁体字 that contrasts with 简体(字). Commonly refers to the traditional character set used in places like Taiwan and Hong Kong.
  - **simplification:** Only 體 → 体 was simplified, adopting a long-used variant to reduce strokes and ease writing; 繁 remained unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 请把网页切换成繁体(請把網頁切換成繁體)。
    - Qǐng bǎ wǎngyè qiēhuàn chéng fántǐ.
    - Please switch the webpage to traditional characters.
  - 他能看繁体也能看简体(他能看繁體也能看簡體)。
    - Tā néng kàn fántǐ yě néng kàn jiǎntǐ.
    - He can read Traditional as well as Simplified.
  - 在设置里可以选择简体或繁体(在設置裡可以選擇簡體或繁體)。
    - Zài shèzhì lǐ kěyǐ xuǎnzé jiǎntǐ huò fántǐ.
    - In settings you can choose Simplified or Traditional.
### 繁体(繁體) → 繁
- **definition:** numerous; many; complex; luxuriant
- **pinyin:** fán
- **components:**
  - 敏
    - mǐn
    - quick; clever; alert
  - 糸
    - mì
    - silk; thread
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 糸 (“silk”) + phonetic: 敏
  - **interpretation:** The silk component points to threads and weaving, evoking ideas of intricacy and abundance. 敏 supplies the pronunciation, while the image of many interlacing threads helps explain meanings like numerous, complex, and luxuriant growth.
- **examples:**
  - 这一时期资料繁多，研究者需要耐心整理。(這一時期資料繁多，研究者需要耐心整理。)
    - Zhè yī shíqī zīliào fánduō, yánjiūzhě xūyào nàixīn zhěnglǐ.
    - In this period the materials are numerous, and researchers need to sort them patiently.
  - 城市的道路网络十分繁复，第一次来的人容易迷路。(城市的道路網絡十分繁複，第一次來的人容易迷路。)
    - Chéngshì de dàolù wǎngluò shífēn fánfù, dì yī cì lái de rén róngyì mílù.
    - The city’s road network is very complex, and first-time visitors can easily get lost.
  - 雨后庭院里草木繁茂，空气格外清新。(雨後庭院裡草木繁茂，空氣格外清新。)
    - Yǔ hòu tíngyuàn lǐ cǎomù fánmào, kōngqì géwài qīngxīn.
    - After the rain, the courtyard’s vegetation is luxuriant, and the air is especially fresh.
### 繁体(繁體) → 繁 → 敏
- **definition:** quick; clever; alert
- **pinyin:** mǐn
- **components:**
  - 每
    - měi
    - each; every
  - 又
    - yòu
    - again; right hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic: 每 + semantic: 攴 ('hand; action'; sometimes written as 又 'hand' in early forms)
  - **interpretation:** Sound is borrowed from 每, while 攴 contributes the sense of the hand acting or striking. Together they evoke quick, deft movement and responsiveness, aligning with meanings like quick, nimble, and sensitive. Early forms sometimes used 又 for the hand element, reinforcing the action/hand idea.
- **examples:**
  - 他反应敏捷(他反應敏捷)，很快做出决定(很快做出決定)。
    - Tā fǎnyìng mǐnjié, hěn kuài zuòchū juédìng.
    - He reacts quickly and makes decisions swiftly.
  - 她对气味很敏感(她對氣味很敏感)，一点烟味都能闻出来(一點煙味都能聞出來)。
    - Tā duì qìwèi hěn mǐngǎn, yīdiǎn yānwèi dōu néng wén chūlái.
    - She is very sensitive to smells and can detect even a trace of smoke.
  - 这位学生思维敏锐(這位學生思維敏銳)，提出了独到的见解(提出了獨到的見解)。
    - Zhè wèi xuéshēng sīwéi mǐnruì, tíchū le dúdào de jiànjiě.
    - This student thinks sharply and offered original insights.
### 繁体(繁體) → 繁 → 敏 → 每
- **definition:** each; every
- **pinyin:** měi
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè
    - sprout; young plant
  - 母
    - mǔ
    - mother
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 屮 + phonetic: 母 (OC *mɯʔ).
  - **interpretation:** 母 provides the pronunciation, while the top element—interpreted as a sprout or bamboo hairpin—adds a semantic layer tied to maturity or individuation. That imagery was later abstracted into the distributive sense of counting items one by one, yielding the meaning “each/every.”
- **examples:**
  - 每个人都喜欢音乐(每個人都喜歡音樂)。
    - Měi gè rén dōu xǐhuan yīnyuè.
    - Everyone likes music.
  - 我们每天学习两个小时(我們每天學習兩個小時)。
    - Wǒmen měi tiān xuéxí liǎng gè xiǎoshí.
    - We study for two hours every day.
  - 每次遇到问题(每次遇到問題)，他都会询问老师(他都會詢問老師)。
    - Měi cì yùdào wèntí, tā dōu huì xúnwèn lǎoshī.
    - Every time he runs into a problem, he asks the teacher.
### 繁体(繁體) → 繁 → 敏 → 每 → 屮
- **definition:** sprout; young plant
- **pinyin:** chè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): a sprout emerging from the ground.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a shoot pushing up through the soil: the horizontal stroke suggests the ground, while the central vertical stroke and side strokes evoke the stem and small leaves. Early oracle and bronze forms closely resemble a budding plant.
- **examples:**
  - 在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽(在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén zhōng, chè biǎoshì zhíwù de nènyá.
    - In oracle script, 屮 represents a plant sprout.
  - 春天雨后，田里冒出许多嫩芽，像“屮”的形状(春天雨後，田裡冒出許多嫩芽，像「屮」的形狀)。
    - Chūntiān yǔ hòu, tián lǐ mào chū xǔduō nènyá, xiàng “chè” de xíngzhuàng.
    - After spring rains, many sprouts emerge in the fields, resembling the shape of “屮”.
  - 屮在古文字中象征嫩芽与新生(屮在古文字中象徵嫩芽與新生)。
    - Chè zài gǔ wénzì zhōng xiàngzhēng nènyá yǔ xīnshēng.
    - In ancient scripts, 屮 symbolizes sprouts and new life.
### 繁体(繁體) → 繁 → 敏 → 每 → 母
- **definition:** mother
- **pinyin:** mǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: differentiated form of 女 with the addition of distinguishing dots; in early oracle bone texts both 女 and 母 were written as 女.
  - **interpretation:** A picture of a woman marked with dots representing breasts to specify the role of a mother rather than a generic woman. Early writing did not distinguish the two graphs, so the dots were added to disambiguate the kinship meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我想念我的母亲(我想念我的母親)。
    - Wǒ xiǎngniàn wǒ de mǔqīn.
    - I miss my mother.
  - 父母每天都很关心我(父母每天都很關心我)。
    - Fùmǔ měitiān dōu hěn guānxīn wǒ.
    - Parents care about me every day.
  - 今天是母亲节，我们给妈妈做早餐(今天是母親節，我們給媽媽做早餐)。
    - Jīntiān shì Mǔqīn Jié, wǒmen gěi māma zuò zǎocān.
    - Today is Mother's Day; we made breakfast for Mom.
### 繁体(繁體) → 繁 → 敏 → 又
- **definition:** again; right hand
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 繁体(繁體) → 繁 → 糸
- **definition:** silk; thread
- **pinyin:** mì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** a skein of silk.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a bundle of silk threads tied in the middle, with strands hanging above and below. The stylized sketch highlights twisted filaments, which later served as the standard radical for thread- and textile-related characters.
  - **simplification:** In Simplified Chinese the silk radical was streamlined to 纟 to reduce strokes and speed writing; 糸 is rarely used independently, but 纟 replaces it as a left-side component.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢丝绸(我喜歡絲綢)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan sīchóu.
    - I like silk fabrics.
  - 这件衣服是用蚕丝做的(這件衣服是用蠶絲做的)。
    - Zhè jiàn yīfu shì yòng cánsī zuò de.
    - This piece of clothing is made from silkworm silk.
  - 丝线很细(絲線很細)。
    - Sīxiàn hěn xì.
    - Silk thread is very thin.
### 繁体(繁體) → 体(體)
- **definition:** body; form; style; system
- **pinyin:** tǐ
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 本
    - běn
    - root; basis
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 亻 ‘person’ + 本 ‘basis’; in trad. 體: semantic 骨 ‘bone’ + phonetic 豐.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the idea of a person with a foundational base, pointing to one’s fundamental constitution and thus ‘body’, and by extension ‘form’ or ‘style’. In the traditional form, the ‘bone’ element underscores the bodily frame while 豐 supplies the sound, reinforcing a physical framework sense.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by replacing the phono-semantic 體 (骨 + 豐) with the more transparent and lower-stroke form 体 (亻 + 本), aligning meaning with ‘person’ and easing writing.
- **examples:**
  - 他的身体很健康(他的身體很健康)。
    - Tā de shēntǐ hěn jiànkāng.
    - His body is very healthy.
  - 这款字体很优雅(這款字體很優雅)。
    - Zhè kuǎn zìtǐ hěn yōuyǎ.
    - This typeface is very elegant.
  - 改革现有体制需要时间(改革現有體制需要時間)。
    - Gǎigé xiànyǒu tǐzhì xūyào shíjiān.
    - Reforming the existing system takes time.
### 繁体(繁體) → 体(體) → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg; left-side form 亻.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person in profile, with one stroke suggesting the torso/arm and the other the leg, hence the slightly splayed shape. In compounds it often appears as the left-hand form 亻 to mark human-related meaning; not to be confused with 彳.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个很好的人(他是一個很好的人)。
    - Tā shì yí gè hěn hǎo de rén.
    - He is a very good person.
  - 我认识很多人(我認識很多人)。
    - Wǒ rènshi hěn duō rén.
    - I know many people.
  - 这里有三个人(這裡有三個人)。
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu sān gè rén.
    - There are three people here.
### 繁体(繁體) → 体(體) → 本
- **definition:** root; basis
- **pinyin:** běn
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree; wood
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogram (指事)
  - **description:** indicative: 木 (mù ‘tree’) with its bottom highlighted by an extra stroke to accentuate the roots; contrast 末, 朱.
  - **interpretation:** Emphasizes the base of a tree to point to the idea of origin, foundation, or root. The extra stroke marks the bottom, distinguishing it from 末, which highlights the top.
- **examples:**
  - 以史为本(以史為本)，可以知兴替(可以知興替)。
    - Yǐ shǐ wéi běn, kěyǐ zhī xīngtì.
    - Taking history as the basis allows one to understand rise and decline.
  - 事情的本质很重要(事情的本質很重要)。
    - Shìqíng de běnzhì hěn zhòngyào.
    - The fundamental nature of the matter is very important.
  - 我们要回到问题的本源来思考(我們要回到問題的本源來思考)。
    - Wǒmen yào huídào wèntí de běnyuán lái sīkǎo.
    - We need to return to the origin of the problem to think it through.
### 繁体(繁體) → 体(體) → 本 → 木
- **definition:** tree; wood
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram (象形)
  - **description:** a tree: branches on top, roots on the bottom (more visible in earlier forms).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a tree with spreading branches above and roots below, as seen clearly in early scripts. The vertical stroke is the trunk, while the short horizontal strokes became stylized limbs and roots in the regular form.
- **examples:**
  - 这张桌子是木头做的(這張桌子是木頭做的)。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi shì mùtou zuò de.
    - This table is made of wood.
  - 山上长满了树木(山上長滿了樹木)。
    - Shān shang zhǎng mǎn le shùmù.
    - The mountainside is covered with trees.
  - 木材被运到工厂加工(木材被運到工廠加工)。
    - Mùcái bèi yùn dào gōngchǎng jiāgōng.
    - The timber was transported to the factory for processing.
