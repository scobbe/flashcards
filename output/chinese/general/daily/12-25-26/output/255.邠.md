## Bin (ancient place name)
### Bīn
---
- **definition:** Bin (ancient place name)
- **pinyin:** Bīn
- **components:**
  - 分
    - fēn
    - divide; part
  - 邑
    - yì
    - city
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 邑 (‘city’) + phonetic: 分 (OC *pɯn, *bɯns).
  - **interpretation:** The city/place component 邑 signals that the meaning relates to a settlement or place-name. 分 supplies the pronunciation, which in Old Chinese aligned with forms that developed into modern Bīn.
- **examples:**
  - 周人先居于邠(周人先居於邠)。
    - Zhōu rén xiān jū yú Bīn.
    - The Zhou people first dwelled in Bin.
  - 学者考证邠是古地名(學者考證邠是古地名)。
    - Xuézhě kǎozhèng Bīn shì gǔ dìmíng.
    - Scholars have verified that Bin is an ancient place name.
  - 在邠附近出土了青铜器(在邠附近出土了青銅器)。
    - Zài Bīn fùjìn chūtǔ le qīngtóngqì.
    - Bronze artifacts were unearthed near Bin.
### 邠 → 分
- **definition:** divide; part
- **pinyin:** fēn
- **components:**
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (会意)
  - **description:** semantic: 八 ‘to separate’ + semantic: 刀 ‘knife’ — separate with a knife
  - **interpretation:** Combines 八, depicting separation or splitting, with 刀, a knife, to express cutting something apart. This core idea underlies senses like dividing, apportioning, and by extension small fractional units such as minutes and cents.
- **examples:**
  - 我们把蛋糕分开了(我們把蛋糕分開了)。
    - Wǒmen bǎ dàngāo fēnkāi le.
    - We divided the cake.
  - 现在是三点十分(現在是三點十分)。
    - Xiànzài shì sān diǎn shí fēn.
    - It is 3:10.
  - 他只给了我一分钱(他只給了我一分錢)。
    - Tā zhǐ gěi le wǒ yì fēnqián.
    - He only gave me one cent.
### 邠 → 分 → 八
- **definition:** eight
- **pinyin:** bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: two bent lines indicating “to divide”; later borrowed to mean “eight” via homophony (original sense now in 分, 別).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two strokes diverging to suggest splitting or separation. Later became a rebus for the number eight because it shared the same sound, while the original sense lived on in 分 and 別. Not the “two hands” component found in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我有八本书(我有八本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu bā běn shū.
    - I have eight books.
  - 她八岁了(她八歲了)。
    - Tā bā suì le.
    - She is eight years old.
  - 我买了八个苹果(我買了八個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le bā gè píngguǒ.
    - I bought eight apples.
### 邠 → 分 → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — a knife; original form 𠚣, seen in a variant of 利 (𥝢) and atop 黎.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a knife with a distinct blade and handle. The graph was streamlined into the current two-stroke form and also serves as a component in characters like 利 and 黎.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿着一把刀(他拿著一把刀)。
    - Tā názhe yì bǎ dāo.
    - He is holding a knife.
  - 厨师用刀切蔬菜(廚師用刀切蔬菜)。
    - Chúshī yòng dāo qiē shūcài.
    - The chef uses a knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这把刀很锋利(這把刀很鋒利)。
    - Zhè bǎ dāo hěn fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
### 邠 → 邑
- **definition:** city
- **pinyin:** yì
- **components:**
  - 卪
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** Ideogrammic compound: 囗 ‘enclosure; city wall’ + 卪 ‘kneeling person’; bottom part unrelated to 巴 and top most likely unrelated to original 丁.
  - **interpretation:** Combines a walled enclosure with a person to depict a community where people live or gather within protective walls. By showing someone inside fortifications, it evokes the idea of a town or settlement organized around its walls and civic center.
- **examples:**
  - 古代诸侯在其邑中修筑城墙(古代諸侯在其邑中修築城牆)。
    - Gǔdài zhūhóu zài qí yì zhōng xiūzhù chéngqiáng.
    - In ancient times, feudal lords built city walls within their settlements.
  - 此邑临河(此邑臨河)，贸易兴盛(貿易興盛)。
    - Cǐ yì lín hé, màoyì xīngshèng.
    - This town lies by a river, and trade flourishes.
  - 史书记载此邑曾为郡治(史書記載此邑曾為郡治)。
    - Shǐshū jìzǎi cǐ yì céng wéi jùnzhì.
    - Historical records note this settlement once served as a commandery seat.
### 邠 → 邑 → 卪
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram of a kneeling person.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a person kneeling in profile, with the bent leg and foot suggested by the hooked strokes. It later serves as the ‘kneel/seal’ component in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 在甲骨文里(在甲骨文裡)，卪表示跪着的人(卪表示跪著的人)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén lǐ, jié biǎoshì guìzhe de rén.
    - In oracle bone script, 卪 represents a kneeling person.
  - 这个字很少见(這個字很少見)，多用作部件(多用作部件)。
    - Zhège zì hěn shǎojiàn, duō yòngzuò bùjiàn.
    - This character is rarely seen and is mostly used as a component.
  - 写字时(寫字時)，别把卪和卩混淆(別把卪和卩混淆)。
    - Xiězì shí, bié bǎ jié hé jié hùnxiáo.
    - When writing, don’t confuse 卪 with 卩.
