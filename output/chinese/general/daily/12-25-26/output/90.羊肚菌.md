## morel mushroom
### yángdùjùn
---
- **definition:** morel mushroom
- **pinyin:** yángdùjùn
- **components:**
  - 羊
    - yáng
    - sheep;goat;mutton;ram
  - 肚
    - dù
    - belly;stomach;tripe;gut
  - 菌
    - jùn
    - fungus;mold;germ;microbe
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 羊 + 肚 + 菌 = sheep-tripe + fungus → morel mushroom (cap resembles sheep tripe).
  - **interpretation:** Named for the honeycomb folds of the cap that look like sheep tripe. Refers to the edible morel mushroom used in cuisine and for its aroma.
- **examples:**
  - 我们在松林里采到几朵羊肚菌(我們在松林裡採到幾朵羊肚菌)。
    - Wǒmen zài sōnglín lǐ cǎi dào jǐ duǒ yángdùjùn.
    - We picked a few morels in the pine forest.
  - 羊肚菌味道鲜美(羊肚菌味道鮮美)，用来做汤很合适(用來做湯很合適)。
    - Yángdùjùn wèidào xiānměi, yòng lái zuò tāng hěn héshì.
    - Morels taste delicious and are well suited for making soup.
  - 由于菌盖像羊肚(由於菌蓋像羊肚)，人们称它为“羊肚菌”(人們稱它為「羊肚菌」)。
    - Yóuyú jūngài xiàng yángdù, rénmen chēng tā wéi “yángdùjùn”.
    - Because the cap looks like sheep tripe, people call it “yangdujun.”
### 羊肚菌 → 羊
- **definition:** sheep;goat;mutton;ram
- **pinyin:** yáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – picture of a ram’s head.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a ram’s head with prominent horns above and facial/beard lines below. The image directly conveys the meaning of “sheep,” and its recognizable silhouette later served as a component in related characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我家养了三只羊(我家養了三隻羊)。
    - Wǒ jiā yǎng le sān zhī yáng.
    - My family raises three sheep.
  - 那只羊很温顺(那隻羊很溫順)。
    - Nà zhī yáng hěn wénshùn.
    - That sheep is very gentle.
  - 山上有很多野羊(山上有很多野羊)。
    - Shān shàng yǒu hěn duō yěyáng.
    - There are many wild sheep in the mountains.
### 羊肚菌 → 肚
- **definition:** belly;stomach;tripe;gut
- **pinyin:** dù
- **components:**
  - 肉
    - ròu
    - meat; flesh
  - 土
    - tǔ
    - earth; soil
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic ⺼ (flesh) + phonetic 土.
  - **interpretation:** The flesh component signals a body-related meaning, pointing to the belly or stomach. 土 served as the sound carrier, and historical sound changes brought its reading close to dù/dǔ, while its literal meaning ‘earth’ is not involved here.
- **examples:**
  - 我肚子饿了(我肚子餓了)。
    - Wǒ dùzi è le.
    - I'm hungry.
  - 他摸着肚子说不舒服(他摸著肚子說不舒服)。
    - Tā mōzhe dùzi shuō bù shūfu.
    - He held his stomach and said he felt unwell.
  - 我喜欢吃牛肚(我喜歡吃牛肚)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan chī niú dǔ.
    - I like to eat beef tripe.
### 羊肚菌 → 肚 → 肉
- **definition:** meat; flesh
- **pinyin:** ròu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: ribs/section of meat; stylized as 月 when beside a phonetic, and as 𱼀 or 夕 inside some characters.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts the cross-section of flesh with ribs or a slice of meat. In compounds it often appears as a component written like 月, or reduced to 𱼀/夕, which can be mistaken for the moon component but actually signals ‘meat/flesh.’
- **examples:**
  - 我不吃猪肉(我不吃豬肉)。
    - Wǒ bù chī zhūròu.
    - I don't eat pork.
  - 这道菜肉很多，很鲜嫩(這道菜肉很多，很鮮嫩)。
    - Zhè dào cài ròu hěn duō, hěn xiānnèn.
    - This dish has a lot of meat; it's very tender.
  - 受伤后，他手上的肉裂开了(受傷後，他手上的肉裂開了)。
    - Shòushāng hòu, tā shǒushang de ròu liè kāi le.
    - After being injured, the flesh on his hand split open.
### 羊肚菌 → 肚 → 土
- **definition:** earth; soil
- **pinyin:** tǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a lump of clay on the ground or on a potter’s wheel; the central dot was later stylized into a short horizontal stroke.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a clod of earth resting on the ground, later streamlined into a vertical line with top and bottom bars. The upper bar developed from an earlier dot to distinguish it from similar graphs.
- **examples:**
  - 这里的土很干(這裡的土很乾)。
    - Zhèlǐ de tǔ hěn gān.
    - The soil here is very dry.
  - 他把一把土撒在花盆里(他把一把土撒在花盆裡)。
    - Tā bǎ yì bǎ tǔ sǎ zài huāpén lǐ.
    - He sprinkled a handful of soil into the flowerpot.
  - 建房前要先检测土质(建房前要先檢測土質)。
    - Jiàn fáng qián yào xiān jiǎncè tǔzhì.
    - Before building a house, the soil quality must be tested.
### 羊肚菌 → 菌
- **definition:** fungus;mold;germ;microbe
- **pinyin:** jùn
- **components:**
  - 囷
    - qūn
    - granary; grain stack
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 艹 + phonetic: 囷 (OC *kʰrun, *ɡrun).
  - **interpretation:** 艹 points to a plant/biological domain, fitting senses like fungus, mold, and microbes. 囷 supplies the sound value historically and, being associated with grain stores, loosely echoes environments where molds and fungi may grow.
- **examples:**
  - 医生说这种细菌对抗生素有抗性(醫生說這種細菌對抗生素有抗性)。
    - Yīshēng shuō zhè zhǒng xìjùn duì kàngshēngsù yǒu kàngxìng.
    - The doctor said this bacterium is resistant to antibiotics.
  - 潮湿的墙面容易滋生霉菌(潮濕的牆面容易滋生黴菌)。
    - Cháoshī de qiángmiàn róngyì zīshēng méijùn.
    - Damp walls easily breed mold.
  - 真菌在森林的腐木上生长(真菌在森林的腐木上生長)。
    - Zhēnjùn zài sēnlín de fǔmù shàng shēngzhǎng.
    - Fungi grow on decaying wood in forests.
### 羊肚菌 → 菌 → 囷
- **definition:** granary; grain stack
- **pinyin:** qūn
- **components:**
  - 君
    - jūn
    - lord; ruler
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic compound: semantic 囗 (“enclosure”) + phonetic 君 (jūn).
  - **interpretation:** Enclosure hints at a place for keeping things, while 君 supplies the sound. Together they point to grain gathered and stored within an enclosure, hence a granary or grain stack.
- **examples:**
  - 门前的稻谷堆成一个大囷(門前的稻穀堆成一個大囷)。
    - Ménqián de dàogǔ duī chéng yí ge dà qūn.
    - The rice was piled into a big grain stack in front of the gate.
  - 村里修了新囷，用来储存小麦(村裡修了新囷，用來儲存小麥)。
    - Cūn lǐ xiū le xīn qūn, yòng lái chǔcún xiǎomài.
    - The village built a new granary to store wheat.
  - 秋后，粮食被收进囷中防潮(秋後，糧食被收進囷中防潮)。
    - Qiū hòu, liángshi bèi shōu jìn qūn zhōng fáng cháo.
    - After autumn, the grain is brought into the granary to keep out moisture.
### 羊肚菌 → 菌 → 囷 → 君
- **definition:** lord; ruler
- **pinyin:** jūn
- **components:**
  - 尹
    - yǐn
    - to govern; overseer; surname
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phonetic 尹 (OC *ɢʷlinʔ) + semantic 口; OC *klun. In oracle bones and early Zhou bronzes, often interchanged with 尹; Shuowen says 口 marks commands, possibly decorative.
  - **interpretation:** Combining 尹 for sound with 口 suggests authority expressed through issuing commands. Early interchange with 尹 points to a tight link between the ideas of governing and the word’s pronunciation. The mouth element likely hints at spoken orders, even if it may have been added decoratively.
- **examples:**
  - 君主制度在历史上影响深远(君主制度在歷史上影響深遠)。
    - jūnzhǔ zhìdù zài lìshǐ shàng yǐngxiǎng shēnyuǎn
    - The monarchical system has had a far-reaching impact in history.
  - 古代的国君保护百姓(古代的國君保護百姓)。
    - gǔdài de guójūn bǎohù bǎixìng
    - In ancient times, the rulers protected the common people.
  - 君王发布诏令(君王發布詔令)。
    - jūnwáng fābù zhàolìng
    - The sovereign issued an imperial edict.
### 羊肚菌 → 菌 → 囷 → 君 → 尹
- **definition:** to govern; overseer; surname
- **pinyin:** yǐn
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - again; (right hand component)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a hand holding a rod or brush; similar to 事 and 支.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a hand grasping a staff or writing brush, linking the image to authority, administration, and control. This association led to meanings like overseer or to govern, and it later became established as a surname.
- **examples:**
  - 尹先生今天来访(尹先生今天來訪)。
    - Yǐn xiānsheng jīntiān láifǎng.
    - Mr. Yin paid a visit today.
  - 新任县尹上任后(新任縣尹上任後)，整顿吏治(整頓吏治)。
    - Xīnrèn xiànyǐn shàngrèn hòu, zhěngdùn lìzhì.
    - After the newly appointed county magistrate took office, he reformed the administration.
  - 我班上有个同学姓尹(我班上有個同學姓尹)。
    - Wǒ bān shàng yǒu gè tóngxué xìng Yǐn.
    - There is a classmate in my class whose surname is Yin.
### 羊肚菌 → 菌 → 囷 → 君 → 尹 → 又
- **definition:** again; (right hand component)
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – a right hand; original form of 右; unrelated to 𠬤 (> 译). Often written as 彐 in many modern characters (e.g., 事, 聿, 雪, 急, 爭, 尹, 建).
  - **interpretation:** Early graphs depict a right hand, which later gave rise to 右 ‘right (side)’. The adverbial sense ‘again’ developed later by semantic extension. In many compounds a similar shape is written as 彐, but that is a graphic convention rather than a direct historical component here.
- **examples:**
  - 他又来了(他又來了)。
    - Tā yòu lái le.
    - He came again.
  - 请向右转(請向右轉)。
    - Qǐng xiàng yòu zhuǎn.
    - Please turn right.
  - 她又买了一本书(她又買了一本書)。
    - Tā yòu mǎi le yī běn shū.
    - She bought another book.
### 羊肚菌 → 菌 → 囷 → 君 → 口
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
