## Go game
### wéiqí
---
- **definition:** Go game
- **pinyin:** wéiqí
- **components:**
  - 围(圍)
    - wéi
    - to surround; to encircle; to besiege; enclosure
  - 棋
    - qí
    - board game; chess; Go; game piece
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 围 + 棋 = encirclement board game (Go).
  - **interpretation:** Combines 围 ‘to surround’ with 棋 ‘board game,’ pointing to the game’s core objective of surrounding territory. Highlights encirclement as the defining strategy of Go.
  - **simplification:** Component simplification: 圍 → 围 by replacing 韋 with its simplified form 韦 inside the 囗 enclosure; 棋 unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我从小喜欢下围棋(我從小喜歡下圍棋)。
    - Wǒ cóng xiǎo xǐhuān xià wéiqí.
    - I’ve liked playing Go since childhood.
  - 围棋强调“围地”(圍棋強調「圍地」)，胜负取决于谁控制更多的地盘(勝負取決於誰控制更多的地盤)。
    - Wéiqí qiángdiào “wéidì”, shèngfù qǔjué yú shéi kòngzhì gèng duō de dìpán.
    - Go emphasizes ‘encircling territory’; victory depends on who controls more territory.
  - 周末我们去棋院参加围棋比赛(週末我們去棋院參加圍棋比賽)。
    - Zhōumò wǒmen qù qíyuàn cānjiā wéiqí bǐsài.
    - On the weekend we go to the Go institute to take part in a Go tournament.
### 围棋(圍棋) → 围(圍)
- **definition:** to surround; to encircle; to besiege; enclosure
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **components:**
  - 韦(韋)
    - wéi
    - tanned leather; surname Wei
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 囗 (“enclosure”) + phonetic: 韋 (simplified as 韦).
  - **interpretation:** 囗 conveys the idea of enclosing or surrounding, while 韋 supplies the sound wéi and was the original graph for this word. In simplification, 韋 was streamlined to 韦, preserving the pronunciation and core meaning.
  - **simplification:** Phonetic component simplified (韋 → 韦) to reduce strokes, while the enclosure 囗 remained unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我们把敌人团团围住(我們把敵人團團圍住)。
    - Wǒmen bǎ dírén tuántuán wéizhù.
    - We surrounded the enemy on all sides.
  - 城市被洪水围成一片孤岛(城市被洪水圍成一片孤島)。
    - Chéngshì bèi hóngshuǐ wéi chéng yī piàn gūdǎo.
    - The city was encircled by floodwaters and became an island.
  - 村民在田地周围修围墙(村民在田地周圍修圍牆)。
    - Cūnmín zài tiándì zhōuwéi xiū wéiqiáng.
    - Villagers built an enclosing wall around the fields.

### 围棋(圍棋) → 棋
- **definition:** board game; chess; Go; game piece
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood; tree
  - 其
    - qí
    - its; that
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 木 (wood) + phonetic: 其 (OC *kɯ, *ɡɯ)
  - **interpretation:** Wood signals that the word relates to wooden objects such as boards and pieces used in games. 其 supplies the sound value close to qí while adding little semantic content.
- **examples:**
  - 我们下棋吧(我們下棋吧)。
    - Wǒmen xià qí ba.
    - Let's play a board game.
  - 他喜欢围棋，也会国际象棋(他喜歡圍棋，也會國際象棋)。
    - Tā xǐhuān wéiqí, yě huì guójì xiàngqí.
    - He likes Go and can also play international chess.
  - 桌上放着几枚棋子和一个棋盘(桌上放著幾枚棋子和一個棋盤)。
    - Zhuō shàng fàngzhe jǐ méi qízǐ hé yí gè qípán.
    - There are several game pieces and a board on the table.
### 围棋(圍棋) → 棋 → 木
- **definition:** wood; tree
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram (象形)
  - **description:** a tree: branches on top, roots on the bottom (more visible in earlier forms).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a tree with spreading branches above and roots below, as seen clearly in early scripts. The vertical stroke is the trunk, while the short horizontal strokes became stylized limbs and roots in the regular form.
- **examples:**
  - 这张桌子是木头做的(這張桌子是木頭做的)。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi shì mùtou zuò de.
    - This table is made of wood.
  - 山上长满了树木(山上長滿了樹木)。
    - Shān shang zhǎng mǎn le shùmù.
    - The mountainside is covered with trees.
  - 木材被运到工厂加工(木材被運到工廠加工)。
    - Mùcái bèi yùn dào gōngchǎng jiāgōng.
    - The timber was transported to the factory for processing.
### 围棋(圍棋) → 棋 → 其
- **definition:** its; that
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 丌
    - jī
    - stand; table
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Originally a pictogram of a basket; later had 丌 ‘stand’ added (possibly as a phonetic), and was then borrowed to write a pronoun; the original sense survives in 箕.
  - **interpretation:** First depicted a basket, later drawn as a basket on a stand, with 丌 possibly contributing sound. Its common pronoun meaning comes from loan usage, while words like 箕 preserve the original ‘basket’ sense.
- **examples:**
  - 其结果令人意外(其結果令人意外)。
    - Qí jiéguǒ lìng rén yìwài.
    - Its result was unexpected.
  - 我们尊重其选择(我們尊重其選擇)。
    - Wǒmen zūnzhòng qí xuǎnzé.
    - We respect his/her/its choice.
  - 研究指出，其影响深远(研究指出，其影響深遠)。
    - Yánjiū zhǐchū, qí yǐngxiǎng shēnyuǎn.
    - Research indicates that its impact is far-reaching.
### 围棋(圍棋) → 棋 → 其 → 丌
- **definition:** stand; table
- **pinyin:** jī
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 丨
    - gǔn
    - vertical line
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: depicts a stand/table with a horizontal board over a vertical support.
  - **interpretation:** A stylized drawing of a tabletop with a central support conveys the idea of a small stand or altar. Early forms show a horizontal board above a vertical post, aligning with the meanings 'stand; table'.
- **examples:**
  - 他把器皿放在丌上(他把器皿放在丌上)。
    - Tā bǎ qìmǐn fàng zài jī shàng.
    - He put the utensils on the stand.
  - 祭司立于丌旁(祭司立於丌旁)。
    - Jìsī lì yú jī páng.
    - The priest stood beside the altar table.
  - 博物馆展出了一张古丌(博物館展出了一張古丌)。
    - Bówùguǎn zhǎnchūle yì zhāng gǔ jī.
    - The museum displayed an ancient stand.
### 围棋(圍棋) → 棋 → 其 → 丌 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**
  - 我只有一本书(我只有一本書)。
    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.
    - I only have one book.
  - 他一到就给我打电话(他一到就給我打電話)。
    - Tā yí dào jiù gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.
    - As soon as he arrives, he calls me.
  - 一个问题都没有(一個問題都沒有)。
    - Yí gè wèntí dōu méiyǒu.
    - There isn’t a single problem.
### 围棋(圍棋) → 棋 → 其 → 丌 → 丨
- **definition:** vertical line
- **pinyin:** gǔn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a vertical wooden stick; appears in early 朕 (originally “to mend a boat”) and in 尹, where the stick symbolizes authority.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an upright staff, emphasizing straightness and verticality. Its use inside graphs like 朕 and 尹 shows it originally pictured a tool or staff and later generalized to the basic vertical stroke.
- **examples:**
  - 汉字的第二号部首是丨(漢字的第二號部首是丨)。
    - Hànzì de dì-èr hào bùshǒu shì gǔn.
    - The second Chinese radical is 丨.
  - 写字时，要把这一笔丨写直(寫字時，要把這一筆丨寫直)。
    - Xiězì shí, yào bǎ zhè yì bǐ gǔn xiě zhí.
    - When writing, this stroke 丨 should be written straight.
  - 他写了一笔丨(他寫了一筆丨)。
    - Tā xiě le yì bǐ gǔn.
    - He wrote a single vertical stroke.
