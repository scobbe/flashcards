## control; accuse
### kòng
---
- **definition:** control; accuse
- **pinyin:** kòng
- **components:**
  - 空
    - kōng
    - empty; sky
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 扌 + phonetic: 空
  - **interpretation:** The hand component hints at actions performed by hand, such as handling, managing, or bringing a matter to court. 空 supplies the kòng sound and subtly evokes space/emptiness, extending to the idea of exerting control or formally pressing charges.
- **examples:**
  - 他很难控制自己的情绪(他很難控制自己的情緒)。
    - Tā hěn nán kòngzhì zìjǐ de qíngxù.
    - He finds it hard to control his emotions.
  - 警方控告他诈骗(警方控告他詐騙)。
    - Jǐngfāng kònggào tā zhàpiàn.
    - The police accused him of fraud.
  - 公司实行严格的质量控制(公司實行嚴格的質量控制)。
    - Gōngsī shíxíng yángé de zhìliàng kòngzhì.
    - The company enforces strict quality control.
### 控 → 空
- **definition:** empty; sky
- **pinyin:** kōng
- **components:**
  - 穴
    - xué
    - cave; hole
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work; craft
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 穴 ‘cave, hole’ + phonetic: 工
  - **interpretation:** 穴 signals the idea of a hollow or cavity, pointing to emptiness or open space, while 工 supplies a similar sound. The combination evokes a hollow void, which naturally extended to the vast open expanse above, hence the sense ‘sky’.
- **examples:**
  - 天空很蓝(天空很藍)。
    - Tiānkōng hěn lán.
    - The sky is very blue.
  - 这个箱子是空的(這個箱子是空的)。
    - Zhège xiāngzi shì kōng de.
    - This box is empty.
  - 房间里还空着两张床(房間裡還空著兩張床)。
    - Fángjiān lǐ hái kōngzhe liǎng zhāng chuáng.
    - There are still two empty beds in the room.
### 控 → 空 → 穴
- **definition:** cave; hole
- **pinyin:** xué
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram—possibly an entrance to a cave or a door flap; conservative form 𠕉; see also 奐 and the top component of 罙.
  - **interpretation:** Graphic of a covered opening suggesting the mouth of a cave or a hanging flap over an entrance. Early shapes like 𠕉 emphasize a shaded or screened hole, matching the meanings “cave; hole.”
- **examples:**
  - 狐狸躲在地穴里(狐狸躲在地穴裡)。
    - Húlí duǒ zài dìxué lǐ.
    - The fox hides in a burrow.
  - 他在墙上钻了一个小穴(他在牆上鑽了一個小穴)。
    - Tā zài qiáng shàng zuān le yí gè xiǎo xué.
    - He drilled a small hole in the wall.
  - 考古队发现了古人的岩穴(考古隊發現了古人的岩穴)。
    - Kǎogǔ duì fāxiàn le gǔrén de yánxué.
    - The archaeology team discovered a rock cave used by ancient people.
### 控 → 空 → 工
- **definition:** work; craft
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a bladed tool to plow and mark a border; alternatively a carpenter’s square (cf. original meaning of 巨).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a tool used to prepare the ground and set boundaries, naturally extending to the idea of labor and work. Some traditions instead see a carpenter’s square, connecting it with making and constructing, and with early senses linked to 巨.
- **examples:**
  - 我在工厂工作(我在工廠工作)。
    - Wǒ zài gōngchǎng gōngzuò.
    - I work in a factory.
  - 这些工人正在劳动(這些工人正在勞動)。
    - Zhèxiē gōngrén zhèngzài láodòng.
    - These workers are laboring now.
  - 他的工钱很高(他的工錢很高)。
    - Tā de gōngqián hěn gāo.
    - His wages are high.
### 控 → 手
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: hand and fingers; five digits, with top stroke as bent middle finger and horizontal strokes as fingers.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a human hand: the top stroke stands for a bent middle finger, and the horizontal strokes stand for the remaining fingers. In contrast to related claw forms that show three digits, it consistently presents a five‑finger human hand.
- **examples:**
  - 我手上有伤口(我手上有傷口)。
    - Wǒ shǒu shàng yǒu shāngkǒu.
    - I have a wound on my hand.
  - 请举起右手(請舉起右手)。
    - Qǐng jǔ qǐ yòu shǒu.
    - Please raise your right hand.
  - 他动手做饭(他動手做飯)。
    - Tā dòngshǒu zuò fàn.
    - He started cooking by hand.
