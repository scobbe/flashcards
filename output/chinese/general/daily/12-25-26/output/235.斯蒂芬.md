## Stephen
### Sīdìfēn
---
- **definition:** Stephen
- **pinyin:** Sīdìfēn
- **components:**
  - 斯
    - sī
    - this; thus; such; used in transliteration
  - 蒂
    - dì
    - peduncle; fruit stem; flower base; calyx
  - 芬
    - fēn
    - fragrance; perfume; aromatic; elegant
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phonetic transliteration (personal name)
  - **description:** 斯(si) + 蒂(di) + 芬(fen) = transliteration of “Stephen.”
  - **interpretation:** A Mandarin transliteration of the English given name “Stephen,” chosen for sound rather than literal meaning. Characters are neutral-to-positive in sense, a common practice for foreign names.
- **examples:**
  - 我叫斯蒂芬(我叫斯蒂芬)。
    - Wǒ jiào Sīdìfēn.
    - My name is Stephen.
  - 斯蒂芬来自英国(斯蒂芬來自英國)。
    - Sīdìfēn láizì Yīngguó.
    - Stephen comes from the UK.
  - 斯蒂芬正在学习中文(斯蒂芬正在學習中文)。
    - Sīdìfēn zhèngzài xuéxí Zhōngwén.
    - Stephen is studying Chinese.
### 斯蒂芬 → 斯
- **definition:** this; thus; such; used in transliteration
- **pinyin:** sī
- **components:**
  - 其
    - qí
    - its; that; such; (orig.) sieve
  - 斤
    - jīn
    - axe; catty (unit of weight)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (會意)
  - **description:** semantic: 其 ‘sieve’ + 斤 ‘axe’ → ‘divide/break with an axe’; Xu Shen: synonym of 析; sense preserved in 撕.
  - **interpretation:** A sieve set beside an axe evokes the act of chopping or splitting, yielding the early sense ‘to divide’. The form later broadened into a literary demonstrative meaning ‘this/thus’, while the original splitting sense lives on in derivatives like 撕 ‘to tear’.
- **examples:**
  - 斯人已逝(斯人已逝)，风骨长存(風骨長存)。
    - Sī rén yǐ shì, fēnggǔ chángcún.
    - This person has passed away, but their integrity endures.
  - 他在斯地求学多年(他在斯地求學多年)。
    - Tā zài sī dì qiúxué duōnián.
    - He studied in this place for many years.
  - 我们将访问斯里兰卡(我們將訪問斯里蘭卡)。
    - Wǒmen jiāng fǎngwèn Sīlǐlánkǎ.
    - We will visit Sri Lanka.
### 斯蒂芬 → 斯 → 其
- **definition:** its; that; such; (orig.) sieve
- **pinyin:** qí
- **components:**
  - 丌
    - jī
    - stand; table
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Originally a pictogram of a basket; later had 丌 ‘stand’ added (possibly as a phonetic), and was then borrowed to write a pronoun; the original sense survives in 箕.
  - **interpretation:** First depicted a basket, later drawn as a basket on a stand, with 丌 possibly contributing sound. Its common pronoun meaning comes from loan usage, while words like 箕 preserve the original ‘basket’ sense.
- **examples:**
  - 其结果令人意外(其結果令人意外)。
    - Qí jiéguǒ lìng rén yìwài.
    - Its result was unexpected.
  - 我们尊重其选择(我們尊重其選擇)。
    - Wǒmen zūnzhòng qí xuǎnzé.
    - We respect his/her/its choice.
  - 研究指出，其影响深远(研究指出，其影響深遠)。
    - Yánjiū zhǐchū, qí yǐngxiǎng shēnyuǎn.
    - Research indicates that its impact is far-reaching.

### 斯蒂芬 → 斯 → 其 → 丌 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** a horizontal stroke, indicating the number 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** A single horizontal line visually represents the concept of oneness. As the simplest numeral, it set the pattern for 二 and 三 by repeating the stroke to show increasing quantity.
- **examples:**

    - Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yì běn shū.

### 斯蒂芬 → 斯 → 其 → 丌 → 丨
- **definition:** vertical line
- **pinyin:** gǔn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: a vertical wooden stick; appears in early 朕 (originally “to mend a boat”) and in 尹, where the stick symbolizes authority.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts an upright staff, emphasizing straightness and verticality. Its use inside graphs like 朕 and 尹 shows it originally pictured a tool or staff and later generalized to the basic vertical stroke.
- **examples:**
  - 汉字的第二号部首是丨(漢字的第二號部首是丨)。
    - Hànzì de dì-èr hào bùshǒu shì gǔn.
    - The second Chinese radical is 丨.
  - 写字时，要把这一笔丨写直(寫字時，要把這一筆丨寫直)。
    - Xiězì shí, yào bǎ zhè yì bǐ gǔn xiě zhí.
    - When writing, this stroke 丨 should be written straight.
  - 他写了一笔丨(他寫了一筆丨)。
    - Tā xiě le yì bǐ gǔn.
    - He wrote a single vertical stroke.
### 斯蒂芬 → 斯 → 斤
- **definition:** axe; catty (unit of weight)
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – an axe.
  - **interpretation:** Originally a drawing of a hand axe: the long vertical suggests the handle and the hooked stroke the blade. Over time the form was regularized, and the word also came to denote the traditional weight unit ‘catty’.
- **examples:**
  - 我买了两斤苹果(我買了兩斤蘋果)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le liǎng jīn píngguǒ.
    - I bought two jin of apples.
  - 这块肉一斤多一点(這塊肉一斤多一點)。
    - Zhè kuài ròu yì jīn duō yìdiǎn.
    - This piece of meat is a bit over one jin.
  - 这两支队伍水平半斤八两(這兩支隊伍水平半斤八兩)。
    - Zhè liǎng zhī duìwǔ shuǐpíng bàn jīn bā liǎng.
    - These two teams are about the same level (six of one, half a dozen of the other).
### 斯蒂芬 → 蒂
- **definition:** peduncle; fruit stem; flower base; calyx
- **pinyin:** dì
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 帝
    - dì
    - emperor
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 艸 (“grass”) + phonetic: 帝
  - **interpretation:** 艸 signals a plant-related meaning, pointing to parts like stems or calyxes. 帝 provides the sound value (dì), guiding pronunciation while adding little to the meaning.
- **examples:**

    - Xīhóngshì de guǒdì yào xiān qù diào.
    - Remove the tomato’s stem first.
  - 花谢后(花謝後)，蒂还留在枝上(蒂還留在枝上)。
    - Huā xiè hòu, dì hái liú zài zhī shàng.
    - After the flowers wither, the stem base still remains on the branch.
### 斯蒂芬 → 蒂 → 艸
- **definition:** grass
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **components:**
  - 屮
    - chè
    - sprout; shoot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram – grass blades; duplication of 屮 (“grass”).
  - **interpretation:** Shows multiple blades sprouting by doubling 屮, evoking a patch of grass or vegetation. The graphic later served as the grass radical atop many characters and relates to the original top of 春 in its early form (萅).
  - **simplification:** As a component it was standardized to the two-stroke 艹 for stroke economy and print clarity; as an independent word in modern Chinese, it is replaced by 草.
- **examples:**
  - 古文中常写作“艸”，今作“草”(古文中常寫作「艸」，今作「草」。)
    - Gǔwén zhōng cháng xiězuò “cǎo”, jīn zuò “cǎo”.
    - In classical texts it was often written as “艸”, while today it is written “草”.
  - 春天里，草木茂盛(春天裡，艸木茂盛)。
    - Chūntiān lǐ, cǎomù màoshèng.
    - In spring, plants flourish.
  - 农夫割草(農夫割艸)。
    - Nóngfū gē cǎo.
    - The farmer cuts the grass.
### 斯蒂芬 → 蒂 → 艸 → 屮
- **definition:** sprout; shoot
- **pinyin:** chè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): a sprout emerging from the ground.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a shoot pushing up through the soil: the horizontal stroke suggests the ground, while the central vertical stroke and side strokes evoke the stem and small leaves. Early oracle and bronze forms closely resemble a budding plant.
- **examples:**
  - 在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽(在甲骨文中，屮表示植物的嫩芽)。
    - Zài jiǎgǔwén zhōng, chè biǎoshì zhíwù de nènyá.
    - In oracle script, 屮 represents a plant sprout.
  - 春天雨后，田里冒出许多嫩芽，像“屮”的形状(春天雨後，田裡冒出許多嫩芽，像「屮」的形狀)。
    - Chūntiān yǔ hòu, tián lǐ mào chū xǔduō nènyá, xiàng “chè” de xíngzhuàng.
    - After spring rains, many sprouts emerge in the fields, resembling the shape of “屮”.
  - 屮在古文字中象征嫩芽与新生(屮在古文字中象徵嫩芽與新生)。
    - Chè zài gǔ wénzì zhōng xiàngzhēng nènyá yǔ xīnshēng.
    - In ancient scripts, 屮 symbolizes sprouts and new life.
### 斯蒂芬 → 蒂 → 帝
- **definition:** emperor
- **pinyin:** dì
- **components:**
  - 上
    - shàng
    - up; above
  - 朿
    - cì
    - thorn; prickle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: earliest use denoted the “God of Heaven”; later an imperial title. Shuowen (erroneously) analyzed it as semantic 上 + phonetic 朿.
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a divine or celestial ruler, which is why its earliest sense is the ‘God of Heaven’. Over time it broadened to mean an earthly emperor; the Shuowen’s reading as 上 plus 朿 is a later misinterpretation rather than its true formation.
- **examples:**
  - 秦始皇称帝(秦始皇稱帝)。
    - Qín Shǐhuáng chēng dì.
    - Qin Shi Huang proclaimed himself emperor.
  - 人们祭祀天帝(人們祭祀天帝)。
    - Rénmen jìsì Tiāndì.
    - People worship the Heavenly Emperor.
  - 罗马帝国的皇帝享有至高权力(羅馬帝國的皇帝享有至高權力)。
    - Luómǎ Dìguó de huángdì xiǎngyǒu zhìgāo quánlì.
    - The emperors of the Roman Empire held supreme power.
### 斯蒂芬 → 蒂 → 帝 → 上
- **definition:** up; above
- **pinyin:** shàng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** Ideogram: a line above another—‘up’; originally 𠄞, later changed to avoid confusion with 二.
  - **interpretation:** A short stroke sits above a longer baseline, picturing something positioned over another. In contrast with 下, it indicates upward or superior position. The form was standardized to distinguish it clearly from 二.
- **examples:**
  - 书在桌子上(書在桌子上)。
    - Shū zài zhuōzi shàng.
    - The book is on the table.
  - 我们上楼去(我們上樓去)。
    - Wǒmen shàng lóu qù.
    - Let's go upstairs.
  - 上课时(上課時)，请把手机放在桌上(請把手機放在桌上)。
    - Shàng kè shí, qǐng bǎ shǒujī fàng zài zhuō shàng.
    - During class, please put your phone on the desk.
### 斯蒂芬 → 蒂 → 帝 → 朿

  - **interpretation:** Depicts a trunk with protruding spikes, conveying the idea of something that pricks. Some scholars see a multi-pronged spear, but both readings emphasize sharp points that pierce.

    - Gǔrén yǐ cì bǐyù xiǎo ér jiān de shānghài.
    - The ancients used ‘thorn’ metaphorically for small, sharp harms.

### 斯蒂芬 → 芬
- **definition:** fragrance; perfume; aromatic; elegant
- **pinyin:** fēn
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass; plants
  - 分
    - fēn
    - divide; part; minute
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 艸 (“grass”) + phonetic 分
  - **interpretation:** Combines the grass element with the sound of 分 to signal a plant-related word associated with aroma. The grassy semantic hints at herbs and scents, while 分 contributes the fēn sound and helps distinguish pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 花园里空气很芬芳(花園裡空氣很芬芳)。
    - Huāyuán lǐ kōngqì hěn fēnfāng.

  - 这款香水散发出淡淡的芬芳(這款香水散發出淡淡的芬芳)。
    - Zhè kuǎn xiāngshuǐ sànfā chū dàndàn de fēnfāng.
    - This perfume gives off a light fragrance.
  - 茶叶一入热水，屋里便充满了芬芳(茶葉一入熱水，屋裡便充滿了芬芳)。
    - Cháyè yī rù rèshuǐ, wū lǐ biàn chōngmǎn le fēnfāng.
    - Once the tea leaves go into hot water, the room fills with fragrance.
### 斯蒂芬 → 芬 → 分
- **definition:** divide; part; minute
- **pinyin:** fēn
- **components:**
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound (会意)
  - **description:** semantic: 八 ‘to separate’ + semantic: 刀 ‘knife’ — separate with a knife
  - **interpretation:** Combines 八, depicting separation or splitting, with 刀, a knife, to express cutting something apart. This core idea underlies senses like dividing, apportioning, and by extension small fractional units such as minutes and cents.
- **examples:**
  - 我们把蛋糕分开了(我們把蛋糕分開了)。
    - Wǒmen bǎ dàngāo fēnkāi le.
    - We divided the cake.
  - 现在是三点十分(現在是三點十分)。
    - Xiànzài shì sān diǎn shí fēn.
    - It is 3:10.
  - 他只给了我一分钱(他只給了我一分錢)。
    - Tā zhǐ gěi le wǒ yì fēnqián.
    - He only gave me one cent.
### 斯蒂芬 → 芬 → 分 → 八
- **definition:** eight
- **pinyin:** bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: two bent lines indicating “to divide”; later borrowed to mean “eight” via homophony (original sense now in 分, 別).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two strokes diverging to suggest splitting or separation. Later became a rebus for the number eight because it shared the same sound, while the original sense lived on in 分 and 別. Not the “two hands” component found in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我有八本书(我有八本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu bā běn shū.
    - I have eight books.
  - 她八岁了(她八歲了)。
    - Tā bā suì le.
    - She is eight years old.
  - 我买了八个苹果(我買了八個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le bā gè píngguǒ.
    - I bought eight apples.
### 斯蒂芬 → 芬 → 分 → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — a knife; original form 𠚣, seen in a variant of 利 (𥝢) and atop 黎.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a knife with a distinct blade and handle. The graph was streamlined into the current two-stroke form and also serves as a component in characters like 利 and 黎.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿着一把刀(他拿著一把刀)。
    - Tā názhe yì bǎ dāo.

  - 这把刀很锋利(這把刀很鋒利)。
    - Zhè bǎ dāo hěn fēnglì.
