## doubanjiang; chili bean paste
### dòubànjiàng
---
- **definition:** doubanjiang; chili bean paste
- **pinyin:** dòubànjiàng
- **components:**
  - 豆
    - dòu
    - bean; legumes; ancient food vessel; small bean-shaped thing
  - 瓣
    - bàn
    - petal; segment; clove; half
  - 酱(醬)
    - jiàng
    - sauce; paste; thick soy/bean paste; condiment
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 豆(bean) + 瓣(segment/split) + 酱(sauce/paste) = paste made from split beans (often broad beans), typically with chili.
  - **interpretation:** Literally “bean-segment sauce,” referring to a thick fermented paste made from split broad beans; in Sichuan cuisine it commonly includes chili, hence ‘chili bean paste’.
  - **simplification:** Only 醬 was simplified to 酱, replacing the complex phonetic 將 with 将 under the 酉 ‘fermentation/wine’ radical to reduce strokes and ease writing; 豆 and 瓣 are unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 这道鱼香肉丝要用一勺豆瓣酱(這道魚香肉絲要用一勺豆瓣醬)。
    - Zhè dào yúxiāng ròusī yào yòng yì sháo dòubànjiàng.
    - This yuxiang shredded pork needs a spoonful of doubanjiang.
  - 四川人常用豆瓣酱炒菜(四川人常用豆瓣醬炒菜)。
    - Sìchuān rén cháng yòng dòubànjiàng chǎo cài.
    - People in Sichuan often stir-fry with doubanjiang.
  - 我喜欢把豆瓣酱拌进面条里(我喜歡把豆瓣醬拌進麵條裡)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan bǎ dòubànjiàng bàn jìn miàntiáo lǐ.
    - I like to mix doubanjiang into my noodles.

### 豆瓣酱(豆瓣醬) → 酱(醬)
- **definition:** sauce; paste; thick soy/bean paste; condiment
- **pinyin:** jiàng
- **components:**
  - 酉
    - yǒu
    - wine; alcohol; the 10th Earthly Branch
  - 将(將)
    - jiāng
    - to lead; will; about to; take (coverb)
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 酉 + phonetic: 将(將)
  - **interpretation:** 酉 signals fermentation, alcoholic liquids, and by extension condiments, pointing to the meaning of a fermented sauce. 将/將 contributes the sound jiang and helps form the word family, while the overall structure keeps the flavor-related sense.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by replacing the phonetic 將 with its simplified form 将 (⿰丬夕), reducing strokes while preserving pronunciation and the original phono-semantic structure.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢吃辣酱(我喜歡吃辣醬)。
    - Wǒ xǐhuan chī làjiàng.
    - I like to eat spicy sauce.

    - She tossed the noodles with doubanjiang.
  - 这道菜需要加一点酱油(這道菜需要加一點醬油)。
    - Zhè dào cài xūyào jiā yīdiǎn jiàngyóu.
    - This dish needs a little soy sauce.

    - The span from Mao to You represents the east–west direction.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
