## run; jog
### pǎobù
---
- **definition:** run; jog
- **pinyin:** pǎobù
- **components:**
  - 跑
    - pǎo
    - run; flee; run about; leak (gas)
  - 步
    - bù
    - step; pace; walk; stage
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** verb-object compound
  - **description:** 跑 + 步 = run + steps → to run/jog.
  - **interpretation:** Combines the action 'run' with 'steps' to emphasize moving by taking running steps. Often behaves as a separable verb-object (离合词).
- **examples:**
  - 我每天早上跑步半小时(我每天早上跑步半小時)。
    - Wǒ měitiān zǎoshang pǎobù bàn xiǎoshí.
    - I jog for half an hour every morning.
  - 医生建议我饭后慢慢跑步(醫生建議我飯後慢慢跑步)。
    - Yīshēng jiànyì wǒ fànhòu mànman pǎobù.
    - The doctor suggests that I jog slowly after meals.
  - 他不喜欢在雨天跑步(他不喜歡在雨天跑步)，所以改在室内锻炼(所以改在室內鍛鍊)。
    - Tā bù xǐhuan zài yǔtiān pǎobù, suǒyǐ gǎi zài shìnèi duànliàn.
    - He doesn't like running on rainy days, so he switched to exercising indoors.
### 跑步 → 跑
- **definition:** run; flee; run about; leak (gas)
- **pinyin:** pǎo
- **components:**
  - 足
    - zú
    - foot
  - 包
    - bāo
    - wrap; bag
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 足 (“foot”) + phonetic: 包
  - **interpretation:** Left component 足 ‘foot’ signals meaning related to movement and actions done with the feet, such as running. Right component 包 serves as the phonetic, guiding the pronunciation toward pǎo. Together they point to senses like running or fleeing.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天早上去公园跑步(我每天早上去公園跑步)。
    - Wǒ měitiān zǎoshang qù gōngyuán pǎobù.
    - I go running in the park every morning.
  - 孩子害怕大狗(孩子害怕大狗)，转身就跑(轉身就跑)。
    - Háizi hàipà dà gǒu, zhuǎnshēn jiù pǎo.
    - The child was afraid of the big dog and immediately ran away.
  - 管道跑气了(管道跑氣了)，大家赶紧往外跑(大家趕緊往外跑)。
    - Guǎndào pǎo qì le, dàjiā gǎnjǐn wǎng wài pǎo.
    - The pipe is leaking gas; everyone hurry and run outside.
### 跑步 → 跑 → 足
- **definition:** foot
- **pinyin:** zú
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) of a leg; similar to ⽦. See also 𠯣, pointing to 之 as a footprint.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a leg with the bend as the knee and the base as the foot, a straightforward image-to-meaning link. The reference to 之 as a footprint underscores its association with walking and feet.
- **examples:**
  - 他赤足走在沙滩上(他赤足走在沙灘上)。
    - Tā chìzú zǒu zài shātān shàng.
    - He walked barefoot on the beach.
  - 地上留下了很多足迹(地上留下了很多足跡)。
    - Dì shàng liú xià le hěn duō zújì.
    - Many footprints were left on the ground.
  - 医生检查他的足部(醫生檢查他的足部)。
    - Yīshēng jiǎnchá tā de zúbù.
    - The doctor examined his foot.
### 跑步 → 跑 → 包
- **definition:** wrap; bag
- **pinyin:** bāo
- **components:**
  - 巳
    - sì
    - 6th Earthly Branch; zodiac sign Snake
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形): a fetus (巳 sì) in the womb (勹), or a swaddled baby.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a swaddled infant or a curled fetus enclosed by a wrapper, conveying enclosure and bundling. The image naturally extends to actions and objects for wrapping, hence meanings like “wrap,” “package,” and “bag.”
- **examples:**
  - 我买了一个背包(我買了一個背包)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le yī gè bèibāo.
    - I bought a backpack.
  - 请把礼物包起来(請把禮物包起來)。
    - Qǐng bǎ lǐwù bāo qǐlái.
    - Please wrap the gift.
  - 快递已经把包裹送到了(快遞已經把包裹送到了)。
    - Kuàidì yǐjīng bǎ bāoguǒ sòng dào le.
    - The courier has already delivered the package.
### 跑步 → 跑 → 包 → 巳
- **definition:** 6th Earthly Branch; zodiac sign Snake
- **pinyin:** sì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram (象形): coiled snake; an alternate theory analyzes it as a foetus; graph similar to 已.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a coiled serpent, which aligns with its use as the sixth Earthly Branch associated with the Snake. Its visual similarity to 已/己 has led to historical confusion and variant explanations of the graph.
- **examples:**
  - 现在是巳时(現在是巳時)。
    - Xiànzài shì sì shí.
    - It is the Si hour (9–11 a.m.).
  - 他属蛇，对应的地支是巳(他屬蛇，對應的地支是巳)。
    - Tā shǔ shé, duìyìng de dìzhī shì sì.
    - He is of the Snake sign; the corresponding Earthly Branch is Si.
  - 农历常用十二地支记时，其中之一是巳(農曆常用十二地支記時，其中之一是巳)。
    - Nónglì cháng yòng shí’èr dìzhī jì shí, qízhōng zhī yī shì sì.
    - The lunar calendar often uses the twelve Earthly Branches to mark hours; one of them is Si.
### 跑步 → 步
- **definition:** step; pace; walk; stage
- **pinyin:** bù
- **components:**
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - stop; halt
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** 止 (“foot”) + reversed 止 — one foot in front of the other.
  - **interpretation:** Two feet are shown, one flipped to suggest alternating movement. This imagery conveys walking and, by extension, a step or pace.
- **examples:**
  - 他向前迈出一步(他向前邁出一步)。
    - Tā xiàng qián màichū yí bù.
    - He took a step forward.
  - 请放慢脚步(請放慢腳步)。
    - Qǐng fàngmàn jiǎobù.
    - Please slow your pace.
  - 项目分为三个阶段，每一步都很重要(項目分為三個階段，每一步都很重要)。
    - Xiàngmù fēn wéi sān gè jiēduàn, měi yí bù dōu hěn zhòngyào.
    - The project is divided into three stages, and each step is important.
### 跑步 → 步 → 止
- **definition:** stop; halt
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: a footprint pointing up (originally “foot”), later stylized as 龰; oracle forms show three toes and a sole.
  - **interpretation:** A footprint with toes pointing upward represents the foot. The sense broadened from the physical foot to the act of halting one’s step, hence “to stop”; the specific ‘toe’ meaning was preserved in 趾.
- **examples:**
  - 请立即停止前进(請立即停止前進)。
    - Qǐng lìjí tíngzhǐ qiánjìn.
    - Please stop advancing immediately.
  - 此处止步，非请勿入(此處止步，非請勿入)。
    - Cǐ chù zhǐbù, fēi qǐng wù rù.
    - Stop here; do not enter without invitation.
  - 药到病除，疼痛已止(藥到病除，疼痛已止)。
    - Yào dào bìng chú, téngtòng yǐ zhǐ.
    - With the right medicine, the illness is cured and the pain has stopped.
