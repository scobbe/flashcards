## bath towel
### yùjīn
---
- **definition:** bath towel
- **pinyin:** yùjīn
- **components:**
  - 浴
    - yù
    - bathe; to take a bath; to wash; to immerse
  - 巾
    - jīn
    - towel; cloth; kerchief; headscarf
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 浴 (bathe) + 巾 (cloth/towel) = towel used for bathing.
  - **interpretation:** Pairs the act of bathing with a piece of cloth to specify its function: a towel for use during or after bathing. A purpose-driven verb–noun compound.
- **examples:**
  - 洗完澡(洗完澡)，我用浴巾擦干身体(我用浴巾擦乾身體)。
    - Xǐ wán zǎo, wǒ yòng yùjīn cā gān shēntǐ.
    - After showering, I dry my body with a bath towel.
  - 浴室里有备用的浴巾(浴室裡有備用的浴巾)。
    - Yùshì lǐ yǒu bèiyòng de yùjīn.
    - There are spare bath towels in the bathroom.
  - 请把干净的浴巾递给我(請把乾淨的浴巾遞給我)。
    - Qǐng bǎ gānjìng de yùjīn dì gěi wǒ.
    - Please hand me a clean bath towel.
### 浴巾 → 浴
- **definition:** bathe; to take a bath; to wash; to immerse
- **pinyin:** yù
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 谷
    - gǔ
    - valley
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 氵 + phonetic: 谷
  - **interpretation:** Water element signals a liquid-related action, while 谷 contributes the sound, aligning with historical -uk/-ok rimes. Together they point to washing or immersing the body in water.
- **examples:**
  - 我们在温泉里沐浴(我們在溫泉裡沐浴)。
    - Wǒmen zài wēnquán lǐ mùyù.
    - We bathe in the hot springs.
  - 他走进浴室准备洗澡(他走進浴室準備洗澡)。
    - Tā zǒu jìn yùshì zhǔnbèi xǐzǎo.
    - He walks into the bathroom to get ready to bathe.
  - 我更喜欢淋浴而不是泡澡(我更喜歡淋浴而不是泡澡)。
    - Wǒ gèng xǐhuan línyù ér bú shì pào zǎo.
    - I prefer taking a shower rather than a soak.
### 浴巾 → 浴 → 水
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram of water flowing; sometimes written as the variant 氺; next to a character it is compressed and stylized into 氵 (unrelated to 冫).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts flowing water with a main stream and side droplets. In compound characters it condenses to the left-side form 氵 to signal a water-related meaning, distinct from 冫 which indicates ice or cold.
- **examples:**
  - 我想喝一杯水(我想喝一杯水)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng hē yì bēi shuǐ.
    - I want to drink a glass of water.
  - 城市的自来水很干净(城市的自來水很乾淨)。
    - Chéngshì de zìláishuǐ hěn gānjìng.
    - The tap water in the city is very clean.
  - 旱季来了(旱季來了)，我们要节约用水(我們要節約用水)。
    - Hànjì lái le, wǒmen yào jiéyuē yòngshuǐ.
    - The dry season has arrived; we should conserve water.
### 浴巾 → 浴 → 谷
- **definition:** valley
- **pinyin:** gǔ
- **components:**
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth; opening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram — valley (口) between two mountain walls shown as near-parallel lines; original form 𠔌.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a valley opening between two slopes, with the central opening like a mouth. The earlier form 𠔌 makes the two walls and the gap more explicit, later stylized into the current graph.
- **examples:**
  - 我们在山谷里露营(我們在山谷裡露營)。
    - Wǒmen zài shāngǔ lǐ lùyíng.
    - We camped in the valley.
  - 这条河穿过狭窄的谷地(這條河穿過狹窄的谷地)。
    - Zhè tiáo hé chuānguò xiázhǎi de gǔdì.
    - This river passes through a narrow valley.
  - 回声在谷中回荡(回聲在谷中回盪)。
    - Huíshēng zài gǔ zhōng huídàng.
    - Echoes resounded in the valley.
### 浴巾 → 浴 → 谷 → 八
- **definition:** eight
- **pinyin:** bā
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram: two bent lines indicating “to divide”; later borrowed to mean “eight” via homophony (original sense now in 分, 別).
  - **interpretation:** Depicts two strokes diverging to suggest splitting or separation. Later became a rebus for the number eight because it shared the same sound, while the original sense lived on in 分 and 別. Not the “two hands” component found in other characters.
- **examples:**
  - 我有八本书(我有八本書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu bā běn shū.
    - I have eight books.
  - 她八岁了(她八歲了)。
    - Tā bā suì le.
    - She is eight years old.
  - 我买了八个苹果(我買了八個蘋果)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le bā gè píngguǒ.
    - I bought eight apples.
### 浴巾 → 浴 → 谷 → 口
- **definition:** mouth; opening
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – resembles an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** A simple square outlines the opening of a mouth, with the empty center representing the oral cavity. It later became a common semantic component indicating speech, sounds, or openings, and in early compounds sometimes marked abstract meanings via association or borrowing.
- **examples:**
  - 他张开口(他張開口)，说了几句(說了幾句)。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, shuō le jǐ jù.
    - He opened his mouth and said a few sentences.
  - 一家三口住在这个小城(一家三口住在這個小城)。
    - Yì jiā sān kǒu zhù zài zhège xiǎo chéng.
    - A family of three lives in this small town.
  - 她咬了一口苹果(她咬了一口蘋果)。
    - Tā yǎo le yì kǒu píngguǒ.
    - She took a bite of the apple.
### 浴巾 → 巾
- **definition:** towel; cloth; kerchief; headscarf
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Pictogram (象形) – hanging piece of cloth.
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a cloth draped and hanging, with the long stroke as the edge and the shorter strokes suggesting the folds. The image conveys a towel or piece of fabric suspended from a line or rod.
- **examples:**
  - 我用湿毛巾擦脸(我用濕毛巾擦臉)。
    - Wǒ yòng shī máojīn cā liǎn.
    - I use a wet towel to wipe my face.
  - 她戴着红色的头巾(她戴著紅色的頭巾)。
    - Tā dài zhe hóngsè de tóujīn.
    - She is wearing a red headscarf.
  - 冬天出门要围上围巾(冬天出門要圍上圍巾)。
    - Dōngtiān chūmén yào wéi shàng wéijīn.
    - In winter, put on a scarf before going out.
