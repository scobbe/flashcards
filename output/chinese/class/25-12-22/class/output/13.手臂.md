- **definition:** arm
- **pinyin:** shǒu bì
- **components:**
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
  - 臂
    - bì

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 手 (shǒu, "hand") + 臂 (bì, "arm") =
    - hand-arm ->
    - arm
  - **interpretation:** The word 手臂(shǒubì) combines 手 (shǒu, "hand") and 臂 (bì, "arm") to describe the whole arm, emphasizing the idea of the arm being an extension of the hand.

### 手臂 → 臂
- **definition:** arm
- **pinyin:** bì
- **components:**
  - 辟
    - bì
    - law
  - 肉
    - ròu

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic: 辟 (bì, "law") + Semantic: 肉 (ròu, "meat; flesh") ->
    - represents part of the body made of flesh ->
    - denotes an 'arm'
  - **interpretation:** The character 臂(bì, "arm") uses 辟(bì, "law") for its phonetic component and 肉(ròu, "meat; flesh") as the semantic element to indicate a part of the body that is fleshy, thus referring to an 'arm'.

### 手臂 → 臂 → 辟
- **definition:** law
- **pinyin:** bì
- **components:**
  - 卩
    - jié

  - 䇂
    - qū
    - sickle-like tool
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 卩 (kneeling person) + 䇂 (sickle-like tool) ->
    - administer; regulate; punish
  - **interpretation:** The character 辟(bì, 'distinguish') originally combined 卩 (a kneeling person) and 䇂 (a sickle-like tool) to convey the act of administration or regulation, possibly hinting at the implementation of justice or punishment. Over time, 〇 was added but its role is debated, with some suggesting a phonetic or decorative function. The earlier form's focus on law enforcement leads directly to the modern sense of differentiation through regulation.

### 手臂 → 臂 → 辟 → 卩
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.

### 手臂 → 臂 → 辟 → 䇂
- **definition:** sickle-like tool
- **pinyin:** qū
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a chisel or sickle-like tool with a sharp point in the middle, used for marking
  - **interpretation:** The character 䇂 originally represented a tool with a pointed tip, embodying the function of marking or inscribing, akin to a chisel used for branding prisoners. This shape conveys not only the appearance but also the purpose of this ancient implement.
