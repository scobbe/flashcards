## cannot afford
### mǎi bù qǐ
---
- **traditional:** 買不起
- **definition:** cannot afford
- **pinyin:** mǎi bù qǐ
- **components:**
  - 买(買)
    - mǎi
    - buy
  - 不起
    - bù qǐ
    - cannot; fail to
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 買 (mǎi, "buy") and 起 (qǐ, "to rise; to begin") together express the concept of being unable to afford something. 起 in this context relates to the idea of 'affordable' as if initiating or starting an action, with buying power.
  - **interpretation:** 買 (mǎi, "buy") indicates the action of purchasing. 起 (qǐ, "to rise; to begin") suggests capability or potential in a different context, such as starting an action. The combination of 買 and 起 with the negative marker 不 (bù, "not") conveys the specific inability to 'rise to the occasion' of buying, effectively meaning 'cannot afford'. In the figurative sense, the rising action represents the capability to afford something.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)些( )衣( )服( )太( )贵(貴)了( )，我( )买(買)不( )起( )。
    - Zhèxiē yīfu tài guì le, wǒ mǎi bù qǐ.
