## waiting
### děng zhe
---
- **traditional:** 等著
- **definition:** waiting
- **pinyin:** děng zhe
- **components:**
  - 等
    - děng
    - wait; rank
  - 着(著)
    - zhe
    - -ing; to touch
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 等 (děng, "wait; rank") suggests someone is pausing or anticipating an event or person. 著 (zhe, "-ing; to touch") is a verb suffix indicating a continuous state of an action, similar to the English '-ing'.
  - **interpretation:** 等 (děng, "wait") implies a period of patience or anticipation, as when pausing before an expected action. 著 (zhe, "-ing") transforms this into the continuous aspect, like 'waiting', emphasizing the ongoing nature of the wait. Together, they convey someone actively in the process of waiting.
- **examples:**
  - 我在车(車)站等着(著)你。
    - Wǒ zài chēzhàn děng zhe nǐ.
    - I am waiting for you at the station.
  - 他等着(著)电(電)话(話)铃(鈴)响(響)。
    - Tā děng zhe diànhuà líng xiǎng.
    - He is waiting for the phone to ring.
  - 我们(們)等着(著)开(開)会(會)。
    - Wǒmen děng zhe kāihuì.
    - We are waiting for the meeting to start.

- **examples:**

### 等着(著) → 着(著)
- **traditional:** 著
- **definition:** -ing; to touch
- **pinyin:** zhe
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass; plant
  - 者
    - zhě
    - person who
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艸 (grass; plant) + phonetic 者 (zhě, 'person who') ->
    - wear, put on; then corrupted in 着: 艸 → 䒑, 日 → 目
  - **interpretation:** Originally, 著 was a phono-semantic compound with 艸 (indicating connection to 'clothing' or 'decoration' over the body) and 者 as its phonetic part, relating to 'acting' or 'performing an action'. It developed into usage for 'wear' or 'put on' clothes, often associated with the touch and sensation. 着 as a variant with 目 (eye) broadens it to imply the state of being observable or noticed, thus extending to the continuous aspect in grammar, marking actions in progress.
  - **simplification:** 着 is a corrupted form of 著 where 艸 became 䒑, and 日 became 目, reflecting a historical scribal alteration often seen in informal or distinct textual contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 他正看着(著)书(書)。
    - Tā zhèng kànzhe shū.
    - He is reading a book.
  - 小猫(貓)睡着(著)了。
    - Xiǎo māo shuìzháo le.
    - The kitten is asleep.
  - 她穿着(著)一件蓝(藍)色的外套。
    - Tā chuānzhe yī jiàn lán sè de wàitào.
    - She is wearing a blue coat.
