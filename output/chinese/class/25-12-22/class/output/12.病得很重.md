## seriously ill
### bìng de hěn zhòng
---
- **definition:** seriously ill
- **pinyin:** bìng de hěn zhòng
- **components:**
  - 病
    - bìng
    - illness; disease; to fall ill
  - 得
    - de
    - structural particle
  - 很
    - hěn
    - very; quite
  - 重
    - zhòng
    - heavy; important
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 病 (bìng, "illness") + 得 (de, "structural particle") + 很 (hěn, "very") + 重 (zhòng, "heavy") together form the phrase, indicating a severe state of illness.
  - **interpretation:** 病 (bìng, "illness") refers to a state of being unwell. 得 (de, "structural particle") is used after an adjective to form a complement indicating the degree or manner of the preceding action or state. 很 (hěn, "very") intensifies the adjective following it. 重 (zhòng, "heavy") conveys the seriousness or gravity of the condition. Together, '病得很重' literally describes an illness that is 'very heavy,' metaphorically indicating a severe or serious illness.
- **examples:**
  - 他病得很重，需要住院治疗(療)。
    - Tā bìng de hěn zhòng, xūyào zhùyuàn zhìliáo.
    - He is seriously ill and needs to be hospitalized for treatment.
  - 医(醫)生说(說)她病得很重，必须(須)马(馬)上送去急诊(診)。
    - Yīshēng shuō tā bìng de hěn zhòng, bìxū mǎshàng sòng qù jízhěn.
    - The doctor said she is seriously ill and must be sent to the emergency room immediately.
  - 他的父亲(親)病得很重，因此家人非常担(擔)心。
    - Tā de fùqīn bìng de hěn zhòng, yīncǐ jiārén fēicháng dānxīn.
    - His father is seriously ill, so the family is very worried.

### 病得很重 → 得
- **definition:** structural particle
- **pinyin:** de
- **components:**
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry
  - 又
    - yòu
    - hand
  - 彳
    - chì
    - step
  - 寸
    - cùn
    - inch
  - 旦
    - dàn
    - dawn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 貝 (bèi, 'cowry') + 又 (yòu, 'hand') ->
    - obtaining valuables ->
    - 彳 (chì, 'step') added, 又 changed to 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), 貝 corrupted to 旦 (dàn, 'dawn') ->
    - final form
  - **interpretation:** The character originated as a depiction of a hand picking up a cowry, symbolizing 'obtaining' valuables. The 彳 (chì, 'step') was added to suggest movement on a road, enhancing the idea of acquisition. Over time, script evolutions saw substitute elements: the component 又 (yòu, 'hand') changed to 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), reflecting the Qin script's tendency, and 貝 (bèi, 'cowry') was simplified to 旦 (dàn, 'dawn'). These changes led to the character's modern form. This transition mirrors the evolved meanings in usage: 'to get', from obtaining something physically; 'must', representing the necessity of movement or action; and its role as a structural particle in grammar for completion or necessity in expressions.
  - **simplification:** In simplified form, 得 remains unchanged from its traditional representation.
- **examples:**
  - 他跑得很快。
    - Tā pǎo de hěn kuài.
    - He runs very fast.
  - 我得去工作。
    - Wǒ děi qù gōngzuò.
    - I have to go to work.
  - 她昨天赢(贏)得了比赛(賽)。
    - Tā zuótiān yíngdé le bǐsài.
    - She won the competition yesterday.
