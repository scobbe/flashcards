## seriously ill
### bìng de hěn zhòng
---
- **definition:** seriously ill
- **pinyin:** bìng de hěn zhòng
- **components:**
  - 病
    - bìng
    - illness; disease; to fall ill
  - 得
    - de
    - structural particle
  - 很
    - hěn
    - very; quite
  - 重
    - zhòng
    - heavy; important
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 病 (bìng, "illness") + 得 (de, "structural particle") + 很 (hěn, "very") + 重 (zhòng, "heavy") together form the phrase, indicating a severe state of illness.
  - **interpretation:** 病 (bìng, "illness") refers to a state of being unwell. 得 (de, "structural particle") is used after an adjective to form a complement indicating the degree or manner of the preceding action or state. 很 (hěn, "very") intensifies the adjective following it. 重 (zhòng, "heavy") conveys the seriousness or gravity of the condition. Together, '病得很重' literally describes an illness that is 'very heavy,' metaphorically indicating a severe or serious illness.

  - **interpretation:** The character originated as a depiction of a hand picking up a cowry, symbolizing 'obtaining' valuables. These changes led to the character's modern form. This transition mirrors the evolved meanings in usage: 'to get', from obtaining something physically; 'must', representing the necessity of movement or action; and its role as a structural particle in grammar for completion or necessity in expressions.
  - **simplification:** In simplified form, 得 remains unchanged from its traditional representation.

- **definition:** heavy; important
- **pinyin:** zhòng
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - man
  - 東
    - dōng
    - bag
  - 土
    - tǔ
    - earth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 人 (rén, "man") + 東 (dōng, "bag") =
    - a man carrying a bag ->
    - 土 (tǔ, "earth") added ->
    - symbol of load ->
    - final form
  - **interpretation:** Originally, it depicts a man carrying a bag, indicating the concept of weight. With 土 (tǔ, "earth") added to signify being grounded, it amplifies the idea of heaviness due to the man's connection to the ground or earth, suggesting substantial weight or burden. This visual representation naturally evolved into "heavy", as a person burdened by earth is weighed down.

### 病得很重 → 重 → 東
- **definition:** bag
- **pinyin:** dōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bundle or bag tied at both ends
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicting a bundle or bag tied at both ends, it suggests convergence or collection, visually resembling a tied shape. This pictorial representation is historically connected to the direction 'east', as a bag captures and holds items, analogous to the rising sun being 'held' in the east. Despite alternative interpretations involving the sun and tree, the pictogram of a bundle remains the most direct visual link.
- **examples:**
  - 他的東里(裏)装(裝)满(滿)了( )书(書)。
