## count it
### shǔ yīxià
---
- **traditional:** 數一下
- **definition:** count it
- **pinyin:** shǔ yīxià
- **components:**
  - 數
    - shǔ
    - count; number; several
  - 一下
    - yīxià
    - a bit; for a moment; briefly
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 數 (shǔ, "count") suggests the act of enumerating or calculating something, while 一下 (yīxià, "a bit; for a moment") refers to doing something quickly or briefly, often for a short duration or an experiment.
  - **interpretation:** 數 (shǔ, "count") specifically refers to the action of counting items or making calculations. 一下 (yīxià, "a bit; for a moment") contributes the sense of immediacy or briefness, implying the counting is done quickly or as a test. Together, the phrase suggests doing a quick count.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)你数(數)一下库(庫)存。
    - Qǐng nǐ shǔ yīxià kùcún.
    - Please count the inventory.
  - 孩子们(們)正在数(數)一下他们(們)的糖果。
    - Háizimen zhèngzài shǔ yīxià tāmen de tángguǒ.
    - The children are counting their candies.
  - 老师(師)让(讓)我们(們)数(數)一下书(書)本的数(數)量。
    - Lǎoshī ràng wǒmen shǔ yīxià shūběn de shùliàng.
    - The teacher asked us to count the number of books.
### 数(數)一下 → 數
- **definition:** count; number; several
- **pinyin:** shǔ
- **components:**
  - 婁
    - lǒu
    - a surname
  - 攴
    - pū
    - tap
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 婁 (lǒu) + semantic 攴 (pū, "tap") ->
    - count; number; several
  - **interpretation:** The character 數 combines the phonetic 婁(lǒu) with the semantic 攴 (pū, "tap"), suggesting an action involving counting or enumerating, akin to tapping items or keeping track of numbers. This leads to its meaning of 'count' and 'number'. Over time, the sense extended to 'several,' referring to a count of multiple items.
  - **simplification:** 數 was simplified to 数 by replacing 婁 (lǒu, "a surname") with its simpler variant 娄(婁).
- **examples:**
  - 我开(開)始数(數)星星。
    - Wǒ kāishǐ shǔ xīngxīng.
    - I started counting the stars.
  - 这(這)几(幾)天，我有很多事情要处(處)理。
    - Zhè jǐ tiān, wǒ yǒu hěn duō shìqíng yào chǔlǐ.
    - In the past few days, I have had many things to handle.
  - 数(數)一数(數)，看看我们(們)有多少苹(蘋)果。
    - Shǔ yī shǔ, kàn kàn wǒmen yǒu duōshǎo píngguǒ.
    - Count and see how many apples we have.

Over time, its representation evolved into an ideogrammic compound with 母(母, "mother"), 中 suggesting centrality or encompassing, and 女(女, "daughter"), potentially symbolizing the concept of bringing things together or bundling, which fits its meaning as 'bundle'.

### 数(數)一下 → 數 → 婁 → 母
- **definition:** mother
- **pinyin:** mǔ
- **components:**
  - 女
    - nǚ
    - woman
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** differentiated form
  - **description:**
    - 女 (nǚ, "woman") + addition of two dots ->
    - distinguished as 母 (mǔ, "mother")
  - **interpretation:** The character 母 (mǔ, "mother") is a differentiated form of 女 (nǚ, "woman") with the addition of two dots to distinguish it. In early script forms, both 母 (mǔ, "mother") and 女 (nǚ, "woman") were represented by the same form as 女, necessitating the addition of dots to create a visual distinction. The character 毋, originally borrowed from 母, further specialized by changing the dots to a stroke to avoid confusion between the characters.
- **examples:**
  - 母给(給)了我无(無)限的温(溫)暖。
    - mǔ gěi le wǒ wúxiàn de wēnnuǎn
    - Mother has given me endless warmth.
  - 我的母温(溫)柔而坚(堅)毅，培养(養)了我良好的品格。
    - wǒ de mǔ wēnróu ér jiānyì, péiyǎng le wǒ liánghǎo de pǐngé
    - My mother is gentle and strong, nurturing my good character.

### 数(數)一下 → 一下
- **definition:** a bit; for a moment; briefly
- **pinyin:** yīxià
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; single; whole
  - 下
    - xià
    - down; below; short of; next
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** In this word, 一 (yī, "one") is used to signify a single or brief occurrence, while 下 (xià, "down; below") suggests a short interval. Together, 一下 (yíxià, "a moment") conveys the idea of something happening briefly or temporarily.
  - **interpretation:** The sense 'a moment; a while' emerges because 一 (yī, "one") implies a single or specific instance, and 下 (xià, "down; below") indicates a downward transition or brief action. The combination suggests doing something 'for a single moment' or 'briefly' as if applying an effort or action in a singular, complete manner.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)等一下。
    - Qǐng děng yíxià.
    - Please wait for a moment.
  - 他想休息一下。
    - Tā xiǎng xiūxi yíxià.
    - He wants to rest for a while.
  - 过(過)来(來)一下，我有事情告诉(訴)你。
    - Guòlái yíxià, wǒ yǒu shìqíng gàosù nǐ.
    - Come over for a moment, I have something to tell you.
