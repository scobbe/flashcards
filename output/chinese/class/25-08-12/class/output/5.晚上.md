## evening
### wǎnshang
---
- **definition:** evening
- **pinyin:** wǎnshang
- **components:**
  - 晚
    - wǎn
    - evening; late
  - 上
    - shàng
    - up; above; on
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 晚 (wǎn, "evening; late") and 上 (shàng, "up; above; on") combine to depict the concept of evening.
  - **interpretation:** 晚 (wǎn, "evening; late") denotes the time of day after sunset, when activities typically slow down. 上 (shàng, "up; above; on") can give the sense of something being on or upon, here suggesting the time that follows the day being 'upon' us, in the sense of happening now. Together, 晚上 ('evening') describes the period of transition from day to night when it becomes late.
- **examples:**
  - 晚上，我们(們)常常去散步。
    - Wǎnshang, wǒmen chángcháng qù sànbù.
    - In the evening, we often go for a walk.
  - 晚上，星星在天上闪(閃)烁(爍)。
    - Wǎnshang, xīngxing zài tiān shàng shǎnshuò.
    - In the evening, the stars twinkle in the sky.
  - 我喜欢(歡)晚上喝热(熱)茶。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān wǎnshang hē rè chá.
    - I like drinking hot tea in the evening.
### 晚上 → 晚
- **definition:** evening; late
- **pinyin:** wǎn
- **components:**
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun
  - 免
    - miǎn
    - avoid
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Semantic: 日 (rì, "sun") + Phonetic: 免 (miǎn, "avoid") ->
    - time after sunset ->
    - evening
  - **interpretation:** 晚 (wǎn, "evening; late") combines 日 (rì, "sun") to indicate the time of day, and 免 (miǎn, "avoid") as the phonetic component, suggesting the period when the sun is no longer visible.
- **examples:**
  - 现(現)在很晚。
    - Xiànzài hěn wǎn。
    - It is late now.
  - 太阳(陽)下山了，夜幕降临(臨)。
    - Tàiyáng xiàshān le, yèmù jiànglín。
    - The sun has set, and the night has fallen.
### 晚上 → 晚 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
- **examples:**
  - 晨曦来(來)临(臨)时(時)，日出东(東)方。
    - Chénxī láilín shí, rìchū dōngfāng.
    - At the break of dawn, the sun rises in the east.
  - 温(溫)暖的日光洒(灑)满(滿)大地。
    - Wēnnuǎn de rìguāng sǎ mǎn dàdì.
    - The warm sunlight spreads over the earth.
  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。
    - Zhōngwǔ shífēn, rì gāo guà tiān.
    - At noon, the sun hangs high in the sky.

### 晚上 → 晚 → 免
- **definition:** avoid
- **pinyin:** miǎn
- **components:**
  - 冕
    - miǎn
    - crown
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a man wearing a ceremonial hat ->
    - borrowed for sound and abstract meanings (e.g., 'exempt', 'dismiss', 'avoid') ->
    - 冕 (miǎn, 'crown') confirms original ceremonial context
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted a man with a ceremonial hat, which over time became associated with the idea of exemption or dismissal like one being ceremonially released or avoided from obligations. This ceremonial context still lingers in the derived character 冕 (miǎn, 'crown'), reinforcing the visual origin.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)可以免去会(會)议(議)。
    - Wǒmen kěyǐ miǎn qù huìyì.
    - We can dismiss the meeting.
  - 为(為)避免误(誤)解，我们(們)要仔细(細)沟(溝)通。
    - Wèi bìmiǎn wùjiě, wǒmen yào zǐxì gōutōng.
    - To avoid misunderstanding, we need to communicate carefully.
  - 他被免除了责(責)任。
    - Tā bèi miǎnchúle zérèn.
    - He was exempted from responsibility.

### 晚上 → 晚 → 免 → 冕
- **definition:** crown
- **pinyin:** miǎn
- **components:**
  - 冃
    - mào
    - crown
  - 頁(页)
    - yè
    - head
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 冃 (mào, "crown") + 頁(页) (yè, "head") =
    - crown on a head ->
    - crown
  - **interpretation:** The character 冕(miǎn, "crown") combines 冃 (mào, "crown"), depicting the shape of a crown, with 頁(页) (yè, "head"), representing the positioning on a head. This composition effectively illustrates the concept of a crown being worn on one's head.
- **examples:**
  - 国(國)王戴冕，威仪(儀)四射。
    - Guówáng dài miǎn, wēiyí sìshè.
    - The king wears a crown, exuding majestic authority.
  - 古代帝王的冕象征(徵)着(著)无(無)上的荣(榮)耀。
    - Gǔdài dìwáng de miǎn xiàngzhēngzhe wúshàng de róngyào.
    - The crown of ancient emperors symbolizes supreme glory.
### 晚上 → 晚 → 免 → 冕 → 冃
- **definition:** crown
- **pinyin:** mào
- **components:**
  - 帽
    - mào
    - hat
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a hat ->
    - original form of 帽
  - **interpretation:** The character 冃 (mào, "crown") is a pictogram depicting a hat, which serves as the original form for 帽 (mào, "hat") where 冃 appears as the component ⺜. The simplicity of its design reflects its function, representing a crown or cap. Over time, as the meaning became more specific, 帽 incorporated additional components like 巾 (jīn, "cloth") to emphasize its connection to headwear.

### 晚上 → 晚 → 免 → 冕 → 冃 → 帽
- **definition:** hat
- **pinyin:** mào
- **components:**
  - 冒
    - mào
    - to risk; to face
  - 巾
    - jīn
    - cloth
  - 冃
    - mào
    - covering
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 冃 ('covering') ->
    - phonetic 冒 + semantic 巾 ('cloth') added ->
    - 帽
  - **interpretation:** The character 帽 originally depicted a covering with the element 冃. By adding 冒 (mào), the phonetic element, and 巾 (jīn, 'cloth'), the semantic component, it specified something that covers the head made of cloth, which logically leads to the meaning 'hat'.
- **examples:**
  - 他戴了一顶(頂)红(紅)色的帽子。
    - Tā dài le yī dǐng hóngsè de màozi.
    - He wore a red hat.
  - 这(這)顶(頂)帽子是我去年买(買)的。
    - Zhè dǐng màozi shì wǒ qùnián mǎi de.
    - This hat is the one I bought last year.
  - 她在商店看见(見)了一顶(頂)漂亮的帽子。
    - Tā zài shāngdiàn kànjiàn le yī dǐng piàoliang de màozi.
    - She saw a beautiful hat at the store.

### 晚上 → 晚 → 免 → 冕 → 頁
- **definition:** head
- **pinyin:** yè
- **components:**
  - 首
    - shǒu
    - head; first
  - 卩
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 首 (head/face) + 卩 (kneeling person) ->
    - depicts a person's head and body
  - **interpretation:** Originally represented a head and its body in a kneeling posture, emphasizing “head”. The depiction was extended semantically to represent a 'page', as pages bear leading information like a head does for a body. The concept of 'head' is also reflected in its modern usage as a radical in characters related to parts of the head or face.
  - **simplification:** 页 is a cursive shorthand of the traditional 頁, simplifying the structure while maintaining the core shape of a head.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)打开(開)书(書)的第十页(頁)。
    - Qǐng dǎkāi shū de dì shí yè.
    - Please open to page ten of the book.
  - 这(這)本书(書)每一页(頁)都有插图(圖)。
    - Zhè běn shū měi yī yè dōu yǒu chātú.
    - Every page of this book has illustrations.
  - 我昨天晚上读(讀)了三十页(頁)。
    - Wǒ zuótiān wǎnshang dúle sānshí yè.
    - I read thirty pages last night.

### 晚上 → 晚 → 免 → 冕 → 頁 → 卩
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.
- **examples:**
  - 那位卩正虔诚(誠)祈祷(禱)。
    - Nà wèi jié zhèng qiánchéng qídǎo.
    - That kneeling person is praying devoutly.
  - 礼(禮)仪(儀)师(師)指出，卩代表谦(謙)卑与(與)敬意。
    - Lǐyí shī zhǐchū, jié dàibiǎo qiānbēi yǔ jìngyì.
    - The ritual master pointed out that the kneeling person represents humility and respect.
  - 在庄(莊)严(嚴)仪(儀)式中，卩象征(徵)着(著)内(內)心的虔敬。
    - Zài zhuāngyán yíshì zhōng, jié xiàngzhēngzhe nèixīn de qiánjìng.
    - In the solemn ceremony, the kneeling figure symbolizes inner devotion.

### 晚上 → 上
- **definition:** up; above; on
- **pinyin:** shàng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - A line above another line ->
    - indicates 'up'
  - **interpretation:** The character visually represents a line positioned above another, symbolizing upward direction or the concept of being 'on top.' This simplicity highlights its clear graphical indication of elevation or ascension.
- **examples:**
  - 鸟(鳥)儿(兒)飞(飛)上树(樹)梢。
