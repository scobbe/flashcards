## Sunday
### xīngqītiān
---
- **definition:** Sunday
- **pinyin:** xīngqītiān
- **components:**
  - 星
    - xīng
    - star
  - 期
    - qī
    - period; phase; time
  - 天
    - tiān
    - day; sky
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 星 (xīng, "star") + 期 (qī, "period") + 天 (tiān, "day; sky") forms a term used for naming days of the week in modern Chinese. 星 (xīng, "star") originally refers to the stars in the sky and implies celestial patterns, while 期 (qī, "period") denotes a recurring interval or cycle. 天 (tiān, "day; sky") signifies a day within that cycle.
  - **interpretation:** 星 (xīng, "star") hints at a heavenly cycle like the repeating patterns of stars, combined with 期 (qī, "period") signifying a set timeframe. The phrase thus describes a cyclical series of days. 天 (tiān, "day") specifies the particular day in this sequence, here meaning 'a day within the cosmic week,' specifically 星期天, the day we call Sunday.
- **examples:**
  - 星期天我( )们(們)去( )公( )园(園)。
    - Xīngqītiān wǒmen qù gōngyuán.
    - On Sunday, we go to the park.

### 星期天 → 天
- **definition:** day; sky
- **pinyin:** tiān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Horizontal line above 大(dà, "big") to depict sky ->
    - anthropomorphic man with large head symbolizing heaven
  - **interpretation:** The character 天(tiān) originally depicts the sky as a horizontal line above a person with outstretched arms, 大(dà, "big"), emphasizing the vastness or domain of heaven. Over time, the character evolved into an anthropomorphic representation of heaven, which later influenced its interpretation in classical texts.
