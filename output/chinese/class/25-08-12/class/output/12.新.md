## new
### xīn
---
- **definition:** new
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **components:**
  - 辛
    - xīn
    - bitter
  - 斤
    - jīn
    - axe
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood
  - 薪
    - xīn
    - firewood
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 辛 (xīn) + semantic 斤 (jīn, "axe") ->
    - originally "to chop wood"; semantic 木 (mù, "wood") later added ->
    - borrowed for "new"
  - **interpretation:** The character originally depicted actions associated with chopping wood, with 斤 (jīn, "axe") indicating the semantic idea and 辛 (xīn, "bitter") providing the sound. Over time, the semantic element 木 (mù, "wood") was incorporated in the bronze inscriptions, closely merging with 辛 (xīn, "bitter"). The character later served as a phonetic loan to express the concept of "new," borrowing its sound from 辛 (xīn), as new growth or change often marks the unfamiliar or novel.
- **examples:**
  - 我买(買)了一辆(輛)新车(車)。
    - Wǒ mǎi le yī liàng xīn chē.
    - I bought a new car.
  - 他们(們)搬进(進)了新家。
    - Tāmen bān jìnle xīn jiā.
    - They moved into a new home.
  - 新政策实(實)施后(後)，公司开(開)始发(發)展得更快。
    - Xīn zhèngcè shíshī hòu, gōngsī kāishǐ fāzhǎn de gèng kuài.
    - After the new policy was implemented, the company began to develop faster.

### 新 → 辛
- **definition:** bitter
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a tool—chisel or awl—used to mark slaves and criminals ->
    - 辛
  - **interpretation:** The character 辛 (xīn) originally depicted a tool such as a chisel, used for marking or branding. The association with a sharp tool helps explain its extended meaning of 'spice,' as spices can have a pungent, sharp quality reminiscent of a tool's point or effect.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)菜有一种(種)辛辣的味道。
    - Zhège cài yǒu yī zhǒng xīnlà de wèidào.
    - This dish has a spicy taste.
  - 他喜欢(歡)在食物里(裡)添加辛香料。
    - Tā xǐhuān zài shíwù lǐ tiānjiā xīn xiāngliào.
    - He likes to add spices to the food.
  - 这(這)种(種)辛口的调(調)料非常受欢(歡)迎。
    - Zhè zhǒng xīnkǒu de tiáoliào fēicháng shòu huānyíng.
    - This type of spicy seasoning is very popular.

### 新 → 斤
- **definition:** axe
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an axe
  - **interpretation:** The character 斤 (jīn, 'axe') is a pictogram that visually represents an axe. Its shape resembles the tool's blade and handle.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿起一把斤，熟练(練)地劈开(開)木头(頭)。
    - Tā ná qǐ yī bǎ jīn, shú liàn de pī kāi mù tóu.
    - He picked up an axe and skillfully split the firewood.
  - 古人制(製)造精巧的斤，以制(製)作雕刻工具。
    - Gǔ rén zhì zào jīng qiǎo de jīn, yǐ zhì zuò diāo kè gōng jù.
    - In ancient times, people crafted exquisite axes for carving tools.

### 新 → 木
- **definition:** wood
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tree with branches and roots
  - **interpretation:** The character 木(mù) depicts a tree, showing the trunk in the center and two slanting lines representing branches or roots. It directly signifies the concept of wood or a tree.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)把椅子的主要材料是木。
    - Zhè bǎ yǐzi de zhǔyào cáiliào shì mù.
    - The main material of this chair is wood.
  - 工匠精心雕刻木，制成精美家具。
    - Gōngjiàng jīngxīn diāo kè mù, zhìchéng jīngměi jiājù.
    - The craftsman carefully carved the wood, crafting exquisite furniture.

### 新 → 薪
- **definition:** firewood
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass; vegetation
  - 新
    - xīn
    - new
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, 'grass; vegetation') + phonetic 新 (xīn, 'new') ->
    - firewood
  - **interpretation:** 薪 (xīn, 'firewood') combines the semantic component 艹 (cǎo, 'grass; vegetation'), indicating a plant-related material, with the phonetic 新 (xīn, 'new'), which suggests the pronunciation. This form intuitively links the cutting of fresh plants or wood to create firewood.
- **examples:**
  - 农(農)夫每天收集柴薪。
    - Nóngfū měitiān shōují chái xīn.
    - The farmer collects firewood every day.
  - 冬天来(來)临(臨)时(時)，人们(們)需要更多的薪。
    - Dōngtiān láilín shí, rénmen xūyào gèng duō de xīn.
    - When winter arrives, people need more firewood.
  - 他在山上砍了很多薪。
    - Tā zài shānshàng kǎnle hěn duō xīn.
    - He chopped a lot of firewood on the mountain.

### 新 → 薪 → 艹
- **definition:** grass; vegetation
- **pinyin:** cǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts blades of grass
  - **interpretation:** 艹 is a pictogram representing grass or plants, resembling blades of grass. It is commonly used as a radical in other characters related to grass or vegetation.
- **examples:**
  - 他写(寫)字时(時)用艹作草字的偏旁。
    - Tā xiězì shí yòng cǎo zuò cǎozì de piānpáng.
    - He uses '艹' as the radical for grass in his characters.
  - 春天到来(來)时(時)，艹生长(長)得很快。
    - Chūntiān dàolái shí, cǎo shēngzhǎng de hěn kuài.
    - When spring arrives, grass grows rapidly.
