## time
### shíjiān
---
- **traditional:** 時間
- **definition:** time
- **pinyin:** shíjiān
- **components:**
  - 时(時)
    - shí
    - time; season; hour; era
  - 间(間)
    - jiān
    - between; space; room; interval
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The word 時間 (shíjiān, "time") is composed of two morphemes: 時 (shí, "time; season; hour; era") and 間 (jiān, "between; space; room; interval"). In combination, it designates the abstract concept of time as a continuum by which events are sequenced.
  - **interpretation:** 時 (shí, "time; season; hour; era") indicates the specific or cyclical nature of time, like hours or seasons; 間 (jiān, "between; space; room; interval") introduces the concept of a measured span or interval. Together, 時間 (shíjiān) embraces the perception of time as segmented and measurable, like intervals between events, which is essential to understanding history and planning for the future.
- **examples:**
  - 时(時)间(間)过(過)得( )真( )快( )。
    - Shíjiān guò de zhēn kuài.
    - Time passes really quickly.
  - 我( )们(們)需( )要( )更( )多( )的( )时(時)间(間)，才( )能( )完( )成( )任( )务(務)。
    - Wǒmen xūyào gèng duō de shíjiān, cái néng wánchéng rènwù.
    - We need more time to complete the task.
  - 现(現)在( )是( )时(時)候( )喝( )茶( )了( )。
    - Xiànzài shì shíhòu hē chá le.
    - Now it's time to drink tea.
### 时(時)间(間) → 时(時)
- **traditional:** 時
- **definition:** time; season; hour; era
- **pinyin:** shí
- **components:**
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun; day
  - 寺
    - sì
    - temple
  - 之
    - zhī

  - 寸
    - cùn
    - inch
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 日 (rì, 'sun; day') as semantic + 寺 as phonetic ->
    - associated with time; ancient form 旹 with 日 and 之 (zhī, 'governing'), referring to the passing of time ->
    - over time, 寸 (cùn, 'inch') added to form current 時
  - **interpretation:** The character 時 (shí, 'time; season') stems from the interplay between 日 (rì, 'sun; day'), which signifies the passage of time marked by the sun, and 寺 as the phonetic component capturing the sound. The historical forms show an evolution from 旹, merging 日 and 之 (zhī, 'governing') to imply governance over time, to its modern form, where 寸 ('inch') likely emphasizes precision or measurement in the concept of time.
  - **simplification:** In 时 (shí), the component 寺 was retained for phonetic purposes, while 日 (rì, 'sun; day') was simplified to resemble 日 with fewer strokes, streamlining the character.
