## how many
### duōshao
---
- **definition:** how many
- **pinyin:** duōshao
- **components:**
  - 多
    - duō
    - many; much
  - 少
    - shǎo
    - few; little
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 多 (duō, "many") refers to a large quantity, while 少 (shǎo, "few") refers to a small quantity or lack. Together, 多少 (duōshao, "how many") asks about the number of items, functioning as an interrogative phrase inquiring about quantity.
  - **interpretation:** The character 多 (duō, "many") suggests an abundance or a large number, while 少 (shǎo, "few") signifies a small number or scarcity. Combining these contrasting notions forms a question about numerical quantity, 'how many,' blending the concepts of more and less.
- **examples:**
  - 你想买(買)多少苹(蘋)果？
    - Nǐ xiǎng mǎi duōshao píngguǒ?
    - How many apples do you want to buy?
  - 这(這)里(裡)有多少人？
    - Zhèlǐ yǒu duōshao rén?
    - How many people are here?
  - 你们(們)学(學)校有多少老师(師)？
    - Nǐmen xuéxiào yǒu duōshao lǎoshī?
    - How many teachers are at your school?
### 多少 → 多
- **definition:** many; much
- **pinyin:** duō
- **components:**
  - 肉
    - ròu
    - meat
  - 夕
    - xī
    - evening
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 肉 (ròu, 'meat') corrupted to 夕 (xī) ->
    - duplicated 夕 =
    - many
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted two pieces of meat 肉 (ròu, 'meat'), suggesting abundance or 'many'. Over time, 肉 was corrupted to 夕 (xī), and the character became a duplication of 夕, signifying multiplicity or 'many'. This aligns with other duplicative characters like 林 (lín, 'forest').
- **examples:**
  - 书(書)架上有很多书(書)。
    - Shūjià shàng yǒu hěn duō shū.
    - There are many books on the shelf.
  - 他工作得比我多。
    - Tā gōngzuò de bǐ wǒ duō.
    - He works more than I do.
  - 多喝水有益健康。
    - Duō hē shuǐ yǒuyì jiànkāng.
    - Drinking more water is beneficial to health.

### 多少 → 多 → 肉
- **definition:** meat
- **pinyin:** ròu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a piece of flesh or meat with stripes representing texture.
  - **interpretation:** The character 肉(ròu, "flesh") depicts a piece of meat, meant to evoke the concept of flesh through its visual resemblance to a striped or sectioned piece of meat.
- **examples:**
  - 我喜欢(歡)吃肉。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān chī ròu.
    - I like eating meat.
  - 这(這)块(塊)肉很鲜(鮮)美，营(營)养(養)丰(豐)富。
    - Zhè kuài ròu hěn xiānměi, yíngyǎng fēngfù.
    - This piece of meat is very fresh and delicious, and it is nutritious.

### 多少 → 多 → 夕
- **definition:** evening
- **pinyin:** xī
- **components:**
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a moon crescent
  - **interpretation:** The character 夕 (xī, 'evening') is a pictogram that represents a crescent moon, capturing the image of the moon in the evening sky. The comparison to 月 (yuè, 'moon') underscores its depiction of the moon phase commonly associated with evening.
- **examples:**
  - 夕阳(陽)西下。
    - xī yáng xīxià.
    - The setting sun descends.
  - 夕阳(陽)西下，晚风(風)徐徐。
    - xī yáng xīxià, wǎn fēng xúxú.
    - The sun sets as the gentle evening breeze blows.

### 多少 → 多 → 夕 → 月
- **definition:** moon
- **pinyin:** yuè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a crescent moon
  - **interpretation:** The character 月(yuè, "moon") is a pictographic representation of a crescent moon, straightforwardly capturing the curved shape often associated with the moon's appearance. Though interchangeable with 夕 (xī, "evening") in the past, it distinctly represents the moon in this usage.
- **examples:**
  - 夜空挂(掛)着(著)明月。

### 多少 → 少
- **definition:** few; little
- **pinyin:** shǎo
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts four dots, perhaps three grains of sand ->
    - indicates "a few, a little" ->
    - phonetically borrowed from 小 (xiǎo, "small")
  - **interpretation:** The character 少 (shǎo, "few") originally depicted four dots, possibly representing grains of sand, conveying the impression of a small quantity. It was phonetically borrowed from 小 (xiǎo, "small"), which uses three dots to signify smallness, reinforcing the sense of 'few' through the idea of minimal amounts.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)本书(書)有很少的字。
    - Zhè běn shū yǒu hěn shǎo de zì.
    - This book has very few words.
  - 来(來)参(參)加演出的人很少。
    - Lái cānjiā yǎnchū de rén hěn shǎo.
    - Few people came to attend the performance.
  - 这(這)个(個)季节(節)雨水很少。
    - Zhège jìjié yǔshuǐ hěn shǎo.
    - There is very little rain this season.

### 多少 → 少 → 小
- **definition:** small
- **pinyin:** xiǎo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts three small dots arranged symmetrically to represent something small
  - **interpretation:** The character 小(xiǎo, 'small') uses three small strokes to symbolize size or quantity, visually capturing the essence of 'smallness'.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)小猫(貓)很可爱(愛)。
    - Zhège xiǎo māo hěn kě'ài.
    - This small cat is very cute.
  - 这(這)座小桥(橋)横(橫)跨河流，连(連)接两(兩)岸。
    - Zhè zuò xiǎo qiáo héngkuà héliú, liánjiē liǎng àn.
    - This small bridge spans the river, connecting both banks.
  - 请(請)给(給)我一小杯水。
    - Qǐng gěi wǒ yī xiǎo bēi shuǐ.
    - Please give me a small cup of water.
