## from … to …
### cóng … dào …
---
- **traditional:** 從到
- **definition:** from … to …
- **pinyin:** cóng … dào …
- **components:**
  - 從
    - cóng
    - follow; from
  - 到
    - dào
    - arrive; to
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 從 (cóng, "follow; from") combined with 到 (dào, "arrive; to") creates a phrase that indicates a span or movement from one point to another.
  - **interpretation:** 從 (cóng, "follow; from") indicates the starting point or origin of a movement or action. 到 (dào, "arrive; to") signifies the end or destination point. Together, this structure describes a range or transition, illustrating the path or timeline from one point (從) to another (到), effectively mapping out a continuous trajectory or sequence.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)从(從)学(學)校到公园(園)。
    - Wǒmen cóng xuéxiào dào gōngyuán.
    - We go from the school to the park.
  - 从(從)春季到夏季，花儿(兒)都在盛开(開)。
    - Cóng chūnjì dào xiàjì, huā'ér dōu zài shèngkāi.
    - From spring to summer, flowers are in full bloom.
  - 他的工作时(時)间(間)是从(從)早上九点(點)到晚上五点(點)。
    - Tā de gōngzuò shíjiān shì cóng zǎoshang jiǔ diǎn dào wǎnshang wǔ diǎn.
    - His working hours are from 9 AM to 5 PM.
### 从(從)到 → 從
- **definition:** follow; from
- **pinyin:** cóng
- **components:**
  - 辵
    - chuò
    - walk; movement
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 辵 (chuò, "walk; movement") + phonetic 从(從) (cóng, "follow") ->
    - follow; from
  - **interpretation:** 從 (cóng, "follow") uses the movement component, 辵 (chuò, "walk"), to signify the concept of walking or following in a direction. Together with 从(從) as the phonetic element providing the sound, this combination effectively represents the idea of following someone or something on a path, hence the meanings 'follow' and 'from'.
  - **simplification:** This character retains its original traditional form from Chinese history, whereas its simplified counterpart 从 suggests a person walking with another person, highlighting a reduction of strokes for simplicity.
- **examples:**
  - 我从(從)家里(裡)出发(發)，去学(學)校。
    - Wǒ cóng jiā lǐ chūfā, qù xuéxiào.
    - I set off from home to go to school.
  - 他从(從)不迟(遲)到，总(總)是准(準)时(時)来(來)。
    - Tā cóng bù chídào, zǒng shì zhǔnshí lái.
    - He is never late and always arrives on time.
  - 从(從)今天开(開)始，我要努力学(學)习(習)。
    - Cóng jīntiān kāishǐ, wǒ yào nǔlì xuéxí.
    - Starting from today, I will study hard.

### 从(從)到 → 從 → 辵
- **definition:** walk; movement
- **pinyin:** chuò
- **components:**
  - 彳
    - chì
    - walk
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 彳 (chì, "walk") + 止 (zhǐ, "foot" or "to stop") ->
    - 𣥆 ->
    - stylized as 龰 ->
    - 辵
  - **interpretation:** The character 辵 combines 彳 (chì, "walk"), which represents movement, with 止 (zhǐ, "foot" or "to stop"), suggesting the concept of stepping or traveling forward. This character represents the idea of movement, particularly the action of walking or traveling, as originally depicted by the more conservative form 𣥆. Over time, the bottom component has been stylized as 龰, forming the current representation.
- **examples:**
  - 他辵在乡(鄉)间(間)的小路上。
    - Tā chuò zài xiāngjiān de xiǎolù shàng.
    - He walks on the rural path.
  - 晨光洒(灑)满(滿)大地，他辵出门(門)开(開)始旅程。
    - Chén guāng sǎ mǎn dàdì, tā chuò chūmén kāishǐ lǚchéng.
    - As the morning light fills the earth, he sets out to begin his journey.

### 从(從)到 → 從 → 辵 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').

  - 至
    - zhì
    - to arrive
  - 刀
    - dāo

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
