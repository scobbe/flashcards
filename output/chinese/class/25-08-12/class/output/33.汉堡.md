### hànbǎo
---
- **traditional:** 漢堡
- **definition:** hamburger
- **pinyin:** hànbǎo
- **components:**
  - 汉(漢)
    - hàn
    - Han
  - 堡
    - bǎo
    - fort; castle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 漢 (hàn, "Han"); 堡 (bǎo, "fort; castle")
  - **interpretation:** 漢 (hàn, "Han") often represents the ethnic group or language but here is used phonetically. 堡 (bǎo, "fort; castle") is a phonetic component capturing the pronunciation of the word. When combined, 漢堡 (hànbǎo, "hamburger") represents the borrowed term for 'hamburger', utilizing both characters for their phonetic similarity to the English term rather than semantic meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天中午吃了一个(個)汉(漢)堡。
    - Wǒ jīntiān zhōngwǔ chīle yī gè hànbǎo.
    - I had a hamburger for lunch today.
  - 这(這)家店的汉(漢)堡非常美味。
    - Zhè jiā diàn de hànbǎo fēicháng měiwèi.
    - The hamburgers at this place are very delicious.
  - 小明买(買)了两(兩)个(個)汉(漢)堡，还(還)买(買)了一瓶可乐(樂)。
    - Xiǎo Míng mǎile liǎng gè hànbǎo, hái mǎile yī píng kělè.
    - Xiao Ming bought two hamburgers and also bought a bottle of cola.
### 汉(漢)堡 → 汉(漢)
- **traditional:** 漢
- **definition:** Han
- **pinyin:** hàn
- **components:**
  - 水
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 又
    - yòu
    - again
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 水 (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic component 暵 or 熯 ->
    - refers to river in China 漢江/漢水 ->
    - simplified by removing components to form 又 (yòu, "again")
  - **interpretation:** The character 漢 initially depicts the Han River with water radical 水 (shuǐ, "water") and a phonetic element. Its simplification to 汉 involved reducing complexity by retaining the 又 (yòu, "again") shape, reflecting modern streamlining efforts.
  - **simplification:** 汉(漢) was simplified by eliminating components, retaining the shape 又 (yòu, "again"), a common simplification strategy to reduce strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 他是汉(漢)人。
    - Tā shì hànrén.

### 汉(漢)堡 → 堡

    - bǎo

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 土 (tǔ, "earth") + phonetic 保 (bǎo) ->

  - **interpretation:** 堡(bǎo) uses 土 (tǔ, "earth") to emphasize its connection to earthworks or constructions made of earth, such as a fort or castle. 保 (bǎo) provides the pronunciation. The character thus evokes the idea of a fortified structure built upon or within the earth.
- **examples:**

    - Bǎo shàng de qízhì suí fēng piāoyáng.

  - 子
    - zǐ

  - 爪
    - zhuǎ
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 人 (rén, "person") + 子 (zǐ, "child") ->
    - person carrying a child ->
    - to carry (a child) ->
    - 爪 (zhuǎ, "hand") added to emphasize carrying ->
    - simplified to enhance meaning ->
    - current form with two strokes for hand
  - **interpretation:** The character 保 (bǎo, "protect") originates from the image of a person carrying a child, representing protection and caregiving. The addition of 爪 (zhuǎ, "hand") in early forms highlights the act of carrying with hands, reinforcing the protective role of the person. The modern form retains the strokes that symbolize hands, maintaining the theme of protection through physical support and care, which follows from the basic image of human protection of offspring.
  - **simplification:** 徤 replaced with a simpler form for ease of writing without changing pronunciation or meaning.
