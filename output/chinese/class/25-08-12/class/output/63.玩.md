## to have fun; to play
### wán
---
- **definition:** to have fun; to play
- **pinyin:** wán
- **components:**
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
  - 元
    - yuán
    - origin
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 玉 (yù, "jade") + 元 (yuán, "origin") =
    - jade toys (ancient) ->
    - playing, having fun
  - **interpretation:** The character 玩(wán) associates the idea of valuable jade 玉 with 元, reflecting the historical use of jade objects in play or as toys, symbolizing amusement or leisure.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)去玩吧。
    - Wǒmen qù wán ba.
    - Let's go have fun.
  - 孩子们(們)在公园(園)玩游(遊)戏(戲)。
    - Háizimen zài gōngyuán wán yóuxì.
    - The children play games in the park.
  - 周(週)末到了，我们(們)全家一起玩。
    - Zhōumò dàole, wǒmen quánjiā yīqǐ wán.
    - The weekend has arrived, and our whole family plays together.
### 玩 → 玉
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a picture of a cong (琮), a sort of ritual jade totem ->
    - dot added to differentiate from 王 (wáng, "king") ->
    - final form 玉 (yù, "jade")
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a picture of a jade cong, emphasizing its role in rituals and its cultural significance. The image eventually included a distinguishing dot to ensure clarity from similar characters like 王, underscoring the value jade held as a material in ancient contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 她收到了一个漂亮的玉手镯(鐲)。
    - Tā shōu dào le yī gè piàoliang de yù shǒuzhuó.
    - She received a beautiful jade bracelet.
  - 皇帝的王冠上镶(鑲)嵌着(著)闪(閃)耀的玉石。
    - Huángdì de wángguān shàng xiāngqiànzhe shǎnyào de yùshí.
    - The emperor's crown was inlaid with shining jade stones.
  - 古代的玉器常常被视(視)为(為)珍贵(貴)的艺(藝)术(術)品。
    - Gǔdài de yùqì chángcháng bèi shìwéi zhēnguì de yìshùpǐn.
    - Ancient jade artifacts are often regarded as precious works of art.

### 玩 → 元
- **definition:** origin
- **pinyin:** yuán
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a human head above a horizontal line, symbolizing the origin or the head of something
  - **interpretation:** 元 (yuán, 'origin') represents the head, which is often considered the starting point or leader, aligning with the concept of 'origin'. This setup also connects metaphorically to beginnings or first principles.
- **examples:**
  - 万(萬)物之元来(來)自于(於)混沌。
    - Wànwù zhī yuán láizì yú húndùn.
    - The origin of all things comes from chaos.
  - 道家的理念追寻(尋)心灵(靈)的元。
    - Dàojiā de lǐniàn zhuīxún xīnlíng de yuán.
    - Taoist philosophy seeks the origin of the soul.
