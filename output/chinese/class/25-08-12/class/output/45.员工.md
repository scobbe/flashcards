## employee
### yuángōng
---
- **traditional:** 員工
- **definition:** employee
- **pinyin:** yuángōng
- **components:**
  - 员(員)
    - yuán
    - person
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work; worker; craft
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 员(員) (yuán, "person") + 工 (gōng, "work") =
    - person who works ->
    - employee
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 员(員) (yuán, "person") and 工 (gōng, "work") logically denotes an "employee," someone who is employed to perform work. The term succinctly captures the role of an individual in a workplace setting.
- **examples:**
  - 我是公司的员(員)工。
    - Wǒ shì gōngsī de yuángōng.
    - I’m an employee of the company.
  - 这(這)个(個)公司有很多员(員)工。
    - Zhège gōngsī yǒu hěn duō yuángōng.
    - The company has many employees.
### 员(員)工 → 员(員)
- **traditional:** 員
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** yuán
- **components:**
  - 鼎
    - dǐng
    - ding
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 〇 representing top edge of 鼎 (dǐng, "ding") ->
    - roundness ->
    - member
  - **interpretation:** 〇 indicates the top edge of a 鼎 (dǐng, "ding") representing roundness, which metaphorically connects to the idea of a group of members forming a unified whole.
  - **simplification:** 員: 貝 (bèi, "cowry") was simplified to 贝(貝) in the character 員 to form the simplified character 员, which visually reduces the strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 他是一名团(團)队(隊)成员(員)。
    - Tā shì yī míng tuánduì chéngyuán.
    - He is a member of the team.
  - 公司需要招募新员(員)工。
    - Gōngsī xūyào zhāomù xīn yuángōng.
    - The company needs to recruit new members of staff.
  - 俱乐(樂)部的所有成员(員)都喜欢(歡)运(運)动(動)。
    - Jùlèbù de suǒyǒu chéngyuán dōu xǐhuān yùndòng.
    - All the members of the club like sports.
### 员(員)工 → 员(員) → 鼎
- **definition:** ding
- **pinyin:** dǐng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a three-legged bronze vessel ->
    - later used for divination
  - **interpretation:** The character 鼎(dǐng, 'tripod') is a pictogram that depicts a tripod vessel with three legs, symbolizing its use in cooking and ritual sacrifices. Over time, it was also borrowed phonetically to represent the concept of divination.
- **examples:**
  - 商朝的青铜(銅)鼎象征(徵)着(著)权(權)力。
    - Shāng cháo de qīngtóng dǐng xiàngzhēng zhe quánlì.
    - The bronze tripod from the Shang Dynasty symbolizes power.
  - 在古代，鼎是用来(來)烹饪(飪)食物的器具。
    - Zài gǔdài, dǐng shì yòng lái pēngrèn shíwù de qìjù.
    - In ancient times, the tripod was a utensil used for cooking food.
  - 这(這)个(個)博物馆(館)里(裏)展示了一尊精美的铜(銅)鼎。
    - Zhège bówùguǎn lǐ zhǎnshì le yī zūn jīngměi de tóngdǐng.
    - This museum exhibits an exquisite bronze tripod.

### 员(員)工 → 工
- **definition:** work; worker; craft
- **pinyin:** gōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bladed tool to plow the ground and to mark a border, or a carpenter's square
  - **interpretation:** 工 (gōng, "work") represents either a tool used in agriculture for marking or a carpenter's square, reflecting its connection to traditional manual labor and craftsmanship.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天下午去做工。
    - Tā měitiān xiàwǔ qù zuò gōng.
    - He goes to work every afternoon.
  - 工厂(廠)里(裡)的机(機)器不停地运(運)转(轉)。
    - Gōng chǎng lǐ de jīqì bù tíng de yùnzhuǎn.
    - The machines in the factory run nonstop.
  - 这(這)位工人技艺(藝)精湛。
    - Zhè wèi gōngrén jìyì jīngzhàn.
    - This worker is highly skilled.
