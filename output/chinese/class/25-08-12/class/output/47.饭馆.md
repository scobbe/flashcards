## restaurant
### fànguǎn
---
- **traditional:** 飯館
- **definition:** restaurant
- **pinyin:** fànguǎn
- **components:**
  - 饭(飯)
    - fàn
    - meal; cooked rice
  - 馆(館)
    - guǎn
    - building
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 饭(飯) (fàn, "meal; cooked rice") + 馆(館) (guǎn, "building") =
    - a place where meals are served ->
    - restaurant
  - **interpretation:** The term combines 饭(飯) (fàn, "meal; cooked rice"), a fundamental part of Chinese diet, with 馆(館) (guǎn, "building"), indicating a place where meals are prepared and served to guests, which perfectly describes a restaurant.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)去饭(飯)馆(館)吃饭(飯)。
    - Wǒmen qù fànguǎn chī fàn.
    - Let's go eat at a restaurant.
  - 这(這)个(個)饭(飯)馆(館)很热(熱)闹(鬧)，服务(務)态(態)度好。
    - Zhège fànguǎn hěn rènào, fúwù tàidù hǎo.
    - This restaurant is very lively, and the service is good.
  - 小李梦(夢)想着(著)有一天开(開)自己的饭(飯)馆(館)。
    - Xiǎo Lǐ mèngxiǎngzhe yǒu yītiān kāi zìjǐ de fànguǎn.
    - Little Li dreams of one day owning his own restaurant.
### 饭(飯)馆(館) → 饭(飯)
- **traditional:** 飯
- **definition:** meal; cooked rice
- **pinyin:** fàn
- **components:**
  - 食
    - shí
    - food
  - 反
    - fǎn
    - return
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 食 (food) + phonetic 反 ->
    - meal
  - **interpretation:** 食 (shí, "food") provides the semantic element indicating the character's relation to food, while 反 (fǎn, "return") provides the sound. The association of 食 with the sound of 反 conveys the concept of 'meal' or 'food to return to'.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by replacing 食 (shí, "food") with 饣, a reduced form.
- **examples:**
  - 每天我都和家人一起吃饭(飯)。
    - Měitiān wǒ dōu hé jiārén yīqǐ chī fàn.
    - Every day, I eat a meal with my family.
  - 他做的晚餐很美味，我吃了两(兩)碗饭(飯)。
    - Tā zuò de wǎncān hěn měiwèi, wǒ chīle liǎng wǎn fàn.
    - The dinner he made was very delicious, I ate two bowls of rice.
  - 饭(飯)后(後)我们(們)去散步。
    - Fàn hòu wǒmen qù sànbù.
    - We go for a walk after the meal.
### 饭(飯)馆(館) → 饭(飯) → 食
- **definition:** food
- **pinyin:** shí
- **components:**
  - 亼
    - jí
    - assemble
  - 𠊊
    - jí
    - bowl with stand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Pictogram
  - **description:** 亼 (jí, "assemble") over a pictographic bowl of rice on a stand, symbolized by 𠊊 (jí, "bowl with stand")
  - **interpretation:** Initially, the character 食 (shí, "eat") visually represented a mouth over a bowl of rice, conveying the act of eating or a meal intuitively through this imagery.
- **examples:**
  - 他食得很快。
    - Tā shí dé hěn kuài.
    - He eats very quickly.
  - 我准(準)备(備)午食，与(與)朋友共享佳肴(餚)。
    - Wǒ zhǔnbèi wǔ shí, yǔ péngyǒu gòngxiǎng jiāyáo.
    - I am preparing lunch to share a delicious meal with my friends.
  - 民以食为(為)天。
    - Mín yǐ shí wéi tiān.
    - Food is regarded as the most important element for the people.
### 饭(飯)馆(館) → 饭(飯) → 食 → 亼
- **definition:** assemble
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 今
    - jīn
    - now
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 入 (rù, "enter") + 一 (yī, "one") =
    - gathering from three sides ->
    - original form of 今 (jīn, "now")
  - **interpretation:** 亼 (jī, "open mouth flipped upside down") originally represented the action of gathering, with 入 (rù, "enter") suggesting bringing things together and 一 (yī, "one") implying unification into a single entity. This concept aligns traditionally with 集 (jí, "gather"), where the sense of gathering from three sides connects to assembling or coming together. The visual comparison to an open mouth reversed is a less common interpretation, relating to a shape rather than function.

- **examples:**
  - 我在图(圖)书(書)馆(館)借阅(閱)书(書)籍。

    - The Palace Museum collects precious cultural relics.
