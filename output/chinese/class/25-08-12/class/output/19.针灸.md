## acupuncture
### zhēnjiǔ
---
- **traditional:** 針灸
- **definition:** acupuncture
- **pinyin:** zhēnjiǔ
- **components:**
  - 针(針)
    - zhēn
    - needle
  - 灸
    - jiǔ
    - moxibustion
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 針 (zhēn, "needle") and 灸 (jiǔ, "moxibustion") combine to describe the practice of acupuncture, which involves using needles and heat to stimulate specific points on the body for therapeutic purposes.
  - **interpretation:** 針 (zhēn, "needle") refers to the slender pointed tool used in acupuncture, while 灸 (jiǔ, "moxibustion") involves the burning of moxa (a plant material) to create therapeutic heat. Together, 針灸 (zhēnjiǔ) denotes the combined use of both these methods to apply physical stimulation and heat to specific points on the body, based on traditional Chinese medicine principles to balance and regulate the flow of qi (vital energy).
- **examples:**
  - 他( )对(對)针(針)灸( )疗(療)法( )很感兴趣。
    - Tā duì zhēnjiǔ liáofǎ hěn gǎn xìngqù.
    - He is very interested in acupuncture therapy.
  - 针(針)灸( )可( )以( )缓(緩)解( )疼( )痛( )。
    - Zhēnjiǔ kěyǐ huǎnjiě téngtòng.
    - Acupuncture can relieve pain.
  - 医(醫)生( )建( )议(議)我( )尝(嘗)试(試)针(針)灸( )疗(療)法( )，因( )为(為)效( )果( )显(顯)著( )。

    - The doctor suggested I try acupuncture therapy because the effects are remarkable.

- **pinyin:** zhēn

    - shí

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
