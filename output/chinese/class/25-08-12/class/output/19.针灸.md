## acupuncture
### zhēnjiǔ
---
- **traditional:** 針灸
- **definition:** acupuncture
- **pinyin:** zhēnjiǔ
- **components:**
  - 针(針)
    - zhēn
    - needle
  - 灸
    - jiǔ
    - moxibustion
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 針 (zhēn, "needle") and 灸 (jiǔ, "moxibustion") combine to describe the practice of acupuncture, which involves using needles and heat to stimulate specific points on the body for therapeutic purposes.
  - **interpretation:** 針 (zhēn, "needle") refers to the slender pointed tool used in acupuncture, while 灸 (jiǔ, "moxibustion") involves the burning of moxa (a plant material) to create therapeutic heat. Together, 針灸 (zhēnjiǔ) denotes the combined use of both these methods to apply physical stimulation and heat to specific points on the body, based on traditional Chinese medicine principles to balance and regulate the flow of qi (vital energy).
- **examples:**
  - 他对(對)针(針)灸疗(療)法很感兴趣。
    - Tā duì zhēnjiǔ liáofǎ hěn gǎn xìngqù.
    - He is very interested in acupuncture therapy.
  - 针(針)灸可以缓(緩)解疼痛。
    - Zhēnjiǔ kěyǐ huǎnjiě téngtòng.
    - Acupuncture can relieve pain.
  - 医(醫)生建议(議)我尝(嘗)试(試)针(針)灸疗(療)法，因为(為)效果显(顯)著。
    - Yīshēng jiànyì wǒ chángshì zhēnjiǔ liáofǎ, yīnwèi xiàoguǒ xiǎnzhù.
    - The doctor suggested I try acupuncture therapy because the effects are remarkable.
### 针(針)灸 → 针(針)
- **traditional:** 針
- **definition:** needle
- **pinyin:** zhēn
- **components:**
  - 釒
    - jīn
    - metal
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic 釒 (jīn, "metal") + phonetic 十 (shí)
  - **interpretation:** 釒 (jīn, "metal") indicates the material and practical use of needles being made of metal. 十 (shí) provides the phonetic component. This combination suggests a small metal object, like a needle.
  - **simplification:** Simplified by reducing 釒 (jīn, "metal") to 钅, keeping the phonetic 十 (shí) part unchanged.
- **examples:**
  - 我找不到针(針)。
    - Wǒ zhǎo bù dào zhēn.
    - I can't find the needle.
  - 医(醫)生用一根细(細)针(針)给(給)我打针(針)。
    - Yīshēng yòng yī gēn xì zhēn gěi wǒ dǎ zhēn.
    - The doctor used a thin needle to give me an injection.
  - 这(這)枚针(針)是用来(來)缝(縫)衣服的。
    - Zhè méi zhēn shì yòng lái féng yīfú de.
    - This needle is used for sewing clothes.
### 针(針)灸 → 针(針) → 釒
- **definition:** metal
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts metal or gold with two nuggets on the sides and vertical lines representing gleaming or brightness ->
    - metal elements
  - **interpretation:** It visually represents pieces of metal or gold, suggesting the value and substance associated with metals, particularly gold, due to its shine and durability.
### 针(針)灸 → 针(針) → 十
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园(園)里(裡)有十棵树(樹)，景色十分优(優)美。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.

### 针(針)灸 → 灸
- **definition:** moxibustion
- **pinyin:** jiǔ
- **components:**
  - 火
    - huǒ
    - fire
  - 久
    - jiǔ
    - long time
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 火 (huǒ, "fire") + phonetic 久 (jiǔ) ->
    - meaning
  - **interpretation:** The character 灸 (jiǔ, "moxibustion") combines the semantic 火 (huǒ, "fire") with the phonetic 久 (jiǔ) to signify the practice of applying fire or heat, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine. The 火 component directly refers to the application of heat, while 久 helps to provide the pronunciation.
- **examples:**
  - 她最近在学(學)习(習)灸法。
    - Tā zuìjìn zài xuéxí jiǔfǎ.
    - She has recently been studying the art of moxibustion.
  - 医(醫)生建议(議)我试(試)试(試)灸疗(療)，来(來)缓(緩)解腰痛。
    - Yīshēng jiànyì wǒ shìshì jiǔliáo, lái huǎnjiě yāotòng.
    - The doctor suggested I try moxibustion to alleviate back pain.
  - 在中医(醫)中，灸是一种(種)常用的疗(療)法。
    - Zài zhōngyī zhōng, jiǔ shì yī zhǒng chángyòng de liáofǎ.
    - In traditional Chinese medicine, moxibustion is a common treatment method.
### 针(針)灸 → 灸 → 火
- **definition:** fire
- **pinyin:** huǒ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts flames rising from a fire
  - **interpretation:** The character 火 (huǒ, "fire") visually represents flames flickering upwards, which intuitively conveys the essence of fire. This simple depiction is an effective way to capture the dynamic and essential nature of fire.
- **examples:**
  - 火在壁炉(爐)中熊熊燃烧(燒)。
    - Huǒ zài bìlú zhōng xióngxióng ránshāo.
    - The fire burns fiercely in the fireplace.
  - 野外露营(營)时(時)，火能驱(驅)走寒冷。
    - Yěwài lùyíng shí, huǒ néng qūzǒu hánlěng.
    - When camping outdoors, the fire can drive away the cold.
  - 遇到火灾(災)时(時)，迅速疏散最为(為)重要。
    - Yù dào huǒzāi shí, xùnsù shū sàn zuìwéi zhòngyào.
    - In case of a fire, evacuating quickly is of utmost importance.

### 针(針)灸 → 灸 → 久
- **definition:** long time
- **pinyin:** jiǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a person bending the lower back ->
    - extended time
  - **interpretation:** The character 久 (jiǔ, "long time") visually represents a person leaning forward, symbolizing the passage of a significant amount of time. The bending posture suggests protracted duration.
- **examples:**
  - 我等待了很久。
    - Wǒ děngdài le hěn jiǔ.
    - I waited for a long time.
  - 分别(別)了很久，朋友仍然相聚。
    - Fēnbié le hěn jiǔ, péngyǒu réngrán xiāngjù.
    - It has been a long time since we parted, yet friends still meet.
  - 远(遠)行归(歸)来(來)已久，家门(門)依旧(舊)温(溫)暖。
    - Yuǎnxíng guīlái yǐ jiǔ, jiāmén yījiù wēnnuǎn.
    - It has been a long time since returning from a journey, yet the home remains welcoming.
