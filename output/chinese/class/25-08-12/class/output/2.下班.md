## to get off work
### xiàbān
---
- **definition:** to get off work
- **pinyin:** xiàbān
- **components:**
  - 下
    - xià
    - below; down
  - 班
    - bān
    - shift; work
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 下 (xià, "below; down") + 班 (bān, "shift; work") =
    - to get off work ->
    - when the work shift or period is over
  - **interpretation:** 下 (xià, "below; down") indicates finishing or concluding a task, while 班 (bān, "shift; work") refers to a work period. Together, 下班 (xiàbān, "to get off work") describes the action of completing your work shift and leaving your workplace.
- **examples:**
  - 我下午五点(點)下班。
    - Wǒ xiàwǔ wǔ diǎn xiàbān.
    - I get off work at five in the afternoon.
  - 今天工作很辛苦，下班后(後)他喝了一杯咖啡。
    - Jīntiān gōngzuò hěn xīnkǔ, xiàbān hòu tā hē le yī bēi kāfēi.
    - Today work is arduous; after getting off work, he had a cup of coffee.
  - 公交车(車)缓(緩)缓(緩)驶(駛)来(來)，下班的人群开(開)始涌(湧)动(動)。
    - Gōngjiāo chē huǎn huǎn shǐ lái, xiàbān de rénqún kāishǐ yǒngdòng.
    - The bus slowly arrives, and the crowd getting off work begins to surge.
### 下班 → 下
- **definition:** below; down
- **pinyin:** xià
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - a line below another ->
    - later evolved into 下
  - **interpretation:** The character 下(xià, 'down') is an ideogram showing a line positioned below another line, visually representing 'below' or 'downward'. This layout intuitively depicts the concept of being beneath or beneath another object.
- **examples:**
  - 水从(從)山上流下。
    - Shuǐ cóng shān shàng liú xià.
    - The water flows down from the mountain.
  - 他坐下来(來)看书(書)。
    - Tā zuò xià lái kàn shū.
    - He sits down to read a book.
  - 请(請)把箱子放在地下。
    - Qǐng bǎ xiāngzi fàng zài dìxià.
    - Please put the box down on the ground.

### 下班 → 班
- **definition:** shift; work
- **pinyin:** bān
- **components:**
  - 玨
    - jué
    - pieces of jade
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 玨 (jué, "pieces of jade") + 刀 (dāo, "knife") ->
    - to cut jade into pieces with a knife ->
    - shift; work
  - **interpretation:** The character 班(bān, "shift; work") originally depicted the action of cutting a piece of jade into parts with a knife. Just as jade is divided into smaller parts, work or tasks are divided into shifts or segments, bridging the concept of dividing valuable items and dividing time or tasks.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天的班结(結)束后(後)我们(們)去喝饮(飲)料。
    - Wǒ jīntiān de bān jiéshù hòu wǒmen qù hē yǐnliào.
    - After my shift ends today, we will go for drinks.
  - 他新换(換)了一个(個)班，觉(覺)得很适(適)应(應)。
    - Tā xīn huànle yī gè bān, juéde hěn shìyìng.
    - He has switched to a new shift and feels very adapted to it.
  - 这(這)个(個)班的工作时(時)间(間)很长(長)，但是报(報)酬不错(錯)。
    - Zhège bān de gōngzuò shíjiān hěn cháng, dànshì bàochóu bùcuò.
    - This shift has long working hours, but the pay is good.

### 下班 → 班 → 玨 → 玉
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a picture of a cong (琮), a sort of ritual jade totem ->
    - dot added to differentiate from 王 (wáng, "king") ->
    - final form 玉 (yù, "jade")
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a picture of a jade cong, emphasizing its role in rituals and its cultural significance. The image eventually included a distinguishing dot to ensure clarity from similar characters like 王, underscoring the value jade held as a material in ancient contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 她收到了一个漂亮的玉手镯(鐲)。
    - Tā shōu dào le yī gè piàoliang de yù shǒuzhuó.
    - She received a beautiful jade bracelet.
  - 皇帝的王冠上镶(鑲)嵌着(著)闪(閃)耀的玉石。
    - Huángdì de wángguān shàng xiāngqiànzhe shǎnyào de yùshí.
    - The emperor's crown was inlaid with shining jade stones.
  - 古代的玉器常常被视(視)为(為)珍贵(貴)的艺(藝)术(術)品。
    - Gǔdài de yùqì chángcháng bèi shìwéi zhēnguì de yìshùpǐn.
    - Ancient jade artifacts are often regarded as precious works of art.

### 下班 → 班 → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a knife
  - **interpretation:** 刀 (dāo, "knife") is a pictographic representation of a knife or blade, with a sharp edge and a handle, resembling the form of a basic cutting tool.
- **examples:**
  - 他用锋(鋒)利的刀，切开(開)蔬菜。
    - tā yòng fēnglì de dāo, qiē kāi shūcài.
    - He uses a sharp knife to cut vegetables.
  - 这(這)把刀非常锋(鋒)利。
    - zhè bǎ dāo fēicháng fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
  - 厨(廚)师(師)小心地持刀，为(為)客人切肉。
    - chúshī xiǎoxīn de chí dāo, wèi kèrén qiē ròu.
    - The chef holds the knife carefully and slices meat for the guest.
