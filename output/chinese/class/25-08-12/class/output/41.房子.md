## house
### fángzi
---
- **definition:** house
- **pinyin:** fángzi
- **components:**
  - 房
    - fáng
    - house; building
  - 子
    - zi
    - child; small thing; noun suffix
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 房 (fáng, "house") refers to a building or structure for dwelling; 子 (zi, "child; small thing") often indicates a diminutive or noun suffix, implying a specific type or instance.
  - **interpretation:** 房 (fáng, "house") conveys the primary concept of a building or space for living. 子 (zi, "child; small thing") functions as a nominalizing suffix, turning the general idea into a concrete, specific term for 'a house' or 'home'. The use of 子 suggests intimacy or familiarity, as if referring to a 'little home' or a place of belonging.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)座房子很漂亮。
    - Zhè zuò fángzi hěn piàoliang.
    - This house is very beautiful.
  - 村里(裡)的人正在建造一座新房子。
    - Cūn lǐ de rén zhèngzài jiànzào yī zuò xīn fángzi.
    - The villagers are building a new house.
  - 他们(們)打算卖(賣)掉老房子，然后(後)搬到城市。
    - Tāmen dǎsuàn mài diào lǎo fángzi, ránhòu bān dào chéngshì.
    - They plan to sell the old house and then move to the city.
### 房子 → 房
- **definition:** house; building
- **pinyin:** fáng
- **components:**
  - 戶
    - hù
    - door
  - 方
    - fāng
    - side
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 戶 (hù, "door") + 方 (fāng, "side") =
    - door on the side ->
    - room
  - **interpretation:** 房(fáng, "house; room") combines the semantic element 戶 (hù, "door") with the phonetic element 方 (fāng, "side"), suggesting a space associated with a door, like a side room in a house.
- **examples:**
  - 他在城里(裡)租了一间(間)房。
    - Tā zài chénglǐ zū le yī jiān fáng.
    - He rented a room in the city.
  - 我的房子很温(溫)暖。
    - Wǒ de fángzi hěn wēnnuǎn.
    - My house is very warm.
  - 夏天时(時)房间(間)有空调(調)，感觉(覺)十分舒适(適)。
    - Xiàtiān shí fángjiān yǒu kōngtiáo, gǎnjué shífēn shūshì.
    - In summer, the room has air conditioning and feels very comfortable.

### 房子 → 房 → 戶

  - 𠁣
    - unknown

  - **description:**
    - Depicts one-half of 門 (mén, "gate") ->

### 房子 → 房 → 方
- **definition:** side
- **pinyin:** fāng
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - sword
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 一 (yī, "one") + 刀 (dāo, "sword") =
    - original depiction of blade's tip ->
    - borrowed for "side" ->
    - now "square"
  - **interpretation:** Initially depicting the blade of a sword, 方 (fāng, "square") was later borrowed for its current meaning due to its association with edges and boundaries, metaphorically expanded to define sides and squares.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)张(張)桌子是方的。
    - Zhè zhāng zhuōzi shì fāng de.
    - This table is square.
  - 院子里(裡)铺(鋪)满(滿)了方砖(磚)。
    - Yuànzi lǐ pū mǎn le fāng zhuān.
    - The courtyard is paved with square bricks.
  - 我买(買)的花瓶是方形的，设(設)计(計)简(簡)洁(潔)优(優)美。
    - Wǒ mǎi de huāpíng shì fāngxíng de, shèjì jiǎnjié yōuměi.
