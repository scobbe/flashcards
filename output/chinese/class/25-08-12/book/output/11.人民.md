## people
### rénmín
---
- **definition:** people
- **pinyin:** rénmín
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person; human; individual; people
  - 民
    - mín
    - people; populace; citizenry; folk
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 人 (rén, "person") denotes an individual or human being. 民 (mín, "people") signifies the people or populace. Together, 人民 (rénmín, "the people") represents the collective body of individuals within a community or nation.
  - **interpretation:** 人 (rén, "person") signifies an individual human, while 民 (mín, "people") refers to the collective populace. The combination of these characters captures the concept of all individuals forming a nation or society. The image of many 人 (rén, "person") constituting a single 民 (mín, "people") group effectively conveys the idea of a community or population as a whole.

### 人民 → 民
- **definition:** people; populace; citizenry; folk
- **pinyin:** mín
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts an eye stabbed by a needle, symbolizing oppression of the people ->
    - people
  - **interpretation:** The character 民 (mín, 'people') originally depicted an eye being pierced by a tool, a metaphor for the suffering and experiences of the common people. This imagery evolved to represent the broader concept of the populace.
