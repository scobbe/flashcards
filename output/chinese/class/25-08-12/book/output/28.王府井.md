## Wangfujing commercial district in Beijing
### Wángfǔjǐng
---
- **definition:** Wangfujing commercial district in Beijing
- **pinyin:** Wángfǔjǐng
- **components:**
  - 王
    - wáng
    - king
  - 府
    - fǔ
    - official residence; government office; mansion
  - 井
    - jǐng
    - well
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 王 (wáng, "king"), 府 (fǔ, "official residence"), 井 (jǐng, "well")
  - **interpretation:** 王府井 (Wángfǔjǐng) historically referred to an area where there was an official residence, likely belonging to someone of high status ('king'), and a well. This area in Beijing is now a famous commercial district.
- **examples:**
  - 我今天去了王府井。
    - Wǒ jīntiān qùle Wángfǔjǐng.
    - I went to Wangfujing today.
  - 王府井是北京著名的商业(業)区(區)，每天吸引大量游(遊)客。
    - Wángfǔjǐng shì Běijīng zhùmíng de shāngyèqū, měitiān xīyǐn dàliàng yóukè.
    - Wangfujing is a famous commercial district in Beijing, attracting a large number of tourists every day.

- **definition:** king
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**

  - **interpretation:** This character represents the form of a ceremonial axe, which was the symbol of a king's power in ancient China. The structure emphasizes the sacred and authoritative role of the king, separate from the nuanced folk interpretations linking Heaven, Man, and Earth.
- **examples:**
  - 他被称(稱)为(為)王，领(領)导(導)人民。

### 王府井 → 府 → 广(廣) → 廣 → 黃
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally a pictogram depicting a disabled person or man with a ring of jade on the chest ->
    - phonetically borrowed for 'yellow' ->
    - 黃
  - **interpretation:** The character 黃 (huáng, 'yellow') originated from a pictogram representing either a disabled person or a man with a jade ring, showing a connection to cultural rituals or symbols of status. It was later phonetically borrowed to represent the color 'yellow' without semantic connection to its original depiction. In modern Chinese, its primary use is to denote the color yellow, disconnecting entirely from its debated pictographic origins.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)朵花是黄(黃)色的。
    - Zhè duǒ huā shì huángsè de.
    - This flower is yellow.
  - 秋天来(來)临(臨)时(時)，树(樹)叶(葉)变(變)成金黄(黃)色。
    - Qiūtiān láilín shí, shùyè biàn chéng jīnhuáng sè.
    - When autumn arrives, the leaves turn golden yellow.
  - 他穿了一件黄(黃)色的外套。
    - Tā chuānle yī jiàn huángsè de wàitào.

### 王府井 → 府 → 付 → 寸

- **etymology:**
