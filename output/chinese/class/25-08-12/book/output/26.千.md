## thousand
### qiān
---
- **definition:** thousand
- **pinyin:** qiān
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one, signifying a number
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 一 (yī, "one, signifying a number") + phonetic 人 (rén) ->
    - borrowing pronunciation to represent a large number
  - **interpretation:** The character is formed by adding a line to 人 (rén, "person"), signifying an extension, combined with 一 (yī, "one") to form the concept of a thousand – a milestone number reached through cumulative counting.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)本( )书(書)要( )卖(賣)一( )千( )块(塊)钱(錢)。
    - Zhè běn shū yào mài yī qiān kuài qián.

    - Chángchéng měinián xīyǐn chéngqiānshàngwàn de yóukè.
    - The Great Wall attracts thousands upon thousands of tourists every year.
  - 她( )拥(擁)有( )数(數)千( )本( )书(書)。

### 千 → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg ->
    - visual representation of a person
  - **interpretation:** The character 人 (rén, 'person') effectively uses simple lines to depict the essential form of a human, making it immediately recognizable and intuitive as meaning 'person.'
