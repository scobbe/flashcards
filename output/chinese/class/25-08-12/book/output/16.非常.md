## very; extremely
### fēicháng
---
- **definition:** very; extremely
- **pinyin:** fēicháng
- **components:**
  - 非
    - fēi
    - not; wrong
  - 常
    - cháng
    - ordinary; usual
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 非常 (fēicháng, "very; extremely") combines two characters: 非 (fēi, "not; wrong") and 常 (cháng, "ordinary; usual"). 非 indicates negation or contrariety, while 常 refers to the usual or regular state of things.
  - **interpretation:** The combination 非 (fēi, "not; wrong") + 常 (cháng, "ordinary; usual") creates an image of something that is 'not ordinary' or 'out of the ordinary', leading to an intensified sense of something being beyond the norm or extremely so. This mechanism forms the basis for its use to mean 'very' or 'extremely', as it indicates a departure from the expected or typical state.
- **examples:**
  - 天( )气(氣)非( )常( )好( )。
    - Tiānqì fēicháng hǎo.
    - The weather is very good.
  - 今天的食物非常美味。
    - Jīntiān de shíwù fēicháng měiwèi.
    - Today's food is extremely delicious.
  - 他工作非常努力，因( )此( )得( )到( )了( )晋(晉)升( )。
    - Tā gōngzuò fēicháng nǔlì, yīncǐ dédào le jìnshēng.
    - He works very hard, so he got a promotion.
