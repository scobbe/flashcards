## very; extremely
### fēicháng
---
- **definition:** very; extremely
- **pinyin:** fēicháng
- **components:**
  - 非
    - fēi
    - not; wrong
  - 常
    - cháng
    - ordinary; usual
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 非常 (fēicháng, "very; extremely") combines two characters: 非 (fēi, "not; wrong") and 常 (cháng, "ordinary; usual"). 非 indicates negation or contrariety, while 常 refers to the usual or regular state of things.
  - **interpretation:** The combination 非 (fēi, "not; wrong") + 常 (cháng, "ordinary; usual") creates an image of something that is 'not ordinary' or 'out of the ordinary', leading to an intensified sense of something being beyond the norm or extremely so. This mechanism forms the basis for its use to mean 'very' or 'extremely', as it indicates a departure from the expected or typical state.
- **examples:**
  - 天气(氣)非常好。
    - Tiānqì fēicháng hǎo.
    - The weather is very good.
  - 今天的食物非常美味。
    - Jīntiān de shíwù fēicháng měiwèi.
    - Today's food is extremely delicious.
  - 他工作非常努力，因此得到了晋(晉)升。
    - Tā gōngzuò fēicháng nǔlì, yīncǐ dédào le jìnshēng.
    - He works very hard, so he got a promotion.
### 非常 → 非

- **pinyin:** fēi
- **etymology:**

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 非 (fēi, "not") visually resembles two wings turned away from each other, symbolizing opposition and disagreement. This imagery captures the essence of negation, which is central to the character's meaning.

### 非常 → 常
- **definition:** ordinary; usual
- **pinyin:** cháng
- **components:**
  - 巾
    - jīn
    - cloth
  - 尚
    - shàng
    - still; yet
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 巾 (jīn, "cloth") + phonetic 尚 (shàng) ->
    - originally "lower garment" ->
    - borrowed for "long-lasting; frequently" ->
    - "ordinary; usual"
  - **interpretation:** The semantic component 巾 (jīn, "cloth") conveys the original idea of a cloth-like lower garment, which was later phonetically borrowed with 尚 (shàng) for its sound rather than meaning, expanding to "long-lasting" and thus "ordinary; usual".
- **examples:**
  - 他是一个(個)非常普通的人。
    - Tā shì yī gè fēicháng pǔtōng de rén.
    - He is a very ordinary person.
  - 我们(們)常常去那家咖啡店。
    - Wǒmen chángcháng qù nà jiā kāfēidiàn.
    - We usually go to that coffee shop.
  - 这(這)个(個)问(問)题(題)在日常生活中很常见(見)。
    - Zhège wèntí zài rìcháng shēnghuó zhōng hěn chángjiàn.
    - This issue is very common in everyday life.

### 非常 → 常 → 尚
- **definition:** still; yet
- **pinyin:** shàng
- **components:**
  - 八
    - bā
    - eight
  - 向
    - xiàng
    - face; towards
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 八 (bā, "eight") + phonetic 向 (xiàng, "face; towards") ->
    - meaning
  - **interpretation:** The character 尚(shàng, "esteem") combines 八 (bā, "eight"), which acts as the semantic component, with 向 (xiàng, "face; towards"), which provides the phonetic sound. The sense of moving towards something in 向 supports ideas of ascending or elevating, hence developing into the sense of 'esteem' associated with elevating status or regard.
- **examples:**
  - 他的作风(風)很时(時)尚。
    - Tā de zuòfēng hěn shíshàng.
    - His style is very fashionable.

    - She always pursues noble ideals.

### 非常 → 常 → 尚 → 向
- **definition:** face; towards
- **pinyin:** xiàng
- **components:**
  - 宀
    - mián
    - house
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 宀 (house) + 口 (mouth) =
    - echos in a place ->
    - original form of 響
  - **interpretation:** The character 向 (xiàng, 'towards') originally depicted echoes in a space, suggesting a sound emanating within a house. Over time, this visual evolved into denoting a direction or orientation, similar to how a sound moves towards something. Thus, it was used to express the concept of 'towards.'
- **examples:**

### 非常 → 常 → 尚 → 向 → 宀
- **definition:** house
- **pinyin:** mián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a house with four walls ->
    - roof
  - **interpretation:** 宀(mián, "roof") is a pictogram representing a house with four walls. It emphasizes an enclosed space, distinguishing it from similar structures like 广(guǎng, "broad") which depicts a building with three walls. This makes 宀 a logical choice for representing a roof in various characters.
- **examples:**
  - 含有宀的汉(漢)字代表家的温(溫)暖。
    - Hán yǒu mián de hànzì dàibiǎo jiā de wēnnuǎn.
    - Chinese characters that include the radical '宀' represent the warmth of home.
  - 屋顶(頂)遮风(風)挡(擋)雨，家园(園)散发(發)温(溫)馨。
    - Wūdǐng zhē fēng dǎng yǔ, jiāyuán sànfā wēnxīn.

### 非常 → 常 → 尚 → 向 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
