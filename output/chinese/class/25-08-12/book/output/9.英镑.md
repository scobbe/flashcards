## pound sterling
### yīngbàng
---
- **traditional:** 英鎊
- **definition:** pound sterling
- **pinyin:** yīngbàng
- **components:**
  - 英
    - yīng
    - hero; outstanding; English
  - 镑(鎊)
    - bàng
    - pound
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 英 (yīng, "hero; outstanding; English") is used here to refer to England or English, and by extension, the British currency. 鎊(镑) (bàng, "pound") refers specifically to the unit of currency known as the pound, marked by its symbol (£).
  - **interpretation:** 英 (yīng, "hero; outstanding; English") connects to England, thus linking to the British context, while 鎊(镑) (bàng, "pound") identifies the specific unit of British currency. Together, 英 (yīng, "English") and 鎊(镑) (bàng, "pound") form the term for the British currency, the pound sterling.
- **examples:**
  - 银(銀)行可以兑(兌)换(換)英镑(鎊)。
    - Yínháng kěyǐ duìhuàn yīngbàng.
    - The bank can exchange pound sterling.
  - 英镑(鎊)的汇(匯)率今天上涨(漲)了。
    - Yīngbàng de huìlǜ jīntiān shàngzhǎng le.
    - The exchange rate of pound sterling rose today.
  - 我用英镑(鎊)买(買)了一些纪(紀)念品。
    - Wǒ yòng yīngbàng mǎi le yīxiē jìniànpǐn.
    - I bought some souvenirs with pound sterling.
