## Terracotta Warriors
### bīngmǎyǒng
---
- **traditional:** 兵馬俑
- **definition:** Terracotta Warriors
- **pinyin:** bīngmǎyǒng
- **components:**
  - 兵
    - bīng
    - soldier
  - 马(馬)
    - mǎ
    - horse
  - 俑
    - yǒng
    - figurine; burial statue
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 兵 (bīng, "soldier") refers to the military personnel or troops. 馬 (mǎ, "horse") represents the mounts for soldiers, highlighting the cavalry element. 俑 (yǒng, "figurine") specifically denotes burial figurines or statues, often crafted to serve the deceased in the afterlife.
  - **interpretation:** 兵 (bīng, "soldier") conveys the concept of military troops, while 馬 (mǎ, "horse") emphasizes the involvement of cavalry, indicating the military aspect of the figures. 俑 (yǒng, "figurine") suggests these are not real individuals but sculpted figures meant for burial use, enhancing the sacred duty of serving in the afterlife. Together, 兵马俑 signifies the Terracotta Warriors, illustrating an army of lifelike soldier and horse statues buried to protect the emperor in the afterlife.
- **examples:**
  - 兵马(馬)俑是中国(國)古代文化的瑰宝(寶)。
    - Bīngmǎyǒng shì Zhōngguó gǔdài wénhuà de guībǎo.
    - The Terracotta Warriors are a treasure of ancient Chinese culture.
  - 参(參)观(觀)兵马(馬)俑后，他对(對)中国(國)历(歷)史更感兴(興)趣了。
    - Cānguān bīngmǎyǒng hòu, tā duì Zhōngguó lìshǐ gèng gǎn xìngqù le.
    - After visiting the Terracotta Warriors, he became more interested in Chinese history.
  - 许(許)多人来(來)到西安，就是为(為)了一睹兵马(馬)俑的风(風)采。
    - Xǔduō rén láidào Xī'ān, jiùshì wèile yī dǔ bīngmǎyǒng de fēngcǎi.
    - Many people come to Xi'an just to witness the grandeur of the Terracotta Warriors.

### 兵马(馬)俑 → 马(馬)
- **traditional:** 馬
- **definition:** horse
- **pinyin:** mǎ
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 火
    - huǒ
    - fire
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a horse with head facing left and flowing mane ->
    - head often simplified into an eye 目 (mù, "eye") ->
    - legs evolved into four dots 灬 unrelated to 火 (huǒ, "fire")
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a horse with its mane flowing sideways, and the simplification over time captured the essence of its most visible features.
  - **simplification:** 馬 was simplified to 马(馬) by fusing the head and body, using the simplified line forms and dots to represent its features more compactly.
- **examples:**
