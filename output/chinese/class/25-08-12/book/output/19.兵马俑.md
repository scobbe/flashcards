## Terracotta Warriors
### bīngmǎyǒng
---
- **traditional:** 兵馬俑
- **definition:** Terracotta Warriors
- **pinyin:** bīngmǎyǒng
- **components:**
  - 兵
    - bīng
    - soldier
  - 马(馬)
    - mǎ
    - horse
  - 俑
    - yǒng
    - figurine; burial statue
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 兵 (bīng, "soldier") refers to the military personnel or troops. 馬 (mǎ, "horse") represents the mounts for soldiers, highlighting the cavalry element. 俑 (yǒng, "figurine") specifically denotes burial figurines or statues, often crafted to serve the deceased in the afterlife.
  - **interpretation:** 兵 (bīng, "soldier") conveys the concept of military troops, while 馬 (mǎ, "horse") emphasizes the involvement of cavalry, indicating the military aspect of the figures. 俑 (yǒng, "figurine") suggests these are not real individuals but sculpted figures meant for burial use, enhancing the sacred duty of serving in the afterlife. Together, 兵马俑 signifies the Terracotta Warriors, illustrating an army of lifelike soldier and horse statues buried to protect the emperor in the afterlife.
