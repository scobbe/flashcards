## working personnel; staff member
### gōngzuò rényuán
---
- **traditional:** 工作人員
- **definition:** working personnel; staff member
- **pinyin:** gōngzuò rényuán
- **components:**
  - 工作
    - gōngzuò
    - work; job; task
  - 人员(員)
    - rényuán
    - personnel; staff; crew
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 工作 (gōngzuò, "work") + 人员(員) (rényuán, "personnel") =
    - working personnel; staff member. The word combines the concept of work and the people involved, signifying individuals whose role is defined by their occupational duties.
  - **interpretation:** 工作 (gōngzuò, "work") refers to the act of performing tasks or labor. 人员(員) (rényuán, "personnel") refers to a group of people in a specified capacity. Together, these morphemes create an intuitive concept of individuals who are engaged in tasks related to their occupational role, embodying the idea of staff members or working personnel.
- **examples:**
  - 图(圖)书(書)馆(館)的工作人员(員)很友好。
    - Túshūguǎn de gōngzuò rényuán hěn yǒuhǎo.
    - The library staff are very friendly.
  - 工作人员(員)正在准(準)备(備)活动(動)。
    - Gōngzuò rényuán zhèngzài zhǔnbèi huódòng.
    - The staff members are preparing for the event.
  - 医(醫)院的工作人员(員)每天工作很努力。
    - Yīyuàn de gōngzuò rényuán měitiān gōngzuò hěn nǔlì.
    - The hospital staff work hard every day.
### 工作人员(員) → 工作
- **definition:** work; job; task
- **pinyin:** gōngzuò
- **components:**
  - 工
    - gōng
    - work; labor; trade; craft
  - 作
    - zuò
    - to make; to produce; to do; to act
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** The word 工作 (gōngzuò, "work") consists of two characters. 工 (gōng, "work; labor") often refers to physical tasks or tasks that require effort and skill. 作 (zuò, "to make; to produce") indicates the action of producing or creating something. Together, 工作 conveys the idea of engaging in labor or tasks to produce results or achieve goals.
  - **interpretation:** 工 (gōng, "work") conveys the idea of effort directed towards a task, often in a skilled trade or craft. 作 (zuò, "to make") adds the dynamic aspect of bringing something about through one's actions. Together, these elements form the concept of work as an ongoing activity where one's efforts produce tangible outputs or results.
- **examples:**
  - 他正在忙着(著)工作。
    - Tā zhèngzài mángzhe gōngzuò.
    - He is busy working.
  - 她想找到一份理想的工作。
    - Tā xiǎng zhǎodào yī fèn lǐxiǎng de gōngzuò.
    - She wants to find an ideal job.
  - 工作结(結)束后(後)，我们(們)可以一起去喝咖啡。
    - Gōngzuò jiéshù hòu, wǒmen kěyǐ yīqǐ qù hē kāfēi.
    - After work ends, we can go for coffee together.

### 工作人员(員) → 人员(員)
- **traditional:** 人員
- **definition:** personnel; staff; crew
- **pinyin:** rényuán
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 员(員)
    - yuán
    - member; employee; personnel; staff
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 人 (rén, "person") refers to individual people, while 員 (yuán, "member") denotes a member of a group or organization. Together, 人員(员) (rényuán, "personnel; staff") refers to the people who are members of a workforce or staff.
  - **interpretation:** 人 (rén, "person") represents individuals, and 員 (yuán, "member") implies belonging to a group or organization. Thus, 人員(员) (rényuán) signifies individuals collectively as members of an organization's staff. This combination highlights the idea of individuals functioning collectively within the framework of a workforce or team.
- **examples:**
  - 公司人员(員)正在开(開)会(會)。
    - Gōngsī rényuán zhèngzài kāihuì.
    - The company personnel are having a meeting.
  - 所有的医(醫)务(務)人员(員)都很专(專)业(業)。
    - Suǒyǒu de yīwù rényuán dōu hěn zhuānyè.
    - All the medical staff are very professional.
  - 新员(員)工在接受培训(訓)，以便融入团(團)队(隊)。
    - Xīn yuángōng zài jiēshòu péixùn, yǐbiàn róngrù tuánduì.
    - The new employees are undergoing training to integrate into the team.

- **pinyin:** yuán

  - **interpretation:** 〇 indicates the top edge of a 鼎 (dǐng, "ding") representing roundness, which metaphorically connects to the idea of a group of members forming a unified whole.
