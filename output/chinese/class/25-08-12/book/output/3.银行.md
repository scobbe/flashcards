- **traditional:** 銀行
- **definition:** bank
- **pinyin:** yínháng
- **components:**
  - 银(銀)
    - yín
    - silver
  - 行
    - háng
    - business; row; trade; profession
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 银(銀) (yín, "silver") + 行 (háng, "business") =
    - place for financial activities
  - **interpretation:** The word 銀行 (yínháng) literally means a place where transactions involving silver occur, reflecting the historical use of silver as currency. This makes sense as banks are institutions that manage currency and financial transactions.
- **examples:**
  - 我把钱(錢)存进(進)了银(銀)行。
    - Wǒ bǎ qián cún jìnle yínháng.
    - I deposited money into the bank.
  - 我需要取钱(錢)，所以去了银(銀)行。
    - Wǒ xūyào qǔ qián, suǒyǐ qùle yínháng.
    - I needed to withdraw money, so I went to the bank.
### 银(銀)行 → 银(銀)
- **traditional:** 銀
- **definition:** silver
- **pinyin:** yín
- **components:**
  - 金
    - jīn
    - metal
  - 艮
    - gèn
    - stopping; stubborn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 金 (jīn, "metal") + phonetic 艮 (gèn) ->
    - silver
  - **interpretation:** The character 銀 (yín, "silver") combines 金 (jīn, "metal"), indicating that it is a type of metal, with the phonetic component 艮 (gèn), which contributes the sound. This combination refers specifically to the metal silver.
  - **simplification:** 銷(金) was reduced to 钅 to create a simpler form while retaining the phonetic 艮.
- **examples:**
  - 银(銀)色在阳(陽)光下很耀眼。
    - Yínsè zài yángguāng xià hěn yàoyǎn.
    - Silver is very dazzling in the sunlight.
  - 老奶奶给(給)了我一个(個)银(銀)戒指。
    - Lǎo nǎinai gěi le wǒ yī gè yín jièzhǐ.
    - The grandmother gave me a silver ring.
  - 银(銀)色的月光洒(灑)在湖面。
    - Yínsè de yuèguāng sǎ zài húmiàn.
    - The silver moonlight spreads over the lake.

  - 呂
    - lǚ
    - two blocks of metal
  - 亼
    - jí

    - 士 (upside-down ritual axe) + 呂 (two blocks of metal) + phonetic 亼 (open mouth upside down) ->

The extension to gold reflects its value and prominence. The phonetic 亼 indicates the character's pronunciation, with semantic elements showing early associations with precious materials.
- **examples:**

### 银(銀)行 → 银(銀) → 金 → 呂
- **definition:** two blocks of metal
- **pinyin:** lǚ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts two disconnected blocks of metal, later connected in seal script ->
    - Original form of 鋁 (lǚ, 'ingot') ->
    - Borrowed phonetically for 'spine' and 'music notes'
  - **interpretation:** The character 呂(lǚ) ('two blocks of metal') visually represents two separate blocks aligned vertically. In its earliest forms, these blocks were disconnected, symbolizing distinct metal segments, which aligns with its early use for 'ingot'. Over time, as seen in seal script, a connecting line was added to form the modern appearance. This visual interpretation supports its original meaning related to metal. It was later phonetically borrowed to convey concepts like 'spine' and 'music notes', where no direct semantic link exists with metal, but the sound similarity allowed its reuse in these contexts.
- **examples:**
  - 他用两(兩)个(個)大的金属(屬)块(塊)铸(鑄)造了一个(個)雕像。
    - Tā yòng liǎng gè dà de jīnshǔ kuài zhùzào le yī gè diāoxiàng.
    - He cast a statue using two large blocks of metal.
  - 现(現)代工业(業)中，金属(屬)块(塊)常用于(於)制(製)造重型设(設)备(備)。
    - Xiàndài gōngyè zhōng, jīnshǔ kuài cháng yòng yú zhìzào zhòngxíng shèbèi.
    - In modern industry, metal blocks are often used for manufacturing heavy equipment.
  - 这(這)个(個)设(設)计(計)师(師)用金属(屬)块(塊)建造了一个(個)现(現)代化建筑(築)。
    - Zhège shèjì shī yòng jīnshǔ kuài jiànzào le yī gè xiàndàihuà jiànzhù.
    - The designer built a modern structure using metal blocks.
