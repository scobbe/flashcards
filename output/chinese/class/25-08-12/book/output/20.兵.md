## soldier; fighter
### bīng
---
- **definition:** soldier; fighter
- **pinyin:** bīng
- **components:**
  - 斤
    - jīn
    - a short axe
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - two hands
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 斤 (axe) + 廾 (two hands) =
    - hands holding a weapon ->
    - soldier; military
  - **interpretation:** The character 兵 (bīng, "soldier; military") is composed of 斤 (jīn, "a short axe"), representing a weapon, and 廾 (gǒng, "two hands"), depicting a pair of hands gripping the weapon. This combination intuitively symbolizes a person equipped with a weapon, directly leading to the meanings of "soldier" and "military."
- **examples:**
  - 他的梦(夢)想是当(當)一名优(優)秀的兵。
    - Tā de mèngxiǎng shì dāng yī míng yōuxiù de bīng.
    - His dream is to become an excellent soldier.
  - 兵营(營)里(裡)的生活很有纪(紀)律。
    - Bīngyíng lǐ de shēnghuó hěn yǒu jìlǜ.
    - Life in the barracks is very disciplined.
  - 军(軍)队(隊)集结(結)完毕(畢)，准(準)备(備)出发(發)。
    - Jūnduì jíjié wánbì, zhǔnbèi chūfā.
    - The troops have assembled and are ready to depart.

### 兵 → 斤
- **definition:** a short axe
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an axe
  - **interpretation:** The character 斤 (jīn, 'axe') is a pictogram that visually represents an axe. Its shape resembles the tool's blade and handle.
- **examples:**
  - 他拿起一把斤，熟练(練)地劈开(開)木头(頭)。
    - Tā ná qǐ yī bǎ jīn, shú liàn de pī kāi mù tóu.
    - He picked up an axe and skillfully split the firewood.
  - 古人制(製)造精巧的斤，以制(製)作雕刻工具。
    - Gǔ rén zhì zào jīng qiǎo de jīn, yǐ zhì zuò diāo kè gōng jù.
    - In ancient times, people crafted exquisite axes for carving tools.

### 兵 → 廾
- **definition:** two hands
- **pinyin:** gǒng
- **components:**
  - 𠂇
    - shǒu
    - left hand
  - 又
    - yòu
    - right hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 𠂇 (left hand) + 又 (right hand) =
    - two hands
  - **interpretation:** The character combines depictions of the left hand 𠂇 and the right hand 又, representing the concept of using both hands together. This combination naturally conveys the meaning of 'two hands.'

### 兵 → 廾 → 𠂇
- **definition:** left hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **components:**
  - 又
    - yòu
    - again
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts 又 (yòu, "again") flipped, representing a left hand.
  - **interpretation:** 𠂇 is a pictogram representing a left hand, visually derived from the character 又 (yòu, "again") by flipping it.
### 兵 → 廾 → 𠂇 → 又
- **definition:** again
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a right hand
  - **interpretation:** The character 又 (yòu, "hand") is a pictogram representing a right hand, showing the fingers and palm, which highlights its original meaning related to manual actions.
- **examples:**
  - 「又」这(這)个(個)字最早来(來)源于(於)手。
    - 「Yòu」 zhè gè zì zuì zǎo láiyuán yú shǒu。
    - The character '又' originally comes from the hand.
  - 很多汉(漢)字含有又部首，表示与(與)手有关(關)的动(動)作。
    - Hěn duō hànzì hányǒu Yòu bùshǒu, biǎoshì yǔ shǒu yǒuguān de dòngzuò。
    - Many Chinese characters include the '又' radical, indicating actions related to the hand.
  - 古人用又描绘(繪)手的形态(態)，体(體)现(現)了古汉(漢)字的独(獨)特魅力。
    - Gǔrén yòng Yòu miáohuì shǒu de xíngtài, tǐxiàn le gǔ hànzì de dútè mèilì。
    - Ancient people used '又' to depict the shape of the hand, showcasing the unique charm of ancient Chinese characters.
