## continuous aspect particle
---
- **traditional:** 著
- **simplified:** 着
- **pronunciation:** zhe
- **definition:** continuous aspect particle
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 说(說)着(著) (shuō zhe, "speaking")
  - 看着(著) (kàn zhe, "watching")
  - 带(帶)着(著) (dài zhe, "carrying")
  - 笑着(著) (xiào zhe, "smiling")
- **etymology (著):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 艹(艸) (cǎo, "grass") + phonetic: 者 (zhě, "person") → 着(著) (zhuó, "to attach").
  - **interpretation:** 着(著) combines the semantic 艹(艸) (plant indicator) with the phonetic 者 to convey its sound, which developed into modern readings zhuó/zháo/zhe. The original sense ‘to put/place; to attach’ broadened, yielding resultative uses and, by grammaticalization, the continuous-aspect particle used today.
  - **component characters:**
    - 艹(艸) (cǎo, "grass")
    - 者 (zhě, "person")
    - 著 (zhuó, "to attach")
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%91%97
  - **simplification rule (着):** The simplified form adopts a long-used popular variant by reducing the grass radical from 艸 to 䒑 and altering the 日 element within the phonetic 者 to 目, regularizing and reducing strokes.
