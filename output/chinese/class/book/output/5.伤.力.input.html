Pictogram (象形) – alternative interpretation, either an arm bending out from the side of the body, or a wooden plough. The original version was 𠠲 and the letter ㄌ in zhuyin alphabet was inspired by this character.

See also the original version of 爭, in which another plow can be seen. Unrelated to 工 and perhaps to 耒 since they are two different tools. Also unrelated to 另.

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Sino-Tibetan *k-ra (“strength; to win; classifier for people”) + *-k (STEDT; Schuessler, 2007). Cognate with Burmese အား (a:, “strength; force”); Proto-Loloish *ra² (“strength”), whence Nuosu ꊋ (we); Panyi Bai [script needed] (ɣɯ̱⁴², “strength”); Central Bai hhep (“strength”). A causative derivative is 飭 (OC rhək) (Schuessler, 2007).

Schuessler (2007) also suggests an etymological connection with 來 (OC *m·rɯːɡ, “to come”).

Definitions

[edit]

力

  1. physical strength; physical power; energy
    她個子雖小但力大驚人。 [MSC, trad.]
    她个子虽小但力大惊人。 [MSC, simp.]
    Tā gèzǐ suī xiǎo dàn lì dà jīngrén. [Pinyin]
    For a small woman she has surprising strength.
  2. force; strength; power
    汽力  ―  qìlì  ―  steam power
    藥力／药力  ―  yàolì  ―  efficacy of a drug
    力量  ―  lìliàng  ―  power
    攻擊力／攻击力  ―  gōngjīlì  ―  firepower
  3. (physics) force
    這個力的方向與位移相同。 [MSC, trad.]
    这个力的方向与位移相同。 [MSC, simp.]
    Zhè ge lì de fāngxiàng yǔ wèiyí xiāngtóng. [Pinyin]
    This force is the same direction as the displacement.
  4. ability; capability
    能力  ―  nénglì  ―  ability
    他對古董很有鑑賞力。 [MSC, trad.]
    他对古董很有鉴赏力。 [MSC, simp.]
    Tā duì gǔdǒng hěn yǒu jiànshǎng lì. [Pinyin]
    He has an eye for antiques.
  5. power and influence; might
  6. (literary) to make an effort; to strive; to endeavor; to work hard
  7. with all one's might; doing all in one's power
    力爭／力争  ―  lìzhēng  ―  to work hard for
    力挽狂瀾／力挽狂澜  ―  lìwǎnkuánglán  ―  to do one's utmost to save a desperate situation
  8. a surname, Li

  • IPA(key): [t͡ɕi̥ka̠ɾa̠]

Noun

[edit]

力(ちから) • (chikara) 

  1. strength; power; force
  2. (physics) force
  3. ability; capacity
  4. effort
  5. (tarot): Strength
Derived terms
[edit]
  • 力(ちから)足(あし) (chikara ashi): a tensed leg; tensing one's legs; (sumo) the traditional sumo stomp at the beginning of a match
  • 力(ちから)合(あ)わせ (chikara awase): matching strength: a competition of strength; (by extension) a sumo match
  • 力(ちから)軍(いくさ) (chikara ikusa): a test of strength, a competition of strength
  • 力(ちから)石(いし) (chikara ishi): a large stone used as a dumbbell for purposes of tests of strength
  • 力(ちから)一杯(いっぱい) (chikara ippai): with all one's strength, as hard as one can
  • 力(ちから)持(も)ち (chikaramochi): a very strong person
  • 力(ちから)添(ぞ)え (chikarazoe): help, assistance, aid

Proper noun

[edit]

力(ちから) • (Chikara) 

  1. a male given name
  2. a surname

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • (Tokyo) りき [ríꜜkì] (Atamadaka – [1])[2]
  • IPA(key): [ɾʲikʲi]

Affix

[edit]

力(りき) • (riki) 

  1. power, strength
    力(りき)学(がく)、力(りき)士(し)、二馬(にば)力(りき)
    rikigaku, rikishi, nibariki
    “power studies” (dynamics), “strength person” (sumo wrestler), two horsepower

Noun

[edit]

力(りき) • (riki) 

  1. physical strength
  2. (Buddhism) capability, especially mystical power
  3. (Buddhism) the suchness of ability, one of the Ten Suchnesses in Mahayana Buddhism
  4. short for 力者 (rikisha): a strong person
  5. (archaic) short for 車力 (shariki), 力車 (rikisha): a rickshaw
  6. (archaic) a rickshaw operator's wages
Derived terms
[edit]
  • 動(どう)力(りき) (dōriki, “energy, motive force”)
  • 馬(ば)力(りき) (bariki, “horsepower”)
  • 力(りき)士(し) (rikishi, “sumo wrestler”)

Etymology 3

[edit]
Kanji in this term
力
りょく
Grade: 1
kan'on

From Middle Chinese 力 (lì), but apparently from a different source dialect or at a different time than 力 (lik). The kan'on reading, so likely a later borrowing. Compare reconstructed Middle Chinese reading /li̯ək/ or Korean reading ryeok.

Only found in compounds and idioms in modern Japanese.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): [ɾʲo̞kɯ̟]

Affix

[edit]

力(りょく) • (ryoku) 

  1. strength, power
    力(りょく)争(そう)、権(けん)力(りょく)
    ryokusō, kenryoku
    “strength struggle” (a hard fight), “right power” (authority)
Derived terms
[edit]
  • 引(いん)力(りょく) (inryoku, “attractive force”)
  • 営(えい)力(りょく) (eiryoku, “geomorphic agent”)
  • 気(き)力(りょく) (kiryoku, “spirit; drive; morale; vigor”)
  • 斥(せき)力(りょく) (sekiryoku, “repulsive force”)
  • 動(どう)力(りょく) (dōryoku, “energy, motive force”)
  • 力(りょく)争(そう) (ryokusō, “test of strength; hard fight”)
  • 努(ど)力(りょく) (doryoku, “effort, endeavor”)
  • 協(きょう)力(りょく) (kyōryoku, “cooperation; working together”)
  • 体(たい)力(りょく) (tairyoku, “bodily strength; physical power”)
  • 魅(み)力(りょく) (miryoku, “allure, charm, glamour”)
  • 実(じつ)力(りょく) (jitsuryoku, “merit, true ability; force”)
