## leg
---
- **traditional:** 腿
- **simplified:** 腿
- **pronunciation:** tuǐ
- **definition:** leg
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 撞伤(傷)腿 (zhuàng shāng tuǐ, "injure the leg")
  - 大腿 (dà tuǐ, "thigh")
  - 小腿 (xiǎo tuǐ, "lower leg")
  - 桌腿 (zhuō tuǐ, "table leg")
- **etymology (腿):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 肉 (ròu, "flesh") + phonetic: 退 (tuì, "retreat").
  - **interpretation:** 肉 (ròu, "flesh") serves as the semantic determinative indicating a body part, aligning with the modern sense "leg". 退 (tuì, "retreat") functions as the phonetic, with regular historical sound change yielding the Mandarin reading tuǐ.
  - **component characters:**
    - 肉
    - 退
