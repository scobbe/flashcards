## groan
### sub-component
#### medical; doctor
---
- **traditional:** 殹
- **simplified:** 殹
- **pronunciation:** yì / yī
- **definition:** groan
- **contemporary usage:**
  - None, not in contemporary use
- **etymology (殹):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic: semantic: 殳 (shū, "weapon") + phonetic: 医(醫) (yī, "doctor").
  - **interpretation:** Formed with 殳 as the semantic determinative indicating striking or injury, and 医 as the phonetic, giving the readings yì/yī. The core sense “sound of groaning” aligns with pain or injury implied by 殳, and the character later extended to use as a sentence particle in classical texts via phonetic association.
  - **component characters:**
    - 殳
    - 医
    - 醫
