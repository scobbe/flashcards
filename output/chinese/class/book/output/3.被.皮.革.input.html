革 *krɯːɡ
諽 *krɯːɡ, *kʰrɯːɡ
緙 *kʰrɯːɡ
鞂 *kriːd

Pictogram (象形) – a stretched animal hide: the upper is head, the bottom is the tail and legs. The hands 臼 around the hide were briefly added later.

Compare 皮 (pí), which also features a hide.

Etymology 1

[edit]

Appears to be related to 殼 (OC *kʰroːɡ, “shell, husk”); see there for more (STEDT).

Definitions

[edit]

革

  1. leather; animal hide
  2. (historical) leather armour and weaponry
  3. (historical) ornament on the front of the cart
  4. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) alternative form of 勒 (“headstall”)
  5. to change; to alter; to reform
  6. to remove; to expel
    革職／革职  ―  gézhí  ―  to dismiss; to remove from office
    革扯 [Cantonese]  ―  gaak3 ce2 [Jyutping]  ―  to fire; to dismiss; to remove from office
    伊偷提廠內的物件，予廠的共伊革出去。 [Hokkien, trad.]
    伊偷提厂内的物件，予厂的共伊革出去。 [Hokkien, simp.]
    I thau-the̍h chhiúⁿ lāi ê mi̍h-kiāⁿ, hō͘ chhiúⁿ ê kā i kek chhut-khì. [Pe̍h-ōe-jī]
    He stole things inside the factory, making the factory fire him.
    • 乃命爾先祖成湯革夏，俊民甸四方。 [Classical Chinese, trad.]
      乃命尔先祖成汤革夏，俊民甸四方。 [Classical Chinese, simp.]
      From: The Book of Documents, circa 7th – 4th centuries BCE
      Nǎi mìng ěr xiānzǔ chéng Tāng gé Xià, jùn mín diàn sìfāng. [Pinyin]
      [Heaven] then directed your first ancestor, Tang the Accomplished, to overturn Xia, [Tang then employed] outstanding men to govern the four quarters of the empire.
    • 惟爾知，惟殷先人有冊有典，殷革夏命。 [Classical Chinese, trad.]
      惟尔知，惟殷先人有册有典，殷革夏命。 [Classical Chinese, simp.]
      From: The Book of Documents, circa 7th – 4th centuries BCE
      Wéi ěr zhī, wéi Yīn xiānrén yǒu cè yǒu diǎn, Yīn gé Xià mìng. [Pinyin]
      You are aware that Yin’s former rulers possessed records and statutes, and that the Yin dynasty overturned Xia’s mandate [to rule].
  7. 49th hexagram of the I Ching
  8. a surname
