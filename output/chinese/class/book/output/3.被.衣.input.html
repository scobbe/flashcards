Pictogram (象形) Outline of the chest, upper clothing. Compare to the upper part of 文.

This pictogram 衣 can be split into two parts and hold another component (e.g., 裹, 衮, 褱, 襄, and 𧝑 as used in 㞡 > 展). See also the top component of 雜.

Etymology

[edit]

Traditionally derived from Proto-Sino-Tibetan *w(y)a-t ~ wit (“to wear; to clothe”), whence Burmese ဝတ် (wat, “to wear”) (Schuessler, 2007), though the Baxter-Sagart reconstruction makes this phonetically suspect.

Pronunciation 2 ("to wear") is the exoactive form of pronunciation 1 ("garment; clothes") formed with the Old Chinese suffix *-s.

Definitions

[edit]

衣

  1. (upper) garment; clothes; clothing (Classifier: 件)
    衣服  ―  yīfú  ―  clothes
    內衣／内衣  ―  nèiyī  ―  underwear
    睡衣  ―  shuìyī  ―  night clothes; pajamas
    • 我情願節衣不願縮食。 [MSC, trad.]
      我情愿节衣不愿缩食。 [MSC, simp.]
      Wǒ qíngyuàn jié yī bù yuàn suō shí. [Pinyin]
      I'd rather economize on clothes than food.
  2. coating; covering; skin
    糖衣  ―  tángyī  ―  sugar coating
    槍衣／枪衣  ―  qiāngyī  ―  gun cover
  3. (in compounds, archaic) lichen; bryophytes
  4. membrane; film
  5. (literary or dialectal) afterbirth; placenta
  6. a surname

Definitions

[edit]

衣

  1. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese, literary) to dress; to wear; to put on clothes
    衣錦還鄉／衣锦还乡  ―  yìjǐnhuánxiāng  ―  to return home in glory
    • 相如度秦王雖齋，決負約不償城，乃使其從者衣褐，懷其璧，從徑道亡，歸璧於趙。 [Classical Chinese, trad.]
      相如度秦王虽斋，决负约不偿城，乃使其从者衣褐，怀其璧，从径道亡，归璧于赵。 [Classical Chinese, simp.]
      From: The Records of the Grand Historian, by Sima Qian, c. 91 BCE
      Xiàngrú duò Qín wáng suī zhāi, jué fùyuē bù cháng chéng, nǎi shǐ qí cóngzhě yì hè, huái qí bì, cóng jìngdào wáng, guī bì yú Zhào. [Pinyin]
      Lin Xiangru reckoned even though the King of Qin agreed to fast, he would certainly break his promise and not hand over the cities. Hence, he ordered his servant to put on ragged clothes, hide the jade under his bosom, and flee Qin on trails, in order to smuggle the jade back to Zhao.
  2. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to cover; to place something over; to wrap up; to bind up
  3. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) Original form of 依 (yī, “to rely on”).

Etymology 1

[edit]
Kanji in this term
衣
ころも
Grade: 4
kun'yomi

⟨ko₂ro₂mo₂⟩ → /koromo/

From Old Japanese. Appears in the Kojiki completed in 712 CE. Attested in the Nihon Shoki of 720 with the phonetic spelling 虚呂望 ⟨ko₂ro₂mo₂⟩.[1]

Ultimate derivation unclear. A surface analysis might suggest that this is a compound of Old Japanese *転 (*⟨ko₂ro₂⟩, “rolling, going around”) + 裳 (⟨mo₂⟩, “skirt”). However, the koro morpheme is not attested for any sense of concrete things until the 1200s, with Old Japanese attestations instead expressing the abstract sense of 頃 (koro, “around a particular time, rough time period, season”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • (Tokyo) ころも [kòrómó] (Heiban – [0])[2][3]
  • IPA(key): [ko̞ɾo̞mo̞]

Noun

[edit]

衣(ころも) • (koromo) 

  1. (archaic) clothes, clothing
    Synonyms: 服 (fuku), 衣服 (ifuku)
  2. a robe (as worn by a monk, nun, or priest)
    Synonyms: (non-clerical) 着物 (kimono), (clerical) 法衣 (hōi), (clerical) 僧衣 (sōi)
  3. a coating on an item of food, such as batter or icing
    Synonym: 外皮 (gaihi)
  4. the outer shell or carapace of an insect or other arthropod
    Synonym: 外皮 (gaihi)
  5. the hide or feathers of a small animal
    Synonym: 外皮 (gaihi)
