Pictogram (象形) – a hand (又) stripping the fur from an animal pelt (similar to 革). The pelt moved to the top left and became abstracted.

The character 叚 is similar, but actually it depicts two hands and some rocks 石 on oracle bones; a conservative variant is 𠭊.

Etymology

[edit]
“skin”
According to STEDT, 皮 (OC *m-pra-j) is likely derived from root 膚 (OC *pra) – further from Proto-Sino-Tibetan *p(r/j)a (“skin”) – and a voicing prefix – likely *m- which is found in several OC words for body parts like 鼻 (OC *m-bi[t]-s, “nose”), 肚 (OC *m-tˤaʔ, “belly”), 右 (OC *[ɢ]ʷəʔ-s (~ *m-qʷəʔ), “right-hand”) and 頭 (OC *[m-t]ˤo, “head”) (Baxter & Sagart, 2014).
“pico-”
Borrowed from English pico-.

Definitions

[edit]

皮

  1. skin; peel; shell; rind (Classifier: 塊／块 m c; 層／层 m c)
    甩皮 [Cantonese]  ―  lat1 pei4 [Jyutping]  ―  to have flaky skin; to have peeling skin
    樹皮／树皮  ―  shùpí  ―  bark
    香蕉皮  ―  xiāngjiāo pí  ―  banana peel
    • 牛則有皮，犀兕尚多。 [Classical Chinese, trad.]
      牛则有皮，犀兕尚多。 [Classical Chinese, simp.]
      From: Commentary of Zuo, c. 4th century BCE, translated based on James Legge's version
      Niú zé yǒu pí, xīsì shàng duō. [Pinyin]
      Bulls still have skins; rhinoceroses and wild bulls still are many.
    • 誰家老-馬棄城-陰，毛-暗皮-乾瘦不-禁。 [Vietnamese Literary Sinitic, trad.]
      From: 阮攸 (Nguyễn Du),〈城下棄馬〉(Thành hạ khí mã, "The Abandoned Horse at the Wall's Bottom") in 《南中雜吟》(Nam trung tạp ngâm "Assorted Recitations while in the South"), 1805-1812
      Thùy gia lão mã khí thành âm, mao ám bì can sấu bất câm. [Sino-Vietnamese]
      Someone's old horse is abandoned under the wall's shadow; he is gaunt, his hair darkened, his skin parched, beyond bearing.
  2. hide; leather
    皮鞋  ―  píxié  ―  leather shoes
    • 牛皮為鼓，正三軍之眾。 [Classical Chinese, trad.]
      牛皮为鼓，正三军之众。 [Classical Chinese, simp.]
      From: Huainanzi, 2nd century BCE
      Niúpí wéi gǔ, zhèng sānjūn zhī zhòng. [Pinyin]
      The hide of an ox made into a drum directs the multitudes of the Three Armies.
  3. wrapper; cover; skin
    餃子皮／饺子皮  ―  jiǎozi pí  ―  jiaozi wrapper
    書皮／书皮  ―  shūpí  ―  book cover
  4. surface; top part
    水皮  ―  shuǐpí  ―  water surface
  5. rubber
    皮筋  ―  píjīn  ―  rubber band
  6. thin sheet; broad, flat piece (of some thin material)
    鐵皮／铁皮  ―  tiěpí  ―  iron sheet
  7. soggy; no longer crispy due to moisture
    花生好像變皮了，吃起來沒那麼香了。 [MSC, trad.]
    花生好像变皮了，吃起来没那么香了。 [MSC, simp.]
    Huāshēng hǎoxiàng biàn pí le, chī qǐlái méi nàme xiāng le. [Pinyin]
    The peanuts seem to have become soggy, so they don't taste as good.
  8. naughty; disobedient
    調皮／调皮  ―  tiáopí  ―  naughty; mischievous
    這些孩子太皮了。 [MSC, trad.]
    这些孩子太皮了。 [MSC, simp.]
    Zhèxiē háizǐ tài pí le. [Pinyin]
    These children are too naughty.
    這個囡仔傷過皮。 [Hokkien, trad.]
    这个囡仔伤过皮。 [Hokkien, simp.]
    Chit-ê gín-á siong-kè phî. [Pe̍h-ōe-jī]
    These children are too naughty.
  9. feeling indifferent due to excessive reprimands or chastisements; thick-skinned; callous
    他早就被媽媽罵皮了。 [MSC, trad.]
    他早就被妈妈骂皮了。 [MSC, simp.]
    Tā zǎojiù bèi māmā mà pí le. [Pinyin]
    He has long been scolded by his mother to not caring anymore.
  10. pico- (SI unit prefix)
    皮米  ―  pímǐ  ―  picometre
  11. sticky; glutinous; tenacious
    皮糖  ―  pítáng  ―  sticky candy
  12. brazen; cheeky; shameless; impudent
    皮皮 [Taiwanese Hokkien]  ―  phî-phî [Pe̍h-ōe-jī]  ―  shameless
  13. outer; superficial
  14. (Harbin Mandarin) face
  15. (Cantonese) side; edge
    海皮 [Cantonese]  ―  hoi2 pei4 [Jyutping]  ―  seaside
  16. (Cantonese, colloquial) Classifier for money: yuan; dollar; buck
    廿幾皮／廿几皮 [Cantonese]  ―  jaa6 gei2 pei4 [Jyutping]  ―  over twenty dollars
