## be OK or all right; profession or trade
### sub-word
#### bicycle
---
- **traditional:** 行
- **simplified:** 行
- **pronunciation:** xíng; háng
- **definition:** be OK or all right; profession or trade
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 行动(動) (xíng dòng, "take action")
  - 行程 (xíng chéng, "travel itinerary")
  - 行李 (xíng lǐ, "travel luggage")
  - 行骗(騙) (xíng piàn, "commit fraud")
- **etymology (行):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: depiction of a street intersection → semantic: 彳 (chì, "step") + 亍 (chù, "step").
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a street intersection, originally tied to paths and movement. From the sense ‘to walk’ it extended to ‘to proceed, carry out’, which underlies modern uses like ‘OK’. Later folk analysis reinterpreted it as 彳 and 亍 ‘step’ components; some scholars also suggest the word may be a Mon-Khmer loan, but the character itself began as a pictograph.
  - **component characters:**
    - 彳 (chì, "step")
    - 亍 (chù, "step")
