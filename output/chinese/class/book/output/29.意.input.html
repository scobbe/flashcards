Modern form is a compound of 音 and 心 (“heart”). However, the top component is etymologically unrelated to 音. According to Lin (1920), 𠶷 (a compound of 言 and 中) found in bronze inscriptions is the same character as 意 (yì).

Definitions

[edit]

意

  1. wish; desire; intention
    好意  ―  hǎoyì  ―  good intention; kindness
    隨意／随意  ―  suíyì  ―  as one pleases; at will
  2. meaning; sense
    詞不達意／词不达意  ―  cíbùdáyì  ―  the words fail to convey the meaning
  3. to anticipate; to expect
    意外  ―  yìwài  ―  unexpected
    出其不意  ―  chūqíbùyì  ―  to take somebody by surprise
  4. (Mainland China, Hong Kong) short for 意大利 (Yìdàlì): Italy (a country in Southern Europe); Italian
    意政府  ―  yì zhèngfǔ  ―  Italian government
    赴意  ―  fù yì  ―  (formal) to go to Italy
    訪意／访意  ―  fǎng yì  ―  (formal) to visit Italy
  5. (Hong Kong Cantonese) short for 意粉 (ji3 fan2, “spaghetti”)
    乾炒叉燒意／干炒叉烧意 [Cantonese]  ―  gon1 caau2 caa1 siu1 ji3 [Jyutping]  ―  fried spaghetti with barbecue pork
