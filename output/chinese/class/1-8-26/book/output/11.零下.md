## zero; below zero
### líng (xià)
---
- **definition:** zero; below zero
- **pinyin:** líng (xià)
- **components:**
  - 零
    - líng
    - zero
  - 下
    - xià
    - below; under; down
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 零 (líng, "zero"), 下 (xià, "below")
  - **interpretation:** The word 零下 (língxià) combines 零 (líng, "zero") with 下 (xià, "below") to convey the concept of being 'below zero'. This typically refers to temperatures that are less than zero degrees, commonly used in discussions of weather conditions, indicating freezing or sub-zero temperatures.
- **examples:**

    - Last night, the temperature dropped drastically, and subzero cold enveloped the entire city.
  - 寒( )冬( )将(將)至( )，零( )下( )气(氣)温(溫)考( )验(驗)着(著)人( )们(們)的( )耐( )寒( )能( )力( )。

### 零下 → 零
- **definition:** zero
- **pinyin:** líng
- **components:**
  - 雨
    - yǔ
    - rain
  - 令
    - lìng
    - command
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 雨 (yǔ, "rain") + 令 (lìng, "command") =
    - rain with large droplets as depicted in oracle script ->
    - associated with the loss or remainder of rain droplets ->
    - zero
  - **interpretation:** The character 零(líng) combines the semantic element of 雨 (yǔ, "rain") with the phonetic component 令 (lìng, "command"). Originally, it depicted raindrops falling from the sky, denoting something remaining or leftover. This visual representation of raindrops reduced to nothing contributed to its current meaning of "zero."
