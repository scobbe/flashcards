## thirsty
### kě
---
- **definition:** thirsty
- **pinyin:** kě
- **components:**
  - 氵
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 曷
    - hé
    - dry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 氵(水) (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic: 曷 (hé, "dry") =
    - dry or exhausted of water ->
    - thirsty
  - **interpretation:** The character 渴(kě, "thirsty") combines the water radical 氵(水) to indicate the relation to water, with 曷 (hé, "dry") as the phonetic component, suggesting dryness or a lack of water, which directly conveys the concept of thirst.
- **examples:**
  - 我感觉(覺)非常渴。
    - Wǒ gǎnjué fēicháng kě.
    - I feel very thirsty.
  - 长时间运动后(長時間運動後)，他开(開)始感到渴。
    - Cháng shíjiān yùndòng hòu, tā kāishǐ gǎndào kě.
    - After long exercise, he started to feel thirsty.
  - 口渴时(時)，喝一杯冰水很解乏。
    - Kǒu kě shí, hē yì bēi bīngshuǐ hěn jiěfá.
    - When thirsty, drinking a glass of iced water is very refreshing.
### 渴 → 氵
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 氵is the radical form of 水 (shuǐ, "water") ->
    - depicts flowing water, compressed and stylized next to characters.
  - **interpretation:** The character 氵is a simplified variant used as a radical to represent water-related meanings in other characters. It retains the flowing water depiction of 水 (shuǐ, "water") but is stylized to fit alongside other components.
- **examples:**
  - 水是生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 汉(漢)字中偏旁氵，代表水。
    - Hànzì zhōng piān páng shuǐ, dàibiǎo shuǐ.
    - In Chinese characters, the radical '氵' signifies water.
  - 河水清澈透明，鱼儿欢(魚兒歡)快戏(戲)水。
    - Hé shuǐ qīngchè tòumíng, yú er huānkuaì xì shuǐ.
    - The river water is clear and transparent, and the fish happily play in it.
### 渴 → 曷
- **definition:** dry
- **pinyin:** hé
- **components:**
  - 匃
    - gài
    - lack
  - 曰
    - yuē
    - say
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 匃 (gài, "lack") + semantic 曰 (yuē, "say") ->
    - final form 曷
  - **interpretation:** The character 曷(hé, "dry") combines 匃 (gài, "lack") as a phonetic component and 曰 (yuē, "say") as the semantic component. The depiction could suggest a lack or absence, which by extension relates to the meaning "dry" as it implies the absence of moisture.
### 渴 → 曷 → 匃
- **definition:** lack
- **pinyin:** gài
- **components:**
  - 丐
    - gài
    - beggar; lack
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** variant character
  - **description:** 匃 is a variant form of 丐 (gài, "beggar; lack")
  - **interpretation:** The character 匃 is a variant form of 丐 (gài, "beggar; lack"), sharing a similar meaning and usage in the context of indicating lack or poverty. It is not frequently used in modern writing, where 丐 is the standard form.
### 渴 → 曷 → 匃 → 丐
- **definition:** beggar; lack
- **pinyin:** gài
- **components:**
  - 匄
    - gài
    - original meaning unknown
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Corruption of 匄 (gài, original meaning unknown) ->
    - final form 丐 (gài, 'beggar')
  - **interpretation:** 丐 (gài) is derived from 匄, which originally had an unknown meaning, possibly 'to damage'. Over time, 丐 evolved into its current use to mean 'beggar'. The transformation appears to involve a simplification or corruption of the original form, indicating a shift in both form and meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 街上有个(個)丐，他四处讨饭(處討飯)。
    - Jiē shàng yǒu gè gài, tā sì chù tǎo fàn.
    - There is a beggar on the street, and he begs for food everywhere.
  - 他曾是繁华(華)都市边缘(邊緣)的丐，今日落魄街头(頭)。
    - Tā céng shì fán huá dū shì biān yuán de gài, jīn rì luò pò jiē tóu.
    - He used to be a beggar on the outskirts of a bustling city, but today he is destitute on the street.

### 渴 → 曷 → 匃 → 丐 → 匄 → 刀
- **definition:** knife
- **pinyin:** dāo
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a knife
  - **interpretation:** 刀 (dāo, "knife") is a pictographic representation of a knife or blade, with a sharp edge and a handle, resembling the form of a basic cutting tool.
- **examples:**
  - 他用锋(鋒)利的刀，切开(開)蔬菜。

  - 这(這)把刀非常锋(鋒)利。
    - zhè bǎ dāo fēicháng fēnglì.
    - This knife is very sharp.
  - 厨师(廚師)小心地持刀，为(為)客人切肉。
    - chúshī xiǎoxīn de chí dāo, wèi kèrén qiē ròu.

### 渴 → 曷 → 匃 → 丐 → 匄 → 亡
- **definition:** tip
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **components:**
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - Line on 刀 (dāo, "knife") indicates cutting edge ->
    - disappearance of knowledge ->
    - perish
  - **interpretation:** The character 亡(wáng) visually represents the sharp, cutting edge of a knife, which metaphorically implies the loss of life or things 'perishing'. Over time, it came to signify broader concepts of disappearance and extinction.
- **examples:**
  - 国(國)破，家亡。
    - Guó pò, jiā wáng.
    - The country is broken, the family perishes.
  - 经过(經過)激战时(戰時)，士兵亡。
    - Jīngguò jīzhàn shí, shìbīng wáng.
    - During the intense battle, the soldiers perished.
### 渴 → 曷 → 曰
- **definition:** say
- **pinyin:** yuē
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth, signifying speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 曰 (yuē, "say") visually resembles a mouth or an opening, symbolizing the action of speaking or saying something.
- **examples:**
  - 老师(師)曰：勤奋(奮)是成功之母。
    - Lǎoshī yuē：qínfèn shì chénggōng zhī mǔ。
    - The teacher said, 'Diligence is the mother of success.'
  - 孔子曰，学(學)而不思则(則)罔。
    - Kǒngzǐ yuē, xué ér bù sī zé wǎng。
    - Confucius said, 'Learning without thought leads to confusion.'
  - 老子曰，无为(無為)而治。
    - Lǎozǐ yuē, wúwéi ér zhì。
    - Laozi said, 'Governance should be achieved through non-action.'
