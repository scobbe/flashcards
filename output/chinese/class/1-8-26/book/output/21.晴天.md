## sunny day; clear sky
### qíngtiān
---
- **definition:** sunny day; clear sky
- **pinyin:** qíngtiān
- **components:**
  - 晴
    - qíng
    - clear; fine [weather]
  - 天
    - tiān
    - sky; day
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 晴 (qíng, "clear; fine [weather]") + 天 (tiān, "sky; day") =
    - a day with clear sky and good weather.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 晴 (qíng, "clear; fine [weather]") and 天 (tiān, "sky; day") conveys the idea of a day where the sky is clear and the weather is favorable. This aligns with the term 'sunny day' or 'clear sky,' describing conditions without clouds or rain, highlighting pleasant weather.

### 晴天 → 天
- **definition:** sky; day
- **pinyin:** tiān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Horizontal line above 大(dà, "big") to depict sky ->
    - anthropomorphic man with large head symbolizing heaven
  - **interpretation:** The character 天(tiān) originally depicts the sky as a horizontal line above a person with outstretched arms, 大(dà, "big"), emphasizing the vastness or domain of heaven. Over time, the character evolved into an anthropomorphic representation of heaven, which later influenced its interpretation in classical texts.
