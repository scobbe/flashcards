- **pinyin:** càidān
- **components:**

  - 单(單)
    - dān

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 菜 (cài, "vegetable; dish") + 单(單) (dān, "list") =
    - 菜( )單(单) (càidān, "menu")
  - **interpretation:** The word 菜( )單(单) (càidān) combines 菜 (cài, "vegetable; dish") which refers to food or dishes, and 单(單) (dān, "list") indicating a list or record. Together, it logically refers to a list of dishes, or a menu. This reflects the exact purpose of a menu as it provides a list of dishes available to choose from.

- **pinyin:** cài
- **components:**
  - 艹

    - cǎi

  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, "grass; plant") + phonetic 采 (cǎi, "pluck") =

    - vegetable; dish
  - **interpretation:** The character 菜(cài) conveys the idea of plants that are gathered, emphasizing the connection between agriculture and cuisine. The 艹 component implies vegetation, while 采 provides the phonetic cue and ties to the action of picking or collecting.

### 菜( )单(單) → 菜 → 采(採)
- **traditional:** 採
- **definition:** pluck
- **pinyin:** cǎi
- **components:**
  - 扌(手)
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 扌(hand) + 采(to pick) =
    - action of picking with hand ->
    - pluck
  - **interpretation:** The character 採 represents the action of picking or plucking using one's hand. The semantic component 扌(shǒu, "hand") indicates the action being performed, while 采(cǎi, "to pick") serves the phonetic role. Over time, 采 was retained in simplified usage, whereas 採 is used traditionally.
  - **simplification:** 采 is a simplification of 採 by using the original form that omits the 手 hand radical for brevity while retaining the phonetic element.
- **examples:**

### 菜( )单(單) → 菜 → 采(採) → 扌
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stylized hand. The top stroke is the bent middle finger, and the horizontal strokes each represent two fingers.
  - **interpretation:** 扌 is a simplified form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), retaining the key elements that visually represent a human hand. It is commonly used as a component in other characters related to hand actions.
  - **simplification:** 扌 is derived as a cursive or shorthand form of 手 (shǒu, 'hand'), making it easier to incorporate into complex characters as a radical or component.

### 菜( )单(單) → 单(單)
- **traditional:** 單
- **definition:** list
- **pinyin:** dān
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 田
    - tián
    - field
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Unorthodox variant of 單.
  - **description:**
    - Pictogram of a Y-shaped wooden stick A + two stones attached on top B =
    - weapon for hunting ->
    - later stylized to resemble 口 (kǒu, 'mouth') resembling 中 (zhōng, 'middle') ->
    - further stylized as 田 (tián, 'field') with additional horizontal stroke below ->
    - final form 單.
  - **interpretation:** The character 單 intuitively represents a hunting tool or weapon, emphasizing its initial function and eventual simplification and stylization over time. The component 单 in this case represents a simplified form, which might have been used for practical writing needs.
  - **simplification:** 單 was simplified to 单 by eliminating additional strokes, creating a more straightforward representation that aligns with modern writing practices.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)将(將)参(參)加( )聚( )会(會)的( )人( )员(員)名( )单(單)准(準)备(備)好( )。

  - 老( )师(師)检(檢)查( )了( )学(學)生( )考( )试(試)名( )单(單)。
