## to visit (a place)
### cānguān
---
- **traditional:** 參觀
- **definition:** to visit (a place)
- **pinyin:** cānguān
- **components:**
  - 参(參)
    - cān
    - participate; reference; take part in
  - 观(觀)
    - guān
    - observe; watch; look
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 参(參) (cān, "participate") + 观(觀) (guān, "observe") =
    - to visit
  - **interpretation:** 参(參)观(觀) (cānguān, "to visit") combines the idea of participation with observing. When you visit a place such as a museum or exhibition, you partake in the act of experiencing the environment while taking in its features and details through observation.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )们(們)参(參)观(觀)了( )博( )物( )馆(館)。
    - Wǒmen cānguān le bówùguǎn.
    - We visited the museum.
  - 他( )明( )天( )参(參)观(觀)展( )览(覽)，然( )后(後)去( )咖( )啡( )馆(館)。
    - Tā míngtiān cānguān zhǎnlǎn, ránhòu qù kāfēiguǎn.
    - He will visit the exhibition tomorrow, then go to the coffee shop.

### 参(參)观(觀) → 观(觀)

### 参(參)观(觀) → 观(觀) → 雚
- **definition:** cry of waterfowl
- **pinyin:** guān
- **components:**
  - 雈
    - huán
    - original form of 雚
  - 吅
    - xuān
    - cry
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted a stork ->
    - same as 雈 ->
    - phonetic 吅 (cry) added early in history ->
    - current form 雚
  - **interpretation:** The character 雚 began as a pictogram resembling a stork. To convey both its phonetic properties and its meaning related to the cries of waterfowl, the component 吅 was incorporated early on as an additional phonetic marker.
  - **simplification:** Now written as 鸛(雚) (guàn, "stork") with 鸟(鳥) (niǎo, "bird") signifying a bird's cry.

It serves as an early form closely related to or associated with 雚 (guàn, "bittern"), indicating an origin focused on avian imagery.
