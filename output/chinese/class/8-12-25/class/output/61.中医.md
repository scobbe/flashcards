## traditional Chinese medicine; TCM doctor
---
- **traditional:** 中醫
- **simplified:** 中医
- **pronunciation:** zhōng yī
- **definition:** traditional Chinese medicine; TCM doctor
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 中医师
    - zhōng yī shī
    - TCM doctor
  - 中医学(中医學)
    - zhōng yī xué
    - TCM studies
  - 中医科
    - zhōng yī kē
    - TCM department
  - 中医药
    - zhōng yī yào
    - TCM medicine and drugs
- **etymology (中醫):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 中 (zhōng, "China; Chinese") + 医(醫) (yī, "medical; medicine; doctor")
  - **interpretation:** It combines 中 "China; Chinese" and 医(醫) "medicine; doctor" to label the medical tradition originating in China and, by extension, its practitioners. The term arose to distinguish it from Western medicine after its introduction in the mid-19th century, which explains its modern senses.
  - **component characters:**
    - 中
      - zhōng
      - middle; China
    - 医(醫)
      - yī
      - medical
    - 医(醫)
      - yī
      - medical
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E4%B8%AD%E9%86%AB
  - **simplification rule (中医):** 医 simplifies 醫 by replacing complex components with a reduced form and removing intricate elements to reduce stroke count.
