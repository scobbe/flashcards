- **etymology (告):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - pictogram of 口 (kǒu, "mouth") with a protruding tongue
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a mouth with a tongue protruding, signaling speaking or proclamation. Some scholars instead analyze it as an ideogrammic compound of 壴 (zhù, "drum") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth"), evoking ritual announcements; Shuowen’s 牛 (niú, "cow") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") is generally rejected. The association with speech and announcement motivates modern senses like "tell/inform" and the extended legal sense "accuse/sue".
