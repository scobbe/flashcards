## doctor or physician
---
- **traditional:** 醫生
- **simplified:** 医生
- **pronunciation:** yī shēng
- **definition:** doctor or physician
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 医(醫)生说(說) (yī shēng shuō, "doctor says")
  - 医(醫)生在给(給)病人看病 (yī shēng zài gěi bìngrén kànbìng, "doctor seeing a patient")
  - 医(醫)生的嘱(囑)咐 (yī shēng de zhǔfù, "doctor's instructions")
  - 医(醫)生节(節) (yī shēng jié, "Doctor's Day")
- **etymology (醫生):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 医(醫) (yī, "medicine") + 生 (shēng, "person").
  - **interpretation:** Combines 医 meaning medicine with 生 in the sense of a person or practitioner, yielding “medical person,” i.e., a doctor. The use of 生 as a noun suffix for people aligns with modern usage where 医生 denotes a medical professional.
  - **component characters:**
    - 医
    - 醫
    - 生
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%86%AB%E7%94%9F
  - **simplification rule (医生):** Simplified form uses the long-established shorthand 医 in place of 醫 (component replacement and stroke reduction); 生 is unchanged.
