- **etymology (公):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic: 八 (bā, "opposite") + 厶 (sī, "private")
  - **interpretation:** Combines 八 (bā, "opposite") and 厶 (sī, "private") to express “not private,” hence “public,” which underlies modern senses like “fair, impartial.” Alternative views analyze 八 as indicating 分 (fēn, "divide") with 口 (kǒu, "object") to mean equal sharing, or see 公 as a pictogram of 瓮(甕) (wèng, "jar") later borrowed for the “public/duke” senses.
  - **component characters:**
    - 八
      - bā
      - eight
    - 厶
      - sī
      - private
