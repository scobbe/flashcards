## occupy
*sub-component of*
1. shop or store
---
- **traditional:** 占
- **simplified:** 占
- **pronunciation:** zhàn
- **definition:** occupy
- **breadcrumbs:**
  - 店
    - diàn
    - shop or store
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 占据
    - zhàn jù
    - to occupy
  - 占领
    - zhàn lǐng
    - to capture
  - 占有
    - zhàn yǒu
    - to possess
  - 占用
    - zhàn yòng
    - to take up
- **etymology (占):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - ideogrammic: 卜 (bǔ, "divination") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth")
  - **interpretation:** Combines 卜 “divination” with 口 “mouth”, indicating uttering or announcing a divination result. Originally it meant to divine or foretell. The modern sense “to occupy” is written 佔 in Traditional and is merged into 占 in Simplified orthography.
  - **component characters:**
    - 卜
      - bǔ
      - divine
    - 口
      - kǒu
      - mouth
