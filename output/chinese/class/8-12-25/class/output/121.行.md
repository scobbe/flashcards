## to go; to travel
*sub-word of*
1. travel; trip
---
- **traditional:** 行
- **simplified:** 行
- **pronunciation:** xíng
- **definition:** to go; to travel
- **breadcrumbs:**
  - 旅行
    - lǚ xíng
    - travel; trip
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 行程
    - xíngchéng
    - itinerary
  - 行装
    - xíngzhuāng
    - baggage
  - 行商
    - xíngshāng
    - itinerant merchant
  - 行营
    - xíngyíng
    - field camp
- **etymology (行):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - pictogram: depiction of a street intersection
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a crossroads, naturally extended to the idea of moving along roads. From this, it came to mean to walk or travel, and further generalized to senses like “to go” or “to carry out” in modern use. Some scholars also suggest the word may be a Mon-Khmer loan, but the character’s form is pictographic.

    - None, character is in atomic form.
