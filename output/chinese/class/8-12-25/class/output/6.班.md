- **etymology (班):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - ideogrammic: 珏 (jué, "pieces of jade") + 刀 (dāo, "knife")
  - **interpretation:** Combining pieces of jade with a knife depicts cutting or dividing jade into parts, yielding the sense of separation and distribution. This concrete idea extended to arranging people or things into groups and ranks, which underlies modern meanings like class and work shift.
  - **component characters:**
    - 珏
      - jué
      - two jades
    - 刀
      - dāo
      - knife
