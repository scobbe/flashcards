## open box
*sub-component of*
1. life; person
2. bud
---
- **traditional:** 凵
- **simplified:** 凵
- **pronunciation:** qiǎn
- **definition:** open box
- **breadcrumbs:**
  - 生
    - shēng
    - life; person
  - 屮
    - chè
    - bud
- **contemporary usage:**
  - None, not in contemporary use.
- **etymology (凵):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - pictogram: depiction of a cave
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a cave. By extension it became the radical for an open receptacle or box, and in early Chinese it was also used alone to mean opening the mouth, both reflecting an open aperture. In modern usage it persists mainly as a radical/component.
  - **component characters:**
    - None, character is in atomic form.
