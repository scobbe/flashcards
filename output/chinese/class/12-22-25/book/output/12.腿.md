## 腿
---
- **pinyin:** tuǐ
- **definition:** leg
- **etymology:** Phono-semantic: the left “flesh” radical ⺼/月 marks a body part, while 退 on the right provides the sound and evokes stepping/retreating, fitting the idea of the limb used to move.
- **examples:**
  - 她的腿很长(她的腿很長)。
    - tā de tuǐ hěn cháng
    - Her legs are long.
  - 撞伤腿(撞傷腿)。
    - zhuàng shāng tuǐ
    - To bump and injure one’s leg.
  - 腿有点儿疼(腿有點兒疼)。
    - tuǐ yǒu diǎnr téng
    - My leg hurts a little.
