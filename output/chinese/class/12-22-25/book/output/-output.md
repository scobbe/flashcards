## 着(著)
---
- **pinyin:** zhe
- **definition:** indicating the continuous aspect
- **etymology:** Phono-semantic: traditionally built with the 'grass' radical (signaling something manifest/standing out) plus a phonetic element; the simplified form features an 'eye,' evoking something perceptible. This notion of being attached/visible underlies its role marking a state currently in effect (continuous aspect).
- **examples:**
  - 灯还亮着(燈還亮著)。
    - dēng hái liàng zhe
    - The light is still on.
  - 他小声说着(他小聲說著)。
    - tā xiǎo shēng shuō zhe
    - He kept speaking in a low voice.
  - 她笑着向我招手(她笑著向我招手)。
    - tā xiào zhe xiàng wǒ zhāo shǒu
    - She waved to me with a smile.
  - 我在门口等着你(我在門口等著你)。
    - wǒ zài ménkǒu děng zhe nǐ
    - I’m waiting for you at the door.
  - 他开着车打电话很危险(他開著車打電話很危險)。
    - tā kāi zhe chē dǎ diànhuà hěn wēixiǎn
    - It’s dangerous to talk on the phone while driving.
  - 她带着花儿来了(她帶著花兒來了)。
    - tā dài zhe huār lái le
    - She came carrying flowers.
  - 她穿着旗袍参加晚会(她穿著旗袍參加晚會)。
    - tā chuān zhe qípáo cānjiā wǎnhuì
    - She attended the evening party wearing a qipao.

## 重

## 完
---
- **pinyin:** wán
- **definition:** to finish; to run out
- **etymology:** A phonetic-semantic compound: 宀 ‘roof/house’ over 元 ‘origin/primary’ (phonetic), evoking a structure made whole—hence ‘complete/finished.’ By extension, when something is fully completed, nothing remains, giving the sense ‘to run out.’
- **examples:**
  - 我已经写完报告了(我已經寫完報告了)。
    - wǒ yǐjīng xiě wán bàogào le
    - I’ve finished writing the report.
  - 电池用完了(電池用完了)。
    - diànchí yòng wán le
    - The batteries have run out.
  - 看完电影(看完電影)。
    - kàn wán diànyǐng
    - Finish watching the movie.
  - 做完练习(做完練習)。
    - zuò wán liànxí
    - Finish the exercises.
  - 用完钱(用完錢)。
    - yòng wán qián
    - Run out of money.
  - 吃完饭(吃完飯)。
    - chī wán fàn
    - Finish eating the meal.
