## take turns
### lún
---
- **traditional:** 輪
- **definition:** take turns
- **pinyin:** lún
- **components:**
  - 車
    - chē
    - carriage
  - 侖
    - lún
    - order
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 車 (chē, "carriage") + phonetic 侖 (lún, "order") ->
    - take turns
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 車 (chē, "carriage"), indicating the wheel's association with vehicles, and 侖 (lún, "order"), suggesting the orderly sequence or turning. This imagery of a wheel turning in an orderly fashion leads to the idea of 'taking turns'.
  - **simplification:** The traditional form 輪 was simplified by replacing 車 (chē, "carriage") with 车, and 侖 (lún, "order") with 仑, reducing the character's complexity while preserving both the semantic vehicle and the sound.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)应(應)该(該)轮(輪)流洗碗。
    - Wǒmen yīnggāi lúnliú xǐwǎn.
    - We should take turns washing the dishes.
  - 老师(師)让(讓)每个(個)学(學)生轮(輪)流发(發)言。
    - Lǎoshī ràng měi gè xuéshēng lúnliú fāyán.
    - The teacher asked each student to take turns speaking.
  - 在晚会(會)上，他们(們)轮(輪)流表演节(節)目。
    - Zài wǎnhuì shàng, tāmen lúnliú biǎoyǎn jiémù.
    - At the party, they took turns performing.
### 轮(輪) → 車
- **definition:** carriage
- **pinyin:** chē
- **components:**
  - 田
    - tián
    - field
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a carriage viewed from above; wheels on both sides + sun shade on top ->
    - depicted vertically with wheels as strokes and loading area marked by 田 (tián, "field") ->
    - 車
  - **interpretation:** The character 車(chē, "vehicle") originally depicts a carriage from an overhead perspective. The transformation into the vertical form involved simplifying the depiction of wheels and emphasizing the loading area, marked by a field symbol, creating a streamlined pictographic representation.
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 車 was simplified to 车 by reducing the number of strokes, maintaining the overall shape of the vehicle but removing intricate details.
- **examples:**
  - 我坐车(車)去上班。
    - Wǒ zuò chē qù shàngbān.
    - I take a car to go to work.
  - 这(這)辆(輛)车(車)很快。
    - Zhè liàng chē hěn kuài.
    - This car is very fast.
  - 我想买(買)一辆(輛)新车(車)。
    - Wǒ xiǎng mǎi yī liàng xīn chē.
    - I want to buy a new car.

### 轮(輪) → 車 → 田
- **definition:** field
- **pinyin:** tián
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a field divided into four sections
  - **interpretation:** The character 田(tián, 'field') visually represents a field with sections, indicating its agricultural use and importance in ancient times.
- **examples:**
  - 农(農)民在田里(裡)耕作。
    - Nóngmín zài tián lǐ gēngzuò.
    - The farmer works in the field.
  - 那边(邊)的田长(長)满(滿)了庄(莊)稼，农(農)民收获(穫)忙碌。
    - Nàbiān de tián cháng mǎn le zhuāngjià, nóngmín shōuhuò mánglù.
    - The field over there is full of crops, and the farmer is busy harvesting.
  - 秋收时(時)节(節)，人们(們)忙于(於)收割田里(裡)的稻谷。
    - Qiūshōu shíjié, rénmen máng yú shōugē tián lǐ de dàogǔ.
    - During the harvest season, people are busy reaping the rice in the fields.

  - **interpretation:** The character 侖(lún) combines 亼, which can symbolize gathering or a mouth speaking, with 冊, meaning 'book', to depict an action associated with organizing or arranging books, ultimately indicating order or coherence.

### 轮(輪) → 侖 → 亼
- **definition:** gather
- **pinyin:** jí
- **components:**
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter
  - 一
    - yī
    - one
  - 今
    - jīn
    - now
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 入 (rù, "enter") + 一 (yī, "one") =
    - gathering from three sides ->
    - original form of 今 (jīn, "now")
  - **interpretation:** 亼 (jī, "open mouth flipped upside down") originally represented the action of gathering, with 入 (rù, "enter") suggesting bringing things together and 一 (yī, "one") implying unification into a single entity. This concept aligns traditionally with 集 (jí, "gather"), where the sense of gathering from three sides connects to assembling or coming together. The visual comparison to an open mouth reversed is a less common interpretation, relating to a shape rather than function.

### 轮(輪) → 侖 → 亼 → 入
- **definition:** enter
- **pinyin:** rù
- **etymology:**

  - **description:** An arrowhead indicating 'to enter'
  - **interpretation:** The character 入(rù, 'enter') visually represents the concept of entering, with the form resembling an arrowhead suggesting movement through an opening.

    - Yóukè zǒu rù gǔchéng, tànsuǒ lìshǐ.
    - The tourists enter the ancient city and explore its history.

### 轮(輪) → 侖 → 亼 → 一
- **definition:** one
- **pinyin:** yī
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** A horizontal stroke representing the concept of 'one'.
  - **interpretation:** The character 一(yī, "one") is a simple horizontal line signifying the singular and unified concept of 'one'. It directly represents being singular or the number one.
- **examples:**

    - Wǒ yǒu yī gè píngguǒ.
    - I have one apple.
  - 他只看到一只(隻)鸟(鳥)，飞(飛)进(進)了花园(園)。

    - He only saw one bird, which flew into the garden.
  - 一条(條)直路通向远(遠)方。
    - Yī tiáo zhí lù tōng xiàng yuǎn fāng.

### 轮(輪) → 侖 → 亼 → 今
- **definition:** now
- **pinyin:** jīn
- **components:**
  - 曰
    - yuē
    - to speak
  - 亼
    - jí
    - collect; assemble
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 曰 (yuē, "to speak") inverted with 亼-like structure + horizontal stroke ->
    - original form implying 'not to speak' or 'keep in mouth'
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts an inverted version of 曰 (yuē, "to speak"), with a structure resembling 亼 and a horizontal stroke, representing something placed in the mouth, leading to the sense of 'keeping in the mouth' or 'not speaking'. This image of being contained or holding leads to 'now', as in holding or having the present moment.
- **examples:**
  - 今天的天气(氣)不错(錯)。
    - Jīntiān de tiānqì bùcuò.
    - Today's weather is nice.
  - 现(現)在是吃晚饭(飯)的时(時)候。
    - Xiànzài shì chī wǎnfàn de shíhòu.
    - Now is the time to have dinner.
  - 她今天特别(別)忙，没(沒)时(時)间(間)见(見)面。
    - Tā jīntiān tèbié máng, méi shíjiān jiànmiàn.
    - She is especially busy today and doesn't have time to meet.

- **definition:** to speak
- **pinyin:** yuē
- **etymology:**

  - 老子曰，无(無)为(為)而治。

### 轮(輪) → 侖 → 冊
- **definition:** book
- **pinyin:** cè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts bamboo slips strung together ->
    - books; documents
  - **interpretation:** The character 冊(cè, 'a form made by lining up pieces of wood and binding them together') represents bamboo slips tied together with string, symbolizing early forms of books or documents compiled in this manner.
  - **simplification:** 冊 was simplified to 册 by removing interior strokes, forming a more straightforward and less intricate version.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)本书(書)共有五冊。
    - Zhè běn shū gòngyǒu wǔ cè.
    - This book consists of five volumes.
  - 古代的册(冊)子是用竹片制作的。
    - Gǔdài de cèzi shì yòng zhúpiàn zhìzuò de.
    - Ancient booklets were made using bamboo strips.
  - 他在研究一件古老的册(冊)。
    - Tā zài yánjiū yī jiàn gǔlǎo de cè.
    - He is studying an ancient tome.
