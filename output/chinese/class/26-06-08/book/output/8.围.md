## wrap; swathe
### wéi
---
- **traditional:** 圍
- **definition:** wrap; swathe
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **components:**
  - 囗
    - wéi
    - enclosure
  - 韋
    - wéi
    - soft leather
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 囗 (wéi, "enclosure") + 韋 (wéi, "soft leather") =
    - something surrounded by an enclosure ->
    - surround
  - **interpretation:** The character 围(圍) (wéi, "surround") combines the concept of an enclosure with the phonetic element 韋 (wéi, "soft leather"). This combination intuitively suggests the idea of surrounding something, much like enclosing soft leather around an object.
  - **simplification:** 圍 → 围(圍): 囗 (wéi, "enclosure") was simplified by removing the internal component 韋 (wéi, "soft leather").
- **examples:**
  - 士兵围(圍)困敌(敵)军(軍)。
    - Shìbīng wéikùn díjūn.
    - Soldiers surrounded the enemy army.
  - 村民围(圍)绕(繞)湖边(邊)举(舉)行庆(慶)典。
    - Cūnmín wéirào húbiān jǔxíng qìngdiǎn.
    - Villagers held a celebration around the lake.
  - 老师(師)围(圍)着(著)学(學)生讲(講)解科学(學)。
    - Lǎoshī wéizhe xuéshēng jiǎngjiě kēxué.
    - The teacher explained science while gathering the students around.
### 围(圍) → 囗
- **definition:** enclosure
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an enclosure
  - **interpretation:** The character 囗 (wéi, 'enclosure') is a pictogram representing a square or rectangular enclosure, emblematic of walls or boundaries. It visually reflects a basic enclosed area delineated by straight lines.
- **examples:**
  - 博物馆(館)外有坚(堅)固的囗，保护(護)着(著)历(歷)史文物。
    - Bówùguǎn wài yǒu jiāngù de wéi, bǎohùzhe lìshǐ wénwù.
    - Outside the museum, a sturdy enclosure protects historical artifacts.
  - 古城外围(圍)的囗，确(確)保了居民的安全。
    - Gǔchéng wàiwéi de wéi, quèbǎole jūmín de ānquán.

### 围(圍) → 韋
- **definition:** soft leather
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **components:**
  - 舛
    - chuǎn
    - footsteps
  - 囗
    - wéi
    - enclosure
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 舛 (footsteps) + 囗 (enclosure) ->
    - footsteps around an enclosure ->
    - originally for 圍 (to surround); phonetically borrowed for 'soft leather'
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 舛 (footsteps) and 囗 (enclosure) to depict the idea of footsteps moving around an enclosed area, which was originally associated with the concept of surrounding. This shape was later borrowed for its phonetic value to mean 'soft leather,' as the structural composition resembles a process of encasing or wrapping, similar to the treatment of leather.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)件夹(夾)克是用韦(韋)材料制(製)成的。
    - Zhè jiàn jiákè shì yòng wéi cáiliào zhìchéng de.
    - This jacket is made from soft leather material.
  - 古代的盔甲常以韦(韋)作装(裝)饰(飾)。
    - Gǔdài de kuījiǎ cháng yǐ wéi zuò zhuāngshì.
    - Ancient armor was often decorated with soft leather.
  - 他的书(書)包用韦(韋)边(邊)制(製)成。
    - Tā de shūbāo yòng wéi biān zhìchéng.

    - foot; walk slowly
  - 𡕒

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 夊("foot") + 𡕒("foot") =
    - two feet facing the same direction
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicted two feet facing the same direction, indicative of alignment or a specific movement. - **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**

### 围(圍) → 韋 → 舛 → 𡕒 → 夸(誇)

  - **description:**
