## dislike
### tǎoyàn
---
- **traditional:** 討厭
- **definition:** dislike
- **pinyin:** tǎoyàn
- **components:**
  - 讨(討)
    - tǎo
    - demand; discuss; ask for
  - 厌(厭)
    - yàn
    - aversion; detest; be fed up with; be tired of
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 討 (tǎo, "demand") and 厭 (yàn, "aversion") together convey developing a feeling of aversion in response to a demand or bother, leading to 'dislike' or 'annoyance'.
  - **interpretation:** 討 (tǎo, "demand") represents the idea of demands or engagements, often leading to irritation. 厭 (yàn, "aversion") captures the feeling of detachment or weariness that can result from such demands. Together, they imply a state where ongoing demands lead to annoyance or dislike, forming the idea of 'dislike'. This reflects the emotional response to feeling burdened or overwhelmed by unwanted interactions.
- **examples:**
  - 我讨(討)厌(厭)吃辣的食物。
    - Wǒ tǎoyàn chī là de shíwù.
    - I dislike eating spicy food.
  - 他总(總)是迟(遲)到，真让(讓)人讨(討)厌(厭)。
    - Tā zǒng shì chídào, zhēn ràng rén tǎoyàn.
    - He's always late, which is really annoying.
  - 这(這)个(個)天气(氣)让(讓)我很讨(討)厌(厭)。
    - Zhège tiānqì ràng wǒ hěn tǎoyàn.
    - This weather makes me very annoyed.
