## effective
### líng
---
- **traditional:** 靈
- **definition:** effective
- **pinyin:** líng
- **components:**
  - 霝
    - líng
    - clear, bright weather
  - 巫
    - wū
    - witch
  - 玉
    - yù
    - jade
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 霝 (phonetic) + 玉 (original semantic 'jade') ->
    - later 巫 ('witch') used as semantic in current form ->
    - final form 靈
  - **interpretation:** The character 靈 originally combined a phonetic element with a semantic component that suggested something precious or revered, like 玉(jade). Over time, the semantic component evolved to 巫, associating the character with spirituality or otherworldly power.
  - **simplification:** 靈 was simplified to 灵 by removing the semantic component 巫 and retaining the core phonetic element of 霝.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)药(藥)非( )常( )灵(靈)。
    - Zhège yào fēicháng líng.
    - This medicine is very effective.
  - 他( )的( )方( )法( )真( )灵(靈)！
    - Tā de fāngfǎ zhēn líng!
    - His method is truly effective!
  - 她( )的( )灵(靈)感( )总(總)是( )很( )灵(靈)。
    - Tā de línggǎn zǒngshì hěn líng.

### 灵(靈) → 霝
- **definition:** clear, bright weather
- **pinyin:** líng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts rain with combination of big and small drops
  - **interpretation:** The character 霝(líng) is a pictogram symbolizing the varied sizes of raindrops falling from the sky, capturing a natural scene of rainfall.
### 灵(靈) → 巫
- **definition:** witch
- **pinyin:** wū
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a shaman or witch with ritualistic gestures
  - **interpretation:** The character 巫(wū, 'witch') is traditionally believed to represent a person engaging in shamanic or ritualistic activities, highlighted by the dynamic gestures that suggest the role of a witch or a medium.

### 灵(靈) → 玉
- **definition:** jade
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a picture of a cong (琮), a sort of ritual jade totem ->
    - dot added to differentiate from 王 (wáng, "king") ->
    - final form 玉 (yù, "jade")
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a picture of a jade cong, emphasizing its role in rituals and its cultural significance. The image eventually included a distinguishing dot to ensure clarity from similar characters like 王, underscoring the value jade held as a material in ancient contexts.
- **examples:**
