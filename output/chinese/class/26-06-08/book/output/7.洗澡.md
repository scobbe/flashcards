## have a bath
### xǐzǎo
---
- **definition:** have a bath
- **pinyin:** xǐzǎo
- **components:**
  - 洗
    - xǐ
    - wash
  - 澡
    - zǎo
    - bath
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 洗 (xǐ, "wash") refers to the act of cleansing, typically involving water. 澡 (zǎo, "bath") indicates a soaking process to cleanse the body. The combination implies the action of having a bath or a thorough wash.
  - **interpretation:** 洗 (xǐ, "wash") involves the action of cleansing by scrubbing or rinsing. 澡 (zǎo, "bath") suggests the traditional method of immersing oneself in water for purification or relaxation. Together, 洗澡 (xǐzǎo, "have a bath") conveys the act of immersing in water and washing away dirt, thereby achieving cleanliness.
- **examples:**
  - 我每天睡觉(覺)之前都会(會)洗澡。
    - Wǒ měitiān shuìjiào zhīqián dōu huì xǐzǎo.
    - I have a bath every day before going to bed.
  - 他在运(運)动(動)后(後)马(馬)上去洗澡。
    - Tā zài yùndòng hòu mǎshàng qù xǐzǎo.
    - He goes to have a bath immediately after exercising.
  - 小王喜欢(歡)用热(熱)水洗澡。
    - Xiǎo Wáng xǐhuān yòng rèshuǐ xǐzǎo.
    - Xiao Wang likes to have a bath with hot water.
