## primary school
### xiǎoxué
---
- **traditional:** 小學
- **definition:** primary school
- **pinyin:** xiǎoxué
- **components:**
  - 小
    - xiǎo
    - small
  - 学(學)
    - xué
    - study; learn; school; knowledge
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 小 (xiǎo, "small") + 学(學) (xué, "study") =
    - a place or institution for small or young students to study, hence 'primary school'.
  - **interpretation:** 小学(學) (xiǎoxué, "primary school") refers to the initial stage of formal education, where young children first begin their structured learning. The use of 'small' signifies the early and foundational level of schooling.
- **examples:**
  - 我的小女儿(兒)今年开(開)始上小学(學)。
    - Wǒ de xiǎo nǚ'ér jīnnián kāishǐ shàng xiǎoxué.
    - My little daughter is starting primary school this year.
  - 小学(學)的课(課)程比较(較)简(簡)单(單)。
    - Xiǎoxué de kèchéng bǐjiào jiǎndān.
    - The curriculum in primary school is relatively simple.
  - 我第一次见(見)他是在小学(學)。
    - Wǒ dì yī cì jiàn tā shì zài xiǎoxué.
    - The first time I met him was in primary school.

### 小学(學) → 学(學)
- **traditional:** 學
- **definition:** study; learn; school; knowledge
- **pinyin:** xué
- **components:**
  - 爻
    - yáo
    - double cross
  - 六
    - liù
    - six
  - 𦥑
    - kuk
    - hands around
  - 子
    - zǐ
    - child
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 爻 + phonetic 六 ->
    - 𦥑 added ->
    - semantic 子 added ->
    - simplified 六 ->
    - final form
  - **interpretation:** The character 學 (xué, "study") originally combined two phonetic components, 爻 (yáo, "double cross") and 六 (liù, "six"), to represent its sound. Over time, the character evolved with the addition of 𦥑 (components positioned like hands around 爻) and the semantic 子 (zǐ, "child") to specify a child learning or studying, under a cover (冖). In its modern form, 六 is simplified to resemble 冖, which visually merges with the hands around 爻, reflecting the learning process enclosed as children gather to study.
  - **simplification:** "学" is the simplified form of "學." The transformation occurred by merging the visual elements into a compact form, where 冖 serves as a common phonetic and visual element, while the complex structure of 學 was streamlined.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天都努力学(學)习(習)。
    - Tā měitiān dōu nǔlì xuéxí.
    - He studies diligently every day.
  - 我在北京大学(學)学(學)习(習)中文。
    - Wǒ zài Běijīng Dàxué xuéxí zhōngwén.
    - I am studying Chinese at Peking University.
  - 这(這)个(個)学(學)校有很多优(優)秀的老师(師)。
    - Zhège xuéxiào yǒu hěnduō yōuxiù de lǎoshī.
    - This school has many excellent teachers.
### 小学(學) → 学(學) → 爻
- **definition:** double cross
- **pinyin:** yáo
- **components:**
  - 乂
    - yì
    - to govern
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 乂 (yì, "to govern") + 乂 =
    - two stacked 'X' marks representing divination lines
  - **interpretation:** The character 爻(yáo, "divination lines") depicts two crossing lines, resembling 'X' marks, symbolizing the divination lines used in traditional methods. These marks can be interpreted as representing either pairs of yarrow stalks used for divination or bamboo slips used for calculations. Though it might visually resemble 交 (jiāo, "cross") or 駁 (bó, "contradict"), its primary function is depicting the meaningful crossing patterns in divination.
- **examples:**
  - 易经(經)卜卦时(時)，观(觀)察爻的变(變)化。
    - Yì jīng bǔ guà shí, guānchá yáo de biànhuà.
    - When consulting the I Ching, one observes the changes of the divination lines.
  - 卦中的每一个(個)爻都蕴(蘊)含宇宙奥(奧)秘。
    - Guà zhōng de měi yīgè yáo dōu yùnhán yǔzhòu àomì.
    - Every divination line in the hexagram contains the mysteries of the universe.
  - 大师(師)解读(讀)时(時)，详(詳)细(細)说(說)明爻的象征(徵)意义(義)。
    - Dàshī jiědú shí, xiángxì shuōmíng yáo de xiàngzhēng yìyì.
    - When the master interprets, he elaborates on the symbolic meaning of the divination lines.

### 小学(學) → 学(學) → 爻 → 乂
- **definition:** to govern
- **pinyin:** yì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a tool (similar to a sickle or a pair of scissors) for mowing or weeding ->
    - used for governing ->
    - final form 乂
  - **interpretation:** The character 乂 originally depicted a tool used for cutting, and by extension, it was associated with governing due to the notion of cutting or managing weeds as a metaphor for ruling effectively. This sense of order is crucial in governance.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)些年，他致力于(於)地方的治理。
    - Zhèxiē nián, tā zhìlì yú dìfāng de zhìlǐ.
    - Over the years, he has dedicated himself to local governance.
  - 为(為)民者，先自齐(齊)以治家。
    - Wèi mín zhě, xiān zì qí yǐ zhì jiā.
    - Those who govern for the people, first manage their own households.
  - 长(長)者都希望后(後)代能好好治理天下。
    - Zhǎng zhě dōu xīwàng hòudài néng hǎohǎo zhìlǐ tiānxià.
    - The elders hope that future generations can govern the world well.

### 小学(學) → 学(學) → 六
- **definition:** six
- **pinyin:** liù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Ancient counting symbol ->
    - character for 'six'
  - **interpretation:** 六 (liù, 'six') is a pictogram that may originally have depicted a shed, but was primarily used as an ancient counting symbol. The form resembles primitive tally markings used for numerical purposes.
- **examples:**
  - 我有六个(個)苹(蘋)果。
    - Wǒ yǒu liù gè píngguǒ.
    - I have six apples.
  - 这(這)个(個)盒子可以放六瓶水。
    - Zhè gè hézi kěyǐ fàng liù píng shuǐ.
    - This box can hold six bottles of water.
  - 六只(隻)猫(貓)在花园(園)里(裡)玩耍。
    - Liù zhī māo zài huāyuán lǐ wánshuǎ.
    - Six cats are playing in the garden.

### 小学(學) → 学(學) → 子
- **definition:** child
- **pinyin:** zǐ
- **etymology:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 子(zǐ, "child") is a pictorial representation of a baby, emphasizing characteristics like a large head and outstretched arms typical of infants, which conveys the essence of a child. This visual element helps infer its meaning.
- **examples:**
