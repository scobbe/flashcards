## appear
### chūxiàn
---
- **traditional:** 出現
- **definition:** appear
- **pinyin:** chūxiàn
- **components:**
  - 出
    - chū
    - go out; exit
  - 现(現)
    - xiàn
    - appear; present
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 出 (chū, "go out; exit") and 現 (xiàn, "appear; present") together describe the act of coming into view or becoming visible.
  - **interpretation:** This word combines 出 (chū, "go out") with 現 (xiàn, "appear"), suggesting the action of emerging or manifesting into view, akin to 'coming out and becoming present'.
- **examples:**
  - 天( )空( )中( )出( )现(現)了( )彩( )虹( )。
    - Tiānkōng zhōng chūxiàn le cǎihóng.

  - 问(問)题(題)又( )出( )现(現)了( )，需( )要( )立( )即( )解( )决(決)。
    - Wèntí yòu chūxiàn le, xūyào lìjí jiějué.

  - 每( )当(當)他( )出( )现(現)时(時)，大( )家( )都( )会(會)很( )高( )兴(興)。
    - Měidāng tā chūxiàn shí, dàjiā dōu huì hěn gāoxìng.

### 出( )现(現) → 出 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').
