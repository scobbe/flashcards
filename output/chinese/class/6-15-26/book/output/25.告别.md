## say goodbye to; part from
### gàobié
---
- **traditional:** 告別
- **definition:** say goodbye to; part from
- **pinyin:** gàobié
- **components:**
  - 告
    - gào
    - inform; announce; tell
  - 别(別)
    - bié
    - leave; part; farewell; separate
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 告 (gào, "inform") + 别(別) (bié, "leave; part") =
    - say goodbye; farewell
  - **interpretation:** The word 告( )别(別) (gàobié, "say goodbye; farewell") consists of 告 (gào, "inform") and 别(別) (bié, "leave; part"). When you 'inform' someone about a 'parting,' you are essentially saying goodbye. This concept is relevant in both personal departures and formal farewells.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )们(們)告( )别(別)。
    - Wǒmen gàobié.
    - We say farewell.
  - 他( )在( )码(碼)头(頭)告( )别(別)，依( )依( )不( )舍( )地( )离(離)去( )。
    - Tā zài mǎtóu gàobié, yīyī bùshě dì líqù.
    - He said farewell at the dock and left reluctantly.
  - 朋( )友( )聚( )会(會)后(後)告( )别( )，彼此期待重逢。
    - Péngyǒu jùhuì hòu gàobié, bǐcǐ qídài chóngféng.
    - After the gathering, friends said farewell, each looking forward to reuniting.

### 告( )别(別) → 别(別)
- **traditional:** 別
- **definition:** leave; part; farewell; separate
- **pinyin:** bié
- **components:**
  - 冎
    - guǎ
    - bone
  - 刂
    - dāo
    - knife
  - 另
    - lìng
    - other
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 冎 (bone) + 刂 (knife) =
    - to cut up bone and flesh ->
    - stylized as 別
  - **interpretation:** The character 別 (bié, "separate") originally depicted the action of cutting apart bones, highlighting the idea of separation. It combines the components 冎 (guǎ, "bone") and 刂 (dāo, "knife") to convey this meaning.
  - **simplification:** The simplified form 别 is a variation of 別 where the component 冎 (guǎ, "bone") is replaced by the simpler 另 (lìng, "other"), maintaining the concept of separation.
- **examples:**
