## just; no more than
### gānggāng
---
- **traditional:** 剛剛
- **definition:** just; no more than
- **pinyin:** gānggāng
- **components:**
  - 刚(剛)
    - gāng
    - just; firm
  - 刚(剛)
    - gāng
    - just; firm
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 刚(剛) (gāng, "just; firm") + 刚(剛) (gāng, "just; firm") reinforce the sense of immediacy or recent occurrence.
  - **interpretation:** The repetition of 刚(剛) (gāng, "just; firm") emphasizes the idea of something having happened very recently or just moments before. This concept is reflected in its common use to indicate 'just now' or 'just recently' in time expressions. The doubling intensifies the immediacy.
- **examples:**
  - 他刚(剛)刚(剛)走。
    - Tā gānggāng zǒu.
    - He just left.
  - 我刚(剛)刚(剛)到了，看到朋友站在门(門)外。
    - Wǒ gānggāng dào le, kàn dào péngyǒu zhàn zài ménwài.
    - I just arrived and saw a friend standing outside the door.
### 刚(剛)刚(剛) → 刚(剛)
- **traditional:** 剛
- **definition:** just; firm
- **pinyin:** gāng
- **components:**
  - 岡
    - gāng
    - mountain ridge
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 岡 (gāng) + semantic 刀 (knife) ->
    - hard, firm
  - **interpretation:** 岡 (gāng, 'mountain ridge') provides the sound, suggesting a firm or solid structure, with 刀 (dāo, 'knife') indicating sharpness or firmness. Thus, 刚(剛) (gāng, 'firm') connotes hardness or strength, while the definite, exact nature of a firm edge lends to meanings of precise timing ('just now') and minimal sufficiency ('barely').
  - **simplification:** 剛 (gāng, 'firm') was simplified to 刚(剛) by replacing the phonetic 岡 (gāng, 'mountain ridge') with 冈, retaining the knife radical 刀.
- **examples:**
  - 我刚(剛)吃完晚饭(飯)。
    - Wǒ gāng chī wán wǎnfàn.
    - I just finished dinner.
  - 他刚(剛)走进(進)教室，铃(鈴)声(聲)就响(響)了。
    - Tā gāng zǒu jìn jiàoshì, língshēng jiù xiǎng le.
    - He just entered the classroom when the bell rang.
  - 这(這)个(個)盒子很坚(堅)硬，像石头(頭)一样(樣)。
    - Zhège hézi hěn jiānyìng, xiàng shítou yīyàng.
    - This box is very hard, like a stone.
### 刚(剛)刚(剛) → 刚(剛) → 岡
- **definition:** mountain ridge
- **pinyin:** gāng
- **components:**
  - 网(網)
    - mǎng
    - net
  - 山
    - shān
    - mountain
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 网 (mǎng) + semantic 山 (shān, "mountain") ->
    - ridge
  - **interpretation:** The character combines the semantic 山 (shān, "mountain") suggesting a topographical feature, with the phonetic 网 (mǎng) to provide the sound. A ridge is a prominent mountain feature, making this combination intuitive for indicating 'ridge'.
- **examples:**
  - 那座高冈(岡)，是徒步旅行的好地方。
    - Nà zuò gāo gāng, shì túbù lǚxíng de hǎo dìfāng.
    - That high ridge is a great place for hiking.
  - 太阳(陽)从(從)山冈(岡)升起，洒(灑)下金色的光芒。
    - Tàiyáng cóng shān gāng shēng qǐ, sǎ xià jīnsè de guāngmáng.
    - The sun rises from the ridge, casting golden rays.
  - 在冈(岡)顶(頂)上，你可以看到整个(個)村庄(莊)。
    - Zài gāng dǐng shàng, nǐ kěyǐ kàn dào zhěnggè cūnzhuāng.
    - On the top of the ridge, you can see the entire village.

- **etymology:**

  - **description:**
    - Depicts a net with crossing lines or mesh ->
    - symbol for 'net'
  - **interpretation:** The character 网(網) (wǎng, 'net') visually represents a net with lines intersecting to form a grid, making it intuitive as a symbol for a net or mesh.
  - **simplification:** The simplified character 网 (wǎng) retains the essential visual structure of intersecting lines to depict a net. It conveys the idea of a net through a simplified representation without additional semantic or phonetic elements.

  - **description:** Depicts three mountain peaks
