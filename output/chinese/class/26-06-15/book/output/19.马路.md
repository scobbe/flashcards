## road; street
### mǎlù
---
- **traditional:** 馬路
- **definition:** road; street
- **pinyin:** mǎlù
- **components:**
  - 马(馬)
    - mǎ
    - horse
  - 路
    - lù
    - road; path; journey
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 馬 (mǎ, "horse") refers to horses, which were the primary mode of transport in historical times, and 路 (lù, "road") signifies a path or street. This combination conveys the concept of a 'road' or 'street' suitable for vehicles, originally horse-drawn.
  - **interpretation:** The word 馬路 (mǎlù) draws on the historical imagery of roads used for horse-drawn vehicles. Horses symbolize travel and movement, making them a natural fit for 路 (lù, "road"), which defines the structured path meant for them. Thus, 馬路 (mǎlù) captures the essence of a thoroughfare built for transit.
- **examples:**
  - 马(馬)路上有很多汽车(車)。
    - Mǎlù shàng yǒu hěn duō qìchē.
    - There are many cars on the road.
  - 儿(兒)童不应(應)该(該)在马(馬)路上玩耍。
    - Értóng bù yīnggāi zài mǎlù shàng wánshuǎ.
    - Children should not play on the street.
  - 这(這)条(條)马(馬)路通向城中心。
    - Zhè tiáo mǎlù tōng xiàng chéng zhōngxīn.
    - This road leads to the city center.
### 马(馬)路 → 马(馬)
- **traditional:** 馬
- **definition:** horse
- **pinyin:** mǎ
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 火
    - huǒ
    - fire
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a horse with head facing left and flowing mane ->
    - head often simplified into an eye 目 (mù, "eye") ->
    - legs evolved into four dots 灬 unrelated to 火 (huǒ, "fire")
  - **interpretation:** The character represents a horse with its mane flowing sideways, and the simplification over time captured the essence of its most visible features.
  - **simplification:** 馬 was simplified to 马(馬) by fusing the head and body, using the simplified line forms and dots to represent its features more compactly.
- **examples:**
  - 小马(馬)在草地上奔跑。
    - Xiǎo mǎ zài cǎodì shàng bēnpǎo.
    - The little horse runs on the grassland.
  - 我骑(騎)马(馬)看风(風)景，感觉(覺)非常自在。
    - Wǒ qí mǎ kàn fēngjǐng, gǎnjué fēicháng zìzài.
    - I ride a horse to enjoy the scenery, feeling very at ease.
  - 马(馬)是人类(類)历(歷)史上的好伙(夥)伴。
    - Mǎ shì rénlèi lìshǐ shàng de hǎo huǒbàn.
    - Horses are good companions in human history.

### 马(馬)路 → 路
- **definition:** road; path; journey
- **pinyin:** lù
- **components:**
  - 足
    - zú
    - foot
  - 各
    - gè
    - each
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 足 (zú, 'foot') + phonetic 各 (gè) ->
    - road
  - **interpretation:** The semantic component 足 (zú, 'foot') represents walking or movement, suggesting the idea of a path or road. 各 (gè) serves as the phonetic component, contributing to the pronunciation of the character.
  - **simplification:** The character retains its form in simplified Chinese, with no changes.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)里(裡)的路很宽(寬)敞。
    - Zhèlǐ de lù hěn kuānchǎng.
    - The road here is very wide.
  - 他选(選)择(擇)了一条(條)不同的路。
    - Tā xuǎnzéle yī tiáo bùtóng de lù.
    - He chose a different path.
  - 走这(這)条(條)路会(會)更快。
    - Zǒu zhè tiáo lù huì gèng kuài.
    - Taking this road will be faster.
