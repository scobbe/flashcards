## bygones; past events
### wǎngshì
---
- **definition:** bygones; past events
- **pinyin:** wǎngshì
- **components:**
  - 往
    - wǎng
    - past; toward
  - 事
    - shì
    - affair; event; matter
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 往 (wǎng, "past; toward") refers to the direction of going away, or the time that has passed, emphasizing what has been and is no more. 事 (shì, "affair; event; matter") denotes occurrences or activities. Thus, 往事 (wǎngshì, "bygones; past events") combines these elements to refer to events or matters of the past.
  - **interpretation:** The word 往事 (wǎngshì, "bygones; past events") captures the sense of looking back at the 'affairs or events' (事) that belong to the 'past' (往). Anything that once occurred is considered a 'past event,' emphasizing the separation between then and now.
- **examples:**
  - 他常常回忆(憶)往事。
    - Tā chángcháng huíyì wǎngshì.
    - He often reminisces about past events.
  - 记(記)住往事，才能更好地走向未来(來)。
    - Jìzhu wǎngshì, cáinéng gèng hǎo de zǒuxiàng wèilái.
    - Remembering the past helps to better move towards the future.
  - 往事已成过(過)眼云(雲)烟(煙)。
    - Wǎngshì yǐ chéng guòyǎn yúnyān.
    - Past events have already become fleeting clouds.

- **pinyin:** wǎng

  - 王
    - wáng

  - 主
    - zhǔ

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Originally 止 (zhǐ, "foot") + 王 (wáng, "king") =
    - phonetic for pronunciation ->
    - 彳 (chì, "step") added for movement ->

  - **interpretation:** The character 往(wǎng) incorporates semantic and phonetic elements: 彳(chì, "step") suggests movement and 止(zhǐ, "foot") indicates direction, while 王(wáng, "king") originally provided the pronunciation, which was later obscured as the character evolved.
- **examples:**
  - 他往门(門)口走。
    - Tā wǎng ménkǒu zǒu.
    - He walked towards the door.
  - 我们(們)往东(東)边(邊)旅行。

### 往事 → 往 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').

### 往事 → 往 → 王
- **definition:** king
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a ritual axe representing the king's authority
  - **interpretation:** This character represents the form of a ceremonial axe, which was the symbol of a king's power in ancient China. The structure emphasizes the sacred and authoritative role of the king, separate from the nuanced folk interpretations linking Heaven, Man, and Earth.
- **examples:**
