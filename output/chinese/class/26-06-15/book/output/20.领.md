## lead; usher; take
### lǐng
---
- **traditional:** 領
- **definition:** lead; usher; take
- **pinyin:** lǐng
- **components:**
  - 令
    - lìng
    - order
  - 頁
    - yè
    - head
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 頁 (yè, "head") + phonetic 令 (lìng, "order") ->
    - lead; usher; take
  - **interpretation:** Semantic 頁 (yè, "head") suggests direction or leadership, as the head leads the body. Phonetic 令 (lìng, "order") provides the sound, enhancing the concept of directing or leading, where orders are followed. The combination reflects the idea of guiding or taking the lead, akin to a head giving direction to an order.
  - **simplification:** 領 was simplified by reducing 頁 to 页(頁), decreasing stroke count without altering phonetic 令.
- **examples:**
  - 他带(帶)领(領)我们(們)去参(參)观(觀)博物馆(館)。
    - Tā dàilǐng wǒmen qù cānguān bówùguǎn.
    - He led us to visit the museum.
  - 她在课(課)堂上总(總)是能领(領)会(會)到老师(師)的意思。
    - Tā zài kètáng shàng zǒng shì néng lǐnghuì dào lǎoshī de yìsi.
    - She always grasps the teacher's meaning in class.
  - 经(經)过(過)他的领(領)导(導)，团(團)队(隊)取得了巨大进(進)步。
    - Jīngguò tā de lǐngdǎo, tuánduì qǔdéle jùdà jìnbù.
    - Under his leadership, the team made significant progress.
### 领(領) → 令
- **definition:** order
- **pinyin:** lìng
- **components:**
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 卩
    - jié
    - seal
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 人 (rén, "person") + 卩 (jié, "seal") =
    - person with seal ->
    - issuing orders or commands
  - **interpretation:** The character 令 (lǐng, "order") combines 人 (rén, "person") and 卩 (jié, "seal") to depict someone with a seal or authority, signifying the power to issue orders or commands.
- **examples:**
  - 将(將)军(軍)令部队(隊)保持警戒，士兵严(嚴)格服从(從)。
    - jiāngjūn lǐng bùduì bǎochí jǐngjiè, shìbīng yángé fúcóng.
    - The general orders the troops to maintain vigilance, and the soldiers strictly obey.
  - 老师(師)令学(學)生认(認)真听(聽)讲(講)。
    - lǎoshī lǐng xuéshēng rènzhēn tīngjiǎng.
    - The teacher orders the students to listen attentively.
  - 国(國)王令大臣速速筹(籌)备(備)，迎接远(遠)方使节(節)。
    - guówáng lǐng dàchén sùsù chóubèi, yíngjiē yuǎnfāng shǐjié.
    - The king orders his ministers to quickly make preparations to welcome the foreign envoy.

### 领(領) → 令 → 人
- **definition:** person
- **pinyin:** rén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts side view of a standing man, highlighting an arm and a leg ->
    - visual representation of a person
  - **interpretation:** The character 人 (rén, 'person') effectively uses simple lines to depict the essential form of a human, making it immediately recognizable and intuitive as meaning 'person.'
- **examples:**
  - 他是个(個)好人。
    - Tā shì gè hǎo rén.
    - He is a good person.
  - 每个(個)人都有责(責)任，为(為)社会(會)作出贡(貢)献(獻)。
    - Měi gè rén dōu yǒu zérèn, wèi shèhuì zuòchū gòngxiàn.
    - Every person has a responsibility to contribute to society.
  - 人在旅途中，心灵(靈)获(獲)得了成长(長)。
    - Rén zài lǚtú zhōng, xīnlíng huòdé le chéngzhǎng.
    - People gain growth along their journey.

### 领(領) → 令 → 卩
- **definition:** seal
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.
- **examples:**
  - 那位卩正虔诚(誠)祈祷(禱)。
    - Nà wèi jié zhèng qiánchéng qídǎo.
    - That kneeling person is praying devoutly.
  - 礼(禮)仪(儀)师(師)指出，卩代表谦(謙)卑与(與)敬意。
    - Lǐyí shī zhǐchū, jié dàibiǎo qiānbēi yǔ jìngyì.
    - The ritual master pointed out that the kneeling person represents humility and respect.
  - 在庄(莊)严(嚴)仪(儀)式中，卩象征(徵)着(著)内(內)心的虔敬。
    - Zài zhuāngyán yíshì zhōng, jié xiàngzhēngzhe nèixīn de qiánjìng.
    - In the solemn ceremony, the kneeling figure symbolizes inner devotion.

### 领(領) → 頁
- **definition:** head
- **pinyin:** yè
- **components:**
  - 首
    - shǒu
    - head; first
  - 卩
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 首 (head/face) + 卩 (kneeling person) ->
    - depicts a person's head and body
  - **interpretation:** Originally represented a head and its body in a kneeling posture, emphasizing “head”. The depiction was extended semantically to represent a 'page', as pages bear leading information like a head does for a body. The concept of 'head' is also reflected in its modern usage as a radical in characters related to parts of the head or face.
  - **simplification:** 页 is a cursive shorthand of the traditional 頁, simplifying the structure while maintaining the core shape of a head.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)打开(開)书(書)的第十页(頁)。
    - Qǐng dǎkāi shū de dì shí yè.
    - Please open to page ten of the book.
  - 这(這)本书(書)每一页(頁)都有插图(圖)。
    - Zhè běn shū měi yī yè dōu yǒu chātú.
    - Every page of this book has illustrations.
  - 我昨天晚上读(讀)了三十页(頁)。
    - Wǒ zuótiān wǎnshang dúle sānshí yè.
    - I read thirty pages last night.

### 领(領) → 頁 → 首
- **definition:** head; first
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts the head of an animal with a long mouth and horns, often shown with or without hair ( 巛) ->
    - perpetuated as 𩠐 (form with hair) ->
    - streamlined to 首
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts the head of an animal, with prominent features like a long mouth and horns, emphasizing the notion of a 'head' both physically and symbolically as the foremost part.
- **examples:**
  - 他是这(這)个(個)团(團)队(隊)的首领(領)。
    - Tā shì zhège tuánduì de shǒulǐng.
    - He is the head of this team.
  - 这(這)首歌是我最喜欢(歡)的歌曲。
    - Zhè shǒu gē shì wǒ zuì xǐhuān de gēqǔ.
    - This song is my favorite track.
  - 他抬起头(頭)，看向远(遠)方的山。
    - Tā tái qǐ tóu, kàn xiàng yuǎnfāng de shān.
    - He raised his head and looked towards the distant mountains.
