## take care of; look after
### zhàogù
---
- **traditional:** 照顧
- **definition:** take care of; look after
- **pinyin:** zhàogù
- **components:**
  - 照
    - zhào
    - shine; illuminate; reflect
  - 顾(顧)
    - gù
    - look after; attend to; care for
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 照 (zhào, "shine; illuminate; reflect") refers to the act of shining or projecting light, suggesting awareness and consideration. 顾(顧) (gù, "look after; attend to; care for") means to look in a certain direction or turn the head to regard something, implying attention and concern.
  - **interpretation:** 照 (zhào, "shine; illuminate") involves the projection of light, which can be linked to the notion of providing attention and insight, as if 'shining a light' on someone; this connects to the idea of looking after. 顾(顧) (gù, "look after; attend to") literally means turning the head to look, indicating focus and care. Together, 照顾(顧) (zhàogù, "take care of; look after") conveys the concept of paying careful attention to someone or something, as if continually illuminating and tending to it with care.
- **examples:**
  - 妈(媽)妈(媽)每天都照顾(顧)我。
    - Māma měitiān dōu zhàogù wǒ.
    - Mom takes care of me every day.
  - 请(請)多多照顾(顧)新来(來)的同事。
    - Qǐng duōduō zhàogù xīn lái de tóngshì.
    - Please take good care of the new colleague.
  - 他生病了，需要有人照顾(顧)他。
    - Tā shēngbìng le, xūyào yǒurén zhàogù tā.
    - He is sick and needs someone to take care of him.

### 照顾(顧) → 顾(顧) → 頁 → 卩
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female.
