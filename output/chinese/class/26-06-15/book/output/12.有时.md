## sometimes
### yǒushí
---
- **traditional:** 有時
- **definition:** sometimes
- **pinyin:** yǒushí
- **components:**
  - 有
    - yǒu
    - have; there is
  - 时(時)
    - shí
    - time; o'clock; when; hour
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 有 (yǒu, "have; there is") and 时(時) (shí, "time") combine to describe an occurrence that happens occasionally by having certain times or instances.
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 有 (yǒu, "have; there is") and 时(時) (shí, "time") conceptualizes the idea of having certain times, which translates to the occasional or non-regular occurrence of something.
- **examples:**
  - 有( )时(時)天气很热。
    - Yǒushí tiānqì hěn rè.
    - Sometimes the weather is very hot.
  - 他( )有( )时(時)会(會)在( )早( )上( )跑( )步( )。

  - 我( )有( )时(時)在( )周( )末( )去( )看( )电(電)影( )。
    - Wǒ yǒushí zài zhōumò qù kàn diànyǐng.

### 有( )时(時) → 时(時)

- **definition:** time; o'clock; when; hour
- **pinyin:** shí
- **components:**

  - 之
    - zhī

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 日 (rì, 'sun; day') as semantic + 寺 as phonetic ->

The historical forms show an evolution from 旹, merging 日 and 之 (zhī, 'governing') to imply governance over time, to its modern form, where 寸 ('inch') likely emphasizes precision or measurement in the concept of time.
  - **simplification:** In 时 (shí), the component 寺 was retained for phonetic purposes, while 日 (rì, 'sun; day') was simplified to resemble 日 with fewer strokes, streamlining the character.
