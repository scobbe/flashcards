## enter; get into
### jìnrù
---
- **traditional:** 進入
- **definition:** enter; get into
- **pinyin:** jìnrù
- **components:**
  - 进(進)
    - jìn
    - advance; move forward
  - 入
    - rù
    - enter; join; get into; go in
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 進 (jìn, "advance") conveys moving forward or ahead, suggesting motion or progression into something. 入 (rù, "enter") directly means to step into or become part of a space or realm, conveying entry.
  - **interpretation:** 進 (jìn, "advance") implies moving forward towards a destination. 入 (rù, "enter") suggests completing the motion of crossing a boundary into a new space. Combine these to express the idea of 'entering' as moving forward into a space or state.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)进(進)入公园(園)。
    - Wǒmen jìnrù gōngyuán.
    - We entered the park.
  - 请(請)在进(進)入房间(間)前脱(脫)鞋。
    - Qǐng zài jìnrù fángjiān qián tuōxié.
    - Please remove your shoes before entering the room.
  - 他进(進)入大学(學)后(後)学(學)习(習)很勤奋(奮)。
    - Tā jìnrù dàxué hòu xuéxí hěn qínfèn.
    - He studied diligently after entering the university.
### 进(進)入 → 进(進)
- **traditional:** 進
- **definition:** advance; move forward
- **pinyin:** jìn
- **components:**
  - 辵
    - chuò
    - walk; movement
  - 隹
    - zhuī
    - short-tail bird
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 辵 ('walk/movement') + 隹 (zhuī, 'short-tail bird') =
    - a bird that can only move forward
  - **interpretation:** The character 進 (jìn, 'to advance') combines the idea of movement with the imagery of a bird, symbolizing forward movement, as the bird naturally progresses forward on foot.
  - **simplification:** 進 was simplified to 进(進) by replacing the phonetic component 隹 (zhuī, 'short-tail bird') with 井 (jǐng, 'well') to reduce strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 他决(決)定继(繼)续(續)努力，以便在职(職)业(業)生涯中进(進)步。
    - Tā juédìng jìxù nǔlì, yǐbiàn zài zhíyè shēngyá zhōng jìnbù.
    - He decided to keep working hard in order to advance in his career.
  - 在我们(們)进(進)入隧道时(時)，速度变(變)得越来(來)越快。
    - Zài wǒmen jìnrù suìdào shí, sùdù biàn de yuè lái yuè kuài.
    - As we entered the tunnel, the speed became faster and faster.
  - 大门(門)刚(剛)一打开(開)，他就立刻走了进(進)去。
    - Dàmén gāng yī dǎkāi, tā jiù lìkè zǒu le jìnqù.
    - As soon as the door opened, he immediately walked in.
### 进(進)入 → 进(進) → 辵
- **definition:** walk; movement
- **pinyin:** chuò
- **components:**
  - 彳
    - chì
    - walk
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - foot
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 彳 (chì, "walk") + 止 (zhǐ, "foot" or "to stop") ->
    - 𣥆 ->
    - stylized as 龰 ->
    - 辵
  - **interpretation:** The character 辵 combines 彳 (chì, "walk"), which represents movement, with 止 (zhǐ, "foot" or "to stop"), suggesting the concept of stepping or traveling forward. This character represents the idea of movement, particularly the action of walking or traveling, as originally depicted by the more conservative form 𣥆. Over time, the bottom component has been stylized as 龰, forming the current representation.
- **examples:**
  - 他辵在乡(鄉)间(間)的小路上。
    - Tā chuò zài xiāngjiān de xiǎolù shàng.
    - He walks on the rural path.

### 进(進)入 → 进(進) → 辵 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').
