## hold (hands)
### lā (shǒu)
---
- **definition:** hold (hands)
- **pinyin:** lā (shǒu)
- **components:**
  - 拉
    - lā
    - pull; drag; haul
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 拉手 (lāshǒu, "hold hands") consists of 拉 (lā, "pull") + 手 (shǒu, "hand").
  - **interpretation:** 拉 (lā, "pull") involves motion toward oneself, evoking closeness or connection when applied to 手 (shǒu, "hand"). Therefore, 拉手 (lāshǒu) literally describes the action of pulling hands together, thus intuitively depicting the act of holding hands.
- **examples:**
  - 在公园(園)里(裏)，我们(們)常常拉手。
    - Zài gōngyuán lǐ, wǒmen chángcháng lāshǒu.
    - In the park, we often hold hands.
  - 小孩拉着(著)手，一起过(過)马(馬)路。
    - Xiǎohái lāzhe shǒu, yīqǐ guò mǎlù.
    - The children hold hands while crossing the road.
  - 他们(們)见(見)面时(時)，总(總)是会(會)拉手。
    - Tāmen jiànmiàn shí, zǒng shì huì lāshǒu.
    - When they meet, they always hold hands.
### 拉手 → 拉
- **definition:** pull; drag; haul
- **pinyin:** lā
- **components:**
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
  - 立
    - lì
    - stand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 手 (shǒu, "hand") + phonetic 立 (lì) ->
    - pull
  - **interpretation:** The character 拉 (lā, "pull; drag; draw out") combines 手 (shǒu, "hand") as the semantic element, suggesting an action performed with the hand, with 立 (lì) as the phonetic component, providing the 'lā' sound. The intuitive image is a hand performing the action of pulling or dragging.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)帮(幫)我把椅子拉过(過)来(來)。
    - Qǐng bāng wǒ bǎ yǐzi lā guòlái.
    - Please help me pull the chair over.
  - 他喜欢(歡)在地上拉着(著)玩具车(車)。
    - Tā xǐhuān zài dìshàng lā zhe wánjùchē.
    - He likes to drag toy cars on the floor.
  - 我们(們)要用力把船拉到岸边(邊)。
    - Wǒmen yào yònglì bǎ chuán lā dào ànbiān.
    - We need to pull the boat to the shore with force.

### 拉手 → 拉 → 手
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a hand with fingers; top stroke is a bent over middle finger, horizontal strokes represent fingers
  - **interpretation:** The character 手 (shǒu, "hand") is a direct pictorial representation of a human hand with all five fingers, which distinguishes it from similar characters representing claws or simplified fingers. This visually conveys its meaning as a hand clearly.
- **examples:**
  - 他用手写(寫)字。
    - Tā yòng shǒu xiě zì.
    - He writes with his hand.
  - 妈(媽)妈(媽)牵(牽)着(著)我的手走路。
    - Māmā qiān zhe wǒ de shǒu zǒu lù.
    - Mom holds my hand while walking.
  - 他的手很温(溫)暖，带(帶)给(給)我力量。
    - Tā de shǒu hěn wēn nuǎn, dài gěi wǒ lìliàng.
    - His hand is very warm, giving me strength.

### 拉手 → 拉 → 立
- **definition:** stand
- **pinyin:** lì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a person standing on the ground
  - **interpretation:** The character 立 (lì, 'stand') intuitively represents the concept of standing by depicting a person in an upright position, emphasizing the posture.
- **examples:**
  - 小王立在窗前，等待朋友到来(來)。
    - Xiǎo Wáng lì zài chuāng qián, děngdài péngyǒu dàolái.
    - Xiao Wang stands at the window, waiting for his friend to arrive.
  - 园(園)中雕像静(靜)立于(於)石台(臺)上。
    - Yuán zhōng diāoxiàng jìng lì yú shí tái shàng.
    - The statue in the garden stands quietly on the stone platform.
  - 士兵集合后(後)，整齐(齊)地立定阵(陣)型。
    - Shìbīng jíhé hòu, zhěngqí de lì dìng zhènxíng.
    - After the soldiers assemble, they stand in formation neatly.
