## all one's life; a lifetime
### yìshēng
---
- **definition:** all one's life; a lifetime
- **pinyin:** yìshēng
- **components:**
  - 一
    - yī
    - one; single; whole
  - 生
    - shēng
    - life; birth; alive; grow
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 一 (yī, "one") indicates a singularity or unity, while 生 (shēng, "life") signifies the concept of living or existence. Combined, they refer to everything from birth to death, encapsulating a complete span of life.
  - **interpretation:** The word 一生 (yìshēng) portrays the idea of a single, complete life journey. 一 (yī, "one") emphasizes the entirety or singularity of one's existence, while 生 (shēng, "life") denotes the period of living from birth to death. Together, they convey the notion of an individual's entire life span.
