## age (of a person, animal or plant, etc.)
### niánlíng
---
- **traditional:** 年齡
- **definition:** age (of a person, animal or plant, etc.)
- **pinyin:** niánlíng
- **components:**
  - 年
    - nián
    - year
  - 龄(齡)
    - líng
    - age
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 年 (nián, "year") refers to the passage of time in terms of years, marking the cycle of seasons. 齡 (líng, "age") denotes the number of years a person, animal, or plant has lived, derived from the idea of teeth, which metaphorically reflects stages in life as teeth emerge in cycles across different age milestones.
  - **interpretation:** 年 (nián, "year") represents the passage of time as measured in increments of years, essential for counting the duration of life. 齡 (líng, "age") uses the metaphor of teeth, which grow and change in well-defined stages, to denote life stages; thus, 'age' is the count of annual cycles a being has experienced. Combining these gives 年齡 (niánlíng, "age") as the measure of how many year-cycles a person, animal, or plant has traversed.
