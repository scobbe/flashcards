## age (of a person, animal or plant, etc.)
### niánlíng
---
- **traditional:** 年齡
- **definition:** age (of a person, animal or plant, etc.)
- **pinyin:** niánlíng
- **components:**
  - 年
    - nián
    - year
  - 龄(齡)
    - líng
    - age
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 年 (nián, "year") refers to the passage of time in terms of years, marking the cycle of seasons. 齡 (líng, "age") denotes the number of years a person, animal, or plant has lived, derived from the idea of teeth, which metaphorically reflects stages in life as teeth emerge in cycles across different age milestones.
  - **interpretation:** 年 (nián, "year") represents the passage of time as measured in increments of years, essential for counting the duration of life. 齡 (líng, "age") uses the metaphor of teeth, which grow and change in well-defined stages, to denote life stages; thus, 'age' is the count of annual cycles a being has experienced. Combining these gives 年齡 (niánlíng, "age") as the measure of how many year-cycles a person, animal, or plant has traversed.
- **examples:**
  - 他的年龄(齡)比我大。
    - Tā de niánlíng bǐ wǒ dà.
    - He is older than me.
  - 在这(這)个(個)年龄(齡)，开(開)始学(學)习(習)新东(東)西是很重要的。
    - Zài zhège niánlíng, kāishǐ xuéxí xīn dōngxī shì hěn zhòngyào de.
    - At this age, it is important to start learning new things.
  - 树(樹)的年龄(齡)可以通过(過)年轮(輪)来(來)判断(斷)。
    - Shù de niánlíng kěyǐ tōngguò niánlún lái pànduàn.
    - The age of a tree can be determined by its rings.
### 年龄(齡) → 年
- **definition:** year
- **pinyin:** nián
- **components:**
  - 禾
    - hé
    - grain
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 禾(“wheat; grain”) + 人(“person”) as phonetic ->
    - originally “harvest” ->
    - later “year”
  - **interpretation:** The character 年 (nián, "year") originally depicted a harvest of grains, linking the concept of agricultural cycles with the passing of a year. Over time, the form evolved, retaining the semantic component 禾 (hé, "grain") and using 人 (rén, "person") for its phonetic sound in early stages, which later transformed into the current form through script evolution.
- **examples:**
  - 今年是兔年。
    - Jīnnián shì tùnián.
    - This year is the Year of the Rabbit.
  - 他今年二十岁(歲)。
    - Tā jīnnián èrshí suì.
    - He is twenty years old this year.
  - 五年后(後)我希望能去旅行。
    - Wǔ nián hòu wǒ xīwàng néng qù lǚxíng.
    - I hope to travel in five years.

### 年龄(齡) → 年 → 禾
- **definition:** grain
- **pinyin:** hé
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a stalk of grain or cereal plant
  - **interpretation:** The character 禾 (hé, 'grain') visually represents a plant with roots and leaves, focusing on the appearance of staple crops like rice or millet. This clearly conveys its meaning as it resembles the actual shape of a plant grown for its grain.
- **examples:**
  - 收获(穫)的禾香弥(彌)漫在田野中。

    - Chūnyǔ zīrùn le hé miáo, yùshì zhe fēngshōu.
    - The spring rain nourished the grain seedlings, foreshadowing a bountiful harvest.

### 年龄(齡) → 龄(齡)
- **traditional:** 齡
- **definition:** age
- **pinyin:** líng
- **components:**
  - 齒
    - chǐ
    - tooth
  - 令
    - lìng
    - order
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 齒 (chǐ, "tooth") + phonetic 令 (lìng) ->
    - meaning related to counting years
  - **interpretation:** The character 齡 (líng, "age") is formed by combining 齒 (chǐ, "tooth"), which represents 'age' through the symbolism of teeth as a marker of maturity, with the phonetic 令 (lìng), which contributes the sound. Teeth are often associated with stages of life, lending themselves naturally to the concept of age or duration.
  - **simplification:** Simplified to 龄 by changing 齒 (chǐ, "tooth") to its simplified form 齿.
- **examples:**
  - 她的年龄(齡)不大。
    - Tā de niánlíng bù dà.
    - She is not very old.
  - 他的实(實)际(際)年龄(齡)比看起来(來)要大。
    - Tā de shíjì niánlíng bǐ kàn qǐlái yào dà.
    - His actual age is older than he looks.
  - 这(這)份工作的最低年龄(齡)要求是十八岁(歲)。
    - Zhè fèn gōngzuò de zuìdī niánlíng yāoqiú shì shíbā suì.
    - The minimum age requirement for this job is eighteen years old.
### 年龄(齡) → 龄(齡) → 齒
- **definition:** tooth
- **pinyin:** chǐ
- **components:**
  - 止
    - zhǐ
    - stop
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Originally a pictogram depicting a mouth full of teeth ->
    - phonetic 止 (zhǐ, "stop") added to aid pronunciation
  - **interpretation:** The character started as a visual representation of teeth and later incorporated 止 as a phonetic component to reinforce its pronunciation while maintaining the original pictographic representation.
- **examples:**
  - 他每天早上刷齒，晚上睡覺前再刷齒。
    - Tā měitiān zǎoshang shuā chǐ, wǎnshàng shuìjiào qián zài shuā chǐ.
    - He brushes his teeth every morning, and brushes them again before bed.
  - 医(醫)生检(檢)查齒。
    - Yīshēng jiǎnchá chǐ.
    - The doctor examined the tooth.
  - 小猫(貓)露出洁(潔)白的齒，显(顯)得十分可爱(愛)。
    - Xiǎo māo lùchū jiébái de chǐ, xiǎn de shífēn kě'ài.
    - The little cat shows its white tooth, looking very cute.
### 年龄(齡) → 龄(齡) → 齒 → 止
- **definition:** stop
- **pinyin:** zhǐ
- **components:**
  - 趾
    - zhǐ
    - foot
  - 龰
    - yù
    - stylized feet
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original: Foot pointing up with 3 toes and a sole ->
    - Stylized as 龰 ->
    - Derivative 趾 refers to original 'foot' meaning
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicting a footprint with toes and a sole, 止 (zhǐ, "foot") represents the foot. The character's form resembles a foot with three toes, analogous to how the hand is represented with fingers in other characters.
- **examples:**

### 年龄(齡) → 龄(齡) → 齒 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').

### 年龄(齡) → 龄(齡) → 令 → 卩
- **definition:** seal
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.
- **examples:**
  - 那位卩正虔诚(誠)祈祷(禱)。
    - Nà wèi jié zhèng qiánchéng qídǎo.

    - Lǐyí shī zhǐchū, jié dàibiǎo qiānbēi yǔ jìngyì.
    - The ritual master pointed out that the kneeling person represents humility and respect.
  - 在庄(莊)严(嚴)仪(儀)式中，卩象征(徵)着(著)内(內)心的虔敬。
    - Zài zhuāngyán yíshì zhōng, jié xiàngzhēngzhe nèixīn de qiánjìng.
