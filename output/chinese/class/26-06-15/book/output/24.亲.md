### qīn
---
- **traditional:** 親
- **definition:** kiss
- **pinyin:** qīn
- **components:**
  - 辛
    - xīn
    - bitter
  - 見
    - jiàn
    - see
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 辛 (chisel) + semantic 見 (see) ->
    - stylized as 亲 (variant of 辛) ->
    - 親
  - **interpretation:** 親 (qīn, 'kiss') combines 辛 (xīn, 'chisel') phonetically with 見 (jiàn, 'see') semantically. The character suggests a close action that involves attentiveness, like seeing. The association with kissing likely comes from the intimate gesture of 'coming close to see'.
  - **simplification:** The traditional character 親 is simplified to 亲 by replacing 見 with the simpler component 见.
- **examples:**

### 亲(親) → 辛
- **definition:** bitter
- **pinyin:** xīn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a tool—chisel or awl—used to mark slaves and criminals ->
    - 辛
  - **interpretation:** The character 辛 (xīn) originally depicted a tool such as a chisel, used for marking or branding. The association with a sharp tool helps explain its extended meaning of 'spice,' as spices can have a pungent, sharp quality reminiscent of a tool's point or effect.
- **examples:**

### 亲(親) → 見
- **definition:** see
- **pinyin:** jiàn
- **components:**
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 卩
    - jié
    - kneeling person
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 目 (mù, "eye") + 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") =
    - a kneeling figure with large eye ->
    - see
  - **interpretation:** The combination of a human figure and an eye visually represents the act of seeing. The eye symbolizes vision, while the person emphasizes the human context of this action, making the meaning intuitive.
  - **simplification:** 见(見) is derived from using a cursive variant of the character in place of the traditional form.
- **examples:**

### 亲(親) → 見 → 目
- **definition:** eye
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a human eye, now represented and rotated 90 degrees ->
    - eye
  - **interpretation:** The character 目(mù, 'eye') is a pictogram representing a human eye. Its original orientation has changed over time, resembling an eye seen from a sideways view.

### 亲(親) → 見 → 卩
- **definition:** kneeling person
- **pinyin:** jié
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a kneeling person from sideview
  - **interpretation:** The character 卩 (jié, "kneeling person") is a simple pictogram showing the silhouette of a person kneeling. It is unrelated to other kneeling or similar figures in different contexts such as 欠 (qiàn, "yawn") where the mouth is open, or 女 (nǚ, "woman") where the figure represents a female. Its form is solely based on the posture of kneeling.
