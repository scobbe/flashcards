## Cantonese; a Chinese dialect
### Guǎngdōnghuà
---
- **traditional:** 廣東話
- **definition:** Cantonese; a Chinese dialect
- **pinyin:** Guǎngdōnghuà
- **components:**
  - 廣
    - guǎng
    - broad
  - 東
    - dōng
    - east
  - 話
    - huà
    - speech; language
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 廣 (guǎng, "broad") + 東 (dōng, "east") + 話 (huà, "speech; language") forms the name for a specific regional dialect often used by the people in the Guangdong province, leading to its designation.
  - **interpretation:** 廣 (guǎng, "broad") and 東 (dōng, "east") together refer to the Guangdong province, a region in the southeastern part of China known for its expanse to the east. 話 (huà, "speech; language") indicates the spoken language. Thus, 廣東話 (Guǎngdōnghuà) signifies the language of the broad eastern region, i.e., Cantonese, the specific language used predominantly by people in Guangdong.
- **examples:**
  - 很多香港人会(會)说(說)广(廣)东(東)话(話)。
    - Hěnduō Xiānggǎng rén huì shuō Guǎngdōnghuà.
    - Many people in Hong Kong can speak Cantonese.
  - 我学(學)习(習)广(廣)东(東)话(話)，因为(為)我想去广(廣)东(東)旅行。
    - Wǒ xuéxí Guǎngdōnghuà, yīnwèi wǒ xiǎng qù Guǎngdōng lǚxíng.
    - I study Cantonese because I want to travel to Guangdong.
  - 听(聽)懂广(廣)东(東)话(話)对(對)我来(來)说(說)有点(點)难(難)。
    - Tīng dǒng Guǎngdōnghuà duì wǒ lái shuō yǒudiǎn nán.
    - Understanding Cantonese is a bit difficult for me.
### 广(廣)东(東)话(話) → 廣
- **definition:** broad
- **pinyin:** guǎng
- **components:**
  - 广
    - guǎng
    - roof; shelter
  - 黃
    - huáng
    - yellow
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 广 (roof; shelter) + phonetic 黃 (huáng, sound) ->
    - shelter
  - **interpretation:** The character 廣 (guǎng, 'shelter') combines the semantic component 广, which suggests the idea of covering or enclosure like a roof, with the phonetic component 黃 (huáng). The enclosure aspect of 广 underlines the meaning of providing shelter or an expansive space.
  - **simplification:** 廣 was simplified to 广 by retaining the roof radical but removing 黃, simplifying the structure considerably.
- **examples:**
  - 我们正在寻找一个可以提供广(廣)的地方。
    - Wǒmen zhèngzài xúnzhǎo yī gè kěyǐ tígōng guǎng de dìfāng.
    - We are looking for a place that can provide shelter.
  - 那里的广(廣)设施非常完善，适合长时间停留。
    - Nàlǐ de guǎng shèshī fēicháng wánshàn, shìhé cháng shíjiān tíngliú.
    - The shelter facilities there are very complete, suitable for long stays.
  - 如果有恶劣天气，我们需要一个广(廣)来保护自己。
    - Rúguǒ yǒu èliè tiānqì, wǒmen xūyào yī gè guǎng lái bǎohù zìjǐ.
    - If there is bad weather, we need a shelter to protect ourselves.

### 广(廣)东(東)话(話) → 廣 → 广(廣) → 黃
- **definition:** yellow
- **pinyin:** huáng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally a pictogram depicting a disabled person or man with a ring of jade on the chest ->
    - phonetically borrowed for 'yellow' ->
    - 黃
  - **interpretation:** The character 黃 (huáng, 'yellow') originated from a pictogram representing either a disabled person or a man with a jade ring, showing a connection to cultural rituals or symbols of status. It was later phonetically borrowed to represent the color 'yellow' without semantic connection to its original depiction. In modern Chinese, its primary use is to denote the color yellow, disconnecting entirely from its debated pictographic origins.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)朵花是黄(黃)色的。
    - Zhè duǒ huā shì huángsè de.
    - This flower is yellow.
  - 秋天来(來)临(臨)时(時)，树(樹)叶(葉)变(變)成金黄(黃)色。
    - Qiūtiān láilín shí, shùyè biàn chéng jīnhuáng sè.
    - When autumn arrives, the leaves turn golden yellow.
  - 他穿了一件黄(黃)色的外套。
    - Tā chuānle yī jiàn huángsè de wàitào.
    - He wore a yellow jacket.

### 广(廣)东(東)话(話) → 東
- **definition:** east
- **pinyin:** dōng
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bundle or bag tied at both ends
  - **interpretation:** Originally depicting a bundle or bag tied at both ends, it suggests convergence or collection, visually resembling a tied shape. This pictorial representation is historically connected to the direction 'east', as a bag captures and holds items, analogous to the rising sun being 'held' in the east. Despite alternative interpretations involving the sun and tree, the pictogram of a bundle remains the most direct visual link.
- **examples:**
  - 他的東里(裏)装(裝)满(滿)了书(書)。
    - Tā de dōng lǐ zhuāng mǎn le shū.
    - His bag is full of books.
  - 她买(買)了一个(個)新的東。
    - Tā mǎi le yī gè xīn de dōng.
    - She bought a new bag.
  - 在办(辦)公室里(裏)，他的東总(總)是放在椅子后(後)面。
    - Zài bàngōngshì lǐ, tā de dōng zǒng shì fàng zài yǐzi hòumiàn.
    - In the office, his bag is always placed behind the chair.

### 广(廣)东(東)话(話) → 話
- **definition:** speech; language
- **pinyin:** huà
- **components:**
  - 言
    - yán
    - word
  - 𠯑
    - gro
    - carry out, perform
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 言 (yán, "word") + phonetic 𠯑 ->
    - speech; language
  - **interpretation:** The character 話 (huà) combines the semantic component 言 (yán, "word"), indicating speech or language, with a phonetic component 𠯑, which provides the sound. The semantic link with 言 aligns with the character's meaning of speech or language.
  - **simplification:** 話 was simplified to 话 by replacing 言 with 讠, reducing strokes using a cursive shorthand for the speech radical.
- **examples:**
  - 老师(師)正在讲(講)课(課)，学(學)生们(們)认(認)真听(聽)话(話)。
    - Lǎoshī zhèngzài jiǎngkè, xuéshēngmen rènzhēn tīnghuà.
    - The teacher is giving a lecture, and the students are listening attentively.
  - 他说(說)了一句感人的话(話)，让(讓)大家都为(為)之动(動)容。
    - Tā shuōle yī jù gǎnrén de huà, ràng dàjiā dōu wèi zhī dòngróng.
    - He said a moving statement, which touched everyone deeply.
  - 我们(們)每天都用普通话(話)交流。
    - Wǒmen měitiān dōu yòng pǔtōnghuà jiāoliú.
    - We use Mandarin to communicate every day.

### 广(廣)东(東)话(話) → 話 → 言
- **definition:** word
- **pinyin:** yán
- **components:**
  - 舌
    - shé
    - tongue
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - 舌 (mouth with a forked tongue) with added stroke at top to suggest tongue movement ->
    - speech
  - **interpretation:** The character 言(yán, "speech") depicts the addition of a horizontal stroke to the ancient form of 舌(shé, "tongue") to indicate the motion of the tongue as it moves to articulate speech. This directly connects the organ involved in speaking to the concept of speech itself.
- **examples:**
  - 他的言语(語)充满(滿)智慧。
    - Tā de yányǔ chōngmǎn zhìhuì.
    - His speech is full of wisdom.
  - 我们(們)应(應)该(該)以行动(動)证(證)明我们(們)的言词(詞)。
    - Wǒmen yīnggāi yǐ xíngdòng zhèngmíng wǒmen de yáncí.
    - We should prove our words with actions.
  - 他说(說)话(話)的方式显(顯)示了他的修养(養)。
    - Tā shuōhuà de fāngshì xiǎnshìle tā de xiūyǎng.
    - The way he speaks shows his refinement.

### 广(廣)东(東)话(話) → 話 → 𠯑
- **definition:** carry out, perform
- **pinyin:** gro
- **components:**
  - 氏
    - shì
    - movement
  - 日
    - rì
    - sun
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 氏氏 (movement) + 日 (sun) ->
    - twilight or nightfall, when sun is lowered
  - **interpretation:** The character 𠯑 combines 氏 (shì, "movement") with 日 (rì, "sun") to visually represent the concept of twilight or nightfall, a time when the sun lowers below the horizon. As the sun transitions into this downward position, it symbolically signifies the idea of concluding or carrying out activities as the day ends, which connects to broader meanings of performing or carrying out tasks.
### 广(廣)东(東)话(話) → 話 → 𠯑 → 氏
- **definition:** movement
- **pinyin:** shì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a man bending over or holding an object ->
    - later forms included decoration ->
    - final form with a stroke
  - **interpretation:** The character 氏 (shì, "clan") is a pictogram that has undergone several interpretations. It may represent a figure bending over to sow a field or other activities, capturing the act of forming or being part of a group or collective effort, which aligns with the meaning of 'clan' as an extended family or group.
- **examples:**

### 广(廣)东(東)话(話) → 話 → 𠯑 → 日
- **definition:** sun
- **pinyin:** rì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts the sun; a dot or line added in the center to distinguish from similar characters e.g. 囗
  - **interpretation:** As a pictogram, 日(rì, 'sun') directly represents the sun. In ancient times, the added dot helped visually differentiate it from other characters. This is analogous to the astronomical symbol ☉ and Egyptian hieroglyph 𓇳, both depicting the sun.
- **examples:**
  - 晨曦来(來)临(臨)时(時)，日出东(東)方。
    - Chénxī láilín shí, rìchū dōngfāng.
    - At the break of dawn, the sun rises in the east.
  - 温(溫)暖的日光洒(灑)满(滿)大地。
    - Wēnnuǎn de rìguāng sǎ mǎn dàdì.
    - The warm sunlight spreads over the earth.
  - 中午时(時)分，日高挂(掛)天。
    - Zhōngwǔ shífēn, rì gāo guà tiān.
    - At noon, the sun hangs high in the sky.
