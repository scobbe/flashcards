## dream
### mèng
---
- **traditional:** 夢
- **definition:** dream
- **pinyin:** mèng
- **components:**
  - 爿
    - pán
    - bed
  - 人
    - rén
    - person
  - 目
    - mù
    - eye
  - 夕
    - xī
    - night
  - 瞢
    - méng
    - bewildered
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Oracle bone script: person 人 (rén, 'person') lying on bed 爿 (pán, 'bed') + eye 目 (mù, 'eye') with emphasis ->
    - Bronze inscriptions: 目 with eyebrows 眉 + person as dots + moon 夕 (xī, 'night') added ->
    - 夢: eyebrows 卝 + eyes 罒 + person's body 冖 + 夕 ->
    - phono-semantic compound: phonetic 瞢 + semantic 夕
  - **interpretation:** In the oracle bone script, 夢 evokes a person on a bed with an emphasized eye, suggesting a state of being where one's vision is active despite resting, which aligns with dreaming. By the Bronze inscriptions, with the addition of 夕, it highlights the nocturnal and unconscious nature of dreams. Modern 夢 combines phonetic 瞢 with semantic 夕, reflecting dreaming as something occurring at night.
  - **simplification:** 梦 is a simplified form of 夢 where 目 + 冖 is simplified to a simpler eye component and 冖.
- **examples:**
  - 我昨晚做了一个(個)美好的梦(夢)。
    - Wǒ zuó wǎn zuò le yī gè měihǎo de mèng.
    - I had a wonderful dream last night.
  - 她希望有一天能实(實)现(現)自己的梦(夢)想。
    - Tā xīwàng yǒu yī tiān néng shíxiàn zìjǐ de mèngxiǎng.
    - She hopes to fulfill her dreams one day.
  - 每个(個)人都应(應)该(該)追寻(尋)自己的梦(夢)。
    - Měi gè rén dōu yīnggāi zhuīxún zìjǐ de mèng.
    - Everyone should pursue their own dreams.
### 梦(夢) → 爿
- **definition:** bed
- **pinyin:** pán
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a bed ->
    - original form of 牀 (chuáng, "bed") ->
    - contracted form in 臧 (zāng, "possess")
  - **interpretation:** The character 爿 (pán, "bed") visually represents a bed, emphasizing its usage in denoting places for resting or sleeping. It served as the original form for the character 牀 (chuáng, "bed"), reinforcing its meaning. Additionally, in the character 臧 (zāng, "possess"), it appears as a contracted form, reflecting its historical usage in composite forms.

### 梦(夢) → 夕
- **definition:** night
- **pinyin:** xī
- **components:**
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a moon crescent
  - **interpretation:** The character 夕 (xī, 'evening') is a pictogram that represents a crescent moon, capturing the image of the moon in the evening sky. The comparison to 月 (yuè, 'moon') underscores its depiction of the moon phase commonly associated with evening.
- **examples:**
  - 夕阳(陽)西下。
    - xī yáng xīxià.
    - The setting sun descends.
  - 夕阳(陽)西下，晚风(風)徐徐。
    - xī yáng xīxià, wǎn fēng xúxú.
    - The sun sets as the gentle evening breeze blows.

### 梦(夢) → 夕 → 月
- **definition:** moon
- **pinyin:** yuè
- **etymology:**

  - **description:** Depicts a crescent moon
  - **interpretation:** The character 月(yuè, "moon") is a pictographic representation of a crescent moon, straightforwardly capturing the curved shape often associated with the moon's appearance. ### 梦(夢) → 瞢 → 亡
- **definition:** departed; gone; lost
- **pinyin:** wáng
- **components:**
  - 刀
    - dāo
    - knife
  - 芒
    - máng
    - edge of a knife
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:**
    - A line on 刀 (dāo, 'knife') ->
    - cutting edge of a knife ->
    - original form of 芒 (máng, 'edge of a knife')
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts a line on a knife, representing the sharp edge. This connection to sharpness and cutting leads to the concept of 'perish,' as an edge can cut or end something, symbolically indicating destruction or the end of life.
- **examples:**

  - 如果不努力经(經)营(營)，这(這)家公司可能会(會)亡。
    - Rúguǒ bù nǔlì jīngyíng, zhè jiā gōngsī kěnéng huì wáng.

### 梦(夢) → 瞢 → 亡 → 芒
- **definition:** edge of a knife
- **pinyin:** máng
- **components:**
  - 艹
    - cǎo
    - grass
  - 亡
    - wáng
    - to flee
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艹 (cǎo, "grass") + phonetic 亡 (wáng) ->
    - edge of a knife
  - **interpretation:** The character 芒(máng, "edge of a knife") uses the 艹 (cǎo, "grass") component to highlight its relation to plant life or natural elements, while 亡 (wáng) provides the phonetic indication. Originally it may have depicted something like 方 but evolved to illustrate the thinness or sharpness of grass edges, metaphorically linking to the sharp edge of a knife.
- **examples:**
  - 刀的芒在阳(陽)光下闪(閃)闪(閃)发(發)光。
    - Dāo de máng zài yángguāng xià shǎnshǎn fāguāng.
    - The edge of the knife glistens in the sunlight.
  - 我们(們)要小心刀的芒，因为(為)它非常锋(鋒)利。
    - Wǒmen yào xiǎoxīn dāo de máng, yīnwèi tā fēicháng fēnglì.
    - We must be careful of the knife's edge because it is very sharp.
  - 他用手指轻(輕)轻(輕)触(觸)摸刀的芒，感受到它的锋(鋒)利。
    - Tā yòng shǒuzhǐ qīngqīng chùmō dāo de máng, gǎnshòudào tā de fēnglì.
