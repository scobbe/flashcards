## pair
### shuāng
---
- **traditional:** 雙
- **definition:** pair
- **pinyin:** shuāng
- **components:**
  - 雔
    - chóu
    - two birds
  - 又
    - yòu
    - hand
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 雔 (two birds) + 又 (hand) =
    - holding two birds in hand ->
    - pair
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts two birds (雔) held together by a hand (又), creating the image of a pair of birds, which conveys the meaning of 'pair' or 'double'. The concept of holding a pair of something is naturally associated with 'pair', reinforcing the meaning.
  - **simplification:** The traditional form 䨇 underwent simplification by eliminating the 雔 component, leading to the modern form 双 that retains the notion of duplicity through duplication of 又.
- **examples:**
  - 她买(買)了一双(雙)新鞋。
    - Tā mǎi le yī shuāng xīn xié.
    - She bought a new pair of shoes.
  - 我们(們)各吃了两(兩)个(個)苹(蘋)果，一人一双(雙)。
    - Wǒmen gè chī le liǎng gè píngguǒ, yī rén yī shuāng.
    - We each ate two apples, one pair per person.
  - 他给(給)我买(買)了一双(雙)手套，因为(為)天气(氣)冷了。
    - Tā gěi wǒ mǎi le yī shuāng shǒutào, yīnwèi tiānqì lěng le.
    - He bought me a pair of gloves because it's getting cold.

### 双(雙) → 雔 → 隹
- **definition:** short-tailed bird
- **pinyin:** zhuī
- **components:**
  - 鷕
    - yǎo
    - cry of a bird
  - 唯
    - wéi
    - only
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a short‐tailed bird (e.g., a sparrow) ->
    - also original form of 唯 (wéi, "only") before evolution into 鷕 (yǎo, "cry of a bird")
  - **interpretation:** 隹 (zhuī, "short-tailed bird") is a pictogram representing a bird with a short tail. Its depiction served as the foundation for other characters related to birds, such as 鷕 (yǎo, "cry of a bird").
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)只短尾的隹在树(樹)林中飞(飛)翔。

    - Bówùguǎn lǐ zhǎnchūle yī jiàn yǐ zhuī wèi tícái de gǔdài huìhuà.
    - The museum displays an ancient painting themed on the short-tailed bird.
  - 远(遠)古诗(詩)篇中常用隹来(來)象征(徵)自由，寓意生动(動)。
    - Yuǎngǔ shīpiān zhōng cháng yòng zhuī lái xiàngzhēng zìyóu, yùyì shēngdòng.

### 双(雙) → 雔 → 隹 → 鷕
- **definition:** cry of a bird
- **pinyin:** yǎo
- **components:**
  - 唯
    - wéi
    - only
  - 鳥
    - niǎo
    - bird
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - 唯 (phonetic) + 鳥 (semantic, "bird") ->
    - cry of a bird
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 唯 (wéi, used phonetically for its sound) with 鳥 (niǎo, "bird") to denote the specific sound relating to a bird's cry.
### 双(雙) → 雔 → 隹 → 鷕 → 唯
- **definition:** only
- **pinyin:** wéi
- **components:**
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
  - 隹
    - zhuī
    - short-tailed bird
  - 鷕
    - yǎo
    - cry of a bird
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + phonetic 隹 (zhuī, "short-tailed bird") ->
    - original form of 鷕 ->
    - borrowed phonetically for 'only'
  - **interpretation:** The character 唯 originally represented the cry of a bird, with the mouth radical indicating the sound aspect. It was later phonetically borrowed for the abstract meaning 'only', without retaining its original context related to bird sounds.
- **examples:**

  - 我唯爱(愛)读(讀)书(書)，因为(為)书(書)籍能带(帶)给(給)我无(無)限的可能。
    - Wǒ wéi ài dúshū, yīnwèi shūjí néng dài gěi wǒ wúxiàn de kěnéng.

### 双(雙) → 雔 → 隹 → 鷕 → 唯 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

### 双(雙) → 又
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** yòu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a right hand
  - **interpretation:** The character 又 (yòu, "hand") is a pictogram representing a right hand, showing the fingers and palm, which highlights its original meaning related to manual actions.
- **examples:**
  - 「又」这(這)个(個)字最早来(來)源于(於)手。
    - 「Yòu」 zhè gè zì zuì zǎo láiyuán yú shǒu。
    - The character '又' originally comes from the hand.
  - 很多汉(漢)字含有又部首，表示与(與)手有关(關)的动(動)作。
    - Hěn duō hànzì hányǒu Yòu bùshǒu, biǎoshì yǔ shǒu yǒuguān de dòngzuò。
    - Many Chinese characters include the '又' radical, indicating actions related to the hand.
  - 古人用又描绘(繪)手的形态(態)，体(體)现(現)了古汉(漢)字的独(獨)特魅力。
    - Gǔrén yòng Yòu miáohuì shǒu de xíngtài, tǐxiàn le gǔ hànzì de dútè mèilì。
    - Ancient people used '又' to depict the shape of the hand, showcasing the unique charm of ancient Chinese characters.
