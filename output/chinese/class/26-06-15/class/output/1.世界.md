## world
### shìjiè
---
- **definition:** world
- **pinyin:** shìjiè
- **components:**
  - 世
    - shì
    - generation; life; era
  - 界
    - jiè
    - boundary; sphere; realm
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 世 (shì, "generation; life; era") refers to the succession of human generations or life stages, encompassing time and overall human existence. 界 (jiè, "boundary; sphere") represents the limits or divisions between different entities or areas, leading to a conception of different realms or domains. Together, they form the idea of the "world" as a sphere consisting of all generations and realms, encompassing all of humankind and the physical domain around them.
  - **interpretation:** 世 (shì, "generation; life; era") emphasizes continuity and the passage of time across human generations, implying a collective human existence over eras. 界 (jiè, "boundary; sphere") is about demarcation, defining spaces or worlds in philosophical, physical, or existential terms. Thus, 世界 (shìjiè) embodies the concept of the world as the overarching domain that includes all human societies and the physical universe in which they exist.
- **examples:**
  - 我们(們)住在一个(個)美丽(麗)的世界。
    - Wǒmen zhù zài yī gè měilì de shìjiè.
    - We live in a beautiful world.
  - 世界上有很多不同的文化。
    - Shìjiè shàng yǒu hěn duō bùtóng de wénhuà.
    - There are many different cultures in the world.
  - 科学(學)家在探索新的世界。
    - Kēxuéjiā zài tànsuǒ xīn de shìjiè.
    - Scientists are exploring new worlds.
### 世界 → 世
- **definition:** generation; life; era
- **pinyin:** shì
- **components:**
  - 枼
    - yè
    - leaf
  - 葉
    - yè
    - leaf
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** jiajie
  - **description:**
    - Variant form of 枼 (original form of 葉) with the tree removed ->
    - borrowed meaning for 'generation'; traditionally explained as triplicated 十 ('thirty'), referencing thirty years as a standard generation ->
    - simplified to adopt new meaning
  - **interpretation:** The character 世 originated as a variant of 枼, the word for 'leaf', with the tree component removed, representing a borrowing of its shape for the phonetic similarity. Over time, it acquired a meaning related to 'generation' due to the interpretation of triplicated 十, symbolizing a traditional thirty-year generation cycle, which is how it came to represent age or lifetime.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)一代人肩负(負)着(著)重大的责(責)任。
    - Zhè yī dài rén jiānfùzhe zhòngdà de zérèn.
    - This generation shoulders a great responsibility.
  - 他用一生的努力创(創)造了伟(偉)大的成就。
    - Tā yòng yī shēng de nǔlì chuàngzàole wěidà de chéngjiù.
    - He used a lifetime of effort to create great achievements.
  - 整个(個)世纪(紀)的历(歷)史都记(記)载(載)在这(這)本书(書)里(裡)。
    - Zhěnggè shìjì de lìshǐ dōu jìzǎi zài zhè běn shū lǐ.
    - The history of the entire century is recorded in this book.

### 世界 → 世 → 枼
- **definition:** leaf
- **pinyin:** yè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts leaves growing from a tree (original form of 葉 (yè, "leaf"))
  - **interpretation:** Depicts leaves on a branch, representing both the specific physical forms of 'leaf' as well as the larger conceptual category of things that are flat like leaves. This intuitive extension made it a fitting choice to also represent the concept of 'flat' due to the physical characteristic of flatness associated with leaves.

### 世界 → 世 → 葉
- **definition:** leaf
- **pinyin:** yè
- **components:**
  - 艸
    - cǎo
    - grass; plant
  - 枼
    - yè
    - leaf
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 艸 (cǎo, "grass; plant") + phonetic 枼 (yè, "leaf") ->
    - depiction of a leaf
  - **interpretation:** The character 葉(yè, "leaf") is formed by combining 艸 (cǎo, "grass; plant") as the semantic component, representing its association with plants, with 枼 (yè, "leaf") as the phonetic component conveying the pronunciation. This results in the character meaning 'leaf,' representing the part of a plant.
  - **simplification:** In the simplified version 叶(葉), the semantic 艸 (cǎo, "grass; plant") is removed while retaining the phonetic 十, maintaining the connection to the sound but with fewer strokes.
- **examples:**
  - 秋天到了，树(樹)上的叶(葉)子变(變)黄(黃)了。
    - Qiūtiān dàole, shù shàng de yèzi biàn huáng le.
    - Autumn has arrived, and the leaves on the trees have turned yellow.
  - 一片叶(葉)子飘(飄)落在地上。
    - Yí piàn yèzi piāoluò zài dì shàng.
    - A leaf drifted down to the ground.
  - 风(風)吹过(過)时(時)，树(樹)叶(葉)沙沙作响(響)。
    - Fēng chuīguò shí, shùyè shāshā zuòxiǎng.
    - When the wind blows, the leaves rustle.

  - **description:**

  - **interpretation:** The character 艸(cǎo, 'grass') is a pictorial representation of grass blades. It is essentially a duplication of the character 屮 (chè, 'grass'), visually suggesting the appearance of grass tufts through repetition, emphasizing the concept of grass or vegetation.

### 世界 → 世 → 十
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园(園)里(裡)有十棵树(樹)，景色十分优(優)美。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.

### 世界 → 界
- **definition:** boundary; sphere; realm
- **pinyin:** jiè
- **components:**
  - 田
    - tián
    - field
  - 介
    - jiè
    - to mediate
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 田 (tián, "field") + phonetic 介 (jiè) ->
    - boundary; realm
  - **interpretation:** The character 界 (jiè, "boundary; realm") combines 田 (tián, "field") as the semantic component and 介 (jiè) as the phonetic component. Fields often have boundaries, and this association naturally leads to the concept of 'boundary' which is essential in defining realms or territories.
- **examples:**
  - 科学(學)界不断(斷)地探索新领(領)域。

    - Yìshùjiè yǒu xǔduō yǒu cáihuá de rén.
    - There are many talented people in the art world.
  - 电(電)脑(腦)的世界中，科技发(發)展迅速。
    - Diànnǎo de shìjiè zhōng, kējì fāzhǎn xùnsù.

### 世界 → 界 → 介
- **definition:** to mediate
- **pinyin:** jiè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a person standing between two lines or boundaries
  - **interpretation:** The character 介 (jiè, 'to derive an idea from') represents the concept of being in-between or acting as a mediator. It conceptually fits the idea of deriving by positioning oneself between different ideas or entities.
- **examples:**
  - 他从(從)古代诗(詩)词(詞)中介出艺(藝)术(術)灵(靈)感。
    - Tā cóng gǔdài shīcí zhōng jiè chū yìshù línggǎn.
    - He derives artistic inspiration from ancient poems.
  - 科学(學)家阅(閱)读(讀)文献(獻)时(時)介出创(創)新思想，工程师(師)从(從)实(實)验(驗)结(結)果中介出改进(進)方案。

    - Scientists derive innovative ideas when reading literature, and engineers derive improvement plans from experimental results.
  - 研究者从(從)历(歷)史资(資)料中介出重要信(資)息(訊)。
