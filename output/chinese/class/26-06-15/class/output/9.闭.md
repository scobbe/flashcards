## to close; to shut
### bì
---
- **traditional:** 閉
- **definition:** to close; to shut
- **pinyin:** bì
- **components:**
  - 門
    - mén
    - door
  - 材
    - cái
    - material; timber
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 門 (mén, "door") + abbreviated 材 (cái, "material; timber") =
    - a door with material blocking it ->
    - shut; close
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts a door being obstructed by some material, symbolizing the act of shutting or closing it.
  - **simplification:** The traditional form 閉 was simplified by reducing 門 (mén, "door") to 门 in the simplified character 闭.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)把( )门(門)闭(閉)上( )。
    - Qǐng bǎ mén bì shàng.
    - Please shut the door.
  - 他( )闭(閉)了( )一( )只(隻)眼( )睛( )，假( )装(裝)在( )睡( )觉(覺)。
    - Tā bìle yī zhī yǎnjīng, jiǎzhuāng zài shuìjiào.
    - He closed one eye, pretending to sleep.
  - 这(這)个(個)窗( )户(戶)很( )难(難)闭(閉)上( )，你( )需( )要( )用( )点(點)力( )气(氣)。
    - Zhège chuānghù hěn nán bì shàng, nǐ xūyào yòng diǎn lìqi.
    - This window is hard to close; you need to use some force.
### 闭(閉) → 門
- **definition:** door
- **pinyin:** mén
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a double-door gate
  - **interpretation:** The character depicts a pair of doors, visually representing the concept of a door or gate, which is fundamental in indicating entrance and exit points in structures.
- **examples:**
  - 我( )走( )到( )门(門)口( )。
    - Wǒ zǒu dào ménkǒu.
    - I walk to the door.
  - 门(門)轻(輕)轻(輕)打( )开(開)，房( )客( )进(進)来(來)了( )。
    - Mén qīngqīng dǎkāi, fángkè jìn láile.

### 闭(閉) → 材
- **definition:** material; timber
- **pinyin:** cái
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - tree
  - 才
    - cái
    - talent
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 木 (mù, "tree") + phonetic 才 (cái, "talent (phonetic)") ->
    - material; timber
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 木 (mù, "tree") as the semantic component, representing trees or wood, with 才 (cái), which provides the phonetic element. The combination indicates timber as processed wood material, derived from trees, and this extends metaphorically to 'material' in general as a resource or substance that can be worked with, much like wood.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)座( )房( )子( )是( )用( )优(優)质(質)的( )木( )材( )建( )造( )的( )。

    - This house is built with high-quality timber.
  - 他( )具( )备(備)成( )为(為)优(優)秀( )工( )程( )师(師)的( )材( )料( )。
    - Tā jùbèi chéngwéi yōuxiù gōngchéngshī de cáiliào.
    - He has the material to become an excellent engineer.
### 闭(閉) → 材 → 木
- **definition:** tree
- **pinyin:** mù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a tree with branches and roots
  - **interpretation:** The character 木(mù) depicts a tree, showing the trunk in the center and two slanting lines representing branches or roots. It directly signifies the concept of wood or a tree.
- **examples:**

### 闭(閉) → 材 → 才
- **definition:** talent
- **pinyin:** cái
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a sharp peg ->
    - originally referred to pegs used in board games ->
    - conceptually extended to 'talent' as skill pegs straight into a task
  - **interpretation:** The character 才 (cái) initially depicted a sharp peg, which was associated with playing board games that required skill. This sense of skill was then extended metaphorically to mean 'talent'. The leap to 'only then' likely comes from the notion that one's capabilities are unlocked or recognized through achievement, akin to placing the right peg in the right spot.
- **examples:**
  - 他( )的( )音( )乐(樂)才( )华(華)令( )人( )惊(驚)叹(嘆)。
    - Tā de yīnyuè cáihuá lìngrén jīngtàn.
    - His musical talent is astonishing.
  - 我( )刚(剛)刚(剛)才( )知( )道( )这(這)个(個)消( )息( )。
    - Wǒ gānggāng cái zhīdào zhège xiāoxi.
    - I just now found out about this news.
  - 只有努力工作才能取得成功。
    - Zhǐyǒu nǔlì gōngzuò cái néng qǔdé chénggōng.
    - Only by working hard can one achieve success.
