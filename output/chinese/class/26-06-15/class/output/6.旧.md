## old (of things)
### jiù
---
- **traditional:** 舊
- **definition:** old (of things)
- **pinyin:** jiù
- **components:**
  - 雈
    - huán
    - owl
  - 臼
    - jiù
    - mortar
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 雈 (huán, 'owl') + phonetic 臼 (jiù) ->
    - originally 'a type of owl', borrowed for phonetic similarity to 'old'
  - **interpretation:** The character 舊 (jiù, 'old') combines the semantic component 雈 (huán, 'owl') with the phonetic component 臼 (jiù, 'mortar'). It was initially used to represent a type of owl, which was later borrowed for its sound to denote 'old', associating the unchanging nature of the owl with the concept of 'oldness' or the passage of time.
  - **simplification:** 旧 is the simplified form, reducing complexity from 舊 by removing the semantic component 雈 (huán, 'owl') while preserving the sound with 臼 (jiù).
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)本书(書)很旧(舊)。
    - Zhè běn shū hěn jiù.
    - This book is very old.
  - 他穿着(著)旧(舊)衬(襯)衫，看起来(來)有些疲倦。
    - Tā chuānzhe jiù chènshān, kàn qǐlái yǒu xiē píjuàn.
    - He is wearing a worn shirt and looks a bit tired.
  - 她时(時)常怀(懷)念过(過)去的旧(舊)时(時)光。
    - Tā shícháng huáiniàn guòqù de jiù shíguāng.
    - She often reminisces about the old times.
### 旧(舊) → 雈
- **definition:** owl
- **pinyin:** huán
- **components:**
  - 雚
    - guàn
    - type of bird
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an owl, similar to 雚 (guàn, "type of bird")
  - **interpretation:** The character 雈 (huán, "owl") is a pictogram depicting the silhouette or characteristics of an owl. Historically, it is visually similar to 雚 (guàn, "type of bird"), emphasizing its avian nature. This is a direct representation of the creature it denotes.
### 旧(舊) → 雈 → 雚
- **definition:** type of bird
- **pinyin:** guàn
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Originally depicted a stork ->
    - additional component 吅 (xūn) suggests vocalization ->
    - final form 雚 (guān, "cry of waterfowl")
  - **interpretation:** The character 雚 (guān) originally depicted a stork and was a pictogram. Over time, it evolved with the addition of 吅 (xūn) to indicate the vocalization of the bird, becoming associated with the cry of waterfowl.

### 旧(舊) → 臼
- **definition:** mortar
- **pinyin:** jiù
- **components:**
  - 舂
    - chōng
    - pound grain
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a mortar, often used with a pestle in the ancient form of 舂 (chōng, "pound grain")
  - **interpretation:** The character 臼 (jiù, "mortar") is an ancient pictogram representing a mortar used for grinding, commonly seen in traditional processes such as grain pounding, as depicted in the related character 舂 (chōng, "pound grain"). Its form visually represents the receptacle used to process grains.
- **examples:**
  - 他用一臼轻(輕)轻(輕)拂去脸(臉)上的尘(塵)土。
    - Tā yòng yī jiù qīngqīng fú qù liǎn shàng de chéntǔ.
    - He used his pair of hands to gently brush away the dust on his face.
  - 运(運)动(動)员(員)凭(憑)借一臼快速接过(過)球。
    - Yùndòngyuán píngjiè yī jiù kuàisù jiē guò qiú.
    - The athlete quickly caught the ball with his pair of hands.

### 旧(舊) → 臼 → 舂
- **definition:** pound grain
- **pinyin:** chōng
- **components:**
  - 廾
    - gǒng
    - both hands
  - 午
    - wǔ
    - pestle
  - 臼
    - jiù
    - mortar
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 廾 (both hands) + 午 (pestle) + 臼 (mortar) =
    - using both hands to pound with a mortar and pestle
  - **interpretation:** The character 舂(chōng, 'pound grain') represents the action of using both hands to pound grain with a pestle in a mortar. The combination of these components reflects the traditional method of processing grains. 廾 (gǒng, 'both hands') suggests the manual aspect, 午 (wǔ, 'pestle') indicates the tool used, and 臼 (jiù, 'mortar') represents the container. This imagery directly ties to its meaning of pounding grain.

### 旧(舊) → 臼 → 舂 → 午
- **definition:** pestle
- **pinyin:** wǔ
- **components:**
  - 杵
    - chǔ
    - pestle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Pictogram: Depicts a pestle (modern 杵 (chǔ, "pestle")) ->
    - original meaning unrelated to time concepts
  - **interpretation:** 午 (wǔ, "noon") originally depicted a pestle, but over time it was borrowed for usage in the context of time. Despite its current association with noon, its visual origin is agricultural, related to pounding or grinding.
- **examples:**

### 旧(舊) → 臼 → 舂 → 午 → 杵
- **definition:** pestle
- **pinyin:** chǔ
- **components:**
  - 木
    - mù
    - wood
  - 午
    - wǔ
    - pestle
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 木 (mù, "wood") + phonetic 午 (wǔ, "pestle") ->
    - a wooden pestle
  - **interpretation:** Combines 木 (mù, "wood"), which signifies that the object is made of wood, with 午 (wǔ, "pestle") serving as the phonetic component to suggest the word's pronunciation. The character effectively depicts a pestle made from wood, as was traditionally used.
- **examples:**
  - 他用杵捣(搗)碎坚(堅)果。
    - Tā yòng chǔ dǎosuì jiānguǒ.
    - He used a pestle to crush the nuts.
  - 这(這)根杵是用来(來)捣(搗)蒜的。
    - Zhè gēn chǔ shì yòng lái dǎo suàn de.
    - This pestle is used for crushing garlic.
  - 传(傳)统(統)上，杵和臼一起使用来(來)制(製)作药(藥)物。
    - Chuántǒng shàng, chǔ hé jiù yìqǐ shǐyòng lái zhìzuò yàowù.
    - Traditionally, a pestle is used with a mortar to make medicine.
