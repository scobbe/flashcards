## to win
### yíng
---
- **traditional:** 贏
- **definition:** to win
- **pinyin:** yíng
- **components:**
  - 𦝠
    - luó
    - food, eat
  - 貝
    - bèi
    - cowry; money
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - phonetic 𦝠 (luó) + semantic 貝 (bèi, "cowry; money") ->
    - to gain (as in winning) wealth or advantage
  - **interpretation:** The character 贏 (yíng, "to win") combines the phonetic element 𦝠 (luó) with the semantic 貝 (bèi, "cowry; money"), reflecting the concept of acquiring wealth or advantage through victory.
  - **simplification:** 赢 is the simplified form of 贏 and involves the simplification of 貝 to 贝 (bèi, "cowry") and removing 𦝠 (luó).
- **examples:**
  - 他赢(贏)得了比赛(賽)。
    - Tā yíngdéle bǐsài.
    - He won the competition.
  - 我们(們)必须(須)努力才能赢(贏)。
    - Wǒmen bìxū nǔlì cáinéng yíng.
    - We must work hard to win.
  - 她赢(贏)了一大笔(筆)奖(獎)金。
    - Tā yíngle yī dà bǐ jiǎngjīn.
    - She won a large prize.
### 赢(贏) → 𦝠
- **definition:** food, eat
- **pinyin:** luó
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a kind of dragon, either a Panlong (aquatic dragon) or a Chi (dragon without horns) ->
    - used as a decorative motif in ancient Chinese art
  - **interpretation:** Though primarily depicting specific types of dragons, the character 𦝠 was not widely adopted in contemporary language to convey the meaning 'food, eat.' Instead, the depiction reinforced its cultural role associated with mythology and art, with uses reflecting its original artistic significance.
### 赢(贏) → 貝
- **definition:** cowry; money
- **pinyin:** bèi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a cowrie shell ->
    - used as currency in ancient China ->
    - stylized as 貝
  - **interpretation:** The character 貝(bèi, 'shell/money') visually represents a cowrie shell, which was used as a form of currency in ancient China. This explains why it is associated with money or valuables.
  - **simplification:** 贝 is a cursive script simplification of 貝, transforming the character into a more streamlined form while retaining its essence.
- **examples:**
  - 他把贝(貝)当作护(護)身符，放在口袋里(裡)。
    - Tā bǎ bèi dāng zuò hùshēn fú, fàng zài kǒudài lǐ.
    - He uses shells as a talisman, placing them in his pocket.
  - 古代人用贝(貝)作为(為)货(貨)币(幣)。
    - Gǔdài rén yòng bèi zuòwéi huòbì.
    - In ancient times, people used shells as currency.
