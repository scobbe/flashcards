## neighbor
### línjū
---
- **traditional:** 鄰居
- **definition:** neighbor
- **pinyin:** línjū
- **components:**
  - 邻(鄰)
    - lín
    - neighbor
  - 居
    - jū
    - live; dwell; reside
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 邻(鄰) (lín, "neighbor") originally depicted ears of grain stacked close together, illustrating proximity. 居 (jū, "live") shows a person under a roof, representing habitation.
  - **interpretation:** 鄰 (lín, "neighbor") reflects people or homes situated close to one another, similar to how stacked grain ears are adjacent. 居 (jū, "live") indicates a place of residence. Together, 鄰居 (línjū, "neighbor") describes those who live nearby in relation to one's home.
- **examples:**
  - 我的邻(鄰)居每天早上都遛狗。
    - Wǒ de línjū měitiān zǎoshang dōu liù gǒu.
    - My neighbor walks the dog every morning.
  - 新来(來)的邻(鄰)居很友好，我们(們)常常一起聊天。
    - Xīn lái de línjū hěn yǒuhǎo, wǒmen chángcháng yìqǐ liáotiān.
    - The new neighbor is very friendly, and we often chat together.
  - 邻(鄰)居家的孩子每天都在院子里(裡)玩耍。
    - Línjū jiā de háizi měitiān dōu zài yuànzi lǐ wánshuǎ.
    - The neighbor's children play in the yard every day.
