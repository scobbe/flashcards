## to take off
### qǐfēi
---
- **traditional:** 起飛
- **definition:** to take off
- **pinyin:** qǐfēi
- **components:**
  - 起
    - qǐ
    - rise; initiate; begin; get up
  - 飛
    - fēi
    - fly; flutter; hover; rush
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:**
    - 起 (qǐ, "rise") + 飛 (fēi, "fly") =
    - rise up to fly ->
    - take off
  - **interpretation:** The combination of 起 (qǐ, "rise") and 飛 (fēi, "fly") conveys the action of an aircraft lifting off the ground to begin flying. This is a straightforward and literal representation of the concept of taking off.
- **examples:**
  - 飞(飛)机(機)起飞(飛)。
    - Fēijī qǐfēi.
    - The airplane takes off.
  - 飞(飛)机(機)在跑道上加速，很快起飞(飛)。
    - Fēijī zài pǎodào shàng jiāsù, hěn kuài qǐfēi.
    - The airplane accelerates on the runway, then takes off quickly.
  - 准(準)备(備)完毕(畢)，飞(飛)机(機)起飞(飛)。
    - Zhǔnbèi wánbì, fēijī qǐfēi.
    - After getting ready, the airplane takes off.
### 起飞(飛) → 起
- **definition:** rise; initiate; begin; get up
- **pinyin:** qǐ
- **components:**
  - 走
    - zǒu
    - walk
  - 巳
    - sì
    - sixth of the Twelve Earthly Branches
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 走 (zǒu, "walk") + phonetic 巳 (sì) ->
    - rise; lift; begin; get up
  - **interpretation:** The semantic component 走 (zǒu, "walk") suggests movement or action, fitting with the meaning of rising or getting up. The phonetic component 巳 (sì) was chosen for its sound, contributing to the pronunciation without directly affecting the meaning.
- **examples:**

    - Tàiyáng shēng qǐ, tiānbiān rǎn shàng jú sè.

    - Wǒmen yào qǐchuáng le.
    - We need to get up.
  - 新的一天开(開)始了，大家都充满(滿)活力。
    - Xīn de yī tiān kāishǐ le, dàjiā dōu chōngmǎn huólì.

- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**

### 起飞(飛) → 起 → 走 → 止 → 龰
- **definition:** stylized feet
- **pinyin:** yù
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts a stylized footprint pointing upward ->
    - originally meaning 'foot' ->
    - later forms like 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') refer back to original meaning
  - **interpretation:** 龰 (yù, 'stylized feet') is a pictogram showing a footprint pointing up, originally indicating the concept of a foot. The character 趾 (zhǐ, 'toe') evolved from this and retains the reference to the original meaning. It visually resembles a sole with three toes, similar to representations of the human hand in characters like 爪 (zhuǎ, 'claw'), 寸 (cùn, 'inch'), and 又 (yòu, 'again').

### 起飞(飛) → 飛
- **definition:** fly; flutter; hover; rush
- **pinyin:** fēi
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bird flying upwards
  - **interpretation:** The character 飛(fēi, "fly") depicts the image of a bird in flight, which naturally evokes the concepts of flying, fluttering, or hovering as a bird does in the sky.
  - **simplification:** 飞 is a simplified form of 飛, created by reducing the complexity of the character while maintaining its original flying bird form.
- **examples:**
  - 小鸟(鳥)在空中飞(飛)翔。
    - Xiǎo niǎo zài kōngzhōng fēixiáng.
    - The bird is flying in the sky.
  - 秋天来(來)了，树(樹)叶(葉)纷(紛)纷(紛)飞(飛)落。
    - Qiūtiān láile, shùyè fēnfēn fēiluò.
    - Autumn has arrived, and leaves are fluttering down.
  - 时(時)间(間)飞(飛)快地流逝。
    - Shíjiān fēikuài de liúshì.
    - Time flies by quickly.
