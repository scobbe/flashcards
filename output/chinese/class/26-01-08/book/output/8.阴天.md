## cloudy sky; overcast weather
### yīntiān
---
- **traditional:** 陰天
- **definition:** cloudy sky; overcast weather
- **pinyin:** yīntiān
- **components:**
  - 阴(陰)
    - yīn
    - cloudy; negative; hidden
  - 天
    - tiān
    - sky; heaven; day
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 陰 (yīn, "cloudy; negative; hidden") indicates cloudy or shadowed conditions, as it typically represents the shaded or obscure side of a hill, metaphorically linked to overcast weather; 天 (tiān, "sky; heaven; day") is the sky or celestial space, relating to the weather above us.
  - **interpretation:** 陰 (yīn, "cloudy; hidden") conveys shadowy or overcast weather, implying the presence of clouds that obscure sunlight; it also connotes negativity as clouds can bring gloom. 天 (tiān, "sky") naturally represents the weather or atmosphere, so 阴(陰)天( ) (yīntiān) literally combines these to signify 'cloudy sky' or 'overcast weather', as a sky shadowed by clouds blocking the sun's light.
- **examples:**
  - 今( )天( )是( )阴(陰)天( )。
    - Jīntiān shì yīntiān.
    - Today is cloudy.
  - 阴(陰)天( )的( )时(時)候( )，我( )喜( )欢(歡)喝( )热(熱)茶( )。
    - Yīntiān de shíhòu, wǒ xǐhuān hē rè chá.
    - When it's cloudy, I like to drink hot tea.
  - 阴(陰)天( )让(讓)人( )感( )觉(覺)很( )放( )松(鬆)。
    - Yīntiān ràng rén gǎnjué hěn fàngsōng.

### 阴(陰)天( ) → 阴(陰)
- **traditional:** 陰
- **definition:** cloudy; negative; hidden
- **pinyin:** yīn
- **components:**
  - 阜
    - fù
    - mound
  - 侌
    - yīn
    - concealed
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 阜 (fù, "mound") + phonetic 侌 (yīn, "concealed") ->
    - concealed location ->
    - shade, moon, female
  - **interpretation:** The character 陰 (yīn, "concealed; shade; moon; female") combines 阜 (fù, "mound") suggesting a hill or elevation that blocks light, with 侌 (yīn, "concealed") indicating the hidden or shadowy aspect of such a place. Together, they convey the concept of something being hidden in the shadow of a hill or mound, thus connecting to ideas of shade and concealment. The association with the moon and female stems from the moon's own phases of visibility and its yin-feminine qualities in Chinese philosophy.
  - **simplification:** An unorthodox variant where 侌 (yīn, "concealed") was replaced with the component 月 (yuè, "moon") was eventually adopted as the official simplified form in 1956.
- **examples:**
  - 天( )气(氣)阴(陰)沉( )，似乎要下雨。

### 阴(陰)天( ) → 天
- **definition:** sky; heaven; day
- **pinyin:** tiān
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Horizontal line above 大(dà, "big") to depict sky ->
    - anthropomorphic man with large head symbolizing heaven
  - **interpretation:** The character 天(tiān) originally depicts the sky as a horizontal line above a person with outstretched arms, 大(dà, "big"), emphasizing the vastness or domain of heaven. Over time, the character evolved into an anthropomorphic representation of heaven, which later influenced its interpretation in classical texts.
