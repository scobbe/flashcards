## to make an extra effort; to cheer someone on
### jiāyóu
---
- **definition:** to make an extra effort; to cheer someone on
- **pinyin:** jiāyóu
- **components:**
  - 加
    - jiā
    - add; increase; join
  - 油
    - yóu
    - oil; fuel; grease
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** compound word
  - **description:** 加 (jiā, "add") + 油 (yóu, "oil") - in racing contexts, to add oil originally referred to adding fuel for more power, hence cheering someone on to do better.
  - **interpretation:** 加油 (jiāyóu) literally means 'add oil,' a metaphorical expression encouraging someone to work harder, persevere, or do better. Historically, the term derived from motor sports where adding oil meant boosting vehicle performance, thus symbolically encouraging someone to enhance their efforts.
- **examples:**
  - 比赛(賽)前，教练(練)对(對)运(運)动(動)员(員)说(說)要加油。
    - Bǐsài qián, jiàoliàn duì yùndòngyuán shuō yào jiāyóu.
    - Before the match, the coach told the athletes to make an extra effort.
  - 考试(試)时(時)，同学(學)互相加油。
    - Kǎoshì shí, tóngxué hùxiāng jiāyóu.
    - During the exam, classmates cheered each other on.
  - 比赛(賽)中，观(觀)众(眾)不断(斷)喊加油。
    - Bǐsài zhōng, guānzhòng bùduàn hǎn jiāyóu.
    - During the competition, the audience continuously shouted encouragement.
### 加油 → 加
- **definition:** add; increase; join
- **pinyin:** jiā
- **components:**
  - 力
    - lì
    - strength
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 力 (lì, "strength") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") ->
    - exertion with additional effort or breath ->
    - add
  - **interpretation:** The character 加 (jiā, "add") combines 力 (lì, "strength"), indicating physical effort or force, with 口 (kǒu, "mouth"), suggesting either verbal command or the exertion of breath when effort is applied. This combination evokes the image of putting in more effort, hence the meaning "add" as in adding effort or increasing in quantity. The evolution from the effortful action to 'add' bridges the concept of augmenting or increasing.
- **examples:**
  - 请(請)在茶里(裡)加些糖。
    - Qǐng zài chá lǐ jiā xiē táng.
    - Please add some sugar to the tea.
  - 你可以加我为(為)好友吗？
    - Nǐ kěyǐ jiā wǒ wéi hǎoyǒu ma?
    - Can you add me as a friend?
  - 我们(們)需要加倍努力，才能完成这(這)个(個)项(項)目。
    - Wǒmen xūyào jiābèi nǔlì, cái néng wánchéng zhège xiàngmù.
    - We need to double our efforts in order to complete this project.

### 加油 → 加 → 力
- **definition:** strength
- **pinyin:** lì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts either an arm bending out from the side of the body, or a wooden plough
  - **interpretation:** The character 力(lì, 'strength') visually represents physical power through the image of an arm or tool, emphasizing the fundamental concept of muscular force.
- **examples:**
  - 他用尽(盡)全力，赢(贏)得比赛(賽)。
    - Tā yòng jìn quánlì, yíng dé bǐsài.
    - He used all his strength to win the competition.
  - 他把力气(氣)用在学(學)习(習)上。
    - Tā bǎ lìqì yòng zài xuéxí shàng.
    - He applies his strength to his studies.
  - 面对(對)困难(難)，他毫不退缩(縮)，全力以赴。
    - Miànduì kùnnán, tā háo bù tuìsuō, quánlì yǐ fù.
    - Facing difficulties, he neither retreats nor gives up, but goes all out.

### 加油 → 加 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.

### 加油 → 油
- **definition:** oil; fuel; grease
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 氵
    - shuǐ
    - water
  - 由
    - yóu
    - reason
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 氵 (shuǐ, 'water') + phonetic 由 (yóu, 'reason') =
    - liquid that flows ->
    - oil, sauce
  - **interpretation:** The character 油(yóu) combines the water radical 氵 (shuǐ, 'water') with the phonetic 由 (yóu, 'reason'). This suggests something liquid, aligning with the meanings 'oil' and 'sauce', both of which flow like water.
- **examples:**
  - 油可以用来(來)炒菜。
    - Yóu kěyǐ yòng lái chǎo cài.
    - Oil can be used for stir-frying dishes.
  - 我喜欢(歡)辣椒油，因为(為)它增加了味道。
    - Wǒ xǐhuān làjiāo yóu, yīnwèi tā zēngjiāle wèidào.
    - I like chili oil because it enhances the flavor.
  - 煎鱼(魚)时(時)可以先在鱼(魚)上抹点(點)油，这(這)样(樣)鱼(魚)更滑嫩。
    - Jiān yú shí kěyǐ xiān zài yú shàng mǒ diǎn yóu, zhèyàng yú gèng huá nèn.
    - When pan-frying fish, you can first spread a little oil on the fish, so that the fish becomes more tender.

### 加油 → 油 → 氵
- **definition:** water
- **pinyin:** shuǐ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - 氵is the radical form of 水 (shuǐ, "water") ->
    - depicts flowing water, compressed and stylized next to characters.
  - **interpretation:** The character 氵is a simplified variant used as a radical to represent water-related meanings in other characters. It retains the flowing water depiction of 水 (shuǐ, "water") but is stylized to fit alongside other components.
- **examples:**
  - 水是生命之源。
    - Shuǐ shì shēngmìng zhī yuán.
    - Water is the source of life.
  - 汉(漢)字中偏旁氵，代表水。
    - Hànzì zhōng piān páng shuǐ, dàibiǎo shuǐ.
    - In Chinese characters, the radical '氵' signifies water.
  - 河水清澈透明，鱼(魚)儿(兒)欢(歡)快戏(戲)水。
    - Hé shuǐ qīngchè tòumíng, yú er huānkuaì xì shuǐ.
    - The river water is clear and transparent, and the fish happily play in it.
### 加油 → 油 → 由
- **definition:** reason
- **pinyin:** yóu
- **components:**
  - 十
    - shí
    - ten
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - Phonetic 十 (needle) + semantic 口 (mouth) ->
    - container with a needle symbolizing an ancient pronunciation
  - **interpretation:** The character 由(yóu, 'cause') depicts a container with a top resembling a needle (十), phonetic for sound with 口 (mouth) for semantic influence, symbolizing something that originates from a cause, just as contents originate from a container.
- **examples:**
  - 这(這)个(個)问(問)题(題)由他负(負)责(責)。
    - Zhège wèntí yóu tā fùzé.
    - This issue is caused by him to be responsible.
  - 一切的变(變)化都是由时(時)间(間)引起的。
    - Yīqiè de biànhuà dōu shì yóu shíjiān yǐnqǐ de.
    - All changes are caused by the passage of time.
  - 胜(勝)利由努力而来(來)。
    - Shènglì yóu nǔlì ér lái.
    - Victory is caused by effort.

### 加油 → 油 → 由 → 十
- **definition:** ten
- **pinyin:** shí
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Original vertical stroke ->
    - later horizontal stroke added ->
    - forms a cross ->
    - represents 'ten'
  - **interpretation:** The character 十(shí, 'ten') represents the number ten with a cross shape that could be seen as tally marks. It evolved from a simple vertical stroke to a cross by adding a horizontal stroke, symbolizing a complete set of ten.
- **examples:**
  - 我有十本书(書)。
    - Wǒ yǒu shí běn shū.
    - I have ten books.
  - 花园(園)里(裡)有十棵树(樹)，景色十分优(優)美。
    - Huāyuán lǐ yǒu shí kē shù, jǐngsè shífēn yōuměi.
    - There are ten trees in the garden, and the scenery is very beautiful.
  - 十年磨一剑(劍)。
    - Shí nián mó yī jiàn.
    - It takes ten years of practice to hone a sword.
