### xióng
---
- **definition:** bear
- **pinyin:** xióng
- **components:**
  - 能
    - néng
    - able
  - 炎
    - yán
    - flame
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 能 (néng, "able") + abbreviated phonetic 炎 (yán, "flame") ->
    - original representation of an onomatopoetic word for flaming ->
    - borrowed for the word 'bear'
  - **interpretation:** Originally, 熊(xióng, "bear") depicted the sound of flames raging, with 炎 (yán, "flame") as the sound component and 能 (néng, "able") representing the original semantic concept of a familiar powerful presence, later shifting to mean 'bear' when 能 (néng, "bear") was borrowed for the animal name.
- **examples:**

### 熊 → 能
- **definition:** able
- **pinyin:** néng
- **components:**
  - 匕
    - bǐ
    - spoon
  - 月
    - yuè
    - moon
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a bear: two paws stylized as 匕 (bǐ, "spoon") + face/mouth stylized as 月 (yuè, "moon")
  - **interpretation:** The character 能(néng), originally meant to represent a bear, combines elements stylized as paws and a face to visually convey an animal's features. Over time, this depiction of a bear has lent its phonetic value to words associated with ability and energy.

- **pinyin:** bǐ

  - **description:** Depicts a spoon
  - **interpretation:** The character 匕(bǐ, 'cane') originally depicts a spoon, which has a similar shape to the character itself. It is unrelated to the meanings of 'walking cane', 'knife', or 'person', which appear in other characters with similar forms.

### 熊 → 能 → 月
- **definition:** moon
- **pinyin:** yuè
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a crescent moon
  - **interpretation:** The character 月(yuè, "moon") is a pictographic representation of a crescent moon, straightforwardly capturing the curved shape often associated with the moon's appearance. Though interchangeable with 夕 (xī, "evening") in the past, it distinctly represents the moon in this usage.

- **definition:** fire
