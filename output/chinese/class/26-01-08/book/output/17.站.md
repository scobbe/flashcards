## to stand
### zhàn
---
- **definition:** to stand
- **pinyin:** zhàn
- **components:**
  - 立
    - lì
    - stand
  - 占
    - zhān
    - divine; take possession of
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 立 (lì, "stand") + phonetic 占 (zhān) ->
    - to stand
  - **interpretation:** The character 站 represents the action of standing through the combination of 立 (lì, "stand"), which provides the semantic element of standing, and the phonetic 占 (zhān), which contributes the pronunciation. The semantic element 立 directly conveys the meaning of 'standing,' thus underscoring the character's overall meaning.
- **examples:**
  - 他站在门(門)口。
    - Tā zhàn zài ménkǒu.
    - He is standing at the doorway.
  - 请(請)你站过(過)来(來)，我要给(給)你拍照。
    - Qǐng nǐ zhàn guòlái, wǒ yào gěi nǐ pāizhào.
    - Please stand over here, I want to take a photo of you.
  - 站久了，我就累了。
    - Zhàn jiǔle, wǒ jiù lèile.
    - After standing for a long time, I became tired.
### 站 → 立
- **definition:** stand
- **pinyin:** lì
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a person standing on the ground
  - **interpretation:** The character 立 (lì, 'stand') intuitively represents the concept of standing by depicting a person in an upright position, emphasizing the posture.
- **examples:**
  - 小王立在窗前，等待朋友到来(來)。
    - Xiǎo Wáng lì zài chuāng qián, děngdài péngyǒu dàolái.
    - Xiao Wang stands at the window, waiting for his friend to arrive.
  - 园(園)中雕像静(靜)立于(於)石台(臺)上。
    - Yuán zhōng diāoxiàng jìng lì yú shí tái shàng.
    - The statue in the garden stands quietly on the stone platform.
  - 士兵集合后(後)，整齐(齊)地立定阵(陣)型。
    - Shìbīng jíhé hòu, zhěngqí de lì dìng zhènxíng.
    - After the soldiers assemble, they stand in formation neatly.

### 站 → 占
- **definition:** divine; take possession of
- **pinyin:** zhān
- **components:**
  - 卜
    - bǔ
    - divination
  - 口
    - kǒu
    - mouth
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** Ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**
    - 卜 (bǔ, "divination") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") =
    - speaking about divination ->
    - divination
  - **interpretation:** The character 占 (zhān, "divination") represents the act of divination, combining the symbol for divination itself with the mouth, which may imply speaking or chanting during the divinatory practice.
- **examples:**
  - 古人借占，测(測)定吉凶。
    - Gǔ rén jiè zhān, cè dìng jí xiōng.
    - Ancient people used divination to determine auspiciousness and inauspiciousness.
  - 他占了一卦。
    - Tā zhānle yī guà.
    - He performed a divination.
  - 他运(運)用占，预(預)测(測)未来(來)。
    - Tā yùnyòng zhān, yùcè wèilái.
    - He relied on divination to predict the future.

### 站 → 占 → 卜
- **definition:** divination
- **pinyin:** bǔ
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a crack in an oracle bone
  - **interpretation:** The character 卜 (bǔ, 'divination') directly depicts a crack being used in oracle bones to divine future events. This literal representation naturally extends to the concept of divination.
- **examples:**
  - 在古代，人们(們)常用龟(龜)甲进(進)行卜卦。
    - Zài gǔdài, rénmen cháng yòng guījiǎ jìnxíng bǔguà.
    - In ancient times, people often used turtle shells for divination.
  - 他对(對)未来(來)充满(滿)疑虑(慮)，所以决(決)定找人卜卦。
    - Tā duì wèilái chōngmǎn yílǜ, suǒyǐ juédìng zhǎo rén bǔguà.
    - He was full of doubts about the future, so he decided to find someone for divination.
  - 在占星术(術)中，卜法常被用来(來)预(預)测(測)未来(來)。
    - Zài zhānxīngshù zhōng, bǔfǎ cháng bèi yòng lái yùcè wèilái.
    - In astrology, divination methods are often used to predict the future.

### 站 → 占 → 口
- **definition:** mouth
- **pinyin:** kǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts an open mouth.
  - **interpretation:** The character 口(kǒu, 'mouth') is a straightforward pictogram that visually represents an open mouth. It is a fundamental character in the Chinese language, often used both as an independent word and as a component in more complex characters, lending a semantic association to ideas relating to speech, openings, or enclosures.
- **examples:**
  - 他张(張)开(開)口，大声(聲)喊叫。
    - Tā zhāngkāi kǒu, dàshēng hǎnjiào.
    - He opened his mouth and shouted loudly.
  - 这(這)口饭(飯)入口即化。
    - Zhè kǒu fàn rùkǒu jíhuà.
    - This mouthful of food melts in your mouth.
  - 小女孩咬了一口苹(蘋)果，露出甜美的笑容。
    - Xiǎo nǚhái yǎole yī kǒu píngguǒ, lùchū tiánměi de xiàoróng.
    - The little girl took a bite of the apple and smiled sweetly.
