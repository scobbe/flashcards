## to pat; to beat; to take (a picture)
### pāi
---
- **definition:** to pat; to beat; to take (a picture)
- **pinyin:** pāi
- **components:**
  - 手
    - shǒu
    - hand
  - 白
    - bái
    - white
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**
    - semantic 扌(手) (shǒu, "hand") + phonetic 白 (bái, "white") ->
    - hand action ->
    - to pat; to beat
  - **interpretation:** 拍(pāi, "to pat; to beat; to take (a picture)") combines the semantic component 扌(手) (shǒu, "hand"), indicating movement or using the hand, with the phonetic component 白 (bái). The action of a hand repeatedly contacting a surface naturally conveys the sense of 'pat' or 'beat'. This character was adapted for the meaning 'to take (a picture)' as taking a photograph is a rapid capturing process similar to a pat.

### 拍 → 手
- **definition:** hand
- **pinyin:** shǒu
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** Depicts a hand with fingers; top stroke is a bent over middle finger, horizontal strokes represent fingers
  - **interpretation:** The character 手 (shǒu, "hand") is a direct pictorial representation of a human hand with all five fingers, which distinguishes it from similar characters representing claws or simplified fingers. This visually conveys its meaning as a hand clearly.
- **examples:**

### 拍 → 白
- **definition:** white
- **pinyin:** bái
- **etymology:**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:**
    - Depicts an acorn ->
    - white from the color of acorn's inside
  - **interpretation:** The character 白(bái, "white") is thought to depict an acorn, as suggested by its use in words related to oak and acorns. The character's meaning of "white" is likely derived from the white color of an acorn's interior.
