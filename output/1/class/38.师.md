## master; specialist
### sub-word
#### engineer
---
- **traditional:**: 師
- **simplified:**: 师
- **pronunciation:**: shī
- **definition:**: master; specialist
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 师(師)父 (shī fu, "master")
  - 大师(師) (dà shī, "grand master")
  - 名师(師) (míng shī, "famous teacher")
  - 全科医(醫)师(師) (quán kē yī shī, "general practitioner")
- **etymology (師):**
  - **type:**: ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**: ideogrammic: 𠂤 (qiū, "mound, hill") + 帀 (zā, "encircle").
  - **interpretation:**: The character combines 𠂤, depicting a hill, with 帀, meaning to go around, picturing troops encamped around a hill and thus ‘army’. From the military sense, it extended to ‘leader/teacher’ and then to ‘master; specialist’ in modern usage. Some sources note the exact role of 帀 is debated, but the overall ‘encirclement at a position’ explains the base sense.
  - **component characters:**
    - 𠂤 (qiū, "mound")
    - 帀 (zā, "encircle")
  - **reference:**: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%B8%AB
  - **simplification rule (师):**: The simplified form 师 replaced the left component 𠂤 with the simpler component 刂, reducing strokes while retaining the right element 帀.
