## noon
### sub-word
#### afternoon
---
- **traditional:**: 午
- **simplified:**: 午
- **pronunciation:**: wǔ
- **definition:**: noon
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 中午 (zhōngwǔ, "at noon")
  - 上午 (shàngwǔ, "the morning")
  - 下午 (xiàwǔ, "the afternoon")
  - 午饭(飯) (wǔfàn, "midday meal")
- **etymology (午):**
  - **type:**: pictogram
  - **description:**: pictogram of 杵 (chǔ, "pestle") → semantic: adopted as the earthly branch denoting noon.
  - **interpretation:**: It depicts a pestle. The graph was later borrowed for the Earthly Branch wǔ and extended to mean the midday period, hence "noon". Alternative proposals discuss the word’s etymon (e.g., links to "horse" or to verbs meaning "oppose"), but the graphic origin is widely taken as a tool pictogram.
  - **component characters:**
    - 杵
