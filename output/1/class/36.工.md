## work; worker
### sub-word
#### engineer
---
- **traditional:**: 工
- **simplified:**: 工
- **pronunciation:**: gōng
- **definition:**: work; worker
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 工作 (gōngzuò, "work or job")
  - 工地 (gōngdì, "construction site")
  - 工农(農) (gōngnóng, "workers and peasants")
  - 罢(罷)工 (bàgōng, "go on strike")
- **etymology (工):**
  - **type:**: pictogram
  - **description:**: pictogram: depiction of a bladed tool used to plow soil and mark borders; alternatively a carpenter's square.
  - **interpretation:**: It depicts a concrete tool associated with manual labor, which naturally extended to mean artisanship and thus ‘work’ and ‘worker’ in modern usage. An alternative sees it as a carpenter’s square, another tool of craft, leading to the same semantic domain.
  - **component characters:**
    - None, character is in atomic form
