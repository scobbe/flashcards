## knife
### sub-component
#### just; firm
---
- **traditional:**: 刀
- **simplified:**: 刀
- **pronunciation:**: dāo
- **definition:**: knife
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 可小刀 (kě xiǎo dāo, "price negotiable")
  - 刀片 (dāo piàn, "razor blade")
  - 菜刀 (cài dāo, "kitchen knife")
  - 开(開)刀 (kāi dāo, "perform surgery")
- **etymology (刀):**
  - **type:**: pictogram
  - **description:**: pictogram of 刀 (dāo, "knife").
  - **interpretation:**: Depicts a knife with blade and handle. The concrete image directly yielded the meanings ‘knife’ and ‘blade’, and by extension usage for cutting actions and as a classifier for incisions. The internet sense ‘to bargain’ reflects metaphorical ‘cutting’ of a price.

    - 刀
