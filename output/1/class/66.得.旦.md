## dawn
### sub-component
#### structural particle; must or have to
---
- **traditional:**: 旦
- **simplified:**: 旦
- **pronunciation:**: dàn
- **definition:**: dawn
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 通宵达(達)旦 (tōng xiāo dá dàn, "all night until dawn")
  - 一旦 (yī dàn, "once it happens")
  - 元旦 (yuán dàn, "New Year's Day")
  - 危在旦夕 (wēi zài dàn xī, "in imminent danger")
- **etymology (旦):**
  - **type:**: phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**: semantic: 日 (rì, "sun") + phonetic: 丁 (dīng, "nail").
  - **interpretation:**: The semantic element 日 signals a relation to daylight and time, matching meanings like dawn and day. 丁 functions as the phonetic, yielding the dàn reading. Shuowen’s depiction of the sun over the horizon (日 over 一) is noted but regarded as a later folk interpretation.
  - **component characters:**
    - 日 (rì, "sun")
    - 丁 (dīng, "nail")
