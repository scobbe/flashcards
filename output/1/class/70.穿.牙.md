## tooth
### sub-component
#### wear; put on
---
- **traditional:**: 牙
- **simplified:**: 牙
- **pronunciation:**: yá
- **definition:**: tooth
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 拔牙 (bá yá, "tooth extraction")
  - 牙齿(齒) (yá chǐ, "teeth")
  - 牙刷 (yá shuā, "toothbrush")
  - 牙雕 (yá diāo, "ivory carving")
- **etymology (牙):**
  - **type:**: pictogram
  - **description:**: pictogram: depiction of elephant tusks → used for human molars (not incisors) → general sense 'tooth; tusk'.
  - **interpretation:**: It depicts a pair of elephant tusks. As noted by Xu Shen, it originally referred specifically to human molars rather than incisors, then generalized to mean tooth in general while also retaining the tusk/ivory sense. Some scholars propose the word is a loan related to ivory or tusk, while others argue Old Chinese was the donor.
  - **component characters:**
    - None, character is in atomic form
