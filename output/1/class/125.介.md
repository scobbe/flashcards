## to introduce; between
### sub-word
#### introduce; introduction
---
- **traditional:**: 介
- **simplified:**: 介
- **pronunciation:**: jiè
- **definition:**: to introduce; between
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 介绍(紹) (jièshào, "to introduce")
  - 介于(於) (jièyú, "to lie between")
  - 介入 (jièrù, "to intervene")
  - 介意 (jièyì, "to mind")
- **etymology (介):**
  - **type:**: ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**: ideogrammic: 人 (rén, "person") + 丿 (piě, "slash") + 丨 (gǔn, "line") → 介 (jiè, "between").
  - **interpretation:**: A person is set between two bounding strokes, conveying the idea of being in-between or interposed. This naturally extends to modern senses like introducing or mediating between people. An alternative folk interpretation sees the two marks as armor plates, linking the character to ‘armor/scale’.
  - **component characters:**
    - 人
    - 丿
    - 丨
    - 介
