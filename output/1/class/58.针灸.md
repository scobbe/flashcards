## acupuncture and moxibustion
---
- **traditional:**: 針灸
- **simplified:**: 针灸
- **pronunciation:**: zhēn jiǔ
- **definition:**: acupuncture and moxibustion
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 针(針)灸治疗(療) (zhēnjiǔ zhìliáo, "acupuncture treatment")
  - 针(針)灸学(學) (zhēnjiǔ xué, "acupuncture studies")
  - 针(針)灸铜(銅)人 (zhēnjiǔ tóng rén, "bronze acupuncture model")
  - 针(針)灸麻醉 (zhēnjiǔ mázuì, "acupuncture anesthesia")
- **etymology (針灸):**
  - **type:**: semantic compound
  - **description:**: semantic: 针(針) (zhēn, "needle") + 灸 (jiǔ, "moxibustion") → "acupuncture and moxibustion".
  - **interpretation:**: The term combines 针 “needle” and 灸 “moxibustion” to name the two therapeutic techniques used in traditional Chinese medicine. It is a coordinative compound that simply enumerates the paired procedures, which explains the modern sense as the combined practice.
  - **component characters:**
    - 针
    - 針
    - 灸
  - **reference:**: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%87%9D%E7%81%B8
  - **simplification rule (针灸):**: The traditional 針 was simplified to 针 by replacing the full 金 component with the simplified metal radical 钅 and reducing strokes; 灸 was unchanged.
