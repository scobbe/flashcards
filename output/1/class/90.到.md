## to; arrive
---
- **traditional:** 到
- **simplified:** 到
- **pronunciation:** dào
- **definition:** to; arrive
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 从(從)九点(點)到十八点(點) (cóng jiǔ diǎn dào shí bā diǎn, "from 9 to 6")
  - 找到 (zhǎo dào, "to find")
  - 做到 (zuò dào, "achieve it")
  - 听(聽)到 (tīng dào, "to hear")
- **etymology (到):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 至 (zhì, "to arrive") + phonetic: 刀 (dāo, "knife").
  - **interpretation:** 至 supplies the core meaning of arriving or reaching, which matches the modern senses of motion and attainment. 刀 functions as the phonetic, giving the reading dào, while the semantic part anchors the character’s domain of meaning.
  - **component characters:**
    - 至
    - 刀
