## medical
### sub-word
#### doctor or physician
---
- **traditional:**: 醫
- **simplified:**: 医
- **pronunciation:**: yī
- **definition:**: medical
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 医(醫)院 (yī yuàn, "hospital")
  - 医(醫)疗(療) (yī liáo, "medical care")
  - 医(醫)生 (yī shēng, "doctor")
  - 医(醫)学(學) (yī xué, "medical science")
- **etymology (醫):**
  - **type:**: ideogrammic compound 1 phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**: ideogrammic: 匸 (xì, "that which stores") + 矢 (sh, "arrow") 1 phono-semantic: phonetic  (yì, "groan") + semantic  (yd, "liquor") 1 simplified: 医(醫) (yī, "medical").
  - **interpretation:**: Originally, 医 depicted a container and an arrow, referring to a quiver for storing weapons. The medical word was later formed as 醫 with 酉 as the semantic indicator of liquor/medications and 殹 as the phonetic, aligning with the medical domain. Modern 医 is the simplified form of 醫, preserving the sound yī and its medical meanings.
  - **component characters:**
    - 匸
    - 矢
    - 医
    - 醫
  - **reference:**: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%86%AB
  - **simplification rule (医):**: Component elimination and stroke reduction: 殳 and 酉 were removed from 醫, leaving the compact 医 form.
