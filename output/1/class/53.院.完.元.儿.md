### sub-component
#### origin
---
- **traditional:**: 兒
- **simplified:**: 儿
- **pronunciation:**: ér
- **definition:**: 
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 儿(兒)童 (értóng, "children")
  - 儿(兒)子 (érzi, "son")
  - 儿(兒)女 (érnǚ, "sons and daughters")
  - 儿(兒)科 (érkē, "pediatrics")
- **etymology (兒):**
  - **type:**: pictogram
  - **description:**: pictogram of 人 (rén, "person").
  - **interpretation:**: It depicts a standing person. In simplified Chinese it was also designated as the simplified form of 兒 by removing 臼, so the modern senses 'child; son' come via 兒 while the graphic still reflects the 'person' radical.
  - **component characters:**
    - 人 (rén, "person")
  - **reference:**: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%85%92
  - **simplification rule (儿):**: Component elimination: the traditional 兒 dropped the top component 臼, leaving 儿 to reduce strokes.
