## trigram lines
### sub-component
#### to study; learning
---
- **traditional:**: 爻
- **simplified:**: 爻
- **pronunciation:**: yáo
- **definition:**: trigram lines
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 六爻 (liù yáo, "six lines")
  - 卦爻 (guà yáo, "hexagram lines")
  - 阴(陰)爻 (yīn yáo, "yin line")
  - 阳(陽)爻 (yáng yáo, "yang line")
- **etymology (爻):**
  - **type:**: pictogram
  - **description:**: pictogram: 乂 (yì, "cross") + 乂 (yì, "cross") → depiction of two stacked X marks indicating crossed lines for divination.
  - **interpretation:**: Depicts two stacked X-shaped marks used to denote crossed lines in divination. This visual motif aligns with lines in the I Ching and thus came to name the lines of hexagrams. Alternatives noted include pairs of yarrow stalks or bamboo slips, or simply crossing marks.
  - **component characters:**
    - 乂
