## to need
### sub-word
#### need or require
---
- **traditional:**: 需
- **simplified:**: 需
- **pronunciation:**: xū
- **definition:**: to need
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 需要 (xū yào, "to need")
  - 需求 (xū qiú, "market demand")
  - 必需品 (bì xū pǐn, "essential goods")
  - 无(無)需 (wú xū, "not required")
- **etymology (需):**
  - **type:**: ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**: ideogrammic: 天 (tiān, "person") + 雨 (yǔ, "rain") → 雨 (yǔ, "rain") + 而 (ér, "and").
  - **interpretation:**: It depicts a person getting wet in the rain, hence an original sense of becoming wet; it later served as the graph for ‘wait’ in early usage. The form was standardized with the rain radical over 而, and the sense extended to ‘need/require’ in modern compounds where necessities or awaited items are implied.
  - **component characters:**
    - 天
    - 雨
    - 而
