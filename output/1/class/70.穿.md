## wear; put on
---
- **traditional:**: 穿
- **simplified:**: 穿
- **pronunciation:**: chuān
- **definition:**: wear; put on
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 穿衣服 (chuān yīfu, "wear clothes")
  - 穿上 (chuān shàng, "put on")
  - 穿越 (chuān yuè, "time-travel")
  - 穿墙(牆) (chuān qiáng, "pass through walls")
- **etymology (穿):**
  - **type:**: ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**: ideogrammic: 穴 (xué, "cave") + 牙 (yá, "tooth") → 穿 (chuān, "penetrate through").
  - **interpretation:**: Combines 穴 and 牙 to evoke biting or boring through a hole, yielding the core sense "to penetrate/pass through". The clothing sense extends this idea to passing the body through garments (sleeves, legs), hence "to wear" in modern usage.
  - **component characters:**
    - 穴 (xué, "cave")
    - 牙 (yá, "tooth")
