## child
### sub-component
#### to study; learning
---
- **traditional:** 子
- **simplified:** 子
- **pronunciation:** zǐ
- **definition:** child
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 子目录(錄) (zǐ mù lù, "computer subdirectory")
  - 独(獨)生子 (dú shēng zǐ, "only son")
  - 爱(愛)子 (ài zǐ, "beloved son")
  - 女子 (nǚ zǐ, "female person")
- **etymology (子):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: depiction of a baby with a large head and outstretched arms, legs wrapped in a blanket.
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a baby, hence the original sense ‘child’. From this core meaning it extended to ‘son’, the respectful title ‘Master’, and productive suffix uses for ‘person’ or diminutive, and later to other domains such as the first earthly branch.
  - **component characters:**
    - None, character is in atomic form
