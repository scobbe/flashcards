## many or much; more
---
- **traditional:**: 多
- **simplified:**: 多
- **pronunciation:**: duō
- **definition:**: many or much; more
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 多少人 (duō shǎo rén, "how many people")
  - 多久 (duō jiǔ, "how long")
  - 多大 (duō dà, "how old")
  - 多谢(謝) (duō xiè, "many thanks")
- **etymology (多):**
  - **type:**: ideogrammic compound
  - **description:**: ideogrammic: 肉 (ròu, "meat") + 肉 (ròu, "meat") → 夕 (xī, "evening") + 夕 (xī, "evening") → 多 (duō, "many").
  - **interpretation:**: Originally depicts two pieces of 肉 (ròu, "meat"), conveying abundance. In bronze script the shapes were reinterpreted as two 夕 (xī, "evening"), and this duplicative form persisted. Some analyses stress the duplication itself as marking plurality, while others note the cultural sense that two portions of meat signify “many.”
  - **component characters:**
    - 肉
    - 夕
    - 多
