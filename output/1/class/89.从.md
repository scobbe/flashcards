## from; since
---
- **traditional:** 從
- **simplified:** 从
- **pronunciation:** cóng
- **definition:** from; since
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 从(從)…到… (cóng ... dào ..., "from ... to ...")
  - 从(從)来(來) (cóng lái, "always")
  - 从(從)今以后(後) (cóng jīn yǐ hòu, "from now on")
  - 从(從)不 (cóng bù, "never")
- **etymology (從):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 人 (rén, "person") + 人 (rén, "person") → two persons moving together, "follow".
  - **interpretation:** Two instances of 人 depict people going in the same direction, yielding the core meaning "to follow". This image was extended to mark the source or starting point, giving modern prepositional uses like "from" and "since".
  - **component characters:**
    - 人
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%BE%9E
  - **simplification rule (从):** The simplified form 从 adopts the older two-person graph, replacing the multi-component 從 and dropping 彳 and 龰 to reduce strokes.
