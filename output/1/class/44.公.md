## public; fair or impartial
### sub-word
#### startup company
---
- **traditional:** 公
- **simplified:** 公
- **pronunciation:** gōng
- **definition:** public; fair or impartial
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 公平 (gōng píng, "fair and just")
  - 公开(開) (gōng kāi, "make public")
  - 公众(眾) (gōng zhòng, "the public")
  - 公园(園) (gōng yuán, "public park")
- **etymology (公):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 八 (bā, "opposite") + 厶 (sī, "private").
  - **interpretation:** Combines 八 (bā, "opposite") and 厶 (sī, "private") to express “not private,” hence “public,” which underlies modern senses like “fair, impartial.” Alternative views analyze 八 as indicating 分 (fēn, "divide") with 口 (kǒu, "object") to mean equal sharing, or see 公 as a pictogram of 瓮(甕) (wèng, "jar") later borrowed for the “public/duke” senses.
  - **component characters:**
    - 八
    - 厶
