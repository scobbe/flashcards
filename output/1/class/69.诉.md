## to sue; to appeal
### sub-word
#### tell; inform
---
- **traditional:**: 訴
- **simplified:**: 诉
- **pronunciation:**: sù
- **definition:**: to sue; to appeal
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 诉(訴)讼(訟) (sùsòng, "litigation")
  - 上诉(訴) (shàngsù, "appeal a case")
  - 投诉(訴) (tóusù, "file a complaint")
  - 倾(傾)诉(訴) (qīngsù, "pour out feelings")
- **etymology (訴):**
  - **type:**: phono-semantic compound
  - **description:**: phono-semantic: semantic: 言 (yán, "word, speak") + phonetic: 斥 (chì, "accuse, blame").
  - **interpretation:**: Combines 言 as the semantic determinative for speech with 斥 as the phonetic, pointing to verbal accusation or telling. This aligns with modern senses like suing, appealing, and complaining, all expressed through speech or formal statements.
  - **component characters:**
    - 言
    - 斥
  - **reference:**: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%A8%B4
  - **simplification rule (诉):**: Component replacement: 訁 was simplified to 讠 to reduce strokes, while the right component 斥 remained unchanged.
