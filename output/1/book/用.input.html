Pictogram (象形): a water bucket. Compare 桶. Similar but unrelated to 甬, which represents a bronze bell, and 角, which represents a horn. Unrelated to the bottom part of 備, which represents a quiver. Eventually, unrelated to 甫, in which it is the stylization of 田.

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Sino-Tibetan *z(j)um ~ *zuŋ, provisionally set up by STEDT. Cognates to 傭 (OC loŋ) and 庸 (OC loŋ), outside Sinitic, Southern Bai zv³¹ (“to use”) Burmese သုံး (sum:, “to use”), Ersu zi⁵⁵ (“to use”), Nuosu ꌬ (ssi, “to use”), Pa'o Karen [script needed] (sūŋ, “to use”).

Sun (1999) and Schuessler (2007) consider this word a cognate to Tibetan ལོངས (longs, “use”)

      Definitions

      [edit]

      用

      1. to use; to employ; to operate
        你會不會用電腦？ [MSC, trad.]
        你会不会用电脑？ [MSC, simp.]
        Nǐ huì bù huì yòng diànnǎo? [Pinyin]
        Can you use a computer?
        我用海綿來吸水。 [MSC, trad.]
        我用海绵来吸水。 [MSC, simp.]
        Wǒ yòng hǎimián lái xī shuǐ. [Pinyin]
        I used a sponge to draw the water.
      2. use; utility; usefulness
        有用  ―  yǒuyòng  ―  useful
        沒用／没用  ―  méiyòng  ―  useless
      3. (honorific) to eat; to drink
        是喝茶的時間啦，請用一杯茶吧！ [MSC, trad.]
        是喝茶的时间啦，请用一杯茶吧！ [MSC, simp.]
        Shì hē chá de shíjiān la, qǐng yòng yī bēi chá ba! [Pinyin]
        It is tea time. Please have a cup of tea!
      4. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) expenses; outlay
        費用／费用  ―  fèiyòng  ―  cost, expense
        用度  ―  yòngdù  ―  expense
        家用  ―  jiāyòng  ―  family expenses
      5. with; by; using
        用叉子吃東西／用叉子吃东西  ―  yòng chāzi chī dōngxi  ―  to eat with a fork
        我用筷子吃飯。／我用筷子吃饭。  ―  Wǒ yòng kuàizǐ chīfàn.  ―  I eat food with chopsticks.
        我用濕抹布擦桌子。 [MSC, trad.]
        我用湿抹布擦桌子。 [MSC, simp.]
        Wǒ yòng shī mǒbù cā zhuōzǐ. [Pinyin]
        I wash the table with a wet cloth.
        我老闆用姓稱呼我。 [MSC, trad.]
        我老板用姓称呼我。 [MSC, simp.]
        Wǒ lǎobǎn yòng xìng chēnghū wǒ. [Pinyin]
        My boss calls me by my last name.
      6. (chiefly used in the negative or interrogative) to need
        不用急，慢慢來。 [MSC, trad.]
        不用急，慢慢来。 [MSC, simp.]
        Bùyòng jí, mànmān lái. [Pinyin]
        No need to hurry. Please take it easy.
      7. (literary) therefore
      8. (obsolete) because of; due to; because
        • 子曰：「伯夷叔齊，不念舊惡，怨是用希。」 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
          子曰：「伯夷叔齐，不念旧恶，怨是用希。」 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
          From: The Analects of Confucius, c. 475 – 221 BCE, translated based on James Legge's version
          Zǐyuē: “Bóyí Shūqí, bùniànjiù'è, yuàn shìyòng xī.” [Pinyin]
          The Master said, "Bo Yi and Shu Qi did not keep the former wickednesses of men in mind, and hence (i.e. "because of this") the resentments directed towards them were few."
      9. a surname

      • Kan-on: よう (yō, Jōyō)
      • Kun: もちいる (mochiiru, 用いる, Jōyō)←もちゐる (motiwiru, 用ゐる, historical)

      Usage notes

      [edit]

      Formerly used as man'yōgana for the back-vowel variant (甲類) of よ, romanized as yo1.

      Pronunciation

      [edit]
      • (Tokyo) よー [yóꜜò] (Atamadaka – [1])
      • IPA(key): [jo̞ː]

      Noun

      [edit]
      Kanji in this term
      用
      よう
      Grade: 2
      kan'on

      用(よう) • (yō) 

      1. use (noun)
        事(じ)業(ぎょう)の用(よう)に供(きょう)している資(し)産(さん)
        jigyō no yō ni kyō shite iru shisan
        assets provided for business use
      2. a task, business, an errand
        今(こん)晩(ばん)用(よう)があるから出(で)かける。
        Konban yō ga aru kara dekakeru.
        I'll be out this evening because I have an errand.

      Suffix

      [edit]

      用(よう) • (-yō) 

      1. for the use of, for the purpose of, in case of
        家(か)庭(てい)用(よう)石(せっ)鹸(けん)、散(さん)歩(ぽ)用(よう)のブーツ、非常(ひじょう)用(よう)
        kateiyō sekken, sanpoyō no būtsu, hijōyō
        soap for household use, boots for [the purpose of] walking, in case of emergency

      Korean

      [edit]

      Etymology

      [edit]

      From Middle Chinese 用 (MC yowngH). Recorded as Middle Korean 용 (ywong) (Yale: yong) in Sinjeung Yuhap (新增類合 / 신증유합), 1576.

      Pronunciation

      [edit]
      • (SK Standard/Seoul) IPA(key): [jo(ː)ŋ]
      • Phonetic hangul: [용(ː)]
        • Though still prescribed in Standard Korean, most speakers in both Koreas no longer distinguish vowel length.
