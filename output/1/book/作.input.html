Etymology

[edit]

Derivative: 做 (“to do”) (the departing-tone variant).

Definitions

[edit]

作

  1. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to get up (from a place)
  2. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to get up (in the morning) and go to work
  3. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to begin to grow; to come into being
  4. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to arise; to come about; to happen; to occur; to break out
  5. to do; to perform; to carry out
    你在作什麼？／你在作什么？  ―  Nǐ zài zuò shénme?  ―  What are you doing?
  6. to work; to exert oneself
  7. to make; to manufacture
  8. to compose; to write
    作者  ―  zuòzhě  ―  author
    你作緊乜嘢呀？ [Guangzhou Cantonese, trad.]
    你作紧乜嘢呀？ [Guangzhou Cantonese, simp.]
    nei5 zok3 gan2 mat1 je5 aa3? [Jyutping]
    What are you writing?
  9. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to brace oneself; to boost
  10. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to feign; to pretend
  11. to work as; to serve as
  12. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to build; to construct
    • 作百佛寺，不如活一人。 [Classical Chinese, trad. and simp.]
      From: 《佛說罵意經》 (“The Sūtra Wherein the Buddha Speaks of Thoughts about Scolding”); 2nd century CE translation by An Shigao
      Zuò bǎi fósì, bùrú huó yīrén. [Pinyin]
      Building one hundred Buddhist temples cannot compare to saving just one human life.
  13. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to play; to perform
  14. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to emit; to give out
  15. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to start; to launch
  16. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) to be like; similar
  17. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) alternative form of 詛 / 诅 (zǔ, “to curse; to damn”)
  18. work; composition; article
  19. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) affair; matter
  20. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) measure; action
  21. (Hokkien) to farm; to plow; to till

Noun
[edit]

作(さく) • (saku) 

  1. a crop, a harvest

Etymology 2

[edit]
Kanji in this term
作
つく(り)
Grade: 2
kun'yomi

The 連(れん)用(よう)形(けい) (ren'yōkei, “stem or continuative form”) of the verb 作(つく)る (tsukuru, “to make; to build; to work the land, to grow something”), itself from Old Japanese.[2]

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • (Tokyo) つくり [tsùkúríꜜ] (Odaka – [3])[1]
  • IPA(key): [t͡sɨkɯ̟ɾʲi]

Noun

[edit]

作(つくり) • (tsukuri) 

  1. the workmanship or make of something, how something is made
    作(つく)りのいい家具(かぐ)
    tsukuri no ii kagu
    furniture of good make or workmanship, well-made furniture
  2. make-up, dress, comportment: how one dresses and comports oneself
    派手(はで)な作(つく)りは嫌(きら)いだ。
    Hade na tsukuri wa kirai da.
    I dislike colorful / flashy dress.
  3. a helping or serving of fresh sashimi, usually several slices
    鮭(しゃけ)の作(つく)りを楽(たの)しみにしている。
    Shake no tsukuri o tanoshimi ni shite iru.
    I'm looking forward to a helping of salmon sashimi.
Usage notes
[edit]

More often spelled with the okurigana, as 作(つく)り (tsukuri).

Etymology 3

[edit]
Kanji in this term
作
つく(り) > づく(り)
Grade: 2
kun'yomi
Alternative spelling
作り

From 作(つくり) (tsukuri) above. The -tsukuri changes to -zukuri as an instance of rendaku (連(れん)濁(だく)).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): [d͡zɨkɯ̟ɾʲi]

Suffix

[edit]

作(づくり) • (-zukuri) 

  1. the making of something, particularly as in cultivation
    庭(にわ)作(づく)り、バラ作(づく)り
    niwazukuri, barazukuri
    gardening / garden cultivation, rose cultivation / growing roses
Usage notes
[edit]

More often spelled with the okurigana, as 作(づく)り (-zukuri).
