Definitions

[edit]

兵

  1. weapon
    • 是何異於刺人而殺之，曰：「非我也，兵也。」 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      是何异于刺人而杀之，曰：「非我也，兵也。」 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: Mencius, c. 4th century BCE, translated based on James Legge's version
      Shì hé yìyú cì rén ér shā zhī, yuē: “Fēi wǒ yě, bīng yě.” [Pinyin]
      In what does this differ from stabbing a man and killing him, and then saying - "It was not I; it was the weapon?"
  2. army; troops
    • 幽王舉烽火徵兵，兵莫至。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      幽王举烽火征兵，兵莫至。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: The Records of the Grand Historian, by Sima Qian, c. 91 BCE
      Yōu Wáng jǔ fēnghuǒ zhēng bīng, bīng mò zhì. [Pinyin]
      King You lit the signal beacons to summon the troops, but none arrived.
    • 欲強兵者，務富其民。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      欲强兵者，务富其民。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: Zhanguo Ce, circa 5th – 3rd centuries BCE
      Yù qiáng bīng zhě, wù fù qí mín. [Pinyin]
      To empower the armies, one must first enrich the civilians.
  3. soldier; warrior (Classifier: 個／个)
    士兵  ―  shìbīng  ―  soldier
  4. warfare
    • 兵者，國之大事。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      兵者，国之大事。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: The Art of War, circa 5th century BCE, translated based on Lionel Giles's version
      Bīng zhě, guó zhī dàshì. [Pinyin]
      Warfare is of vital importance to the State.
  5. (xiangqi) pawn; private; soldier: 🩦 (on the red side)
  6. (chess) pawn
  7. (Cantonese) short for 觀音兵 / 观音兵 (guānyīnbīng)

kan'on

From Middle Chinese 兵 (MC pjaeng). The kan'on, so a later borrowing.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • (Tokyo) へー [héꜜè] (Atamadaka – [1])[1]
  • IPA(key): [he̞ː]

Noun

[edit]

兵(へい) • (hei) 

  1. a soldier
  2. a war machine; a weapon
  3. a battle, a war
  4. in the old Japanese military structure, the lowest set of ranks for a soldier: a private, corporal, lance corporal
