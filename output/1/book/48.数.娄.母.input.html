Differentiated form of 女 with the addition of distinguishing dots. In the early oracle bone texts both words 女 (OC *naʔ, *nas, “woman”) and 母 were written as 女 (Yao, 1989, Liu, 2011, Huang, 2014). Part of this character appears in 乳.

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Sino-Tibetan *məʔ (“mother”).

Definitions

[edit]

母

  1. mother
    Antonym: 父
    父母  ―  fùmǔ  ―  father and mother
    母子  ―  mǔzǐ  ―  mother and son
  2. Used to address a female elder member of a family.
    Antonym: 父
    姑母  ―  gūmǔ  ―  father's sister
    祖母  ―  zǔmǔ  ―  grandmother
  3. (of animal) female
    Synonym: 雌 (cí)
    Antonym: 公 (gōng)
    母牛  ―  mǔniú  ―  female cow
  4. (figuratively, of instruments, tools, or connectors) female
  5. pertaining to origin
    母校  ―  mǔxiào  ―  alma mater
  6. a surname
