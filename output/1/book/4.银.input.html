Phono-semantic compound (形聲 / 形声, OC *ŋrɯn): semantic 金 (“metal”) + phonetic 艮 (OC *kɯːns).

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Sino-Tibetan *d-ŋurl. Cognate with Tibetan དངུལ (dngul), Burmese ငွေ (ngwe).

Definitions

[edit]

銀

  1. silver
    白銀／白银  ―  báiyín  ―  silver
  2. silvern; made of silver
  3. silvery; silver-colored; lustrous; white; shiny
  4. cash; money; currency
  5. (Cantonese) coin
    銀仔／银仔 [Cantonese]  ―  ngan4-2 zai2 [Jyutping]  ―  coins
    兩蚊銀／两蚊银 [Cantonese]  ―  loeng5 man1 ngan4-2 [Jyutping]  ―  two-dollar coin
  6. (Cantonese, Teochew, Hainanese, Zhao'an Hakka) Classifier for money: yuan; dollar; buck
    幾千銀／几千银 [Cantonese]  ―  gei2 cin1 ngan4 [Jyutping]  ―  a few thousand bucks
    • 1969, 潘藩, directed by 陸邦, 相思甜如蜜 [My Sweetheart]‎[1], spoken by 陳維才 (高魯泉 [Ko Lo Chuen]):
      我呢兩個乾隆花樽啊值成萬銀㗎。 [Cantonese, trad.]
      我呢两个乾隆花樽啊值成万银㗎。 [Cantonese, simp.]
      ngo5 ni1 loeng5 go3 kin4 lung4-2 faa1 zeon1 aa3 zik6 sing4 maan6 ngan4 gaa3. [Jyutping]
      These two Qianlong-era vases of mine are worth ten grand.
    • 蔡俊明 [Choy Chun-ming], editor (1991), “銀 / 银 (yín) ngeng˥”, in 潮州方言詞滙 / 潮州方言词滙 (Cháozhōu fāngyán cíhuì), 香港 [Hong Kong]: 香港中文大學 / 香港中文大学 [The Chinese University of Hong Kong]
      一件五個銀，三件十五銀 [Teochew, trad.]
      一件五个银，三件十五银 [Teochew, simp.]
      zêg8 gian6 ngou6 gai5 ngêng5, san1 gian6 zab8 ngou6 ngêng5 [Peng'im]
      five bucks for one, fifteen bucks for three
  7. a surname
