Ideogrammic compound (會意 / 会意): 水 (“water”) + 少 (“to lack; to be short of”).

Etymology 1

[edit]

From Proto-Sino-Tibetan *s(r)aj (“earth, sand”). Compare Tibetan ས (sa, “sand”) and Burmese သဲ (sai:, “sand”).

Definitions

[edit]

沙

  1. sand (Classifier: 粒 c mn)
    泥沙  ―  níshā  ―  silt
    沙漠  ―  shāmò  ―  desert
  2. (usually used in place names) shoal
  3. something granular
    豆沙  ―  dòushā  ―  bean paste, especially red bean paste
  4. mushy and rough (as sand), texture of a watermelon of good quality.
    沙瓤  ―  shāráng  ―  mushy watermelon pulp, Refers to the watermelon when the flesh becomes loose and fine-grained
    沙梨  ―  shālí  ―  Pyrus pyrifolia
    西沙瓜  ―  xīshāguā  ―  alternative of 硒砂瓜 (xī shā guā)
  5. (of a voice) hoarse; husky
    沙啞／沙哑  ―  shāyǎ  ―  hoarse
    叫到把聲沙嗮 [Cantonese, trad.]
    叫到把声沙嗮 [Cantonese, simp.]
    giu3 dou3 baa2 seng1 saa1 saai3 [Jyutping]
    shout so much that one's voice is all hoarse
  6. (obsolete) the number 10−8
  7. short for 沙皇 (shāhuáng)
    沙俄  ―  Shā'é  ―  Russian Empire
  8. (Malaysia, Singapore) short for 沙巴 (Shābā, “Sabah”)
  9. (Mainland China) short for 沙特阿拉伯 (Shātè Ālābó, “Saudi Arabia”)
  10. (Hong Kong) short for 沙田 (shātián, “Sha Tin”)
  11. a surname
  12. (Hong Kong) short for 長沙灣／长沙湾 (Chángshāwān, “Cheung Sha Wan”)
