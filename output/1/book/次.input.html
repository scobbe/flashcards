Pictogram (象形): a person exhaling.[字源 1] Possibly the original form of 咨 (OC *ʔsli, “to sigh”).

Shuowen interprets it as a phono-semantic compound of 二 (OC *njis) + 欠. However, the ancient forms of the character do not support this.[字源 2]

The breath or saliva is is represented by 二 or 冫, depending on the font. An old variant of the character is 𣄭.

See 曰, 丂, 于, 只, which also feature depictions of breathing. Unrelated to 㳄, which means "saliva" instead.

Etymology 1

[edit]

Probably from *s- + 二 (OC *njis, “two”), from Proto-Sino-Tibetan *kV-ni-s (Pulleyblank, 1962; Sagart and Baxter, 2012; STEDT).

Definitions

[edit]

次

  1. order; sequence
    名次  ―  míngcì  ―  position in a name list; place in a competition
    • 及鄢，亂次以濟，遂無次，且不設備。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      及鄢，乱次以济，遂无次，且不设备。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: Commentary of Zuo, c. 4th century BCE
      Jí Yān, luàn cì yǐ jì, suì wú cì, qiě bù shèbèi. [Pinyin]
      (please add an English translation of this usage example)
  2. (algebra) order
  3. next in sequence; second
    次子  ―  cìzǐ  ―  second son
  4. (literary) to come next in order
    • 民為貴，社稷次之，君為輕。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      民为贵，社稷次之，君为轻。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: Mencius, c. 4th century BCE
      Mín wéi guì, shèjì cì zhī, jūn wéi qīng. [Pinyin]
      The people are the most important element in a nation; the spirits of the land and grain are the next; the sovereign is the lightest.
  5. (literary) to arrange into ordered form; to put into sequence; to impose order; to compile
    • 蕭何次律令，韓信申軍法。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      萧何次律令，韩信申军法。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: The Records of the Grand Historian, by Sima Qian, c. 91 BCE
      Xiāo Hé cì lǜlìng, Hán Xìn shēn jūnfǎ. [Pinyin]
      Xiao He compiled laws and regulations, and Han Xin re-established military disciplines.
  6. inferior; substandard
    次貨／次货  ―  cìhuò  ―  substandard product
  7. Classifier for times, as in frequency. ⇒ all nouns using this classifier
    上次  ―  shàngcì  ―  last time
    次數／次数  ―  cìshù  ―  number of times; frequency
    這是我第一次來到北京。 [MSC, trad.]
    这是我第一次来到北京。 [MSC, simp.]
    Zhè shì wǒ dì yī cì lái dào běijīng. [Pinyin]
    This is my first time in Beijing.
  8. (archaic, military) to station the troops at some place; to hold position at some place
    • 春王正月，師次于郎，以俟陳人、蔡人。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      春王正月，师次于郎，以俟陈人、蔡人。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: Commentary of Zuo, c. 4th century BCE
      Chūn wáng zhēngyuè, shī cì yú Láng, yǐ sì Chénrén, Càirén. [Pinyin]
      Spring, the first month (of Zhou calendar), the armies of Lu were stationed at Lang to await those of Chen and Cai.
    • 王出三江按五湖，樓船跨海次揚都。 [Classical Chinese, trad.]
      王出三江按五湖，楼船跨海次扬都。 [Classical Chinese, simp.]
      From: c. 757, Li Bai, 永王東巡歌·其七
      Wáng chū Sānjiāng àn Wǔhú, lóuchuán kuàhǎi cì Yángdū. [Pinyin]
      (please add an English translation of this usage example)
  9. (literary) resting place
    客次  ―  kècì  ―  (please add an English translation of this usage example)
    • 六二：旅即次，懷其資，得童僕貞。 [Pre-Classical Chinese, trad.]
      六二：旅即次，怀其资，得童仆贞。 [Pre-Classical Chinese, simp.]
      From: I Ching, 11th – 8th century BCE
      Liù èr: lǚ jí cì, huái qí zī, dé tóngpú zhēn. [Pinyin]
      The second SIX, divided, shows the stranger, occupying his lodging-house, carrying with him his means of livelihood, and provided with good and trusty servants.
  10. (literary) middle
    • 喜怒哀樂不入於胸次。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      喜怒哀乐不入于胸次。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: Zhuangzi, circa 3rd – 2nd centuries BCE
      Xǐnù'āilè bù rù yú xiōngcì. [Pinyin]
      [J]oy, anger, sadness, and delight do not enter into their breasts (in connexion with such events).
