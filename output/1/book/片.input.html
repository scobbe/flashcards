Glyph origin

[edit]
Historical forms of the character 片
Shuowen Jiezi (compiled in Han) Liushutong (compiled in Ming)
Small seal script Transcribed ancient scripts
Characters in the same phonetic series (片) (Zhengzhang, 2003) 
Old Chinese
爿 *braːn, *zaŋ
片 *pʰeːns
沜 *pʰaːns

Mirror image of 爿 (OC *braːn, “piece”), which is the pictogram of a bed.

Shuowen: half of 木 (“wood; tree”) to denote “to pare; to slice”; but this interpretation is not based on oracle bones and bronzes.

Definitions

[edit]

片

  1. thin piece; flake; slice
    片子  ―  piànzi  ―  flake, small piece
  2. film; TV play; photograph; record
    影片  ―  yǐngpiàn  ―  film, movie
    片子  ―  piānzi  ―  film, movie, film reel
    1. (specifically) film (form of mass media); movie (Classifier: 部 m; 套 c)
      賀歲片／贺岁片 [Cantonese]  ―  ho6 seoi3 pin3-2 [Jyutping]  ―  Chinese New Year film
      動作片／动作片  ―  dòngzuò piān  ―  action movie
      片商 [Cantonese]  ―  pin3-2 soeng1 [Jyutping]  ―  (please add an English translation of this usage example)
    2. (by extension, Cantonese) video (Classifier: 條／条 c)
      去片 [Cantonese]  ―  heoi3 pin3-2 [Jyutping]  ―  to play a video
  3. (medicine) tablet
  4. (dialectal) diaper; nappy (Classifier: 條／条 c)
    幫BB換片／帮BB换片 [Cantonese]  ―  bong1 bi4 bi1 wun6 pin3-2 [Jyutping]  ―  to change diapers for the baby
    纜片／缆片 [Cantonese]  ―  laam6 pin3-2 [Jyutping]  ―  to wear a diaper
  5. part of a place; portion on the earth; area
    • 2000, 梁左 and 梁欢, 闲人马大姐, episode 100, 金雅琴 (actor):
      就是嘛！咱们这片儿啊苍蝇太多。 [MSC, simp.]
      就是嘛！咱們這片兒啊蒼蠅太多。 [MSC, trad.]
      Jiù shi ma! Zánmen zhèi piànr a cāngying tài duō. [Pinyin]
      Right indeed! This region of ours has too many flies.
  6. (linguistics) subgroup; subdivision of a language
    廣府片／广府片 [Cantonese]  ―  gwong2 fu2 pin3 [Jyutping]  ―  Guangfu subgroup (of Yue Chinese)
  7. to slice; to carve thin
  8. (Cantonese) to fight
  9. (Cantonese) to throw something along its thin side
    片石仔 [Cantonese]  ―  pin3-2 sek6 zai2 [Jyutping]  ―  stone skipping
  10. (Cantonese, of a vehicle) to squeeze into a gap
  11. (Cantonese) bribe
    收片 [Cantonese]  ―  sau1 pin3-2 [Jyutping]  ―  to collect bribes
  12. partial; incomplete; one-sided
    片面  ―  piànmiàn  ―  unilateral, one-sided
  13. Classifier for flat objects such as leaves, pieces of paper, slices, etc.
    一片麵包／一片面包  ―  yī piàn miànbāo  ―  a slice of bread
  14. Classifier for small nonspherical medicinal objects such as pills, tablets.
  15. Classifier for tracts of land and areas of water.
    • 1984, “似水流年 [sìshuǐliúnián]”, performed by 梅艷芳:
      望著海一片　滿懷倦　無淚也無言 [Literary Cantonese, trad.]
      望着海一片　满怀倦　无泪也无言 [Literary Cantonese, simp.]
      mong6 zoek6 hoi2 jat1 pin3, mun5 waai4 gyun6, mou4 leoi6 jaa5 mou4 jin4 [Jyutping]
      Gazing at the sea, tired, without tears or words
  16. Classifier for CDs, movies and DVDs.
  17. (used with numeral 一) Classifier for scenarios, scenes, feelings, atmospheres, sounds, clouds.
    • 1983, “偏偏喜歡你”, in 偏偏喜歡你, performed by 陳百強:
      為何我心一片空虛 [Literary Cantonese, trad.]
      为何我心一片空虚 [Literary Cantonese, simp.]
      wai6 ho4 ngo5 sam1 jat1 pin3 hung1 heoi1 [Jyutping]
      Why is my heart a tract of emptiness?
  18. (Teochew) Classifier for doors and windows.
  19. (Hakka, Teochew) side
    東片／东片 [Teochew]  ―  dang1 pin3 [Peng'im]  ―  east; east side
    許片／许片 [Teochew]  ―  he2 pin3 [Peng'im]  ―  there; that side

Etymology 1

[edit]
Kanji in this term
片
かた
Grade: 6
kun'yomi

From Old Japanese, from Proto-Japonic *kata-. First cited in the Nihon Shoki of 720.[2]

Japanese sources describe this as cognate with 方(かた) (kata, “side, direction”).[3] However, the pitch accents differ: compounds starting with 片 show <LL-> accent in the Heian period, in contrast to 方, which is recorded with a <HL> accent instead, suggesting no direct relationship between the two.

Alternatively, 肩(かた) (kata, “shoulder”) might be a more likely cognate given that term's matching <LH> accent and compounds also indicating homonymy: compare 傾(かた)げる (katageru, “to tilt, to incline”), 担(かた)げる (katageru, “to carry on one’s shoulder”). Also, accent comparison might support that this word derives from the verb root 勝(か)つ (katsu, “to win; to tend to”) or 搗(か)つ (katsu, “to pound”) (both have an <L-> accent), and adverb 且(か)つ (katsu, “besides; moreover; also; and”) which has an <LF> accent.

Prefix

[edit]

片(かた) • (kata-) 

  1. [from 720] (prefixed to a noun) one side or part of a pair or larger set
    片(かた)手(て)、片(かた)身(み)
    katate, katami
    one hand, one half of the body
  2. [from 720] (prefixed to a noun or verb) one-sided
    片(かた)思(おも)い、傾(かたむ)ける
    kataomoi, katamukeru
    one-sided thoughts / unrequited affection (as between two people), to lean to one side
  3. [from 720] (prefixed to a noun or verb) incomplete, partial, small
    片(かた)山(やま)、片(かた)時(とき)、片(かた)笑(え)む
    katayama, katatoki, kataemu
    a small mountain, a short time, to smile slightly
  4. [from 720] (prefixed to a noun) leaning or trending that way (emphasizing the quality of the prefixed noun)
    片(かた)田舎(いなか)
    katainaka
    the remote countryside
  5. [from 720] (prefixed to a verb, archaic) frequently, intently, earnestly
    片(かた)設(ま)く
    katamaku
    to focus intently on doing one thing
