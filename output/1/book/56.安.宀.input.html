Glyph origin

[edit]
Historical forms of the character 宀
Shang Western Zhou Shuowen Jiezi (compiled in Han) Liushutong (compiled in Ming)
Oracle bone script Bronze inscriptions Small seal script Transcribed ancient scripts

Pictogram (象形) – a house; similar to 广. According to Wang Yun (王筠), the latter character depicts a building with three walls, while the former depicts a house with four walls.

Today, the pictogram appears smaller, but a conservative character is 𰃦, which appears in 向. See also the top component of 冡 and the original version of 叟.

Definitions

[edit]

宀

  1. a type of deep house with walls on all four sides, a roof, a main hall, and back rooms
  2. a kind of flower
  3. Kangxi radical #40 宀 (“roof”)

Usage notes

[edit]

As a component, often referred to as 寶蓋／宝盖 (bǎogài) or 寶蓋頭／宝盖头 (bǎogàitóu).
