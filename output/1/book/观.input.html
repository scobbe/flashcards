Phono-semantic compound (形聲 / 形声, OC *koːn, *koːns): phonetic 雚 (OC *koːns) + semantic 見 (“see”).

Etymology

[edit]

“To watch; to observe” (Pronunciation 1) > “watchtower; platform; temple” (Pronunciation 2).

Schuessler (2007) connects it with 侯 (OC *ɡoː, “target”) and 候 (OC *ɡoːs, “to watch; to be on the lookout for; to wait”).

Definitions

[edit]

觀

  1. to observe; to watch; to view; to see
    • 予若觀火，予亦拙謀作乃逸。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      予若观火，予亦拙谋作乃逸。 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: The Book of Documents, circa 7th – 4th centuries BCE
      Yǔ ruò guān huǒ, yǔ yì zhuōmóu zuò nǎi yì. [Pinyin]
      I [see you as clearly] as one sees a fire; but I, likewise, by my undecided plans, have produced your error.
  2. to observe closely; to scrutinise; to investigate
    • 子曰：「父在，觀其志；父沒，觀其行；三年無改於父之道，可謂孝矣。」 [Traditional Chinese poetry, trad.]
      子曰：「父在，观其志；父没，观其行；三年无改于父之道，可谓孝矣。」 [Traditional Chinese poetry, simp.]
      From: The Analects of Confucius, c. 475 – 221 BCE
      Zǐ yuē: “Fù zài, guān qí zhì; fù mò, guān qí xíng; sān nián wú gǎi yú fù zhī dào, kěwèi xiào yǐ.” [Pinyin]
      The Master said, "While a man's father is alive, look at the bent of his will; when his father is dead, look at his conduct. If for three years he does not alter from the way of his father, he may be called filial."
  3. to read; to peruse
  4. to display; to show
  5. to visit; to go sightseeing
  6. to play; to enjoy
  7. appearance; looks
  8. sight; view; perspective
  9. concept; outlook
  10. a surname

Definitions

[edit]

觀

  1. (historical) watchtower on either side of a palace gate
  2. (historical) platform; stage; dais; rostrum
  3. (Taoism) Taoist temple
  4. 20th hexagram of the I Ching
  5. a surname

Korean

Etymology

[edit]
“to see; etc.”
From Middle Chinese 觀 (MC kwan). Recorded as Middle Korean 관 (kwan)訓 (Yale: kwan) in Hunmong Jahoe (訓蒙字會 / 훈몽자회), 1527.
“Taoist temple”
From Middle Chinese 觀 (MC kwanH). Recorded as Middle Korean 관〮 (kwán)訓 (Yale: kwán) in Hunmong Jahoe (訓蒙字會 / 훈몽자회), 1527.
