## Kakistocracy
---
- **definition:**
  - government by the worst, least qualified, or most unscrupulous people
  - a state or society run by such people
- **etymology:**
  - from Greek kakistos “worst” (superlative of kakos “bad”) + -kratia “rule, power”; rendered in English as -cracy
  - coined in English in the early 1800s by combining Greek roots, modeled on formations like aristocracy and bureaucracy
  - literally means “rule of the worst”
- **history:**
  - first recorded in English in 1829, during an era of intense debate over corruption and political reform
  - popularized by American writer James Russell Lowell in an 1876 letter criticizing misgovernment during Reconstruction
  - saw recurrent spikes in use in the 20th and 21st centuries, especially in political commentary and satire during periods of scandal
- **pronunciation:**
  - kak-iss-TAH-kruh-see
