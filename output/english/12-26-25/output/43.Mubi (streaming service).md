## Mubi (streaming service)
---
- **definition:**
  - a curated film-streaming service focusing on arthouse, independent, and international cinema, available by subscription worldwide
  - the company that operates the service, also involved in theatrical distribution, digital rentals, and select film production
- **etymology:**
  - a coined brand name with no established linguistic roots; the service launched as “The Auteurs” (2007) and rebranded “MUBI” in 2010 to create a short, globally pronounceable name
  - stylized as MUBI; as an invented proper noun it is not derived from earlier words or morphemes and has no literal root meaning
- **history:**
  - founded in 2007 by Efe Çakarel as The Auteurs amid the early rise of online streaming (Netflix began streaming that year), aiming to serve cinephiles through curation
  - the name MUBI first appeared in English in 2010 with a rebrand; that year it partnered with Sony to launch on PlayStation 3 in Europe and popularized a “one new film a day” rotating slate
  - in the 2010s–2020s it expanded globally, added rentals and theatrical releases, and in 2022 acquired The Match Factory, signaling a deeper role in film distribution and production
- **pronunciation:**
  - MOO-bee
