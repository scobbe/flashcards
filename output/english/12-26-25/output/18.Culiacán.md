## Culiacán
---
- **definition:**
  - capital city of the Mexican state of Sinaloa in northwest Mexico
  - also the name of the surrounding municipality that includes the city and nearby communities
- **etymology:**
  - from Spanish adaptation of Nahuatl Culhuacán (also spelled Colhuacan), literally "place of the Culhua/ancient lineage"
  - built from Nahuatl colhua "ancient, of noble lineage" + -can "place"; the sequence -lhua- was hispanicized to -lia- in the modern form
- **history:**
  - first documented by Spaniards in 1531, when Villa de San Miguel de Culiacán was founded during early colonial campaigns in northwest New Spain
  - later in the 19th century it appended "Rosales" to honor General Antonio Rosales, a local hero of the 1860s French Intervention
  - in the 20th century large irrigation projects made it an agricultural hub; in the 21st century it drew global attention in news about organized crime, notably the 2019 "Culiacanazo"
- **pronunciation:**
  - koo-lee-ah-KAHN
