## Culiacán
---
- **definition:**
  - a city in northwestern Mexico; the capital and largest city of the state of Sinaloa
  - a regional hub for agriculture and commerce, located where the Humaya and Tamazula rivers meet
- **origin:**
  - from Nahuatl colhuacan (colhua 'ancient, bent' + -cān 'place of'), meaning 'place of the culhua/ancients'; the -cán ending is common in place names
  - the settlement was founded in 1531 by the Spanish conqueror Nuño de Guzmán as Villa de San Miguel de Culiacán, spreading a Nahuatlized name via central Mexican allies
  - some scholars suggest a Cahitan (Uto‑Aztecan) root for the name, but the Nahuatl etymology is most widely cited
- **pronunciation:**
  - koo-lee-ah-KAHN
