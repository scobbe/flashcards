## Yi jing
---
- **definition:** Yi jing
  - an ancient Chinese classic, also called the I Ching or Book of Changes, made up of 64 hexagrams with accompanying texts
  - a traditional system of divination and reflection that interprets hexagrams to offer guidance
- **origin:**
  - from Mandarin Yì Jīng (易经): yì = change, jīng = classic/scripture
  - compiled in early Zhou-era China (c. 1000–500 BCE) and expanded by later commentaries influential in Confucianism and Daoism
  - entered English via the older Wade–Giles spelling I Ching; widely known through 19th–20th century translations (e.g., James Legge, Richard Wilhelm)
- **pronunciation:**
  - YEE-jing
