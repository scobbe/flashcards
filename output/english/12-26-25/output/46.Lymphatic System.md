## Lymphatic System
---
- **definition:** Lymphatic System
  - the body's network of vessels, nodes, and organs that returns excess fluid to the blood and keeps tissues balanced
  - a major part of immunity that filters lymph and houses white blood cells to fight infection
  - a pathway that absorbs fats from the small intestine and carries them to the bloodstream
- **origin:**
  - from lymphatic, built on Latin lympha “clear water; spring” (influenced by nympha “water nymph”), later applied to the body’s clear fluid
  - from system, via Latin from Greek systēma “an organized whole; arrangement”
  - the term gained modern use after 17th‑century anatomists (e.g., Thomas Bartholin, Olaus Rudbeck) mapped the body’s lymph vessels
- **pronunciation:**
  - lim-FAT-ik SIS-tem
