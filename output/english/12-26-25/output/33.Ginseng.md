## Ginseng
---
- **definition:**
  - any of several Panax plants with fleshy, forked roots, especially Asian ginseng (Panax ginseng) and American ginseng (Panax quinquefolius)
  - the dried root of these plants, used as a tonic or herbal remedy
- **etymology:**
  - from Mandarin rénshēn (人参/人蔘) meaning "person root," referring to the human-like shape of the root
  - entered English via southern Chinese pronunciations used by traders (e.g., Cantonese/Amoy), then adapted in 17th‑century European spellings
  - the scientific genus name Panax comes from Greek pan "all" + akos "cure," meaning "all-heal"
- **history:**
  - valued in East Asian medicine since antiquity; noted in early Chinese herbals such as the Shennong Bencao Jing (1st–2nd century CE), and highly prized in China and Korea
  - appeared in English in the mid‑1600s as European traders and Jesuit writers described it to Western audiences
  - american ginseng was identified in 1716 by Jesuit Joseph‑François Lafitau in Canada, sparking a major export trade to China; overharvesting later led to protections, including CITES Appendix II listing in 1975
- **pronunciation:**
  - JIN-seng
