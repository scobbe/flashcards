## Ginseng
---
- **definition:** Ginseng
  - a slow-growing medicinal root, especially from Panax species, used as a tonic to boost energy and reduce fatigue
  - the plant that produces this root, native to East Asia and North America
- **origin:**
  - from Chinese rénshēn, meaning “person-root” (rén ‘person’ + shēn ‘ginseng’), inspired by the root’s human-like shape; entered English via Cantonese/Hokkien through Dutch or French in the 1600s
  - the genus name Panax comes from Greek panakos/panacea, meaning “all-heal,” reflecting its reputation as a cure-all
  - historically prized in East Asian medicine; American ginseng became a major export to China in the 18th century
- **pronunciation:**
  - JIN-seng
