## Roman Curia
---
- **definition:** Roman Curia
  - the central group of departments and offices that help the Pope run the Catholic Church and manage the Holy See
  - historically, the Pope’s court and its legal tribunals handling church governance and disputes
- **origin:**
  - from Latin Curia Romana, meaning “Roman court/council”: curia = court or council; romanus = of Rome
  - in ancient Rome, a curia was a senate house or assembly; the Church adopted the term for the papal court as it organized its administration
  - its structure was formalized by Pope Sixtus V (1588) and later reformed by Paul VI (1967), John Paul II (1988), and Francis (2022)
- **pronunciation:**
  - ROH-mun KYUR-ee-uh
