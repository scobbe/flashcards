## Alpha
---
- **definition:**
  - the first letter of the Greek alphabet; also used to mean the beginning or first in a series
  - a person or thing that is dominant or highest-ranking; the leader of a group
  - an early test release of a product, especially software, not yet ready for general use
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin alpha, borrowed from Greek alpha (Α, α); Greek took the name from Phoenician ʾāleph
  - ultimately from a Semitic root meaning “ox,” with the letter originally depicting an ox head
  - greek retained the name but repurposed the sign to represent the vowel “a,” a key step in forming a true alphabet
- **history:**
  - attested in English by the late 14th century, often in the biblical phrase “Alpha and Omega” (Revelation), meaning “the beginning and the end”
  - mid-20th century ethologists used “alpha male/female” for dominant animals (notably Schenkel, 1947), and the term later spread to human social description
  - by the 1970s–80s, software developers adopted “alpha” to label early internal test versions preceding “beta,” reflecting evolving release practices
- **pronunciation:**
  - AL-fuh
