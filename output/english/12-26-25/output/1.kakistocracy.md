## Kakistocracy
---
- **definition:**
  - a government run by the worst, least qualified, or most unprincipled people
  - a state or society under the control of such leaders
- **etymology:**
  - from ancient Greek kakistos meaning worst (superlative of kakos meaning bad) + kratia meaning rule or power, literally rule by the worst
  - formed in English with the Greek-derived suffix -cracy (via French -cratie and Latin -cratia), modeled on terms like aristocracy and bureaucracy
- **history:**
  - first recorded in English in 1829 in political writing during debates over corruption and democratic reform
  - gained wider notice when James Russell Lowell used it in an 1876 letter criticizing Gilded Age politics
  - usage has spiked during periods of scandal or crisis, notably in the late 20th century and again in the 2010s
- **pronunciation:**
  - kah-kis-TAH-kruh-see
