## Holy See
---
- **definition:**
  - the central governing authority of the Roman Catholic Church, led by the pope
  - the sovereign legal entity that maintains diplomatic relations worldwide; distinct from the territorial Vatican City State
- **origin:**
  - from ecclesiastical Latin Sancta Sedes, meaning "holy seat"; see comes from Latin sedes "seat, chair"
  - in early Christianity a "see" was a bishop’s seat or city; the Roman bishop’s see became preeminent, hence "the Holy See"
  - over time the term came to denote the papal office and its legal‑diplomatic persona recognized by states
- **pronunciation:**
  - HOH-lee see
