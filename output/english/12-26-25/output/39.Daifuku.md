## Daifuku
---
- **definition:** Daifuku
  - a Japanese sweet made of soft mochi (pounded sticky rice) wrapped around a filling, often sweet red bean paste
  - also used for modern versions with fillings like strawberries, custard, or ice cream
- **origin:**
  - from Japanese daifuku mochi, literally “great luck rice cake” (大福餅): dai “big/great,” fuku “good fortune,” mochi “rice cake”
  - originally called “big-bellied rice cake” (大腹餅); in the Edo period the name shifted to the pun meaning “good fortune”
  - traditionally given at celebrations and New Year as a good-luck treat
- **pronunciation:**
  - dye-FOO-koo
