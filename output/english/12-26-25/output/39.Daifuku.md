## Daifuku
---
- **definition:**
  - a Japanese confection of soft mochi (pounded sticky rice cake) stuffed with a sweet filling, usually red bean paste
  - also refers to variations with fillings like strawberry, cream, or other flavors
- **etymology:**
  - from Japanese daifuku (大福) meaning “great fortune,” originally in the compound daifuku mochi (大福餅) “great fortune rice cake”
  - earlier name was harabuto mochi (腹太餅) “thick-bellied rice cake,” later reinterpreted by phonetic pun to use the luckier 福 “fortune”
  - shortened over time from daifuku mochi to simply daifuku in modern usage
- **history:**
  - emerged in Japan during the Edo period (18th century), when filled mochi became a popular street sweet in cities like Edo (Tokyo)
  - by the 19th century it was associated with celebrations and gift-giving, benefiting from the auspicious meaning of “great fortune”
  - modern twists such as ichigo daifuku (strawberry-filled) spread in the 1980s, and the sweet gained global popularity in the late 20th–early 21st centuries
- **pronunciation:**
  - DYE-foo-koo
