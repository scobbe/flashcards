## Chabad
---
- **definition:**
  - a Hasidic movement within Orthodox Judaism, also called Lubavitch, known for outreach and emphasis on study and mitzvot
  - a local community center or synagogue run by this movement, often called a “Chabad House”
- **origin:**
  - from the Hebrew acronym ChaBaD: Chokhmah (wisdom), Binah (understanding), Da’at (knowledge), reflecting its intellectual approach to faith
  - founded in the late 1700s by Rabbi Shneur Zalman of Liadi; later based in the town of Lubavitch, hence the alternate name
  - expanded into a global outreach network in the 20th century under Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson (“the Rebbe”)
- **pronunciation:**
  - khah-BAHD
