## Chabad
---
- **definition:**
  - a Hasidic movement within Orthodox Judaism, also called Chabad-Lubavitch, known for its outreach and education
  - the worldwide network of community centers, synagogues, and programs run by that movement
- **etymology:**
  - from Hebrew Chabad (חב״ד), an acronym of chochmah, binah, da’at meaning “wisdom, understanding, knowledge”
  - formed as a Hebrew initialism; the English spelling uses “Ch” to represent the guttural kh sound in Hebrew/Yiddish
- **history:**
  - founded in the late 18th century by Rabbi Shneur Zalman of Liadi; his work Tanya (1797) set its intellectual approach
  - the movement’s center was in Lyubavichi (1813–1915) and later moved to Brooklyn, New York, in 1940
  - under Rabbi Menachem Mendel Schneerson (1951–1994), Chabad expanded global outreach, making the term widely used in English by the late 20th century
- **pronunciation:**
  - khah-BAHD
