## Vodka sauce
---
- **definition:**
  - a tomato-and-cream pasta sauce flavored with vodka, often with garlic, onion, chili, and cheese
  - by extension, any jarred or restaurant pasta sauce labeled as such, commonly served with penne
- **etymology:**
  - a compound noun in English formed from vodka + sauce
  - vodka from Russian "vodka," diminutive of "voda" meaning "water" (literally "little water")
  - sauce from Old French "sauce," from Latin "salsa" meaning "salted (food)," from "sal" meaning "salt"
- **history:**
  - appeared in U.S. Italian-American cooking in the late 1970s; the term is attested in English by the early 1980s amid a pasta boom
  - origins are disputed (claims from New York restaurants and from Italian chefs/promoters), but the sauce surged in popularity in the 1980s in both the U.S. and Italy
  - commercial jarred versions spread by the 1990s; the dish saw a revival in the 2020s via social media "spicy vodka pasta" trends
- **pronunciation:**
  - VOD-kuh SAWSS
