## Forró
---
- **definition:**
  - a lively Brazilian music genre and partner dance from the Northeast, led by accordion, triangle, and zabumba drum
  - a social event or party where this music is played and danced
- **origin:**
  - from Brazilian Portuguese forró, likely a shortening of forrobodó, meaning an uproarious party or shindig
  - a popular folk tale links it to the English phrase “for all” on dance posters, but linguists consider this a myth
  - rose to national fame in mid-20th-century Brazil through artists like Luiz Gonzaga, becoming a symbol of Northeastern culture
- **pronunciation:**
  - foh-HAW
