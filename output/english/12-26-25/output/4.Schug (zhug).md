## Schug (zhug)
---
- **definition:**
  - a fiery chili, garlic, and herb paste used as a hot sauce in Yemeni and Israeli cuisines
  - a condiment spooned onto falafel, shawarma, grilled meats, or stirred into dips for heat and brightness
- **etymology:**
  - from Modern Hebrew s'khug/zhug (סחוג), adopted into English via Israeli usage
  - ultimately from Yemeni Arabic sahawiq (سحاوق), from the verb saḥaqa meaning “to crush/grind,” referring to pounded sauces
  - english spellings (schug/zhug/zhoug/skhug) reflect different transliterations of Hebrew and Yemeni Arabic sounds
- **history:**
  - popularized in Israel in the mid‑20th century by Yemeni Jewish immigrants, especially after the 1949–50 airlifts known as Operation Magic Carpet
  - appeared in English-language cookbooks and food writing by the late 20th century and rose in profile with modern Israeli cuisine in the 2000s–2010s
  - entered mainstream U.S. retail by the late 2010s (e.g., supermarket “zhoug sauce”), spreading its name and variations
- **pronunciation:**
  - ZHOOG
