## Mutual funds
---
- **definition:**
  - a pooled investment that gathers money from many investors to buy a diversified portfolio (stocks, bonds, etc.) run by professional managers
  - investors buy shares priced at the fund’s net asset value (NAV) and earn returns from gains, dividends, and interest, minus fees
- **origin:**
  - from “mutual” (Latin mutuus, “reciprocal”) + “fund” (Latin fundus, “base, estate”) via French/English, meaning a shared pool of money
  - early pooled investments appeared in 18th-century Netherlands; the modern U.S. mutual fund model began in the 1920s (e.g., Massachusetts Investors Trust)
  - the term is chiefly American; in the UK similar vehicles are called unit trusts or open-ended investment companies
- **pronunciation:**
  - MYOO-choo-uhl FUNDZ
