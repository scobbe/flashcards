## Jacques Marie Mage
---
- **definition:**
  - a Los Angeles–based luxury eyewear brand known for limited-edition, sculptural frames
  - the French-born designer behind the brand, Jérôme Jacques Marie Mage
- **etymology:**
  - french personal names: Jacques from Latin Jacobus (from Hebrew Ya'aqov, ‘supplanter’); Marie from Latin Maria (from Hebrew Miryam, of uncertain meaning)
  - mage as a French surname aligns with Old French mage ‘magus, wise man,’ from Latin magus, from Greek magos, ultimately from Old Persian magush
  - the brand name is eponymous, using the founder’s given names plus surname in full
- **history:**
  - founded in 2014 in Los Angeles by French designer Jérôme Jacques Marie Mage; launched with small-batch, numbered eyewear handcrafted in Japan
  - by the late 2010s, the name spread in fashion press and collector communities, with the abbreviation “JMM” becoming common
  - in the 2020s, high-profile wearers (e.g., Jeff Goldblum) and limited releases reinforced its standing in luxury eyewear culture
- **pronunciation:**
  - zhahk mah-REE mahzh
