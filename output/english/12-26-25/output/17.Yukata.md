## Yukata
---
- **definition:** Yukata
  - a lightweight, unlined cotton robe like a casual kimono, worn in summer or after bathing
  - commonly worn at festivals or provided as loungewear in Japanese inns and hot-spring resorts
- **origin:**
  - from Japanese yukata (浴衣), literally "bath clothing" (浴 "to bathe" + 衣 "garment")
  - evolved from yukatabira (湯帷子), a bath robe used by Heian-era courtiers; later adopted as a cotton summer kimono
  - by the Edo period it spread beyond baths to festivals and everyday summer wear
- **pronunciation:**
  - yoo-KAH-tah
