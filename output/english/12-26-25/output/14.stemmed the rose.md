## Stemmed the rose
---
- **definition:** Stemmed the rose
  - literal: removed the stem from a rose, as when preparing flowers
  - archaic, euphemistic (rare): had sexual intercourse with a woman; deflowered her
- **origin:**
  - from the rose as a long-standing symbol of female sexuality and virginity in medieval and Renaissance literature
  - early modern phrases like “pluck the rose” or “gather the rose” meant to deflower; “stem the rose” echoes this bawdy metaphor
  - stem as a verb means to cut or remove a plant’s stem, which fed the figurative sense of taking or cutting a “rose”
- **pronunciation:**
  - STEMD thuh ROHZ
