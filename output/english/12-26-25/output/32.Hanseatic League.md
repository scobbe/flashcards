## Hanseatic League
---
- **definition:**
  - a medieval alliance of merchant guilds and market towns in northern Europe that coordinated trade and mutual defense
  - the network that dominated Baltic and North Sea commerce from the 1200s to the 1600s, centered on cities like Lübeck and Hamburg
- **origin:**
  - from Middle Low German hanse meaning "guild" or "troop" + English league via French ligue, from Latin ligare "to bind"
  - formed around Lübeck in the late 12th–13th centuries as towns banded together for safe routes, shared rules, and protection
  - peaked in the 14th–15th centuries and declined with nation-states and Atlantic trade; last general diet met in 1669; some cities still style themselves "Hanseatic"
- **pronunciation:**
  - han-zee-AT-ik leeg
