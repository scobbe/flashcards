## Ecclesiastes 3
---
- **definition:**
  - the third chapter of the biblical book Ecclesiastes, noted for the poem beginning "to everything there is a season"
  - a poetic reflection on life's appointed times and opposites, often quoted in literature, ceremonies, and song
- **origin:**
  - from Greek "Ekklēsiastēs" (“preacher; member of the assembly”), via Latin, translating Hebrew "Qoheleth" (“assembler/teacher”)
  - chapter numbers were added in the Middle Ages; chapter 3 preserves an ancient Hebrew poem on appointed times (3:1–8)
  - well known in English through the King James Bible; its lines inspired the song "Turn! Turn! Turn!" (1965)
- **pronunciation:**
  - eh-klee-zee-AS-teez three
