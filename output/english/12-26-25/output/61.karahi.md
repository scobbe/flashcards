## Karahi
---
- **definition:**
  - a deep, round cooking pan with two loop handles, used across South Asia for frying and simmering
  - a dish cooked in that pan, especially spicy meat or vegetable preparations served in restaurants
- **etymology:**
  - from Urdu karahi/karāhī and Hindi kadhai/kaṛāhī, meaning “deep frying pan”
  - ultimately from Sanskrit kaṭāha, “large pan” or “cauldron,” with the modern feminine -ī ending
  - borrowed into English via South Asian languages through contact with the subcontinent
- **history:**
  - recorded in English by the early 20th century during the British Raj (1858–1947), as South Asian culinary terms entered English
  - originally named the heavy iron or copper vessel; by the late 20th century it also labeled dishes like chicken karahi in Pakistan and Punjab
  - post-1947 South Asian migration spread the term; from the 1970s–1990s UK curry houses popularized “karahi” on international menus
- **pronunciation:**
  - kuh-RAH-hee
