## I Ching / Yijing
---
- **definition:** I Ching / Yijing
  - an ancient Chinese classic, the Book of Changes, used for divination and moral guidance
  - the system of 64 hexagrams and line texts used to interpret patterns and offer advice
  - by extension, a cornerstone of Chinese thought that influenced Confucian and Daoist traditions
- **origin:**
  - from Chinese Yijing (易经) meaning Classic of Changes; I Ching is the older Wade–Giles spelling, Yijing the modern Pinyin
  - began as the Zhouyi, a Bronze Age oracle using yarrow-stalk casting; later commentaries (the Ten Wings, traditionally linked to Confucius) expanded its philosophy
  - entered the West via Jesuit and later scholarly translations; notable English versions include James Legge (19th c.) and Wilhelm–Baynes (20th c.)
- **pronunciation:**
  - eye-CHING (I Ching); YEE-jing (Yijing)
