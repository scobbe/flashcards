## Crepuscular
---
- **definition:**
  - relating to twilight; happening at dawn or dusk
  - active mainly during twilight (used of animals)
  - dim or shadowy like twilight
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin crepusculum “twilight, dimness,” from creper/creperus “dusky, uncertain”
  - formed with the adjectival suffix -ar (Latin -aris) meaning “pertaining to”
  - entered English through learned Latin; compare French crépusculaire with the same root
- **history:**
  - first recorded in English in the 1640s, amid a surge of Latin-derived scholarly terms
  - by the 19th century, used in zoology to label dawn-and-dusk activity, alongside diurnal and nocturnal
  - late 19th century saw wider use in phrases like “crepuscular rays” and in literature to evoke dim, transitional light
- **pronunciation:**
  - kri-PUHS-kyuh-ler
