## Nazca lines
---
- **definition:** Nazca lines
  - vast ancient geoglyphs etched into the desert of southern Peru, best viewed from the air
  - made by clearing dark stones to reveal lighter ground, forming long lines, shapes, and figures; their purpose is debated (ritual, water, or astronomical uses)
- **origin:**
  - from Spanish "Líneas de Nazca"—"Nazca lines"—named for the Nazca Desert and the Nazca culture of Peru
  - nazca is an indigenous place-name (often spelled Nasca locally); the term spread after 20th‑century aerial surveys and studies by researchers like María Reiche
  - gained global recognition in the mid-1900s and was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1994
- **pronunciation:**
  - NAZ-kuh lines
