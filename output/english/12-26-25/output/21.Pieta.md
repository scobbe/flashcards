## Pieta
---
- **definition:**
  - a depiction in Christian art of the Virgin Mary cradling the dead body of Jesus after the Crucifixion
  - specifically, the renowned marble sculpture by Michelangelo in St. Peter's Basilica, Rome
- **etymology:**
  - from Italian pietà meaning "pity" or "compassion," used to name this devotional image
  - from Latin pietas "piety, dutifulness, compassion," from pius "devout, dutiful"
  - adopted into English through art-historical usage that retained the Italian term
- **history:**
  - the motif arose in late medieval Central Europe (c. 1300–1350), called the Vesperbild in German, and spread to Italy
  - Michelangelo carved his Pietà (1498–1500) for a French cardinal; its fame cemented the subject’s prominence in Renaissance art
  - the Italian term became common in English by the 19th century with Grand Tour culture and formal art history, extending to similar compositions across media
- **pronunciation:**
  - pee-eh-TAH
