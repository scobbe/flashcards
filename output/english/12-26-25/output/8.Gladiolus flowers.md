## Gladiolus flowers
---
- **definition:**
  - tall garden plants in the iris family with sword-shaped leaves and a spike of colorful, funnel-shaped blooms
  - the individual blossoms of a gladiolus plant, often used in bouquets and floral displays
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin gladiolus, meaning "little sword," diminutive of gladius "sword," referring to the blade-like leaves
  - adopted in New Latin as the botanical genus name; entered English via scientific Latin in the 1700s, with the Latin plural gladioli also used
- **history:**
  - classified by Carl Linnaeus in 1753; numerous species were introduced to Europe from South Africa in the late 1700s and 1800s amid active plant collecting
  - rose to popularity in Victorian gardens (mid–late 1800s) and featured in floriography, often symbolizing strength of character and remembrance
  - intensive hybridization from the 1840s–1900s in Belgium, the Netherlands, Britain, and the U.S. produced large, showy cultivars that dominated 20th-century cut-flower markets; widely recognized as an August birth flower
- **pronunciation:**
  - glad-ee-OH-lus FLOW-erz
