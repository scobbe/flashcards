## Amgen
---
- **definition:**
  - a us-based biotechnology company known for developing and manufacturing biologic medicines
  - the corporate name of the firm originally called Applied Molecular Genetics, often used to refer to the company or its stock
- **etymology:**
  - english corporate name formed as a clipped blend of “Applied Molecular Genetics” → “Amgen”
  - built from the roots “applied” (put into practice) + “molecular” (relating to molecules) + “genetics” (study of genes), reflecting the company’s focus
- **history:**
  - founded in 1980 in california amid the first wave of modern biotech startups; the shortened name “Amgen” soon became the brand
  - rose to prominence with early biologics such as Epogen (1989) and Neupogen (1991), helping shape the biotech industry in the 1980s–1990s
  - expanded through major deals, notably acquiring Immunex in 2002 (gaining Enbrel rights) and Horizon Therapeutics in 2023, becoming one of the largest global biotechs
- **pronunciation:**
  - AM-jen
