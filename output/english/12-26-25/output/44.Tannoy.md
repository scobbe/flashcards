## Tannoy
---
- **definition:**
  - a public-address system, or an announcement made over it (especially in the UK)
  - a brand name for loudspeakers and public-address equipment made by Tannoy Ltd.
- **etymology:**
  - english trademark coined in 1932 by contracting “tantalum alloy,” the material in the company’s early rectifiers, to Tannoy
  - from tantalum (named after Tantalus in Greek myth) + alloy (Old French origin), literally “tantalum metal mixture”
- **history:**
  - founded by Guy R. Fountain in London in 1926; the Tannoy trademark was registered in 1932 for power-supply and PA components
  - during WWII and postwar Britain, Tannoy systems were widely installed in factories, airfields, and railways, making the brand name synonymous with public announcements
  - by the late 20th century tannoy was commonly used generically in British English, though the company has continued to defend the trademark
- **pronunciation:**
  - TAN-oy
