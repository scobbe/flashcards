## Bris
---
- **definition:**
  - the Jewish religious circumcision ceremony for a baby boy, traditionally held on the eighth day after birth
  - by extension, the circumcision performed at that ceremony
- **etymology:**
  - from Yiddish bris, the Ashkenazic pronunciation of Hebrew brit meaning ‘covenant’
  - short for the Hebrew phrase brit milah ‘covenant of circumcision’ (milah ‘circumcision’), adopted into English through Jewish communities
  - the spelling bris reflects Yiddish/European Hebrew, while brit reflects modern Israeli Hebrew
- **history:**
  - rooted in the biblical covenant with Abraham (Genesis 17), the rite has been practiced in Jewish communities since antiquity, with trained mohels officiating
  - the word bris appears in English by the late 19th century (c. 1880s–1890s), especially in North America during large Eastern European Jewish immigration (c. 1880–1924)
  - in the 20th–21st centuries, the term gained wider public familiarity through journalism and literature, while modern debates over infant circumcision and religious freedom brought the ceremony into broader discourse
- **pronunciation:**
  - BRISS
