## Audio interface
---
- **definition:**
  - a hardware device that converts sound to digital data and back, letting microphones, instruments, and speakers connect to a computer
  - more broadly, any connection point or software layer used to send and receive audio between systems
- **etymology:**
  - from audio, based on Latin audire 'to hear', adopted in English in the 20th century for sound-related technology
  - from interface, via French, formed from Latin inter 'between' + facies 'face/surface', meaning a boundary or connection
  - combined as a noun–noun compound in technical English to label the unit linking audio gear and computing
- **history:**
  - in technical writing by the late 1970s–1980s, as computers and digital converters began linking with studio equipment
  - usage grew in the 1990s with affordable PCs and digital audio workstations; standards like ASIO (1997) and Core Audio (2001) enabled low-latency recording
  - widespread from the 2000s onward as USB/FireWire models made home recording, podcasting, and streaming accessible to non-experts
- **pronunciation:**
  - AW-dee-oh IN-ter-fays
