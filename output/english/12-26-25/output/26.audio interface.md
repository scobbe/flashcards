## Audio interface
---
- **definition:** Audio interface
  - a device that connects microphones, instruments, and speakers to a computer, converting analog sound to digital and back
  - more generally, the hardware or software layer that manages audio input and output between systems
- **origin:**
  - audio comes from Latin audire, meaning 'to hear'; it entered English in the early 1900s for sound-related media
  - interface blends Latin inter, 'between,' and facies, 'form/face' (via French); in science it meant a boundary where two systems meet
  - the compound spread with digital recording and USB/FireWire gear in the 1990s-2000s, often replacing 'sound card' for external units
- **pronunciation:**
  - AW-dee-oh IN-ter-face
