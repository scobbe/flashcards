## Philip Johnson
---
- **definition:**
  - american architect (1906–2005) known for influential modernist and postmodernist buildings
  - curator and critic who helped popularize the International Style in the United States
- **etymology:**
  - philip comes from Greek 'Philippos' meaning 'lover of horses' (phil- 'love' + hippos 'horse'), entering English via Latin and French
  - johnson is an English patronymic meaning 'son of John'; 'John' derives from Hebrew 'Yohanan' meaning 'Yahweh is gracious'
- **history:**
  - in 1932 he co-curated the Museum of Modern Art's International Style exhibition, bringing European modernism to american audiences between the world wars
  - from 1949 to 1984, key works from the Glass House (1949) to the AT&T Building (1984) traced a shift from high modernism to postmodernism in late 20th-century architecture
  - in 1979 he became the inaugural Pritzker Architecture Prize laureate; in the 2000s–2020s, his 1930s fascist sympathies prompted institutions to reevaluate his legacy
- **pronunciation:**
  - FIL-ip JOHN-suhn
