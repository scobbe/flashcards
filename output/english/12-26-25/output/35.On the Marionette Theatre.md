## On the Marionette Theatre
---
- **definition:** On the Marionette Theatre
  - the English title of Heinrich von Kleist’s 1810 essay that uses puppet theatre to explore grace, consciousness, and movement
  - often cited in aesthetics and performance studies as a meditation on human versus mechanical elegance
- **origin:**
  - from the 1810 German essay Über das Marionettentheater by Heinrich von Kleist, first printed as a dialogue in Berliner Abendblätter
  - marionette comes via French marionnette, a diminutive of Marion (Mary), meaning a small puppet
  - theatre traces to Greek theatron ‘seeing place’ via Latin and French; the English title is a direct translation
- **pronunciation:**
  - on thuh mar-ee-uh-NET THEE-uh-ter
