## On the Marionette Theatre
---
- **definition:**
  - the standard English title of Heinrich von Kleist’s 1810 essay that uses puppet theatre to explore grace, consciousness, and human movement
  - by extension, a touchstone reference in aesthetics and performance theory when discussing humans, puppets, and automatons
- **etymology:**
  - english title translating the German Über das Marionettentheater ('about the puppet theater')
  - marionette from French marionnette, a diminutive of Marion (from Marie, 'Mary'), literally 'little Mary' → 'puppet'
  - theatre from Greek theatron ('viewing place'), via Latin theatrum and French théâtre; on from Old English on ('on, about')
- **history:**
  - 1810: first published in two parts in Kleist’s Berlin newspaper Berliner Abendblätter, amid German Romanticism and debates on art, nature, and the machine
  - 19th century: read alongside contemporaneous fascination with automata and grace (e.g., E. T. A. Hoffmann’s tales), shaping discourse on embodiment
  - mid-20th century onward: widely translated and anthologized in English under this title, becoming influential in dance, theater, and literary theory
- **pronunciation:**
  - on the mar-ee-uh-NET THEE-uh-ter
