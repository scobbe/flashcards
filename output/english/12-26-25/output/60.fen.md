## Fen
---
- **definition:**
  - a low-lying, waterlogged wetland, typically with grasses, sedges, and reeds
  - in britain, often the fenland region (the fens) of eastern england
- **origin:**
  - from old english “fenn,” meaning marsh, bog, or muddy place
  - from proto-germanic *fanją (cf. old norse “fen,” dutch “ven,” german “fenn”), all meaning marsh
  - the famous fenland (the fens) of eastern england helped keep the term common; large areas were drained in the 1600s
- **pronunciation:**
  - FEN
