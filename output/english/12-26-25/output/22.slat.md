## Slat
---
- **definition:**
  - a thin, flat strip of wood, metal, or plastic used in things like fences, chairs, or window blinds
  - in aviation, a narrow movable strip on the front edge of a wing that helps an aircraft lift at low speeds
  - to fit or furnish with slats
- **origin:**
  - from Middle English slat (late 1300s), meaning "thin strip"; ultimate origin uncertain, probably a Scandinavian root meaning "flat strip" or "splinter"
  - related in sense to the family of words slot and slit, all about narrow strips or openings
  - extended in the early 1900s to the aircraft wing device; a separate Scots verb slat "to slap/throw" appears in the 1700s, likely imitative
- **pronunciation:**
  - SLAT
