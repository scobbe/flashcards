## Yukata
---
- **definition:**
  - a casual, unlined summer kimono, typically cotton, worn in Japan at festivals, inns, and after bathing
  - the lightweight robe provided at ryokan or hot-spring resorts for lounging and going to baths
- **etymology:**
  - from Japanese yukata (浴衣) meaning "bath garment," a shortening of yukatabira (湯帷子) "bath robe"
  - from yu "hot water; bath" + katabira "unlined robe"; later written with characters 浴 "to bathe" + 衣 "clothing"
  - borrowed into English unchanged via travel and cultural writings in the early 20th century
- **history:**
  - originating in the Heian period (794–1185) as a linen robe worn after steam baths by court nobles
  - in the Edo period (1603–1868) it spread to townspeople; cotton, indigo-dyed yukata became common for summer and public baths
  - by the early 20th century the word entered English; today it is standard attire at ryokan and summer festivals, with modern prints and accessories
- **pronunciation:**
  - yoo-KAH-tah
