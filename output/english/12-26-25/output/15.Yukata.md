## Yukata
---
- **definition:**
  - a casual, unlined cotton kimono worn in summer or after bathing
  - commonly worn at festivals, ryokan, and hot springs, tied with a simple obi sash
- **origin:**
  - from Japanese yukata, originally yukatabira: yu "bath" + katabira "under-robe"
  - first a linen bath garment for Heian-period nobles, later cotton wear for commoners at public bathhouses
  - now written with characters meaning "bathing clothes" (浴 "bathe" + 衣 "garment"), used as light summer wear
- **pronunciation:**
  - yoo-KAH-tah
