## Sool
---
- **definition:**
  - to incite or urge, especially a dog, to attack or chase; often used with “on” (e.g., “sool him on”)
  - by extension, to egg on or provoke a person to act
  - an interjection or command to a dog meaning “get him!”
- **etymology:**
  - english dialectal word of uncertain origin, first noted in Scots and northern English speech
  - often linked to Middle English “sue” meaning “to follow, pursue,” from Old French “suivre,” from Latin “sequi” “to follow”
  - also viewed as an imitative handler’s cry, with the -oo- spelling reflecting the shouted sound
- **history:**
  - attested in British sources by the late 18th–early 19th century in hunting and dog-handling contexts
  - widely used in 19th‑century sporting and ratting reports; later broadened colloquially to mean “egg on” a person
  - carried into Australian and New Zealand English as “sool (on)” in the 20th century; now chiefly dialectal or informal
- **pronunciation:**
  - SOOL
