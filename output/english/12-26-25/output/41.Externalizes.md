## Externalizes
---
- **definition:** Externalizes
  - makes something internal (such as feelings or ideas) outward, visible, or expressed
  - in psychology, attributes causes or blame to outside forces rather than oneself
  - in economics/business, shifts costs, risks, or harms onto others or the environment
- **origin:**
  - from Latin externus (“outward, outside”), from ex (“out”), combined with the verb-forming suffix -ize (via French/Greek meaning “to make or render”)
  - first recorded in early 19th‑century English as externalize; later gained psychological and economic uses in the 20th century (e.g., “externalize costs”)
  - british English often spells it externalises; externalizes is the third-person singular present of externalize
- **pronunciation:**
  - ek-STER-nuh-lie-ziz
