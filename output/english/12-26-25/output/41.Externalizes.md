## Externalizes
---
- **definition:**
  - make something outward or visible; express an inner thought or feeling in words or actions
  - attribute an inner issue to outside forces, especially in psychology
  - shift costs, risks, or tasks to others, as when a company passes harms to the public or outsources work
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin externus “outward, from the outside” (via the English adjective external) + the verb-forming suffix -ize “to make, render,” from Greek -izein
  - the final -s marks third-person singular present tense: “he/she/it externalizes”
  - american English favors -ize (from Greek/Latin), while British English often uses -ise
- **history:**
  - mid-19th century: entered English meaning “to make external or objective,” used in philosophy and art discourse about expressing inner states
  - early 20th century: adopted in psychology for a defense mechanism—placing one’s feelings or motives onto the outside world—alongside terms like projection
  - mid–late 20th century: “externalize costs” became common in economics and environmental policy debates; late 20th century management extended it to outsourcing and risk transfer
- **pronunciation:**
  - ik-STUR-nuh-lye-ziz
