## Tochucha
---
- **definition:**
  - a Japanese herbal tea made from the leaves or bark of the Eucommia tree
  - used as a wellness drink, often called Eucommia tea
- **etymology:**
  - from Japanese tochu-cha (杜仲茶), literally "Eucommia tea" (tochu = Eucommia, cha = tea)
  - ultimately from Chinese duzhong cha (杜仲茶), with cha being the East Asian word for tea
- **history:**
  - eucommia bark has been used in Chinese medicine since at least the Han dynasty (1st–2nd century CE) as a tonic
  - the beverage became popular in Japan as a health tea in the late 20th century amid wellness trends
  - the term entered English in the late 20th century via Japanese imports and alternative medicine writing; usage remains niche
- **pronunciation:**
  - toh-CHOO-chah
