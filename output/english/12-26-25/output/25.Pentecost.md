## Pentecost
---
- **definition:** Pentecost
  - in Christianity: the festival held 50 days after Easter, marking the Holy Spirit’s descent on the apostles
  - in Judaism: the Greek name for Shavuot (Feast of Weeks), observed 50 days after Passover
- **origin:**
  - from Greek pentēkostē (hēmera) meaning “fiftieth (day),” from pente “five” and pentēkonta “fifty”
  - came through Latin (Pentecoste) into Old/Middle English as Pentecost, first referring to the Jewish feast
  - early Christians applied the term to the 50th day after Easter; in English tradition it’s also called Whitsunday
- **pronunciation:**
  - PEN-tuh-kost
