## Pentecost
---
- **definition:**
  - the Christian festival on the seventh Sunday after Easter, commemorating the Holy Spirit’s descent on the apostles; also called Whitsunday
  - the Jewish festival of Shavuot (Feast of Weeks), especially in older or scholarly use
- **etymology:**
  - from Koine Greek pentekoste “fiftieth,” a shortened form of pentekoste hemera “fiftieth day”
  - via Latin Pentecoste(s) into Old English Pentecosten and Middle English Pentecost
  - built from Greek pente “five” + -konta “tens,” literally “five tens”
- **history:**
  - 3rd–1st centuries bce: Greek-speaking Jews used pentekoste in the Septuagint for the Feast of Weeks; the term later entered Christian usage
  - 1st century ce: Acts 2 describes the Jerusalem event; by the 2nd–4th centuries it became a principal Christian feast marking the Church’s birth
  - attested in Old English (c. 900s); in medieval England Whitsuntide featured baptisms, pageants, and royal courts; Whit Monday remains a holiday in some countries
- **pronunciation:**
  - PEN-tuh-kost
