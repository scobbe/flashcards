## Sentir
---
- **definition:**
  - a Romance-language verb meaning “to feel; to sense” (in French also “to smell”)
  - used in English only as a foreign/loanword, mainly in linguistic, literary, or translation contexts
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin sentire “to perceive, feel, think,” which yielded Old French sentir and Spanish/Portuguese sentir
  - built on the Latin stem sent- + infinitive ending -ire/-ir; Romance languages preserved the stem and -ir infinitive
- **history:**
  - attested in Old French by the 12th century and in medieval Spanish and Portuguese soon after, used widely for perception and feeling
  - appears in English-language writing from the 18th century onward as an italicized foreign verb in philology and translation
  - remains a foreign term in English; native equivalents are “feel,” “sense,” and (for French usage) “smell”
- **pronunciation:**
  - sen-TEER
