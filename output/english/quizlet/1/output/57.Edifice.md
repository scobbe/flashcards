## Edifice
---
- **definition:**
  - a large, imposing building
  - a complex system or structure of ideas (used figuratively)
- **etymology:**
  - from Old French edifice, ultimately from Latin aedificium meaning "building"
  - formed from Latin roots aedes/aedis "dwelling, temple" + facere "to make, do" via aedificare "to build"
  - borrowed into Middle English in the 14th century through French mediation
- **history:**
  - recorded in English by the early 14th century, amid the era of Gothic cathedrals and grand civic constructions
  - widely used during the Renaissance and neoclassical periods (16th-18th c.) for monumental public and ecclesiastical architecture
  - the figurative sense (an "edifice" of theory or belief) emerged by the 17th century and spread in Enlightenment prose
- **pronunciation:**
  - ED-uh-fiss
