## Nike (mythology)
---
- **definition:**
  - the Greek goddess who personifies victory in war, sport, and competition
  - a winged female figure used as a symbol of victory in ancient art
- **etymology:**
  - from ancient Greek Nikē (Νίκη) meaning "victory," the abstract noun turned into a deity’s name
  - related to Greek nikaō "to win, conquer," from Proto-Indo-European root *neik- "to attack, be forceful, win"
  - connected to names like Nikolaos (Nicholas), from nikē "victory" + laos "people"
- **history:**
  - worshiped in classical Greece; often shown with Zeus or Athena; the Temple of Athena Nike on the Acropolis was built c. 427–424 BCE
  - prominent in Hellenistic and Roman eras; famous statue Nike of Samothrace (Winged Victory) dates to about 190 BCE; Romans identified her as Victoria
  - appears in English by the 16th century through classical studies; the name gained broad modern visibility when a sportswear company adopted it in 1971
- **pronunciation:**
  - NEE-kay
