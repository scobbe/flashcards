## Satrap
---
- **definition:**
  - a provincial governor in the ancient Persian Empire, ruling a region (satrapy) for the king
  - by extension, a subordinate ruler or local boss under a more powerful authority, often autocratic
- **etymology:**
  - from Greek satrapes and Latin satrapes, taken from Old Persian khshathrapavan, literally "protector of the realm"
  - formed from Old Persian khshathra "realm, rule" + pavan "protector"; entered English via Latin/French in the late Middle Ages
- **history:**
  - the office emerged under the Achaemenid Empire (6th century BCE) as Cyrus and Darius organized provinces overseen by satraps
  - classical writers like Herodotus and Xenophon described satrapal power and periodic revolts; Alexander the Great retained and reshaped satrapies after 330 BCE
