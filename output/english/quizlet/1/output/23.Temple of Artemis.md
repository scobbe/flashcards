## Temple of Artemis
---
- **definition:**
  - a monumental ancient Greek temple at Ephesus dedicated to the goddess Artemis, famed as one of the Seven Wonders
  - by extension, can refer to other sanctuaries of Artemis, though the Ephesian temple is usually meant
- **etymology:**
  - english phrase combining temple (from Latin templum, “sacred space”) with the Greek name Artemis (Ἄρτεμις)
  - modeled on Greek Artemision, literally “shrine of Artemis,” from Artemis + -ion place suffix; Artemis may relate to Greek artemēs “safe, unharmed,” though the origin is uncertain
- **history:**
  - c. 8th–6th century BCE: a sanctuary at Ephesus grew into a grand marble temple c. 550 BCE under King Croesus, designed by Chersiphron and Metagenes
  - 356 BCE: burned by Herostratus; rebuilt in the Hellenistic era and celebrated as a Wonder by writers like Antipater of Sidon (2nd century BCE)
  - 262 CE: damaged in a Gothic raid and later quarried as Christianity rose; the site was identified in 1869–74 by John Turtle Wood near modern Selçuk, Turkey
- **pronunciation:**
  - TEM-puhl uhv AR-tuh-mis
