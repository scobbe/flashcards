## Thomas Piketty
---
- **definition:**
  - french economist specializing in wealth and income inequality
  - author of "Capital in the Twenty-First Century" and "Capital and Ideology"
  - influential public intellectual in policy debates on taxation and inequality
- **etymology:**
  - thomas from Aramaic ta'oma' “twin,” via Greek and Latin Thomas, spread through Christian tradition
  - piketty is a French surname likely from Old French piquet/picot “stake; small point,” from piquer “to prick,” with -y as a diminutive/variant ending
- **history:**
  - born 1971; trained at École Normale Supérieure; in the late 1990s–2000s with Atkinson and Saez built long-run inequality series from tax records
  - his 2013 book "Capital in the Twenty-First Century" (Eng. 2014) became a bestseller after the 2008 crisis, popularizing the idea that returns on capital outpace growth (r > g)
  - co-founded the Paris School of Economics (2006) and World Inequality Lab (2015); later wrote "Capital and Ideology" (2019; Eng. 2020) and advocates progressive wealth taxes
- **pronunciation:**
  - toh-MAH pee-keh-TEE
