## Alhambra
---
- **definition:**
  - a fortified palace and citadel in Granada, Spain, famed for its Moorish architecture and ornate courtyards
  - a name adopted for various theaters, hotels, and towns inspired by the palace
- **etymology:**
  - from Spanish Alhambra, via Arabic al-ḥamrā’ “the red (one),” with al- “the” + ḥamrā’ “red”
  - originally from Arabic Qal‘at al-ḥamrā’ “the Red Fortress,” referring to the reddish walls of the complex
- **history:**
  - built mainly in the 13th–14th centuries by the Nasrid dynasty in Granada; after the 1492 conquest, the Catholic Monarchs took it over and Charles V began a Renaissance palace (1527)
  - entered English by the 1700s; Washington Irving’s Tales of the Alhambra (1832) popularized it in the Romantic era and inspired many namesakes
  - underwent major restorations in the 19th–20th centuries after periods of neglect; designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1984
- **pronunciation:**
  - al-HAM-bruh
