## Folie à deux
---
- **definition:**
  - a shared delusional belief or psychosis experienced by two closely connected people
  - by extension, a situation where two people reinforce each other’s irrational ideas or harmful behavior
- **etymology:**
  - from French: folie “madness/folly” + à deux “of two; for two”
  - folie comes from Old French fol “foolish,” ultimately from Latin follis “bellows, empty bag” (figuratively, a foolish person)
  - deux is from Latin duo “two”; the whole phrase entered English unchanged as a loan expression
- **history:**
  - coined and described in 1877 by French psychiatrists Charles Lasègue and Jules Falret during the rise of clinical psychiatry
  - entered English medical writing by the late 19th–early 20th century and was widely used through the 1900s
  - classified in DSM-IV (1994) as Shared Psychotic Disorder; in DSM-5 (2013) it was folded into other specified schizophrenia spectrum disorders
- **pronunciation:**
  - foh-LEE ah DUH
