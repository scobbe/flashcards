## Ishtar gate
---
- **definition:**
  - a monumental ceremonial gate of ancient Babylon, faced with blue-glazed bricks and decorated with bulls and dragons
  - the museum reconstruction in Berlin assembled from original Babylonian bricks and tiles
- **etymology:**
  - a compound of Ishtar (the Mesopotamian goddess’s name) + gate
  - Ishtar < Akkadian Ištar, related to West Semitic Astarte; ultimately from a Proto-Semitic root meaning “Venus, the morning/evening star”
  - gate < Old English geat “opening, passage,” from Proto-Germanic gatą “gap”
- **history:**
  - c. 575 BCE: commissioned by Nebuchadnezzar II during the Neo-Babylonian Empire as the eighth gate of Babylon, marking the start of the Processional Way used for New Year festivals
  - 1902–1914: excavated by Robert Koldewey; thousands of glazed bricks were shipped to Germany and reassembled; a large reconstruction opened at Berlin’s Pergamon Museum in 1930
  - since the 20th century it has become an icon of Mesopotamian art; Babylon, including the gate’s remains, was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2019
- **pronunciation:**
  - ISH-tar GATE
