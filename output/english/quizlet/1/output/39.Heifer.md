## Heifer
---
- **definition:**
  - a young female cow, especially one that has not yet had a calf
  - slang, derogatory: an insulting term for a woman, often implying she is large
- **etymology:**
  - from Old English heahfore meaning “young cow,” later Middle English heyfer/heifer; ultimate origin uncertain
  - possibly from Old English heah “high” + a second element related to fearr “ox, bull,” with cognates in Old High German farah “young bull/calf” and Dutch vaars “heifer”
  - the modern ei spelling settled in Middle English, while pronunciation simplified to sound like “heffer”
- **history:**
  - attested in Old English by c. 1000, used in agrarian Britain to classify young female cattle before first calving
  - featured in early English Bible translations (e.g., Tyndale 1530s; King James 1611), including the phrase “red heifer,” reinforcing its ritual and cultural visibility
  - by the 19th–20th centuries, livestock handbooks standardized its technical sense in ranching; a derogatory slang use for a woman emerged in American English in the mid-1900s
- **pronunciation:**
  - HEF-er
