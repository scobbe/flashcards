## Coquille
---
- **definition:**
  - a shell or shell-shaped baking/serving dish, especially for scallops
  - a printer's error or typo (borrowed from French publishing jargon)
  - a decorative shell motif in art and architecture, notably in Rococo design
- **etymology:**
  - from French coquille "shell," from Old French coquille
  - ultimately from Late/Vulgar Latin conchilia or conchicula, diminutive of Latin concha "shell" (from Greek konkhe "shell")
  - entered English as a direct French loanword, especially in culinary, art-historical, and printing contexts
- **history:**
  - attested in English by the late 1800s on menus and in cookery writing; it gained wider popularity mid-1900s with the vogue for French cuisine (e.g., Julia Child's 1961 scallops en coquille)
  - the printing sense meaning a misprint is well established in 19th-century French and appears in English-language publishing circles from the mid-20th century, though it remains specialized
  - art historians adopted the French term for shell ornament associated with Rococo (18th century), reflecting the period's fascination with marine motifs
- **pronunciation:**
  - koh-KEEL
