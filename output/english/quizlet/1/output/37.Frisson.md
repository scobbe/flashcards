## Frisson
---
- **definition:**
  - a sudden thrill or shiver of excitement, fear, or pleasure
  - a brief chill sensation, often with goosebumps, triggered by music, art, or drama
- **etymology:**
  - from French frisson meaning shiver or chill, formed from the verb frissonner to shiver
  - ultimately from Latin frigus cold and frigere to be cold, roots that also gave English frigid
- **history:**
  - entered English in the late 18th century (attested by 1777) during a vogue for French terms describing feelings and moods
  - in the 20th century it became common in arts journalism; by the late 1900s it was used in psychology and neuroscience for aesthetic chills in music studies
- **pronunciation:**
  - free-SOHN
