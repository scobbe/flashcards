## Potage
---
- **definition:**
  - a thick soup or puréed soup, especially in French cuisine
  - historically, any simple stew or porridge of vegetables, grains, and sometimes meat cooked in a pot
- **etymology:**
  - from Old French potage meaning “food cooked in a pot; soup”
  - formed from pot “pot” (a Germanic loanword in French) + the noun suffix -age “result/collective”
  - english also borrowed the same root earlier as “pottage”; the French spelling “potage” marks the culinary sense
- **history:**
  - attested in English (as “pottage”) by the 1200s, when one-pot soups and stews were staples of the medieval diet
  - by the 17th–18th centuries, “soup” became common in English while French haute cuisine used the term potage
  - in the 19th–early 20th centuries, chefs like Carême and Escoffier (e.g., 1903) codified “potages,” popularizing the French term on English menus
- **pronunciation:**
  - po-TAHZH
