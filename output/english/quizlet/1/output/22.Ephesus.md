## Ephesus
---
- **definition:**
  - an ancient Greek and later Roman city in western Asia Minor (near modern Selcuk, Turkey), famed for the Temple of Artemis
  - a key center of early Christianity mentioned in the New Testament and the site of major church councils
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin Ephesus, via ancient Greek Ephesos, the historical name of the city
  - ultimate origin likely pre-Greek Anatolian and of uncertain meaning; English adopted the classical form through biblical and classical traditions
  - related adjective and demonym Ephesians comes from Greek Ephesios, meaning "of Ephesus"
- **history:**
  - an Ionian Greek city by at least the 10thâ€“9th centuries BCE; renowned for the Temple of Artemis, rebuilt after its burning in 356 BCE
  - became the Roman provincial capital of Asia by the 1st century BCE; associated with the apostle Paul (c. 50s CE) and the Council of Ephesus (431 CE)
  - attested in English by the late 14th century via Bible translations; the city declined as its harbor silted up, was sacked by Goths in 262 CE, and has been excavated since 1869 (UNESCO World Heritage, 2015)
- **pronunciation:**
  - EF-uh-sus
