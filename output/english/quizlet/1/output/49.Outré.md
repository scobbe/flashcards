## Outré
---
- **definition:**
  - very unusual or shocking; extremely unconventional, especially in style, art, or fashion
  - exaggerated or overdone to the point of seeming strange
- **etymology:**
  - from French outré “excessive, overdone; beyond the usual,” the past participle of outrer “to exaggerate, push to extremes”
  - from Old French outrer “to go beyond,” ultimately from Latin ultra “beyond”
  - borrowed directly into English, typically retaining the acute accent to signal the final vowel sound
- **history:**
  - entered English in the late 18th century (attested 1768), during a period of strong French cultural influence on British and American art and society
  - in the 19th century, critics of the aesthetic and decadent movements used it for avant‑garde or eccentric works, and it carried into early modernist discourse
  - by the mid‑20th century it was common in fashion and entertainment writing to mark deliberate shock value, with the accented spelling still preferred in formal contexts
- **pronunciation:**
  - oo-TRAY
