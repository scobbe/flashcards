## Goshen
---
- **definition:**
  - a fertile region of ancient Egypt in the Bible where the Israelites lived before the Exodus
  - used mainly in the phrase "land of Goshen" as a mild exclamation of surprise in American English
- **etymology:**
  - from Hebrew Gōšen (גֹּשֶׁן), a biblical place-name; literal meaning uncertain and likely of Egyptian origin
  - entered English through biblical tradition via Greek (Gesén) and Latin (Gessen), settling as "Goshen" in early modern English Bibles
- **history:**
  - in the Hebrew Bible, Goshen is the Nile-delta district where Jacob’s family settled (Genesis) and from which the Exodus began; in English it appears in medieval and early modern translations, with the King James Version (1611) fixing the familiar form
  - by the late 18th–19th centuries in American English, the phrase "land of Goshen!" emerged as a mild exclamation of astonishment, reflecting widespread biblical literacy
  - the name was adopted for numerous U.S. towns in the 18th–19th centuries, and "land of Goshen" has sometimes been used figuratively for a place of plenty or refuge
- **pronunciation:**
  - GOH-shen
