## Steer (bullock)
---
- **definition:**
  - a castrated male bovine, especially raised for beef
  - bullock: chiefly British/Commonwealth for a young bull or a castrated male; in Australia/India also a draft ox
- **etymology:**
  - steer: from Old English steor “bullock, young bull,” from Proto-Germanic *steuraz “bull, male bovine” (compare German Stier, Dutch stier)
  - bullock: from Old English bulluc “young bull,” a diminutive of bula “bull,” with -uc/-ock meaning “little/young”
- **history:**
  - attested in Old English (before 900) for livestock, reflecting early Anglo-Saxon cattle keeping and castration practices
  - by the Middle Ages, steer narrowed to a castrated male for beef, while an adult trained draft animal was called an ox
  - bullock stayed common in British and Commonwealth use (e.g., bullock teams in 18th–19th‑century Australia; bullock carts in South Asia), while American English favored steer during 19th‑century cattle‑drive ranching
- **pronunciation:**
  - STEER (BULL-uk)
