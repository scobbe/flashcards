## Kidush
---
- **definition:**
  - a blessing recited over wine to sanctify the Jewish Sabbath or a festival
  - a light reception or meal after synagogue services, often featuring wine, challah, and snacks
- **etymology:**
  - from Hebrew qiddush/kiddush meaning “sanctification,” from the root q-d-sh “holy; set apart”
  - entered English via liturgical Hebrew and Yiddish used in Ashkenazic communities
  - often spelled “kiddush”; the double d reflects the Hebrew geminate consonant (also seen as “kidush”)
- **history:**
  - the ritual is attested in the Mishnah and Talmud (2nd–5th centuries CE), formalizing the command to sanctify the Sabbath over wine
  - codified in medieval Jewish law and practiced in homes and synagogues; a communal post-service “kiddush” spread in European communities
  - the term appears in English by the late 19th to early 20th century, with the reception sense becoming common in American synagogues by mid-20th century
- **pronunciation:**
  - KID-ush
