## Battle of Plataea
---
- **definition:**
  - a decisive land battle in 479 BCE where Greek city-states defeated the Persian army near Plataea in Boeotia
  - a turning point that ended the second Persian invasion and secured Greek independence on land
- **etymology:**
  - battle: from Old French bataille, from Late Latin battalia, from Latin battuere 'to beat, strike'
  - plataea: the Latinized name of Greek Plataiai (Πλαταιαί), a Boeotian city; origin uncertain, possibly from Greek platys 'broad'
  - the phrase follows the English pattern 'battle of + place-name' used to label historical engagements
- **history:**
  - fought in 479 BCE during the Greco-Persian Wars near the Boeotian city of Plataea; later described by Herodotus
  - greek forces under Pausanias (Sparta) and Aristides (Athens) broke Mardonius’s Persian army; Mardonius was killed and Persian losses were heavy
  - together with the near-contemporary Battle of Mycale, it ended Xerxes’ land campaign and paved the way for the Delian League; commemorated by a burial mound and the Eleutheria festival at Plataea
- **pronunciation:**
  - BAT-ul uhv plah-TEE-uh
