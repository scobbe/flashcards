## Hank Williams
---
- **definition:**
  - an American country music singer-songwriter (1923â€“1953) and honky-tonk pioneer known for songs like Your Cheatin' Heart and I'm So Lonesome I Could Cry
  - also known as Hank Williams Sr.; a defining influence on country songwriting and performance
- **etymology:**
  - the forename Hank is an American diminutive historically of Henry, from Germanic Heinrich: heim meaning home + ric meaning ruler
  - the surname Williams is a patronymic meaning son of William, from Germanic wil meaning will, desire + helm meaning helmet, protection
  - the full name follows the English pattern of forename plus patronymic surname; the artist was born Hiram King Williams and adopted Hank professionally
- **history:**
  - born in 1923 and died in 1953; rose from Alabama radio to national fame with Move It On Over (1947) and joined the Grand Ole Opry in 1949
  - early 1950s honky-tonk era: wrote and recorded Cold, Cold Heart, Hey, Good Lookin', and I'm So Lonesome I Could Cry; fired from the Opry in 1952 amid health and alcohol issues
  - died on Jan 1, 1953 while en route to a concert; later celebrated as a country icon and inducted into the Country Music Hall of Fame (1961), Songwriters Hall of Fame (1970), and Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (1987)
- **pronunciation:**
  - HANK WIL-yumz
