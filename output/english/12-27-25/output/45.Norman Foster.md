## Norman Foster
---
- **definition:**
  - british architect known for high-tech, glass-and-steel designs; founder of Foster + Partners
  - designer of landmark works such as the London “Gherkin,” Berlin’s Reichstag dome, and Apple Park
- **etymology:**
  - norman comes from Old French/Norman French “Normant,” meaning “Northman,” originally a Norse settler in medieval France
  - foster is an English surname from Middle English “forster/forester” (woodland keeper), also linked to Old English “fostre” meaning “foster parent”
  - the full name combines a given name of Norman origin with a common English occupational surname
- **history:**
  - born in Manchester in 1935; co-founded Team 4 in 1963 and founded Foster Associates (now Foster + Partners) in 1967 during the rise of high-tech architecture
  - achieved global prominence with the HSBC headquarters in Hong Kong (completed 1985) and the Reichstag renovation with its glass dome in Berlin (1999)
  - won the Pritzker Architecture Prize in 1999; knighted in 1990 and created Baron Foster of Thames Bank in 1999, with later icons including 30 St Mary Axe (2004) and Apple Park (2017)
- **pronunciation:**
  - NOR-man FOS-ter
