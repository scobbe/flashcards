## Daniyal Mueenuddin
---
- **definition:**
  - a Pakistani-American short-story writer and former lawyer, best known for the collection In Other Rooms, Other Wonders (2009)
  - his fiction portrays class, power, and social change on a feudal Punjabi estate in contemporary Pakistan
- **etymology:**
  - daniyal is the Arabic/Persian form of the Hebrew name Daniel (Daniyyel), literally “God is my judge”
  - mueenuddin comes from Arabic mu'in “helper, supporter” + al-din/ud-din “of the faith,” meaning “helper of the faith”
  - the fused spelling reflects South Asian romanization, merging ud-din into the surname
- **history:**
  - his stories gained wide attention in English in the late 2000s, notably through publications in The New Yorker (2008–2009)
  - his debut collection In Other Rooms, Other Wonders appeared in 2009 and was widely acclaimed amid a surge of interest in Pakistani English fiction
  - earlier he studied and worked in the United States, then returned to manage family farmland in Punjab, experiences that shaped his literary settings
- **pronunciation:**
  - dahn-YAHL moo-EEN-uh-deen
