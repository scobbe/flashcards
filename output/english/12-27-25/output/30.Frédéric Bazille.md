## Frédéric Bazille
---
- **definition:**
  - a French painter (1841–1870) associated with the early Impressionist circle
  - known for luminous outdoor scenes and figure paintings; close friend and supporter of Monet, Renoir, and Sisley
- **etymology:**
  - frédéric is the French form of a Germanic name from frid/frið "peace" + rīk "ruler," via Medieval Latin Fredericus
  - bazille is a French surname likely from Basile (Basil), from Greek Basileios meaning "royal" or "kingly"
  - the full name follows French naming conventions: given name + family name, with diacritics marking vowel quality
- **history:**
  - born 1841 in Montpellier; moved to Paris in the early 1860s, painting alongside Monet, Renoir, and Sisley as plein‑air practice reshaped French art
  - exhibited at the official Salon in the late 1860s; died in 1870 at the Battle of Beaune‑la‑Rolande during the Franco‑Prussian War, cutting short a promising career
  - his work was reassessed in the 20th century; major retrospectives at the Musée Fabre and Musée d’Orsay (2016–2017) broadened international recognition
- **pronunciation:**
  - fray-day-REEK ba-ZEE
