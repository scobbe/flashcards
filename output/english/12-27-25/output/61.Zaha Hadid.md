## Zaha Hadid
---
- **definition:**
  - iraqi-british architect celebrated for radical, flowing buildings and innovative use of geometry
  - used informally to refer to a building or design by her or in her distinctive style
- **etymology:**
  - arabic personal name; given name "zaha" (زها) from a verb meaning "to be splendid, to shine" or "to flourish"
  - surname "hadid" (حديد) is arabic for "iron," widely used as a family name in arabic-speaking regions
  - the full name entered english via transliteration from arabic, typically without diacritics
- **history:**
  - born 1950 in baghdad; trained at london’s architectural association; founded zaha hadid architects in 1980 amid the deconstructivist wave
  - drew global attention in moma’s 1988 "deconstructivist architecture" show; early landmark: vitra fire station (1993)
  - first woman to win the pritzker prize (2004); later icons include maxxi (2009) and the london aquatics centre (2011/12); received the riba gold medal in 2016, the year she died
- **pronunciation:**
  - ZAH-ha ha-DEED
