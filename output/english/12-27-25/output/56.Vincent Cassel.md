## Vincent Cassel
---
- **definition:**
  - a French actor and producer known for intense, complex roles in both French and international films
  - notable credits include La Haine, Mesrine, Ocean's Twelve/Thirteen, Black Swan, and Irreversible
- **etymology:**
  - given name from French Vincent, ultimately from Latin Vincentius, from vincens (present participle of vincere) meaning “conquering”
  - surname from French place-name Cassel (Nord), ultimately from Latin castellum meaning “fort, castle”
- **history:**
  - born 1966 in Paris; broke through with La Haine (1995), a landmark film capturing mid‑1990s French banlieue tensions and widely noted in English‑language media after its festival success
  - gained broad international recognition with Ocean's Twelve (2004) and Black Swan (2010); won the César Award for Best Actor in 2009 for portraying gangster Jacques Mesrine in the two‑part 2008 biopic
  - continued crossover work into the 2010s–2020s (e.g., Eastern Promises 2007, Jason Bourne 2016, Westworld season 3 in 2020), cementing a career bridging French cinema and Hollywood
- **pronunciation:**
  - VIN-sent ka-SELL
