## Extrude
---
- **definition:**
  - to push or force something out, especially through a narrow opening
  - to shape a material by forcing it through a die (as in making pipes, rods, or noodles)
  - of magma or lava: to be forced out onto the earth’s surface
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin extrudere “to drive out, thrust out,” from ex- “out” + trudere “to push”
  - entered English through learned use modeled directly on the Latin verb stem
  - related to intrude, protrude, and obtrude, all from the root trudere “push”
- **history:**
  - first recorded in English in the early 1600s, used generally to mean “drive or force out”
  - the industrial sense grew after 1797, when Joseph Bramah patented a hydraulic press to extrude lead pipe during the early Industrial Revolution
  - by the early to mid-1900s, extrusion became central in aluminum and plastics manufacturing, and the verb spread in engineering and geology (e.g., extruded lava)
- **pronunciation:**
  - ek-STROOD
