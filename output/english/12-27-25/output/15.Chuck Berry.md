## Chuck Berry
---
- **definition:**
  - american guitarist, singer, and songwriter who helped define early rock and roll in the 1950s
  - cultural icon known for driving guitar riffs, witty storytelling, and the onstage 'duck walk'
- **etymology:**
  - chuck is a familiar English nickname for charles; charles comes via Old French Charles and Latin Carolus from Germanic Karl, meaning 'man' or 'free man'
  - berry is an English surname from Old English berie/beri, meaning 'berry', likely for someone who lived near or gathered berries
  - in some cases berry reflects a place‑name origin from Old English byrig, 'fortified place' (modern 'bury'), with 'Berry' as a spelling variant
- **history:**
  - born in 1926 in St. Louis; broke through in 1955 with 'Maybellene,' blending rhythm and blues with country and shaping early rock and roll
  - scored seminal late‑1950s hits like 'Roll Over Beethoven' (1956) and 'Johnny B. Goode' (1958); his riffs and teen‑focused lyrics influenced the Beatles and Rolling Stones
  - after a 1959 Mann Act conviction and career dips, he enjoyed revivals; inducted into the inaugural Rock & Roll Hall of Fame in 1986; died in 2017, leaving a foundational legacy
- **pronunciation:**
  - CHUK BEH-ree
