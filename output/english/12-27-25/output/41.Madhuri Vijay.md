## Madhuri Vijay
---
- **definition:**
  - an Indian author of contemporary English-language fiction, best known for the novel The Far Field (2019)
- **etymology:**
  - madhuri comes from Sanskrit madhurī (from mādhurya), meaning “sweetness” or “charm,” used as a female given name in Hindi and related languages
  - vijay comes from Sanskrit vijaya, meaning “victory” or “triumph,” used as both a given name and a surname in South Asia
  - the full name follows common Indian naming patterns pairing a given name with a family name
- **history:**
  - her debut novel The Far Field appeared in 2019 and won the JCB Prize for Literature the same year, marking a notable moment in 21st‑century Indian English fiction
  - the novel’s setting and themes—partly in Kashmir—resonated with ongoing social and political discussions in India in the late 2010s
  - since 2019, she has been cited among emerging Indian writers in English, with growing international readership through the 2020s
- **pronunciation:**
  - mah-DOO-ree vi-JAY
