## The Bank of East Asia
---
- **definition:**
  - a Hong Kong–based banking and financial services company, commonly known as BEA
  - a proper noun naming a regional bank headquartered in Hong Kong with operations across Greater China and Asia
- **etymology:**
  - a compound proper name formed from “bank” + the prepositional phrase “of East Asia,” identifying a financial institution tied to a region
  - bank comes via French from Italian banca, “bench, moneylender’s table,” the workplace of early lenders
  - east is from Old English ēast, “toward the sunrise,” and Asia comes via Latin Asia from Greek Asia, a name for lands to the east of Europe
- **history:**
  - founded in 1918 in British Hong Kong to serve local Chinese merchants and businesses who were underserved by foreign banks
  - endured major upheavals—the Great Depression and the Japanese occupation of Hong Kong (1941–45)—and rebuilt postwar, growing under Li family leadership (notably Sir David Li) in the late 20th century
  - during the 2008 global financial crisis it faced rumor-driven withdrawals but stabilized with official reassurances; in the 2010s–2020s it emphasized regional expansion and digital banking
- **pronunciation:**
  - thuh BANK uv eest AY-zhuh
