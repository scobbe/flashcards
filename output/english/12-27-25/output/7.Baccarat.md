## Baccarat
---
- **definition:**
  - a casino card game where players bet on the player, banker, or tie, and the hand closest to nine wins
  - a luxury brand of fine crystal glassware made by the French company based in the town of Baccarat
- **etymology:**
  - from French baccarat, probably from Italian baccarà/baccara ‘zero,’ referring to tens and face cards counting as zero; ultimate origin uncertain
  - the glassware sense is a proper name from Baccarat, a town in northeastern France; English borrowed the French name unchanged
- **history:**
  - the game likely arose in 15th-century Italy, spread to French nobility, and entered English by the mid-1800s; in 1891 the “Royal Baccarat Scandal” at Tranby Croft made it widely known in Britain
  - 20th-century casino variants (chemin de fer, punto banco) flourished; punto banco reached Las Vegas in 1959 and later became a high-roller staple, especially in Macau in the 2000s
  - the crystal house Baccarat was founded in 1764 in Lorraine; by the 19th century its crystal was prized by European courts and luxury retailers worldwide
- **pronunciation:**
  - bah-kuh-RAH
