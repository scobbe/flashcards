## Impecunious
---
- **definition:**
  - having little or no money; poor
  - lacking funds to pay for something or meet expenses
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin pecunia “money, wealth,” ultimately from pecu “cattle,” an early measure of wealth
  - formed with the negative prefix im- (from Latin in- “not”) + pecunious (“wealthy, moneyed”)
  - related to pecuniary (“relating to money”), sharing the same Latin root
- **history:**
  - first recorded in English in the late 1500s (c. 1590s), as Latin-based learned vocabulary expanded in Early Modern English
  - common in 18th–19th-century British writing, often describing cash-strapped gentlemen, students, or artists in Victorian and Regency settings
  - in modern use it appears in formal and legal contexts (e.g., “impecunious litigant”) and sometimes carries a mildly wry or genteel tone
- **pronunciation:**
  - im-pih-KYOO-nee-us
