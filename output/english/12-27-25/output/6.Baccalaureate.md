## Baccalaureate
---
- **definition:**
  - a bachelor's degree; an undergraduate academic degree
  - a religious or inspirational service held for graduating students, usually before commencement
  - an academic exam or program qualifying secondary students for university admission (e.g., the French or International Baccalaureate)
- **etymology:**
  - from Medieval Latin baccalaureatus “degree of bachelor,” from baccalaureus “bachelor” (junior student)
  - spelling reshaped by folk association with Latin bacca lauri “laurel berry” and laureatus “crowned with laurel”
  - also influenced by French baccalauréat, the term for the national secondary-school leaving exam
- **history:**
  - first recorded in English in the early 17th century (c. 1620s) to mean a bachelor’s degree
  - in 1808, France established the baccalauréat as the state exam for university entry under Napoleon
  - the “baccalaureate service” grew in 19th‑century North America as a pre‑commencement sermon; since 1968, the International Baccalaureate has expanded the term’s global profile
- **pronunciation:**
  - bak-uh-LOR-ee-it
