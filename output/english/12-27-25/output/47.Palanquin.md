## Palanquin
---
- **definition:**
  - a covered litter with a seat or bed for one person, carried by bearers on poles
  - by extension, a sedan-like chair used for transport in South and East Asia
- **etymology:**
  - from Portuguese palanquim, adopted by European traders in the 1500s
  - from Hindi/Urdu pālkī “litter,” ultimately tracing to Sanskrit paryaṅka “bed, couch”
  - english kept the Portuguese-style spelling; the older variant palankeen reflects the Indian pronunciation
- **history:**
  - first recorded in English in the late 16th century, as Europeans described elite transport in Mughal India and other parts of Asia
  - widely used in the 17th–19th centuries for officials and travelers; in British India, palanquin “dak” relay services carried mail and passengers
  - declined in the late 19th century with roads, carriages, and railways; now mainly ceremonial in weddings and religious processions
- **pronunciation:**
  - pal-uhn-KEEN
