## Willie Nelson
---
- **definition:**
  - an American country singer-songwriter, guitarist, and actor known for his distinctive voice and phrasing
  - a leading figure of the 1970s outlaw country movement and a lasting icon of Texas music and counterculture
- **etymology:**
  - willie is a diminutive of william, from Old French Guillaume, from Germanic Wilhelm meaning "will/desire" + "helmet/protection"
  - nelson is an English/Scots patronymic surname meaning "son of Neil" (from Gaelic Niall "champion"), sometimes also "son of Nell" (a pet form of Eleanor/Helen)
  - the full name follows the common English pattern of a given name plus a patronymic surname
- **history:**
  - born in 1933 in Abbott, Texas; wrote early classics like "Crazy" (1961) for Patsy Cline before rising as a performer
  - helped drive the 1970s outlaw country wave; albums such as Red Headed Stranger (1975) and Wanted! The Outlaws (1976) expanded countryâ€™s audience
  - co-founded Farm Aid in 1985; faced an IRS tax case in 1990 and issued The IRS Tapes (1992); later honors include Country Music Hall of Fame (1993), Kennedy Center Honors (1998), Gershwin Prize (2015), and Rock & Roll Hall of Fame (2023)
- **pronunciation:**
  - WILL-ee NEL-sun
