## Shiba Kōkan
---
- **definition:**
  - a Japanese Edo-period painter, printmaker, and scholar who pioneered Western-style oil painting and copperplate engraving in Japan
  - the artist’s adopted art name under which he signed works; also romanized as Shiba Kokan
- **etymology:**
  - japanese proper name written 司馬 江漢; Shiba (司馬) is a Sino-Japanese surname from Chinese Sima meaning “master of the horse”
  - kōkan (江漢) combines kō “river/Yangtze” and kan “Han (people/dynasty),” a classical Chinese toponym; used as his art name
  - formed in the Edo period as a gō (art name) using Sino-Japanese (Chinese-derived) readings of the characters
- **history:**
  - born 1747, died 1818, during the late Edo period when Japan cautiously engaged European science via Dutch traders at Nagasaki
  - in the early 1780s he created some of Japan’s first signed copperplate engravings and explored oil painting and linear perspective
  - in 1799 he published writings on Western art and astronomy (e.g., Seiyō Gadan), and later Meiji and 20th‑century scholars hailed him as a bridge between ukiyo‑e and Western techniques
- **pronunciation:**
  - SHEE-bah KOH-kahn
