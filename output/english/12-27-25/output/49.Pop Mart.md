## Pop Mart
---
- **definition:**
  - a Chinese designer-toy brand (stylized POP MART) known for “blind box” collectibles and character figurines
  - by extension, a retail outlet or vending machine operated by that brand selling pop-culture toys
- **etymology:**
  - a modern English coinage: pop (short for popular; linked to pop culture) + mart (meaning market/store)
  - mart is a shortened form of market, from Middle English and Middle Dutch markt, ultimately from Latin mercatus “trade”
- **history:**
  - founded in 2010 in Beijing by Wang Ning; broke out in 2016 after licensing Kenny Wong’s “Molly,” which helped ignite China’s designer-toy craze
  - rolled out “Robo Shop” vending machines and boutiques in the late 2010s; listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in Dec 2020 amid a blind-box boom
  - from 2021 onward, expanded globally (across Asia, Europe, and North America) and added popular IPs like Dimoo and Skullpanda, while sparking debate over blind-box consumerism
- **pronunciation:**
  - POP MART
