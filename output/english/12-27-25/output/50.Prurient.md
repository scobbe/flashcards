## Prurient
---
- **definition:**
  - having or showing too much interest in sexual matters
  - causing or meant to arouse sexual desire
  - formerly: eagerly longing or figuratively “itching” for something
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin pruriens (present participle of prurire), literally “itching; longing”
  - entered English in the 17th century via learned/medical Latin; related to pruritus (“itching”)
  - built from Latin root prur- (“itch”) + participial suffix -ient (“-ing, being”)
- **history:**
  - first attested in English in the 1650s, amid heavy borrowing from Latin in medical and moral discourse
  - in the 18th–19th centuries, used by critics and censors to brand erotic literature and theater as improper
  - became a key term in U.S. obscenity law—“prurient interest” appears in Roth v. United States (1957) and Miller v. California (1973)
- **pronunciation:**
  - PROOR-ee-ent
