## Fernando Botero
---
- **definition:**
  - a Colombian painter and sculptor famed for voluptuous, oversized figures and satirical, humanist scenes
  - the artist behind the style nicknamed “Boterismo,” using exaggerated volume to explore power, humor, and pain
- **etymology:**
  - spanish given name Fernando from medieval “Ferdinand,” with Germanic roots fardi/farð “journey” + nand “brave,” literally “bold traveler”
  - spanish surname Botero from bota “leather wineskin; boot” + -ero “maker/seller,” meaning “wineskin maker” or “leatherworker”
  - formed as a Spanish given name plus an occupational surname; Botero is the paternal family name
- **history:**
  - born April 19, 1932, in Medellín; studied in Madrid and Florence (early 1950s); MoMA’s 1961 purchase of Mona Lisa, Age Twelve catapulted his international reputation
  - from the 1970s he created monumental bronzes for public spaces worldwide; his Medellín Bird was bombed in 1995 and later paired with a new Bird of Peace in 2000
  - in 2004–2005 he painted the Abu Ghraib series protesting Iraq War abuses; he died September 15, 2023, leaving a widely recognizable, accessible body of work
- **pronunciation:**
  - fer-NAN-do bo-TEH-ro
