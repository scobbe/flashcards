## Locarno Film Festival
---
- **definition:**
  - an annual international film festival in Locarno, Switzerland, noted for auteur cinema and large open-air screenings in the Piazza Grande
  - the event’s highest award is the Golden Leopard (Pardo d'oro)
- **etymology:**
  - locarno from the Italian name of the Swiss town; attested in medieval Latin as Locarnum/Leocarno; ultimate origin uncertain, likely pre-Roman
  - film from early Germanic/Old English film/filmen meaning 'membrane' or 'thin skin,' later applied to motion-picture stock and movies
  - festival from Middle French festival and Latin festum meaning 'feast' or 'holiday'; the English name is a rendering of Italian Festival del film Locarno
- **history:**
  - founded in 1946 in post–World War II Europe amid a boom of international film festivals, making it one of the oldest still running
  - built a reputation for championing art-house and world cinema; open-air screenings on the Piazza Grande became a defining feature
  - the Golden Leopard became its hallmark top prize; in 2017 the event rebranded internationally as 'Locarno Festival' while remaining widely known in English as the Locarno Film Festival
- **pronunciation:**
  - loh-KAR-noh film FES-ti-vuhl
