## Cosseted
---
- **definition:**
  - treated with too much care and protection; pampered or overindulged
  - sheltered from difficulty or competition so that life feels unusually easy
- **etymology:**
  - from english dialect noun "cosset" meaning "a pet lamb raised by hand" (16th century), origin uncertain
  - verb "to cosset" formed from the noun; past participle "cosseted" used as an adjective meaning "pampered"
  - possibly influenced by old english "cot/cote" meaning "cottage; sheepfold," though this link is not certain
- **history:**
  - first recorded in english in the 1500s as a noun for a hand-reared "pet lamb"; the verb "to cosset" appears in the early 1600s
  - by the 17thâ€“18th centuries the meaning broadened to people, especially children, as "pamper" or "overprotect," mirroring domestic ideals of care
  - in the 20th century it became common in journalism and political commentary to describe "cosseted" groups or industries protected from hardship or competition
- **pronunciation:**
  - KOSS-uh-tid
