## Blue Girl Beer
---
- **definition:**
  - a brand of pale lager brewed in Germany and widely sold in Hong Kong and Macau
  - informally, a bottle or can of that brand (e.g., “two Blue Girl, please”)
- **etymology:**
  - a compound of the English words blue + girl + beer, literally “blue girl beer”
  - blue from Old French bleu (Germanic origin) meaning the color blue; girl from Middle English girle/gurle meaning a young person (later a female child); beer from Germanic sources (German Bier, Old English beor)
- **history:**
  - 1906: introduced to Hong Kong by Jebsen & Co., importing a German-brewed lager for the local market
  - distribution was disrupted during the World Wars, with postwar resumption as Hong Kong’s beer market expanded
  - by the 2000s–2010s it became one of Hong Kong’s best-selling beers, commonly seen in convenience stores, cha chaan tengs, and bars
- **pronunciation:**
  - BLUE GIRL BEER
