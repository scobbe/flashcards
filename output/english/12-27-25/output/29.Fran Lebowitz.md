## Fran Lebowitz
---
- **definition:**
  - an American essayist and social commentator known for sharp, sardonic observations of New York City life
  - a public speaker and media personality featured in documentaries and talk shows
- **etymology:**
  - fran is a short form of frances, from Latin Franciscus “Frenchman; a Frank,” ultimately from the Germanic tribal name of the Franks
  - lebowitz is an Ashkenazic surname from the Yiddish/German given name Leib/Loeb “lion” + Slavic patronymic -witz/-owicz “son of,” meaning “son of Leib”
- **history:**
  - born in 1950; rose in 1970s New York writing for Andy Warhol’s Interview magazine; published Metropolitan Life (1978) and Social Studies (1981)
  - became a noted New York wit despite a long-standing writer’s block; her essays were collected in The Fran Lebowitz Reader (1994)
  - renewed prominence with Martin Scorsese’s documentaries Public Speaking (2010) and Pretend It’s a City (2021), highlighting her cultural commentary
- **pronunciation:**
  - FRAN LEE-buh-wits
