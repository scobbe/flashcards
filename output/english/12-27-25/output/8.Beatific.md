## Beatific
---
- **definition:**
  - showing a calm, blissful, or saintly joy; radiantly happy
  - in Christian theology, relating to the perfect blessedness of seeing God (the Beatific Vision)
- **etymology:**
  - from Late Latin beātificus “making blessed, bringing happiness,” formed from beātus “happy, blessed” + -ficus from facere “to make”
  - entered English via ecclesiastical Latin and French (béatifique), preserving the religious sense of blessed happiness
- **history:**
  - early 17th century: first recorded in English sermons and theological prose to describe heavenly blessedness and the Beatific Vision
  - rooted in medieval scholastic thought; Thomas Aquinas (13th c.) discussed the visio beatifica, and Pope Benedict XII’s bull Benedictus Deus (1336) defined its timing
  - by the 19th century it broadened into secular use to describe a serene, blissful expression (e.g., “a beatific smile”)
- **pronunciation:**
  - bee-uh-TIF-ik
