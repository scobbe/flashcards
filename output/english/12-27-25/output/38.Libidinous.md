## Libidinous
---
- **definition:**
  - showing strong or excessive sexual desire; lustful
  - relating to sexual drive or appetite
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin libidinosus “lustful; full of desire,” built on libido “desire, longing, lust”
  - formed with the suffix -ous (from Latin -osus “full of”) and entering English in the early modern period, sometimes via French libidineux
- **history:**
  - recorded in English by the early 17th century, amid intense religious and moral debates in early modern England where writers condemned “libidinous” conduct
  - used widely in 18th and 19th century moral, medical, and legal discourse, including Victorian-era discussions of obscenity such as those surrounding the 1868 Hicklin test
  - after Sigmund Freud popularized libido in the early 1900s, the root gained scientific currency while libidinous remained a literary or moralizing term and declined in everyday speech
- **pronunciation:**
  - lih-BID-uh-nuss
