## Magpies
---
- **definition:**
  - black-and-white, crow-like birds known for loud chattering and bold behavior
  - a person who habitually collects small or shiny things; also, a chatterbox
  - often capitalized, a nickname for sports teams that wear black-and-white colors
- **etymology:**
  - from Middle English pie/pye “magpie,” via Old French pie, from Latin pica “magpie”
  - magpie formed by adding Mag—short for Margaret, used for a talkative woman—to pie; literally “Mag + pie”
  - magpies is the regular English plural of magpie
- **history:**
  - the bird was called pie/pye in English by c. 1300; magpie appears in the late 1500s, and Shakespeare (1606) wrote “magot-pies” in Macbeth
  - in the 1800s, European settlers applied the name to the unrelated Australian magpie, a common black-and-white songbird
  - from the late 19th century, black‑and‑white sports teams adopted Magpies as a nickname (e.g., Notts County, Newcastle United, Collingwood)
- **pronunciation:**
  - MAG-piez
