## Goethe-Institut
---
- **definition:**
  - germany’s official cultural institute that promotes german language and culture worldwide
  - a global network of centers offering language courses, exams, libraries, and cultural programs
- **etymology:**
  - german compound of Goethe + Institut, literally “Goethe Institute”
  - “Goethe” is the surname of Johann Wolfgang von Goethe, the celebrated german writer after whom the institute is named
  - “Institut” comes via french institut from latin institūtum “established arrangement; institute,” from instituere “to set up, establish”
- **history:**
  - 1951: founded in munich, west germany, as a successor to earlier cultural-outreach efforts (e.g., the prewar deutsche akademie) to promote language teaching abroad
  - 1950s–1989: expanded during the cold war as a key instrument of cultural diplomacy, opening centers worldwide and developing standardized german-as-foreign-language exams
  - 2001–2004: merged with inter nationes (briefly styled goethe-institut inter nationes), then reverted to goethe-institut; after reunification it grew across central and eastern europe and today runs a global network of institutes and partners
- **pronunciation:**
  - GUR-tuh in-sti-TOOT
