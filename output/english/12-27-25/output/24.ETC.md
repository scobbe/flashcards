## ETC
---
- **definition:**
  - abbreviation meaning 'and other similar things; and so on,' used to end a list
  - sometimes used as a noun (etceteras) to mean odds and ends or additional, unspecified items
- **etymology:**
  - from Latin et cētera, literally 'and the rest,' where et means 'and' and cētera is the neuter plural of 'other'
  - adopted into English through learned and clerical Latin; abbreviated as etc. from the initial letters of the phrase
  - the single-word form etcetera arose in early modern English by compounding the Latin words
- **history:**
  - recorded in English by the 15th century, when scribes used the Latin phrase to shorten repetitive lists
  - the printed abbreviation etc. became common by the 17th century as printers standardized punctuation and list conventions
  - usage expanded from scholarly and administrative writing to everyday prose; modern style guides often recommend using specific examples instead of overusing etc.
- **pronunciation:**
  - et SET-uh-ruh
