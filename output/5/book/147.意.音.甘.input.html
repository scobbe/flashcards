Ideogram (指事) – something (perhaps sweet) in an open mouth; the original drawing is 𠁿. Original form of 含. See also 敢, 旨, 香, and 音.

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Sino-Tibetan *s-klum (“sweet”) (Schuessler, 2007; STEDT). Cognate with Mizo thlum, Lepcha [script needed] (khlyam).

Possibly related to 含 (OC *ɡɯːm, “to keep in mouth”) and 甜 (OC *l'iːm, “sweet”), 柑 (MC kam, “mandarin orange”) (Schuessler, 2007).

Definitions

[edit]

甘

  1. sweet
    甘甜  ―  gāntián  ―  sweet
    甘蔗  ―  gānzhe  ―  sugar cane
  2. pleasant; satisfactory
  3. willingly; readily
    甘心  ―  gānxīn  ―  to be willing to
    甘願／甘愿  ―  gānyuàn  ―  willingly
  4. (Cantonese, ironic) bitter; difficult to bear
    Alternative form: 金 (gam1)
    輸到好甘／输到好甘 [Cantonese]  ―  syu1 dou3 hou2 gam1 [Jyutping]  ―  to lose tremendously
    畀人𨃩鑊甘／畀人𨃩镬甘 [Cantonese]  ―  bei2 jan4 sin3 wok6 gam1 [Jyutping]  ―  (please add an English translation of this usage example)
    鑊鑊新鮮鑊鑊甘 [Cantonese, trad.]
    镬镬新鲜镬镬甘 [Cantonese, simp.]
    wok6 wok6 san1 sin1 wok6 wok6 gam1 [Jyutping]
    (please add an English translation of this usage example)
  5. a surname
    甘寧／甘宁  ―  Gān Níng  ―  Gan Ning (military general serving under Sun Quan in the late Eastern Han dynasty)
  6. short for 甘肅／甘肃 (Gānsù, “Gansu”)
    Synonym: 隴 / 陇 (lǒng)
