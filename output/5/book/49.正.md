## proper; formal
### sub-word
#### formal
---
- **traditional:** 正
- **simplified:** 正
- **pronunciation:** zhèng
- **definition:** proper; formal
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 正确(確) (zhèngquè, "correct and accurate")
  - 正式 (zhèngshì, "formal style")
  - 正规(規) (zhèngguī, "standard form")
  - 正派 (zhèngpài, "upright and decent")
- **etymology (正):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic: phonetic: 丁 (dīng, "fourth heavenly stem") + semantic: 止 (zhǐ, "foot") → 正 (zhèng, "correct").
  - **interpretation:** 止 (zhǐ, "foot") supplies the semantic field of movement, while 丁 (dīng, "fourth heavenly stem") provides the sound. The original sense was ‘to march; go on campaign’, later differentiated as 征 (zhēng, "to go on campaign"), while 正 generalized to ‘upright, correct’ through association with straightness and proper alignment.
  - **component characters:**
    - 丁
    - 止
    - 正
