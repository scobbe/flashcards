## direction; side
### sub-word
#### the West; western
---
- **traditional:** 方
- **simplified:** 方
- **pronunciation:** fāng
- **definition:** direction; side
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 东(東)方 (dōng fāng, "the east")
  - 前方 (qián fāng, "the front")
  - 方面 (fāng miàn, "aspect or side")
  - 甲方 (jiǎ fāng, "Party A")
- **etymology (方):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 一 (yī, "one") + 刀 (dāo, "sword") → original form of 芒 (máng, "awn; tip") → borrowed to mean "side".
  - **interpretation:** Combining 一 (yī, "one") and 刀 (dāo, "sword") depicts a blade’s tip or edge, yielding the sense "blade tip". That graph was later borrowed and broadened to mean "side" and by extension direction/aspect, matching modern senses.
  - **component characters:**
    - 一
    - 刀
    - 芒
