## river or stream
### sub-word
#### traditional music composition
---
- **traditional:** 江
- **simplified:** 江
- **pronunciation:** jiāng
- **definition:** river or stream
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 长(長)江 (Cháng Jiāng, "Yangtze River")
  - 江南 (Jiāng nán, "region south of the Yangtze")
  - 江口 (Jiāng kǒu, "river mouth")
  - 江畔 (Jiāng pàn, "river bank")
- **etymology (江):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic: semantic: 氵 (shuǐ, "water") + phonetic: 工 (gōng, "work").
  - **interpretation:** The semantic determinative 氵 marks the water domain, while 工 serves as the phonetic, reflecting an Old Chinese reading that led to modern jiāng. The character originally named the Yangtze and later generalized to mean a large river. Some sources suggest the word may be a substrate loan, but the graph still follows the standard phono-semantic pattern.
  - **component characters:**
    - 水
    - 工
