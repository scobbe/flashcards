## everything
### sub-word
#### may all your wishes come true
---
- **traditional:** 萬事
- **simplified:** 万事
- **pronunciation:** wànshì
- **definition:** everything
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 万(萬)事亨通 (wàn shì hēng tōng, "everything goes smoothly")
  - 万(萬)事如意 (wàn shì rú yì, "all wishes fulfilled")
  - 万(萬)事大吉 (wàn shì dà jí, "great fortune in everything")
  - 万(萬)事俱备(備)，只欠东(東)风(風) (wàn shì jù bèi, zhǐ qiàn dōng fēng, "all ready, missing key element")
- **etymology (萬事):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 万(萬) (wàn, "ten thousand") + 事 (shì, "affair").
  - **interpretation:** 万(萬) (wàn, "ten thousand") conveys an exhaustive or myriad quantity, while 事 (shì, "affair") denotes matters or things. Together they literally mean ten-thousand affairs, yielding the idiomatic sense of everything in modern usage.
  - **component characters:**
    - 万
    - 萬
    - 事
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%90%AC%E4%BA%8B
  - **simplification rule (万事):** The simplified form replaces the traditional component 萬 with 万 in the first character while 事 remains unchanged, reducing strokes and regularizing the graph.
