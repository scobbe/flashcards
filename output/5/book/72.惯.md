## accustomed; habit
### sub-word
#### to be accustomed to; habit
---
- **traditional:** 慣
- **simplified:** 惯
- **pronunciation:** guàn
- **definition:** accustomed; habit
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 惯(慣)例 (guàn lì, "convention")
  - 惯(慣)性 (guàn xìng, "inertia")
  - 惯(慣)用语(語) (guàn yòng yǔ, "idiomatic expression")
  - 惯(慣)犯 (guàn fàn, "habitual offender")
- **etymology (慣):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic: semantic: 心 (xīn, "heart") + phonetic: 贯(貫) (guàn, "string through").
  - **interpretation:** 心 serves as the semantic determinative, pointing to feelings and mental states, matching senses like being accustomed and habits. 贯 provides the sound guàn and also evokes threading or continuity, which aligns with repeated, habitual action.
  - **component characters:**
    - 心
    - 贯
    - 貫
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%85%A3
  - **simplification rule (惯):** Simplified by replacing the phonetic 貫 with its simplified form 贯 within 慣, reducing strokes and yielding 惯.
