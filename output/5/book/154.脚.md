## foot
---
- **traditional:** 腳
- **simplified:** 脚
- **pronunciation:** jiǎo
- **definition:** foot
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 脚(腳)疼 (jiǎo téng, "foot hurts")
  - 脚(腳)踝 (jiǎo huái, "ankle")
  - 脚(腳)趾 (jiǎo zhǐ, "toe")
  - 脚(腳)步 (jiǎo bù, "footsteps")
- **etymology (腳):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic: semantic: 肉 (ròu, "meat") + phonetic: 却(卻) (què, "retreat").
  - **interpretation:** Formed as a phono-semantic character: the semantic determinative 肉 (ròu, "meat") marks a body part, matching the modern sense of a foot or leg. The phonetic 却(卻) (què, "retreat") provides the approximate sound while the semantic side anchors the meaning to the body.
  - **component characters:**
    - 肉 (ròu, "meat")
    - 却(卻) (què, "retreat")
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%85%B3
  - **simplification rule (脚):** The traditional phonetic 卻 was simplified to 却, reducing strokes while retaining the flesh radical form on the left.
