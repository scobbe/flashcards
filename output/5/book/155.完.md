## to finish
---
- **traditional:** 完
- **simplified:** 完
- **pronunciation:** wán
- **definition:** to finish
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 我写(寫)完了 (Wǒ xiě wán le, "I finished writing")
  - 做完作业(業) (zuò wán zuò yè, "finish homework")
  - 说(說)完 (shuō wán, "finish speaking")
  - 用完 (yòng wán, "use up")
- **etymology (完):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic: semantic: 宀 (mián, "roof") + phonetic: 元 (yuán, "origin").
  - **interpretation:** The character combines 宀 as the semantic determinative for house/enclosure, suggesting intactness or completeness, with 元 as the phonetic, giving the reading wán. This aligns with the modern senses of “complete/finish,” while 元 supplies the sound and reinforces the idea of wholeness.
  - **component characters:**
    - 宀 (mián, "roof")
    - 元 (yuán, "origin")
