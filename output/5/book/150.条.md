## measure word for long objects
---
- **traditional:** 條
- **simplified:** 条
- **pronunciation:** tiáo
- **definition:** measure word for long objects
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 一条(條)裤(褲)子 (yī tiáo kù zi, "a pair of pants")
  - 一条(條)红(紅)鱼(魚) (yī tiáo hóng yú, "a red fish")
  - 一条(條)蛇 (yī tiáo shé, "a snake")
  - 一条(條)河 (yī tiáo hé, "a river")
- **etymology (條):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** phono-semantic: phonetic: 攸 (yōu, "where") + semantic: 木 (mù, "wood") → small branch.
  - **interpretation:** 木 serves as the semantic determinative signaling the domain of wood and branches, while 攸 supplies the pronunciation. The original sense ‘small branch’ extended to ‘strip; long, thin object’, which underlies the modern classifier use for long, thin things.
  - **component characters:**
    - 攸
    - 木
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%A2%9D
  - **simplification rule (条):** Adopted the historical variant 条 with fewer strokes, replacing the complex 條 form by collapsing the 攸+木 structure into a simpler 夂 over 木 arrangement to reduce strokes.
