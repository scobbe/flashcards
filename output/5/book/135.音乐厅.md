## concert hall
---
- **traditional:** 音樂廳
- **simplified:** 音乐厅
- **pronunciation:** yīnyuètīng
- **definition:** concert hall
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 音乐(樂)厅(廳)很大 (yīn yuè tīng hěn dà, "concert hall is big")
  - 音乐(樂)厅(廳)门(門)口 (yīn yuè tīng mén kǒu, "hall entrance")
  - 音乐(樂)厅(廳)演出 (yīn yuè tīng yǎn chū, "concert performance")
  - 音乐(樂)厅(廳)门(門)票 (yīn yuè tīng mén piào, "admission tickets")
- **etymology (音樂廳):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 音 (yīn, "sound") + 乐(樂) (yuè, "music") → semantic: 音乐(音樂) (yīnyuè, "music") + 厅(廳) (tīng, "hall").
  - **interpretation:** 音 (yīn, "sound") and 乐(樂) (yuè, "music") combine as 音乐(音樂) (yīnyuè, "music"). Adding 厅(廳) (tīng, "hall") yields a music hall, i.e., a venue for concerts. All parts are semantic and transparently denote a hall for musical performances.
  - **component characters:**
    - 音 (yīn, "sound")
    - 乐(樂) (yuè, "music")
    - 厅(廳) (tīng, "hall")
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%9F%B3%E6%A8%82%E5%BB%B3
  - **simplification rule (音乐厅):** The phrase is simplified by replacing complex 樂 and 廳 with the simplified forms 乐 and 厅 to reduce strokes and regularize components.
