## as one wishes
### sub-word
#### may all your wishes come true
---
- **traditional:** 如意
- **simplified:** 如意
- **pronunciation:** rúyì
- **definition:** as one wishes
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 如意算盘(盤) (rú yì suàn pán, "wishful thinking")
  - 如意郎君 (rú yì láng jūn, "ideal husband")
  - 如意结(結) (rú yì jié, "ruyi knot")
  - 如意金箍棒 (rú yì jīn gū bàng, "gold-banded staff")
- **etymology (如意):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 如 (rú, "as") + 意 (yì, "wish").
  - **interpretation:** 如 means ‘as/according to’, and 意 means ‘wish/desire’; combined, the compound means that things go according to one’s wishes. The noun sense for a ceremonial sceptre arises as an auspicious object named for this idea, symbolizing the fulfillment of wishes.
  - **component characters:**
    - 如
    - 意
