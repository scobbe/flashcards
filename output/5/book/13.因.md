## cause; reason
### sub-word
#### because
---
- **traditional:** 因
- **simplified:** 因
- **pronunciation:** yīn
- **definition:** cause; reason
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 因此 (yīn cǐ, "therefore")
  - 因人而异(異) (yīn rén ér yì, "vary by person")
  - 原因 (yuán yīn, "cause; reason")
  - 因素 (yīn sù, "factor")
- **etymology (因):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 囗 (wéi, "sheet") + 大 (dà, "person").
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a person on a sheet or mat, originally referring to a mattress/cushion and serving as the original form of words like 裀 or 茵. By semantic extension from “lying/resting on,” it came to mean “to rely on; be based on,” which further developed into causal senses like “because” and “cause.” An alternative view interprets it as a man wearing clothing.
  - **component characters:**
    - 囗
    - 大
