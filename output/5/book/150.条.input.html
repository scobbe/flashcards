Phono-semantic compound (形聲 / 形声, OC *l'ɯːw): phonetic 攸 (OC *lɯw) + semantic 木. Originally it indicates a small branch, perhaps with some reference to a wooden stick (in the top component, there is a man warned by a hand holding a vertical stick). The bottom part of the character is unrelated to 茶.

Etymology

[edit]

Related to 悠 (OC *lɯw, “remote; distant”), 修 (OC *slɯw, “long and thin”) (Schuessler, 2007).

Definitions

[edit]

條

  1. strip; slip
    紙條／纸条  ―  zhǐtiáo  ―  slip of paper
  2. twig; branch
    柳條／柳条  ―  liǔtiáo  ―  willow branch
  3. long and thin objects
    麵條／面条  ―  miàntiáo  ―  noodles
  4. (of law or treaty) item; article; section; clause; condition
    條款／条款  ―  tiáokuǎn  ―  term; condition; clause; provision
    • 郵政服務人員犯前條之罪或刑法第二百零二條或第二百零四條關於郵票之罪者，加重其刑至二分之一。 [MSC, trad.]

Noun

[edit]

條

  1. chữ Hán form of điều
    1. something to say or tell
    2. something to think about or discuss
    3. (business, law, of a contract or legislative bill) a clause
    4. (law, of a constitution) an article
