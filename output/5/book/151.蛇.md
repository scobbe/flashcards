## snake
---
- **traditional:** 蛇
- **simplified:** 蛇
- **pronunciation:** shé
- **definition:** snake
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 怕蛇 (pà shé, "afraid of snakes")
  - 蛇头(頭) (shé tóu, "people smuggler")
  - 人蛇 (rén shé, "illegal immigrant")
  - 蛇毒 (shé dú, "snake venom")
- **etymology (蛇):**
  - **type:** pictogram → phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** pictogram of 它 (tā, "snake") → phono-semantic: semantic 虫(蟲) (chóng, "insects") + phonetic 它 (tā, "snake").
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a snake. Later the semantic determinative 虫(蟲) was added to mark the animal/reptile domain, while 它 was retained as the phonetic to indicate the sound. This composition directly supports the modern meaning "snake".
  - **component characters:**
    - 它
    - 虫
    - 蟲
