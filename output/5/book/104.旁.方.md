## direction
### sub-component
#### side
---
- **traditional:** 方
- **simplified:** 方
- **pronunciation:** fāng / fáng / páng / wǎng / fēng
- **definition:** direction
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 东(東)方 (dōngfāng, "the east")
  - 前方 (qiánfāng, "front area")
  - 上方 (shàngfāng, "upper side")
  - 下方 (xiàfāng, "lower side")
- **etymology (方):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 一 (yī, "one") + 刀 (dāo, "sword") → used as 芒 (máng, "awn") → borrowed to mean "side".
  - **interpretation:** Combining 一 (yī, "one") and 刀 (dāo, "sword") depicts a blade or its tip, explaining the early value now written as 芒 (máng, "awn"). The graph was later borrowed to mean "side/edge", which naturally extended to modern senses like "direction" and "region".
  - **component characters:**
    - 一
    - 刀
    - 芒
