## hall
### sub-word
#### concert hall
---
- **traditional:** 廳
- **simplified:** 厅
- **pronunciation:** tīng
- **definition:** hall
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 客厅(廳) (kè tīng, "living room")
  - 餐厅(廳) (cān tīng, "restaurant")
  - 办(辦)公厅(廳) (bàn gōng tīng, "general office")
  - 音乐(樂)厅(廳) (yīn yuè tīng, "concert hall")
- **etymology (廳):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 广(廣) (guǎng, "house") + phonetic: 听(聽) (tīng, "listen") → simplified graph uses 厂 (chǎng, "cliff") + 丁 (dīng, "fourth stem").
  - **interpretation:** 广(廣) serves as the semantic determinative indicating a building or room, matching the modern sense of a hall. 听(聽) supplies the sound value tīng and originally evoked the idea of ‘hearing cases’ in an official hall; the simplified form streamlines the graph to 厂 + 丁 while retaining the pronunciation and domain.
  - **component characters:**
    - 广
    - 廣
    - 听
    - 聽
    - 厂
    - 丁
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%BB%B3
  - **simplification rule (厅):** Form simplified by replacing the complex phonetic 聽 with 丁 and reducing the shelter component 广 to the shorthand 厂, cutting strokes while keeping the reading tīng.
