## sweet
### sub-component
#### sound
---
- **traditional:** 甘
- **simplified:** 甘
- **pronunciation:** gān
- **definition:** sweet
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 甘甜 (gāntián, "pleasantly sweet")
  - 甘心 (gānxīn, "be willing to")
  - 甘蔗 (gānzhè, "sugar cane")
  - 甘草 (gāncǎo, "licorice root")
- **etymology (甘):**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** ideogram: 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + 一 (yī, "one") indicating something in an open mouth → 甘 (gān, "sweet").
  - **interpretation:** It depicts an open mouth with an object inside, evoking the experience of tasting something pleasant, hence the sense "sweet". Some analyses connect the original graph to 含 (hán, "to keep in mouth"), reinforcing the mouth-based semantics.
  - **component characters:**
    - 口
    - 一
    - 甘
