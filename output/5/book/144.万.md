## ten thousand or myriad
### sub-word
#### may all your wishes come true
---
- **traditional:** 萬
- **simplified:** 万
- **pronunciation:** wàn
- **definition:** ten thousand or myriad
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 万(萬)一 (wàn yī, "just in case")
  - 万(萬)岁(歲) (wàn suì, "long live")
  - 万(萬)能 (wàn néng, "all-purpose")
  - 万(萬)里 (wàn lǐ, "ten thousand li")
- **etymology (萬):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram of 蠆 (chài, "scorpion") → semantic: extended to the numeral 'myriad; ten thousand'.
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a scorpion. The graph was later borrowed for the numeral sense 'ten thousand', while the insect sense was written with the insect radical as 蠆 to disambiguate. Some alternative origin theories exist for the simplified form, but the scorpion pictograph analysis is the mainstream view for the traditional character.
  - **component characters:**
    - 蠆
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%90%AC
  - **simplification rule (万):** Simplification adopted an ancient variant with radical removal and stroke reduction, replacing the complex 艹 over 禺 structure of 萬 with the concise graph 万.
