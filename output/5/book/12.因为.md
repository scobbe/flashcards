## because
---
- **traditional:** 因為
- **simplified:** 因为
- **pronunciation:** yīnwèi
- **definition:** because
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 因为(為)他感冒了，所以他没(沒)有来(來)上课(課)。(yīn wèi tā gǎn mào le, suǒ yǐ tā méi yǒu lái shàng kè, "because he caught a cold, he missed class")
  - 因为(為)天气(氣)不好，我们(們)取消了活动(動)。(yīn wèi tiān qì bù hǎo, wǒ men qǔ xiāo le huó dòng, "because of bad weather, we canceled the event")
  - 因为(為)工作忙，他没(沒)时(時)间(間)。(yīn wèi gōng zuò máng, tā méi shí jiān, "because he's busy at work")
  - 因为(為)交通堵塞，我们(們)迟(遲)到了。(yīn wèi jiāo tōng dǔ sè, wǒ men chí dào le, "because of traffic, we were late")
- **etymology (因為):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 因 (yīn, "cause") + 为(為) (wéi, "for") → 因为(因為) (yīn wèi, "because").
  - **interpretation:** 因 (yīn, "cause") names the reason, while 为(為) (wéi, "for") marks relation or agency. Together as 因为(因為), they literally mean “for a cause,” which developed into a preposition/conjunction expressing causation in modern usage.
  - **component characters:**
    - 因 (yīn, "cause")
    - 为(為) (wéi, "for")
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%9B%A0%E7%82%BA
  - **simplification rule (因为):** 为 replaces the traditional 為 through stroke reduction and component regularization, while 因 remains unchanged.
