## each
### sub-component
#### road
---
- **traditional:** 各
- **simplified:** 各
- **pronunciation:** gè / gě
- **definition:** each
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 各位 (gèwèi, "everyone present")
  - 各种(種)各样(樣) (gèzhǒnggèyàng, "all sorts")
  - 各自 (gèzì, "each individually")
  - 各行各业(業) (gèhánggèyè, "all trades and professions")
- **etymology (各):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 夂 (zhǐ, "sole of foot") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth") → morphological: 举(舉) (jǔ, "raise") + distributive suffix -k → distributive determiner "each".
  - **interpretation:** 夂 (zhǐ, "sole of foot") combines with 口 (kǒu, "mouth/entrance") to depict approaching an opening, hence the sense "to come, to enter". The graph was later applied to write a Sino-Tibetan distributive word derived from 举(舉) (jǔ, "raise") plus a suffix -k, so the modern meaning "each" is historical rather than graphically motivated. Some interpret 口 as an entrance opening to a cave or building.
  - **component characters:**
    - 夂 (zhǐ, "sole of foot")
    - 口 (kǒu, "mouth")
    - 举(舉) (jǔ, "raise")
