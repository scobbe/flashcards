## punishment
### sub-component
#### side
---
- **traditional:** 辛
- **simplified:** 辛
- **pronunciation:** xīn
- **definition:** punishment
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 辛苦 (xīn kǔ, "hardship")
  - 辛辣 (xīn là, "spicy and pungent")
  - 辛勤 (xīn qín, "diligent")
  - 辛酸 (xīn suān, "bitter hardship")
- **etymology (辛):**
  - **type:** pictogram
  - **description:** pictogram: depiction of a chisel or awl used to mark slaves and criminals → semantic extension to "bitter; painful; pungent".
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a sharp branding or tattooing tool used as punishment, conveying pain and harshness. This concrete image motivated later senses such as bitter hardship and pungent taste in modern usage. Some link the heavenly stem name to the same sharp-tool imagery.
  - **component characters:**
    - None, character is in atomic form
