Phono-semantic compound (形聲 / 形声, OC *krɯb): phonetic 及 (OC *ɡrɯb) + semantic 心 (“heart”).

Etymology

[edit]

Etymology not clear. Not a variant of 亟 (OC *kɯɡ, “urgent”) (Schuessler, 2007). Old Burmese ခုပ် (hkup, “swift, quick, urgent”) bears a resemblance to the Old Chinese lemma.

Definitions

[edit]

急

  1. rapid; quick
    急病  ―  jíbìng  ―  acute illness
    水流很急  ―  shuǐliú hěn jí  ―  the current is rapid
  2. urgent; pressing; critical
    急事  ―  jíshì  ―  urgent matter
  3. urgency; emergency
    當務之急／当务之急  ―  dāngwùzhījí  ―  pressing matter of the moment
  4. to hurry; to hasten
    唔使急。 [Cantonese]  ―  m4 sai2 gap1. [Jyutping]  ―  There's no need to rush/hurry.
    急住返屋企 [Cantonese]  ―  gap1 zyu6 faan1 uk1 kei2 [Jyutping]  ―  hurrying to get home
    1. (specifically) to need to use the toilet urgently
      急尿 [Cantonese]  ―  gap1 niu6 [Jyutping]  ―  to need to urinate
      我好急啊！ [Cantonese]  ―  ngo5 hou2 gap1 aa3! [Jyutping]  ―  I really need to go to the washroom!
  5. to be eager to help
    急公好義／急公好义  ―  jígōnghàoyì  ―  (please add an English translation of this usage example)
  6. irascible; hot-tempered
    急性子  ―  jíxìngzǐ  ―  impatient; impetuous
  7. to get angry; to take offense; to be ruffled
  8. to be anxious; to worry; to be worried

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • (Tokyo) きゅう [kyùú] (Heiban – [0])[2]
  • IPA(key): [kʲɨː]

Adjective

[edit]

急(きゅう) • (kyū) ←きふ (kifu)?-na (adnominal 急(きゅう)な (kyū na), adverbial 急(きゅう)に (kyū ni))

  1. sudden
  2. immediate, rapid
