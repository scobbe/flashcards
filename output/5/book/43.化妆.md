## to put on makeup
---
- **traditional:** 化妝
- **simplified:** 化妆
- **pronunciation:** huàzhuāng
- **definition:** to put on makeup
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 化妆(妝)中 (huàzhuāngzhōng, "putting on makeup")
  - 化妆(妝)品 (huàzhuāngpǐn, "cosmetic products")
  - 化妆(妝)师(師) (huàzhuāngshī, "makeup artist")
  - 化妆(妝)室 (huàzhuāngshì, "makeup room")
- **etymology (化妝):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 化 (huà, "make into") + 妆(妝) (zhuāng, "adornment").
  - **interpretation:** 化 means to transform or make into, and 妆 means adornment or makeup. Together they denote transforming one’s appearance with adornment, yielding the modern sense “to put on makeup.”
  - **component characters:**
    - 化
    - 妆
    - 妝
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%8C%96%E5%A6%9D
  - **simplification rule (化妆):** The traditional 妝 was simplified to 妆 by replacing the complex right-side component with a simpler form, reducing strokes; 化 remained unchanged.
