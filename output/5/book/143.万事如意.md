## may all your wishes come true
---
- **traditional:** 萬事如意
- **simplified:** 万事如意
- **pronunciation:** wàn shì rú yì
- **definition:** may all your wishes come true
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 万(萬)事如意，新年快乐(樂) (wàn shì rú yì, xīn nián kuài lè, "happy new year and all the best")
  - 万(萬)事如意，心想事成 (wàn shì rú yì, xīn xiǎng shì chéng, "all the best, wishes granted")
  - 万(萬)事如意，出入平安 (wàn shì rú yì, chū rù píng ān, "all the best, safe travels")
  - 万(萬)事如意红(紅)包 (wàn shì rú yì hóng bāo, "greeting red envelope")
- **etymology (萬事如意):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 万(萬) (wàn, "ten thousand") + 事 (shì, "affairs") + 如 (rú, "as") + 意 (yì, "wish").
  - **interpretation:** Literally “ten thousand affairs as (one’s) wish,” expressing the hope that everything goes according to one’s intentions. The parts combine to convey a comprehensive blessing for all matters to align with one’s desires, hence its use as a well-wishing greeting.
  - **component characters:**
    - 万
    - 萬
    - 事
    - 如
    - 意
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%90%AC%E4%BA%8B%E5%A6%82%E6%84%8F
  - **simplification rule (万事如意):** The simplified form replaces 萬 with 万 through component replacement and stroke reduction, while 事、如、意 remain unchanged.
