## anxious; worried
---
- **traditional:** 著急
- **simplified:** 着急
- **pronunciation:** zháojí
- **definition:** anxious; worried
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 着(著)急什么(麼) (zháo jí shénme, "what are you worried about")
  - 着(著)急上火 (zháo jí shàng huǒ, "get worked up")
  - 着(著)急忙慌 (zháo jí máng huāng, "in a flurry")
  - 着(著)急得很 (zháo jí de hěn, "very anxious")
- **etymology (著急):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 着(著) (zháo, "be affected") + 急 (jí, "urgent") → affected by urgency → to feel anxious.
  - **interpretation:** 着(著) here means to be affected by or to catch something; 急 means urgency or anxiety. Together they literally mean being affected by urgency, hence feeling anxious in modern usage.
  - **component characters:**
    - 着
    - 著
    - 急
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%91%97%E6%80%A5
  - **simplification rule (着急):** Character merger: 著 was replaced with 着 in Simplified Chinese for this word, unifying forms and reducing strokes.
