## utensil
### sub-word
#### musical instrument
---
- **traditional:** 器
- **simplified:** 器
- **pronunciation:** qì
- **definition:** utensil
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 器官 (qìguān, "organ")
  - 器材 (qìcái, "equipment")
  - 器具 (qìjù, "utensils")
  - 器乐(樂) (qìyuè, "instrumental music")
- **etymology (器):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 犬 (quǎn, "dog") + 口 (kǒu, "vessel") + 口 (kǒu, "vessel") + 口 (kǒu, "vessel") + 口 (kǒu, "vessel").
  - **interpretation:** Classical interpretation holds it depicts a 犬 (quǎn, "dog") guarding four 口 (kǒu, "vessels"), yielding the idea of containers/implements, hence the modern senses 'utensil, device' and extension to abstract 'capacity' and functional 'organs'. Alternative views suggest a phonetic loan meaning 'to bark' or a derivation from 喪 (sāng, "mourning") as an ideogram for a funerary urn; these remain debated.
  - **component characters:**
    - 犬
    - 口
