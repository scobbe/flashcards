## to drive a car
---
- **traditional:** 開車
- **simplified:** 开车
- **pronunciation:** kāichē
- **definition:** to drive a car
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 开(開)车(車)上班 (kāi chē shàng bān, "drive to work")
  - 开(開)车(車)的 (kāi chē de, "driver")
  - 开(開)车(車)人 (kāi chē rén, "driver")
  - 开(開)车(車)去接你 (kāi chē qù jiē nǐ, "drive to pick you up")
- **etymology (開車):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 开(開) (kāi, "start") + 车(車) (chē, "vehicle").
  - **interpretation:** 开 denotes starting or operating, while 车 specifies the domain of vehicles; together they mean to start or operate a vehicle, i.e., to drive. Modern slang extensions relating to sexual content are noted as inspired by a Yunnan folk song.
  - **component characters:**
    - 开
    - 開
    - 车
    - 車
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E9%96%8B%E8%BB%8A
  - **simplification rule (开车):** Simplified by replacing 開 with 开 and 車 with 车, reducing strokes and regularizing components under PRC reforms.
