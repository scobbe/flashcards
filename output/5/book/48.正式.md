## formal
---
- **traditional:** 正式
- **simplified:** 正式
- **pronunciation:** zhèngshì
- **definition:** formal
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 正式穿着(著) (zhèngshì chuānzhuó, "formal attire")
  - 正式报告(報告) (zhèngshì bàogào, "official report")
  - 正式场(場)合 (zhèngshì chǎnghé, "formal setting")
  - 正式工作 (zhèngshì gōngzuò, "regular job")
- **etymology (正式):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 正 (zhèng, "upright") + 式 (shì, "style").
  - **interpretation:** 正 means upright/proper and 式 means style/form. Together they literally mean “proper style,” which naturally extends to the modern sense “formal” and, by context, “official.”
  - **component characters:**
    - 正
    - 式
