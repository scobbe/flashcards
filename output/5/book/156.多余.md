## surplus; unneeded
---
- **traditional:** 多餘
- **simplified:** 多余
- **pronunciation:** duōyú
- **definition:** surplus; unneeded
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 多余(餘)的 (duōyú de, "not necessary")
  - 多余(餘)的话(話) (duōyú de huà, "needless words")
  - 多余(餘)的时(時)间(間) (duōyú de shíjiān, "spare time")
  - 多余(餘)的人 (duōyú de rén, "unneeded person")
- **etymology (多餘):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 多 (duō, "many") + 余(餘) (yú, "surplus").
  - **interpretation:** Formed from 多 (duō, "many") and 余(餘) (yú, "surplus"), indicating an excess beyond what is needed. This yields the modern sense of something being unnecessary or redundant.
  - **component characters:**
    - 多
    - 余
    - 餘
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%A4%9A%E9%A4%98
  - **simplification rule (多余):** Component replacement: 餘 was simplified to 余 by dropping the 食 component, giving the simplified disyllable 多余.
