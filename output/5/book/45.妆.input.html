Translingual

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Simplified from 妝 (爿 → 丬)

Han character

[edit]

妆 (Kangxi radical 38, 女+3, 6 strokes, cangjie input 中一女 (LMV), composition ⿰丬女)

  1. to adorn oneself, dress up, use makeup

References

[edit]
  • Kangxi Dictionary: not present, would follow page 256, character 4
  • Hanyu Da Zidian (first edition): volume 2, page 1027, character 7
  • Unihan data for U+5986

Chinese

[edit]
For pronunciation and definitions of 妆 – see 妝 (“to put on makeup; to adorn oneself; adornment, makeup and dress; etc.”).
(This character is the simplified form of 妝).
Notes:
  • Simplified Chinese is mainly used in Mainland China, Malaysia, and Singapore.
  • Traditional Chinese is mainly used in Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan.

Definitions

[edit]

妝

  1. to put on makeup; to adorn oneself
  2. adornment, makeup and dress
    • (Can we date this quote?), “晨光照入外马路”, 沈丹扬 (lyrics), 李亦瀚 (music)‎[1]performed by 黄堃:
      歲月淡了你個妝　濃了你個情 [Teochew, trad.]
      岁月淡了你个妆　浓了你个情 [Teochew, simp.]
      suê3 ghuêh8 dan6 liao2 le2 gai7 zuang1, nong5 liao2 le2 gai7 cêng5 [Peng'im]
      The passing of time has lightened your makeup and enriched your affection
  3. trousseau; dowry
  4. (Wenzhounese) to do; to make
