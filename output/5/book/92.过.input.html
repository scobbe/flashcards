Chinese

[edit]
For pronunciation and definitions of 过 – see 過 (“to cross; to pass; to go across; to pass through; to pass by; to pass; etc.”).
(This character is the simplified and variant form of 過).
Notes:
  • Simplified Chinese is mainly used in Mainland China, Malaysia, and Singapore.
  • Traditional Chinese is mainly used in Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan.

Etymology 1

[edit]

From Proto-Sino-Tibetan *m/s-ka-j (“to go, to stride”) (STEDT). Related to 跨 (OC *kʰʷraː, *kʰʷraːʔ, *kʰʷraːs, *kʰʷaːs, “to pass over”) and 騎 (OC *ɡral, *ɡrals, “to ride”).

    坐這艘船過河很安全。 [MSC, trad.]

    Please hurry up to pass the security check, it's already boarding time.
    過了百貨公司便向右拐。 [MSC, trad.]
    过了百货公司便向右拐。 [MSC, simp.]
    Guò le bǎihuò gōngsī biàn xiàng yòu guǎi. [Pinyin]
    Turn to the right after passing the department store.
  2. (of time) to pass by; to pass
    時間過得很快。／时间过得很快。  ―  Shíjiān guò de hěn kuài.  ―  Time flies quickly.
    過了許多年我們才偶然相逢。 [MSC, trad.]

    Guò le xǔduō nián wǒmen cái ǒurán xiāngféng. [Pinyin]
    Many years were to pass before we crossed our paths again.
  3. to spend time; to live
    過著艱苦的生活 [MSC, trad.]
    过着艰苦的生活 [MSC, simp.]
    guò zhe jiānkǔ de shēnghuó [Pinyin]

  4. to transfer; to move to

    Put the boiled noodles through cold water.
  6. to go over; to look at carefully; to go through

Usage notes

    • In Cantonese, 未 (mei6) is used instead of 沒 / 没.
