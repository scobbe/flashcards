## musical composition
---
- **traditional:** 樂曲
- **simplified:** 乐曲
- **pronunciation:** yuèqǔ
- **definition:** musical composition
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 乐(樂)曲名作 (yuè qǔ míng zuò, "famous composition")
  - 乐(樂)曲欣赏(賞) (yuè qǔ xīn shǎng, "music appreciation")
  - 乐(樂)曲演奏 (yuè qǔ yǎn zòu, "perform a piece")
  - 乐(樂)曲创(創)作 (yuè qǔ chuàng zuò, "compose music")
- **etymology (樂曲):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 乐(樂) (yuè, "music") + 曲 (qǔ, "tune").
  - **interpretation:** Combines 乐(樂) (yuè, "music") and 曲 (qǔ, "tune") to denote a musical piece or composition. In modern usage it refers to musical compositions, often contrasted with 歌曲 (gēqǔ, "song with lyrics").
  - **component characters:**
    - 乐
    - 樂
    - 曲
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E6%A8%82%E6%9B%B2
  - **simplification rule (乐曲):** Component replacement and stroke reduction: 樂 was simplified to 乐 while 曲 remained unchanged, so the disyllabic word adopts the simplified first character.
