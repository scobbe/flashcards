Ideogram (指事): a man with a painted or tattooed chest – original form of 紋. The tattoo was usually a cross, a V shape or a stylized heart. In current form, tattoo has vanished, so simply an outline of a man (head, outstretched arms, legs, chest) similar to 立. Compare 大, which has no chest.

Unrelated to the Kangxi radicals 齐 and 攵.

Etymology

[edit]

Possibly a derivation, with -n nominal suffix, of 煤 (*mɯː) "soot (obs.), ink (obs.), coal" (Schuessler, 2007).

Semantic shift: "pattern, tattoo (made of soot)", "written glyph (made of ink)" > "literature" > "refined, cultured" (Schuessler, 2007).

Definitions

[edit]

文

  1. (obsolete on its own in Standard Chinese) Original form of 紋／纹 (wén, “mark, pattern, design, tattoo, decoration”).
    文如貍／文如狸 [Classical Chinese]  ―  wén rú lí [Pinyin]  ―  having a pattern like that of a wild cat
  2. Original form of 紋／纹 (wén, “to tattoo”).
    文身  ―  wénshēn  ―  to tattoo
    文面  ―  wénmiàn  ―  face tattoo
  3. writing; literary composition; literature (Classifier: 篇 c)
    文章  ―  wénzhāng  ―  article, essay
    散文  ―  sǎnwén  ―  prose
    文不對題／文不对题  ―  wénbùduìtí  ―  (of writing or speech) to be beside the point; to be off-topic; to be irrelevant to the subject
    呢篇文寫得唔錯。 [Cantonese, trad.]
    呢篇文写得唔错。 [Cantonese, simp.]
    ni1 pin1 man4-2 se2 dak1 m4 co3. [Jyutping]
    This essay is written quite well.
  4. official document
  5. literary language; Literary Chinese
    半文半白  ―  bànwénbànbái  ―  half literary, half vernacular
  6. short for 文讀／文读 (wéndú, “literary reading”)
  7. script; written form
    甲骨文  ―  jiǎgǔwén  ―  oracle bone script
    書同文，車同軌。 [Classical Chinese, trad.]
    书同文，车同轨。 [Classical Chinese, simp.]
    Shū tóng wén, chē tóng guǐ. [Pinyin]
    Same ruts for carriages, same script for writing. (i.e., universal standards)
    天城文  ―  tiānchéngwén  ―  Devanagari
  8. (written) language
    Antonym: 語 / 语
    中文  ―  zhōngwén  ―  Chinese
    英文  ―  yīngwén  ―  English
  9. culture
    文化  ―  wénhuà  ―  culture
    文明  ―  wénmíng  ―  civilization
  10. liberal arts; humanities
  11. natural or social phenomenon
    天文  ―  tiānwén  ―  astronomy
    人文  ―  rénwén  ―  humanities; letters
  12. ritual; etiquette
    繁文縟節／繁文缛节  ―  fánwénrùjié  ―  (please add an English translation of this usage example)
  13. of a government position, non-military: civil, civilian
    文職／文职  ―  wénzhí  ―  civilian post
    文官  ―  wénguān  ―  civil official
    文武  ―  wénwǔ  ―  civilian and military
  14. gentle; refined
    斯文  ―  sīwén  ―  refined, cultured
    文雅  ―  wényǎ  ―  elegant, refined
  15. 47th tetragram of the Taixuanjing; "pattern, repetition" (𝌴)
  16. (archaic) Classifier for coins.
  17. (numismatics) mill (a tenth of a cent)
    一文  ―  yī wén  ―  one mill
  18. (～日) (telegraphy) the twelfth day of a month
    文夕大火  ―  Wénxī Dàhuǒ  ―  1938 Changsha fire
  19. a surname
    文天祥  ―  Wén Tiānxiáng  ―  Wen Tianxiang (Chinese politician and poet in the last years of the Southern Song Dynasty)

Noun

[edit]

文(もん) • (mon) 

  1. the mon, an old currency
  2. a unit of length for measuring the size of one's foot (from the way that the mon coins would be lined up and used as a kind of inch marker)

  4. writing, something written
  5. a magical spell
  6. (also spelled 紋) a design, a pattern

Etymology 3

[edit]
Kanji in this term
文
ふみ
Grade: 1
kun'yomi

Possibly a shift from an older Middle Chinese-derived reading pun.[3] Earlier Japanese writing was ambiguous regarding the final n sound spelled ん in modern Japanese, with this sound often spelled (and possibly pronounced) as む (mu) instead.

*/pumu/ → /pumi/ → /ɸumi/

This reading appears in the Heian period, in works such as The Tale of Genji.[3][1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • (Tokyo) ふみ [fúꜜmì] (Atamadaka – [1])[1][2]
  • IPA(key): [ɸɯ̟mʲi]

Noun

[edit]

文(ふみ) • (fumi) 

  1. writing, something written
  2. a letter (written communication), an epistle
  3. (archaic) study, scholarship (especially of classical Chinese)
  4. a style of 家紋 (kamon, “family crest”)

[edit]

kun'yomi

Uncertain. Appears in texts from the early Heian period.[3][1] Compare Miyako あやぐ (ayagu), あーぐ (āgu).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • (Tokyo) あや [àyáꜜ] (Odaka – [2])[1][2]
  • IPA(key): [a̠ja̠]

Noun

[edit]

文(あや) • (aya) 

  1. (also spelled 紋, 綾)
    1. 紋, 綾: a pattern, a design, especially one with diagonally crossing lines
    2. 紋, 綾: a diagonal crossing
    3. 紋, 綾: a flowery expression or turn of phrase
    4. 紋, 綾: a plot, a scheme
    5. 紋, 綾: a refrain or recurring phrase in a piece of music
    6. 紋, 綾: a harder vein or grain in a leaf or piece of wood or bamboo
    7. 紋, 綾: a stain, a blemish
    8. 紋, 綾: a mediator, a go-between (apparently from the way that a go-between can make the relationship more indirect and indistinct)
    9. 紋, 綾: a small indistinct fluctuation in a trend line
  2. (also spelled 綾, 絢)
    1. 綾, 絢: a kind of woven silk textile with a diagonally crossing pattern
    2. 綾, 絢: short for 綾織 (ayaori): a twill weave; someone who weaves twill
    3. 綾, 絢: short for 綾竹 (ayadake): in a loom, a bamboo pole placed between the heddle and the back beam or platen, used to improve the handling
    4. 綾, 絢: short for 綾取り (ayatori): cat's cradle
Usage notes
[edit]

For the second group of senses, the 綾 spelling is more common.
