## to play (instrumental)
### sub-word
#### to perform (instrumental)
---
- **traditional:** 奏
- **simplified:** 奏
- **pronunciation:** zòu
- **definition:** to play (instrumental)
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 演奏 (yǎn zòu, "perform music")
  - 吹奏 (chuī zòu, "play a wind instrument")
  - 奏乐(樂) (zòu yuè, "play music")
  - 奏鸣(鳴)曲 (zòu míng qǔ, "a sonata")
- **etymology (奏):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 廾 (gǒng, "two hands") + 夲 (běn, "base") → two hands holding a ritual instrument.
  - **interpretation:** It depicts two hands holding a ritual musical implement, evoking the act of performance. This concrete imagery motivates the modern musical sense seen in compounds meaning to perform or play instruments. An alternative view describes 廾 (gǒng, "two hands") with 屮 (chè, "sprout") and 桼 (qī, "resin"), showing resin collection, but the performance-based reading better fits today’s musical usage.
  - **component characters:**
    - 廾
    - 夲
