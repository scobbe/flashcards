## house
### sub-component
#### surplus; remainder
---
- **traditional:** 舍
- **simplified:** 舍
- **pronunciation:** shè
- **definition:** house
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 宿舍 (sùshè, "dormitory")
  - 左邻(鄰)右舍 (zuǒlín yòushè, "neighbors next door")
  - 猪(豬)舍 (zhūshè, "pigsty")
  - 校舍 (xiàoshè, "school building")
- **etymology (舍):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** pictogram of 余 (yú, "surplus") → phono-semantic: phonetic: 余 (yú, "surplus") + semantic: 口 (kǒu, "mouth").
  - **interpretation:** 余 originally wrote this word; 口 was later added as a semantic determinative. 余 provides the sound, while 口, denoting an opening or entrance, signals a place to stop or lodge, which underlies the modern senses of a lodging or house.
  - **component characters:**
    - 余 (yú, "surplus")
    - 口 (kǒu, "mouth")
