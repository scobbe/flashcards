## so
---
- **traditional:** 所以
- **simplified:** 所以
- **pronunciation:** suǒyǐ
- **definition:** so
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 所以他 (suǒyǐ tā, "so he")
  - 所以说(說) (suǒyǐ shuō, "so, therefore")
  - 所以呢 (suǒyǐ ne, "so then")
  - 所以导(導)致 (suǒyǐ dǎozhì, "therefore leads to")
- **etymology (所以):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 所 (suǒ, "that which") + 以 (yǐ, "by means of").
  - **interpretation:** Combining 所 ‘that which’ with 以 ‘by means of’ yields the literal sense ‘that by which; means,’ which in Classical Chinese functioned as a nominal phrase. This semantic basis extended to mark reasons and results, giving the modern conjunction sense ‘therefore; so’ that links cause to effect.
  - **component characters:**
    - 所
    - 以
