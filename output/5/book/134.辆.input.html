Glyph origin

[edit]
Characters in the same phonetic series (兩) (Zhengzhang, 2003) 
Old Chinese
倆 *raŋʔ
兩 *raŋʔ, *raŋs
脼 *raŋʔ
緉 *raŋʔ, *raŋs
蜽 *raŋʔ
魎 *raŋʔ
輛 *raŋs

Phono-semantic compound (形聲 / 形声, OC *raŋs): semantic 車 (“vehicle”) + phonetic 兩 (OC *raŋʔ, *raŋs).

Etymology

[edit]

Generally thought to be the exopassive of 兩 (OC *raŋʔ, *raŋs, “two”), i.e. "that which is paired (a set of wheels)" (Schuessler, 2007). That said, note similarities to Proto-Tibeto-Burman *s-ljaŋ (“cart”), whence Burmese လှည်း (hlany:, “cart”) (Luce, 1981).

Definitions

[edit]

輛

  1. Classifier for wheeled vehicles. ⇒ all nouns using this classifier
    一輛車／一辆车  ―  yī liàng chē  ―  a car
    一輛跑車／一辆跑车  ―  yī liàng pǎochē  ―  a race car
