## to go
### sub-word
#### in or of the past
---
- **traditional:** 去
- **simplified:** 去
- **pronunciation:** qù
- **definition:** to go
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 你去哪儿(兒) (Nǐ qù nǎr, "Where are you going")
  - 去世 (qùshì, "pass away")
  - 去年 (qùnián, "last year")
  - 去掉 (qùdiào, "remove")
- **etymology (去):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 大 (dà, "man") + 口 (kǒu, "mouth").
  - **interpretation:** Traditionally analyzed as an ideogram combining 大 (dà, "man") and 口 (kǒu, "mouth"), depicting a person leaving an opening, hence 'to depart' and by extension 'to remove'. Some scholars instead see a man with a marked orifice (anus), conveying 'to get rid of'. Shuowen treats it as phono-semantic, with semantic 大 (dà, "man") and a phonetic element pronounced qū.
  - **component characters:**
    - 大
    - 口
