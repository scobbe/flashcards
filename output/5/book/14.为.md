## for
### sub-word
#### because
---
- **traditional:** 為
- **simplified:** 为
- **pronunciation:** wèi
- **definition:** for
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 为(為)主 (wéi zhǔ, "be primary")
  - 为(為)止 (wéi zhǐ, "until")
  - 为(為)期 (wéi qī, "for a duration")
  - 为(為)数(數) (wéi shù, "numbering")
- **etymology (為):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 又 (yòu, "hand") + 象 (xiàng, "elephant") → 爪 (zhǎo, "hand; claw") + 象 (xiàng, "elephant").
  - **interpretation:** It depicts a hand handling an elephant, conveying the idea of acting or doing. This original sense extends to modern uses meaning to do/act and, by a derivative usage noted in historical sources, the prepositional sense “for; because”. Shuowen alternatively interprets the graphic as a female macaque.
  - **component characters:**
    - 又
    - 象
    - 爪
  - **reference:** https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%82%BA
  - **simplification rule (为):** Simplified from 爲 by adopting a cursive-derived form, collapsing the 爪 and 象 components into a streamlined shape with fewer strokes.
