## meaning or intention
### sub-word
#### may all your wishes come true
---
- **traditional:** 意
- **simplified:** 意
- **pronunciation:** yì
- **definition:** meaning or intention
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 意义(義) (yìyì, "meaning and significance")
  - 意见(見) (yìjiàn, "opinion and view")
  - 意识(識) (yìshí, "awareness and consciousness")
  - 意愿(願) (yìyuàn, "wish and desire")
- **etymology (意):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 音 (yīn, "sound") + 心 (xīn, "heart") → 意 (yì, "meaning").
  - **interpretation:** Combines 音 (yīn, "sound") above 心 (xīn, "heart") to depict what the heart voices, hence thought, meaning, and intention. Although the modern top resembles 音, sources note it may not be historically 音; Lin (1920) analyzes an earlier form as 言 (yán, "speech") + 中 (zhōng, "middle"), emphasizing expressed sense. In all analyses, the semantic core links heart/mind with expression, matching the modern meanings.
  - **component characters:**
    - 音 (yīn, "sound")
    - 心 (xīn, "heart")
