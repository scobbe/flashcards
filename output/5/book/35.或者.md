## or
---
- **traditional:** 或者
- **simplified:** 或者
- **pronunciation:** huòzhě
- **definition:** or
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 或者去听(聽)音乐(樂)会(會) (huò zhě qù tīng yīn yuè huì, "or go to a concert")
  - 或者是 (huò zhě shì, "or maybe")
  - 或者说(說) (huò zhě shuō, "in other words")
  - 或者明天 (huò zhě míng tiān, "maybe tomorrow")
- **etymology (或者):**
  - **type:** semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 或 (huò, "or") + 者 (zhě, "one") → Classical phrase meaning "some people" → later extended to adverb/conjunction "perhaps; or".
  - **interpretation:** In Classical Chinese, 或 meant possibility or some, and 者 is a nominalizer meaning one/person, so 或者 literally meant some people. The phrase then generalized to mark alternative possibilities and uncertainty, yielding the modern conjunction or and adverb perhaps.
  - **component characters:**
    - 或
    - 者
