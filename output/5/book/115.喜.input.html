Definitions

[edit]

喜

  1. happy; pleased; joyful
    喜迎  ―  xǐ yíng  ―  to welcome happily
    喜出望外  ―  xǐchūwàngwài  ―  to be pleasantly surprised
  2. joyful; worth celebrating
    喜訊／喜讯  ―  xǐxùn  ―  happy news
    喜筵  ―  xǐyán  ―  banquet for a happy event (typically a wedding feast)
  3. joyful thing; happy event
    報喜／报喜  ―  bàoxǐ  ―  to announce good news
  4. pregnancy
    有喜  ―  yǒuxǐ  ―  to be pregnant
    害喜  ―  hàixǐ  ―  to have morning sickness
  5. (dated, euphemistic) variola; smallpox
  6. to be fond of; to like; to love; to enjoy
  7. (of an organism) to need; to require
    喜光植物  ―  xǐ guāng zhíwù  ―  photophilous plant
    • 仙人掌喜充足陽光，耐旱不耐寒，喜排水良好的砂質壤土。 [MSC, trad.]
      仙人掌喜充足阳光，耐旱不耐寒，喜排水良好的砂质壤土。 [MSC, simp.]
      From: 彭光途; 陆家怡; 梅慧敏 (1987), 花卉栽培技术, Beijing: 中国林业出版社, →ISBN, page 177
      Xiānrénzhǎng xǐ chōngzú yángguāng, nàihàn bù nàihán, xǐ páishuǐ liánghǎo de shā zhì rǎngtǔ. [Pinyin]
      (please add an English translation of this usage example)
  8. (literary) to be prone to
  9. a surname

喜

(Fifth grade kyōiku kanji)

  1. to rejoice

Readings

[edit]
  • Go-on: こ (ko)
  • Kan-on: き (ki, Jōyō)
  • Kun: よろこぶ (yorokobu, 喜ぶ, Jōyō)、よろこび (yorokobi, 喜び)

Compounds

[edit]
  • 喜(き)劇(げき) (kigeki, “comedy”)
  • 喜(き)捨(しゃ) (kisha)
  • 悲(ひ)喜(き) (hiki, “joys and sorrows”)

Etymology 1

[edit]

喜
よし
Grade: 5
nanori

Likely from Classical Japanese adjective 良(よ)し (yoshi, “good”), modern 良(よ)い (yoi) or いい (ii).

Pronunciation

[edit]

Proper noun

[edit]

  1. a male given name
