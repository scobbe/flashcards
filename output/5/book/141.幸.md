## good fortune
### sub-word
#### happy
---
- **traditional:** 幸
- **simplified:** 幸
- **pronunciation:** xìng
- **definition:** good fortune
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 幸运(運) (xìngyùn, "good luck")
  - 幸福 (xìngfú, "happiness")
  - 幸亏(虧) (xìngkuī, "luckily")
  - 幸好 (xìnghǎo, "fortunately")
- **etymology (幸):**
  - **type:** phono-semantic compound
  - **description:** semantic: 犬 (quǎn, "dog") + phonetic: 羊 (yáng, "sheep") → 幸 (xìng, "fortunate").
  - **interpretation:** In Warring States forms, 犬 (quǎn, "dog") supplied the semantic domain of auspicious animals, while 羊 (yáng, "sheep") provided the sound; together they yielded the sense of good fortune that matches modern usage. An alternative Shuowen view analyzes it as 屰 (nì, "opposite") + 夭 (yāo, "premature death"), interpreted as being lucky to escape death.
  - **component characters:**
    - 犬
    - 羊
    - 幸
