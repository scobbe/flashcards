## utensil
---
- **traditional:** 器
- **simplified:** 器
- **pronunciation:** qì
- **definition:** utensil
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 乐(樂)器 (yuèqì, "musical instrument")
  - 武器 (wǔqì, "weapon")
  - 器官 (qìguān, "organ")
  - 容器 (róngqì, "container")
- **etymology (器):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 犬 (quǎn, "dog") + 口 (kǒu, "vessel") + 口 (kǒu, "vessel") + 口 (kǒu, "vessel") + 口 (kǒu, "vessel").
  - **interpretation:** Traditionally interpreted as a composition of 犬 (quǎn, "dog") with four 口 (kǒu, "vessel"), evoking a guard dog watching over vessels, hence the sense of containers and by extension tools and utensils. Alternative views note a possible phonetic borrowing ‘to bark’ or derivation from 喪 (sāng, "mourning") as an ideogram for a funerary urn, but the vessel/tool sense best explains its modern meanings.
  - **component characters:**
    - 犬
    - 口
