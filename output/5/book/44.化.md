## to change
### sub-word
#### to put on makeup
---
- **traditional:** 化
- **simplified:** 化
- **pronunciation:** huà
- **definition:** to change
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 化学(學) (huà xué, "chemical science")
  - 变化 (biàn huà, "change and transformation")
  - 美化 (měi huà, "to beautify")
  - 简(簡)化 (jiǎn huà, "to simplify")
- **etymology (化):**
  - **type:** ideogrammic compound
  - **description:** ideogrammic: 人 (rén, "person") + 人 (rén, "person").
  - **interpretation:** It depicts two persons, with one inverted to express reversal or transformation, yielding the sense of change. This visual idea underlies the modern meanings ‘to change/transform’ and the suffix for -ize/-ization. Some sources (e.g., Shuowen) also treat the right component 𠤎 as phonetic, but the primary analysis is semantic.
  - **component characters:**
    - 人
