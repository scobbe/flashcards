## sound or tone
### sub-word
#### concert hall
---
- **traditional:** 音
- **simplified:** 音
- **pronunciation:** yīn
- **definition:** sound or tone
- **contemporary usage:**
  - 音乐(樂) (yīn yuè, "music")
  - 音频(頻) (yīn pín, "audio")
  - 音响(響) (yīn xiǎng, "audio system")
  - 音质(質) (yīn zhì, "sound quality")
- **etymology (音):**
  - **type:** ideogram
  - **description:** ideogram: 口 (kǒu, "mouth") + 言 (yán, "speech") with a tongue mark written as 甘 (gān, "sweet") → 甘 (gān, "sweet") stylized as 曰 (yuē, "say").
  - **interpretation:** Depicts a mouth 口 (kǒu, "mouth") with a tongue-like mark written as 甘 (gān, "sweet") together with speech 言 (yán, "speech"), representing sound emitted from the mouth. Later the vertical tongue stroke was dropped and 甘 (gān, "sweet") was stylized to 曰 (yuē, "say"), while the sense broadened to modern "sound/voice" and related tonal and phonetic meanings.
  - **component characters:**
    - 口
    - 言
    - 甘
    - 曰
